procurer:/prəˈkjʊərər/| n. |拉皮条的人；（尤指）为妓院招揽妓女的皮条客；采购员| A procurer is someone who obtains something, especially something difficult to get.  The term often carries a negative connotation, especially when referring to someone who procures prostitutes for others. |Example: He was arrested for acting as a procurer for a large prostitution ring. 他因充当大型卖淫团伙的拉皮条的人而被捕。|近义词：pimp, procurer, agent, supplier|反义词：customer, client


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The company procures all its supplies from a single supplier. = 这家公司所有的物资都从一个供应商那里采购。
He procures rare books for wealthy collectors. = 他为富有的收藏家采购珍本书籍。


一般过去时:
The police procured evidence to convict the criminal. = 警方找到证据将罪犯绳之以法。
She procured a visa after months of waiting. = 她经过几个月的等待后获得了签证。


现在完成时:
The government has procured enough vaccines for the entire population. = 政府已经为全国人民采购了足够的疫苗。
They have procured a new building for their expanding business. = 他们为不断扩张的业务采购了一栋新大楼。


过去完成时:
The detective had procured all the necessary information before the trial. = 侦探在审判前已经收集了所有必要的信息。
She had procured a ticket to the concert weeks in advance. = 她几周前就买好了音乐会的票。


将来时:
The company will procure new equipment next year. = 公司明年将采购新设备。
They will procure the necessary materials for the project. = 他们将为该项目采购必要的材料。


虚拟语气:
If I could procure a meeting with the CEO, I would. = 如果我能安排与首席执行官会面，我会的。
If she had procured the documents earlier, the deadline would have been met. = 如果她早点拿到文件，就能按时完成。


被动语态:
The necessary materials were procured by the project manager. = 项目经理采购了必要的材料。
The evidence was procured illegally. = 证据是非法获得的。


疑问句:
Where did you procure that antique vase? = 你在哪儿买到那个古董花瓶的？
How did they procure such a large sum of money? = 他们是怎么弄到这么多钱的？


否定句:
They could not procure the needed parts for the machine. = 他们无法为机器采购所需的零件。
He failed to procure the necessary funding for his research. = 他未能为他的研究筹集到必要的资金。
