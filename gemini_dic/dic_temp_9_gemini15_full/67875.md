fabricative:/ˈfæbrɪkeɪtɪv/| adj.|虚构的；捏造的；编造的| relating to or characterized by fabrication; invented or made up rather than genuine or true.  The fabricative nature of the story was evident in its inconsistencies.  这个故事的虚构性质在其前后矛盾之处显而易见。|近义词：fictitious, invented, contrived, artificial|反义词：genuine, authentic, real, factual|形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The evidence presented is fabricative. = 提出的证据是捏造的。
His claims are often fabricative. = 他的说法常常是虚构的。


一般过去时:
He fabricated a story to avoid punishment. = 他编造了一个故事来逃避惩罚。
She fabricated a false alibi. = 她编造了一个虚假的不在场证明。


现在完成时:
They have fabricated numerous excuses. = 他们已经编造了许多借口。
She has fabricated a whole new identity. = 她已经编造了一个全新的身份。


过去完成时:
He had fabricated a false document before the trial. = 他在审判前伪造了一份文件。
They had fabricated a lie to protect themselves. = 他们编造了一个谎言来保护自己。


将来时:
They will fabricate a new narrative to deflect blame. = 他们将编造一个新的说法来转移责任。
He will fabricate evidence to support his case. = 他将伪造证据来支持他的案情。


虚拟语气:
If he fabricated the evidence, he would be in trouble. = 如果他伪造了证据，他会有麻烦。
I wish he hadn't fabricated that story. = 我真希望他没有编造那个故事。


被动语态:
A false story was fabricated by the media. = 一个虚假的故事是由媒体编造的。
The evidence was fabricated to mislead the investigators. = 证据是伪造的，目的是误导调查人员。


疑问句:
Was the report fabricated? = 这份报告是伪造的吗？
Is the testimony fabricative? = 这份证词是捏造的吗？


否定句:
The account wasn't fabricated; it was truthful. = 这份叙述不是捏造的；它是真实的。
The evidence was not fabricative; it was genuine. = 证据不是虚构的；它是真实的。
