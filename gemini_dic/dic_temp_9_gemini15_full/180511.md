regent's:/ˈriːdʒənts/| n.|摄政王/女王的；摄政的；学院校董的|A regent is a person who governs a country or state in place of a monarch, typically because the monarch is too young or incapacitated.  The term can also refer to a member of a governing board, especially of a college or university.|例句：The regent ruled the kingdom wisely during the king's absence. 摄政王在国王缺席期间英明地统治着王国。|例句：The university's regents approved the new budget. 大学董事会批准了新的预算。|近义词：governor, ruler, trustee|反义词：subject, ruled
