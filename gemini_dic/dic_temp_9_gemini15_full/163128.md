precombatants:/ˌpriːkəmˈbætənts/| n. |战斗前人员，潜在战斗人员|Individuals or groups who are not yet actively engaged in combat but are likely to become combatants.  Precombatants are often civilians who may be mobilized or conscripted into military service, or who may take up arms in self-defense or rebellion. The term emphasizes the potential for future conflict and the vulnerability of these individuals.|例句：The humanitarian organization focused its efforts on providing aid to precombatants in the conflict zone.人道主义组织将工作重点放在向冲突地区潜在战斗人员提供援助。|近义词：potential combatants, civilians at risk, prospective soldiers|反义词：combatants, veterans
