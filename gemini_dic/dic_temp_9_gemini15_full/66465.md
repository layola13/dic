exequatur:/ˌɛksɪˈkweɪtər/| n. |许可证；批准；认可|A formal authorization, typically from a government, allowing a foreign official or organization to operate within its territory.  It's often granted to consuls, diplomats, or companies operating internationally.  The word itself implies the act of executing or carrying out a decree or authorization.|The foreign consul received his exequatur from the Ministry of Foreign Affairs.  外国领事从外交部收到了他的任职许可证。

The company obtained an exequatur to operate its business in the country. 这家公司获得了在该国开展业务的许可证。|许可，批准|拒绝，否决|名词
