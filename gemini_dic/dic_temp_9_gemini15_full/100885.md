intrusives:/ɪnˈtruːsɪvz/| n.|侵入体，侵入岩|Rocks that have intruded into surrounding rocks, often forming igneous bodies like batholiths, dikes, sills, and volcanic necks.  They are typically formed from magma that cools and solidifies beneath the Earth's surface.|例句：The granite mountains are examples of intrusives, formed from magma that cooled slowly deep underground.  花岗岩山脉是侵入岩的例子，由在地下深处缓慢冷却的岩浆形成。|近义词：intrusions, igneous intrusions|反义词：extrusives
