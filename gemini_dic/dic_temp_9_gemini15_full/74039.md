foryetten: /fərˈɡɒtən/ | adj. | 被遗忘的，被忽略的 | forgotten means something has been overlooked or left behind, not remembered. It often refers to something that happened in the past.  | Example: The old photograph lay forgotten in the attic.  老照片被遗忘在阁楼上了。 The forgotten toy lay under the bed. 被遗忘的玩具躺在床底下。|近义词：neglected, overlooked, disregarded | 反义词：remembered, recalled, remembered


USAGE->
一般现在时:
This old book is forgotten by many. = 这本旧书被许多人遗忘了。
The problem is often forgotten. = 这个问题经常被遗忘。


一般过去时:
I forgot my keys, and I was locked out. = 我忘了带钥匙，被锁在了门外。
She forgot her appointment, and was late. = 她忘记了约会，迟到了。


现在完成时:

过去完成时:
I had forgotten my promise, until he reminded me. = 我已经忘记了我的承诺，直到他提醒我。
They had forgotten about the meeting until the day before. = 他们直到前一天才想起会议。


将来时:
I will never forget this day. = 我永远不会忘记这一天。
This experience will not be forgotten. =  这段经历不会被遗忘。


虚拟语气:

被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:

词源:
The word "forgotten" is the past participle of the verb "forget."  It originates from Old English *forgietan, composed of *for- ("from," "away") and *gietan ("to get," "to seize").  The meaning evolved to imply losing something from one's mind or memory.
