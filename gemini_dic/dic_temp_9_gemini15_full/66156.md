excerpt:/ˈɛksɜːrpt/ | n. |节选，摘录 | A passage selected from a larger work.  |例句：This excerpt from the novel shows the author's style at its best.  这段小说的节选展现了作者最佳的文风。 |近义词：extract, passage, selection |反义词：whole, entirety |名词

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The article includes an excerpt from his new book. = 这篇文章包括了他新书中的节选。
This excerpt summarizes the key findings. = 这段摘录总结了主要发现。


一般过去时:
She excerpted the most important parts of the speech. = 她摘录了演讲中最重要的部分。
He excerpted relevant sections from the report. = 他从报告中摘录了相关的部分。


现在完成时:
I have excerpted several key paragraphs from the manuscript. = 我已经从手稿中摘录了几段关键段落。
She has excerpted the most relevant data for her presentation. = 她已经为演示文稿摘录了最相关的资料。


过去完成时:
They had excerpted the relevant information before the meeting. = 他们在会议之前已经摘录了相关信息。
He had excerpted the essential points from the lengthy document. = 他已从冗长的文件中摘录了要点。


将来时:

虚拟语气:
If I had more time, I would excerpt more passages from the book. = 如果我有更多时间，我会从书中摘录更多段落。

疑问句:
