enshrined:/ɪnˈʃraɪnd/ | v. | 奉为神圣；珍藏；铭记 | To enshrine something means to cherish and protect it, often in a sacred or formal way.  It implies a deep respect and reverence for the thing being enshrined. | The Constitution enshrines the rights of all citizens.  宪法保障所有公民的权利。| The museum enshrines the history of the city. 博物馆珍藏着这座城市的历史。 | cherish, preserve, protect | desecrate, destroy, violate | 动词


USAGE->

The museum enshrines many historical artifacts. = 博物馆珍藏着许多历史文物。

词源:

The word "enshrine" comes from the Old English "in-scrinan,"  meaning "to place in a shrine."  "Shrine" itself derives from Old English "scrin," meaning "a chest or casket, a receptacle for sacred objects."  The prefix "en-" intensifies the meaning.
