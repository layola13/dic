catastrophes:/ˈkætəstrɑːfiz/| n.|大灾难，灾祸|A catastrophe is a sudden and widespread disaster.  It often refers to events that cause significant loss of life, destruction of property, and disruption of social order.  The term is often used to describe natural disasters, such as earthquakes, floods, and hurricanes, but it can also refer to man-made disasters, such as wars and industrial accidents.|例句：The earthquake was a terrible catastrophe, leaving thousands homeless.  这场地震是一场可怕的灾难，导致数千人无家可归。

The Chernobyl nuclear disaster was a major catastrophe. 切尔诺贝利核灾难是一场重大的灾难。|近义词：disaster, calamity, cataclysm|反义词：blessing, good fortune
