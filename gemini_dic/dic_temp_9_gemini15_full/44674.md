crucifies:/ˈkruːsɪfaɪz/ | vt. | 钉死在十字架上；折磨；使痛苦 | To put someone to death by nailing them to a cross.  Figuratively, to severely criticize or punish someone. |例句：They crucified him on a hill outside the city. = 他们在城外的一座山上将他钉死在十字架上。

They crucified him with their criticism. = 他们用批评折磨他。|近义词：execute, torment, afflict | 反义词：save, liberate, comfort |


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He crucifies his opponents in the press. = 他在媒体上痛批他的对手。
The media crucifies celebrities for the slightest mistake. = 媒体因一点小错就严厉批评名人。


一般过去时:
The Romans crucified Jesus. = 罗马人将耶稣钉死在十字架上。
The public crucified the politician for his lies. = 公众因他的谎言而严厉批评这位政治家。


现在完成时:
The press has crucified him for his past actions. = 媒体因他过去的行动而严厉批评他。
She has felt crucified by the constant criticism. = 她一直受到批评的折磨。


过去完成时:
They had already crucified him in the court of public opinion before the trial. = 在审判之前，舆论已经将他钉在了耻辱柱上。
She had been crucified by the media for years before she finally spoke out. = 在她最终说出真相之前，她多年来一直受到媒体的严厉批评。


将来时:
They will crucify him if he doesn't apologize. = 如果他不道歉，他们将会严厉批评他。
The public will crucify the company if the product is defective. = 如果产品有缺陷，公众将会严厉批评该公司。


虚拟语气:
If he had told the truth, he wouldn't have been crucified. = 如果他说了实话，他就不会受到严厉批评。
I wish they hadn't crucified him so publicly. = 我希望他们不要在公众面前那样严厉地批评他。


被动语态:
He was crucified for his beliefs. = 他因他的信仰而被钉死在十字架上。
The politician was crucified by the media for his scandal. = 这位政治家因丑闻而受到媒体的严厉批评。


疑问句:
Will they crucify him for his mistake? = 他们会因他的错误而严厉批评他吗？
Was he crucified for his political views? = 他因为他的政治观点而被钉死在十字架上吗？


否定句:
They didn't crucify him for his actions. = 他们没有因为他的行为而严厉批评他。
The judge didn't crucify the defendant, but gave him a lenient sentence. = 法官没有严厉惩罚被告，而是给了他一个宽大的判决。
