extreme:/ɪkˈstriːm/| adj. n.| 极端的；极度；极端的事物 | extremely great in degree; very severe or intense.  |例句：The extreme heat caused the power outage.  极度高温导致了停电。

例句：He went to extremes to prove his point. 他为了证明自己的观点不择手段。


近义词：radical, intense, utmost, severe | 反义词：moderate, mild, average, temperate


词性: adjective, noun


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The weather is extreme today. = 今天天气很极端。
This task is extremely difficult. = 这个任务极其困难。


一般过去时:
The situation was extreme yesterday. = 昨天情况很危急。
The pain was extreme after the surgery. = 手术后疼痛剧烈。


现在完成时:
The weather has been extreme this week. = 这周天气一直很极端。
The pressure has become extreme recently. = 最近压力变得很大。


过去完成时:
The damage had become extreme before the rescue team arrived. = 在救援队到达之前，损害已经非常严重了。
The situation had been extreme before the intervention. = 在干预之前，情况非常危急。


将来时:
The weather will be extreme next week. = 下周天气将非常恶劣。
The consequences will be extreme if we don't act now. = 如果我们现在不采取行动，后果将不堪设想。


虚拟语气:
If the weather were extreme, we would stay home. = 如果天气很恶劣，我们就待在家里。
If the situation had been less extreme, we could have acted differently. = 如果情况没那么危急，我们本可以采取不同的行动。


被动语态:
Extreme measures were taken to control the fire. = 采取了极端措施来控制火势。
The problem is considered extreme by many experts. = 许多专家认为这个问题非常严重。


疑问句:
Is the situation really that extreme? = 情况真的那么糟糕吗？
Are the conditions extreme enough to warrant a change of plans? = 条件是否极端到需要改变计划？


否定句:
The weather isn't extreme today. = 今天天气并不极端。
The measures taken were not extreme enough. = 采取的措施不够极端。

词源:
From Middle English extremite, from Old French extremite, from Latin extremitas, from extremus ("farthest, outermost"), superlative of exter ("outside, without").
