hypersuggestibleness: /ˌhaɪpərˌsədʒɛstəˈbɪlnəs/ | n. |高度暗示性，极度易受暗示性 |  The state of being highly susceptible to suggestion; an excessive responsiveness to suggestion.  This term often implies an unusual or unhealthy degree of influenceability, potentially linked to psychological vulnerabilities. |


例句：

His hypersuggestibleness made him an easy target for manipulative individuals.  他的高度暗示性使他很容易成为操纵者的目标。

The hypnotist exploited the subject's hypersuggestibleness to induce a deep trance.  催眠师利用受试者的高度暗示性诱导其进入深度催眠状态。


近义词：suggestibility, susceptibility, impressionability, vulnerability


反义词：resistance to suggestion, strong will, independence


词源：

* **hyper-**:  prefix meaning "excessive," "above," or "beyond."  (来自希腊语 ὑπέρ, hyper, 意为“超过”、“之上”)
* **suggest-**:  from Latin *suggestare* "to bring to mind, hint at"  (来自拉丁语suggestare，意为“使想起，暗示”)
* **-ible**: suffix meaning "capable of," "worthy of," or forming adjectives. (形容词后缀)
* **-ness**: suffix forming nouns of state or quality. (名词后缀)

Therefore, "hypersuggestibleness" literally means the state of being excessively suggestible.
