karaite:/ˈkærəɪt/| n.|卡拉派犹太人|A member of a Jewish sect that rejects rabbinic traditions and adheres strictly to the Hebrew Bible.  They are known for their adherence to the literal interpretation of the Tanakh (Hebrew Bible) and rejection of the Oral Torah.|The Karaites have a long and rich history, tracing their origins back to the 8th century.|近义词：Jewish sect member|反义词：Rabbinic Jew
