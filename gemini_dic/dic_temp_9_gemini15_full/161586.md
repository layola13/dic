pottle:/ˈpɒtl/| n.|半加仑；小桶|A pottle is an obsolete unit of liquid measure, equal to half a gallon (approximately 2.27 liters).  It was commonly used in England for measuring milk and other liquids. The word is now largely archaic, but might be encountered in historical contexts.|The farmer sold a pottle of milk to the baker.|近义词：half-gallon (modern equivalent)|反义词:gallon
