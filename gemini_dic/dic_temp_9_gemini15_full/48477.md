deep-seated:/ˌdiːpˈsiːtɪd/ | adj. | 根深蒂固的，积习难改的 | deeply rooted; ingrained; firmly established |  His deep-seated prejudice against foreigners made it difficult for him to be fair. 他对外国人的根深蒂固的偏见使他难以做到公平。  His deep-seated fear of failure prevented him from taking risks. 他对失败的根深蒂固的恐惧阻止了他冒险。| ingrained, entrenched, firmly established | superficial, shallow


USAGE->

一般现在时:
His prejudice is deep-seated. = 他的偏见根深蒂固。
That belief is deep-seated in his mind. = 那种信念深深植根于他的脑海中。


一般过去时:
Her fear was deep-seated. = 她的恐惧根深蒂固。
The problem was deep-seated and difficult to solve. = 这个问题根深蒂固，难以解决。


现在完成时:
His distrust has become deep-seated. = 他的不信任已经根深蒂固。

过去完成时:

将来时:

虚拟语气:

If the problem weren't so deep-seated, it would be easier to solve. = 如果问题不那么根深蒂固，就更容易解决了。

否定句:
