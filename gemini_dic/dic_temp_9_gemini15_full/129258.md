nodded:/ˈnɒdɪd/|/ˈnɑːdɪd/| v.|点头；表示同意|To move your head up and down as a way of showing agreement, understanding, or greeting.|He nodded his head in agreement. 他点头表示同意。|She nodded to show she understood. 她点头表示她明白了。|shook one's head, shook, bowed| shook one's head, shook|

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He nods his head when he agrees. = 他同意时点头。
She nods to greet her friends. = 她点头向朋友们打招呼。

一般过去时:
He nodded his head in approval. = 他点头表示赞成。
She nodded politely to the stranger. = 她礼貌地向陌生人点头示意。

现在完成时:
He has nodded off during the meeting. = 他在会议期间打盹了。(此处nodded off表示睡着)
She has nodded her assent to the proposal. = 她已经点头同意了这个提案。

过去完成时:
He had nodded off before the speaker finished. = 演讲者还没讲完他就睡着了。(此处nodded off表示睡着)
She had nodded her agreement before realizing the implications. = 她点头同意之前没有意识到其中的含义。

将来时:
He will nod his head when he understands. = 他明白了就会点头。
She will nod to acknowledge my presence. = 她会点头承认我的存在。

虚拟语气:
If he understood, he would nod. = 如果他明白了，他会点头。
If she had known, she would have nodded. = 如果她知道，她就会点头。

被动语态:
(The verb "nod" is rarely used in the passive voice because it implies a voluntary action.)  This is not a common usage.

疑问句:
Did he nod his head? = 他点头了吗？
Will she nod in agreement? = 她会点头表示同意吗？

否定句:
He didn't nod his head. = 他没有点头。
She didn't nod in response. = 她没有点头回应。
