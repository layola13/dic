jolts:/dʒoʊlts/| n. v.|猛推；震动；剧烈摇晃|A sudden, sharp, and brief push or shake.|The earthquake jolted the city. 地震震动了这座城市。The news jolted him out of his complacency.  这个消息使他摆脱了自满情绪。|shake, shock, jar|calm, soothe|名词，动词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The news jolts me. = 这个消息让我震惊。
He jolts the table. = 他猛推桌子。


一般过去时:
The car jolted over the rough road. = 汽车在崎岖的道路上颠簸。
The sudden noise jolted me awake. = 突然的噪音把我惊醒了。


现在完成时:
The experience has jolted him into action. = 这段经历促使他采取行动。
The news has jolted the market. = 这条消息震动了市场。


过去完成时:
The previous events had jolted the country into a state of fear. = 先前的事件已使全国陷入恐惧之中。
She had jolted herself awake with a cup of strong coffee. = 她用一杯浓咖啡让自己清醒过来。


将来时:
The announcement will jolt the public. = 这个公告将震惊公众。
The impact will jolt the vehicle. = 冲击力将使车辆剧烈震动。


虚拟语气:
If the news had jolted him more, he might have acted sooner. = 如果这个消息更让他震惊，他可能会早点采取行动。
I wish the coffee hadn't jolted me awake so early. = 我真希望咖啡没有那么早就把我弄醒了。


被动语态:
The building was jolted by the explosion. = 建筑物被爆炸震动了。
He was jolted from his sleep by a loud crash. = 一声巨响把他从睡梦中惊醒。


疑问句:
Did the earthquake jolt the city significantly? = 地震是否剧烈震动了这座城市？
Will the news jolt the stock market? = 这个消息会震动股市吗？


否定句:
The news didn't jolt him. = 这个消息没有让他震惊。
The small bump didn't jolt the car. = 小的颠簸并没有震动汽车。
