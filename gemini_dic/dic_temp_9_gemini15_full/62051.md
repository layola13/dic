encapsulation:/ˌɛnkæpʃuˈleɪʃən/| n. |封装，打包；胶囊化 |The act of enclosing something in or as if in a capsule; the state of being so enclosed.  It also refers to the bundling of data and methods that operate on that data within a single unit, often a class in object-oriented programming.  This protects the data from outside access and modification, promoting code organization and maintainability. |Example:  The encapsulation of the drug in a time-release capsule ensured a steady dosage.  |Example:  Object-oriented programming relies heavily on encapsulation to protect data integrity. |近义词：enclosure, containment, bundling |反义词：exposure, revelation
