mesocoracoid:/ˌmezoʊˈkɔːrəkɔɪd/| n. |中喙骨|A bony process or structure found in some vertebrates, particularly in certain reptiles and extinct amphibians, located between the coracoid and scapula.  It's an element of the pectoral girdle, contributing to the articulation of the forelimb.  The mesocoracoid's presence and size vary significantly across different species and are often used in phylogenetic analyses.| The presence of a prominent mesocoracoid is a characteristic feature of some early amniotes. |近义词:coracoid (partially), pectoral girdle element|反义词:None.
