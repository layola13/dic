degenerateness: /dɪˈdʒɛnərətnəs/ | n. | 退化；堕落；简并性 | The state or quality of being degenerate; deterioration; decay.  It can refer to a decline in quality, morality, or function; or, in mathematics and physics,  a state where distinct solutions to an equation or system become identical. |

词源：

From degenerate + -ness.  "Degenerate" itself comes from Latin *dēgenerātus, the past participle of *dēgenerāre, meaning "to deviate from the norm," from *dē- ("away from") + *genus ("kind, race").
