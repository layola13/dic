faltering: /ˈfɔːltərɪŋ/ | adj. | 犹豫的；蹒跚的；衰弱的 | Hesitating or unsteady; showing weakness or lack of confidence.  |例句：His voice was faltering as he tried to explain. 他试图解释时，声音颤抖着。


例句：Her steps were faltering as she walked across the icy pavement. 她走在结冰的人行道上，步履蹒跚。


近义词：hesitant, wavering, unsteady, weak, shaky


反义词：steady, firm, confident, strong


词性:形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
His voice is faltering. = 他的声音颤抖着。
The economy is faltering. = 经济正在衰退。


一般过去时:
His voice faltered. = 他的声音颤抖了。
The engine faltered and stopped. = 发动机发出咯噔声然后停了。


现在完成时:
His confidence has faltered. = 他的信心动摇了。
The market has been faltering for months. = 市场已经动荡了几个月了。


过去完成时:
He had faltered in his attempt to climb the mountain. = 他在尝试攀登这座山时已经犹豫了。
The negotiations had faltered before they even began. = 谈判甚至在开始之前就已经失败了。


将来时:
I think my courage will falter. = 我想我的勇气将会衰退。
The company's growth will falter without investment. = 如果没有投资，公司的增长将会停滞。


虚拟语气:
If his voice faltered, no one would hear him. = 如果他的声音颤抖了，没有人会听到他。
I wish my resolve wouldn't falter. = 我希望我的决心不会动摇。


被动语态:
His voice was faltered by emotion. = 他的声音因激动而颤抖。
The project was faltered by lack of funding. = 该项目因缺乏资金而受阻。


疑问句:
Is his performance faltering? = 他的表现是否犹豫不决？
Is the project faltering? = 项目是否进展缓慢？


否定句:
His resolve did not falter. = 他的决心没有动摇。
The progress did not falter. = 进步没有停滞。


PHRASE->
a faltering step = 蹒跚的脚步
a faltering economy = 疲软的经济
a faltering voice = 颤抖的声音
a faltering start = 慢吞吞的开始
faltering steps = 蹒跚的脚步


词源:
源自古英语 *fealt,  意思是“to stumble, to hesitate”。  它与“fault”和“false”有词源关系，都反映了某种不稳定或不完美的状态。
