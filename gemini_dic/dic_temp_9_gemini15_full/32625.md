chirrups:/ˈtʃɜːrps/ | v. | 啁啾地叫；高兴地说话 | To make a series of short, high-pitched sounds, like a bird; to speak in a cheerful, lively way. | 例句：The crickets chirruped all night. 蟋蟀整夜都在唧唧喳喳地叫。The children chirruped excitedly about their trip. 孩子们兴奋地叽叽喳喳地谈论着他们的旅行。 | 近义词：twitter, chirp, cheep | 反义词：growl, moan


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The birds chirrup merrily in the morning. = 鸟儿在早晨快乐地啁啾。
He chirrups a cheerful greeting. = 他高兴地打了个招呼。


一般过去时:
The sparrows chirruped sweetly in the garden. = 麻雀在花园里甜蜜地啁啾。
She chirruped a response. = 她高兴地回应道。


现在完成时:
The crickets have chirruped all night. = 蟋蟀整夜都在唧唧喳喳地叫。
The children have chirruped excitedly about their presents. = 孩子们兴奋地叽叽喳喳地谈论着他们的礼物。


过去完成时:
The birds had chirruped before sunrise. = 太阳升起前鸟儿就已经开始鸣叫了。

将来时:

虚拟语气:
If the birds chirruped, I would wake up. = 如果鸟儿鸣叫，我会醒来。
I wish the birds would chirrup more often. = 我希望鸟儿能更频繁地鸣叫。


被动语态:

The song was chirruped softly. = 这首歌被轻轻地哼唱出来。(此用法较少见，通常用主动语态)


疑问句:

否定句:
