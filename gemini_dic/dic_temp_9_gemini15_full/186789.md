rimes:/raɪmz/ | n. |押韵；诗歌；韵律|Rhymes are repetitions of similar sounds in two or more words, especially at the end of lines in poems or songs.  They create a musical effect and often contribute to the overall meaning and feeling of the work.|Example: The poem's rhymes enhanced its lyrical quality. 这首诗的押韵增强了它的抒情性。|近义词:rhythm, verse, meter|反义词:prose


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The poem rhymes perfectly. = 这首诗押韵完美。
These two words rhyme. = 这两个词押韵。


一般过去时:
The song rhymed beautifully. = 这首歌押韵很美。
The children rhymed words during the game. = 孩子们在游戏中押韵单词。


现在完成时:
She has rhymed many poems. = 她已经写过很多押韵的诗歌。
The poet has successfully rhymed the verses. = 这位诗人成功地使诗句押韵。


过去完成时:
He had rhymed the last two lines before submitting the poem. = 他在提交诗歌之前已经把最后两行押上了韵。
They had already rhymed the song before the recording session. = 他们在录音之前就已经把歌押韵了。


将来时:
I will rhyme the words in my next poem. = 我将在我的下一首诗中使单词押韵。
The songwriter will rhyme the chorus with the verses. = 作词人将使副歌与诗句押韵。


虚拟语气:
If I could rhyme better, I would write more poetry. = 如果我能够更好地押韵，我会写更多诗歌。
I wish I could rhyme as well as Shakespeare. = 我希望我能够像莎士比亚那样押韵。


被动语态:
The poem was rhymed skillfully. = 这首诗的押韵技巧娴熟。
The lyrics were rhymed to create a catchy tune. = 歌词押韵以创造朗朗上口的旋律。


疑问句:
Do these words rhyme? = 这些词押韵吗？
Does the poem rhyme consistently? = 这首诗的押韵是否一致？


否定句:
The poem doesn't rhyme well. = 这首诗押韵不好。
Those words don't rhyme at all. = 那些词根本不押韵。
