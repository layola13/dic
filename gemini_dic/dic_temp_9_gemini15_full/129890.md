non-intuitive:/ˌnɒnɪnˈtjuːɪtɪv/ | adj. | 非直观的，反直觉的 | Not easily understood or learned; contrary to intuition.  Something non-intuitive is not easily grasped or understood instinctively; it requires conscious thought and effort to comprehend. | Example: The software's interface is non-intuitive and difficult to use.  这个软件的界面不直观，难以使用。


The user manual for the new printer is non-intuitive; I couldn't figure out how to print a simple document. 新打印机的使用手册不直观；我无法弄清楚如何打印简单的文档。


近义词：counterintuitive, unintuitive, illogical, obscure, perplexing


反义词：intuitive, self-evident, obvious, clear, straightforward


词源:  The word "non-intuitive" is formed by adding the prefix "non-" (meaning "not") to the adjective "intuitive."  "Intuitive" comes from the Latin word "intueri," meaning "to look at, contemplate, consider."  Therefore, "non-intuitive" literally means "not easily understood through instinct or inherent understanding."
