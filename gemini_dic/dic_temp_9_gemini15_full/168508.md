problockade:/ˌproʊblɒˈkeɪd/| n. |预先封锁；预防性封锁|A problockade is a preventative measure taken to block or prevent something before it happens, often in a military or political context.  It anticipates and aims to neutralize a potential threat or problem.  It's a proactive strategy rather than a reactive one.|例句：The government implemented a problockade of the border to prevent the spread of the disease.  政府对边境实施了预先封锁以防止疾病蔓延。

The navy conducted a problockade of the enemy's port to disrupt their supply lines. 海军对敌方港口进行了预防性封锁以扰乱其补给线。|近义词：preemptive strike, preventative measure, blockade, embargo|反义词：reactive measure, laissez-faire, open border
