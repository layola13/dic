grouchiness:/ˈɡraʊtʃɪnɪs/| n. |脾气暴躁；坏脾气；不快|A state of being grumpy, irritable, and bad-tempered.  It often involves complaining and showing dissatisfaction.|例句：His grouchiness was evident in his sharp tone and furrowed brow. 他的坏脾气从他尖刻的语气和紧锁的眉头中显而易见。

例句：The constant rain contributed to her general grouchiness.  持续的雨水加剧了她的坏脾气。


近义词：grumpiness, irritability, ill temper, crankiness, surliness|反义词：cheerfulness, good humor, pleasantness, amiability


词源:  The word "grouchiness" derives from the noun "grouch," which first appeared in the late 19th century.  The origin of "grouch" is uncertain, but it's likely an alteration of an earlier word with a similar meaning, possibly related to the Old French word "gruncher," meaning "to grumble" or "to murmur."  The suffix "-iness" is added to create the noun "grouchiness," indicating a state or quality of being a grouch.
