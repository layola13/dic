leveraging:/ˈliːvərɪdʒɪŋ/ | v. | 利用，杠杆作用 |  To use something to maximum advantage.  It often implies using existing resources or assets in a strategic way to achieve a greater outcome. |  例句：The company is leveraging its brand recognition to launch new products.  公司正在利用其品牌知名度来推出新产品。

例句：We need to leverage our existing technology to improve efficiency. 我们需要利用我们现有的技术来提高效率。


近义词：utilizing, exploiting, employing, harnessing | 反义词：neglecting, ignoring, wasting


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They are leveraging their skills to find better jobs. = 他们正在利用自己的技能寻找更好的工作。
The company is leveraging social media for marketing. = 公司正在利用社交媒体进行营销。


一般过去时:
We leveraged our network to secure funding. = 我们利用我们的关系网获得了资金。
She leveraged her experience to get a promotion. = 她利用自己的经验获得了晋升。


现在完成时:
They have leveraged their expertise to create a successful business. = 他们已经利用自己的专业知识创建了一家成功的企业。
The marketing team has leveraged data analytics to improve campaigns. = 营销团队已经利用数据分析来改进营销活动。


过去完成时:
They had leveraged their resources before the crisis hit. = 在危机爆发之前，他们已经利用了他们的资源。
The company had leveraged its brand equity to expand into new markets. = 公司已经利用其品牌资产拓展到新的市场。


将来时:
We will leverage artificial intelligence to automate tasks. = 我们将利用人工智能来自动化任务。
The project will leverage cutting-edge technology to achieve its goals. = 该项目将利用尖端技术来实现其目标。


虚拟语气:
If we leveraged our resources effectively, we would be more successful. = 如果我们有效地利用资源，我们会更成功。

被动语态:
