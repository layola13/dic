/luˈkeɪən/| n. |卢卡亚人 |The Lucayans were the indigenous people of the Bahamas before European colonization. They are believed to be closely related to the Taíno people of Cuba and other Caribbean islands.  Their language and culture were largely lost after contact with Europeans.  |The Lucayans' sophisticated agricultural practices allowed them to thrive in the islands. |近义词：Bahamian Indians (loosely)|反义词：无
