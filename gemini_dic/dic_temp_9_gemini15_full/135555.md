nonpolarizable: /ˌnɒnpoʊləˈraɪzəbəl/ | adj. | 不可极化的 | Not capable of being polarized; resistant to changes in electrical polarization.  This term is primarily used in physics and chemistry, referring to molecules or materials that do not readily develop a dipole moment in response to an electric field.  | Example: The nonpolarizable nature of the solvent ensured that the reaction proceeded smoothly.  非极性溶剂的特性确保了反应顺利进行。 | 近义词: unpolarizable | 反义词: polarizable
