empyreal:/ɛmˈpɪriəl/| adj.|天上的，超凡的，极乐的|Of or relating to the sky or heavens; celestial.  The word often implies a sense of sublimity, grandeur, or spiritual transcendence. |例句：The poem describes an empyreal realm of celestial beauty. 这首诗描述了一个天上美丽的境界。

例句：The empyreal heights inspired awe and wonder in the onlookers.  天上高耸的山峰激起了旁观者的敬畏和惊奇。


近义词：celestial, heavenly, ethereal, sublime|反义词：earthly, terrestrial, mundane


词源：From Middle English *empyreal, from Old French empyréal, from Late Latin empyreus, from Greek empýrios "in the fire," from en "in" + pýr "fire."  The original sense referred to the highest heaven, believed to be made of fire.


用法说明：

empyreal主要用作形容词，很少作名词。其含义通常与天空、天堂或超凡脱俗的事物有关，常常带有崇高、宏伟或精神超越的意味。  它在文学作品中较为常见，用来创造一种庄严或神秘的氛围。


虽然词源上与“火”有关，但现代用法中很少强调“火”的含义。


例句补充：The artist attempted to capture the empyreal glow of the setting sun. 艺术家试图捕捉落日的天上光芒。

The music evoked an empyreal feeling of peace and tranquility. 音乐唤起了一种超凡脱俗的平和与宁静之感。
