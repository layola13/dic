incident:/ˈɪnsɪdənt/| n.|事件，事变|An incident is a single event, especially one that is unusual, important, or dangerous.  It can refer to a relatively minor event or a significant one.|例句：There was a minor incident in the street this morning. 今早街上发生了一件小事故。
例句：The police are investigating the incident.  警方正在调查这起事件。|近义词：event, occurrence, happening, episode|反义词：calm, peace


USAGE->
一般现在时:
An incident occurs frequently in this area. = 这个地区经常发生事件。
This incident reveals a serious problem. = 这件事暴露出一个严重的问题。

一般过去时:
An incident happened yesterday. = 昨天发生了一件事。
The police investigated the incident thoroughly. = 警方彻底调查了这起事件。

现在完成时:
Several incidents have occurred this week. = 本周已经发生了几起事件。
No serious incidents have been reported. = 没有报告发生严重的事件。

过去完成时:
Before the accident, no such incidents had been reported. = 事故发生前，没有报告过类似事件。
The investigation revealed that many similar incidents had happened before. = 调查显示在此之前发生过许多类似的事件。

将来时:
A similar incident will likely happen again. = 类似的事件很可能会再次发生。
We must prevent such incidents from happening in the future. = 我们必须防止此类事件将来发生。

虚拟语气:
If this incident hadn't happened, everything would have been fine. = 如果这件事没有发生，一切都会很好。
I wish this incident had never occurred. = 我希望这件事从未发生过。

被动语态:
The incident was reported to the police. = 这件事已向警方报告。
The cause of the incident is being investigated. = 正在调查事故的原因。

疑问句:
What caused the incident? =  是什么导致了这起事件？
Did anyone witness the incident? = 有人目睹了这起事件吗？

否定句:
No incident occurred during the meeting. = 会议期间没有发生任何事件。
The police found no evidence of foul play in the incident. = 警方没有发现这起事件中存在不当行为的证据。
