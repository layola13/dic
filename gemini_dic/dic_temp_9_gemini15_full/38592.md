concorde:/ˈkɒnkɔːrd/ /ˈkɑːnkɔːrd/ | n. |协和式飞机；和谐；一致 | The Concorde was a supersonic passenger airliner, famous for its speed and elegance.  The Concorde represented a peak of technological achievement and a symbol of international cooperation. |近义词：harmony, agreement, accord |反义词：discord, disagreement, conflict |


英文解析：
Concorde, with a capital "C," refers specifically to the Anglo-French supersonic passenger jet.  As a general noun, "concord" means agreement or harmony between people or things.

例句：

The Concorde could cross the Atlantic in a fraction of the time it took conventional jets. = 协和式飞机能够以比传统喷气式飞机快得多的速度横渡大西洋。

There was a general concord among the members of the committee. = 委员会成员之间达成了普遍共识。


词源:
The word comes from the Old French word "concorde," meaning "agreement, harmony," derived from the Latin word "concordia," which is a combination of "con-" (together) and "cor" (heart).  The sense of agreement and harmony is central to its meaning in both historical and modern usage.
