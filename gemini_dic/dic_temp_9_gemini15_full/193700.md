self-confessed:/ˌsɛlf kənˈfɛst/ | adj. | 自认的，自己承认的 |  Describes someone who has openly admitted to something, usually something negative or embarrassing.  It emphasizes the person's own admission rather than external evidence. | Example:  He was a self-confessed liar. 他是一个自认的骗子。

The self-confessed criminal turned himself in.  这位自认的罪犯自首了。| 近义词：admitted, avowed, acknowledged | 反义词：denied, refuted, disavowed | 形容词

词源:

The word is a compound of "self," meaning oneself, and "confessed," the past participle of the verb "confess," meaning to admit to a wrongdoing or a belief.  The combination indicates an admission made by the person themselves.
