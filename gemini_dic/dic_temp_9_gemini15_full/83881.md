half-altered: /ˈhælf ˈɔːltərd/ | adj. | 半改变的，部分改变的 | Partially changed or modified; not completely transformed.  The painting was half-altered, showing traces of its original form. | 近义词：partially changed, modified, transformed (partially), incompletely altered | 反义词：unaltered, unchanged, completely altered


例句：

The half-altered manuscript was difficult to decipher.  = 半改变的手稿难以辨认。

His half-altered plans led to unexpected delays. = 他半改变的计划导致了意想不到的延误。


词源:

* **half:**  From Old English *healf, *healf, from Proto-Germanic *halbaz (compare Dutch *half, German *halb).  Meaning "one of two equal parts."

* **altered:** From the past participle of the verb "alter," which comes from Old French *alterer, from Latin *alterāre "to make other, change," from *alter "other."


用法说明：

half-altered 主要用作形容词，描述某事物处于部分改变的状态，并未完全改变其原始形态。  它强调的是不完整或不彻底的改变。  它通常用于描述物理物体或抽象概念（如计划、想法）的部分修改。
