marsebanker:/ˈmɑːrsˌbæŋkər/| n. |马赛银行家，欺诈者|A Marse banker was a type of swindler or confidence trickster, particularly active in Marseilles, France, in the 18th and 19th centuries.  They were known for elaborate schemes and often targeted wealthy individuals.  The term reflects the city's reputation at that time as a center for such activities. | Example: The notorious Marse banker bilked several wealthy merchants out of their fortunes.  臭名昭著的马赛银行家骗取了几位富商的财富。 |近义词：swindler, con man, confidence trickster, sharper|反义词：honest person, philanthropist
