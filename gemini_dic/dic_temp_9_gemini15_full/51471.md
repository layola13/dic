detoured:/ˌdiːˈtʊərd/| adj.|绕行的；改道的| diverted from the usual or intended course.  The word describes a route or journey that has been changed, often due to an obstacle or closure.|例句：Due to the accident, traffic was detoured onto a side street.  由于事故，交通被改道到一条小街上。|例句：Our flight was detoured because of the storm. 我们的航班因暴风雨而改道。|近义词：diverted, rerouted, redirected|反义词：direct, straight


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The road is detoured because of construction. = 这条路因为施工而改道了。
Traffic is often detoured during rush hour. = 高峰时间交通经常改道。


一般过去时:
The bus was detoured due to a flooded road. = 公共汽车由于道路被淹而改道了。
We were detoured around the mountain. = 我们绕过山路行驶。


现在完成时:
The race has been detoured because of the bad weather. = 比赛由于恶劣天气而改道了。
They have been detoured several times on their journey. = 在他们的旅途中，他们已经改道了好几次。


过去完成时:
The hikers had been detoured before reaching the summit. = 登山者在到达山顶之前就已经改道了。
We had been detoured twice before finding our way. = 我们在找到路之前已经绕路两次了。


将来时:
The marathon will be detoured due to the anticipated crowds. = 马拉松比赛预计将因预料中的人群而改道。
Traffic will likely be detoured next week. = 下周交通可能会改道。


虚拟语气:
If the bridge were closed, we would be detoured. = 如果桥梁关闭，我们将改道。
I wish the traffic hadn't been detoured. = 我真希望交通没有改道。


被动语态:
The route was detoured to avoid the construction zone. = 路线被改道以避开施工区域。
The cars were detoured onto the highway. = 汽车被改道到高速公路上。


疑问句:
Was the traffic detoured? = 交通是否改道了？
Will the train be detoured? = 火车会改道吗？


否定句:
The traffic wasn't detoured this morning. = 今早交通没有改道。
The plane wasn't detoured by the bad weather. = 飞机没有因为恶劣天气而改道。
