roughnecked: /ˈrʌfˌnekt/ | adj., n. | 粗鲁的；粗野的；（尤指）油田工人 |

英文解析:  As an adjective, "roughnecked" describes someone who is rough, uncouth, and unrefined in their manner or appearance. It often implies a lack of sophistication or education.  As a noun, it specifically refers to a worker in the oil fields, typically involved in physically demanding tasks. This usage is largely a North American term.

例句:

adj. The roughnecked foreman barked orders at his crew.  粗鲁的工头朝他的手下吼叫着命令。

adj. He had a roughnecked appearance, with dirt under his fingernails and calloused hands. 他看起来很粗鲁，指甲缝里全是泥土，双手粗糙。


n.  The roughnecks worked tirelessly, battling the elements and the demanding nature of their jobs. 油田工人不知疲倦地工作，与恶劣的环境和艰巨的工作作斗争。

n.  Many roughnecks found camaraderie in their shared experiences and hardships. 许多油田工人从共同的经历和苦难中找到了友情。


近义词: (adj.) uncouth, boorish, coarse, unrefined, gruff; (n.) oilfield worker, roustabout

反义词: (adj.) refined, polished, sophisticated, cultured, gentle

词性: adjective, noun
