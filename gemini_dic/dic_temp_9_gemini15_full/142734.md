otolith:/ˈoʊtəˌlɪθ/| n. |耳石；平衡石|A small, calcium carbonate structure in the inner ear of many vertebrates, including fish and some amphibians, that helps maintain balance and detect movement and orientation.  Otoliths grow throughout the life of the organism, and their size and structure can provide information about the age and growth history of the fish.|例句：The otoliths of this fish species are unusually large. 这类鱼的耳石异常大。

例句：Scientists analyze otoliths to determine the age and growth rate of fish. 科学家分析耳石来确定鱼的年龄和生长速度。


近义词：statolith (in some contexts)|反义词：None|词性:Noun
