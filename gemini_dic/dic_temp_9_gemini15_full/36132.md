coeternity:/ˌkoʊiːˈtɜːrnəti/ | n. |同时永恒；同时存在；同等永恒 | The state of existing simultaneously and eternally; the quality of being coeternal.  It often refers to the relationship between the Father, Son, and Holy Spirit in the Trinity in Christian theology. |例句：The doctrine of the Trinity posits the coeternity of the Father, Son, and Holy Spirit.  三位一体教义认为圣父、圣子和圣灵是同等永恒的。

The concept of coeternity is central to understanding the nature of God in many theological systems.  同时永恒的概念对理解许多神学体系中上帝的本质至关重要。


近义词：simultaneous eternity, eternal coexistence | 反义词：temporal existence, separate existence | 词性:名词

词源:
The word "coeternity" comes from the prefix "co-" (meaning "together" or "jointly") and "eternity" (meaning "lasting forever").  Therefore, coeternity literally means "existing together eternally."  The word is a relatively uncommon term, primarily used in theological and philosophical contexts.
