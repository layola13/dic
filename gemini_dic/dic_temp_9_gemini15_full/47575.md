debars:/dɪˈbɑːrz/ | vt. |阻止；禁止；排除 | To prevent someone from doing something or to prevent something from happening.  |例句：The judge debarred him from practicing law. 法官禁止他执业。 |近义词：prohibit, exclude, prevent, bar |反义词：allow, permit, admit


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The rule debars employees from using personal devices at work. = 规定禁止员工在工作中使用个人设备。
He debars himself from participating in the competition. = 他自己放弃了参加比赛。


一般过去时:
The company debarred him from accessing sensitive data. = 公司禁止他访问敏感数据。
They debarred her from the meeting due to her tardiness. = 因为她迟到，他们把她拒之门外。


现在完成时:
The scandal has debarred him from public life. = 这场丑闻已将他逐出公众生活。
This incident has debarred many students from attending the graduation ceremony. = 这次事件导致许多学生无法参加毕业典礼。


过去完成时:

They had debarred her from the club for violating the rules. = 因为违反规定，他们之前已经把她逐出俱乐部。


将来时:

The judge will likely debar him from holding public office for at least five years. = 法官可能会禁止他至少五年内担任公职。


虚拟语气:

被动语态:
