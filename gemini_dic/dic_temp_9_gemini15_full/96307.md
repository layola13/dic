indigence:/ˈɪndɪdʒəns/| n. |贫困；穷困；缺乏 |The state of being indigent; poverty; destitution.  It refers to a lack of the basic necessities of life, often implying a chronic and severe lack of resources. |例句：The indigence of the homeless population is a pressing social issue.  = 无家可归人口的贫困是一个紧迫的社会问题。

The widespread indigence in the rural areas prompted the government to launch a relief program. = 农村地区普遍存在的贫困促使政府启动了一项救济计划。|近义词：poverty, destitution, penury, want |反义词：wealth, affluence, opulence |名词


WORD ORIGIN->
The word "indigence" originates from the Latin word "indigere," meaning "to need" or "to be in want of."  The prefix "in-" implies a state of being, and the suffix "-ence" denotes a state or condition.  Therefore, "indigence" literally translates to "the state of being in need."
