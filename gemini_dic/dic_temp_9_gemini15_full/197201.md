shapen:/ʃeɪpn/| vt.|塑造，形成，影响；使成形|to give a particular form or shape to something; to influence the development of something.|The sculptor carefully shaped the clay.  雕塑家小心地塑造粘土。|The events shaped his future.  这些事件塑造了他的未来。|form, mold, mold, fashion|amorphous, formless

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He shapes the clay with his hands. = 他用手塑造粘土。
The wind shapes the sand dunes. = 风塑造沙丘。

一般过去时:
She shaped the dough into a loaf. = 她把面团塑造成面包。
The experience shaped his character. = 那次经历塑造了他的性格。

现在完成时:
The artist has shaped many sculptures. =这位艺术家已经塑造了许多雕塑。
Years of hardship have shaped her outlook on life. =多年的苦难塑造了她的人生观。

过去完成时:
They had shaped the policy before the meeting. =他们在会议前已经制定了政策。
The sculptor had shaped the clay before adding the details. = 雕塑家在添加细节之前已经塑造了粘土。

将来时:
She will shape the metal into a bird. = 她将把金属塑造成一只鸟。
The new law will shape the future of the industry. = 新法将塑造该行业的未来。

虚拟语气:
If I had the skills, I would shape the wood into a chair. = 如果我有这方面的技能，我会把木头做成椅子。
I wish I could shape my destiny. = 我希望我能掌控自己的命运。

被动语态:
The clay was shaped by the sculptor. = 粘土是由雕塑家塑造的。
The policy was shaped by public opinion. = 政策是由公众舆论塑造的。

疑问句:
How did the artist shape the sculpture? =艺术家是如何塑造雕塑的？
What factors shaped your decision? =什么因素影响了你的决定？

否定句:
He didn't shape the wood properly. = 他没有正确地塑造木材。
The event did not shape her life as she expected. = 这件事并没有像她预期的那样改变她的生活。
