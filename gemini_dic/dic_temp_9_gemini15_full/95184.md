inaugurate:/ɪˈnɔːɡjʊreɪt/ | vt. | 开始；为…举行就职典礼；开创；启用 | To begin or introduce something new; to formally install someone into a position, especially a high office.  |例句：The new president will be inaugurated next week.  = 下周新总统将就职。

The company inaugurated its new headquarters with a ribbon-cutting ceremony. = 公司以剪彩仪式为其新总部启用。


近义词：begin, commence, initiate, install, launch


反义词：terminate, conclude, end


USAGE->

一般现在时:
They inaugurate new policies every year. = 他们每年都推出新的政策。
He inaugurates new projects with great enthusiasm. = 他以极大的热情启动新的项目。


一般过去时:
The museum was inaugurated last year. = 博物馆去年启用。
They inaugurated the new bridge with a grand ceremony. = 他们以盛大的仪式启用新桥。


现在完成时:
They have inaugurated several new programs this year. = 他们今年已经启动了几个新项目。
The government has inaugurated a new economic policy. = 政府已经启动了一项新的经济政策。


过去完成时:
Before the new president took office, they had inaugurated a new security system. = 新总统就职前，他们已经启用了一个新的安全系统。
They had inaugurated the project long before the funding was secured. = 他们在资金到位之前很久就启动了该项目。


将来时:
The company will inaugurate its new product line next month. = 公司将在下个月推出其新的产品线。
They will inaugurate the new hospital in the spring. = 他们将在春天启用新医院。


被动语态:
The new stadium was inaugurated last month. = 新体育场在上个月启用。
The project will be inaugurated by the mayor next week. = 该项目将于下周由市长启用。


PHRASE->

inaugurate a new era = 开创一个新时代
inaugurate a new policy = 实施一项新政策
inaugurate a new building = 为新建筑举行落成典礼
inaugurate a new president = 为新总统举行就职典礼
inaugurate a new partnership = 启动新的合作关系


词源:

From Latin *inaugurare, from *in-  "in" + augurāre "to predict," from augur "soothsayer, diviner."  The original meaning related to performing religious rites to consecrate something, hence the connection to the installation of officials.
