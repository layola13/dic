predisplacement: /ˌpriːdɪsˈpleɪsmənt/ | n. | 预先移动，预先安置 | The action or process of moving something or someone into a position before something else happens.  It often implies preparation or anticipation of a subsequent event. |例句：The predisplacement of the troops ensured a swift attack.  部队的预先部署确保了迅速的进攻。


The predisplacement of resources to the disaster zone was crucial.  将资源预先调动到灾区至关重要。|近义词：prepositioning, pre-arrangement, advance deployment |反义词：postplacement, delayed deployment |
