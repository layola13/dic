pressurize:/ˈprɛʃəraɪz/ | vt. |施加压力；使增压；迫使 | To put pressure on someone to do something; to increase the pressure of a gas or liquid. |例句：They tried to pressurize him into accepting the job. 他们试图迫使他接受这份工作。|例句：The cabin was pressurized to a comfortable altitude. 机舱被增压到舒适的海拔高度。|近义词：coerce, compel, force, influence |反义词：relieve, ease


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He pressurizes his employees to meet unrealistic deadlines. 他给他的员工施加压力，要求他们完成不切实际的最后期限。
The airline pressurizes the cabins of its planes during flight. 这家航空公司在飞行期间对飞机的客舱进行增压。


一般过去时:
She pressurized me into signing the contract. 她强迫我签署了合同。
The engineers pressurized the system to test its strength. 工程师们对系统增压以测试其强度。


现在完成时:
They have pressurized the government to change its policy. 他们一直在向政府施压，要求其改变政策。
The company has pressurized the pipes to prevent leaks. 公司已经对管道增压以防止泄漏。


过去完成时:

The tank had been pressurized to its maximum capacity. 油箱已经被增压到最大容量。


将来时:

虚拟语气:

被动语态:

疑问句:
