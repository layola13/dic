intranscalent: /ˌɪntrænˈskæələnt/ | adj. | 不服从的；倔强的；不屈服的 |  Intransigent means unwilling or refusing to change one's views or to agree about something.  It implies a stubborn resistance to compromise or persuasion. |  The intransigent union leader refused to negotiate with management.  顽固的工会领导人拒绝与管理层谈判。  The intransigent defendant maintained his innocence despite overwhelming evidence.  尽管证据确凿，但固执的被告仍然坚持自己的清白。 |  obstinate, stubborn, inflexible, adamant | compliant, yielding, amenable, tractable | 形容词

词源:
The word "intransigent" comes from the Latin "in-" (not) + "transigere" (to come to an agreement, to transact).  Therefore, it literally means "not coming to an agreement."
