mispicturing: /ˌmɪsˈpɪktʃərɪŋ/ | v. | 错误描绘，歪曲，误解 |  The act of representing something inaccurately or falsely; a misrepresentation. It implies a distortion of the truth or a failure to understand the true nature of something. |


例句：

His biography mispictured his early life, omitting crucial details of his struggles.  = 他的传记歪曲了他的早年生活，省略了他奋斗的关键细节。

The media's mispicturing of the event led to widespread public misunderstanding. = 媒体对事件的错误描绘导致公众普遍误解。


近义词：misrepresenting, distorting, misinterpreting, misconstruing, falsifying


反义词：accurately portraying, truthfully representing, correctly understanding


词性：动词

词源:

The word "mispicturing" is a combination of the prefix "mis-" (meaning wrong or bad) and the word "picturing" (the act of creating a picture or representation).  "Picturing" derives from "picture," ultimately from the Old French "piture," and before that, the Latin "pictura" (painting).
