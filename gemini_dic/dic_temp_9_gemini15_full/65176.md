ethnolinguists:/ˌeθnoʊˈlɪŋɡwɪsts/| n. |民族语言学家| Scholars who study the relationship between language and culture, including how language reflects and shapes cultural identities and practices.  They investigate the impact of social structures, cultural beliefs, and historical events on language use and evolution.  Ethnolinguistics draws on linguistics, anthropology, and sociology. | Example: Ethnolinguists are interested in how language use varies across different social groups within a culture.  民族语言学家对语言在文化中不同社会群体间的差异很感兴趣。|近义词：sociolinguists, anthropological linguists|反义词：N/A
