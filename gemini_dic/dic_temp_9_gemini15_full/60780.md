electromagnetism:/ˌɪˌlektrəʊˈmæɡnɪtɪzəm/ | n. | 电磁学 | The branch of physics that studies the interactions between electricity and magnetism.  Electromagnetism explains how electric and magnetic fields are related and how they affect charged particles and materials.  | Example:  The principles of electromagnetism are fundamental to the operation of many modern technologies, such as electric motors and generators. 电磁学的原理是许多现代技术（如电动机和发电机）运行的基础。 | 近义词：electromagnetics | 反义词：None (Not a concept with a direct opposite) | 名词
