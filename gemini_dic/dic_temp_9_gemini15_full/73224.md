forcible:/ˈfɔːrsəbl/ | adj. | 强制的；强迫的；有力的；有效的 |  Characterized by force or violence; done by force.  It can also describe something done with strong and effective action. | 例句：The police made a forcible entry into the building.  警察强行进入大楼。

The evidence presented was forcible enough to convince the jury.  提出的证据足以让陪审团信服。

| 近义词：compulsory, obligatory, forceful, powerful | 反义词：gentle, voluntary, weak, ineffective | 词性：形容词

一般过去时:

现在完成时:
The authorities have forcibly relocated many families. = 当局已经强行迁移了许多家庭。
The regime has forcibly suppressed all opposition. = 政权已经强行压制了所有反对声音。


过去完成时:

虚拟语气:
If they were to forcibly take my land, I would fight them in court. = 如果他们强行夺走我的土地，我会在法庭上与他们对抗。
I wish they hadn't forcibly evicted those families. = 我真希望他们没有强行驱逐那些家庭。


被动语态:
The suspect was forcibly restrained by the police. = 嫌疑人被警方强行控制。
The protesters were forcibly dispersed by the riot police. = 抗议者被防暴警察强行驱散。


疑问句:
Was the entry into the house forcible? = 进入这所房子是强行的吗？
Were the confessions obtained forcibly? = 这些供词是强行获得的吗？


否定句:
The government did not forcibly conscript young men into the army. = 政府没有强行征召年轻男子入伍。
The police did not forcibly enter the premises. = 警察没有强行进入该处所。

词源:
Forcible comes from the Old French word "forcible," meaning "powerful," which in turn derives from the Latin word "fortis," meaning "strong."  The suffix "-ible" indicates capability or susceptibility.
