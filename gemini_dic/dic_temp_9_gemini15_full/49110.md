deinornis:/daɪˈnɔːrnɪs/| n. |恐鸟属（一种已灭绝的巨型鸟类）|Deinornis is an extinct genus of large, flightless birds that lived in Australia during the Pleistocene epoch.  They were closely related to the much better-known Dromornithidae.  Further research is needed to fully understand their evolutionary relationships and characteristics.|The discovery of Deinornis remains provided valuable insights into the megafauna of Pleistocene Australia.|近义词：Dromornis (close relative)|反义词：无|名词
