intermissions:/ˌɪntərˈmɪʃənz/| n.|幕间休息；中断；暂停|A pause or break in the continuity of something, especially in a performance or broadcast.  It often refers to the short breaks between acts of a play or parts of a concert.|例句：The intermission lasted for fifteen minutes. 幕间休息持续了十五分钟。|例句：There was a brief intermission in the fighting. 战斗中短暂地停顿了一下。|近义词：break, pause, recess, interruption|反义词：continuation, resumption|名词

词源:
The word "intermission" comes from the Latin word "intermissio," which is a combination of "inter" (between) and "missio" (sending, letting go).  It literally means "a sending between" or "a break in the action".
