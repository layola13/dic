settlerdom:/ˈsɛtlərdəm/| n. |殖民者社会；定居者社会|The social and political structures established by settlers in a new territory, often characterized by dominance over the indigenous population and the imposition of their own culture and values.  It also refers to the collective experience and identity of settlers.|例句：The history of settlerdom in Australia is complex and often painful for Indigenous Australians.  澳大利亚殖民者社会的历史复杂且对土著澳大利亚人来说常常痛苦不堪。|近义词：colonialism, settlement, dominion|反义词：indigenous society, native population|名词
