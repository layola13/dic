eclipses:/ɪˈklɪpsɪz/| n.|日食；月食；黯然失色 |The obscuring of one celestial body by another.  For example, a solar eclipse occurs when the moon passes between the sun and the Earth, blocking the sun's light. A lunar eclipse happens when the Earth passes between the sun and the moon, casting a shadow on the moon.  Figuratively, it can also refer to something that overshadows or surpasses another. |例句：The total solar eclipse was a spectacular sight.  这次日全食景象壮观。|例句：Her success eclipsed his achievements. 她的成功使他的成就黯然失色。|近义词：overshadow, obscure, dim |反义词：illumination, brightening
