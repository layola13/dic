cookie:/ˈkʊki/ | n. | 曲奇饼干，小甜饼 | A small, flat, sweet baked food, typically made from flour, sugar, and butter.  | Example: I baked a batch of chocolate chip cookies. 我烤了一批巧克力曲奇饼干。 |近义词：biscuit, cracker | 反义词: None


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I eat cookies every day. = 我每天都吃饼干。
She bakes cookies for her friends. = 她为朋友们烤饼干。


一般过去时:
I ate a cookie yesterday. = 我昨天吃了一块饼干。
They baked cookies for the party. = 他们为派对烤了饼干。


现在完成时:
I have eaten all the cookies. = 我已经把所有的饼干都吃掉了。
She has baked many cookies this week. = 她这周已经烤了很多饼干。


过去完成时:
I had eaten all the cookies before they arrived. = 他们在来之前我已经把所有的饼干都吃掉了。
She had baked the cookies before the guests came. = 客人来之前，她已经烤好了饼干。


将来时:
I will bake cookies tomorrow. = 我明天将烤饼干。
She will eat cookies after dinner. = 她晚饭后会吃饼干。


虚拟语气:
If I had more time, I would bake cookies. = 如果我有更多的时间，我会烤饼干。
I wish I had some cookies right now. = 我真希望现在能有一些饼干。


被动语态:
The cookies were baked by my mother. = 这些饼干是我妈妈烤的。
The cookies are being eaten by the children. = 饼干正在被孩子们吃。


疑问句:
Do you like cookies? = 你喜欢饼干吗？
Are there any cookies left? = 还剩下饼干吗？


否定句:
I don't like cookies. = 我不喜欢饼干。
She didn't bake any cookies today. = 她今天没有烤饼干。

词源:
The word "cookie" originates from the Dutch word "koekje," a diminutive of "koek," meaning "cake."  It entered English in the 1700s via American colonists of Dutch descent.  The spelling and pronunciation evolved over time within English.
