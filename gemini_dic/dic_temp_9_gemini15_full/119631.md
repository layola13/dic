middle-class: /ˈmɪdlˌklæs/ | adj., n. | 中产阶级的；中产阶级 |

英文解析:  Relating to or characteristic of the social class between the upper and lower classes, typically having secure employment and above-average income.  The term can refer to both a social group and their economic status.  The specific definition can vary across cultures and time periods.

例句:

He comes from a middle-class family. = 他来自一个中产阶级家庭。

Many middle-class families are struggling to make ends meet. = 许多中产阶级家庭都在努力维持生计。


近义词: bourgeois (more formal), average, mainstream

反义词: upper-class, lower-class, working-class, impoverished


词性:  adjective, noun

词源:

The term "middle class" emerged in the 18th and 19th centuries, reflecting the growing social and economic stratification of societies during the Industrial Revolution.  "Middle" denotes a position between the extremes of wealth and poverty, while "class" refers to a group of people sharing similar social and economic status.  The precise meaning has evolved over time, influenced by changing social structures and economic conditions.
