Word Origin:

The word "hypoglycaemia" is a combination of several Greek roots:

* "hypo-" (ὑπό-): meaning "under" or "below"
* "glyc-" (γλυκύς): meaning "sweet" (referring to glucose)
* "-aemia" (-αιμία): meaning "blood condition"


Therefore, the word literally translates to "a condition of below-normal blood sugar."
