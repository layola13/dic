parasceve:/ˈpærəsiv/| n. |预备日；星期五；准备|The word "parasceve" refers to the day before a Jewish Sabbath or a Christian holy day, particularly the Friday before Easter. It comes from the Greek word παρασκευή (paraskeuē), meaning "preparation."  In Christian tradition, it refers specifically to Good Friday.  In Jewish tradition, it's the day before Shabbat. | The parasceve before Easter was a day of somber reflection.  复活节前的预备日是沉思默想的一天。|近义词: preparation, eve, vigil |反义词: none
