sicked:/sɪkt/| vt.|使恶心；使厌恶；使呕吐|To make someone feel sick or disgusted.  The smell sickened me. |The spoiled food sickened the whole family.|近义词：disgusted, nauseated, revolted|反义词：pleased, delighted|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The smell of rotten eggs sickens me. = 烂鸡蛋的气味让我恶心。
That movie sickens many viewers. = 那部电影让许多观众感到恶心。

一般过去时:
The sight of blood sickened her. = 看到血让她恶心。
The bad news sickened him to the core. = 这个坏消息让他非常难受。

现在完成时:
The constant negativity has sickened me. = 持续的消极情绪让我厌烦了。
This repetitive task has sickened me of my job. = 这重复性的工作让我对我的工作感到厌倦。

过去完成时:
He had sickened of the endless meetings before the project ended. = 在项目结束之前，他已经厌倦了没完没了的会议。
She had sickened of his constant complaints by the time he finished. = 等他说完的时候，她已经厌烦了他的不断抱怨了。

将来时:
The constant exposure to pollution will sicken many people. = 持续暴露在污染中会让许多人感到恶心。
I think that new policy will sicken a lot of people. = 我认为这项新政策会让很多人感到厌恶。

虚拟语气:
If I had known how bad it would be, I would have sickened sooner. = 如果我知道会这么糟，我就会早点感到厌恶。
If only he hadn't eaten that, he wouldn't have sickened. = 要是他没吃那个就好了，他就不会恶心了。

被动语态:
Many people were sickened by the news. = 许多人对这个消息感到恶心。
He was sickened by the cruelty of the dictator. = 他被独裁者的残暴行为激怒了。

疑问句:
Does the smell sicken you? = 这气味让你恶心吗？
What sickened you about the movie? = 你对这部电影的什么感到恶心？

否定句:
The food didn't sicken me. = 这食物没有让我恶心。
The situation didn't sicken her, rather it made her angry. = 这种情况并没有让她恶心，而是让她生气。
