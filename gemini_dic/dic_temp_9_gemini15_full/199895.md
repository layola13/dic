sixpenny:/ˈsɪkspɛni/| n.|六便士的；六便士硬币|A sixpenny piece was a British coin worth six pence.  The term is now largely historical, referring to something costing six pence or having a value of six pence.  It can also be used adjectivally to describe something costing that amount.|Example:  He bought a sixpenny newspaper. 他买了一份六便士的报纸。  She had a sixpenny chocolate. 她有一块六便士的巧克力。|近义词:sixpence (noun)|反义词:None specifically, but words like 'expensive' or 'costly' could be considered antonyms in context.|名词
