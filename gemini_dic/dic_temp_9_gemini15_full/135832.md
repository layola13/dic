nonpropitiative: /ˌnɒnprəˈpɪʃɪətiv/ | adj. | 不讨好的；不得人心的；不和解的 | Not intended to appease or conciliate; unpropitiating.  This adjective describes actions, words, or attitudes that are not intended to win favor or forgiveness.  It suggests a lack of effort to please or reconcile. |

例句：

His nonpropitiative response only angered her further.  = 他不得人心的回应只会让她更加生气。

The company's nonpropitiative stance during the strike led to further conflict. = 公司在罢工期间不妥协的态度导致了进一步的冲突。


近义词：unconciliatory, unappeasing, antagonistic, hostile, offensive


反义词：conciliatory, appeasing, propitiatory, placatory, amicable


词源:

* **non-**:  Prefix meaning "not."
* **propitiative**:  Derived from the verb "propitiate," meaning "to appease or conciliate (someone)."  This in turn comes from the Latin "propitiare," meaning "to make favorable, appease."
