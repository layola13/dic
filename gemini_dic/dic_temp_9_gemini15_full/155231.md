phagocytability:/ˌfæɡəʊsaɪtəˈbɪləti/| n. |吞噬性，可吞噬性 |The ability of a cell or organism to be engulfed by a phagocyte.  The degree to which a substance or particle can be phagocytosed.  |Example: The increased phagocytability of the bacteria after opsonization contributed to their rapid clearance from the bloodstream.  细菌经调理作用后吞噬性增强，促使其迅速清除于血液。|近义词：ingestibility,  susceptibility to phagocytosis|反义词：resistance to phagocytosis|名词
