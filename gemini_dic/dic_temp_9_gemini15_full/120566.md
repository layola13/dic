mire: /ˈmaɪər/ | n., v. | 泥沼；困境；使陷入困境 |

英文解析:
As a noun, "mire" refers to a bog or marsh; soft, wet, muddy ground.  As a verb, it means to involve someone or something in a difficult situation; to cause someone or something to become stuck or entangled.

例句:
The car got stuck in the mire. = 车陷进了泥沼。

The scandal mired the politician's reputation. = 丑闻损害了这位政治家的名誉。


近义词:
Noun: bog, marsh, swamp, quagmire
Verb: entangle, involve, embroil, bog down


反义词:
Noun: firm ground, dry land
Verb: extricate, free, release


词性:
Noun, Verb


词源:
Old English *myre, of Germanic origin; related to Dutch meer and German Moor ‘moor, bog’.
