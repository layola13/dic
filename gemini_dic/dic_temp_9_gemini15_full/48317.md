decree:/dɪˈkriː/| vt. n. |法令；命令；判决| To officially decide or announce something;  a formal order or decision, especially one made by a person in authority. |The king decreed a new law. 国王颁布了一项新法令。  The judge decreed that the defendant was guilty.  法官判决被告有罪。|ordain, enact, mandate|revoke, repeal|


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government decrees new regulations every year. = 政府每年都会颁布新的规章制度。
The judge decrees the sentence after hearing the evidence. = 法官在听取证据后宣判。


一般过去时:
The emperor decreed a nationwide holiday. = 皇帝下令全国放假。
The court decreed that the property should be divided equally. = 法院判决财产应平均分配。


现在完成时:
The council has decreed a ban on smoking in public places. = 理事会已颁布禁令，禁止在公共场所吸烟。
The court has decreed that the company must pay compensation. = 法院已判决该公司必须支付赔偿金。


过去完成时:
The king had decreed that no one should leave the castle. = 国王已下令任何人不得离开城堡。
The parliament had decreed the new tax before the election. = 议会在选举前已颁布了新的税收。


将来时:
The president will decree a state of emergency. = 总统将宣布紧急状态。
The committee will decree the winner of the competition. = 委员会将宣布比赛的获胜者。


虚拟语气:
If the king decreed a pardon, he would save many lives. = 如果国王下令赦免，他就能挽救许多人的生命。
I wish the government would decree a fairer tax system. = 我希望政府能颁布一个更公平的税收制度。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
