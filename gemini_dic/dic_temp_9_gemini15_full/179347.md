redounded:/rɪˈdaʊndɪd/| v.| 反作用，导致，归于 | To have a specified effect, especially a beneficial one.  |例句：His efforts redounded to the benefit of the entire community. = 他的努力使整个社区受益。
His arrogance redounded to his downfall. = 他的傲慢导致了他的失败。|近义词：contributed, resulted, led |反义词：hindered, impeded


USAGE->
一般现在时:
His actions redound to his credit. = 他的行为为他赢得了荣誉。
The policy redounds to the benefit of the wealthy. = 这项政策使富人受益。


一般过去时:
The reforms redounded to the country's economic growth. = 改革促进了国家的经济增长。
Her kindness redounded to her reputation. = 她的善良提升了她的声誉。


现在完成时:
His hard work has redounded to his success. = 他的努力成就了他的成功。
The investment has redounded to the company's profits. = 这项投资增加了公司的利润。


过去完成时:
The previous government's policies had redounded to the nation's debt. = 前政府的政策导致了国家的债务。
Their efforts had redounded to a significant improvement in living standards. = 他们的努力使生活水平有了显著提高。


将来时:
This decision will redound to the company's advantage. = 这个决定将对公司有利。
These actions will redound to the glory of the nation. = 这些行动将为国家赢得荣誉。


虚拟语气:
If he had worked harder, his efforts might have redounded to his greater success. = 如果他更努力工作，他的努力可能会带来更大的成功。
If the plan were implemented, it would redound to the benefit of all. = 如果实施该计划，将使所有人受益。


被动语态:
The success of the project was redounded to the team's hard work. = 项目的成功归功于团队的辛勤工作。
The benefits of the program were redounded to the community. = 该项目的益处惠及社区。


疑问句:
Will this decision redound to our advantage? = 这个决定对我们有利吗？
How will this project redound to the company's reputation? = 这个项目将如何影响公司的声誉？


否定句:
His actions did not redound to his credit. = 他的行为没有给他带来荣誉。
The policy did not redound to the benefit of the poor. = 这项政策并没有使穷人受益。
