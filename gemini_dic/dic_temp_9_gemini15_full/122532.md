modals:/ˈmoʊdəlz/| n.|情态动词|Modal verbs are auxiliary verbs that express a modality, such as possibility, necessity, permission, or ability. They are always used with another verb, and they change the meaning of the main verb.  They don't conjugate in the same way as other verbs.| Examples: can, could, may, might, must, should, will, would, shall.|近义词:auxiliary verbs|反义词:None.


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I can swim. = 我会游泳。
She may come later. = 她可能晚点来。


一般过去时:
I could swim when I was younger. = 我年轻的时候会游泳。
He might have gone home. = 他可能回家了。


现在完成时:
She may have finished her work. = 她可能已经完成了她的工作。
We should have left earlier. = 我们应该早点离开。


过去完成时:
They might have already eaten. = 他们可能已经吃过了。
I should have studied harder. = 我本应该更努力学习。


将来时:
I will help you. = 我会帮助你。
We shall overcome. = 我们将会克服。


虚拟语气:
I wish I could fly. = 我希望我会飞。
If I were you, I would go. = 如果我是你，我会去。


被动语态:
It can be done. = 它可以被完成。
It should be finished by tomorrow. = 它应该在明天之前完成。


疑问句:
Can you help me? = 你能帮我吗？
Should I call him? = 我应该给他打电话吗？


否定句:
I cannot swim. = 我不会游泳。
She may not come. = 她可能不来。


PHRASE->
can't help but = 不禁
would rather = 宁愿
had better = 最好
might as well = 也许不妨
must have = 一定
