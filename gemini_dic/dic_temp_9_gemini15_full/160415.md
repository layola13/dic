popping-off:/ˈpɒpɪŋ ɒf/|/ˈpɑːpɪŋ ɒf/| v. |突然离开；突然爆发；夸夸其谈；(非正式)炫耀|To leave suddenly; to erupt suddenly; to boast or talk boastfully; (informal) to show off. |例句：He popped off without saying goodbye. 他没说再见就突然离开了。

He popped off about his achievements. 他夸夸其谈自己的成就。


近义词：leaving abruptly, bursting forth, showing off, boasting |反义词：staying put, remaining calm, being modest


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He pops off whenever he feels like it. = 他想走就走。
She pops off about her new car all the time. = 她总是夸耀她的新车。


一般过去时:
He popped off to the store without a word. = 他一言不发地跑到商店去了。
She popped off at the meeting, interrupting everyone. = 她在会议上突然插话，打断了大家。


现在完成时:
He's popped off to London for the weekend. = 他周末去了伦敦。
She's popped off about her promotion to everyone she knows. = 她把她升职的事情告诉了她认识的每一个人。


过去完成时:
He had popped off before I could ask him a question. = 我还没来得及问他问题，他就离开了。
She had popped off several times during the lecture. = 她在讲座期间已经好几次突然离席了。


将来时:
He will pop off to the beach as soon as he finishes work. = 他一下班就去海滩。
She will pop off to her parents' house for Christmas. = 她圣诞节要去她父母家。


虚拟语气:
If he popped off now, who would finish the work? = 如果他现在离开，谁来完成工作？
If she had popped off earlier, she wouldn't have missed the bus. = 如果她早点离开，她就不会错过公共汽车了。


被动语态: *(This is not a common usage for "popping off".)*  The meeting was interrupted when someone popped off. = 当有人突然离席时，会议被打断了。(被动语态不太常用)
The news was popped off to the press immediately. = 消息立即被发布给媒体。(此处的pop off 含义不同，指迅速发布)


疑问句:
Will he pop off again soon? = 他还会很快离开吗？
Did she pop off at the party? = 她在派对上突然离开了吗？


否定句:
He didn't pop off without saying goodbye this time. = 这次他没有不告而别。
She didn't pop off about her success at all. = 她根本没有炫耀她的成功。


PHRASE->
pop off to (somewhere) = 迅速前往某地
pop off about (something) = 夸耀某事
pop off without warning = 毫无预兆地离开
pop off at (someone) = 对某人大发脾气
pop off and on = 不时地离开又回来
