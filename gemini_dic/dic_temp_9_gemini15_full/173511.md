quasi-fortunately:/ˈkwɑːzaɪ ˈfɔːrtʃənətli/| adv. |几近幸运地，算是幸运地 |It means something was almost fortunate, but not entirely so.  There's a sense of relief mixed with a lingering element of misfortune or uncertainty. |例句：Quasi-fortunately, the rain stopped just as we reached the shelter. = 算是幸运的是，我们到达避难所时雨刚好停了。


例句：Quasi-fortunately, the surgery was successful, but he'll need extensive physical therapy. = 算是幸运的是，手术成功了，但他需要进行大量的物理治疗。


近义词：luckily, fortunately (but with a nuance of incompleteness) |反义词：unfortunately, unluckily


词源:
* **quasi-**:  From Latin "quasi," meaning "as if," "almost," or "seemingly."
* **fortunately**: From "fortune" (good luck or chance) + "-ately" (adverbial suffix).

USAGE->
This word is an adverb, so it doesn't conjugate like verbs.  Its usage is similar to other adverbs of manner.  It modifies a verb, describing how an action was performed in relation to fortune.

PHRASE->
There aren't common set phrases using "quasi-fortunately" due to its relatively uncommon and nuanced usage.  It's typically used as a standalone adverb.
