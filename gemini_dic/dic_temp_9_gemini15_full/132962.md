nonempiricism: /ˌnɒnɪmˈpɪrɪsɪzəm/ | n. | 非经验主义 | The rejection of empiricism; a philosophical stance that emphasizes non-sensory sources of knowledge, such as reason, intuition, or revelation.  It holds that knowledge can be acquired through methods other than sensory experience and observation.  |  Many religious beliefs are based on nonempiricism, accepting faith as a source of truth.  | 近义词: rationalism, intuitionism, apriorism | 反义词: empiricism, positivism
