lacker:/ˈlækər/| n.|清漆；漆器|A lacquer is a protective coating made from a resinous substance, often applied to wood or metal to give it a glossy finish.  It can also refer to an object coated in lacquer.  |例句：The antique table was covered in a beautiful, dark lacquer. 这张古董桌子涂上了漂亮深色的漆。

The bowl is made of lacquered wood. 这个碗是用漆木做的。|近义词：varnish, enamel, shellac|反义词：uncoated, bare|名词

词源:
The word "lacquer" ultimately derives from the Hindi word "lakh," referring to the resinous substance secreted by the lac insect.  This resin was historically used to create lacquers.  The word entered English via various languages including Portuguese and French.
