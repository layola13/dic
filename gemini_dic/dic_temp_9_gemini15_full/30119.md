cedent:/ˈsiːdnt/| n. |先行词，前项|A word or phrase that is replaced by a pronoun or other substitute later in the sentence or passage.  It refers to the word or phrase to which a pronoun, pro-form, or other anaphoric expression refers.  |Example:  In the sentence "John saw Mary, and he waved at her," "John" is the antecedent of "he" and "Mary" is the antecedent of "her".  |近义词：antecedent, precursor|反义词：consequent, result
