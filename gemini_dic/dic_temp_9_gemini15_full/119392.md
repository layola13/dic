microeconomics:/ˌmaɪkroʊˌiːkəˈnɑːmɪks/| n. |微观经济学 |The branch of economics that studies the behavior of individual households and firms in making decisions on the allocation of limited resources.  It typically analyzes supply and demand in individual markets and their interaction. | Example:  Microeconomics examines how individual consumers make purchasing decisions. = 微观经济学研究单个消费者如何做出购买决策。

Microeconomics also explores the behavior of individual firms in setting prices and determining output levels. = 微观经济学还探讨了单个企业在设定价格和确定产量方面的行为。|近义词：individual economics |反义词：macroeconomics |名词
