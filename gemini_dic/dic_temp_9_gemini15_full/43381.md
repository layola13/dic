cranium:/ˈkreɪniəm/| n. |颅骨，头盖骨 |The cranium protects the brain. 颅骨保护大脑。|近义词：skull |反义词：None


英文解析:The cranium is the bony structure that forms the head of vertebrates.  It encloses and protects the brain.  The term can refer to the entire skull, or just the upper part, excluding the mandible (jawbone).


例句:

The doctor examined the patient's cranium for signs of injury.医生检查病人的颅骨是否有受伤的迹象。

A fractured cranium is a serious medical emergency.颅骨骨折是一项严重的医疗紧急情况。


词源:

From Late Latin *cranium, from Greek κρᾱνίον (kraníon) "skull," diminutive of κρᾱς (krās) "head."  The word's history reflects the ancient understanding of the skull's crucial role in protecting the head and brain.


用法说明:

由于cranium主要作为名词使用，以下只提供名词的用法说明。


名词用法：

单数：a cranium, the cranium

复数：crania


在句中作主语：The cranium is a complex structure.  (颅骨是一个复杂的结构。)

在句中作宾语：Scientists studied the ancient cranium. (科学家们研究了古代的颅骨。)

在句中作表语：That is a human cranium. (那是个人类颅骨。)

在句中作同位语：The skull, or cranium, protects the brain. (头骨，或者说颅骨，保护大脑。)
