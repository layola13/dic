overprizes:/ˌoʊvərˈpraɪzɪz/| vt.|过高评价；估价过高|To place too high a value on something.  It implies an inaccurate assessment of worth, often leading to inflated prices or expectations.|例句：The company overprizes its products, leading to lower sales.  这家公司对其产品估价过高，导致销量下降。

例句：He overprizes his own abilities and often takes on tasks he cannot handle. 他过高估计了自己的能力，经常承担力所不及的任务。


近义词：overvalue, overrate, exaggerate the worth of

反义词：undervalue, underrate


词源:over-(超过)+prize(估价) + -es(动词第三人称单数)
