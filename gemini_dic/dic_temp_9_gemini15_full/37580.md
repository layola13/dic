communalizing: /kəˈmjuːnəlaɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 使公有化；使集体化 | The process of making something communal or shared;  transforming private property or resources into collective ownership or use.  | 例句：The government is communalizing the land to promote equitable distribution of resources. = 政府正在将土地公有化，以促进资源的公平分配。

The ongoing communalizing of the forest resources has led to better management. = 森林资源的持续公有化导致了更好的管理。


近义词：collectivizing, socializing, nationalizing | 反义词：privatizing, individualizing | 词性：动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They are communalizing the water supply. = 他们正在使供水公有化。
The village communalizes its farmland. = 村庄将农田公有化。


一般过去时:
The government communalized the railways in the 1950s. = 政府在20世纪50年代将铁路公有化。
They communalized the village's resources. = 他们将村庄的资源公有化。


现在完成时:
The city has communalized its transportation system. = 该城市已将其交通系统公有化。
They have communalized the forest for sustainable use. = 他们已经将森林公有化以实现可持续利用。


过去完成时:
Before the revolution, they had already communalized the factories. = 在革命之前，他们已经将工厂公有化。
The community had communalized the well long before the drought. = 在旱灾很久以前，社区就已经将井公有化了。


将来时:
The council will communalize the park's facilities next year. = 理事会明年将对公园的设施进行公有化。
They will communalize the energy grid in the coming decade. = 他们将在未来十年内对电网进行公有化。


虚拟语气:
If they communalized the land, it might increase agricultural productivity. = 如果他们将土地公有化，可能会提高农业生产力。
I wish they had communalized the internet access sooner. = 我希望他们早点对互联网接入进行公有化。


被动语态:
The land was communalized by the government. = 土地被政府公有化了。
The resources are being communalized for the benefit of all. = 资源正在被公有化，以造福所有人。


疑问句:
Will the government communalize the healthcare system? = 政府会将医疗系统公有化吗？
Have they communalized the water resources yet? = 他们已经将水资源公有化了吗？


否定句:
They did not communalize the forests. = 他们没有将森林公有化。
The company will not communalize its profits. = 公司不会将其利润公有化。


PHRASE->
communalizing resources = 资源公有化
communalizing property =  财产公有化
communalizing land = 土地公有化
communalizing services = 服务公有化
communalizing efforts =  集体努力


词源:  Comes from "communal" (relating to a community or shared by a community) + "-izing" (forming verbs indicating the process of making something).  The root is ultimately from Latin "communis" (common).
