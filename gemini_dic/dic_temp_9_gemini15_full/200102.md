skidder:/ˈskɪdər/| n. vt.| 拖拉机, 滑动者 | A skidder is a piece of heavy equipment used in logging to drag felled trees from the cutting area to a landing.  It can also refer to someone or something that skids or slides. | 例句：The skidder pulled the massive logs through the forest.  拖拉机将巨大的原木拖过森林。  The car skidded on the icy road. 车子在结冰的路上打滑了。 | 近义词：tractor, logger,  | 反义词：None specifically, but words suggesting stability or control could be considered antonyms in certain contexts. | 名词，动词

词源:
The word "skidder" is derived from the verb "skid," meaning to slide or slip.  The "-er" suffix transforms the verb into a noun, denoting a person or thing that performs the action of skidding.  In the context of logging equipment, it specifically describes a machine that skids logs.
