epidemiologies:/ˌɛpɪˌdiːmiˈɒlədʒiz/ | n. | 流行病学(复数) | The study of the distribution and determinants of health-related states or events in specified populations, and the application of this study to the control of health problems.  |  The epidemiologies of various diseases were discussed at the conference.  各种疾病的流行病学在会议上进行了讨论。  The latest epidemiologies suggest a rise in flu cases. 最新的流行病学研究表明流感病例有所增加。 |  epidemiology (单数), public health |  cure (反义词，并非完全对立，而是研究对象不同)

一般现在时:
Epidemiologies reveal important trends in disease patterns. 流行病学揭示了疾病模式的重要趋势。
These epidemiologies suggest a link between pollution and respiratory illness. 这些流行病学研究表明污染与呼吸系统疾病之间存在联系。


一般过去时:
Early epidemiologies often relied on limited data. 早期的流行病学研究往往依赖于有限的数据。
The epidemiologies of the 1918 influenza pandemic were extensively studied. 1918 年流感大流行的流行病学研究得到了广泛的研究。


现在完成时:
Recent epidemiologies have shown a decrease in measles cases. 最近的流行病学研究表明麻疹病例有所减少。
Researchers have completed several epidemiologies on the impact of climate change on health. 研究人员已经完成了关于气候变化对健康影响的几项流行病学研究。


过去完成时:
Before the study, epidemiologies had not fully explained the disease's spread. 在这项研究之前，流行病学尚未完全解释这种疾病的传播方式。
By the time the outbreak ended, epidemiologies had already identified the source. 在疫情结束时，流行病学已经确定了病源。


将来时:
Future epidemiologies will likely focus on the impact of artificial intelligence on health. 未来的流行病学研究可能会侧重于人工智能对健康的影响。
We will need more sophisticated epidemiologies to understand this new virus. 我们需要更复杂的流行病学研究来了解这种新型病毒。


虚拟语气:
If we had better epidemiologies, we could have prevented the outbreak. 如果我们有更好的流行病学研究，我们就能预防疫情爆发。
It is essential that accurate epidemiologies be available to guide public health policies.  准确的流行病学研究对于指导公共卫生政策至关重要。
