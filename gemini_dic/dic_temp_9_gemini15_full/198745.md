/sɪˈsɪliən/| n. |西西里人；西西里语|The word "Sicilians" refers to the people of Sicily, an island in the Mediterranean Sea, off the southern coast of Italy.  It can also refer to the Sicilian language, which is a dialect of Italian.  The plural form indicates multiple people from Sicily or instances of the language.|Example: Many Sicilians emigrated to the United States in the late 19th and early 20th centuries.  许多西西里人在19世纪末和20世纪初移民到美国。|Example:  The unique culinary traditions of the Sicilians are celebrated worldwide. 西西里人的独特烹饪传统在世界各地受到赞誉。|Italians, Mediterraneans|No direct antonyms, but could be contrasted with people from other regions.|Noun
