/ɪntərˈkɒlənaɪzd/ | adj. |  相互殖民的；互相殖民的 |  Describes a situation where two or more groups or societies engage in mutual colonization, influencing and shaping each other's cultures, politics, and social structures.  This often implies a complex and reciprocal relationship, rather than a simple dominance of one group over another.  | The intercolonized relationship between the two island nations resulted in a unique blend of cultural traditions. |  mutually colonized, interconnected |  uncolonized, independent
