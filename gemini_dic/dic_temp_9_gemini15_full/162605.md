preannounce:/priːəˈnaʊns/| vt.|预先宣布，提前公布|To announce something in advance.  The company preannounced its earnings for the quarter.|例句：The company preannounced its strong earnings, exceeding analysts' expectations. 公司提前公布了强劲的盈利，超过了分析师的预期。

The government preannounced a new policy on environmental protection. 政府提前公布了新的环保政策。|近义词：announce beforehand, give advance notice of, foreshadow|反义词：postpone, delay


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They preannounce their sales every year. = 他们每年都预先公布他们的销售业绩。
The company preannounces its product launches. = 公司预先公布其产品发布。

一般过去时:
The company preannounced its new product last month. = 公司上个月提前公布了它的新产品。
They preannounced the merger before the official announcement. = 他们在正式宣布之前就提前公布了合并的消息。

过去完成时:

They had preannounced the price increase, so the customers were prepared. = 他们已经提前公布了价格上涨的消息，所以客户已经做好了准备。

虚拟语气:
