preopposition:/ˌpriːˌɒpəˈzɪʃən/| n. | 事先反对；预先反对 | The act of opposing something before it happens;  anticipation of opposition.  It often involves taking proactive measures to prevent or mitigate potential opposition. |例句：The preopposition to the new policy was fierce, stemming from concerns about its impact on small businesses. 事先对这项新政策的反对非常激烈，源于对其对小型企业影响的担忧。|近义词：proactive opposition, preventative opposition, anticipatory objection |反义词：acquiescence, acceptance, approval |  词性: 名词
