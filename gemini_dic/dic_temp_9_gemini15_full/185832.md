revitalizing:/ˌriˈvaɪtəlaɪzɪŋ/| adj.|使恢复活力的；使复兴的； |Giving new life, energy, or vigor to something.| The revitalizing effects of the spa treatment were immediately noticeable. 水疗护理的恢复活力效果立即显而易见。|近义词：rejuvenating, invigorating, refreshing |反义词：debilitating, weakening, exhausting


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The program is revitalizing the downtown area.  = 这个项目正在使市中心地区恢复活力。
This new policy is revitalizing the company's image. = 这项新政策正在使公司的形象焕然一新。


一般过去时:
The rain revitalized the wilting plants. = 雨水使枯萎的植物恢复了生机。
The renovation revitalized the old building. = 翻新使旧建筑焕发了青春。


现在完成时:
The investment has revitalized the local economy. = 这项投资已经使当地经济恢复了活力。
The new management has revitalized the struggling business. = 新的管理层已经使这家苦苦挣扎的企业恢复了生机。


过去完成时:
They had revitalized the community before the new mayor arrived. = 在新市长到来之前，他们已经使社区恢复了活力。
The artist had revitalized his career with a new exhibition. = 艺术家通过一个新的展览使他的事业恢复了活力。


将来时:
The project will revitalize the neglected park. = 该项目将使被忽视的公园恢复活力。
The new technology will revitalize the manufacturing process. = 新技术将使制造流程焕发生机。


虚拟语气:
If we invested more, we could revitalize the town. = 如果我们投资更多，我们就能使这个城镇恢复活力。
I wish they would revitalize the old theater. = 我希望他们能使旧剧院恢复活力。


被动语态:

The old factory was revitalized through government funding. = 老工厂通过政府资金得到了恢复。


疑问句:

否定句:
