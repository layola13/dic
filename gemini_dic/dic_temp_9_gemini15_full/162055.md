pre-Augustan:/ˌpriːɔːˈɡʌstən/| adj. |奥古斯都时期之前的|Relating to the period in Roman literature and culture before the reign of Augustus (27 BC – 14 AD).  This era is characterized by a less polished and more experimental style compared to the Augustan period, which is known for its refinement and adherence to classical models. |The pre-Augustan poets experimented with various forms and styles. = 奥古斯都时期之前的诗人们尝试了各种不同的形式和风格。|近义词：ante-Augustan, pre-imperial (in a Roman context)|反义词：Augustan, imperial (in a Roman context)
