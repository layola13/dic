quasi-financially: /ˈkwɑːzaɪ faɪˈnænʃəli/ | adj. |准财务的，似财务的 |  Relating to or resembling financial matters, but not strictly or fully financial.  It often suggests an activity or situation that has some, but not all, of the characteristics of a financial transaction or system. | 例句：The company's quasi-financial activities, such as barter arrangements, were not reflected in the traditional accounting statements. 这家公司的准财务活动，例如易货交易，没有反映在传统的会计报表中。
The government's quasi-financial support for the struggling industry involved indirect subsidies and tax breaks. 政府对陷入困境的行业的准财务支持包括间接补贴和减税。 | 近义词：parafiscal, semi-financial | 反义词：fully financial, purely financial

Interrogative sentences:

Are these quasi-financial arrangements subject to the same regulations as formal financial transactions? = 这些准财务安排是否与正式的财务交易受制于相同的规章制度？


Negative sentences:

Their activities were not deemed quasi-financial by the regulatory body. = 他们的活动未被监管机构认定为准财务活动。
