make hay while the sun shines: /meɪk heɪ waɪl ðə sʌn ʃaɪnz/ | idiom |  趁热打铁；抓紧时机；好时光要把握 | This proverb advises taking advantage of favorable circumstances before they pass.  It originates from the agricultural practice of making hay (drying cut grass for animal feed), a task best done while the sun is shining. |

例句：

We should make hay while the sun shines and finish this project before the deadline. = 我们应该趁热打铁，在截止日期之前完成这个项目。

The market is booming; let's make hay while the sun shines and expand our business. = 市场正在蓬勃发展；让我们抓住时机，扩大我们的业务。


近义词：seize the day, strike while the iron is hot, take advantage of, make the most of


反义词：procrastinate, delay, wait and see


词性：idiom (习语)


USAGE->  (由于make hay while the sun shines是一个习语，而非一个可以进行时态变化的句子，因此没有对应的时态变化例句。)
