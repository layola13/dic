nondeprivable: /ˌnɒndɪˈpraɪvəbəl/ | adj. | 不可剥夺的，不能被剥夺的 | Incapable of being deprived or taken away.  It refers to rights, privileges, or possessions that cannot be legitimately removed or denied. | Example:  The right to life is considered a nondeprivable human right.  生命权被认为是一项不可剥夺的人权。 | 近义词: inalienable, inherent, inviolable | 反义词: forfeitable, removable, dispensable


USAGE->
This word is primarily an adjective and doesn't have verb conjugations like "what".  The grammatical usage revolves around its descriptive function within a sentence.

Example Sentences:

Affirmative Sentences:
The constitution guarantees certain nondeprivable rights to its citizens.  宪法保障公民享有某些不可剥夺的权利。
His nondeprivable inheritance ensured his financial security. 他不可剥夺的遗产确保了他的财务安全。


Negative Sentences:
There are no completely nondeprivable rights in practice; even fundamental rights can be restricted under exceptional circumstances. 在实践中，没有任何完全不可剥夺的权利；即使是基本权利，在特殊情况下也可能受到限制。
The claim that this possession is nondeprivable is questionable under the existing legal framework. 在现行法律框架下，这项财产不可剥夺的说法值得怀疑。


PHRASE->
There aren't common phrases specifically using "nondeprivable".  Its use is largely within legal or philosophical contexts.  Examples of phrases *related* to the concept:

inherent rights: 固有权利
fundamental rights: 基本权利
inalienable rights: 不可转让的权利
civil liberties: 公民自由
due process: 正当程序
