sinkholes:/ˈsɪŋkˌhoʊlz/| n.|天坑，陷坑|A sinkhole is a cavity in the ground, especially in a karst region, formed by the collapse of a subterranean cavern.  They can range in size from a few feet to hundreds of feet across and deep. Sinkholes are often caused by the dissolution of underlying soluble rocks, such as limestone or gypsum, by groundwater. |例句：The recent heavy rains caused several sinkholes to open up in the town. 最近的大雨导致城镇出现了几个天坑。

The sinkhole swallowed the car whole.  天坑整个吞没了汽车。|近义词：cavity, chasm, crater|反义词：elevation, hill, mound|名词
