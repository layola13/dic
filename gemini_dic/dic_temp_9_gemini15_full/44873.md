cry-babies: /ˈkraɪˌbeɪbiz/ | n. |爱哭鬼；爱抱怨的人 | A crybaby is a person, typically a child, who cries frequently or easily.  The term is often used derisively to describe someone who is overly sensitive or complains excessively. | Example:  He's such a crybaby; he cries every time he doesn't get his way. 他真是个爱哭鬼；每次没得到想要的东西就哭。


Example: Stop being such a crybaby and face your problems. 别再像个爱哭鬼一样，勇敢面对你的问题吧。

近义词: whiner, complainer,  sissy (informal),  wimp (informal)

反义词: stoic, resilient, tough, unflappable


词源:  The word is a compound of "cry" and "baby."  "Cry" comes from the Old English *crīdan, meaning "to cry out," and "baby" is derived from Middle Dutch babe, meaning "child." The combination creates a descriptive term for someone who behaves like a crying baby.
