pasticheurs:/pæʃˈtiːʃɜːrz/| n. |拼贴艺术家；模仿者|A pasticheur is an artist, writer, or composer who creates works that imitate the style of another artist or period.  They blend elements from different sources to create something new, often in a playful or ironic way.  The term often carries a slightly negative connotation, suggesting a lack of originality or a superficial imitation, but it can also be used neutrally or even positively to describe artists who skillfully blend styles. |例句：The film is a dazzling pastiche, borrowing freely from classic Hollywood musicals and silent cinema. 这部电影是一部令人眼花缭乱的拼贴作品，它大量借鉴了经典的荷里活音乐剧和默片。

The young writer is considered a pasticheur, drawing heavily from the works of Hemingway and Fitzgerald.这位年轻的作家被认为是一位拼贴艺术家，大量借鉴了海明威和菲茨杰拉德的作品。|近义词：imitator, parodist, emulator, stylist|反义词：innovator, original, pioneer|名词
