punched:/pʌntʃt/| v.| 打孔；猛击；冲压 | To strike with a fist; to make a hole in something with a tool.  |例句：He punched the wall in anger. 他愤怒地打了墙一拳。|例句：She punched a hole in the paper. 她在一张纸上打了个洞。|近义词：hit, struck, hammered |反义词：caressed, stroked


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He punches the bag every day. = 他每天都打沙袋。
She punches holes in the tickets. = 她把票打孔。

一般过去时:
He punched the bully in the face. = 他打了那个恶霸的脸。
She punched the clock at 5 pm. = 她下午五点打卡。

现在完成时:
He has punched several holes in the wall. = 他已经在墙上打了好几个洞了。
She has punched in for the day. = 她已经打卡上班了。

过去完成时:
He had punched the ticket before boarding the train. = 他在登上火车前已经打过票了。
She had punched the data into the computer before the system crashed. = 系统崩溃之前，她已经把数据输入电脑了。

将来时:
He will punch the time clock tomorrow morning. = 他明天早上将打卡。
She will punch the holes using a special tool. = 她将用特殊的工具打孔。

虚拟语气:
If he had punched harder, he might have won the fight. = 如果他打得更用力，他可能就赢了比赛。
I wish I hadn't punched him. = 我真希望我没有打他。

被动语态:
The hole was punched by a machine. = 这个洞是用机器打出来的。
The tickets are punched by the conductor. = 车票由售票员打孔。

疑问句:
Did he punch him? = 他打了他吗？
Has she punched the data in yet? = 她已经输入数据了吗？

否定句:
He didn't punch back. = 他没有还手。
She hasn't punched the ticket yet. = 她还没打票呢。
