quasi-intellectually:/ˈkwɑːzaɪ ɪnˈtɛləktʃuəli/ | adj. | 似是而非的知识分子般地；貌似知识分子地 | Seemingly intellectual but lacking true depth or substance; superficially intellectual.  It suggests an imitation or pretense of intellectualism. |例句：He engaged in quasi-intellectual discussions, spouting jargon without real understanding. 他参与了似是而非的知识分子讨论，夸夸其谈专业术语却缺乏真正的理解。


例句：Her quasi-intellectual pronouncements often masked a lack of genuine insight. 她的貌似知识分子的言论往往掩盖了缺乏真正的洞察力。


近义词：pseudo-intellectual, superficially intellectual, intellectualizing


反义词：truly intellectual, genuinely insightful, deeply thoughtful


词性：形容词


词源：

* **quasi-**:  from Latin *quasi*, meaning "as if," "as it were," "almost," or "somewhat."  It indicates a resemblance or approximation.

* **intellectual**: from Latin *intellectus*, past participle of *intellegere*, meaning "to understand," "to perceive," or "to comprehend."


用法说明：

"Quasi-intellectually" is used to describe actions, behaviors, or discussions that appear intellectual but lack genuine depth, insight, or understanding.  It often implies a display of superficial knowledge or the use of intellectual jargon to impress rather than to inform or engage in meaningful discourse.  The word carries a slightly negative connotation, suggesting a lack of authenticity or sincerity.
