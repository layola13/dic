inhering:/ɪnˈhɪərɪŋ/ | adj. | 内在的；固有的；固存的 | Existing in something as a permanent, essential, or characteristic attribute.  Inhering qualities are intrinsic and inseparable from the thing itself. |例句：The inherent dangers of mountain climbing are often underestimated. 登山固有的危险常常被低估。

The inherent beauty of the landscape captivated us. 这片景色的内在美让我们着迷。


近义词：intrinsic, innate, inborn, integral, essential | 反义词：extrinsic, external, acquired, added

词源:
From Middle English inhering, present participle of inheren, from Old French inherer, from Latin inhaerēre "to stick in, cling to," from in- "in" + haerēre "to stick, cling."  The sense development is from "clinging to" to "being an essential part of."
