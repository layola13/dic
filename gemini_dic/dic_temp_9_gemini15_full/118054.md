mendacious:/mənˈdeɪʃəs/ | adj. | 虚假的；说谎的；不真实的 | Given the speaker's known mendacious nature, the audience was skeptical of his claims.  鉴于演讲者为人已知不诚实，听众对其说法表示怀疑。 |近义词：false, deceitful, dishonest, untruthful |反义词：truthful, honest, veracious


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is mendacious in his dealings. = 他在交易中不诚实。
The politician's statements are often mendacious. = 这位政治家的言论往往是虚假的。


一般过去时:
His testimony was mendacious and misleading. = 他的证词是虚假的和误导性的。
She was mendacious about her whereabouts. = 她对自己的行踪撒谎了。


现在完成时:
He has been mendacious throughout this entire affair. = 在整件事中，他一直都在撒谎。
They have been mendacious about their intentions. = 他们一直在隐瞒他们的意图。


过去完成时:
The witness had been mendacious in his earlier statements. = 证人在之前的陈述中撒了谎。
It turned out that she had been mendacious about her qualifications. = 事实证明，她关于自己资格的陈述是虚假的。


将来时:
He will likely be mendacious in his response. = 他很可能会在他的回答中撒谎。
I suspect she will be mendacious in her explanation. = 我怀疑她会在她的解释中撒谎。


虚拟语气:
If he were not mendacious, we would trust him. = 如果他不说谎，我们会相信他。
If she hadn't been mendacious, the situation would be different. = 如果她没有撒谎，情况就会不同。


被动语态:
His lies were considered mendacious by the jury. = 陪审团认为他的谎言是虚假的。
The information presented was deemed mendacious by the investigators. = 调查人员认为所提供的信息是虚假的。


疑问句:
Is his account mendacious? = 他的说法是虚假的吗？
Are these claims mendacious? = 这些说法是虚假的吗？


否定句:
His statements were not mendacious; they were simply misunderstood. = 他的说法并非虚假；只是被误解了。
The report was not mendacious, it was simply incomplete. = 报告并非虚假，只是不完整。


PHRASE->
a mendacious statement = 一个虚假的陈述
mendacious behavior = 虚伪的行为
a mendacious character = 一个不诚实的人
mendacious propaganda = 虚假的宣传
utterly mendacious = 完全虚假


词源:
From Latin *mendax ("lying"), from *mendāri ("to tell lies").  源自拉丁语 *mendax（“说谎的”），来自 *mendāri（“说谎”）。
