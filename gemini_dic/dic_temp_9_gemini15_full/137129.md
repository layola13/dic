nonsubsiding: /ˌnɒn səbˈsaɪdɪŋ/ | adj. | 不消退的；持续的 | Not subsiding; continuing.  The word describes something that doesn't decrease, lessen, or settle down. It implies persistence or continuation of a state or condition, often in a negative or problematic context. | 例句：The nonsubsiding pain in his leg required immediate medical attention.  他的腿持续不断的疼痛需要立即就医。


The nonsubsiding floodwaters forced many families to evacuate their homes. 持续不退的洪水迫使许多家庭撤离家园。


近义词：persistent, continuing, unrelenting, unabating, incessant


反义词：subsiding, diminishing, decreasing, abating


词性：形容词


词源：

* **non-**:  A prefix meaning "not" or "opposite of".
* **subsiding**: From the verb "to subside," meaning to become less intense or violent; to settle down; to sink.  This ultimately derives from the Latin "subsidere,"  composed of "sub-" (under) and "sidere" (to sit).
