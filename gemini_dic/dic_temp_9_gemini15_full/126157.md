mutterer:/ˈmʌtərər/| n. |抱怨者；嘟囔的人| A person who mutters frequently.  They often express dissatisfaction or unhappiness through low, indistinct speech. | He's a constant mutterer, always complaining about something. 他是个喋喋不休的抱怨者，总是抱怨一些事情。| grumbler, complainer |  (no direct antonym, but could be considered the opposite of someone cheerful and optimistic)| 名词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The old man mutters to himself every morning. = 老人家每天早上都在自言自语。
She mutters complaints under her breath. = 她小声嘟囔着抱怨。

一般过去时:
He muttered something about the weather. = 他嘟囔着说了些什么关于天气的话。
She muttered an apology before leaving. = 离开前她嘟囔着道了歉。

现在完成时:
He has muttered his dissatisfaction for weeks. = 他已经抱怨了好几个星期了。
She has muttered about the unfairness of it all. = 她一直在抱怨这一切的不公平。

过去完成时:
He had muttered his concerns before the meeting began. = 会议开始前他已经嘟囔着表达了他的担忧。
She had muttered a warning just moments before the accident. = 事故发生前几分钟她低声警告过。

将来时:
He will mutter his disapproval again tomorrow. = 他明天还会再次嘟囔着表示他的不满。
She will mutter about the noise all night. = 她整晚都会抱怨噪音。

虚拟语气:
If he muttered those words, I didn't hear them. = 如果他说了那些话，我没听到。
I wish he wouldn't mutter so much. = 我希望他不要那么多嘴。

被动语态:
His complaints were often muttered under his breath. = 他的抱怨经常是低声嘟囔的。
The dissatisfaction was muttered throughout the meeting. = 在整个会议期间，人们都在私下抱怨不满。

疑问句:
What was he muttering about? = 他在嘟囔些什么？
Is she still muttering? = 她还在嘟囔吗？

否定句:
He didn't mutter a word. = 他一句话也没说。
She didn't mutter any complaints. = 她没有抱怨任何事情。
