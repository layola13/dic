ecocides:/ˈɛkoʊsaɪdz/| n.|生态灭绝；环境破坏|The large-scale destruction of the environment, especially the destruction of ecosystems.  This term is often used to describe actions that cause widespread and irreversible damage to the environment, including the loss of biodiversity and the disruption of ecological processes.  It's a relatively new term gaining traction in discussions about environmental crimes and the need for stronger legal frameworks to protect the environment. |例句：The widespread deforestation in the Amazon rainforest is considered by some to be an ecocide. 亚马逊雨林的广泛砍伐被一些人认为是一种生态灭绝。  The industrial pollution of the river led to an ecocide of the aquatic life. 河流的工业污染导致了水生生物的生态灭绝。|近义词：environmental destruction, environmental devastation, ecological damage, genocide (in the context of species extinction) |反义词：environmental protection, conservation, restoration|名词
