sabering:/ˈseɪbəriŋ/| v.|用军刀砍；用马刀砍；|The act of cutting or striking with a saber.  It can refer to a military action, a celebratory action (like sabering a champagne bottle), or a more metaphorical sense of forceful cutting or removal.|例句：The soldier sabered his way through the enemy lines.  士兵用军刀砍开敌人的防线。


The cavalry officer expertly sabered the champagne bottle. 骑兵军官熟练地用军刀砍开了香槟酒瓶。|近义词：cutting, slashing, cleaving|反义词：joining, uniting

词源:
The word "sabering" comes from the noun "saber," which ultimately derives from the French word "sabre," which in turn is of uncertain origin, possibly from a Slavic word.  The word entered English in the 17th century.
