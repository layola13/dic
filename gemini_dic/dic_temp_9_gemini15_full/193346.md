segmentally:/ˌsɛɡˈmɛntəli/ | adv. | 分节地，分段地 | In linguistics, segmentally refers to the analysis of speech sounds in terms of individual segments (phonemes) and their combinations. This contrasts with suprasegmental analysis, which considers features like stress, intonation, and tone.  | Example: The data was analyzed segmentally, focusing on the individual phonemes.  | 近义词：in segments, piecemeal | 反义词：suprasegmentally
