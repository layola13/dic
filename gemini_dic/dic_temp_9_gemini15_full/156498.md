picaroon:/pɪkəˈruːn/ | n. | 海盗；恶棍；淘气鬼 | A picaroon is a rogue, adventurer, or pirate, especially one who is characterized by roguishness and daring.  They are often depicted as engaging in daring acts, sometimes for personal gain, but not necessarily always associated with large-scale organized piracy.  | Example:  The novel featured a charismatic picaroon who plundered Spanish galleons in the Caribbean.  这个小说塑造了一个有魅力的海盗，他在加勒比海抢劫西班牙的大帆船。


近义词：pirate, buccaneer, rogue, rascal, adventurer | 反义词：law-abiding citizen, honest person


词源：The word "picaroon" originates from the Spanish word "picarón," meaning "rascal" or "rogue."  It entered English in the 16th century, likely through contact with Spanish sailors and colonists.  The word's evolution reflects the historical context of piracy and seafaring adventures in the Americas.
