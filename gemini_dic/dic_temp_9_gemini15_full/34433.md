claustrophobic: /ˌklɔːstrəˈfoʊbɪk/ | adj. |幽闭恐惧症的；令人感到压抑的 | Feeling or relating to an extreme or irrational fear of confined places.  |例句：The small, dark room felt claustrophobic.  狭小黑暗的房间让人感觉压抑。

The low ceiling made the room feel claustrophobic. 低矮的天花板使房间感觉压抑。


近义词：suffocating, oppressive, confining, stifling | 反义词：open, spacious, airy


词源:  From  *claustrum* (Latin for "a closed place") +  *-phobic* (from Greek *phobos*, meaning "fear").  The word was coined in the late 19th century.
