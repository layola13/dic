pill: /pɪl/ | n. |药丸，药片 | A small, solid dose of medicine.  |She took a pill for her headache. 她吃了一颗药丸治头痛。| tablet, capsule | antidote, cure | 名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He takes a pill every day. = 他每天吃一颗药丸。
She takes vitamins in pill form. = 她服用片剂形式的维生素。

一般过去时:
I took a pain reliever pill last night. = 我昨晚吃了一颗止痛药。
The doctor gave him a sleeping pill. = 医生给他开了一颗安眠药。

现在完成时:
She has taken all her medication. = 她已经服用了所有药物。
He has taken several pills today. = 他今天已经服用了几片药。

过去完成时:
I had already taken my pill before the headache started. = 头痛开始之前我已经服药了。
She had taken two pills before she fell asleep. = 她睡着之前已经服了两片药。

将来时:
I will take a pill before I go to bed. = 我睡前会吃药。
She will need to take more pills if the pain continues. = 如果疼痛持续，她将需要服用更多药片。

虚拟语气:
If I had taken the pill earlier, I wouldn't feel so bad now. = 如果我早点吃药，我现在就不会感觉这么难受了。
I wish I had taken a pill before I went out. = 我真希望出门前吃过药。

被动语态:
The pill was swallowed quickly. = 药丸很快被吞了下去。
The medicine is often given in pill form. = 药物通常以药丸的形式给药。

疑问句:
Have you taken your pill today? = 你今天吃药了吗？
Should I take this pill with food? = 我应该饭后吃这药丸吗？

否定句:
He didn't take his medicine. = 他没吃药。
She didn't want to take the bitter pill. = 她不想吃那苦药。
