detract:/dɪˈtrækt/ | vt. | 减损；贬低；转移（注意力） | To diminish the value, importance, or effectiveness of something. |例句：The constant interruptions detracted from her concentration.  持续的打断分散了她的注意力。

例句：His bad temper detracted from his many good qualities. 他的坏脾气掩盖了他的许多优点。


近义词：reduce, diminish, lessen, impair, detract from, take away from | 反义词：enhance, improve, augment, add to


词源:  From Old French *detraire "to detract," from Latin detrahere "to draw off, drag down," from de- "from, away" + trahere "to draw, pull."


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The loud music detracts from the peaceful atmosphere. = 嘈杂的音乐破坏了宁静的氛围。
His arrogance detracts from his credibility. = 他的傲慢减损了他的可信度。


一般过去时:
The scandal detracted from the company's reputation. = 丑闻损害了公司的声誉。
The bad weather detracted from our enjoyment of the trip. = 恶劣的天气影响了我们旅行的乐趣。


现在完成时:
The constant negativity has detracted from her overall happiness. = 持续的消极情绪已经影响了她的整体幸福感。
His lies have detracted from the trust we placed in him. = 他的谎言已经损害了我们对他的信任。


过去完成时:
The previous failures had detracted from their confidence in the project. = 之前的失败已经削弱了他们对项目的信心。
His earlier mistakes had detracted from his overall performance. = 他早期的错误已经影响了他的整体表现。


将来时:
The upcoming election will likely detract from the focus on other important issues. = 即将到来的选举可能会分散对其他重要问题的关注。
Increased regulation will detract from the company’s profits. = 加强的监管将会减少公司的利润。


虚拟语气:
If the noise didn't detract from the performance, it would have been much better. = 如果噪音没有影响演出，它本来会好得多。
I wish the bad weather hadn't detracted from our holiday. = 我真希望糟糕的天气没有影响我们的假期。


被动语态:
His achievements were detracted from by the media's negative coverage. = 他的成就被媒体的负面报道所掩盖。
The project's success was detracted from by unforeseen complications. = 该项目的成功由于不可预见的复杂情况而受到影响。


疑问句:
Does the poor lighting detract from the beauty of the painting? = 昏暗的灯光是否影响了这幅画的美感？
Will the delays detract from the overall project timeline? = 延误会影响项目的整体时间表吗？


否定句:
The positive feedback did not detract from the criticism. = 积极的反馈并没有减少批评。
Nothing could detract from the joy of the moment. = 没有任何事情能够减少那一刻的快乐。
