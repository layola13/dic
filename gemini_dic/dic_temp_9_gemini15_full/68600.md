fantasize:/ˈfæntəsaɪz/| v.|幻想；梦想；编造|To imagine or daydream about something, especially something pleasant or exciting.  It often involves creating scenarios or events that are unlikely or impossible to happen in reality.|例句：I sometimes fantasize about winning the lottery. 我有时会幻想赢得彩票。

例句：She likes to fantasize about living in a tropical paradise. 她喜欢幻想住在热带天堂。


近义词：dream, imagine, daydream, envision

反义词：realize, achieve


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He fantasizes about becoming a famous musician. 他幻想成为一名著名的音乐家。
I fantasize about traveling the world. 我幻想环游世界。


一般过去时:
She fantasized about her childhood. 她幻想她的童年。
He fantasized about escaping his boring job. 他幻想逃离他无聊的工作。


现在完成时:
I have fantasized about this moment for years.  多年来我一直幻想这一刻。
She has fantasized about owning a big house. 她一直幻想拥有一栋大房子。


过去完成时:
He had fantasized about meeting her for months before they actually met. 在他们真正见面之前，他几个月来一直幻想与她相遇。
She had fantasized about quitting her job before she finally did it. 在她最终辞职之前，她曾幻想过辞职。


将来时:
I will fantasize about this trip all week. 我整个星期都会幻想这次旅行。
She will fantasize about her future until she gets married. 她会在结婚之前一直幻想她的未来。


虚拟语气:
If I had the time, I would fantasize more often. 如果我有时间，我会更频繁地幻想。
I wish I could fantasize more creatively. 我希望我能更有创意地幻想。


被动语态:  (The verb "fantasize" is rarely used in the passive voice because it usually requires a person actively doing the fantasizing.)


疑问句:
Do you ever fantasize about your future? 你曾经幻想过你的未来吗？
What do you fantasize about most? 你最常幻想什么？


否定句:
I don't fantasize often. 我不经常幻想。
He didn't fantasize about success; he worked for it. 他没有幻想成功；他为之奋斗。


PHRASE->
fantasize about: 幻想……
fantasize constantly: 不停地幻想
fantasize wildly: 疯狂地幻想
fantasize incessantly:  不停地幻想
live in a fantasy/fantasize: 生活在幻想中/沉迷于幻想


词源:
From the Italian word "fantastico," meaning "fantastic," ultimately from Greek "phantastikos" (φανταστικός), meaning "imaginative," from "phantasia" (φαντασία), meaning "imagination, appearance."
