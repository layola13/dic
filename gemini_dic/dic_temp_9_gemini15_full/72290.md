flounce:/flaʊns/| n. v.| 飘动；饰边；生气地离开 | A sudden, dramatic movement, often of a skirt or other garment;  a decorative frill or ruffle; to move in a dramatic or exaggerated way, often expressing anger or annoyance. |例句：Her skirt flounced as she danced.  她的裙子跳舞时飘动着。|近义词：frill, ruffle, swish, stomp, sashay|反义词：calm, composed, still


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The fabric flounces in the breeze. = 布料在微风中飘动。
She flounces her skirt to show off the new design. = 她抖动裙子炫耀新的设计。

一般过去时:
The dancer flounced across the stage. = 舞者在舞台上翩翩起舞。
She flounced out of the room in a huff. = 她气冲冲地离开了房间。

现在完成时:
The skirt has flounced beautifully throughout the performance. = 在整场演出中，裙子都漂亮地飘动着。
She has flounced around all day, showing off her new dress. = 她一整天都穿着新衣服四处炫耀。

过去完成时:
The dancer had flounced gracefully before the music stopped. = 音乐停止前，舞者优雅地跳动着。
She had flounced off before I could apologize. = 我还没来得及道歉她就气呼呼地走了。

虚拟语气:

被动语态:
The skirt was flounced with delicate lace. = 裙子饰以精美的蕾丝花边。
The issue was flounced over in the meeting. = 会议上对这个问题轻描淡写地带过了。

疑问句:
Does her dress flounce when she walks? = 她走路时裙子会飘动吗？

否定句:
