/ɔːrvet/| n. |欧洲石龙子|A type of lizard, specifically a slow worm, characterized by its limbless body and smooth scales.  Orvets are often mistaken for snakes due to their lack of legs. They are harmless and feed primarily on insects and other invertebrates.|Example: The garden was home to several orvets, which slithered silently through the undergrowth. 花园里住着几条石龙子，它们悄悄地从灌木丛中滑过。|近义词：slow worm, blindworm|反义词：None specifically, but words implying dangerous reptiles (e.g., viper, cobra) could be considered antonyms in a broad sense.|noun
