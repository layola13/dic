judged:/dʒʌdʒd/| v.|判断，裁决，认为|To form an opinion or conclusion about; to make a judgment or decision about something or someone.|The judge judged the defendant guilty. 法官判决被告有罪。The painting was judged to be a masterpiece. 这幅画被认为是杰作。|determined, decided, assessed|undecided, unjudged,  uncertain|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He judges people by their actions. 他根据人们的行为来判断他们。
She judges the quality of the wine carefully. 她仔细地判断葡萄酒的质量。


一般过去时:
The committee judged the competition entries. 委员会评判了比赛参赛作品。
I judged him to be honest. 我认为他是诚实的。


现在完成时:
They have judged the winners of the contest. 他们已经评选出了比赛的获胜者。
She has judged many cooking competitions. 她已经评判过许多烹饪比赛。


过去完成时:
The jury had judged the case before the new evidence emerged. 在新的证据出现之前，陪审团已经对案件作出了判决。
He had judged the situation incorrectly. 他对局势的判断是错误的。


将来时:
The panel will judge the submissions next week.  评审小组下周将对提交的作品进行评判。
She will judge the dance competition tomorrow. 她明天将评判舞蹈比赛。


虚拟语气:
If I were to judge, I would say he's innocent. 如果要我判断，我会说他是无辜的。
I wish I hadn't judged him so harshly. 我真希望我没有那么严厉地评价他。


被动语态:
The painting was judged to be priceless. 这幅画被认为是无价之宝。
The case was judged fairly. 此案审判公正。


疑问句:
How did you judge the situation? 你是如何判断局势的？
Do you think she judged him fairly? 你认为她对他评价公平吗？


否定句:
I didn't judge him to be capable. 我认为他能力不足。
She didn't judge the situation correctly. 她没有正确判断形势。
