capitalises: /ˈkæpɪtəlaɪz/ | v. | 将…大写；利用；获益 | To write or print (something) in capital letters.  To take advantage of (something) to one's benefit. | 例句：Always capitalise the first letter of a sentence. = 句子的第一个字母总是要大写。 They capitalised on the opportunity to expand their business. = 他们利用机会扩大业务。 | 近义词：capitalize, utilize, exploit | 反义词：lowercase, minimize


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He capitalises every proper noun. = 他把每个专有名词都大写。
The company capitalises on its strong brand reputation. = 这家公司利用其强大的品牌声誉。


一般过去时:
She capitalised the title of the book. = 她把书名大写了。
They capitalised on the low interest rates to refinance their mortgage. = 他们利用低利率为抵押贷款再融资。


现在完成时:
He has capitalised all the important words in the document. = 他已经将文档中的所有重要单词都大写了。
The company has capitalised on the growing demand for sustainable products. = 该公司已经利用了对可持续产品的日益增长的需求。


过去完成时:
They had capitalised the company name before the launch. = 他们在发布之前已经将公司名称大写了。
She had capitalised on her previous experience to secure the new job.  = 她利用之前的经验获得了这份新工作。


将来时:
They will capitalise the headings in the report. = 他们将把报告中的标题大写。
The entrepreneur will capitalise on the emerging market trend. = 企业家将利用新兴的市场趋势。


虚拟语气:
If I had known better, I would have capitalised on that opportunity. = 如果我早知道，我就会抓住那个机会。
If they capitalised on this trend, they would significantly increase their profits. = 如果他们利用这一趋势，他们的利润将大幅增加。


被动语态:
The names were capitalised automatically by the software. = 名字由软件自动大写。
The opportunity was capitalised on by a rival company. = 机会被一家竞争对手公司利用了。


疑问句:
Do you always capitalise proper nouns in your writing? = 你写作时总是把专有名词大写吗？
Did they capitalise on the marketing campaign's success? = 他们利用了营销活动的成功吗？


否定句:
He didn't capitalise the first letter of his name. = 他没有把名字的首字母大写。
The company did not capitalise on the potential of the new technology. = 公司没有利用新技术的潜力。
