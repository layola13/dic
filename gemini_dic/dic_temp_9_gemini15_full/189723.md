saltboxes:/ˈsɔːltbɒksɪz/| n. |盐盒式房屋；盐盒房|A saltbox house is a type of New England colonial house characterized by its long, sloping roof that extends almost to the ground on one side.  This design was practical for storing salt, hence the name.  The longer roofline allowed for additional attic space and provided protection from the elements.|The old saltbox house on the hill has been in the family for generations.  古老的盐盒式房屋坐落在山丘上，代代相传。|colonial house, cape cod|—|n.
