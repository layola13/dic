circumclusion:/ˌsɜːrkəmˈkluːʒən/| n.|包涵；围堵；圈定|The act of enclosing or surrounding; the state of being enclosed or surrounded.  It often implies a gradual or indirect process of enclosure or restriction.  The word carries a sense of something being confined or limited, though not necessarily in a negative way.|例句：The gradual circumclusion of the city by the expanding suburbs led to increased traffic congestion.  逐渐被扩张的郊区包围，导致城市交通拥堵加剧。The circumclusion of the debate by the moderator prevented any meaningful discussion. 主持人对辩论的限制阻止了任何有意义的讨论。|近义词：enclosure, containment, confinement, circumscription|反义词：opening, release, liberation|名词
