digressive:/daɪˈɡrɛsɪv/| adj.|离题的；枝节的；漫无边际的| straying from the main point; rambling; wandering; departing from the central theme.|The speaker's digressive remarks bored the audience. 演讲者的离题发言让听众感到厌烦。|His lecture was long and digressive. 他的演讲冗长且离题。|focused, concise|relevant, pertinent


USAGE->

一般现在时:
His speech is often digressive. = 他的演讲经常离题。
The novel's plot is digressive and confusing. = 这部小说的情节离题且令人困惑。


一般过去时:
The professor's lecture was digressive yesterday. = 教授昨天的讲座离题了。
Her conversation digressed into a discussion of her childhood. = 她的谈话偏离了主题，转向了对童年岁月的讨论。


现在完成时:
The writer has become more digressive in his later works. = 这名作家在他后期的作品中变得更离题了。
The meeting has digressed considerably from its original agenda. = 会议已经大大偏离了原定的议程。


过去完成时:
The author had digressed several times before finally returning to the main plot. = 作者在最终回到主要情节之前已经多次离题。
They had digressed so far from the topic that nobody remembered the original question. = 他们已经离题太远，以至于没有人记得最初的问题了。


将来时:
He will likely digress into another anecdote. = 他很可能会再次离题讲个故事。
The discussion will probably digress if we don't stay focused. = 如果我们不集中注意力，讨论可能会偏离主题。


虚拟语气:
If he hadn't digressed so much, the presentation would have been much shorter. = 如果他没有那么离题，演示文稿就会短得多。
I wish he hadn't digressed from the main point of his argument. = 我真希望他没有偏离他论点的中心思想。


被动语态:
The conversation was digressed from by an irrelevant detail. = 谈话因一个无关紧要的细节而偏离了主题。
The presentation was considered too digressive by many attendees. = 许多与会者认为该演示文稿过于离题。


疑问句:
Is his writing style too digressive? = 他的写作风格过于离题了吗？
Was the meeting digressive? = 会议离题了吗？


否定句:
His arguments were not digressive; they were directly relevant. = 他的论点并非离题；它们与主题直接相关。
The presentation was not digressive; it followed a clear and concise structure. = 演示文稿并非离题；它遵循清晰简洁的结构。


PHRASE->
a digressive remark 离题的评论
a digressive style 离题的风格

digressive thinking  离题的思维
digressive approach 离题的方法


词源:
From Latin *digressus, past participle of digredi 'to step aside', from dis- 'apart' + gradi 'to step'.
