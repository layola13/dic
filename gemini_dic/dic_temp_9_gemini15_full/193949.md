self-esteems:/ˌsɛlfɪˈstiːmz/| n.|自尊心；自我评价|The plural form of "self-esteem," referring to one's overall subjective emotional evaluation of their own worth.  It encompasses beliefs about oneself (for example, "I am competent," "I am worthy"), as well as emotional states, such as triumph, despair, pride, and shame.|例句：Her self-esteems were boosted after winning the competition. 她赢得比赛后，自尊心得到了提升。
例句：Building strong self-esteems in children is crucial for their future well-being. 在孩子心中建立强大的自尊心对他们未来的幸福至关重要。|近义词：self-respect, self-worth, confidence|反义词：self-loathing, inferiority complex|名词

WORD ORIGIN->
The word "self-esteem" is a compound word. "Self" refers to oneself, and "esteem" comes from the Old French word "estimer," meaning "to estimate, value, or prize."  Therefore, self-esteem literally means the value or worth one places on oneself.
