obedient: /oʊˈbiːdiənt/ | adj. | 顺从的；服从的 |  Willing to obey; submissive to another's authority.  | The obedient dog followed its owner everywhere.  这只听话的狗跟着它的主人到处跑。 | compliant, submissive, docile | rebellious, disobedient, defiant |  形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is obedient to his parents. = 他很听父母的话。
The children are obedient in class. = 孩子们在课堂上很听话。

一般过去时:
She was obedient as a child. = 她小时候很听话。
The soldiers were obedient to their commander. = 士兵们服从他们的指挥官。

现在完成时:
He has always been obedient. = 他一直都很听话。
The dog has been obedient since its training. = 这只狗自从训练以来就一直很听话。

过去完成时:
She had been obedient throughout her childhood. = 她从小到大都很听话。
The students had been obedient until the substitute teacher arrived. = 直到代课老师到来之前，学生们都很听话。

将来时:
He will be obedient in the future. = 他将来会很听话。
The new recruits will be obedient to their superiors. = 新兵将服从他们的上级。

虚拟语气:
If he were obedient, things would be easier. = 如果他听话一点，事情就会容易得多。
I wish my cat were more obedient. = 我希望我的猫更听话一点。

被动语态:
His obedience is expected. = 预期他会服从。
Obedience is valued in this culture. = 在这种文化中，服从是被重视的。

疑问句:
Is he obedient? = 他听话吗？
Are the children obedient today? = 孩子们今天听话吗？

否定句:
He is not obedient. = 他不听话。
The horse was not obedient to the rider. = 这匹马不服从骑手。


PHRASE->
obedient child = 听话的孩子
obedient dog = 听话的狗
obedient student = 听话的学生
blindly obedient = 盲目服从的
perfectly obedient = 完全服从的


词源:
From Old French *obedient, from Latin obediēns, present participle of obēdīre "to obey," from ob- "to," + audīre "to hear,"  related to  audire, meaning "to hear," implying that obedience involves listening and following instructions.
