radiocarbon-dating: /ˌreɪdiəʊˈkɑːrbənˌdeɪtɪŋ/ | n. |放射性碳定年法|  A method of determining the age of an object containing organic material by using the properties of radiocarbon (carbon-14). This technique is used to date artifacts and remains from the past, providing insights into history and archaeology.  |The radiocarbon dating of the artifact revealed it to be approximately 3,000 years old. 放射性碳定年法显示该文物大约有3000年的历史。|  carbon dating, radiometric dating |  (no direct antonym, but contrasting methods include dendrochronology, potassium-argon dating) | 名词
