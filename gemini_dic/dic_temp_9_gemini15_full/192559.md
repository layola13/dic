scull:/skʌl/|/skʊl/| n., v.|  头骨；划桨；用力划桨 | A scull is a light oar used in rowing, typically one oar in each hand.  It can also refer to the skull, the bony framework of the head. 词源:
The word "scull" as a noun referring to an oar comes from Middle English *sculle*, from Old Norse *skúla*, meaning "to row." The word "scull" as a noun referring to the skull, is derived from Old English *sculle*, from Proto-Germanic *skulla*, ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *skel-*, meaning "to cut," referring to the skull's shape as if cut from bone.  The verb "to scull" derives from the noun meaning "oar."
