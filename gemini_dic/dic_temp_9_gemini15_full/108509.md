layoffs:/ˈleɪɔːfs/ | n. |裁员；解雇 |The noun "layoffs" refers to the termination of employment of a group of employees by a company, often due to economic downturn, restructuring, or downsizing.  | Example: The recent layoffs at the factory left many families struggling. 最近工厂的裁员让许多家庭陷入困境。 |近义词：job cuts, dismissals, redundancies |反义词：hirings, recruitments |名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company announces layoffs every year. = 公司每年都会宣布裁员。
Layoffs are a common occurrence in this industry. = 裁员在这个行业很常见。


一般过去时:
The factory experienced significant layoffs last quarter. = 工厂上个季度经历了大规模裁员。
Many employees were affected by the unexpected layoffs. = 许多员工受到意外裁员的影响。

过去完成时:

虚拟语气:
