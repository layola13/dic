muddying: /ˈmʌdiɪŋ/ | v. | 使浑浊；使模糊；使混乱 | To make something muddy or unclear.  | 近义词：clouding, obscuring, confusing | 反义词：clarifying, clearing up


英文解析:The verb "muddying" refers to the act of making something muddy, literally or figuratively.  Literally, it means to make something dirty with mud. Figuratively, it means to make something unclear, confusing, or less distinct.


例句:

The heavy rain muddied the stream. = 大雨使小溪变得浑浊。

His explanation only muddied the waters further. = 他的解释只会让事情更加混乱。

词源:

The word "muddying" derives from the Old English word "mōd," meaning "dung" or "earth." Over time, this evolved into the Middle English word "mudde," and eventually to the modern English "mud."  The "-ing" suffix indicates the present participle, denoting the act of making something muddy.
