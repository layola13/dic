nondissipated: /ˌnɒnˈdɪsɪpeɪtɪd/ | adj. |未消散的；未浪费的；未挥霍的 | Not dissipated; not wasted or squandered.  The nondissipated energy was harnessed for future use.  | 近义词：undissipated, conserved, intact, preserved | 反义词：dissipated, wasted, squandered, depleted


例句：

The nondissipated energy from the solar panels was stored in batteries.  = 太阳能电池板产生的未消散能量储存在电池中。

His nondissipated efforts finally led to success. = 他未浪费的努力最终取得了成功。


词源：

* **non-**:  prefix meaning "not"
* **dissipated**:  past participle of the verb "dissipate," meaning "to scatter or disperse; to waste (money, time, etc.)"  The word "dissipate" itself comes from the Latin "dissipare," meaning "to scatter," from "dis-" (apart) + "sipare" (to scatter).


用法说明:

"Nondissipated" is primarily used as an adjective to describe something that has not been scattered, wasted, or squandered.  It emphasizes the preservation or intactness of something, typically energy, resources, or effort.  The word is relatively uncommon in everyday conversation but appears more frequently in scientific or technical contexts.
