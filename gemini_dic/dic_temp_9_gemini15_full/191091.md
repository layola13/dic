scantiest: /ˈskæntiɪst/ | adj. | 最少的；不足的；微薄的 |  Meaning:  The superlative form of "scanty," indicating the least amount or the most insufficient quantity. It describes something that is barely sufficient, meager, or lacking. | Example: The scantiest of evidence was presented in court. 法庭上提出的证据微乎其微。  The scantiest rainfall led to a severe drought.  降雨量极少导致了严重的干旱。| Near synonyms: meager, insufficient, minimal, paltry, sparse | Antonyms: abundant, plentiful, ample, copious |  词性:形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The evidence is scantiest. = 证据最少。
His knowledge of the subject is scantiest. = 他对这个主题的了解最少。

一般过去时:
The information provided was scantiest. = 提供的信息最少。
His explanation was scantiest. = 他的解释最简略。

现在完成时:
The harvest has been scantiest this year. = 今年收成最少。
The evidence we have gathered has been scantiest. = 我们收集到的证据最少。

过去完成时:
The resources available had been scantiest in the past. = 过去可用的资源最少。
The information they had was scantiest. = 他们掌握的信息最少。

将来时:
The supply of water will be scantiest next summer. = 明年夏天水供应将最少。
The details provided in the next report will be scantiest. = 下份报告中提供的细节将最少。

虚拟语气:
If the evidence were scantiest, the case would be difficult to prove. = 如果证据最少，这个案子就很难证明。
I wish the information available were scantiest. = 我希望可用的信息最少。（通常表达反面意思，希望信息充足）

被动语态:
The details were considered scantiest. = 细节被认为最少。
The resources were described as scantiest. = 资源被描述为最少。

疑问句:
Is the evidence scantiest? = 证据最少吗？
Are the resources scantiest? = 资源最少吗？

否定句:
The evidence is not scantiest. = 证据并非最少。
The information is not scantiest. = 信息并非最少。

词源:
源自于中古英语 *scant，意为“不足的，缺乏的”，  最终来源于古挪威语 *skamt， 同源词包括 scant, scanty。  "-iest" 是形容词最高级后缀。
