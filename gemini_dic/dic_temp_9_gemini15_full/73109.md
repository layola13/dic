for-all-that:/fɔːrˈɔːlðæt/| adv.|尽管如此，即便如此|Despite that; notwithstanding; in spite of that. This phrase is used to introduce a statement that contrasts with a previous statement. It emphasizes that something is true or will happen even though something else might seem to suggest otherwise.|例句：He's a bit of a scoundrel, for all that, he’s a good friend.  尽管他有点无赖，但他是个好朋友。|例句：For all that he protested, he secretly agreed. 尽管他抗议，但他私下里同意了。|近义词：nevertheless, nonetheless, however, even so, still|反义词：therefore, consequently, thus
