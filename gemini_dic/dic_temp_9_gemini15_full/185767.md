revile:/rɪˈvaɪl/| vt.|辱骂；斥责；痛斥|To criticize someone or something harshly; to verbally abuse.|The angry mob reviled the politician for his unpopular policies.  暴怒的民众因其不得人心的政策而痛斥这位政治家。|The critics reviled his latest novel as a work of mediocrity. 批评家们痛斥他的最新小说是一部平庸之作。|denounce, condemn, berate, lambaste|praise, commend, applaud|动词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
They revile him daily. = 他们每天都辱骂他。
She reviles her enemies openly. = 她公开辱骂她的敌人。


一般过去时:
The crowd reviled the speaker. = 人群辱骂了演讲者。
He reviled his opponent in the debate. = 他在辩论中辱骂了他的对手。


现在完成时:
They have reviled him for years. = 他们多年来一直辱骂他。
She has reviled the system for its injustices. = 她一直痛斥这个制度的不公正。


过去完成时:
They had reviled him long before the incident. = 在事件发生很久以前，他们就辱骂他了。
She had reviled his actions in private before confronting him publicly. = 在公开谴责他之前，她私下里就已辱骂了他的行为。


将来时:
They will revile him for his betrayal. = 他们会因为他的背叛而辱骂他。
She will revile the injustice until it is rectified. = 她会一直痛斥这种不公正，直到它得到纠正。


虚拟语气:
If he were to do that, they would revile him. = 如果他那样做，他们会辱骂他。
I wish they wouldn't revile him so much. = 我希望他们不要那么辱骂他。


被动语态:
He was reviled by the press for his comments. = 他因其言论而受到媒体的辱骂。
The policy was reviled by the public as unfair. = 该政策被公众斥责为不公平。


疑问句:
Why do they revile him so? = 他们为什么那样辱骂他？
Will they revile him again? = 他们还会再次辱骂他吗？


否定句:
They did not revile him openly. = 他们没有公开辱骂他。
She would not revile him even when provoked. = 即使被激怒，她也不会辱骂他。


PHRASE->

revile against: 反对...而辱骂
revile someone to their face: 当面辱骂某人
openly revile: 公开辱骂
secretly revile: 暗中辱骂
bitterly revile: 痛恨地辱骂
