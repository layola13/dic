coefficient:/ˌkoʊɪˈfɪʃənt/ | n. | 系数，系数 | A coefficient is a numerical or constant quantity placed before and multiplying the variable in an algebraic expression (e.g., the 3 in 3x).  It represents a multiplicative factor in a term. In mathematics and science, coefficients are used extensively in various contexts, such as in equations, formulas, and series.  For example, in the equation 2x + 5 = 9, '2' is the coefficient of 'x'. | The coefficient of friction determines how easily two surfaces slide past each other. = 摩擦系数决定了两个表面彼此滑动容易程度。
