side-saddle:/ˈsaɪdˌsædl/| n.|侧鞍；骑侧鞍|A type of saddle designed for women to ride a horse with their legs on either side of the horse's body, rather than astride.|例句：She rode side-saddle through the park.|近义词：woman's saddle|反义词：None.

词源：
The word "side-saddle" is a compound word.  "Side" refers to the position of the rider, and "saddle" is the seat used for riding a horse.  The term reflects the historical practice of women riding horses with their legs to either side of the horse's body.
