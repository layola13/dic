retroduction:/ˌrɛtrəˈdʌkʃən/| n. |逆推，反向推论|The process of reasoning backwards from an observation to a hypothesis that could explain it.  It's a form of inference where the conclusion is already known, and the task is to find premises that could lead to it.  This is distinct from deduction, where the premises are known and the conclusion is derived, and induction, where observations are made and a general conclusion is drawn.|例句：The detective used retroduction to figure out the sequence of events leading to the crime.  侦探用逆向推论来弄清楚导致犯罪的事件顺序。|近义词：abduction, inference, retrodiction |反义词：deduction, induction
