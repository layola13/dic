coleopteran:/ˌkoʊliˈɒptərən/| n. |鞘翅目昆虫；甲虫|A coleopteran is an insect of the order Coleoptera, which includes beetles.  Coleopterans are characterized by their hardened forewings, called elytra, which protect their hindwings used for flight. They exhibit a wide range of sizes, shapes, and colors, and occupy diverse habitats.|Example: The museum showcased a remarkable collection of coleopterans from around the world.  博物馆展示了来自世界各地的 remarkable 的鞘翅目昆虫收藏。|近义词：beetle, weevil|反义词：无

词源:
The word "coleopteran" comes from the scientific Latin *Coleoptera*, which itself is derived from Greek κολεός (koleos, "sheath") + πτερόν (pteron, "wing").  This refers to the hardened forewings (elytra) characteristic of beetles.
