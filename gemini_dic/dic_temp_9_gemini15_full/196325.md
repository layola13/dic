serialising: /ˈsɪəriəlaɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 序列化；连续剧化 | The process of converting a data structure or object into a sequence of bytes that can be stored in a file or transmitted over a network.  This is often used in programming to save complex data structures efficiently.  It can also refer to the adaptation of a story into a series of episodes, often for television or radio. | Example:  The program serialises the user data before saving it to the database. = 该程序在将用户数据保存到数据库之前对其进行了序列化。

The process of adapting a novel or other long-form narrative into a series of episodes for television or radio.  | Example: The novel was serialised over six weeks on the radio. = 这部小说在广播电台连载了六周。|近义词：serialize,  (for narrative) adapt into a series | 反义词：deserialise (反序列化), (for narrative)  present as a single unit
