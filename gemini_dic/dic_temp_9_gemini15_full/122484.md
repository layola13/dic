/mɒk/| n. v.|模仿；嘲弄；模型|To copy someone's behaviour or speech in a way that is intended to be funny or insulting; a small model of something.|例句：He mocked her accent. 他嘲笑她的口音。|例句：The children made a mock of the teacher.孩子们模仿老师的样子，以此取笑。|近义词：mimic, imitate, ridicule|反义词：praise, commend


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He mocks his friends often. = 他经常嘲笑他的朋友们。
She mocks the way he talks. = 她模仿他说话的样子。


一般过去时:
They mocked the politician's speech. = 他们嘲笑了这位政治家的演讲。
He mocked his brother's dance moves. = 他模仿了他弟弟的舞蹈动作。


现在完成时:
I have mocked him before, and I regret it. = 我以前嘲笑过他，现在后悔了。
She has mocked that hairstyle many times. = 她多次模仿过那种发型。


过去完成时:
They had mocked him relentlessly before he finally snapped. = 在他最终爆发之前，他们无情地嘲笑了他。
She had mocked his singing before, so he was hesitant to perform. = 她以前嘲笑过他的歌声，所以他犹豫是否要表演。


将来时:
He will mock her again if she continues to act like that. = 如果她继续那样做，他会再次嘲笑她。
They will mock his attempt at a joke. = 他们会嘲笑他尝试讲的笑话。


虚拟语气:
If I mocked him, he would be very upset. = 如果我嘲笑他，他会非常沮丧。
I wish I hadn't mocked her. = 我真希望我没有嘲笑她。


被动语态:
His accent was mocked by many people. = 他的口音被许多人嘲笑。
His performance was mocked by the critics. = 他的表演受到了评论家的嘲笑。


疑问句:
Does he often mock people? = 他经常嘲笑别人吗？
Why did they mock him? = 他们为什么嘲笑他？


否定句:
He does not mock her anymore. = 他不再嘲笑她了。
They did not mean to mock his efforts. = 他们并非有意嘲笑他的努力。
