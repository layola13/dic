postcartesian:/ˌpoʊstˌkɑːrˈtiʒən/| adj.|后笛卡尔的；超越笛卡尔的|Post-Cartesian philosophy refers to philosophical thought that emerged after the work of René Descartes, often challenging or building upon his ideas.  It encompasses a wide range of approaches and thinkers who grapple with issues raised, or unresolved, by Descartes's philosophy.|The post-Cartesian emphasis on subjectivity led to new understandings of consciousness and the self.|近义词：post-Descartian|反义词：Cartesian
