kowtowed:/ˈkaʊtaʊd/| v.| 叩头；卑躬屈膝；奴颜婢膝 | To bow very low, especially as a sign of respect or worship; to behave in a subservient manner. |例句：He kowtowed to the dictator to save his business. 他为了保住生意而对独裁者卑躬屈膝。  They kowtowed to the wishes of their superiors. 他们唯命是从。|近义词：bow, prostrate, cringe, fawn, obsequious |反义词：defy, resist, stand up to


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He kowtows to his boss. = 他对他的老板卑躬屈膝。
They kowtow to the powerful. = 他们对权势者卑躬屈膝。

一般过去时:
He kowtowed to the emperor. = 他向皇帝叩头。
She kowtowed to pressure from her family. = 她屈服于家庭的压力。

现在完成时:
He has kowtowed to every whim of his client. = 他一直对客户的每一个怪癖都百依百顺。
They have kowtowed to the demands of the market. = 他们一直屈服于市场的需求。

过去完成时:
He had kowtowed to the authorities for years. = 他多年来一直对当局卑躬屈膝。
She had kowtowed to her husband's every wish. = 她一直顺从她丈夫的每一个愿望。

将来时:
He will kowtow to anyone for money. = 他为了钱会对任何人卑躬屈膝。
They will kowtow to the pressure from investors. = 他们将屈服于投资者的压力。

虚拟语气:
If he wanted the job, he would kowtow to the management. = 如果他想要这份工作，他会对管理层卑躬屈膝。
I wish I hadn't kowtowed to their demands. = 我真希望我没有屈服于他们的要求。

被动语态:
His ideas were kowtowed to by the committee. = 他的想法被委员会接受了（暗含卑躬屈膝的意味）。
The company's policies were kowtowed to by its employees. = 公司的政策被员工们奉为圭臬（暗含卑躬屈膝的意味）。

疑问句:
Did he kowtow to the pressure? = 他屈服于压力了吗？
Will they kowtow to the demands of the public? = 他们会屈服于公众的要求吗？

否定句:
He didn't kowtow to the corrupt officials. = 他没有向腐败官员卑躬屈膝。
She refused to kowtow to the unreasonable rules. = 她拒绝屈服于不合理的规定。
