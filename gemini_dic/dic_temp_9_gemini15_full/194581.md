self-subdued:/ˌsɛlf səbˈdjuːd/| adj.| 自抑的；克制的；压抑自己的 |  Characterized by or showing self-restraint; subdued in one's own behavior or feelings. It implies a conscious effort to control one's emotions or actions, often to avoid causing trouble or drawing attention.  | 例句：He maintained a self-subdued demeanor throughout the tense meeting. 他在紧张的会议期间一直保持着克制的态度。


His self-subdued laughter barely registered in the quiet room. 他压抑的笑声在安静的房间里几乎听不见。


近义词：reserved, restrained, controlled, subdued, quiet, unassuming | 反义词：outgoing, boisterous, flamboyant, unrestrained, unrestrained


词源:

* **self-**:  Prefix indicating "of or relating to oneself."  From Old English *self.

* **subdued**: Past participle of the verb "subdue," meaning "to overcome, conquer, or bring under control."  From Old French *subduire, from Latin *subducere ("to draw away, withdraw").  The sense of "making quiet or less intense" is a later development.

用法说明:

self-subdued 主要用作形容词，修饰人或其行为举止，强调一种内在的自我控制和压抑。  它不同于仅仅是“安静”或“沉默”，更侧重于对自身情绪或行为的主动控制。


例句中体现了 self-subdued 的不同语境：


第一句强调的是在特定情境下（紧张会议）保持克制的态度。


第二句则强调的是对情绪（笑声）的压抑。


需要注意的是，self-subdued 并不一定意味着消极或压抑，它可以是一种成熟的表现，或者是一种为了适应环境而采取的策略。
