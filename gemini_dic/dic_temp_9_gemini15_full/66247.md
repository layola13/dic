excludability:/ɪkˌskluːdəˈbɪləti/ | n. |排他性，可排除性 | The ability to prevent someone from accessing or using a good or service.  It's a key concept in economics, particularly in the discussion of public goods versus private goods.  A good is excludable if it is possible to prevent people who haven't paid for it from consuming it. |  The high excludability of the concert tickets meant only those who bought them could attend.  |  exclusiveness, restrictiveness | inclusiveness, accessibility | 名词
