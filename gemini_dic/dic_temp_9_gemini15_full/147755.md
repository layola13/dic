overprogrammes:/ˌoʊvərˈproʊɡræmz/ | n. | 过度编程；程序过多 | The act of adding more features or functionality to a program than is necessary or desirable, often leading to complexity, inefficiency, and reduced maintainability.  It can also refer to having too many programs running concurrently, causing system slowdown or instability.  | Example: The software suffered from overprogramming, making it bloated and difficult to use.  过度编程导致该软件臃肿且难以使用。 | 近义词：over-engineering, feature creep | 反义词：minimalism, simplification | 名词
