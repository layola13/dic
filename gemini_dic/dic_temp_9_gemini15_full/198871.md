sidestepping:/ˈsaɪdstepɪŋ/| v.|避开；回避；绕过|The act of stepping to one side to avoid something or someone; to evade or avoid (a problem, question, etc.) by cleverly changing the subject or taking evasive action.|例句：He sidestepped the question about his past. 他避开了关于他过去的问题。

例句：She skillfully sidestepped the puddles in the street. 她巧妙地避开了街上的水坑。


近义词：avoiding, evading, circumventing, dodging, skirting


反义词：confronting, facing, addressing


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He sidesteps the issue. = 他回避这个问题。
They sidestep the difficult questions. = 他们回避那些棘手的问题。


一般过去时:
She sidestepped the reporters. = 她避开了记者。

词源:
The word "sidestep" is a compound word formed from "side" and "step."  "Side" refers to a lateral direction, and "step" means to move one's foot.  Therefore, "sidestep" literally means to move to the side with a step.  Its figurative meaning of avoiding an issue developed later.
