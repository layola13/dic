roughhousing: /ˈrʌfˌhaʊzɪŋ/ | n. |粗暴地玩耍；打闹；嬉戏 |  Roughhousing refers to boisterous and playful physical interaction, often involving wrestling, tumbling, or other energetic activities. It's typically associated with children or young adults, but can also occur between adults in a playful context.  The key is that despite the physicality, it's intended to be fun and not harmful. | Example 1: The children were roughhousing in the backyard, making a lot of noise.  孩子们在后院打闹，弄出很大的噪音。| Example 2:  The brothers engaged in some friendly roughhousing after their soccer game.  兄弟们在足球比赛后进行了一些友好的打闹。| 近义词：tumbling, wrestling, horseplay, rough-and-tumble play | 反义词：gentle play, calm behavior
