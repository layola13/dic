self-satirist: /ˈsɛlf ˈsætərist/ | n. | 自嘲者 | A person who uses satire to make fun of themselves, often highlighting their own flaws or shortcomings.  This involves a self-aware and often humorous critique of one's own personality, behavior, or experiences.  | Example:  The comedian was a brilliant self-satirist, often making jokes at his own expense about his awkwardness and social blunders.  自嘲喜剧演员非常出色，经常拿自己的笨拙和社交失误开玩笑。 | 近义词: self-deprecating humorist, ironic commentator | 反义词: self-aggrandizer, narcissist
