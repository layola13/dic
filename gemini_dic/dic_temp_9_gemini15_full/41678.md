correspondency:/ˌkɒrəˈspɒndənsi/| /ˌkɔːrəˈspɑːndənsi/| n.|对应，一致；通信；关系|The correspondence between the two sets of data is striking.  两组数据的对应关系非常显著。|The company receives a large volume of correspondence every day.  公司每天收到大量的信件。|近义词：agreement, conformity, communication, relation|反义词：discrepancy, difference, dissimilarity|名词


词源:

The word "correspondency" comes from the Middle English word "correspondence," which itself is derived from the Old French word "correspondance," ultimately stemming from the Latin word "correspondentia."  The Latin root combines "correspondere," meaning "to agree, correspond," from "cor-" (together) and "respondere" (to answer, reply).

用法说明:

1.  **Agreement or Similarity:**  This is the most common usage. It refers to a similarity or agreement between things.

    例句1：There's a clear correspondence between the map and the actual terrain. 地图和实际地形之间存在明显的对应关系。

    例句2：The correspondence in their handwriting is remarkable. 他们笔迹的相似程度令人惊叹。


2. **Communication (usually written):**  This refers to the exchange of letters or emails.

    例句1： I maintain a regular correspondence with my pen pal in France. 我定期与我在法国的笔友通信。

    例句2：  The volume of correspondence increased dramatically after the announcement. 公告发布后，信件数量急剧增加。


3. **Relationship or Connection:** This usage is less frequent but still valid. It points to a connection or relationship between different things or ideas.

    例句1：There is a strong correspondence between economic growth and improved living standards. 经济增长与生活水平提高之间存在密切的关系。

    例句2：The research explores the correspondence between art and society. 这项研究探讨了艺术与社会之间的关系。
