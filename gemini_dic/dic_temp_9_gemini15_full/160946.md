post-Renaissance: /ˌpoʊst rɛnəˈsɑːns/ | adj. |文艺复兴后的，后文艺复兴的 |  Post-Renaissance refers to the period of time following the Renaissance, generally considered to be from the late 16th century onwards.  It encompasses various artistic, intellectual, and cultural movements that built upon or reacted against the Renaissance's ideals. | The post-Renaissance period saw a shift in artistic styles, with the rise of Baroque and later Rococo. | 近义词: post-classical, late Renaissance (in some contexts) | 反义词: Renaissance, pre-Renaissance
