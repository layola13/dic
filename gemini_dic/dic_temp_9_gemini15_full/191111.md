scapegoatisms:/ˈskeɪpˌɡoʊtɪzəm/| n. |替罪羊行为；诿过于人；推卸责任|The practice of blaming an individual or group for problems that are not their fault.  It often involves unfairly targeting someone as a scapegoat to deflect attention from the real causes of an issue or to avoid accountability.|例句：The scapegoatisms surrounding the economic crisis led to widespread public anger.  这场经济危机的诿过于人行为导致了公众的广泛愤怒。|近义词：blame-shifting, scapegoating, buck-passing|反义词：accountability, responsibility, transparency|名词

词源:
The word "scapegoatisms" is derived from "scapegoat," which itself comes from the biblical practice of symbolically transferring sins onto a goat and sending it into the wilderness.  The "-ism" suffix indicates a system, practice, or ideology.  Therefore, "scapegoatisms" refers to the actions and beliefs associated with using scapegoats.
