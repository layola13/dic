outwings:/ˈaʊtwɪŋz/| n. |翼展，超出翼展的范围|The outwings refer to the distance between the wingtips of an aircraft or bird in flight.  It is also used figuratively to describe something that extends beyond the usual limits or scope.|例句：The aircraft's outwings measured over 70 meters. 这架飞机的翼展超过70米。

The eagle soared, its outwings catching the wind.  鹰翱翔，它的翅膀掠过风。|近义词：wingspan, extent|反义词：None specifically, but concepts like "limited scope" or "confined space" could be considered opposites.
