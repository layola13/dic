prenegotiated:/ˌpriːnɪˈɡoʊʃieɪtɪd/ | adj. | 预先协商的，事先商定的 | Already agreed upon or arranged before a particular event or process.  |例句：The terms of the contract were prenegotiated, saving us considerable time.  合同条款已预先协商好，为我们节省了大量时间。

The prenegotiated price for the property was significantly lower than the market value. 这处房产的预先协商价格远低于市场价值。


近义词：prearranged, predetermined, settled, agreed-upon | 反义词：unnegotiated, undecided, unsettled


词源:

* **pre-**:  prefix meaning "before" or "in advance"
* **negotiate**: verb meaning "to discuss and agree upon something"
* **-ed**: past participle suffix indicating something that has been done or completed.

Therefore, "prenegotiated" means something that has been negotiated beforehand.
