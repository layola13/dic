jackaroos:/ˌdʒækəˈruːz/| n.|澳大利亚牧场见习牛仔|A jackaroo is a young person, typically a man, who works on a cattle station in Australia to learn the skills of cattle farming and stock work.  They are essentially apprentices in the cattle industry.|Many young people from the city come to the outback to become jackaroos and experience rural life.|近义词：station hand, rouseabout|反义词：None specifically.
