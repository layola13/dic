impetuous:/ɪmˈpetʃuəs/| adj.|鲁莽的，冲动的|Acting or done quickly and without thought or care.  Characterized by sudden or rash action, emotion, or feeling.|例句：His impetuous nature often led him into trouble. 他鲁莽的性格常常使他陷入困境。

例句：Her impetuous decision to quit her job surprised everyone. 她冲动地决定辞职让每个人都感到惊讶。


近义词：rash, impulsive, hasty, reckless|反义词：cautious, deliberate, thoughtful, prudent|词性：形容词


词源:  From Middle French *impetueux, from Latin impetuousus, from impetus "attack, force, impulse," from impellere "to drive, urge."  The word ultimately derives from the Proto-Indo-European root *pel- "to push, drive."


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is impetuous in his actions. = 他做事很冲动。
She is always impetuous when she's angry. = 她生气的时候总是很冲动。


一般过去时:
He was impetuous when he proposed to her. = 他向她求婚时很冲动。
She was impetuous and bought the dress without trying it on. = 她很冲动，没试穿就买了那件裙子。


现在完成时:
He has been impetuous throughout his life. = 他一生都很冲动。
She has been impetuous in her investments. = 她投资很冲动。


过去完成时:
He had been impetuous in his youth, but now he was more cautious. = 他年轻时很冲动，但现在谨慎多了。
She had been impetuous and regretted her hasty words. = 她曾经很冲动，后悔自己说过的话。


将来时:
He will be impetuous and regret his decision later. = 他将会很冲动，以后会后悔他的决定。
She will likely be impetuous again given the opportunity. = 如果有机会，她很可能会再次冲动。


虚拟语气:
If he weren't so impetuous, he would have a better life. = 如果他不那么冲动，生活会更好。
If she hadn't been so impetuous, she wouldn't have lost her job. = 如果她不那么冲动，就不会丢掉工作了。


被动语态:  The decision was seen as impetuous by many. = 许多人认为这个决定很鲁莽。(Note:  "Impetuous" is rarely used passively)
His actions were described as impetuous and reckless. = 他的行为被描述为鲁莽和不计后果的。(Note:  The focus is on the actions, not the inherent quality of the person.)


疑问句:
Was his behavior impetuous? = 他的行为鲁莽吗？
Is she often impetuous in her dealings with others? = 她与人交往时经常冲动吗？


否定句:
He was not impetuous in his approach to the problem. = 他处理这个问题的方式并不冲动。
She was not impetuous; she thought carefully before acting. = 她并不冲动；她在行动前仔细考虑过。
