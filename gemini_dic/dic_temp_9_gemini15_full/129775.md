non-fatal: /ˌnɒnˈfeɪtəl/ | adj. | 非致命的，不致死的 | Not resulting in death.  | The injuries sustained in the accident were non-fatal.  这次事故造成的伤势是非致命的。| fatal, deadly, lethal | non-lethal, harmless


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The injury is non-fatal. = 伤势不致命。
This condition is usually non-fatal. = 这种病症通常不致命。

一般过去时:
The accident was non-fatal. = 事故是非致命的。
The wound, thankfully, proved non-fatal. = 谢天谢地，伤口证明并不致命。

现在完成时:
The effects have been non-fatal so far. = 到目前为止，影响是非致命的。
All reported cases have been non-fatal. = 所有报告的病例均未致命。

过去完成时:
The doctor confirmed that the injury had been non-fatal. = 医生确认伤势不致命。
They were relieved to learn the attack had been non-fatal. = 他们得知袭击没有造成死亡而松了一口气。

将来时:
The prognosis is that the condition will be non-fatal. = 预后是病情不会致命。
We hope the surgery will result in a non-fatal outcome. = 我们希望手术的结果不会致命。

虚拟语气:
If the wound had been non-fatal, he would have recovered faster. = 如果伤口不致命，他就会恢复得更快。
If the poison were non-fatal, I wouldn't be so worried. = 如果毒药不致命，我就不会这么担心了。

被动语态:
The condition is considered non-fatal. = 这种病症被认为是非致命的。
The injury was reported as non-fatal. = 伤势被报告为非致命的。

疑问句:
Is the injury non-fatal? = 伤势是非致命的吗？
Are the side effects non-fatal? = 副作用是非致命的吗？

否定句:
The injuries were not non-fatal; they were fatal. = 伤势并非不致命；而是致命的。
The outcome was not non-fatal; it was tragic. = 结果并非不致命；而是悲惨的。
