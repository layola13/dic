hyperrealist: /ˈhaɪpərˈriːəlɪst/ | adj. | 超现实主义的；极度逼真的 |  Describes art that is so realistic it surpasses the appearance of reality, often employing meticulous detail and techniques to achieve a hyperrealistic effect. It aims to create an image that is more real than real, exceeding the limitations of ordinary photographic representation. |  The hyperrealist painting was so lifelike it was hard to believe it wasn't a photograph.  | 近义词：photorealistic, extremely realistic | 反义词：abstract, impressionistic

词源:

The word "hyperrealist" is a combination of "hyper-" (meaning "excessive" or "beyond") and "realist" (referring to a style of art that aims for accurate representation of reality).  Therefore, it literally means "excessively realistic" or "beyond realistic".
