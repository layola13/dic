quasi-foolishly: /ˈkwɑːzaɪ ˈfuːlɪʃli/ | adv. |近乎愚蠢地，看似愚蠢地 |  The adverb "quasi-foolishly" describes an action or behavior that appears foolish or unwise, but perhaps not entirely so. It suggests a degree of foolishness without being completely or undeniably foolish.  The "quasi" prefix indicates something that is seemingly or partially, but not wholly, something else. | 例句：He quasi-foolishly invested all his savings in a risky startup. 他近乎愚蠢地将他所有的积蓄投资到一个高风险的初创公司。


She quasi-foolishly agreed to help him move the entire furniture set alone. 她看似愚蠢地答应独自帮他搬动整套家具。| 近义词：seemingly foolishly, apparently unwisely, somewhat stupidly | 反义词：wisely, intelligently, prudently | 词性: 副词
