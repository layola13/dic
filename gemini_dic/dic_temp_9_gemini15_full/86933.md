hemothorax:/ˌhiːmoʊˈθɔːræks/| n. |血胸|The accumulation of blood in the pleural cavity (the space between the lungs and the chest wall).  This condition is typically caused by trauma to the chest, such as a rib fracture or a penetrating wound, which damages blood vessels.  It can also occur as a complication of surgery or other medical procedures.|Example: The patient presented with a hemothorax following a motor vehicle accident.  患者在车祸后出现血胸。 The surgeon drained the hemothorax to relieve the pressure on the lungs.  外科医生排出了血胸以减轻肺部压力。|近义词：pleural effusion (if the effusion is primarily blood)|反义词：pneumothorax (air in the pleural cavity)
