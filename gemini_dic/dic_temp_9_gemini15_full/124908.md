mudfish:/ˈmʌdˌfɪʃ/| n. |非洲肺鱼；泥鱼|A mudfish is a type of fish that lives in muddy waters and can survive out of water for extended periods.  They are known for their ability to estivate (a state of dormancy) during dry seasons.  Many species are lungfish, possessing lungs as well as gills. |例句：The mudfish burrowed deep into the mud to survive the drought.  非洲肺鱼深深地钻进泥土里以度过干旱。

The mudfish are a fascinating example of adaptation to harsh environments. 泥鱼是适应恶劣环境的迷人例子。|近义词：lungfish (肺鱼),  |反义词:  (no direct antonym, as it describes a specific type of fish)
