postfix:/ˈpɒstfɪks/ /ˈpoʊstfɪks/| n. |后缀，后置词|A postfix is a morpheme added to the end of a word to modify its meaning or grammatical function.  It is a suffix that changes the part of speech of the word it is added to or indicates tense, number, etc.  Postfixes are used in many languages, and their presence is a key characteristic of inflectional morphology.|Example: The suffix "-ing" in "running" is a postfix that transforms the verb "run" into a present participle.|近义词：suffix, affix|反义词：prefix
