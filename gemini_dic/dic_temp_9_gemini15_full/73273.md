forearms:/ˈfɔːrɑːmz/ | n. |前臂 | The forearms are the parts of the arms between the elbows and the wrists.  They are crucial for many movements and actions, involving a complex interplay of muscles and bones. |例句：He flexed his forearms, showing off his muscles. 他弯曲他的前臂，炫耀他的肌肉。

例句：She injured her forearm during the fall. 她在摔倒时伤到了前臂。


近义词：lower arms


反义词：无


词性:名词


Etymology:

The word "forearm" comes from the Old English "forarm," a compound word combining "for-" (meaning "before" or "in front of") and "arm."  The "for-" element indicates the part of the arm that is closer to the body compared to the hand.  This etymological root highlights the anatomical position of the forearm.
