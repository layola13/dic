funguses:/ˈfʌŋɡəsiːz/ | n. |真菌（复数） |Funguses are eukaryotic organisms that include microorganisms such as yeasts, molds, and mushrooms. They are characterized by their lack of chlorophyll and their ability to obtain nutrients through absorption.  They play crucial roles in decomposition, nutrient cycling, and various symbiotic relationships. |Example: Many different types of funguses grow in damp environments.  许多不同种类的真菌在潮湿的环境中生长。
