quasi-inevitably:/ˈkwɑːziˌɪnɛvɪtəbli/ | adj. | 几乎不可避免地；差不多必然地 |  Almost inevitably; nearly certainly.  It suggests a high probability but not absolute certainty. The event is likely to happen, but there's still a small chance it might not. | The project, quasi-inevitably, ran over budget.  这个项目几乎不可避免地超支了。


The economic downturn, quasi-inevitably, led to job losses.  经济衰退几乎不可避免地导致了失业。

近义词：almost inevitably, virtually inevitably, practically inevitably

反义词：almost certainly avoidable, highly improbable


词源:

* **quasi-**:  From Latin *quasi*, meaning "as if," "as it were," "almost," or "seemingly."  It indicates a resemblance or approximation.

* **inevitably**: From "inevitable," which comes from the Old French *inevitabel*, ultimately from the Latin *inevitabilis*, formed from *in-* (not) + *evitābilis* (avoidable).


用法说明：

quasi-inevitably is an adverbial phrase modifying a verb.  It describes the manner in which an action or event occurs, highlighting the strong likelihood of its occurrence despite the absence of absolute certainty.  It implies a near-certainty based on circumstances or trends, leaving open a small possibility of the outcome not happening.
