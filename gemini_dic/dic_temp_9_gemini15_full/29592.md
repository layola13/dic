catechistical: /ˌkætɪˈkɪstɪkəl/ | adj. | 教义问答的；问答式的 | Relating to or characteristic of a catechism;  involving or resembling a catechism.  | 例句：The catechistical method of instruction was common in the past.  过去，教义问答式的教学方法很常见。

The catechistical instruction prepared the children for confirmation. 教义问答式的指导为孩子们受洗做好了准备。


近义词：didactic, instructional, tutorial | 反义词：informal, unstructured


词源:  From  "catechism" + "-ical".  "Catechism" originates from the Greek word  κατηχεῖν (katēchein), meaning "to resound," "to echo," or "to instruct orally."  This evolved through  Late Latin  *catechesis  (instruction by word of mouth) to the English word "catechism".
