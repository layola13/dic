curialism: /ˈkjʊəriəlɪzəm/ | n. |教权主义；教权政治；重视教会权威的倾向 | The belief in or practice of curialism, emphasizing the authority and power of the Roman Catholic Church's Curia (the central administrative body).  Curialism often involves a hierarchical structure and centralized decision-making within the church, sometimes leading to criticisms of inefficiency and lack of local responsiveness. | Example:  The reforms aimed to reduce curialism and increase the involvement of local bishops in decision-making.  改革旨在减少教权主义，并增加地方主教在决策中的参与。 | 近义词:clericalism, ultramontanism | 反义词:decentralization, laicism
