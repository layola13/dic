deglorifying: /ˌdiːˈɡlɔːrɪfaɪɪŋ/ | v. | 去光荣化；贬低；使不光彩 |  The act of removing or diminishing glory, honor, or prestige from something or someone.  It often involves exposing flaws, weaknesses, or negative aspects to counter a previously idealized or glorified image. | 例句：The historian's work deglorified the nation's founding fathers by revealing their involvement in slavery.  历史学家的著作揭露了建国元勋们参与奴隶贸易的事实，从而去光荣化了他们。

The ongoing media coverage deglorifies the celebrity's image by exposing their unethical behavior.  持续的媒体报道通过揭露这位名人的不道德行为来贬低其形象。

近义词：denigrating, discrediting, defaming, belittling, downplaying

反义词：glorifying, extolling, praising, celebrating, idealizing

词性：动词
