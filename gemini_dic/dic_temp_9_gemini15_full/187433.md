roiled:/rɔɪld/| v. adj.| 搅动；使不安；使混乱；汹涌的；动荡的 | To make (a liquid)  turbulent or muddy by stirring or agitating; to disturb or make (something) agitated or upset;  to be in a state of turbulence or agitation. |例句：The storm roiled the sea.暴风雨使大海汹涌澎湃。The news roiled the stock market. 这则新闻扰乱了股市。近义词：agitated, turbulent, disturbed, upset |反义词：calm, peaceful, tranquil, still |动词，形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The wind roils the dust. = 风卷起尘土。
He roils the waters with his accusations. = 他用他的指控搅乱了局面。


一般过去时:
The earthquake roiled the city. = 地震震动了这座城市。
The unexpected news roiled her emotions. = 意外的消息扰乱了她的情绪。


现在完成时:
The scandal has roiled the political landscape. = 丑闻已经扰乱了政治格局。
The storm has roiled the ocean for days. = 暴风雨已经让海洋汹涌了好几天了。


过去完成时:
The team had been roiled by internal disagreements before the game. = 在比赛之前，球队内部的分歧已经让他们处于动荡之中。
The country had been roiled by years of civil war. = 这个国家已经被多年的内战所扰乱。


将来时:
The announcement will roil the markets. = 这一声明将扰乱市场。
The impending election will roil the political climate. = 即将到来的选举将使政治气氛动荡不安。


虚拟语气:
If the news were released, it would roil the public. = 如果新闻发布，它将会激怒公众。
If the government had acted sooner, it might have prevented the situation from roiling. = 如果政府早点采取行动，它可能已经阻止了局势动荡。


被动语态:
The waters were roiled by the storm. = 海水被暴风雨搅动了。
The public was roiled by the politician's lies. = 公众被政客的谎言激怒了。


疑问句:
Will this decision roil the shareholders? = 这个决定会激怒股东吗？
Does the prospect of war roil you? = 战争的前景是否让你不安？


否定句:
The calm waters were not roiled by the gentle breeze. = 平静的水面没有被轻柔的微风搅动。
His announcement did not roil the markets as much as expected. = 他的声明并没有像预期的那样扰乱市场。
