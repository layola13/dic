prejudicing:/ˈpredʒʊdɪsɪŋ/| v.| 损害；使有偏见；对…不利 |To harm or damage something or someone; to influence someone's opinion unfairly; to act in a way that is unfair to someone.  |例句：His comments prejudiced the jury against the defendant. 他的评论使陪审团对被告产生了偏见。 |例句：The bad weather prejudiced our chances of a successful picnic. 恶劣的天气影响了我们野餐成功的几率。|近义词： harming, biasing, impairing |反义词： benefiting, assisting, favoring |动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is prejudicing the case with his biased statements. = 他用带有偏见的陈述损害了这个案子。
The company's actions are prejudicing its reputation. = 公司的行为正在损害其声誉。

一般过去时:
The judge's remarks prejudiced the outcome of the trial. = 法官的言论影响了审判的结果。
Her early mistakes prejudiced her chances of promotion. = 她早期的错误影响了她晋升的机会。

现在完成时:
His constant negativity has prejudiced people against him. = 他持续的消极态度已经使人们对他产生偏见。
The scandal has prejudiced the public's opinion of the politician. = 丑闻已经损害了公众对这位政治家的看法。

过去完成时:
The leaked information had prejudiced the investigation before it even began. = 泄露的信息甚至在调查开始之前就已经对其造成了损害。
They had prejudiced the negotiations by making unreasonable demands. = 他们通过提出不合理的要求损害了谈判。

将来时:
His actions will prejudice his future career prospects. = 他的行为将会损害他未来的职业前景。
The delay will prejudice the project's timeline. = 延迟将会影响项目的进度。

虚拟语气:
If he hadn't prejudiced the jury, the defendant might have been acquitted. = 如果他没有影响陪审团，被告可能已经被无罪释放。
I wish he hadn't prejudiced the situation with his rash comments. = 我真希望他不要用他鲁莽的评论来影响局势。

被动语态:
The defendant felt prejudiced by the judge's remarks. = 被告感到受到了法官言论的不利影响。
The company's image was prejudiced by the negative press coverage. = 公司的形象受到了负面新闻报道的不利影响。

疑问句:
Will his testimony prejudice the court's decision? = 他的证词会影响法院的判决吗？
Does his behavior prejudice his chances of getting the job? = 他的行为会影响他获得这份工作的机会吗？

否定句:
The witness's testimony did not prejudice the trial. = 证人的证词没有影响审判。
His actions did not prejudice his chances of success. = 他的行为没有影响他成功的几率。
