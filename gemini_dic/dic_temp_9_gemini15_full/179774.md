reevacuating: /ˌriːˈvækjueɪtɪŋ/ | v. | 重新疏散；再次撤离 |  The act of evacuating again;  moving people or things out of a place a second time, typically because of a continuing or renewed threat. |例句：After the initial evacuation, the residents were reevacuated due to the approaching wildfire.  = 初次疏散后，由于逼近的野火，居民们再次撤离。

The city is reevacuating its citizens due to the rising floodwaters. = 由于洪水上涨，城市正在再次疏散其市民。


近义词：relocate, remove, displace,  evacuate (again) | 反义词：populate, settle, occupy

They reevacuate the building every time there's a fire drill. = 每次消防演习他们都会重新疏散大楼。
