froze:/froʊz/| v.|冻结；凝固；僵住|The verb "freeze" means to become or make something become solid as a result of extreme cold. It can also refer to a sudden stillness or cessation of activity.|例句：The lake froze over during the cold snap.  寒冷突然袭来，湖面结冰了。

The project froze due to lack of funding.  由于缺乏资金，项目暂停了。|近义词：freeze over, solidify, congeal |反义词：melt, thaw


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The water freezes at 0 degrees Celsius. = 水在零摄氏度结冰。
He freezes when he sees a spider. = 他看到蜘蛛就吓僵了。


一般过去时:
The river froze solid last night. = 河水昨晚冻结成冰了。
She froze with fear when she heard the noise. = 她听到噪音后吓得僵住了。


现在完成时:
The pond has frozen over. = 池塘已经结冰了。
My account has been frozen. = 我的账户已被冻结了。


过去完成时:
The pipes had frozen before we noticed the problem. = 我们注意到问题之前，管道就已经冻住了。
They had frozen their assets to avoid bankruptcy. = 为了避免破产，他们已经冻结了他们的资产。


将来时:
The forecast predicts that it will freeze tonight. = 预报今晚会结冰。
I think the market will freeze if the news is confirmed. = 我认为如果消息得到证实，市场将会冻结。


虚拟语气:
If it froze tonight, the plants would die. = 如果今晚结冰，植物就会死掉。
I wish my account hadn't been frozen. = 我真希望我的账户没有被冻结。


被动语态:
The food was frozen for preservation. = 食物被冷冻以保存。
The assets were frozen by the court. = 资产被法院冻结了。


疑问句:

否定句:

词源:
The word "froze" is the past tense of the verb "freeze," ultimately deriving from the Old English verb *frēosan, meaning "to freeze."  This is related to other Germanic words with similar meanings.
