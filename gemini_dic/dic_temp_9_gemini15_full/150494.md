pantograph:/ˈpæn.tə.ɡræf/| n. |绘图仪；复写器；缩放器|A pantograph is a mechanical device that is used to copy drawings or designs, usually by enlarging or reducing them. It consists of a system of hinged rods, allowing a tracing point to follow the original drawing while a pen or pencil at another point on the device creates a scaled copy.  |The architect used a pantograph to enlarge the blueprint. 建筑师用绘图仪放大蓝图。

The engineer employed a pantograph to reduce the design. 工程师使用绘图仪缩小设计。|enlarger, copier, duplicator |  |n.
