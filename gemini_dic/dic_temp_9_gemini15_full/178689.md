recriticizing: /ˌriːˈkrɪtɪsaɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 再批评；重新批判 |  The act of criticizing something again, often after a previous criticism or after some changes have been made. It implies a renewed or repeated assessment, suggesting that the initial criticism was not sufficient or that the issues persist. |

例句：

The professor recriticized the student's essay, pointing out new grammatical errors. = 教授再次批评了学生的论文，指出了新的语法错误。


After the initial review, the committee recriticized the project proposal, focusing on its feasibility. = 初审之后，委员会再次批评了项目提案，重点关注其可行性。


近义词：re-evaluating, reassessing, reviewing, reconsidering


反义词：approving, endorsing, praising


词性：动词
