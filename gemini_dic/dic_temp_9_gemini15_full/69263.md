featherer:/ˈfɛðərər/| n. v.| 羽毛装饰者；使长羽毛者 |A person or thing that decorates with feathers or causes something to grow feathers.  The term is primarily used in a figurative or specialized context, rarely in general conversation.  It's not a common word. |例句：The milliner was a skilled featherer, creating breathtaking hats.  制帽匠是一位熟练的羽毛装饰者，制作出令人惊叹的帽子。


The antique featherer was used to add decorative feathers to hats.  这台古董羽毛装饰器被用来在帽子上添加装饰羽毛。| 近义词：decorator, ornamenter | 反义词：stripper (in the context of removing feathers) |名词，动词

词源:
The word "featherer" is derived directly from the noun "feather," meaning a light, flat outgrowth from a bird's skin. The suffix "-er" is an agentive suffix indicating a person or thing that performs the action named by the stem.  Therefore, a "featherer" is someone or something that applies or attaches feathers.
