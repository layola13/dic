co-unite:/koʊˈjuːnaɪt/| v.|联合，团结|To unite or join together; to combine or merge.|The two companies decided to co-unite to increase their market share.  两家公司决定合并以增加市场份额。|近义词：unite, combine, merge, consolidate|反义词：separate, divide, disunite


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They co-unite their efforts to achieve a common goal. = 他们团结一致努力实现共同目标。
The two teams co-unite their resources for the project. = 两个团队为项目联合资源。

一般过去时:
The two countries co-united to form a new nation. = 这两个国家联合起来组成了一个新国家。
The opposing factions co-united under a new leader. = 对立派系在一个新领导人的领导下团结起来。


现在完成时:
They have co-united their resources to overcome the crisis. = 他们已经联合资源来克服危机。
The communities have co-united to rebuild after the disaster. = 社区们已经联合起来重建灾后家园。


过去完成时:
They had co-united their forces before the final battle. = 在最后一场战斗之前，他们已经联合了他们的军队。
The groups had co-united their strategies before launching the campaign. = 各团体在发起运动之前已经联合了他们的策略。


将来时:
The organizations will co-unite to address the environmental issue. = 各组织将联合起来解决环境问题。
The two companies will co-unite to expand their global reach. = 两家公司将联合起来扩大其全球影响力。


虚拟语气:
If they co-united their efforts, they would succeed. = 如果他们团结一致，他们就会成功。
I wish they had co-united earlier to prevent the conflict. = 我希望他们早点团结起来以防止冲突。


被动语态:
Their efforts were co-united to achieve the project's objectives. = 他们的努力得到了统一以实现项目的既定目标。
Resources were co-united to support the research. = 资源被联合起来以支持研究。


疑问句:
Will the two parties co-unite to form a coalition government? = 这两个政党是否会联合起来组建联合政府？
Have the different departments co-united their perspectives? = 不同的部门是否已经统一了他们的观点？


否定句:
They did not co-unite their efforts, resulting in failure. = 他们没有团结一致，导致失败。
The groups failed to co-unite, hindering progress. = 各团体未能团结一致，阻碍了进步。
