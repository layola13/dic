ragged: /ˈræɡɪd/ | adj. | 粗糙的；破旧的；衣衫褴褛的；参差不齐的；不规则的 |

英文解析:  Describes something that is uneven, torn, or worn out, often in a way that is untidy or messy.  It can refer to physical objects, clothing, or even sounds or surfaces.

例句：

His ragged clothes showed his poverty.  他的破旧衣服显示了他的贫困。

The ragged edges of the torn paper needed mending.  撕破的纸张参差不齐的边缘需要修补。


近义词：torn, tattered, worn, uneven, irregular, rough, shabbily, dilapidated

反义词：smooth, neat, even, tidy, pristine, well-maintained


词源:  From Middle English *raggede, from Old French *ragé "shaggy," from rager "to rage, be furious," probably from Vulgar Latin *rabiare "to rave, be mad" (source of English *rabies).  The connection is likely through the idea of something being wildly uneven or disordered.
