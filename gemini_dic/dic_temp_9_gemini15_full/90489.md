huck:/hʌk/| vt. n.| 投掷；猛推；叫卖；骗子；粗鲁的人；|to throw something forcefully; to promote or sell something aggressively; a swindler or con artist; a rough, uncouth person |例句：He hucked the ball across the field. 他用力把球扔过球场。 The salesman huckstered his wares loudly.  推销员大声叫卖他的商品。|近义词：toss, fling, throw, swindler, rogue |反义词：receive, catch, honest person

词源:
The word "huck" likely originated from the Middle English word "hok," meaning "hook" or "to hook." This connection stems from the act of throwing something with a hooking motion. The sense of aggressive selling and swindling developed later.
