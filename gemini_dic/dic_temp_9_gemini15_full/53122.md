diplodocuses:/ˈdɪpləˌdoʊsəs/| n. |梁龙属（复数）|A genus of large, quadrupedal, herbivorous sauropod dinosaurs that lived in the Late Jurassic period.  Diplodocuses are known for their extremely long necks and tails, as well as their relatively small heads. |The diplodocuses roamed the Earth millions of years ago. = 梁龙在数百万年前漫游地球。

The discovery of several diplodocuses skeletons has greatly advanced our understanding of these magnificent creatures. = 多具梁龙骨架的发现极大地促进了我们对这些宏伟生物的了解。|近义词：sauropods (更广泛的类别)|反义词：无
