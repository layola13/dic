institutionalised:/ˌɪnstɪtjuːʃənəlaɪzd/| adj.|制度化的；使制度化；使成为习俗|Made into an established institution;  officially recognized or accepted.  The process of embedding something within a system or institution, often to the point where it becomes difficult to change or challenge.|The prison system has institutionalised violence.  监狱制度已经制度化了暴力。|The practice of institutionalized racism is deeply ingrained in society. 制度化的种族主义实践已深深根植于社会。|systematized, formalized, established|individualized, personalized, informal
