debility:/dɪˈbɪləti/ | n. |虚弱；衰弱；无力 |Weakness or lack of strength; feebleness.  It can refer to physical weakness, mental weakness, or a general lack of strength or energy. |例句：The patient suffered from extreme debility after the surgery. 手术后，病人极度虚弱。

例句：Prolonged illness led to a significant debility in his physical capabilities.  长期生病导致他的体力严重衰弱。


近义词：weakness, feebleness, frailty, infirmity, prostration |反义词：strength, vigor, robustness


词源:  From Middle French *debilité, from Latin debilitat- (stem of debilitare) "to weaken," from de- "down, away" + habilis "able, fit."  The word's development reflects the sense of a weakening or loss of ability.
