hyperbarbarously:/ˌhaɪpəbɑːˈbærəsli/| adj.|极端野蛮地，极端残暴地|Extremely barbaric; excessively cruel and savage.  The word combines "hyper-" (excessive) and "barbarously" (in a barbaric manner). It suggests a degree of barbarity exceeding the norm. |例句：The dictator ruled hyperbarbarously, inflicting unimaginable suffering on his people. 独裁者统治极其野蛮，给人民带来了难以想象的痛苦。  The invaders behaved hyperbarbarously, leaving a trail of destruction in their wake. 侵略者行为极其野蛮，在其身后留下了一片破坏。|近义词：brutally, savagely, cruelly, fiendishly, inhumanely |反义词：mercifully, humanely, kindly, gently


USAGE->
This word, being an adverb, doesn't have the same grammatical variations as verbs or nouns.  Its usage is primarily descriptive and modifying.  Therefore, traditional tense analysis doesn't apply.


PHRASE->
There are no common phrases specifically using "hyperbarbarously."  Its usage is generally standalone to emphasize the extreme nature of the barbarity.
