criticize:/ˈkrɪtɪsaɪz/ | vt. | 批评；批判；指责 | To express disapproval of someone or something.  | 例句：The professor criticized my essay for its lack of organization. 教授批评我的论文缺乏条理。

例句：He criticized the government's handling of the crisis. 他批评政府处理危机的措施。


近义词：condemn, censure, rebuke, reprimand, fault, find fault with | 反义词：praise, commend, applaud


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He criticizes the government regularly. 他经常批评政府。
She criticizes her friends' choices sometimes. 她有时批评朋友的选择。


一般过去时:
They criticized the movie's plot. 他们批评了电影的情节。
He criticized her performance harshly. 他严厉地批评了她的表演。


现在完成时:
I have criticized his work many times. 我多次批评过他的工作。
She has criticized the new policy since its announcement. 自从新政策宣布以来，她就一直在批评它。


过去完成时:
They had criticized the plan before the meeting. 他们在会议之前就批评了这个计划。
She had criticized his behavior repeatedly. 她一再批评过他的行为。


将来时:
He will criticize the proposal if he doesn't agree with it. 如果他不赞同这个提案，他会批评它。
They will criticize the decision publicly. 他们将在公开场合批评这个决定。


虚拟语气:
I wish he wouldn't criticize me so much. 我希望他不那么批评我。
If I were you, I wouldn't criticize him in front of others. 如果我是你，我不会当着别人的面批评他。


被动语态:
His work was criticized by many experts. 他的作品受到了许多专家的批评。
The policy is being criticized by the public. 这项政策正受到公众的批评。


疑问句:
Does she often criticize her colleagues? 她经常批评她的同事吗？
Why did they criticize his ideas? 他们为什么批评他的想法？


否定句:
He didn't criticize her work. 他没有批评她的工作。
They don't criticize without good reason. 他们不是没有理由就批评。


PHRASE->
criticize sharply: 严厉批评
criticize severely: 严重批评
criticize constructively: 建设性地批评
criticize unfairly: 不公平地批评
criticize openly: 公开批评

词源:
源自古法语 *critiquer，最终源自古希腊语 *kritēs ("judge")，与拉丁语 *crītis 相同。
