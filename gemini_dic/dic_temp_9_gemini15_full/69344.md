federalism:/ˈfedərəlɪzəm/| n. |联邦制，联邦主义|A system of government in which power is divided between a central authority and constituent political units (such as states or provinces).  This division of power aims to balance the need for national unity with the desire for local autonomy.  The specific distribution of powers varies significantly across federal systems.|The United States operates under a system of federalism, dividing powers between the federal government and individual states. |近义词：federal structure, decentralization |反义词：unitarianism, centralism
