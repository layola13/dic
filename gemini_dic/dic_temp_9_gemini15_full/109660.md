leucocytes:/ˈluːkəsaɪts/| n. |白细胞|White blood cells that are part of the body's immune system, protecting against infection and disease.  They are produced in the bone marrow and circulate in the blood and lymphatic system.  There are several types of leucocytes, each with a specific function. |Example:  The doctor ordered a blood test to check the patient's leucocyte count.  医生下令进行血液检查以检查病人的白细胞计数。|近义词：white blood cell, WBC |反义词：None (no direct antonym)|名词
