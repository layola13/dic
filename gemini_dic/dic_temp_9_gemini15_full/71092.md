fish-in-troubled-waters: /fɪʃɪntrʌbldˈwɔːtərz/ | n. | 浑水摸鱼；乘乱捞好处；渔翁得利 |  A person who takes advantage of a chaotic or difficult situation to gain an advantage. It implies that the person is opportunistic and not necessarily concerned with the well-being of others involved. | 例句：He's a real fish in troubled waters, always looking for ways to profit from other people's misfortune. 他真是个浑水摸鱼的人，总是想方设法从别人的不幸中获利。


例句：She skillfully fished in troubled waters, securing a lucrative contract during the company's financial crisis. 她巧妙地浑水摸鱼，在公司财务危机期间获得了一份利润丰厚的合同。


近义词：opportunist, profiteer, scavenger


反义词：altruist, philanthropist


词性：名词性短语

词源:
The phrase is derived from the proverb  "to fish in troubled waters," which originates from the Latin phrase "turbido in aquis piscari."  The imagery reflects the ease with which one can catch fish when the water is muddy and visibility is poor, mirroring the opportunistic advantage taken in chaotic situations.
