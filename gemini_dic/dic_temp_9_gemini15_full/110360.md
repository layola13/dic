lightships:/ˈlaɪtʃɪps/| n.|灯船|A lightship is a seagoing vessel that is anchored in a specific location to act as a navigational aid, typically equipped with powerful lights and other signaling devices to warn ships of dangers such as shallow waters, reefs, or other hazards.  They were particularly important before the widespread use of GPS and other modern navigational technology.|The lightship warned approaching vessels of the treacherous rocks. 这艘灯船警告驶近的船只注意危险的礁石。|近义词：beacon, buoy|反义词:None
