hustled:/ˈhʌsəld/| v.| 催促；猛推；奔忙；欺骗 | To move or act quickly and energetically.  To force or push someone roughly. To cheat someone. | 例句：He hustled the children out of the house. 他催促孩子们离开房子。


He hustled the papers across the desk. 他把文件猛推过桌子。

近义词：rushed, hurried, pushed, shoved, swindled | 反义词：dawdled, lingered, delayed


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He hustles the papers every day. = 他每天都忙着处理文件。
She hustles to get her work done on time. = 她努力按时完成工作。

一般过去时:
He hustled me out of my money. = 他骗走了我的钱。
They hustled to finish the project before the deadline. = 他们努力赶在截止日期前完成项目。

现在完成时:
He has hustled his way to the top. = 他一路打拼才到达顶峰。
She has hustled all day and is very tired. = 她忙活了一整天，非常疲惫。

过去完成时:
They had hustled to get everything ready before the guests arrived. = 客人到达之前，他们已经忙着准备一切了。
He had hustled the deal through quickly. = 他迅速地完成了那笔交易。

将来时:
I will hustle to get the report finished by tomorrow. = 我会努力在明天之前完成报告。

虚拟语气:

被动语态:

词源:
The word "hustle" originates from the early 19th century, likely from the verb "hustle" meaning "to shake or jostle," possibly from an alteration of "hostle" (to shake).  Its meaning evolved to include the sense of energetic activity, and later, the connotation of swindling or cheating.
