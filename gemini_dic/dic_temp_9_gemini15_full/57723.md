dreading:/ˈdrɛdɪŋ/| v.| 害怕，恐惧，担忧 | To feel extreme apprehension or anxiety about something that might happen.  |例句：I'm dreading the upcoming exam. 我害怕即将到来的考试。|例句：She was dreading telling him the bad news. 她害怕告诉他这个坏消息。|近义词：fearing, anticipating with anxiety, apprehensive about |反义词：looking forward to, eagerly awaiting


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I am dreading the dentist appointment. = 我害怕去看牙医。
He dreads public speaking. = 他害怕公开演讲。


一般过去时:
I dreaded going to school that day. = 我那天很害怕去上学。
She dreaded the thought of losing her job. = 她害怕失去工作。


现在完成时:
I have been dreading this moment for weeks. = 我几周来一直害怕这一刻。
They have dreaded the possibility of failure. = 他们一直害怕失败的可能性。


过去完成时:
I had dreaded the meeting all week. = 我一整周都很害怕这次会议。
She had dreaded telling her parents the truth. = 她一直害怕告诉父母真相。


将来时:
I will be dreading the surgery next week. = 我下周将害怕手术。
He will dread the consequences of his actions. = 他将害怕自己行为的后果。


虚拟语气:
I would dread to think what would happen if... = 我不敢想象如果...会发生什么。
If I were you, I would dread that possibility. = 如果我是你，我会害怕那种可能性。


被动语态:
The upcoming test was dreaded by all the students. = 即将到来的考试让所有学生都感到害怕。
The thought of war was dreaded by the people. = 人们害怕战争的降临。


疑问句:
Are you dreading the trip? = 你害怕这次旅行吗？
What are you dreading the most? = 你最害怕什么？


否定句:
I don't dread the work, I enjoy it. = 我不害怕工作，我喜欢它。
She didn't dread the challenge; she embraced it. = 她不害怕挑战，而是接受了它。


PHRASE->
dread to think: 不敢想象
dread the thought of: 害怕想到
dread the consequences: 害怕后果
dreadful experience: 可怕的经历
dreadful news: 可怕的消息
