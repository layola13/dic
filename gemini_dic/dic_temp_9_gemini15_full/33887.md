circumstantial:/ˌsɜːrkəmˈstænʃəl/| adj.|间接的；依情况而定的；偶然的 | Relating to or dependent on circumstances; incidental.  Circumstantial evidence is indirect evidence that implies a fact but does not directly prove it.  The adjective can also describe something that is merely accidental or coincidental, not essential or inherent. |例句：The circumstantial evidence pointed towards him, but it wasn't enough for a conviction.  间接证据指向他，但这不足以定罪。

例句：His promotion was entirely circumstantial; he was in the right place at the right time.  他的升职完全是偶然的；他只是在正确的时间出现在正确的地点。


近义词：incidental, coincidental, indirect, contingent, casual

反义词：direct, conclusive, definitive, essential, inherent
