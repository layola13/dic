rejigged:/ˌriːˈdʒɪɡd/| v. |重新调整；重新安排；修改 |To reorganize or rearrange something, typically in order to improve it.  It implies a significant alteration or improvement, often involving multiple parts. |例句：The company rejigged its marketing strategy after a poor sales quarter. 公司在销售业绩不佳的一个季度后重新调整了其营销策略。
The schedule was rejigged to accommodate the new speaker.  时间表被重新安排以适应新的演讲者。|近义词：reorganize, rearrange, revamp, overhaul, restructure |反义词：leave unchanged, maintain status quo
