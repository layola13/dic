serf:/sɜːrf/| n. |农奴|A peasant who is legally bound to a lord's land and required to perform labor in return for protection and the right to cultivate a small plot of land.  Serfs were not slaves, but their lives were strictly controlled by their lords.  They were bound to the land and couldn't leave without permission. |Example:  In medieval Europe, serfs constituted the majority of the rural population.  在中世纪的欧洲，农奴构成了农村人口的大多数。|近义词：peasant, villein|反义词：lord, nobleman
