instigated:/ˈɪnstɪɡeɪtɪd/ | v. | 煽动；激起；唆使 | To incite or stir up (violent or unlawful behavior).  | The protesters instigated a riot.  抗议者煽动了一场暴动。 | incite, provoke, foment | appease, calm, pacify | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He instigates trouble wherever he goes. 他走到哪里都煽风点火。
She instigates change within the company. 她积极推动公司内部变革。


一般过去时:
The rumor was instigated by a rival company.  这个谣言是竞争对手公司散布的。
He instigated a fight between his friends. 他挑拨了他的朋友们打架。


现在完成时:
They have instigated several lawsuits against the company. 他们已经对该公司提起了几起诉讼。
She has instigated a major shift in company policy. 她已经促使公司政策发生了重大转变。


过去完成时:
The police discovered that he had instigated the crime. 警方发现是他唆使了这起犯罪。
It was later revealed that she had instigated the whole affair. 后来发现是她挑起了整件事。


将来时:
They will instigate a new marketing campaign next month.  他们下个月将发起一场新的营销活动。
He will instigate legal action if necessary.  如有必要，他会采取法律行动。


虚拟语气:
If he had not instigated the argument, there would have been no fight. 如果他没有挑起争论，就不会打架了。
I wish he hadn't instigated such a conflict. 我真希望他没有挑起这样的冲突。


被动语态:
The riot was instigated by a small group of extremists.  暴乱是由一小撮极端分子煽动的。
The changes were instigated by a concerned employee. 这些改变是由一位关注此事的工作人员促成的。


疑问句:
Who instigated the fight? 是谁挑起了这场争斗？
Did he instigate the rumors? 他是否散布了谣言？


否定句:
He did not instigate the violence. 他没有煽动暴力。
The government denied instigating the coup. 政府否认策划了政变。


PHRASE->
instigate a riot 煽动暴乱
instigate a war 发动战争
instigate change 推动变革
instigate an investigation 发起调查
instigate a rebellion 发动叛乱

词源:
From Middle French *instiguer, from Latin instigare "to urge, incite," from instinguere "to prick, goad," from in- "in" +  stingere "to prick."  The original sense suggests a "pricking" or "goading" action that motivates someone to act.
