resists:/rɪˈzɪsts/| v.|抵抗，反抗，抵制|To withstand the force or effect of; oppose.  It also means to oppose or fight against something.|例句：The soldiers resisted the enemy attack. 士兵们抵抗了敌人的进攻。
The government resisted calls for reform. 政府抵制了改革的呼吁。|近义词：oppose, withstand, defy|反义词：yield, surrender, succumb|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He resists change. = 他抵制改变。
She resists temptation. = 她抵制诱惑。

一般过去时:
They resisted arrest. = 他们拒捕。
The building resisted the earthquake. = 这座建筑物抵御了地震。

现在完成时:
She has resisted pressure to resign. = 她一直抵制辞职的压力。
He has resisted all attempts to influence him. = 他抵制了所有试图影响他的企图。

过去完成时:
The army had resisted the invasion for months. = 这支军队抵抗入侵已达数月之久。
They had resisted the urge to give up. = 他们一直抵制放弃的冲动。

将来时:
I believe they will resist any attempt to control them. = 我相信他们会抵制任何试图控制他们的企图。
The company will resist any price increase. = 公司将抵制任何价格上涨。

虚拟语气:
If he resisted the temptation, he would succeed. = 如果他抵制诱惑，他就会成功。
I wish I had resisted the urge to spend money. = 我真希望我没有冲动花钱。

被动语态:
The proposal was resisted by the committee. = 该提案遭到委员会的抵制。
His authority is resisted by many. = 他的权威遭到许多人的抵制。

疑问句:
Does he resist authority? = 他反抗权威吗？
Will they resist the new policy? = 他们会抵制新政策吗？

否定句:
He didn't resist the temptation. = 他没有抵制诱惑。
They didn't resist the changes. = 他们没有抵制这些变化。
