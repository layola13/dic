disillusioned: /ˌdɪsɪˈluʒənd/ | adj. | 幻想破灭的；醒悟的；失望的 | Disillusioned describes someone who has lost their illusions or ideals, often as a result of a negative experience.  It implies a sense of disappointment and a loss of faith in something or someone. |  例句：After years of hard work, he felt disillusioned with his career.  多年努力后，他对自己的事业感到幻灭。

He became disillusioned with politics after witnessing corruption firsthand. 他亲眼目睹腐败后，对政治感到失望。


近义词：disappointed, disenchanted, disillusioned, disheartened | 反义词：hopeful, optimistic, enthusiastic

词源:

The word "disillusioned" is derived from the verb "disillusion," which itself is formed from the prefix "dis-" (meaning "to remove or deprive of") and the noun "illusion" (meaning "a false belief or idea").  Therefore, "disillusioned" means "having had one's illusions removed or destroyed."
