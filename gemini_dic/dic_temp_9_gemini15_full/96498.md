indo-british:/ˌɪndoʊˈbrɪtɪʃ/| adj.|英属印度的；印度英国的；|Relating to or characteristic of the period of British rule in India (roughly 1757-1947).  The term often evokes the cultural fusion and complex power dynamics of this era.  It can refer to people, institutions, art, architecture, and other aspects of life during that time.|例句：The architecture of many buildings in Kolkata reflects the Indo-British style.  许多加尔各答的建筑反映了英属印度的风格。|近义词：Anglo-Indian|反义词：None specifically, but terms referring to post-independence India could be considered contrasting.


USAGE->
This word is primarily an adjective, so it doesn't have verb conjugations.  It describes nouns.

The museum showcased a remarkable collection of Indo-British paintings. = 博物馆展出了大量非凡的英属印度绘画作品。
