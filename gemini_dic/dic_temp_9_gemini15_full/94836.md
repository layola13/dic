imputably:/ɪmˈpjuːtəbli/ | adv. | 可归咎地，可归责地 |  Attributable; able to be imputed or attributed to someone or something.  It indicates that something can be blamed on or assigned to a particular person or cause. |例句：The accident was imputably to driver negligence. 事故可以归咎于司机的疏忽。

The damage was imputably to the storm.  损失可归咎于风暴。|近义词：attributably, ascribably |反义词：unaccountably, inexplicably


WORD ORIGIN->
The word "imputably" is derived from the verb "impute," which comes from the Latin word "imputare," meaning "to charge, to reckon, to attribute."  The prefix "im-" means "in" or "into," and "putare" means "to think" or "to consider."  Therefore, "imputare" literally means "to think into" or "to attribute to."  The suffix "-ably" transforms the verb into an adverb, signifying the manner or way in which something can be imputed.


USAGE->

This word is primarily used in formal contexts, often in legal or academic writing.  It's less common in everyday conversation.  There aren't specific tenses to conjugate as it's an adverb.  However, it modifies verbs and can be used in various sentence structures.


Example Sentences:

1.  The failure of the project is imputably to poor planning. 项目的失败可归咎于糟糕的计划。(Simple Present)
2. The increase in crime was imputably to social factors. 犯罪率的上升可归咎于社会因素。(Simple Past)
3.  The decision, imputably to pressure from investors, proved disastrous.  这个决定由于投资者的压力，结果证明是灾难性的。(Past Participle as Adjective modifying "decision")
