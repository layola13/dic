parodize:/ˈpærədaɪz/| vt.|模仿嘲弄；戏仿|To imitate the style of (a writer, artist, or work) with deliberate exaggeration for comic effect.|例句：He parodied the president's speech. 他模仿总统的讲话，进行嘲讽。|例句：The comedian parodied several famous actors.  这位喜剧演员模仿了好几位著名演员。|近义词：satirize, lampoon, mimic, burlesque|反义词：praise, applaud


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He parodies famous politicians. = 他模仿著名的政治家。
She parodies popular songs. = 她戏仿流行歌曲。


一般过去时:
They parodied the film's plot. = 他们戏仿了这部电影的情节。
He parodied his teacher's accent. = 他模仿了他老师的口音。


现在完成时:
She has parodied many different styles of writing. = 她已经模仿了许多不同的写作风格。
He has parodied that song several times. = 他已经多次戏仿那首歌了。


过去完成时:
They had parodied the play before the premiere. = 他们在首映之前就已经戏仿了这部戏剧。
She had parodied his mannerisms for years. = 她多年来一直在模仿他的举止。


将来时:
He will parody the latest news story. = 他将要戏仿最新的新闻报道。
They will parody the famous painting. = 他们将要模仿这幅名画。


虚拟语气:
If I could parody Shakespeare, I would. = 如果我能模仿莎士比亚，我会的。
I wish I could parody that celebrity's style. = 我真希望我能模仿那位名人的风格。


被动语态:
The play was parodied by several comedians. = 这部剧被几位喜剧演员戏仿了。
His work has been parodied extensively. = 他的作品被广泛地戏仿了。


疑问句:
Does he often parody famous authors? = 他经常模仿著名的作家吗？
Will they parody the original work? = 他们会模仿原作吗？


否定句:
He doesn't usually parody serious subjects. = 他通常不模仿严肃的话题。
They didn't parody the book's ending. = 他们没有戏仿这本书的结局。
