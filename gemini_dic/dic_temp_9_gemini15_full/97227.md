inflations:/ɪnˈfleɪʃənz/ | n. |通货膨胀；膨胀；充气 | The general increase in prices and fall in the purchasing value of money.  Inflation is a sustained increase in the general price level of goods and services in an economy over a period of time. When the price level rises, each unit of currency buys fewer goods and services. Consequently, inflation reflects a reduction in the purchasing power per unit of money – a loss of real value in the medium of exchange and unit of account within the economy. | 例句：High inflation rates are eroding people's savings.  高通货膨胀率正在侵蚀人们的储蓄。

例句：The rapid inflation of the balloon surprised the children. 气球迅速膨胀让孩子们大吃一惊。| 近义词：price increases, cost escalation, expansion, swelling | 反义词：deflation, contraction, shrinkage |  词性：名词
