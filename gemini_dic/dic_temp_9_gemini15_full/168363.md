pro-Whig: /ˌproʊˈwɪɡ/ | adj. | 亲辉格党的；支持辉格党的 |  Supporting or favoring the Whig Party, a major political party in Britain and the United States during the 18th and 19th centuries.  The term often implies a moderate or liberal stance, particularly in the context of the American political landscape. |  Many pro-Whig newspapers championed the cause of religious toleration. | 近义词: Whig-supporting, liberal (in a historical context), progressive (in a historical context) | 反义词: anti-Whig, Tory, conservative (in a historical context)
