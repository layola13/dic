conjecturably:/kənˈdʒɛktʃərəbli/ | adv. | 推测地；可能地 |  Based on conjecture or guesswork; possibly.  It suggests a lack of certainty. | Example: Conjecturably, the lost city lies beneath the jungle.  推测之下，失落的城市位于丛林之下。 |近义词: presumably, supposedly, perhaps, possibly | 反义词: certainly, definitely, undeniably


USAGE->
This word is an adverb, and therefore doesn't have tenses like verbs.  Its usage is primarily in modifying verbs or entire clauses to indicate that the statement is based on conjecture.


Example 1: He conjecturably arrived late due to traffic. 他可能因为交通堵塞而迟到。
Example 2: The painting is conjecturably a forgery. 这幅画可能是赝品。


PHRASE->
There are no common phrases specifically using "conjecturably".  Its usage is generally standalone.  Phrases built around the idea of conjecture could include:

It's conjectured that...  据推测……
It can be conjectured that... 可以推测……
It is possibly the case that...  可能是……
It is plausible that...  很可能……
In all likelihood...  很可能……
