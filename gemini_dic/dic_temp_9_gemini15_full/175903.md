ratified:/ˈrætɪfaɪd/ | v. | 批准；认可；正式批准 | To approve and formally sanction; to confirm.  The treaty was ratified by the Senate.  | 近义词：approved, confirmed, sanctioned, validated | 反义词：rejected, annulled, repealed


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The Senate ratifies treaties. = 参议院批准条约。
He ratifies the decision. = 他批准了这个决定。

一般过去时:
The agreement was ratified last week. = 该协议上周获得批准。
She ratified the contract. = 她批准了合同。

现在完成时:
They have ratified the amendment. = 他们已经批准了这项修正案。
The treaty has been ratified by all parties. = 所有各方都已批准了该条约。

过去完成时:
The court had ratified the earlier decision. = 法院已经批准了早先的决定。
Before the changes were implemented, the board had ratified the proposal. = 在实施这些变更之前，董事会已经批准了该提案。


将来时:
The parliament will ratify the bill next month. = 议会将在下个月批准该法案。
We expect they will ratify the agreement soon. = 我们预计他们很快就会批准该协议。


被动语态:
The bill was ratified by a large majority. = 该法案以压倒性多数获得批准。
The agreement is being ratified as we speak. = 正如我们所说，该协议正在被批准。


疑问句:
Has the treaty been ratified yet? = 条约已经批准了吗？
Will the amendment be ratified? = 修正案会被批准吗？


否定句:
The proposal was not ratified. = 该提案未获批准。
The Senate did not ratify the treaty. = 参议院没有批准该条约。
