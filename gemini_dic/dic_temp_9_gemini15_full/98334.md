insinuate:/ˈɪnsɪnjuˌeɪt/| vt.|暗示；暗指；逐渐地使…进入|To suggest or hint at something indirectly or subtly.  It often implies something unpleasant or malicious.|例句：He insinuated that I was responsible for the mistake. 他暗示是我犯了错误。

例句：She tried to insinuate herself into the conversation. 她试图插进谈话中。


近义词：imply, suggest, hint, intimate|反义词：state directly, declare openly|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He insinuates that I am lazy. = 他暗示我懒惰。
She insinuates herself into every conversation. = 她总是试图插进每一场谈话。

一般过去时:
He insinuated that the project was failing. = 他暗示这个项目快失败了。
She insinuated herself into the VIP section. = 她设法混进了贵宾区。

现在完成时:
He has insinuated his way into the company's inner circle. = 他已经设法混进了公司的核心圈子。
She has insinuated that I am dishonest. = 她一直暗示我不诚实。

过去完成时:
He had insinuated doubts about her abilities before the interview. = 在面试之前，他已经暗示了对她的能力的怀疑。
She had insinuated herself into the family's affairs. = 她已经介入到这个家庭的私事了。

将来时:
He will insinuate that the report is biased. = 他会暗示这份报告有偏见。
She will insinuate herself into the board meeting. = 她将会设法混进董事会会议。

虚拟语气:
If he insinuated that, I would be very angry. = 如果他那样暗示，我会非常生气。
I wish he hadn't insinuated that I was incompetent. = 我真希望他没暗示我无能。

被动语态:
It was insinuated that the company was in financial trouble. = 有人暗示公司正面临财务困境。
She felt that her motives were being insinuated against. = 她觉得她的动机受到了质疑。

疑问句:
What are you insinuating? = 你在暗示什么？
Is he insinuating that I'm lying? = 他是在暗示我在撒谎吗？

否定句:
He didn't insinuate anything. = 他什么也没暗示。
She never insinuates herself into others' business. = 她从不插手别人的事。


PHRASE->
insinuate oneself into =  设法混入；逐渐取得…的地位
insinuate something to someone = 向某人暗示某事
insinuate a suggestion = 暗示一个建议
insinuate a doubt = 暗示怀疑
insinuate an idea = 暗示一个想法
