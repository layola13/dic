ill-advised: /ˌɪl ədˈvaɪzd/ | adj. | 不明智的，欠考虑的 |  Describes an action, decision, or plan that is unwise or likely to have negative consequences. It suggests a lack of careful consideration or foresight.  | Example:  His ill-advised attempt to climb the mountain alone nearly cost him his life.  他的鲁莽尝试独自攀登这座山差点要了他的命。


近义词: unwise, imprudent, foolish, reckless, injudicious, shortsighted

反义词: wise, prudent, judicious, well-considered, sensible


词源:  The word is a compound of "ill," meaning bad or wrong, and "advised," the past participle of "advise," meaning to give counsel or guidance.  Therefore, "ill-advised" literally means "badly advised" or "given bad advice."
