generic: /dʒəˈnɛrɪk/ | adj. |通用的，一般的；非品牌的 | Relating to or characteristic of a whole group or class rather than of any particular member; general.  A generic term or concept applies to a whole category of things rather than to any specific example.  | 例句：The doctor prescribed a generic drug.医生开了一种非品牌药。

例句：This software has a generic interface that is easy to use.  这个软件有一个通用的界面，易于使用。


近义词：general, common, universal, typical | 反义词：specific, particular, brand-name, unique


词源:  From late Middle English *generik, from Old French *generique, from Latin genericus, from genus "kind, race, stock".  The modern English usage developed from botanical and biological classifications in the 19th century.
