individualizing: /ˌɪndɪˈvɪdʒuəlaɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 使个性化；使个别化 |  To make or become individual or distinct.  The process of adapting something to suit the needs of a particular person or group. |

例句：
The teacher individualizes her lessons to meet the needs of each student. = 老师个性化地授课以满足每个学生的需要。

The company is individualizing its marketing strategies to target specific demographics. = 公司正在个性化其营销策略以针对特定人群。


近义词：personalizing, customizing, tailoring, differentiating


反义词：standardizing, generalizing, homogenizing


词性：动词

词源:
individualizing comes from the word "individual," which originates from the Latin word "individuus," meaning "indivisible" or "single."  The "-izing" suffix indicates the process of making something individual.
