counterbidding:/ˈkaʊntərbɪdɪŋ/| n. |反价，还价|The act of making a higher bid than a previous bid in an auction or negotiation.  It involves responding to an offer with a competing offer. |例句：The buyer's counterbidding pushed the price of the painting significantly higher. 买家的反价使这幅画的价格大幅上涨。


例句：After the initial offer, the seller received several rounds of counterbidding before finally accepting a deal. 初始报价之后，卖家在最终达成交易之前收到了几轮反价。


近义词：rebidding,  outbidding


反义词：accepting (an offer without a counteroffer)


词性：名词
