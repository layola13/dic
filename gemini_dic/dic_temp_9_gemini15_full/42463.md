counterfactuals: /ˌkaʊntərˈfæktʃuəlz/ | n. | 反事实陈述 |  A counterfactual is a statement that describes a situation that is contrary to fact; it describes what would have happened if something different had occurred.  Counterfactuals often involve hypothetical situations, and are frequently used in discussions of causality, decision-making, and historical analysis. They are often marked by conditional clauses (e.g., "If I had studied harder, I would have passed the exam.")  |  Example: "If I had won the lottery, I would have traveled the world." = 如果我中了彩票，我就会周游世界。  "She regrets not going to college; it's a constant counterfactual in her life." = 她后悔没上大学；这是她生活中一个持续存在的反事实。 |近义词：hypotheticals, what-ifs | 反义词：factuals, realities |  名词
