like-mindednesses:/ˈlaɪkˌmaɪndɪdnɪsəz/ | n. |志同道合，思想一致 |The plural form of "like-mindedness," referring to instances or examples of shared beliefs, values, or interests.  It emphasizes the multiplicity of such shared perspectives. |例句：The conference brought together people from diverse backgrounds, but their like-mindednesses on the issue of climate change were evident.  会议汇集了来自不同背景的人，但他们在气候变化问题上的思想一致是显而易见的。

USAGE->
This word is primarily a noun and doesn't have verb conjugations like "to be," "to have," etc.  The grammatical usage focuses on its role within sentences.


Example sentences illustrating different grammatical functions:

Subject:  The like-mindednesses among the participants were striking.  参与者之间的思想一致令人瞩目。

Object:  We celebrated the like-mindednesses that united our group. 我们庆祝将我们团队团结在一起的思想一致。

Appositive: The group, characterized by its like-mindednesses, achieved great success. 以其思想一致为特征的团队取得了巨大的成功。

Prepositional Phrase: Because of their like-mindednesses, they worked together seamlessly. 因为他们的思想一致，他们无缝地合作。
