consol:/ˈkɒnsəl/| /kənˈsoʊl/ | v. |安慰；慰藉 |To comfort someone who is feeling sad or disappointed.  |He consoled his friend after her dog died. 他在她的狗死后安慰了他的朋友。| comfort, soothe, solace | anger, upset | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He consoles her regularly. = 他经常安慰她。
She consoles herself with music. = 她用音乐来安慰自己。

一般过去时:
My mother consoled me when I failed the exam. = 我考试不及格时，妈妈安慰了我。
The teacher consoled the crying child. = 老师安慰了哭泣的孩子。


现在完成时:
She has consoled many grieving families. = 她已经安慰了许多悲伤的家庭。
He has consoled himself with the thought that he did his best. = 他一直安慰自己，认为自己已经尽力了。


过去完成时:
I had consoled myself with the hope that things would improve. = 我一直抱有希望事情会好转来安慰自己。
She had consoled him many times before. = 她以前多次安慰过他。


将来时:
I will console her when she hears the news. = 当她听到这个消息时，我会安慰她。
They will console themselves with the knowledge that they tried their best. = 他们将会安慰自己，知道他们已经尽力了。


虚拟语气:
If I knew what to say, I would console her. = 如果我知道该说什么，我会安慰她。
I wish I could console him, but I don't know what to say. = 我希望我能安慰他，但我不知道该说什么。


被动语态:
She was consoled by her friends. = 她受到了朋友们的安慰。
The grieving family was consoled by the priest. = 悲伤的家庭受到了牧师的安慰。


疑问句:
How can I console her? = 我该如何安慰她？
Will anyone console him? = 会有人安慰他吗？


否定句:
He couldn't console her. = 他无法安慰她。
I didn't know how to console her. = 我不知道该如何安慰她。


PHRASE->
console oneself = 自我安慰
console sb. about sth. = 就某事安慰某人
offer consolation = 提供安慰
give consolation = 给予安慰
find consolation in = 从…中得到安慰
