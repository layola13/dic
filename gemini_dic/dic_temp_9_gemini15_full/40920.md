coolie: /ˈkuːli/ | n. |苦力，华工 | A derogatory term for a manual laborer, especially one of Asian descent, historically used in a racist and offensive manner.  The term is now widely considered offensive and should be avoided. | Example: The use of the word "coolie" is deeply offensive and perpetuates harmful stereotypes.  使用“苦力”一词具有严重的冒犯性，并延续了有害的刻板印象。| 近义词：laborer, worker (neutral terms) | 反义词：N/A (The word itself is offensive, not a descriptor with an antonym) | 名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He works as a laborer. = 他做苦力工作。
They carry heavy loads. = 他们搬运重物。


一般过去时:
He worked as a coolie in the plantations. = 他曾经在种植园里做过苦力。(注意：此例句展示了该词过去的不当用法)
They carried heavy burdens on their backs. = 他们背着沉重的负担。


现在完成时:
He has worked hard all his life. = 他一生辛勤工作。
They have transported countless goods. = 他们运输了无数的货物。


过去完成时:
He had already worked for ten hours before he took a break. = 他休息前已经工作了十个小时。
They had carried the entire shipment before nightfall. = 他们在夜幕降临前已经运送了整批货物。


将来时:
He will continue to work hard. = 他将继续努力工作。
They will transport the goods to the warehouse. = 他们将把货物运送到仓库。


虚拟语气:
If he were a wealthy man, he wouldn't need to work so hard. = 如果他是个有钱人，他就不用这么辛苦地工作了。
If they had better equipment, they could work faster. = 如果他们有更好的设备，他们可以工作得更快。


被动语态:
The goods were transported by truck. = 货物是用卡车运输的。
The heavy loads were carried by many men. = 沉重的货物由许多人搬运。


疑问句:
What kind of work does he do? = 他做什么工作？
How many goods were transported yesterday? = 昨天运送了多少货物？


否定句:
He doesn't work as a coolie anymore. = 他不再做苦力了。
They did not complain about their workload. = 他们没有抱怨他们的工作量。
