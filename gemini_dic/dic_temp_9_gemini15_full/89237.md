homilete:/ˈhɒmɪliːt/ | n. |训诫；说教；说教式的演讲 | A homilete is a sermon or a discourse that is intended to instruct or preach.  It often focuses on moral or religious themes and aims to guide the listener's behavior or beliefs.  | Example: The priest delivered a powerful homilete on the importance of forgiveness.  牧师发表了一篇关于宽恕重要性的精彩布道。 | 近义词：sermon, lecture, discourse, preachment | 反义词：None specifically, but words suggesting lighthearted conversation or informal discussion could be considered opposite in tone. | 名词

词源:
The word "homilete" comes from the Greek word "homiletikos," meaning "pertaining to conversation or discourse."  It is related to the word "homily," which itself originates from the Greek "homilia," meaning "a conversation, discourse, sermon."
