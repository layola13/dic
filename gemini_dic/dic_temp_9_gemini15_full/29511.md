catastrophe:/kəˈtæstrəfi/| n. |大灾难；灾祸 |A sudden and widespread disaster.  A catastrophe is an event that causes great and often sudden damage or suffering; a calamity. |例句：The earthquake was a terrible catastrophe. 这场地震是一场可怕的大灾难。

例句：The hurricane caused a major catastrophe in the coastal region.  飓风在沿海地区造成了巨大的灾难。


近义词：disaster, calamity, cataclysm, debacle

反义词：blessing, good fortune, success


词性:名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The earthquake causes catastrophe. = 地震造成灾难。
This policy will bring about a catastrophe. =这项政策将会导致一场灾难。


一般过去时:
The flood caused a great catastrophe. = 洪水造成了一场巨大的灾难。
The fire resulted in a terrible catastrophe. = 火灾导致了一场可怕的灾难。


现在完成时:
This drought has already caused a catastrophe for farmers. = 这次旱灾已经给农民带来了灾难。
The war has led to a humanitarian catastrophe. =这场战争导致了人道主义灾难。


过去完成时:
The dam had already broken before the catastrophe occurred. = 大坝在灾难发生前就已经决堤了。
They had no idea the severity of the catastrophe that was about to unfold. = 他们不知道即将发生的灾难的严重性。


将来时:
A nuclear war would be a global catastrophe. = 核战争将是一场全球性的灾难。
Experts warn that climate change could lead to a future catastrophe. = 专家警告说，气候变化可能导致未来的灾难。


虚拟语气:
If we don't act now, it could be a catastrophe. = 如果我们现在不采取行动，那将是一场灾难。
It would have been a catastrophe if the plane had crashed. = 如果飞机坠毁了，那将是一场灾难。


被动语态:
The city was devastated by the catastrophe. = 城市被灾难摧毁了。
The catastrophe was caused by human error. = 灾难是由人为错误造成的。


疑问句:
Could this be a catastrophe in the making? = 这会不会是一场正在酝酿的灾难？
What kind of catastrophe could occur? = 会发生什么类型的灾难？


否定句:
It wasn't a catastrophe, but it was a serious setback. = 这不是一场灾难，但它是一个严重的挫折。
The situation wasn't as catastrophic as initially feared. = 情况没有最初担心的那么灾难性。
