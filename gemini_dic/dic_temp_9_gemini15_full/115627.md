marches:/ˈmɑːrtʃɪz/ | n. v. | 行军；游行；进行；步伐 |  The rhythmic movement of troops or people walking together, often in a military or ceremonial context;  to walk with a steady, measured pace; to make progress. |

例句：
The soldiers marched towards the enemy.  = 士兵们向敌人行进。

The protesters marched through the city streets. = 抗议者们游行穿过城市街道。


近义词：
proceeds, advances, progresses, parades, treads

反义词：
retreats, withdraws, halts, stops


词性：
Noun and Verb


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The army marches every morning. = 军队每天早上都行军。
He marches to the beat of a different drum. = 他走的是与众不同的路。


一般过去时:
The band marched in the parade. = 乐队在游行队伍中行进。
They marched for hours in the protest. = 他们在抗议活动中游行了数小时。


现在完成时:
The troops have marched many miles. = 部队已经行军了许多英里。
She has marched steadily towards her goals. = 她一直在稳步地朝着自己的目标前进。


过去完成时:
They had marched all day before they reached the town. = 他们行军了一整天后才到达城镇。
The soldiers had marched for weeks before the battle. = 士兵们在战斗前已经行军了几周。


将来时:
The soldiers will march tomorrow. = 士兵们明天将行军。
We will march on Washington. = 我们将前往华盛顿游行。


虚拟语气:
If they marched faster, they would arrive sooner. = 如果他们走得快一点，他们就会早点到达。
I wish I had marched with them. = 我真希望当时我和他们一起游行。


被动语态:
The troops were marched to the front line. = 部队被带到了前线。
The parade was marched through the main street. = 游行队伍从主街经过。


疑问句:
Did they march today? = 他们今天行军了吗？
Will the soldiers march tomorrow? = 士兵们明天会行军吗？


否定句:
The soldiers did not march today. = 士兵们今天没有行军。
They will not march without orders. = 没有命令他们不会行军。


PHRASE->
forced marches = 强行军
border marches = 边境地区
death marches = 死行军
on the march = 在行军中
a forced march = 强行军


词源:
From Old French *marcher, from Frankish *markōn, from Proto-Germanic *markōną, from Proto-Indo-European *(h₁)merk- “to go, travel”.  The meaning extended to the regular, rhythmic movement of troops.
