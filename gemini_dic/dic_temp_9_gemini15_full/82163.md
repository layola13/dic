grooves:/ɡruːvz/ | n. |凹槽，槽纹；习惯，常规；节奏感 | A groove is a long, narrow cut or channel in a surface.  It can also refer to a rut or established pattern of behavior. In music, it refers to a rhythmic feel or pattern. |例句：The record player needle followed the grooves in the vinyl. (唱机唱针沿着黑胶唱片上的凹槽移动。)

The car tires left deep grooves in the mud. (汽车轮胎在泥里留下了深深的凹槽。)


近义词：rut, furrow, channel, rhythm | 反义词：smoothness, flatness


词源:  Old English *grafan "to dig, carve, engrave," from Proto-Germanic *grabaną (source also of Old High German graban, Old Norse grafa, Dutch graven, German graben "to dig," Old English grafan "to dig, bury"), from PIE *ghrebh- "to scratch, scrape, dig."  The sense evolution is from "digging a channel" to the resulting "channel" itself.
