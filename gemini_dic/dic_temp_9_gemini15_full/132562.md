nondiaphanously:/ˌnɒnˌdaɪəˈfeɪnəsli/| adj. |不透明地；不透光地|Not allowing light to pass through; opaque.  The meaning extends figuratively to situations or communication that are not easily understood or transparent.  |例句：The stained-glass window, though beautiful, was nondiaphanously thick. = 尽管美丽，但彩色玻璃窗却厚实不透光。 The politician's speech was nondiaphanously vague, leaving the audience confused. = 这位政治家的讲话含糊不清，让听众感到困惑。|近义词：opaque, obscure, impenetrable, unclear |反义词：transparent, lucid, clear, pellucid
