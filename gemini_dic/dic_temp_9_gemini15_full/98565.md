instituter: /ˈɪnstɪˌtuːtər/ | n. |发起人；创立者；提议者 | One who institutes or establishes something, such as a legal action, organization, or system.  | The instituter of the lawsuit claimed significant damages.  发起诉讼的人声称遭受了重大损失。 | founder, originator, initiator | destroyer, terminator | 名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He institutes new policies every year. 他每年都制定新的政策。
The company institutes a new training program. 公司实施了一个新的培训计划。


一般过去时:
She instituted legal proceedings against the company. 她对该公司提起诉讼。
They instituted a new system for managing projects. 他们建立了一个新的项目管理系统。


现在完成时:
The government has instituted several reforms. 政府已经实施了几项改革。
They have instituted a new code of conduct. 他们已经制定了一套新的行为准则。


过去完成时:
Before the war, they had instituted strict rationing. 在战争爆发前，他们已经实行严格的配给制度。
The company had instituted a new safety policy before the accident. 事故发生前，公司已经实施了新的安全政策。


将来时:
The university will institute a new scholarship program next year.  大学明年将设立新的奖学金项目。
They will institute stricter regulations. 他们将实施更严格的法规。


虚拟语气:
If they instituted a new system, things would improve. 如果他们实施一个新的系统，情况就会改善。
I wish they had instituted stricter controls earlier. 我希望他们早些实施更严格的控制。


被动语态:
A new policy was instituted by the government. 一项新的政策由政府实施。
Stricter regulations were instituted to improve safety. 实施了更严格的法规以提高安全性。


疑问句:
Who instituted this policy? 谁制定的这项政策？
When was the new system instituted? 新系统是什么时候实施的？


否定句:
The company did not institute any changes. 公司没有进行任何更改。
They did not institute the new program on time. 他们没有按时实施新的计划。
