conditionalism: /kənˈdɪʃənəlɪzəm/ | n. | 条件论，条件主义 | The philosophical doctrine that God's actions are conditional upon certain factors, such as human free will or the fulfillment of specific conditions.  It contrasts with unconditionalism, which holds that God's actions are independent of any conditions.  Conditionalism often appears in discussions of God's grace, salvation, and predestination.  | Example:  The debate between conditionalism and unconditionalism has occupied theologians for centuries. =  几个世纪以来，条件论和无条件论之间的辩论一直困扰着神学家们。  | 近义词:  hypotheticalism (in some contexts) | 反义词: unconditionalism |  名词
