cornices:/ˈkɔːrnɪsɪz/ | n. |  檐口，壁冠，(建筑物)顶部的装饰线 |  A cornice is a decorative molding or projection that crowns a building, wall, or piece of furniture.  It typically projects outward from the wall and often has a decorative profile.  | The building's cornices were intricately carved.  这栋建筑物的檐口雕刻精美。 The antique dresser boasted elegant cornices. 这古董梳妆台拥有优雅的檐口。 |  molding, frieze, capping |  (无明确反义词，可考虑unadorned, plain) | 名词

词源:
The word "cornice" comes from the Old French word "cornise," which ultimately derives from the Latin word "cornu," meaning "horn." This connection is due to the often-projecting, horn-like shape of cornices.
