presses:/ˈprɛsɪz/| n. v.|印刷机；压力；新闻；压；按；逼迫|The act of pressing or the state of being pressed;  a machine or tool for pressing.|例句：The presses are running at full speed. 印刷机正在全速运转。  The weight of the books presses down on the shelf. 书的重量压在书架上。|近义词：push, squeeze, urge, compel|反义词：release, loosen


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He presses the button. = 他按按钮。
She presses her clothes. = 她熨烫她的衣服。


一般过去时:
I pressed the doorbell. = 我按了门铃。
They pressed charges against him. = 他们对他提起诉讼。


现在完成时:
She has pressed the issue for weeks. = 她几周来一直在坚持这个问题。
He has pressed all the laundry. = 他已经熨烫了所有的衣服。


过去完成时:
I had pressed the button before the alarm sounded. = 警报响起之前我已经按下了按钮。
They had pressed for a change in policy. = 他们曾要求改变政策。


将来时:
She will press the charges against him tomorrow. = 她明天将对他提起诉讼。
He will press the iron against the fabric. = 他会把熨斗熨在布料上。


虚拟语气:
If I pressed the button, the machine would start. = 如果我按下按钮，机器就会启动。
I wish I had pressed the issue earlier. = 我真希望我早点坚持这个问题。


被动语态:
The button was pressed accidentally. = 按钮被意外按动了。
The clothes are being pressed at the dry cleaners. = 衣服正在干洗店熨烫。


疑问句:
Are you pressing the button hard enough? = 你按按钮的力度够吗？
What's pressing you so much? = 什么事让你这么着急？


否定句:
He didn't press the issue. = 他没有坚持这个问题。
She doesn't press her clothes very often. = 她不经常熨烫衣服。
