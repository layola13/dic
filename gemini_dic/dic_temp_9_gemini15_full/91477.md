hylozoist:/ˈhaɪləʊzoʊɪst/| n. |泛灵论者|A hylozoist is a person who believes that all matter is alive or has a soul.  This philosophical position posits that life is an inherent property of matter itself, not something that arises from a specific combination of elements.  Hylozoism is a form of panpsychism, which is the view that consciousness is a fundamental and ubiquitous aspect of reality. |The ancient Greeks, particularly some pre-Socratic philosophers, held hylozoist views.  |近义词：panpsychist |反义词：materialist, mechanist
