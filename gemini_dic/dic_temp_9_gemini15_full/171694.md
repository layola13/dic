pulvillus:/pʌlˈvɪljəs/| n. |吸垫，趾垫|A pulvillus is a small, adhesive pad found on the feet of some insects and other arthropods.  These pads allow the insects to cling to smooth surfaces. They are typically composed of soft, hairy tissue and secrete adhesive substances. |Example: The spider's pulvilli enabled it to climb the smooth glass wall.  这只蜘蛛的吸垫使它能够攀爬光滑的玻璃墙。|近义词：adhesive pad, arolium (in some contexts)|反义词：无|名词
