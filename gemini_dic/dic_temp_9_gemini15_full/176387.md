re-garrisons:/ˌriːˈɡærɪsən/| vt.|重新驻军；再次配置守军|To station troops in (a place) again.  The government decided to re-garrison the border region after the recent unrest.|近义词：reoccupy, reinforce, repopulate |反义词：evacuate, abandon


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The army re-garrisons the fort every year. = 军队每年都会重新驻守该要塞。
They re-garrison the city after the riots. = 骚乱之后，他们重新驻军该城市。


一般过去时:
The general re-garrisoned the strategic town. = 将军重新驻守了这个战略要地。
They re-garrisoned the island after the enemy attack. = 敌人袭击后，他们重新驻守了该岛屿。


现在完成时:
The government has re-garrisoned the disputed territory. = 政府已经重新驻守了有争议的领土。
They have re-garrisoned the area to maintain order. = 为了维持秩序，他们已经重新驻守了该地区。


过去完成时:
The commander had re-garrisoned the fort before the enemy arrived. = 在敌人到达之前，指挥官已经重新驻守了要塞。
They had re-garrisoned the city before the storm hit. = 在暴风雨来临之前，他们已经重新驻守了这座城市。


将来时:

虚拟语气:
