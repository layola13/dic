inconspicuousness: /ˌɪnkənˈspɪkjuəsnis/ | n. | 不显眼；不引人注目；默默无闻 | The state or quality of being inconspicuous; lack of attention-drawing qualities.  It refers to something or someone that is not easily noticed or seen. | 例句：The chameleon's inconspicuousness helped it blend seamlessly with its surroundings.  变色龙的不显眼帮助它与周围环境完美融合。

The inconspicuousness of the security camera made it very effective.  保安摄像头的隐蔽性使其非常有效。


近义词：unobtrusiveness, subtlety, inconspicuousness, low profile, concealment | 反义词：conspicuousness, prominence, visibility, noticeability


词源:  The word derives from the adjective "inconspicuous," formed from the prefix "in-" (meaning "not") + "conspicuous" (meaning "easily seen or noticed").  "Conspicuous" itself comes from the Latin word "conspicuus," meaning "easily seen," from "conspicere," meaning "to look at attentively," from "con-" (meaning "together") + "specere" (meaning "to look").
