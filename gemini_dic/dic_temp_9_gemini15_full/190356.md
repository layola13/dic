sardine:/sɑːˈdiːn/| n. |沙丁鱼|A small, oily fish, typically preserved in oil or brine.  Sardines are a common food source, known for their nutritional value and distinctive flavor. They are often canned and sold as a convenient and affordable protein source.| 例句： We had sardines on toast for dinner. = 晚饭我们吃了烤面包配沙丁鱼。

The can of sardines was surprisingly delicious. = 那罐沙丁鱼出奇的好吃。


近义词：pilchard (though there are subtle biological differences)| 反义词：None specifically, but could be contrasted with larger, less oily fish like tuna or salmon.
