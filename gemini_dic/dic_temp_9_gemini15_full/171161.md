psychogeographic:/ˌsaɪkoʊdʒiːəˈɡræfɪk/| adj. n.|心理地理学的；心理地理学|The study of the psychological effects of geographical environments on individuals and groups.  It explores how the built environment and geographical locations influence people’s emotions, behaviors, and perceptions.|Example: The city's psychogeographic map revealed areas of high anxiety and low social interaction.  这座城市的心理地理地图揭示了高度焦虑和低社交互动的区域。|近义词：psychosocial, environmental psychology|反义词：N/A
