hormone:/ˈhɔːrmoʊn/| n. |荷尔蒙，激素 |A hormone is a regulatory substance produced in an organism and transported in tissue fluids such as blood or sap to stimulate specific cells or tissues into action.  Hormones are chemical messengers that are secreted into the bloodstream and travel to target cells, which have specific receptors for the hormone. They play a crucial role in regulating various bodily functions. | Example:  Insulin is a hormone that regulates blood sugar levels.  胰岛素是一种调节血糖水平的激素。


The thyroid gland produces several important hormones. 甲状腺产生几种重要的激素。


近义词：endocrine, regulator | 反义词：None (no direct antonym exists)

词源:
