cover-up: /ˈkʌvər ʌp/ | n., v. |掩盖；掩饰；遮盖物 |

英文解析:To cover up means to hide something, usually something bad or embarrassing, from public view.  As a noun, it refers to the act of hiding or the thing used to hide something.

例句:

n. The cover-up of the scandal was unsuccessful.  掩盖丑闻的企图并不成功。

v. They tried to cover up their mistakes. 他们试图掩盖他们的错误。


近义词:concealment, disguise, hush-up, whitewash

反义词:reveal, expose, disclose

词性: noun, verb

词源:
The word "cover-up" is a compound word formed from the verb "cover" and the adverb "up".  "Cover" has Germanic roots, related to words meaning to protect or shelter.  The addition of "up" intensifies the action, indicating a thorough or complete concealment.
