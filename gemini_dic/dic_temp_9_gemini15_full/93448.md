illuminer:/ɪˈluːmɪnər/| n. v.|启发者；照亮者；阐明者| A person or thing that illuminates or inspires.| The artist was an illuminer of the human spirit.  这位艺术家是人类精神的启发者。|近义词：inspirer, enlightener, illuminator|反义词：obscurer, darkener


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The sun illuminates the earth. = 太阳照亮地球。
The teacher illuminates complex concepts for her students. = 老师为学生阐明复杂的概念。

一般过去时:
The moon illuminated the night sky. = 月亮照亮了夜空。
His speech illuminated the problem's root cause. = 他的演讲阐明了问题的根源。

现在完成时:
His work has illuminated many aspects of history. = 他的作品阐明了许多历史方面的内容。
The artist's paintings have illuminated the beauty of nature. = 艺术家的绘画作品展现了大自然的美丽。

过去完成时:
The discovery had illuminated the mystery surrounding the ancient civilization. = 这一发现阐明了围绕古代文明的神秘面纱。
The detective's investigation had illuminated the criminal's motive. = 侦探的调查已经阐明了罪犯的动机。

将来时:
The new research will illuminate the effects of climate change. = 新的研究将阐明气候变化的影响。
The upcoming exhibition will illuminate the artist's creative journey. = 即将举行的展览将阐明艺术家的创作历程。


虚拟语气:
If the evidence illuminated the case, the trial would be over quickly. = 如果证据能阐明案情，审判就会很快结束。
I wish the instructions illuminated the process better. = 我希望说明能更好地阐明这个过程。

被动语态:
The room was illuminated by a single lamp. = 房间里只有一盏灯在照明。
The mystery was illuminated by a breakthrough in the investigation. = 调查的突破阐明了这个谜团。

疑问句:
Does this painting illuminate the artist's intent? = 这幅画是否阐明了艺术家的意图？
Will the new technology illuminate the dark corners of the universe? = 新技术能否照亮宇宙的黑暗角落？

否定句:
The evidence did not illuminate the case. = 证据没有阐明案情。
His explanation did not illuminate the situation at all. = 他的解释根本没有阐明情况。


PHRASE->
illuminate a subject = 阐明一个主题
illuminate a path = 照亮一条路
illuminate a room = 照亮一个房间
illuminate a manuscript = 手抄本的装饰性插图（特指中世纪）
illuminate the future = 照亮未来


词源:
From Latin *illumināre "to light up," from *lūmen "light."  The verb sense of "to make clear" developed later.
