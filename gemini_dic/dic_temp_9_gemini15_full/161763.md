powers:/ˈpaʊərz/ | n. | 力量；权力；能力 | The word "powers" refers to the ability to do something, or the authority to control or influence something or someone.  It can also refer to a specific ability or skill, or to a nation's military strength. |例句：She has the power to influence people. 她有影响人的能力。

The government holds considerable powers. 政府拥有相当大的权力。


近义词：abilities, capabilities, strengths, authority, influence | 反义词：weakness, impotence, inability

词源:
The word "power" originates from the Old French "pouer," meaning "to be able," which itself comes from the Latin "potēre," meaning "to be able, to have the power."  The suffix "-s" indicates plurality, thus "powers" signifies multiple abilities, authorities, or strengths.
