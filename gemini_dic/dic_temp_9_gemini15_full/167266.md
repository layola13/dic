pretext:/ˈpriːtekst/| n.|借口，托辞|A pretext is a false reason or excuse given to conceal the real reason for something.|例句：He used a headache as a pretext for not going to work. 他以头痛为借口不去上班。|近义词：excuse, reason, justification|反义词：truth, reality

词源:
From Old French *pretexte, from Latin praetextum, literally "something woven in front," from prae- "before" + texere "to weave."  The original sense referred to a decorative border on a garment, later evolving to mean a deceptive covering or excuse.


用法说明:

例句：
He used a flimsy pretext to avoid answering the question. 他用了一个站不住脚的借口来避免回答这个问题。

She resigned from her job under the pretext of illness. 她以生病为借口辞去了工作。


近义词例句:
He gave an excuse for his lateness. 他解释了他迟到的原因。
His reason for leaving was unclear. 他离开的原因不明确。
She offered a justification for her actions. 她为自己的行为辩解。

反义词例句:
The truth was, he simply didn't want to go. 事实是，他只是不想去。
The reality was far more complex than he had imagined. 现实远比他想象的要复杂得多。
