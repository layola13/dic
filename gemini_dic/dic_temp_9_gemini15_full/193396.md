seisin:/ˈsiːzɪn/ | n. | 占有；所有权；占有权 | The legal right to possession of property;  ownership.  It implies not just the right to possess but also the right to control and use the property. In older legal systems, seisin was a crucial concept, often involving a formal act of taking possession.  | Example: The lord of the manor claimed seisin of all the land within his boundaries.  领主宣称拥有其边界内所有土地的所有权。 | 近义词：possession, ownership, tenure | 反义词：dispossession, alienation
