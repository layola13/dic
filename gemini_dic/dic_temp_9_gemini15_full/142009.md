organiz: /ˈɔːrɡənaɪz/ | vt. |组织；安排；筹备 | To arrange or put in order; to form into a structured whole. |例句：She organized a fundraising event for the charity. 她为慈善机构组织了一场募捐活动。|近义词：arrange, structure, coordinate |反义词：disorganize, disorder


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He organizes his work efficiently. = 他有效率地组织他的工作。
They organize meetings regularly. = 他们定期组织会议。

一般过去时:
She organized a surprise party for her friend. = 她为她的朋友组织了一个惊喜派对。
The team organized the project effectively. = 团队有效地组织了这个项目。

现在完成时:
They have organized several successful campaigns. = 他们已经组织了几次成功的活动。
We have organized all the materials for the presentation. = 我们已经组织好了演示文稿的所有材料。

过去完成时:
He had organized everything before the guests arrived. = 客人到达之前他已经把一切都安排好了。
They had organized the schedule for the trip weeks in advance. = 他们几周前就安排好了旅行的行程。

将来时:
We will organize a workshop next month. = 我们下个月将组织一个研讨会。
She will organize the files according to their dates. = 她将按照日期组织文件。

虚拟语气:
If I had more time, I would organize a trip. = 如果我有更多的时间，我会组织一次旅行。
It would be helpful if someone organized the data. = 如果有人能整理一下数据就好了。

被动语态:
The event was organized by a professional team. = 这个活动是由一个专业的团队组织的。
The meeting will be organized next week. = 会议将在下周组织。

疑问句:
Who organized the conference? = 谁组织了这次会议？
How will the tasks be organized? = 任务将如何组织？

否定句:
He didn't organize the meeting properly. = 他没有妥善组织会议。
They haven't organized the logistics yet. = 他们还没有组织后勤工作。
