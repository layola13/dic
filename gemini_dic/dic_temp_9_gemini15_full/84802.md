handsprings:/ˈhændˌsprɪŋz/| n.|侧手翻；空翻；兴奋；突变|A handspring is an acrobatic movement in which a person springs from their hands to their feet, or vice versa.  It can also refer to a sudden or unexpected change or a display of great excitement or energy. |例句：The gymnast performed a series of impressive handsprings.  体操运动员表演了一系列令人印象深刻的侧手翻。

The news sent him into a fit of handsprings.  这个消息使他欣喜若狂。|近义词：somersault, cartwheel, flip, leap|反义词：None specifically, but the opposite of "excitement" would apply in the figurative sense.
