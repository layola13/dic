damned's: /dæmdz/ | adj. n. | 该死的，可恶的；被诅咒的 |  The word "damned's" is a possessive form of the adjective "damned,"  meaning something is cursed, extremely bad, or deserving of condemnation. It is often used as an intensifier, expressing anger, frustration, or emphasis. Note that it's informal and often considered profane. | Example: "That damned dog's muddy paws are all over the carpet!" (那该死的狗的泥爪子弄脏了地毯！)|近义词: cursed, blasted, confounded, infernal | 反义词: blessed, holy, sacred |

词源:
The word "damned" originates from the past participle of the verb "damn," which ultimately comes from the Old English word "demmian," meaning "to condemn."  The addition of the possessive 's' simply indicates possession or attribution.
