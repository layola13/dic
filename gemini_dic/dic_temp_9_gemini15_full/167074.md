/ˌpriːˈsuːpərvaɪzər/ | n. | 预备主管；助理主管 | A presupervisor is a person who assists a supervisor in their duties, often as a training or preparatory role.  They may have some supervisory responsibilities but ultimately report to and work under the main supervisor. | Example:  The presupervisor helped the new team members understand their roles.  预备主管帮助新团队成员了解他们的角色。  | 近义词：assistant supervisor, trainee supervisor | 反义词：supervisor, manager

词源:  The word "presupervisor" is a compound word formed by combining "pre-" (meaning "before" or "in preparation for") and "supervisor."  It indicates a role that is preparatory to, or a step towards, becoming a full supervisor.
