hotbed:/ˈhɑːtbed/| n. |温床；滋生地；发源地 |A place where something undesirable, such as crime or disease, thrives and spreads rapidly.  It can also refer to a place where something positive, such as innovation or creativity, flourishes. |例句：The city's slums are a hotbed of crime.  城市的贫民窟是犯罪的温床。|近义词：breeding ground, source, incubator |反义词：sterile environment, dormant state

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The city remains a hotbed of political unrest. = 这座城市仍然是政治动荡的温床。
This fertile valley is a hotbed of agricultural innovation. = 这片肥沃的山谷是农业创新的温床。

一般过去时:
The bar was a hotbed of gossip and scandal last night. = 昨晚酒吧里谣言和丑闻四起。
The university was once a hotbed of revolutionary ideas. = 这所大学曾经是革命思想的温床。


现在完成时:
The region has become a hotbed of infectious diseases. = 该地区已成为传染病的温床。
The small town has become a hotbed of artistic talent. = 这个小镇已经成为艺术人才的聚集地。


过去完成时:
Before the war, the region had been a hotbed of cultural exchange. = 战争爆发前，该地区曾是文化交流的中心。
The country had long been a hotbed of religious conflict. = 这个国家长期以来一直是宗教冲突的温床。


将来时:
Experts predict that the area will become a hotbed of economic activity. = 专家预测该地区将成为经济活动的中心。
They fear the new policy will create a hotbed of social inequality. = 他们担心新政策将导致严重的社会不平等。


虚拟语气:
If the government didn't intervene, the country would become a hotbed of corruption. = 如果政府不干预，这个国家将成为腐败的温床。
If we were to neglect this issue, it could become a hotbed of problems. = 如果我们忽视这个问题，它可能会成为问题的根源。


被动语态:
The school was considered a hotbed of radicalism. = 这所学校被认为是激进主义的温床。
The forest is often described as a hotbed of biodiversity. = 这片森林经常被描述为生物多样性的中心。


疑问句:
Is this neighborhood a hotbed of criminal activity? = 这个街区是不是犯罪活动的温床？
Could this new technology become a hotbed of innovation? = 这项新技术能成为创新的温床吗？


否定句:
The city is not a hotbed of crime, contrary to popular belief. = 与普遍看法相反，这座城市并非犯罪的温床。
That company is not a hotbed of creativity. = 那家公司并非创意的温床。
