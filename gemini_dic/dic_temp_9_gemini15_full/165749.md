prenominal: /ˌpriːˈnɒmɪnəl/ /ˌpriːˈnɑːmɪnəl/ | adj. | 前置的，放在名词前的 |  Placed before a noun; preceding a noun.  It describes words, phrases, or clauses that come before a noun and modify it, similar to adjectives but often with a more specific function.  | Example:  The *prenominal* adjective "happy" modifies the noun "dog" in the phrase "happy dog".  | 近义词: preceding, anteposed | 反义词: postnominal
