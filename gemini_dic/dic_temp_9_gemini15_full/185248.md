retranscription:/ˌriːtrænˈskrɪpʃən/ | n. |重新抄写；转录；改写 |The act of transcribing something again, often with corrections or modifications.  It involves writing or recording something a second time, usually to improve accuracy or clarity.  This could apply to audio recordings, handwritten notes, or other forms of text. | Example: The researcher performed a retranscription of the interview, correcting errors in the original transcript. = 研究人员对访谈进行了重新转录，纠正了原始记录中的错误。|近义词：rewriting, recopying, transcription |反义词：original transcription
