ousting: /ˈaʊstɪŋ/ | v. |罢免；驱逐；逐出 | The act of removing someone from a position of power or authority; forcefully ejecting someone from a place. |例句：The CEO was ousted in a boardroom coup.  首席执行官在一场董事会政变中被罢免了。


例句：The protesters were ousted from the building by police. 抗议者被警方驱逐出大楼。


近义词：dismissal, removal, expulsion, ejection


反义词：appointment, installation, reinstatement


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He ousts his rivals from the competition. 他把竞争对手逐出比赛。
The government ousts corrupt officials regularly. 政府定期罢免腐败官员。

一般过去时:
They ousted the dictator after a long struggle. 经过长期斗争后，他们罢免了独裁者。
The rebels ousted the ruling party from power. 反叛者将执政党赶下台。

现在完成时:
The new CEO has ousted several senior managers. 新任首席执行官已经罢免了几位高级经理。
She has been ousted from her position due to misconduct. 由于行为不端，她已被免职。

过去完成时:
The company had ousted him before the scandal broke. 在丑闻爆发之前，公司已经将他解雇了。
They had already ousted the previous leader before the election. 他们在选举之前就已经罢免了前任领导人。

将来时:
They will oust the current champion in the next match. 他们将在下一场比赛中击败现任冠军。
The opposition party plans to oust the government in the next election. 反对党计划在下届选举中将政府赶下台。

虚拟语气:
If they had more evidence, they could have ousted him sooner. 如果他们有更多证据，他们本来可以早点将他罢免。
If the people had risen up earlier, they might have ousted the tyrant. 如果人民早些起义，他们也许就能推翻暴君。

被动语态:
He was ousted from his position for incompetence. 他因不称职而被免职。
The old regime was ousted in a bloody revolution. 旧政权在一场血腥的革命中被推翻。

疑问句:
Will the opposition party be able to oust the government? 反对党能否将政府赶下台？
Was he ousted for political reasons? 他是被出于政治原因而被罢免的吗？

否定句:
They did not oust him despite the allegations. 尽管有指控，他们也没有罢免他。
The rebellion failed to oust the president. 反叛未能推翻总统。
