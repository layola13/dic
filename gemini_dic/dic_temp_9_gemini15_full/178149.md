recognizing: /ˈrɛkəɡnaɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 识别；认出；承认；表彰 |  The act of identifying someone or something or of accepting something as valid.  |例句：I recognized him from his picture. 我从照片上认出了他。 |例句：The government recognized the new republic. 政府承认了新的共和国。|近义词： identifying, acknowledging, appreciating | 反义词： ignoring, overlooking, disregarding


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He recognizes his mistakes. = 他认识到自己的错误。
She recognizes the importance of education. = 她认识到教育的重要性。

一般过去时:
I recognized her voice immediately. = 我立刻认出了她的声音。
They recognized the danger of the situation. = 他们意识到了情况的危险性。

现在完成时:
We have recognized the need for change. = 我们已经认识到改变的必要性。
She has recognized his talent for years. = 她多年来一直认可他的才能。

过去完成时:
I had recognized him before, but I couldn't remember his name. = 我以前认出过他，但我记不起他的名字了。
They had recognized the pattern before making the decision. = 他们在做出决定之前已经认出了这种模式。

将来时:
I will recognize him when I see him. = 我见到他时就会认出他。
She will recognize the symptoms of the disease. = 她会识别出这种疾病的症状。

虚拟语气:
If I recognized the signs, I would have acted differently. = 如果我认出了这些迹象，我会采取不同的行动。
I wish I had recognized the problem earlier. = 我希望我早点发现这个问题。

被动语态:
His contribution was recognized by the company. = 他的贡献得到了公司的认可。
The problem is being recognized by more and more people. = 越来越多人认识到这个问题。

疑问句:
Do you recognize that song? = 你认得那首歌吗？
Does she recognize the potential risks? = 她是否认识到潜在的风险？

否定句:
I didn't recognize him at first. = 我一开始没认出他。
They didn't recognize the seriousness of the situation. = 他们没有意识到情况的严重性。
