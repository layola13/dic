firing-line: /ˈfaɪərɪŋ laɪn/ | n. |  前线；攻击目标；被攻击的对象 | The place or position where someone is most likely to be attacked or criticized.  It refers to the position of soldiers or others in a battle or conflict who are most exposed to enemy fire.  It can also be used metaphorically to describe a situation where someone is under intense scrutiny or criticism. | 例句：He was on the firing line during the debate. 他在辩论中处于风口浪尖。  The company president found himself on the firing line after the scandal broke.  丑闻爆发后，公司总裁发现自己成了众矢之的。 | 近义词：front line, target, crosshairs | 反义词：safety, protection, sanctuary
