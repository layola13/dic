penalization:/ˌpiːnəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. |惩罚；处罚；不利影响 | The act of penalizing or the state of being penalized;  a penalty or punishment.  It can also refer to a disadvantage or drawback imposed. |例句：The penalization for speeding is a hefty fine. 超速的惩罚是一大笔罚款。

The penalization of late submissions resulted in lower grades. 迟交作业的处罚导致成绩较低。


近义词：punishment, sanction, penalty,  deterrent | 反义词：reward,  incentive


词源：源自法语“pénalisation”，最终源于拉丁语“poena”（惩罚）。


USAGE->

The company faces penalization for violating safety regulations. = 这家公司因违反安全条例而面临处罚。
Penalization often discourages undesirable behavior. = 处罚通常会阻止不良行为。
