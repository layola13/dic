cheats:/tʃiːts/ | n. v. |欺骗，作弊；诀窍，技巧 |

英文解析:
As a noun, "cheats" refers to acts of deception or dishonesty, often to gain an unfair advantage. It can also refer to a person who engages in such acts or to a trick or shortcut.  As a verb, "cheat" means to act dishonestly or fraudulently, typically to gain an advantage.

例句:
He cheated on the exam and got caught. 他考试作弊被抓住了。

She used cheats to win the game. 她用作弊手段赢得了比赛。


近义词:
tricks, deceits, frauds, scams, shortcuts, swindles

反义词:
honesty, fairness, integrity


词性:
Noun, Verb

词源:
The word "cheat" ultimately derives from the Old French word "escheter," meaning "to escape, to avoid,"  which further originates from the Latin "excadere," meaning "to fall out, to fail."  The evolution of meaning reflects a shift from avoiding something to avoiding honest behavior.
