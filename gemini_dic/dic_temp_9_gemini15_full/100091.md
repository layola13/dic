interpose:/ˌɪntərˈpoʊz/| vt. |插入；介入；提出（异议）|To place or insert between other things; to interrupt or intervene.|例句：He interposed himself between the two arguing men. 他介入两个争吵的人中间。|例句：She interposed an objection to the plan. 她对该计划提出了异议。|近义词：insert, intervene, interject|反义词：remove, withdraw, accept


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He interposes himself in every conversation. = 他插手每一次谈话。
She interposes objections whenever possible. = 她尽可能地提出异议。


一般过去时:
The judge interposed to prevent a fight. = 法官介入以阻止一场打斗。
He interposed a question during the lecture. = 他在讲座期间插问了一个问题。


现在完成时:
They have interposed several times during the negotiations. = 在谈判期间，他们已经多次介入。
She has interposed her opinion on many occasions. = 她多次表达了自己的意见。


过去完成时:
Before I could speak, he had interposed a comment. = 我还没来得及说话，他就插了一句嘴。
They had interposed several times before reaching an agreement. = 他们在达成协议之前已经多次介入。


将来时:
I will interpose if necessary. = 必要时我会介入。
He will interpose his authority if the situation escalates. = 如果情况恶化，他会运用他的权力。


虚拟语气:
If I were to interpose, it might worsen the situation. = 如果我介入，可能会使情况恶化。
I wish I had not interposed in their argument. = 我真希望我没有介入他们的争论。


被动语态:
An objection was interposed by the defense lawyer. = 辩护律师提出异议。
A new clause was interposed into the contract. = 在合同中插入了一个新的条款。


疑问句:
Will you interpose on my behalf? = 你会代表我介入吗？
Should we interpose at this point? = 我们应该在这个时候介入吗？


否定句:
He did not interpose during the meeting. = 他在会议期间没有插话。
She chose not to interpose her opinion. = 她选择不发表自己的意见。
