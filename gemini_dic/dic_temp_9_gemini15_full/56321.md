dogmata:/ˈdɒɡmətə/ | n. |教条；信条；教义 | Dogmata are principles or beliefs laid down by an authority as incontrovertibly true.  They are often accepted without question or critical examination.  The term often carries a negative connotation, implying rigidity and a resistance to alternative viewpoints. | Example:  The religious dogmata of the sect were strictly enforced.  这个教派的宗教教条被严格执行。  The scientific community rejects dogmata in favor of evidence-based reasoning. 科学界摒弃教条，而支持基于证据的推理。|近义词：doctrines, tenets, creeds, principles |反义词： skepticism, relativism
