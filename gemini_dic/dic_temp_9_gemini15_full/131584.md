noncompensation: /ˌnɒnˌkɒmpɛnˈseɪʃən/ | n. | 不补偿，未补偿 | The absence of compensation;  a failure to compensate or make amends for something.  It often refers to a lack of payment, reward, or redress for a loss, injury, or service. | 例句：The noncompensation of the workers led to a strike. 工人们没有得到补偿，导致了罢工。

The company's noncompensation for damages caused by their product resulted in a lawsuit. 公司对其产品造成的损害未予赔偿，导致了诉讼。


近义词：lack of compensation, failure to compensate, unrequited, unrewarded | 反义词：compensation, recompense, remuneration, redress


词源:  The word is formed from the prefix "non-" (meaning "not") and the noun "compensation" (meaning "something given to make amends for a loss, injury, or service").  "Compensation" itself originates from the Latin word "compensatio," meaning "weighing together,"  derived from "compensare," meaning "to weigh against, to balance."
