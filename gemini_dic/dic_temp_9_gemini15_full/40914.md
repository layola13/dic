cooled-off:/kʊldˈɔːf/| adj. | 冷却的；平静下来的；不再感兴趣的 |  Describes something that has become less intense or passionate;  a person or situation that has calmed down after being angry or excited. | 例句：The argument cooled off after a while.  争吵过了一会儿就平息了。  His initial enthusiasm for the project has cooled off. 他对这个项目的最初热情已经消退了。| 近义词：calmed, quieted, subdued, less intense | 反义词：inflamed, heated, excited, passionate


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The engine cools off quickly. = 引擎冷却得很快。
The soup cools off slowly. = 汤冷却得很慢。

一般过去时:
The coffee cooled off before I could drink it. = 咖啡在我喝之前就凉了。
He cooled off after his anger subsided. = 他的怒气消退后，他平静了下来。

现在完成时:
The lava has cooled off considerably. = 岩浆已经冷却了很多。
The situation has cooled off since yesterday. = 自从昨天以来，情况已经平息了。

过去完成时:
The metal had cooled off before they could shape it. = 金属在他们成型之前就已经冷却了。
The argument had cooled off by the time we arrived. = 等我们到达时，争吵已经平息了。

将来时:
The tea will cool off soon. = 茶很快就会凉。
His anger will cool off eventually. = 他的怒气最终会消退。

虚拟语气:
If the room cooled off faster, we would be more comfortable. = 如果房间冷却得更快，我们会更舒服。
I wish the situation had cooled off sooner. = 我希望情况早点平息。

被动语态:
The food was cooled off before serving. = 食物在食用前已冷却。
The engine is cooled off by a fan. = 引擎由风扇冷却。

疑问句:
Has the engine cooled off yet? = 引擎冷却了吗？
Has the excitement cooled off? = 热情消退了吗？

否定句:
The soup hasn't cooled off enough yet. = 汤还没凉透。
His anger didn't cool off easily. = 他的怒气不容易消退。
