parallactic:/ˌpærəlˈæktɪk/| adj.|视差的，恒星视差的|Relating to or caused by parallax.  Parallax is the apparent shift in the position of an object when viewed from different positions.  This is commonly used in astronomy to measure the distance to stars.|例句：The parallactic angle is crucial for accurate astronomical measurements. 视差角对于精确的天文测量至关重要。

The parallactic displacement of nearby stars is measurable. 附近恒星的视差位移是可以测量的。


词源: From Greek *parallaxis, from *parallassein ("to change, alter"), from para- ("beside") + allassein ("to change").


近义词：perspective, apparent
反义词：None directly opposite, but words implying lack of perspective could be considered antonyms in certain contexts.


USAGE->
This word is primarily an adjective and doesn't have verb or noun conjugations in the way "what" does.  Therefore, tense, voice, etc., are not applicable.  The usage revolves around describing something related to parallax.

例句1：Astronomers use parallactic measurements to determine stellar distances. 天文学家使用视差测量来确定恒星距离。
例句2：The image shows the parallactic effect on the apparent position of the star.  图像显示了恒星视位置上的视差效应。


PHRASE->
Parallactic angle: 视差角
Parallactic displacement: 视差位移
Parallactic ellipse: 视差椭圆
Parallactic inequality: 视差不等式
Stellar parallax: 恒星视差
