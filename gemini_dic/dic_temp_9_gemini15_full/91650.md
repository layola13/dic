hypercalcaemic: /ˌhaɪpərkælˈsiːmɪk/ | adj. | 高钙血症的 |  Characterized by abnormally high levels of calcium in the blood.  This condition can be caused by various factors, including overactive parathyroid glands, certain cancers, and some medications.  Symptoms can range from mild to severe, and may include fatigue, weakness, nausea, and kidney stones. |  The patient presented with hypercalcaemic symptoms, including fatigue and kidney stones.  |近义词: hypercalcemic | 反义词: hypocalcaemic
