predetrimental: /ˌpriːˌdetɪˈmɛntəl/ | adj. |有害的，不利的 |  Not detrimental; not harmful or disadvantageous.  This word is a relatively uncommon and somewhat artificial construction, formed by adding the prefix "pre-" to "detrimental" to suggest something *before* it becomes detrimental or potentially detrimental.  It implies a state or action that *could* lead to harm, but hasn't yet caused significant damage. |  例句：While the initial investment seemed predetrimental,  it ultimately led to significant profits.  = 虽然最初的投资似乎是不利的，但最终却带来了可观的利润。


例句：The early signs of the disease were predetrimental, meaning that early intervention could prevent severe consequences. = 疾病的早期迹象是有害的，这意味着早期干预可以预防严重的后果。


近义词：potentially harmful, potentially damaging, incipiently harmful


反义词：beneficial, advantageous, helpful


词性: adjective
