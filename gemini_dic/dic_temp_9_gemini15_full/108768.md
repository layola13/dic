leasebacks:/ˈliːsˌbæks/ | n. | 售后回租 | A financial transaction in which a company sells an asset (such as property or equipment) and simultaneously leases it back from the buyer. This allows the company to raise capital while retaining the use of the asset.  | Example: The company used a leaseback arrangement to free up capital for expansion.  该公司利用售后回租安排腾出资金用于扩张。 | 近义词: sale and leaseback | 反义词:  direct sale (without leaseback)


USAGE->

The company leasebacks its properties to raise capital. = 这家公司将房产售后回租以筹集资金。
They regularly leaseback equipment to improve cash flow. = 他们定期将设备售后回租以改善现金流。
