disciplinableness:/ˌdɪsɪplɪˈneɪbəlnəs/| n. | 服从性；可管教性；纪律性 | The capacity or willingness to be disciplined; the state of being easily disciplined.  It refers to the degree to which someone or something can be subjected to discipline and controlled. |  The teacher praised the class for their exceptional disciplinableness. 老师称赞这个班级的学生非常听话。The experiment demonstrated the high disciplinableness of the new material. 实验表明这种新材料具有很高的可控性。|近义词：obedience, docility, tractability |反义词：unruliness, recalcitrance, intractability | 名词

词源:
The word "disciplinableness" is derived from the noun "discipline" and the suffix "-ableness," which indicates the capability or quality of being something.  "Discipline" itself has roots in the Latin word "disciplina," meaning "instruction, teaching, training."  Therefore, "disciplinableness" essentially means the capacity or quality of being easily disciplined or trained.
