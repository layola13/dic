cataloguize:/ˈkætəlɒɡaɪz/ | vt. | 编目；分类；登记 | To arrange or list systematically in a catalog.  | The librarian meticulously catalogued the new books. 图书管理员一丝不苟地为新书编目。  The museum needs to catalogue its vast collection of artifacts.  博物馆需要为其大量的文物编目。 | index, classify, register |  uncatalogue (反义词并非完全相反，而是指从目录中移除) | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They catalogue the books every week. = 他们每周都为书籍编目。
We catalogue our inventory monthly. = 我们每月对库存进行编目。

一般过去时:
The archivist catalogued the historical documents. = 档案管理员为历史文件编目。
She catalogued her stamp collection. = 她为她的邮票收藏编目。

现在完成时:
They have catalogued all the new acquisitions. = 他们已经为所有新的藏品编目。
The museum has catalogued over a million artifacts. = 博物馆已经为超过一百万件文物编目。

过去完成时:
They had catalogued the manuscripts before the exhibition. = 他们在展览前已经为手稿编目。
She had catalogued her entire library before moving. = 她在搬家前已经为她的整个图书馆编目。

将来时:
We will catalogue the paintings next month. = 我们下个月将为绘画编目。
They will catalogue the donations soon. = 他们很快就会为捐赠的物品编目。

虚拟语气:
If we had more time, we would catalogue the entire collection. = 如果我们有更多时间，我们将为整个收藏编目。
It would be helpful if they catalogued the items more efficiently. = 如果他们更有效率地为物品编目，那就太好了。

被动语态:
The books are catalogued by the librarian. = 书籍由图书管理员编目。
The collection is being catalogued at the moment. = 收藏品正在编目中。

疑问句:
Have they catalogued the documents yet? = 他们已经为文件编目了吗？
Will the items be catalogued before the deadline? = 物品会在截止日期前编目吗？

否定句:
They did not catalogue the books properly. = 他们没有正确地为书籍编目。
We haven't yet catalogued the entire archive. = 我们还没有为整个档案编目。
