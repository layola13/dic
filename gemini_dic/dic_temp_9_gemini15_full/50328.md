deplorement:/dɪˈplɔːrmənt/ | n. | 谴责；悲叹；哀悼 | The act of deploring; expression of disapproval or regret.  It often implies a strong feeling of sadness or disapproval, sometimes even outrage. |例句：The deplorement of the war crimes was widespread.  = 对战争罪行的谴责是普遍的。

The deplorement of the environmental damage caused by the oil spill was heartfelt. = 对石油泄漏造成的环境破坏的悲叹发自肺腑。| 近义词：condemnation, denunciation, regret, lament | 反义词：praise, approval, commendation | 词性：名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government deplores the rise in crime. = 政府谴责犯罪率的上升。
Many people deplore the lack of funding for education. = 许多人对教育经费不足表示遗憾。

一般过去时:
She deplored the injustice of the situation. = 她谴责这种情形的非正义性。
They deplored the loss of so many lives. = 他们对如此多的生命损失表示悲痛。

现在完成时:
We have deplored this policy for years. = 多年来我们一直谴责这项政策。
The public has deplored the actions of the company. = 公众谴责了该公司的行为。

过去完成时:
She had deplored the decision before it was even made. = 这项决定做出之前她就已表示谴责。
They had deplored the lack of communication for months. = 他们几个月来一直对缺乏沟通表示不满。

将来时:
They will deplore the consequences of their actions. = 他们将对他们行为的后果表示谴责。
We will deplore the loss of this natural habitat.  = 我们将对这种自然栖息地的丧失表示惋惜。

虚拟语气:
I would deplore such behavior if I saw it. = 如果我看到这种行为，我会谴责它。
We would have deplored the decision if we had known the consequences. = 如果我们知道后果，我们会谴责这个决定的。

被动语态:
The decision was deplored by many. = 许多人谴责了这一决定。
The lack of action was widely deplored. = 不作为受到了广泛的谴责。

疑问句:
Do you deplore the violence in the city? = 你谴责城市中的暴力吗？
Would they deplore the outcome if they knew? = 如果他们知道结果，他们会谴责吗？

否定句:
I don't deplore his actions; I understand them. = 我并不谴责他的行为；我理解他的行为。
They did not deplore the change in policy. = 他们并不谴责政策的改变。
