counter-price: /ˈkaʊntərpraɪs/ | n. |还价；反价；对价 | A price offered in response to an initial price, typically lower.  It's a price offered in negotiation, often as a counter-offer to a seller's asking price. | Example:  The buyer offered a counter-price of $10,000, significantly lower than the seller's asking price of $15,000.  买方还价1万美元，远低于卖方1.5万美元的要价。 |近义词：counter-offer, bid, offer | 反义词：asking price, initial price |  词性：名词


USAGE->
This word is primarily a noun and doesn't conjugate like a verb.  Therefore, tense conjugations are not applicable.  The usage focuses on its role within a sentence.


Example 1 (Simple Sentence):  Their counter-price was unacceptable.  他们的还价无法接受。

Example 2 (Complex Sentence):  After much negotiation, they finally agreed on a counter-price that was satisfactory to both parties.  经过多次协商，他们最终商定了一个双方都满意的还价。


PHRASE->
There aren't common multi-word phrases directly built around "counter-price," as it's a fairly self-contained term.  However, we can illustrate its use in phrases:

Phrase 1:  Negotiate a counter-price.  协商还价。
Phrase 2:  Accept the counter-price. 接受还价。
Phrase 3:  Reject the counter-price.  拒绝还价。
Phrase 4:  Propose a counter-price.  提出还价。
