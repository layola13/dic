preinhabitation: /ˌpriːɪnˌhæbɪˈteɪʃən/ | n. | 先期居住；先住；先民居住 | The state or period of being inhabited before a later, more significant habitation; often referring to a period before recorded history or significant cultural development.  It implies a less developed or less documented form of human occupation compared to subsequent periods. | Example:  Archaeological evidence suggests a long period of preinhabitation in the cave system before the arrival of the known Neolithic settlers. 考古证据表明，在已知的史前新石器时代定居者到来之前，这个洞穴系统经历了漫长的先期居住时期。 | 近义词：proto-occupation, early settlement, prior inhabitation | 反义词：post-habitation, depopulation
