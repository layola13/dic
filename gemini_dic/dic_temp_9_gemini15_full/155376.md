pharyngeals:/fəˈrɪndʒiəlz/ | n. |咽音|Sounds produced by constricting the pharynx (the part of the throat behind the mouth).  Pharyngeals are typically consonants, and their articulation involves narrowing or closing the pharynx.  They are relatively rare in the world's languages.| Example: Arabic and Hebrew feature pharyngeals in their sound inventories.  阿拉伯语和希伯来语在其语音系统中包含咽音。|近义词：back sounds (a broader term), guttural sounds (often used interchangeably but can be slightly different)|反义词：front sounds (sounds produced in the front of the mouth)
