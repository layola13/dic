imitativeness: /ˌɪmɪˈteɪtɪvnəs/ | n. | 模仿性；模仿能力；模仿行为 | The quality or state of being imitative; the tendency or ability to imitate.  It refers to the act of copying or mimicking someone or something. This can manifest in various forms, from children learning language by repeating words to artists drawing inspiration from other works.  | Example: The child's drawings showed a remarkable imitativeness, closely resembling the style of their teacher.  孩子的绘画作品展现出非凡的模仿能力，非常接近老师的风格。 |近义词：copying, mimicry, replication | 反义词：originality, innovation |  名词
