death-toll: /ˈdɛθ ˌtoʊl/ | n. |死亡人数，死亡总数 | The number of deaths resulting from a particular cause or event.  It often refers to the casualties of a disaster, war, or epidemic. |例句：The death toll from the earthquake is expected to rise. 地震的死亡人数预计还会上升。

The death toll from the pandemic has been devastating. 这场大流行的死亡人数是毁灭性的。


近义词：casualty count, loss of life, fatalities |反义词：survival rate, number of survivors |词性：名词

词源:
"Death" comes from the Old English word "dēaþ," meaning "death."  "Toll" in this context refers to a count or reckoning, derived from the Middle English word "tol," meaning "a payment; a tax; a counting."  Thus, "death toll" literally means a "counting of deaths."
