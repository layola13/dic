sackville:/ˈsækˌvɪl/| n. |萨克维尔（人名，地名）|Sackville is a surname of English and French origin, and also the name of several places.  The name is derived from the Norman French place name "de Sackville," which ultimately comes from the Old English elements "sac" (meaning "sack" or "bag") and "ville" (meaning "town" or "village"). Therefore, the name likely referred to a town associated with sacks or possibly a fortified settlement.  It is also used as a place name, notably in New Brunswick, Canada.|The Sackvilles were a prominent noble family in England.  Many places are named Sackville, for example, Sackville, New Brunswick. |近义词: (as a surname)  similar sounding names; (as a place) other town names. |反义词:  N/A
