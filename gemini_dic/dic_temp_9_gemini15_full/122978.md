monadology:/ˌmɒnəˈdɒlədʒi/|/ˈmɑːnədɑːlədʒi/| n. |单子论；微粒论|The philosophical system of Leibniz, which holds that the universe consists of simple, indivisible substances called monads.  Leibniz's monadology posits that monads are the fundamental building blocks of reality, each possessing perception and appetite.  It is a metaphysical system that attempts to reconcile the mind and body problem, and addresses issues of determinism, free will, and the nature of reality.|The study of monadology is complex and requires a deep understanding of Leibniz's philosophical framework.|近义词：monism, metaphysics|反义词：pluralism
