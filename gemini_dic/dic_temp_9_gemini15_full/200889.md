slip-on: /ˈslɪpɑːn/ | n. | 便鞋，套鞋 | A shoe that is easy to slip on and off, typically without laces or fasteners.  | 例句：These slip-ons are perfect for a quick trip to the store. 这些便鞋很适合快速去商店一趟。

例句：I bought a pair of comfortable slip-ons for the summer. 我买了一双舒适的便鞋来过夏天。


近义词：loafer, moccasin | 反义词：lace-up shoe, boot


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He slips on his shoes every morning. 他每天早上都穿上他的鞋子。
She slips on her sandals before going to the beach. 她去海滩之前会穿上凉鞋。

一般过去时:
He slipped on his shoes and rushed out the door. 他穿上鞋子就冲出门外。
She slipped on her slippers after a long day at work.  下班后她换上了拖鞋。

现在完成时:
I have slipped on my running shoes and I'm ready to go. 我已经穿上了跑鞋，准备好了。
She has slipped on her new boots for the hiking trip. 为了这次徒步旅行，她穿上了她的新靴子。

过去完成时:
He had slipped on his warmest coat before leaving for the ski trip. 他在去滑雪旅行之前已经穿上了最暖和的大衣。
She had slipped on her pajamas before getting into bed. 她上床睡觉前已经穿上了睡衣。


将来时:
I will slip on my dancing shoes for the party tonight. 今晚我将穿上我的舞鞋参加派对。
She will slip on her rain boots before going outside in the storm.  暴风雨来临前她会穿上雨靴。


虚拟语气:
If I had a pair of slip-ons, I would wear them every day. 如果我有一双便鞋，我每天都会穿。
I wish I had slipped on my sneakers before the race started. 我真希望比赛开始前我穿上了运动鞋。


被动语态:
The shoes were easily slipped on. 这些鞋子很容易穿上。
Her new sandals were slipped on effortlessly. 她的新凉鞋穿起来毫不费力。


疑问句:
Have you slipped on your shoes yet? 你穿鞋了吗？
Will she slip on her coat before leaving? 她走之前会穿上外套吗？


否定句:
He didn't slip on his shoes properly, so they fell off. 他没有穿好鞋，所以鞋子掉了。
She didn't slip on her gloves before handling the hot dish.  她在处理热菜之前没有戴上手套。
