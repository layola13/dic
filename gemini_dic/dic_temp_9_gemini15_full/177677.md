recalibrating:/ˌriːˈkælibˌreɪtɪŋ/ | v. |重新校准；重新调整；重新评估 | To adjust or check the calibration of something again.  The process involves making small adjustments to ensure accuracy and precision.  This can apply to instruments, systems, or even one's own thinking or approach. | 例句：The scientist spent hours recalibrating the delicate instrument. 科学家花了数小时重新校准精密的仪器。

例句：The company is recalibrating its marketing strategy after a disappointing sales quarter.  公司在销售季度令人失望后正在重新调整其营销策略。


近义词： readjust, readjustment, recalculate, re-evaluate, refine


反义词： miscalibrate,  maladjust


词性：动词
