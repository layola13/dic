preexcited: /ˌpriːɪkˈsaɪtɪd/ | adj. | 预先兴奋的；预激的 |

英文解析:  Preexcited describes a state of being excited or stimulated before the usual or expected time or event. In a medical context, particularly cardiology, it refers to a condition where part of the heart's conduction system is activated prematurely, leading to altered heart rhythm.


例句:

The children were preexcited about the upcoming trip to the zoo.  孩子们对即将到来的动物园之旅感到兴奋不已。

The patient presented with preexcited atrial fibrillation. 病人出现了预激性房颤。


近义词:  stimulated, anticipatory, beforehand excited


反义词:  unexcited, calm, unanticipated


词性: adjective
