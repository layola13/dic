nonattachment: /ˌnɒnəˈtætʃmənt/ | n. |不执着；超然；无牵挂| The state of being free from attachment to material possessions or worldly concerns; detachment.  It often refers to a Buddhist concept of releasing the desire for material things and emotional attachments. |例句：Practicing nonattachment helped her overcome her grief.  练习不执着帮助她克服了悲伤。

例句：He achieved a state of nonattachment after years of meditation.  经过多年的冥想，他达到了一种超然的状态。


近义词：detachment, disengagement, unconcern, indifference |反义词：attachment, obsession, preoccupation, clinging


词源:  The word "nonattachment" is a combination of the prefix "non-" (meaning "not") and the noun "attachment" (meaning a connection or bond).  "Attachment" comes from the Old French "attachement," ultimately from the verb "attacher," meaning "to attach."
