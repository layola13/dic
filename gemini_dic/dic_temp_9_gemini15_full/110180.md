liers:/ˈlaɪərz/| n. |说谎者，骗子 |People who tell lies.  They are untruthful and deceptive. |Example: The liars were eventually caught and exposed. 说谎者最终被抓住并暴露了。|近义词：deceivers, fibbers, prevaricators |反义词：truth-tellers, honest people


USAGE->

一般现在时:
Liars often get caught in their own web of deceit. 说谎者经常陷入自己编织的谎言之网。
Those liars consistently mislead others.  那些说谎者总是误导他人。


一般过去时:
The liars were punished for their dishonesty. 说谎者因不诚实而受到惩罚。
The jury found the liars guilty of perjury. 陪审团认定说谎者犯有伪证罪。


现在完成时:
The liars have finally confessed their lies. 说谎者终于承认了他们的谎言。
She has been a liar all her life. 她一生都是个说谎者。


过去完成时:
The police had already identified the liars before the trial. 在审判之前，警方已经认出了说谎者。
I had suspected he was a liar long before he confessed. 在他坦白之前很久我就怀疑他是个骗子了。


将来时:
The liars will face the consequences of their actions. 说谎者将面临其行为的后果。
The truth will out; liars will be exposed.  真相总会大白；说谎者会被揭露。


虚拟语气:
