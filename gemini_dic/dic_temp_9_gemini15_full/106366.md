komatite:/kəˈmeɪtaɪt/| n. |科马提岩|A type of volcanic rock, very rich in magnesium and low in silica, characteristic of the Archean Eon.  Komatiites are named after the Komati River in South Africa, where they were first discovered.| Komatiites are exceptionally high-temperature volcanic rocks, often exhibiting spinifex texture.| The discovery of komatiites has provided valuable insights into the early Earth's mantle composition and geodynamic processes. |近义词：ultramafic volcanic rock|反义词：felsic volcanic rock
