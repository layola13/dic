refusal: /rɪˈfjuːzəl/ | n. | 拒绝；谢绝 | The act of refusing or declining something offered, proposed, or requested.  It implies a definite and often firm rejection. |例句：His refusal to cooperate hampered the investigation.  他的拒绝合作妨碍了调查。

例句：Her refusal of the offer was unexpected.  她拒绝了这个提议，这很出乎意料。

近义词：rejection, denial, declination, repudiation

反义词：acceptance, agreement, consent


USAGE->
一般现在时:
His refusal to help is disappointing. = 他拒绝帮忙令人失望。
Their refusal of the offer is final. = 他们拒绝该提议是最终决定。


一般过去时:
The company's refusal of the merger proposal surprised everyone. = 公司拒绝合并提案让每个人都感到惊讶。
My refusal to answer his questions led to further conflict. = 我拒绝回答他的问题导致了进一步的冲突。


现在完成时:
His repeated refusal to attend meetings has caused problems. = 他一再拒绝参加会议已经造成了问题。
Their refusal to compromise has stalled negotiations. = 他们拒绝妥协使谈判陷入僵局。


过去完成时:
His previous refusal to sign the contract made it impossible to proceed. = 他之前拒绝签署合同使得无法继续进行。
The team's refusal to accept defeat had inspired them to try again. = 球队拒绝接受失败激励他们再次尝试。


将来时:
His refusal to participate is likely to affect the outcome. = 他拒绝参与可能会影响结果。
We anticipate their refusal to sign the agreement. = 我们预料他们会拒绝签署协议。


虚拟语气:
If only he hadn't shown such a strong refusal to change his mind. = 如果他当时没有表现出如此强烈的拒绝改变主意就好了。
I wish there hadn't been such a swift refusal from the board. = 我真希望董事会没有如此迅速地拒绝。


被动语态:
His proposal was met with a swift refusal. = 他的提案遭到了迅速拒绝。
The request for funding was met with immediate refusal. = 资金申请受到了立即拒绝。


疑问句:

否定句:
