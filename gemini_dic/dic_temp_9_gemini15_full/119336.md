microburin:/ˈmaɪkroʊbʊrɪn/| n. | 微型细石器 | A small, carefully made stone tool, typically less than 2 centimeters long, produced by a specific technique of flint knapping.  Microburins are characterized by their tiny size and often have a notch or notches along one edge. They were used for a variety of purposes, depending on the culture and period. | The archaeologist discovered several microburins at the dig site, suggesting a sophisticated level of toolmaking.  考古学家在发掘现场发现了几个微型细石器，这表明存在着高超的工具制作水平。|  small stone tool, burin | large stone tool | 名词
