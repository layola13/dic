disadvantageous: /ˌdɪsˌædvənˈteɪdʒəs/ | adj. | 不利的；有害的；有缺点的 |  Unfavorable; harmful; presenting difficulties.  It implies a condition or circumstance that hinders progress, success, or well-being. |

例句：The mountainous terrain proved disadvantageous to the army's advance.  山区地形对军队的推进造成了不利。

例句：  His lack of experience was disadvantageous in the job interview.  他缺乏经验在面试中处于不利地位。


近义词：unfavorable, detrimental, harmful, undesirable, unhelpful

反义词：advantageous, beneficial, favorable, helpful, advantageous


词源：dis- (否定前缀) + advantageous (有利的)  The word "advantageous" itself comes from the Old French "avantageous," derived from "avantage" (advantage), ultimately from the Vulgar Latin "ad vantagio" (to advantage).

一般过去时:

现在完成时:

过去完成时:

虚拟语气:
