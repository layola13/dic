coecilian: /siːˈsɪliən/ | n. |蚓螈 |A limbless amphibian of the order Gymnophiona, resembling a worm or snake. Coecilians are found in tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. They are characterized by their smooth, moist skin, small eyes, and often a pointed snout.  They are primarily fossorial (burrowing) animals. |例句：The researchers discovered a new species of coecilian in the Amazon rainforest.  研究人员在亚马逊雨林中发现了一种新的蚓螈物种。
