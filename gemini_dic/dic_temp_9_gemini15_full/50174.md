deon:/ˈdiːɒn/|/ˈdiːɑːn/| n. |应尽的义务；应做的；责任|The word "deon" refers to something that is morally obligatory or a duty. It originates from the Greek word "deon," meaning "that which is binding," "necessary," or "fitting."  The concept is central to deontological ethics, a moral philosophy that emphasizes duties and rules rather than consequences.|Example:  The deon to care for one's children is widely accepted across cultures.  应尽的子女抚养义务在各种文化中都被广泛接受。|近义词：obligation, duty, responsibility, moral imperative|反义词：privilege, right, option
