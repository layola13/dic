irruptions:/ɪˈrʌpʃənz/| n.|突然爆发；入侵；闯入|Sudden, violent, or forceful outbursts or entries.  An irruption can refer to a sudden and often forceful appearance or outbreak,  like a volcanic eruption or a sudden influx of people. It often implies a disruptive or unwelcome nature.|例句：The irruptions of the volcano caused widespread devastation. 火山的爆发造成了大范围的破坏。

The sudden irruption of protesters disrupted the meeting. 抗议者的突然闯入扰乱了会议。|近义词：eruptions, outbursts, invasions, incursions|反义词：calm, peace, quiet, gradual entry


USAGE->

The volcano erupts regularly.  火山定期喷发。
Many people irrupt into the stadium. 很多人冲进体育场。


一般过去时:

过去完成时:

The rioters had irrupted into the streets before the curfew.  宵禁前暴徒已经涌上街头。

虚拟语气:
