pretraditional:/ˌpriːtræˈdɪʃənəl/| adj. |前传统的，先于传统的|  Relating to or characteristic of a period or style that precedes or anticipates traditional forms or conventions.  This word suggests something that came before a established tradition, often hinting at influences that shaped the later tradition. It implies a stage of development or a precursor to what would later be considered traditional.  |The pretraditional art of the region shows influences from neighboring cultures.  该地区的先于传统的艺术展现了来自周边文化的影响。| archaic, precursory, prototypical |traditional, conventional |
