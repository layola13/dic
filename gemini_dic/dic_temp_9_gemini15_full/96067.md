indecomposableness: /ˌɪndɪˌkɒmpəʊˈzeɪbəlnəs/ | n. |不可分解性，不可分割性 | The quality or state of being indecomposable;  inability to be broken down into simpler components.  This term is often used in mathematics, chemistry, and other scientific fields to describe something that cannot be further analyzed or separated into constituent parts. |
