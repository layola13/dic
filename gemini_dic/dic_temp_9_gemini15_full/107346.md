lame-duck: /leɪm dʌk/ | adj. n. | 跛脚的鸭子；（政治人物）即将离任的；失去影响力的 |

英文解析：A lame duck is an official (especially a politician) in the final period of their term of office, after their replacement has been elected but before they have left office.  The term implies a loss of power and influence. It can also refer to anything that has lost its effectiveness or is nearing the end of its usefulness.

例句：

The lame-duck president had little power to influence policy. =  这位即将离任的总统几乎没有能力影响政策。

The company, a lame duck after the merger, struggled to stay afloat. = 合并后，这家公司已经成为一个即将倒闭的公司，艰难地维持运转。


近义词：  ineffective, powerless, debilitated, spent force (for the noun form)  finishing, outgoing (for the adjective form describing a politician)

反义词： powerful, influential, effective, in power (for the noun form),  new, incoming (for the adjective form describing a politician)


词性：adjective, noun
