prefinancial:/ˌpriːfaɪˈnænʃəl/| adj. |金融之前的，金融业发展之前的|  Describing a period or state before the significant development of modern financial systems and institutions.  It often refers to economic activities and structures that existed before the widespread use of sophisticated financial instruments and markets.|例句：Prefinancial economies relied heavily on barter and direct exchange.  金融业发展之前的经济严重依赖物物交换和直接交易。

The term "prefinancial" is a relatively recent coinage, combining the prefix "pre-" (meaning "before") with "financial."  There's no deep historical etymology like with words that have existed for centuries. Its meaning is derived directly from the combination of its constituent parts.
