devil-may-care: /ˈdɛvəlmeɪkɛər/ | adj. | 漠不关心的；放荡不羁的；满不在乎的 | Recklessly unconcerned; indifferent to consequences.  It describes someone who behaves in a reckless and irresponsible way, showing a lack of concern for the consequences of their actions. | 例句：He had a devil-may-care attitude towards his studies. 他对学习采取了一种漠不关心的态度。

He drove with a devil-may-care abandon. 他开车时非常放荡不羁。


近义词：reckless, irresponsible, nonchalant, carefree, brazen, audacious | 反义词：cautious, careful, responsible, considerate, timid


词源:  The phrase is a combination of "devil" and "may care".  "Devil" here refers to the devil, a malevolent supernatural being, suggesting a lack of morality or fear of consequences. "May care" implies a lack of concern, as if one doesn't care what the devil thinks. The phrase's origin is uncertain, but it's believed to have emerged in the 19th century.
