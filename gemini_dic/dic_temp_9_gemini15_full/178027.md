reckoner:/ˈrɛkənər/| n. |计算者；估计者；考虑者| A person who calculates or estimates something.  It can also refer to something used for calculating or a person who considers or assesses something.|The accountant is a skilled reckoner of profits and losses. 会计师是精通计算损益的人。  He's a shrewd reckoner of the political landscape. 他是一位精明的政治局势评估者。|calculator, estimator, assessor | - | n.


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He reckons the cost of the project. 他计算项目的成本。
She reckons the journey will take three hours. 她估计旅程需要三个小时。

一般过去时:
They reckoned the total number of attendees. 他们计算了与会者的总数。
He reckoned without the opposition's strength. 他没有考虑到反对派的势力。


现在完成时:
She has reckoned the final score. 她已经计算出最终得分。
They have reckoned with the consequences of their actions. 他们已经承担了他们行为的后果。


过去完成时:
We had reckoned on a larger turnout. 我们原本预计会有更多人来。
She had reckoned without his unexpected intervention. 她没有料到他的意外介入。


将来时:
They will reckon the damages after the inspection. 他们将在检查后计算损失。
He will reckon with his critics eventually. 他最终将应对他的批评者。


虚拟语气:
If he reckoned correctly, the project would be completed on time. 如果他计算正确，项目就能按时完成。
I wish I had reckoned the risks beforehand. 我真希望我事先考虑过风险。


被动语态:
The cost was reckoned to be too high. 成本被认为太高了。
The damages are being reckoned by the insurance company. 保险公司正在计算损失。


疑问句:
How do you reckon the total amount? 你怎么计算总数？
Do you reckon he will agree? 你认为他会同意吗？


否定句:
I don't reckon he'll be there. 我认为他不会在那里。
She didn't reckon on such a strong response. 她没有料到如此强烈的反应。


PHRASE->

reckon without:  没有考虑到；忽略
reckon up:  计算；总结
to reckon something as:  把…看作
