rapiers:/ˈreɪpiərz/| n. |细剑；刺剑|A rapier is a slender, sharp-pointed sword, typically used for thrusting rather than cutting.  It was popular among swordsmen in the 16th and 17th centuries.  The word evokes images of elegance, skill, and dueling.| Example: The duelist gracefully wielded his rapier, a flash of steel in the dim light.  =  决斗者优雅地挥舞着他的细剑，在昏暗的灯光下闪过一丝寒光。 The museum displayed a collection of antique rapiers, each a testament to the artistry of its creation. = 博物馆展出了一批古董细剑，每一把都证明了其制作工艺的精湛。|近义词：foil, sword|反义词：broad sword,club
