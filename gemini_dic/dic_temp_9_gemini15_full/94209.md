imperfectly:/ɪmˈpɜːrfɪktli/ | adv. | 不完美地，有缺陷地 |  Adverb describing something done or existing not perfectly; lacking perfection or flawlessness.  It implies a degree of imperfection, not necessarily a complete failure. | Example: The painting was imperfectly executed, but still held a certain charm.  这幅画的完成不够完美，但仍然具有一定的魅力。 |近义词：faultily, incompletely, inadequately | 反义词：perfectly, flawlessly


USAGE->

He speaks English imperfectly. = 他英语说得不流利。
She plays the piano imperfectly. = 她钢琴弹得不太好。
