contriving:/kənˈtraɪvɪŋ/ | v. | 设计；发明；图谋；设法做到 |  To plan cleverly or skillfully; to invent or devise something in a clever way, often in a deceptive or underhanded manner; to manage to do something with difficulty. | 例句：He contrived a clever plan to escape. 他设计了一个巧妙的计划来逃跑。

She contrived to meet him secretly. 她设法秘密地会见了他。


近义词：devise, invent, scheme, plot, manage | 反义词：happen, chance, occur

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He contrives elaborate excuses. 他编造了复杂的借口。
They are contriving a way to solve the problem. 他们在想办法解决这个问题。


一般过去时:
She contrived a false alibi. 她编造了一个虚假的不在场证明。
He contrived to get the job done despite the difficulties.  尽管困难重重，他还是设法完成了这项工作。


现在完成时:
They have contrived to keep the secret for years. 他们多年来一直设法保守这个秘密。
She has contrived a successful business from scratch. 她白手起家，创办了一家成功的企业。


过去完成时:
He had contrived a way to cheat on the test before he even started. 他甚至在考试开始之前就已经想好作弊的方法了。
They had contrived to meet secretly several times before they were discovered. 他们在被发现之前已经秘密会面过好几次了。


将来时:
She will contrive a plan to win the competition. 她将想出一个计划来赢得比赛。
They will contrive to finish the project on time. 他们将设法按时完成项目。


虚拟语气:
If he had contrived a better plan, he might have succeeded. 如果他制定了一个更好的计划，他也许会成功。
I wish I had contrived to finish my work earlier. 我真希望我早点完成了工作。


被动语态:

否定句:
