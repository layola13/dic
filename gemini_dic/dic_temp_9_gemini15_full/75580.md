fuddy-duddies:/ˈfʌdiˌdʌdiz/| n. |守旧的人；古板的人；老顽固|A fuddy-duddy is a person who is old-fashioned and resistant to new ideas or ways of doing things. They are often seen as stuffy, inflexible, and out of touch with modern trends.  The term carries a slightly humorous or condescending tone. | Example: He's such a fuddy-duddy; he refuses to use email. 他真是个老顽固；他拒绝使用电子邮件。|近义词：old-fashioned person, stick-in-the-mud, reactionary, conservative, traditionalist |反义词：progressive, forward-thinking, modern, trendy


USAGE->
This word is primarily a noun,  so verb conjugations don't apply directly to it.  We can use it in various sentence structures to demonstrate its usage:

Simple Present:
He is a fuddy-duddy who clings to outdated traditions. = 他是个守旧的人，坚持过时的传统。
Many older people are wrongly labeled as fuddy-duddies. = 许多老年人被错误地贴上老顽固的标签。


Simple Past:
My grandfather was a fuddy-duddy, but he had a kind heart. = 我爷爷是个老顽固，但他有一颗善良的心。
She acted like a fuddy-duddy when she refused the new technology. = 当她拒绝新技术时，她表现得像个老顽固。


Present Perfect:
They have become fuddy-duddies in their old age. = 他们上了年纪后变得保守了。
I haven't met many fuddy-duddies recently. = 我最近没遇到几个老顽固。


Past Perfect:
Before his retirement, he hadn't been such a fuddy-duddy. = 在退休前，他并没有那么保守。
She had always been a fuddy-duddy, even in her youth. = 她年轻的时候就一直很保守。


Future Simple:
He will likely remain a fuddy-duddy until the day he dies. = 他很可能直到死的那一天都会保持保守。
They will probably become fuddy-duddies as they get older. = 随着年龄的增长，他们可能会变得保守。


Future Continuous:
They will be being fuddy-duddies, resisting all forms of change. = 他们将一直是保守派，抵制一切形式的改变。
He will be acting like a fuddy-duddy at the meeting. = 他将在会议上表现得像个老顽固。
