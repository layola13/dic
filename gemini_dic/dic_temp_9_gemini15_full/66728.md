exorbitate:/ɪɡˈzɔːrbɪteɪt/| v.| 昂贵；过高；过分； |To be excessive or unreasonable in cost or price.  It implies a far exceeding of what is considered normal or acceptable.|例句：The prices of the hotels in this area exorbitate. 这个地区的酒店价格过高。 The cost of the project exorbitated the budget.  这个项目的成本超出了预算。|近义词：excessive, extravagant, exorbitant, immoderate|反义词：reasonable, moderate, affordable


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The hotel prices exorbitate during peak season. = 在旅游旺季，酒店价格非常高。
His demands exorbitate the company's capabilities. = 他的要求超出了公司的能力。


一般过去时:
The cost of the renovation exorbitated the original budget. = 装修费用超过了最初的预算。
Their spending exorbitated their income significantly. = 他们的支出大大超过了他们的收入。


现在完成时:

过去完成时:

将来时:

The rising inflation will exorbitate the price of goods. = 持续上涨的通货膨胀将导致商品价格上涨。


虚拟语气:
