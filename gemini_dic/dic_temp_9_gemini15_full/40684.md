conservative:/kənˈsɜːrvətɪv/| adj.|保守的，传统的|  Relating to or supporting conservative principles.  Characterized by avoidance of risk or change; cautious. |例句：He holds conservative views on social issues. 他对社会问题持有保守的观点。|例句：She adopted a conservative investment strategy. 她采取了保守的投资策略。|近义词：traditional, traditionalist, conventional, orthodox |反义词：liberal, progressive, radical, avant-garde


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is conservative in his political views. = 他在政治观点上很保守。
She is a conservative dresser. = 她衣着保守。

一般过去时:
They were conservative in their spending habits. = 他们过去在消费习惯上很保守。
He remained conservative throughout his career. = 他在整个职业生涯中都保持着保守的态度。

现在完成时:
She has always been conservative in her approach to life. = 她一直以来对生活都采取保守的态度。
They have remained conservative despite recent changes. = 尽管最近发生了变化，他们仍然保持着保守的态度。

过去完成时:
He had been conservative all his life before he made that decision. = 在做出那个决定之前，他一生都非常保守。
She had always been conservative, so the change surprised everyone. = 她一直以来都很保守，所以这次改变让每个人都感到惊讶。

将来时:
They will likely remain conservative in their policies. = 他们的政策很可能仍然会保持保守。
She will continue to be conservative in her investments. = 她将继续在投资方面保持保守。

虚拟语气:
If he were conservative, he wouldn't have done that. = 如果他很保守，他就不会那样做了。
I wish she were more conservative in her spending. = 我希望她在花钱方面能更保守一些。

被动语态:
His views are considered conservative by many. = 他的观点被许多人认为是保守的。
The policy was described as conservative by the media. = 该政策被媒体描述为保守的。

疑问句:
Is he a conservative politician? = 他是一个保守的政治家吗？
Are her views considered conservative? = 她的观点被认为是保守的吗？

否定句:
He is not conservative at all. = 他一点也不保守。
Her style is not conservative; it’s quite avant-garde. = 她的风格并不保守；它相当前卫。
