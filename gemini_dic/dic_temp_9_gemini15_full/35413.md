co-ordinate: /koʊˈɔːrdɪneɪt/ | /kəʊˈɔːdɪneɪt/ | n., v. | 坐标；协调；同等重要的 |

英文解析:As a noun, it refers to one of a set of numbers used to locate a point on a graph or in space. As a verb, it means to bring the different parts of something into a consistent, harmonious, or effective relationship.  It can also mean to be of equal rank or importance.

例句:

Noun:
The coordinates of the point are (2, 3). = 该点的坐标是 (2, 3)。

The map showed the exact coordinates of the treasure. = 地图显示了宝藏的确切坐标。


Verb:
We need to coordinate our efforts to achieve the goal. = 我们需要协调我们的努力以实现目标。

The event was flawlessly coordinated. = 活动被完美地协调了。


近义词: (Noun) coordinate; (Verb) harmonize, synchronize, integrate, correlate

反义词: (Verb) disorganize, disconnect, clash


词性: noun, verb


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They coordinate their schedules carefully. = 他们仔细协调他们的日程安排。
The two departments coordinate their activities closely. = 这两个部门密切协调他们的活动。


一般过去时:
She coordinated the team's efforts. = 她协调了团队的努力。
The project was coordinated by a skilled manager. = 项目由一位熟练的经理协调。


现在完成时:
We have coordinated all the logistics for the conference. = 我们已经协调了会议的所有后勤工作。
They have successfully coordinated the launch of the new product. = 他们成功地协调了新产品的发布。


过去完成时:
They had coordinated their plans before the meeting. = 会议前他们已经协调了他们的计划。
She had already coordinated the details with the client. = 她已经与客户协调好了细节。


将来时:

虚拟语气:

被动语态:
The event was well-coordinated. = 活动组织得很好。

疑问句:

否定句:

词源:
The word "coordinate" comes from the Latin words "co-" (meaning "together") and "ordinatus" (meaning "ordered" or "arranged").  It reflects the idea of bringing things together in an organized way.
