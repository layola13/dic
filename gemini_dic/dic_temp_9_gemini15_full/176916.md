realignments:/ˌriːəˈlaɪnmənts/| n. |重新调整；重新排列；重新组合 | The process of changing the arrangement or structure of something, often to improve efficiency or effectiveness.  It can refer to adjustments in political alliances, business strategies, or any system requiring reorganization. |例句：The company underwent several realignments to improve its profitability. 公司进行了多次重组以提高盈利能力。

例句：The political realignments after the election resulted in a new governing coalition.  选举后的政治重组导致了一个新的执政联盟。


近义词：rearrangements, reorganizations, readjustments, restructurings | 反义词：disorganizations, disruptions
