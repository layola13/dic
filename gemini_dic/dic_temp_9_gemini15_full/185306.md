retrench:/rɪˈtrentʃ/| v.|紧缩；削减；退却；减少开支 |To reduce costs or spending, typically by reducing staff or services.  It can also mean to withdraw or retreat. |例句：The company had to retrench after losing a major contract. 公司在失去一个大合同后不得不紧缩开支。|例句：The army retrenched to a more defensible position.  军队退却到一个更易防守的位置。|近义词：cut back, reduce, economize, curtail, withdraw|反义词：expand, increase, enlarge


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The company retrenches every year to improve profitability. = 公司每年都削减开支以提高盈利能力。
He retrenches his spending on luxury goods. = 他减少了在奢侈品上的开支。


一般过去时:
They retrenched their operations after the market crash. = 市场崩盘后，他们缩减了业务规模。
The government retrenched its social programs due to budget cuts. = 政府由于预算削减而削减了社会福利项目。


现在完成时:
The company has retrenched significantly since the recession. = 自经济衰退以来，该公司已经大幅度地削减了开支。
We have retrenched our marketing budget to focus on more effective strategies. = 我们已经削减了营销预算，以专注于更有效的策略。


过去完成时:
The company had retrenched before the merger, hoping to attract a buyer. = 在合并之前，该公司已经进行了裁员，希望吸引买家。
They had retrenched their workforce significantly before the announcement. = 在宣布之前，他们已经大幅度地裁员了。


将来时:
The government will retrench spending on defense next year. = 政府明年将削减国防开支。
We will retrench our operations in the less profitable regions. = 我们将在盈利较低的地区缩减业务规模。


虚拟语气:
If the company hadn't retrenched, it might have gone bankrupt. = 如果公司没有削减开支，它可能已经破产了。
I wish we hadn't retrenched so drastically last year. = 我真希望我们去年没有那么大幅度地削减开支。


被动语态:
Spending was retrenched by the government to balance the budget. = 政府削减了开支以平衡预算。
The workforce was retrenched due to the economic downturn. = 由于经济低迷，员工被裁员了。


疑问句:
Will the company retrench this year? = 公司今年会裁员吗？
Has the government retrenched its spending on education? = 政府削减了教育经费吗？


否定句:
The company did not retrench despite the financial difficulties. = 尽管面临财务困难，公司并没有裁员。
They haven't retrenched their staff, even though profits are down. = 尽管利润下降，他们也没有裁员。
