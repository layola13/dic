rib:/rɪb/| n.|肋骨；排骨；加强筋；|A rib is one of the long, curved bones that are attached to the spine and form the rib cage.  It protects the heart and lungs.|例句：The doctor examined his broken rib. 医生检查了他断裂的肋骨。|例句：We had roast rib of beef for dinner. 晚饭我们吃了烤牛肉排骨。|近义词：costal bone|反义词：无


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He has twelve ribs on each side. = 他每侧有十二根肋骨。
The rib cage protects vital organs. = 肋骨保护着重要器官。

一般过去时:
He cracked a rib in the accident. = 他在事故中肋骨断了。
The surgeon repaired the broken rib. = 外科医生修复了断裂的肋骨。

现在完成时:
She has broken a rib playing sports. = 她运动时肋骨断了。
He has had recurring rib pain for months. = 他几个月来反复肋骨疼痛。

过去完成时:
He had already broken a rib before the fall. = 在摔倒之前，他已经摔断了肋骨。
The patient had injured her ribs in a previous accident. = 病人在之前的意外中伤到了肋骨。

将来时:
The doctor thinks he will break a rib if he continues to play. = 医生认为如果他继续比赛，他会肋骨断裂。
The athlete expects to have his ribs examined next week. = 运动员预计下周会检查他的肋骨。

虚拟语气:
If I had not worn my seatbelt, I might have broken a rib. = 如果我没有系安全带，我的肋骨可能会断。
I wish I hadn't injured my ribs last week. = 我真希望我上周没有伤到肋骨。


被动语态:
His ribs were examined by the doctor. = 他的肋骨被医生检查了。
The broken rib was successfully repaired. = 断裂的肋骨被成功修复了。


疑问句:

否定句:
