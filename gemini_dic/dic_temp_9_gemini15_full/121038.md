misconception:/ˌmɪskənˈsɛpʃən/| n. |误解，错误观念 |A misconception is a belief or understanding that is incorrect. It is a misunderstanding or a mistaken idea about something.  |Example:  The common misconception that all snakes are venomous is dangerous.  常见的误解是所有蛇都有毒，这是很危险的。|近义词：misunderstanding, delusion, fallacy |反义词：truth, reality, fact


USAGE->
一般现在时:
There is a widespread misconception about the benefits of sugar. = 对于糖的好处存在着普遍的误解。
Many people hold the misconception that exercise is only beneficial for weight loss. = 许多人存在着运动只对减肥有益的误解。

一般过去时:
His misconception about the project led to a costly mistake. = 他对项目的误解导致了一个代价高昂的错误。
The misconception that the earth was flat was prevalent for centuries. = 地球是平的这种误解曾经盛行几个世纪。


现在完成时:
The public's misconception about the issue has persisted for too long. = 公众对这个问题的误解已经持续太久了。
Scientists have worked hard to dispel the misconception that vaccines cause autism. = 科学家们一直在努力消除疫苗会导致自闭症的误解。


过去完成时:
Before the discovery, there had been a long-held misconception about the nature of the universe. = 在发现之前，人们长期以来对宇宙的本质存在误解。
I had a misconception about her character until I got to know her better. = 在我更好地了解她之前，我对她的性格存在误解。


将来时:
Future generations may hold misconceptions about our current technology. = 后代人可能会对我们目前的科技存在误解。
We hope to correct the misconception that will prevail next year. = 我们希望纠正明年将会盛行的误解。


虚拟语气:
If I hadn't had that misconception, I would have made a different decision. = 如果我没有那个误解，我会做出不同的决定。
I wish I hadn't had that misconception about him. = 我真希望我没有对他产生那种误解。


被动语态:
The misconception was quickly corrected by the professor. = 教授很快纠正了这个误解。
The misconception is often perpetuated by the media. = 这种误解常常被媒体所延续。


疑问句:
What is the most common misconception about this topic? = 关于这个话题最常见的误解是什么？
Is there a misconception that people are generally unaware of? = 人们普遍没有意识到某种误解吗？


否定句:
There is no misconception about the urgency of the situation. = 对局势的紧迫性没有任何误解。
She made it clear that there was no misconception about her intentions. = 她明确表示，对她意图没有任何误解。
