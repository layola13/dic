inflation:/ɪnˈfleɪʃən/ | n. |通货膨胀，膨胀 |A general increase in the prices of goods and services in an economy over a period of time.  When the price level rises, each unit of currency buys fewer goods and services. Consequently, inflation reflects a reduction in the purchasing power per unit of money – a loss of real value in the medium of exchange and unit of account within the economy. |例句：High inflation is eroding the value of savings.  高通货膨胀正在侵蚀储蓄的价值。

例句：The government is struggling to control inflation. 政府正在努力控制通货膨胀。


近义词：price increase, escalation, rise |反义词：deflation


词源:  From Middle French *inflation, from Latin inflāre "to inflate," from in- "in" + flāre "to blow."  The economic sense developed in the late 19th century.
