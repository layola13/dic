glissade:/ɡlɪˈsɑːd/ | n. v. | 滑行；滑跤；（舞蹈中的）滑步 | A glissade is a gliding movement, typically across a surface of snow or ice, or a sliding step in ballet.  It can also refer to a slip or fall. |例句：The skier executed a graceful glissade down the slope. 滑雪者优雅地在斜坡上滑行。

The dancer performed a series of elegant glissades across the stage. 舞者在舞台上表演了一系列优雅的滑步。


近义词：slide, glide, skid, slip | 反义词：climb, ascend


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He glissades down the hill with ease. = 他轻松地滑下山坡。
The ballerina glissades across the stage. = 芭蕾舞演员在舞台上滑行。

一般过去时:
She glissaded on the icy pavement and fell. = 她在一块结冰的人行道上滑倒了。
The children glissaded down the grassy slope. = 孩子们从草坡上滑了下去。

现在完成时:
The skier has glissaded down every run on the mountain. = 滑雪者已经滑下了山上的每一条雪道。
He has glissaded several times during this performance. = 在这次表演中他已经滑了几次了。

过去完成时:
Before the accident, he had glissaded down that slope many times. = 事故发生前，他多次滑下那个斜坡。
She had glissaded across the ice before she lost her balance. = 在她失去平衡之前，她已经在冰面上滑行了。


将来时:
They will glissade down the mountain tomorrow. = 他们明天将从山上滑下。
The ice skater will glissade across the rink with grace. = 花样滑冰运动员将优雅地滑过溜冰场。

虚拟语气:
If I were a better skier, I would glissade with more confidence. = 如果我滑雪技术更好，我会更有信心地滑行。
I wish I could glissade as gracefully as that professional. = 我希望我能像那个专业人士一样优雅地滑行。

被动语态:
The slope was glissaded down by many skiers that day. = 那天许多滑雪者从斜坡上滑下。 (This sentence is somewhat awkward, as "glissade" is less commonly used passively)
The ice was glissaded upon by several skaters. = 好几个滑冰者在冰面上滑行。(This sentence is also somewhat awkward)


疑问句:
Will you glissade down the hill with me? = 你要和我一起滑下山吗？
Did he glissade gracefully across the stage? = 他在舞台上优雅地滑行了吗？

否定句:
He didn't glissade down the slope; he skied. = 他没有从斜坡上滑下来；他是滑雪下来的。
She couldn't glissade on the uneven surface. = 她无法在不平坦的表面上滑行。


PHRASE->
a controlled glissade = 受控的滑行
a graceful glissade = 优雅的滑行
a sudden glissade = 突然的滑行
a dangerous glissade = 危险的滑行
a breathtaking glissade = 令人惊叹的滑行


词源:
From French *glisser* (to slide) + *-ade (a suffix indicating an action or process).  The word entered English through the world of ballet and winter sports.
