entailers: /ɪnˈteɪlər/ | n. | 蕴含者；推论者 |  A person or thing that entails something; something that implies or suggests something else.  Entailers are often used in the context of linguistic analysis, referring to words or phrases that logically imply the presence of another word or phrase. For example, "bought" entails "possessed" because if someone bought something, they then possess it. | The evidence entails a conclusion of guilt.  证据意味着有罪的结论。| implicators, indicators |  (No direct antonym, but consider words like "contradictors" or "refuters" as opposites in meaning) | n.
