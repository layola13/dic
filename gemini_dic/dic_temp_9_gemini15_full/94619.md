impregnators:/ɪmˈprɛɡneɪtərz/| n.|施胶剂；浸渍剂；防腐剂|Substances used to saturate or fill the pores of a material, often to improve its properties (e.g., waterproofing, strengthening, preserving).  They are commonly used in wood treatment, textiles, and concrete. |Example:  The wood was treated with impregnators to protect it from rot.  木材经过防腐剂处理，以防止腐烂。

The concrete was treated with special impregnators to increase its strength.混凝土经过特殊浸渍剂处理以提高其强度。|近义词：treating agents, saturants, preservatives|反义词：N/A

词源:
The word "impregnators" derives from the verb "impregnate," which comes from the Latin word "impraegnāre," meaning "to make pregnant."  The figurative extension of the meaning refers to saturating or filling something thoroughly, similar to how a woman becomes pregnant.
