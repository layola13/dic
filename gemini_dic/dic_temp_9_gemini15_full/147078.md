overliberalization: /ˌoʊvərˌlɪbərəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. |过度自由化 | The excessive relaxation of government controls or regulations, often leading to negative consequences.  This term implies that the liberalization process has gone too far, resulting in undesirable outcomes such as market instability, social disruption, or environmental damage. | Example: The overliberalization of the financial markets led to the 2008 financial crisis.  过度自由化的金融市场导致了2008年的金融危机。 The overliberalization of the fishing industry has depleted fish stocks in many areas.  捕捞业的过度自由化已导致许多地区的鱼类资源枯竭。 | 近义词: deregulation, excessive liberalization, laissez-faire excesses | 反义词: strict regulation, control, restriction | 名词
