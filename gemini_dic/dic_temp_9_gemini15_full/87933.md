hieroscopy:/haɪəˈrɒskəpi/| n. |圣物瞻仰；圣物显灵|The study or practice of interpreting sacred objects or images, often for religious or divinatory purposes.  It involves a careful examination of the object's physical characteristics, symbolic meaning, and historical context to uncover hidden knowledge or divine messages.  |Example: The hieroscopy of the ancient Egyptian scarab beetles revealed much about their religious beliefs.|近义词：iconography,  iconology|反义词：暂无
