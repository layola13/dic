reprimands:/ˈrɛprɪmændz/ | n. v. | 斥责；训斥；谴责 |  A reprimand is a formal expression of disapproval.  It can be a noun referring to the act of reprimanding or the reprimand itself, or a verb meaning to reprimand someone. |

例句：

The teacher reprimanded the student for talking in class. = 老师因学生在课堂上讲话而训斥了他。

She received a written reprimand for her unprofessional conduct. = 由于她的不专业行为，她受到了书面警告。


近义词：rebuke, reproach, scold, admonish, chastise

反义词：praise, commend, applaud


词源:

The word "reprimand" comes from the Old French word "reprimande," which in turn derives from the verb "reprendre," meaning "to retake" or "to reprove."  The prefix "re-" indicates repetition or intensification, while the root "-prendre" relates to seizing or taking hold of something, in this case, taking hold of someone's attention to correct their behavior.
