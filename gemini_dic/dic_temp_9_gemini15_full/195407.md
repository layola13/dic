seminality: /ˌsɛmɪˈnælɪti/ | n. | 重要性；开创性；开端；精液 | The quality or state of being seminal; the power or influence to be seminal.  It refers to something that is highly influential and original, often serving as a foundation or starting point for future developments.  |例句：The discovery of penicillin was of immense seminality in the field of medicine.  青霉素的发现对医学领域具有极其重要的开创性意义。

The novel's seminality lies in its exploration of complex social issues. 这部小说的重要性在于它对复杂社会问题的探讨。| 近义词：significance, importance, originality, innovativeness, ground-breakingness | 反义词：unimportance, insignificance, triviality


词源:

The word "seminality" is derived from the Latin word "semen," meaning "seed."  This connection highlights the idea of something acting as a seed or origin point for subsequent growth and development. The suffix "-ality" transforms the noun "semen" into an abstract noun denoting a quality or state.
