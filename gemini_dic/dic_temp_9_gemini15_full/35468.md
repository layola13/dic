coaches:/ˈkoʊtʃɪz/ | n. |教练们，辅导员们 |  The word "coaches" is the plural form of "coach," referring to individuals who instruct, train, or guide others, typically in sports, academics, or other areas.  It implies a mentoring and skill-development role. |例句：The coaches are reviewing the game footage. 教练们正在回顾比赛录像。

The basketball coaches devised a new strategy. 篮球教练们制定了一个新的策略。


近义词：trainers, instructors, mentors, tutors | 反义词：students, trainees (反义词相对而言，并非完全反义) | 词性:名词


WORD ORIGIN->

The word "coach" originates from the Hungarian word "kocsi," referring to a type of carriage.  In the 16th century, these carriages were used to transport people, and the term evolved to refer to those who drove or guided these carriages.  Later, the term came to be used for those who guided or trained others, particularly in sports.
