choreograph:/kɔːriˈɒɡræf/| v.|编舞，设计舞蹈动作|To plan and arrange the movements of dancers or performers in a ballet, play, or other performance.|The choreographer created a stunning dance sequence for the musical.|近义词：compose, arrange, devise|反义词：improvise


USAGE->
一般现在时:
She choreographs the dance routines for the school's annual show. = 她为学校的年度演出编排舞蹈。
The director choreographs the movements of the actors on stage. = 导演编排演员在舞台上的动作。


一般过去时:
He choreographed the ballet that won the award. = 他编排了获得奖项的芭蕾舞剧。
The famous dancer choreographed the performance last year. = 这位著名的舞者去年编排了这场演出。


现在完成时:
She has choreographed several successful Broadway musicals. = 她已经为几部成功的百老汇音乐剧编排了舞蹈。
They have choreographed the dance for the wedding. = 他们已经为婚礼编排了舞蹈。


过去完成时:
The choreographer had already choreographed the opening scene before the rehearsal. = 编舞师在排练之前已经编排好了开场场景。
Before the show, she had choreographed all the dances for the cast. = 演出前，她已经为全体演员编排了所有的舞蹈。


将来时:
He will choreograph a new ballet next year. = 他明年将编排一部新的芭蕾舞剧。
The company will choreograph a contemporary dance piece for the festival. = 这家公司将为节日编排一个当代舞蹈作品。


虚拟语气:
If I were a choreographer, I would choreograph a dance about nature. = 如果我是一个编舞师，我会编排一个关于自然的舞蹈。
I wish I could choreograph a dance as beautiful as that one. = 我希望我能编排出一个像那样的美丽的舞蹈。


被动语态:
The dance was choreographed by a renowned artist. = 这支舞蹈是由一位著名的艺术家编排的。
The play's movements were choreographed with great precision. = 这部剧的动作编排得非常精确。


疑问句:
Who choreographed this amazing dance? = 谁编排了这支令人惊叹的舞蹈？
Will you choreograph the dance for the competition? = 你会为比赛编排舞蹈吗？


否定句:
He didn't choreograph the dance; he only assisted. = 他并没有编排舞蹈；他只是协助。
She doesn't choreograph often these days. = 她现在不经常编舞了。


PHRASE->
choreograph a dance = 编排一支舞
choreograph a scene = 编排一个场景
choreograph a performance = 编排一场演出
choreograph movements = 编排动作
choreograph a routine = 编排一套程序


词源:
The word "choreograph" comes from the French word "chorégraphie," which itself is derived from the Greek words "choros" (χορός), meaning "dance," and "graphein" (γράφειν), meaning "to write."  Therefore, the word literally means "to write dance."
