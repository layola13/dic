in-love: /ˌɪnˈlʌv/ | adj. |恋爱中的，相爱的|  Being in a state of romantic love; deeply affectionate towards someone. |例句：They are deeply in love and plan to marry soon. 他们热恋中，并计划很快结婚。 |例句：She's been in love with him since college. 她从大学时代就爱着他。 |近义词： enamored, smitten, lovestruck |反义词：unloved, loveless


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They are in love. = 他们相爱。
We are deeply in love. = 我们深深相爱。


一般过去时:
They were in love. = 他们曾经相爱。
We were in love, but it didn't last. = 我们曾经相爱，但没有持续下去。


现在完成时:
They have been in love for five years. = 他们相爱五年了。
We have been in love since high school. = 我们从高中时代就相爱了。


过去完成时:
They had been in love for a long time before they got married. = 他们在结婚前已经相爱很久了。
We had been in love, but we broke up. = 我们曾经相爱，但我们分手了。


将来时:
They will still be in love next year. = 明年他们还会相爱。
We will always be in love. = 我们会永远相爱。


虚拟语气:
If they were in love, they would be happier. = 如果他们相爱，他们会更快乐。
I wish I were in love. = 我希望我能恋爱。


被动语态:  (The adjective "in love" doesn't typically take a passive voice.)


疑问句:
Are they in love? = 他们相爱吗？
Were they in love? = 他们曾经相爱吗？


否定句:
They are not in love. = 他们并不相爱。
They were not in love at the time. = 当时他们并不相爱。
