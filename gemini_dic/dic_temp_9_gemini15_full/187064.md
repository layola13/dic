river:/ˈrɪvər/| n. |河流|A natural stream of water of fairly large size flowing in a definite course.  Rivers are important sources of fresh water, transportation routes, and habitats for various species. They are formed by the accumulation of water from rainfall, melting snow, and groundwater.  The course of a river can be influenced by factors like geology, topography, and climate. |例句：The Amazon River is the largest river in the world by volume. 亚马逊河是世界上按水量计算最大的河流。

The Mississippi River flows through several states. 密西西比河流经多个州。


近义词：stream, creek (smaller), current

反义词：None (Antonyms are not directly applicable, but one might contrast "river" with "land" or "ocean" depending on the context)

词性:名词
