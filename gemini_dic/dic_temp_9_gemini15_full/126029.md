mussolini:/muːsəˈliːni/| n. |墨索里尼| Benito Mussolini (1883-1945), the Italian dictator who ruled from 1922 to 1943.  He was a fascist leader known for his aggressive foreign policy and brutal suppression of opposition. His regime was characterized by authoritarianism, militarism, and the promotion of a totalitarian state.  He formed alliances with Adolf Hitler and played a significant role in World War II.  He was eventually overthrown and executed.|Example: Mussolini's fascist regime brought Italy into World War II.|近义词: dictator, fascist, autocrat|反义词: democrat, liberal
