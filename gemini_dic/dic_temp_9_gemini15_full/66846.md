expect:/ɪkˈspekt/ | v. | 预料；期待；期望 | to think or believe that something will happen or that something will be true | We expect the results next week. 我们预计下周得到结果。  I expect him to arrive soon. 我预计他很快就会到。 | anticipate, foresee, await | disappoint, fail | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I expect a lot of visitors this weekend. = 我预计这个周末会有很多访客。
They expect to finish the project by Friday. = 他们预计在星期五之前完成项目。


一般过去时:
I expected it to rain, but it didn't. = 我预料会下雨，但是没下。
She expected a better grade on her test. = 她期盼在考试中获得更好的分数。


现在完成时:
I have been expecting your call all day. = 我一整天都在等着你的电话。
We have expected this outcome for some time. = 我们已经期待这个结果有一段时间了。


过去完成时:
I had expected him to be there, but he wasn't. = 我原以为他会在那儿，但他没来。
They had expected the meeting to be shorter. = 他们原以为会议会短一些。


将来时:
I expect to be there by 6 pm. = 我预计下午六点之前到达那里。
We expect the work to be completed next month. = 我们预计这项工作将在下个月完成。


虚拟语气:
I would expect you to be more careful. = 我希望你会更小心。
I expect he would have told me if he knew. = 我想如果他知道的话他会告诉我的。


被动语态:
The results are expected to be announced soon. = 预计结果很快就会公布。
He is expected to attend the conference. = 预计他会参加会议。


疑问句:
Do you expect to see him soon? = 你预计很快就会见到他吗？
What do you expect me to do? = 你期望我做什么？


否定句:
I don't expect any problems. = 我预计不会有任何问题。
She didn't expect him to be so angry. = 她没料到他会这么生气。


PHRASE->
expect the best = 抱最好的希望
expect nothing less = 毫不意外
expectantly = 期待地
to be expected = 预料之中
as expected = 正如预期
