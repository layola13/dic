debunk:/dɪˈbʌŋk/ | vt. |揭穿；戳穿；驳斥 | To expose the falseness or hollowness of (a claim, belief, etc.); to disprove.  | Example:  The scientist debunked the myth that the earth is flat.  科学家揭穿了地球是平的这一谬论。

Example:  He debunked the conspiracy theories surrounding the president's death. 他揭穿了围绕总统死因的阴谋论。 | 近义词：expose, refute, disprove, discredit | 反义词：confirm, corroborate, substantiate, endorse


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He debunks pseudoscience regularly. = 他定期揭穿伪科学。
Scientists debunk myths about health. = 科学家揭穿关于健康的谬误。


一般过去时:
She debunked the politician's false promises. = 她揭穿了这位政客的虚假承诺。
The journalist debunked the story as a hoax. = 记者揭穿了这个故事是一个骗局。


现在完成时:
He has debunked many conspiracy theories over the years. = 多年来，他已经揭穿了许多阴谋论。
Researchers have debunked the idea that vaccines cause autism. = 研究人员已经揭穿了疫苗会导致自闭症的观点。


过去完成时:
They had debunked the fraud before the police arrived. = 警方到达之前，他们就已经揭穿了这个骗局。
She had debunked the rumors before they spread too far. = 在谣言传播得太广之前，她就已经揭穿了它们。


将来时:
He will debunk the latest internet craze. = 他将揭穿最新的互联网热潮。
Scientists will debunk the false claims about the new drug. = 科学家将揭穿关于这种新药的虚假说法。


虚拟语气:
If he had evidence, he would debunk the claim. = 如果他有证据，他就会揭穿这一说法。
I wish I could debunk all the misinformation online. = 我希望我能揭穿网上所有错误信息。


被动语态:
The theory was debunked by a recent study. = 最近的一项研究揭穿了这一理论。
His claims were quickly debunked by experts. = 他的说法很快就被专家们揭穿了。


疑问句:
Can we debunk this widespread misconception? = 我们能揭穿这种普遍的误解吗？
Will the new evidence debunk the existing theory? = 新证据会推翻现有的理论吗？


否定句:
He couldn't debunk the rumor despite his efforts. = 尽管他努力了，但他还是无法揭穿这个谣言。
They failed to debunk the conspiracy theory completely. = 他们未能完全揭穿这个阴谋论。


PHRASE->
debunk a myth = 揭穿一个神话
debunk a conspiracy theory = 揭穿一个阴谋论
debunk a claim = 驳斥一个说法
debunk false information = 揭穿虚假信息
debunk a hoax = 揭穿一个骗局


词源:
The word "debunk" is a blend of "de-" (meaning "to remove" or "to deprive of") and "bunk," meaning "nonsense" or "humbug."  It emerged in the early 20th century.
