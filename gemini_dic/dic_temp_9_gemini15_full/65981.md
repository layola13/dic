exaltate:/ɪɡˈzɔːlteɪt/ | vt. | 提升；赞扬；使得意；使兴奋 | To raise to a higher rank, power, or dignity; to praise highly; to elate; to make joyful. |例句：The king exalted his loyal knight. 国王提升了他忠诚的骑士。

The critics exalted the novel as a masterpiece. 批评家们高度赞扬这部小说是一部杰作。


近义词：elevate, uplift, praise, glorify | 反义词：debase, degrade, humiliate, depress


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He exalts his friends whenever possible. 他尽可能地赞扬他的朋友们。
The company exalts its products through effective advertising. 公司通过有效的广告宣传来提升其产品。


一般过去时:
The general exalted his soldiers after the victory. 战斗胜利后，将军赞扬了他的士兵们。
She exalted in her success, feeling a surge of joy. 她为自己的成功而兴奋，感到一阵喜悦。


现在完成时:
They have exalted him to the position of CEO. 他们已将他提升为首席执行官。
The media has exalted her achievements in the field of science. 媒体已经高度赞扬她在科学领域的成就。


过去完成时:
He had exalted her above all others, making her his favorite. 他把她提升到高于所有人的地位，让她成为他的最爱。
The people had exalted their leader, believing in his promises. 人民曾高度赞扬他们的领导人，相信他的承诺。


将来时:
They will exalt the new champion at the ceremony. 他们将在仪式上表彰新的冠军。
The church will exalt God through prayer and song. 教堂将通过祈祷和歌曲来赞美上帝。


虚拟语气:
If I were to exalt myself, I would be perceived as arrogant. 如果我自吹自擂，人们会认为我很傲慢。
I wish I could exalt the beauty of nature in my paintings. 我希望能够在我的画作中展现大自然的美丽。


被动语态:
He was exalted to a position of great power. 他被提升到一个拥有巨大权力的职位。
Her work was exalted by critics and the public alike. 她的作品受到了评论家和公众的一致好评。


疑问句:
Will they exalt the winner of the competition? 他们会表彰比赛的获胜者吗？
Does this poem exalt the virtues of humanity? 这首诗歌是否赞扬了人类的美德？


否定句:
He did not exalt himself despite his accomplishments. 尽管取得了成就，但他并没有自吹自擂。
The article did not exalt the company's new product. 这篇文章并没有赞扬该公司的新品。
