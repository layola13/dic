rhyme:/raɪm/ | n. | 韵，押韵；押韵诗 | The correspondence of sounds in words or the endings of words, especially when these are used at the ends of lines of a poem.  | Example: The poem has a perfect rhyme scheme of AABB. 这首诗有完美的AABB韵律。 |近义词：rhythm, verse | 反义词：prose


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The words "cat" and "hat" rhyme. = "cat"和"hat"押韵。
This poem rhymes perfectly. = 这首诗押韵很完美。


一般过去时:
The children rhymed words during the game. = 孩子们在游戏中押韵。
The poet rhymed the final two lines. = 诗人让最后两行押韵。


现在完成时:
She has rhymed several poems this year. = 她今年已经写了几首押韵的诗。
They have been rhyming words since childhood. = 他们从小就一直在玩押韵游戏。


过去完成时:
He had already rhymed the first stanza before he started the second. = 他在开始写第二节之前已经完成了第一节的押韵。
We had never rhymed words before that class. = 在那节课之前我们从未玩过押韵游戏。


将来时:
I will rhyme the last two lines with “sun” and “fun”. = 我将用“sun”和“fun”来押最后两行的韵。
The students will be rhyming words in their next English lesson. = 学生们将在下一节英语课上练习押韵。


虚拟语气:
If I could rhyme, I would write poetry. = 如果我会押韵，我会写诗。
I wish I could rhyme as well as Shakespeare. = 我希望我的押韵功力能像莎士比亚一样好。


被动语态:
The poem was rhymed beautifully. = 这首诗押韵得很美。
The words are often rhymed in children's songs. = 儿童歌曲中经常使用押韵的词语。


疑问句:
Do these two words rhyme? = 这两个词押韵吗？
Does the poem rhyme consistently? = 这首诗的押韵是否一致？


否定句:
These words don't rhyme. = 这些词不押韵。
The poem doesn't rhyme well. = 这首诗押韵不好。
