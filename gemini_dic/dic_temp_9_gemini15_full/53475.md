disarrangement:/ˌdɪsəˈreɪndʒmənt/ | n. |混乱，杂乱；扰乱 |The state of being disordered or disorganized; a disruption or derangement.  It often refers to a state of things, not necessarily a planned action. |例句：The disarrangement of the files made it difficult to find the necessary documents. 文件的混乱使得难以找到必要的文档。

例句：The sudden disarrangement of her schedule caused considerable stress. 她日程的突然被打乱造成了相当大的压力。


近义词：disorder, confusion, chaos, disorganization |反义词：order, organization, arrangement


词源：dis- (否定前缀) + arrangement (安排，排列)  The prefix "dis-" negates the meaning of "arrangement," indicating a lack of order or organization.
