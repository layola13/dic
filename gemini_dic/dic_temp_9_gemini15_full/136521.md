nonrudimentary: /ˌnɒnˈruːdɪmɛntəri/ | adj. |非基本的；非初级的；发达的|

英文解析:Not rudimentary; developed beyond a basic or elementary stage.  Implying a level of complexity or sophistication beyond the simplest form.


例句:

The nonrudimentary understanding of quantum physics required years of study.  = 对量子物理学的非基本理解需要多年的学习。

His nonrudimentary grasp of the subject impressed the professor. = 他对该学科的非初级掌握给教授留下了深刻的印象。


近义词: advanced, sophisticated, complex, developed, mature, refined


反义词: rudimentary, basic, elementary, primitive, undeveloped


词源:

* **non-**:  prefix meaning "not" or "opposite of"
* **rudimentary**:  adjective meaning "relating to or being in the earliest stages of development; basic; undeveloped". This in turn derives from the Latin word *rudimentum*, meaning "a first principle; a beginning; a rudiment".
