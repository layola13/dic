low-rises:/ˈloʊˌraɪzɪz/| n. |低层建筑|Low-rises refer to buildings that are relatively short in height, typically having only a few stories.  They are the opposite of high-rises.  They are often found in residential areas and can offer a more intimate and community-oriented living environment. |例句：The neighborhood is characterized by charming low-rises and tree-lined streets.  这个街区以迷人的低层建筑和绿树成荫的街道为特色。

例句：Many people prefer the quieter atmosphere of low-rises compared to the hustle and bustle of high-rise apartments. 许多人更喜欢低层建筑比高层公寓更安静的气氛。|近义词：short buildings, multi-story buildings (if they have more than one story) |反义词：high-rises, skyscrapers
