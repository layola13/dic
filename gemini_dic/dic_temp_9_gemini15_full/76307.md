gabbys:/ˈɡæbiːz/ | adj. |喋喋不休的；饶舌的 |  Characterized by excessive talkativeness; loquacious.  |例句：She became quite gabby after a few glasses of wine.  她喝了几杯酒后变得非常喋喋不休。|近义词：talkative, loquacious, voluble, garrulous |反义词：taciturn, reticent, reserved |形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is always gabby at parties. = 他在聚会上总是喋喋不休。
She's usually quite gabby, but today she's quiet. = 她通常很健谈，但今天她很安静。


一般过去时:
He was so gabby last night. = 他昨晚话真多。

He has become increasingly gabby in his old age. = 他上了年纪后变得越来越唠叨了。


过去完成时:

She had been gabby for hours before she finally ran out of breath. = 她喋喋不休地说了几个小时才终于说累了。


将来时:

虚拟语气:

被动语态:
