cash-in-your-chips:/kæʃ ɪn jɔːr tʃɪps/| phrasal verb|兑现筹码；孤注一掷；放弃努力|To use all of one's resources or remaining assets in a final attempt to succeed, often in a risky or desperate manner.  It implies a high-stakes situation where one is putting everything on the line.|例句：He cashed in his chips and invested all his savings in the startup. 他孤注一掷，把所有积蓄都投资到那家初创公司了。|例句：After years of struggling, she decided to cash in her chips and try a new career.  经过多年的奋斗，她决定孤注一掷，尝试一个新的职业。|近义词：go for broke, stake everything, bet the farm |反义词：play it safe, hold back, conserve resources|
