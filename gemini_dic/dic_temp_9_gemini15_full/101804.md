irritability:/ˌɪrɪtəˈbɪləti/ | n. |易怒；烦躁；过敏性 |The state of being easily irritated or annoyed; a tendency to become angry or upset quickly.  It can also refer to the state of an organ or system being abnormally sensitive to stimuli.|例句：His irritability was a symptom of his underlying illness. 他的易怒是潜在疾病的症状。

例句：The patient's irritability increased after the surgery.  手术后，病人的烦躁加剧了。


近义词：irritableness, choler, irascibility, testiness, touchiness |反义词：calmness, serenity, placidity, equanimity


词源:  Derived from the adjective "irritable," which comes from the verb "irritate" (to annoy or provoke).  "Irritate" ultimately stems from the Latin word "irritare," meaning "to provoke," "to excite," or "to irritate."  The suffix "-ility" denotes a state or quality.
