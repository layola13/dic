preconfiguring: /ˌpriːkənˈfɪɡəriŋ/ | v. | 预先配置；预先设定 |  The act of configuring something beforehand, setting parameters or options in advance. This often involves setting up software, hardware, or systems before they are put into use.  | Example:  The technician was preconfiguring the network settings before the server launch. = 技术人员在服务器启动之前预先配置网络设置。


The system administrator preconfigured the user accounts to ensure a smooth rollout. = 系统管理员预先配置用户帐户以确保顺利推出。


近义词:  presetting, setting up in advance, initializing

反义词: post-configuring, configuring after use


词性: 动词

词源:  The word "preconfiguring" is a combination of the prefix "pre-" (meaning "before") and the verb "configuring" (from "configure," meaning "to arrange or set up").  Therefore, it literally means "to configure beforehand."
