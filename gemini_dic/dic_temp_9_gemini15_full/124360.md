motiv: /ˈmoʊtɪv/ | n. | 动机，目的 |  A reason for doing something, especially one that is hidden or not obvious.  |  例句：His motive for committing the crime remains unclear.  他的犯罪动机仍然不明朗。

例句：The company's motive in sponsoring the event was to increase brand awareness. 这家公司赞助该活动的动机是为了提高品牌知名度。


近义词：incentive, reason, impulse, drive, purpose | 反义词：disincentive


词源:  From Latin *motīvus (“moving”), from *movēre (“to move”).  The word ultimately derives from Proto-Indo-European *meue- (“to move”).
