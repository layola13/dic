nonius:/ˈnoʊniəs/| n. |游标，游标尺|A nonius is a scale, usually a sliding scale, used in conjunction with a main scale to make more precise measurements.  It works by subdividing the smallest divisions of the main scale, thus allowing for readings to a fraction of the main scale's smallest unit.  The nonius is a precursor to the vernier scale, which is more commonly used today. |The surveyor used a nonius to accurately measure the distance between two points.  The old micrometer used a nonius for precise measurements.| 近义词：vernier scale | 反义词：无
