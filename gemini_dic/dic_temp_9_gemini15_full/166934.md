prestimulus: /ˌpriːˈstɪmjʊləs/ | adj. | 刺激前的；预刺激的 |  Relating to the period or conditions before the application of a stimulus.  This term is primarily used in scientific contexts, particularly in psychology and neuroscience, to describe the state of a system or organism before an experimental stimulus is introduced.  It helps researchers to establish a baseline against which to measure the effects of the stimulus. | Example: The prestimulus baseline activity of the neurons was carefully monitored. 刺激前的基线神经元活动被仔细监测。|近义词: antecedent, prior, preceding | 反义词: poststimulus | 词性: adjective
