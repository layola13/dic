procoercion:/ˌproʊkɔːˈɜːrʒən/| n. |强行劝诱；胁迫；强迫|The act of persuading someone to do something by using force or threats.  It's a less common word suggesting a more subtle form of coercion, often involving manipulation or undue influence.|例句：The sales tactics bordered on procoercion, as the customer felt pressured into buying the expensive product.  销售策略近乎于强行劝诱，因为顾客感到被迫购买昂贵的产品。

例句：The government's procoercion of the media to support its policies was met with widespread criticism. 政府强迫媒体支持其政策的做法受到了广泛批评。|近义词：coercion, duress, pressure, intimidation, strong-arming|反义词：persuasion, inducement, encouragement|名词
