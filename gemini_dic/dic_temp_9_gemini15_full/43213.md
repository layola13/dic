crack-up: /ˈkræk ʌp/ | n., v. |崩溃；垮掉；事故；大笑 |

英文解析:As a noun, "crack-up" refers to a complete failure or breakdown, either physical or mental. It can also describe a serious accident, especially a vehicle crash. As a verb, it means to suffer a mental breakdown or to cause something to break down.  It can also mean to laugh uncontrollably.

例句:

n. 崩溃：The company experienced a complete crack-up after the CEO's resignation.  公司在首席执行官辞职后彻底崩溃了。

n. 事故：The crack-up involved three vehicles and resulted in several injuries. 这起事故涉及三辆车，造成多人受伤。


v. 崩溃：He had a nervous crack-up after years of stress.  多年压力之后，他精神崩溃了。

v. 大笑：The comedian's jokes had the audience in stitches; it was a real crack-up.  喜剧演员的笑话让观众捧腹大笑；真是太搞笑了。


近义词: (n.) breakdown, collapse, crash, accident; (v.) break down, collapse, go crazy, burst out laughing


反义词: (n.) success, stability, triumph; (v.) succeed, stabilize, recover


词性: noun, verb
