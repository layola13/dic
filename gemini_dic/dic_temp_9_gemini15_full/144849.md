over-officious:/ˌoʊvər əˈfɪʃəs/| adj.| 多管闲事的；过于殷勤的；过分热心的 | Excessively eager to help or offer advice; interfering excessively.  |例句：His over-officious manner annoyed everyone.  他的多管闲事惹恼了所有人。

例句：The over-officious waiter kept interrupting our conversation.  那个过分殷勤的服务员一直打断我们的谈话。


近义词：meddlesome, interfering, officious, intrusive, busybody

反义词：unhelpful, indifferent, apathetic, unconcerned


词源:  The word "over-officious" is a combination of "over-" (meaning excessively or too much) and "officious" (meaning excessively eager to help or offer advice; intrusive).  "Officious" itself derives from the Late Latin *officiosus, from officium ("duty, service").
