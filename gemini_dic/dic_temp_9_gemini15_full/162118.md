pre-describe:/ˌpriːdɪˈskraɪb/| v.|预先描述，事先说明 |To describe something before it happens or is fully known.  It implies a prediction or anticipation of future events or characteristics.|例句：The architect pre-described the building's features in the blueprints. 建筑师在蓝图中预先描述了建筑物的特征。


例句：The scientist pre-described the likely outcome of the experiment. 科学家预先描述了实验可能的结果。


近义词：foretell, predict, forecast, anticipate


反义词：post-describe (Not a standard word)


词性:动词
