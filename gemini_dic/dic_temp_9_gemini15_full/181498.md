rejuvenated:/rɪˈdʒuːvənˌeɪtɪd/ | adj. |使恢复活力的；使年轻的；更新的 | Made young or vigorous again; renewed in strength or appearance.  The word implies a restoration to a previous state of youthfulness or vitality, often after a period of decline or exhaustion. |例句：After a relaxing vacation, she felt rejuvenated and ready to tackle new challenges.  度假后，她感到精力充沛，准备迎接新的挑战。

The garden was rejuvenated with the arrival of spring, bursting with new life. 春天来了，花园焕发了生机，充满了新的活力。


近义词：revitalized, refreshed, renewed, invigorated, revitalising

反义词：exhausted, depleted, aged, worn-out


词源:  The word "rejuvenated" is derived from the Latin word "iuvenis," meaning "young." The prefix "re-" means "again" or "back," and the suffix "-ated" indicates that something has been made or done.  Therefore, "rejuvenated" literally means "made young again."
