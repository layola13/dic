feuillants:/fjuːˈjɑːnts/| n.|费扬派, 费扬修道院|Members of a monastic order founded in France in 1563.  The order, known for its moderate stance, was suppressed during the French Revolution. The term is also used to refer to a political club during the French Revolution, known for its initially moderate but later more radical positions. |The Feuillants were initially supporters of the constitutional monarchy. |近义词：Jacobin (in the context of French Revolutionary political factions), Girondin|反义词：N/A
