organoid:/ˈɔːrɡənɔɪd/ | n. |类器官，类器官体 |A three-dimensional tissue culture that mimics the structure and function of an organ.  Organoids are grown from stem cells or other progenitor cells and can be used to study organ development, disease, and drug effects. | Example: Researchers are using brain organoids to study Alzheimer's disease.  研究人员正在使用脑类器官来研究阿尔茨海默病。|近义词:mini-organ, organ model|反义词:N/A
