proboscidean:/ˌproʊbəˈsɪdiən/| n. |长鼻类动物|A proboscidean is a mammal of the order Proboscidea, characterized by a long trunk (proboscis).  This order includes elephants, both living and extinct.  The term refers to the defining characteristic of the group: their long, prehensile nose. |Elephants are the only surviving proboscideans.  |近义词：elephant (in common usage, though not strictly accurate as it only refers to one family within the order)|反义词：None specifically, but could be contrasted with animals lacking a trunk. |名词
