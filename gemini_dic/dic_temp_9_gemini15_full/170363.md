provisionary: /prəˈvɪʒənəri/ | adj. |临时的；暂时的；备用的|Temporary; provisional; serving as a substitute until a permanent arrangement is made.  |The provisionary government was established after the revolution.|近义词：temporary, provisional, interim, makeshift |反义词：permanent, lasting, final

例句：

The provisionary government lasted only a few months before elections were held.
临时政府只存在了几个月，之后就举行了选举。


The school used a provisionary classroom while the main building was being renovated.
学校在主教学楼装修期间使用了临时教室。


词源:

From Middle French *provisionnaire, from Old French *provisoire, from Latin provisorius, from providere "to foresee, provide".  The word ultimately derives from the Latin verb *providere, meaning "to foresee" or "to provide."  This reflects the sense of something being put in place to meet an immediate need,  a temporary solution until a more permanent arrangement can be made.
