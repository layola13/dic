ridiculed:/rɪˈdɪkjuːld/| vt.| 嘲笑；奚落；愚弄 | To make fun of someone or something in a cruel or unkind way; to treat with contempt. |例句：The children ridiculed him for his strange clothes. 孩子们因为他奇怪的衣服嘲笑他。

例句：He was ridiculed for his unconventional ideas. 他因为他的非传统思想而遭到嘲笑。


近义词：mocked, derided, scorned, jeered at, made fun of |反义词：praised, commended, applauded


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They ridicule his efforts. = 他们嘲笑他的努力。
People ridicule his opinions. = 人们嘲笑他的观点。


一般过去时:
The critics ridiculed his latest novel. = 批评家们嘲笑了他最新的那部小说。
She ridiculed his attempt to cook. = 她嘲笑了他尝试做饭。


现在完成时:
They have ridiculed him for years. = 他们多年来一直嘲笑他。
The media has ridiculed the politician's policies. = 媒体嘲笑了这位政治家的政策。


过去完成时:
They had ridiculed him before he even began. = 在他开始之前，他们就嘲笑他了。
She had ridiculed his ideas many times. = 她多次嘲笑过他的想法。


将来时:
They will ridicule his performance. = 他们将会嘲笑他的表演。
The audience will ridicule the comedian's jokes. = 观众会嘲笑这位喜剧演员的笑话。


虚拟语气:
If he knew they would ridicule him, he wouldn't have tried. = 如果他知道他们会嘲笑他，他不会尝试的。
I wish they hadn't ridiculed her so much. = 我真希望他们没有那么嘲笑她。


被动语态:
His ideas were ridiculed by the public. = 他的想法被公众嘲笑了。
His performance was ridiculed by the critics. = 他的表演被评论家们嘲笑了。


疑问句:
Were his ideas ridiculed? = 他的想法被嘲笑了吗？
Will his proposal be ridiculed? = 他的提议会被嘲笑吗？


否定句:
They did not ridicule his efforts. = 他们没有嘲笑他的努力。
She didn't ridicule his attempt to sing. = 她没有嘲笑他尝试唱歌。


PHRASE->
be ridiculed for = 因...而被嘲笑
open to ridicule = 容易受到嘲笑
subject to ridicule = 容易受到嘲笑
hold up to ridicule =  подвергать осмеянию (俄语，意为“公开嘲笑”)
become the object of ridicule = 成为嘲笑的对象

词源:
From Middle French *ridiculer, from Latin ridiculus "laughable," diminutive of ridere "to laugh."
