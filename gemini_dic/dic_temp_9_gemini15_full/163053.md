preclosure: /ˈpriːˌkloʊʒər/ | n. | 预先关闭；提前关闭；预先结束 | The act of closing something before its scheduled or expected time.  It often implies a premature or unexpected ending.  The term is often used in contexts involving legal proceedings, financial transactions, or software processes. | 例句：The judge ordered the preclosure of the case due to lack of evidence.  法官因证据不足而下令提前结案。

The bank initiated a preclosure of the loan after the borrower defaulted. 借款人违约后，银行提前关闭了贷款。


近义词：early termination, premature closure, preemptive closure | 反义词：postponement, extension, continuation | 词性: 名词
