calico:/ˈkælɪkoʊ/| n.|花布，小花布；杂色猫|a strong, plain-woven cotton fabric, usually printed with a multicolored pattern.  Also refers to a cat with a calico coat.|例句：She wore a dress made of calico. 她穿着一件棉布花布做的连衣裙。

The cat had a beautiful calico coat. 这只猫有着美丽的杂色毛。|近义词：chintz, cotton print|反义词：plain, solid-colored

词源:
The word "calico" originates from the Indian port city of Calicut (Kozhikode) in Kerala, India, where cotton cloth was produced and exported.  The word entered English via the Portuguese and Dutch.
