recontaminating: /ˌriːkənˈtæmɪneɪtɪŋ/ | v. | 再污染；重新污染 |  The act of contaminating something again after it has been cleaned or disinfected.  It implies a return to a previous state of contamination. |

例句：

The improperly sterilized equipment led to the recontamination of the surgical site. = 不正确消毒的设备导致手术部位再次污染。

The floodwaters caused recontamination of the already cleaned-up area. = 洪水导致已经清理过的区域再次污染。


近义词：reinfecting, polluting again, contaminating anew


反义词：decontaminating, sterilizing, purifying


词性：动词 (现在分词)


词源:

* **re-**: prefix meaning "again" or "back"
* **contaminate**: from Latin *contamināre*  "to pollute, defile," from *con-* "together" + *tamināre* "to stain, defile," of unknown origin.  The meaning evolved to encompass the introduction of harmful substances.
* **-ing**: present participle suffix
