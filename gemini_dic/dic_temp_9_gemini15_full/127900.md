neoliberalism:/ˌniːoʊˈlɪbərəlɪzəm/ | n. |新自由主义|

英文解析:Neoliberalism is a broad term referring to a set of economic policies that promote free market capitalism and deregulation.  It emphasizes individual responsibility, competition, and limited government intervention in the economy.  Specific policies often associated with neoliberalism include privatization of state-owned enterprises, deregulation of industries, tax cuts (especially for corporations and high-income earners), free trade agreements, and austerity measures. However, the precise meaning and application of the term are often debated, with critics arguing that it masks policies that benefit the wealthy at the expense of the poor and the environment.

例句:

The rise of neoliberalism in the 1980s led to significant changes in global economic policy.  = 20世纪80年代新自由主义的兴起导致了全球经济政策的重大变化。

Many critics argue that neoliberalism exacerbates income inequality and social injustice. = 许多批评者认为新自由主义加剧了收入不平等和社会不公。


近义词:free market capitalism, laissez-faire economics, market liberalization

反义词:socialism, communism, state interventionism
