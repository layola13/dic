pander:/ˈpændər/ | vt. n. | 迎合；逢迎；拉皮条 | To cater to or exploit the weaknesses or desires of others, often in a manipulative or exploitative way; to act as a pimp. | 例句：The politician pandered to the prejudices of his constituents to win their votes.  这个政客迎合选民的偏见以赢得选票。

例句：The magazine panders to the lowest common denominator with its sensationalist articles. 这本杂志以耸人听闻的文章迎合大众的低级趣味。


近义词：cater to, indulge, appease, gratify, exploit | 反义词：oppose, defy, resist, ignore


词性：动词(vt.)，名词(n.)


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He panders to the wealthy. = 他奉承富人。
The company panders to consumer desires. = 这家公司迎合消费者的欲望。

一般过去时:
She pandered to his every whim. = 她满足他每一个怪念头。
The newspaper pandered to public fear. = 这家报纸迎合公众的恐惧。

现在完成时:
They have pandered to the media for years. = 他们多年来一直在讨好媒体。
The government has pandered to special interests. = 政府一直在迎合特殊利益集团。

过去完成时:
He had pandered to the crowd before the election. = 他在选举前就一直在讨好人群。
The author had pandered to popular taste in her previous novels. = 作者在她之前的几部小说中迎合了大众口味。

将来时:
They will pander to the voters' concerns. = 他们将迎合选民的担忧。
The company will pander to the market trend. = 公司将迎合市场趋势。

虚拟语气:
If he pandered to them, he might get the job. = 如果他逢迎他们，他可能会得到这份工作。
I wish he hadn't pandered to the mob. = 我真希望他没有迎合暴民。

被动语态:
His ideas were pandered to by the media. = 他的想法被媒体所迎合。
The public's fears were pandered to by the politicians. = 政客们迎合了公众的恐惧。

疑问句:
Does he pander to the rich? = 他是否奉承富人？
Does the film pander to violence? = 这部电影是否迎合暴力？

否定句:
He doesn't pander to anyone. = 他不奉承任何人。
The artist doesn't pander to popular taste. = 这位艺术家不迎合大众口味。



PHRASE->
pander to someone's desires = 迎合某人的欲望
pander to the crowd = 迎合大众
pander to the lowest common denominator = 迎合大众的低级趣味
pander to prejudice = 迎合偏见
pander to fear = 迎合恐惧


词源:
The word "pander" ultimately derives from the Greek word "pandēros," which referred to a procurer or pimp in ancient Greek literature.  This meaning evolved over time, ultimately leading to the modern sense of catering to the base desires or weaknesses of others.
