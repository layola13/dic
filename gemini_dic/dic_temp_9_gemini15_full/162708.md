preballot:/ˈpriːˌbælət/ | n. | 选举前的民意调查；预选投票 | A preballot is a poll or survey conducted before an election to gauge public opinion or predict the outcome.  It can also refer to a preliminary vote held before the main election. | Example: The preballot showed strong support for the incumbent.  预选投票显示对现任者有强烈的支持。  The preballot survey indicated a close race.  选举前的民意调查显示这是一场势均力敌的竞赛。 | 近义词：pre-election poll, preliminary vote, straw poll | 反义词：post-election analysis | 词性:名词
