crunk:/krʌŋk/| adj., v.|亢奋的；醉醺醺的；（俚语）兴奋的，精力充沛的；使…兴奋；|英文解析：Crunk is a slang term, originating in Southern hip hop culture, used to describe a state of being extremely energetic, hyped up, often associated with alcohol or drug use, and characterized by wild, uninhibited behavior.  As a verb, it means to make someone excited or hyped up. |例句：The party was completely crunk.  派对完全high翻了。

The music got everyone crunk. 音乐让每个人都兴奋起来。

近义词：excited, hyped, energized, intoxicated, drunk, lit|反义词：calm, subdued, relaxed, sober

词源:
The exact origins of "crunk" are debated, but it's widely believed to have emerged in the early 2000s within the Southern hip hop scene in Atlanta, Georgia.  It's likely a blend of words or a portmanteau, though there's no definitive etymological breakdown.  Some speculate it's a combination of "drunk" and "funk," reflecting the energetic and often intoxicated state associated with the term.  Others propose a connection to the word "crank," suggesting a high-energy, frenetic quality.  Regardless, its usage solidified within a specific cultural context and spread through hip-hop music and culture.
