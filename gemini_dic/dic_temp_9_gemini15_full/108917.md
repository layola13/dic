leeboard:/ˈliːbɔːrd/ | n. |侧板，侧舵 | A retractable board on the side of a small boat, used to provide sideways stability and control, particularly when sailing close-hauled or in light winds.  Leeboards are typically hinged to the hull and lowered into the water when needed. | Example: The small sailboat relied on its leeboards to prevent capsizing in the strong crosswind.  这条小帆船依靠它的侧板来防止在强烈的侧风中倾覆。 | 近义词：centerboard (if central) | 反义词：keel (for larger vessels)
