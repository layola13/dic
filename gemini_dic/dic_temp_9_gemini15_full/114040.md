magnet:/ˈmæɡnɪt/ | n. |磁铁，磁体 | A piece of iron (or an ore, alloy, or other material) that has the property of attracting iron and certain other materials.  It works due to its magnetic field. | Example: The refrigerator magnet held the shopping list firmly in place.  冰箱磁铁牢牢地固定着购物清单。

The Earth itself acts as a giant magnet. 地球本身就是一个巨大的磁铁。|近义词：lodestone, attractor |反义词：repellent

词源:
The word "magnet" comes from the Greek word "magnētis lithos," meaning "Magnesian stone," referring to the magnetite ore found near the ancient city of Magnesia in Asia Minor.  The stone's magnetic properties were known to the ancients.
