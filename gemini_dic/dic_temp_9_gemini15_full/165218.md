preinscribe:/ˌpriːɪnˈskraɪb/ | v. | 预先铭刻；预先刻写 | To write or engrave something beforehand.  The term suggests an action taken in advance of a later event or purpose.  | Example:  The artisan preinscribed the name on the medal before the ceremony.  = 工匠在仪式前预先在奖牌上刻上了名字。

Example: The architect preinscribed the cornerstone with the building's completion date. = 建筑师预先在奠基石上刻上了建筑竣工日期。| 近义词:  pre-engrave, pre-etch, beforehand inscribe | 反义词: post-inscribe
