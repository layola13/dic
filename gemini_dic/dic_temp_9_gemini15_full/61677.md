emmetropy:/ɪˈmɛtrəpi/| n. |正常视力，正视|The state of having normal vision without refractive error;  the ability to see distant objects clearly without the need for corrective lenses.  It refers to the perfect alignment of the eye's refractive components (cornea, lens, etc.) allowing light to focus precisely on the retina.|Example:  She enjoys emmetropy and doesn't need glasses.|近义词：normal vision, clear vision|反义词：ametropia, refractive error
