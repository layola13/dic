inaugur:/ˈɔːɡjʊər/| vt.| 开始；为…举行就职典礼；开创 | To begin or introduce something new; to formally install someone into office. |例句：The new president will be inaugurated next week.  下周新总统将宣誓就职。
|例句：The new library was inaugurated with a special ceremony. 新图书馆以一场特别的仪式启用。|近义词：commence, initiate, install, begin|反义词：conclude, end, terminate|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He inaugurates the new program today. 他今天启动了新项目。
They inaugurate a new era of cooperation. 他们开启了一个合作的新时代。

一般过去时:
The mayor inaugurated the new park last month. 市长上个月为新的公园举行了落成典礼。
They inaugurated the new hospital with a ribbon-cutting ceremony. 他们用剪彩仪式为新医院举行了落成典礼。

现在完成时:
The company has inaugurated a new marketing strategy.  这家公司已经启动了一项新的营销策略。
They have inaugurated a new policy to combat crime. 他们已经启动了一项打击犯罪的新政策。


过去完成时:
They had inaugurated the project before the funding was secured. 在资金到位之前，他们就已经启动了这个项目。
Before the ceremony, they had inaugurated the new statue. 典礼之前，他们已经为新雕像举行了揭幕仪式。

将来时:
The president-elect will be inaugurated next January. 当选总统将于明年一月就职。
They will inaugurate the new bridge next spring.  他们将在明年春天启用这座新桥。

虚拟语气:
If they had inaugurated the project earlier, it would be completed by now. 如果他们早点启动这个项目，现在就完成了。
I wish they had inaugurated the new policy sooner. 我希望他们早点启动这项新政策。

被动语态:
The new museum was inaugurated last year. 新博物馆去年落成。
The program will be inaugurated next month.  该项目将于下个月启动。

疑问句:
When will the new stadium be inaugurated? 新体育馆什么时候启用？
Has the new law been inaugurated yet? 新法已经实施了吗？

否定句:
They did not inaugurate the project on time. 他们没有按时启动该项目。
The new building has not yet been inaugurated. 新大楼尚未启用。
