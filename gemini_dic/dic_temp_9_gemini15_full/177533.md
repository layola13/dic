reboot:/ˌriːˈbuːt/ | vt. n. |重启；重新启动；彻底改革 | To restart a computer or other electronic device by turning it off and then on again; to restart something, especially a project or organization, in a way that involves significant changes. |例句：I had to reboot my computer after the software update. 我在软件更新后不得不重启我的电脑。


例句：The company decided to reboot its marketing strategy after a period of declining sales.  公司在销售额下降一段时间后决定彻底改革其营销策略。


近义词：restart, relaunch, revamp | 反义词：shutdown, terminate


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He reboots his computer every morning. = 他每天早上都重启他的电脑。
They reboot the server regularly to prevent crashes. = 他们定期重启服务器以防止崩溃。

一般过去时:
I rebooted my phone after it froze. = 我的手机死机后我重启了它。
She rebooted the project with a new team. = 她用一个新的团队重启了这个项目。

现在完成时:
We have rebooted our website several times. = 我们已经多次重新设计了我们的网站。
They have rebooted the campaign with a fresh approach. = 他们已经用一种新的方法重启了这场运动。

过去完成时:
He had rebooted the system before the presentation. = 他在演示之前已经重启了系统。
They had rebooted the marketing plan before launching the new product. = 他们在推出新产品之前已经重新规划了营销计划。

将来时:
I will reboot my computer later. = 我稍后会重启我的电脑。
They will reboot the application after fixing the bugs. = 他们修复错误后将重启应用程序。

虚拟语气:
If the computer freezes, I would reboot it. = 如果电脑死机，我会重启它。
It's time we rebooted our business strategy, or else we'll fail. = 是时候我们彻底改革商业策略了，否则我们会失败。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
