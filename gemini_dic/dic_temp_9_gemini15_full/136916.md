nonsoluble: /ˈnɒnsɒljʊbl/ | adj. | 不溶解的，不可溶的 |  Incapable of being dissolved.  A substance that does not dissolve in a given solvent is described as nonsoluble. This term is often used in chemistry and related fields to describe the inability of a solid, liquid, or gas to dissolve in another substance. | Example:  The sediment is nonsoluble in water.  沉淀物不溶于水。

The paint is nonsoluble in most solvents.  这种油漆在大多数溶剂中不溶解。


近义词: insoluble, undissolvable

反义词: soluble, dissolvable


词源:  The word "nonsoluble" is a combination of the prefix "non-" (meaning "not") and the adjective "soluble" (meaning "capable of being dissolved"). "Soluble"  itself originates from the Latin word "solvere," meaning "to loosen, to dissolve."


USAGE->
This word is primarily an adjective and does not have verb or noun forms.  Therefore, tense and voice considerations do not apply directly to "nonsoluble."  It functions as a simple descriptive adjective modifying a noun.

Example sentences illustrating its usage in different sentence structures:

肯定句:
The material is nonsoluble in acid. = 这种材料不溶于酸。
This compound is nonsoluble under normal conditions. = 在正常条件下，这种化合物不溶解。


否定句:
The substance is not nonsoluble in alcohol; it dissolves readily. = 该物质不不溶于酒精；它很容易溶解。(This is a double negative, meaning it's soluble.)
That chemical is soluble, it is not nonsoluble. = 那种化学物质是可溶的，它不是不溶的。


疑问句:
Is this substance nonsoluble in water? = 这种物质不溶于水吗？
Are these pigments nonsoluble in the paint thinner? = 这些颜料不溶于稀释剂吗？

There are no common idiomatic phrases directly using "nonsoluble."  Its usage is primarily limited to scientific and technical contexts.
