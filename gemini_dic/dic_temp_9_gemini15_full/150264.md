pandore:/pænˈdɔːrə/| n.|潘多拉；潘多拉的盒子|Pandora is a name from Greek mythology, referring to the first woman created by Hephaestus on the orders of Zeus.  She was given a jar (often mistakenly called a box) containing all the evils of the world.  The word is now often used metaphorically to refer to something that unleashes widespread trouble or misfortune. |例句：Opening that Pandora's Box unleashed a torrent of problems. 打开潘多拉的盒子释放了一连串的问题。|例句：The new policy proved to be a Pandora's box, creating unforeseen complications. 新政策证明是一个潘多拉的盒子，造成了不可预见的复杂情况。|近义词：calamity, disaster, catastrophe |反义词：blessing, boon
