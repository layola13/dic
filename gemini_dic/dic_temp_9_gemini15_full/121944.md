missing out:/ˈmɪsɪŋ aʊt/| v. |错过，失去（机会、体验等）|To fail to benefit from something or to experience something enjoyable because one is not present or involved.|I'm missing out on all the fun. 我错过了所有的乐趣。|近义词：miss, lose, forfeit |反义词：gaining, enjoying


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is missing out on a great opportunity. 他正在错过一个好机会。
They miss out on many activities because of their busy schedules. 他们因为忙碌的日程而错过了许多活动。


一般过去时:
I missed out on the concert last night. 我错过了昨晚的音乐会。
She missed out on a promotion due to her poor performance. 她因为表现不佳而错过了晋升机会。


现在完成时:
I have missed out on so many things this year. 今年我错过了很多事情。
He has missed out on several chances to travel abroad. 他错过了几次出国旅行的机会。


过去完成时:
I realised I had missed out on a wonderful experience. 我意识到我错过了一次美好的经历。
She regretted that she had missed out on the chance to study abroad. 她后悔错过了出国留学的机会。


将来时:
If you don't hurry, you will miss out on the sale. 如果你不快点，你就会错过这次促销。
We will miss out on the sunset if we don't leave now. 如果我们现在不走，我们会错过日落。


虚拟语气:
I wish I hadn't missed out on that opportunity. 我真希望我没有错过那个机会。
If I had known, I wouldn't have missed out on the party. 如果我知道的话，我就不会错过派对了。


被动语态:
He felt he was being missed out on the decision-making process. 他觉得他在决策过程中被忽视了。(Note: This is a slightly unusual use, focusing on being excluded rather than missing something.)
Many talented individuals are missed out on due to bias in hiring. 许多有才华的人因为招聘中的偏见而被忽略。(Note: This is a slightly unusual use, focusing on being excluded rather than missing something.)


疑问句:
Are you missing out on something good? 你是不是错过了什么好事？
Will I miss out if I don't attend the meeting? 如果我不参加会议，我会错过什么吗？


否定句:
I didn't miss out on anything important. 我没有错过任何重要的事情。
She made sure she didn't miss out on the deadline. 她确保自己没有错过截止日期。
