latents:/ˈleɪtənts/| n.|潜变量，潜在因素|Latent variables are hidden or unobserved variables that are inferred from observed variables.  They are used in statistical modeling to explain relationships between observed variables that cannot be directly measured.|例句：The model uses latent variables to represent the underlying psychological constructs.|近义词：hidden variables, underlying factors|反义词：manifest variables, observable factors
