floor:/flɔːr/ /flɔːr/| n. |地板，楼层，底部，河床|The surface of a room on which one walks;  a level in a building; the bottom of something.  |例句：The floor is made of wood.  地板是用木头做的。

The building has ten floors. 这栋建筑有十层楼。|近义词：level, story, bottom |反义词：ceiling, top


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The floor is clean. = 地板很干净。
This floor needs polishing. = 这层楼需要抛光。

一般过去时:
The floor was dirty. = 地板很脏。
He swept the floor yesterday. = 他昨天扫了地。

现在完成时:
We have cleaned the floor. = 我们已经打扫了地板。
They have polished the floor many times. = 他们已经抛光地板很多次了。

过去完成时:
The floor had been cleaned before the party. = 派对前地板已经打扫干净了。
She had mopped the floor before the guests arrived. = 客人到达之前，她已经擦过地板了。

将来时:
We will clean the floor tomorrow. = 我们明天会打扫地板。
They will replace the damaged floor next week. = 他们下周会更换损坏的地板。

虚拟语气:
If the floor were clean, I would sit on it. = 如果地板干净，我会坐在上面。
I wish the floor wasn’t so cold. = 我希望地板不要那么冷。

被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
The floor isn't clean. = 地板不干净。

词源:
The word "floor" comes from the Old English word "flōr," which ultimately derives from Proto-Germanic *flūr,  meaning "floor" or "ground".  Its meaning has remained relatively consistent throughout its history.
