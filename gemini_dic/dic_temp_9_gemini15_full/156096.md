photoreversal:/ˌfoʊtoʊrɪˈvɜːrsəl/ | n. | 光致逆转 | The reversal of a chemical or physical process by the action of light.  This term is often used in the context of photography or photochemistry, where light is used to reverse a previous chemical reaction or change. For example, in some photographic processes, exposure to light can reverse the effects of previous development, leading to a "negative" image becoming a "positive" image. | Example: The photoreversal of the latent image allowed for the creation of a positive print.  光致逆转潜像使得可以制作出正片。  | 近义词: light reversal, photo-induced reversal | 反义词: photoactivation, photodegradation |
