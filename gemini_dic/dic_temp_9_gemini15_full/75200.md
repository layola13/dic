frocking:/ˈfrɒkɪŋ/| v. |穿长袍；打扮；责备|The act of dressing in a frock or long gown, or the act of scolding or reprimanding someone severely.  It can also refer to the process of fitting a garment, especially a frock. |例句：She was frocking the children for their Sunday best. = 她正在给孩子们穿上他们最好的星期天衣服。


例句：The teacher frocked the student for talking in class. = 老师因为学生在课堂上讲话而严厉斥责了他。


近义词：dressing, scolding, reprimanding, chastising|反义词：praising, commending


词源:  The word "frocking" derives from "frock," which ultimately comes from Old French "froc," meaning "a monk's robe."  The meaning evolved to encompass various types of long gowns and dresses. The verb form "frocking" reflects the action of putting on or fitting such garments.  The sense of scolding is a later, figurative development.
