fish:/fɪʃ/| n. v.|鱼；钓鱼|A cold-blooded vertebrate animal living wholly in water, having gills, and typically having fins and scales.  |例句：I caught a big fish this morning. 今天早上我钓到了一条大鱼。
例句：We had fish and chips for dinner. 我们晚餐吃了炸鱼薯条。|近义词：seafood, aquatic animal |反义词：None specifically, but could be contrasted with land animals.


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I fish every weekend. = 我每个周末都钓鱼。
He fishes in the river. = 他在河里钓鱼。

一般过去时:
I fished all day yesterday. = 我昨天钓了一整天鱼。
They fished in the lake. = 他们在湖里钓鱼。

现在完成时:
I have fished in this lake many times. = 我在这湖里钓过很多次鱼。
She has never fished before. = 她以前从未钓过鱼。

过去完成时:
I had fished for hours before I caught anything. = 我钓了几个小时才钓到东西。
They had fished there before. = 他们以前在那里钓过鱼。

将来时:
I will fish tomorrow. = 我明天将去钓鱼。
We will be fishing all day. = 我们将钓一整天鱼。

虚拟语气:
If I had more time, I would fish more often. = 如果我有更多时间，我会更经常钓鱼。
I wish I could fish in that beautiful lake. = 我真希望我能在那美丽的湖里钓鱼。

被动语态:
The fish are often caught by tourists. = 游客经常捕捞这些鱼。
The lake is stocked with fish every year. = 湖里每年都放养鱼类。

疑问句:
Do you like to fish? = 你喜欢钓鱼吗？
Where do you fish? = 你在哪里钓鱼？

否定句:

词源:
The word "fish" has a long and complex etymology, tracing back to Proto-Germanic *fiską and further to Proto-Indo-European *písḱ-.  Many Indo-European languages show cognates related to fish.  The English word has evolved organically through various stages of Old English, Middle English, and Modern English, retaining a consistent core meaning.
