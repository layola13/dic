ghettos:/ˈɡɛtoʊz/| n.|贫民窟，犹太人居住区|A ghetto is a part of a city, especially a slum area, occupied by a minority group or groups.  Historically, the term referred specifically to the areas where Jews were forced to live in many European cities. Now, it is used more broadly to describe any impoverished and often segregated neighborhood.|例句：Many families in the ghetto struggled with poverty and lack of opportunity. 贫民窟里的许多家庭都在为贫困和缺乏机会而挣扎。|例句：The historical ghettos of Europe serve as a grim reminder of persecution and discrimination. 欧洲历史上那些犹太人居住区是迫害和歧视的可怕提醒。|近义词：slum, shantytown,  barrio (specifically for Hispanic communities)|反义词：affluent neighborhood, upscale area, suburb
