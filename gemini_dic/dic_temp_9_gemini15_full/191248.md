scatologist:/ˌskætˈɒlədʒɪst/| n. |粪便学家；排泄物研究者|A scatologist is a person who studies excrement, often in the context of its composition, origin, or significance in various fields such as medicine, anthropology, or archaeology.  They might analyze fecal matter to diagnose diseases, understand dietary habits of ancient populations, or investigate crime scenes. |The scatologist analyzed the samples to determine the cause of the outbreak. 粪便学家分析样本以确定疫情的原因。|  --- | ---
