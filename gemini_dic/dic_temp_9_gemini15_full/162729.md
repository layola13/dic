prebelievers:/ˌpriːbɪˈliːvərz/| n. | 预先信徒；先期信徒 |  A prebeliever is a person who believed in a particular religion or ideology before it became widely accepted or popular.  They were followers or adherents before the mainstream adoption.  The term often implies a sense of pioneering or early adoption. | Example:  Many early Christians considered themselves prebelievers, having adopted their faith before the Roman Empire's official recognition of Christianity.  许多早期基督徒认为自己是先期信徒，在罗马帝国正式承认基督教之前就皈依了他们的信仰。 | 近义词：early adopter, pioneer, forefather (in a religious context) | 反义词：latecomer, skeptic, unbeliever
