mantua:/ˈmæntjuːə/| n.|长袍；曼图亚式长袍|A long, loose robe, especially one worn by women in the 16th and 17th centuries.  The term often refers to a particular style of gown popular during that period, characterized by a close-fitting bodice and a full, flowing skirt. |例句：The painting depicts a noblewoman in a rich, crimson mantua.  这幅画描绘了一位穿着鲜艳红色长袍的女贵族。

The actress wore a beautiful mantua on stage. 女演员在舞台上穿着一件美丽的曼图亚长袍。|近义词：robe, gown, dress|反义词：None specifically, but terms like "simple dress" or "short skirt" could be considered contextual opposites.
