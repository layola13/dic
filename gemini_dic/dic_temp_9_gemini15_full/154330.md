perjurement:/ˈpɜːdʒəriment/| n. |伪证罪；伪证；作伪证|The act of committing perjury; the offense of willfully telling a lie under oath in a judicial proceeding.  It involves making a false statement under oath, knowing it to be false, and with the intent to deceive.|Example: He was charged with perjurement for lying under oath during the trial. 他因在审判中作伪证而被指控犯有伪证罪。|近义词：false swearing, perjury, lying under oath|反义词：truthfulness, veracity|


USAGE->
This word is primarily a noun and doesn't conjugate like a verb.  Therefore, tense considerations are irrelevant.  The usage is limited to describing the act or instance of perjury.

Example 1: The lawyer presented evidence of perjurement by the witness. 律师提供了证人作伪证的证据。

Example 2:  The judge ruled that the perjurement significantly impacted the outcome of the case. 法官裁定伪证严重影响了案件的结果。


PHRASE->
There are no common idiomatic phrases using "perjurement."  The word is used in a formal legal context.
