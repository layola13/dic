low:/loʊ/ | adj. |低的，矮的，低的，微弱的，低下的 |  Low describes something that is not high or tall, or that has a low level or intensity. It can also refer to a lower social status or a depressed mood. |例句：The sun is low in the sky.  太阳低垂在地平线。

The price is low. 价格很低。


近义词：small, short, slight, weak, humble | 反义词：high, tall, elevated, strong, superior


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The sun is low in the sky. = 太阳低垂在地平线。
The temperature is low today. = 今天气温很低。

一般过去时:
The plane flew low over the houses. = 飞机低飞掠过房屋。
The volume was low during the movie. = 电影期间音量很低。

现在完成时:

词源:
The word "low" has Old English roots, deriving from the Proto-Germanic *lagaz, meaning "lying down," "flat," or "level."  Its evolution reflects a semantic shift from a physical description of position to a broader sense of inferiority or lesser degree.
