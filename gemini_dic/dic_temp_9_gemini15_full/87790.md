hibernators:/ˈhaɪbərˌneɪtərz/| n. |冬眠动物|Animals that hibernate, meaning they enter a state of dormancy during cold weather to conserve energy. They reduce their metabolic rate, body temperature, and heart rate to survive periods of scarcity.  |Example: Bears are well-known hibernators.  熊是著名的冬眠动物。 Many small mammals, such as hamsters and squirrels, are also hibernators. 许多小型哺乳动物，如仓鼠和松鼠，也是冬眠动物。|近义词：dormant animals, winter sleepers |反义词：active animals, summer dwellers
