foreshot:/ˈfɔːrʃɒt/ | n. |预兆，先兆；预见；先期拍摄的镜头 | A foreshot is a preliminary indication or warning of something that will happen later. It can also refer to a shot taken in advance, especially in filmmaking.  |例句：The storm clouds were a foreshot of the heavy rain to come.  暴雨来临前的乌云预示着暴雨的来临。

The director used a foreshot to establish the setting before the main action began. 导演在主要动作开始之前使用了一个预先拍摄的镜头来建立场景。|近义词：premonition, presentiment, omen, precursor |反义词：aftermath, consequence, result |名词


USAGE->
This word is primarily a noun and doesn't conjugate through tenses like verbs.  Therefore, standard verb tense examples are not applicable.  The usage revolves around its meaning as a noun.

例句：The unusually warm weather in February was a foreshot of a hot summer.  二月异常温暖的天气预示着炎热的夏天。

The quick cut to the burning building was a dramatic foreshot to the climax of the film. 快速切换到燃烧的建筑物是电影高潮的戏剧性预兆。
