flustering: /ˈflʌstərɪŋ/ | v. |使慌乱；使激动；使心烦意乱 | Making someone nervous and confused, typically to the point of being unable to act effectively. |例句：The unexpected question flustered her, and she stammered a response.  意外的问题使她慌乱，她结结巴巴地回答。
The sudden announcement flustered the entire team.突如其来的公告使整个团队慌乱。|近义词：agitate, perturb, disconcert, unsettle |反义词：calm, soothe, reassure |动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The news flusters him. = 这个消息让他慌乱。
Loud noises fluster the baby. = 吵闹的声音让婴儿烦躁不安。

一般过去时:
The interview flustered me. = 面试让我慌乱。
The unexpected guest flustered her plans. = 意外的客人打乱了她的计划。

现在完成时:
The constant interruptions have flustered me all day. = 一天到晚的不断打断让我心烦意乱。
The unexpected changes have flustered the project team. = 意外的变化让项目团队慌了手脚。

过去完成时:
She had been flustered by the earlier criticism before the presentation. = 演示之前，她已经被早些时候的批评弄得心烦意乱。
He had flustered himself before the meeting, which affected his performance. = 会议前他已经让自己慌乱了，这影响了他的表现。

将来时:
The announcement will fluster many people. = 这个公告会让很多人慌张。
I think the surprise party will fluster her. = 我认为惊喜派对会让她慌乱。

虚拟语气:
If I hadn't prepared, the questions would have flustered me. = 如果我没有准备，那些问题会让我慌乱。
I wish the loud music hadn't flustered the children. = 我希望嘈杂的音乐没有让孩子们心烦意乱。

被动语态:
He was flustered by the unexpected visit. = 他被突如其来的拜访弄得慌乱。
The students were flustered by the difficult exam. = 学生们被那场艰难的考试弄得慌乱。

疑问句:
Does this situation fluster you? = 这种情况让你慌乱吗？
Will the upcoming deadline fluster the team? = 即将到来的截止日期会让团队慌乱吗？

否定句:
The news didn't fluster her at all. = 这个消息一点也没有让她慌乱。
He wasn't flustered by the difficult questions. = 他并没有被那些难题难倒。
