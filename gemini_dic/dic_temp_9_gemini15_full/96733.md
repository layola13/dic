inelasticity: /ˌɪnˌɛləˈstɪsəti/ | n. |非弹性；缺乏弹性；需求缺乏弹性| The lack of responsiveness of supply or demand to changes in price.  This concept is often used in economics to describe situations where a change in price leads to a proportionally smaller change in quantity demanded or supplied. | Example: The inelasticity of demand for gasoline means that even significant price increases do not drastically reduce consumption.  汽油需求的非弹性意味着即使价格大幅上涨也不会大幅减少消费。|近义词：rigidity, inflexibility |反义词：elasticity
