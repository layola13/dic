mildewed:/ˈmɪlduːd/| adj.|发霉的，长霉的|Covered with mildew; affected by mildew.|The old book was mildewed and smelled musty. 那本旧书发霉了，闻起来很霉。 The bread had become mildewed and was thrown away. 面包发霉了，被扔掉了。|moldy, musty, fusty|fresh, clean


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The old clothes are mildewed. = 旧衣服发霉了。
This bread is mildewed; we should throw it away. = 这个面包发霉了；我们应该扔掉它。

一般过去时:
The basement was mildewed after the flood. = 洪水过后，地下室发霉了。
The painting became mildewed in the damp room. = 这幅画在潮湿的房间里发霉了。

现在完成时:
The walls have become mildewed due to the leak. = 由于漏水，墙壁已经发霉了。
The shoes have mildewed since they were left outside. = 鞋子自从放在外面就发霉了。

过去完成时:
The food had mildewed before we could eat it. = 我们还没来得及吃，食物就发霉了。
The books had mildewed because they were stored in a damp place. = 书因为储存在潮湿的地方而发霉了。

将来时:
The towels will mildew if you don't dry them properly. = 如果你不把毛巾弄干，它们就会发霉。
The stored grain will mildew in this humid climate. = 在这种潮湿的气候下，储存的谷物会发霉。

虚拟语气:
If the room were better ventilated, the clothes wouldn't mildew. = 如果房间通风更好，衣服就不会发霉。
I wish the bread hadn't mildewed before the picnic. = 我希望面包在野餐前没有发霉。

被动语态:
The fabric was mildewed by the humidity. = 面料因潮湿而发霉。
The leather goods are easily mildewed if not properly stored. = 如果储存不当，皮革制品很容易发霉。

疑问句:
Is the food mildewed? = 食物发霉了吗？
Are the walls mildewed in the bathroom? = 浴室的墙壁发霉了吗？

否定句:
The clothes are not mildewed. = 衣服没有发霉。
The stored goods were not mildewed, because the warehouse was dry. = 储存的货物没有发霉，因为仓库很干燥。
