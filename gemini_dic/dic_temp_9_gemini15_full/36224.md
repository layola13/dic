cognates:/ˈkɑːɡneɪts/|/ˈkɒɡneɪts/ | n. |同源词，同根词 |Words that have a common ancestor and share a similar meaning and often a similar form. Cognates are useful for language learners because they can help them understand new words more easily.  |Example:  The English word "animal" and the Spanish word "animal" are cognates. They share a Latin root and have the same meaning. |近义词：related words, etymologically related words |反义词：None specifically, but antonyms could be considered for the words the cognates represent. |名词
