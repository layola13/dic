radiotelegraph:/ˈreɪdioʊˌtɛləɡræf/| n. |无线电报机；无线电报|A radiotelegraph is a system for transmitting messages over long distances using radio waves to send Morse code. It combines the technologies of radio and telegraphy.|Example:  Early maritime communication relied heavily on the radiotelegraph.|近义词：wireless telegraph|反义词:None

词源:
radio-  (来自拉丁语radius,  meaning "ray" or "spoke") + telegraph (来自希腊语tele, meaning "far off" + graphein, meaning "to write")  组合而成，表示“远距离书写（通过无线电波）”。
