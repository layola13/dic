pollarded:/ˈpɒlədɪd/| adj. v.|截顶的；修剪树枝的；（树木）被截顶的|Cut back (a tree) by removing the upper branches.  The term often refers to a regular practice to promote new growth.  A pollarded tree is one that has had its branches cut back in this way. |例句：The ancient willows were pollarded to maintain their shape and size. 古老的柳树被截顶以保持其形状和大小。

The oak tree had been pollarded many years ago, resulting in a distinctive, bushy top. 那棵橡树多年前就被截顶了，形成了独特的灌木状树冠。


近义词：coppiced, lopped, pruned, trimmed|反义词：untrimmed, unpruned


词源：From Middle English *pollarde, from Old French *poler, from Vulgar Latin *pollare "to lop, poll" , ultimately from Latin *pollex "thumb" (referring to the action of cutting with a thumb-sized instrument).  The association stems from the short, stubby appearance of a pollarded tree, reminiscent of a thumb.
