ingroup:/ˈɪnɡruːp/| n. |内群体，内部群体 |A group of people who perceive themselves to share a common identity and who feel a sense of belonging and loyalty to each other.  They often share similar values, beliefs, and experiences.  This sense of shared identity can lead to in-group bias, where members favor their own group over out-groups. |Example:  The employees of the company formed a strong ingroup, often socializing outside of work hours.  公司员工组成了一个强大的内部群体，经常在工作时间以外进行社交。


Example: The debate team bonded and formed a strong ingroup, supporting each other through thick and thin. 辩论队成员建立了联系，并形成了一个强大的内部群体，互相支持，风雨同舟。|近义词：inner circle, clique, team |反义词：outgroup
