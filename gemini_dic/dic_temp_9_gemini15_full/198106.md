shoot-ups:/ˈʃuːtʌps/| n.|突然增长；飙升；毒品注射；枪击事件 |A sudden and significant increase in something, often referring to prices, crime rates, or drug use.  It can also refer to an incident involving multiple gunshots.|例句：There was a shoot-up in the stock market this week. 本周股市暴涨。  The neighborhood has seen a recent shoot-up in violent crime.  最近该社区暴力犯罪事件激增。|近义词：surge, spike, upsurge, escalation|反义词：decline, decrease, drop


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The price of gas is shooting up. = 油价正在上涨。
Crime rates are shooting up in this city. = 这个城市的犯罪率正在上升。

一般过去时:
The temperature shot up to 40 degrees. = 温度飙升至40度。
The number of applicants shot up dramatically last year. = 申请人数去年急剧增加。

现在完成时:
The cost of living has shot up significantly. = 生活成本已大幅上涨。
The company's profits have shot up this quarter. = 公司本季度的利润已大幅增长。

过去完成时:
The stock prices had shot up before the news broke. = 在新闻发布之前，股价就已经飙升了。
The crime rate had shot up considerably before the police intervention. = 在警方干预之前，犯罪率已经大幅上升。


将来时:
The demand for electric cars will shoot up in the coming years. = 未来几年对电动汽车的需求将会激增。
Experts predict that inflation will shoot up next year. = 专家预测通货膨胀明年将会飙升。


虚拟语气:
If the interest rates shot up, many people would lose their homes. = 如果利率飙升，许多人将失去他们的家园。
If the price of oil shot up, the economy would suffer. = 如果油价飙升，经济将遭受损失。


被动语态:
The prices were shot up by the unexpected demand. = 价格因意外需求而飙升。
The crime rate was shot up by the lack of police presence. = 犯罪率因缺乏警力而上升。


疑问句:
Will the housing prices shoot up next year? = 房价明年会飙升吗？
Did the unemployment rate shoot up after the pandemic? = 大流行之后失业率是否飙升？


否定句:
The stock prices didn't shoot up as expected. = 股价没有像预期那样飙升。
The sales didn't shoot up despite the marketing campaign. = 尽管进行了营销活动，销售额也没有飙升。


PHRASE->
shoot up dramatically = 急剧上升
shoot up quickly = 快速上升
shoot up suddenly = 突然上升
shoot up significantly = 大幅上升
shoot up unexpectedly = 出乎意料地增长
