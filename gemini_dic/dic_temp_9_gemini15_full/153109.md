pegs:/peɡz/ | n. v. |钉子；栓；桩；衣夹；(俚语)腿 | A peg is a small, usually cylindrical, piece of wood or metal used to fasten something or to hang something on.  It can also refer to a marker indicating a level or position.  As a verb, it means to fasten or secure with pegs. |例句：He hammered the pegs into the ground. 他把木桩钉进土里。


The clothes are hanging on pegs in the closet. 衣服挂在壁橱里的衣架上。|近义词：pin, fastener, stake, dowel |反义词：unfasten, detach

He pegs the clothes on the line. 他把衣服别在绳子上。
The price pegs at $10. 价格定在10美元。

词源:
The word "peg" ultimately derives from the Old English word *pæc, meaning "a stick" or "a piece of wood".  Its usage expanded over time to include its current meanings.
