overpollinating: /ˌoʊvərˈpɑːləneɪtɪŋ/ | v. |过度授粉|The act of pollinating a plant or area to an excessive degree, often resulting in negative consequences.  This can lead to reduced fruit set, smaller fruit size, or even plant damage.  It's a relatively new term reflecting the growing concern over human intervention in natural pollination processes. | The farmer overpollinated the apple orchard, leading to a smaller than expected apple yield. | underpollinating |  (no direct antonym, but the opposite effect would be underpollination) | 动词
