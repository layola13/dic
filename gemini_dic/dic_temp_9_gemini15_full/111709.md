lockup: /ˈlɒkʌp/ | /ˈlɑːkʌp/ | n., v. | 禁闭；拘留；停工；(资金的)冻结 |

英文解析:
As a noun, "lockup" refers to a temporary jail or a period of confinement.  It can also describe a situation where assets are frozen or unavailable. As a verb, it means to confine or imprison someone temporarily, or to prevent something from being used or accessed.

例句:
The police took the suspect to the lockup for questioning.  警察把嫌疑犯带到拘留所进行审问。

The company experienced a lockup period after the IPO.  公司在首次公开募股后经历了一段资金冻结期。


近义词:
Noun: confinement, detention, jail, imprisonment, freeze
Verb: confine, detain, imprison, freeze, secure

反义词:
Noun: release, freedom, liberty
Verb: release, liberate, free


词性:
Noun, Verb
