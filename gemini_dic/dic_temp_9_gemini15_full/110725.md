liners:/ˈlaɪnərz/ | n. | 班轮；衬里；(诗歌的)分行；(铁路的)线路 | Large ships carrying passengers and freight on regular routes.  Liners are often associated with luxury and elegance, especially in the past. They can also refer to the inner lining of something, such as a garment or a container. In poetry, it refers to a single line of verse. In railway context, it can mean a railway line. |例句：The cruise liner sailed smoothly across the ocean. 这艘游轮平稳地航行在海洋上。
The coat has a warm fleece liner. 这件外套有一个温暖的羊毛衬里。|近义词：vessel, ship, lining, layer |反义词：None directly applicable.
