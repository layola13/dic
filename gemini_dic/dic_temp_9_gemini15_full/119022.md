methodological:/ˌmɛθədəˈlɒdʒɪkəl/| adj.|方法论的；方法学的；有条理的 | Relating to the system of methods used in a particular area of study or activity.  It emphasizes the systematic and rigorous approach to research or problem-solving. |例句：The researcher adopted a rigorous methodological approach to ensure the validity of the findings. 研究人员采用了严格的方法论方法以确保研究结果的有效性。

例句：His methodological flaws rendered his conclusions unreliable. 他的方法论缺陷导致他的结论不可靠。


近义词：systematic, rigorous, organized, structured |反义词：unsystematic, haphazard, disorganized, chaotic |词性:形容词

词源:
methodological comes from "methodology," which is itself derived from the Greek words "methodos" (μέθοδος), meaning "way of doing something," and "-logia" (-λογία), meaning "study of" or "science of."  Therefore, methodology literally means "the study of methods."  Adding "-ical" creates the adjective "methodological," describing something relating to or characteristic of methodology.
