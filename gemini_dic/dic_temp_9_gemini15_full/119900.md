militarizes: /ˈmɪlɪtəraɪzɪz/ | vt. | 使军事化；赋予军事力量 | To equip (a country, area, or organization) with military resources and personnel; to give a military character to.  |  例句：The government is accused of militarizing the police force. = 政府被指控将警察部队军事化。

例句：The country is rapidly militarizing its borders in response to the escalating threat. = 面对不断升级的威胁，该国正在迅速将其边界军事化。


近义词：arm, fortify, weaponize | 反义词：demilitarize, pacifize | 词性:动词

词源:
The word "militarizes" comes from the noun "military" (from Latin *militaris, from miles "soldier") + "-ize" (a suffix forming verbs meaning "to make or become").  The suffix "-ize" ultimately derives from Greek -ίζειν (-izein).  Therefore, the word literally means "to make military" or "to cause to become military."
