curtailed:/kərˈteɪld/| vt.|缩减；削减；限制；中断|to reduce or limit something|The government curtailed funding for the arts. 政府削减了对艺术的资助。|近义词：abridge, reduce, shorten, restrict|反义词：extend, lengthen, increase


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He curtails his spending. = 他缩减了他的开支。
The company curtails its production. = 公司缩减了它的产量。

一般过去时:
They curtailed the project due to budget cuts. = 由于预算削减，他们缩减了项目。
The meeting was curtailed because of the fire alarm. = 由于火警，会议被缩短了。

现在完成时:
The government has curtailed its military spending. = 政府已经削减了其军事开支。
She has curtailed her social activities recently. = 她最近减少了社交活动。

过去完成时:
They had curtailed their holiday because of the bad weather. = 他们因为坏天气而缩短了假期。
The company had curtailed its investment plans before the crisis. = 在危机之前，公司已经缩减了其投资计划。

将来时:
They will curtail the program if funding is not secured. = 如果资金没有得到保障，他们将缩减该项目。
We will curtail our expenses to save money. = 我们将削减开支以省钱。

虚拟语气:
If they curtailed their spending, they would have more money. = 如果他们削减开支，他们就会有更多钱。
I wish they had curtailed the project earlier. = 我希望他们早点缩减项目。

被动语态:
The program was curtailed due to lack of interest. = 该项目由于缺乏兴趣而被缩减。
His speech was curtailed by the chairman. = 他的讲话被主席打断了。

疑问句:
Were the benefits curtailed? = 福利被削减了吗？
Will the project be curtailed? = 项目会被缩减吗？

否定句:
They did not curtail their spending. = 他们没有削减开支。
The government will not curtail social programs. = 政府不会削减社会项目。
