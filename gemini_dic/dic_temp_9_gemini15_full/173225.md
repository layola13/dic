quasi-commercially:/ˈkwɑːzaɪ kəˈmɜːrʃəli/|/ˌkwɑːzi kəˈmɜːrʃəli/| adj. |准商业的，似商业的 |Almost but not completely commercial; having some but not all the characteristics of a commercial activity.  It suggests an activity that operates in a way similar to a commercial enterprise but lacks full commercial status or intent. For example, a non-profit organization might engage in quasi-commercial activities to generate revenue to support its mission. |例句：The university's technology transfer office engages in quasi-commercial activities, licensing its research to companies.  大学的技术转让办公室从事准商业活动，将其研究成果许可给公司。

The museum's gift shop, while generating revenue, is considered a quasi-commercial venture due to its primary focus on supporting the museum's educational mission.  尽管博物馆的礼品店能产生收入，但由于其主要目标是支持博物馆的教育使命，因此被认为是一项准商业性企业。


近义词：semi-commercial, near-commercial, commercially-oriented (to some degree)

反义词：purely non-profit, entirely non-commercial, purely charitable


词源:
* **quasi-**: From Latin, meaning "as if," "seemingly," or "apparently."  It indicates a resemblance or approximation.
* **commercially**: From "commerce," ultimately from Latin "commercium," meaning "intercourse," "trade," or "exchange."
