potlatch:/ˈpɑːtlætʃ/ | n. |盛大的赠礼仪式；挥霍性聚会 | A ceremonial gift-giving feast practiced by indigenous peoples of the Pacific Northwest Coast of North America.  It involves a competitive display of wealth and generosity, where hosts give away valuable possessions to their guests.  The potlatch is a significant social and economic institution that reinforces social status and establishes alliances. | The Kwakiutl tribe held a grand potlatch to celebrate the birth of their chief's son.  = 克瓦基特尔部落举行了一场盛大的赠礼仪式来庆祝他们酋长儿子的诞生。| feast, ceremony | stinginess, miserliness | 名词
