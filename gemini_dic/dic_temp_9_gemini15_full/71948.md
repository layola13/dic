flies-out: /flaɪz aʊt/ | v. |飞出，冲出 |  The phrase "flies out" describes something moving quickly and forcefully through the air, often unexpectedly. It implies a sudden and rapid departure. The word "flies" is the third-person singular present tense of the verb "to fly," and "out" is an adverb indicating direction. |


例句：

The bird flies out of the cage.  = 鸟从笼子里飞了出来。

The cork flies out of the bottle when you open it. =当你打开瓶子时，软木塞会飞出来。


近义词：zooms out, dashes out, shoots out, bursts out


反义词：flies in, enters


词性: 动词 (verb)
