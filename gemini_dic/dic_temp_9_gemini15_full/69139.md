favour:/ˈfeɪvər/ | n., v. | 恩惠；喜爱；偏袒；支持；帮忙 |  A kind action;  a thing that one likes or is fond of;  to support or prefer; to help someone. |例句：Could you do me a favour?  你能帮我个忙吗？|近义词：kindness, assistance, help, preference, support |反义词：disfavor, dislike, opposition, hindrance


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I favour the blue dress. = 我喜欢那件蓝色的裙子。
He favours his eldest son. = 他偏袒他的大儿子。


一般过去时:
She favoured him with a smile. = 她对他笑了笑。
The judge favoured the defendant. = 法官偏袒被告。


现在完成时:
They have favoured us with their presence. = 他们赏光来了。
I have always favoured a peaceful solution. = 我一直都赞成和平解决。


过去完成时:
The teacher had favoured him with extra attention. = 老师对他格外关照。
She had always favoured her younger sister. = 她总是偏袒她妹妹。


将来时:
I will favour you with an answer tomorrow. = 我明天会给你答复。
The government will favour the economic growth plan. = 政府将支持经济增长计划。


虚拟语气:
If I were you, I would favour the red one. = 如果我是你，我会选择红色的那个。
I wish they would favour my application. = 我希望他们会批准我的申请。


被动语态:
His proposal was favoured by the committee. = 他的提案得到了委员会的支持。
The blue dress was favoured by many customers. = 那件蓝色的裙子受到了很多顾客的青睐。


疑问句:
Do you favour this plan? = 你赞成这个计划吗？
Does he favour the new policy? = 他支持新的政策吗？


否定句:
I don't favour violence. = 我不支持暴力。
She didn't favour his request. = 她没有答应他的请求。


PHRASE->
in favour of: 支持…；赞成…
out of favour: 失宠；不受欢迎
do sb a favour: 帮某人一个忙
ask a favour of sb: 请求某人帮忙
in one's favour: 对某人有利


词源:
From Old French *favour, from Latin *favor, meaning "kindliness, goodwill".  The modern spelling reflects the influence of the related Latin word "favor."
