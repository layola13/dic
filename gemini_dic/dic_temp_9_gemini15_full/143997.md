outraze:/ˈaʊtreɪz/| v.|使震怒；激怒；使震惊|To shock or horrify someone greatly; to outrage someone's sense of decency or morality.|例句：The judge's decision outraged the public.  法官的判决激怒了公众。|例句：The brutality of the war outraged the world.  战争的残酷震惊了全世界。|近义词：infuriate, incense, appall, scandalize|反义词：please, delight, soothe


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The news outrages many people. = 这个消息激怒了许多人。
His behavior outrages his parents. = 他的行为激怒了他的父母。


一般过去时:
The decision outraged the public. = 这个决定激怒了公众。
Her words outraged her friend. = 她的言语激怒了她的朋友。


现在完成时:
The film has outraged many viewers. = 这部电影激怒了许多观众。
His actions have outraged the community. = 他的行为激怒了整个社区。


过去完成时:
I had been outraged by his behaviour before. = 我以前就曾被他之前的行为激怒过。
They had been outraged by the injustice. = 他们因不公正而感到愤怒。


将来时:
This news will outrage many people. = 这个消息将会激怒许多人。
His actions will outrage the public. = 他的行为将激怒公众。


虚拟语气:
If I knew the truth, I would be outraged. = 如果我知道真相，我会很愤怒。
I wish I hadn't been outraged by such trivial things. = 我真希望我没有因为这些琐事而生气。


被动语态:
The public was outraged by the verdict. = 公众对判决感到愤怒。
The audience was outraged by the actor's behaviour. = 观众对演员的行为感到愤怒。


疑问句:
Does this news outrage you? = 这个消息让你生气吗？
Were you outraged by the article? = 你对这篇文章感到愤怒吗？


否定句:
The news didn't outrage me. = 这个消息并没有让我生气。
The speech didn't outrage the audience. = 这篇演讲并没有激怒观众。
