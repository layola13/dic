quasi-validly: /ˈkwɑːziˈvælɪdli/ | adv. | 准有效地，似是而非地有效 |

英文解析:  The adverb "quasi-validly" describes something that is almost or seemingly valid, but not entirely or strictly so. It suggests a partial or conditional validity, often with some underlying flaw or limitation.  It implies a degree of approximation or resemblance to true validity.

例句:

The argument, while presented quasi-validly, ultimately rests on a flawed premise.  =  这个论点虽然表面上看起来有效，但最终还是建立在一个错误的前提上。


The results, obtained quasi-validly due to limitations in the experimental design, require further investigation. =  由于实验设计上的局限性，这些结果虽然看似有效，但需要进一步调查。


近义词:  partially validly, seemingly validly, conditionally validly, approximately validly


反义词: invalidly, completely invalidly, utterly invalidly


词源:

* **quasi-**:  From Latin, meaning "as if," "almost," or "seemingly."  It indicates a resemblance or approximation to something but not its complete essence.

* **valid**: From Latin "validus," meaning "strong," "powerful," or "effective." In logic and argumentation, it means sound and logically correct.

* **-ly**:  A common English suffix that forms adverbs from adjectives.


USAGE->  (Since "quasi-validly" is an adverb, its usage is primarily within the context of modifying verbs and clauses.  It doesn't have tense variations like verbs.)

Example sentences illustrating its use:

The study proceeded quasi-validly, despite the small sample size. = 尽管样本量很小，这项研究还是以一种似是而非的方式进行了。

He argued quasi-validly, but his conclusions were ultimately unsubstantiated. = 他提出的论点看似合理，但结论最终并未得到证实。


PHRASE->  (Phrases using "quasi-validly" are less common and would depend heavily on context.  It is not typically used in fixed phrases.)
