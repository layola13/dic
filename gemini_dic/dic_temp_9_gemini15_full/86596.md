heliographs:/ˈhiːliəɡræfs/| n. |日光照相，日影仪|A heliograph is a device that uses sunlight reflected from a mirror to send signals over long distances by flashing the light.  It was used in the 19th century for long-distance communication, particularly by the military.  |Example: The soldiers used a heliograph to communicate with their base camp many miles away.|近义词：sun telegraph,  signal mirror|反义词：None
