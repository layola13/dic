dispiriting: /dɪˈspɪrɪtɪŋ/ | adj. |令人沮丧的，使人灰心的 | Causing loss of spirit or morale; disheartening.  |例句：The long, cold winter was dispiriting. 漫长寒冷的冬天令人沮丧。

例句：The news of the company's failure was utterly dispiriting to its employees. 公司倒闭的消息让员工们极其沮丧。


近义词：dejecting, disheartening, depressing, discouraging, demoralizing | 反义词：encouraging, uplifting, inspiring, heartening


词源:  From *dis-* (expressing negation) + *spirit* (mind, courage, vital force) + *-ing* (present participle suffix).  It literally means "taking away spirit".
