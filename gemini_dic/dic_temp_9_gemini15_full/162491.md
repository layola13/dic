preaffirm:/priːəˈfɜːrm/| vt.|预先肯定；预先确认|To affirm or confirm something beforehand or in advance.  It suggests a proactive and anticipatory confirmation, often implying a strong belief or expectation that something will be true or happen.|例句：The council preaffirmed its commitment to environmental protection. 理事会预先确认了其对环境保护的承诺。
例句：She preaffirmed her intention to attend the meeting, despite her busy schedule. 尽管日程繁忙，她还是预先确认了将参加会议的意愿。|近义词：confirm, assure, ratify, endorse|反义词：deny, refute, contradict|动词
