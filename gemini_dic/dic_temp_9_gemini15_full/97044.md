infelicity:/ˌɪnfeˈlɪsəti/| n.| 不幸；不当；不得体；表达不当 | An infelicity is an inappropriate, clumsy, or unfortunate expression or action.  It suggests a lack of grace, suitability, or effectiveness. This can range from a minor awkwardness in phrasing to a serious blunder in social interaction or communication. |例句：The infelicity of his remarks caused considerable embarrassment.  他的话语不得体，引起了相当大的尴尬。


例句：The speaker's infelicity in choosing his words led to a misunderstanding.  说话者用词不当导致了误解。


近义词：awkwardness, inappropriateness, clumsiness, blunder, gaffe, impropriety


反义词：appropriateness, felicity, grace, suitability, aptness


词源：来自拉丁语 *infelix,  in- (不) + felix (幸运的，成功的)。  因此，infelicity 本质上是指缺乏幸运或成功，体现在表达或行动上。
