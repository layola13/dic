coagulations:/koʊæɡjuˈleɪʃənz/ | n. | 凝结；凝固；凝块 | The process of changing from a liquid to a solid or semi-solid state, often due to chemical reaction or enzymatic action.  This term often refers to the clumping together of blood cells or other particles. | Example: Blood coagulation is essential to stop bleeding after an injury.  血液凝固对于受伤后止血至关重要。

The formation of blood clots is a vital part of the body's healing process.  血凝块的形成是人体愈合过程中的重要组成部分。


近义词：clotting, thickening, solidification | 反义词：liquefaction, dissolution
