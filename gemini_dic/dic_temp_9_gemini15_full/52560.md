disenfranchisement: /ˌdɪsɪnˈfrænˌtʃaɪzmənt/ | n. |剥夺公民权；丧失选举权 | The act of depriving someone of the right to vote or participate in political processes.  It often refers to systematic efforts to prevent certain groups from exercising their voting rights. | Example:  The disenfranchisement of Black Americans in the Jim Crow South was a significant impediment to social justice.  剥夺美国黑人在吉姆·克劳南方时代的投票权严重阻碍了社会正义的实现。 |近义词: deprivation of rights, disqualification | 反义词: enfranchisement, empowerment | 名词
