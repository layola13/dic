non-exemplary:/ˌnɒnɪɡˈzɛmpləri/ | adj. |非典型的；不作为榜样的；非示范性的 | Not serving as an example; not typical or representative.  This word describes something that doesn't serve as a good example or model, often because it's atypical or substandard. |例句：His behavior was non-exemplary and led to his dismissal.他的行为不符合规范，导致他被解雇。

The company's response to the crisis was non-exemplary, setting a poor precedent for future incidents. 公司对危机的应对是非典型的，为以后的事件树立了不良先例。|近义词： atypical, untypical, unrepresentative, substandard, poor, inadequate |反义词： exemplary, typical, representative, model, ideal


词源:

* **non-**:  Prefix meaning "not" or "without".
* **exemplary**: Adjective meaning "serving as a desirable model; excellent".  It comes from the Latin word *exemplum*, meaning "example".


用法说明:


The word "non-exemplary" is primarily used as an adjective to describe something that fails to meet a standard or expectation, acting as a negative counterpoint to "exemplary".  It highlights a lack of positive qualities expected in a model or example.  It is often used in formal contexts, such as in reports or critical assessments.
