freshen:/ˈfreʃən/| vt.  |使新鲜；使振作；使精力充沛|To make or become fresh or fresher.  This can refer to physical objects, like air or food, or to people's feelings or appearance.|例句：Let's open the window to freshen the air.  让我们打开窗户使空气清新。

例句：A short walk helped to freshen her up.  短暂的散步使她精神焕发。


近义词：invigorate, refresh, revitalize |反义词：stale, dull, exhaust|动词


USAGE->

He freshens the air with a spray. = 他用喷雾使空气清新。
She freshens up before the meeting. = 她在会议前整理仪容，让自己精神焕发。


一般过去时:

现在完成时:

词源:
The word "freshen" originates from the Old English word "frisc," meaning "fresh."  The suffix "-en" is a verb-forming suffix common in English, indicating the act of making something fresh.  Therefore, "freshen" literally means "to make fresh."
