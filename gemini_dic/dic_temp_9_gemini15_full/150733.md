parachuter:/ˈpærəʃuːtər/ | n. | 跳伞员，伞兵 | A person who parachutes, especially as a member of the armed forces.  | Example: The experienced parachuter landed gracefully in the designated zone.  经验丰富的跳伞员优雅地降落在指定区域。  The army parachuters were deployed behind enemy lines.  伞兵被部署到敌后。 | 近义词: skydiver, airborne trooper | 反义词:  None specifically antonymous, but perhaps "ground trooper" in a military context.


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He parachutes every weekend. = 他每个周末都跳伞。
She parachutes from high altitudes. = 她从高空跳伞。

一般过去时:
They parachuted into the field. = 他们跳伞到田野里。
He parachuted from the plane. = 他从飞机上跳伞。

现在完成时:
I have parachuted many times. = 我跳伞很多次了。
She has never parachuted before. = 她以前从未跳伞过。

过去完成时:
He had parachuted before, so he wasn't nervous. = 他以前跳过伞，所以他不紧张。
They had already parachuted when the storm hit. = 暴风雨来临时，他们已经跳伞了。

将来时:
We will parachute tomorrow. = 我们明天将跳伞。
They will parachute over the city. = 他们将在城市上空跳伞。

虚拟语气:
If I were a parachuter, I would jump from a higher altitude. = 如果我是个跳伞员，我会从更高的海拔跳伞。
I wish I had parachuted last weekend. = 我真希望上周末我跳伞了。

被动语态:
The supplies were parachuted into the disaster zone. = 补给品被空投到灾区。
The soldiers were parachuted behind enemy lines. = 士兵们被空投到敌后。

疑问句:
Has he ever parachuted before? = 他以前跳过伞吗？
When will they parachute? = 他们什么时候跳伞？

否定句:
I didn't parachute yesterday. = 我昨天没有跳伞。
She will not parachute today. = 她今天不会跳伞。
