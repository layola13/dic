euromaidan:/ˌjʊərəʊˈmaɪdæn/| n. | 欧洲广场运动 | The Euromaidan was a series of pro-democracy protests, demonstrations, and civil uprisings in Ukraine that began in late November 2013.  The protests were sparked by the Ukrainian government's decision to suspend the signing of an association agreement with the European Union.  The movement played a significant role in the 2014 Ukrainian revolution. | The Euromaidan protests led to the overthrow of President Viktor Yanukovych. = 欧洲广场运动导致了维克托·亚努科维奇总统的倒台。  The impact of Euromaidan is still felt in Ukrainian politics today. = 欧洲广场运动的影响至今仍体现在乌克兰的政治中。 | Ukrainian revolution, Maidan protests |  anti-Euromaidan (though this term is less common and often used by those opposed to the movement)
