militarist:/ˈmɪlɪtərɪst/| n. adj.|军国主义者；好战分子；军事主义的|A person who advocates for a strong military and the use of military force in international relations.  Someone who believes that military power should be dominant in a country's policies.  As an adjective, it describes something relating to or characteristic of militarism.|例句：He was a staunch militarist, believing that only through military strength could a nation truly prosper. 他是一个坚定的军国主义者，认为只有通过军事实力，一个国家才能真正繁荣。

The rise of militarist ideologies in the early 20th century contributed to the outbreak of World War I. 20世纪初期军国主义意识形态的兴起导致了第一次世界大战的爆发。|近义词：warmonger, hawk, expansionist|反义词：pacifist, dove, appeaser|词性: noun, adjective
