mandate:/ˈmænˌdeɪt/| n. v.|授权；命令；委托；任务|

英文解析：A mandate is an official order or commission to do something.  As a noun, it refers to the authority given to someone to do something, often by an election. As a verb, it means to officially order or authorize something.

例句：
The government has a mandate to reduce taxes.  政府有减税的授权。

The UN mandate requires us to provide humanitarian aid.  联合国的授权要求我们提供人道主义援助。


近义词：authorization, order, command, instruction, commission

反义词：prohibition, ban, veto


词性：Noun, Verb


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government mandates new safety regulations. = 政府强制执行新的安全条例。
The company mandates the use of specific software. = 公司强制使用特定的软件。

一般过去时:
The court mandated the release of the prisoner. = 法院下令释放囚犯。
The president mandated a review of the policy. = 总统下令审查该政策。

现在完成时:
The new law has mandated significant changes. = 新法律已强制实施重大变革。
The committee has mandated a thorough investigation. = 委员会已责成进行彻底调查。

过去完成时:
The judge had mandated a gag order before the trial. = 审判前法官已发布了禁言令。
They had mandated the use of seat belts years ago. = 他们几年前就强制使用安全带了。

将来时:
The council will mandate the construction of a new bridge. = 理事会将强制建造一座新桥。
The company will mandate a new dress code next year. = 公司明年将强制执行新的着装规范。

虚拟语气:
If the court mandated it, we would comply. = 如果法院强制执行，我们会遵守。
I wish the government mandated better environmental protection. = 我希望政府强制执行更好的环境保护措施。

被动语态:
New regulations were mandated by the government. = 新规章由政府强制执行。
A thorough investigation is being mandated by the committee. = 委员会正在责成进行彻底调查。

疑问句:
Does the law mandate this action? = 这项法律是否强制要求采取此行动？
What does the contract mandate? = 合同规定了什么？

否定句:
The company does not mandate overtime work. = 公司不要求加班。
The agreement did not mandate any specific action. = 协议没有强制要求任何具体的行动。
