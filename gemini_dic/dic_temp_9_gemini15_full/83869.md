half-abandoned: /ˌhæf əˈbændənd/ | adj. | 半被遗弃的，半废弃的 | Partially abandoned; in a state of neglect but not completely deserted.  The term suggests a gradual or incomplete process of abandonment. |例句：The half-abandoned farmhouse stood silently amidst overgrown weeds.  = 半废弃的农舍静静地矗立在杂草丛生的环境中。

The old mine shafts were half-abandoned, a dangerous reminder of the town's past. =  那些旧矿井半被废弃，是这座城镇过去的危险提醒。

近义词：partially deserted, neglected, derelict (more severe), forsaken (more emotional)

反义词：inhabited, occupied, well-maintained, thriving


词性：形容词

词源:

The word is a compound of "half" (meaning "to a certain extent" or "partially") and "abandoned" (from the verb "abandon," ultimately from Old French "abandonner").  Therefore, it describes something that is only partly in a state of abandonment.
