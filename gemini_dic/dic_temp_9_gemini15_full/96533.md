indolin: /ˈɪndəˌlɪn/ | n. | 二氢吲哚 | Indoline is a bicyclic organic compound consisting of a benzene ring fused to a pyrrolidine ring.  It is a colorless liquid that is soluble in organic solvents.  It is used as a precursor in the synthesis of various indole derivatives which have important applications in pharmaceuticals and other fields. | Example:  Indoline derivatives are frequently used in the synthesis of pharmaceuticals.  二氢吲哚衍生物常用于药物合成。 |近义词：暂无直接近义词，可根据语境用indole或其他相关化合物替代| 反义词：暂无 | 词性:名词

词源:  The word "indoline" is derived from "indole"  with the addition of the suffix "-ine," indicating a related but slightly different chemical structure. Indole itself is ultimately from the Latin *indicum, referring to indigo dye, from which many indole derivatives are obtained.
