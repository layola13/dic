medicalization:/ˌmɛdɪkəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. | 医疗化；医学化 | The process by which non-medical problems become defined and treated as medical problems, usually in terms of illnesses or disorders.  This often involves the expansion of medical authority and influence into areas of life previously considered outside of the medical domain.  | Example: The medicalization of childbirth has led to increased intervention in the birthing process.  医学化分娩导致分娩过程中干预增多。|近义词：medicalisation (British spelling) |反义词：demedicalization
