shipping:/ˈʃɪpɪŋ/| n. v.|航运；运输；装运；托运|The process of transporting goods by sea, air, or land.  It can refer to the goods themselves, the act of transporting them, or the industry involved. |例句：The shipping of the goods was delayed due to bad weather. 货物运输因恶劣天气而延误。

例句：The shipping costs were higher than expected. 运费比预期的要高。


近义词：transportation, conveyance, freightage |反义词：receiving, delivery (in the sense of the opposite action)


词性：Noun and Verb


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He ships his products overseas. 他把他的产品运往海外。
The company ships goods worldwide. 这家公司在全球范围内运送货物。


一般过去时:
They shipped the package yesterday. 他们昨天寄出了包裹。
We shipped the furniture last week. 我们上周运送了家具。


现在完成时:
We have shipped all the orders. 我们已经寄出了所有订单。
They have shipped the goods to the client. 他们已将货物运送给客户。


过去完成时:
The goods had been shipped before the payment arrived. 付款到达之前，货物已经发货了。
They had shipped the package before the deadline. 他们在截止日期前寄出了包裹。


将来时:
We will ship the order tomorrow. 我们明天将寄出订单。
They will ship the goods next week. 他们下周将运送货物。


虚拟语气:
If we shipped the goods earlier, we would have avoided the delay. 如果我们早点发货，就能避免延误。
I wish we had shipped the goods sooner. 我真希望我们早点发货。


被动语态:
The goods are shipped by sea. 货物通过海运运输。
The order was shipped last week. 订单已于上周发货。


疑问句:
When will the goods be shipped? 货物什么时候发货？
Has the shipment been confirmed? 货物运输已确认了吗？


否定句:
They didn't ship the goods on time. 他们没有按时发货。
We haven't shipped the order yet. 我们还没有发货。
