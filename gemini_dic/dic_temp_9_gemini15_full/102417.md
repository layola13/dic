jack-in-the-box:/dʒæk ɪn ðə bɒks/| n. |玩具盒; 突然出现的人或事物 |A small toy consisting of a figure that springs out of a box when the lid is opened.  It is often used as a children's toy or a metaphor for something unexpected or surprising.|例句：The children screamed with delight when the jack-in-the-box popped up. 孩子们看到玩具盒里的小人跳出来时高兴地尖叫起来。|例句：His sudden appearance at the party was like a jack-in-the-box. 他突然出现在派对上，就像一个从盒子里跳出来的小人一样。|近义词：surprise, unexpected event |反义词：predictable, expected
