goggle:/ˈɡɒɡl/ /ˈɡɑːɡəl/| n. v.|护目镜；瞪眼|Protective eyewear that covers the eyes and often the surrounding area; to stare with one's eyes wide open.|例句：He wore goggles to protect his eyes from the chemicals. 他戴着护目镜以保护眼睛免受化学品的伤害。|近义词：spectacles, eye protection, to stare, to glare|反义词：squint, glance

词源:
The word "goggle" likely comes from the Middle English word "goglen," which meant "to look askance."  The modern meaning evolved over time, associating the wide-eyed stare with the protective eyewear designed to shield the eyes.
