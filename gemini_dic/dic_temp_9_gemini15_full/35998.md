codex:/ˈkoʊdɛks/ | n. |法典；古抄本；手稿|A codex is a book made of sheets of paper or papyrus, bound together at one edge.  It's a manuscript, often an ancient one,  that's been compiled in this way, as opposed to a scroll.  The term often refers to important legal or religious texts. |例句：The Codex Sinaiticus is a very important early biblical manuscript.  |近义词：manuscript, scroll, volume |反义词：N/A
