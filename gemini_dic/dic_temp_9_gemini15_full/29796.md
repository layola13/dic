caucusing: /ˈkɔːkəsɪŋ/ | n. | 党团会议；秘密会议 | The act of holding a caucus; a meeting of members of a political party or faction to select candidates or plan strategy.  |例句：The Democrats held a caucus to discuss their strategy for the upcoming election.  民主党举行了一次党团会议来讨论即将到来的选举策略。

例句：The senators were caucusing in a private room before the vote. 参议员们在投票前在一个私人房间里举行秘密会议。|近义词：meeting, conference, convention |反义词：public meeting, open discussion |词性:名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The committee is caucusing to decide on the budget. = 委员会正在开会讨论预算。
They caucus regularly to coordinate their efforts. = 他们定期举行党团会议以协调工作。

一般过去时:
The party caucused last night to nominate a candidate. = 该党昨晚举行党团会议提名候选人。
The senators caucused before the crucial vote. = 参议员们在关键投票前举行了党团会议。

现在完成时:
They have caucused several times this week. = 他们本周已经开过几次会了。
The group has caucused to finalize their platform. = 该团体已经开会最终确定了他们的纲领。

过去完成时:
They had caucused before the announcement was made. = 在宣布之前，他们已经开过会了。
The committee had caucused extensively on the issue. = 委员会已经就这个问题进行了广泛的讨论。

将来时:
The party will caucus next week to select their nominee. = 该党将于下周举行党团会议来挑选他们的候选人。
They will caucus before making a final decision. = 他们将在做出最终决定之前举行党团会议。


虚拟语气:
If they had caucused earlier, they might have avoided the conflict. = 如果他们早点开会，他们可能就能避免冲突。
It would be beneficial if the group caucused more frequently. = 如果该团体更频繁地开会，那将是有益的。
