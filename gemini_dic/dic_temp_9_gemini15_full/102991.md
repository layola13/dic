jeerer:/ˈdʒɪərər/| n.|嘲笑者，挖苦者|A person who jeers; a scoffer.  |例句：The jeerer in the crowd shouted insults at the losing team.  人群中的嘲笑者向输球的队伍大声喊叫侮辱性的话。|近义词：mocker, scoffer, taunter|反义词：supporter, admirer


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He jeers at the homeless people. = 他嘲笑无家可归的人。
The jeerer makes fun of everyone. = 嘲笑者取笑每一个人。


一般过去时:
They jeered at the politician's speech. = 他们嘲笑这位政治家的演讲。

现在完成时:

词源:
The word "jeerer" comes from the verb "jeer," which is of uncertain origin. Some etymologists suggest a possible connection to Old Norse or Old French, but no definitive source has been established.  It entered English around the 16th century.
