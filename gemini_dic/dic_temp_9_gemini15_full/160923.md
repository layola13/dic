post-mortems:/ˈpoʊst ˈmɔːrtəmz/| n.|事后分析，验尸报告|A post-mortem is a discussion or examination held after an event, typically a failure or setback, to analyze what happened and learn from mistakes.  It aims to identify causes, contributing factors, and potential improvements to prevent similar incidents in the future.  The term originates from the medical term for an autopsy, but is now widely used in various fields.|例句：The team held a post-mortem to determine the causes of the software crash.  团队进行了一次事后分析以确定软件崩溃的原因。|The company conducted a thorough post-mortem after the product launch failed. 公司在产品发布失败后进行了彻底的事后分析。|近义词：review, analysis, evaluation, autopsy (in a medical context)|反义词：prevention, anticipation|名词
