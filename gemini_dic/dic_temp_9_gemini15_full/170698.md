pseudoanaemias:/ˌsuːdoʊəˈniːmiəz/| n.|假性贫血|Apparent anemia (reduction in the number of red blood cells or quantity of hemoglobin) that isn't due to a true blood disorder but rather a condition that mimics the symptoms.  This can be caused by various factors that dilute the blood or alter the red blood cells’ appearance, leading to a false reading of anemia during a blood test.|Example:  Nutritional deficiencies can sometimes lead to pseudoanaemias, where the blood values appear anemic but the underlying cause isn't a blood disease.|近义词：relative anemia, apparent anemia|反义词：true anemia
