macrodactyly: /ˌmækrəˈdæktɪli/ | adj. n.|大指症，巨指（症）|  Characterized by abnormally large fingers or toes.  The term refers to a condition where one or more digits are significantly larger than normal, often disproportionate to the rest of the hand or foot. This can be caused by various genetic conditions, tumors, or other medical issues. | Example:  The child was diagnosed with macrodactyly, requiring surgery to correct the oversized fingers.  孩子被诊断出患有巨指症，需要手术矫正过大的手指。|近义词：megalodactyly|反义词：microdactyly|名词，形容词
