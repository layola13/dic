nervous: /ˈnɜːr.vəs/ | adj. | 紧张的，焦虑的 | Feeling worried and anxious.  | 例句：She felt nervous before the interview. 她面试前感到紧张。

例句：He was too nervous to speak. 他紧张得说不出话来。


近义词：anxious, apprehensive, uneasy, tense, stressed | 反义词：calm, relaxed, composed, tranquil


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is nervous about the exam. 他对考试感到紧张。
She is always nervous when speaking in public. 她公开演讲时总是很紧张。

一般过去时:
I was nervous before my first date. 我第一次约会前很紧张。
They were nervous about the flight delay. 他们对航班延误感到紧张。

现在完成时:
I have been nervous all day. 我一整天都紧张兮兮的。
She has never been so nervous in her life. 她一生从未如此紧张过。

过去完成时:
I had been nervous all week leading up to the presentation.  我在演讲前的整个星期都很紧张。
They had been nervous about the results, waiting for the announcement. 他们一直在焦急地等待结果公布。

将来时:
I will be nervous until the results are announced. 在结果公布之前我都会很紧张。
She will probably be nervous during the performance.  她表演期间可能会很紧张。

虚拟语气:
If I weren't nervous, I would perform better. 如果我不紧张，我会表现得更好。
I wish I weren't so nervous right now. 我希望我现在不要这么紧张。

被动语态:  (This adjective is rarely used in the passive voice.)  *He was made nervous by the loud noise.* (他被巨大的噪音弄得很紧张。)

疑问句:
Are you nervous about the upcoming trip? 你对即将到来的旅行感到紧张吗？
Is he nervous about the interview? 他对面试感到紧张吗？

否定句:
I wasn't nervous at all. 我一点也不紧张。
She wasn't nervous despite the difficult situation. 尽管情况艰难，她却一点也不紧张。


PHRASE->
nervous breakdown: 精神崩溃
nervous energy: 紧张不安的精力
nervous system: 神经系统
get nervous: 变得紧张
make someone nervous: 使某人紧张
