gig:/ɡɪɡ/ | n. |演出，零工，大量工作 | A single performance or job, especially one that is short-term or freelance.  | 例句：He landed a great gig playing guitar in a popular band. 他得到了一份很棒的演出，在一个受欢迎的乐队里弹吉他。 | 例句：She picked up a few gigs to make extra money. 她接了几份零工来赚外快。 | 近义词：job, concert, performance, assignment | 反义词：permanent job, full-time employment | 名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He gigs regularly at that club. = 他经常在那家俱乐部演出。
She finds gigs through an online platform. = 她通过一个在线平台找零工。

一般过去时:
They gigged all summer. = 他们整个夏天都在演出。
He gigged at a local festival last weekend. = 他上周末在当地节日演出。

现在完成时:
I have gigged in many different cities. = 我在许多不同的城市演出过。
She has gigged with some famous musicians. = 她曾与一些著名的音乐家一起演出。

过去完成时:
He had gigged for years before he got his record deal. = 在他得到唱片合约之前，他已经演出多年了。
They had gigged in small venues before moving to larger ones. = 他们之前在小型场地演出过，然后才搬到较大的场地。

将来时:
We will gig in London next month. = 我们下个月将在伦敦演出。
She will gig at the jazz festival. = 她将在爵士音乐节演出。

虚拟语气:
If I could gig every night, I would. = 如果我每晚都能演出，我会的。
I wish I could gig in that famous venue. = 我希望我能在那个著名的场地演出。

被动语态:
The band was gigging in a small town.  = 乐队在一个小镇上演出。
The musician was gigged at the festival. = 这位音乐家在音乐节上演出。

疑问句:
Are you gigging this weekend? = 你这个周末演出吗？
Where are they gigging tonight? = 他们今晚在哪里演出？

否定句:
He doesn't gig often. = 他不经常演出。
She didn't gig last night. = 她昨晚没有演出。
