generated:/ˈdʒɛnəreɪtɪd/ | adj. |生成的，产生的|  Produced or created; brought into existence.  The term often implies a process or system that creates something new. |例句：The software generated a report summarizing the data.  该软件生成了一个总结数据的报告。|例句：The storm generated high winds and heavy rain.  暴风雨产生了强风和暴雨。|近义词：produced, created, formed, made|反义词：destroyed, eliminated, ended


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The system generates reports automatically. = 系统自动生成报告。
This machine generates electricity. = 这台机器发电。

一般过去时:
The artist generated a stunning piece of art. = 艺术家创作了一件令人惊叹的艺术作品。
The earthquake generated a tsunami. = 地震引发了海啸。

现在完成时:
The company has generated significant profits this year. = 公司今年获得了可观的利润。
We have generated a lot of new leads. = 我们已经产生了大量的潜在客户。

过去完成时:
They had generated enough data before the analysis. = 他们在分析之前已经生成了足够的数据。
The volcano had generated a massive cloud of ash. = 火山喷发出大量的火山灰云。

将来时:
The new technology will generate more jobs. = 新技术将创造更多就业机会。
The project will generate significant revenue. = 该项目将产生可观的收入。

虚拟语气:
If the project were successful, it would generate a lot of income. = 如果项目成功，它将产生大量收入。
I wish the machine generated more power. = 我希望这台机器能产生更多的电力。

被动语态:
The report was generated by the computer. = 报告是由电脑生成的。
The electricity is generated by a hydroelectric dam. = 电力是由水电大坝产生的。

疑问句:
How is the energy generated? = 能量是如何产生的？
What kind of reports are generated by the system? = 系统生成什么样的报告？

否定句:
The experiment did not generate the expected results. = 实验没有产生预期的结果。
The engine failed to generate sufficient power. = 发动机未能产生足够的动力。
