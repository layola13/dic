implicity: /ɪmˈplɪsɪti/ | n. |含蓄；暗示；隐含；不直接表达 |The state of being implicit; something implied but not expressed directly.  It refers to something that is understood or implied without being directly stated.  |例句：The agreement was reached with a degree of implicity that surprised everyone.  协议以一种让每个人都感到惊讶的含蓄方式达成。

There was an implicity in her tone that suggested she disapproved. 她语气中含蓄地暗示了她不赞成。


近义词：implication, inference, suggestion, insinuation |反义词：explicitness, directness


词源:  From Latin *implicitus, past participle of implicare "to enfold, involve," from in- "in" + plicare "to fold."  The sense development is from "something folded in" to "something implied but not explicitly stated."
