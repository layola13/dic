deputised:/ˈdɛpjʊtaɪzd/| v.|代理；授权；委任|To act or serve as a deputy or substitute for someone; to appoint someone as a deputy.|例句：The manager deputised for the CEO during his absence.  经理在他缺席期间代理首席执行官。|例句：The sheriff deputised several volunteers to help with crowd control. 警长委任了几名志愿者来帮助维持秩序。|近义词：represent, substitute, delegate|反义词：None specifically, but antonyms could be related to the context, such as "to refuse," or "to neglect".


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He deputizes for the manager when he is away. = 他经理不在的时候代理经理。
She deputizes for her sister at the family business. = 她代理她姐姐在家族企业工作。


一般过去时:
The teacher deputised for the principal at the assembly. = 老师在集会上代表校长。
He deputised for his colleague at the conference. = 他在会议上代理他的同事。


现在完成时:
She has deputised for her boss on several occasions. = 她已经多次代理她的老板。
He has deputised for the team leader throughout the project. = 他在整个项目中一直代理团队领导。


过去完成时:
They had deputised for the head of department before the crisis. = 危机发生前，他们已经代理部门主管。
She had deputised for her colleague many times in the past. = 她过去多次代理她的同事。


将来时:

虚拟语气:
If he were to be absent, I would deputise for him. = 如果他缺席，我会代理他。
I wish she had deputised for me at the meeting. = 我希望她在会议上能代理我。


被动语态:
A substitute was deputised to take charge. = 一名代理人被委任负责。
Several volunteers were deputised to assist with the event. = 几名志愿者被委任协助活动。


疑问句:

否定句:
