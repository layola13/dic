leading-out: /ˈliːdɪŋ aʊt/ | v. |引导出去，带领离开 |To guide or escort someone or something out of a place.  It often implies a more formal or deliberate act of guiding than simply "leading." |例句：The usher leading-out the audience after the performance.  引导观众在演出结束后离开。

The fire marshal led-out the occupants of the building. 消防队长带领大楼里的住户撤离。


近义词：escorting, guiding, conducting, ushering |反义词：leading in, bringing in

词源:
The word "leading-out" is a combination of "lead" and "out."  "Lead" comes from Old English *lǣdan, meaning "to lead, guide, conduct."  "Out" is also of Old English origin, *ūt, meaning "out, outside." The phrasal verb thus literally means "to lead outwards."
