do-or-die: /ˌduː ɔːr ˈdaɪ/ | adj. | 孤注一掷的；不成功便成仁的 |  Describes a situation or action where success is essential, and failure has severe consequences.  It implies a final, decisive attempt with no room for retreat. | 例句：It's a do-or-die situation; we must win this contract to stay afloat.  = 这是一场孤注一掷的局面；我们必须赢得这份合同才能维持下去。  

The team launched a do-or-die attack in the final minutes of the game. = 球队在比赛最后几分钟发起了孤注一掷的进攻。


近义词：critical, crucial, decisive, desperate, all-or-nothing, sink-or-swim

反义词：cautious, tentative, gradual, measured


词源:  The phrase is a compound of the verbs "do" and "die," reflecting the binary outcome of the situation.  It's an idiom that arose in the English language over time, its exact origin difficult to pinpoint, but reflecting the high-stakes nature of many historical endeavors.  The meaning reflects the risk and commitment involved.
