domestications:/ˌdəʊmɛstɪˈkeɪʃənz/ | n. |驯化；家养；使驯服|The process of bringing plants or animals under human control and care, often involving selective breeding to enhance desirable traits.  It refers to the process by which wild plants and animals are adapted for human use.  This can involve changes in the organism's genetics, behavior, and morphology. |例句：The domestications of dogs and cats occurred thousands of years ago.  狗和猫的驯化发生在几千年前。

The domestications of various crops revolutionized human civilization. 各种作物的驯化彻底改变了人类文明。|近义词：taming, cultivation, breeding |反义词：wildness, untamed

词源:
Domestication comes from the Latin word "domesticus," meaning "belonging to the house" or "of the home."  It's a combination of "domus" (house) and "-icus" (belonging to). The suffix "-ation" indicates the process or action of something.
