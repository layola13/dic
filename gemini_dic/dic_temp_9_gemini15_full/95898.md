incredulity: /ˌɪnkrɛdʒuˈlɪti/ | n. | 怀疑；不相信；难以置信 |  A feeling of disbelief; the state of being incredulous.  It often implies a strong reaction to something unbelievable or unexpected. |

例句：

His story was met with incredulity.  = 他的故事遭到了怀疑。

The sheer scale of the disaster filled everyone with incredulity. = 灾难的巨大规模让每个人都难以置信。


近义词：disbelief, skepticism, doubt, mistrust, suspicion

反义词：belief, acceptance, faith, credence

词源:

From Latin *incredulus "not believing," from in- "not" + credere "to believe."  The suffix "-ity" forms nouns of state or quality.
