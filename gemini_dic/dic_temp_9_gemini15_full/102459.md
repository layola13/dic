jackknifing: /ˈdʒækˌnaɪfɪŋ/ | v. |  车辆突然侧翻；(尤指车辆)突然剧烈旋转；折叠；(指刀)快速折叠 |

英文解析: To jackknife means to fold or bend sharply in the middle, like a jackknife (a folding pocketknife). When used to describe a vehicle, it usually refers to a sudden, uncontrolled sideways swing or turn, often resulting in the vehicle’s trailer or rear portion swinging out and potentially causing an accident.  This is typically caused by braking too hard or losing control on slippery surfaces.


例句:

The truck jackknifed on the icy road.  =卡车在结冰的路上突然侧翻。

The trailer jackknifed, blocking the highway. = 拖车突然剧烈旋转，堵塞了高速公路。



近义词:  sideswipe, skid, overturn, collapse

反义词:  straighten, stabilize

PHRASE->

There are limited common phrases directly using "jackknifing".  The verb is primarily used independently to describe the action.  However, phrases describing the *result* of jackknifing are common:

Jackknifed trailer: 侧翻的拖车
Jackknifed lorry: 侧翻的卡车
Jackknifed on the highway: 在高速公路上侧翻
Sudden jackknifing: 突然侧翻
Avoid jackknifing: 避免侧翻

词源:  The word comes from the action of a jackknife, a folding knife, sharply folding in half.  The application to vehicles reflects the similar sharp bending motion.
