creakiest:/ˈkriːkieɪst/| adj.|最吱吱作响的，最破旧的|most likely to creak; showing the effects of age and wear; dilapidated|The creakiest door in the house finally gave way.  = 房子里最吱吱作响的门终于坏了。 The old house, with its creakiest stairs, felt like it might collapse at any moment. = 那栋老房子，带着它最吱吱作响的楼梯，感觉随时都可能倒塌。| rickety, shaky, ramshackle | sturdy, solid, robust |


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The old chair creaks every time I sit on it. = 我每次坐在那把旧椅子上，它都会吱吱作响。
That wooden floor creaks underfoot. = 那木地板踩上去会吱吱响。


一般过去时:
The floorboard creaked loudly as he walked across the room. = 他穿过房间时，地板发出很大的吱吱声。
The old bed creaked throughout the night. = 老床整夜都在吱吱作响。


现在完成时:
The hinges have creaked for years, but they've never broken. = 合页已经吱吱作响多年了，但从未坏过。
That door has creaked since I moved in. = 自从我搬进来，那扇门就一直吱吱作响。


过去完成时:
The old swing had creaked for years before it finally broke. = 那把旧秋千吱吱作响了好几年才最终坏掉。
The floorboards had creaked ominously before the collapse. = 地板在倒塌之前发出了不祥的吱吱声。


将来时:
I think that chair will creak when you sit on it. = 我想你坐在那把椅子上它会吱吱作响。
That old house will creak and groan in the wind. = 那栋老房子会在风中吱吱作响和呻吟。


虚拟语气:
If the floorboards creaked less, I would sleep better. = 如果地板吱吱作响的声音小一些，我会睡得更好。
I wish the stairs didn't creak so loudly. = 我希望楼梯不要吱吱响得那么大声。


被动语态:

疑问句:
