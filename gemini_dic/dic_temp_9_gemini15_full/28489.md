carcinomas:/ˌkɑːrsɪˈnoʊməz/| n.|癌瘤；癌；癌肿| Malignant tumors of epithelial origin. Carcinomas are a type of cancer that develops from epithelial cells, which are cells that line the surfaces of the body, such as the skin, organs, and lining of body cavities.  They are the most common type of cancer.  Different types of carcinomas are classified based on the location and type of epithelial cells from which they originate.| Example: Lung carcinomas are a leading cause of cancer-related deaths. 肺癌是癌症相关死亡的主要原因。  The patient was diagnosed with basal cell carcinoma. 病人被诊断为基底细胞癌。|近义词：cancer, malignancy, tumor|反义词：benign tumor
