lycopods:/ˈlaɪkəpɒdz/| n. |石松类植物|Lycopods are a group of vascular plants that includes club mosses, spike mosses, and quillworts. They are characterized by their small, scale-like leaves and their branching stems.  Many lycopods are epiphytes, growing on other plants, while others are terrestrial. They are an ancient lineage of plants, with fossils dating back to the Devonian period. |  The ancient forests of the Carboniferous period were dominated by giant lycopods.  |近义词:club mosses, spike mosses, quillworts|反义词: (无明确反义词，可考虑与种子植物等相对)|名词
