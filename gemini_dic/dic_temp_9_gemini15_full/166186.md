prepsychologies:/ˌpriːˈsaɪkɒlədʒiz/ | n. |前心理学理论，心理学前史 |  The study of the various systems of thought and practices that preceded the development of modern psychology, including folk psychology, early philosophical inquiries into the mind, and pre-scientific approaches to mental health.  This field explores the intellectual and cultural context from which modern psychology emerged.  | Example:  The course on the history of psychology began with a survey of prepsychologies, examining ancient Greek conceptions of the soul and medieval understandings of madness. = 这门心理学史课程从前心理学理论的概述开始，考察了古希腊对灵魂的观念和中世纪对疯狂的理解。 | 近义词：proto-psychology, pre-scientific psychology | 反义词：modern psychology
