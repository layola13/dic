misreader: /ˈmɪsˌriːdər/ | n. | 误读者，曲解者 | A person who misinterprets or misunderstands written or spoken material.  Someone who fails to grasp the intended meaning. |例句：He's a notorious misreader of contracts, often causing disputes. 他是个臭名昭著的合同误读者，经常引发纠纷。

The misreader of the poem missed the subtle irony.  这首诗的误读者错过了其微妙的讽刺意味。|近义词：misinterpreter, wrong interpreter |反义词：expert reader, insightful reader

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He misreads the instructions frequently. = 他经常误读说明。
She misreads the situation consistently. = 她总是误读情况。


一般过去时:
He misread the map and got lost. = 他读错地图迷路了。
She misread the email and responded inappropriately. = 她读错了邮件并做出了不恰当的回应。


现在完成时:

否定句:

词源:
The word "misreader" is a compound word formed from the prefix "mis-" (meaning wrongly or badly) and the noun "reader."  The suffix "-er" indicates an agent or doer of an action. Therefore, it literally means "one who reads wrongly."
