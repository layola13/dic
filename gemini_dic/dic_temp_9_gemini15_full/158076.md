plasteriness:/ˈplæstərɪnəs/| n. |粉质感，呆板，做作|The quality of being like plaster; a lack of naturalness or spontaneity; artificiality.  It can refer to a texture, appearance, or manner.  |例句：The actor's performance lacked subtlety, showing a distinct plasteriness.  演员的表演缺乏微妙之处，显得相当做作。

The painting's plasteriness was evident in its overly smooth surface. 这幅画的粉质感在其过于光滑的表面上显而易见。|近义词：artificiality, stiffness, formality, affectation, woodenness |反义词：naturalness, spontaneity, genuineness, realism


USAGE->
This word is primarily a noun and doesn't conjugate like verbs.  Therefore, tense considerations are not applicable.  The usage focuses on describing a quality or characteristic.

词源:
The word "plasteriness" is derived from the noun "plaster," meaning a paste used for coating surfaces.  The suffix "-iness" adds the sense of a quality or characteristic.  The implication is that something possessing "plasteriness" is stiff, unnatural, or lacking in life or flexibility, like a plaster cast.
