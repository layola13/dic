inhere:/ɪnˈhɪr/| v.|内在存在；固有；|to exist as a permanent, essential, or characteristic attribute; to be inherent in something.|The right to free speech inheres in all citizens.言论自由的权利是所有公民固有的。|exist, reside, be inherent|


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The property of color inheres in the object itself.  = 颜色的属性存在于物体本身。
This principle inheres in the very nature of democracy. = 这个原则内在于民主的本质。


一般过去时:
The right to self-determination inhered in the people. = 自决权属于人民。
Many problems inhered in the original plan. = 原计划中存在许多问题。


现在完成时:
These rights have inhered in the community for centuries. = 这些权利在社区中存在了几个世纪。
The flaw has inhered in the system since its inception. = 自从系统创建以来，这个缺陷就存在了。


过去完成时:
The belief had inhered in society for generations.  = 这种信仰在社会中传承了几代人。
It had inhered in his family for many years. = 它在他的家族中存在了很多年。


将来时:
The power will inhere in the newly elected president. = 权力将属于新当选的总统。
These qualities will inhere in the next generation. = 这些品质将遗传给下一代。


虚拟语气:
If this right did inhere in us by nature, we would not need laws. = 如果这项权利天生就属于我们，我们就无需法律。
If the problem had inhered in the design, it would have been harder to fix. = 如果问题存在于设计中，那么解决起来就会更难。


被动语态:
The authority is inhered in the constitution.  = 权力赋予宪法。
The responsibility was inhered in the management team. = 责任归属于管理团队。


疑问句:
Does the right to vote inhere in all citizens? = 所有公民都享有投票权吗？
Where does the problem inhere? = 问题出在哪里？


否定句:
The problem does not inhere in the software. = 问题不在于软件。
The success did not inhere in luck alone. = 成功并非仅仅取决于运气。
