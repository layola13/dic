disfellowship: /dɪsˈfɛloʊʃɪp/ | n., vt. |除名；开除；逐出教派 | The act of excluding someone from a fellowship or community, especially a religious one; to exclude someone from a fellowship or community. |

英文解析:Disfellowship is the act of formally expelling someone from a group, organization, or community, often due to a violation of rules or beliefs.  It's commonly used in religious contexts, particularly within certain denominations of Christianity, to describe the exclusion of a member deemed to have acted against the faith or its principles.  However, it can also apply to secular groups.

例句:

The church decided to disfellowship the member for spreading false teachings.  = 教堂决定因散布错误教义而将该成员除名。

He was disfellowshipped from the society for unethical conduct. = 他因不道德行为而被逐出该协会。


近义词: expel, exclude, ostracize, banish, excommunicate

反义词: include, accept, welcome, integrate


词性: noun, verb
