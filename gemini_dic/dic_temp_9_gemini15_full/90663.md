humble:/ˈhʌmbl/| adj.|谦逊的；卑微的；简陋的|modest and respectful; not arrogant or proud.  It can also describe something small and insignificant or something simple and unpretentious.|例句：He was humble despite his great success. 他尽管获得了巨大的成功，仍然很谦逊。

例句：She lived in a humble cottage. 她住在一间简陋的小屋里。


近义词：modest, meek, unassuming, lowly |反义词：proud, arrogant, haughty, boastful


词源：源自古法语 *humble，源于拉丁语 *humilis，意思是“低矮的”，由 *humus（“地面”）构成。


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is humble in his dealings with others. = 他待人接物很谦逊。
She remains humble despite her achievements. = 尽管她取得了成就，她仍然保持谦逊。


一般过去时:
He was humble in victory. = 他胜而不骄。
They were humble in their requests. = 他们的要求很谦逊。


现在完成时:
She has remained humble throughout her career. = 她的职业生涯一直保持谦逊。
He has humbled himself before his mistakes. = 他为自己的错误而自责。


过去完成时:
He had humbled himself before the king. = 他在国王面前自谦。
She had been humble in her approach to the problem. = 她处理问题的方式很谦逊。


将来时:
He will be humble in his acceptance of the award. = 他将谦逊地接受这个奖项。
They will remain humble even if they win. = 即使他们赢了，他们也会保持谦逊。


虚拟语气:
If he were more humble, he would have more friends. = 如果他更谦逊一些，他就会有更多朋友。
I wish I had been more humble in that situation. = 我希望当时我能更谦逊一些。


被动语态:
His achievements were humbled by his modesty. = 他的成就因他的谦逊而黯然失色。（也可理解为他的谦逊使他的成就显得不那么突出）
He was humbled by the experience. = 他被这次经历所挫败/使他变得谦卑。


疑问句:
Is he truly humble, or just pretending? = 他是真的谦逊，还是在装腔作势？
Are they humble enough to admit their mistakes? = 他们足够谦逊以承认自己的错误吗？


否定句:
He is not humble at all; he's very arrogant. = 他一点也不谦逊；他非常傲慢。
She wasn't humble in her victory speech. = 她在胜利的演讲中并不谦逊。
