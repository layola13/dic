parliaments: /ˈpɑːrləmənts/ | n. |议会；国会 | A parliament is a formal assembly of representatives who have been elected to govern a country or region.  It is responsible for making laws, debating important issues, and overseeing the government.  The specific powers and functions of a parliament vary depending on the country's political system. | Example: The British Parliament consists of the House of Commons and the House of Lords.  英国议会由下议院和上议院组成。

The Australian Parliament passed a new law last week. 澳大利亚议会上周通过了一项新法律。


近义词: legislature, congress, assembly, diet (in Japan)
反义词: None (no direct antonym, but could consider terms indicating lack of representative government, like dictatorship or autocracy)

词性: noun

词源:  From Old French *parlement, from parler "to speak", ultimately from Latin *parabolare "to speak beside", from Greek παρα- (para-) "beside" + βολή (bolē) "a throw, a casting".  The original meaning referred to a place of speaking or discussion, evolving to its modern political sense.
