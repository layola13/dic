connive:/kəˈnaɪv/ | v. |暗中勾结；纵容；默许 | to secretly plan with someone to do something that is wrong or illegal; to secretly allow something bad to happen | 例句：They connived to overthrow the government. = 他们密谋推翻政府。

They connived at his wrongdoing. = 他们纵容他的恶行。


近义词：plot, scheme, conspire, collude | 反义词：oppose, prevent, obstruct


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He connives with his rivals to fix prices. = 他与竞争对手串通起来操纵价格。
They often connive at illegal activities. = 他们经常纵容非法活动。


一般过去时:

She connived at her son's lies. = 她纵容她儿子的谎言。

虚拟语气:

PHRASE->
connive at = 纵容
connive with = 与…串通
connive in = 参与
connive to = 密谋
connive against = 密谋反对


词源:
The word "connive" comes from the Old French word "conniver," meaning "to wink," "to close one's eyes," or "to be blind to."  This reflects the idea of secretly allowing something to happen.  The sense of secret plotting evolved later.
