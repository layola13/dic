reproofed:/rɪˈpruːft/| v. | 斥责；谴责；纠正 | To reprove someone means to criticize or correct them, often gently but firmly, for their wrongdoing or mistake.  It implies a desire to improve their behavior or understanding. |例句：The teacher reproofed the student for talking during class. 老师因为学生上课说话而责备了他。

The manager reproofed the employee for their repeated lateness.  经理因员工屡次迟到而责备了他。|近义词：rebuke, reprimand, admonish, chide |反义词：praise, commend, applaud |词性:动词
