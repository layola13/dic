preregulates:/ˌpriːˈreɡjuleɪts/| vt.|预先调节；预先规定|To regulate or control something beforehand or in advance.|The government preregulates the price of essential goods to prevent inflation. 政府预先调控必需品的價格以防止通貨膨脹。|近义词：predetermine, prearrange, control|反义词：deregulate, leave uncontrolled|动词

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company preregulates its production schedule every month. = 公司每月预先调整其生产计划。
They preregulate the temperature in the lab to ensure accuracy. = 他们预先调节实验室的温度以确保精度。

一般过去时:
The committee preregulated the rules for the competition last year. = 委员会去年预先制定了比赛规则。
The scientist preregulated the experiment parameters before starting. = 科学家在开始实验之前预先设定了实验参数。

现在完成时:
The authorities have preregulated the water supply for the drought-stricken area. = 当局已经为干旱地区预先调控了供水。
The school has preregulated the student's timetable for the next semester. = 学校已经为下学期预先制定了学生的课表。

过去完成时:
They had preregulated the system before the emergency. = 他们在紧急情况发生前已经预先调整了系统。
The engineers had preregulated the engine settings before the test flight. = 工程师们在试飞前已经预先设定了发动机参数。

将来时:
The government will preregulate the import of certain goods next year. = 政府明年将预先调控某些商品的进口。
The organizers will preregulate the seating arrangement for the concert. = 组织者将预先安排音乐会的座位安排。

虚拟语气:
If the company preregulated its inventory, it would have avoided the shortage. = 如果公司预先调节库存，它就能避免短缺。
I wish the department had preregulated the budget more effectively. = 我希望部门能更有效地预先编制预算。

被动语态:
The prices were preregulated by the government. = 价格由政府预先调控。
The parameters of the experiment were preregulated before commencement. = 实验参数在开始之前已预先设定。


疑问句:
Were the safety measures preregulated before the construction began? = 在施工开始前是否预先制定了安全措施？
Has the company preregulated its marketing strategy for the new product? = 公司是否已经为新产品预先制定了营销策略？

否定句:
The company did not preregulate its expenses for the project. = 公司没有为该项目预先控制支出。
The organizers did not preregulate the ticket sales resulting in chaos. = 组织者没有预先调控门票销售，导致混乱。
