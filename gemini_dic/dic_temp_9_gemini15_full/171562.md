pull-up-stakes:/pʊl ʌp steɪks/ | phrasal verb | 拔营起程；离开；搬家 | To pull up stakes means to leave a place, especially permanently, often after having lived there for a long time.  It implies a complete departure, often involving the dismantling of one's home or business. |  例句：After the harvest, the farmers pulled up stakes and moved south for the winter.  秋收过后，农民们拔营南下过冬。

They decided to pull up stakes and move to a warmer climate. 他们决定搬到气候更温暖的地方去。


近义词：move, relocate, depart, leave, emigrate | 反义词：settle down, establish oneself
