pharyngals:/ˈfærɪŋɡəlz/ | n. |咽音，咽部音 |Sounds produced by constricting the pharynx (the part of the throat behind the mouth).  Pharyngeal sounds are rare in most languages, but are found in some Semitic and African languages. They are often described as being guttural or back-of-the-throat sounds. |Example:  Arabic has several pharyngals in its consonant inventory.  阿拉伯语在其辅音系统中包含几种咽音。  Some languages use pharyngals to create a distinction in meaning between words. 一些语言使用咽音来区分单词之间的含义。|近义词：guttural sounds, back sounds |反义词：front vowels (as an opposite location of articulation)


USAGE->
This word is primarily a noun and doesn't typically conjugate like verbs.  Therefore, tense considerations are not applicable.  Its usage revolves around describing or classifying sounds.
