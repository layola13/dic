hindus:/ˈhɪnduːz/| n.|印度教徒；印度人|The word "Hindus" refers to people who follow Hinduism, a religion originating in the Indian subcontinent.  It can also sometimes be used (though less precisely) to refer to people of Indian descent, regardless of their religious beliefs.  The term's usage can be sensitive, and it's generally preferred to use more specific descriptors when possible.|例句：Many Hindus celebrate Diwali with lights and festivities. 许多印度教徒以灯火和庆祝活动来庆祝排灯节。|例句：The community is comprised of Hindus, Muslims, and Christians.  这个社区由印度教徒、穆斯林和基督徒组成。|近义词：Indians (in some contexts), followers of Hinduism|反义词：None directly applicable.  The antonym would depend on the context (e.g., non-Hindus, Muslims, Christians).|名词
