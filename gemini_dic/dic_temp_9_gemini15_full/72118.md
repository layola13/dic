flogged:/flɒɡd/| /flɑːɡd/| v.|鞭打；痛斥；竭力推销；| to beat (someone) with a whip or stick; to criticize (someone) severely; to promote (something) aggressively.|例句：The slaves were flogged mercilessly. 奴隶们被无情地鞭打。|例句：The critics flogged the new movie. 批评家们痛斥这部新电影。|近义词：whipped, lashed, castigated, denounced, marketed aggressively|反义词：praised, commended, ignored


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He flogs his products relentlessly. = 他不遗余力地推销他的产品。
They flog the horses to make them run faster. = 他们鞭打马匹使它们跑得更快。


一般过去时:
The farmer flogged the donkey for being stubborn. = 农民鞭打了那头倔强的驴子。
She flogged the idea to the board, but they weren't convinced. = 她向董事会极力推销那个主意，但他们并不信服。


现在完成时:
He has flogged himself into exhaustion. = 他把自己鞭打得筋疲力尽。
The company has flogged its new product successfully in the market. = 这家公司已成功地在市场上推销了它的新产品。


过去完成时:
He had flogged the horse before the race. = 他在比赛前已经鞭打过那匹马了。
They had flogged the issue in the media for weeks before the election. = 他们在选举前几周就一直在媒体上大力宣传这个问题。


将来时:
They will flog the horses again tomorrow. = 他们明天还会再次鞭打那些马。
The marketing team will flog the new software through social media. = 营销团队将通过社交媒体大力宣传这款新软件。


虚拟语气:
If he flogged the horse any harder, it would collapse. = 如果他再用力鞭打那匹马，它就会倒下。
If they had flogged the issue more aggressively, they might have won the election. = 如果他们更积极地宣传这个问题，他们可能会赢得选举。


被动语态:
The slaves were flogged daily. = 奴隶们每天都被鞭打。
The new product was flogged relentlessly by the marketing team. = 新产品被营销团队大力推销。


疑问句:
Were the prisoners flogged? = 囚犯们被鞭打了吗？
Will the new product be flogged on television? = 新产品会在电视上大力宣传吗？


否定句:
He didn't flog the horse. = 他没有鞭打那匹马。
They didn't flog the idea to the public. = 他们没有向公众大力宣传那个主意。
