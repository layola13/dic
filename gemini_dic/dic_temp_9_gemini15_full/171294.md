ptilosis:/taɪˈloʊsɪs/| n. |羽脱落，换羽|The shedding of feathers, especially in birds; molting.  Ptilosis refers to the process of feather development and the arrangement of feathers on a bird's body.  It also encompasses the subsequent shedding (molting) of feathers.  This process is crucial for bird health, growth, and adaptation to environmental conditions.|Example: The ptilosis of the juvenile eagle was nearly complete, its adult plumage nearly fully formed.  = 幼鹰的换羽几乎完成，成年的羽毛几乎完全长成。 The study focused on the ptilosis variations in different subspecies of the hummingbird. = 这项研究关注不同亚种蜂鸟的换羽变化。|近义词：molting, feather shedding, ecdysis (general term for shedding)|反义词：feather growth, plumage development
