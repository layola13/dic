imputability:/ɪmˌpjuːtəˈbɪləti/| n. |可归咎性；可归责性 |The quality or state of being imputable; the ability to be attributed to a particular cause or person.  Imputability refers to the possibility of assigning responsibility or blame for an action or event. This concept is crucial in legal and ethical contexts, particularly when determining culpability.| Example: The imputability of the accident is still under investigation. 事故的可归咎性仍在调查中。The imputability of the crime to the suspect is questionable.  犯罪是否可归咎于嫌疑人还有待商榷。|近义词：accountability, responsibility, culpability |反义词：exculpability, irresponsibility
