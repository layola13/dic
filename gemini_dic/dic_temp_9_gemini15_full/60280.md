egg:/ɛɡ/| n. |鸡蛋，卵|A hard-shelled oval object laid by a female bird, reptile, or fish, containing a developing embryo.  It is typically used as food. |例句：The hen laid a brown egg. 这只母鸡下了一个棕色的蛋。|例句：We had scrambled eggs for breakfast. 我们早餐吃了炒蛋。|近义词：ovum|反义词：None


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The hen lays eggs every day. = 母鸡每天都下蛋。
She eats an egg for breakfast. = 她早餐吃一个鸡蛋。

一般过去时:
The bird laid an egg yesterday. = 鸟昨天下一颗蛋。
We ate boiled eggs for lunch. = 我们午餐吃了水煮蛋。


现在完成时:
The farmer has collected all the eggs. = 农民已经收集了所有的鸡蛋。
She has never eaten a raw egg. = 她从未吃过生鸡蛋。


过去完成时:
The bird had laid several eggs before I arrived. = 我到达之前，鸟已经下了几颗蛋。
We had finished eating the eggs when he came in. = 他进来的时候，我们已经吃完鸡蛋了。


将来时:
She will buy some eggs tomorrow. = 她明天会买些鸡蛋。
The chickens will lay many eggs next week. = 下周鸡会下很多蛋。


虚拟语气:
If I had more eggs, I would make a cake. = 如果我有更多的鸡蛋，我会做蛋糕。
I wish I had enough eggs for the recipe. = 我希望我有足够的鸡蛋来做这个食谱。


被动语态:
The eggs were collected by the farmer. = 鸡蛋是由农民收集的。
The cake was made with fresh eggs. = 蛋糕是用新鲜鸡蛋做的。


疑问句:
Do you like eggs? = 你喜欢鸡蛋吗？
How many eggs do we need? = 我们需要多少个鸡蛋？


否定句:
I don't like fried eggs. = 我不喜欢煎蛋。
We didn't have any eggs for breakfast. = 我们早餐没有鸡蛋。


PHRASE->
egg on (someone) = 怂恿某人
a bad egg = 坏人；不靠谱的人
walk on eggshells = 如履薄冰
have egg on your face = 出丑；丢脸
crack an egg = 打破鸡蛋


词源:
The word "egg" has Proto-Germanic roots, tracing back to a word meaning "egg" or "ovum".  The modern English word is a direct descendant through Old English and various Germanic languages.  The core meaning has remained remarkably consistent over time.
