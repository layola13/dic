intrench:/ɪnˈtrentʃ/ | vt. | 确立；使…处于牢固地位；挖掘战壕 | to establish (something, typically a right, principle, or system) so firmly that it cannot be easily changed; to dig trenches for defensive purposes | 例句：The government has intrenched itself in power.  政府已牢牢掌握权力。


The new regulations are deeply intrenched in the system. 新规已深深地融入体系之中。


近义词：establish, fortify, entrench | 反义词：undermine, weaken, destabilize | 词性:动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They intrench their position in the market. = 他们在市场上巩固了自己的地位。
The army intrenches itself before the battle. = 军队在战斗前构筑了防御工事。


一般过去时:
The company intrenched itself against competition. = 这家公司巩固了自己的地位以对抗竞争。
Soldiers intrenched themselves on the hilltop. = 士兵们在山顶上挖掘了战壕。


现在完成时:
They have intrenched their beliefs for generations. = 他们世世代代坚守着自己的信仰。
The rebels have intrenched themselves in the mountains. = 反叛者已在山区构筑了防御工事。


过去完成时:
The dictator had intrenched his power before the revolution. = 独裁者在革命爆发前已巩固了自己的权力。
The army had intrenched itself before the enemy's attack. = 军队在敌人进攻前已构筑好了防御工事。


将来时:
They will intrench their influence in the coming years. = 他们在未来几年将巩固自己的影响力。
The general will intrench his troops before the dawn. = 将军将在黎明前让他的部队构筑防御工事。


虚拟语气:
If they had intrenched their position earlier, they would have been more successful. = 如果他们早些巩固自己的地位，他们会更成功。
If the soldiers had intrenched themselves better, they wouldn't have lost so many men. = 如果士兵们更好地构筑防御工事，他们就不会损失那么多人。


被动语态:
Their position was intrenched by years of hard work. = 他们的地位是多年辛勤工作的成果。
The defensive line was intrenched quickly before the attack. = 防御线在进攻前迅速构筑完成。


疑问句:
Has the government intrenched its policies against criticism? = 政府是否已巩固其政策以抵制批评？
Were the soldiers properly intrenched before the battle? = 士兵们在战斗前是否已妥善构筑防御工事？


否定句:
The new ideas did not intrench themselves easily. = 新思想并没有轻易地确立起来。
The army failed to intrench its position in time. = 军队未能及时巩固自己的阵地。



PHRASE->
intrench oneself =  巩固自己的地位
intrench a position = 确立一个立场
intrench a right = 确立一项权利
intrench a principle = 确立一个原则
intrench a system = 确立一个体系

词源:  From Old French *entranchier, from tranche "slice, section".  The original meaning related to digging trenches to create a defensive position.  The figurative meaning of establishing something firmly developed later.
