lepidodendrid: /ˌlɛpɪdəˈdɛndrɪd/ | n. |鳞木属植物 |  A genus of extinct tree-like plants belonging to the Lycopsida, characterized by their scale-like leaves and distinctive diamond-shaped leaf scars on the fossilized stems.  These plants were prominent components of Carboniferous coal swamps. |  The lepidodendrids dominated the Carboniferous landscape.  | 近义词: sigillaria (another type of scale tree) | 反义词:  (no direct antonym, as it refers to a specific extinct genus)
