colligating: /ˈkɒlɪɡeɪtɪŋ/ | v. |  联合；联系；搭配 |  The act of connecting or associating words, phrases, or concepts based on their frequent co-occurrence in a corpus of text. It refers to the statistical tendency for certain words or phrases to appear together more often than chance would predict.  |  Example:  The researchers found a strong colligation between the words "climate" and "change" in the news articles.  研究人员发现新闻文章中“气候”和“变化”这两个词之间存在很强的搭配关系。 | 近义词：associating, connecting, linking | 反义词：dissociating, separating, disconnecting
