going-down: /ˈɡoʊɪŋ daʊn/ | adj. | 下降的；衰落的；正在下降的 |  Describes something that is decreasing, declining, or falling. It can refer to a physical process, a trend, or a state of being. |


例句：

The going-down market is a concern for investors.  = 下跌的市场让投资者担忧。

The going-down sun cast long shadows. = 下落的太阳投下长长的影子。


近义词：descending, falling, declining, decreasing, dropping

反义词：rising, increasing, ascending, climbing, soaring

词性:形容词 (participle adjective)


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The price is going down. = 价格正在下降。
The sun is going down. = 太阳正在下山。


一般过去时:
The temperature went down yesterday. = 昨天的温度下降了。
The plane went down in the ocean. = 飞机坠毁在大海里。


现在完成时:
The stock market has been going down all week. = 股市本周一直在下跌。
The number of cases has gone down significantly. = 病例数量显著下降。


过去完成时:
The temperature had gone down before we arrived. = 我们到达之前温度已经下降了。
The sales had been going down for months. = 销售额已经下降了几个月。


将来时:
The price will go down next month. = 价格下个月会下降。
The sun will go down soon. = 太阳很快就要下山了。


虚拟语气:
If the price went down, I would buy it. = 如果价格下降，我会买它。
I wish the noise would go down. = 我希望噪音能小点。


被动语态:
The plane was going down rapidly. = 飞机正在快速下降。
The matter is being gone down by the committee. = 这件事正在由委员会调查处理。


疑问句:

Is the sun going down? = 太阳正在下山吗？


否定句:
The price isn't going down. = 价格没有下降。
The sun isn't going down yet. = 太阳还没下山。
