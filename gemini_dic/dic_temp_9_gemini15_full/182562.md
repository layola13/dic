reobliged:/ˌriːˈɒblɑːdʒd/| adj.| 再次受约束的；再次被强求的 |  The word "reobliged" implies a renewed or reinstated obligation. It suggests a previous obligation that had perhaps lapsed or been temporarily suspended, but is now in effect again.  The term is relatively uncommon in modern English and often feels somewhat formal or archaic. | Example:  After a period of negotiation, the two parties found themselves reobliged to the original contract. = 在经过一段时间的谈判后，双方发现自己再次受制于原合同。| 近义词：rebound, recommitted | 反义词：freed, released | 词性: adjective
