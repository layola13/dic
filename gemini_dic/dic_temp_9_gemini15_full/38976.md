confinement:/kənˈfaɪnmənt/ | n. |限制；监禁；分娩 | The state of being kept or restricted to a place or area.  It can also refer to the period of time a woman is pregnant and giving birth. |例句：Her confinement to the house was driving her mad. 她被限制在家中，快要把她逼疯了。

例句：The confinement of childbirth was a difficult but rewarding experience. 分娩的经历虽然艰难，但却令人欣慰。


近义词：restriction, imprisonment, limitation, incarceration, childbirth

反义词：freedom, liberty, release

The strict rules confine their activities. = 严格的规定限制了他们的活动。
This job confines me to the office. = 这份工作把我限制在办公室里。


一般过去时:
They confined the prisoner to a small cell. = 他们把犯人关在一个小牢房里。
The storm confined us to the house. = 暴风雨把我们困在了家里。


现在完成时:
She has been confined to bed for a week. = 她卧床一周了。
We have confined our research to this specific area. = 我们已经将我们的研究限制在这个特定领域。


过去完成时:

The family had confined their son to his room as punishment. = 家人把儿子关在房间里作为惩罚。


将来时:

虚拟语气:
If they confined the dog, it wouldn't run away. = 如果他们把狗关起来，它就不会跑掉了。
I wish I weren't confined to this boring job. = 我希望自己不用做这份无聊的工作。


被动语态:

疑问句:
