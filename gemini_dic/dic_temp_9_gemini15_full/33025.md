chortling:/ˈtʃɔːrtlɪŋ/| v.|咯咯地笑，得意地笑|A hearty, chuckle-like laugh expressing great amusement or satisfaction.  It often implies a slightly self-satisfied or triumphant tone.|例句：The children were chortling at the funny clown. 孩子们看着滑稽的小丑咯咯地笑着。|例句：She chortled with delight at her unexpected success. 她对意外的成功感到高兴，得意地笑了。|近义词：chuckling, giggling, snickering|反义词：sobbing, weeping


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He chortles at every joke. = 他对每一个笑话都咯咯地笑。
They chortle with glee. = 他们高兴地咯咯地笑。


一般过去时:
She chortled at his misfortune. = 她对他遭遇的不幸咯咯地笑。
He chortled after scoring the winning goal. = 他在射入制胜球后得意地笑了。


现在完成时:
She has chortled throughout the entire movie. = 她在整部电影中一直咯咯地笑。
They have chortled since they heard the news. = 自从他们听到这个消息后就一直咯咯地笑。


过去完成时:
He had chortled before realizing the consequences. = 他在意识到后果之前就得意地笑了。
They had chortled all evening. = 他们整个晚上都在咯咯地笑。


将来时:
She will chortle when she hears this. = 当她听到这个时她会咯咯地笑。
They will chortle at the party. = 他们将在聚会上咯咯地笑。


虚拟语气:
If I were you, I would chortle at their foolishness. = 如果我是你，我会嘲笑他们的愚蠢。
I wish I could chortle like that. = 我真希望我能像那样咯咯地笑。


被动语态:
His joke was chortled at by everyone. = 他的笑话被每个人都嘲笑。
(Note: The passive voice is less common with this verb)


疑问句:
Are they chortling? = 他们在咯咯地笑吗？
Was he chortling at the joke? = 他在嘲笑那个笑话吗？


否定句:
He didn't chortle at the joke. = 他没有嘲笑那个笑话。
They didn't chortle, they were actually crying. = 他们没有咯咯地笑，他们实际上在哭。


PHRASE->

chortling with delight = 高兴地咯咯地笑
chortling to oneself = 自言自语地咯咯地笑
chortling uncontrollably =  无法控制地咯咯地笑
chortling softly = 轻声咯咯地笑
chortling merrily = 开心地咯咯地笑


词源:

The word "chortle" is a blend of "chuckle" and "snort."  It was coined by Lewis Carroll in his poem "Jabberwocky" in *Through the Looking-Glass*, and has since entered common usage.
