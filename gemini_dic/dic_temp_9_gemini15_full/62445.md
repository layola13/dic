endorphin:/ɛnˈdɔːrfɪn/| n. |内啡肽|A group of hormones secreted within the brain and nervous system and having a number of physiological functions. They are peptides that activate opioid receptors, causing an analgesic effect.  Endorphins are often associated with feelings of pleasure and well-being, and are released in response to exercise, stress, or pain.|例句：Running releases endorphins, which can lead to a feeling of euphoria.  跑步会释放内啡肽，这会导致欣快感。|例句：The endorphin rush after a long workout is incredibly rewarding. 长时间锻炼后的内啡肽激增令人难以置信地有益。|近义词：neurotransmitter, hormone|反义词：N/A
