placebo:/pləˈsiːboʊ/| n. |安慰剂；无效治疗|A placebo is a substance with no therapeutic effect, given to a patient who believes it to be a genuine treatment.  This is often used in controlled experiments to assess the effectiveness of a real treatment by comparing its effects to those of the placebo. The effect is often psychological, with patients reporting improvement due to the belief that they are receiving treatment.|The doctor gave her a placebo to see if her symptoms were psychosomatic.|近义词：sham, dummy, simulation|反义词：remedy, cure, treatment
