counterstream: /ˈkaʊntərstrim/ | n. |逆流；反向流动；对抗潮流 | A flow in the opposite direction to a main stream.  It often refers to a current of opinion, thought, or action that opposes the dominant trend.  | Example:  The counterstream of public opinion against the war grew stronger.  公众对战争的反向舆论越来越强烈。  The counterstream of innovation challenged the established norms.  创新的逆流挑战了既定的规范。 |近义词：countercurrent, opposing current, contrary flow |反义词：mainstream, dominant trend


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The river flows strongly, but a small counterstream exists near the bank. = 河水流得很急，但在河岸附近有一股小逆流。
A counterstream of negative reviews is impacting the product's sales. = 一股负面评论的逆流正在影响产品的销售。


一般过去时:
A strong counterstream of refugees flowed back into the war-torn country. = 一股强大的难民逆流涌回饱受战争摧残的国家。
There was a counterstream of thought that questioned the scientific consensus. = 有一种逆向思维质疑了科学共识。


现在完成时:
A counterstream of dissent has built up against the new policy. = 一股反对新政策的异议已经积聚起来。
The company has experienced a counterstream of customer complaints following the product launch. = 产品发布后，公司经历了一股客户投诉的逆流。


过去完成时:

A counterstream of opposition had formed before the bill was even introduced. = 在法案提出之前，就已经形成一股反对的逆流。


将来时:
Experts predict a counterstream of migration from urban areas to rural areas in the coming years. = 专家预测未来几年将出现一股从城市地区到农村地区的逆向迁移。
We anticipate a counterstream of criticism once the details are revealed.  = 我们预计一旦细节披露，就会出现一股批评的逆流。


虚拟语气:

否定句:
