pouting:/ˈpaʊtɪŋ/ | v. adj. n.|撅嘴，生气地不说话； 嘟嘴的； 撅嘴的样子 |  To push out one's lower lip in a expression of annoyance, displeasure, or sulkiness.  Also used as an adjective to describe a person's facial expression, or as a noun to refer to the act of pouting. | 例句：The child was pouting because he couldn't have any more candy.  这个孩子因为不能再吃糖果而撅着嘴。

The dog sat pouting after being told to stay. 狗被告知要待着后，坐那儿撅着嘴。


近义词：sulking, sulky, frowning, mouthing | 反义词：smiling, cheerful, happy | 动词，形容词，名词

词源:

The word "pout" comes from the Middle English word "pouten," meaning "to thrust out the lips."  This, in turn, likely has origins in a Proto-Germanic root related to pushing or swelling.
