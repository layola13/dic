esteem:/ɪˈstiːm/| n., vt.| 尊重；敬重；自尊；评价|

英文解析：To esteem someone or something is to hold them in high regard; to respect and admire them. As a noun, it refers to the high regard in which someone or something is held; or one's own sense of self-worth.

例句：

I esteem her for her intelligence and kindness. = 我敬重她的聪明和善良。

He has a high self-esteem. = 他有着很高的自尊心。


近义词：respect, admire, regard, value, appreciate

反义词：despise, scorn, disdain, contempt

词性：名词，动词


词源:

The word "esteem" comes from the Old French word "estimer," which in turn derives from the Latin word "aestimare."  "Aestimare" is composed of "ad-" (to) and "aestimare" (to value, estimate, or assess).  Thus, the root meaning points to a judgment of worth or value.
