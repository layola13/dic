frizzled:/ˈfrɪzəld/| adj.|卷曲的；蓬松的；焦了的|Curled or crisped; also, cooked until crisp.  The word often describes hair that is very curly or a food that is cooked until it is crispy.|例句：Her hair was frizzled from the humidity.  她的头发因为潮湿而卷曲蓬松。

例句：The bacon was perfectly frizzled. 培根煎得恰到好处，酥脆可口。

近义词：curly, crispy, crisp,  kinky

反义词：straight, smooth, limp


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The sun frizzles my hair. = 太阳把我的头发晒得卷曲蓬松。
His hair is always frizzled. = 他的头发总是卷曲蓬松的。


一般过去时:
The heat frizzled the leaves. = 炎热使树叶卷曲焦枯。
I frizzled the bacon until it was crispy. = 我把培根煎到酥脆。


现在完成时:

She has frizzled her hair with a curling iron. = 她用卷发器把头发烫卷了。


过去完成时:
The chef had frizzled the onions before adding them to the soup. = 厨师在把洋葱加入汤中之前已经将其煎得酥脆。
The intense heat had frizzled the paint on the car. = 强烈的热度已经把汽车上的油漆烤焦了。

词源:
From Middle English *friselen, from Old French friseler "to curl, crisp," from Old French friser "to curl," from Vulgar Latin *frisare, perhaps related to Latin frisus "curled."  The culinary sense developed later.
