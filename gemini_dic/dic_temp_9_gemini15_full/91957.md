hypermodernity:/ˌhaɪpərmɒˈdɜːrnɪti/ | /ˌhaɪpɚmɑːˈdɝnɪti/ | n. |超现代性 |Hypermodernity refers to the social, cultural, and technological conditions of the late 20th and early 21st centuries, characterized by a rapid pace of change, globalization, and the pervasive influence of technology. It is often seen as a continuation and intensification of modernity, but with significant differences.  It emphasizes fluidity, fragmentation, and the constant renegotiation of meaning and identity in a globalized world. |The pervasive influence of the internet exemplifies hypermodernity's impact on social interaction and information dissemination.|近义词：postmodernity (though distinct), late modernity |反义词：traditionalism, premodernity
