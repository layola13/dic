intermeasurable: /ˌɪntərˈmɛʒərəbl/ | adj. | 可相互测量的 |  capable of being mutually measured or compared;  mutually comparable.  This term suggests a reciprocal relationship where the measurement of one entity provides information about the other, and vice-versa.  It often implies a system of units or a shared standard allowing for direct comparison.


例句：

The two datasets are intermeasurable using a common scale of measurement.  = 使用共同的测量尺度，这两个数据集是可以相互测量的。

Their performance levels are intermeasurable, allowing for a fair comparison. = 他们的绩效水平是可以相互测量的，从而可以进行公平的比较。


近义词：comparable, commensurable, mutually measurable


反义词：incommensurable, incomparable


词性：形容词


USAGE->
(由于intermeasurable是一个形容词，因此没有动词的时态变化，也没有被动语态和虚拟语气。以下例句展示了其在不同句式中的用法)


肯定句:

The results are intermeasurable, confirming the validity of the method. = 结果是可相互测量的，证实了该方法的有效性。
These two quantities are intermeasurable under certain conditions. = 在特定条件下，这两个量是可以相互测量的。

否定句:

The data from these experiments are not intermeasurable without further calibration. = 这些实验的数据在没有进一步校准的情况下是不可相互测量的。
Unfortunately, these two scales are not intermeasurable at this time. = 不幸的是，这两个尺度目前是不可相互测量的。


疑问句:

Are the two systems intermeasurable? = 这两个系统是否可以相互测量？
Is the proposed metric intermeasurable with existing data? = 提出的指标与现有数据是否可以相互测量？
