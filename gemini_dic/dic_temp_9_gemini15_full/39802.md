consorting:/kənˈsɔːrtɪŋ/| v.|结交；厮混；陪伴|The act of associating with someone, especially regularly or intimately.  It often implies a negative connotation, suggesting association with questionable individuals or activities.|例句：He was accused of consorting with known criminals. 他被指控与已知的罪犯交往。|例句：She was seen consorting with the enemy. 她被看到与敌人来往。|近义词：associating, fraternizing, hobnobbing|反义词：avoiding, shunning, isolating|动词


USAGE->

He consorts with the wealthy elite. = 他与富有的精英们交往。
