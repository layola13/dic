liguliflorous: /ˌlɪɡjuˈlɪflɔːrəs/ | adj. | 舌状花的；具舌状花的 | Having ligulate florets.  This term describes a flower head (inflorescence) composed solely of ray florets (ligulate florets), which are strap-shaped and typically have a single petal-like structure.  These are often found in plants of the Asteraceae family (e.g., daisies, sunflowers). | The dandelion's flower head is liguliflorous, consisting entirely of yellow ray florets.  | 近义词: radiate (in the context of florets) | 反义词: discoid (referring to flower heads with only disc florets)
