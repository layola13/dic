self-outlaws: /ˌsɛlfˈaʊtˌlɔːz/ | n. | 自我放逐者；离群索居者 | Individuals who have chosen to live outside the established social norms or laws, often by their own volition.  They are not necessarily criminals, but rather people who reject societal expectations and choose a life of self-imposed isolation or unconventional living. | Example:  The self-outlaws lived in a remote cabin, far from the influence of civilization.  自我放逐者们住在远离文明影响的偏远小屋里。 | 近义词:  recluse, hermit, loner, maverick | 反义词: conformist, follower
