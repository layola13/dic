futhork:/ˈfuːθɔːrk/ | n. | 古日耳曼字母表 | The futhark is the name given to the runic alphabets used by the Germanic peoples of northern Europe.  It consists of 24 runes, each representing a sound or concept.  The name comes from the first six runes of the Elder Futhark: F, U, Þ, A, R, K.  Different versions of the futhark existed throughout time and across different Germanic groups, with variations in the number of runes and their shapes.  Scholars study the futhark to understand the language and culture of the ancient Germanic peoples. | Example:  The inscription on the stone was written in the Elder Futhark.  这块石头上的铭文是用古弗萨克字母写的。 | 近义词: runic alphabet | 反义词:  (no direct antonym)
