rehearsing:/rɪˈhɜːrsɪŋ/| v.|排练，预演，复习|To practice or go over something, such as a play, speech, or musical piece, in preparation for a performance.|例句：The actors are rehearsing a new play.  演员们正在排练一部新戏。|例句：She spent hours rehearsing her speech. 她花了数小时排练她的演讲。|近义词：practicing, preparing, reviewing|反义词：performing


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They are rehearsing the play every evening. = 他们每天晚上都在排练戏剧。
The band is rehearsing for their upcoming tour. = 乐队正在为即将到来的巡演排练。

一般过去时:
We rehearsed the scene several times. = 我们排练了那个场景好几次。
She rehearsed her presentation before the conference. = 她在会议之前排练了她的演讲。

现在完成时:
They have been rehearsing for weeks. = 他们已经排练了好几个星期了。
The orchestra has rehearsed this symphony many times. = 管弦乐队排练过这首交响曲很多次了。

过去完成时:
They had rehearsed the song before the concert. = 他们在音乐会之前已经排练过这首歌了。
We had rehearsed the entire play by the time the director arrived. = 当导演到达时，我们已经排练完了整部戏。

将来时:
We will be rehearsing all day tomorrow. = 我们明天一整天都会在排练。
The choir will rehearse the anthem next week. = 合唱团下周将排练圣歌。

虚拟语气:
If they had rehearsed more, the performance would have been better. = 如果他们排练得更多，演出就会更好。
I wish I had rehearsed more for my exam. = 我真希望我为考试多复习一些。

被动语态:
The play was rehearsed extensively. = 这部戏进行了广泛的排练。
The song is being rehearsed by the choir. = 这首歌正在由合唱团排练。

疑问句:
Are they rehearsing tonight? = 他们今晚在排练吗？
Have you rehearsed your lines? = 你排练过你的台词了吗？

否定句:
They did not rehearse the dance routine properly. = 他们没有好好排练舞蹈动作。
We haven't rehearsed enough for the competition. = 我们为比赛的排练还不够。
