interned:/ɪnˈtɜːrnd/|/ɪnˈtɜːrn/| vt. |拘留；关押；实习|To confine or detain, especially under arrest; to undergo a period of supervised practical training.|例句：The political dissidents were interned in a remote island.  政治异见者被拘留在偏远岛屿上。  |例句：She interned at a law firm last summer. 她去年夏天在一家律师事务所实习。|近义词：imprison, confine, detain, incarcerate |反义词：release, liberate, free |


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They intern foreign journalists. = 他们拘留外国记者。
The company interns many students. = 这家公司让很多学生实习。


一般过去时:
The government interned suspected spies. = 政府拘留了涉嫌间谍活动的人。
He interned at a hospital during his medical school. = 他医学院期间在医院实习。


现在完成时:
The authorities have interned hundreds of protestors. = 当局已拘留了数百名抗议者。
She has interned at three different companies. = 她已经在三家不同的公司实习过。


过去完成时:
They had interned him before the trial. = 他们曾在审判前将他拘留。
He had interned at a tech company before joining our team. = 他在加入我们团队之前曾在一家科技公司实习。


将来时:
They will intern him for questioning. = 他们将拘留他进行审问。
She will intern at a marketing agency next semester. = 下学期她将在一家营销机构实习。


虚拟语气:
If they interned him, there would be a public outcry. = 如果他们拘留他，将会引发公众抗议。
If I had interned at Google, I would have learned a lot. = 如果我在谷歌实习过，我会学到很多东西。


被动语态:
He was interned without charge. = 他被拘留，没有指控。
Many students were interned at the company last year. = 去年很多学生在这家公司实习。


疑问句:
Were the prisoners interned fairly? = 囚犯的拘留是否公平？
Will you be interned at a bank this summer? = 你今年夏天会在银行实习吗？


否定句:
They did not intern the journalist. = 他们没有拘留记者。
He did not intern at any company last year. = 他去年没有在任何公司实习。
