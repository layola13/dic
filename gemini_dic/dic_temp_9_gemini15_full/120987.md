miscitations:/ˌmɪsˌsaɪˈteɪʃənz/ | n. | 误引用；错引 | Instances of quoting something incorrectly or out of context.  Miscitations can range from minor inaccuracies to deliberate distortions of the original meaning. They can occur in academic writing, legal documents, journalistic reports, or everyday conversation.  | Example: The article contained several miscitations of historical documents, leading to a flawed argument. 这篇文章包含几处历史文献的误引用，导致论证有缺陷。|近义词：misquotations, incorrect citations, paraphrases (if significantly altered)|反义词：accurate citations, correct quotations |名词
