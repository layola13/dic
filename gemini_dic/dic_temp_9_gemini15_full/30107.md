cecum:/ˈsiːkəm/| n. |盲肠|The cecum is a pouch-like structure at the beginning of the large intestine. It's a vestigial organ in humans, meaning it has lost most of its original function.  It plays a minor role in immune function and houses some gut bacteria.|Example:  The appendix is attached to the cecum.|近义词：caecum (alternative spelling)|反义词：None
