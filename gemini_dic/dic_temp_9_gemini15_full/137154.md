nonsubtlenesses: /ˌnɑːnˈsʌt(ə)lnəsɪz/ | n. | 不微妙之处；不精细之处；粗糙之处 | The nonsubtlenesses of his argument were readily apparent.  他的论点粗糙之处显而易见。| 近义词：crudities, clumsinesses, gaucheries | 反义词：subtleties, refinements


英文解析：This word is the plural form of "nonsubtlety," which itself is formed by adding the prefix "non-" (meaning "not") to "subtlety."  It refers to aspects or qualities that lack subtlety, finesse, or delicacy; they are blunt, obvious, or crude.  The word highlights a lack of sophistication or nuance.


例句：

The nonsubtlenesses of his approach alienated potential allies.  他方法的粗糙之处疏远了潜在的盟友。

His performance was marred by numerous nonsubtlenesses. 他的表演因诸多不精细之处而大打折扣。


词源:

* **subtlety (n.):**  From Old French *subtilete*, from Latin *subtilitas*, from *subtilis* ("fine, delicate").  The root meaning relates to something thin or fine in texture, which evolved to describe something delicate or nuanced in meaning or perception.

* **non- (prefix):**  A negative prefix meaning "not" or "opposite of."


用法说明：

"Nonsubtlenesses" is used to describe instances where something lacks subtlety. It is generally used in a critical or analytical context to point out flaws in argumentation, artistic expression, or other endeavors that require finesse.  It's a relatively uncommon word, used mostly in formal writing or sophisticated speech.  It suggests a deliberate or noticeable lack of refinement, rather than simply a lack of skill.
