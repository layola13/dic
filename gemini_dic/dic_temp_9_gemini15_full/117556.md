megacity:/ˈmɛɡəˌsɪti/ | n. |特大城市；百万人口城市 | A very large city, typically one with a population of over 10 million.  Megacities are characterized by rapid urbanization, often leading to challenges in infrastructure, resource management, and social services. | Example: Tokyo is a megacity with a population exceeding 37 million.  东京是一个人口超过3700万的特大城市。|近义词：metropolis, large city |反义词：small town, village

词源:
The word "megacity" is a blend of "mega" (meaning "large" or "great," from the Greek μέγας, *mégas*) and "city."  It emerged in the late 20th century to describe the rapidly growing urban centers with populations exceeding 10 million.
