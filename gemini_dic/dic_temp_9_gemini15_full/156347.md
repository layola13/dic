physiocrat:/ˈfɪzioʊkræt/| n. |重农主义者|A member of an 18th-century school of economic thought that believed that agriculture was the only true source of wealth and that the government should intervene minimally in the economy.  |The physiocrats advocated for laissez-faire policies, believing that the natural order of the economy would lead to prosperity. |近义词：economist, reformer|反义词：mercantilist
