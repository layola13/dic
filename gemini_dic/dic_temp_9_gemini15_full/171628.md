pulpit: /ˈpʊlpɪt/ | n. |讲坛；（教堂的）讲道台 | A raised platform or lectern in a church or other place of worship from which a preacher or speaker delivers a sermon or address.  | The preacher delivered a powerful sermon from the pulpit.  牧师在讲台上发表了充满力量的布道。| rostrum, lectern |  (无明显反义词) | 名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The preacher stands at the pulpit. = 牧师站在讲台上。
The pulpit is made of wood. = 这讲台是用木头做的。


一般过去时:
The pastor preached from the pulpit yesterday. = 牧师昨天在讲台上布道。
He stood on the pulpit and addressed the congregation. = 他站在讲台上并向会众讲话。


现在完成时:
The church has had that pulpit for over a hundred years. = 这座教堂的讲台已经使用了超过一百年。
They have recently renovated the pulpit. = 他们最近翻修了讲台。


过去完成时:

The previous speaker had left the pulpit before the next one arrived. = 前一位发言者在下一位到达之前离开了讲台。

The pulpit will be decorated for the special occasion. = 讲台将为这个特殊的场合进行装饰。
