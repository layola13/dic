/riˈveɪtoʊɪŋ/ | v. |否决；否决权的行使 |The act of vetoing or rejecting something.  It often refers to the power of a person or body to reject a proposed decision. | The president vetoed the bill.  总统否决了该法案。 The council vetoed the proposed development. 理事会否决了拟议的开发项目。 | reject, nix, override | approve, accept |动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He vetoes every proposal. 他否决每一个提案。
She vetoes the plan regularly. 她定期否决该计划。

一般过去时:
The governor vetoed the legislation. 州长否决了这项立法。
They vetoed the budget last year. 他们去年否决了预算。

现在完成时:
The senate has vetoed several amendments. 参议院否决了好几个修正案。
She has vetoed the project twice. 她已经两次否决了这个项目。

过去完成时:
The committee had vetoed the changes before the meeting. 委员会在会议之前就否决了这些改变。
He had vetoed the plan prior to the vote. 他在投票之前就否决了该计划。

将来时:
The president will veto the bill if it passes the House. 如果该法案通过众议院，总统将否决它。
They will veto any measure that harms the environment. 他们将否决任何损害环境的措施。

虚拟语气:
If he were to veto the bill, there would be a lot of controversy. 如果他否决该法案，将会引起很多争议。
I wish the council hadn't vetoed my proposal. 我希望理事会没有否决我的提案。

被动语态:
The bill was vetoed by the president. 该法案被总统否决了。
The proposal was vetoed last week. 该提案上周被否决了。

疑问句:
Will the mayor veto the budget? 市长会否决预算吗？
Did they veto the resolution? 他们否决了决议吗？

否定句:
The president did not veto the bill. 总统没有否决该法案。
She did not veto the changes. 她没有否决这些改变。
