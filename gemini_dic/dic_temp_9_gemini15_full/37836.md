competent:/ˈkɑːmpɪtənt/|adj.|胜任的；有能力的|Having the necessary ability, skill, or knowledge to do something successfully.|The surgeon is a competent professional.|近义词：capable, proficient, skilled|反义词：incompetent, unskilled, incapable


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is competent in his job. = 他胜任他的工作。
She is competent to handle this project. = 她有能力处理这个项目。


一般过去时:
He was competent enough to lead the team. = 他足够胜任领导这个团队。
She was competent at playing the piano. = 她钢琴弹得很熟练。


现在完成时:
They have proven themselves competent in many situations. = 他们在许多情况下都证明了自己的能力。
He has been competent throughout his career. = 他在整个职业生涯中都表现出色。


过去完成时:
She had already proven herself competent before taking on the new role. = 在承担新角色之前，她已经证明了自己的能力。
They had been competent managers for years. = 他们多年来一直是称职的经理。


将来时:
He will be competent enough to take on more responsibility. = 他将有能力承担更多的责任。
She will certainly be competent for the position. = 她肯定胜任这个职位。


虚拟语气:
If he were competent, he would have finished the task on time. = 如果他胜任，他就会按时完成任务。
I wish I were more competent in this area. = 我希望我在这个领域更有能力。


被动语态:
He was considered competent by his superiors. = 他被上司认为是胜任的。
The task was deemed too difficult for someone so incompetent. = 这项任务对如此无能的人来说太难了。


疑问句:
Is he competent enough for the job? = 他胜任这项工作吗？
Are you competent to give this testimony? = 你有资格作证吗？


否定句:
He is not competent to make such decisions. = 他无权做出这样的决定。
She wasn't competent enough to handle the pressure. = 她能力不足以应付压力。


PHRASE->
competent authority = 主管部门
competent person = 合格的人
highly competent = 高度胜任的
fully competent = 完全胜任的
be competent to do sth = 能够胜任某事


词源:
源自拉丁语 *competēns,  现在分词形式，意为“适合的，适当的”，来自 *competere，意为“适合，与…竞争”。
