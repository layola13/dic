dermatophyte:/ˈdɜːrmətəfaɪt/| n. |皮肤真菌|A dermatophyte is a type of fungus that causes skin infections.  They thrive in warm, moist environments and feed on keratin, a protein found in skin, hair, and nails.  Different species of dermatophytes cause various types of skin infections, including athlete's foot, ringworm, and jock itch.|例句：The athlete's foot is caused by a dermatophyte infection.  运动员的脚气是由皮肤真菌感染引起的。


The doctor confirmed that the rash was caused by a dermatophyte.医生确认皮疹是由皮肤真菌引起的。|近义词：fungus, dermatophyton|反义词：无

The word "dermatophyte" is a compound word.  It combines "dermat-", derived from the Greek word "derma" (skin), and "-phyte", derived from the Greek word "phyton" (plant).  Therefore, it literally means "skin plant," referring to the fungal nature of these organisms.
