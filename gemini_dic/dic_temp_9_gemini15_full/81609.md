greatly:/ˈɡreɪtli/ | adv. | 非常；大大地；极其 |  Significantly; to a large extent.  It modifies verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs to emphasize the degree or extent of something. | 例句：The new policy greatly improved the company's efficiency. 新政策大大提高了公司的效率。

例句：His health greatly improved after the surgery. 手术后他的健康状况大大改善了。


近义词：significantly, considerably, substantially, immensely, enormously | 反义词：slightly, marginally, insignificantly


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He greatly enjoys his work. = 他非常享受他的工作。
She greatly appreciates your help. = 她非常感谢你的帮助。

一般过去时:
The situation greatly improved after the storm. = 暴风雨过后，情况大为好转。
They greatly underestimated the challenge. = 他们大大低估了挑战。

现在完成时:
The project has greatly benefited the community. = 该项目极大地造福了社区。
His reputation has been greatly enhanced by his recent achievements. = 他最近的成就大大提升了他的声誉。

过去完成时:

将来时:

The investment will greatly benefit the company in the long run. = 这项投资从长远来看将大大有利于公司。

虚拟语气:

被动语态:

否定句:
