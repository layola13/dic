misconceptions:/ˌmɪskənˈsɛpʃənz/| n. |误解，错误观念 | A misconception is a belief or idea that is wrong because it is based on a misunderstanding of a situation or facts.  Misconceptions often arise from incomplete or inaccurate information. | Example:  Many people harbor the misconception that all snakes are poisonous.  很多人抱有蛇都是有毒的这种误解。

There is a common misconception that exercise is harmful during pregnancy. 有一种普遍的误解，认为在怀孕期间运动是有害的。 |近义词：misunderstandings, errors, fallacies |反义词：truths, facts, realities |名词
