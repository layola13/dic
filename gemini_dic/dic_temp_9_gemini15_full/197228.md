sharecropping:/ˈʃɛərˌkrɒpɪŋ/| n. |股份耕作；佃农制度 |A system of agriculture in which a landowner allows a tenant to use the land in return for a share of the crops produced on their land.  This system was prevalent in the American South after the Civil War, often involving African Americans who were formerly enslaved. |Example: Sharecropping was a common practice in the post-Civil War South, trapping many families in cycles of poverty.  股份耕作是战后南方的一种常见做法，它使许多家庭陷入贫困的循环中。|近义词：tenant farming, metayage |反义词：land ownership, freeholding
