neurolinguistics:/ˌnʊroʊlɪŋˈɡwɪstɪks/| n. |神经语言学|The study of the relationship between language and the brain.  It explores how the brain processes and uses language, including the neural mechanisms underlying language comprehension, production, and acquisition.|Example: Neurolinguistics investigates how different brain regions contribute to speech perception.|近义词：psycholinguistics (心理语言学)|反义词：无
