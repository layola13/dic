postponer:/ˌpoʊstˈpoʊn/ | v. | 推迟，延期 | To put off to a later time; to delay.  |  The meeting has been postponed until next week.  会议推迟到下周举行。 They decided to postpone their trip due to the bad weather. 由于天气不好，他们决定推迟旅行。 | delay, defer, adjourn |  expedite, accelerate | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They postpone the game every year. = 他们每年都推迟比赛。
We postpone our decision until tomorrow. = 我们把决定推迟到明天。

一般过去时:
He postponed his vacation. = 他推迟了他的假期。
She postponed the meeting because of illness. = 她因为生病而推迟了会议。

现在完成时:
They have postponed the concert several times. = 他们已经几次推迟了音乐会。
We have postponed making a decision. = 我们一直推迟做决定。

过去完成时:
They had postponed the launch several times before it finally happened. = 在它最终发生之前，他们已经几次推迟了发射。
She had postponed her trip many times before finally cancelling it. = 在最终取消之前，她已经多次推迟了她的旅行。

将来时:
They will postpone the wedding if it rains. = 如果下雨，他们将推迟婚礼。
We will postpone the project until we get more funding. = 我们将推迟项目，直到获得更多资金。

虚拟语气:
If I were you, I would postpone the surgery. = 如果我是你，我会推迟手术。
It would have been better if they had postponed the event. = 如果他们推迟了活动，那就好了。

被动语态:
The game was postponed due to rain. = 比赛因雨推迟。
The meeting has been postponed until further notice. = 会议已推迟，另行通知。

疑问句:
Will the meeting be postponed? = 会议会推迟吗？
When was the launch postponed? = 发射什么时候推迟的？

否定句:
They did not postpone the project. = 他们没有推迟项目。
We will not postpone the deadline. = 我们不会推迟截止日期。


PHRASE->
postpone indefinitely = 无限期推迟

postpone a decision = 推迟决定
postpone the meeting = 推迟会议
postpone one's plans = 推迟计划
