restrictive:/rɪˈstrɪktɪv/| adj. |限制性的，限定性的 |Restricting or limiting something.  A restrictive clause is a subordinate clause that limits or defines the meaning of the noun or noun phrase it modifies.  It is essential to the meaning of the sentence and cannot be removed without changing the sentence's meaning.  |例句：The restrictive clause, "who lives next door," identifies which John is being referred to.  限制性从句“住在隔壁的”，指明了指的是哪一个约翰。

The restrictive agreement only allows for a limited number of participants.  这个限制性协议只允许有限数量的参与者。|近义词：limiting, confining, qualifying |反义词：nonrestrictive, unlimited
