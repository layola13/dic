laryngeals:/læˈrɪndʒiəlz/ | n. | 喉音；喉音字母 | Sounds produced by constricting or closing the glottis (the opening between the vocal cords).  They are often described as voiceless or voiced sounds made in the larynx.  Laryngeals are found in many languages, but their exact phonetic realization varies.  Some languages have lost laryngeals entirely, leaving only traces in the morphology or phonology of the language. | Example:  The Proto-Indo-European language is reconstructed as having a number of laryngeals, which are now lost in most daughter languages. = 原印欧语被重建为具有许多喉音，而这些喉音现在在大多数子语言中都消失了。  The study of laryngeals is crucial for understanding the historical development of certain language families. = 喉音的研究对于理解某些语系的历史发展至关重要。 | 近义词: glottal sounds | 反义词: oral sounds
