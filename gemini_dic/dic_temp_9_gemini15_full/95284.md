incarcerate:/ɪnˈkɑːrsəreɪt/| vt.|监禁，关押|To imprison or confine someone.  The verb implies confinement, usually in a prison or jail, and often as a result of a legal process.|例句：He was incarcerated for armed robbery. 他因武装抢劫被监禁。|例句：The protesters were incarcerated without trial. 抗议者未经审判就被监禁。|近义词：imprison, jail, confine, imprison, detain|反义词：release, liberate, free|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They incarcerate criminals in prisons. = 他们把罪犯关进监狱。
The government incarcerates political dissidents. = 政府监禁政治异见者。

一般过去时:
The judge incarcerated him for ten years. = 法官判处他十年监禁。
They incarcerated the suspect immediately after the arrest. = 他们在逮捕嫌犯后立即将其监禁。

现在完成时:
He has been incarcerated for over a decade. = 他已被监禁十年多了。
The authorities have incarcerated many protestors recently. = 当局最近监禁了许多抗议者。

过去完成时:
He had been incarcerated before for similar offences. = 他以前曾因类似罪行被监禁过。
The police had incarcerated the suspect before the trial began. = 在审判开始之前，警方已经将嫌疑人监禁起来。

将来时:
They will incarcerate him if he's found guilty. = 如果他被判有罪，他们将把他监禁起来。
The court will likely incarcerate the defendant for a lengthy period. = 法院可能会判处被告长期监禁。

虚拟语气:
If he committed the crime, he would be incarcerated. = 如果他犯了罪，他将被监禁。
I wish they hadn't incarcerated him so quickly. = 我真希望他们没有那么快就把他监禁起来。

被动语态:
He was incarcerated for his crimes. = 他因犯罪而被监禁。
The prisoners were incarcerated in harsh conditions. = 囚犯们在恶劣的条件下被监禁。

疑问句:
Will he be incarcerated? = 他会被监禁吗？
How long was she incarcerated? = 她被监禁了多久？

否定句:
They did not incarcerate him. = 他们没有监禁他。
The judge decided not to incarcerate the defendant. = 法官决定不监禁被告。
