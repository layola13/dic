fresh-water: /ˈfreʃˌwɔːtər/ | adj. |淡水的；生长在淡水中的|Relating to or inhabiting fresh water, as opposed to salt water.  Fresh water is naturally occurring water that is relatively pure and free from salts and other minerals.|例句：The lake is a fresh-water habitat for many species of fish.  这个湖是许多鱼类的淡水栖息地。

例句：They are studying the impact of pollution on fresh-water ecosystems. 他们正在研究污染对淡水生态系统的影响。


近义词：non-saline, unsalty | 反义词：salt-water, marine


词源:

* **fresh:**  From Old English *frisc*, meaning "fresh, vigorous, strong."  This word has a long history and its meaning has evolved to include the idea of purity and lack of staleness, which is relevant to its use in "fresh water."

* **water:** From Old English *wæter*, of Germanic origin; related to Dutch *water* and German *Wasser*.
