misfashioned:/ˌmɪsˈfæʃənd/| adj.|  做工粗糙的；设计拙劣的；畸形的 | Ill-formed or badly made; deformed.  The misfashioned chair wobbled precariously. 这把做工粗糙的椅子摇摇晃晃地很不稳。 The sculpture, though misfashioned, held a certain charm.  这尊雕塑虽然做工粗糙，却别有一番魅力。|近义词：malformed, ill-made, deformed, imperfect|反义词：well-made, perfectly formed, flawless|


词源:

mis- (否定前缀) + fashioned (过去分词，源于fashion “制作，塑造”)


用法说明:

misfashioned 主要用作形容词，描述物体或人的形态或制作工艺不佳。它强调的是一种不完美、粗糙或畸形的状态，而不是简单的不好看。


例句：

The misfashioned toy broke easily.  这件做工粗糙的玩具很容易坏。

His misfashioned argument was unconvincing. 他那拙劣的论点令人无法信服。


近义词：

malformed (畸形的)
ill-made (做工差的)
deformed (畸形的)
awkward (笨拙的，难看的)


反义词：

well-made (做工精良的)
perfectly formed (形态完美的)
flawless (完美的)
elegant (优雅的)


PHRASE->

There are no common set phrases using "misfashioned".  The word itself functions as a standalone adjective.
