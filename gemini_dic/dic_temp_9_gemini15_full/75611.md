fugacity:/fjuːˈɡæsɪti/ | n. |逸度，逃逸度 |The fugacity of a substance is a measure of its tendency to escape from a phase.  It is particularly useful in describing the behavior of gases and liquids under non-ideal conditions, where the ideal gas law does not accurately reflect reality. Fugacity takes into account the intermolecular forces that affect the behavior of real gases and liquids. |Example:  The fugacity of the gas increased with pressure.  气体的逸度随着压力的增加而增加。|近义词：activity (in some contexts)|反义词：N/A
