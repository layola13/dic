mayfly: /ˈmeɪflaɪ/ | n. |蜉蝣| A mayfly is an insect with a very short adult life, typically only a few hours or days.  They are characterized by their delicate bodies and two or three tails. Mayflies are important indicators of water quality. | Example:  The mayflies danced over the river in a fleeting display of life. 蜉蝣在河面上翩翩起舞，展现着短暂的生命。  The life cycle of a mayfly is fascinating. 蜉蝣的生命周期令人着迷。|近义词: ephemeron |反义词: long-lived insect

词源:
The word "mayfly" comes from the Old English "mǣigflēoge," literally meaning "May fly," referring to their appearance in the month of May.  The etymology highlights their strong association with the springtime.
