drastic: /ˈdræstɪk/ | adj. | 激烈的；极端的；猛烈的 |  extremely severe or sudden;  markedly different from the usual or expected.  |  例句：They had to take drastic measures to control the fire.  他们不得不采取严厉措施来控制火势。


例句：The doctor suggested drastic surgery to remove the tumor. 医生建议进行剧烈的外科手术以切除肿瘤。


近义词：extreme, radical, severe, desperate, urgent


反义词：gradual, moderate, mild, gentle


词性：形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The situation requires drastic measures. =  这种情况需要采取严厉措施。
Drastic changes are needed to improve the company's performance. =  公司业绩的改善需要进行剧烈的改变。


一般过去时:
He took drastic action to stop the bleeding. = 他采取了紧急措施止血。
She made a drastic decision to quit her job. = 她做出了一个离开工作的重大决定。


现在完成时:
We have taken drastic steps to reduce costs. = 我们已经采取了严厉措施以降低成本。
They have made drastic improvements to their product. = 他们对他们的产品做了很大的改进。


过去完成时:
The company had already taken drastic measures before the crisis hit. = 在危机来临之前，公司已经采取了严厉措施。
She had made a drastic change in her lifestyle before she moved. = 在搬家之前，她已经对自己的生活方式做出了巨大的改变。


将来时:
We will have to take drastic action if the problem persists. = 如果问题持续存在，我们将不得不采取严厉措施。
The government will likely implement drastic reforms. = 政府可能会实施激烈的改革。


虚拟语气:
If we took drastic measures, we might have prevented the disaster. = 如果我们采取严厉措施，我们也许能够预防这场灾难。
I wish I hadn't made such a drastic decision. = 我真希望我没有做出如此激烈的决定。


被动语态:
Drastic measures were taken to prevent further damage. = 采取了严厉措施以防止进一步的损害。
Drastic changes are being implemented to improve the system. = 正在实施剧烈的改变以改善系统。


疑问句:
Are drastic changes necessary? = 剧烈的改变有必要吗？
Will drastic measures be effective? = 严厉措施会有效吗？


否定句:
The situation doesn't require drastic measures. = 这种情况不需要采取严厉措施。
We didn't take drastic action at that time. = 我们当时没有采取严厉措施。

词源:
From Medieval Latin *drasticus, from Greek drastikos, from draō "I do, act".  The word ultimately derives from the Greek verb "draō" meaning "to do" or "to act," suggesting something done forcefully or decisively.
