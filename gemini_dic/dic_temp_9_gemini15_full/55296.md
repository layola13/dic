dissolute:/ˈdɪsəluːt/| adj.|放荡的；荒淫的；颓废的| characterized by or given to excessive indulgence in pleasure or sensuality; lacking restraint; dissipated.  The word implies a moral decay and a lack of self-control leading to a life of debauchery.|例句：His dissolute lifestyle led to his ruin.  他放荡的生活导致了他的毁灭。

例句：The novel depicts a dissolute nobleman who squanders his fortune on wine, women, and song. 这部小说描绘了一位挥霍家财于酒色财气上的放荡贵族。


近义词：debauched, depraved, licentious, profligate, dissipated, immoral|反义词：chaste, virtuous, moral, upright, temperate


词源:  The word "dissolute" comes from the Latin word "dis-” (meaning "apart" or "away") and "solvere" (meaning "to loosen" or "to dissolve").  It originally referred to something being loosened or dissolved, but evolved to describe a moral loosening or dissolution of character.
