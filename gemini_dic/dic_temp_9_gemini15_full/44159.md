criticises:/ˈkrɪtɪsaɪz/| vt.|批评，指责|To express disapproval of someone or something.|The professor criticised my essay for its lack of originality.  教授批评我的论文缺乏原创性。|He criticised the government's handling of the crisis. 他批评政府处理危机的措施。|praise, condemn, rebuke|support, praise, commend|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He criticises the government's policies regularly. 他定期批评政府的政策。
She criticises her friends' choices sometimes. 她有时批评朋友的选择。


一般过去时:
The teacher criticised John's poor handwriting. 老师批评了约翰糟糕的字迹。
They criticised the film's weak plot. 他们批评了这部电影薄弱的情节。


现在完成时:
He has criticised the company's unethical practices for years. 他多年来一直批评这家公司的违反道德行为。
She has criticised the new law since its inception. 自从这项新法律出台以来，她就一直批评它。


过去完成时:
The critics had criticised the play before its premiere. 批评家们在该剧首映之前就批评了它。
They had criticised the plan's feasibility before they agreed to it. 在同意这项计划之前，他们已经批评了它的可行性。


将来时:
He will criticise the proposal during the meeting. 他将在会议上批评这项提案。
She will criticise his behaviour if he continues to act inappropriately. 如果他继续行为不当，她将会批评他的行为。


虚拟语气:
If he criticised the system, he might face consequences. 如果他批评这个制度，他可能会面临后果。
I wish he hadn't criticised me in front of everyone. 我真希望他没在大家面前批评我。


被动语态:
His work was criticised for being too theoretical. 他的作品因过于理论化而受到批评。
The new policy is being criticised by many people. 许多人正在批评这项新政策。


疑问句:
Does he often criticise his colleagues? 他经常批评他的同事吗？
Will she criticise my presentation? 她会批评我的演示文稿吗？


否定句:
He didn't criticise her performance. 他没有批评她的表现。
She doesn't criticise her children often. 她不经常批评她的孩子。
