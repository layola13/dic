equal:/ˈiːkwəl/ | adj., n., v. | 等同的；平等的；相等的；同等的人；使相等 |

英文解析:  As an adjective, "equal" means being the same in quantity, size, degree, or value. As a noun, it refers to a person or thing that is equal to another. As a verb, it means to be or make equal.


例句:
They received equal pay for equal work. = 他们做相同的工作获得相同的报酬。

The two teams are fairly equal in strength. = 这两支球队实力相当。


近义词:equivalent, alike, same, uniform, even


反义词:unequal, different, dissimilar, unlike


词性: adjective, noun, verb

词源:
The word "equal" comes from the Old French word "equal," which in turn originates from the Latin word "aequalis," meaning "equal," "uniform," or "level."  The Latin word is derived from "aequus," meaning "level," "even," or "just."
