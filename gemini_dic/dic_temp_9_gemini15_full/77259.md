gastropod:/ˈɡæstrəpɒd/|/ˈɡæstrəpɑːd/| n. |腹足动物|A mollusk of the class Gastropoda, such as a snail or slug, that has a soft, unsegmented body and typically a spiral shell.  Gastropods are characterized by their single, spirally coiled shell (in most species) and a muscular foot used for locomotion.|Example: Snails and slugs are common examples of gastropods.|近义词：mollusk, shellfish|反义词：None specifically, but could be contrasted with other mollusk classes like bivalves.
