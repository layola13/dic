leaping:/ˈliːpɪŋ/| adj. |跳跃的；飞跃的|  Describes the act of jumping or springing a long way, often with great force or excitement.  It can also describe something that is sudden, dramatic, or significant in its progress. |例句：The leaping flames consumed the building quickly.  跃动的火焰迅速吞噬了建筑物。

The horse gave a powerful leaping bound over the fence. 马纵身一跃跳过了栅栏。


近义词：jumping, springing, vaulting, soaring


反义词：crawling, creeping, inching

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is leaping for joy. 他高兴地跳跃着。
The deer are leaping across the field. 鹿在田野里跳跃。

一般过去时:
She leaped over the puddle. 她跳过了水坑。
The rabbit leaped into the bushes. 兔子跳进了灌木丛。

现在完成时:
He has leaped to fame. 他突然成名了。
They have leaped over many obstacles. 他们克服了许多障碍。

过去完成时:
The athlete had leaped before the whistle blew. 运动员在哨子吹响前就跳了。
She had leaped to a conclusion. 她得出了一个草率的结论。

将来时:
He will leap into action. 他将立即行动。
The frog will leap into the pond. 青蛙将跳入池塘。

虚拟语气:
If he were to leap, he might break his leg. 如果他跳的话，可能会摔断腿。
I wish I could leap tall buildings in a single bound. 我真希望我能一跃而过高楼大厦。

被动语态:
The distance was leaped in record time. 这段距离以创纪录的时间被跨越。
The hurdle was easily leaped by the runner. 跑步者轻松地越过了障碍。

疑问句:
Is he leaping? 他在跳跃吗？
Are the children leaping with excitement? 孩子们兴奋地跳跃吗？

否定句:
He did not leap to conclusions. 他没有草率下结论。
The cat did not leap onto the table. 猫没有跳到桌子上。
