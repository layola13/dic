dallying: /ˈdæliɪŋ/ | n. | 耽搁；磨蹭；嬉戏；调情 |  The act of delaying or wasting time; playful or flirtatious behavior. |

例句：

His dallying with the project led to missed deadlines.  他的项目拖延导致错过最后期限。

The couple's dallying in the park was interrupted by a sudden downpour.  这对情侣在公园的嬉戏被突如其来的大雨打断了。


近义词：procrastination, delaying, trifling, flirting, toying


反义词：promptness, haste, diligence, seriousness


词性：名词 (gerund,  present participle of the verb "dally")


USAGE->

He is dallying with the idea. 他在考虑那个主意。

They are dallying on the beach. 他们在海滩上嬉戏。


一般过去时:

She dallied over her coffee. 她慢慢地喝着咖啡。

He dallied with several women before settling down. 他在安定下来之前和好几个女人调情。

They have been dallying for too long. 他们磨蹭太久了。

过去完成时:

He had dallied too long and missed the train. 他磨蹭太久，错过了火车。

They had dallied with the enemy before the battle. 他们在战斗前曾与敌人勾结。

被动语态:  (The verb "dally" is rarely used in the passive voice.)

否定句:

词源:

The word "dally" comes from the Middle English word "dalien," meaning "to play" or "to trifle."  This, in turn, is believed to have roots in Old Norse words related to delay or idle talk.
