daunt:/dɔːnt/| vt.|使胆怯；使气馁；使畏惧|to make (someone) feel intimidated or apprehensive|The task of writing a 1000-page book daunted her.  写一本1000页的书的任务让她感到气馁。|The steep climb didn't daunt the experienced hikers.  陡峭的攀登并没有吓倒经验丰富的徒步旅行者。|intimidate, discourage, dismay|encourage, embolden, inspire|动词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The difficult task daunts him. = 困难的任务使他气馁。
The huge workload daunted the employees. = 巨大的工作量使员工们感到气馁。

一般过去时:
The storm daunted the sailors. = 暴风雨使水手们胆怯。
The unexpected news daunted her. = 意想不到的消息让她吓了一跳。

现在完成时:
Nothing has daunted her ambition. = 没有什么能阻碍她的雄心壮志。
The challenges have not daunted his spirit. = 这些挑战并没有让他灰心。

过去完成时:
She had been daunted by the previous failure. = 她曾被之前的失败吓倒过。
They had been daunted by the complexity of the problem. = 他们曾被问题的复杂性吓倒过。

将来时:
The upcoming exam will daunt many students. = 即将来临的考试将使许多学生感到害怕。
The long journey will daunt some travelers. = 漫长的旅程将会使一些旅行者望而却步。


虚拟语气:
If the task didn't daunt me, I would have tried it. = 如果这项任务没有让我气馁，我会尝试的。
If the difficulty hadn't daunted him, he would have succeeded. = 如果困难没有让他气馁，他会成功的。

被动语态:
He was daunted by the prospect of public speaking. = 他被公开演讲的前景吓到了。
She was daunted by the sheer scale of the project. = 她被项目的巨大规模吓到了。

疑问句:
Does the challenge daunt you? = 挑战吓倒你了吗？
Does the prospect of failure daunt them? = 失败的前景吓倒他们了吗？

否定句:
The difficult task didn't daunt her. = 困难的任务没有吓倒她。
The threat didn't daunt him. = 威胁并没有吓倒他。



PHRASE->
be daunted by = 被…吓倒
daunt someone's courage = 吓破某人的胆
daunt the spirit = 使…灰心丧气
nothing daunts sb. = 什么也吓不倒某人
daunt one's hopes = 使某人的希望破灭
