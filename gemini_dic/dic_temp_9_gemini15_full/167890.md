princesses:/ˈprɪnsɛsɪz/| n. |公主们，女王子们 |The word "princesses" is the plural form of "princess," referring to multiple female members of a royal family.  It implies a high social standing and often suggests elegance, grace, and privilege. However, modern portrayals of princesses in literature and media often explore a wider range of personalities and experiences. |例句：The princesses waved to the cheering crowds. 公主们向欢呼的人群挥手致意。

The three princesses each had a unique talent. 三位公主各有各的才能。


近义词：royal women, noblewomen | 反义词：commoners, paupers


WORD ORIGIN->

The word "princess" originates from the Old French word "princesse," which in turn derives from the Latin word "principem" (meaning "chief" or "principal").  The suffix "-ess" indicates the feminine form, signifying a female ruler or member of a ruling family.  The plural "princesses" simply adds the standard English plural suffix "-es."
