conflicting:/kənˈflɪktɪŋ/ | adj. | 冲突的；矛盾的 |  Describes things that are in opposition or disagreement; incompatible.  | Example:  The witness gave conflicting accounts of the accident.  目击者对事故的描述相互矛盾。

The team faced conflicting priorities: speed and quality. 团队面临着速度和质量相互冲突的优先事项。|近义词：contradictory, inconsistent, opposing |反义词：consistent, harmonious, compatible |形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The reports present conflicting information. = 报告提供了相互矛盾的信息。
His statements are conflicting with the facts. = 他的陈述与事实相冲突。

一般过去时:
The two stories gave conflicting details. = 这两个故事提供了相互矛盾的细节。
Their plans conflicted with ours. = 他们的计划与我们的计划相冲突。

现在完成时:

过去完成时:

词源:
The word "conflicting" comes from the verb "conflict," which ultimately derives from the Latin word "confligere," meaning "to strike together," "to clash," or "to fight."  The suffix "-ing" transforms the verb into a present participle, used here as an adjective.
