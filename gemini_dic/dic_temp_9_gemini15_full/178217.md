recommissioned:/ˌriːkəˈmɪʃənd/| v. |重新委托；重新启用；再启用 | To commission again; to put back into service after a period of inactivity or disuse.  The ship was recommissioned after extensive repairs. 这艘船经过大修后重新启用。 |近义词：reactivate, reinstate, re-engage | 反义词： decommission


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company recommissions old equipment regularly. = 公司定期重新启用旧设备。
They recommission the software every year. = 他们每年都重新启用该软件。


一般过去时:
The army recommissioned the old tanks. = 军队重新启用旧坦克。
The bridge was recommissioned after the earthquake. = 地震后，桥梁重新启用。


现在完成时:
The museum has recommissioned several artists for new exhibitions. = 博物馆已经委托了几位艺术家举办新的展览。
They have recommissioned the power plant after the upgrade. = 升级后，他们已经重新启用发电厂。


过去完成时:
The government had recommissioned the warship before the conflict began. = 冲突开始前，政府已重新启用军舰。
The factory had recommissioned the machinery by the time the order arrived. = 订单到达时，工厂已重新启用机器。


将来时:
The airline will recommission the plane after the maintenance. = 维修后，航空公司将重新启用飞机。
They will recommission the building after the renovation is complete. = 装修完成后，他们将重新启用这座建筑物。


虚拟语气:
If they had enough funding, they would recommission the project. = 如果他们有足够的资金，他们就会重新启动项目。

被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:

PHRASE->

词源:
re- (prefix indicating again) + commission (to officially request someone to create something or to do a job)
