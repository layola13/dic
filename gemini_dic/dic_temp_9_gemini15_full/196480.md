servants:/ˈsɜːrvənts/| n. |仆人，佣人，服务员|People employed in the household to perform duties such as cleaning, cooking, and other domestic tasks.  Servants were common in wealthy households before the 20th century, and the term can also refer to those who serve in a more general sense, such as civil servants.|例句：In the past, many wealthy families employed numerous servants.  过去，许多富裕家庭雇佣了许多仆人。

The hotel provides excellent service with friendly servants. 这家酒店提供优质服务和友好的服务员。|近义词：domestic, employee, attendant, retainer|反义词：employer, master

词源:
The word "servant" comes from the Old French word "servant," which itself derives from the Latin word "serviens," the present participle of "servire," meaning "to serve."  The evolution reflects the core meaning of the word, which centers on the act of service.
