catalyzing:/ˈkætəlaɪzɪŋ/| v.| 催化；促成；激发 | To cause or accelerate (a reaction, process, or change)  |例句：The discovery catalyzed a surge in research.  发现促使了研究的激增。 |例句：The teacher's enthusiasm catalyzed learning in the classroom. 老师的热情激发了课堂上的学习。|近义词：accelerating, stimulating, triggering |反义词：inhibiting, hindering, retarding |动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The enzyme catalyzes the reaction. = 这种酶催化该反应。
This event catalyzes social change. = 这件事促进了社会变革。

一般过去时:
The catalyst catalyzed the chemical reaction. = 催化剂催化了化学反应。
His speech catalyzed the movement. = 他的演讲促进了这场运动。

现在完成时:
The reforms have catalyzed economic growth. = 改革催生了经济增长。
Her work has catalyzed a new era of scientific discovery. = 她的工作催生了科学发现的新时代。

过去完成时:
The previous events had catalyzed the current crisis. = 先前的事件已经导致了当前的危机。
The discovery had catalyzed further investigation. = 这一发现促进了进一步的调查。

将来时:
The new technology will catalyze innovation. = 新技术将催化创新。
This policy will catalyze economic development. = 这项政策将促进经济发展。

虚拟语气:
If we had a catalyst, the reaction would catalyze faster. = 如果我们有催化剂，反应就会更快。
If the government invested more, it would catalyze economic growth. = 如果政府投入更多资金，它将促进经济增长。

被动语态:
The reaction was catalyzed by the enzyme. = 该反应是由酶催化的。
The change was catalyzed by a series of events. = 这一变化是由一系列事件催化的。

疑问句:
What catalyzed the change? = 什么促成了这种变化？
What will catalyze innovation in this field? = 什么才能促进该领域的创新？

否定句:
The lack of funding did not catalyze the project. = 缺乏资金并没有促进该项目。
The inhibitor did not catalyze the reaction; instead, it slowed it down. = 抑制剂并没有催化反应，反而减缓了反应速度。



PHRASE->
catalyze change = 催化变革
catalyze growth = 催化增长
catalyze innovation = 催化创新
catalyze a reaction = 催化反应
catalyze development = 催化发展


词源:
来自希腊语 *katalysis，由kata-（向下）和lysis（松开，分解）构成，字面意思是“分解”。  最初指化学催化作用，后来引申出更广泛的意义。
