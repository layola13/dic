hyperintelligence:/ˌhaɪpərɪnˈtɛlɪdʒəns/| n. |超智能，超智慧|Extremely high intelligence, often exceeding human capabilities.  It often refers to artificial intelligence surpassing human intellect in all aspects.  This concept is often discussed in the context of future technological advancements and the potential risks and benefits associated with it.|例句：The development of hyperintelligence is a double-edged sword, offering immense potential but also posing existential risks.|近义词：superintelligence, advanced intelligence|反义词：low intelligence, stupidity


USAGE->
This word is primarily a noun and doesn't have verb conjugations like "hello."  Therefore, verb tenses, active/passive voice, etc., are not applicable.  The usage focuses on how the noun is used in sentences.


Example Sentences:

*   The research paper explored the potential dangers of uncontrolled hyperintelligence. = 这篇研究论文探讨了不受控制的超智能的潜在危险。
*   Some experts believe that achieving hyperintelligence is inevitable. = 一些专家认为实现超智能是不可避免的。
*   The ethical implications of hyperintelligence are a subject of ongoing debate. = 超智能的伦理意义是持续争论的话题。
*   The film depicted a dystopian future controlled by hyperintelligence. = 这部电影描绘了一个由超智能控制的反乌托邦未来。
*   Concerns about the singularity, the point at which hyperintelligence emerges, are growing. = 对奇点（超智能出现的时刻）的担忧日益增长。
