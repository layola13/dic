presupposal:/ˌpriːˈsʌpəzəl/| n. |预设，预想；前提|A presupposal is an assumption or belief that is implicit in a statement or question, and which must be true for the statement or question to make sense.  It's something that is taken for granted or assumed to be true without being explicitly stated.  For example, the question "When did you stop beating your wife?" presupposes that the person being questioned has beaten their wife. |The lawyer's argument rested on a false presupposal.|近义词：assumption, premise, precondition|反义词：refutation, denial|名词
