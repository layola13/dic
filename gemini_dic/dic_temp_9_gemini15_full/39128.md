confuse:/kənˈfjuːz/ | vt. |使困惑；使混乱 | To make someone unable to think clearly or understand something. |The complicated instructions confused me.  复杂的说明把我弄糊涂了。|  The similar packaging confused the customers.  相似的包装让顾客感到困惑。| bewilder, perplex | clarify, enlighten | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The noise confuses the baby. = 噪音使婴儿感到困惑。
This question confuses many students. = 这个问题让许多学生感到困惑。

一般过去时:
The sudden change confused him. = 突然的变化使他感到困惑。
The complex plot confused the audience. = 复杂的剧情让观众感到困惑。

现在完成时:
The new rules have confused everyone. = 新规章已经让每个人都感到困惑。
This ambiguous statement has confused the public. = 这句模棱两可的话语让公众感到困惑。

过去完成时:
The conflicting information had confused her earlier. = 之前相互矛盾的信息让她感到困惑。
He had confused the dates, leading to a scheduling conflict. = 他把日期搞混了，导致了时间冲突。

将来时:
The new software will confuse some users. = 新软件会让一些用户感到困惑。
The unexpected announcement will confuse the market. = 意外的公告将会扰乱市场。

虚拟语气:
If I hadn't confused the addresses, I wouldn't have been late. = 如果我没有把地址搞混，我就不会迟到了。
I wish I hadn't confused the two files. = 我真希望我没有把这两个文件搞混。

被动语态:
The students were confused by the difficult exam. = 学生们被那场艰难的考试难住了。
He was confused by the contradictory evidence. = 他被相互矛盾的证据搞糊涂了。

疑问句:
Are you confused about the instructions? = 你对说明感到困惑吗？
Is the situation confusing to you? = 这种情况让你感到困惑吗？

否定句:
Don't confuse the two concepts. = 不要混淆这两个概念。
I wasn't confused by his explanation. = 我没有被他解释的东西弄糊涂。


PHRASE->
be confused about = 对...感到困惑
