hothoused:/ˈhɒthaʊzd/| adj.|  温室栽培的；娇生惯养的；过度保护的 |  The word describes something that has been grown or developed in artificially favorable conditions, often resulting in a lack of resilience or robustness. It can refer to plants, animals, or even people. | 例句：The hothoused orchids were unable to survive the harsh winter. 温室里的兰花无法熬过严酷的冬天。The hothoused children struggled to adapt to the challenges of independent life. 这些娇生惯养的孩子难以适应独立生活的挑战。| 近义词：pampered, sheltered, coddled, overprotected | 反义词：hardy, robust, resilient
