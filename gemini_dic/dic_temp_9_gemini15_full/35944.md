coddled:/ˈkɒdld/ /ˈkɑːdld/| vt.| 溺爱；娇惯；细心照料；煨；炖| to treat someone too carefully and protectively, often to the point of hindering their independence and development; to cook (food) gently in liquid|例句：She coddled her son, and he never learned to cope with difficulties. 她溺爱她的儿子，以至于他从未学会如何应对困难。
The chef coddled the eggs in warm milk. 厨师用温牛奶煨鸡蛋。|近义词：pamper, spoil, indulge, cherish|反义词：neglect, ignore, harsh


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He coddles his children excessively. = 他过分溺爱他的孩子。
She coddles her plants every day. = 她每天都细心照料她的植物。

一般过去时:
My grandmother coddled me when I was sick. = 我生病的时候奶奶细心照料我。
They coddled the baby all day. = 他们整天都娇惯着婴儿。


现在完成时:
She has coddled him for years. = 她已经溺爱他好几年了。
The cook has coddled the vegetables until they are tender. = 厨师已经把蔬菜煨得很软了。


过去完成时:
