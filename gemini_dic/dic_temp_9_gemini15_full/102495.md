jacobinical:/ˌdʒækəˈbɪnɪkəl/ | adj. | 雅各宾派的；激进的；好暴动的 | Relating to the Jacobin Club, a radical political club in the French Revolution, or to its principles and methods.  The term often implies extreme revolutionary fervor, often associated with violence and disregard for established order. |  His jacobinical views led him to advocate for the overthrow of the monarchy. 他的雅各宾派观点使他主张推翻君主制。 The government accused the opposition party of harboring jacobinical tendencies. 政府指责反对党怀有雅各宾派的倾向。 | 近义词：radical, revolutionary, extremist, fervent | 反义词：conservative, moderate, reactionary
