quasi-patient:/ˈkwɑːziˌpeɪʃənt/| n.|准病人，类病人|A quasi-patient is someone who exhibits some, but not all, of the characteristics of a patient.  They may be seeking medical attention, but not in the traditional sense of being diagnosed with a specific illness or undergoing treatment.  This term is often used in contexts like research studies or in situations where individuals present with symptoms but don't fully meet the criteria for a formal diagnosis.|例句：The study included quasi-patients who reported symptoms but had not yet received a formal diagnosis. 这项研究包括那些报告症状但尚未得到正式诊断的准病人。|例句：The therapist worked with quasi-patients to improve their coping mechanisms, even without a clear clinical diagnosis.  治疗师与准病人一起改善他们的应对机制，即使没有明确的临床诊断。|近义词：potential patient, symptomatic individual|反义词：patient, healthy individual|n.
