scandalize:/ˈskændəlaɪz/| vt.|使震惊；使愤慨；使不齿|To shock or offend someone by doing something considered morally wrong or unacceptable.  The politician's behavior scandalized the public.|近义词：shock, offend, appall, horrify|反义词：please, delight


USAGE->
一般现在时:
His actions scandalize the community. = 他的行为令社区震惊。
The news scandalizes many people. = 这则新闻令许多人感到震惊。

一般过去时:
The revelation scandalized the entire nation. = 这一揭露震惊了全国。
Her behavior scandalized her family. = 她的行为令她的家人感到不齿。

现在完成时:
His behavior has scandalized everyone who knew him. = 他的行为令所有认识他的人都感到震惊。
The scandal has scandalized the whole country. = 这场丑闻震惊了全国。

过去完成时:
The details of the case had scandalized the court. = 此案的细节曾令法庭震惊。
They had scandalized the town with their behaviour. = 他们的行为曾令小镇不齿。


将来时:
The upcoming report will scandalize the public. = 即将来临的报告将令公众震惊。
His actions will scandalize future generations. = 他的行为将令后代震惊。

虚拟语气:
If the truth were known, it would scandalize everyone. = 如果真相大白，它将令所有人震惊。
I wish this news hadn't scandalized my parents. = 我真希望这条新闻没有让我父母震惊。

被动语态:
The public was scandalized by the politician's lies. = 公众被这位政客的谎言震惊了。
The town was scandalized by the recent events. = 小镇因最近发生的事件而感到震惊。

疑问句:
Would this news scandalize the general public? = 这则新闻会令大众震惊吗？
Does his behavior scandalize you? = 他的行为令你震惊吗？

否定句:
His actions did not scandalize me. = 他的行为并没有令我震惊。
The movie did not scandalize the critics. = 这部电影并没有令影评家们感到震惊。
