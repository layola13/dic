marginal:/ˈmɑːrdʒɪnəl/| adj.|边缘的；微不足道的；不重要的；|  relating to the edge or margin of something; of little importance; barely sufficient. |例句：The marginal notes in the book were helpful. 书中的边缘笔记很有帮助。
The marginal cost of producing one more unit was negligible. 生产更多一个单位的边际成本可以忽略不计。|近义词：peripheral, insignificant, minor, trifling |反义词：central, important, significant, major


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The marginal cost is low. = 边际成本很低。
His marginal contribution is small. = 他的边际贡献很小。

一般过去时:
The marginal profit was high last year. = 去年边际利润很高。
The marginal land was unproductive. = 边缘土地不产出。

现在完成时:
They have achieved only marginal improvements. = 他们只取得了微小的改进。
The project has had a marginal impact. = 该项目的影响微乎其微。

过去完成时:
The company had already reached its marginal capacity. = 公司已经达到了其边际产能。
The farmers had experienced marginal gains in yield. = 农民的产量只略有增加。

将来时:
The new policy will have only a marginal effect. = 新政策的影响将微乎其微。
The investment will yield marginal returns. = 这项投资将产生微薄的回报。

虚拟语气:
If the marginal benefit were greater, we would invest more. = 如果边际收益更大，我们会投入更多。
I wish the marginal cost were lower. = 我希望边际成本更低。

被动语态:
The proposal was deemed marginal. = 该提案被认为不重要。
The changes were described as marginal. = 这些变化被描述为微不足道的。

疑问句:
Are the marginal benefits worth the cost? = 边际效益值得付出代价吗？
Is the marginal profit sufficient for expansion? = 边际利润足以扩张吗？

否定句:
The changes were not marginal; they were significant. = 这些变化并非微不足道；它们意义重大。
The impact was not marginal; it was substantial. = 影响并非微不足道；而是巨大的。
