reductionally:/rɪˈdʌkʃənəli/| adv.| 还原地；简化地 |  Approaching or explaining something in a simplified or overly simplified manner, often by reducing it to its most basic or essential components.  This often involves neglecting complexities or nuances. | Example:  The argument was presented reductionally, ignoring the many social and economic factors involved.  这个论点被简化地阐述了，忽略了许多相关的社会和经济因素。|近义词：simply, basically, superficially|反义词：holistically, comprehensively, thoroughly
