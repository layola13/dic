misinformed:/ˌmɪsɪnˈfɔːrmd/| adj.| 错误信息的，被误导的 |Having received inaccurate or misleading information.  Being wrongly informed. |例句：He was misinformed about the meeting time. 他被误导了会议的时间。

例句：The public was misinformed about the true nature of the event. 公众对事件的真实性质被误导了。


近义词：deceived, misled, deluded, mistaken


反义词：well-informed, enlightened, educated


词源: mis- (wrongly) + informed (having been given information)  The prefix "mis-" indicates a negative or wrong action. "Informed" comes from the verb "inform," meaning to give someone information.


USAGE->

They are misinformed regarding the new regulations. 他们对新的规章制度了解错误。


一般过去时:

现在完成时:

过去完成时:

将来时:

虚拟语气:
If he hadn't been misinformed, he would have attended the meeting. 如果他没有被误导，他就会参加会议。

被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:

They were not misinformed; they chose to ignore the facts. 他们没有被误导；他们选择忽视事实。
