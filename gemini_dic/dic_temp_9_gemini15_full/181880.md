reluctance:/rɪˈlʌktəns/| n. |勉强，不愿意，迟疑| unwillingness or disinclination to do something.|He showed some reluctance to answer my questions. 他对回答我的问题有些犹豫。|hesitation, unwillingness, resistance|eagerness, willingness|名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
His reluctance is understandable. = 他的不情愿是可以理解的。
Her reluctance to help is concerning. = 她不愿帮忙令人担忧。


一般过去时:
His reluctance surprised me. = 他的犹豫让我吃惊。
There was a great reluctance to change. = 当时存在着很大的抵制变化的情绪。


现在完成时:
My reluctance has grown over time. = 我随着时间的推移越来越不愿意了。
Their reluctance to cooperate has hindered progress. = 他们不愿合作阻碍了进展。


过去完成时:
His reluctance had been evident throughout the discussion. = 在整个讨论过程中，他的不情愿都很明显。
I understood her reluctance; she had already had a bad day. = 我理解她的不情愿；她已经度过了糟糕的一天。


将来时:
His reluctance will likely continue. = 他的不情愿可能会持续下去。
I anticipate some reluctance from the team. = 我预料团队会有一些抵触情绪。


虚拟语气:
If there were no reluctance, the project would have finished earlier. = 如果没有犹豫，项目本来可以早点完成。
I wish there were less reluctance to embrace new technologies. = 我希望对新技术的接受度能更高一些。


被动语态:
His reluctance was noted by the committee. = 委员会注意到了他的不情愿。
The reluctance to invest was widely reported. = 投资的犹豫受到了广泛报道。


疑问句:
What is the reason for your reluctance? = 你为什么这么不愿意？
Is there any reluctance to proceed? = 有什么不愿意继续进行的理由吗？


否定句:
There was no reluctance on his part. = 他没有任何不情愿。
She showed no reluctance to accept the offer. = 她毫不犹豫地接受了这个提议。


PHRASE->
show reluctance = 表现出不情愿
overcome reluctance = 克服不情愿
express reluctance = 表达不情愿
with reluctance = 勉强地
feel reluctance = 感到不情愿

词源:
reluctance源自于动词reluct，其词根为拉丁语的“reluctari”，意为“to struggle against, resist”。  因此，reluctance的核心含义是抗拒或抵制某种行动的意愿。
