prenup:/ˈpriːnʌp/| n. |婚前协议|A prenuptial agreement is a legal agreement made by a couple before they get married.  It outlines how their assets and property will be divided in the event of a divorce or separation.  This can include details on finances, property ownership, and even child custody arrangements (though child custody is often handled separately).  Prenups are designed to protect each party's assets and provide clarity in a potentially contentious situation. |Example: They signed a prenuptial agreement before their wedding to protect their individual businesses. 他们婚前签署了一份婚前协议以保护各自的事业。 |近义词：prenuptial agreement, ante-nuptial agreement |反义词：postnuptial agreement
