car-pooled:/ˈkɑːrpuːld/ | v. |拼车；合乘 |  The past tense and past participle of "carpool". It describes the act of sharing a car ride with others, typically to reduce costs or environmental impact. |  例句：We car-pooled to work this morning to save on gas. = 今天早上我们拼车上班以节省汽油。

We often car-pooled with our neighbors during the school year. = 在上学期间，我们经常和邻居们拼车。| 近义词：shared a ride, rode together | 反义词：drove alone, solo drive |

词源:
The word "carpool" is a compound word formed from "car" and "pool".  "Car" refers to an automobile, and "pool" in this context means to share or combine resources.  The term emerged in the mid-20th century as car ownership became more common, and the need for efficient transportation sharing increased.
