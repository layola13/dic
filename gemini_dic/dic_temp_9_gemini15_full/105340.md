kick-started:/ˈkɪkˌstɑːrtɪd/| v. |启动；促进；激发；|To give something a sudden boost or impetus to get it started or going again.|The government's new policies kick-started the economy. 政府的新政策刺激了经济。|近义词：initiate, launch, boost, stimulate |反义词：halt, stop, hinder


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He kick-starts his day with a cup of coffee. = 他每天早上喝一杯咖啡来开始新的一天。
The new marketing campaign kick-starts sales. = 新的营销活动刺激了销售。


一般过去时:
The new CEO kick-started a company-wide restructuring. = 新任首席执行官启动了公司范围内的重组。
The grant kick-started the research project. = 这笔拨款启动了该研究项目。


现在完成时:
The investment has kick-started several new businesses. = 这项投资已经启动了几家新企业。
The new technology has kick-started a revolution in the industry. = 新技术已经引发了行业革命。


过去完成时:
They had kick-started the project before the funding was secured. = 在资金到位之前，他们就已经启动了该项目。
The team had kick-started the negotiations before the deadline. = 团队在截止日期之前已经启动了谈判。


将来时:
The company will kick-start a new product line next year. = 公司明年将启动一条新的产品线。
The conference will kick-start discussions on climate change. = 会议将启动关于气候变化的讨论。


虚拟语气:
If they had kick-started the project earlier, they would have finished on time. = 如果他们早些启动项目，他们就能按时完成。
I wish they had kick-started the reforms sooner. = 我希望他们早点启动改革。


被动语态:
The project was kick-started by a generous donation. = 该项目由一笔慷慨的捐款启动。
The economy was kick-started by government spending. = 政府支出刺激了经济。


疑问句:
Did the new policy kick-start economic growth? = 新政策是否刺激了经济增长？
Will the new marketing campaign kick-start sales? = 新的营销活动能否刺激销售？


否定句:
The lack of funding failed to kick-start the project. = 资金不足未能启动该项目。
The initial marketing campaign didn't kick-start sales as expected. = 初始的营销活动没有像预期那样刺激销售。
