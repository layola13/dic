genteelish:/dʒɛnˈtiːlɪʃ/ | adj. |略显优雅的，假装优雅的，做作优雅的 | Somewhat genteel; affectedly genteel.  It suggests a pretense or superficiality to the elegance.  The word implies a slightly condescending or ironic tone. |例句：Her genteelish manners felt forced and unnatural.  她的举止略显优雅，感觉做作而不自然。

Her genteelish attempts at playing the piano were more amusing than impressive. 她假装优雅地弹钢琴，与其说令人印象深刻，不如说更滑稽。


近义词：affected, pretentious, refined (in a negative connotation),  high-falutin'


反义词：crude, unrefined, vulgar, common, uncouth


词源：源自 genteel (adj.) + -ish (suffix).  Genteel itself comes from Old French *gentil, from Latin *gentilis ("of a family," "noble"), ultimately from *genus ("race," "kind"). The suffix "-ish" adds a sense of slightness, approximation, or diminutiveness.


USAGE->
This word is primarily an adjective and doesn't have verb or noun forms.  Therefore, tenses like past, present, future, etc., are not applicable.  The usage is always descriptive.

例句1:  He adopted a genteelish air, hoping to impress the wealthy patrons. 他摆出一副略显优雅的姿态，希望能给那些富有的赞助人留下深刻印象。

例句2: The restaurant had a genteelish atmosphere, but the food was surprisingly ordinary. 这家餐厅氛围略显优雅，但食物却出奇地普通。


PHRASE->
There are no common idiomatic phrases specifically using "genteelish".  The word is used independently to describe a quality.
