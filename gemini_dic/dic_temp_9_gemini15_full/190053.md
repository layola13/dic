sandpipers:/ˈsændˌpaɪpərz/| n. |滨鹬（复数）|Small, long-legged wading birds that feed on small invertebrates found in sandy or muddy areas near the water's edge. They are known for their distinctive, often repetitive calls and their habit of running along the shoreline.  |例句：We saw flocks of sandpipers scurrying along the beach at low tide. 我们在退潮时看到成群的滨鹬在沙滩上奔跑。  |例句：The sandpipers probed the mudflats for small crustaceans. 滨鹬在泥滩上探寻小型甲壳类动物。|近义词：shorebirds, waders|反义词：N/A
