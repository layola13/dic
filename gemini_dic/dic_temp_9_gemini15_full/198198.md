short-staffed:/ʃɔːrtˈstæft/| adj.|人手不足的，人员短缺的|Having too few employees to do the work that needs to be done.|The restaurant is short-staffed tonight, so service may be slower than usual. 今晚餐厅人手不足，所以服务可能比平时慢。|understaffed, undermanned|fully staffed, well-staffed

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The hospital is short-staffed. = 医院人手不足。
We are short-staffed in the marketing department. = 我们的市场部人手不足。

一般过去时:
The project was short-staffed last year. = 去年这个项目人手不足。
The company was short-staffed during the busy season. = 公司在旺季人手不足。

现在完成时:
The team has been short-staffed for months. = 这个团队几个月来一直人手不足。
The department has been consistently short-staffed. = 该部门一直人手不足。

过去完成时:
They had been short-staffed before the expansion. = 扩张之前他们就人手不足。
We realized that we had been short-staffed all along. = 我们意识到我们一直以来都人手不足。

将来时:
The company will be short-staffed after the layoffs. = 公司裁员后将人手不足。
We will likely be short-staffed during the holiday season. = 我们在节假日很可能会人手不足。

虚拟语气:
If we were not short-staffed, we could finish the project on time. = 如果我们人手充足，就能按时完成项目。
It would be helpful if we weren't short-staffed. = 如果我们人手充足就好了。

被动语态:
The project was hampered because it was short-staffed. = 项目因为人手不足而受阻。
The problem was exacerbated by being short-staffed. = 人手不足加剧了问题。

疑问句:
Is the office short-staffed at the moment? = 办公室目前人手不足吗？
Are we short-staffed enough to justify hiring more people? = 我们人手不足到需要雇佣更多人吗？

否定句:
The company is not short-staffed this year. = 公司今年人手充足。
The team is not short-staffed; they have plenty of personnel. = 团队人手充足；他们有足够的人员。


PHRASE->
severely short-staffed = 严重人手不足
chronically short-staffed = 长期人手不足
dangerously short-staffed = 危险地人手不足
become short-staffed = 变得人手不足
remain short-staffed = 仍然人手不足
