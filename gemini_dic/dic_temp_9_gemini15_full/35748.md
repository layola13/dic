cochlea:/ˈkoʊkliə/| n. |耳蜗|The cochlea is a spiral-shaped, fluid-filled cavity in the inner ear that contains the sensory receptors for hearing.  It converts sound vibrations into electrical signals that are sent to the brain. |例句：The cochlea's delicate hair cells are responsible for transforming sound waves into neural impulses.|近义词：inner ear (partially) |反义词：无
