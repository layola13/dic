pro-Unitarians: /ˌproʊ juːnɪˈtɛəriən/ | adj. | 支持一神论的；拥护一神论的 |  Supporting or advocating Unitarianism.  The term indicates a positive stance towards Unitarian beliefs and practices.  | Example:  His pro-Unitarian views were evident in his writings.  他的著作中明显表达了他支持一神论的观点。 | 近义词：Unitarian-supporting, pro-Unitarianism | 反义词：anti-Unitarian


USAGE->
This word, being an adjective, doesn't have verb conjugations like "What" in the example.  It modifies nouns.

Example usages with different grammatical structures:

A.  Simple Sentence:  He held pro-Unitarian beliefs. 他持有支持一神论的信仰。
B.  Complex Sentence: Because of his pro-Unitarian stance, he was often invited to speak at Unitarian gatherings. 因为他支持一神论的立场，他经常被邀请在 一神论者的聚会上发言。
C.  Compound Sentence:  She was a pro-Unitarian advocate, and she tirelessly defended the principles of Unitarianism. 她是一位支持一神论的倡导者，她不知疲倦地捍卫一神论的原则。


PHRASE->
There are no common idiomatic phrases directly using "pro-Unitarian".  Phrases would involve combining it with other words, for instance:

A. pro-Unitarian movement: 一神论运动
B. pro-Unitarian sentiment: 支持一神论的情绪
C.  a pro-Unitarian publication: 一本支持一神论的出版物
D.  pro-Unitarian theology: 支持一神论的神学
E.  pro-Unitarian arguments: 支持一神论的论点


词源:

The word is a compound of "pro-" (meaning "in favor of," "supporting") and "Unitarian" (referring to the religious denomination Unitarianism).  Unitarianism itself has roots in the Protestant Reformation and emphasizes the oneness of God.
