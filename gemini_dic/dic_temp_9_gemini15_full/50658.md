derivationist: /ˌdɛrɪˈveɪʃənɪst/ | n. | 词源学者 | A scholar who studies the origin and development of words and their meanings.  They trace words back to their etymological roots and analyze how their forms and meanings have changed over time.  |  Example: The derivationist meticulously traced the word "nice" back to its Latin origins.  | 近义词: etymologist | 反义词:  (无明确反义词，可考虑与不研究词源的人对比)
