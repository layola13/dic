civilising: /ˈsɪvəlaɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 使文明；使开化 |  The process of bringing a person, society, or place to a more advanced state, particularly in terms of culture, social organization, and technology.  | Example:  The government implemented programs aimed at civilising the remote tribal communities. = 政府实施了旨在使偏远部落社区文明化的计划。


The impact of Western civilising influences on the local culture was significant. = 西方文明的影响对当地文化产生了重大影响。


近义词: civilizing, culturing, refining, enlightening, educating


反义词: barbarizing, brutalizing, dehumanizing


词性:动词

PHRASE->
There are no common set phrases directly using "civilising" as a verb.  However, related phrases exist, such as:  "civilising influence," "civilising process," "civilising mission,"  "efforts to civilise," "the civilising hand of..."


词源:
The word "civilising" derives from the verb "civilise," which in turn comes from the Latin word "civilis," meaning "of or relating to citizens," "political," or "polite." The suffix "-ising" indicates the act of making something or someone become more like that which is described by the root word.
