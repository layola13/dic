dozy:/ˈdəʊzi/ | adj. |昏昏欲睡的，瞌睡的，愚笨的 |Sleepy and lethargic; also, lacking intelligence or alertness.  |例句：The long journey made me feel dozy. 长途旅行让我感到昏昏欲睡。

例句：He made a dozy mistake in the calculation. 他在计算中犯了一个愚蠢的错误。

近义词：sleepy, drowsy, sluggish, lethargic, stupid, dull

反义词：alert, awake, lively, sharp, intelligent


词源:  The word "dozy" ultimately derives from the Old English word "dūsian," meaning "to be dull, sluggish, or drowsy."  This word evolved through Middle English forms before arriving at its modern form.
