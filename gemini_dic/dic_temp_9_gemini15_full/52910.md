dimorph:/ˈdaɪmɔːrf/| n. |二态性，二形性 |A dimorph is an organism or structure that exists in two distinct forms.  This often refers to the existence of two different forms within a single species,  particularly in the context of polymorphism.  For example, some fungi exhibit dimorphism, existing as both yeast and hyphae forms depending on environmental conditions. | Example:  The fungus *Candida albicans* is a well-known dimorph, switching between yeast and hyphal forms.  这种真菌白色念珠菌是一种著名的二态性生物，会在酵母菌和菌丝体形态之间切换。|近义词：polymorph, polymorphism|反义词：monomorph
