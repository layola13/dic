pedunculate:/pɪˈdʌŋkjuleɪt/| adj.| 有花梗的；有柄的 | Having a peduncle; stalked.  A pedunculate flower is one that has a stalk or peduncle.  The term is often used in botany to describe flowers, fruits, or other structures that are attached to a stem by a distinct stalk. | Example: The pedunculate oak trees are known for their acorns hanging from long stems.  这棵有花梗的橡树以其长茎上的橡子而闻名。|近义词：stalked, stemmed | 反义词：sessile
