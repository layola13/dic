self-amputations: /ˌsɛlf ˈæmpjʊˈteɪʃənz/ | n. | 自我切断（肢体）；自行截肢 | The deliberate act of removing one's own limb or body part.  This is often done in the context of severe pain, mental illness, or a perceived need to escape a difficult situation.  It is a serious act with potentially fatal consequences. | 例句：The case study detailed the patient's history of self-amputations due to a delusional belief that his limbs were contaminated.|近义词：self-mutilation (更广义)|反义词：limb preservation, medical amputation
