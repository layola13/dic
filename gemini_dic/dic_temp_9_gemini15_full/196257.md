/sɪrd/ | adj. | 枯萎的；干枯的；憔悴的 | Withered; dried up; faded;  having lost freshness or vitality.  The word often describes plants or other organic matter that has lost its moisture and become shriveled. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a person who looks tired and worn out. |例句：The sered leaves crunched underfoot.  枯萎的树叶在脚下嘎吱作响。

The sered landscape offered little hope of survival.  干枯的景象几乎没有生存的希望。


近义词：withered, dried, shriveled, parched, faded, desiccated | 反义词：fresh, vibrant, lush, blooming


词源：源自古英语 *searean，意思是“枯萎”。  It is related to the verb "sear," which means to burn or scorch the surface of something.  The connection lies in the similar appearance of something scorched and something withered.
