prepunish: /ˌpriːˈpʌnɪʃ/ | v. | 预先惩罚；提前惩罚 | To punish someone before they have actually committed an offense.  This is often done as a preventative measure or as a form of intimidation.  The term is relatively uncommon and often implies an unfair or unjust punishment. |例句：The dictator prepunished his political opponents by imprisoning them without trial. = 独裁者通过未经审判就监禁政治对手来预先惩罚他们。


The company prepunished the employees by reducing their bonuses before they even knew if the project would fail. = 公司在员工甚至不知道项目是否会失败之前就通过减少奖金来预先惩罚他们。|近义词：preemptively punish, anticipate punishment, proactively punish |反义词：reward, pardon, forgive


词性：动词
