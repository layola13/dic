choreographing: /ˌkɔːriˈɒɡrɑːfɪŋ/ | v. | 编舞；策划；安排 | The act of planning and arranging the steps and movements of a dance or other performance. It can also refer to the careful planning and organization of something, often a complex event or project. |

例句：

The choreographer was meticulously choreographing the ballet's final scene. = 编舞师正在精心编排芭蕾舞剧的最后一场戏。

She spent months choreographing the company's marketing campaign. = 她花了几个月的时间来策划公司的营销活动。


近义词：planning, arranging, orchestrating, staging

反义词：improvising, winging it


词性：动词


USAGE->

He choreographs dances for Broadway shows. = 他为百老汇演出编舞。
They are choreographing a new marketing strategy. = 他们正在策划一个新的营销策略。

一般过去时:
The director choreographed the entire film sequence. = 导演编排了整个电影片段。
She choreographed a surprise party for her friend's birthday. = 她为朋友的生日策划了一个惊喜派对。

现在完成时:

They have choreographed many memorable performances. = 他们已经编排了许多令人难忘的演出。

过去完成时:
The dancers had choreographed their routine for weeks before the competition. = 舞蹈演员们在比赛前几周就编排好了他们的舞蹈套路。
She had choreographed the entire event down to the smallest detail. = 她已经将整个活动安排得井井有条，连最小的细节都考虑到了。

将来时:

虚拟语气:
If I could choreograph a musical, I would make it a masterpiece. = 如果我能编排一部音乐剧，我会把它制作成杰作。
I wish I had choreographed that scene differently. = 我希望我当时能把那个场景编排得不一样。

被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
He did not choreograph the play's fight scenes. = 他没有编排这出戏的打斗场面。

词源:  From French *chorégraphie*, from Greek *χορεία* (khoreia), "dance," + *γράφειν* (graphein), "to write."  The original meaning literally meant "dance writing."
