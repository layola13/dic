pixelated:/ˈpɪksəleɪtɪd/| adj.| 像素化的；马赛克的；模糊的 | Having a pixelated appearance, typically because of low resolution or digital distortion.  The image is characterized by a visible grid of pixels, resulting in a blocky or grainy effect. | 例句：The old video game had very pixelated graphics.  = 那款旧电子游戏画面非常像素化。

The photograph appeared pixelated after being zoomed in excessively. = 照片过度放大后显得像素化。

近义词：blocky, grainy, blurry, fuzzy

反义词：sharp, clear, high-resolution

词源:

The word "pixelated" is derived from "pixel," a contraction of "picture element."  The suffix "-ated" indicates a process or state of being. Therefore, "pixelated" describes something that is made up of or characterized by pixels, often implying a low-resolution or distorted appearance.
