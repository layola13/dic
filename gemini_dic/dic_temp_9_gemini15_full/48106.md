declined:/dɪˈklaɪnd/ | v. | 减少；下降；谢绝；衰退 | To become less in amount, value, or strength; to refuse politely. |例句：The company's profits declined last year.  公司的利润去年下降了。|例句：He politely declined the invitation. 他礼貌地谢绝了邀请。|近义词：decreased, refused, rejected, deteriorated |反义词：increased, accepted, improved, prospered

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The temperature declines in the evening. = 晚上温度下降。
His health is declining steadily. = 他的健康状况正在稳步下降。

一般过去时:
She declined the offer of a promotion. = 她拒绝了晋升的机会。
The number of students declined last semester. = 上个学期学生人数减少了。

现在完成时:
Sales have declined significantly this year. = 今年销售额大幅下降。
His influence has declined since the scandal. = 自丑闻以来，他的影响力下降了。

过去完成时:
His health had declined rapidly before his death. = 他死前健康状况迅速恶化。
The population had declined significantly by the time the census was taken. = 人口普查时人口数量已经大幅下降。

将来时:
The value of the property will likely decline. = 房地产价值可能会下降。
The economy is expected to decline next year. = 经济预计明年将衰退。

虚拟语气:
If he had declined the offer, he would have regretted it. = 如果他拒绝了这个提议，他会后悔的。
I wish I had declined the extra work. = 我真希望我拒绝了额外的工作。

被动语态:
The proposal was declined by the committee. = 该提案被委员会否决了。
His application was declined due to insufficient qualifications. = 他的申请因资质不足而被拒绝。

疑问句:
Did he decline the invitation? = 他拒绝了邀请吗？
Has the value of the stock declined? = 股票价值下降了吗？

否定句:
The economy did not decline as much as expected. = 经济下滑程度没有预期那么大。
He did not decline the opportunity to travel. = 他没有拒绝旅行的机会。


PHRASE->
decline in = 下降
decline to = 拒绝
a sharp decline = 急剧下降
a gradual decline = 逐渐下降
on the decline = 在衰退中
