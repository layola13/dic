oxford-cloths: /ˈɒksfərd klɒθz/ | n. |牛津纺布| A closely woven, plain-weave cotton fabric, typically used for shirts and blouses.  It's known for its durability, smoothness, and subtle texture. | Example:  The shirt is made of high-quality oxford cloth. 这件衬衫是由优质牛津纺布制成的。|近义词：broadcloth, poplin|反义词：无|名词

词源:
The name "oxford cloth" is believed to have originated in Oxford, England, although the precise origin is uncertain.  The fabric's popularity rose significantly during the late 19th and early 20th centuries, solidifying its association with the university town and its academic style.  While not definitively linked to Oxford's production, the name stuck due to its perceived association with quality and tradition.
