rubbing:/ˈrʌbɪŋ/| n. v.|摩擦；擦拭；按摩|The act of rubbing one surface against another.  It can refer to the action itself or the result of the action. As a noun, it often refers to a print made by rubbing a surface with ink or pigment.  As a verb, it describes the act of rubbing.|例句：The rubbing of the two stones created sparks.  (摩擦两块石头产生了火花。)

The constant rubbing against the rough fabric wore a hole in my shirt.(不断摩擦粗糙的布料在我的衬衫上磨出了一个洞。)


近义词：friction, abrasion, chafing, massage|反义词：polishing (in some contexts)|词性：noun, verb


USAGE->

He rubs his eyes every morning. = 他每天早上揉眼睛。
She rubs the table clean. = 她擦干净桌子。

I rubbed my hands together to warm them. = 我搓了搓手来暖和它们。

现在完成时:
She has rubbed the paint off the wall. = 她已经把墙上的油漆擦掉了。

过去完成时:

She had rubbed the dirt from her shoes before entering. = 她进门前已经把鞋上的泥土擦掉了。


将来时:
