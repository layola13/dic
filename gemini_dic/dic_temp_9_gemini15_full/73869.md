forecast:/ˈfɔːrkæst/ | n. v. |预报，预测 | A statement of what is judged likely to happen in the future, especially in connection with a particular situation, or a prediction.  |例句：The weather forecast predicts rain tomorrow.  天气预报预测明天会下雨。

The sales forecast for the next quarter is optimistic. 下个季度的销售预测很乐观。


近义词：prediction, projection, prognosis |反义词：retrospect, hindsight |词性: noun, verb


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The weather forecast predicts sunshine. = 天气预报预测阳光明媚。
She forecasts a successful outcome. = 她预测结果会成功。


一般过去时:
The meteorologist forecast heavy snow. = 气象学家预测了大雪。
They forecast a decline in profits. = 他们预测利润下降。


现在完成时:
They have forecast a rise in interest rates. = 他们已经预测到利率会上涨。
The economists have forecast a period of slow growth. = 经济学家们预测了一段缓慢增长的时期。


过去完成时:
They had forecast a strong economic recovery. = 他们之前预测经济将强劲复苏。
Analysts had forecast a drop in the stock market. = 分析师们之前预测股市会下跌。


将来时:
They will forecast a decrease in tourism. = 他们将预测旅游业将会减少。
Experts will forecast the impact of climate change. = 专家们将预测气候变化的影响。


虚拟语气:
If the forecast had been accurate, we would have stayed home. = 如果预测准确的话，我们就待在家里了。
I wish they had forecast the storm earlier. = 我希望他们早点预测到风暴。


被动语态:
The election results were forecast accurately. = 大选结果被准确预测到了。
The economic downturn was forecast by many experts. = 许多专家预测到了经济低迷。


疑问句:
What does the weather forecast say? = 天气预报怎么说？
What did the analysts forecast for the next year? = 分析师们对下一年做了什么预测？


否定句:
The forecast did not predict the hurricane. = 预报没有预测到飓风。
They did not forecast such a dramatic change. = 他们没有预测到如此剧烈的变化。
