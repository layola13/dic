panslavism:/ˈpænˌsleɪvɪzəm/| n. |泛斯拉夫主义|A political ideology advocating the unity of all Slavic peoples.  It is based on the belief that Slavic peoples share a common culture, language, and destiny, and should therefore unite politically.  This ideology had significant influence in the 19th and early 20th centuries, often manifesting as political movements aiming for unification or mutual support among Slavic nations.  |The rise of panslavism in the 19th century fueled tensions in the Balkans. |Slavic unity, pan-Slavism|Anti-Slavic sentiment, nationalism (in the context of opposing pan-Slavic unity)
