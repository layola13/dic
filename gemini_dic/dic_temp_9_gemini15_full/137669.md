nonunisons: /ˌnɒnˈjuːnɪzən/ | n. |非同时发生；非齐奏 | Sounds or events that do not occur simultaneously or in unison.  The term is primarily used in music to describe notes or chords that are not played or sung at the same time.  It can also be used more broadly to describe any lack of simultaneity or harmony. | Example: The orchestra's performance was marred by numerous nonunisons, disrupting the intended musical effect.  The protesters' actions lacked nonunison; they moved and chanted as one. | 近义词: discord, disharmony, asynchrony | 反义词: unison, harmony, synchrony |  名词
