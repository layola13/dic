dionis:  /daɪˈaɪnɪs/ | n. |狄俄尼索斯（希腊酒神）|Dionysus, in Greek mythology, is the god of wine, fertility, theatre, and religious ecstasy.  He is often depicted as a handsome young man, sometimes with a beard, carrying a thyrsus (a staff tipped with a pine cone) and accompanied by satyrs and maenads. His festivals, the Dionysia, were characterized by wild revelry and dramatic performances. | Example: The play was a tribute to the spirit of Dionysus, full of wild energy and passionate emotion. |近义词：Bacchus (Roman equivalent) |反义词：无
