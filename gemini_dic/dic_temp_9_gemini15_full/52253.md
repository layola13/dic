dibs:/dɪbz/| n.|认领权，优先选择权|the right to claim something before others; a claim to something.|例句：I called dibs on the last slice of pizza. 我第一个选了最后一片披萨。|近义词：claim, first pick|反义词:None.


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He has dibs on the front seat. = 他有权先坐前排。
She calls dibs on the biggest piece of cake. = 她认领了最大块的蛋糕。


一般过去时:
I dibs-ed the blue pen yesterday. = 我昨天先拿了那支蓝色的钢笔。
They dibbsed the best seats in the theater. = 他们抢先选了剧院里最好的座位。


现在完成时:
He has dibs-ed the last cookie. = 他已经把最后一块饼干拿走了。
We've dibs-ed the corner booth at the restaurant. = 我们已经预定了餐厅角落里的卡座。


过去完成时:
She had dibs-ed the book before I got there. = 我到那儿之前，她已经把那本书拿走了。
They had dibs-ed the best seats before we arrived. = 我们到达之前，他们就已经选好了最好的座位。


将来时:
I will dibs the biggest piece of watermelon. = 我会先挑最大的西瓜块。
They will dibs the best view in the house. = 他们会选择房子里视野最好的位置。



被动语态:
The best seats were dibsed before we arrived. = 我们到达之前最好的座位已经被别人拿走了。
The last piece of cake was dibsed by my brother. = 我弟弟抢先拿走了最后一块蛋糕。


疑问句:
Did he dibs the new car? = 他认领了那辆新车吗？
Has she dibs-ed the biggest piece of cake? = 她已经拿了最大块的蛋糕了吗？


否定句:
I didn't dibs the last slice of pizza. = 我没有选最后一片披萨。
She didn't dibs that particular book. = 她没有选那本特别的书。


PHRASE->
