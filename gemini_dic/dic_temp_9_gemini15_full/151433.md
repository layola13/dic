parody:/ˈpærədi/| n. v.|模仿作品；滑稽模仿；拙劣模仿|A parody is a humorous imitation of something, typically a literary or musical work, characteristically by exaggerating or distorting certain features.  It aims to entertain by making fun of the original work or its style. |例句：The comedian’s act was a hilarious parody of a politician’s speech.  这位喜剧演员的表演是对一位政治家演讲的滑稽模仿。

例句：That movie is a poor parody of classic horror films. 那部电影是对经典恐怖电影的拙劣模仿。


近义词：satire, lampoon, burlesque, caricature|反义词：original, authentic


词性:名词，动词


词源:  From Late Middle English *parodye, borrowed from Anglo-French *parodie, from Medieval Latin *parodia, from Greek parodia “a song sung beside or alongside another,” from para- “beside” + ōidē “song,” related to aeidein “to sing.”
