interject:/ˌɪntərˈdʒekt/| vt.| 插嘴；突然插入|To insert (a word, remark, etc.) into a conversation|He interjected a witty remark into the serious discussion. 他在严肃的讨论中插了一句俏皮话。|近义词：interrupt, insert, butt in|反义词：omit, exclude|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He interjects comments during the lecture. 他在讲座期间插话。
She interjects witty remarks into the conversation. 她不时地插进一些俏皮话到谈话中。


一般过去时:
He interjected a question during the presentation. 他在演示过程中插了一句问话。
She interjected a comment about the weather. 她插话说了一下天气。


现在完成时:
He has interjected several times already. 他已经插话好几次了。
She has interjected her opinion into the debate. 她已经把她的意见插进了辩论中。


过去完成时:
He had interjected several times before the speaker finished.  在演讲者结束之前，他已经插话好几次了。
She had interjected a crucial point before the vote. 在投票之前，她已经插进了一个关键点。


将来时:
He will interject when he feels it's necessary. 他觉得有必要的时候会插话。
She will interject a comment about the proposal. 她会就提议发表评论。


虚拟语气:
If he interjected again, I would ask him to stop. 如果他再次插话，我会让他停止。
I wish she hadn't interjected so frequently. 我真希望她不要那么频繁地插话。


被动语态:
A comment was interjected during the silence.  在寂静中插进了一句话。
Her opinion was interjected into the discussion. 她的意见被插进了讨论中。


疑问句:
Will he interject during the meeting? 他会在会议上插话吗？
Did she interject at an inappropriate time? 她是不是在不恰当的时候插话了？


否定句:
He didn't interject during the important part of the speech. 他在演讲的重要部分没有插话。
She didn't interject at all during the ceremony.  她在仪式上根本没有插话。


PHRASE->

interject a remark  插一句评语
