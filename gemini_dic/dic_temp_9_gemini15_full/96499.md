indo-briton: /ˈɪndoʊˌbrɪtən/ | n. | 印度英国人；英属印度人 | A person of British descent born or living in India, or a person of Indian descent living in Britain.  The term often refers to someone whose family has a long history associated with British India. | Example: Many Indo-Britons maintain strong ties to both their British and Indian heritage.  许多印度英国人保持着与他们英国和印度遗产的紧密联系。 | 近义词: Anglo-Indian (though this term can be considered outdated or even offensive by some) | 反义词:  None directly applicable;  the antonym would depend on the context (e.g.,  a purely Indian person, a purely British person).
