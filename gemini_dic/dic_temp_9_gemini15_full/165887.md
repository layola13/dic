preoperations:/ˌpriːˌɒpəˈreɪʃənz/| n. |前运算阶段|The stage of cognitive development before a child develops logical thought, typically between ages 2 and 7, characterized by egocentrism, centration, and irreversibility.  This is a key concept in Piaget's theory of cognitive development.|Example:  During the preoperations stage, children struggle with tasks requiring logical reasoning, such as conservation of liquid.|近义词：prelogical thought, early childhood cognition|反义词：concrete operations, formal operations|名词
