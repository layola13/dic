overrealism: /ˌoʊvərˈriːəlɪzəm/ | n. | 超现实主义；过度现实主义 |  A style or movement in art or literature characterized by an exaggerated or distorted representation of reality, often exceeding the bounds of what is considered realistically possible or believable. It goes beyond realism by emphasizing elements that are surreal, fantastical, or dreamlike, while still maintaining a connection to the tangible world.  | Example: The painting, with its impossibly vibrant colors and distorted perspectives, is a prime example of overrealism. 这幅画，以其不可思议的鲜艳色彩和扭曲的视角，是超现实主义的典型例子。 | 近义词: hyperrealism, surrealism, magical realism | 反义词: minimalism, abstraction
