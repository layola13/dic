litigate:/ˈlɪtɪɡeɪt/| vt.|提起诉讼；打官司|To engage in a lawsuit or legal proceedings.|例句：They decided to litigate the matter rather than settle out of court. 他们决定提起诉讼而不是庭外和解。
例句：The company is litigating against its former employee for breach of contract.  该公司正在起诉其前雇员违反合同。|近义词：sue, prosecute, contend|反义词：settle, reconcile


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He litigates every small dispute. = 他为每一个小争端打官司。
They litigate most of their business disagreements. = 他们大部分的商业纠纷都通过诉讼解决。

一般过去时:
She litigated the case for years. = 她打了多年的官司。
The company litigated the patent infringement. = 公司对专利侵权提起了诉讼。

现在完成时:
He has litigated several cases this year. = 他今年已经打了几场官司了。
They have litigated against the government for environmental protection. = 他们已经就环境保护问题对政府提起诉讼。

过去完成时:
She had litigated the matter before she retired. = 她退休前已经就此事打过官司了。
They had litigated for years before reaching a settlement. = 他们在达成和解之前已经打了多年的官司。

将来时:
He will litigate if necessary. = 必要时他会提起诉讼。
They will litigate to protect their intellectual property rights. = 他们将提起诉讼以保护其知识产权。

虚拟语气:
If he were to litigate, he would need a good lawyer. = 如果他要打官司，他需要一个好律师。
I wish I hadn't litigated that case. = 我真希望我没有打那场官司。

被动语态:
The case was litigated in the High Court. = 此案在高等法院审理。
The matter is being litigated at present. = 此事目前正在审理中。

疑问句:
Will they litigate this dispute? = 他们会对这场纠纷提起诉讼吗？
Are you planning to litigate? = 你计划提起诉讼吗？

否定句:
He did not litigate the matter. = 他没有就此事提起诉讼。
They chose not to litigate the issue. = 他们选择不就这个问题提起诉讼。


PHRASE->
to litigate a claim = 提起索赔诉讼
to litigate a dispute = 解决纠纷
to litigate a case = 打官司
to litigate aggressively = 积极地打官司
to litigate in good faith = 善意地打官司
