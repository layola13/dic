microfracture:/ˈmaɪkroʊˌfrækʃər/ | n. |微骨折，微裂 |A microfracture is a small crack or break in a bone that is not large enough to cause a complete fracture.  It is often too small to be detected by a standard X-ray.  Microfractures are often caused by repetitive stress or overuse injuries.|例句：Repeated stress on the bone can lead to microfractures.  反复的骨骼压力会导致微骨折。

The athlete suffered from numerous microfractures in his leg.  这位运动员腿部有多处微骨折。|近义词： hairline fracture, stress fracture |反义词：complete fracture, macrofracture|

词源:

* **micro-:**  From Greek *mikros*, meaning "small".
* **fracture:** From Latin *fractura*, meaning "a breaking".
