isomer:/ˈaɪsəmər/| n. |异构体|A molecule with the same molecular formula as another molecule, but with a different arrangement of atoms.| 例句：Glucose and fructose are isomers, both having the formula C6H12O6 but different structures. 葡萄糖和果糖是异构体，两者都有C6H12O6的分子式，但结构不同。|近义词：stereoisomer, structural isomer |反义词：无

词源:
The word "isomer" is derived from the Greek words "isos" (ἴσος), meaning "equal," and "meros" (μέρος), meaning "part."  This reflects the fact that isomers have the same number and type of atoms (equal parts) but different arrangements.
