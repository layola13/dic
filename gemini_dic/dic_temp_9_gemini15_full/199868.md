situationalist:/ˌsɪtʃuˈeɪʃənəlɪst/| n. |情境主义者|A person who advocates or practices situationism.  Situationists believe that social and political structures are created by individuals' interactions within specific circumstances, and that these structures are not inherently fixed or natural. They emphasize the importance of understanding the context and conditions that shape individual behavior and social phenomena. | Example:  The artist's work was heavily influenced by the ideas of the prominent situationalist Guy Debord.  这位艺术家的作品深受著名情境主义者吉多·德波思想的影响。|近义词:  social constructionist, contextualist |反义词: essentialist, determinist
