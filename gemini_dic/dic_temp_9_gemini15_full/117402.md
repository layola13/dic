medication:/ˌmɛdɪˈkeɪʃən/ | n. |药物，药剂 | Substances used to treat or prevent disease or relieve pain.  Medication can be administered in many forms, including pills, capsules, liquids, injections, and inhalers. | 例句：The doctor prescribed some medication for my headache.  医生给我开了些治头痛的药。

例句：She's on medication for high blood pressure. 她正在服用降血压药。


近义词：medicine, drug, remedy, treatment | 反义词：cure (in some contexts, medication is a treatment, not a cure)


词性：名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He takes his medication every day. = 他每天都按时服药。
The doctor prescribes the right medication for the patient. = 医生为病人开具合适的药物。


一般过去时:
She took her medication before bed. = 她睡前服了药。
The pharmacist dispensed the medication yesterday. = 药剂师昨天配发了药物。


现在完成时:
I have taken all my medication this week. = 我这周已经服完了所有的药。
The patient has been on this medication for months. = 病人服用这种药已经好几个月了。


过去完成时:
He had taken his medication before the symptoms worsened. = 症状恶化之前，他已经服过药了。
She had never taken this type of medication before. = 她以前从未服用过这种药。


将来时:
I will take my medication as soon as I get home. = 我一回家就服药。
The doctor will prescribe a new medication if this one doesn't work. = 如果这种药不管用，医生会开一种新药。


虚拟语气:

I wish I had taken the medication as prescribed. = 我真希望我按照处方服药。
