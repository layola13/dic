chutzpah:/ˈhʊtspə/| n. | 厚颜无耻；胆量；勇气 |  Audacious, aggressive, or brazen self-confidence.  It often implies a disregard for conventional rules or propriety.  While sometimes used negatively, it can also suggest admirable nerve or resilience. |


例句：

He had the chutzpah to ask for a raise after being late every day. = 他厚颜无耻地在每天都迟到后要求加薪。

It took real chutzpah to stand up to the bully. =  对抗那个恶霸需要真正的勇气。


近义词：audacity, nerve, gall, temerity, brazenness, effrontery

反义词：timidity, shyness, meekness, diffidence


词源:

The word "chutzpah" is of Yiddish origin.  It comes from the Hebrew word חוצפה (ḥuṣpā), meaning "impudence," "cheek," or "effrontery."  It entered English in the early 20th century, largely through the influence of Yiddish culture and literature.
