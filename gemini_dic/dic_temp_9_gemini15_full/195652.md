semirevolutionary: /ˌsɛmiˌrɛvəˈluːʃənəri/ | adj. | 半革命的，部分革命的 | Partially revolutionary; involving or characterized by a partial or incomplete revolution.  It suggests a significant change but not a complete overthrow of the existing system. |

例句：

The government implemented semirevolutionary economic reforms, privatizing some industries but leaving others under state control. = 政府实施了部分革命性的经济改革，将一些产业私有化，但其他产业仍保留国有控制。


The artist's work is considered semirevolutionary, challenging traditional techniques but not entirely abandoning them. =这位艺术家的作品被认为是半革命性的，它挑战了传统技法，但并没有完全抛弃它们。


近义词：partially revolutionary, moderately radical, transformative (to a degree)


反义词：conservative, reactionary, traditional, status quo


词源:

* **semi-**:  A prefix meaning "half," "partly," or "partially."  Derived from Latin *semi*.

* **revolutionary**:  Referring to a revolution, a fundamental and often violent change in a political or social system.  Derived from "revolution," ultimately from Latin *revolvere* (to roll back).
