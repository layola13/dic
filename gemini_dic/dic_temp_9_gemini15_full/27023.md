calderas:/kælˈdɛrəz/ | n. | 火山口，破火山口 | A caldera is a large, basin-shaped depression that forms following the emptying of a magma chamber in a volcanic eruption.  The collapse of the overlying rocks creates a vast, often circular, crater. | Example: The island is actually a massive caldera formed by a catastrophic eruption thousands of years ago.  这座岛实际上是一个巨大的火山口，形成于数千年前的一次灾难性喷发。| 近义词：crater (though generally smaller), volcanic crater | 反义词：volcano (the structure *before* caldera formation)
