porpoise:/ˈpɔːrpəs/| n.|海豚|A porpoise is a small, toothed whale that lives in oceans and seas around the world. They are known for their intelligence and playful behavior.  Porpoises are often mistaken for dolphins, but they have a more robust build and shorter snout.|  例句：We saw a pod of porpoises leaping and playing in the waves. 我们看到一群海豚在波浪中跳跃和嬉戏。


例句：The porpoise effortlessly navigated the currents. 这只海豚轻松地穿越了水流。


近义词：dolphin (though technically distinct)|反义词：无

词源:
The word "porpoise" comes from the Old French "porpois," which is a combination of "porc" (pig) and "poisson" (fish).  This reflects the historical perception of the animal as a pig-like fish. The word's evolution traces back through Latin and ultimately to Greek roots.
