pentadactyl: /ˌpentəˈdæktɪl/ | adj. |五指的，五趾的|Having five digits on each hand or foot.  This term is primarily used in zoology and comparative anatomy to describe the limb structure of many vertebrates.  |例句：Many mammals, including humans, are pentadactyl. =许多哺乳动物，包括人类，都是五指（趾）的。

The pentadactyl limb is a homologous structure found in various species. =五指（趾）肢体是在各种物种中发现的同源结构。


近义词：five-fingered, five-toed (for specific contexts) | 反义词：没有直接的反义词，可以考虑用lacking digits 或polydactylous (多指/趾的) 来表达相反的概念。


词源:
From Greek *penta- (five) + *dactylos (finger, toe).  The word reflects the common ancestral pattern of five digits in the limbs of tetrapods (four-limbed vertebrates).
