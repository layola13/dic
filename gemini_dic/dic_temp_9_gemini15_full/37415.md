commercializers: /ˌkəˈmɜːrʃəlaɪzərz/ | n. |商业化者，使商业化的人 | Those who engage in the process of commercialization, turning something into a commodity for sale or profit. They might be individuals, companies, or even governments involved in making something commercially viable.  | Example:  The rapid commercializers of new technologies often reap the biggest rewards. = 快速将新技术商业化的人通常会获得最大的回报。 | 近义词: entrepreneurs, marketers, promoters | 反义词: preservationists, conservationists

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company commercializes new products every year. = 这家公司每年都会将新产品商业化。
They commercialize their inventions quickly. = 他们迅速将他们的发明商业化。

一般过去时:
The government commercialized the railway system. = 政府将铁路系统商业化。
They commercialized the software last year. = 他们去年将该软件商业化。

现在完成时:
They have commercialized many innovative ideas. = 他们已经将许多创新理念商业化。
The company has commercialized its technology worldwide. = 这家公司已将其技术在全球范围内商业化。

过去完成时:
Before the merger, they had commercialized several successful products. = 在合并之前，他们已经将几款成功的产品商业化。
By 2010, they had commercialized their research findings. = 到2010年，他们已经将他们的研究成果商业化。

将来时:
They will commercialize their new drug next year. = 他们将在明年将他们的新药商业化。
The company will commercialize the technology globally. = 公司将在全球范围内将这项技术商业化。

虚拟语气:
If they commercialized their idea, they would become very wealthy. = 如果他们将他们的想法商业化，他们将会变得非常富有。
I wish they had commercialized their invention sooner. = 我希望他们早点将他们的发明商业化。

被动语态:
