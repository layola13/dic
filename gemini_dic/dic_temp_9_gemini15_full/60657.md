electiveness:/ɪˈlɛktɪvnɪs/| n. |可选性；选择权；随意性|The quality of being elective; the state or fact of being optional or able to be chosen.  It refers to the freedom or right to choose, particularly in the context of courses of study or options available.|Example: The university offers a high degree of electiveness in its curriculum, allowing students to tailor their studies to their interests. 大学课程提供了高度的选课自由度，允许学生根据自己的兴趣定制学习内容。|近义词：optionality, choice, freedom of choice|反义词：compulsory, mandatory, obligatory
