decani:/dɛˈkeɪnaɪ/| n.| 十天，旬；（古代埃及）十个星群|  A decani is a group of ten stars or a period of ten days. In ancient Egypt, the decani were 36 groups of stars used to mark the passage of time and in religious rituals.  The term also refers to a group of ten things or a period of ten days in general usage.|Example: The ancient Egyptians used the decani to create a 360-day calendar. 古代埃及人用十天星群创造了一个360天的历法。|近义词：decade (but refers to ten years, not days or stars)|反义词：None directly applicable|名词
