kalif:/ˈkeɪlɪf/| n. |哈里发，伊斯兰教领袖|The word "kalif" or "caliph" refers to the religious and political successor to the Prophet Muhammad in Islam.  The caliphate was a significant institution in Islamic history, ruling over vast territories and influencing various aspects of Muslim life.  The term derives from the Arabic word خليفة (khalīfa), meaning "successor" or "deputy".  Different interpretations and forms of caliphate existed throughout history, leading to various political and religious dynamics within the Muslim world.|Example: The Abbasid Caliphate was a powerful empire that flourished for centuries.  哈里发王朝是一个繁荣了几个世纪的强大帝国。|近义词：imam (in some contexts), sultan (in some contexts)|反义词：None directly applicable, as it describes a specific role.
