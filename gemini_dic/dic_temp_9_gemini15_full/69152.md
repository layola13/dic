favours:/ˈfeɪvərz/ | n. v. | 恩惠；偏袒；赞成；支持 |  A favour is an act of kindness, help, or support; to favour means to show preference or support for something or someone. |


英文解析:
As a noun, "favours" refers to acts of kindness or help given to someone. It can also mean showing preference or partiality towards something or someone.  As a verb, "favour" means to support or prefer something or someone.


例句:
Could you do me a favour? = 你能帮我个忙吗？

She showed him great favour. = 她对他非常偏袒。


近义词:
Noun: kindnesses, benefits, assistance, support, preferences
Verb: support, prefer, back, endorse, aid


反义词:
Noun:  injustice, disadvantages, hinderances
Verb: oppose, hinder, reject, disfavour


词性:
Noun, Verb


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He favours the blue team. = 他偏袒蓝队。
She always favours her youngest child. = 她总是偏袒她最小的孩子。


一般过去时:
The judge favoured the defendant. = 法官偏袒被告。
They favoured a quick solution. = 他们倾向于快速的解决方案。


现在完成时:
The government has favoured large corporations. = 政府一直偏袒大公司。
He has always favoured honesty. = 他一直都很重视诚实。


过去完成时:
The committee had favoured the proposal before the meeting. = 委员会在会议之前就已赞成这项提案。
She had favoured him over her other friends. = 她以前更偏爱他胜过其他朋友。


将来时:
They will favour the candidate with the most experience. = 他们将支持最有经验的候选人。
The company will favour innovative ideas. = 公司将青睐创新的想法。


虚拟语气:
If the weather favoured us, we would have gone hiking. = 如果天气允许，我们就去远足了。
I wish the judge had favoured my case. = 我希望法官能支持我的案子。


被动语态:

His ideas were favoured by the board. = 他的想法得到了董事会的支持。


疑问句:

否定句:
