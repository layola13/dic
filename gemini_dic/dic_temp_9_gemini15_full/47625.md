debilitant: /ˈdɛbɪlɪtənt/ | adj. |使衰弱的；使虚弱的 | weakening; causing debilitation.  |The debilitating effects of the illness kept him bedridden for weeks.  疾病的衰弱作用使他卧床数周。| weakening, enervating, incapacitating | strengthening, invigorating


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The illness debilitates him. = 这疾病使他虚弱。
This work debilitates my body and mind. = 这工作使我身心疲惫。


一般过去时:
The accident debilitated him for months. = 那次事故使他虚弱了几个月。
The heat debilitated the soldiers. = 炎热使士兵们虚弱无力。


现在完成时:
The long journey has debilitated her. = 漫长的旅程使她虚弱了。
Years of overwork have debilitated him. = 多年的过度劳累使他衰弱了。


过去完成时:
He had been debilitated by the disease before he sought treatment. = 他在就医之前就已经被疾病折磨得虚弱不堪了。
The drought had debilitated the crops before the rains came. = 干旱在雨水来临之前已经使庄稼枯萎了。


将来时:
The surgery will temporarily debilitate him. = 手术会暂时使他虚弱。
The lack of sleep will debilitate her performance. = 睡眠不足会影响她的表现，使她变得虚弱。


虚拟语气:
If he continued to work so hard, he would be debilitated. = 如果他继续如此努力工作，他将会被削弱。
If she hadn't rested, she would have been debilitated by the journey. = 如果她没有休息，旅途将会使她虚弱不堪。


被动语态:
He was debilitated by the virus. = 他被病毒削弱了。
The athlete was debilitated by injury. = 运动员因伤势而虚弱。


疑问句:
Is he debilitated by the disease? = 他是被疾病削弱了吗？
Will the treatment debilitate her? = 治疗会使她虚弱吗？


否定句:
The treatment did not debilitate him. = 治疗并没有使他虚弱。
She was not debilitated by the long walk. = 漫长的步行并没有使她虚弱。


PHRASE->
debilitating illness = 令人衰弱的疾病
debilitating effects = 削弱的影响
debilitating fatigue = 令人衰弱的疲劳
debilitating stress = 令人衰弱的压力
severely debilitating = 严重削弱
