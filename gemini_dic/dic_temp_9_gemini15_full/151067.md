paraplegic:/ˌpærəˈpliːdʒɪk/| adj. n.|瘫痪的；下半身瘫痪的；下肢瘫痪者|Suffering from paralysis of the lower half of the body.  A person affected by paraplegia.|例句：He became a paraplegic after a car accident. 他在车祸后成了瘫痪者。  She is a paraplegic who participates in wheelchair racing. 她是一位参加轮椅竞赛的下肢瘫痪者。|近义词：paralyzed, crippled (informal)|反义词：able-bodied


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He is paraplegic. = 他是瘫痪的。
She is a paraplegic athlete. = 她是一位残疾运动员。


一般过去时:
The accident left him paraplegic. = 那次事故使他瘫痪了。

现在完成时:
She has been paraplegic for ten years. = 她瘫痪十年了。
He has remained paraplegic despite intensive therapy. = 尽管进行了强化治疗，他仍然瘫痪。


过去完成时:
Before the surgery, he had already been paraplegic for several months. = 手术前，他已经瘫痪了几个月。
They had known he was paraplegic for years. = 他们已经知道他瘫痪多年了。


将来时:
With new technology, he will be able to walk again, even though he is paraplegic. = 凭借新技术，即使瘫痪，他也能再次行走。
She will remain paraplegic unless a miracle happens. = 除非奇迹发生，否则她将保持瘫痪状态。


虚拟语气:
