intoxicated:/ɪnˈtɒksɪkeɪtɪd/| adj.|醉的；陶醉的；欣喜若狂的|Being affected by a substance, typically alcohol, to the point of impaired judgment, coordination, or behavior.  It can also refer to a feeling of overwhelming excitement or happiness.|例句：He was intoxicated and unable to drive home safely. 他喝醉了，无法安全开车回家。

例句：The beauty of the sunset left her feeling intoxicated. 日落的美丽让她感到陶醉。|近义词：drunk, inebriated, tipsy, exhilarated|反义词：sober, clear-headed


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is intoxicated. = 他喝醉了。
She is intoxicated by the success. = 她被成功冲昏了头脑。

一般过去时:
He was intoxicated last night. = 他昨晚喝醉了。
They were intoxicated with joy. = 他们欣喜若狂。

现在完成时:
He has been intoxicated several times. = 他喝醉过好几次了。
She has been intoxicated by his charm. = 她被他迷住了。

过去完成时:
He had been intoxicated before the accident. = 事故发生前他已经喝醉了。
They had been intoxicated with power. = 他们已经被权力冲昏了头脑。

将来时:
He will be intoxicated after drinking so much. = 他喝这么多酒后会醉的。
They will be intoxicated by the atmosphere at the concert. = 他们在音乐会的氛围中会陶醉其中。

虚拟语气:
If he drank that much, he would be intoxicated. = 如果他喝那么多酒，他会醉的。
If I had known the wine was so strong, I wouldn't have become intoxicated. = 如果我知道酒这么烈，我就不会喝醉了。

被动语态:
He was intoxicated by the fumes. = 他被烟熏醉了。(被动语态在此处较少见，通常用主动语态)
She felt intoxicated, as if she had been overwhelmed by a positive force. = 她感到陶醉，仿佛被一股积极的力量所征服。(被动语态在此处表达一种被动的感受)

疑问句:
Is he intoxicated? = 他醉了吗？
Are you intoxicated by the music? = 你被音乐陶醉了吗？

否定句:
He is not intoxicated. = 他没有喝醉。
She was not intoxicated by the praise. = 她并没有被赞扬冲昏头脑。
