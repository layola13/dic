diacoustic: /ˌdaɪəˈkuːstɪk/ | adj. | 双声的，双音的 | Relating to or characterized by the transmission of sound in two different ways or through two different media.  It often refers to situations where sound travels through both air and water, or through different layers of material with varying acoustic properties. | Example: The diacoustic properties of the concert hall, with its unique combination of air and reflective surfaces, created an unusual sonic experience. | 近义词: binaural (in the context of sound perception), dual-path (in the context of sound transmission) | 反义词: monaural (in the context of sound perception), single-path (in the context of sound transmission)
