dignify:/ˈdɪɡnɪfaɪ/| vt.|使尊贵；使有尊严；抬高；使显得重要 |To make someone or something seem worthy of respect or honor. |例句：His actions did not dignify his position. 他的行为没有给他职位增光。 |例句：The ceremony dignified the occasion.  典礼使这次活动显得隆重。|近义词：elevate, exalt, ennoble |反义词：degrade, demean, humiliate


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He dignifies his work with great care. = 他认真细致地工作，使之显得高贵。
They dignify their traditions with elaborate ceremonies. = 他们以精心设计的仪式来尊崇他们的传统。


一般过去时:
The award dignified her years of hard work. = 那个奖项肯定了她多年的辛勤工作。
The artist dignified the simple subject matter with masterful brushstrokes. = 艺术家用精湛的笔触使简单的主题显得高贵。


现在完成时:
His accomplishments have dignified his family name. = 他的成就使他的家族名声显赫。
The museum has dignified the local art scene with its impressive collection. = 这个博物馆以其令人印象深刻的收藏，提升了当地的艺术氛围。


过去完成时:
The king had dignified the knight with a special honor. = 国王曾授予这位骑士一项特别的荣誉。
She had dignified the occasion by wearing her finest gown. = 她穿上了她最漂亮的礼服，使这次场合显得隆重。


将来时:
The new building will dignify the city skyline. = 新建筑将使城市天际线更加壮观。
The upcoming exhibition will dignify the work of young artists. = 即将举行的展览将使年轻艺术家的作品更加出彩。


虚拟语气:
If he had behaved properly, it would have dignified the situation. = 如果他行为得体，情况就会显得体面得多。
I wish the media would dignify the seriousness of the issue. = 我希望媒体能够重视这个问题的严重性。


被动语态:
The event was dignified by the presence of the president. = 总统的出席使活动增色不少。
His reputation was dignified by his selfless acts of charity. = 他无私的慈善行为提升了他的声誉。


疑问句:
Does this action dignify your profession? = 这种行为对你的职业有益吗？
Will this decision dignify the company's image? = 这个决定会提升公司的形象吗？


否定句:
His behavior did not dignify the occasion. = 他的行为没有使这次场合显得体面。
The cheap materials did not dignify the design. = 廉价的材料并没有提升设计。
