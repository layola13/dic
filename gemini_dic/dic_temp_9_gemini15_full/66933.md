expense:/ɪkˈspɛns/| n.|花费；开支；代价|The cost of something; the money spent on something.  It can also refer to something that is used up or consumed.|The expense of the trip was unexpectedly high. 这趟旅行的费用出乎意料地高。  The company is cutting expenses to increase profits. 公司正在削减开支以增加利润。|cost, outlay, expenditure|income, profit, saving|名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The project incurs significant expenses. = 这个项目产生了巨大的开支。
Monthly expenses are eating into our savings. = 每月的开支正在消耗我们的储蓄。


一般过去时:
Last year's expenses exceeded our budget. = 去年开支超过了我们的预算。
We carefully tracked all expenses during the renovation. = 我们在装修期间仔细追踪了所有开支。


现在完成时:
The company has incurred substantial expenses this quarter. = 本季度公司已产生了巨额开支。
We have already exceeded our planned expenses for the year. = 我们已经超过了今年的计划开支。


过去完成时:
By the end of the month, we had already exhausted our expenses budget. = 到月底，我们已经用光了预算开支。
They had underestimated the expenses involved in the project. = 他们低估了该项目涉及的开支。


将来时:
Next year's expenses are projected to be higher. = 明年的开支预计会更高。
We will carefully monitor expenses to avoid exceeding the budget. = 我们将仔细监控开支，以避免超过预算。


虚拟语气:
If the expenses were lower, we could afford the project. = 如果费用更低，我们就能负担这个项目。
I wish the expenses hadn't been so high. = 我真希望费用没有那么高。


被动语态:
Expenses are carefully monitored by the finance department. = 开支由财务部门仔细监控。

疑问句:

否定句:
