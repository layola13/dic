cremocarp:/ˈkrɛməkɑːrp/| n. |分果|A cremocarp is a type of dry fruit that develops from a compound ovary and breaks up at maturity into several one-seeded units called mericarps, which are attached to a central axis.  It's characteristic of plants in the family Apiaceae (formerly Umbelliferae), such as carrots, parsley, and dill.|Example: The botanist carefully examined the cremocarp to identify the plant species.|近义词：schizocarp (a more general term for a fruit splitting into mericarps)|反义词：N/A (no direct antonym)|
