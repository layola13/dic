post-vedic:/ˌpoʊstˈveɪdɪk/| adj.|吠陀后期|Post-Vedic refers to the period and the literature in ancient India that followed the Vedic period.  This era is characterized by a shift in religious and philosophical thought, with the rise of new schools of philosophy like Buddhism and Jainism, and the development of new literary forms like the epics and Puranas. |The post-Vedic period saw the flourishing of Sanskrit literature.  |近义词：later Vedic, post-Vedic period |反义词：Vedic
