overprogramming: /ˌoʊvərˈproʊɡræmɪŋ/ | n. | 过度编程 | The act of writing more code than necessary to achieve a specific task, often leading to unnecessary complexity and reduced efficiency.  It often involves adding features that are not required or implementing solutions in overly complex ways. | Example: The software suffered from overprogramming; its many convoluted features made it difficult to maintain and update.  | 近义词：gold plating, code bloat, unnecessary complexity | 反义词：minimalism, simplicity, elegance
