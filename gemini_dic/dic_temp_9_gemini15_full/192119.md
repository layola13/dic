scowler:/ˈskaʊlə(r)/| n. |怒视的人，皱眉的人|A person who scowls; someone habitually frowning.|He was a scowler, his face permanently creased with displeasure. 他是个怒视的人，脸上总是带着不悦的表情。|glowerer, grumbler|smiler, laugher|名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He scowls at the rude customer. = 他怒视着那个粗鲁的顾客。
She scowls when she is angry. = 她生气的时候会皱眉。

一般过去时:
He scowled at the insult. = 他对侮辱皱起了眉头。
She scowled at her reflection in the mirror. = 她对着镜子里的倒影皱起了眉头。

现在完成时:
He has scowled all day. = 他整天都在皱眉。
She has scowled at me since this morning. = 从今天早上开始她就一直怒视着我。

过去完成时:
He had scowled before I even spoke. = 我还没开口他就已经皱眉了。
She had scowled throughout the entire meeting. = 整个会议期间她都一直皱眉。

将来时:
He will scowl if you mention it again. = 如果你再提这件事，他会皱眉。
She will scowl at anyone who disagrees with her. = 她会怒视任何反对她的人。

虚拟语气:
If he were nicer, he wouldn't scowl so much. = 如果他更好相处一点，他就不会总是皱眉。
I wish she wouldn't scowl all the time. = 我希望她不要总是皱眉。

被动语态:
(Scowl is rarely used in passive voice.  A more natural phrasing would be needed.)  His anger was shown by his scowl. = 他的怒气表现在他的皱眉上。
His displeasure was evident in his scowl. = 他的不满从他的皱眉中显而易见。

疑问句:
Why does he scowl so often? = 他为什么这么经常皱眉？
Does she always scowl like that? = 她总是那样皱眉吗？

否定句:
He didn't scowl at all. = 他根本没有皱眉。
She didn't scowl, but her expression was serious. = 她没有皱眉，但她的表情很严肃。


PHRASE->
a constant scowler = 一个总是皱眉的人
a deep scowl = 深深的皱眉
a fierce scowl = 凶狠的皱眉
a threatening scowl = 恐吓性的皱眉
to wear a scowl = 愁眉苦脸
