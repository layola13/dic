mysteries:/ˈmɪstəriːz/| n.|谜团，神秘事件|Things that are difficult or impossible to understand or explain.  They often involve secrets, hidden information, or unusual circumstances.|例句：The disappearance of the painting remains one of the greatest mysteries of the art world.  这幅画的失踪仍然是艺术界最大的谜团之一。|例句：She loves reading detective novels filled with thrilling mysteries. 她喜欢读充满惊险谜团的侦探小说。|近义词：enigmas, puzzles, secrets, riddles|反义词：certainties, knowns, solutions|名词

词源:
From Middle English *mysterie, from Old French *misteri, from Late Latin *ministerium ("service, duty, office"), from Latin ministerium ("ministry, service"), from minister ("minister, servant"). The meaning shifted from "craft, trade" to "secret, mystery" in the Middle Ages.
