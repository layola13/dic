curators:/kjʊˈreɪtərz/| n.|馆长；策展人；管理者|A curator is a person who is in charge of a museum or other collection of objects. They select, organize, and interpret the items in the collection for public display and understanding.  They may also conduct research, write catalogs, and give lectures.|The museum curator gave a fascinating talk about the new exhibit. = 博物馆馆长就新的展览做了一个引人入胜的演讲。

The art curator carefully selected the pieces for the upcoming show. = 艺术策展人仔细挑选了即将展出的作品。|conservator, keeper, archivist| (no direct antonym, but a possible contrasting role could be a destroyer or vandal)|名词
