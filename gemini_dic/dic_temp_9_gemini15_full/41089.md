copping:/ˈkɒpɪŋ/| /ˈkɑːpɪŋ/ | v. | 抓住；获得；应对；承认；模仿 | To seize or grab something quickly; to obtain or acquire something, often illicitly; to deal with or manage a situation; to admit or confess to something; to imitate or copy someone or something. |例句：He was copping a feel. 他在吃豆腐。|例句：She's copping a lot of flak for her decision. 她的决定受到了很多批评。|近义词：grabbing, seizing, getting, admitting, imitating |反义词：releasing, relinquishing, denying, ignoring


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He cops a lot of grief from his boss. = 他经常受到老板的责骂。
They are copping out of their responsibilities. = 他们在逃避责任。


一般过去时:
The thief copped a plea bargain. = 小偷达成了认罪协议。
She copped a cold from her brother. = 她从她哥哥那里得了感冒。


现在完成时:
He has copped to the crime. = 他已经承认了罪行。
She has copped a lot of criticism for her work. = 她因为她的工作受到了很多批评。


过去完成时:
He had copped a beating before the police arrived. = 在警察到达之前，他已经挨了一顿打。
They had copped out of the project before the deadline. = 他们在截止日期之前就放弃了这个项目。


将来时:
He will cop a lot of flack for his actions. = 他将因为他的行为而受到很多批评。
They will cop a fine for parking illegally. = 他们将因为非法停车而被罚款。


虚拟语气:
If he copped to the crime, he would get a reduced sentence. = 如果他承认罪行，他将获得减刑。
I wish I hadn't copped that attitude with him. = 我真希望我没有对他那样态度。


被动语态:
The criminal was copped by the police. = 罪犯被警察抓住了。(This usage is less common.)
The problem was easily copped. = 这个问题很容易解决。(This usage is informal and focuses on handling the problem.)


疑问句:

否定句:

She didn't cop a single injury in the accident. = 她在事故中一点伤都没受。
