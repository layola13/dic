monacid:/ˈmɒnəsɪd/| adj.|单酸的；一元酸的|Containing only one acid group in the molecule.  A monacid is a type of acid that has only one replaceable hydrogen atom per molecule. This means that it can only donate one proton (H+) in an acid-base reaction.| Examples include hydrochloric acid (HCl) and nitric acid (HNO3).|近义词：monobasic|反义词：polybasic, dibasic
