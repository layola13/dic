genre:/ˈʒɑːnrə/| n. |类型，体裁|A genre is a category of artistic, musical, or literary composition characterized by a particular style, form, or content.  Genres are used to classify works of art based on shared characteristics.|Examples: The film is a horror genre. 这部电影是恐怖片类型。  Her novels fall into the romance genre. 她的小说属于言情小说类型。|近义词：category, type, kind, style |反义词：无


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The genre of this book is science fiction. = 这本书的类型是科幻小说。
This film belongs to the action genre. = 这部电影属于动作片类型。

一般过去时:
The play belonged to the tragedy genre. = 这部戏剧属于悲剧类型。
Her early work was considered experimental genre. = 她早期的作品被认为是实验类型。

现在完成时:
This author has mastered the fantasy genre. =这位作者已经掌握了奇幻类型的写作。
Many films have explored the horror genre. = 许多电影都探索了恐怖片类型。

过去完成时:
Before the 1960s, the sci-fi genre had not achieved mainstream success. = 在20世纪60年代之前，科幻小说类型还没有获得主流成功。
By the time the book was published, the genre had already evolved. = 到这本书出版的时候，这种类型已经发展了。


将来时:
The upcoming film will redefine the superhero genre. =即将上映的电影将会重新定义超级英雄类型。
Future authors will likely experiment with new genres. =未来的作者可能会尝试新的类型。


虚拟语气:
If this were a different genre, the ending would be different. = 如果这是另一种类型，结局就会不同。
I wish this book were in a more accessible genre. = 我希望这本书的类型更容易理解。


被动语态:
The film was initially categorized into the comedy genre. = 这部电影最初被归类为喜剧类型。
The genre is often misunderstood by critics. = 这种类型经常被评论家误解。


疑问句:
What genre is your favorite? = 你最喜欢的类型是什么？
To what genre does this painting belong? = 这幅画属于什么类型？


否定句:
This movie is not strictly a horror genre. = 这部电影并非严格意义上的恐怖片。
That book doesn't fit neatly into any single genre. = 那本书不完全属于任何单一类型。
