invasions:/ɪnˈveɪʒənz/ | n. |入侵；侵略；侵袭 | The noun "invasions" refers to the act of invading a place or country with an armed force, or the act of something spreading and taking over an area.  It implies a forceful and unwelcome entry. |例句：The Roman invasions of Britain significantly changed the landscape and culture. 罗马对不列颠的入侵极大地改变了其景观和文化。

The barbarian invasions weakened the Roman Empire.  蛮族入侵削弱了罗马帝国。


近义词：incursions, raids, attacks, encroachments |反义词：withdrawals, retreats, departures |词性: 名词
