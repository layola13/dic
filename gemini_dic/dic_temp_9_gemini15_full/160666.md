/pɔːrtɡreɪv/| n. |港务长；港口管理员 |Portgrave is a now largely obsolete term referring to the chief officer or magistrate of a port or harbour, responsible for its administration and regulation.  The term combines "port" (harbour) and "grave" (meaning a serious or important person, often in a position of authority).  It suggests a person of significant importance within the port's governance. | The portgrave oversaw the loading and unloading of ships and ensured the smooth operation of the port. | 近义词：harbour master, port warden | 反义词：None specifically, but terms implying lack of authority or responsibility could be considered antonyms. | 名词
