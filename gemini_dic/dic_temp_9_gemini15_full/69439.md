feet:/fiːt/| n.|英尺；脚|The plural of "foot," referring to the lower extremities of the body or a unit of length.  It can also refer to the bottom part of something.|例句：My feet are sore after the hike.  我的脚在远足后很疼。|例句：The table is three feet long.  这张桌子有三英尺长。|近义词：legs, paws (for animals)|反义词:head, top


USAGE->
一般现在时:
My feet hurt. = 我的脚疼。
His feet are cold. = 他的脚冷。

一般过去时:
My feet were tired. = 我的脚很累。
She washed her feet. = 她洗了她的脚。

现在完成时:
I have washed my feet. = 我已经洗了我的脚。
He has hurt his feet. = 他伤了他的脚。

过去完成时:
I had already washed my feet before bed. = 我睡觉前已经洗过脚了。
They had walked many miles on their feet. = 他们已经走了很多英里路。

将来时:
I will wash my feet later. = 我一会儿会洗脚。
My feet will be sore tomorrow. = 明天我的脚会很疼。

虚拟语气:
If I had worn better shoes, my feet wouldn't hurt so much. = 如果我穿更好的鞋子，我的脚就不会这么疼了。
I wish my feet weren't so tired. = 我希望我的脚不要这么累。

被动语态:
Her feet were massaged by a therapist. = 她的脚被治疗师按摩了。
The feet of the statue were damaged. = 雕像的脚被损坏了。

疑问句:
Are your feet cold? = 你的脚冷吗？
How big are your feet? = 你的脚多大？

否定句:
My feet are not cold. = 我的脚不冷。
She didn't wash her feet. = 她没有洗脚。
