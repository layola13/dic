foolhardier:/ˈfulˌhɑːrdiər/| adj.|更鲁莽的，更大胆的，更轻率的|More reckless or daring than usual; bolder.  It implies a degree of recklessness or disregard for danger. |例句：He became foolhardier in his old age, taking on increasingly risky ventures. 他上了年纪后变得更加鲁莽，承担越来越大的风险。

例句：Her foolhardier actions led to unexpected consequences. 她鲁莽的行动导致了意想不到的后果。


近义词：reckless, rash, audacious, daredevil, impetuous

反义词：cautious, careful, prudent, timid, wary


词源:  The word comes from "foolhardy," meaning recklessly bold.  The "-er" suffix indicates a comparative degree, meaning "more foolhardy."  "Fool" derives from Old English *fōl, and "hardy" from Old French hardi "bold, brave."
