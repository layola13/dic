jitterbuggers:/ˈdʒɪtərˌbʌɡərz/ | n. |跳吉特巴舞的人，跳吉特巴舞的青年男女| Enthusiastic dancers of the jitterbug, especially young couples in the 1940s.  The term often implies a lively and energetic style of dancing. |Example: The jitterbuggers filled the dance floor, their feet moving in a blur. 跳吉特巴舞的人挤满了舞池，他们的脚动得像一阵模糊。|近义词：swing dancers, Lindy Hoppers |反义词：None specifically;  the antonym would relate to the opposite of energetic dancing, perhaps "sedentary individuals".|名词
