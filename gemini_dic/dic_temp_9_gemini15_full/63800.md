epidemiologically:/ˌɛpiˌdiːmiəˈlɑːdʒɪkli/| adv. | 流行病学地；从流行病学的角度 |  From the perspective of epidemiology;  in a manner relating to the study of the distribution, patterns, and determinants of health-related states or events in specified populations. | Example: The outbreak was analyzed epidemiologically to identify its source and prevent further spread.  这次疫情从流行病学的角度进行了分析，以确定其来源并防止进一步蔓延。| 近义词：epidemiologically speaking | 反义词：N/A

词源:
Comes from "epidemiology" + "-logically".  Epidemiology itself originates from the Greek words  "epi" (upon, among), "demos" (people), and "logos" (study).  Therefore, the word literally means "the study of what is upon the people," referring to the study of disease patterns in populations.
