marshalling: /ˈmɑːʃəlɪŋ/ | v. | 整理，调动，排列 | The act of assembling and organizing something, typically people, resources, or ideas.  |例句：The general marshalled his troops for the attack. = 将军整顿军队准备进攻。|例句：The company is marshalling its resources to launch a new product. = 公司正在调集资源以推出新产品。|近义词：organizing, arranging, mobilizing | 反义词：disorganizing, scattering, disbanding | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He marshals his arguments effectively. = 他有效地组织了他的论据。
The company marshals its resources carefully. = 公司谨慎地调动其资源。

一般过去时:
She marshalled her thoughts before speaking. = 她发言前整理了一下思绪。
They marshalled their evidence to support their claim. = 他们收集证据来支持他们的说法。

现在完成时:
The team has marshalled its resources to meet the deadline. = 团队已经调集资源来赶上截止日期。
He has marshalled a strong case against the defendant. = 他已经收集了强有力的证据来指控被告。

过去完成时:
She had marshalled all her energy for the final sprint. = 她已经为最后的冲刺积蓄了全部精力。
They had marshalled their forces before the battle began. = 战斗开始前他们已经集结了兵力。
