relitigating:/ˌriːˈlɪtɪɡeɪtɪŋ/| v. | 重审；再次诉讼 | The act of bringing a legal case back to court after it has already been decided.  This often involves arguing points that were already considered.  It can be challenging and requires demonstrating new evidence or a significant procedural error in the original trial. | Example: The company is relitigating the patent infringement case, claiming new evidence has come to light.  公司正在重审专利侵权案件，声称出现了新的证据。

The judge refused to allow the relitigation of the settled matter.  法官拒绝允许重审已解决的事务。|近义词：re-examining, reconsidering, retrying |反义词：settling, concluding, finalizing |动词
