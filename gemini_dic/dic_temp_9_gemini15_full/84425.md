hallucinate:/həˈluːsɪneɪt/ | v. | 产生幻觉；幻想 | To perceive things that are not actually present; to experience sensory perceptions in the absence of external stimuli.  |例句：He began to hallucinate after taking the drug. 他服药后开始产生幻觉。|例句：She hallucinated that she was flying. 她幻想自己正在飞翔。|近义词：fantasize, imagine, dream |反义词：perceive accurately, see clearly


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He hallucinates frequently. = 他经常产生幻觉。
She hallucinates vivid colors. = 她幻想出鲜艳的色彩。

一般过去时:
I hallucinated after the surgery. = 我手术后产生了幻觉。
He hallucinated monsters under his bed. = 他幻想床底下有怪物。

现在完成时:
She has hallucinated since the accident. = 自从事故发生后，她一直有幻觉。
They have never hallucinated before. = 他们以前从未有过幻觉。

过去完成时:
He had hallucinated for days before seeking help. = 他在寻求帮助之前已经产生幻觉好几天了。
She had never hallucinated until that night. = 直到那天晚上她才第一次产生幻觉。

将来时:
I think I will hallucinate if I take more of that medicine. = 我想如果我再服用那种药，我就会产生幻觉。
He will probably hallucinate again if he doesn't get enough sleep. = 如果他不睡够觉，他可能会再次产生幻觉。

虚拟语气:
If I took that drug, I might hallucinate. = 如果我服用那种药物，我可能会产生幻觉。
I wish I hadn't hallucinated during the presentation. = 我真希望我在演示期间没有产生幻觉。

被动语态:
(This verb is rarely used in the passive voice.)  
The effects of the drug were such that hallucinations were induced. =  药物的作用导致了幻觉。

疑问句:
Do you ever hallucinate? = 你曾经有过幻觉吗？
Is he hallucinating again? = 他又在产生幻觉了吗？

否定句:
I don't hallucinate. = 我没有幻觉。
She doesn't usually hallucinate. = 她通常不会产生幻觉。
