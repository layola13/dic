rouse:/raʊz/ | vt. |唤醒，激起，鼓动 | To awaken from sleep; to stir to action or excitement; to incite. |例句：The noise roused me from my sleep.  噪音把我吵醒了。|例句：His speech roused the crowd to anger. 他的演讲激起了群众的愤怒。|近义词：awaken, stir, excite, incite |反义词：calm, soothe, quiet


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He rouses himself before dawn every day. 他每天黎明前都起床。
The wind rouses the leaves. 风吹动树叶。

一般过去时:
The news roused the nation.  这个消息激起了全国的愤怒。
She roused herself and went to the door. 她振作起来走到门口。

现在完成时:
The scandal has roused public outrage. 这丑闻激起了公众的愤怒。
He has roused himself to meet the challenge. 他已振作起来迎接挑战。

过去完成时:
The unexpected news had roused her from her depression.  意外的消息使她摆脱了抑郁。
They had roused the villagers to protest. 他们已煽动村民抗议。

将来时:
The storm will rouse the ocean waves.  暴风雨将掀起海浪。
The speech will rouse the audience to action.  这演讲将激起听众的行动。

虚拟语气:
If only I could rouse myself to finish the project.  如果我能振作起来完成这个项目就好了。
I wish I could rouse him from his apathy. 我希望我能唤醒他摆脱冷漠。

被动语态:
The sleeping giant was roused by the thunder.  沉睡的巨人被雷声惊醒了。
The crowd was roused to a frenzy by his speech. 听众被他讲话煽动得狂热起来。

疑问句:
Can anything rouse him from his despair?  有什么能使他摆脱绝望吗？
What will rouse the public from its apathy? 什么能唤醒公众摆脱冷漠？

否定句:
Nothing could rouse him from his stupor.  什么都不能使他从昏迷中醒来。
The announcement failed to rouse any interest.  这个公告未能引起任何兴趣。
