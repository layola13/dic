pout:/paʊt/| v. n.|撅嘴，生气地不说话|To push out one's lips in a expression of annoyance, displeasure, or sulkiness.  It can also refer to the resulting expression itself.|例句：The child pouted when he didn't get the toy.  孩子没拿到玩具就撅起了嘴。  She pouted her lips in displeasure. 她不高兴地撅起了嘴。|近义词：sulk, sulkiness, mope|反义词：smile, grin


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He pouts when he's unhappy. = 他不开心的时候会撅嘴。
She often pouts after arguments. = 她争吵后经常撅嘴。

一般过去时:
The baby pouted after his nap. = 婴儿午睡后撅起了嘴。
He pouted all evening because of the bad grade. = 他因为坏成绩而闷闷不乐地过了整个晚上。

现在完成时:
She has pouted all day. = 她整天都撅着嘴。
He has pouted since he lost the game. = 自从输了比赛后，他就一直撅着嘴。

过去完成时:
She had pouted for hours before finally speaking. = 她撅着嘴好几个小时才最终开口说话。
He had pouted all morning before his mother gave in. = 他早上撅着嘴了好久，他妈妈才让步。

将来时:
He will pout if he doesn't get his way. = 如果他不能如愿以偿，他会撅嘴。
She will probably pout if you don't invite her. = 如果你不邀请她，她可能会撅嘴。

虚拟语气:
If he were a child, he would pout. = 如果他是个孩子，他会撅嘴。
I wish she wouldn't pout so much. = 我希望她不要总是撅嘴。

被动语态:
(Not applicable in a meaningful way.  "Pout" is generally an intransitive verb.)

疑问句:
Is he pouting again? = 他又在撅嘴吗？

否定句:
