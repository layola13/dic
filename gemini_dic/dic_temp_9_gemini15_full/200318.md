skull:/skʌl/| n.|头骨，脑壳|The bony framework of the head; it encloses and protects the brain.  It is also often used metaphorically to represent death or danger.|例句：The archaeologist carefully examined the ancient skull. 考古学家仔细检查了古代的头骨。|例句：The accident left him with a fractured skull.  事故导致他头骨骨折。|近义词：cranium|反义词:无


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The skull protects the brain. = 头骨保护大脑。
His skull is unusually large. = 他的头骨异乎寻常的大。

一般过去时:
The impact fractured his skull. = 撞击使他的头骨骨折。
They found a skull buried in the garden. = 他们在花园里发现了一具头骨。

现在完成时:
Scientists have studied many ancient skulls. = 科学家们已经研究了许多古代的头骨。
Archaeologists have unearthed a complete skull. = 考古学家们已经挖掘出一具完整的头骨。

过去完成时:

They discovered the skull had been carefully cleaned. = 他们发现头骨已经被仔细清洗过了。

将来时:

虚拟语气:
