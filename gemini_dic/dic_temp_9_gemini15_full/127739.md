negotiated:/nɪˈɡoʊʃieɪtɪd/| v. |协商；谈判；谈成 | To reach an agreement through discussion, especially in business or politics.  |例句：They negotiated a new contract with the union. 他们与工会协商达成了一份新合同。|例句：The two countries negotiated a peace treaty.  两国协商达成了一项和平条约。|近义词：bargained, haggled, discussed |反义词：imposed, dictated


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They negotiate a deal every week. = 他们每周都会协商一笔交易。
We negotiate prices with suppliers. = 我们与供应商协商价格。

一般过去时:
The government negotiated a ceasefire. = 政府协商停火。
They negotiated successfully for a better salary. = 他们成功地协商获得了更高的薪水。

现在完成时:
They have negotiated a peace agreement. = 他们已经协商达成了一项和平协议。
She has negotiated several important contracts this year. = 她今年已经谈成了几份重要的合同。

过去完成时:
They had negotiated for hours before reaching an agreement. = 他们在达成协议之前已经协商了几个小时。
The team had negotiated a deal before the deadline. = 团队在截止日期前已经谈妥了一笔交易。

将来时:
We will negotiate the terms of the contract next week. = 我们下周将协商合同条款。
The company will negotiate with its creditors. = 公司将与债权人进行谈判。

虚拟语气:
If they had negotiated harder, they might have got a better price. = 如果他们更努力地谈判，他们可能会得到更好的价格。
I wish we had negotiated a lower interest rate. = 我希望我们当时协商了一个更低的利率。

被动语态:
A new trade agreement was negotiated between the two countries. = 两国之间协商达成了一项新的贸易协定。
The price was negotiated down by 10%. = 价格通过谈判降低了10%。

疑问句:
Were the terms of the contract negotiated fairly? = 合同条款是否公平地协商？
Have they negotiated a solution to the problem? = 他们是否协商出了解决问题的方案？

否定句:
They did not negotiate a satisfactory outcome. = 他们没有协商出一个令人满意的结果。
The two parties failed to negotiate a compromise. = 双方未能协商达成妥协。


PHRASE->
negotiate a deal: 谈成一笔交易
negotiate a price: 协商价格
negotiate a contract: 谈判合同
negotiate a settlement: 协商和解
negotiate a treaty: 谈判条约
