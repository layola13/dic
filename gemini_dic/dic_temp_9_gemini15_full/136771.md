nonsequentially:/ˌnɒnˌsɛkˈwɛnʃəli/ | adv. |非顺序地，无次序地 |Not in a sequential or logical order.  |例句：The chapters in the book were presented nonsequentially, making it difficult to follow the narrative. 这本书的章节排列无序，难以理解故事情节。

例句：The slides were shown nonsequentially, confusing the audience.幻灯片没有按顺序播放，这使得观众感到困惑。


近义词：randomly, haphazardly, out of order, in a jumbled manner | 反义词：sequentially, chronologically, orderly


词源:

* **non-**:  prefix meaning "not"
* **sequentially**: adverb derived from "sequence," meaning a particular order in which related events, movements, or things follow each other.  "Sequence" ultimately derives from the Latin word *sequi*, meaning "to follow."

用法说明:

"Nonsequentially" is an adverb that describes something happening or being arranged without following a particular order or sequence.  It emphasizes the lack of logical progression or systematic arrangement.  It's often used to describe events, information, or items that are presented in a random or disorganized manner.
