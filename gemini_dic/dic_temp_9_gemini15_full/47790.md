decapitating: /dɪˈkæpɪteɪtɪŋ/ | v. |斩首；使…失去首脑；削弱 | The act of cutting off someone's head.  It can also be used figuratively to describe the removal of a leader or the weakening of an organization. |


例句：
The executioner decapitated the prisoner. = 刽子手将囚犯斩首。

The new policy effectively decapitated the opposition party. = 新政策有效地削弱了反对党。


近义词：beheading, severing (the head)


反义词： crowning, elevating (to a position of leadership)


词性:动词(现在分词)

USAGE->

They are decapitating the chicken. = 他们在砍鸡头。
He decapitates his opponents politically. = 他在政治上斩断了对手。


一般过去时:

过去完成时:

虚拟语气:
