harpy:/ˈhɑːrpi/| n.|哈耳庇厄，凶恶的女人|A harpy is, in Greek mythology, a monstrous creature, typically represented as a woman with the head and wings of a bird of prey, known for their foul temper and predatory nature.  They are often depicted as snatching food, children, or even souls.  In modern usage, the word often refers to a shrewish or nagging woman. |例句：The harpies swooped down and carried off the stolen goods.  哈耳庇厄们俯冲下来，夺走了赃物。


The woman's constant complaints made her seem like a harpy to her colleagues.  这个女人的不断抱怨让她在同事眼中像个泼妇。|近义词：shrew, virago, fury, scold|反义词：angel, saint, darling
