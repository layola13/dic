scandalizing: /ˈskændəlaɪzɪŋ/ | adj. | 令人震惊的；引起丑闻的 | causing shock or outrage;  causing a scandal. | The scandalizing details of the affair were splashed across the front page.  这件丑闻的细节被刊登在头版头条上，令人震惊。  |近义词：shocking, outrageous, scandalous, offensive |反义词：inoffensive, respectable, decent


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The news is scandalizing the public. = 这则新闻令公众震惊。
This behavior scandalizes me. = 这种行为让我感到震惊。


一般过去时:
The revelation scandalized the entire town. = 这一爆料令全镇震惊。
His actions scandalized his family. = 他的行为令他的家人感到震惊。


现在完成时:
The politician's actions have scandalized the nation. = 这位政治家的行为令全国震惊。
This event has scandalized many people. = 这件事令许多人感到震惊。


过去完成时:
