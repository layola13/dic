rhonchus:/ˈrɒŋkəs/| n. | 罗音，痰鸣音 | A rattling sound in the lungs, usually caused by mucus or other secretions.  It's often heard during auscultation (listening with a stethoscope).| The doctor heard a rhonchus in the patient's lungs, indicating a possible respiratory infection.  医生在病人的肺部听到罗音，这表明可能存在呼吸道感染。|wheeze, rattle|  (暂无明确反义词，可考虑用表示肺部声音清亮的词语，但需根据语境)|名词
