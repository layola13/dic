insinuator: /ɪnˈsɪnjuˌeɪtər/ | n. | 暗示者；影射者 | A person who insinuates; one who subtly suggests something unpleasant.  |  He was a master insinuator, always dropping hints without ever directly accusing anyone. 他是个暗示高手，总是暗示一些不愉快的事情，却从不直接指责任何人。|近义词：whisperer,suggester,hint-dropper |反义词：outspoken person, straightforward person |名词

词源:
源自动词 "insinuate"，意思是"暗示；暗指"，来自拉丁语 "insinuare"，意思是"弯曲；蜿蜒进入"，由 "in-" (进入) 和 "sinuare" (弯曲) 组成。因此，"insinuator" 字面意思是"通过弯曲或蜿蜒的方式暗示的人"。
