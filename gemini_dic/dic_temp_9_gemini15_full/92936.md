identify:/aɪˈdɛntɪfaɪ/| vt.|识别，认出，鉴定|To recognize someone or something and be able to say who or what they are.|The police were able to identify the suspect from a security camera image.  警方能够从监控录像中认出嫌疑人。|The scientist identified a new species of plant. 科学家鉴定了一种新的植物物种。|recognize, distinguish, pinpoint, ascertain|misidentify, confuse|动词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
I identify the problem quickly. = 我很快地识别出了问题。
She identifies birds by their songs. = 她通过鸟鸣声来辨认鸟类。

一般过去时:
The detective identified the killer. = 侦探认出了凶手。
They identified the need for a new strategy. = 他们确定了需要一个新的策略。

现在完成时:
Scientists have identified a new virus. = 科学家们已经发现了一种新的病毒。
We have identified several potential risks. = 我们已经确定了几个潜在的风险。

过去完成时:
They had identified the flaw before the launch. = 他们在发布之前就发现了缺陷。
She had identified the problem years ago. = 几年前她就发现了这个问题。

将来时:
Researchers will identify the cause of the disease. = 研究人员将确定这种疾病的原因。
We will identify the best candidate for the job. = 我们将确定最适合这份工作的候选人。

虚拟语气:
If I could identify the problem, I would solve it. = 如果我能识别出问题，我就会解决它。
I wish I could identify the source of the noise. = 我希望我能找出噪音的来源。

被动语态:
The painting was identified as a forgery. = 这幅画被鉴定为赝品。
The suspect was quickly identified by the witness. = 嫌疑人很快就被目击者认出来了。

疑问句:
Can you identify the thief in this lineup? = 你能在这排人中认出小偷吗？
Could you identify the specific issue you are facing? = 你能具体说明你面临的问题吗？

否定句:
I couldn't identify the voice on the phone. = 我无法辨认电话里的声音。
The experts failed to identify the cause of the accident. = 专家们未能确定事故的原因。


PHRASE->

identify with:  与...产生共鸣
identify oneself:  表明身份
identify as:  自认为是...
self-identify:  自我认同
positively identify:  明确辨认
