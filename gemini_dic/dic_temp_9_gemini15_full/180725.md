regrooving: /ˌriːˈɡruːvɪŋ/ | v. | 再开槽；重新开槽 | The process of cutting new grooves into a surface, typically a tire or a record, to restore its functionality or improve its performance.  This often involves removing worn-out material to expose fresh, usable material. | 例句：The mechanic suggested regrooving the tires to extend their lifespan. = 技工建议重新开槽轮胎以延长其使用寿命。

The old record was regrooved to improve the sound quality. = 旧唱片重新开槽以提高音质。

近义词：re-grooving, resurfacing, reconditioning | 反义词：wearing down, deteriorating

词源：
The word "regrooving" is a combination of the prefix "re-" (meaning "again" or "anew") and the noun "grooving" (the act of cutting grooves).  "Groove" itself has origins in Old English and Middle English words related to digging or carving.
