clerkly:/ˈklɑːrkli/ | adj. |办事员似的；刻板的；循规蹈矩的 | relating to a clerk or clerks; characteristic of a clerk;  suggesting the work or manner of a clerk.  The word often carries a slightly negative connotation, implying tediousness, routine, or lack of imagination. |

例句：He performed his duties in a very clerkly manner, meticulously following all procedures. 他以非常刻板的方式履行职责，一丝不苟地遵循所有程序。

例句：The clerkly precision of his bookkeeping was both admirable and slightly unnerving.  他簿记的刻板精确令人钦佩，但也略显令人不安。


近义词：bureaucratic, methodical, precise, routine, unimaginative

反义词：creative, imaginative, unconventional, spontaneous


词源：源自古英语 *clerc，意为“学者，神职人员”，最终源自拉丁语 clericus，“教士，神职人员”。  在中世纪，clerks 通常是受过教育的人，负责抄写和管理文件，因此“clerkly”逐渐与他们的工作方式联系起来。
