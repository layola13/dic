pretranslations:/ˌpriːtrænzˈleɪʃənz/ | n. |预翻译|The act or process of translating something before it is officially translated.  It often involves creating a preliminary version of a translation to get a general idea of the content and style.  This can be helpful for planning purposes or for providing feedback to the translator.|Example:  The pretranslations provided a valuable framework for the final translation. = 预翻译为最终翻译提供了一个宝贵的框架。|近义词： preliminary translation, draft translation |反义词：final translation, post-editing|名词
