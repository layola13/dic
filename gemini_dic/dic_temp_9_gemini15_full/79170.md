glamorizes:/ˈɡlæməraɪzɪz/| vt.|使…具有魅力；美化；渲染；|To represent something as glamorous or attractive, often in a way that is unrealistic or misleading.|The film glamorizes the life of a drug dealer. 这部电影美化了毒贩的生活。|近义词：romanticizes, idealizes, glorifies|反义词：denigrates, disparages, criticizes|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The magazine glamorizes unrealistic beauty standards. = 这本杂志美化了不切实际的美丽标准。
He glamorizes his past experiences. = 他美化了他过去的经历。


一般过去时:
The media glamorized the celebrity's lifestyle. = 媒体美化了这位名人的生活方式。
They glamorized the war, ignoring its horrors. = 他们美化了战争，忽略了其恐怖之处。


现在完成时:
The fashion industry has glamorized thinness for decades. = 时尚行业几十年来一直美化瘦弱。
She has glamorized her life on social media. = 她在社交媒体上美化了她的生活。


过去完成时:
The novel had glamorized the lives of pirates before the historical accuracy was questioned. = 这部小说在历史准确性受到质疑之前美化了海盗的生活。
They had glamorized the event to attract more participants. = 他们之前美化了这次活动以吸引更多参与者。


将来时:
The upcoming movie will glamorize the life of a famous artist. = 即将上映的电影将美化一位著名艺术家的生活。
They will glamorize the product through extensive advertising. = 他们将通过广泛的广告来美化产品。


虚拟语气:
If the advertisement glamorized the product less, people might be less likely to buy it. = 如果广告对产品的美化程度较低，人们可能不太会购买它。
I wish the media wouldn't glamorize violence. = 我希望媒体不要美化暴力。


被动语态:
The singer's life was glamorized by the press. = 歌手的生平被媒体美化了。

疑问句:

否定句:
