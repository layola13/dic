presatisfaction:/ˌpriːˌsætɪsˈfækʃən/| n. |预先满足；事先满意|A feeling of satisfaction experienced before an event or achievement actually occurs.  It often involves anticipation and confidence in a positive outcome.  The term is relatively uncommon and often used in academic or specialized contexts.|例句：The presatisfaction he felt before the presentation was palpable, fueled by months of meticulous preparation. 他在演讲前的预先满足感是显而易见的，这得益于几个月细致的准备。

例句：Her presatisfaction with the upcoming trip was evident in her cheerful demeanor. 她对即将到来的旅行的预先满足感在她快乐的态度中显而易见。


近义词：anticipation, expectation, forepleasure (in a sexual context)


反义词：disappointment, apprehension, dread

词性：名词
