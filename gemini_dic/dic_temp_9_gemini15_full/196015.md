sentiments:/ˈsentɪmənts/| n.|感情；情绪；观点|Feelings or opinions, especially about a matter of importance;  The expression of such feelings.|The sentiments expressed in her letter were very moving.  她的信中表达的感情非常令人感动。
The political sentiments of the country are shifting.  这个国家的政治观点正在转变。|feelings, emotions, opinions, views, thoughts|indifference, apathy


USAGE->
一般现在时:
Her sentiments are always positive. = 她的情绪总是积极的。
His sentiments on the matter are unknown. = 他对此事的看法不明。

一般过去时:
Her sentiments changed after the speech. = 演讲之后她的情绪改变了。
Their sentiments towards the project were initially negative. = 他们最初对这个项目的看法是负面的。

现在完成时:
My sentiments have not changed since last week. = 从上周以来我的想法没有改变。
Public sentiments have shifted in favour of the proposal. = 公众情绪已转向支持该提案。

过去完成时:

将来时:
His sentiments will likely be revealed in his upcoming book. = 他的感情可能会在他的下一本书中被揭露。
We believe public sentiments will be favourable to the new policy. = 我们相信公众对这项新政策的看法将会是积极的。

虚拟语气:
If his sentiments were known, the outcome would be different. = 如果他的想法为人所知，结果将会不同。

疑问句:
