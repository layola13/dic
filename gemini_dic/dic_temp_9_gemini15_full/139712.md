ocellated:/ˈɒsɪleɪtɪd/ | adj. |  眼状斑点的；具眼状斑纹的 | Having spots resembling eyes.  The term typically refers to markings on animals or plants that resemble eyes, often as a form of camouflage or warning.  | 例句：The butterfly's wings were ocellated, with large, dark spots that mimicked eyes.  这只蝴蝶的翅膀上有眼状斑点，大的深色斑点模仿眼睛。


The ocellated lizard is a master of disguise.  这种眼斑蜥蜴是伪装大师。


近义词：eyed, spotted, patterned,  ocellate (verb form) | 反义词：unmarked, plain, uniform | 词性：形容词


词源:

From Latin *ocellatus, past participle of ocellare "to have eyes," from ocellus "a little eye," diminutive of oculus "eye."  The word ultimately derives from Proto-Indo-European *awk- "eye."
