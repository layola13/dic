cassowaries:/ˈkæsəˌwɛəriz/ | n. |食火鸡 |A large flightless bird of New Guinea and Australia, having a casque on the head and powerful legs.  Cassowaries are known for their powerful kicks, which can be dangerous to humans. |Example:  The cassowaries are native to the rainforests of New Guinea. 食火鸡原产于新几内亚的雨林。

The cassowary's sharp claws can inflict serious wounds. 食火鸡锋利的爪子会造成严重的伤口。| emu, rhea |  (no direct antonym, but perhaps "small bird" could be considered a relative antonym)|noun
