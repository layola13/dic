lustre: /ˈlʌstər/ | n. |光泽；光彩；荣光|  A gentle sheen or soft glow, especially that of a partly reflective surface;  brightness; brilliance; renown; splendor.| The polished surface of the furniture had a beautiful lustre.  这件家具抛光的表面有着美丽的光泽。| The lustre of her achievements is undeniable. 她成就的光辉是不可否认的。| sheen, gloss, brilliance | dullness, matte | 名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The vase possesses a subtle lustre. = 这花瓶具有细微的光泽。
His work shows great lustre. = 他的作品显示出极大的光彩。

一般过去时:
The diamond lost its lustre after years of wear. = 钻石经过多年的磨损后失去了光泽。
The victory gave him a new lustre. = 这次胜利给他带来了新的荣光。

现在完成时:
The painting has retained its lustre over the centuries. = 这幅画几个世纪以来一直保持着它的光泽。
Her career has gained much lustre. = 她的事业获得了很大的光彩。

过去完成时:
The silver had lost its lustre before it was cleaned. = 银器在清洁之前已经失去了光泽。
His reputation had already gained lustre before the award. = 在获奖之前，他的名声就已经获得了光彩。

将来时:
The new polish will restore the lustre to the car. = 新抛光剂将恢复汽车的光泽。
Her future work will add to her already considerable lustre. = 她未来的作品将为她已有的相当大的光彩锦上添花。

虚拟语气:
If the jewels retained their lustre, they would be worth a fortune. = 如果珠宝保持其光泽，它们将价值不菲。
I wish my old car still had its original lustre. = 我希望我的旧车仍然有它原来的光泽。

被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
