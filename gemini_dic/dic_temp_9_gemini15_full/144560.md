outwalks:/ˌaʊtˈwɔːks/| v.|超过（在散步方面）|to walk farther than someone or something|He outwalks his friends every time they go for a hike. 他每次和朋友去远足都比他们走得远。|outpaces, surpasses| lags behind |动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He outwalks me every morning. = 他每天早上都比我走得远。
She outwalks her dog on their daily walks. = 她每天遛狗时都比她的狗走得远。


一般过去时:
Yesterday, I outwalked my brother. = 昨天我比我哥哥走得远。
They outwalked the competition in the marathon. = 他们在马拉松比赛中超过了竞争对手。


现在完成时:
He has outwalked everyone in this hiking group. = 在这个远足团队中，他已经超过了所有人。
She has outwalked her previous best distance. = 她已经超过了她之前的最佳距离。


过去完成时:
By the end of the day, they had outwalked their planned route. = 到一天结束时，他们已经走过了计划路线。
I realized I had outwalked my companions. = 我意识到我走在了同伴们的前面。


将来时:
I think I will outwalk you tomorrow. = 我想我明天会比你走得远。
She believes she will outwalk her rival in the race. = 她相信她会在比赛中超过她的对手。


虚拟语气:
If I were younger, I think I would outwalk you. = 如果我还年轻，我想我会比你走得远。
If she had trained harder, she would have outwalked her opponent. = 如果她训练更努力，她就会超过她的对手。


被动语态:  (This verb is rarely used in passive voice because it implies an active comparison.)  The passive voice is less natural for this verb.  Consider rephrasing.


疑问句:
Does he often outwalk you? = 他经常比你走得远吗？
Did she outwalk the other runners in the race? = 她在那场比赛中超过其他跑步者了吗？


否定句:
I didn't outwalk him today. = 我今天没有比他走得远。
She didn't outwalk her usual distance. = 她没有走完她通常的距离。


PHRASE->
outwalk one's shadow: 走得很远
outwalk the competition: 在比赛中超过竞争对手
outwalk one's limits: 超过自己的极限
outwalk the storm:  步行穿越暴风雨
outwalk one's fatigue:  克服疲劳继续步行
