catechise:/ˈkætɪkaɪz/| vt.| 详加询问；教导（尤指宗教教义）|To question someone thoroughly, especially to teach religious doctrine.  |例句：The teacher catechized the students on the scriptures. 老师详细询问学生圣经经文。|例句：The priest catechized the children in preparation for their first communion.  牧师对孩子们进行宗教教义问答，为他们的第一次圣餐做准备。|近义词：instruct, quiz, interrogate, examine|反义词：ignore, neglect|动词

The priest catechises the children every Sunday. = 牧师每周日都会对孩子们进行宗教教义问答。
She catechises her students on the history of the French Revolution. = 她向学生们详细讲解法国大革命的历史。

词源:

From Middle French *catéchiser, from Late Latin *catechēsare, from Greek katēchēsis ‘oral instruction’, from katēchein ‘to sound down, instruct orally’.  Ultimately derived from the Greek verb katēchein, meaning "to sound down," "to instruct orally,"  reflecting the method of teaching through oral instruction common in early Christianity.
