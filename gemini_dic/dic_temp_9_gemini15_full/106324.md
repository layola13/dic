koff:/kɒf/| /kɔːf/ | n. vt.|咳嗽；咳出|A sudden, short, noisy expulsion of air from the lungs.  It is often caused by irritation of the respiratory tract. |例句：He had a bad cough. 他咳嗽得很厉害。
例句：She coughed up a bit of blood. 她咳出了一点血。|近义词：cough, hack|反义词:无


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I cough every morning. = 我每天早上都咳嗽。
He coughs a lot when he smokes. = 他抽烟时咳嗽得很厉害。

一般过去时:
I coughed all night. = 我整夜都在咳嗽。
She coughed violently after drinking the cold water. = 她喝了凉水后剧烈咳嗽。

现在完成时:
I have coughed for a week. = 我已经咳嗽了一个星期了。
He has coughed up blood several times. = 他已经好几次咳血了。

过去完成时:
I had coughed for days before I went to the doctor. = 我去看医生之前已经咳嗽了好几天了。
She had coughed so much that she was hoarse. = 她咳得厉害，声音都嘶哑了。

将来时:
I will cough less after taking this medicine. = 服用这种药后，我会少咳嗽一些。
He will probably cough again if he continues to smoke. = 如果他继续抽烟，他可能会再次咳嗽。

虚拟语气:
If I coughed up blood, I would go to the hospital immediately. = 如果我咳血，我会立即去医院。
I wish I hadn't coughed so much last night. = 我真希望我昨晚不要咳得那么厉害。

被动语态:
A cough can be triggered by many things. = 咳嗽可以由许多事情引起。
The cough was finally cured by the new medicine. = 新药最终治好了咳嗽。

疑问句:
Do you cough often? = 你经常咳嗽吗？
Why is he coughing so much? = 他为什么咳嗽得这么厉害？

否定句:
I don't cough very often. = 我不经常咳嗽。
He didn't cough at all during the meeting. = 会议期间他根本没有咳嗽。
