pseudopriestly: /ˌsuːdoʊˈpriːstli/ | adj. | 假祭司的；冒充祭司的 |  Seeming or pretending to be priestly; having the appearance or manner of a priest but lacking genuine religious authority or sanctity.  The term often implies deception or a superficial imitation of priestly qualities. | Example:  The cult leader's pseudopriestly pronouncements were designed to manipulate his followers.  =  邪教领袖伪装成祭司的声明旨在操纵他的追随者。 | Near Synonyms:  quasi-priestly,  priestlike (but lacking the negative connotation) | Antonyms: genuinely priestly, authentically religious |

Word Origin:

The word is a combination of "pseudo-" (Greek, meaning "false" or "pretended") and "priestly" (from "priest," ultimately from Latin *presbyter, meaning "elder").  Therefore, it literally means "falsely priestly" or "pretending to be priestly."
