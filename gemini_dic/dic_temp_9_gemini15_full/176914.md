realignment:/ˌriːəˈlaɪnmənt/ | n. |重新调整；重新排列；改组 |The process of changing something's structure or organization, often to improve efficiency or effectiveness.  It involves adjusting or repositioning elements to create a new alignment or arrangement. |例句：The company underwent a major realignment of its departments to improve productivity. 公司对其部门进行了大规模的重组以提高生产力。

例句：The political realignment after the election led to a new governing coalition. 选举后的政治重组导致了一个新的执政联盟。

近义词：reorganization, restructuring, readjustment, reallocation

反义词：disorganization, disarray, chaos


词性: 名词
