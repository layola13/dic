laird:/lɛrd/| n. |苏格兰地主；庄园主|A laird is a Scottish landowner, especially one of the smaller landowners, who holds land directly from the crown.  The term is often associated with a specific historical and cultural context in Scotland.|例句：The laird of the estate welcomed us warmly.  这位庄园主热情地欢迎了我们。|例句：Many traditional crofting communities were led by their local laird. 许多传统的克罗夫特社区由当地地主领导。|近义词：landowner, estate owner, lord (less common and often implies a higher social rank)|反义词：tenant, peasant
