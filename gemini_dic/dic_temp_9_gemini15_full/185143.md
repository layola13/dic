retinula:/rɛˈtɪnjʊlə/ | n. |视网膜小细胞，感光细胞|A retinula is one of the numerous elongated receptor cells that make up an ommatidium, the visual unit of a compound eye in arthropods such as insects and crustaceans.  Each retinula cell contains a rhabdom, a light-sensitive structure that helps focus light onto the photoreceptor cells.  The combined signals from many retinulae create a mosaic image. |Example: The structure and function of the retinula cells are crucial for the insect's visual perception.  昆虫视网膜小细胞的结构和功能对其视觉感知至关重要。|近义词：photoreceptor cell (感光细胞)|反义词：无
