hocuspocus:/ˈhoʊkəsˈpoʊkəs/|/ˈhoʊkəsˈpoʊkəs/| n. int.|魔术；戏法；欺骗；胡言乱语|A nonsensical word or phrase used to disguise or obscure something; trickery or deception, often associated with magic or illusion.  It can also refer to meaningless or incomprehensible talk.|例句：The magician performed a bit of hocus pocus with cards and scarves. 魔术师用纸牌和围巾变戏法。

The whole thing was just hocus pocus; there was no real magic involved.  整件事都是骗人的把戏；根本没有真正的魔法。|近义词：trickery, deception, illusion, conjuring, mumbo jumbo|反义词：truth, reality, honesty


USAGE->
(Hocus pocus is primarily a noun and interjection;  it doesn't conjugate like a verb.)

PHRASE->
It's primarily used as a standalone word or phrase, not in common phrases.  Examples of its use in context include:

"It was all hocus pocus." = 那全是骗人的把戏。
"Don't believe his hocus pocus." = 不要相信他的花言巧语。
"The speaker's argument was pure hocus pocus." = 演讲者的论点纯粹是胡说八道。
"He used hocus pocus to win the game." = 他用欺骗的手段赢得了比赛。
"With a flick of the wrist and some hocus pocus, the rabbit appeared." =  手腕一挥，变戏法似的，兔子出现了。
