mediocre:/ˈmiːdiˌoʊkər/ | adj. | 平庸的；普通的；二流的 | Of moderate quality; not very good.  It suggests something is neither good nor bad, but simply average or below average, often implying a lack of distinction or excellence.  |例句：His performance was mediocre, lacking the passion and skill of a true professional.  他的表演很平庸，缺乏一个真正专业人士的激情和技巧。

例句：The food at the restaurant was mediocre; nothing special, but nothing terrible either.  这家餐厅的食物很一般；没有什么特别的，但也谈不上糟糕。


近义词：average, ordinary, fair, run-of-the-mill, unexceptional, middling


反义词：excellent, outstanding, superb, exceptional, remarkable


词源:  The word "mediocre" comes from the Latin word "mediocris," which means "of moderate size," "moderate," or "middling."  It's a combination of "medius" (middle) and "-ocris" (sharp, pointed - though this suffix's meaning is less relevant to the modern word's usage).
