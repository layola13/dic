semi-civilized: /ˈsɛmiˌsɪvəlaɪzd/ | adj. | 半开化的；半文明的 | Partially civilized; having some but not all the characteristics of a civilized society.  It implies a lack of full development in social organization, culture, or technology.  The term often carries a negative connotation, suggesting inferiority compared to fully civilized societies. |


例句：

The remote tribe lived a semi-civilized existence, relying on traditional methods of farming and hunting.  偏远的部落过着半开化的生活，依靠传统的耕作和狩猎方式。

Some critics considered the country's infrastructure to be semi-civilized compared to Western standards.  一些评论家认为，与西方标准相比，该国的基础设施属于半开化的状态。


近义词：partially civilized, underdeveloped, primitive, unsophisticated

反义词：civilized, advanced, sophisticated, cultured


词源:

* **semi-**:  From Latin "semi-", meaning "half," "partly," or "partially."

* **civilized**: From Latin "civilis," meaning "of or relating to citizens," implying an ordered and cultured society.
