formate:/ˈfɔːmeɪt/ | n. |甲酸盐；甲酸酯|A formate is a salt or ester of formic acid.  Formates are characterized by the presence of the formate ion (HCOO−). They are commonly used in various industrial applications and are also found naturally in some organisms.| Example: Sodium formate is used as a de-icer and in the textile industry.  甲酸钠用作除冰剂和纺织工业中的原料。|近义词：formate ester (for ester form)|反义词：无|名词

词源:

The word "formate" is derived from "formic acid," which in turn comes from the Latin word "formica," meaning "ant." Formic acid was first isolated from ants, hence the name.  甲酸盐一词源于“甲酸”，而“甲酸”又源于拉丁语单词“formica”，意为“蚂蚁”。甲酸最初是从蚂蚁中分离出来的，因此得名。
