deliriums:/dɪˈlɪriəmz/ | n. | 谵妄（复数） | A state of confusion characterized by disorientation, hallucinations, and incoherent speech, often associated with fever, illness, or intoxication.  Deliriums are temporary and usually reversible once the underlying cause is treated. | 例句：The patient experienced severe deliriums due to high fever.  病人因高烧而出现严重的谵妄。

The elderly patient’s deliriums were attributed to dehydration. 老年病人的谵妄归因于脱水。


近义词：raving, frenzy, madness, confusion | 反义词：lucidity, clarity, coherence | 词性：名词
