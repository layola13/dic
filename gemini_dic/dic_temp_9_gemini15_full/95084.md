词源:
The word "inalienable" comes from the prefix "in-" (not), "alien-" (to make foreign or strange), and the suffix "-able" (capable of).  Therefore, it literally means "not capable of being made foreign or strange," or "not capable of being alienated."  The root "alien" relates to the Latin word "alienus," meaning "belonging to another."
