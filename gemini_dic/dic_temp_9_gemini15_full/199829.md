sit-up:/ˈsɪtʌp/ | n. v. | 仰卧起坐；挺直身体 | An exercise done by lying on your back and raising your body to a sitting position.  Also refers to the act of sitting upright. |例句：I do twenty sit-ups every morning.  我每天早上做二十个仰卧起坐。

例句：He sat up straight in his chair. 他在椅子上坐直了身体。|近义词：crunch (n.), rise (v.), straighten (v.) |反义词：slouch (v.), slump (v.)

词源:
The word "sit-up" is a compound word formed from the verb "sit" and the adverb "up".  "Sit" originates from Old English *sittan, while "up" has Germanic roots. The combination describes the action of raising oneself to a sitting position from a lying-down position.
