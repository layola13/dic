kindled:/ˈkɪnd(ə)ld/| vt.|点燃；激起；煽动|To set something alight; to start a fire.  To arouse or inspire a feeling or quality.|例句：He kindled a fire in the fireplace. 他在壁炉里生了一堆火。
The speech kindled the crowd's enthusiasm. 这篇演讲激起了人群的热情。|近义词：ignite, ignite, inflame, arouse|反义词：extinguish, quench, stifle


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He kindles a fire every morning. = 他每天早上都生火。
She kindles hope in the hearts of many. = 她给许多人心头带来了希望。

一般过去时:
They kindled a bonfire on the beach. = 他们在海滩上燃起了篝火。
The news report kindled fear among the citizens. = 新闻报道在市民中引发了恐惧。

现在完成时:
She has kindled a love for learning in her students. = 她已经在她学生心中点燃了对学习的热爱。
He has kindled a new debate on climate change. = 他已经就气候变化引发了一场新的辩论。

过去完成时:
They had kindled a small fire before it got dark. = 天黑之前他们已经生了一小堆火。
She had kindled a strong sense of community spirit. = 她已经激发了一种强烈的社区精神。

将来时:
We will kindle a fire to keep warm tonight. = 今晚我们会生火取暖。
His words will kindle a revolution. = 他的话将会引发一场革命。

虚拟语气:
If I had matches, I would kindle a fire. = 如果我有火柴，我会生火。
If he had been more careful, he wouldn't have kindled such a fierce argument. = 如果他更小心一点，他就不会引发如此激烈的争论。

被动语态:
The fire was kindled by a careless camper. = 这场大火是由一个粗心的露营者引起的。
Hope was kindled in their hearts by the good news. = 好消息在他们心中燃起了希望。

疑问句:
Did you kindle the fire? = 你生火了吗？
Can you kindle my enthusiasm for this project? = 你能激起我对这个项目的热情吗？

否定句:
He couldn't kindle a fire because he didn't have any matches. = 他因为没有火柴而无法生火。
She failed to kindle the interest of the audience. = 她未能引起观众的兴趣。


PHRASE->

词源:
Old English *cyndelan, from cynd "kind," "nature," + -elan, a verb-forming suffix.  The sense development is from "to set alight" (literally, "to set the nature [of something] aflame") to the metaphorical sense of "arousing or inspiring."
