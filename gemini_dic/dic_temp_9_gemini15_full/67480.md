extorts:/ɪkˈstɔːrts/ | vt. |敲诈；勒索；强索| To obtain (something, especially money) from (someone) by force or threats.  |例句：The criminal extorted money from the businessman.  罪犯勒索了那个商人。

例句：He extorted a confession from the suspect. 他从嫌疑人那里逼供。


近义词：blackmail, coerce, pressure, bully |反义词：donate, give freely


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He extorts money from his victims. 他向受害者勒索钱财。
The government extorts taxes from its citizens.  政府向公民征税。


一般过去时:
The gangster extorted a large sum of money from the shop owner.  那个歹徒从店主那里敲诈了一大笔钱。
They extorted a confession by threatening to reveal their secrets. 他们以泄露秘密相威胁逼供。


现在完成时:
He has extorted millions of dollars through his illegal activities. 他通过非法活动敲诈了数百万美元。
The corrupt officials have extorted bribes for years.  这些腐败官员多年来一直在受贿。


过去完成时:
The police discovered that he had extorted money from several businesses. 警方发现他曾从几家企业勒索钱财。
Before the trial, the defendant had extorted a false confession from a witness.  审判前，被告曾从证人那里敲诈出虚假供词。


将来时:
They will extort more money if they are not stopped. 如果不阻止他们，他们会勒索更多钱财。
The criminal organization plans to extort protection money from local businesses.  该犯罪组织计划向当地企业勒索保护费。


虚拟语气:
If he had not extorted money, he would not be in jail. 如果他没有勒索钱财，就不会坐牢。
I wish he had not extorted that money from the poor old woman.  我希望他不要从那个可怜的老妇人那里勒索钱财。


被动语态:
Money was extorted from the wealthy businessman. 富有的商人被勒索了钱财。

疑问句:
Was money extorted from the victim? 受害人被勒索钱财了吗？

否定句:
The company did not extort money from its clients. 公司没有向客户勒索钱财。
He denies that he extorted any money from anyone. 他否认他向任何人勒索过钱财。
