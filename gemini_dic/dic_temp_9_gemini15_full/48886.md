deframes:/dɪˈfreɪmz/| vt.| 诽谤；中伤；陷害 |To defame someone is to damage their reputation by spreading false and malicious statements about them.  It often involves making false accusations or presenting a distorted picture of their character or actions. |例句：He was defamed by a rival company trying to steal his clients. 他被一家试图窃取他客户的竞争公司诽谤了。|例句：The newspaper article deframed the politician, leading to his resignation. 这篇报纸文章诽谤了这位政治家，导致他辞职。|近义词：slander, libel, vilify, smear |反义词：praise, commend, extol

词源:
The word "deframe" comes from the Old French word "defamer," which means "to slander" or "to defame."  The prefix "de-" implies "to remove or undo," and the root relates to "fame" or reputation, suggesting an action that destroys or removes someone's good name.
