over-expects: /ˌoʊvərɪkˈspɛkts/ | v. | 过高期望；期望过高 | To have unreasonably high expectations of someone or something.  |  例句：He over-expects his employees, leading to burnout and high turnover. 他对员工期望过高，导致员工倦怠和高离职率。  例句：Don't over-expect the outcome of the negotiation; it might not go as planned. 不要对谈判结果期望过高；结果可能并不如预期。|近义词：expect too much from, overestimate, have unrealistic expectations of |反义词：underestimate, have low expectations of |动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He over-expects his team's performance every quarter. = 他每个季度都对团队的表现期望过高。
She over-expects her children's academic achievements. = 她对孩子的学业成就期望过高。

一般过去时:
The manager over-expected the project's completion date. = 经理对项目的完成日期期望过高。
They over-expected the market response to their new product. = 他们对市场对其新产品的反应期望过高。

现在完成时:
He has over-expected from his employees throughout the year. = 他全年都对员工期望过高。
She has over-expected the results of her investment. = 她对投资结果期望过高。

过去完成时:
The investors had over-expected the return on their investment before the market crash. = 在市场崩盘之前，投资者们对投资回报期望过高。
We had over-expected the efficiency of the new software. = 我们之前对新软件的效率期望过高。

将来时:
He will over-expect the results of the upcoming election. = 他将会对即将到来的选举结果期望过高。
They will over-expect the impact of their marketing campaign. = 他们将会对他们的营销活动的影响期望过高。

虚拟语气:
If I hadn't over-expected, I wouldn't be so disappointed. = 如果我没有期望过高，我就不会这么失望。
I wish I hadn't over-expected the outcome of the meeting. = 我真希望我没有对会议的结果期望过高。

被动语态:
The project's success was over-expected by the management team. = 项目的成功被管理团队期望过高。
The potential benefits were over-expected by the research team. = 研究团队对潜在的好处期望过高。

疑问句:
Does he often over-expect the performance of his staff? = 他是否经常对员工的表现期望过高？
Did they over-expect the capabilities of the new technology? = 他们是否对新技术的性能期望过高？

否定句:
He does not over-expect his children's abilities. = 他并不对他孩子的能力期望过高。
They did not over-expect the impact of the crisis. = 他们并没有对危机的冲击期望过高。
