manhandling: /ˈmænˌhændlɪŋ/ | n. & v. |粗暴对待；暴力处理；笨手笨脚地处理 |

英文解析:The act of handling something roughly or violently, often causing damage or injury.  It can refer to treating a person or object roughly or clumsily.

例句:

The protesters accused the police of manhandling them. = 抗议者指责警察粗暴对待他们。

He was accused of manhandling the evidence. = 他被指控粗暴处理证据。


近义词: rough handling, mishandling, brutalizing, maltreating, abusing

反义词: gentle handling, careful handling, delicate treatment


词性: noun & verb

词源:  The word "manhandle" combines "man" (referring to human strength) and "handle," meaning to touch, move, or manage.  Its use suggests a forceful and often inappropriate application of human strength.
