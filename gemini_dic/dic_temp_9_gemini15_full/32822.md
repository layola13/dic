choker:/ˈtʃoʊkər/ | n. | 项圈；扼杀者；令人窒息的事物 | A choker is a piece of jewelry worn around the neck, typically close-fitting and often made of beads, ribbon, or a similar material. It can also refer to something that causes suffocation or a feeling of constriction.  |例句：She wore a delicate silver choker necklace. 她戴了一条精致的银项圈项链。

例句：The thick fog acted as a choker, making it difficult to breathe.  浓雾像扼杀者一样，让人难以呼吸。


近义词：necklace, collar, strangler | 反义词：None specifically, but words suggesting freedom or openness could be considered antonyms in certain contexts. | 词性:名词

词源:  The word "choker" derives from the verb "to choke," meaning to constrict the throat.  Its use as a noun to describe a close-fitting necklace reflects its literal ability to constrict the neck, though modern chokers are rarely tight enough to cause actual choking.
