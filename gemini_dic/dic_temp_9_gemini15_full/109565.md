let-in-on:/ˌlɛtˈɪnˈɑːn/| phrasal verb|告知；使参与；分享秘密|To inform someone of a secret or plan, or to allow someone to participate in something.|例句：They let me in on the secret. 他们把秘密告诉我了。|例句：I wasn't let in on the plan. 我没有参与那个计划。|近义词：inform, include, share, confide in|反义词：exclude, keep secret, withhold


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They let me in on the secret. = 他们把秘密告诉我了。
He lets his friends in on his plans. = 他将他计划告诉他的朋友们。

一般过去时:
She let him in on the surprise party. = 她让他知道惊喜派对的事。
They didn't let me in on the joke. = 他们没有让我知道那个笑话。

现在完成时:
I've been let in on the company's new strategy. = 我已经了解了公司的新战略。
Have they let you in on the details? = 他们已经让你了解细节了吗？

过去完成时:
He had already let her in on the news before I could. = 在我能告诉她之前，他已经告诉她这个消息了。
They had let everyone in on the secret by the end of the meeting. = 到会议结束时，他们已经把秘密告诉了每个人。

将来时:
I will let you in on a secret later. = 我稍后会告诉你一个秘密。
She will let her colleagues in on the project details tomorrow. = 她明天将把项目细节告诉她的同事们。

虚拟语气:
If they let me in on the plan, I would have helped. = 如果他们让我参与计划，我会帮忙的。
I wish he had let me in on the decision-making process. = 我希望他能让我参与决策过程。

被动语态:
I was let in on the secret by my friend. = 我的朋友把秘密告诉我了。
The details of the project were not let in on to the public. = 项目的细节没有公开。

疑问句:
Were you let in on the discussion? = 你参与了讨论吗？
Will I be let in on the future developments? = 我能了解未来的发展吗？

否定句:
They didn't let me in on their plans. = 他们没有让我知道他们的计划。
He was not let in on the secret. = 他不知道这个秘密。


PHRASE->
