sandbar:/ˈsændbɑːr/| n.|沙洲，沙坝|A sandbar is a ridge of sand, gravel, or other sediment that is built up by the action of waves and currents, often near the shore of a body of water.  It can be submerged or exposed depending on the tide.|例句：The boat ran aground on a sandbar.  船搁浅在沙洲上。

例句：We could see a large sandbar extending from the beach. 我们能看到从海滩延伸出来的一大片沙洲。|近义词：sandbank, shoal|反义词：deep water
