pietism:/ˈpaɪəˌtɪzəm/| n. |虔诚主义；敬虔主义|A religious movement in Protestant Christianity that emphasized personal piety and devotion, often characterized by a strict moral code and a focus on inner spiritual experience.  It originated in late 17th-century Germany. |The Pietist movement significantly impacted religious practice in Germany and beyond. |近义词：devotion, piety, religious fervor |反义词：secularism, worldliness, indifference
