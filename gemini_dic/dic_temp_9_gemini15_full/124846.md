mucocele:/ˈmjuːkoʊsiːl/| n. |粘液囊肿|A mucocele is a cyst-like swelling in the mouth that is filled with mucus. It is usually caused by a blocked salivary gland duct. Mucoceles are generally benign and painless, but they can cause discomfort if they become large or infected.  They are most commonly found on the lower lip. |The dentist diagnosed a mucocele on my lower lip.  牙医诊断我的下嘴唇长了一个粘液囊肿。


The mucocele was surgically removed. 粘液囊肿被手术切除了。
