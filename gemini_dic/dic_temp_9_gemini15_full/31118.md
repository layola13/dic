chanced:/tʃænst/| v.|偶然发生，碰巧|To happen by chance; occur unexpectedly.|例句：I chanced to meet an old friend at the supermarket. 我偶然在超市遇到了一个老朋友。|近义词：happened, occurred,  occurred by chance|反义词：planned, intended


USAGE->
一般现在时:
It chances that I meet her every day. = 我每天都碰巧遇到她。
He chances to be there at that time. = 他碰巧在那时在那里。


一般过去时:
It chanced that I met her yesterday. = 我昨天碰巧遇到她。
The meeting chanced to be very productive. = 会议碰巧非常富有成效。


现在完成时:
It has chanced that I've seen him several times this week. = 这周我碰巧见过他好几次了。
It has chanced that the project has been completed ahead of schedule. = 项目碰巧提前完成了。


过去完成时:
It had chanced that we had already discussed this matter. = 我们碰巧已经讨论过这件事了。
It had chanced that they were already familiar with the problem. = 他们碰巧已经熟悉这个问题了。


将来时:
It will chance that we will be working together next year. = 我们明年碰巧会一起工作。
It will chance that the weather will be perfect for our picnic. = 天气碰巧会非常适合我们的野餐。


虚拟语气:
If it chanced that I had more time, I would have helped you. = 如果我碰巧有更多时间，我会帮助你的。
If it chanced that she was available, she would have attended the meeting. = 如果她碰巧有空，她就会参加会议。


被动语态:
(Chanced is rarely used in the passive voice.  A more natural phrasing would be used.)


疑问句:
Did it chance that you saw him there? = 你碰巧在那里看见他了吗？
Did it chance that they arrived late? = 他们碰巧迟到了吗？


否定句:
It didn't chance that we met again. = 我们并没有碰巧再次相遇。
It didn't chance that they knew about the plan. = 他们并没有碰巧知道这个计划。


PHRASE->
chance encounter = 偶然相遇
chance meeting = 偶然的相遇
chance discovery = 偶然发现
by chance = 偶然地
take a chance = 冒险


词源:
From Old French *chacier "to fall, happen," from Vulgar Latin *cadere (from Latin cadere "to fall").  The modern meaning developed from the sense of something happening by chance or falling into place unexpectedly.
