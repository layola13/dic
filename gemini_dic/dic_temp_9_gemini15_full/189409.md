sahul:/ˈsɑːhʊl/| n. |萨胡尔大陆|Sahul is the name given to the combined landmass of Australia, Tasmania, and New Guinea during periods of lower sea level when they were connected.  The term is used in biogeography and archaeology to describe the shared fauna and human migration patterns across this ancient landmass.|The discovery of ancient tools in Sahul provides evidence of early human settlement in the region.|近义词：Greater Australia (less precise)|反义词：无
