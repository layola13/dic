pockmarked:/ˈpɒkˌmɑːrkt/ | adj. | 坑坑洼洼的；麻脸的 | Covered with pocks or pits; pitted.  The word refers to a surface marked with small indentations or scars, often resembling the marks left by smallpox.  It can describe a person's face, skin, or a surface such as the moon. |例句：The moon's surface is pockmarked with craters. 月球表面坑坑洼洼，布满了陨石坑。

The old building's walls were pockmarked with bullet holes. 老建筑的墙壁上布满了弹孔。


近义词：pitted, scarred, cratered, scarred

反义词：smooth, unblemished

词性:形容词

词源:
The word "pockmarked" comes from "pock," meaning a small mark or scar left by smallpox or a similar disease.  The suffix "-marked" indicates that the surface is marked with these pocks.  The origin of "pock" itself traces back to Middle English "pok," possibly from Old French.
