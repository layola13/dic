inunctuosity:/ˌɪnʌŋktuˈɒsəti/| n. |油滑；虚伪的奉承；油腻；谄媚|The excessive smoothness or slipperiness of a substance, often implying insincerity or artificiality. It suggests an overly smooth, oily, or superficially charming manner, often used to deceive or manipulate.  |例句：His speech was full of unctuosity, lacking genuine sincerity. 他的演讲充满了油滑，缺乏真诚。

例句：The unctuosity of his compliments made me suspicious. 他那些油嘴滑舌的恭维让我起了疑心。


近义词：smoothness, oiliness, slipperiness, insincerity, obsequiousness, flattery, blandishment

反义词：genuineness, sincerity, frankness, honesty, forthrightness


词源:From Latin *unctus, past participle of ungere "to anoint," related to Old English *unct, unctuous.  The sense of insincerity developed later, associating the physical slipperiness of an anointed surface with a deceptively smooth demeanor.
