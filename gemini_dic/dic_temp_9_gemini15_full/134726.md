nonmeasurability: /ˌnɒnˌmɛʒərəˈbɪləti/ | n. |不可测量性| The state or quality of being impossible to measure.  It refers to the inability to assign a numerical value or quantify something using established methods. This can be due to the inherent nature of the thing being measured, limitations of measuring instruments, or the lack of a suitable metric.|The nonmeasurability of happiness makes it difficult to study scientifically.  幸福的不可测量性使得对其进行科学研究变得困难。|measurability, quantifiability|measurability, quantifiability|名词
