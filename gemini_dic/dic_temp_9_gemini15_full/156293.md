phyllotaxy:/ˌfaɪləʊˈtæksi/| n. |叶序|The arrangement of leaves on a stem or branch.  Phyllotaxy is a crucial aspect of plant morphology, influencing factors such as light capture and overall plant architecture. Different phyllotaxy patterns, such as alternate, opposite, and whorled, are observed across various plant species.  The study of phyllotaxy often involves mathematical relationships and geometrical patterns. |例句：The study of phyllotaxy reveals fascinating mathematical patterns in the arrangement of leaves.  叶序的研究揭示了叶片排列中引人入胜的数学模式。
