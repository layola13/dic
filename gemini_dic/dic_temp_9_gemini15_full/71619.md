flatfish:/ˈflætˌfɪʃ/| n.|比目鱼，鲽鱼|A flatfish is a type of fish that has both eyes on one side of its head.  They are characterized by their flattened bodies and their ability to lie on the seabed.  Many species are important food sources.|例句：The fisherman caught several flatfish in the shallow water. 渔夫在浅水中捕到几条比目鱼。

例句：Halibut and sole are popular types of flatfish. 大比目鱼和鳎鱼是受欢迎的比目鱼种类。


近义词：sole, flounder, plaice, halibut|反义词：None (no direct antonym)|词性:名词


词源:

The word "flatfish" is a compound word combining "flat" and "fish".  "Flat" describes the fish's body shape, and "fish" is its classification.  The etymology of "flat" traces back to Old English *flæt, meaning "flat, level, smooth."  The word "fish" has a complex etymology with roots in Proto-Germanic and Proto-Indo-European, ultimately referring to aquatic animals.
