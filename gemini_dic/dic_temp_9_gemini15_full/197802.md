shingles:/ˈʃɪŋɡəlz/| n.|  shingles (复数) /ˈʃɪŋɡlz/| n.|屋瓦；带状疱疹；蛇皮藓|

英文解析:
1.  As a noun, "shingles" can refer to overlapping pieces of material, typically wood, asphalt, or slate, used as a covering for roofs or walls.  It can also refer to a viral infection, herpes zoster, that causes a painful rash with blisters.

2.  The word "shingle" in its singular form refers to a single piece of material used in roofing or siding.

例句:
The roof needs new shingles. = 屋顶需要新的屋瓦。

The doctor diagnosed him with shingles. = 医生诊断他患上了带状疱疹。


近义词:
(for roofing material): tiles, roofing, siding
(for the disease): herpes zoster, zoster


反义词:
(There aren't direct antonyms for shingles as a building material or a disease.  However, one might consider "bare roof" as a contrasting concept to shingles.)
