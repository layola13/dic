drylander:/ˈdraɪlændər/| n. |旱地人，内陆人|A person who lives in a dry inland region, often contrasted with someone who lives near the coast or in a wetter area.  The term often carries connotations of being less sophisticated or worldly than coastal dwellers, reflecting historical biases.|例句：The drylanders were suspicious of the coastal traders, believing them to be dishonest.|近义词：inlander, landlubber (informal and often derogatory)|反义词：coastal dweller, seafarer
