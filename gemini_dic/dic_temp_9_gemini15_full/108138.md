latinization: /ˌlætnɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. | 拉丁化；拉丁语化 | The process of adopting Latin words or grammar into a language.  It also refers to the adoption of Roman customs, especially by non-Roman cultures. | Example: The latinization of scientific terminology helped to create a common language for researchers across Europe.  拉丁化科学术语有助于为整个欧洲的研究人员创造一种共同语言。| 近义词：Romanization, classicization | 反义词：vernacularization
