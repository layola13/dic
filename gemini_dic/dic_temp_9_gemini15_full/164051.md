predisposes:/ˌpriːdɪˈspoʊz/ | vt. | 预先处置；使倾向于；使易患(疾病) | To make someone more likely to behave in a particular way or to suffer from a particular illness.  | 例句：His upbringing predisposed him to violence.  他的成长环境使他容易暴力。

例句：Genetic factors can predispose individuals to certain diseases.  遗传因素可能使个体易患某些疾病。


近义词：predetermine, incline, make susceptible,  render susceptible

反义词：protect, safeguard, immunize


词性:动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
His unhealthy lifestyle predisposes him to heart disease. = 他不健康的生活方式使他容易患心脏病。
The warm weather predisposes people to relax and enjoy themselves. = 温暖的天气使人们倾向于放松身心，享受生活。


一般过去时:
His childhood experiences predisposed him to trust others easily. = 他童年的经历使他容易轻信他人。
The doctor explained that a family history of diabetes predisposed her to the condition. = 医生解释说，家族糖尿病史使她易患这种疾病。


现在完成时:
Years of smoking have predisposed him to lung cancer. = 多年的吸烟使他易患肺癌。
Her genetic makeup has predisposed her to a higher risk of developing osteoporosis. = 她的基因构成使她患骨质疏松症的风险更高。


过去完成时:
His stressful job had predisposed him to anxiety disorders. = 他压力大的工作使他容易患焦虑症。
The lack of proper nutrition had predisposed the children to various illnesses. = 营养不良使孩子们容易患各种疾病。


将来时:
This new medication will predispose patients to fewer side effects. = 这款新药将使患者的副作用更少。
Climate change is likely to predispose coastal regions to more frequent flooding. = 气候变化可能会使沿海地区更容易发生洪涝灾害。


虚拟语气:
If he hadn't had that upbringing, he wouldn't have been predisposed to such anger. = 如果他没有那种成长环境，他就不会那么容易生气。
If she had a healthier diet, she might not be predisposed to type 2 diabetes. = 如果她有更健康的饮食，她可能不会容易患上2型糖尿病。


被动语态:
He was predisposed to depression by the loss of his job. = 他因失业而容易患上抑郁症。
Patients are predisposed to infection after surgery. = 患者术后容易感染。


疑问句:
Does a family history of heart disease predispose individuals to the condition? = 家族心脏病史是否会使个体易患这种疾病？
What factors predispose people to addiction? = 哪些因素使人容易上瘾？


否定句:
A healthy lifestyle does not predispose people to heart disease. = 健康的生活方式不会使人容易患心脏病。
Regular exercise does not predispose individuals to injuries. = 定期锻炼不会使个体容易受伤。
