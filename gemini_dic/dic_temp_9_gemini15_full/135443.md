nonpersuadable: /ˌnɒnpərˈsweɪdəbl/| adj. |不可劝说的，固执的 |  Unable to be persuaded or influenced; stubborn.  |  He was nonpersuadable, refusing to change his mind despite all our arguments. 他不可劝说，尽管我们费尽口舌也无法让他改变主意。 |近义词：unyielding, intractable, obstinate, stubborn | 反义词：persuadable, amenable, compliant

词源:
The word is formed from the prefix "non-" (meaning "not") + "persuadable" (meaning "able to be persuaded").  "Persuadable" comes from the verb "persuade," ultimately deriving from the Latin word "persuadere"  meaning "to persuade, convince."
