oscines:/ˈɒsɪnziːz/ | n. |鸣禽 | Oscines are a suborder of passerine birds characterized by a complex syrinx, enabling them to produce a wide variety of songs and calls. They represent the majority of passerine birds, encompassing a vast array of species with diverse ecological roles.  | Example:  Many species of oscines are known for their beautiful and complex songs. 许多鸣禽以其优美复杂的鸣叫声而闻名。  | 近义词: songbirds (less precise, as it's a common name) | 反义词:  suboscines (the other suborder of passerines, with simpler vocalizations) | 名词
