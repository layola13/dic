entrenching: /ɪnˈtrentʃɪŋ/ | v. | 巩固；确立；使…牢固 |  To establish something very firmly so that it is difficult to change.  This word often implies a negative connotation, suggesting that something is being made resistant to change, often unfairly or unjustly. |

例句：

The government's policies are entrenching inequality. = 政府的政策正在加剧不平等。

Years of entrenched prejudice make it difficult to achieve racial harmony. = 多年的根深蒂固的偏见使得实现种族和谐变得困难。


近义词：fortify, consolidate, strengthen, establish

反义词：undermine, weaken, destabilize


词性：动词

USAGE->
一般现在时:
They are entrenching their position in the market. = 他们在市场上巩固自己的地位。
The company is entrenching its power through aggressive marketing. = 公司通过积极的市场营销来巩固其权力。

一般过去时:
The dictator entrenched his power through fear and intimidation. = 独裁者通过恐惧和恐吓巩固了他的权力。
The old system entrenched itself over many years. = 旧制度多年来根深蒂固。


现在完成时:
They have entrenched themselves in the community. = 他们已经在社区扎根。
The new laws have entrenched the rights of workers. = 新法律巩固了工人的权利。


过去完成时:
The previous administration had entrenched the system's inefficiencies. = 前一届政府已经使该体系的低效率根深蒂固。
By then, the habit had entrenched itself deeply in his life. = 到那时，这个习惯已经深深地根植于他的生活中。


将来时:
The proposed changes will entrench the existing power structures. = 提出的变化将巩固现有的权力结构。
This new policy will entrench poverty in the region. = 这项新政策将使该地区的贫困更加严重。


虚拟语气:
If they had not entrenched their position, they would have lost. = 如果他们没有巩固自己的地位，他们就会失败。
If we allowed this to continue, it would entrench social injustice. = 如果我们允许这种情况继续下去，它将加剧社会不公。


被动语态:
The old ways were entrenched in the minds of the people. = 旧习俗根深蒂固于人们的头脑中。
Their ideas are being entrenched by the media. = 他们的观点正在被媒体所巩固。


疑问句:
Is the government entrenching its power? = 政府是否正在巩固其权力？
Are these policies entrenching inequality? = 这些政策是否加剧了不平等？


否定句:
They did not entrench their position effectively. = 他们没有有效地巩固自己的地位。
The reforms did not entrench the necessary changes. = 改革没有巩固必要的变化。


PHRASE->
entrench oneself = 巩固自己的地位
entrench a system = 确立一个制度
entrench privileges = 巩固特权
entrench oneself in power = 巩固权力
entrench in tradition = 固守传统


词源:

The word "entrench" comes from the French word "entrancher," which means "to cut into."  Originally, it referred to the digging of trenches for military defense. The meaning evolved to encompass the establishment of something firmly, like a trench that is difficult to overcome.
