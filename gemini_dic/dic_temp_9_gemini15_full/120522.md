minxish:/ˈmɪŋksɪʃ/ | adj. | 淘气的；轻浮的；放荡的 |  Suggestive of a minx; playfully mischievous or flirtatious; often used to describe a woman's behavior.  The word carries a slightly negative connotation, implying a degree of naughtiness or impudence. |  例句：Her minxish grin suggested she was up to something.  她的淘气笑容暗示她正在策划什么。

例句：The minxish behavior of the young girl caused her mother to scold her.  那个年轻女孩轻浮的行为导致她母亲责骂了她。


近义词：mischievous, flirtatious, impudent, saucy, cheeky, coquettish | 反义词：serious, demure, modest, proper, sedate


词源：Derived from "minx," a playful term for a mischievous or flirtatious young woman.  The suffix "-ish" adds a quality or characteristic to the word, making it an adjective.  The word "minx" itself originates from the Middle Dutch word "minx," meaning "a young girl," but has evolved to carry its modern connotations over time.
