idealize:/aɪˈdiːəlaɪz/| vt.|理想化；美化；将…视为理想|To represent or think of someone or something as being perfect or better than they really are.|He idealized his childhood, remembering only the good times. 他将他的童年理想化，只记得美好的时光。|glorify, romanticize,  perfect|denigrate, criticize,  devalue|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They idealize their relationship. = 他们将他们的关系理想化。
She idealizes her past. = 她将她的过去理想化。

一般过去时:
He idealized her beauty. = 他美化了她的美貌。
The artist idealized the figure in his painting. = 艺术家在他画中的人物形象进行了理想化处理。

现在完成时:
We have idealized this project for too long. = 我们对这个项目理想化的时间太长了。
She has idealized her childhood memories. = 她已经将童年记忆理想化了。

过去完成时:
They had idealized their chances of success. = 他们曾过分理想化了他们成功的几率。
The writer had idealized the hero of his novel. = 作家曾将小说中的主人公理想化。

将来时:
They will idealize the historical event in the movie. = 他们将在电影中将这一历史事件理想化。
She will idealize her future life. = 她将把未来的生活理想化。

虚拟语气:
If I were to idealize my life, I would live on a beach. = 如果我要将我的生活理想化，我会住在海滩上。
I wish I hadn't idealized him so much. = 我真希望我没有那么过分地把他理想化。

被动语态:
The situation was idealized in the report. = 报告中对情况进行了理想化处理。

疑问句:

否定句:
