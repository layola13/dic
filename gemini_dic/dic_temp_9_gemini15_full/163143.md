precommanded:/ˌpriːkəˈmændɪd/| adj. |预先命令的，预先指挥的 |Previously commanded or ordered;  already instructed.  This word implies that instructions were given in advance of an action or event. |例句：The troops were precommanded to advance at dawn. = 部队接到命令要在黎明时前进。

The general's precommanded strategy proved successful. = 将军的预先制定的策略证明是成功的。|近义词：preordained, prearranged, pre-determined|反义词：improvised, spontaneous, unplanned|词性：形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The plan is precommanded by the general. = 这个计划是由将军预先命令的。
The instructions are precommanded in the manual. = 说明书中预先规定了操作步骤。


一般过去时:
The attack was precommanded the night before. = 攻击是在前一天晚上预先命令的。
The evacuation was precommanded due to the impending storm. = 由于即将到来的风暴，疏散工作是预先命令的。


现在完成时:
The maneuvers have been precommanded for weeks. = 演习已经预先命令了数周。
The procedure has been precommanded by the higher authority. = 该程序已由上级预先命令。


过去完成时:
The mission had been precommanded before the team even assembled. = 小组甚至还没集合，任务就已经预先命令了。
The troops had been precommanded to await further instructions. = 部队已接到预先命令，等待进一步指示。


将来时:
The deployment will be precommanded by the command center. = 部署将由指挥中心预先命令。
The retreat will be precommanded if the situation worsens. = 如果情况恶化，撤退将预先命令。


虚拟语气:
If the operation had been precommanded effectively, the casualties would have been fewer. = 如果行动能够有效地预先命令，伤亡就会减少。
It is essential that the response be precommanded in case of emergency. = 紧急情况下，必须预先命令应对措施。


被动语态:
The soldiers were precommanded to remain silent. = 士兵们接到命令保持沉默。
The timetable was precommanded by the project manager. = 时间表是由项目经理预先制定的。


疑问句:
Was the mission precommanded before its commencement? = 任务是在开始之前就预先命令的吗？
Were the troops precommanded to occupy the building? = 部队接到命令要占领那座建筑物吗？


否定句:
The action was not precommanded; it was a spontaneous response. = 行动并非预先命令的；它是自发的反应。
The procedures were not precommanded, leading to confusion. = 程序没有预先命令，导致了混乱。
