gasteromycetes:/ˌɡæstəroʊˈmaɪsiːts/| n. |腹菌类，胃菌类|A group of fungi characterized by their enclosed spore-bearing structures (basidiocarps), which do not release spores actively but instead rely on external factors like decay or animal consumption for spore dispersal.  These fungi are often referred to as puffballs, earthstars, and stinkhorns. They are typically found in various habitats, including forests, grasslands, and even dung.|Example:  Many species of gasteromycetes are edible, while others are poisonous. 许多腹菌类物种是可食用的，而另一些是有毒的。|近义词：fungi (broad term)|反义词：None (no direct antonym)|
