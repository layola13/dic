euphoria:/juːˈfɔːriə/| n. |欣快感；狂喜；过度兴奋|A feeling of intense happiness and well-being, often to an excessive degree.|例句：The euphoria of winning the lottery quickly faded. 中彩票的欣喜若狂很快就消失了。|例句：She was in a state of euphoria after the birth of her child.  她孩子出生后处于欣快状态。|近义词：elation, ecstasy, bliss, exhilaration |反义词：depression, despair, misery, despondency


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The music induces euphoria. = 音乐带来欣快感。
Euphoria often follows a period of great stress. = 欣快感常常伴随一段极度压力的时期之后。


一般过去时:
The victory brought them euphoria. = 胜利给他们带来了欣快感。
She experienced euphoria after completing the marathon. = 完成马拉松后，她体验到了欣快感。


现在完成时:

He has been in a state of euphoria ever since he got the job. = 自从得到这份工作以来，他一直处于欣快状态。


过去完成时:

She had never felt such euphoria before that moment. = 在那一刻之前，她从未感受过如此的欣快。


将来时:

Winning the competition will fill him with euphoria. = 赢得比赛将会让他充满欣快感。


虚拟语气:
If I won the lottery, I would be in a state of euphoria. = 如果我中了彩票，我会处于欣快状态。
I wish I could experience the euphoria of falling in love again. = 我希望我能再次体验坠入爱河的欣快感。


被动语态:
Euphoria is often induced by drugs. = 欣快感常常是由药物引起的。
His success was met with euphoria by his family. = 他的成功受到了家人的欣喜庆祝。

否定句:
