ghazi:/ˈɡɑːziː/| n. |圣战者；为伊斯兰教而战的人|A ghazi is a Muslim warrior who fights in a holy war (jihad).  The term is historically associated with the Ottoman Empire and other Muslim societies.  Historically, Ghazi's were often granted land and privileges for their service.  The term is sometimes used in a broader sense to refer to any Muslim fighting for their faith.|例句：The Ottoman Empire employed many ghazis in its expansion.|近义词：mujahid, warrior, holy warrior|反义词：None specifically, but could be contrasted with terms implying pacifism or surrender.
