ploughshares:/ˈplaʊʃɛrz/ | n. |犁铧；和平的象征 |The metal points of a plough, used for turning the soil.  The term is also used metaphorically to represent the transformation of implements of war into tools of peace. |例句：The blacksmith forged ploughshares from the swords surrendered after the war.  铁匠用战争结束后缴获的剑打造了犁铧。

The farmer sharpened his ploughshares before beginning the spring planting. 农民在开始春季播种前磨快了他的犁铧。


近义词：shares (in this context), plowshares | 反义词：swords, weapons


词源:
Old English *plōh-scearu, from plōh "plough" + scearu "share".  The word "plough" itself has roots in Proto-Germanic *plūgōn, and ultimately Proto-Indo-European *pleu- "to plough".  The "share" element reflects the cutting blade of the plough.
