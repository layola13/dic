detrimentals:/ˌdɛtrɪˈmɛntlz/ | adj. |有害的，不利的 |  Harmful; tending to cause damage.  Detrimental describes something that causes harm or damage. It is often used to describe things that have a negative impact on something else, such as health, the environment, or society.  |  Smoking is detrimental to your health. 吸烟有害健康。  |  harmful, damaging, injurious, adverse | beneficial, advantageous, helpful, positive | 形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
These chemicals are detrimental to the environment. = 这些化学物质对环境有害。
This policy is detrimental to small businesses. = 这一政策对小型企业不利。


一般过去时:
The storm proved detrimental to the harvest. = 暴风雨对收成造成了损害。
His actions were detrimental to his reputation. = 他的行为损害了他的名誉。


现在完成时:
Years of neglect have been detrimental to the building's structure. = 多年的忽视损害了建筑物的结构。
His careless choices have been detrimental to his career. = 他粗心的选择损害了他的职业生涯。


过去完成时:
The previous government's policies had been detrimental to the economy. = 前政府的政策对经济造成了损害。
His earlier mistakes had been detrimental to his chances of success. = 他早期的错误损害了他成功的几率。


将来时:
The proposed changes will be detrimental to the company's profitability. = 拟议的变更将损害公司的盈利能力。
Continued pollution will be detrimental to the ecosystem. = 持续的污染将损害生态系统。


虚拟语气:
If we continue this way, it would be detrimental to our relationship. = 如果我们继续这样下去，这对我们的关系将有害。
I wish that his actions hadn't been so detrimental to the project. = 我希望他的行为没有对项目造成这么大的损害。


被动语态:
The project was deemed detrimental to the local community. = 该项目被认为对当地社区有害。
The environment is being detrimentally affected by pollution. = 环境正在受到污染的有害影响。


疑问句:
Are these chemicals detrimental to human health? = 这些化学物质对人体健康有害吗？
Would this decision be detrimental to the company's image? = 这个决定会损害公司的形象吗？


否定句:
The new policy is not detrimental to economic growth. = 新政策无损于经济增长。
The research showed that the drug was not detrimental to the patients' health. = 研究表明该药物对患者的健康无害。
