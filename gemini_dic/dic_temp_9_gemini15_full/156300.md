phylon:/ˈfaɪlɒn/| n. |支系，宗族；（生物的）进化谱系|A phylon is a branch or lineage in the evolutionary history of a group of organisms.  It represents a distinct evolutionary pathway and the shared ancestry of a group of related species.  The term is often used in phylogenetic analysis to describe the relationships between different taxa.|例句：The phylogenetic analysis revealed three distinct phyla of insects.  该系统发育分析揭示了三种不同的昆虫纲。|近义词：lineage, clade, branch|反义词：None specifically.  The antonym would depend on the context, perhaps "individual" or "species" if contrasting with the broader group represented by the phylon.
