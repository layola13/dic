misanthropes:/ˈmɪzænθroʊps/| n.|厌世者，遁世者|A person who dislikes humankind and avoids human society.|例句：He was considered a misanthrope because he lived alone in the woods and rarely interacted with others. 他被认为是一个厌世者，因为他独自一人住在森林里，很少与他人互动。  例句：Many misanthropes find solace in nature, away from the hustle and bustle of city life. 许多厌世者在远离城市喧嚣的自然中找到了慰藉。|近义词：cynic, recluse, hermit|反义词：philanthropist, altruist|名词


词源:
The word "misanthrope" comes from the Greek words "misos" (μίσος), meaning "hatred," and "anthropos" (ἄνθρωπος), meaning "human being" or "mankind."  Therefore, it literally translates to "hater of mankind."
