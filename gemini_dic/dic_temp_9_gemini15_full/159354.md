poize:/pɔɪz/ | n. v. | 平衡；优雅；沉着；使平衡；使处于适当位置 |  poise refers to a state of balance, equilibrium, or composure.  It can also describe a graceful and elegant manner. As a verb, it means to balance or put something in a proper position. |

例句：

She possessed a remarkable poise even under pressure.  = 她即使在压力下也保持着非凡的镇定。

The dancer held her pose with perfect poise. = 舞者以完美的平衡保持着她的姿势。


近义词：balance, equilibrium, composure, grace, elegance

反义词：clumsiness, awkwardness, imbalance, agitation


词性：名词，动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The statue poises gracefully on the pedestal. = 雕像优雅地伫立在基座上。
She poises herself before speaking. = 她发言前稳住自己。

一般过去时:
He poised the pen above the paper. = 他把笔悬在纸上方。

现在完成时:
She has poised herself for a difficult negotiation. = 她已准备好进行艰难的谈判。
The company has poised itself for expansion into new markets. = 公司已准备好向新市场扩张。

过去完成时:

The athlete had poised her body for the jump. = 运动员已准备好跳跃。

将来时:

The team will poise itself for the upcoming competition. = 该团队将为即将到来的比赛做好准备。

虚拟语气:
If I had more poise, I would be a better public speaker. = 如果我更有风度，我会成为更好的公众演讲者。
I wish I could poise myself as effortlessly as she does. = 我希望我能像她一样轻松地保持镇定。

被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
