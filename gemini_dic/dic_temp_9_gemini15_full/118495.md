mesmerizations:/ˌmɛzməˈreɪʃənz/ | n. |催眠状态；着迷；迷惑 |The act or process of mesmerizing; the state of being mesmerized.  It refers to the captivating power of something, often leading to a trance-like state or intense fascination.  |例句：The mesmerizing beauty of the sunset left her speechless.  日落迷人的景色让她说不出话来。

The speaker's mesmerizing voice held the audience captive. 演讲者迷人的声音吸引住了听众。


近义词：hypnotizations, fascinations, enchantments, captivation |反义词：boredom, disinterest, apathy |词性：名词


词源:  Derived from "mesmerize," which in turn comes from Franz Mesmer (1734-1815), an Austrian physician who developed a theory of "animal magnetism," a type of therapy involving the manipulation of magnetic forces to cure illnesses.  His methods involved inducing a trance-like state in patients, which was later studied and refined as hypnotism.  The suffix "-ation" indicates the process or state of being mesmerized.
