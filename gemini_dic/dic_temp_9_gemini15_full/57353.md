dowser:/ˈdaʊzər/| n.| 占卜者；探水者 | A person who practices dowsing, using a divining rod or other tools to locate underground water, minerals, or other substances.  | Example: The dowser claimed he could find water anywhere using his forked stick.  探水者声称他可以用他的Y形树枝在任何地方找到水。 |近义词：water witch, diviner | 反义词：geologist (in the context of scientific water location)


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The dowser uses a pendulum to find water. = 占卜者用摆来寻找水。
He dowses for minerals in the mountains. = 他在山上探测矿物。


一般过去时:
The dowser found a spring using his rod. = 占卜者用他的探测棒找到一个泉水。
They dowsed for gold but found nothing. = 他们探测黄金但什么也没找到。


现在完成时:
The dowser has located several underground streams. = 占卜者已经找到了几条地下溪流。
She has dowsed for oil in this region for years. = 她多年来一直在该地区探测石油。


过去完成时:
The farmer had dowsed the land before drilling the well. = 农夫在打井之前已经探测过这块土地。
They had dowsed unsuccessfully for months before they gave up. = 他们在放弃之前已经无功地探测了几个月。


将来时:

虚拟语气:
