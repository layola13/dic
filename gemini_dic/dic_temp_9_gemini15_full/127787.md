neighbour:/ˈneɪbər/ (US) /ˈneɪbə(r)/ (UK)| n.|邻居|A person living near another.|例句：Our neighbours are very friendly. 我们的邻居们非常友好。
例句：We often borrow sugar from our next-door neighbour. 我们经常向隔壁邻居借糖。|近义词：neighbor (US spelling), resident, dweller|反义词：stranger, foreigner|名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
My neighbour often waters my plants when I'm away. = 我不在家的时候，我的邻居经常帮我浇花。
The neighbours have a dog that barks all night. = 邻居家养了一条狗，整夜叫个不停。


一般过去时:
My neighbour helped me move my furniture last weekend. = 上周末我的邻居帮我搬家具。
The neighbours complained about the noise from the party. = 邻居们抱怨派对的噪音。


现在完成时:
We've known our neighbours for five years. = 我们认识邻居五年了。
The neighbours have just bought a new car. = 邻居们刚买了一辆新车。


过去完成时:

将来时:

虚拟语气:
If I had a neighbour like that, I would be very lucky. = 如果我有一个这样的邻居，我会很幸运。
I wish my neighbours were quieter. = 我希望我的邻居们安静一点。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:

词源:
The word "neighbour" comes from the Old English word "neahgebur," which is a compound of "neah" (near) and "gebur" (dweller, inhabitant, farmer).  The word's evolution reflects the close proximity and often shared agricultural context of early settlements.
