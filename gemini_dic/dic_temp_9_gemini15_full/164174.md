predistress:/ˌpriːdɪˈstrɛs/| n.| 预先压力，先期压力 |  Stress or strain experienced before a major event or challenge.  It refers to the anticipation of a stressful situation and the resulting anxiety or tension. |例句：The predistress of the upcoming exam kept her awake all night.  即将到来的考试让她整晚都睡不着觉。The predistress associated with public speaking is common among many people.  许多人都有在公开场合讲话前的压力。|近义词：anxiety, apprehension, worry, tension |反义词：calmness, serenity, peace of mind |名词
