chickadees:/ˈtʃɪkeɪdiːz/| n. |山雀（复数）|Small, active North American songbirds of the genus *Poecile*, known for their distinctive calls and acrobatic foraging behavior.  They are often seen hanging upside down from branches while searching for food.| 例句：The chickadees flitted through the branches, searching for insects.  山雀在树枝间飞舞，寻找昆虫。

例句：We saw a flock of chickadees feeding on sunflower seeds in the bird feeder. 我们看到一群山雀在喂食器里吃葵花籽。


近义词：titmice (a broader term encompassing various small birds)


反义词：无


词性：名词(复数)


词源: The name "chickadee" is an onomatopoeia, derived from the bird's characteristic call, a series of high-pitched, repeated "chick-a-dee" sounds.  The word's origins are believed to be from the Algonquian languages of North America.
