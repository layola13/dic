indignantly:/ɪnˈdɪɡnəntli/ | adv. | 愤怒地；愤慨地 | Expressing strong displeasure.  |例句：He indignantly denied the accusations. 他愤怒地否认了这些指控。

She responded indignantly to the unfair criticism. 她对不公平的批评愤怒地回应。|近义词：angrily, resentfully, wrathfully |反义词：calmly, placidly, serenely


词源:

The word "indignantly" is derived from the adjective "indignant," which comes from the Latin word "indignari," meaning "to feel indignation" or "to be angry."  The suffix "-ly" transforms the adjective into an adverb, indicating the manner in which an action is performed.


用法说明:

"Indignantly" is an adverb that modifies a verb, describing how an action is performed. It conveys a strong feeling of anger and resentment, often in response to something perceived as unjust or insulting.  The action described is usually a reaction to the perceived offense.
