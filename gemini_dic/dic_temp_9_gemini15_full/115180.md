manichaeism:/ˈmænɪkiːɪzəm/| n. |摩尼教|A dualistic religious system founded by Mani in Persia in the 3rd century AD, characterized by a belief in two opposing cosmic principles, good and evil, light and darkness, and a complex system of salvation through spiritual enlightenment and ascetic practices.  It spread widely throughout the Roman Empire and Asia before declining in influence.|The spread of Manichaeism in the Roman Empire is a fascinating study in religious syncretism.|近义词：Gnosticism (部分方面)|反义词：无|名词
