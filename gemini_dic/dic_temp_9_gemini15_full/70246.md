fictionalization:/ˌfɪkʃənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/| n. |虚构化；小说化；改编成小说|The process of adapting a true story or event into a fictional narrative.  It involves selecting, arranging, and transforming factual elements to create a fictional world and characters. This often involves adding invented details, changing character names or attributes, and altering the sequence or outcome of events.  |Example: The fictionalization of the historical event resulted in a gripping novel.|近义词：dramatization, novelization|反义词：factualization, documentation|名词
