righty-ho:/ˈraɪtihoʊ/| int.| 好吧；行；知道了 | An informal British English interjection expressing agreement, acceptance, or understanding.  It's a shortened and slightly altered version of "right, ho!".  "Right" indicates agreement, while "ho" is an archaic word of affirmation or encouragement. | Example: "Righto, let's get started."  = 好吧，让我们开始吧。  "Righty-ho, I'll do that." = 行，我会做的。 |近义词:okay, alright, all right, yes, fine |反义词:no, nope


USAGE->
一般现在时:
Righto, I understand. = 好吧，我明白了。
Righty-ho, we'll continue. = 好吧，我们继续。


一般过去时:
He said righty-ho and left. = 他说“好吧”然后离开了。
She replied righty-ho to my suggestion. = 她对我的建议回应“好吧”。

We've finished, righty-ho! = 我们完成了，好！
