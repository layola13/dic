predicated:/ˈprɛdɪkeɪtɪd/| adj.|基于…的；以…为前提的；断言的|Based on or presupposed; affirmed or asserted.|The theory is predicated on the assumption that people are rational actors.  这个理论是以人们是理性行为者为前提的。|His success was predicated on hard work and dedication.  他的成功是基于勤奋和奉献的。|based on, founded on, grounded in|unfounded, baseless|形容词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The argument is predicated on false information. = 这个论点是基于虚假信息之上的。
His decision is predicated on his family's needs. = 他的决定是以家人的需求为前提的。

一般过去时:
The study was predicated on previous research. = 这项研究是以之前的研究为基础的。
Her opinion was predicated on limited evidence. = 她的观点是基于有限证据的。

现在完成时:
The success of the project has been predicated on teamwork. = 项目的成功一直是以团队合作的为前提的。
His recovery has been predicated on consistent treatment. = 他的康复一直以来都依赖于持续的治疗。


过去完成时:
The decision had been predicated on incomplete data. = 这一决定是基于不完整的数据做出的。
The plan had been predicated on the assumption of good weather. = 该计划是基于好天气假设而制定的。


将来时:
The success of the mission will be predicated on careful planning. = 任务的成功将取决于周密的计划。
The outcome of the election will be predicated on voter turnout. = 选举的结果将取决于选民的投票率。


虚拟语气:
If the theory were predicated on sound evidence, it would be more convincing. = 如果这个理论是基于可靠的证据，它会更有说服力。
If the project were predicated on a realistic budget, it would be more feasible. = 如果这个项目是基于一个现实的预算，它会更可行。


被动语态:
The decision was predicated on factors beyond our control. = 这一决定是由我们无法控制的因素决定的。
The argument is predicated on assumptions that are difficult to verify. = 这一论点基于难以验证的假设。


疑问句:
Is the argument predicated on sound reasoning? = 这一论点是基于合理的推理吗？
Is this conclusion predicated on reliable data? = 这个结论是基于可靠的数据吗？


否定句:
The theory is not predicated on empirical evidence. = 这个理论并非基于实证证据。
His actions were not predicated on any logical reasoning. = 他的行为没有任何逻辑推理做基础。



PHRASE->
be predicated on = 基于...
predicated upon = 以...为基础
predicated by = 由...决定
predicated with = 用...为前提
predicated in = 在...中得到论证


词源:
From the verb *predicate*, meaning "to affirm or declare," ultimately from the Latin *praedicāre*, meaning "to proclaim, preach."  The adjective form *predicated* reflects the sense of something being based on or established by a statement or assertion.
