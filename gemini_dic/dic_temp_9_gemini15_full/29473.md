catanadromous: /ˌkætənˈædrəməs/ | adj. | 溯河洄游的；降河洄游的 |  Referring to fish that migrate from freshwater to saltwater to breed, and then return to freshwater.  This is in contrast to anadromous fish, which migrate from saltwater to freshwater to breed. | Example:  Eels are a well-known example of catadromous fish.  鳗鱼是溯河洄游鱼类的著名例子。 | 近义词:  (None perfectly synonymous, but)  migratory, downstream-spawning | 反义词: anadromous


Etymology:  The word "catadromous" is derived from the Greek words "kata" (down) and "dromos" (running or course).  This reflects the fish's movement from freshwater (upstream) to saltwater (downstream) for reproduction.
