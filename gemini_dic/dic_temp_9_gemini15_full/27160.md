call-up: /ˈkɔːl ʌp/ | n., vt. |召集；征召；打电话；回忆；使想起 |

英文解析:As a noun, "call-up" refers to an act of summoning someone, especially for military service or to a meeting.  As a verb, it means to summon someone, to telephone someone, or to bring something back to mind.

例句:

n. (名词)
The army issued a call-up for reservists.  军队向预备役人员发出了征召令。

The call-up for the meeting was sent out last week.  会议的通知上周就发出去了。


vt. (动词)
They called up reinforcements. 他们召集了增援部队。

I'll call you up later this evening. 我今晚晚些时候给你打电话。

He called up the memory of his childhood. 他回忆起童年的记忆。


近义词: (Noun) summons, mobilization, draft; (Verb) summon, telephone, phone, recall, evoke

反义词: (Noun) dismissal, discharge; (Verb) dismiss, forget


词性:Noun, Verb


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company calls up its employees for a meeting every Monday. 公司每周一都会召集员工开会。
He calls up his mother every Sunday. 他每周日都会给他妈妈打电话。


一般过去时:
The government called up reserves during the war. 战争期间，政府征召了预备役人员。
She called up her friend to share the good news. 她打电话给她的朋友分享好消息。


现在完成时:
They have called up all the available troops. 他们已经召集了所有可以调动的部队。
I have called up my insurance company to report the accident. 我已经打电话给我的保险公司报案了。


过去完成时:
The general had called up all his men before the battle. 将军在战斗开始前已经召集了他的所有士兵。
She had called up her sister before she left for the trip. 出发旅行之前，她已经给她姐姐打过电话了。


将来时:
The company will call up its shareholders for a meeting next month. 公司下个月将召集股东开会。
He will call up his doctor to schedule an appointment. 他将打电话给他的医生预约。


虚拟语气:
If they called up the reserves, we would have enough soldiers. 如果他们征召预备役人员，我们就会有足够的士兵。
If I called up my boss, I would get into trouble. 如果我打电话给我老板，我会惹上麻烦。


被动语态:
The troops were called up at dawn. 部队在黎明时分被召集起来。
He was called up by his friend about the project. 他接到朋友关于项目的电话。


疑问句:

否定句:
