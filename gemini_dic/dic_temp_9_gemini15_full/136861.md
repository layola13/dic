nonskeletal: /ˌnɒnˈskɛlɪtəl/ | adj. |非骨骼的；非骨架的 | Not relating to or involving the skeleton.  This term is primarily used in biological and anatomical contexts to describe structures, tissues, or processes that are not part of the skeletal system.  | Example: The nonskeletal muscles are responsible for movement of the limbs and other body parts.  非骨骼肌负责肢体和其他身体部位的运动。 |近义词: extraskeletal,  askeletal (less common) | 反义词: skeletal

词源:

The word "nonskeletal" is a combination of the prefix "non-" (meaning "not") and the adjective "skeletal" (relating to the skeleton).  "Skeletal" derives from "skeleton," ultimately from Greek  σκελετός (skeletos) meaning "dried up," referring to the dried bones of a corpse.
