schwenkfelder:/ˈʃvɛŋkfɛldər/| n. |施文克费尔德教派信徒；施文克费尔德派教徒|A member of a Protestant sect founded in Silesia in the 16th century.  Schwenkfelders are known for their emphasis on the importance of personal experience and direct communion with God.  They rejected the traditional sacraments and emphasized the importance of studying the Bible. They are a relatively small group with communities primarily in the United States.|The Schwenkfelders are known for their rich history and strong community ties.|近义词：Protestant, dissenter|反义词：Catholic (in the context of religious affiliation)
