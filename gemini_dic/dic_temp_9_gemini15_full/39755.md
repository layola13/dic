consoled:/kənˈsoʊld/| vt.|安慰，慰藉|to comfort someone who is unhappy or disappointed|例句：Her friend consoled her after she failed the exam.  她考试不及格后，朋友安慰了她。|例句：The mother consoled her crying baby. 母亲安慰着她哭泣的婴儿。|近义词：comfort, soothe, solace|反义词：distress, upset, agitate|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He consoles his friend every day. = 他每天都安慰他的朋友。
She consoles her children when they are sad. = 孩子们伤心的时候，她会安慰他们。

一般过去时:
My mother consoled me when I was feeling down. = 我情绪低落时，妈妈安慰了我。
He consoled his wife after she lost her job. = 他妻子失业后，他安慰了她。

现在完成时:
I have consoled many people in my life. = 我一生中安慰过很多人。
She has consoled her friend through many difficult times. = 她多次在朋友困难的时候安慰她。

过去完成时:
He had consoled her before she went on stage. = 在她上台之前，他已经安慰了她。
They had consoled each other after the accident. = 事故发生后，他们互相安慰。


将来时:
I will console her when she hears the bad news. = 当她听到坏消息时，我会安慰她。
He will console his friend when he fails the test. = 他考试不及格时，他会安慰他的朋友。


被动语态:
She was consoled by her family after the loss. = 她失去亲人后受到了家人的安慰。
The grieving widow was consoled by her neighbors. = 悲伤的寡妇受到了邻居们的安慰。


疑问句:
Did anyone console him after the accident? = 事故后有人安慰他吗？
Will you console her if she’s upset? = 如果她不高兴，你会安慰她吗？


否定句:
He didn't console her, which made her feel worse. = 他没有安慰她，这让她感觉更糟了。
She couldn't console herself after the heartbreak. = 心碎后她无法自我安慰。


PHRASE->
