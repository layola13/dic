go out:/ɡoʊ aʊt/ | /ɡəʊ aʊt/ | v. |出去，外出；熄灭；出版；约会；停止运作|To leave a place, especially one's home, to go somewhere else; to cease functioning; to be published; to have a romantic date; |例句：I'm going out for a walk. = 我要去散步。

例句：The lights went out suddenly. = 灯突然熄灭了。


近义词：leave, exit, depart, extinguish, publish, date|反义词：come in, stay in, enter


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I go out every evening. = 我每天晚上都出去。
They go out to dinner on Fridays. = 他们星期五晚上出去吃饭。


一般过去时:
We went out to a party last night. = 我们昨晚去参加了一个派对。
She went out with her friends. = 她和朋友们出去了。


现在完成时:
I have gone out several times this week. = 我本周已经出去好几次了。
They haven't gone out since last week. = 从上周起他们就没出去了。


过去完成时:
I had gone out before he arrived. = 他到的时候我已经出去了。
They had gone out for hours before they returned. = 他们出去好几个小时后才回来。


将来时:
I will go out tomorrow. = 我明天要出去。
We will go out to celebrate. = 我们将出去庆祝。


虚拟语气:
If I had time, I would go out tonight. = 如果我有时间，我今晚就会出去。
I wish I could go out more often. = 我希望我能更经常地出去。


被动语态:
(Not applicable in this context.  "Go out" is typically intransitive.)


疑问句:
Are you going out tonight? = 你今晚要出去吗？
Where did they go out last night? = 他们昨晚去哪儿了？


否定句:
I didn't go out last night. = 我昨晚没有出去。
They don't go out very often. = 他们不经常出去。


PHRASE->
go out of business = 倒闭
go out with = 与...约会
go out on a limb = 冒险
go out of one's way = 特地；费力
go out for = 为...而出战 (比赛等)
