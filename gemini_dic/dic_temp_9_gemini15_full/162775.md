preblockades:/ˌpriːˈblɒkeɪdz/| n. | 事先封锁；预先封锁 |  The term "preblockades" refers to actions taken to prevent or hinder something before it happens, often involving the blocking or obstruction of access or movement. It is a relatively uncommon word, primarily used in specialized contexts like political science, military strategy, or public health.  | Example: The government implemented preblockades to prevent the spread of the virus across borders.  政府实施了事前封锁以防止病毒跨境传播。 |近义词: preventative measures, preemptive strikes, embargoes | 反义词:  post-event responses, reactive measures |名词
