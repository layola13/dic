carer:/ˈkeərər/| n.|照料者；护理人员|A person who looks after a child, an elderly person, or a sick person.  It can refer to a professional caregiver or a family member.|例句：She works as a carer for the elderly. 她是一名老年护理人员。|例句：My sister is a carer for our mother. 我的姐姐负责照顾我们的母亲。|近义词：caregiver, attendant, nurse (in some contexts)|反义词：patient (in the sense of the one being cared for)


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The carer helps the patient with their medication. = 照料者帮助病人服药。
She cares for her elderly parents. = 她照顾她年迈的父母。

一般过去时:
He cared for his ailing wife until her death. = 他照顾他生病的妻子直到她去世。
The carer assisted the patient with their bath. = 照料者协助病人洗澡。

现在完成时:
She has cared for her grandmother for five years. = 她已经照顾她祖母五年了。
The family has hired a carer to help with daily tasks. = 这个家庭雇佣了一名护理人员来帮忙处理日常事务。

过去完成时:
The patient had been cared for by several carers before. = 这名病人之前曾由多名护理人员照顾过。
They had already arranged for a carer to visit. = 他们已经安排了一名护理人员来访。

将来时:
He will become a carer for his aging father. = 他将成为他年迈父亲的护理人员。
We will need to find a carer for our child. = 我们需要为我们的孩子找一名护理人员。

虚拟语气:
If I were a carer, I would be more patient. = 如果我是护理人员，我会更有耐心。
I wish I could be a carer for my friend. = 我希望我能成为我朋友的护理人员。

被动语态:

疑问句:
