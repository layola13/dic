preorganizing: /ˌpriːˈɔːrɡənaɪzɪŋ/ | v. |预先组织；预先安排 | The act of organizing something in advance.  It involves planning and preparing before the actual execution of a task or event. This could include gathering resources, outlining steps, assigning roles, and setting a timeline. | Example: Preorganizing the conference materials saved us a lot of time and stress on the day of the event. 预先整理会议资料为我们在活动当天节省了很多时间和压力。| 近义词：planning, preparing, arranging | 反义词：improvising, winging it | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They preorganize their work schedule every Sunday. = 他们每周日都会预先安排工作计划。
He preorganizes his thoughts before writing an essay. = 他在写论文之前会预先组织好自己的思路。

一般过去时:
She preorganized the birthday party for weeks. = 她提前几周就为生日派对做好了准备。

过去完成时:
