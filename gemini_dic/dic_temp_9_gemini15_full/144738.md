over-annotating: /ˌoʊvərˈænəˌteɪtɪŋ/ | v. | 过度标注 | The act of adding too many annotations or tags to something, often to the point of hindering understanding or obscuring the original meaning.  This is common in fields like natural language processing and data annotation where excessive labeling can be detrimental to the task. | Example: Over-annotating the dataset led to confusion and decreased the accuracy of the model.  The researchers realized they had over-annotated the images, making it difficult to extract meaningful features. | 近义词: overlabeling, over-tagging | 反义词: under-annotating
