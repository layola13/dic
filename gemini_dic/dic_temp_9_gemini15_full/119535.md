microscopists:/ˌmaɪkrəˈskɒpɪsts/ | n. | 显微镜学家 | A microscopist is a scientist who uses a microscope to study very small things, such as cells, microorganisms, or minerals. They may specialize in different areas like microbiology, histology, or materials science, applying various microscopy techniques to their research.  | Example: Experienced microscopists are needed for this research project requiring advanced microscopic techniques.  经验丰富的显微镜学家是这个需要先进显微镜技术的科研项目所需要的。 |近义词：microscopic researcher, biologist (depending on specialization) |反义词：macroscopic observer (someone studying large-scale phenomena)
