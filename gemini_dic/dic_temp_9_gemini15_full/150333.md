panic: /ˈpænɪk/ | n., v. | 恐慌，惊慌；使恐慌 |

英文解析:A sudden feeling of great fear and anxiety that causes wildly unthinking behavior.  It can refer to both the feeling itself and the behavior it causes. As a verb, it means to suddenly feel or cause to feel great fear and anxiety.

例句:
The unexpected loud noise caused a panic among the children. = 突如其来的巨响使孩子们惊慌失措。

The news of the fire caused widespread panic. = 火灾的消息引起了广泛的恐慌。


近义词: terror, fright, alarm, fear, hysteria

反义词: calmness, composure, tranquility

否定句:

词源:  The word "panic" comes from the Greek god Pan, a deity associated with the wild, shepherds, and flocks.  In Greek mythology, Pan was believed to be able to inspire sudden, overwhelming fear, a phenomenon later called "panicked."  The word entered English in the 16th century.
