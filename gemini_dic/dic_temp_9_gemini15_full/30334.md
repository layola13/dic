censuring: /ˈsɛnʃərɪŋ/ | v. | 责备；谴责；批评 | The act of expressing strong disapproval of someone or something, typically in a formal or official way.  |例句：The committee censured the member for misconduct. 委员会因行为不端而谴责该成员。

例句：The newspaper article censured the government's handling of the crisis. 报纸文章谴责政府处理危机的办法。


近义词：reprimanding, rebuking, condemning, criticizing, reproving | 反义词：praising, commending, approving | 词性：动词

一般现在时:
The council censures unethical behavior. = 理事会谴责不道德的行为。
She frequently censures her children's bad habits. = 她经常责备孩子们的坏习惯。


一般过去时:
The judge censured the lawyer for his unprofessional conduct. = 法官因律师的不专业行为而谴责他。
The committee censured the report for its inaccuracies. = 委员会因报告不准确而谴责它。


现在完成时:
The organization has censured several members for violating its code of conduct. = 该组织因违反行为准则而谴责了几名成员。
The media has censured the politician's misleading statements. = 媒体谴责了这位政治家的误导性言论。


过去完成时:
The board had already censured him before the scandal broke. = 丑闻爆发前，董事会已经谴责了他。
They had censured the article for its biased reporting. = 他们因文章报道有偏见而谴责了它。


将来时:
The university will censure the student for plagiarism. = 大学将因剽窃而谴责该学生。
The senate is likely to censure the senator for his actions. = 参议院很可能会因其行为而谴责这位参议员。


虚拟语气:
If the evidence were stronger, they would censure him more harshly. = 如果证据更充分，他们会更严厉地谴责他。
I wish they had censured the politician for his lies. = 我希望他们因他的谎言而谴责这位政治家。


被动语态:
The professor was censured for his insensitive remarks. = 教授因其不敏感的言论而受到谴责。
The book was censured by the religious authorities. = 这本书被宗教当局谴责。


疑问句:
Will the company censure the employee for his mistake? = 公司会因员工的错误而谴责他吗？
Has the government been censured for its policies? = 政府因其政策而受到谴责了吗？


否定句:
The school did not censure the student despite his behavior. = 尽管学生的表现不好，学校也没有谴责他。
They refused to censure the article, despite its controversial claims. = 尽管文章的主张有争议，他们还是拒绝谴责它。


PHRASE->
censure someone for something = 因某事谴责某人
a vote of censure = 谴责投票

under censure = 受到谴责
official censure = 正式谴责


词源:  From Old French *censurer, from Latin censura "judgment, assessment," from censere "to assess, judge."  The original meaning related to official assessment and judgment, particularly in Roman times.
