nonskeptic:/ˈnɑːnˌskeptɪk/| adj.|非怀疑论者；相信的|A person who is not skeptical; someone who readily accepts things as true without questioning.  This word is relatively uncommon and often used in academic or philosophical contexts. It emphasizes the lack of skepticism, suggesting a tendency towards belief or acceptance. | Example:  The nonskeptic readily embraced the new theory without critical examination.  非怀疑论者很容易接受新理论，而不加批判性检验。|近义词：credulous, gullible, believing|反义词：skeptical, doubting, incredulous
