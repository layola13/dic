overexpanding: /ˌoʊvərɪkˈspændɪŋ/ | v. |过度扩张；膨胀过度 |  The act of expanding beyond reasonable or sustainable limits. This often refers to businesses, economies, or organizations growing too quickly and potentially unsustainably. It can also refer to the physical expansion of something beyond its capacity. |

例句：

The company's overexpanding into new markets led to financial difficulties. =  公司的过度扩张导致了财务困难。

The balloon overexpanded and burst. = 气球过度膨胀然后爆了。


近义词：overextension, expansionism, overgrowth


反义词：contraction, downsizing, retrenchment


词性：动词

词源:

The word "overexpanding" is a combination of the prefix "over-" (meaning "too much" or "excessively") and the verb "expanding" (from the verb "expand," meaning "to become or make larger").  The root of "expand" comes from the Latin word "expandere," which means "to spread out."
