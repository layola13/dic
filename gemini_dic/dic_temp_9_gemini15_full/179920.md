refederated: /ˌriːˈfɛdəreɪtɪd/ | adj. |  重新联合的；重新联邦化的 |  Having been united or federated again; pertaining to a renewed federation. This word describes a situation where previously separate entities have come together again under a federal system.  It implies a prior separation followed by reunification. | The refederated states formed a new alliance.  重新联合的各州组成了一个新的联盟。|近义词：reunited, recombined, reunified | 反义词：separated, disunited, fragmented | 形容词

词源:  The word "refederated" is formed from the prefix "re-" (meaning "again") and the word "federated," which comes from "federation," ultimately derived from the Latin word "foedus," meaning "treaty" or "league."  Therefore, "refederated" literally means "formed into a federation again."
