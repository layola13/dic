mahayanas:/ˌmɑːhɑːˈjɑːnəz/| n. |大乘佛教;大乘佛教教义|Mahayana Buddhism is a major branch of Buddhism that emphasizes the Bodhisattva path and the pursuit of enlightenment for the benefit of all beings.  It contrasts with Theravada Buddhism, which focuses more on individual enlightenment.|The teachings of Mahayana Buddhism have spread widely across Asia.|近义词：Buddhism (broadly)|反义词：Theravada Buddhism


USAGE->
This word is a noun and doesn't conjugate like verbs.  Therefore, typical verb tenses are not applicable.  Instead, we will look at how it's used in different sentence structures.

Examples using "Mahayana":

Singular Subject:
Mahayana Buddhism is a complex and diverse tradition. = 大乘佛教是一个复杂而多元的传统。

Plural Subject (using the plural form "Mahayana schools"):
Several Mahayana schools have different interpretations of key scriptures. = 几个大乘佛教派别对关键经文有不同的解释。


Prepositional Phrases:
The study of Mahayana philosophy is rewarding. = 研究大乘佛教哲学是很有意义的。

Within a Relative Clause:
The monk who follows the Mahayana path strives for enlightenment for all. = 遵循大乘佛教道路的僧侣为所有众生的解脱而努力。

Comparative Structure:
Mahayana Buddhism differs significantly from Theravada Buddhism in its approach to enlightenment. = 大乘佛教在成佛的途径上与小乘佛教有显著的不同。
