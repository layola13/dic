lententide: /ˈlɛntənˌtaɪd/ | n. |大斋期 |The period of penitence and fasting in the Christian liturgical year, beginning on Ash Wednesday and lasting until Easter.  It is a time of spiritual reflection and preparation for Easter.  The word is a compound of "Lent" and "tide," with "tide" meaning "season" or "time." | Example:  Many Christians observe Lententide with prayer, fasting, and acts of charity.  许多基督徒通过祈祷、禁食和慈善行为来遵守大斋期。|近义词：Lent, Holy Week (部分重合)|反义词：Easter (代表结束)|名词
