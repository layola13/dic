gadzookery:/ɡædˈzuːkəri/| n. |喧闹；混乱；胡闹；令人震惊的景象|A state of wild excitement, uproar, or disorder; a chaotic or nonsensical situation.  It often implies a somewhat comical or absurd aspect to the chaos.|例句：The gadzookery in the marketplace after the football match was unbelievable.  足球赛后市场上的喧闹简直令人难以置信。

例句：The children's gadzookery during the party was exhausting but ultimately harmless.  孩子们在派对上的胡闹很累人，但最终是无害的。


近义词：commotion, pandemonium, mayhem, tumult, hullabaloo

反义词：calm, peace, quiet, order, tranquility


词源:The word is a playful, whimsical coinage, likely influenced by  "gadzooks!" (an exclamation of surprise or astonishment, itself possibly a corruption of "God's hooks!"). The suffix "-ery" denotes a state or condition.  Therefore, "gadzookery" creates a humorous noun describing a state of chaotic excitement.
