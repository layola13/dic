parades:/pəˈreɪdz/| n. |游行，检阅 |A public procession, usually celebrating something.|例句：The St. Patrick's Day parade is always a big event.  圣帕特里克节游行总是盛大的活动。

The military parade was impressive.  这次阅兵式令人印象深刻。|近义词：procession, march, demonstration |反义词：stillness, quiet


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The town parades every year. = 这个小镇每年都举行游行。
People parade through the streets. = 人们在街上游行。

一般过去时:
They paraded down Main Street. = 他们沿着主要街道游行。
We paraded our new car. = 我们炫耀我们的新车。

现在完成时:
They have paraded their support for the cause. = 他们已经公开表示支持这项事业。
The school has paraded many successful graduates. = 这所学校培养出许多成功的毕业生。

过去完成时:
They had paraded their achievements before the judges. = 他们曾在评委面前展示过他们的成就。
The team had paraded their victory through the city. = 球队曾在城市里游行庆祝胜利。

将来时:
We will parade our new uniforms next week. = 我们下周将展示我们的新制服。
The band will parade through the town square. = 乐队将穿过市中心广场游行。

虚拟语气:
If we won the competition, we would parade our trophy. = 如果我们赢得比赛，我们将展示我们的奖杯。
I wish I could parade my skills in front of a large audience. = 我希望我能在广大观众面前展示我的技能。

被动语态:
The winning team was paraded through the streets. = 获胜的球队在街上游行。
The new products were paraded at the trade show. = 新产品在贸易展上展出。

疑问句:
Will they parade this year? = 他们今年会游行吗？
What will be paraded at the festival? = 节日上将展示什么？

否定句:
They didn't parade their wealth. = 他们没有炫耀他们的财富。
The soldiers did not parade in the rain. = 士兵们没有在雨中游行。
