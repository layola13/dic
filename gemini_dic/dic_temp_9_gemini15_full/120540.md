mirabile:/mɪˈræbɪləri/| n.|令人惊奇的事物；奇迹；令人惊叹的事物|Something that causes wonder or astonishment; a marvel; a wonder.|例句：The Grand Canyon is a mirabile of nature.  大峡谷是大自然的奇迹。|近义词：wonder, marvel, miracle, prodigy|反义词：ordinariness, commonplace


USAGE->
This word is a noun and does not conjugate like verbs.  Therefore, tense considerations are not applicable.  The usage is primarily descriptive.


例句1: The magician's performance was a true mirabile.  魔术师的表演真是令人惊叹。
例句2: The intricate design of the clock was a mirabile of engineering.  这个钟表复杂的设计是工程上的奇迹。


PHRASE->
This word doesn't typically appear in common phrases.  It's used more as a standalone noun to describe something amazing.
