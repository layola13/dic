prophoric:/proˈfɔːrɪk/| adj.| 预示的；预兆的 |  Prophoric refers to something that foreshadows or indicates a future event or condition. It suggests a predictive or premonitory quality.  | Example: The unusually dark clouds were prophoric of the impending storm.  异常黑暗的云预示着即将到来的暴风雨。| 近义词：premonitory, portentous, ominous | 反义词：unforeseeable, unexpected


USAGE->

The dark clouds are prophoric of rain. = 黑云预示着下雨。
The strange behavior is prophoric of trouble. = 奇怪的行为预示着麻烦。
