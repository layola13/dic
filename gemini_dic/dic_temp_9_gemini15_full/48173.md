decompiler:/ˌdiːkəmˈpaɪlər/ | n. |反编译器 | A decompiler is a computer program that translates machine code or low-level assembly language back into a higher-level programming language, such as C or Java.  This process is the reverse of compilation. Decompilers are often used in software reverse engineering, to understand how a program works, to recover lost source code, or to find vulnerabilities.  | Example:  The programmer used a decompiler to examine the compiled binary file and understand its functionality.  反编译器用于检查已编译的二进制文件并了解其功能。|近义词:disassembler (but a disassembler typically works at a lower level)|反义词:compiler
