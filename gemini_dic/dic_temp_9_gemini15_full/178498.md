recontending:/ˌriːkənˈtɛndɪŋ/| v. |重新竞争，再次竞争 |To contend again; to renew a contest or struggle.  It implies a previous contest or struggle that has been interrupted or temporarily concluded. |Example: After a brief respite, the two companies are recontending for market share.  = 在短暂的休整之后，两家公司正在重新争夺市场份额。|近义词：recompeting, vying again, struggling anew |反义词：ceasing, surrendering, conceding


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They are recontending for the championship title. = 他们在重新争夺冠军头衔。
The two teams are recontending after a tie. = 两队在打成平局后重新开始比赛。


一般过去时:
The athletes recontended for the gold medal after a protest. = 运动员们在抗议之后重新争夺金牌。
The rivals recontended in the final round of the tournament. = 对手们在锦标赛的决赛中再次竞争。


现在完成时:
They have recontended for the top spot in the rankings multiple times. = 他们已经多次重新争夺排名榜首的位置。
The country has recontended with its neighbors over territorial disputes. = 这个国家已经多次与邻国就领土争端再次发生冲突。


过去完成时:
They had recontended several times before finally reaching a settlement. = 在最终达成协议之前，他们已经多次重新竞争。
The parties had recontended for years before the court's final decision. = 在法院做出最终裁决之前，各方已经争论多年。


将来时:
They will recontend for the throne after the current king's death. = 国王去世后，他们将再次争夺王位。
The political parties will recontend in the next election. = 各政党将在下次选举中再次竞争。


虚拟语气:
If they were to recontend, they would need a better strategy. = 如果他们要重新竞争，他们需要一个更好的策略。
I wish they would recontend, it would make the competition more exciting. = 我希望他们能重新竞争，这会使比赛更精彩。

否定句:

The company decided not to recontend in the market. = 公司决定不再在市场上竞争。
