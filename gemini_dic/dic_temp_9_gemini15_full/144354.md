outstay:/ˌaʊtˈsteɪ/ | v. | 逗留过久；比…待的时间长 | To stay longer than intended or expected, or longer than someone else. | 例句：I outstayed my welcome at the party. 我在派对上待的时间太长了。  

例句：He outstayed his visa by several months. 他逾期居留了好几个月。


近义词：overstay, remain longer than, linger


反义词：leave early, depart promptly


词性:动词

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He outstays his welcome at every party. 他每次参加派对都待得太久。
They usually outstay their guests. 他们通常比客人待的时间长。


一般过去时:
We outstayed our welcome at the hotel. 我们在酒店待的时间太长了。
She outstayed her friends at the cafe. 她在咖啡馆比朋友们待的时间长。


现在完成时:
I have outstayed my visa. 我逾期居留了。
They have outstayed their welcome. 他们待的时间太长了。


过去完成时:
We had outstayed our welcome, so we left. 我们待的时间太长了，所以我们离开了。
They had outstayed their friends, so they went home alone. 他们比朋友们待的时间长，所以独自回家了。


将来时:

虚拟语气:

词源:
The word "outstay" is a combination of the prefix "out-" (meaning "to a greater extent than") and the verb "stay" (meaning "to remain in a place").  It's a relatively straightforward word formation, reflecting the meaning of remaining longer than something else or a specified time.
