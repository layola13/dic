outflash:/ˈaʊtflæʃ/ | vt. |超过…的光亮；比…更耀眼 | to surpass in brightness or brilliance; to exceed in intensity of light or effect. |例句：The diamond outflashed all the other jewels. 这颗钻石比其他所有珠宝都耀眼。|例句：The supernova outflashed the entire galaxy. 超新星的光芒超过了整个星系。|近义词：outshine, outdazzle, eclipse |反义词：dim, pale


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The sun outflashes the moon. = 太阳比月亮更耀眼。
The spotlight outflashes the stage lights. = 聚光灯比舞台灯光更耀眼。


一般过去时:
The lightning outflashed the lamp. = 闪电比灯更耀眼。
Her beauty outflashed everyone else at the party. = 她在派对上比其他人都更耀眼。


现在完成时:
The new technology has outflashed the old methods. = 新技术已经超过了旧方法（在效果上）。
His success has outflashed all expectations. = 他的成功超出了所有人的预期（在程度上）。


过去完成时:
The fireworks had outflashed the city lights before the show ended. = 在演出结束之前，烟花已经比城市灯光更耀眼了。
The rumor had outflashed the official statement. = 在官方声明之前，谣言已经传得更广了（比喻意义）。


将来时:

虚拟语气:

I wish my ideas could outflash my colleagues'. = 我希望我的想法能够比同事们的更出色（比喻意义）。


被动语态:

His achievements were outflashed by his brother's. = 他的成就被弟弟的成就超越了（在程度上）。


疑问句:

否定句:

词源:
The word "outflash" is a combination of the prefix "out-" (meaning "to a greater extent" or "surpassing") and the verb "flash" (meaning "to shine brightly and suddenly").  Therefore, it literally means "to surpass in flashing or brightness."
