finances:/ˈfaɪnænsɪz/ | n. | 财务；资金；金融 |  The management of money and other assets.  It encompasses everything from personal budgeting to large-scale corporate investments. |  例句：The company's finances are in a precarious state.  公司的财务状况岌岌可危。

例句：We need to improve our personal finances. 我们需要改善个人财务状况。


近义词：funds, resources, assets, economics | 反义词：debt (in the context of lacking finances) | 词性：名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
His finances are in good shape. = 他的财务状况良好。
Our finances support our lifestyle. = 我们的财务状况支持我们的生活方式。

一般过去时:
Her finances were severely impacted by the recession. = 她的财务状况受到了经济衰退的严重影响。
Their finances improved after they sold their house. = 他们卖掉房子后，财务状况有所改善。

现在完成时:
They have improved their finances significantly this year. = 他们今年的财务状况有了显著改善。
We have carefully managed our finances over the years. = 多年来，我们一直谨慎地管理着我们的财务。

过去完成时:
By the end of the year, they had depleted their finances. = 到年底，他们的资金已经用尽了。
She had already overspent her finances before the month was over. = 月底之前，她就已经超支了。

将来时:

虚拟语气:
