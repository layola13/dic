hostage-taker: /ˈhɒstɪdʒˌteɪkə(r)/, /ˈhɑːstɪdʒˌteɪkər/ | n. | 人质劫持者 | A person who takes someone hostage.  A hostage-taker typically demands something in exchange for the release of the hostage(s), such as ransom, political concessions, or other specific demands.  They may use violence or threats of violence to ensure compliance. |


例句：The hostage-taker demanded a large sum of money for the safe release of the passengers. 人质劫持者要求支付一大笔钱才能安全释放乘客。

The police negotiators were in constant communication with the hostage-taker to try and de-escalate the situation. 警方谈判人员与人质劫持者保持着持续的沟通，试图缓和局势。


近义词：kidnapper, abductor, captor


反义词：hostage (反义词并非完全对等，而是指其反面角色)


词性：名词
