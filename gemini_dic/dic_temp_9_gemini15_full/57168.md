dowel-pin:/ˈdaʊəl pɪn/| n. |木钉，圆榫 |A dowel pin is a cylindrical pin, usually made of wood, metal, or plastic, used to join two pieces of material together.  It's often used in woodworking and joinery to strengthen joints. |例句：The carpenter used dowel pins to secure the two pieces of wood. 木匠用木钉固定两块木头。The machine parts were held together with metal dowel pins. 机器零件用金属木钉固定在一起。|近义词：peg, pin, tenon |反义词:None specifically, but terms implying separation like "disconnect" or "disjoin" could be considered opposites in a functional sense.
