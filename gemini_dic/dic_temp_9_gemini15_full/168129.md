pro-Aristotelian:/ˌproʊˌærɪstəˈtiːliən/ | adj. | 支持亚里士多德的；拥护亚里士多德哲学的 |  Supporting or advocating the philosophy of Aristotle.  This term describes individuals, ideas, or movements that align with or draw inspiration from the philosophical system of Aristotle.  It often implies a belief in the importance of reason, logic, observation, and empirical evidence in understanding the world. |

例句：

His pro-Aristotelian views on ethics were evident in his lectures. = 他在讲座中表达了他对亚里士多德伦理学的支持观点。

The pro-Aristotelian school of thought emphasized the importance of virtue ethics. = 支持亚里士多德的学派强调了德性伦理的重要性。


近义词：Aristotelian, neo-Aristotelian


反义词：anti-Aristotelian, Platonic (in some contexts)


词性：形容词


词源：

The term is a compound word.  "Pro-" is a prefix meaning "in favor of" or "supporting," derived from Latin. "Aristotelian" is an adjective derived from the name of the ancient Greek philosopher Aristotle (Ἀριστοτέλης, Aristotélēs).  Therefore, "pro-Aristotelian" literally means "in favor of Aristotle" or "supporting Aristotle's philosophy."
