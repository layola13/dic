prepueblo: /ˌpreˈpwebloʊ/ | n. |普韦布洛人之前的| A term used by archaeologists and anthropologists to refer to the ancestral Puebloan cultures that predate the Classic Pueblo period (roughly AD 1150-1350) in the Southwestern United States.  These cultures are characterized by various architectural styles, pottery types, and lifeways that differ from those of the later Classic Puebloans.  The term encompasses a diverse range of groups and periods. | Example:  Archaeological evidence suggests that prepueblo settlements in Chaco Canyon were already quite sophisticated before the construction of the great houses.  考古证据表明，在建造大型房屋之前，查科峡谷的普韦布洛人之前的聚落就已经相当发达了。|近义词: Ancestral Puebloan (often used interchangeably, but technically broader), Early Puebloan |反义词: Classic Puebloan
