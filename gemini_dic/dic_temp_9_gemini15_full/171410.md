puddings:/ˈpʊdɪŋz/| n.|布丁，甜点|A pudding is a sweet dessert, typically made with a combination of ingredients like eggs, milk, sugar, and flour, often baked or steamed. 词源:
The word "pudding" has a long and interesting history.  Its origins trace back to the Old French word "boudin," meaning "black pudding," a type of sausage. Over time, the meaning broadened to encompass various sweet and savory dishes. The modern English word "pudding" evolved from the Old French, ultimately encompassing a wide array of desserts and sweet dishes.
