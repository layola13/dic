scrubs:/skrʌbz/| n. v.|粗布衣服；擦洗；勤杂工；（医务人员的）手术衣|
英文解析：Scrubs can refer to rough, coarse clothing, the act of cleaning something vigorously, low-level workers, or, most commonly, the attire worn by medical personnel during surgery.  The plural form is used even when referring to a single set of clothing.

例句：
The gardener wore old scrubs while working in the garden.  园丁在花园里工作时穿着旧工作服。

He scrubs the floor every day. 他每天擦洗地板。


近义词：
(n.)  clothing, attire, garb, overalls, scrubs(medical),  janitors, cleaners
(v.)  clean, scour, wash, rub


反义词：
(n.)  fine clothes, elegant attire
(v.)  soiled, dirty


词性：名词，动词

词源:
The word "scrubs" as coarse clothing comes from the Old Norse word *skrubba, meaning "to rub."  The meaning evolved to refer to people who perform menial tasks, and later to the clothing they wear. The application to medical attire is a more recent development.
