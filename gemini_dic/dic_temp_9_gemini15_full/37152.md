come to naught:/kʌm tuː nɔːt/ | v. |化为乌有；失败；落空 |To end in failure; to have no result.  |例句：All his efforts came to naught.  他所有的努力都白费了。|例句：The peace talks came to naught. 和谈最终无果而终。|近义词：fail,  prove fruitless,  be in vain |反义词：succeed,  triumph,  prosper


USAGE->
一般现在时:
His plans often come to naught. = 他的计划常常落空。
Their efforts usually come to naught. = 他们的努力通常都白费了。


一般过去时:
The deal came to naught last week. = 这笔交易上周失败了。
Her ambitious project came to naught. = 她的雄心勃勃的项目最终失败了。


现在完成时:
All our hard work has come to naught. = 我们所有的努力都白费了。
His dreams have come to naught. = 他的梦想化为泡影。


过去完成时:
By the time he finished, his work had come to naught. = 等他完成时，他的工作已经白费了。
The negotiations had come to naught before he arrived. = 他到达之前，谈判就已经破裂了。


将来时:
If we don't act quickly, our efforts will come to naught. = 如果我们不迅速采取行动，我们的努力将付诸东流。
Without more funding, the project will come to naught. = 没有更多资金，这个项目将失败。


虚拟语气:
If he had worked harder, his efforts might not have come to naught. = 如果他更努力工作，他的努力可能不会白费。
I wish my plans hadn't come to naught. = 我真希望我的计划没有失败。


被动语态:
(This phrase is rarely used in the passive voice because it is intransitive.)


疑问句:
Will all this effort come to naught? = 所有的努力都会白费吗？
Does everything always come to naught for him? = 他的一切总是失败吗？


否定句:
His hard work did not come to naught; he succeeded. = 他的努力没有白费；他成功了。
This time, their plans will not come to naught. = 这一次，他们的计划不会失败。


PHRASE->

come to a head =达到高潮

come to terms with =接受现实
