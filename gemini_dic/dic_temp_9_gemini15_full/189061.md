sabotei:/ˌsæbəˈtɑːʒ/|/ˈsæbəˌtɑːʒ/| n. v.|破坏；怠工；阴谋破坏|Intentional destruction of property or obstruction of activities, especially for political or ideological reasons.  The act of sabotaging something. |例句：The workers sabotaged the factory equipment to protest their low wages. 工人们破坏了工厂设备以抗议低工资。

The enemy sabotaged the bridge to delay our advance. 敌人破坏了桥梁以延缓我们的前进。|近义词：undermine, wreck, cripple, disrupt |反义词：repair, build, construct, support


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They sabotage the project. = 他们破坏了这个项目。
He sabotages the meeting. = 他破坏了会议。

一般过去时:
The rebels sabotaged the power grid. = 反叛分子破坏了电网。
She sabotaged his career. = 她毁了他的事业。

现在完成时:
They have sabotaged the elections. = 他们已经破坏了选举。
Someone has sabotaged the computer system. = 有人破坏了电脑系统。

过去完成时:
The spies had sabotaged the communication lines before the attack. = 间谍在袭击前已经破坏了通讯线路。
She had sabotaged his chances of promotion. = 她已经破坏了他晋升的机会。

将来时:
They will sabotage the pipeline. = 他们将破坏管道。
He will sabotage the negotiations. = 他将破坏谈判。

虚拟语气:

I wish they hadn't sabotaged my presentation. = 我真希望他们没有破坏我的演示文稿。

被动语态:

The plan was sabotaged by internal dissent. = 计划因内部异议而被破坏。

疑问句:

否定句:
