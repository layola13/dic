dippy:/ˈdɪpi/| adj.|傻的；古怪的；轻率的 | Foolish or silly; lacking seriousness or good sense.  It can also describe something soaked in liquid, usually in a playful or slightly messy way.|例句：He's a bit dippy sometimes. 他有时候有点傻。

The cake is all dippy with frosting. 蛋糕上都沾满了糖霜。|近义词：silly, goofy, daft, wacky |反义词：sensible, intelligent, serious


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is dippy about his new car. = 他对他的新车很痴迷。(有点傻气)
She is always dippy when she's tired. = 她累了的时候总是很傻气。


一般过去时:
He was dippy enough to believe her lies. = 他傻到相信她的谎言。
The dog was dippy with excitement. = 那条狗兴奋得晕头转向。


现在完成时:
He has become dippy in his old age. = 他上了年纪后变得有点傻了。
The bread has become dippy from the soup. = 面包因为汤汁浸泡而变得软烂。


过去完成时:
She had been dippy all day. = 她整天都傻乎乎的。
The ice cream had gone dippy in the sun. = 冰淇淋在太阳下融化了。


将来时:
He will be dippy if he doesn't get enough sleep. = 如果他睡眠不足，他就会变得傻乎乎的。
The cookies will be dippy if we leave them in the milk too long. = 如果我们把饼干在牛奶里泡太久，它们就会变得软烂。


虚拟语气:
If he were dippy, he wouldn't have done that. = 如果他傻的话，他就不会那样做了。
I wish I hadn't been so dippy yesterday. = 我真希望我昨天不要那么傻。


被动语态:
His actions were considered dippy by many. = 许多人认为他的行为很傻。
The biscuits are being made dippy in the custard. = 饼干正在蛋奶糊里浸泡变软。


疑问句:
Is he dippy or just pretending? = 他是傻还是装傻？
Are the strawberries dippy with cream? = 草莓沾了奶油了吗？


否定句:
He wasn't dippy, he was just joking. = 他不是傻，他只是开玩笑。
The cake isn't dippy; it's just moist. = 蛋糕不是湿软的，它只是湿润的。
