headstarts:/ˈhɛdˌstɑːrts/| n.|领先，优势，先机|An advantage at the beginning of something.|例句：The early bird gets the head start.  早起的鸟儿有虫吃。|近义词：advantage, edge, lead|反义词：disadvantage, handicap


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He has a head start in the race. = 他在比赛中领先。
She gets a head start on her studies. = 她提前开始学习。


一般过去时:
They had a head start in the project. = 他们在项目中占据了先机。
He gave his son a head start in life. = 他给了儿子一个良好的开端。


现在完成时:
She has had a head start in her career. = 她在事业上已经领先一步。
They have had a head start in the market. = 他们在市场上已经占据了先机。


过去完成时:
The team had already had a head start before the other team arrived. = 在另一队到达之前，该队已经领先了。
Having had a head start, she easily won the race. = 因为领先，她轻松赢得了比赛。


将来时:
They will have a head start in the negotiations. = 他们在谈判中将占据主动。
She will give her children a head start in education. = 她将为她的孩子提供良好的教育开端。


虚拟语气:
If he had had a head start, he might have won the race. = 如果他一开始就领先，他可能就赢了比赛。
I wish I had had a head start in my career. = 我希望我的职业生涯一开始就领先。


被动语态:
The early investors were given a head start. = 早期投资者获得了先机。
A head start was given to the disadvantaged students. =  弱势学生得到了一个领先的优势。


疑问句:
Does he have a head start? = 他领先吗？

否定句:
He didn't have a head start in the race. = 他在比赛中没有领先。
