perchant:/pərˈʃɑːnt/ | n. |嗜好，偏好，癖好 |A strong inclination or liking for something.  A penchant is a strong liking for something, often something slightly unusual or considered a weakness. |He has a penchant for spicy food. 他喜欢吃辣的食物。|近义词：liking, fondness, preference, inclination |反义词：dislike, aversion, distaste


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He has a penchant for classic cars. = 他对经典汽车情有独钟。
She shows a penchant for wearing bright colors. = 她偏爱穿鲜艳的衣服。


一般过去时:
He had a penchant for collecting stamps. = 他过去喜欢集邮。
She showed a penchant for dramatic storytelling. = 她过去喜欢讲戏剧性的故事。


现在完成时:
He has always had a penchant for adventure. = 他一直都喜欢冒险。
She has developed a penchant for gardening. = 她已经培养了对园艺的爱好。


过去完成时:
He had already developed a penchant for painting before he moved to Paris. = 他在搬到巴黎之前就已经培养了对绘画的爱好。
She had long harbored a penchant for mystery novels. = 她很久以前就对侦探小说情有独钟。


将来时:
He will likely develop a penchant for cooking as he gets older. = 他长大后可能会培养对烹饪的爱好。
She will probably maintain her penchant for travel throughout her retirement. = 她退休后可能会继续保持她对旅行的爱好。


虚拟语气:
If he had a penchant for sports, he would join the team. = 如果他喜欢运动，他会加入球队。
I wish I had a penchant for early rising. = 我希望我早起。


被动语态:
His penchant for gambling was noted by everyone. = 大家都注意到了他嗜赌的习惯。
Her penchant for extravagance was widely known. = 她挥霍无度的习惯是众所周知的。


疑问句:
What is his particular penchant? = 他有什么特别的嗜好？
Does she have a penchant for anything in particular? = 她有什么特别的爱好吗？


否定句:
He doesn't have a penchant for classical music. = 他不喜欢古典音乐。
She doesn't show a penchant for gossiping. = 她不喜欢八卦。


PHRASE->
a penchant for = 对…的偏好
have a penchant for = 有…的嗜好
show a penchant for = 表现出对…的偏好
develop a penchant for = 培养对…的爱好
a strong penchant for = 对…强烈的偏好
