delirate:/ˈdɛlɪreɪt/| adj.| 精神错乱的；胡言乱语的；失去理智的 | suffering from delirium; mentally disturbed; characterized by delirium.  |例句：The patient was delirate and unable to communicate clearly.  病人神志不清，无法清晰地交流。|例句：He spoke in a delirate manner, his words jumbled and nonsensical. 他说话语无伦次，词不达意。|近义词：raving, raving mad, irrational, incoherent,  deranged |反义词：rational, sane, lucid, coherent |形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is delirate from the high fever. = 他因高烧而神志不清。
The old woman is delirate and needs constant care. = 老太太神志不清，需要时刻照顾。


一般过去时:
She was delirate after the accident. = 事故后她神志不清。
He became delirate during the night. = 他夜里开始神志不清。


现在完成时:
He has been delirate for days. = 他神志不清已经好几天了。
She has remained delirate since the surgery. = 手术后她一直神志不清。


过去完成时:
He had been delirate for hours before the doctor arrived. = 医生到达之前他已经神志不清好几个小时了。
They had become delirate before the rescue team could reach them. = 在救援队到达之前他们就已经神志不清了。


将来时:
He will likely be delirate for some time after the operation. = 手术后他很可能会神志不清一段时间。
If she doesn't get treatment, she will become delirate. = 如果她得不到治疗，她将会神志不清。


虚拟语气:
If he were not delirate, he could tell us what happened. = 如果他没有神志不清，他就能告诉我们发生了什么。
I wish he were not delirate so we could communicate. = 我希望他没有神志不清，这样我们就能沟通了。


被动语态:  (Delirate is rarely used in the passive voice because it describes a state of being, not an action performed on someone.)

词源:
From Middle French *délirer, from Latin *dēlīrāre, from dē- ("away, from") + līrāre ("to plow a furrow," figuratively "to go astray").  The original meaning referred to going astray from the straight furrow in plowing, later evolving to mean mental derangement.
