denigrates:/ˈdɛnɪɡreɪts/| vt.|贬低；诋毁；污蔑 |To speak ill of; to criticize unfairly; to belittle.  It involves representing someone or something as less valuable or important than they really are. |例句：He constantly denigrates his opponents' achievements. 他不断贬低对手的成就。


例句：The politician's speech denigrated the efforts of the opposition party.  这位政治家的演讲贬低了反对党的努力。


近义词：belittle, disparage, denigrate, depreciate, slight, undervalue, run down


反义词：praise, commend, extol, laud, acclaim


词性：动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He denigrates his colleagues' work. = 他贬低同事的工作。
She denigrates the value of traditional art. = 她贬低传统艺术的价值。

一般过去时:
The critic denigrated the actor's performance. = 批评家贬低了这位演员的表演。
They denigrated her efforts to help. = 他们贬低了她帮助的努力。

现在完成时:
He has denigrated her reputation for years. = 他多年来一直诋毁她的名誉。
The media has denigrated the importance of the event. = 媒体贬低了该事件的重要性。

过去完成时:
They had denigrated his ideas before he even presented them. = 甚至在他提出想法之前，他们就已经贬低了他的想法。
She had denigrated his work, leaving him feeling disheartened. = 她贬低了他的工作，让他感到灰心。

将来时:
He will denigrate her accomplishments in his next speech. = 他将在下一次演讲中贬低她的成就。
They will denigrate the project to get funding for their own. = 他们将贬低该项目以获得他们自己的资金。

虚拟语气:
If he denigrated her work again, she would leave the company. = 如果他再次贬低她的工作，她就会离开公司。
I wish he hadn't denigrated my efforts. = 我真希望他没有贬低我的努力。

被动语态:
His ideas were denigrated by the committee. = 他的想法被委员会贬低了。
Her achievements are constantly being denigrated by her rivals. = 她的成就不断被她的竞争对手贬低。

疑问句:
Does he often denigrate others' opinions? = 他经常贬低别人的意见吗？
Why did they denigrate the importance of the research? = 为什么他们贬低这项研究的重要性？

否定句:
He did not denigrate the value of her contribution. = 他没有贬低她贡献的价值。
She would never denigrate her friends' successes. = 她永远不会贬低朋友的成功。


PHRASE->
denigrate someone's character = 诋毁某人的品格
denigrate someone's work = 贬低某人的工作
publicly denigrate = 公开贬低
systematically denigrate = 系统性地贬低
to deliberately denigrate =故意贬低


词源:
From Old French *denigrer, from Latin denigrāre "to blacken," from niger "black."  The sense development is from "to blacken" (literally or figuratively) to "to defame" or "to belittle."
