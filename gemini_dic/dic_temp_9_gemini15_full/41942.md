cosmopolitanisms:/ˌkɒzməˈpɒlɪtənɪzəm/| n. |世界主义；国际化；大都会文化|The complex of ideas and attitudes associated with cosmopolitanism; the state or quality of being cosmopolitan.|例句：The city's cosmopolitanism is reflected in its diverse population and vibrant cultural scene.  这座城市的国际化体现在其多元化的居民和充满活力的文化氛围中。|例句：Her writing explores various aspects of cosmopolitanisms, particularly the tensions between global interconnectedness and local identities.她的作品探讨了世界主义的各个方面，特别是全球互联互通与地方认同之间的张力。|近义词：internationalism, globalism, universalism|反义词：provincialism, parochialism, nationalism


USAGE->
This word is primarily a noun and doesn't conjugate like a verb.  Therefore, tense and voice are not applicable.  However, we can show its use in different sentence structures.


In a simple declarative sentence:
Her cosmopolitanism is evident in her travel experiences. = 她的世界主义精神在她丰富的旅行经历中显而易见。

In a complex sentence:
Because of its cosmopolitanism, the city attracts people from all over the world. = 由于其国际化氛围，这座城市吸引了来自世界各地的人们。


In a question:
Does the growing cosmopolitanism of the internet benefit everyone equally? =互联网日益增长的世界主义是否平等地惠及所有人？

In a negative sentence:
The lack of cosmopolitanism in the small town was noticeable. = 小镇缺乏世界主义精神是显而易见的。
