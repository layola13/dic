maladjustment:/ˌmæləˈdʒʌstmənt/ | n. |不良适应，适应不良 |The inability to cope with or adjust to the demands of one's environment or situation, often leading to emotional or behavioral problems.  It refers to a state of being poorly adjusted or ill-suited to one's surroundings or circumstances.  This can manifest in various ways, depending on the individual and the context. |例句：His maladjustment to college life led to poor academic performance and social isolation. 他的大学生活适应不良导致学业成绩差和社交孤立。

His maladjustment to the new job resulted in increased stress and anxiety. 他对新工作的适应不良导致压力和焦虑增加。


近义词：inadaptability,  disadjustment, incompatibility,  imbalance


反义词：adjustment, adaptation,  compatibility,  balance


词源:mal- (bad, ill) + adjustment (the process of adapting or becoming accustomed).  The prefix "mal-" indicates a negative or unfavorable condition.
