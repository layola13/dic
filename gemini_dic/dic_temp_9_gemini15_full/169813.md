proscribed:/prəˈskraɪbd/ | vt. |禁止，明令禁止，严禁|to forbid something, especially by law |The government proscribed the sale of the drug. 政府禁止销售这种药物。|近义词：forbid, ban, prohibit |反义词：permit, allow, authorize


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The school proscribes the use of cell phones. = 学校禁止使用手机。
The doctor proscribes smoking for his patient. = 医生禁止他的病人吸烟。

一般过去时:
They proscribed the book as dangerous. = 他们将这本书列为禁书。
The king proscribed the practice of witchcraft. = 国王禁止巫术行为。

现在完成时:
The authorities have proscribed several extremist groups. = 当局已取缔了几个极端主义组织。
The government has proscribed the import of certain goods. = 政府已禁止进口某些商品。

过去完成时:
The judge had proscribed the evidence as inadmissible. = 法官已裁定证据不可采纳。
They had proscribed public gatherings before the outbreak. = 疫情爆发前，他们已禁止公众集会。

将来时:
The council will proscribe the construction of new buildings in this area. = 理事会将禁止在这个区域建造新建筑。
They will likely proscribe the use of fireworks during the festival. = 节日期间他们可能会禁止使用烟花爆竹。

虚拟语气:
If the law proscribed this activity, we wouldn't do it. = 如果法律禁止这项活动，我们就不会做了。
I wish the government proscribed the use of plastic bags. = 我希望政府禁止使用塑料袋。

被动语态:
The sale of alcohol was proscribed in the dry county. = 在禁酒县，禁止销售酒精。
The demonstration was proscribed by the police. = 警方禁止了示威活动。

疑问句:
Is the use of drones proscribed in this region? = 在这个地区是否禁止使用无人机？
Are certain types of weapons proscribed under international law? = 根据国际法，某些类型的武器是否被禁止？

否定句:
The law does not proscribe peaceful protests. = 法律并不禁止和平抗议。
The regulation doesn't proscribe the use of this software. = 该规章并不禁止使用此软件。
