quasi-forced:/ˈkwɑːzaɪ fɔːrst/| adj.|准强制的，近似强制的 |Approaching or resembling compulsion; not entirely forced but having strong elements of compulsion.  It implies a situation where there is significant pressure to act in a certain way, even if there's no explicit mandate or direct threat.|例句：The company's quasi-forced overtime policy led to widespread employee dissatisfaction. 公司的准强制加班政策导致员工普遍不满。

The government's quasi-forced economic reforms resulted in mixed outcomes. 政府的准强制经济改革导致了多种结果。|近义词：coerced, pressured, compelled, influenced |反义词：voluntary, optional, free, unconstrained
