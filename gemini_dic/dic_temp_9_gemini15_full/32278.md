chief:/tʃiːf/| n. adj.|主要的；首领；酋长|The most important or principal; a leader or head. |例句：He is the chief executive officer. 他是首席执行官。

例句：She is the chief cook and bottle washer.  她是这里的大管家。


近义词：principal, main, head, leader |反义词：subordinate, secondary, minor

词源:
From Old English *ċēf, from Proto-Germanic *kēpaz (“head, chief”), from Proto-Indo-European *(k)ep- (“head”). Cognate with Old High German chēp (“head”), Old Norse höfðingr (“chieftain”), and Latin caput (“head”).  The word's development reflects its evolution from a purely physical sense of "head" to its current multifaceted meanings.


用法说明:

名词：
1.  首领，领导：指一个群体或组织的领导者，例如部落酋长，公司总裁等。

例句：The chief of the police department addressed the press.  警察局局长向媒体发表讲话。

例句：The village chief welcomed us warmly. 村长热情地欢迎了我们。


2. 主要部分，最重要的事物：指某事物中最重要或最主要的部分。

例句：The chief reason for his failure was lack of preparation. 他失败的主要原因是准备不足。

例句：Her chief concern is the well-being of her children. 她最关心的是孩子们的福祉。


形容词：
1. 主要的，最重要的：指在等级或重要性上排在首位的。

例句：The chief ingredient in this dish is tomatoes. 这道菜的主要成分是西红柿。

例句：His chief aim is to finish the project on time. 他的主要目标是按时完成项目。


2. 首席的，最高的：指在某个领域担任最高职位的人。

例句：The chief engineer oversaw the construction of the bridge. 首席工程师负责监督大桥的建设。

例句：She is the chief financial officer of the company. 她是公司首席财务官。


PHRASE->
chief executive officer (CEO): 首席执行官
chief financial officer (CFO): 首席财务官
chief operating officer (COO): 首席运营官
chief information officer (CIO): 首席信息官
in chief:  主要的；首要的
