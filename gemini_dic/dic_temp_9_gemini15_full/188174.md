routinism:/ˈruːtɪnɪzəm/| n. |例行公事；墨守成规；程式化|The rigid routinism of his daily life stifled his creativity. 他日常生活的刻板例行公事扼杀了他的创造力。|近义词：formalism, conventionality, mechanization|反义词：innovation, spontaneity, originality


USAGE->
This word is a noun, and therefore doesn't conjugate through tenses like verbs.  The grammatical function changes depending on the sentence structure.


Example Sentences illustrating different grammatical roles:

Subject: Routinism is the enemy of progress. (例行公事是进步的敌人。)

Object: He despises routinism in his work. (他讨厌工作中的例行公事。)

Complement:  His life became pure routinism. (他的生活变成了纯粹的例行公事。)

Appositive:  His approach, a disheartening routinism, bored everyone. (他的方法，一种令人沮丧的例行公事，让每个人都感到厌烦。)

Prepositional Phrase:  There's no room for creativity under such a strict routinism. (在如此严格的例行公事下，没有创造力的空间。)


PHRASE->
There aren't common idiomatic phrases directly using "routinism."  However, we can use phrases related to its meaning:

break the routine: 打破常规
stick to the routine: 坚持常规
daily routine: 日常工作
get into a routine: 养成习惯
out of routine: 非常规的
