contango:/kənˈtæŋɡoʊ/| n. |期货溢价，期货升水|A contango is a market condition where the futures price of a commodity is higher than its spot price.  This typically occurs when there is a cost associated with storing the commodity until the future date.  For example, if the spot price of oil is $70 per barrel, and the futures price for delivery in six months is $72, then there is a contango of $2. |Example: The oil market is currently in contango, with futures prices exceeding spot prices due to storage costs and anticipated future demand.|近义词：backwardation的反义词|反义词：backwardation
