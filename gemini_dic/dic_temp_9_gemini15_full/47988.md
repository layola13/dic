decimate:/ˈdɛsɪmeɪt/ | vt. | 大量杀害；严重破坏 | To kill or destroy a large part of (something).  The word originally referred to the killing of one in ten soldiers as punishment, but now it implies a significantly greater proportion. | 例句：The hurricane decimated the coastal town.  飓风摧毁了沿海城镇。

例句：The disease decimated the population.  疾病使人口大量减少。


近义词：destroy, devastate, annihilate, wipe out
反义词：preserve, protect, save, conserve


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The disease decimates the population. = 这种疾病正在大量减少人口。
The drought decimates the crops. = 干旱正在严重破坏农作物。

一般过去时:
The earthquake decimated the city. = 地震摧毁了这座城市。
The fire decimated the forest. = 大火烧毁了森林。

现在完成时:
The war has decimated the country's economy. = 战争已经严重破坏了该国的经济。
Pollution has decimated the coral reefs. = 污染已经严重破坏了珊瑚礁。

过去完成时:
The previous drought had decimated the farm's yield. = 前一次干旱已经严重减少了农场的收成。
Years of overfishing had decimated the fish population. = 多年的过度捕捞已经使鱼类数量急剧减少。

将来时:
The predicted storm will decimate the coastal areas. = 预计的暴风雨将摧毁沿海地区。
If we don't act, climate change will decimate many species. = 如果我们不采取行动，气候变化将使许多物种灭绝。

虚拟语气:
If the disease had been controlled earlier, it would not have decimated the village. = 如果早些控制疾病，它就不会摧毁这个村庄了。
If we had acted sooner, the pest wouldn't have decimated our crops. = 如果我们早点采取行动，害虫就不会毁掉我们的庄稼了。


被动语态:
The city was decimated by the bombing. = 这个城市被轰炸摧毁了。
The forests were decimated by illegal logging. = 森林被非法采伐破坏了。

疑问句:
Was the army decimated after the battle? = 那场战役后军队是否被大量杀伤？
Has the population been decimated by the famine? = 人口因饥荒而大量减少了吗？

否定句:
The attack did not decimate the enemy forces. = 这次袭击没有消灭敌军主力。
The flood didn't decimate the entire town, thankfully. = 幸好洪水没有摧毁整个城镇。

词源:
From Late Latin *decimāre, from Latin decimus "tenth," from decem "ten."  Originally it meant "to select by lot and kill every tenth man," a Roman military punishment. The modern usage has broadened to encompass any significant destruction or killing.
