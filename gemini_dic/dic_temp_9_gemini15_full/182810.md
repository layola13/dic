repealability:/ˌriːpɪˈleɪbɪləti/| n. |可废止性，可撤销性 |The ability of a law, decision, or action to be repealed or revoked.  It refers to the ease or difficulty with which something can be undone or reversed.  This term is often used in legal and political contexts. | Example: The repealability of the new tax law is a subject of much debate. 新税法的可废止性是许多人争论的主题。|近义词：revocability, cancellability |反义词：irrevocability, permanence
