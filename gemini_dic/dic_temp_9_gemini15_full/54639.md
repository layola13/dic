dismissiveness:/dɪsˈmɪsɪvnəs/ | n. |轻视；不屑；漠视 |The act or instance of dismissing someone or something; a condescending disregard.  It implies a lack of respect or seriousness.  |例句：Her dismissiveness toward his concerns was hurtful.  她的轻视他的担忧令人伤心。

例句：The manager's dismissiveness of the employee's suggestions led to low morale. 经理对员工建议的轻视导致士气低落。


近义词：contempt, scorn, disdain, indifference, apathy


反义词：respect, consideration, attentiveness, seriousness


词源：源于动词dismiss (摒弃，解雇)，加上后缀-ive (形容词后缀)形成形容词dismissive (轻视的，不屑的)，再加后缀-ness (名词后缀)形成名词dismissiveness (轻视，不屑)。Dismiss itself comes from the Old French *desmettre, from des- (away) + mettre (to put, place).
