sess:/sɛs/ | n. | 会议；学期；期；阶段 |  a period of time devoted to a particular activity; a meeting or session of a group. |The Senate is in session. 参议院正在开会。|The spring session of classes begins next week. 春季学期下周开始。|近义词：meeting, period, term |反义词：recess, adjournment


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The committee holds a session every Tuesday. = 委员会每周二举行会议。
This session of Parliament lasts until next year. = 本届议会持续到明年。

一般过去时:
The court held a session to hear the case. = 法庭开庭审理此案。
The school held a special session to discuss the issue. = 学校召开特别会议讨论这个问题。


现在完成时:
They have finished the session of training. = 他们已经完成了培训课程。
We have had several sessions on this project. = 我们就这个项目已经开了几次会。


过去完成时:
The judge had already adjourned the session before the lawyer arrived. = 律师到达之前，法官已经休庭了。
The students had completed the previous session before the summer break. = 学生们在暑假前完成了之前的学期。


将来时:
The next session will begin in the fall. = 下一学期将在秋季开始。
The committee will hold a session to discuss the budget. = 委员会将召开会议讨论预算。


虚拟语气:
If we had more time, we would have had a longer session. = 如果我们有更多时间，我们会开更长时间的会。
I wish we could have a session to discuss this matter further. = 我希望我们能开个会进一步讨论这件事。


被动语态:
The session was chaired by the director. = 会议由主任主持。
The session was adjourned due to lack of quorum. = 由于缺乏法定人数，会议休会。


疑问句:
When is the next session? = 下一次会议是什么时候？
How long will the session last? = 会议将持续多久？


否定句:
The session did not go as planned. = 会议没有按照计划进行。
We did not have a session today. = 我们今天没有开会。


PHRASE->
