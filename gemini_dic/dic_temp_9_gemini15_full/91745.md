hyperdiploidy:/ˌhaɪpərˈdɪplɔɪdi/| n. |超二倍体|The condition in which a cell or organism has more than two complete sets of chromosomes.  It is often observed in cancer cells, particularly in certain types of leukemia and lymphoma.  Hyperdiploidy can lead to genomic instability and contribute to tumor development and progression. |Example: The researchers found that hyperdiploidy was a common characteristic in the analyzed leukemia samples.  研究人员发现，超二倍体是所分析白血病样本的常见特征。|近义词:polyploidy (more general term)|反义词:haploidy, diploidy
