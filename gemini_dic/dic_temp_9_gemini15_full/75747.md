fullscale:/ˈfʊlˌskeɪl/ | adj. | 全面的；大规模的；完全按比例的 |  Describes something that is complete, comprehensive, or done on a large scale, involving all aspects or elements. It can also refer to a model or replica that is the same size as the original. | 例句：They conducted a full-scale investigation into the accident.  他们对事故进行了全面调查。

例句：The company launched a full-scale marketing campaign. 公司发起了一场大规模的营销活动。

近义词：complete, comprehensive, extensive, large-scale, overall

反义词：partial, limited, small-scale, incomplete


词源:

* **full:**  Old English *full*, meaning "full, complete."  Its origins trace back to Proto-Germanic *fullaz.

* **scale:**  From Old French *escale*, meaning "ladder, stairs," ultimately from Latin *scala*, meaning "ladder."  In the context of "full-scale," it refers to the complete range or extent of something, a metaphorical extension of the literal meaning of a ladder or scale.
