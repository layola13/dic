land-on: /ˈlænd ɒn/ | v. |着陆；降落；偶然发现| To arrive on a surface, especially after flying or falling.  It can also mean to unexpectedly discover or encounter something.| 例句：The plane landed on the runway smoothly.  飞机平稳地降落在跑道上。

The detective landed on a crucial clue during his investigation.  侦探在调查中偶然发现了一个关键线索。|近义词：touch down, alight, descend, discover |反义词：take off, ascend, depart


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The birds land on the branches every evening. = 鸟儿每天晚上都落在树枝上。
The plane lands in New York at 3 pm. = 飞机下午3点降落在纽约。


一般过去时:
The astronaut landed on the moon. = 宇航员登上了月球。
The helicopter landed in a field. = 直升机降落在田野里。


现在完成时:
The pilot has landed the plane safely. = 飞行员已安全地降落飞机。
We have landed in a strange country. = 我们降落在一个陌生的国家。


过去完成时:
The plane had landed before the storm hit. = 暴风雨来临之前飞机已经降落了。
They had landed the cargo before nightfall. = 他们在夜幕降临前已经卸下了货物。


将来时:
The spaceship will land on Mars next year. = 宇宙飞船将于明年登陆火星。
The flight will land at 6:00 a.m. = 航班将于早上6点到达。


虚拟语气:
If the plane had landed on time, we wouldn't have missed the train. = 如果飞机准时降落，我们就不会错过火车了。
I wish the spacecraft had landed more gently. = 我希望宇宙飞船着陆更轻柔一些。


被动语态:
The plane was landed safely by the pilot. = 飞机被飞行员安全地降落了。
The package was landed on the doorstep. = 包裹被放在了门口。


疑问句:

否定句:

He didn't land the deal. = 他没有谈成这笔生意。
