paulician:/pɔːˈlɪʃən/| n. |保力西派教徒；保力西派教徒|A member of a Christian sect in 7th- and 8th-century Armenia and the Byzantine Empire that rejected the veneration of icons and the authority of the Church hierarchy.  They were known for their iconoclasm and adoption of some Manichaean beliefs.|The Paulician sect was suppressed by the Byzantine emperors, but their influence persisted for centuries.|近义词：iconoclast (in the context of religious beliefs)|反义词：iconophile
