rollover:/ˈroʊloʊvər/ /ˈrəʊləʊvər/| n., v.|翻转；延期；转移；(车辆)侧翻|

英文解析:
As a noun, "rollover" refers to the action of something turning over or rotating, often involving a complete revolution.  It can also refer to the extension of a loan, insurance policy, or other agreement into a new term.  In finance, it often means reinvesting earnings or interest into a new period. As a verb, "rollover" means to cause something to turn over or to extend or renew something.


例句:
The car experienced a rollover accident. = 这辆车发生了侧翻事故。

The loan will rollover automatically next month. = 这笔贷款下个月将自动延期。

词源:
The word "rollover" is a compound word formed from "roll" and "over."  "Roll" comes from Old English *rollan, meaning "to move by turning over," and "over" comes from Old English ofer meaning "across" or "above".  The combined meaning emerged naturally from the descriptive nature of the components.
