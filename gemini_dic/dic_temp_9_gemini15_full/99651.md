interleave:/ˌɪntərˈliːv/| vt. n.| 交错；插入；隔页插图 | To insert or arrange (sheets of paper, etc.) alternately with other sheets or materials.  |例句：The printer interleaves the forms with carbon paper. 打印机用复写纸将表格交错排列。

例句：The author interleaved chapters of narrative with pages of historical background. 作者将叙事章节与历史背景页面交错排列。


近义词：intertwine, alternate, intersperse | 反义词：separate, segregate | 词性：动词

词源:
The word "interleave" comes from the Old English prefix "inter-" meaning "between" or "among" and "leave," which in this context means "to insert" or "to place."  The word's development reflects its action of placing something between other things.
