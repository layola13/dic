needlefish: /ˈniːdlˌfɪʃ/ | n. |针鱼 | A long, slender fish with a long, pointed jaw, typically found in warm waters.  Needlefish are known for their speed and ability to leap out of the water. |例句：The fisherman caught a large needlefish while fishing in the shallows.  渔民在浅水区钓鱼时钓到了一条大针鱼。

例句：We saw a school of needlefish jumping out of the water near the reef. 我们在暗礁附近看到一群针鱼从水中跳出来。

词源:  The word "needlefish" is a compound word.  "Needle" refers to the long, slender shape of the fish's body and jaw, resembling a needle. "Fish" simply indicates that it is a type of fish. The etymology of "needle" traces back to Old English "nēdl," and "fish" has roots in Proto-Germanic and beyond.
