lycanthropy:/ˈlaɪkænθrəpi/| n. |狼人症；兽形人变症|The belief that a person can transform into a wolf, or a similar creature.  It is often associated with folklore and mythology, but can also be used to describe a mental disorder involving delusions of transformation. |Example: The novel explores the themes of lycanthropy and the duality of human nature. 这部小说探讨了狼人症和人性的二元性主题。|近义词：werewolfism, therianthropy|反义词：None specifically, but concepts like normalcy or human form could be considered contrasting.
