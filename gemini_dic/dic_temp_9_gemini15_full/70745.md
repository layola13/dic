fine-tune:/faɪnˈtuːn/| vt.|微调，精细调整|To make small adjustments to something in order to improve its performance or effectiveness.|例句：The engineers fine-tuned the engine to improve its fuel efficiency. 工程师们微调了发动机以提高燃油效率。
例句：She fine-tuned her presentation after receiving feedback from colleagues.  她在收到同事的反馈后微调了她的演示文稿。|近义词：adjust, refine, perfect|反义词：roughen, coarsen|动词


USAGE->

He fine-tunes his guitar before every performance. = 他每次演出前都会细调他的吉他。
They fine-tune their marketing strategies regularly. = 他们定期微调他们的营销策略。

词源:
The word "fine-tune" is a compound word, combining "fine" (meaning "small, delicate") and "tune" (meaning "to adjust").  The meaning developed from the context of musical instrument adjustments to encompass broader applications of precise adjustments.
