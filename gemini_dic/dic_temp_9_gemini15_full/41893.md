cosignatory:/koʊˈsaɪnəˌtɛri/ | n. |共同签字人；连署人 | A person who signs a document along with another person or persons.  This is often done to show agreement or to share responsibility. | Example: The cosignatory on the loan application was her father.  这个贷款申请的共同签字人是她的父亲。|近义词：joint signer, secondary signer |反义词：sole signer


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is a cosignatory on the contract. = 他是这份合同的共同签字人。
They are cosignatories on the loan agreement. = 他们都是贷款协议的共同签字人。


一般过去时:
She was a cosignatory on the mortgage. = 她曾是抵押贷款的共同签字人。
He was not a cosignatory on the document. = 他不是这份文件的共同签字人。


现在完成时:
I have been a cosignatory on several business ventures. = 我曾经是几个商业项目的共同签字人。
They have not yet found a cosignatory for the lease. = 他们还没有找到租赁合同的共同签字人。


过去完成时:
He had already been a cosignatory on two previous loans. = 他之前已经做过两次贷款的共同签字人。
She had never been a cosignatory before. = 她以前从未做过共同签字人。


将来时:
He will be a cosignatory on the new partnership agreement. = 他将成为新的合伙协议的共同签字人。
They will need to find a cosignatory for the bank loan. = 他们需要为银行贷款找到一个共同签字人。


虚拟语气:
If I were a cosignatory, I would be more careful. = 如果我是共同签字人，我会更加小心。
I wish I had a cosignatory to share the risk. = 我希望我有一个共同签字人来分担风险。


被动语态:
The contract was signed by two cosignatories. = 合同由两位共同签字人签署。
A cosignatory was required for the large loan. = 巨额贷款需要共同签字人。

否定句:
He is not a cosignatory. = 他不是共同签字人。
She was not a cosignatory on the previous contract. = 她不是之前合同的共同签字人。
