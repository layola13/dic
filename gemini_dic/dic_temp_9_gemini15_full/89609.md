hoodwinked:/ˈhʊdˌwɪŋkt/| v.|欺骗，蒙蔽|To hoodwink someone means to trick or deceive them, often in a clever or cunning way.  The term implies a degree of subtlety and perhaps a bit of charm used to mislead the victim.|The con artist hoodwinked the elderly woman out of her life savings.  骗子骗走了这位老年妇女的毕生积蓄。


He was completely hoodwinked by their false promises. 他完全被他们的虚假承诺所蒙骗。

|近义词：trick, deceive, mislead, dupe, swindle|反义词：enlighten, inform, reveal|动词
