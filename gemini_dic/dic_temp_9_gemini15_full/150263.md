pandoras-boxes:/pænˈdɔːrəzbɒksɪz/ | n. |潘多拉魔盒（复数） |A Pandora's box is a source of unexpected troubles or problems.  The term refers to the mythical Greek box opened by Pandora, releasing all the evils into the world. The plural form "Pandora's boxes" suggests multiple sources of such troubles. |例句：Opening that file proved to be a Pandora's box of technical problems. 打开那个文件证明是一系列技术问题的根源。The new policy unleashed a Pandora's box of unforeseen consequences. 新政策引发了一系列意想不到的后果。| 近义词：source of trouble, can of worms, hornet's nest | 反义词：blessing, boon


USAGE->
This word is a noun phrase and doesn't have verb conjugations.  The usage is always in the plural form  "Pandora's boxes".  Sentences using it will vary based on tense and structure of the main verb.


Example Sentences illustrating different sentence structures:

Present Simple:  Several Pandora's boxes were opened by the recent economic downturn.  最近的经济衰退打开了好几个潘多拉魔盒。

Past Simple: The investigation into the scandal revealed numerous Pandora's boxes of corruption. 对丑闻的调查揭露了许多腐败的潘多拉魔盒。

Future Simple:  Experts predict that climate change will open many Pandora's boxes for coastal communities.  专家预测气候变化将为沿海社区打开许多潘多拉魔盒。

Present Perfect:  The company's merger has already opened several Pandora's boxes of legal issues. 这家公司的合并已经打开了好几个法律问题的潘多拉魔盒。

Passive Voice:  Many Pandora's boxes of ethical concerns were inadvertently opened by the new technology.  许多伦理方面的潘多拉魔盒被新技术无意中打开。


PHRASE->

There aren't common set phrases directly using "Pandora's boxes" like there would be with a single word.  Instead, the phrase is used idiomatically within sentences as described above.  Examples of how it can be used in different contexts:
