ratcheted:/ˈrætʃɪt/| v. |棘轮声地移动；逐步提高；使卡住|To move with a series of small, abrupt movements, like a ratchet; to increase or improve gradually or in stages; to make (something) secure or fixed by a ratchet mechanism.|例句：The tension ratcheted up as the deadline approached.  随着最后期限的临近，紧张气氛越来越高涨。

例句：The government ratcheted up its efforts to combat inflation. 政府加大了打击通货膨胀的力度。

近义词：increase, escalate, tighten, secure

反义词：decrease, loosen, relax

词源:
The word "ratchet" comes from the Middle Dutch word "ratche," meaning "a notched wheel."  This ultimately derives from the Old French word "rachet," also meaning "a notched wheel." The sound imitative nature of the word likely reflects the clicking sound made by a ratchet mechanism.
