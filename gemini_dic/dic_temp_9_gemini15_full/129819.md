non-habitable: /ˌnɒnˈhæbɪtəbl/ | adj. |不宜居住的，不适于居住的 |  Not suitable or fit for habitation; uninhabitable.  The planet's harsh climate renders it non-habitable.  |  例句：The high altitude and thin air make the mountaintop non-habitable.  高山上的高海拔和稀薄的空气使得山顶不宜居住。

The extreme temperatures make this region non-habitable for humans. 极端的气温使得这个地区不适合人类居住。

近义词：uninhabitable, inhospitable, uninhibited

反义词：habitable, livable, hospitable


词源:

* **non-**:  prefix meaning "not" or "without"  (源自拉丁语 *non*)
* **habitable**: adjective meaning "capable of being lived in" (源自拉丁语 *habitare* "to dwell")


USAGE->
This word is an adjective, so it doesn't conjugate like a verb.  It modifies nouns.  Examples illustrating its use in different sentence structures are given below.


肯定句:
The desert is a non-habitable environment. 沙漠是一个不宜居住的环境。
Mars is considered a non-habitable planet. 火星被认为是一个不宜居住的星球。


否定句:
That area is not non-habitable; people live there comfortably.  那个地区并非不宜居住；人们在那里生活得很舒适。(This uses a double negative for emphasis).
This planet is not non-habitable, contrary to earlier beliefs.  与之前的认知相反，这颗星球并非不宜居住。


疑问句:
Is the moon non-habitable? 月球不宜居住吗？
Are these caves non-habitable? 这些洞穴不宜居住吗？


PHRASE->
There are no common phrases specifically using "non-habitable."  Its use is primarily as a stand-alone adjective describing a place or environment.  Phrases would be built around the *noun* it modifies, e.g., "non-habitable zone," "non-habitable planet," etc.
