expedites:/ˈɛkspədaɪts/ | vt. | 加速；促进；便利 | To make (a process or action) happen sooner or more quickly.  | 例句：The new software expedites the workflow. 新软件加快了工作流程。 | 例句：The government's new policies aim to expedite the economic recovery. 政府的新政策旨在加快经济复苏。 | 近义词：accelerate, hasten, facilitate, speed up | 反义词：delay, hinder, impede, obstruct | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The new system expedites the process. = 新系统加快了流程。
He expedites his work efficiently. = 他高效地完成了他的工作。


一般过去时:
The manager expedited the delivery. = 经理加快了送货速度。
They expedited the application process. = 他们加快了申请流程。


现在完成时:
The reforms have expedited the economic growth. = 改革促进了经济增长。
We have expedited the shipment. = 我们已经加快了发货速度。


过去完成时:
They had expedited the project before the deadline. = 他们在截止日期前加快了项目进度。
She had expedited the visa application. = 她已经加快了签证申请。


将来时:
The company will expedite the order. = 公司将加快订单处理速度。
We will expedite the response to your inquiry. = 我们将加快对您询价的回复。


虚拟语气:

被动语态:

疑问句:
Can we expedite the shipping? = 我们能加快运输吗？

否定句:

词源:
From Middle French *expédier, from Latin expedire "to disentangle, free, extricate; to prepare, dispatch," from ex- "out" + pedis "foot."  The sense evolution is from "untying a knot" to "freeing from obstacles" to "making something easier or faster."
