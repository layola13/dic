denigrating: /ˈdɛnɪɡreɪtɪŋ/ | v. |贬低；诋毁；污蔑 | To speak ill of; to belittle; to attack the reputation of someone or something. |例句：His constant denigrating remarks about her work were hurtful.  他不断贬低她工作的言论很伤人。|例句：The article denigrated the efforts of the volunteers. 这篇文章贬低了志愿者的努力。|近义词：belittle, disparage, defame, vilify, slander |反义词：praise, commend, extol, laud


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He denigrates his opponents in every debate. = 他在每一次辩论中都贬低他的对手。
She constantly denigrates the achievements of others. = 她不断贬低别人的成就。


一般过去时:
The newspaper denigrated the mayor's handling of the crisis. = 这家报纸贬低了市长处理危机的能力。
He denigrated her efforts to improve the situation. = 他贬低了她改善局势的努力。


现在完成时:
They have denigrated the reputation of the company through their actions. = 他们通过自己的行为损害了公司的声誉。
She has denigrated her own accomplishments out of modesty. = 出于谦虚，她贬低了自己的成就。


过去完成时:
The critics had denigrated his previous work before his latest success. = 在他最近获得成功之前，评论家们曾贬低过他以前的作品。
They had denigrated the value of the land before the discovery of oil. = 在发现石油之前，他们贬低了这块土地的价值。


将来时:
He will denigrate his opponent's qualifications in the upcoming election. = 在即将到来的选举中，他会贬低对手的资格。
The media will likely denigrate the new policy once it's implemented. = 一旦新政策实施，媒体可能会对其进行贬低。


虚拟语气:
If he denigrated her work again, she would leave the project. = 如果他再次贬低她的工作，她就会离开这个项目。
I wish he hadn't denigrated my efforts to help. = 我真希望他没有贬低我帮助他的努力。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
