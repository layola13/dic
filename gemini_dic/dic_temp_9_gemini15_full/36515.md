colics: /ˈkɒlɪks/ /ˈkɑːlɪks/ | n. | 绞痛；疝痛 |  Severe abdominal pain, especially in infants, often caused by intestinal gas or obstruction.  Colic is characterized by intense crying spells that are difficult to soothe. | Example: The baby was crying inconsolably, suffering from severe colic.  婴儿哭个不停，患有严重的绞痛。

The baby's colic kept the parents up all night.  婴儿的绞痛让父母整夜睡不着觉。


近义词: spasm, cramp, gripes | 反义词: None (colic refers to a specific type of pain)


Etymology: From Medieval Latin *colicus, from Latin *colicus "relating to the colon," from Greek kolon "colon."  The Greek word ultimately derives from the verb kōlon "to hinder, obstruct," likely referring to the blockage that can cause colic pain.
