quasi-elaborately:/ˈkwɑːziˌɪˈlæbərətli/ | adv. |近乎精心制作地，近乎详尽地 |  Describes something done with a degree of care and detail, but not completely or perfectly so;  it suggests an attempt at elaborate detail, falling short of true elaboration. It implies a certain level of complexity, but not necessarily to the highest degree. |The cake was quasi-elaborately decorated, with swirls of icing and a few strategically placed berries, but lacked the professional finish of a pastry chef's creation. = 蛋糕装饰得近乎精致，上面有糖霜漩涡和一些精心摆放的浆果，但缺乏糕点师作品的专业质感。  The report was quasi-elaborately written, containing a wealth of information, but it was disorganized and difficult to follow. = 报告写得近乎详尽，包含大量信息，但组织混乱，难以理解。|近义词: somewhat elaborately, fairly elaborately, relatively elaborately |反义词: simply, plainly, roughly


词源:

* **quasi-**:  From Latin *quasi*, meaning "as if," "almost," or "seemingly."  It indicates a resemblance or approximation, not a complete equivalence.

* **elaborately**: From "elaborate" (verb), meaning to develop or work out in detail; and ultimately from Latin *elaborare* (ex- + laborare, "to work").  "Elaborately" is the adverbial form, describing the manner in which something is done.

用法说明:

This word is an adverb, modifying a verb to describe how an action is performed. It highlights a degree of complexity and detail in the action, but with an implication that it's not entirely complete or perfect.  The "quasi-" prefix softens the intensity of "elaborately," suggesting an approximation or an attempt at elaboration rather than full-fledged elaboration.  It's a useful word for describing something that is intricate but not quite polished or perfect.
