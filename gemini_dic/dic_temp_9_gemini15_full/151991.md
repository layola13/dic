pasty:/ˈpeɪsti/| n.|馅饼；油腻的；苍白的|A pasty is a baked pastry case filled with meat and vegetables, often containing potatoes, turnips and sometimes beef or other meats.  It is typically associated with Cornwall, England.  The word can also be used to describe something that is excessively pale or greasy. |例句：He bought a delicious Cornish pasty for lunch. 他买了份美味的康沃尔馅饼当午餐。

The pasty was so greasy it left a stain on his shirt.  这个馅饼太油腻了，把他的衬衫弄脏了。


近义词：pie, turnover, tart|反义词：vibrant (for the pale meaning)


词源：The word "pasty" comes from the Middle English word "paste," meaning "paste," referring to the pastry dough.  This ultimately derives from the Old French "paste,"  itself from the Latin "pasta," meaning "dough" or "paste."
