offences:/əˈfɛnsɪz/| n. | 罪行；冒犯；违规行为 |  Plural of offense.  Offences refer to acts that violate laws or rules, or actions that cause someone to feel insulted or offended.  The word encompasses a wide range of actions, from minor infractions to serious crimes. |

例句：

He was arrested for a number of serious offences. = 他因多项严重罪行被捕。

Her remarks caused great offence to many people. = 她的言论冒犯了许多人。


近义词：crimes, misdemeanors, violations, transgressions, insults

反义词：obedience, compliance, conformity


词源:  From Old French *ofense, from Latin offensa, from offendere "to strike against, attack, assault, offend," from ob- "against" + fendere "to strike."
