preestablish:/ˌpriːɪˈstæblɪʃ/| vt.|预先建立；预先确定|To establish something beforehand; to arrange or settle something in advance.  The term implies prior planning and arrangement before the actual establishment or occurrence.|例句：The committee preestablished the rules for the competition. 委员会预先制定了比赛规则。
例句：They preestablished a meeting time to discuss the project. 他们预先确定了会议时间来讨论项目。|近义词：establish beforehand, predetermine, prearrange|反义词：postpone, improvise|动词
