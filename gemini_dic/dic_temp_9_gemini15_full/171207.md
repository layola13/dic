psychopolitics: /ˌsaɪkoʊˈpɑːlətɪks/ | n. | 心理政治学 | The study of the manipulation of the population's psychology to achieve political ends.  It involves the use of propaganda, psychological warfare, and other techniques to influence public opinion and behavior.  | Example: The government's use of  psychopolitics to suppress dissent was widely condemned.  政府利用心理政治学压制异见受到了广泛谴责。| 近义词：political psychology, propaganda, social engineering | 反义词：N/A
