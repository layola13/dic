codification:/ˌkəʊdɪfɪˈkeɪʃən/ | n. | 编纂；法典化；编码 | The process of setting down laws, rules, or principles in a systematic form, usually in writing.  It involves organizing and arranging elements into a coherent and consistent system. This can apply to laws, rules, customs, or even programming languages. | Example: The codification of Roman law had a profound impact on European legal systems.  罗马法的编纂对欧洲法律体系产生了深远的影响。|近义词：systematization, formalization, standardization | 反义词：chaos, disorder
