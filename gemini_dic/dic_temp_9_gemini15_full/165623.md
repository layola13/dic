premigratory: /ˌpriːˈmaɪɡrətəri/ | adj. | 迁徙前的；迁移前的 | Relating to or characteristic of the period before migration.  Describes something that happens or exists before a migration.  |例句：The premigratory behavior of birds includes increased feeding and fat storage.  鸟类的迁徙前行为包括增加觅食和脂肪储存。

例句：The researchers studied the premigratory condition of the butterflies. 研究人员研究了蝴蝶的迁徙前状态。


近义词：antecedent, preliminary, prior  | 反义词：postmigratory


词源:  The word "premigratory" is formed from the prefix "pre-" (meaning "before") and the noun "migratory" (relating to migration).  "Migratory" itself derives from the Latin word "migrare" (to move, to wander).  Therefore, the word's etymology clearly indicates its meaning of "before migration."
