panicking: /ˈpænɪkɪŋ/ | v. | 惊慌失措，恐慌 |  The present participle of "panic," indicating the state of being overwhelmed by sudden fear. It describes the act of experiencing or showing panic. | 例句：He was panicking because he couldn't find his passport. 他因为找不到护照而惊慌失措。

She was panicking, her heart pounding in her chest. 她惊慌失措，心跳如雷。


近义词：freaking out, losing it, freaking, stressing, getting anxious


反义词：calm, composed, relaxed, tranquil


词性：动词（现在分词）


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is panicking about the exam. 他正在为考试而惊慌失措。
They are panicking over the news. 他们对这个消息感到恐慌。


一般过去时:
I panicked when I saw the snake. 我看到蛇时惊慌失措了。
She panicked and ran away. 她惊慌失措地跑开了。


现在完成时:
He has been panicking all day. 他整天都惊慌失措。
They have panicked several times this week. 他们本周已经好几次惊慌失措了。


过去完成时:
I had panicked before the interview. 我在面试前就已惊慌失措了。
She had panicked when she realised she was lost. 当她意识到自己迷路时，她已经惊慌失措了。


将来时:
I will panic if I fail the test. 如果我考试不及格，我会惊慌失措。
They will panic when they hear the news. 当他们听到这个消息时，他们会惊慌失措。


虚拟语气:
If I were you, I wouldn't panic. 如果我是你，我不会惊慌失措。
I wish I hadn't panicked so much. 我希望我当时没有那么惊慌失措。


被动语态:  The word "panicking" itself is already active and rarely used passively.  A passive construction would be awkward.


疑问句:
Is he panicking? 他在惊慌失措吗？
Are they panicking about the situation?  他们对这种情况感到恐慌吗？


否定句:
He wasn't panicking at all. 他一点也不惊慌。
They didn't panic despite the emergency. 尽管发生了紧急情况，他们也没有惊慌。



PHRASE->
panicking about:  对...感到惊慌
panicking over:  对...过度担忧
panicking to:  惊慌地去做...
start panicking: 开始惊慌
stop panicking:  停止惊慌


词源:
From the verb "panic," ultimately derived from the Greek god Pan, associated with wild, untamed nature and capable of inspiring sudden, overwhelming fear.  The association led to the use of "panic" to describe sudden, uncontrollable fear.
