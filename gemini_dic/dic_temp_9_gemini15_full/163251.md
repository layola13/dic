preconcerned:/ˌpriːkənˈsɜːrnd/| adj.| 预先担心的；先期关切的 |Preconcerned implies a worry or anxiety that exists before a specific event or situation. It suggests a proactive state of concern, rather than a reaction to something already happening.  |例句：He was preconcerned about the potential dangers of the expedition. 他事先担心探险的潜在危险。  |例句：The parents were preconcerned about their child's academic performance. 父母事先担心孩子的学业成绩。|近义词：apprehensive, anxious, worried |反义词： unconcerned, indifferent, complacent


USAGE->
This word is an adjective and doesn't have tenses like verbs.  It describes a state of being.  Therefore, typical verb tense examples are not applicable.  The usage is primarily in the present tense to describe a current state of pre-existing worry.
