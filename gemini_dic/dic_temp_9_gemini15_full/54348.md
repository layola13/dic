dishing:/ˈdɪʃɪŋ/| v.| 闲聊；八卦；分发；盛；烹调 | To talk about other people, often in a gossipy way; to serve or distribute food; to cook or prepare food. |例句：They were dishing the dirt on their colleagues.他们在说同事的坏话。


例句：She's busy dishing up the dinner. 她正忙着准备晚餐。


近义词：gossiping, spreading rumors, serving, distributing | 反义词：keeping quiet, remaining silent


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They are dishing about the latest celebrity scandal. = 他们在八卦最新的名人丑闻。
She dishes out the soup every Sunday. = 她每个星期天都盛汤。


一般过去时:
We dished the dirt on our boss last night. = 我们昨晚说我们老板的坏话。
He dished up a delicious meal for his family. = 他为他家人做了一顿美味的饭菜。


现在完成时:
They have been dishing about their neighbours for weeks. = 他们几个星期以来一直在说他们邻居的坏话。
She has dished out hundreds of portions of food at the event. =她在活动中分发了数百份食物。


过去完成时:
They had dished all the gossip before I arrived. = 我到达之前他们已经八卦完所有的事情了。
She had dished up the food before the guests arrived. = 客人到达之前，她已经把食物准备好了。


将来时:

They will dish up a special menu for the anniversary dinner. = 他们将为周年纪念晚宴准备一份特别的菜单。


虚拟语气:
If I had known they were dishing, I wouldn't have gone. = 如果我知道他们在八卦，我不会去的。
If she had dished the food earlier, we wouldn't have been so hungry. = 如果她早点把食物盛出来，我们就不会这么饿了。
