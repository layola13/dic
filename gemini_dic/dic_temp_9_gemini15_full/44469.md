crossbeak: /ˈkrɒsbiːk/ | n. | 交喙鸟 | A bird with crossed mandibles (upper and lower parts of the beak), typically a finch.  Crossbeaks are known for their specialized beaks that help them extract seeds from cones. | Example: The crossbeak used its crossed beak to efficiently extract seeds from the pine cone.  The red crossbeak is a common species in coniferous forests. | 近义词:  (There aren't many direct synonyms, as the crossed beak is the defining characteristic)  finch,  (for specific species) red crossbeak, white-winged crossbill | 反义词:  (No direct antonyms)
