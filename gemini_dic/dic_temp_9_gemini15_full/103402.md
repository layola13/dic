jinx:/dʒɪŋks/| n., v.|厄运；坏运气；(使)倒霉；| a person or thing believed to bring bad luck; to spoil something by saying something unlucky before it happens |例句：Don't mention it, it's a jinx!  别提了，这会带来厄运！


He jinxed the game with his bad luck. 他因为他的坏运气而导致比赛失败。|近义词：curse, hex, hoodoo, misfortune|反义词：blessing, good luck, fortune|


USAGE->
一般现在时:
She jinxes every project she touches. = 她碰过的每个项目都会倒霉。
This old house is jinxed. = 这栋老房子很不吉利。


一般过去时:
He jinxed the whole team by mentioning their losing streak. = 他提到了他们的连败纪录，给整个队伍带来了厄运。
The actor jinxed the play with his clumsy fall. = 演员笨拙的摔倒给戏剧带来了厄运。


现在完成时:
I think I've jinxed myself by boasting about my success. = 我想我因为夸耀自己的成功而给自己带来了厄运。
She has jinxed us all with her negativity. = 她消极的态度给我们所有人带来了厄运。


过去完成时:
He had jinxed the deal before we even started negotiations. = 在我们开始谈判之前，他已经把这笔交易搞砸了。
They had jinxed the party by arriving late. = 他们因为迟到而破坏了派对。


将来时:
I'm afraid I'll jinx it if I talk about it now. = 我担心如果我现在谈论它，我会把它搞砸。
If you keep talking about it, you'll jinx it. = 如果你一直谈论它，你会把它搞砸的。


虚拟语气:
If I hadn't jinxed it, I would have won the lottery. = 如果我没有把它搞砸，我就会赢得彩票。
I wish I hadn't jinxed the interview by mentioning my previous job. = 我真希望我没有在面试中提到我的上一份工作而把它搞砸。


被动语态:
The project was jinxed by a series of unfortunate events. = 这个项目因为一系列不幸的事件而倒霉。
The team felt they were jinxed by bad luck. = 球队感觉他们被厄运缠身。


疑问句:
Do you think we're jinxed? = 你认为我们倒霉吗？
Is this project jinxed? = 这个项目是不是很不吉利？


否定句:
I don't want to jinx it, but I think we're going to win. = 我不想把它搞砸，但我认为我们会赢。
They didn't want to jinx their chances by talking about their success. = 他们不想因为谈论他们的成功而毁掉他们的机会。


PHRASE->

break the jinx = 打破厄运
