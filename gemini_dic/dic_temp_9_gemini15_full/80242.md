goeth:/ˈɡoʊθ/ | v. | 去；行走；(古语) |  The third-person singular present indicative form of the verb "go," now largely archaic. It means to move or travel from one place to another; to proceed; to depart. | Example: He goeth forth to battle. 他去参加战斗。  |近义词: goes, departs, proceeds | 反义词: remains, stays


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He goes to school every day. = 他每天去上学。
She goes to the market to buy groceries. = 她去市场买杂货。

一般过去时:
He went to the park yesterday. = 他昨天去了公园。
They went to a concert last night. = 他们昨晚去看了音乐会。

现在完成时:
He has gone to the store. = 他已经去商店了。
She has gone to work. = 她已经去上班了。

过去完成时:
He had gone home before I arrived. = 我到的时候他已经回家了。
They had gone to bed when the phone rang. = 电话响的时候他们已经睡了。

将来时:
He will go to London next week. = 他下周要去伦敦。
She will go to the doctor tomorrow. = 她明天去看医生。

虚拟语气:
If he went to the party, he would have a good time. = 如果他去参加派对，他会玩得很开心。
I wish he went to college. = 我希望他上大学。

被动语态:
The letter was sent to the wrong address. = 这封信寄错了地址。(Note: "go" is rarely used in the passive voice.)
The meeting was postponed; the participants were informed that the meeting would be rescheduled, and participants were informed that the meeting would be rescheduled. 会议被推迟了;与会者被告知会议将被重新安排。

疑问句:
Does he go to school? = 他去上学吗？
Where does she go every weekend? = 她每个周末去哪里？

否定句:
He does not go to work on Sundays. = 他星期天不上班。
She did not go to the party last night. = 她昨晚没有去参加派对。
