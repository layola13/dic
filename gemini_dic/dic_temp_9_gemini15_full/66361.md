excusation:/ɪkˌskjuːˈzeɪʃən/| n. |辩解；借口；道歉|An excusation is an explanation offered to justify a fault or offense.  It's often used to try and lessen the blame or responsibility for something.  However, an excusation can sometimes be seen as a weak or unconvincing excuse.|例句：He offered a weak excusation for his lateness. 他为他的迟到找了一个很牵强的借口。|例句：Her excusation wasn't accepted by the teacher. 老师没有接受她的辩解。|近义词：excuse, apology, justification, explanation |反义词：acceptance of responsibility, confession|
