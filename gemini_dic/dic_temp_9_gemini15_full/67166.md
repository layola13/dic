expostulated:/ɪkˈspɒstʃəleɪtɪd/| v.|据理力争；规劝；抗议|To reason earnestly with someone in an attempt to dissuade them from a course of action.  |例句：He expostulated with her about the dangers of smoking. 他苦口婆心地劝她戒烟。|例句：The teacher expostulated with the students for their disruptive behavior. 老师因学生们的捣乱行为而规劝他们。|近义词：reason with, remonstrate, protest|反义词：acquiesce, concur


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He expostulates with his friend about his reckless driving. = 他苦口婆心地劝说朋友不要鲁莽驾驶。
She expostulates against the unfair treatment. = 她抗议这种不公平的待遇。


一般过去时:
He expostulated with the manager about the unfair dismissal. = 他就公司的不公平解雇向经理据理力争。
She expostulated against the new policy, but to no avail. = 她反对这项新政策，但没有效果。


现在完成时:
He has expostulated with them many times about their behavior. = 他已经多次规劝他们改正行为。
She has expostulated against the injustice for years. = 多年来她一直抗议这种不公正。


过去完成时:
He had expostulated with her before, but she didn't listen. = 他以前曾劝过她，但她没听。
They had expostulated against the plan, but it was already implemented. = 他们曾反对过这个计划，但它已经被实施了。


将来时:
He will expostulate with his son about his poor grades. = 他将就儿子的糟糕成绩规劝他。
She will expostulate against the proposed changes. = 她将反对拟议中的变化。


虚拟语气:
If I had expostulated earlier, perhaps things would have been different. = 如果我早点规劝，也许情况会不一样。
I wish I had expostulated more strongly. = 我真希望我当时能更强烈地抗议。


被动语态:
His arguments were expostulated against by the board. = 他的论点遭到董事会的反对。
The policy was expostulated against by many citizens. = 许多市民反对这项政策。


疑问句:
Did he expostulate with you about the matter? = 他就此事规劝过你吗？
Why didn't you expostulate against the decision? = 你为什么不反对这个决定？


否定句:
He didn't expostulate with her, though he should have. = 他没有规劝她，虽然他应该这样做。
She didn't expostulate against the injustice. = 她没有反对这种不公正。


PHRASE->
expostulate with someone = 向某人据理力争
expostulate against something = 反对某事
expostulate vehemently = 强烈抗议
expostulate in vain = 白费口舌地规劝
expostulate eloquently =  eloquent地规劝


词源:
From Late Latin *expostulatus, past participle of expostulare "to demand," from ex- "out" + postulare "to demand."  The sense evolved from "demand" to "reason earnestly."
