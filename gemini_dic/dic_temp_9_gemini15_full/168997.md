profound:/prəˈfaʊnd/| adj.| 深奥的；深刻的；深远的；极度的 |  Deeply felt or experienced; intense.  It can also refer to something that is intellectually deep or difficult to understand. | 例句：His profound sadness was evident in his eyes.  他的深深悲伤从他的眼睛里流露出来。

例句：The philosopher offered profound insights into the nature of reality.这位哲学家对现实的本质提供了深刻的见解。


近义词：deep, intense, significant, weighty, momentous

反义词：superficial, shallow, trivial, insignificant


词源：From Old French *profund, from Latin profundus, from pro- ("forth, forward") + fundus ("bottom").  The original meaning related to depth (literally or figuratively).
