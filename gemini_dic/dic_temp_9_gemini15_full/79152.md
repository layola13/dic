glaire:/ɡleər/| n.| 蛋清；黏液；光泽 |The viscous, albuminous fluid of an egg white.  It is also used to describe a similar viscous substance or a glossy sheen. |例句：The recipe called for two egg whites, or glaires.  这个食谱需要两个蛋清。


例句：The artist applied a glaire to the painting to give it a glossy finish. 艺术家在画作上涂抹蛋清使其具有光泽的表面。


近义词：white of egg, albumen, sheen, gloss


反义词：dullness, matte finish


词性：名词


词源：源自古法语glaire，意为“蛋清”，最终源自拉丁语clarus，意为“清晰的，明亮的”。  This reflects the clear, transparent nature of egg white.


USAGE->
This word is primarily a noun and doesn't conjugate through tenses like verbs.  Therefore, tense examples are not applicable.  The usage focuses on its noun function in different sentence structures.
