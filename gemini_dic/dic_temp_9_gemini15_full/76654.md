galumph:/ˈɡælʌmp/| v.|笨拙地行走，蹒跚；|to walk in a clumsy, heavy-footed way.  |例句：He galumphed through the mud. 他笨拙地穿过泥泞。|例句：The giant galumphed across the room. 巨人在房间里笨拙地走动。|近义词：stumble, lumber, trudge|反义词：glide, tiptoe


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He galumphs down the street. = 他笨拙地走在街上。
She galumphs across the room. = 她笨拙地穿过房间。

一般过去时:
He galumphed into the room. = 他笨拙地走进房间。
She galumphed over the rocks. = 她笨拙地跨过岩石。

现在完成时:
He has galumphed all the way home. = 他一路笨拙地走回了家。
She has galumphed through the entire garden. = 她笨拙地走过了整个花园。

过去完成时:
He had galumphed through the forest before nightfall. = 他在夜幕降临前笨拙地走过了森林。
She had galumphed across the field before the rain started. = 下雨前她笨拙地走过了田野。

将来时:
He will galumph down the stairs. = 他将笨拙地走下楼梯。
She will galumph through the snow. = 她将笨拙地穿过雪地。

虚拟语气:
If he were to galumph across the stage, it would be comical. = 如果他笨拙地走上舞台，那将很有趣。
I wish she wouldn't galumph so loudly. = 我希望她不要这么大声地笨拙行走。

被动语态: (此词很少用被动语态)
The distance was galumphed across by the exhausted hiker.  =  疲惫的徒步旅行者笨拙地走完了这段距离。(较为不自然)


疑问句:
Does he often galumph around like that? = 他经常那样笨拙地走动吗？
Will she galumph through the mud again? = 她还会笨拙地趟过泥泞吗？

否定句:
He didn't galumph; he walked gracefully. = 他没有笨拙地走；他走得很优雅。
She doesn't usually galumph, but she was tired. = 她通常不会笨拙地走，但她累了。


PHRASE->
(此词本身较为少见，难以形成固定搭配短语)  galumphing gait (笨拙的步态),  galumphing walk (笨拙的行走), to galumph along (笨拙地走着),  a galumphing giant (笨拙的巨人), galumphing around (笨拙地走来走去).
