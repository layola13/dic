confrontation:/ˌkɒnfrʌnˈteɪʃən/| n. |对抗；冲突；对质 | A hostile or argumentative meeting or situation between opposing parties.  |例句：The two leaders avoided direct confrontation.  两位领导人避免了直接对抗。

例句：The border dispute led to a violent confrontation between the two armies. 边界争端导致两军之间发生暴力冲突。


近义词：conflict, clash, encounter, showdown, dispute | 反义词：agreement, harmony, accord, peace


词源:  From Old French *confrontation, from confronter "to confront," from Latin *confrontare, from con- "together" + frons, frontis "forehead, face."  The original sense was of "bringing face to face," implying a direct encounter.
