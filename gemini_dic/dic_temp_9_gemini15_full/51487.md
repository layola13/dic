detracted:/dɪˈtræktɪd/| v.|减损；转移；分散(注意力)|To diminish the value, importance, or effectiveness of something.  It implies a lessening of something positive.|The loud music detracted from our enjoyment of the movie.  吵闹的音乐影响了我们看电影的乐趣。|The constant interruptions detracted from his ability to concentrate.  不断的打断分散了他的注意力。|diminished, reduced, impaired|enhanced, improved, increased|动词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
His constant complaining detracts from the team's morale. = 他不断的抱怨降低了团队士气。
The poor lighting detracts from the beauty of the painting. = 昏暗的灯光减损了这幅画的美感。


一般过去时:
The scandal detracted from the politician's reputation. = 丑闻损害了这位政治家的名誉。
The rain detracted from the enjoyment of the picnic. = 雨水影响了野餐的乐趣。


现在完成时:
The negative reviews have detracted from the book's sales. = 负面评论已经影响了这本书的销量。
Years of neglect have detracted from the value of the property. = 多年的忽视已经降低了这处房产的价值。


过去完成时:
The previous owner's neglect had detracted from the house's charm. = 前房主的忽视已经减损了这栋房子的魅力。
His past mistakes had detracted from his credibility. = 他过去的错误已经损害了他的信誉。


将来时:
The upcoming election will detract from the focus on economic issues. = 即将来临的选举将分散对经济问题的关注。
Future developments might detract from the initial success. = 未来的发展可能会削弱最初的成功。


虚拟语气:
If the noise hadn't detracted from the performance, it would have been perfect. = 如果噪音没有影响演出，它本来会很完美。
I wish the bad weather hadn't detracted from our holiday. = 我真希望糟糕的天气没有影响我们的假期。


被动语态:
His achievements were detracted from by the media's negative coverage. = 他的成就受到了媒体负面报道的影响。
The project's success was detracted from by unforeseen delays. = 该项目的成功因不可预见的延误而受损。


疑问句:
Did the bad weather detract from your trip? = 坏天气是否影响了你的旅行？
Does the noise detract from your work? = 噪音是否影响了你的工作？


否定句:
The positive feedback did not detract from her confidence. = 正面反馈并没有降低她的信心。
Nothing could detract from the beauty of the sunset. = 没有什么可以减损日落的美景。


PHRASE->

detract from something = 削弱...；减损...
detract from the value = 降低价值
detract from the beauty = 降低美感
detract from the enjoyment = 影响乐趣
detract from the reputation = 损害名誉

词源:

The word "detract" comes from the Latin prefix "de-" (away, down, from) and the Latin verb "trahere" (to draw, pull).  Therefore, it literally means "to draw away from."
