recant:/rɪˈkænt/ | vt. |公开否认；撤回；放弃 | To withdraw or retract a statement or belief, especially a formal or previously held one; to renounce or repudiate.  | The witness recanted his testimony after realizing he'd made a mistake.  证人意识到自己犯了错误后撤回了证词。| retract, repudiate, revoke, renounce | affirm, uphold, maintain | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He recants his previous statement. 他公开否认他之前的声明。
She recants her support for the candidate. 她撤回了对候选人的支持。


一般过去时:
The accused recanted his confession. 被告撤回了他的供词。
The artist recanted his controversial artwork. 艺术家放弃了他那有争议的艺术作品。


现在完成时:
He has recanted his earlier claims. 他已经撤回了之前的声明。
She has recanted her criticisms of the government. 她已经撤回了对政府的批评。


过去完成时:
He had recanted his support before the election. 他在选举前就撤回了他的支持。
She had recanted her beliefs after years of study.  经过多年的学习，她放弃了她的信仰。


将来时:
He will recant his statement tomorrow. 他明天将撤回他的声明。
She will likely recant her accusations. 她很可能会撤回她的指控。


虚拟语气:
If he were to recant his testimony, the case would be weakened. 如果他撤回证词，案件就会变得薄弱。
I wish he would recant his harmful words. 我希望他会收回他那些有害的话。


被动语态:
His statement was recanted by the witness. 他的声明被证人撤回了。
The theory has been recanted by many scientists.  许多科学家已经放弃了这个理论。


疑问句:
Will he recant his position? 他会改变立场吗？
Has she recanted her promise? 她收回承诺了吗？


否定句:
He refused to recant his views. 他拒绝收回他的观点。
She did not recant her allegations. 她没有收回她的指控。


PHRASE->
recant a statement  收回声明
recant one's beliefs 放弃信仰
publicly recant 公开否认
fully recant 完全放弃
recant under pressure 在压力下收回


词源:
From Old French *recanter, from re- "again" + chanter "to sing."  Originally meaning "to sing again," it evolved to mean "to repeat," and later, to "repeat a statement with a change of meaning," thus leading to its modern sense of retracting or denying a previous statement.
