iron-clad: /ˈaɪərnˌklæd/ | adj. |坚不可摧的；牢不可破的 |  extremely strong and secure; impossible to break or weaken | The iron-clad agreement ensured that neither party could back out.  这个牢不可破的协议确保任何一方都不能反悔。 |近义词：unbreakable, impregnable, invincible | 反义词：breakable, vulnerable, weak


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The contract is iron-clad. = 这份合同是坚不可摧的。
This system is iron-clad against hacking. = 这个系统对黑客攻击坚不可摧。


一般过去时:
The treaty was iron-clad until the outbreak of war. = 这项条约在战争爆发前是坚不可摧的。
His alibi was iron-clad; the police couldn't find any evidence to disprove it. = 他的不在场证明是铁证如山的；警方找不到任何证据来反驳它。


现在完成时:
The company has maintained an iron-clad reputation for quality. = 这家公司一直保持着坚不可摧的声誉。
Their defense has proven iron-clad throughout the trial. = 在整个审判过程中，他们的辩护已被证明是坚不可摧的。


过去完成时:
The evidence had been iron-clad, leaving no room for doubt. = 证据确凿，不容置疑。
The fortress had been iron-clad, withstanding countless sieges. = 堡垒坚不可摧，经受了无数次围攻。


将来时:
The new security system will be iron-clad against cyberattacks. = 新的安全系统将能抵御网络攻击。
The agreement will be iron-clad, binding both parties legally. = 该协议将是牢不可破的，在法律上约束双方。


虚拟语气:
If the agreement were iron-clad, there would be no disputes. = 如果协议牢不可破，就不会有争议。
I wish the evidence were iron-clad in this case. = 我希望此案证据确凿。


被动语态:
The case was considered iron-clad by the jury. = 陪审团认为此案证据确凿。
The decision was made with an iron-clad guarantee of success. = 该决定是在成功得到坚实保证的情况下做出的。


疑问句:
Is the evidence truly iron-clad? = 证据真的确凿吗？
Is their argument iron-clad and irrefutable? = 他们的论点是否牢不可破且无可辩驳？


否定句:
The alibi wasn't iron-clad; there were some inconsistencies. = 他的不在场证明并非铁证如山；存在一些不一致之处。
The security system is not iron-clad; it can be breached. = 安全系统并非坚不可摧；它可以被突破。
