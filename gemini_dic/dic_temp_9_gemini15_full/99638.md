interlamination:/ˌɪntərˌlæmɪˈneɪʃən/| n. |层间叠合；层间层压|The process of bonding together multiple layers of material, often with an adhesive. This creates a composite material with improved properties.  For example, interlamination is used in manufacturing printed circuit boards (PCBs) to create a multilayer structure.  Another example is the creation of laminated wood products, where thin layers of wood veneer are bonded together. | Example: The interlamination of the fiberglass sheets provided additional strength to the boat hull.  层间叠合的玻璃纤维板增加了船体的强度。|近义词：layering, stratification, lamination |反义词：separation, delamination
