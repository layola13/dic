metallochromy:/ˌmɛtælˈoʊkroʊmi/| n. |金属显色|The change in color of a metal-containing solution due to a change in the concentration of metal ions or the presence of certain substances.|The metallochromic indicator changed color when the concentration of calcium ions increased. 金属显色指示剂在钙离子浓度增加时改变颜色。|近义词：chromogenic reaction, colorimetric reaction |反义词：无

词源:
The word "metallochromy" is a combination of "metallo-" (from "metallum," Latin for "metal") and "-chromy" (from "chroma," Greek for "color").  It literally means "color of metal" or more accurately, "color change related to metal ions".
