by-line:/ˈbaɪlaɪn/| n. |署名行，作者署名|A byline is the line of text at the beginning of a newspaper article, magazine article, or other piece of writing that states the author's name.  It's typically placed above the article itself. |The byline read "Jane Doe, Staff Writer."  署名行写着“简·多伊，特约撰稿人”。|credit line, author's line|unnamed, anonymous|


USAGE->
This word is primarily a noun and doesn't have verb conjugations like the example word "what".  The following examples demonstrate its use in different sentence structures.


Simple sentence:
The byline identified the author as a Pulitzer Prize winner.  署名行表明作者是普利策奖得主。


Complex sentence:
Because the article lacked a byline, the editor was unable to determine the author's identity.  由于文章缺少署名行，编辑无法确定作者的身份。


Compound sentence:
The newspaper changed its style guide, and now all articles must include a clear byline. 报纸更改了风格指南，现在所有文章都必须包含清晰的署名行。


PHRASE->
There are no common multi-word phrases directly using "byline".  Its usage is quite straightforward.
