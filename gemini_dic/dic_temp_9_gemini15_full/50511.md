deprive:/dɪˈpraɪv/| vt.|剥夺，使丧失|To take something away from someone or something, or to prevent someone from having something.|例句：They were deprived of food and water for days.  他们几天没吃没喝。|例句：The accident deprived him of his sight. 这场事故使他失明。|近义词：dispossess, bereave, divest|反义词：bestow, grant, provide


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He deprives his children of their allowance. = 他剥夺了他孩子们的生活费。
The government deprives the people of their rights. = 政府剥夺了人民的权利。

一般过去时:
The storm deprived the town of electricity. = 暴风雨使该镇断电。
They deprived him of his freedom. = 他们剥夺了他的自由。

现在完成时:
Years of war have deprived the country of its resources. = 多年的战争使该国资源匮乏。
Illness has deprived her of her mobility. = 疾病剥夺了她的行动能力。


过去完成时:
The drought had deprived the farmers of their crops. = 干旱使农民们颗粒无收。
His laziness had deprived him of success. = 他的懒惰使他失去了成功的机会。

将来时:
The new law will deprive many people of their jobs. = 新法会使许多人失业。
The flood will deprive the city of clean water. = 洪水将使这座城市缺水。

虚拟语气:
If they deprived him of his position, he would be devastated. = 如果他们剥夺了他的职位，他会非常沮丧。
I wish they hadn't deprived me of my inheritance. = 我真希望他们没有剥夺我的遗产。

被动语态:
He was deprived of his inheritance by his brother. = 他的遗产被他的兄弟剥夺了。
The prisoners were deprived of their basic rights. = 囚犯们被剥夺了基本权利。

疑问句:
Were they deprived of their freedom? = 他们被剥夺了自由吗？
What rights were the citizens deprived of? = 公民被剥夺了什么权利？

否定句:
The judge did not deprive the defendant of his rights. = 法官没有剥夺被告的权利。
They were not deprived of food and shelter. = 他们没有被剥夺食物和住所。


PHRASE->
be deprived of = 被剥夺…
deprive someone of something = 剥夺某人某物
deprive oneself of =  克制自己享用…
deprived area = 贫困地区
deprived children = 贫困儿童
