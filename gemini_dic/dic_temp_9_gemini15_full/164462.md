preentailed:/ˌpriːɪnˈteɪld/| adj.| 牵连的；附带的；预先决定的；|Involved as a necessary condition or consequence.|The inheritance entailed a large debt. 这笔遗产牵涉到一笔巨额债务。|近义词：involved, implicated, predetermined|反义词：unrelated, independent


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The contract entails significant responsibilities. = 合同中包含重要的责任。
This job entails a lot of travel. = 这份工作需要大量的差旅。


一般过去时:
The decision entailed unforeseen consequences. = 这个决定带来了意想不到的后果。
The project entailed extensive research. = 这个项目需要大量的研究。


现在完成时:
The renovations have entailed a considerable expense. = 装修已经花费了相当大的费用。
My actions have entailed unexpected results. = 我的行为带来了意想不到的结果。


过去完成时:
The investigation had entailed numerous interviews. = 调查已经进行了多次访谈。
The war had entailed great suffering for the people. = 那场战争给人民带来了巨大的痛苦。


将来时:
The new policy will entail significant changes. = 新政策将带来重大变化。
The expansion will entail hiring more staff. = 扩张将需要雇佣更多员工。


虚拟语气:
If the plan entailed too much risk, we would abandon it. = 如果计划风险太大，我们就放弃。
I wish the job didn't entail so much overtime. = 我希望这份工作不需要那么多加班。


被动语态:
Significant changes were entailed by the new regulations. = 新规章带来了重大的变化。
More expenses were entailed by the unexpected repairs. = 意外的维修带来了更多费用。


疑问句:
What does this position entail? = 这个职位包含什么？
Does this plan entail any risks? = 这个计划包含任何风险吗？


否定句:
The agreement did not entail any hidden costs. = 协议中没有任何隐藏的成本。
This decision will not entail any negative consequences. = 这个决定不会带来任何负面后果。


PHRASE->
entail upon: 使承担；强加于
entail on: 使承担；加诸于
entail in: 包含在...之中
be entailed in: 牵涉在…中
entail with: 伴随...


词源:
The word "entailed" comes from the Old French word "entailler," meaning "to cut into, notch, engrave."  This evolved into a legal sense, referring to the "cutting off" or limiting of inheritance rights, thus leading to its current meaning of involving something as a necessary condition or consequence.
