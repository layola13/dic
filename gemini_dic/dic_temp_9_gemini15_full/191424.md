scherzo:/ˈskɛrt͡soʊ/| n.|谐谑曲|A scherzo is a musical composition, typically part of a larger work like a symphony or sonata, characterized by a lively, playful, and often mischievous character.  It's usually in a fast tempo and uses a relatively light and humorous style, often contrasting with the more serious movements of the piece. |Example: The third movement of Beethoven's Fifth Symphony is a famous scherzo. 贝多芬第五交响曲的第三乐章是一个著名的谐谑曲。|近义词：capriccio, divertimento|反义词：adagio, lento|名词
