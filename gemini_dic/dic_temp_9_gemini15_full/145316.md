overbroad: /ˌoʊvərˈbrɔːd/ | adj. | 过于宽泛的，范围过大的 |  Too wide or general in scope; excessively broad.  It often implies that something is too inclusive and encompasses things it shouldn't. | 例句：The law was deemed overbroad because it restricted free speech unnecessarily.  法律因不必要地限制言论自由而被认为过于宽泛。


The judge criticized the prosecutor's overbroad questioning of the witness. 法官批评了检察官对证人过于宽泛的盘问。| 近义词：too wide, sweeping, extensive, comprehensive (in a negative context),  unnecessarily broad | 反义词：precise, specific, narrow, limited, focused
