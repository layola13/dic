pretzels:/ˈprɛtsəl/| n.|椒盐卷饼|A pretzel is a type of baked bread snack that is typically made from dough that is twisted into a knot shape and then baked until it is crisp.  Pretzels are often salted, but can also be flavored with other seasonings or coated in chocolate or other toppings.|例句：I bought a bag of pretzels from the store.我从商店买了一袋椒盐卷饼。

例句：She enjoyed eating pretzels while watching a movie.她在看电影时喜欢吃椒盐卷饼。|近义词：cracker, biscuit|反义词：None.

词源:
The word "pretzel" ultimately derives from the Vulgar Latin *brezella, a diminutive of *bracellus, meaning "little arm".  This reflects the characteristic knotted shape of the baked good. The word evolved through various stages in Germanic languages before entering English.
