plantocracy: /plænˈtɒkrəsi/ | n. |种植园统治；由种植园主统治的社会| A system of government or society in which power is held by the owners of large plantations.  It often implies a society characterized by racial inequality and exploitation of agricultural laborers. The term is particularly associated with the pre-Civil War South in the United States. | Example: The plantocracy of the antebellum South exerted immense political and economic power.  种植园主统治的战前南方拥有巨大的政治和经济权力。|近义词: aristocracy, oligarchy |反义词: democracy, egalitarianism
