skunkweeds: /ˈskʌŋkˌwiːdz/ | n. | 臭鼬草 |  A colloquial term for various plants, often weeds, that have a strong, unpleasant odor, reminiscent of a skunk.  The exact species referred to can vary regionally. The smell is usually due to the presence of sulfur-containing compounds. | Example:  "We pulled out all the skunkweeds from the garden; they smelled terrible!" = 我们把花园里所有的臭鼬草都拔掉了；它们闻起来太难闻了！| 近义词：stinky weeds, foul-smelling plants | 反义词： fragrant flowers, sweet-smelling plants | 名词
