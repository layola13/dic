kick-starts:/ˈkɪkˌstɑːrts/| vt.|  启动；促进；激发 |To give something a sudden boost or impetus; to initiate or begin something forcefully.|例句：The new marketing campaign kick-started sales.  新的营销活动刺激了销售。|例句：The government's investment kick-started the local economy. 政府的投资启动了当地经济。|近义词：initiate, launch, stimulate, jump-start|反义词：halt, stop, terminate, stifle|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The new project kick-starts the company's growth. = 新项目启动了公司的增长。
This medicine kick-starts the healing process. = 这药启动了治愈过程。

一般过去时:
The speech kick-started a national debate. = 这篇演讲引发了一场全国性的辩论。
The rain kick-started the growth of the plants. = 雨水促进了植物的生长。

现在完成时:
The new CEO has kick-started a series of reforms. = 新任CEO已经启动了一系列改革。
The advertising campaign has kick-started a surge in demand. = 广告活动已经引发了需求激增。

过去完成时:
They had kick-started the engine before the race. = 比赛前他们已经启动了引擎。
The manager had kick-started the project months ago. = 经理几个月前就启动了这个项目。

将来时:
The investment will kick-start the development of renewable energy. = 这项投资将启动可再生能源的开发。
The new technology will kick-start a revolution in the industry. = 新技术将引发行业革命。

虚拟语气:
If we had more funding, we could kick-start the research project. = 如果我们有更多资金，我们就能启动这个研究项目。
I wish they had kick-started the project earlier. = 我希望他们早点启动这个项目。

被动语态:
The economy was kick-started by government spending. = 经济被政府支出所启动。
The project was kick-started with a large donation. = 该项目在一笔巨额捐款的资助下启动。

疑问句:
Will the new policy kick-start economic growth? = 新政策会启动经济增长吗？
What measures will kick-start the stalled negotiations? = 什么措施可以启动停滞的谈判？

否定句:
The lack of funding prevented them from kick-starting the project. = 资金不足阻止了他们启动该项目。
The marketing campaign failed to kick-start sales. = 营销活动未能刺激销售。
