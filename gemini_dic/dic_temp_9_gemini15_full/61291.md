embankment:/ɪmˈbæŋkmənt/ | n. | 堤岸；路堤；护岸 | A raised bank of earth or stone, typically built to prevent a river from flooding or to carry a road or railway.  | Example: The embankment protects the town from flooding during heavy rains.  这道堤岸保护着小镇免受暴雨洪水的侵袭。|近义词：dam, dike, levee, bank | 反义词：depression, hollow


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The embankment protects the city from floods. = 这条堤坝保护城市免受洪水侵袭。
This embankment runs along the river. = 这条堤岸沿着河流延伸。


一般过去时:
They built a new embankment last year. = 他们去年修建了一条新的堤岸。
The old embankment collapsed during the storm. = 老堤岸在暴风雨中坍塌了。


现在完成时:
They have strengthened the embankment recently. = 他们最近加固了堤岸。
The government has invested heavily in embankment construction. = 政府已大力投资堤坝建设。


过去完成时:
The flood waters had already breached the embankment before help arrived. = 在救援到来之前，洪水已经冲垮了堤岸。
The engineers had inspected the embankment thoroughly before the heavy rains. = 工程师们在大雨之前彻底检查了堤岸。


将来时:
They will construct a new embankment next year. = 他们明年将修建一条新的堤岸。
The rising water levels will soon overwhelm the embankment. = 水位上涨很快就会淹没堤岸。


虚拟语气:
If they had built a stronger embankment, the flood would not have been so devastating. = 如果他们建造一个更坚固的堤岸，洪水就不会造成如此大的破坏。
I wish they had maintained the embankment properly. = 我希望他们能好好维护堤岸。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
