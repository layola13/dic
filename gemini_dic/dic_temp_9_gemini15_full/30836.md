chabouk:/tʃæˈbuːk/| n. |鞭子，马鞭|A chabouk is a long, flexible whip, typically made of leather thongs or plaited leather, used for riding or driving animals, particularly in parts of the Middle East and Central Asia.  It is often heavier and more flexible than a riding crop.|例句：The horseman cracked his chabouk to urge his horse forward.  骑手甩动马鞭催促马前进。|近义词：whip, crop, lash|反义词：None specifically, but words implying gentleness or calmness could be considered antonyms in the context of usage. |名词
