middlebrow:/ˈmɪdlbraʊ/| adj.| 中产阶级的；庸俗的；平庸的 | Relating to or characteristic of the middle class, especially in its tastes and aspirations; often implying a lack of sophistication or originality.  It suggests something that appeals to a broad, undemanding audience, often lacking intellectual depth or artistic merit. | Example: The novel was criticized for its middlebrow plot and predictable characters. 这部小说因其平庸的情节和可预测的人物而受到批评。|近义词：commonplace, pedestrian, mass-market |反义词：highbrow, intellectual, avant-garde
