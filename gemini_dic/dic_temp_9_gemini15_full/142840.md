out-dodges: /ˌaʊtˈdɑːdʒɪz/ | v. |巧妙避开，躲过|  To skillfully avoid or evade something, typically by quick thinking or clever maneuvering.  This word is a compound of "out-" (meaning to surpass or exceed) and "dodges" (the act of evading). It implies a higher level of skill or cunning than simply dodging. | 例句：The boxer out-dodged his opponent's powerful punch. 拳击手巧妙地躲过了对手的重拳。


The cunning thief out-dodged the police surveillance.狡猾的小偷躲过了警方的监控。| 近义词：evade, avoid, outmaneuver, circumvent | 反义词：encounter, confront, face
