indicative:/ɪnˈdɪkətɪv/ | adj. | 陈述的；指示的；象征的 | Relating to or conveying a statement of fact or opinion; not hypothetical or expressing a command.  It describes verbs that express facts or opinions, as opposed to moods like subjunctive or imperative. | The indicative mood is used to make statements of fact or opinion.  |  近义词：declarative, assertive | 反义词：subjunctive, imperative


The indicative mood is the most common verb mood in English. It is used to state facts, ask questions, or make statements about what is likely to be true.

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He walks to school every day. = 他每天步行上学。
She plays the piano beautifully. = 她钢琴弹得很好。

一般过去时:
They went to the park yesterday. = 他们昨天去了公园。
I ate a delicious meal last night. = 昨晚我吃了一顿美味的饭。

现在完成时:
We have finished our homework. = 我们已经完成了作业。
She has lived in London for five years. = 她已经在伦敦住了五年了。

过去完成时:
They had already left when I arrived. = 当我到达时，他们已经离开了。
The movie had started before we got there. = 我们到的时候电影已经开始了。

将来时:
I will go to the beach tomorrow. = 我明天要去海滩。
She will be happy to help you. = 她很乐意帮助你。

虚拟语气: (Indicative mood is not used in typical hypothetical situations. The subjunctive mood is used instead.)
N/A

被动语态:
The car was repaired yesterday. = 车昨天修好了。
The letter was written by my friend. = 这封信是我朋友写的。

疑问句:
Is it raining outside? = 外面在下雨吗？
Where do you live? = 你住在哪里？

否定句:
I don't like spicy food. = 我不喜欢辛辣的食物。
She wasn't at home last night. = 她昨晚不在家。
