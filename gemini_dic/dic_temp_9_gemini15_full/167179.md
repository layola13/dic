pretelegraph:/ˌpriːˈtɛləɡræf/| n. |先驱电报；早期的电报系统|A pretelegraph refers to any system of communication that predates the widespread use of the electric telegraph, often employing visual or mechanical means for long-distance signaling.  These systems were rudimentary compared to the speed and efficiency of the electric telegraph, but they played a crucial role in the development of telecommunications.  |Example:  The use of signal flags represented a type of pretelegraph communication.|近义词：early telegraph system, precursor to the telegraph|反义词：None directly applicable|名词
