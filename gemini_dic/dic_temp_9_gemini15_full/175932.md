rationalistic: /ˌræʃənəlˈɪstɪk/ | adj. | 理性主义的 |

英文解析:  Characterized by or believing in rationalism; emphasizing reason and logic as the primary sources of knowledge and justification.  It often implies a distrust of tradition, emotion, or religious belief in favor of scientific or philosophical reasoning.


例句:

His rationalistic approach to religion led him to question traditional doctrines.  他的理性主义宗教观使他质疑传统教义。

The philosopher's rationalistic worldview was heavily influenced by Enlightenment thinkers.  这位哲学家的理性主义世界观深受启蒙思想家的影响。


近义词:  logical, reasoned, intellectual, empirical, scientific


反义词:  irrational, emotional, mystical, superstitious, faith-based


词源:  Derived from "rationalism" + "-istic,"  meaning "relating to" or "characterized by."  "Rationalism" itself comes from "rational" (from Latin *rationalis, from ratio "reason") + "-ism" (a suffix indicating a doctrine or system of belief).
