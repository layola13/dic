read-write:/ˌriːdˈraɪt/| adj.| 读写 |  Describes something that allows both reading and writing of data.  It's commonly used in the context of computer memory, storage devices, and access permissions. For example, a "read-write memory" can both store and retrieve data, while a "read-only memory" (ROM) can only be read. | Example: This file system is read-write, allowing you to save and modify files.  这个文件系统是读写的，允许你保存和修改文件。| 近义词:input/output, accessible | 反义词:read-only
