radiochemists: /ˌreɪdiəʊˈkɛmɪsts/ | n. | 放射化学家 | Scientists who specialize in radiochemistry, the branch of chemistry that deals with radioactive substances and their chemical behavior. They study the chemical properties of radioactive isotopes, develop methods for separating and identifying them, and apply their knowledge to various fields, including medicine, environmental science, and nuclear energy. |  Many radiochemists are employed in government laboratories or universities. 许多放射化学家受雇于政府实验室或大学。 | nuclear chemists, radiation chemists | (no clear antonym)
