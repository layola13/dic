prominent:/ˈprɒmɪnənt/ /ˈprɑːmɪnənt/| adj.|突出的，显著的，重要的|Standing out; easily noticeable; important or well-known.|The prominent scientist gave a lecture.  这位杰出的科学家做了一场演讲。|He played a prominent role in the negotiations. 他在谈判中扮演了重要角色。|important, leading, significant, notable|insignificant, obscure, unnoticed, humble


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The mountain is prominent on the horizon. = 那座山在地平线上很突出。
Her work is prominent in the field of art. = 她的作品在艺术领域很突出。


一般过去时:
The building was prominent in the city skyline. = 这座建筑在城市天际线上很突出。
His contribution was prominent in the project's success. = 他的贡献对项目的成功至关重要。


现在完成时:
That company has become prominent in the tech industry. = 那家公司已成为科技行业中的佼佼者。
Her name has become prominent in the literary world. = 她的名字在文坛已声名鹊起。


过去完成时:
The problem had been prominent for years before they finally addressed it. = 在他们最终解决这个问题之前，这个问题已经突出存在多年了。
His influence had been prominent in shaping the company's culture. = 他对塑造公司文化的影响一直很显著。


将来时:
The new building will be prominent in the cityscape. = 新建筑将在城市景观中占据显眼位置。
Her research will be prominent in the upcoming conference. = 她的研究将在即将召开的会议上占据重要地位。


虚拟语气:
If the evidence were more prominent, the case would be easier to solve. = 如果证据更明显，案件就更容易破获。
I wish my achievements were more prominent in the company. = 我希望我的成就能够在公司中更受瞩目。


被动语态:
His contributions were prominently featured in the newspaper. = 他的贡献被报纸重点报道。
The issue is prominently discussed in the media. = 这个问题在媒体上得到了广泛讨论。


疑问句:
Is the issue prominently featured in the report? = 这个问题在报告中是否被突出强调？
Are there any prominently displayed warning signs? = 是否有醒目的警告标志？


否定句:
The details were not prominent in the report. = 报告中细节并不突出。
His role in the project was not prominent. = 他在项目中的作用并不突出。
