inform:/ɪnˈfɔːrm/| vt.|通知，告知，报告|To give someone facts or information.|例句：Please inform me of any changes. 请告知我任何变化。|例句：The police informed him of his rights. 警方告知了他他的权利。|近义词：tell, notify, advise|反义词：misinform, conceal


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He informs me of the latest news daily. 他每天都通知我最新的消息。
They inform their clients of any potential risks. 他们会告知他们的客户任何潜在的风险。

一般过去时:
She informed her boss about the problem. 她把这个问题告诉了她的老板。
The teacher informed the students of the exam schedule. 老师告知学生考试时间表。

现在完成时:
He has informed me that he will be late. 他已经通知我他要迟到了。
She has informed the authorities of the incident. 她已经向当局报告了此事。

过去完成时:
I had informed him of the meeting before he left.  在他离开之前我已经通知他会议了。
They had informed the press about the scandal. 他们之前已经向媒体通报了这个丑闻。


将来时:
He will inform us as soon as he arrives. 他一到就会通知我们。
They will inform the public of their decision soon. 他们很快就会向公众宣布他们的决定。

被动语态:
I was informed of the cancellation. 我被告知取消了。
The employees were informed about the company's new policy. 员工们被告知公司的新政策。

疑问句:
Did he inform you of the meeting? 他有没有告诉你会议的事？
Will they inform us of the results? 他们会告诉我们结果吗？

否定句:
He didn't inform me about the change of plans. 他没有告诉我计划的改变。
They did not inform the public of the dangers. 他们没有向公众告知危险。


PHRASE->
inform against = 告发
keep someone informed =  随时向某人汇报
well-informed =  消息灵通的
be informed by = 受...的影响
fully informed = 完全知情的

词源:
From Old French *informer, from Latin informare "to shape, form," from in- "in" + formare "to form," from forma "shape."  The sense evolved from "to give shape or form to" to "to give information to."
