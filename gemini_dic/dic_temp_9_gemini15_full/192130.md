scrabble:/ˈskræbəl/ | v., n. | 摸索；拼字游戏 |

英文解析:
As a verb, "scrabble" means to move about quickly or clumsily in search of something.  It can also refer to scratching or scraping. As a noun, "scrabble" primarily refers to the popular word game where players form words from letter tiles.

例句:
The dog scrabbled at the door, trying to get in. = 狗在门口抓挠，试图进去。

The children scrabbled for the dropped sweets. = 孩子们争抢掉在地上的糖果。


We played a game of Scrabble last night. = 我们昨晚玩了一局拼字游戏。

She scrabbled her letters together, trying to find the right word. = 她把字母拼凑在一起，试图找到合适的单词。


近义词:
(verb) scramble, fumble, claw, scrape

反义词:
(verb)  None directly opposite, but antonyms could relate to the opposite of searching clumsily (e.g., find easily, locate precisely)

词性:
Verb, Noun

词源:
The word "scrabble" originates from the Middle English word "scrabblen," meaning "to scratch."  This in turn likely comes from a combination of Old Norse and Old English roots suggesting scratching or scraping motions. The modern usage of the word, including the board game, developed later.
