microcinematography:/ˌmaɪkroʊˌsɪnəməˈtɑːɡrəfi/ | n. | 显微电影摄影术 | The technique of photographing microscopic objects or processes, often using time-lapse photography to show changes over time.  It involves using a microscope combined with a camera to record images at a very small scale. | Example:  Researchers used microcinematography to study the growth and division of cells.  研究人员使用显微电影摄影术来研究细胞的生长和分裂。 | 近义词：microphotography, time-lapse microscopy | 反义词：macrophotography
