cheapened:/ˈtʃiːpənd/| v.|使廉价；贬低；降低价值 |To reduce the value or quality of something, making it less respected or desirable.  |例句：The mass production of the product cheapened its value. 大规模生产使该产品贬值了。

例句：His constant boasting cheapened his achievements. 他不断的吹嘘贬低了他的成就。


近义词：devalued, degraded, diminished, lowered

反义词：enhanced, improved, elevated, valued


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He cheapens his work by taking shortcuts. = 他通过走捷径来贬低他的工作。
The company cheapens its products by using inferior materials. = 公司通过使用劣质材料来降低其产品价值。


一般过去时:
The scandal cheapened the reputation of the politician. = 丑闻损害了这位政治家的声誉。
Mass production cheapened the cost of the goods. = 大规模生产降低了商品的成本。


现在完成时:
The overuse of the word has cheapened its meaning. = 这个词的过度使用已经贬低了它的含义。
Repeated discounts have cheapened the brand's image. = 反复打折已经损害了品牌的形象。


过去完成时:
They had cheapened the experience by rushing through it. = 他们因为仓促行事而贬低了这次经历。
The repeated compromises had cheapened the original vision. = 反复的妥协已经贬低了最初的愿景。


将来时:
The new marketing strategy will cheapen the brand. = 新的营销策略将贬低品牌形象。
Further price reductions will cheapen the product even more. = 进一步降价将使产品更廉价。


虚拟语气:
If they hadn't cut corners, the product wouldn't have been cheapened. = 如果他们没有偷工减料，产品就不会贬值。
I wish they hadn't cheapened the design with those unnecessary additions. = 我希望他们没有用那些不必要的添加物来贬低设计。


被动语态:
The brand was cheapened by the negative publicity. = 该品牌因负面宣传而贬值。
The quality of the product was cheapened by the use of substandard materials. = 产品的质量因使用劣质材料而降低。


疑问句:

否定句:
