legitimist:/ləˈdʒɪtɪmɪst/| n. |合法主义者，拥护王位继承权者 |A person who supports the principle of legitimacy, especially the hereditary right of monarchs.  A legitimist believes that the only rightful rulers are those who have inherited their power through a legitimate line of succession, typically through hereditary right. They often oppose revolutions or other changes that disrupt established power structures. |The legitimist movement in France struggled for decades to restore the monarchy. |royalist, monarchist |republican, revolutionary |名词
