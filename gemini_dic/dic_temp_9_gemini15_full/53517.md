disaster:/dɪˈzɑːstər/| n.|灾难，灾祸|A disaster is a sudden event, such as an accident or a natural catastrophe, that causes great damage or suffering.|例句：The earthquake was a terrible disaster. 那场地震是一场可怕的灾难。|例句：The fire was a major disaster, destroying many homes. 那场大火是一场重大的灾难，摧毁了许多房屋。|近义词：catastrophe, calamity, tragedy|反义词：blessing, boon, success


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The hurricane poses a disaster to the coastal communities. =飓风对沿海社区构成灾难。
This project is a disaster in the making. =这个项目正在走向灾难。

一般过去时:
The flood was a major disaster. = 那次洪水是一场严重的灾难。
The company's bankruptcy was a financial disaster. = 公司的破产是一场金融灾难。

现在完成时:
The earthquake has caused a major disaster. = 地震已经造成了巨大的灾难。
Many disasters have struck the region in recent years. = 近年来该地区发生了许多灾难。

过去完成时:
The dam had failed before the full extent of the disaster became apparent. = 大坝在灾难的全部严重性显现出来之前就已经垮塌了。
They had never experienced such a disaster before. = 他们以前从未经历过这样的灾难。


将来时:
The impending storm could cause a disaster. = 即将来临的风暴可能会造成灾难。
Experts predict that climate change will lead to more disasters. = 专家预测气候变化将导致更多灾难。


虚拟语气:
If there had been more warning, the disaster could have been prevented. = 如果有更多预警，这场灾难本来是可以避免的。
I wish the disaster hadn't happened. = 我希望这场灾难没有发生。


被动语态:
The city was devastated by the disaster. = 城市被灾难摧毁了。
Many lives were lost in the disaster. = 许多人在灾难中丧生。


疑问句:
What caused the disaster? = 导致这场灾难的原因是什么？
What measures are in place to prevent future disasters? = 为了防止未来的灾难，有哪些措施到位？


否定句:
The situation wasn't a disaster; it was manageable. = 情况并非灾难；它是可控的。
It wasn't a natural disaster; it was caused by human error. = 这不是自然灾害；这是人为错误造成的。
