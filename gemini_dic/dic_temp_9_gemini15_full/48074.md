declare:/dɪˈklɛər/| vt.|宣布，声明，宣告|to make something known officially or publicly; to state something firmly or clearly|例句：The government declared a state of emergency. 政府宣布进入紧急状态。|例句：She declared her love for him. 她向他表白了爱意。|近义词：announce, proclaim, assert|反义词：conceal, hide, suppress|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The judge declares the defendant guilty. = 法官宣判被告有罪。
They declare their independence. = 他们宣布独立。

一般过去时:
He declared war on his rival. = 他向他的竞争对手宣战。
She declared her intention to run for office. = 她宣布她打算竞选公职。

现在完成时:
They have declared their support for the project. = 他们已经宣布支持该项目。
The company has declared bankruptcy. = 公司已经宣布破产。

过去完成时:
He had declared his love before she left. = 她离开之前，他已经表白了。
They had declared a truce before the negotiations began. = 谈判开始前，他们已经宣布停战。

将来时:
The president will declare a national holiday. = 总统将宣布全国假日。
They will declare the winner after the final count. = 他们将在最终统计后宣布获胜者。

虚拟语气:
If he declared his love, she might accept. = 如果他表白，她可能会接受。
I wish he would declare his candidacy. = 我希望他会宣布竞选。

被动语态:
The winner was declared after a long delay. = 获胜者在长时间的延迟后被宣布。
The war was declared in 1939. = 战争于1939年爆发。

疑问句:
Will the government declare a new policy? = 政府会宣布一项新政策吗？
Did they declare their assets? = 他们申报了他们的资产吗？

否定句:
He did not declare his income. = 他没有申报他的收入。
The judge did not declare a mistrial. = 法官没有宣布审判无效。


PHRASE->
declare war = 宣战
declare bankruptcy = 宣告破产
declare independence = 宣布独立
declare oneself = 表明自己的立场
declare an emergency = 宣布紧急状态


词源:
From Old French *declarer, from Latin declarare "to make clear, explain," from clarus "clear."  The word's evolution reflects its core meaning: making something clear or known.
