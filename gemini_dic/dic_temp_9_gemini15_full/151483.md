parallel: /ˈpærəlel/ | adj., n. | 平行的；类似的；相似的 |  A parallel is a line, plane, or surface that is everywhere equidistant from another; something that is similar or analogous to something else.  


英文解析:
As an adjective, "parallel" describes lines, surfaces, or events that are everywhere equidistant and never meet. As a noun, it refers to such lines or surfaces, or to something that is similar or analogous to something else.
