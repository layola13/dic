minces:/mɪns/| vt. n.|切碎；剁碎；做作；矫揉造作|To cut (something, especially meat) into very small pieces.  Also, to speak or behave in an affectedly refined or delicate way.|例句：She minced the garlic before adding it to the sauce. 她把蒜切碎后加到酱汁里。|例句：He minced his words, unwilling to directly address the issue. 他说话吞吞吐吐，不愿直接谈论这个问题。|近义词：chop, dice, mince (v.), affect, pretend|反义词：hack, coarsely chop, straightforward, direct|动词

词源:
The verb "mince" ultimately derives from the Old French word "mincier," meaning "to cut small" or "to chop."  This, in turn, has roots in the Latin word "minuere," meaning "to lessen" or "to diminish," reflecting the action of reducing something to smaller pieces.
