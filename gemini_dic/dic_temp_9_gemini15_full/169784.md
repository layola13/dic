prorogue:/ˈprɒroʊɡ/ /ˈproʊroʊɡ/| v.|休会；延期；|To bring (a session of a parliament or legislature) to a temporary end.|The parliament was prorogued until the autumn.  议会休会到秋季。|The King prorogued Parliament. 国王将议会休会。| adjourn, suspend, postpone | resume, continue |动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The Senate prorogues every year. = 参议院每年休会。
Parliament prorogues for the summer. = 议会休会过夏天。


一般过去时:
The Queen prorogued Parliament last week. = 女王上周休会了议会。
They prorogued the meeting until next Monday. = 他们将会议延期到下周一。


现在完成时:
Parliament has been prorogued several times this year. = 议会今年已经休会过几次了。
The government has prorogued the session unexpectedly. = 政府出人意料地休会了。


过去完成时:
The bill had been prorogued before the election. = 该法案在选举前已被休会。
They had prorogued the debate for a week before reaching a decision. = 他们休会了一周的辩论才做出决定。


将来时:
The Prime Minister will prorogue Parliament next month. = 首相下个月将休会议会。
They will prorogue the conference until further notice. = 他们将会议延期，直到另行通知。


虚拟语气:
If the government prorogued Parliament, it would face criticism. = 如果政府休会，它将面临批评。
I wish they hadn't prorogued the meeting so abruptly. = 我真希望他们不要如此突然地休会。


被动语态:
Parliament was prorogued by the monarch. = 议会被君主休会。
The session was prorogued due to unforeseen circumstances. = 会议由于不可预见的情况而被休会。


疑问句:
Will the Parliament be prorogued soon? = 议会很快就会休会吗？
Was the meeting prorogued yesterday? = 会议昨天休会了吗？


否定句:
The government did not prorogue Parliament this year. = 政府今年没有休会议会。
They did not prorogue the negotiations despite the disagreements. = 尽管存在分歧，他们也没有中断谈判。
