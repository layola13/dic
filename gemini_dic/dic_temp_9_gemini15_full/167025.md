presuccessful: /ˌpriːsəkˈsɛsfəl/ | adj. |  预先成功的；预示成功的 |  Describing something that indicates or suggests future success; showing promise of future success.  It implies a strong likelihood of future achievement based on current indicators. |  例句：Her presuccessful early career hinted at a future filled with awards and recognition.  她的早期职业生涯预示着未来将获得诸多奖项和认可。


The presuccessful research findings encouraged further investment in the project.  预先成功的研究结果促使了对该项目的进一步投资。


近义词：promising, auspicious, encouraging, hopeful | 反义词：unpromising, inauspicious, discouraging, bleak


词源:  The word is a combination of "pre-" (meaning "before" or "in advance of") and "successful" (meaning "achieving desired aims or results").  It's a relatively modern coinage, not found in older dictionaries, reflecting a contemporary need to describe the potential for future success based on present indications.
