looniness:/ˈluːninis/| n. |疯狂；古怪；愚蠢|The state or quality of being loony; extreme foolishness or craziness.  It often implies a whimsical or harmless kind of madness, rather than dangerous or harmful insanity.|例句：His looniness was evident in his decision to paint his house bright pink.  他的古怪在决定把房子漆成亮粉色就显而易见了。

例句：Her looniness manifested itself in a series of bizarre and unpredictable actions. 她的疯狂表现为一系列古怪和不可预测的行为。


近义词：craziness, madness, silliness, absurdity, eccentricity

反义词：sanity, rationality, sense, normalcy


词源:From *loony* + *-ness*.  *Loony* itself is a variant of *looney*, ultimately derived from the obsolete word *loon* meaning "a foolish or crazy person". The word *loon* in this sense is of uncertain origin, possibly connected to the Old English word *leor* meaning "face" or "visage" - implying someone with a vacant or foolish expression.
