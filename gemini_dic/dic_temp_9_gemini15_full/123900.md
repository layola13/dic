mordants:/ˈmɔːrdənts/ | n. |媒染剂；刻薄的人|A mordant is a substance used in dyeing to fix the dye and make the color fast.  It can also refer to something that is biting or sarcastic. |例句：The fabric was treated with a mordant to ensure the color wouldn't fade.  这块布料经过媒染剂处理，以确保颜色不会褪色。|例句：His mordant wit made him a feared but respected commentator. 他辛辣的机智使他成为一个令人畏惧但受人尊敬的评论员。|近义词：fixative, astringent, caustic, sarcastic |反义词：bland, mild, gentle

词源:
From Old French *mordant, present participle of mordre "to bite," from Vulgar Latin *mordere, from Latin mordēre "to bite."  The sense development is from "biting" (as in a sharp, sarcastic remark) to "fixing" (as in a substance that bites into the fabric to hold the dye).
