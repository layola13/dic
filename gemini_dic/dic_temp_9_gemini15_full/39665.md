conservator: /kənˈsɜːrveɪtər/ | n. | 保护者；馆长；保守派 | A conservator is a person who preserves or restores something, especially works of art or historical objects.  A conservator of a museum is responsible for the care and preservation of its collections. | The museum conservator carefully cleaned the ancient artifact.  博物馆馆长仔细地清洁了古代文物。 | curator, preserver, custodian | vandal, destroyer | 名词


conservatorial: /ˌkɒnsɜːvəˈtɔːriəl/ | adj. | 保护的；保存的；馆长的 | Relating to the work of a conservator; concerned with the preservation of something. | The conservatorial work involved meticulous cleaning and repair of the paintings.  保护工作包括对绘画进行细致的清洁和修复。 | preservative, protective | destructive, damaging | 形容词


conservatorial: /ˌkɒnsɜːvəˈtɔːriəl/ | adj. | 保守的；守旧的 |  Relating to or characteristic of conservatism. (Less common usage compared to "conservative") | His conservatorial views on social issues were often challenged. 他关于社会问题的保守观点经常受到挑战。 | conservative, traditional | liberal, progressive | 形容词
