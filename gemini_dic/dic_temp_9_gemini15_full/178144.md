recognized: /ˈrɛkəɡnaɪzd/ | v. | 认出，识别，承认 | to identify someone or something or to accept something as true or valid | 近义词：identified, acknowledged, accepted | 反义词：ignored, overlooked, denied


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The police recognized the suspect from a security camera. = 警方从监控录像中认出了嫌疑人。
She recognized her childhood friend after many years. = 多年后她认出了她的童年朋友。

一般过去时:
I recognized his voice immediately. = 我立刻就认出了他的声音。
The judge recognized the evidence as valid. = 法官承认证据有效。

现在完成时:
Scientists have recognized the importance of environmental protection. = 科学家们已经认识到环境保护的重要性。
She has recognized her mistake and apologized. = 她已经认识到自己的错误并道歉了。

过去完成时:
I had recognized his pattern of behavior before the incident. = 在事件发生前我已经认出了他的行为模式。
They had recognized the need for change long before the crisis. = 在危机很久之前，他们就已经意识到了变革的必要性。

将来时:
They will recognize the benefits of this new technology soon. = 他们很快就会认识到这项新技术的益处。
I will easily recognize him in the crowd. = 我很容易就能在人群中认出他。

虚拟语气:
If he had recognized the danger, he wouldn't have gone there. = 如果他认识到危险，他就不会去了那里。
I wish I had recognized the signs earlier. = 我希望我早点意识到这些迹象。

被动语态:
His contributions were finally recognized by the company. = 他的贡献最终得到了公司的认可。
The problem was recognized as urgent. = 这个问题被认为是紧急的。

疑问句:
Do you recognize that song? = 你认得那首歌吗？
Did you recognize him from the photograph? = 你从照片上认出他了吗？

否定句:
I didn't recognize him at first. = 我一开始没认出他。
The court didn't recognize the contract as legally binding. = 法院不承认该合同具有法律约束力。
