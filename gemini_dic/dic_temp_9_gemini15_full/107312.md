lambasted:/læmˈbæs.tɪd/| vt.|痛斥；严厉批评；猛烈抨击 |To criticize someone or something severely.  |例句：The critics lambasted the movie for its poor plot and acting. 评论家们痛斥这部电影的情节和演技都很糟糕。

例句：The politician was lambasted for his controversial remarks.  这位政治家因其有争议的言论而受到猛烈抨击。


近义词：criticize, denounce, rebuke, condemn, chastise, excoriate

反义词：praise, commend, applaud, acclaim

词源:
The word "lambasted" comes from the now-obsolete verb "lambast," which is believed to have originated in the early 17th century, likely from a combination of Dutch or Low German words.  The exact etymology is uncertain, but the suggested origins involve words associated with beating or striking, reflecting the forceful nature of the act of criticizing heavily.
