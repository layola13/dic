pro-Jacobean: /ˌproʊdʒəˈkoʊbiən/ | adj. | 雅各布一世风格的，雅各布一世时期之前的 |  Relating to the period immediately preceding the reign of King James I of England (1603-1625), often characterized by a transitional style in art and architecture bridging the Elizabethan and Jacobean periods.  The term often encompasses the late Elizabethan era's stylistic developments that foreshadowed the Jacobean style. |

例句：

The pro-Jacobean furniture displayed a blend of late Elizabethan and early Jacobean features. = 这件雅各布一世风格之前的家具融合了晚期伊丽莎白风格和早期雅各布风格的特点。


The artist's pro-Jacobean paintings show a clear progression toward the more ornate Jacobean style. =  这位艺术家的雅各布一世风格之前的绘画作品清晰地展现了向更加华丽的雅各布风格过渡的趋势。



近义词：late Elizabethan, pre-Jacobean

反义词：post-Jacobean, Jacobean


词性：形容词
