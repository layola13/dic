reintervene: /ˌriːɪntərˈviːn/ | vt. | 再干预；再次介入 | To intervene again; to become involved again in a situation.  | 例句：The government had to reintervene in the economic crisis after the initial measures proved insufficient. 政府在最初的措施证明不足后不得不再次干预经济危机。

例句：The United Nations decided to reintervene in the conflict after a ceasefire agreement was broken.  联合国在停火协议被破坏后决定再次介入冲突。


近义词：intercede again,  re-engage,  interfere again

反义词：withdraw, abstain, ignore


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They reintervene in disputes regularly. = 他们定期地再次介入纠纷。
The committee reintervenes when necessary. = 委员会在必要时会再次介入。


一般过去时:
The mediator reintervened to prevent further violence. = 调解员再次介入以防止进一步的暴力。
The bank reintervened in the market to stabilize the currency. = 银行再次干预市场以稳定货币。


现在完成时:
The army has reintervened several times this year. = 军队今年已经多次再次介入。
The court has reintervened to protect the rights of the minority. = 法院再次介入以保护少数民族的权利。


过去完成时:
They had reintervened before the situation escalated. = 在局势升级之前，他们已经再次介入。
The government had reintervened several times before the crisis was resolved. = 在危机解决之前，政府已经多次再次介入。


将来时:

词源:
re-(again) + intervene (to come between; to interfere)  re- 来自拉丁语，表示“再次”；intervene 来自拉丁语 *intervenire,  inter-  "between" + venire "to come".
