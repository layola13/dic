lackadaisical:/ˌlækəˈdeɪzɪkəl/| adj.|懒散的；无精打采的；倦怠的|lackadaisical describes someone who is not showing much interest or enthusiasm. It suggests a careless and languid attitude.|The lackadaisical student barely studied for the exam.|近义词：listless, lethargic, indolent, apathetic|反义词：energetic, enthusiastic, diligent, zealous


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is lackadaisical in his approach to work. = 他对待工作很懒散。
She is always lackadaisical about cleaning her room. = 她总是很懒散地打扫房间。


一般过去时:
He was lackadaisical in his response to the crisis. = 他对危机的反应很懒散。
She was lackadaisical about her studies, resulting in poor grades. = 她学习很懒散，导致成绩很差。


现在完成时:

过去完成时:

She had been lackadaisical about her appearance, so the event spurred her to revamp her style. = 她对自己的外表很懒散，所以这次活动促使她改变了风格。

词源:
The word "lackadaisical" is believed to have originated as a playful alteration of "lack-a-daisy," an expression of mild disappointment or weariness.  Over time, it evolved into its current meaning, with the "lack" suggesting a deficiency of energy and enthusiasm.
