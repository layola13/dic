imperfect:/ɪmˈpɜːrfɪkt/| adj.|不完美的，有缺点的|lacking some of the qualities considered necessary for perfection or completeness.|The plan was imperfect, but it was the best we could devise.|近义词：flawed, defective, incomplete|反义词：perfect, flawless, ideal


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The system is imperfect. = 这个系统不完美。
His work is imperfect in several places. = 他的作品在几个地方不完美。

一般过去时:
The design was imperfect. = 设计不完美。
My understanding of the situation was imperfect. = 我对情况的理解不完善。

现在完成时:
My attempts have been imperfect. = 我的尝试一直不完美。
We have realized that our initial plan was imperfect. = 我们已经意识到我们最初的计划不完美。

过去完成时:
I realized my previous analysis had been imperfect. = 我意识到我之前的分析不完善。
They discovered that their earlier calculations had been imperfect. = 他们发现他们早期的计算有误。

将来时:
The final product may be imperfect. = 最终产品可能不完美。
Future revisions will hopefully correct the imperfect aspects. = 未来的修订有望纠正不完美之处。

虚拟语气:
If the process were perfect, the result would be perfect, but it is imperfect. = 如果过程完美，结果就会完美，但它并不完美。
I wish my work were perfect, but it is imperfect. = 我希望我的作品完美无瑕，但它并不完美。

被动语态:
The product was considered imperfect by the customers. = 产品被顾客认为不完美。
The flaws in the design were deemed imperfect by the engineers. = 设计中的缺陷被工程师认为是不完美的。

疑问句:
Is the solution imperfect? = 解决方案不完美吗？
Are there any imperfect aspects of this proposal? = 这个提案有什么不完美的地方吗？

否定句:
The painting is not imperfect; it's simply different. = 这幅画并不完美；它只是不同而已。
The system isn't imperfect; it just needs some fine-tuning. = 系统并不完美；它只需要一些微调。
