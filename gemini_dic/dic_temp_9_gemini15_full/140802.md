on-call:/ˌɒnˈkɔːl/ /ˌɑːnˈkɔːl/ | adj. |随时待命；值班的 | Ready and available to respond to a call or request for service, typically outside of regular working hours.  |例句：The doctor is on-call tonight.  今晚医生值班。
The IT support team is on-call 24/7.  IT支持团队全天候待命。


近义词：on duty, on standby, available | 反义词：off-duty, unavailable


词源:  The phrase "on call" originates from the practice of having workers available by telephone or other means for immediate response to emergencies or urgent requests.  The "call" refers to the summons to perform a service.


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The nurses are on-call this weekend. = 这些护士这个周末值班。
He is on-call for emergencies. = 他随时待命处理紧急情况。

一般过去时:
The surgeon was on-call all night. = 外科医生整夜值班。
She was on-call during the holiday season. = 节日期间她处于待命状态。

现在完成时:
They have been on-call for three hours. = 他们已经待命三个小时了。
I have been on-call since 6 pm. = 我从下午6点起就一直在待命。

过去完成时:
The doctor had been on-call for 24 hours before he got a break. = 医生连续值班24小时后才休息。
They had been on-call all week before the major incident occurred. = 在重大事件发生之前，他们已经值班了一周。


将来时:
I will be on-call next week. = 我下周要值班。
The team will be on-call throughout the conference. = 在整个会议期间，团队将处于待命状态。


虚拟语气:
If I were on-call, I would have responded immediately. = 如果我当时值班，我会立即做出回应。
It would be helpful if more staff were on-call during peak hours. = 如果在高峰时段有更多员工待命，那就太好了。


被动语态:
He was called in while he was on-call. = 他值班时被叫来。
Many doctors are expected to be on call. = 许多医生都必须待命。


疑问句:

否定句:
