reappraise:/ˌriːəˈpreɪz/ | vt. |重新评估；重新评价 |To evaluate or assess something again.  |例句：The committee decided to reappraise the property after discovering structural damage. 委员会在发现结构性损坏后决定重新评估该房产。

例句：We need to reappraise our marketing strategy in light of the recent sales figures.鉴于最近的销售数据，我们需要重新评估我们的营销策略。


近义词：reassess, re-evaluate, reassess, review | 反义词：accept, endorse


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They reappraise the artwork every year. = 他们每年都会重新评估这件艺术品。
The company reappraises its employees' performance annually. = 公司每年都会重新评估员工的表现。


一般过去时:
The insurance company reappraised the damaged car. = 保险公司重新评估了损坏的汽车。
The judge reappraised the evidence before making a decision. = 法官在做出决定之前重新评估了证据。


现在完成时:
The government has reappraised its economic policies. = 政府已经重新评估了其经济政策。
The school has reappraised its curriculum to better meet student needs. = 学校已经重新评估了其课程，以更好地满足学生的需要。


过去完成时:
They had reappraised the house before putting it on the market. = 他们在将房子上市之前已经重新评估了它。
The architect had reappraised the structural integrity before giving the go-ahead. = 建筑师在批准之前已经重新评估了结构的完整性。


将来时:
The bank will reappraise the loan application next week. = 银行下周将重新评估贷款申请。
The team will reappraise the project's feasibility after the initial trials. = 在初步试验之后，团队将重新评估该项目的可行性。


虚拟语气:
If we reappraised the situation, we might find a better solution. = 如果我们重新评估一下情况，我们可能会找到更好的解决方案。
I wish they had reappraised the risks before starting the project. = 我希望他们在开始项目之前重新评估过风险。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
