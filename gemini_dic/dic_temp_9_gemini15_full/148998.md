ovoviviparously:/ˌoʊvoʊvaɪvɪˈpærəsli/| adv. |卵胎生地；卵胎生方式地 | Adverb describing the manner of reproduction in which eggs are hatched within the body of the parent, with the young being born alive.  | Example:  The reptile reproduced ovoviviparously, giving birth to live young.  爬行动物卵胎生，产下活的幼崽。  | 近义词: viviparously (in some contexts) | 反义词: oviparously


USAGE->
This word is an adverb and doesn't have verb conjugations like "what".  It describes *how* something happens, not an action itself.  Therefore, tense variations are not applicable.  The usage is always in the same form.

Example 1: The snake gave birth ovoviviparously. 这条蛇卵胎生。
Example 2: Many species of sharks reproduce ovoviviparously. 许多种类的鲨鱼都是卵胎生的。


PHRASE->
There are no common phrases directly using "ovoviviparously".  The word itself is used to describe a specific reproductive method.
