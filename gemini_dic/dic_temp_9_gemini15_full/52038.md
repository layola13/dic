dialects:/ˈdaɪəlekt/| n.|方言，土语|A dialect is a variety of a language that is spoken in a particular region or by a particular social group.  Dialects differ from the standard language in pronunciation, vocabulary, and grammar. |Example: The Scottish dialects are quite different from standard English. 苏格兰方言与标准英语大相径庭。


The Cockney dialect is spoken in East London.  伦敦东区通行考克尼方言。|近义词：patois, vernacular|反义词：standard language
