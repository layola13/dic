reelevated:/ˌriːˈɛləveɪtɪd/| adj.|重新提升的；再次提高的；被重新赋予地位的 | Raised or elevated again; restored to a higher position or status.  The reevaluated plan was finally approved.  |近义词：reinstated, upgraded, promoted |反义词：degraded, demoted, lowered


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The city reevaluates its infrastructure plans annually. = 该城市每年都会重新评估其基础设施规划。
This policy reevaluates the needs of the elderly population. = 这一政策重新评估了老年人的需求。


一般过去时:
The committee reevaluated the budget after discovering errors. = 委员会在发现错误后重新评估了预算。
They reevaluated their marketing strategy after poor sales. = 销售业绩不佳后，他们重新评估了营销策略。


现在完成时:
The government has reevaluated its approach to climate change. = 政府已经重新评估了其应对气候变化的方法。
The company has reevaluated its hiring practices following complaints. = 公司在接到投诉后已经重新评估了其招聘流程。


过去完成时:
After the incident, they had reevaluated their security protocols. = 事故发生后，他们已经重新评估了安全协议。
He had reevaluated his career path before making the switch. = 他在转换职业道路之前已经重新评估了自己的职业道路。


将来时:
The council will reevaluate the zoning regulations next year. = 委员会明年将重新评估分区条例。
We will reevaluate the project's feasibility after gathering more data. = 我们将在收集更多数据后重新评估项目的可行性。


虚拟语气:
If the data were different, we would reevaluate our conclusions. = 如果数据不同，我们将重新评估我们的结论。
I wish they had reevaluated the situation before acting. = 我希望他们在采取行动之前已经重新评估了情况。


被动语态:
The proposal was reevaluated by the board of directors. = 该提案由董事会重新评估。
The project's timeline was reevaluated due to unforeseen circumstances. = 由于不可预见的情况，项目的时间表被重新评估。


疑问句:

否定句:
