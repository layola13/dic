languishers:/ˈlæŋɡwɪʃərz/| n.|苦苦挣扎的人；衰弱的人；萎靡不振的人|People or things that are languishing; those experiencing a period of stagnation or decline.  It refers to individuals or entities suffering from neglect, hardship, or a lack of progress. |例句：The languishers in the refugee camp were in desperate need of food and medical attention. 难民营里苦苦挣扎的人们急需食物和医疗救助。


The abandoned project languished, a testament to the company's mismanagement.  被遗弃的项目停滞不前，证明了公司管理不善。|近义词：sufferers, victims, the neglected, the downtrodden|反义词：thrivers, prosperers, the successful


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The prisoners languish in their cells. = 囚犯们在牢房里苦苦挣扎。
Many businesses languish due to lack of investment. = 许多企业由于缺乏投资而停滞不前。

一般过去时:
The flowers languished without water. = 花儿缺水枯萎了。
The economy languished for several years after the crisis. = 危机过后，经济停滞了几年。

现在完成时:
The project has languished for months. = 项目已经停滞了几个月了。
He has languished in poverty for years. = 他多年来一直生活在贫困中。

过去完成时:
The company had languished before the new CEO arrived. = 在新任首席执行官到来之前，公司一直处于停滞状态。
The town had languished for decades before the new highway was built. = 在新建高速公路之前，这个小镇已经衰落了几十年。

将来时:
The project will languish unless we get more funding. = 除非我们获得更多资金，否则该项目将停滞不前。
Without proper care, the plants will languish. = 如果没有适当的照顾，植物将会枯萎。

虚拟语气:
If we didn't get more support, the project would languish. = 如果我们得不到更多支持，项目就会停滞不前。
I wish the situation wouldn't languish. = 我希望这种情况不会持续恶化。

被动语态:
The plan was allowed to languish. = 该计划被搁置了。
The issue has been allowed to languish for too long. = 这个问题被搁置了太久。

疑问句:
Why is the project languishing? = 为什么项目停滞不前？
Are the employees languishing due to low morale? = 员工士气低落导致工作停滞不前吗？

否定句:
The business didn't languish; it thrived. = 这家企业并没有停滞不前；它蓬勃发展。
The plants didn't languish; they flourished. = 植物没有枯萎；它们茁壮成长。
