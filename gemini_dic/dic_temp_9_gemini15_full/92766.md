ichthyornis:/ɪkˈθɪɔːrnɪs/| n. |鱼鸟|An extinct genus of toothed birds from the Late Cretaceous period.  They were likely strong fliers with a long, pointed beak and sharp teeth. They were closely related to modern birds but possessed characteristics more reminiscent of reptiles, such as teeth.  |Example:  Ichthyornis's fossilized remains have provided valuable insights into the evolution of birds.|近义词: Hesperornis (another extinct toothed bird)|反义词: None (as it refers to a specific extinct species)|名词
