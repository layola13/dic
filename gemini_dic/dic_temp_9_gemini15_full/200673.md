slee:/sliː/| n.|睡，睡眠|sleep is a natural periodic state of rest for the body and mind, during which the eyes are closed and consciousness is largely lost.|例句：I need eight hours of sleep every night. 我每晚需要睡八个小时。|例句：The baby fell asleep during the story. 宝宝在听故事的时候睡着了。|近义词：nap, slumber, doze|反义词：wakefulness, insomnia


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He sleeps eight hours a day. = 他每天睡八个小时。
She sleeps soundly every night. = 她每晚睡得很香。

一般过去时:
I slept well last night. = 我昨晚睡得很好。
He slept through the storm. = 他睡过了这场暴风雨。

现在完成时:
I have slept badly for weeks. = 我已经连续几周睡得很糟糕了。
She has slept only four hours. = 她只睡了四个小时。

过去完成时:
I had slept poorly the night before the exam. = 我考试前一天晚上睡得很不好。
They had slept for ten hours before waking up. = 他们睡了十个小时才醒来。

将来时:
I will sleep better tonight. = 我今晚会睡得更好。
We will sleep in tomorrow. = 我们明天睡个懒觉。

虚拟语气:
If I had slept better, I would have done better on the test. = 如果我睡得更好，考试成绩就会更好。
I wish I could sleep longer. = 我希望我能睡久一点。

被动语态:
(The verb "sleep" is rarely used in the passive voice.)  The baby was often put to sleep with a lullaby. = 宝宝经常被摇篮曲哄睡。
The patient was kept asleep during the operation. = 病人在手术期间一直处于睡眠状态。

疑问句:
Did you sleep well? = 你睡得好吗？
How long did you sleep? = 你睡了多久？

否定句:
I didn't sleep at all last night. = 我昨晚根本没睡。
She couldn't sleep because of the noise. = 因为噪音她睡不着。
