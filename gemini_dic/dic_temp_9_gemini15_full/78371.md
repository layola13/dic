gesticulator: /dʒɛˈstɪkjʊleɪtər/ | n. | 手势表达者，打手势的人 | A person who uses gestures, especially excessively.  |  He was a flamboyant gesticulator, punctuating his every word with dramatic hand movements. 他是一个夸夸其谈、喜欢打手势的人，他用夸张的手势来强调每一个词。  |  mimicker, demonstrator |  (反义词较难直接对应，可考虑描述其相反的特性，例如：reserved person, quiet person) | 名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The professor gesticulates while lecturing. = 教授在讲课时打手势。
She gesticulates expressively when she speaks. = 她说话时会做一些富有表现力的肢体动作。


一般过去时:
He gesticulated wildly to emphasize his point. = 他激动地打手势来强调他的观点。
The speaker gesticulated throughout his speech. = 演讲者在整个演讲过程中都在打手势。


现在完成时:

She has gesticulated so much that her arms are tired. = 她做了那么多手势，以至于她的胳膊都累了。

They had gesticulated at each other across the room. = 他们隔着房间互相做手势。

虚拟语气:
