interlingualisms:/ˌɪntərˈlɪŋɡwəlɪzəm/| n. |语言交融现象，语言混合|The phenomenon of mixing elements from different languages in speech or writing.  It encompasses code-switching, borrowing, and other forms of linguistic contact. This can manifest in various ways, from simple loanwords to more complex hybrid structures and grammatical patterns.  The study of interlingualisms provides insights into language contact, language change, and the dynamic nature of language use. | Example:  The use of "okay" (from English) in a primarily Spanish conversation is an example of interlingualism.  The frequent borrowing of English words into Japanese is another example. |近义词:code-switching, language mixing, linguistic borrowing |反义词:linguistic purity (a state rarely achieved)
