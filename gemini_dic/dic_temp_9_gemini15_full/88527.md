hit bottom:/hɪt ˈbɑːtəm/ | v. |触底；跌至谷底；陷入困境 | To reach the lowest point, physically or figuratively.  It implies a decline or a crisis reaching its nadir. |例句：The stock market hit bottom after the financial crisis. 股市在金融危机后触底。

The company hit bottom after losing its biggest client.  这家公司在失去最大的客户后陷入困境。|近义词：reach rock bottom, plummet, crash, collapse |反义词：reach the peak, soar, ascend, boom


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The economy hits bottom every ten years. = 经济每十年触底一次。
He hits bottom every time he drinks too much. = 他每次喝太多酒就会跌入谷底。


一般过去时:
The price of oil hit bottom last month. = 油价上个月跌至谷底。
She hit bottom after her marriage ended. = 她的婚姻结束后，她的人生跌入了谷底。


现在完成时:
The value of the house has hit bottom. = 房子的价值已经跌至谷底。
He has hit bottom several times in his life. = 他一生中多次跌入谷底。


过去完成时:
The company had hit bottom before the new CEO arrived. = 新任CEO上任之前，公司已经触底了。
They had hit bottom financially before they sought help. = 他们在寻求帮助之前已经经济上破产了。


将来时:
The market will hit bottom soon. = 市场很快就会触底。
I fear he will hit bottom if he continues down this path. = 我担心如果他继续走这条路，他会跌入谷底。


虚拟语气:
If the company hadn't cut costs, it would have hit bottom long ago. = 如果公司不削减成本，它早就触底了。
If he hadn't gotten help, he might have hit bottom. = 如果他没有得到帮助，他可能会跌入谷底。


被动语态:
