jumped:/dʒʌmpt/ | v. | 跳跃；猛涨；突然移动 | To move quickly and suddenly from one place to another by pushing oneself off the ground with one's legs.  It can also refer to a sudden increase or a sudden change of position. | 例句：The frog jumped into the pond. 青蛙跳进了池塘。 The price of oil jumped dramatically. 油价大幅上涨。 | 近义词：leaped, sprang, hopped | 反义词：stayed, remained


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He jumps rope every morning. = 他每天早上跳绳。
The kangaroo jumps across the field. = 袋鼠跳过田野。


一般过去时:
She jumped for joy. = 她高兴地跳了起来。
The rabbit jumped over the fence. = 兔子跳过了栅栏。


现在完成时:
I have jumped out of airplanes before. = 我以前跳过伞。
The stock market has jumped significantly this week. = 本周股市大幅上涨。


过去完成时:
He had jumped the queue before I arrived. = 我到之前他已经插队了。
The temperature had jumped ten degrees by noon. = 到中午气温已经上升了十度。


将来时:
They will jump into the swimming pool. = 他们将跳进游泳池。
The sales are expected to jump next quarter. = 预计下季度销售额将大幅增长。


虚拟语气:
If I had jumped, I would have won the race. = 如果我跳了，我就能赢得比赛。
I wish I had jumped in the lake. = 我真希望我跳进了湖里。


被动语态:
The ball was jumped on by the dog. = 球被狗跳上去了。(This usage is less common)
The conclusion was jumped to too quickly. = 结论下得太快了。(Figurative use, meaning the conclusion was reached without sufficient evidence)


疑问句:
Did he jump the fence? = 他跳过栅栏了吗？
Will she jump into the water? = 她会跳进水里吗？


否定句:
He didn't jump high enough to reach the branch. = 他跳得不够高，够不着树枝。
The sales didn't jump as much as we hoped. = 销售额没有像我们希望的那样大幅增长。


PHRASE->
jump to conclusions  =  妄下结论
jump the gun =  抢先；操之过急
jump for joy =  高兴地跳起来
jump in =  加入；插手
jump out =  跳出来；脱颖而出
