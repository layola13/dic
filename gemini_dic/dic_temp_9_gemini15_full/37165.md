come-up:/ˈkʌmʌp/| n. v.|出现，发生；崛起，兴起；意外的难题|The sudden come-up of the problem delayed the project. 问题的突然出现延误了项目。|近义词：emergence,rise,occurrence|反义词：decline, disappearance


USAGE->
一般现在时:
A new problem comes up every day. = 每天都会出现新的问题。
The sun comes up in the east. = 太阳从东方升起。

一般过去时:
A good opportunity came up last week. = 上周出现了一个好机会。
The question came up during the meeting. = 这个问题在会议期间被提出。

现在完成时:
Many new challenges have come up since the project started. = 自从项目启动以来，出现了许多新的挑战。
Several issues have come up that need immediate attention. = 出现了一些需要立即处理的问题。

过去完成时:
The issue had come up several times before the final decision. = 在最终决定之前，这个问题已经出现过几次了。
Before the presentation, a technical problem had come up. = 演示之前，出现了一个技术问题。

将来时:
A new opportunity will come up soon. = 不久就会出现一个新的机会。
I think a problem will come up during the trip. = 我认为旅途中会出现问题。


虚拟语气:
If a better solution came up, we would adopt it. = 如果出现更好的解决方案，我们会采纳它。
I wish a better opportunity had come up earlier. = 我希望早点出现更好的机会。

被动语态:
The issue was brought up by the manager. = 这个问题是由经理提出的。(come up is rarely used passively)
The problem was dealt with after it came up. = 这个问题在出现后得到了处理。


疑问句:
What has come up recently? = 最近发生了什么事？
Did anything come up during your meeting? = 你们的会议上发生了什么事吗？

否定句:
Nothing came up during the inspection. = 检查期间没有任何问题出现。
No new difficulties came up in the process. = 这个过程中没有出现新的困难。


PHRASE->
come up against: 遇到（困难）
come up short: 差一点；未能达到
come up with: 想出；提出
come up to: 达到；符合
come up trumps: 成功；获得成功


词源:
The phrasal verb "come up" originates from Old English, combining the verb "come" (from Old English *cuman) and the adverb "up" (from Old English *upp).  Its meaning has evolved over time, reflecting the various ways something can "rise" or "appear."
