seemingly:/ˈsiːmɪŋli/ | adj. |表面上地；看来；似乎 | Apparently; to all appearances.  It describes something that appears to be true or real, but may not actually be.  |例句：He seemingly had no idea what was going on. 他似乎不知道发生了什么事。

例句：The problem seemingly stemmed from a simple misunderstanding.  问题似乎源于一个简单的误解。


近义词：apparently, outwardly, ostensibly, superficially

反义词：actually, really, truly, in reality

词源：

The word "seemingly" derives from the adjective "seeming," which itself comes from the verb "to seem."  "To seem" has Old English roots, stemming from words related to "appearance" or "show."  The "-ingly" suffix adds an adverbial meaning, indicating "in a seeming manner."
