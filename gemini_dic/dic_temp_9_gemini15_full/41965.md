cosponsorships:/ˈkoʊˌspɑːnsərʃɪps/ | n. |共同发起，联名发起 |The act of two or more individuals or entities jointly sponsoring something, such as a bill, event, or project.  This implies shared responsibility and often, shared costs or benefits. |例句：The bill enjoyed widespread support, receiving numerous cosponsorships from both parties. 这项法案得到了广泛的支持，两党都有许多共同发起人。

The senators secured several cosponsorships for their proposed environmental protection legislation. 参议员们为他们提出的环境保护立法获得了数项共同发起。


近义词：joint sponsorship, collaborative sponsorship |反义词：sole sponsorship, individual sponsorship |词性:名词

词源:
The word "cosponsorships" is a compound word.  "Co-" is a prefix meaning "together" or "jointly." "Sponsorship" comes from the word "sponsor," which ultimately derives from Latin *spondere, meaning "to promise."  Therefore, "cosponsorships" literally means "joint promises of support."
