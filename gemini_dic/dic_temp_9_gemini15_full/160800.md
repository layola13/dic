positrons:/ˈpɒzɪtrɒnz/ | n. | 正电子 | A positron is the antiparticle or antimatter counterpart of the electron. It has the same mass as an electron but an opposite charge (+1).  Positrons are produced in various nuclear processes, such as beta-plus decay. They annihilate when they collide with electrons, producing gamma rays. | Example:  Positron emission tomography (PET) uses positrons to create medical images.  正电子发射断层扫描 (PET) 使用正电子来创建医学图像。
