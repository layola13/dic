institutionalization: /ˌɪnstɪˌtuːʃənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. |体制化；制度化；收容于机构 | The process of embedding something within an institution or making it a part of an established system or social structure.  This can refer to ideas, practices, or individuals.  It often implies a loss of flexibility, creativity, or individual autonomy. | Example: The institutionalization of democracy took centuries.  民主制度化的过程耗费了几个世纪。  The institutionalization of the patient in a mental hospital was a difficult decision for the family. 将病人送入精神病院是一个艰难的决定。|近义词：formalization, systematization, establishment |反义词：individualization, decentralization

词源:

The word "institutionalization" is derived from the noun "institution" and the suffix "-ization".  "Institution" comes from the Latin word "instituere," meaning "to establish," "to set up," or "to found."  The suffix "-ization" indicates the process of becoming or making something into an institution.
