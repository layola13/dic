predicate:/ˈprɛdɪkət/| n. v.|谓语，断言；断定|A predicate is the part of a sentence or clause containing a verb and stating something about the subject (e.g., in "the cat sat on the mat," "sat on the mat" is the predicate).  As a verb, it means to affirm or assert something. |例句：In the sentence "The dog barked loudly," "barked loudly" is the predicate.  在这个句子“狗大声吠叫”中，“大声吠叫”是谓语。

例句：The scientist predicated a rise in global temperatures. 科学家预言全球气温将会上升。|近义词：verb, assertion, affirmation, claim|反义词：subject, premise, conclusion|名词，动词
