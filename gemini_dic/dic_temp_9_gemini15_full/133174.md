nonevanescent: /ˌnɒnɪˈvænɪsnt/ | adj. |不消失的；持久的；永久的 | Not fading or disappearing; lasting; permanent.  The effect of the policy was nonevanescent, lasting for decades.  | 近义词：lasting, enduring, permanent, persistent | 反义词：transient, ephemeral, fleeting, temporary |


例句：

The impact of the artist's work was nonevanescent, continuing to influence generations of painters. =这位艺术家的作品影响深远，持续影响了几代画家。

His contribution to the field of science proved nonevanescent, shaping future research for years to come. = 他对科学领域的贡献是持久性的，在未来几年塑造了未来的研究。


词源：

The word "nonevanescent" is a combination of "non-" (meaning "not") and "evanescent" (meaning "tending to vanish like vapor").  "Evanescent" itself comes from the Late Latin *evanescere, meaning "to vanish," from *e- "out" + vanescere "to vanish," from vanus "empty, void."


用法说明：

虽然"nonevanescent" 在日常口语中并不常见，但在学术写作和技术文档中，特别是当需要强调某种现象或影响的持久性时，它是一个有用的词。它通常用来描述影响深远、持久存在的事物，例如：

* 长期存在的物理现象
* 持久的社会影响
* 持续的文化遗产


由于其相对正式的语体，在非正式场合，可以使用更简单的同义词，例如"lasting," "enduring," 或 "permanent"。
