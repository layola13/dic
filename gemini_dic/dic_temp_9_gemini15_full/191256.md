scatterbrain: /ˈskætərbreɪn/ | n. adj. |  丢三落四的人；健忘的人；心不在焉的 |  A scatterbrain is someone who is disorganized, forgetful, and easily distracted.  They often have trouble focusing and remembering things.  The term can be used as a noun referring to a person or as an adjective describing someone's nature. |例句：He's such a scatterbrain; he lost his keys three times this week. 他真是个丢三落四的人，这周他已经三次丢钥匙了。

She's a bit of a scatterbrain, always forgetting appointments. 她有点丢三落四，总是忘记约会。


近义词：airhead, daydreamer, forgetful person, absent-minded person |反义词：organized person, meticulous person, focused person |词性：noun, adjective

词源:
The word "scatterbrain" combines "scatter," meaning to disperse or spread randomly, and "brain," referring to the organ of thought and intelligence.  The implication is that the thoughts and ideas of a scatterbrain are scattered and disorganized, like things strewn about haphazardly.
