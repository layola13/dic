housecarl:/ˈhaʊskɑːrl/| n.|宫廷卫队成员，家臣|A housecarl was a member of a lord's household guard, typically a warrior serving a king or other important nobleman. They were often personally loyal to their master and responsible for his safety and security.  Housecarls were typically well-equipped and highly skilled warriors, holding a position of significant trust and responsibility.|Example: The king relied heavily on his loyal housecarls for protection during the turbulent times.|近义词：bodyguard, retainer, guardsman|反义词：enemy, opponent
