goblin:/ˈɡɒblɪn/| n. |妖精，小鬼|A goblin is a mythical creature, often depicted as a grotesque, mischievous, and sometimes malevolent sprite or fairy.  They are typically portrayed as small, ugly, and mischievous creatures who inhabit dark places and enjoy playing tricks on humans. |例句：The children told scary stories about goblins living in the woods.孩子们讲述了关于住在森林里的妖精的恐怖故事。

例句：A mischievous goblin stole the princess's jewels. 一个淘气的妖精偷走了公主的珠宝。|近义词：sprite, imp, hobgoblin, gremlin|反义词：angel, saint

词源:
The word "goblin" ultimately derives from Old French "gobelin," meaning "goblin," "boggart," or "sprite."  This term itself has roots in a broader range of medieval terms referring to mischievous spirits. The exact etymology is uncertain, but it's likely related to words associated with ghosts or demons.
