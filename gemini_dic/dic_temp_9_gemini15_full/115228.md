manifoldnesses: /ˈmænɪfoʊldnəsɪz/ | n. | 多样性；复杂性；多种多样 | The manifoldnesses of human experience are vast and varied. 人类经验的多样性是广阔而多样的。|近义词：diversity, variety, multiplicity, complexity |反义词：uniformity, simplicity


英文解析:

Manifoldnesses is the plural form of manifoldness.  It refers to the state or quality of being manifold; having many different parts, forms, or aspects. It suggests a complex and diverse system or collection of things.  The term often implies a richness or intricacy that goes beyond simple variety.


例句:

The artist explored the manifoldnesses of human emotion in her latest exhibition.  艺术家在她的最新展览中探索了人类情感的多样性。

The study revealed the manifoldnesses of opinions on the proposed policy.  这项研究揭示了对拟议政策的各种意见。


词源:

The word derives from "manifold," which itself comes from the Old English "manigfald," meaning "many-fold."  The suffix "-ness" indicates a state or quality.  Therefore, "manifoldnesses" literally means "the state of being many-folded" or "the state of having many forms."
