gossips:/ˈɡɑːsɪps/| n.|流言蜚语；闲话；爱说闲话的人|Informal talk or rumors, especially about other people's private lives; people who spread such talk. |例句：I don't like to listen to gossips. 我不喜欢听闲话。

例句：The office is full of gossips.  办公室里充满了爱说闲话的人。


近义词：rumors, scuttlebutt, hearsay, chatter, idle talk|反义词：truth, facts


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The girls gossip about their friends. = 女孩们在说她们朋友的闲话。
He gossips a lot at work. = 他在工作中说很多闲话。


一般过去时:
They gossiped about the celebrity's divorce. = 他们八卦了那个名人的离婚事件。
She gossiped with her neighbors yesterday. = 她昨天和邻居们闲聊了。


现在完成时:
They have gossiped about this for weeks. = 他们已经为此八卦了好几个星期了。
She has gossiped too much recently. = 她最近说闲话太多了。


过去完成时:
They had gossiped about the news before I arrived. = 我来之前他们就已经八卦过这个新闻了。
She had gossiped with her friends before the party. = 派对之前，她已经和朋友们聊过天了。


将来时:
They will gossip about this at the party. = 他们会在派对上八卦这件事。
She will gossip with her colleagues tomorrow. = 她明天会和同事们闲聊。


虚拟语气:
If they gossiped less, they would have more friends. = 如果他们少说些闲话，就会有更多朋友。
I wish they didn't gossip so much. = 我希望他们不要说那么多闲话。


被动语态:
The news was gossiped about all over town. = 这个消息在全镇都被传遍了。
The scandal was gossiped about extensively. = 这桩丑闻被广泛地八卦。


疑问句:
Are they gossiping again? = 他们又在说闲话了吗？
What are you gossiping about? = 你在八卦什么？


否定句:
They don't gossip about others. = 他们不说别人的闲话。
She didn't gossip at the meeting. = 她没有在会议上说闲话。
