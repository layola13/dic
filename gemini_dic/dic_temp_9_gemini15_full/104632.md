karakoram: /ˌkærəˈkɔːræm/ | n. |喀喇昆仑山脉 | The Karakoram Range is a mountain range in Asia, extending from Afghanistan to India. It is known for its high peaks, including K2, the second-highest mountain in the world. The name "Karakoram" is believed to be derived from the Turkic words "kara" (black) and "koram" (gravel), possibly referring to the dark, gravelly terrain.  | Example: The climbers faced extreme challenges in the Karakoram mountains.  登山者在喀喇昆仑山脉面临极大的挑战。 |近义词：Himalayas (in context)|反义词：plain, lowland
