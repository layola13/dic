evoking: /ɪˈvoʊkɪŋ/ | v. | 唤起，引起，使人想起 | To evoke something means to bring a feeling, memory, or image into the mind.  It suggests a powerful and often emotional response. |


例句：

The music evoked a sense of nostalgia.  这音乐唤起了怀旧之情。

The photograph evoked memories of her childhood.  这张照片使她想起了童年。


近义词：

summoning, conjuring, prompting, suggesting, reminding


反义词：

suppressing, forgetting, ignoring


词性:

动词

The scent of lavender evokes memories of her grandmother's garden. 薰衣草的香味使她想起祖母的花园。

现在完成时:

Her words have evoked a lively debate. 她的言辞引发了一场激烈的辩论。

This book has evoked a lot of interest among readers. 这本书引起了众多读者的兴趣。


过去完成时:

The author had evoked a vivid picture of the past in his novel. 作者在他的小说中生动地描绘了过去。


将来时:

虚拟语气:

If the music were to evoke a certain emotion, it would be sadness. 如果音乐要唤起某种情感，那将是悲伤。

I wish the painting would evoke more positive feelings. 我希望这幅画能唤起更多积极的情感。


被动语态:

A feeling of warmth was evoked by the fireplace. 壁炉唤起了温暖的感觉。

Strong emotions were evoked by the documentary.  纪录片唤起了强烈的情感。


疑问句:

Does the poem evoke any particular memories for you? 这首诗是否唤起了你任何特别的回忆？

What kind of emotions does the story evoke in the reader? 这个故事在读者心中唤起了什么样的情感？


否定句:

The movie failed to evoke any emotional response from me.  这部电影未能引起我的任何情感共鸣。

The presentation did not evoke much interest from the audience.  演示文稿并没有引起听众的太多兴趣。
