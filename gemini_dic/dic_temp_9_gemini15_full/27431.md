camisole: /ˈkæməˌsoʊl/ | n. | 紧身女内衣；吊带衫 | A camisole is a woman's undergarment, typically sleeveless and made of lightweight fabric, often worn under other clothing. It is often used as a layering piece and can be made from various materials like silk, cotton, or lace.  | Example: She wore a silk camisole under her blouse. 她穿着丝绸吊带衫在衬衫里面。  |近义词：undershirt, slip, vest (in some contexts) | 反义词：None specifically, but outerwear would be a contrasting category. | 名词

词源:
The word "camisole" comes from the French word "camisole," which ultimately derives from the Old Spanish "camisa," meaning "shirt."  The Spanish word, in turn, originates from the Latin "camisia."  Over time, the meaning evolved from a general shirt to a specific type of women's undergarment.
