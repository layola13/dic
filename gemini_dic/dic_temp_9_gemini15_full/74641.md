free-falling: /ˈfriːˌfɔːlɪŋ/ | adj. | 自由落体(的)；急速下降(的) |  Describes something falling under the force of gravity without any other forces significantly affecting its motion. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a rapid decline or deterioration. | 例句：The skydiver experienced the thrill of free-falling.  跳伞运动员体验了自由落体的快感。

The company's stock price is free-falling after the scandal.  丑闻之后，公司股价暴跌。| 近义词：plummeting, tumbling, dropping | 反义词：ascending, rising, climbing | 词性：形容词
