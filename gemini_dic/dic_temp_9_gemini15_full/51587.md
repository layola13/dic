devaluation:/ˌdiːvæljuˈeɪʃən/ | n. | 贬值；低估 | The act of reducing the value of something, especially a country's currency.  This often happens when a government officially lowers the value of its currency in relation to other currencies.  It can also refer to the reduction in value of an asset or property. | Example: The recent devaluation of the currency led to higher import prices. 最近货币贬值导致进口价格上涨。

The government's devaluation of the national currency caused widespread economic instability. 政府的货币贬值导致了广泛的经济不稳定。


近义词：depreciation, decline, decrease in value | 反义词：revaluation, appreciation, increase in value


词源：来自de- (表示“减少”或“取消”) + valuation (估价，评价)。  "Valuation" itself comes from the Latin "valere" meaning "to be strong" or "to be worth."
