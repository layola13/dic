get-outs:/ˈɡɛtaʊts/| n.| 逃避；借口；紧急出口 | A way of avoiding a difficult situation or responsibility; an excuse.  Also, a means of escape, typically an exit. |例句：He always has a get-out clause ready when things go wrong. 他总是在事情出错时准备好一个借口。

例句：The fire exit served as a get-out for the panicked crowd.  紧急出口成了惊慌人群的逃生通道。


近义词：escape, excuse, loophole, pretext, avoidance, way out

反义词：responsibility, accountability, commitment, facing up to


词性:名词

词源:  The term is a combination of "get" (meaning to obtain or achieve) and "out" (meaning escape or exit).  Its figurative use as an excuse developed from its literal meaning of an exit.
