lambasting: /læmˈbæstɪŋ/ | n.  |痛斥；严厉批评 | The act of criticizing someone or something severely.  |例句：The coach gave the team a lambasting after their poor performance. 教练在球队糟糕的表现后狠狠地批评了他们。
例句：His lambasting of the government's policies was widely reported in the media. 他对政府政策的严厉批评在媒体上被广泛报道。|近义词：rebuke, castigation, denunciation, condemnation |反义词：praise, commendation, applause |动词

一般现在时:
He lambastes his opponents in every speech. 他在每一次演讲中都痛斥他的对手。
She lambastes the government's inaction on climate change. 她痛斥政府在气候变化问题上的不作为。


一般过去时:
The teacher lambasted the student for cheating on the exam. 老师严厉批评学生考试作弊。
