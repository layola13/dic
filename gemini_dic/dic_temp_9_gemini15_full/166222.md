prequarantining:/ˌpriːˈkwɒrənˌtiːnɪŋ/| v.|隔离前期准备；提前隔离|The act of taking precautions before a quarantine is officially imposed, such as self-isolating or restricting movements to minimize potential exposure to a contagious disease.  This is often done proactively to prevent the spread of an illness.|例句：The university implemented prequarantining measures for students returning from high-risk areas.  大学对从高风险地区返校的学生实施了提前隔离措施。

例句：Prequarantining involved deep cleaning the house and stocking up on supplies.  提前隔离包括彻底清洁房屋和储备物资。|近义词：preemptive isolation, proactive quarantine, preventative measures|反义词：post-quarantine, after-isolation
