pidgin:/ˈpɪdʒɪn/| n.|皮钦语，混合语|A simplified language that develops between two or more groups of people who do not share a common language. It is often used for basic communication, and it typically borrows vocabulary and grammatical structures from the languages of the groups involved. Pidgins are not usually native languages; they are learned as second languages.|Example: Tok Pisin, a pidgin language spoken in Papua New Guinea, is based on English and several local languages.|近义词：creole, jargon, slang|反义词：standard language, formal language
