shutdowns:/ˈʃʌtdaʊnz/| n.| 关闭；停机；停工；关闭事件 | The process of stopping a system, process, or machine.  It can refer to planned or unplanned interruptions.  This word often refers to the temporary cessation of operations, but it can also refer to permanent closures. |例句：The government ordered shutdowns of non-essential services during the pandemic.  政府在疫情期间下令关闭非必要服务。

例句：The unexpected power shutdown caused major disruptions to the factory.  意外的停电给工厂造成了重大破坏。


近义词：closures, suspensions, halts, cessations |反义词：startups, openings, commencements |词性:名词
