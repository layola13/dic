irrefrangibility:/ˌɪrɪfrænˈdʒɪbɪləti/| n. |不可折射性，不屈性|The quality or state of being irrefrangible; inability to be refracted.  It often refers to light's inability to be bent or its resistance to being deflected.  In a figurative sense, it can mean unyielding or inflexible.|Example: The irrefrangibility of diamond is a key characteristic that contributes to its brilliance.  钻石的不可折射性是其光泽的关键特性。|近义词：indeflectability, inflexibility|反义词：refractibility, flexibility|

词源:

* **ir-:**  prefix meaning "not" or "without"
* **refract-:** from Latin *refractus*, past participle of *refractare*, meaning "to break," "to fracture."
* **-ibility:** suffix forming nouns of quality or capacity.


用法说明:

虽然irrefrangibility主要用于描述光的性质，但在比喻意义上也可以指人或事物的坚定不移和不可动摇。


例句:

1. The high irrefrangibility of the laser beam allowed for precise cutting. 激光束的高不可折射性使得精确切割成为可能。

2.  His irrefrangibility in the face of adversity was remarkable. 他面对逆境时的坚定不移令人钦佩。
