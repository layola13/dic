impressionism: /ɪmˈprɛʃənɪzəm/ | n. |印象派；印象主义 | Impressionism is a 19th-century art movement characterized by relatively small, thin, yet visible brush strokes, open composition, emphasis on accurate depiction of light in its changing qualities (often accentuating the effects of the passage of time), ordinary subject matter, and unusual visual angles.  | Example:  The museum's collection of Impressionist paintings is world-renowned. 印象派画作的博物馆收藏举世闻名。  | 近义词: post-impressionism, realism (in contrast to its style)| 反义词:  realism (in terms of artistic style),  classicism (in terms of artistic style)
