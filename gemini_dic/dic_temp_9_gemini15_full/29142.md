caseloads:/ˈkeɪsloʊdz/| n.|案件数量，工作量|The number of cases a person or organization is responsible for.  It often refers to the workload of a social worker, lawyer, or other professional dealing with a large number of individual cases.|例句：The social worker's caseload has increased significantly this year. 社工今年的工作量大大增加了。|例句：The judge's caseload is overwhelming, requiring long hours and dedication.  法官的案件数量非常庞大，需要长时间工作和奉献。|近义词：workload, burden, volume|反义词：light workload, ease
