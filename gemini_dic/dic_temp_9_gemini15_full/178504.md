recontextualize:/ˌriːˈkɒntekstʃuəlaɪz/ | /ˌriːˈkɑːntekstʃuəlaɪz/ | vt. |重新诠释；赋予新的语境；改变语境|To place (something, such as a word, phrase, or idea) in a new context; to reinterpret something by considering its context.  This involves considering the original context and then re-evaluating its meaning and significance within a different framework.|例句：Historians often recontextualize historical events to better understand their impact on present-day society.  历史学家经常重新诠释历史事件，以便更好地理解它们对当今社会的影响。

例句：The artist recontextualized found objects, giving them new meaning within an artistic installation.艺术家重新诠释了寻获的物品，赋予它们在艺术装置中新的意义。


近义词：reframe, reinterpret, reposition, re-evaluate


反义词：misinterpret, misunderstand
