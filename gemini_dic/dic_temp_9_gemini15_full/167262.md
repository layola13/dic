pretestimony:/ˌpriːˈtɛstɪmoʊni/ | n. | 预先证词；预审证词 | Testimony given before a trial, often to prepare for the trial itself.  It might involve reviewing facts, practicing testimony delivery, or exploring potential questions from opposing counsel.  | Example: The lawyer reviewed the pretestimony with the witness to ensure a smooth and effective delivery in court.  律师与证人一起审查预先证词，以确保在法庭上顺利有效地作证。 | 近义词：rehearsal (of testimony), preparation (for testimony) | 反义词：N/A
