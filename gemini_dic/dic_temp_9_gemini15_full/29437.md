catalized:/ˈkætəlaɪzd/| vt.|催化；促成|To cause (a process or activity) to begin or happen faster.|The addition of a catalyst catalized the chemical reaction. 添加催化剂促进了化学反应。|accelerated, expedited, facilitated|hindered, inhibited, retarded|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The enzyme catalyzes the reaction.  = 这种酶催化该反应。
Heat catalyzes the chemical process. = 热量促进了化学过程。

一般过去时:
The discovery catalyzed a surge in investment. = 这一发现促使投资激增。
The meeting catalyzed a productive discussion. = 会议促成了一次富有成效的讨论。

现在完成时:
The new policy has catalyzed significant economic growth. = 新政策促成了显著的经济增长。
Their efforts have catalyzed positive change in the community. = 他们的努力促进了社区的积极变化。

过去完成时:
The prior events had catalyzed the current crisis. = 先前的事件已经促成了当前的危机。
His speech had catalyzed a wave of protests. = 他的演讲引发了一波抗议浪潮。

将来时:
The new technology will catalyze innovation in the industry. = 新技术将推动该行业的创新。
The upcoming conference will catalyze collaboration between researchers. = 即将召开的会议将促进研究人员之间的合作。

虚拟语气:
If we had a better leader, it would catalyze progress. = 如果我们有一个更好的领导者，它将促进进步。
I wish the new strategy would catalyze a turnaround. = 我希望新战略能够带来好转。

被动语态:
The reaction was catalyzed by the enzyme. = 该反应是由酶催化的。
Progress was catalyzed by the government's intervention. = 政府的干预促进了进步。

疑问句:
What catalyzed the sudden change in plans? = 什么促成了计划的突然改变？
What factors catalyzed this economic boom? = 哪些因素促成了这场经济繁荣？

否定句:
The lack of funding did not catalyze the project. = 资金不足并没有推动项目进展。
The meeting failed to catalyze any meaningful solutions. = 会议未能促成任何有意义的解决方案。
