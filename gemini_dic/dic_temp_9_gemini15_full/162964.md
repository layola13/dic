precip:/ˈprɛsɪpɪt/|/prɪˈsɪpɪteɪt/| n. v.| 骤降；沉淀物；促成；使（尤指坏事）突然发生|

英文解析:As a noun, precipitate refers to the product of a chemical reaction that separates from a solution as a solid. As a verb, it means to cause something to happen suddenly or unexpectedly, typically something bad. It can also describe something falling rapidly.

例句：
The sudden drop in temperature caused a precipitate fall in demand. =气温骤降导致需求骤减。

The addition of the acid caused a precipitate to form in the solution. =加入酸导致溶液中形成沉淀物。


近义词: noun: sediment, deposit; verb: trigger, accelerate, hasten, expedite


反义词: noun: supernatant; verb: delay, postpone, hinder


词性:n. v.


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The rain precipitates rapidly. =雨水快速降落。
He precipitates a crisis. = 他引发了一场危机。


一般过去时:
The chemicals precipitated a reaction. = 化学物质引发了反应。
The volcano precipitated ash and rock. = 火山喷发出火山灰和岩石。


现在完成时:
The drought has precipitated a food crisis. = 干旱已经引发了粮食危机。
The evidence has precipitated his resignation. = 证据促使他辞职。


过去完成时:
The argument had precipitated their separation. = 争吵导致了他们的分离。
The heavy rain had precipitated a flood. = 大雨引发了洪水。


将来时:
The government’s actions will precipitate a political storm. = 政府的行为将引发一场政治风暴。
The meeting will precipitate a decision. = 会议将促成一项决定。


虚拟语气:

被动语态:

否定句:
