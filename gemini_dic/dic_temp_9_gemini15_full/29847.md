causationist: /ˌkɔːzˈeɪʃənɪst/ | n. | 因果论者，因果关系的支持者 | A person who believes in or emphasizes causation, the principle that every event has a cause.  They often focus on identifying and understanding the cause-and-effect relationships between events. | Example: The causationist argued that the economic downturn was directly caused by government policies. 因果论者认为经济衰退是由政府政策直接造成的。 | 近义词：causalist, determinist | 反义词：indeterminist
