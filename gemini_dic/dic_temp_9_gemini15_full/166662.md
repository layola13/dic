preseparator: /ˈpriːˌsepəˌreɪtər/ | n. | 预分隔符 | A preseparator is a character or sequence of characters used to mark a point before a main separator in a data stream or file, often to help with parsing or data processing. It helps to distinguish different parts of the data before the main separator is used to divide the data into distinct fields or records.  | Example: In a CSV file, a semicolon might be used as a preseparator to indicate the start of a new record before the comma is used as the main field separator.  | 近义词:  prefix, delimiter (in a broader sense) | 反义词: postseparator (a hypothetical opposite) |  n.
