segmentability:/ˌsɛɡmɛnˈtæbɪləti/ | n. | 可分割性，可分段性 | The ability to be divided into segments or parts.  This term is often used in image processing, speech recognition, and natural language processing to describe how easily something can be broken down into meaningful units. | Example: The high segmentability of the image allowed for efficient object recognition.  图像的高可分割性允许高效的目标识别。 The low segmentability of the speech signal made accurate transcription difficult. 语音信号的可分割性低，使得准确转录变得困难。 |近义词：divisibility, separability | 反义词：indivisibility, inseparability | 名词
