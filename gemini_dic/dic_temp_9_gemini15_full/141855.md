orchestrate:/ˈɔːrkɪstreɪt/| vt.|编排；策划；精心安排|To plan or organize something, especially a complex event or operation, in a skillful way.|例句：The general orchestrated a brilliant military campaign.  将军策划了一场辉煌的军事战役。|例句：She skillfully orchestrated a meeting between the two rival companies. 她巧妙地安排了两家竞争公司之间的会面。|近义词：arrange, organize, plan, manage|反义词：disorganize, disrupt, scramble


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He orchestrates the company's marketing campaigns. = 他策划公司的营销活动。
She orchestrates events for a living. = 她靠策划活动为生。

一般过去时:
They orchestrated a surprise party for their friend. = 他们为朋友策划了一个惊喜派对。
The team orchestrated a successful product launch. = 团队策划了一次成功的产品发布。

现在完成时:
She has orchestrated several major fundraising events. = 她已经策划了几次大型筹款活动。
He has orchestrated a complex negotiation between the two parties. = 他已经策划了两方之间一次复杂的谈判。

过去完成时:
They had orchestrated the entire plan before the meeting. = 会议前他们已经策划好了整个计划。
The rebels had orchestrated a coup d'état. = 反叛者策划了一场政变。

将来时:
We will orchestrate a grand celebration for their anniversary. = 我们将为他们的周年纪念日策划一场盛大的庆祝活动。
The company will orchestrate a new advertising campaign next month. = 公司下个月将策划一场新的广告活动。

虚拟语气:
If I could orchestrate my own destiny, I would. = 如果我能掌控自己的命运，我会这样做。
If they had orchestrated the event differently, it would have been more successful. = 如果他们对活动有不同的策划，它将会更成功。

被动语态:
The event was orchestrated by a team of professionals. = 这场活动是由一支专业团队策划的。
The entire operation was orchestrated with precision. = 整场行动都经过精确的策划。

疑问句:
Who orchestrated this brilliant strategy? = 谁策划了这个绝妙的策略？
How did they orchestrate such a complex project? = 他们是如何策划这样一个复杂的项目的？

否定句:
He didn't orchestrate the meeting; it was spontaneous. = 他没有策划这次会议；它是自发的。
They didn't orchestrate the events; they simply reacted. = 他们没有策划这些事件；他们只是做出了反应。
