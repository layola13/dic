epitomise:/ɪˈpɪtəmaɪz/ | vt. | 成为…的典型；概括；象征 | To be a perfect example of something; to embody or represent the essential characteristics of something. |  His work epitomises the spirit of the age. 他的作品是那个时代精神的缩影。 |  The building epitomises modern architecture. 这座建筑是现代建筑的典范。 | embody, represent, exemplify, typify |  differentiate, contrast


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He epitomises the qualities of a great leader. 他是优秀领导者的典范。
This painting epitomises the artist's style. 这幅画体现了这位艺术家的风格。

一般过去时:
Her performance epitomised grace and elegance. 她的表演完美地诠释了优雅和高贵。
The novel epitomised the struggles of the working class. 这部小说概括了工人阶级的奋斗。

现在完成时:
His career has epitomised dedication and hard work. 他的职业生涯体现了奉献和努力。
Their actions have epitomised the spirit of cooperation. 他们的行动体现了合作精神。

过去完成时:
The previous government’s policies had epitomised inefficiency. 上届政府的政策体现了低效。
Her earlier novels had epitomised romanticism. 她早期的作品是浪漫主义的典范。

将来时:
His future work will epitomise innovative design. 他未来的作品将体现创新设计。
This project will epitomise sustainable development. 这个项目将体现可持续发展。

虚拟语气:
If his work epitomised perfection, it would be widely acclaimed. 如果他的作品完美无瑕，它将会广受赞誉。
I wish his leadership epitomised fairness and justice. 我希望他的领导能体现公平与正义。

被动语态:
The ideals of the revolution were epitomised in the new constitution. 革命的理想在新宪法中得到了体现。
His achievements are epitomised in his autobiography. 他的成就都在他的自传中得到了体现。

疑问句:
Does this sculpture epitomise the beauty of the human form? 这座雕塑是否体现了人体之美？
What qualities does he epitomise? 他体现了哪些品质？

否定句:
His behavior did not epitomise professionalism. 他的行为并不专业。
The film does not epitomise Hollywood glamour. 这部电影并未体现好莱坞的魅力。
