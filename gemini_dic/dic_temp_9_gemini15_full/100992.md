invalidating: /ɪnˈvælɪdeɪtɪŋ/ | v. | 使无效；使作废；否定 | To make something invalid or ineffective; to disprove or refute something. | The new evidence invalidated the original theory. 新证据使原来的理论无效。  The judge's ruling invalidated the contract.  法官的裁决使合同无效。 |  nullify, invalidate, negate, annul | validate, confirm, ratify |  动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The new policy invalidates many existing permits. = 新政策使许多现有许可证失效。
He is constantly invalidating her opinions. = 他一直在否定她的意见。


一般过去时:
The court invalidated the agreement. = 法院宣布该协议无效。
The discovery invalidated his previous research. = 这一发现使他先前的研究成果无效。


现在完成时:
Recent findings have invalidated previous assumptions. = 最近的发现使之前的假设无效。
The evidence has invalidated the defendant's alibi. = 证据推翻了被告的不在场证明。


过去完成时:
They had invalidated the contract before the dispute arose. = 在争议出现之前，他们就已经使合同无效了。
The new data had invalidated the old model. = 新数据已经使旧模型失效。


将来时:
The upcoming legislation will invalidate current regulations. = 即将颁布的立法将使现行法规失效。
The new technology will invalidate many existing systems. = 新技术将使许多现有系统失效。


虚拟语气:
If the results were to invalidate the hypothesis, we would need to reconsider our approach. = 如果结果使假设无效，我们就需要重新考虑我们的方法。
I wish that the data hadn't invalidated my theory. = 我真希望数据没有使我的理论无效。


被动语态:

疑问句:
Does this evidence invalidate the claim? = 这个证据是否推翻了这一说法？
Would a new law invalidate this contract? = 新法会使这份合同无效吗？


否定句:
The experiment did not invalidate the hypothesis. = 实验并没有使假设无效。
The judge's decision did not invalidate the will. = 法官的决定并没有使遗嘱无效。
