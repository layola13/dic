sizarship:/ˈsaɪzəʃɪp/| n. |奖学金；贫困生身份 |A sizarship is a scholarship, typically at a British university, that was historically awarded to students of limited means in exchange for performing certain tasks or services.  It provided financial assistance to allow them to attend the university.  The term and practice have largely disappeared, but the word remains. |Example: He was awarded a sizarship at Cambridge, allowing him to pursue his studies despite his family's poverty. 他获得剑桥大学的奖学金，这使他能够继续学业，尽管他的家庭贫困。|近义词：scholarship, bursary, grant |反义词：tuition fee, full cost
