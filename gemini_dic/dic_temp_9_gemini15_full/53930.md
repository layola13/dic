discourteous:/dɪsˈkɜːrtiəs/ | adj. | 不礼貌的，粗鲁的 |  Lacking courtesy and good manners; rude.  | Example:  It was discourteous of him to interrupt the speaker. 他打断发言者，这是不礼貌的。
He was discourteous to the elderly woman. 他对那位老妇人很粗鲁。| 近义词：rude, impolite, uncivil, ill-mannered | 反义词：courteous, polite, civil, well-mannered


词源：

dis- (否定前缀) + courteous (有礼貌的)  Courteous itself comes from the Old French word *courteis*, meaning "courtly," referring to the refined manners expected of those associated with the royal court.  Ultimately, it traces back to the Latin word *curia*, meaning "court."
