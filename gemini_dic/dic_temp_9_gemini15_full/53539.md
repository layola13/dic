disavowed:/ˌdɪsəˈvaʊd/| vt.|否认；与…断绝关系；拒绝承认|To deny or repudiate; to refuse to acknowledge or accept.|The politician disavowed any connection to the scandal.  这位政客否认与丑闻有任何关联。|He disavowed his earlier statement. 他否认了他先前的声明。|affirm, acknowledge, admit|accept, endorse, own


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He disavows his past actions. 他否认他过去的行动。
She disavows all responsibility. 她否认一切责任。


一般过去时:
The company disavowed the actions of its subsidiary.  公司否认其子公司的行为。
He publicly disavowed his son's involvement. 他公开否认他儿子的参与。


现在完成时:
They have disavowed any knowledge of the plot.  他们否认对阴谋有任何了解。
She has disavowed her former beliefs. 她已经否认了她以前的信仰。


过去完成时:
He had disavowed his allegiance to the king before the rebellion.  他在叛乱之前就已否认效忠国王。
The government had disavowed the treaty by the time the war broke out.  战争爆发时，政府已经否认了该条约。


将来时:
The witness will disavow his testimony under pressure.  证人在压力下将会否认他的证词。
He will disavow the accusations against him. 他将否认针对他的指控。


虚拟语气:
If he disavowed his guilt, he might have been acquitted. 如果他否认他的罪行，他可能已经被无罪释放。
I wish he had disavowed his involvement earlier. 我希望他早点否认他的参与。


被动语态:
His claims were disavowed by the authorities. 他的说法被当局否认了。
The treaty was disavowed by both sides.  该条约被双方否认了。


疑问句:
Did he disavow the agreement? 他是否否认了协议？
Will she disavow her statement? 她会否认她的说法吗？


否定句:
He did not disavow his support for the candidate. 他没有否认他支持该候选人。
The company did not disavow the report. 公司没有否认该报告。
