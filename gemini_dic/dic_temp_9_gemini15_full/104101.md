jump-starting: /ˈdʒʌmpˌstɑːrtɪŋ/ | v. |  启动，使…重新开始 | To jump-start something is to give it a sudden boost of energy or activity to get it going again, often after a period of inactivity or failure.  It often implies a forceful or artificial intervention. |

例句：

We need to jump-start the economy. = 我们需要刺激经济。

The new marketing campaign jump-started sales. = 新的营销活动刺激了销售额。


近义词：revitalize, reinvigorate, kick-start, boost, stimulate

反义词：halt, stop, cease, stifle


词源：

The term "jump-starting" originates from the automotive practice of jump-starting a car battery.  This involves using a charged battery from another car to provide the necessary power to start a car with a dead battery. The "jump" refers to the sudden surge of power, and the "start" refers to the initiation of the engine.  The figurative use of the term extended to other contexts, representing a sudden, forceful initiation or revitalization.
