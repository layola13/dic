dynes:/daɪnz/| n. |达因（力的单位）|A dyne is a unit of force in the centimetre–gram–second (CGS) system of units.  It is defined as the force required to accelerate a mass of one gram at a rate of one centimetre per second squared.  The dyne is a relatively small unit of force and is rarely used in modern scientific work, having largely been replaced by the newton in the International System of Units (SI).|The force exerted on the object was measured in dynes.|近义词：newton (although vastly different in magnitude)|反义词：无|名词
