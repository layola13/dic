semi-beam:/ˈsɛmiˌbiːm/| n. | 半音束 | A semi-beam in musical notation is a beam that connects two or three notes, as opposed to a beam that connects four or more notes.  It is a shorter beam used to group notes together visually, indicating that they should be played as a single rhythmic unit.  | Example: In a piece of music, you might see a semi-beam connecting two eighth notes, showing they are played as a single rhythmic unit. |近义词: beam (in the context of musical notation) |反义词:None| 名词
