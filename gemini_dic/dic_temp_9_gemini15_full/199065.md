signifies:/ˈsɪɡnɪfaɪz/| vt.|意味；表示；象征|To be a sign of; to indicate; to represent.|The dark clouds signifies an approaching storm.  乌云预示着暴风雨即将来临。|The symbol signifies peace. 这个符号象征和平。|connotes, denotes, represents, symbolizes|misinterprets, misunderstands|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
This gesture signifies agreement.  这个手势表示同意。
The red light signifies danger.  红灯表示危险。

一般过去时:
The treaty signified a new era of peace.  该条约标志着和平新时代的到来。
Her silence signified her disapproval.  她的沉默表示她的反对。

现在完成时:
The discovery has signified a breakthrough in cancer research.  这一发现标志着癌症研究取得了突破。
His actions have signified a change in his attitude.  他的行为表明了他的态度发生了变化。

过去完成时:
The old building signified a bygone era.  这座古老的建筑象征着过去的时代。
The letter signified that he had already left.  这封信表明他已经离开了。

将来时:
The new law will signify a major shift in government policy.  新法律将标志着政府政策的重大转变。
His upcoming speech will signify his intention to run for office. 他即将发表的演讲将表明他打算竞选公职。

虚拟语气:
If this symbol signified hope, I would feel better. 如果这个符号象征着希望，我会感觉好一些。
If the results signified success, we would celebrate. 如果结果意味着成功，我们会庆祝。

被动语态:
The situation was signified by a sharp increase in crime rates.  局势以犯罪率的急剧上升为标志。
Success is signified by the achievement of goals. 成功以目标的实现为标志。

疑问句:
What does this symbol signify?  这个符号代表什么？
Does this gesture signify agreement or disagreement? 这个手势表示同意还是不同意？

否定句:
The data does not signify a significant trend. 数据并不代表一个显著的趋势。
His words did not signify his true feelings.  他的话语并没有表达他真实的想法。


PHRASE->
signify something to someone:  向某人表示某事
signify one's approval/disapproval: 表示赞成/反对
signify one's intention: 表示意图
signify one's willingness: 表示愿意
signify consent: 表示同意
