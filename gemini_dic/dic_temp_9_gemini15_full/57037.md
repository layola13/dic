doublets:/ˈdʌblɪts/ | n. | 双词，成对词 | A doublet is a pair of words in a language that have the same etymological origin but have evolved into different words with different meanings.  They often appear to be unrelated at first glance. | Example:  "Cattle" and "chattel" are doublets, both deriving from the same Old French word.  | 近义词: etymological pair, cognate (though cognate has a broader meaning)| 反义词: N/A
