echolocations:/ˌɛkoʊloʊˈkeɪʃənz/ | n. |回声定位；回声定位法 | The process by which animals emit sounds and then use the echoes to locate objects.  This is particularly common in bats and dolphins.  | 例句：Bats use echolocation to navigate in the dark.  蝙蝠利用回声定位在黑暗中导航。

例句：Dolphins rely on echolocation to find prey in murky waters. 海豚依靠回声定位在浑浊的水域中寻找猎物。


近义词：biosonar (生物声纳)


反义词：sight (视觉), vision (视觉)


词性：名词
