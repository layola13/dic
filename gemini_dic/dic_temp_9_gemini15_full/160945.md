post-Reformations:/ˌpoʊstˌrɛfərˈmeɪʃənz/| adj.|宗教改革后的|Post-Reformations refers to the period and events following the Protestant Reformation in Europe, approximately from the mid-16th century onwards.  This era witnessed significant changes in religious, political, and social structures across various European nations.  The term encompasses the lasting impacts and consequences of the Reformation, including religious conflicts, the rise of new denominations, shifts in power dynamics, and evolving societal norms.|The Post-Reformations saw the rise of powerful nation-states and the decline of the Holy Roman Empire.|近义词：post-Protestant Reformation, after the Reformation|反义词：pre-Reformation
