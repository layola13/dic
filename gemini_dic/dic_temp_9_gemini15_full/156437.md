phytozoaria:/ˌfaɪtoʊˈzəriə/| n. |植物动物群；植物性动物化石|Fossil remains of plant-like organisms, often microscopic, that lived in ancient seas.  These fossils provide valuable information about past environments and the evolution of life.  The term is often used in paleontology and geology.|例句：The discovery of phytozoaria in the limestone formation indicates a shallow marine environment existed millions of years ago.  = 在石灰岩地层中发现植物动物群表明数百万年前存在浅海环境。


The study of phytozoaria revealed new insights into the early evolution of marine algae. = 植物动物群的研究揭示了对早期海藻进化的新认识。|近义词：microfossils,  paleoflora (though paleoflora typically refers to plant fossils, not necessarily microscopic and plant-like organisms)|反义词：zoophytes (animals resembling plants)|词性：名词
