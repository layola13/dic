marring:/ˈmærɪŋ/ | v. | 损害；破坏；玷污 | To damage the beauty or perfection of something.  |The scratches marred the surface of the antique table.  刮痕损坏了古董桌的表面。 | damaging, spoiling, impairing | enhancing, improving, perfecting | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The loud music mars the peaceful atmosphere. = 吵闹的音乐破坏了宁静的氛围。
His bad temper mars his otherwise pleasant personality. = 他暴躁的脾气破坏了他原本讨人喜欢的个性。


一般过去时:
The storm marred our picnic plans. = 暴风雨破坏了我们的野餐计划。
The accident marred her otherwise perfect record. = 事故破坏了她原本完美的记录。


现在完成时:
The scandal has marred the reputation of the company. = 丑闻损害了公司的声誉。
Years of neglect have marred the beauty of the old house. = 多年的忽视损害了这座老房子的美丽。


过去完成时:
The previous owner had marred the value of the property with poor maintenance. = 前任房主因维护不善而损害了房产的价值。
The rain had marred the landscape with mudslides. = 雨水引发泥石流破坏了风景。


将来时:
The upcoming election may mar the country's unity. = 即将到来的选举可能会损害国家的团结。
Further delays will mar the project's timeline. = 进一步的延误将影响项目的进度。


虚拟语气:
If only that mistake hadn't marred his otherwise excellent performance! = 如果那个错误没有破坏他原本优秀的表现就好了！
I wish the noise hadn't marred the concert. = 我希望噪音没有破坏这场音乐会。


被动语态:
His reputation was marred by the false accusations. = 他的名誉被不实的指控所损害。
The painting was marred by a clumsy restoration attempt. = 这幅画被一次笨拙的修复尝试破坏了。


疑问句:
Did the criticism mar his confidence? = 批评损害了他的信心吗？
Will the upcoming storm mar our trip? = 即将到来的暴风雨会破坏我们的旅行吗？


否定句:
The minor flaws didn't mar the overall beauty of the sculpture. = 轻微的瑕疵并没有破坏雕塑的整体美感。
Nothing marred the perfect day. = 没有什么破坏了完美的一天。
