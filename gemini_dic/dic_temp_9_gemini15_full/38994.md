confirmed:/kənˈfɜːrmd/| adj. | 证实的；确认的；已确定的 |  Confirmed means having been verified or established as true or genuine. It suggests a high degree of certainty.  | Example: The police confirmed that the suspect was guilty. 警方证实了嫌疑犯有罪。| Example: The date of the meeting is confirmed. 会议日期已确定。 |近义词：verified, established, ratified, substantiated |反义词：unconfirmed, unverified, disputed, doubtful

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The doctor confirms the diagnosis. = 医生确认了诊断结果。
They confirm the booking. = 他们确认了预订。

一般过去时:
He confirmed his attendance at the party. = 他确认了参加聚会的意愿。
She confirmed the story. = 她证实了这个故事。

现在完成时:
They have confirmed their reservations. = 他们已经确认了预订。
We have confirmed the flight details. = 我们已经确认了航班细节。

过去完成时:
He had confirmed his participation before the deadline. = 他在截止日期前已经确认了他的参与。
She had confirmed the news before it was announced publicly. = 在新闻公开发布之前，她已经证实了此事。

将来时:
They will confirm the results tomorrow. = 他们明天将确认结果。
We will confirm your order shortly. = 我们将很快确认您的订单。

虚拟语气:
If they confirmed the deal, we would proceed. = 如果他们确认这笔交易，我们将继续进行。
I wish they had confirmed the appointment earlier. = 我希望他们早点确认约会。

被动语态:
The information was confirmed by several sources. = 该信息已得到多个来源的证实。
The flight has been confirmed. = 航班已得到确认。

疑问句:
Has the shipment been confirmed? = 货物是否已确认？
Have you confirmed your address? = 你确认过你的地址了吗？

否定句:
They did not confirm the rumors. = 他们没有证实这些谣言。
The report has not been confirmed yet. = 报告尚未得到证实。
