/ˌnɒnkənɛktɪvli/ | adv. | 非连接地；不连贯地 |  This word describes something that lacks connection or coherence.  It implies a lack of logical flow or relationship between parts.  The adverb modifies verbs, adjectives, or entire clauses, indicating a manner or way of acting or being.


例句：

He spoke nonconnectively, jumping from one topic to another without any clear transition. 他说话不连贯，从一个话题跳到另一个话题，没有任何明显的过渡。

The ideas in the essay were presented nonconnectively, making it difficult to follow the author's argument.  这篇文章中的观点表达得支离破碎，难以理解作者的论点。


近义词：incoherently, disjointedly, unconnectedly, ramblingly


反义词：coherently, logically, connectedly, systematically


词源：

The word is formed by combining the prefix "non-" (meaning "not") with the adverb "connectively" (derived from the adjective "connective," meaning "serving to connect").  "Connective" itself comes from the verb "connect," ultimately tracing back to Latin *con- ("together") + *nectere ("to bind").


USAGE->

This word is primarily an adverb and therefore doesn't have verb conjugations.  Its usage is primarily descriptive, modifying actions or states of being.  There's no need for tense variations as seen in verb conjugations.  Its meaning remains consistent across different grammatical contexts.
