falernian:/fælˈɜːrnɪən/| n. |法勒尼安酒|A strong, sweet, and highly prized wine of ancient Rome, made in the vicinity of Falernus (modern-day Campania, Italy).  It was known for its aging potential and rich flavor.  The exact grapes used are debated by scholars.|The emperor Augustus was known to enjoy a glass of Falernian.|近义词： (ancient Roman wines - no exact modern equivalent)|反义词： (no direct antonym, but perhaps a light, dry wine could be considered opposite)|名词
