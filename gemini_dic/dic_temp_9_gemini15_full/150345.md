panics:/ˈpænɪks/| n. v.|恐慌；惊慌；使恐慌|The noun "panic" refers to a sudden overwhelming fear, often causing wildly unthinking behavior. The verb "panic" means to suddenly feel or cause to feel overwhelming fear. |例句：The sudden power outage caused widespread panic. 突然的停电造成了广泛的恐慌。  The children panicked when they heard the loud noise. 孩子们听到巨响时惊慌失措。|近义词：terror, fright, alarm, dismay|反义词：calm, composure, serenity|名词、动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He panics easily. = 他很容易惊慌。
She often panics in crowded places. = 她经常在拥挤的地方感到恐慌。

一般过去时:
The crowd panicked when the fire started. = 大火开始时人群开始恐慌。
He panicked and ran away. = 他惊慌失措地跑开了。

现在完成时:
They have panicked the market with their actions. = 他们的行为已经使市场陷入恐慌。
I haven't panicked yet, but I'm starting to feel anxious. = 我还没惊慌，但我开始感到焦虑。

过去完成时:
He had panicked before he even saw the snake. = 他还没看到蛇就已经惊慌了。
She had panicked so much that she couldn't remember her address. = 她惊慌得连自己的地址都记不起来了。

将来时:
The news will panic the investors. = 这个消息将会使投资者恐慌。
If the dam breaks, people will panic. = 如果水坝决堤，人们将会惊慌失措。

虚拟语气:
If I had known what was going on, I wouldn't have panicked. = 如果我知道发生了什么，我就不会惊慌了。
I wish I hadn't panicked during the exam. = 我真希望考试的时候我没有惊慌。

被动语态:
The market was panicked by the unexpected news. = 市场因突发新闻而陷入恐慌。
The audience was panicked by the sudden loud noise. = 观众被突如其来的巨响吓了一跳。

疑问句:
Did the news panic you? = 这个消息让你恐慌了吗？
What panicked him so much? = 什么让他如此惊慌？

否定句:
He didn't panic when the lights went out. = 灯灭了的时候他并没有惊慌。
She didn't let the situation panic her. = 她没有让这种情况让她惊慌。
