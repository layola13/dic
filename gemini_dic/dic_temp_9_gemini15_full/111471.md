load-shedding:/ˈloʊdˌʃɛdɪŋ/| n. | 甩负荷；限电；电力削减 | The intentional reduction of electricity supply to avoid a complete system failure.  This is often implemented during periods of high demand or insufficient power generation capacity. | Example: Due to the extreme heatwave, the city experienced widespread load-shedding.  由于极端高温，城市经历了大范围的限电。 |近义词:power cut, brownout, rolling blackout |反义词: stable power supply
