propagandised:/ˌprɒpəˈɡændaɪzd/| v.|宣传；鼓吹|To spread propaganda; to promote a particular cause or point of view through the systematic dissemination of information, often biased or misleading.|例句：The government propagandised the war effort, portraying it as a fight for freedom. 政府对战争进行了宣传，将其描绘成一场争取自由的斗争。|例句：The company propagandised its new product, exaggerating its benefits. 这家公司对新产品进行了宣传，夸大了它的好处。|近义词：advertised, promoted, publicized, indoctrinated|反义词：counteracted, refuted, debunked


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They propagandise their political ideology through social media. = 他们通过社交媒体宣传他们的政治思想。
The company propagandizes its products with catchy slogans. = 公司用朗朗上口的口号宣传其产品。


一般过去时:
The regime propagandized its achievements to the people. = 政权向人民宣传其成就。
They propagandized the benefits of the new policy to the public. = 他们向公众宣传这项新政策的好处。


现在完成时:
The media has propagandized the idea that violence is necessary. = 媒体一直在宣传暴力是必要的这种观点。
The cult has propagandized its followers for decades. = 这个邪教几十年来一直在向其追随者进行宣传。


过去完成时:

The government had propagandized the public into believing a false narrative. = 政府通过宣传让公众相信了一个虚假的叙事。

虚拟语气:
