consistent: /kənˈsɪstənt/ | adj. | 一致的；始终如一的；坚持的 |

英文解析:  Agreement or harmony;  unchanging in nature, standard, or effect over time.  Something that is consistent remains the same or similar over time.  It can refer to actions, behavior, quality, or results.


例句：

He is consistent in his support for the project.  = 他始终如一地支持这个项目。

Her work has been consistently high quality. = 她的作品一直保持着高水准。


近义词：uniform, steady, regular, unchanging, reliable, dependable


反义词：inconsistent, variable, fluctuating, erratic, unreliable


USAGE->

一般现在时:

He is consistent in his efforts. = 他在努力方面始终如一。
Her opinions are consistent with her actions. = 她的观点和她行为一致。


一般过去时:

His performance was consistent throughout the season. = 他整个赛季的表现都很稳定。
Their responses were consistently positive. = 他们的回应始终积极。


现在完成时:

He has been consistent in his training regimen. = 他一直坚持他的训练计划。
She has consistently exceeded expectations. = 她一直超额完成预期。


过去完成时:

They had been consistent in their opposition to the plan. = 他们一直以来都反对这项计划。
The data had consistently shown the same results. = 数据一直以来都显示相同的结果。


将来时:

He will be consistent in his approach to the problem. = 他将坚持解决问题的方法。
We expect her performance to be consistent in the future. = 我们期望她未来的表现保持一致。


虚拟语气:

If he were more consistent, he would succeed. = 如果他更坚定一些，他就会成功。
I wish she were more consistent in her studies. = 我希望她在学习上更专注一些。


被动语态:

His arguments were consistently found to be valid. = 他的论点始终被认为是有效的。
The results were shown to be consistent across all groups. = 结果显示所有组别都一致。


疑问句:

Is his behavior consistent with his claims? = 他的行为与其说法一致吗？
Are the findings consistent with previous research? = 这些发现与之前的研究结果一致吗？


否定句:

His performance was not consistent. = 他的表现不稳定。
The results were not consistently reproducible. = 结果无法持续复制。
