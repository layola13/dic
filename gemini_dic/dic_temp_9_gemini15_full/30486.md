centrol:/ˈsɛntrəl/| adj.|中心的，中央的，主要的|central means situated at the middle or center of something.|The central theme of the book is love. 这本书的中心主题是爱。|近义词：central, middle, chief, main|反义词:peripheral, outer, marginal


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The sun is central to our solar system.  太阳是我们太阳系的中心。
The library is centrally located in the town. 图书馆位于城镇中心。


一般过去时:
The meeting was central to the decision-making process. 会议对决策过程至关重要。
The old town was the central area of the city. 老城区是城市的中心区域。


现在完成时:
The issue has remained central to the debate.  这个问题一直是辩论的中心。
The government has made it central to its policy. 政府已将其作为其政策的中心。


过去完成时:
The problem had been central to their difficulties.  这个问题一直是他们困难的中心。
The plan had already been central to their strategy. 该计划早已成为他们战略的中心。


将来时:
The project will be central to our future success.  这个项目将对我们未来的成功至关重要。
The new technology will be centrally important to the industry. 新技术将对该行业至关重要。


虚拟语气:
If the proposal were central to the agreement, we would accept it. 如果该提案是协议的核心，我们会接受它。
I wish the issue were more central to the discussion. 我希望这个问题能更受讨论关注。


被动语态:
The decision was deemed central to the outcome.  该决定被认为对结果至关重要。
The plan is considered central to the project's success. 该计划被认为对项目的成功至关重要。


疑问句:
Is the problem central to your concerns?  这个问题是你关注的核心吗？
Is this location centrally situated in the city? 这个位置位于市中心吗？


否定句:
The argument was not central to the debate.  该论点并非辩论的中心。
The issue is not centrally important to the company. 该问题对公司而言并不十分重要。
