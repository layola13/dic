disclosures: /dɪˈskloʊʒərz/ | n. | 披露；揭露；公开 | The act of making something known that was previously secret or unknown.  It often refers to the act of revealing information, especially in a formal or official context. | 例句：The company's financial disclosures revealed significant losses. 公司的财务披露显示出巨大的损失。

The disclosures about the scandal shocked the public. 关于这起丑闻的披露震惊了公众。


近义词：revelations, unveilings, exposures, announcements | 反义词：concealments, secrets, suppressions | 词性: 名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company makes regular disclosures about its financial performance. = 公司定期披露其财务业绩。
She makes full disclosures of her assets. = 她充分披露了自己的资产。


一般过去时:
The government made several important disclosures yesterday. = 政府昨天做了几项重要的披露。
He made a full disclosure of his involvement in the project. = 他充分披露了他参与该项目的经历。


现在完成时:
The company has made several disclosures this year. = 公司今年已经做了几次披露。
They have made full disclosures to the authorities. = 他们已向当局做了全面披露。


过去完成时:
Before the trial, the witness had made no disclosures about the defendant's activities. = 在审判之前，证人没有披露关于被告行为的任何信息。
The investigators had already made several key disclosures before the press conference. = 在新闻发布会之前，调查人员已经做了几项关键的披露。


将来时:

虚拟语气:
If the company had made earlier disclosures, the damage would have been less. = 如果公司早些披露信息，损害就会小一些。
I wish the government would make more transparent disclosures. = 我希望政府能够做出更透明的披露。


被动语态:

The information was disclosed to the public through a press release. = 通过新闻稿向公众披露了该信息。
