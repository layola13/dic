outvoyaging: /ˌaʊtˈvɔɪɪdʒɪŋ/ | v. |超越航行；航行距离更远 | To surpass in extent of voyages; to exceed in the distance sailed. This word is a relatively rare and specialized term, primarily used in nautical contexts to describe one ship or person exceeding another in the distance or extent of their voyages.  It implies a competitive aspect, suggesting a race or contest of seafaring prowess. | Example:  The *Endurance*’s outvoyaging of the *Terra Nova* during Shackleton's Antarctic expedition is a testament to his exceptional seamanship. = 在沙克尔顿的南极探险中，*坚忍号*超越*特拉诺瓦号*的航行距离，证明了他非凡的海上技艺。 | 近义词: outsailed, surpassed, exceeded | 反义词:  undersailed (less common), lagged behind
