nightward:/ˈnaɪtwərd/| adv.|向夜间；朝夜里；夜向|Moving or directed toward the night or nightfall.  It implies a movement or direction towards the time of night, rather than simply being in the night.|例句：The birds flew nightward as darkness fell.  鸟儿在夜幕降临时飞向夜空。
例句：The shadows lengthened, stretching nightward. 影子拉长，伸向夜里。|近义词：towards night, into the night|反义词:dayward


USAGE->
This word is an adverb, and as such it doesn't conjugate like verbs.  Therefore, tense changes are not applicable.  It simply describes the direction of movement or orientation.

PHRASE->
There are no common phrases specifically using "nightward".  Its usage is generally quite straightforward and limited to describing direction.
