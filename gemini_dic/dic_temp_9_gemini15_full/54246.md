disgorgements:/dɪsˈɡɔːrdʒmənts/| n.| 涌出；吐出；(尤指被迫) 返还；廉价抛售 | The act of disgorging; the expulsion or ejection of something from the body, especially by vomiting.  It also refers to the forced return of something obtained illegally or improperly, often money or assets. |例句：The company faced disgorgements of profits obtained through fraudulent activities.  这家公司面临着因欺诈活动获得利润而被强制返还的处罚。

The volcanic eruption caused disgorgements of lava and ash.  火山爆发导致熔岩和火山灰喷出。


近义词：expulsions, ejections, regurgitations, refunds (in the context of forced return)
反义词：retention, withholding, acquisition (in the context of forced return)
词性: 名词

USAGE->

The volcano disgorges lava regularly. = 火山定期喷出熔岩。
He disgorges his opinions freely. = 他自由地表达自己的意见。


一般过去时:
The whale disgorged a large amount of water. = 鲸鱼吐出了大量的水。
The criminal was forced to disgorge his ill-gotten gains. = 罪犯被迫吐出他的不义之财。


现在完成时:

She has disgorged all her secrets.  = 她已经吐露了她所有的秘密。


过去完成时:
The river had disgorged its floodwaters into the sea. = 河流已将洪水排入大海。
He had disgorged the information before the meeting began. = 他在会议开始前就已经透露了信息。

疑问句:

词源:
From Middle English *disgorgen, from Old French desgorger, from des- (from Latin dis-) + gorger "to gorge, swallow," from Vulgar Latin *gurgutio, variant of Latin gurges "whirlpool, gulf."  The sense evolution reflects the image of something being violently expelled from a confined space.
