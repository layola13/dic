repayability: /ˌriːpeɪəˈbɪləti/ | n. |偿还能力，可偿还性 | The ability of a loan or debt to be repaid.  It considers factors like the borrower's income, credit history, and the value of any collateral.  | Example: The bank carefully assessed the repayability of the loan before approving it.  银行在批准贷款之前仔细评估了贷款的可偿还性。  The project's high repayability made it attractive to investors.  该项目的偿还能力强，对投资者很有吸引力。 | 近义词：solvency, creditworthiness | 反义词：insolvency, default risk |  无
