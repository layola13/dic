misconducted:/ˌmɪskənˈdʌktɪd/ | adj. |行为不端的；处理不当的 |  Describes something that has been done wrongly or improperly, often implying a breach of rules or ethical standards. It usually refers to actions or behavior, not things.  |Example: The experiment was misconducted, leading to inaccurate results.  实验进行得不正当，导致结果不准确。

The official was accused of misconducting himself in office.  这位官员被指控在任期间行为不端。


近义词：improperly handled, badly managed, mishandled,  faulty, flawed

反义词：properly conducted, well-managed, correctly handled


词性:形容词

词源:

mis- (错误地, 不好地) + conduct (行为, 处置)  The prefix "mis-" indicates a wrong or improper action.  "Conduct" refers to behavior or the manner in which something is carried out.  Therefore, "misconducted" means "badly or wrongly conducted."
