shmoozed:/ʃmuːzd/| v.| 奉承；巴结；闲聊；拉关系 | To engage in excessive flattery or ingratiation; to chat or network casually, often to gain advantage. | 例句：He shmoozed the investors, hoping to secure funding for his startup. 他奉承投资者，希望能为他的创业公司获得资金。

例句：She shmoozed with the CEO at the conference, hoping to make a good impression.  她在会议上与首席执行官闲聊，希望能留下好印象。


近义词：flatter, ingratiate, network, schmooze (shmooze is a near synonym, often used interchangeably)

反义词：offend, alienate, ignore


词性：动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He shmoozes with important clients. = 他与重要的客户拉关系。
She shmoozes her way to the top. = 她靠奉承往上爬。


一般过去时:
He shmoozed the politician at the gala. = 他在晚会上奉承那位政治家。
She shmoozed her way into the party. = 她通过奉承混进了派对。


现在完成时:
He has shmoozed his way into a lucrative contract. = 他通过奉承获得了一份利润丰厚的合同。
She has shmoozed with many influential people. = 她已经与许多有影响力的人拉过关系。


过去完成时:
He had shmoozed the board before presenting his proposal. = 他在提交提案之前已经奉承了董事会。
She had shmoozed the editor for weeks before getting her article published. = 在她的文章发表之前，她已经奉承编辑好几个星期了。


将来时:
He will shmooze the potential buyers at the trade show. = 他将在贸易展上奉承潜在买家。
She will shmooze her way to a promotion. = 她将通过奉承获得晋升。


虚拟语气:
If he shmoozed the right people, he might get the job. = 如果他奉承了合适的人，他可能会得到这份工作。
I wish I had shmoozed the professor more during the semester. = 我真希望这学期我多奉承一下教授。


被动语态:
The investors were shmoozed by the entrepreneur. = 投资者受到了企业家的奉承。
The new employee was shmoozed by the senior team. = 新员工受到了高级团队的巴结。


疑问句:
Did he shmooze the committee members? = 他是否奉承了委员会成员？
Does she shmooze her colleagues often? = 她经常奉承她的同事吗？


否定句:
He didn't shmooze the audience; he spoke directly to them. = 他没有奉承听众；他直接对他们讲话。
She doesn't shmooze her way to success; she works hard. = 她不是靠奉承获得成功的；她努力工作。


PHRASE->
shmooze with someone = 与某人闲聊/拉关系
shmooze one's way to something = 通过奉承获得某物
shmooze someone into something = 奉承某人做某事
shmooze up to someone = 巴结某人
get shmoozed = 被奉承
