granted:/ˈɡrɑːntɪd/ | adj. v. | 同意的；准予的；承认的 |  to agree to give or allow (something requested) | 近义词： conceded, allowed, admitted | 反义词：denied, refused


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He grants her wish. = 他答应了她的愿望。
The government grants funding for research. = 政府拨款用于研究。


一般过去时:
They granted me permission to enter. = 他们准许我进入。
She granted his request for a loan. = 她批准了他的贷款请求。


现在完成时:
The university has granted him a scholarship. = 大学已给他奖学金。
They have granted many requests for help. = 他们已经批准了许多求助请求。


过去完成时:
She had granted him many favors in the past. = 她过去曾给他很多恩惠。
The judge had granted the defendant's motion for a new trial. = 法官已批准被告关于重新审判的动议。


将来时:
They will grant him asylum. = 他们将给予他政治庇护。
The committee will grant the proposal after review. = 委员会审查后将批准该提案。


虚拟语气:
If they granted my request, I would be very happy. = 如果他们答应我的请求，我会很高兴。
I wish they granted me more time. = 我希望他们给我更多时间。


被动语态:
His request was granted. = 他的请求得到了批准。
Funding was granted to the project. = 该项目获得了资金支持。


疑问句:
Will the permission be granted? = 许可会被批准吗？
Was his application granted? = 他的申请批准了吗？


否定句:
They did not grant his request. = 他们没有批准他的请求。
The visa was not granted. = 签证没有获批。


PHRASE->
take for granted = 想当然
granted that = 姑且认为，假定
it is granted that = 众所周知
take something for granted = 认为…理所当然
granted, but = 诚然，但是


词源:
源自古法语granter，意为“给予”。  最终源自拉丁语grantire，意为“保证，给予”。
