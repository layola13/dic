cognovit:/kɒɡˈnəʊvɪt/ | n. |认罪；承认；认定书 | A formal acknowledgment of debt or liability, especially one made in court to avoid a trial.  It is a written confession of judgment, allowing a creditor to obtain a default judgment against a debtor.  | Example:  The defendant entered a cognovit judgment, accepting the plaintiff's claim. = 被告提交了认罪判决书，接受了原告的索赔。
The debtor signed a cognovit note, waiving his right to a trial. = 债务人签署了一份认罪票据，放弃了受审的权利。 | 近义词：confession, admission, acknowledgment | 反义词：denial, contestation | noun
