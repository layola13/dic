prefigured:/ˈpriːfɪɡərd/| vt.|预示，预兆，预想|To represent or indicate beforehand; foreshadow.|The ancient myths prefigured many of the themes found in modern literature.  古代神话预示了现代文学中许多主题。|The dark clouds prefigured the approaching storm.乌云预示着即将到来的暴风雨。|foreshadow, anticipate, foretell|unforeseen, unexpected|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The artist's early works prefigure his later masterpieces. = 艺术家的早期作品预示着他后来的杰作。
This event prefigures a significant shift in the political landscape. = 此事件预示着政治格局的重大转变。


一般过去时:
The ancient prophecies prefigured the fall of the empire. = 古代预言预示了帝国的灭亡。
His nervous behavior prefigured the bad news he was about to deliver. = 他紧张的行为预示着他即将带来的坏消息。


现在完成时:

过去完成时:

将来时:
Experts predict that these trends will prefigure a new era of technological advancement. = 专家预测，这些趋势将预示着一个新的技术进步时代。
The current negotiations are likely to prefigure a new trade agreement. = 当前的谈判很可能会预示着一项新的贸易协定。


虚拟语气:

被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
The initial signs did not prefigure the severity of the storm. = 初始迹象并没有预示风暴的严重程度。
