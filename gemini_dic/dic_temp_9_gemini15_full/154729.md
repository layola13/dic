perspiring: /pərˈspaɪrɪŋ/ | v. | 出汗的；冒汗的 |  The act of sweating; emitting perspiration.  |例句：He was perspiring profusely after the run. 他跑步后汗流浃背。

例句：The athlete was perspiring heavily in the humid weather.  这位运动员在潮湿的天气里汗流不止。


近义词：sweating, sweating profusely

反义词：dry, dehydrated


词性：现在分词(Verb)


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He perspires easily. = 他容易出汗。
She perspires a lot during exercise. = 她在运动时出汗很多。


一般过去时:
He perspired heavily during the marathon. = 他在马拉松比赛中大量出汗。
She perspired profusely in the sauna. = 她在桑拿房里汗流浃背。


现在完成时:
I have perspired a lot today. = 我今天已经出了很多汗。
He has perspired so much that his shirt is soaked. = 他出了那么多汗，衬衫都湿透了。


过去完成时:
He had perspired before he even started the race. = 他甚至还没开始比赛就已出汗了。
She had perspired so much that she felt weak. = 她出了那么多汗以至于感觉虚弱。


将来时:
I will perspire if I run a marathon. = 如果我跑马拉松，我会出汗。
He will perspire in the hot sun. = 他会在烈日下出汗。


虚拟语气:
If I were to run a marathon, I would perspire. = 如果我要跑马拉松，我会出汗。
I wish I hadn't perspired so much. = 我真希望我没有出那么多汗。


被动语态:  (很少用被动语态)


疑问句:
Are you perspiring? = 你在出汗吗？
Is he perspiring excessively? = 他出汗过多吗？


否定句:
I don't perspire easily. = 我不容易出汗。
She didn't perspire at all during the yoga class. = 她在瑜伽课上一点汗都没出。


PHRASE->
freely perspiring  大量出汗
profusely perspiring 汗流浃背
perspiring heavily  大量出汗
perspiring slightly  轻微出汗
easily perspiring  容易出汗


词源:
From the Latin word "perspirāre", meaning "to breathe through".  It literally means to "breathe through" or "exhale," but over time it evolved to specifically mean sweating.
