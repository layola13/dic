quasi-spiritually: /ˈkwɑːzaɪ ˌspɪrɪtʃuəli/ | adj. | 准精神的；似是而非的精神上的 |

英文解析:  Describes something that resembles or suggests spirituality but lacks its genuine essence or depth. It may involve practices or beliefs that mimic spiritual traditions but without the core tenets of genuine faith or religious experience.  It often implies a superficial or imitative approach to spiritual matters.

例句:

He pursued a quasi-spiritual lifestyle, focusing on meditation and yoga but without any deep religious commitment. = 他追求一种准精神的生活方式，专注于冥想和瑜伽，但没有任何深厚的宗教信仰。


Her interest in crystals and tarot cards represented a quasi-spiritual exploration rather than a true religious devotion. = 她对水晶和塔罗牌的兴趣代表着一种似是而非的精神探索，而不是真正的宗教虔诚。


近义词:  pseudo-spiritual, superficially spiritual, nominally spiritual, seemingly spiritual


反义词:  genuinely spiritual, deeply religious, devout, truly faithful


词性: adjective (形容词)
