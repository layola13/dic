orpharion:/ɔːrˈfeəriən/| n. |奥法里翁（一种文艺复兴时期乐器）|A type of small, plucked lute popular in the 16th and 17th centuries, particularly in England.  It was characterized by a pear-shaped body and a relatively short neck.  The orpharion was often used in consort music and was a popular instrument among amateur musicians.|Example: The orpharion, with its delicate sound, was a favorite instrument in Elizabethan court music.  奥法里翁以其细腻的声音，成为伊丽莎白时代宫廷音乐中备受喜爱的乐器。|近义词：cittern, lute|反义词：None.
