prohibiting:/prəˈhɪbɪtɪŋ/| v. |禁止，阻止|The act of forbidding something; preventing something from happening or being done.|例句：The law prohibiting smoking in public places is strictly enforced.  公共场所禁止吸烟的法律得到严格执行。


例句：The school is prohibiting the use of cell phones during class. 学校禁止上课期间使用手机。


近义词：banning, forbidding, preventing


反义词：allowing, permitting


词性:动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government prohibits the sale of illegal drugs. = 政府禁止销售非法药物。
The rules prohibit eating in the classroom. = 规定禁止在教室里吃饭。


一般过去时:
They prohibited the construction of new buildings. = 他们禁止建造新的建筑物。
The school prohibited students from leaving campus. = 学校禁止学生离开校园。


现在完成时:
The city has prohibited the use of fireworks. = 该城市已禁止使用烟花。
We have prohibited access to the classified documents. = 我们已经禁止访问机密文件。


过去完成时:
The company had prohibited the release of the information. = 公司已经禁止发布该信息。
They had prohibited smoking before the meeting. = 会议前他们已经禁止吸烟。


将来时:
The council will prohibit parking in this area. = 理事会将禁止在此区域停车。
They will prohibit the sale of alcohol to minors. = 他们将禁止向未成年人出售酒精。


虚拟语气:
If they prohibited the import, the economy would suffer. = 如果他们禁止进口，经济就会受损。
I wish they prohibited the use of plastic bags. = 我希望他们禁止使用塑料袋。


被动语态:
The use of drones is prohibited in this airspace. = 在此空域禁止使用无人机。
The event was prohibited due to safety concerns. = 由于安全问题，该活动被禁止。


疑问句:
Does the law prohibit the use of this substance? = 该法律是否禁止使用这种物质？
Does the sign prohibit parking here? = 这个标志是否禁止在此停车？


否定句:
The law does not prohibit free speech. = 法律不禁止言论自由。
The company doesn't prohibit employees from using social media. = 公司不禁止员工使用社交媒体。

PHRASE->
prohibit from doing something = 禁止做某事
strictly prohibit = 严格禁止
prohibit the use of = 禁止使用
prohibit entry = 禁止入内
prohibit access to = 禁止进入
