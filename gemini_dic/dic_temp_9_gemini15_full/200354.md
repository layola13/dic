sky-high:/ˈskaɪ haɪ/| adj.|极高的；非常昂贵的；|Extremely high; very expensive.|例句：The prices are sky-high this year.今年物价飞涨。

The skyscraper is sky-high. 这座摩天大楼非常高。|近义词：very high, exorbitant, astronomical|反义词：very low, inexpensive, cheap|


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The prices are sky-high. = 物价很高。
The building is sky-high. = 这栋建筑物非常高。


一般过去时:
The prices were sky-high last year. = 去年物价很高。
The expectations were sky-high before the game. = 比赛前预期很高。


现在完成时:
The cost of living has become sky-high. = 生活成本已经变得非常高。
Inflation has driven prices sky-high. = 通货膨胀导致物价飞涨。


过去完成时:
The demand had been sky-high before the product was released. = 产品发布前需求非常高。
The hopes had been sky-high, but the results were disappointing. = 之前的希望很高，但结果令人失望。


将来时:
The interest rates will be sky-high next year. = 明年的利率将会非常高。
The demand for this product will be sky-high in the future. = 未来对这种产品的需求将非常高。


虚拟语气:
If the prices were sky-high, we wouldn't buy it. = 如果价格很高，我们就不会买了。
If the expectations had been sky-high, they might have performed better. = 如果预期很高，他们可能表现得更好。


被动语态:
The price was considered sky-high by many consumers. = 许多消费者认为价格过高。
The expectations were described as sky-high by the media. = 媒体将预期描述为非常高。


疑问句:
Are the prices sky-high in this city? = 这个城市的物价很高吗？
Were the expectations sky-high for the new movie? = 对这部新电影的预期很高吗？


否定句:
The prices are not sky-high in that area. = 那个地区的物价并不高。
The building isn't sky-high, it's quite average. = 这栋建筑物并不高，相当一般。
