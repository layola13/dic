prime:/praɪm/| adj., n.|主要的；最好的；首要的；全盛时期；素数|

英文解析:
As an adjective, "prime" means first in importance, rank, or quality; of the best possible quality; or being a prime number (a natural number greater than 1 that is not a product of two smaller natural numbers).  As a noun, it refers to the best or most active period of someone's life or something's development, or a prime number.

例句:
This is their prime selling season. 这是他们主要的销售旺季。

The prime minister addressed the nation.  首相向全国发表讲话。


近义词:
main, chief, principal, best, excellent, first-rate, peak, height


反义词:
secondary, inferior, unimportant, worst, subprime


词性:
adj., n.

词源:
From Old French *prime, from Latin prīmus ("first").  The sense of "first in rank or importance" is the oldest. The mathematical sense of "prime number" developed later, based on the idea that these numbers are fundamental or primary building blocks of all other numbers.
