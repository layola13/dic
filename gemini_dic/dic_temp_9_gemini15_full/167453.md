/ˌpriːvedʒɪˈteɪʃən/| n. |先期植被；先锋植被|Prevegetation refers to the initial plant life that colonizes a previously barren or disturbed area, such as after a volcanic eruption or a wildfire.  It plays a crucial role in ecological succession. |Example: The prevegetation of the lava field consisted mainly of hardy lichens and mosses.|近义词：pioneer vegetation, initial vegetation|反义词：climax vegetation
