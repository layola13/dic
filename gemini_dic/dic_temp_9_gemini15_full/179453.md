redundancy:/rɪˈdʌndənsi/ | n. |冗余；多余；裁员 |The state of being not needed or useful; superfluousness.  It can also refer to the dismissal of employees from a job, often due to a reduction in workforce. |例句：The redundancy of the information made the report unnecessarily long.  冗余的信息使报告不必要地冗长。

例句：The company announced a large-scale redundancy program due to economic downturn.  由于经济低迷，该公司宣布了一项大规模裁员计划。

近义词：superfluity, surplus, superfluousness, dismissal, layoff

反义词：necessity, essentiality


词源：源自于拉丁语 *redundantia，由 *redundare（溢出，过多）派生而来。
