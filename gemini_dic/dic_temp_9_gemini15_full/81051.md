graha:/ˈɡrɑːhɑː/| n. |格哈（梵文，意为“星球”或“行星”）|Graha is a Sanskrit word that refers to a planet or celestial body. In Hindu astrology, grahas are significant celestial bodies believed to influence human lives.  |The nine grahas in Hindu astrology are Sun, Moon, Mars, Mercury, Jupiter, Venus, Saturn, Rahu, and Ketu. |近义词：planet, celestial body |反义词：无


USAGE->
(由于graha本身并非动词，无法进行时态变化。以下例句展示graha在不同语境中的用法。)

例句1:
In Vedic astrology, the position of the grahas at the time of birth is considered crucial.  = 在吠陀占星术中，出生时行星的位置被认为至关重要。

例句2:
The transit of Jupiter, a major graha, is eagerly awaited by many. = 木星（一颗主要的行星）的运行被许多人热切期待。


例句3:
The effects of the different grahas on an individual's life are complex and varied. = 不同行星对个人生活的影响复杂而多样。

例句4:
Understanding the grahas is essential for accurate astrological predictions. = 了解行星对于准确的占星预测至关重要。


PHRASE->
(由于graha本身是一个名词，其搭配通常是与其他名词或形容词构成短语。)

graha parivartan: planetary exchange (行星交换)

graha dosha: planetary affliction (行星不利)

graha gochara: planetary transit (行星运行)

graha yoni: planetary compatibility (行星相容性)

navagraha: nine planets (九大行星)
