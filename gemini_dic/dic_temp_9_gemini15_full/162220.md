pre-Raphaelitism: /ˌpriːræfəˈeɪlaɪtɪzəm/ | n. | 拉斐尔前派主义 | The principles and practices of the Pre-Raphaelite Brotherhood, a group of English painters, poets, and critics who flourished in the mid-19th century.  Their art was characterized by a rejection of academic conventions, a focus on intense realism and detail, and a strong moral and spiritual dimension often inspired by medieval and early Renaissance art.  They aimed to revive what they perceived as the purity and sincerity of art before Raphael's work. | The Pre-Raphaelite paintings are known for their vibrant colors and meticulous detail. | 近义词：Pre-Raphaelitism (the movement itself) | 反义词：Academic art (the style they reacted against)
