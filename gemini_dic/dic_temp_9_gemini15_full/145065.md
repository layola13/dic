overamplifying: /ˌoʊvərˈæmplɪfaɪɪŋ/ | v. | 过度放大；夸大其词 |  The act of exaggerating or emphasizing something to an excessive degree.  It often implies a distortion of the truth or an unfair presentation of facts. | 例句：The media's overamplifying of the minor incident caused unnecessary panic. 媒体对这件小事的过度渲染引起了不必要的恐慌。


The politician's speech was full of overamplifying claims about his achievements.  这位政治家的演讲充满了对他成就的夸大之词。


近义词：exaggerating, overstating, inflating, hyperbolizing | 反义词：downplaying, understating, minimizing


词性：动词 (present participle of overamplify)

词源:
The word "overamplifying" is formed from the prefix "over-" (meaning "too much" or "excessively"), the verb "amplify" (meaning "to increase the strength or intensity of"), and the present participle "-ing".  "Amplify" itself comes from the Latin word "amplificare," meaning "to enlarge."
