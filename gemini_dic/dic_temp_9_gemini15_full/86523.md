hejira:/hɪˈdʒaɪrə/| n.| 迁徙；逃亡；（尤指穆斯林的）迁徙到麦地那|The word "hejira" refers to an emigration or flight, especially the flight of Muhammad and his followers from Mecca to Medina in 622 AD, which marks the beginning of the Islamic calendar.  It can also refer to any flight from a place or situation considered oppressive or dangerous.|例句：The hejira of the family from the war-torn country was a desperate but necessary measure.  这家人从饱受战争摧残的国家逃亡是一项绝望但必要的措施。|近义词：exodus, migration, flight, escape|反义词：immigration, arrival, settlement|名词
