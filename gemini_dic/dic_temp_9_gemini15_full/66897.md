expedited:/ˈɛkspədaɪtɪd/| adj.| 加快的；迅速的；特急的 |  Accelerated or hastened; made to proceed faster.  It implies a process that is made quicker than usual, often due to special measures. | 例句：The expedited shipping ensured the package arrived on time. = 加急邮寄确保包裹准时到达。

例句：We requested expedited service for the repair. = 我们请求对维修进行加急服务。


近义词：fast, quick, rapid, accelerated, hastened, rushed | 反义词：delayed, slow, decelerated, postponed


词源:  From Middle English *expediten, from Old French expédier ("to dispatch, expedite"), from Latin expedīre ("to disentangle, extricate, free, release, clear, get rid of," also "to fit out, equip," from ex- ("out") + pēs ("foot").  The original sense related to freeing something from obstruction, which evolved into the modern meaning of speeding up a process.
