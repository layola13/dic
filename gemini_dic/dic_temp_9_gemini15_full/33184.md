chromolithograph:/ˈkroʊmoʊlɪθəˌɡræf/ | n. |彩色石印画 |A chromolithograph is a print made using chromolithography, a method of printing using multiple lithographic stones or plates, each inked with a different color, to create a multicolored image.  Chromolithographs were popular in the 19th century for producing high-quality, richly colored images for posters, illustrations, and other uses. | Example: The museum displayed a stunning chromolithograph of a Victorian-era botanical garden.  博物馆展出了维多利亚时代植物园的精美彩色石印画。 |近义词：color print, lithograph,  |反义词：monochrome print
