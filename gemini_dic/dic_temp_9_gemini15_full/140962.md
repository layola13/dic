one-upmanship: /ˌwʌnˈʌpmənʃɪp/ | n. | 炫耀，技高一筹 | The act of trying to surpass someone else, especially in a competitive way; an attempt to show that one is superior.  | 例句：His constant one-upmanship at parties quickly made him unpopular.  他的不断炫耀很快就让他在聚会上不受欢迎。  |  近义词：competitive spirit, boasting, showing off, outdoing | 反义词：cooperation, humility, modesty |  名词

词源:
The word "one-upmanship" is a blend of "one-up" (meaning to surpass or outdo someone) and "manship" (referring to skill or expertise).  It was coined in the mid-20th century, reflecting a growing awareness of competitive behaviors in society.
