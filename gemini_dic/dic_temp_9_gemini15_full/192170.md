scrape:/skreɪp/| vt. & n. |刮擦；擦掉；勉强获得；困境 |to rub or drag something rough or sharp against a surface.  To remove something from a surface by scraping. To barely manage to get or achieve something. A difficult or unpleasant situation.|例句：He scraped the mud off his boots. 他刮掉了靴子上的泥巴。
The car scraped against the wall. 车子蹭到了墙上。


He scraped through the exam. 他勉强通过了考试。
They're scraping by on a tiny income. 他们靠微薄的收入勉强维持生计。

近义词：scratch, rub, scrape along, struggle,  | 反义词：polish, smooth,  flourish

词源:
The word "scrape" comes from the Old English word *scroppian, meaning "to scratch."  This further derives from a Proto-Germanic root.  The meaning has evolved to include the various senses we use today, reflecting the versatile nature of the action of scraping.
