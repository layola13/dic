repurposing:/ˌriːˈpɜːrpəsɪŋ/ | v. | 重新利用；赋予新用途 |  The act of finding a new purpose or use for something that is no longer needed for its original purpose. This often involves creativity and innovation to adapt an existing item or resource for a different function. | 例句：The company is repurposing its old factory building into a modern art gallery.  这家公司正在将旧工厂改建成现代美术馆。


例句：We are repurposing plastic bottles into planters. 我们正在将塑料瓶重新利用做成花盆。


近义词：reuse, recycle, refashion, retool, adapt


反义词：discard, dispose of, scrap, destroy


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They repurpose old tires into playground equipment. 他们将旧轮胎改造成操场设备。
The company repurposes waste materials into new products.  这家公司将废料重新利用制成新产品。


一般过去时:
She repurposed her grandmother's old quilt into a stylish jacket. 她把祖母的旧棉被改造成一件时尚的夹克。
The artist repurposed discarded metal into sculptures.  这位艺术家将废弃的金属改造成雕塑。


现在完成时:
They have repurposed many items around the house. 他们已经重新利用了家里的许多物品。
The city has repurposed several abandoned buildings into community centers.  该市已将几栋废弃建筑改造成社区中心。


过去完成时:
They had repurposed the old barn before the storm hit.  暴风雨来临之前，他们已经重新利用了旧谷仓。
The company had repurposed its old machinery before purchasing new equipment.  公司在购买新设备之前已经重新利用了旧机器。


将来时:
We will repurpose the old furniture for the new office. 我们将把旧家具重新利用到新办公室。
The school will repurpose the old library into a computer lab.  学校将把旧图书馆改造成电脑实验室。


虚拟语气:
If we had more time, we would repurpose the old boxes. 如果我们有更多时间，我们会重新利用旧箱子。
I wish we could repurpose this outdated technology.  我希望我们能够重新利用这项过时的技术。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
