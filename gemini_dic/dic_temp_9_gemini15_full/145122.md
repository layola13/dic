overargumentative: /ˌoʊvərˈɑːrɡjumɛntətɪv/ | adj. | 过分争论的，好争论的 | Excessively given to arguing; tending to argue too much or too readily.  |  He's become rather overargumentative in his old age. 他上了年纪后变得过于爱争论了。 | 近义词：contentious, disputatious, argumentative, quarrelsome | 反义词：agreeable, compliant, docile

词源:  The word is formed by combining the prefix "over-" (meaning excessively or too much) with "argumentative" (tending to argue).  "Argumentative" itself comes from "argument" (a reason or set of reasons given in support of an idea, action, or theory) + "-ative" (forming adjectives).
