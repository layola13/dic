initiative:/ɪˈnɪʃətɪv/| n.|主动性；倡议；主动采取的行动|The ability to take action without being told what to do; a new plan or program.  |例句：She showed great initiative in solving the problem. 她展现出极大的主动性解决了这个问题。
例句：The government launched a new initiative to reduce poverty. 政府发起了一项新的扶贫倡议。|近义词：enterprise, drive, leadership, undertaking|反义词：passivity, inaction


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He takes the initiative in most projects. = 他在大多数项目中都主动承担责任。
The company shows initiative in its marketing strategies. = 这家公司在营销策略方面表现出主动性。

一般过去时:
She took the initiative to organize the event. = 她主动组织了这次活动。
The team showed initiative by solving the problem independently. = 团队通过独立解决问题展现了主动性。

现在完成时:
They have taken the initiative to improve customer service. = 他们已经主动改进客户服务。
The government has launched several initiatives to combat climate change. = 政府已经启动了几项应对气候变化的倡议。

过去完成时:
He had taken the initiative to contact the client before the meeting. = 他在会议之前已经主动联系了客户。
The company had already launched several initiatives to increase market share. = 这家公司已经启动了几项增加市场份额的倡议。


将来时:
We will take the initiative to address the issue. = 我们将主动解决这个问题。
The organization will launch a new initiative to support education. = 该组织将启动一项新的教育支持倡议。

虚拟语气:
If he had shown more initiative, he would have been promoted. = 如果他表现出更多主动性，他就会被提升了。
I wish the team had taken the initiative to resolve the conflict earlier. = 我希望团队早点主动解决冲突。


被动语态:
The initiative was launched by the CEO. = 这项倡议是由首席执行官启动的。
Many initiatives are being taken to improve public health. = 正在采取许多措施来改善公共卫生。


疑问句:
Did he take the initiative to help? = 他主动帮忙了吗？
What initiative will the company undertake next? = 公司接下来将采取什么主动措施？


否定句:
He didn't show much initiative in his work. = 他在工作中没有表现出多少主动性。
The company failed to take the initiative in the market. = 这家公司未能抢占市场先机。


PHRASE->
take the initiative: 主动采取行动
on one's own initiative: 主动地，自发地
show initiative: 表现出主动性
a new initiative: 一个新的倡议
lose initiative: 失去主动权

词源:
源自于拉丁语 "initium" (开始) ，表示开始或启动某事物的动作或能力。  "initiative" 最初是指开始或开端，后来演变为指主动采取行动的能力。
