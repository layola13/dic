clamming-up: /ˈklæmɪŋ ʌp/ | v. | 闭嘴；变得沉默寡言 | To become silent or refuse to speak, often because one is unwilling or unable to communicate. | 例句：He clammed up when I asked him about his past.  他当我问起他的过去时就闭口不言了。

例句：The witness clammed up when the lawyer started to press him.  当律师开始追问他时，证人闭嘴了。


近义词：becoming silent, shutting up, falling silent, keeping mum, buttoning up


反义词：speaking openly, being outspoken, chattering, blabbing


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He clams up whenever he's nervous. = 他每当紧张的时候就闭口不言。
She clams up around strangers. = 她在陌生人面前会变得沉默寡言。


一般过去时:
He clammed up when the police arrived. = 警察一来他就闭嘴了。
She clammed up after the accident. = 事故发生后，她变得沉默寡言了。


现在完成时:
He has clammed up since the interrogation. = 自从审讯以来，他就一直闭口不言。
She has clammed up about the whole affair. = 关于整件事，她都守口如瓶。


过去完成时:
He had clammed up before I could ask him anything. = 在我开口问他之前，他就已经闭嘴了。
She had clammed up completely by the time the meeting ended. = 等到会议结束时，她已经完全沉默了。


将来时:
He will clam up if you press him too hard. = 如果你逼问得太紧，他会闭嘴的。
She will probably clam up if you ask her about her personal life. = 如果你问她私生活的事情，她可能会闭口不言。


虚拟语气:
If he weren't so nervous, he wouldn't clam up. = 如果他不那么紧张，他就不会闭口不言。
If she felt more comfortable, she wouldn't clam up so easily. = 如果她感觉更自在一些，她就不会那么容易闭嘴了。


被动语态:  (Clamming up is rarely used passively.)


疑问句:
Did he clam up when you asked him the question? = 当你问他问题的时候，他闭嘴了吗？
Will she clam up during the interview? = 在面试过程中她会闭口不言吗？


否定句:
He didn't clam up; he just seemed lost in thought. = 他并没有闭嘴；他只是看起来在沉思。
She didn't clam up; she answered all my questions honestly. = 她没有闭嘴；她诚实地回答了我所有的问题。


PHRASE->
clam up completely = 完全闭嘴
clam up suddenly = 突然闭嘴
clam up about something = 对某事闭口不言
clam up in fear = 由于害怕而闭嘴
clam up out of embarrassment = 由于尴尬而闭嘴

词源:
The verb "clam up" is an idiom.  The origin is uncertain, but the image is likely associated with clams closing their shells tightly shut, thus refusing to open up or communicate.  The expression likely arose in American English sometime in the 20th century.
