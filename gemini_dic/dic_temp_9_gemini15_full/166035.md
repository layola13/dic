preplace:/priːˈpleɪs/ | v. |预先安置；预先放置|To put something in a place before it is needed or before something else happens.  It implies preparation or anticipation. |例句：We preplaced the emergency supplies in case of a hurricane. 我们预先放置了紧急物资以防飓风。  The technician preplaced the components on the circuit board before soldering. 技术员在焊接前预先将组件放在电路板上。|近义词：position beforehand, prepare beforehand, install in advance |反义词：remove, uninstall, displace

We preplace the orders for the holiday season. = 我们提前为假日季预先下了订单。
