facadism:/ˈfæsədɪzəm/| n. |门面主义；外观主义|The practice of preserving only the facade of a building while demolishing and replacing the rest.  It's a technique often used in urban renewal projects to maintain a sense of historical continuity while modernizing the underlying structure.  This can lead to a disconnect between the historical appearance and the modern functionality of the building.|The city council approved the facadism project, saving the historic building's facade while renovating the interior.|近义词：facade preservation, surface preservation|反义词：complete demolition,  total reconstruction|名词
