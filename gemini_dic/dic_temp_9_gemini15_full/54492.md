disintricate: /ˌdɪsˈɪntrɪkeɪt/ | vt. | 解开；使摆脱；弄清楚 | to free from entanglement or difficulty; to unravel or disentangle |

例句：

He tried to disintricate himself from the complicated legal case.  = 他试图摆脱复杂的法律纠纷。

She carefully disintricated the tangled threads. = 她小心地解开了缠结的线。


近义词： disentangle, unravel, extricate, untangle

反义词： entangle, complicate, confuse

词源:

The word "disintricate" comes from the prefix "dis-" (meaning "apart" or "away"),  "intricate" (meaning "complex" or "entangled"), and the suffix "-ate" (forming verbs).  Therefore, it literally means "to separate from what is intricate."
