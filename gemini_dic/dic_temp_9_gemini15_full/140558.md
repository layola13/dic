olifant: /ˈɒlɪfənt/ | n. |象牙；大象（古语）| An olifant is an archaic term for an elephant tusk, often made into a horn or trumpet.  The word is also sometimes used as an archaic term for an elephant itself. | Example:  The medieval knight blew a mighty blast on his olifant.  中世纪骑士在他的象牙号角上吹响了响亮的号角。  |近义词: tusk, ivory horn (in the context of a horn made from ivory) |反义词: N/A |名词
