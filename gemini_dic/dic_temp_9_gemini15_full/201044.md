sloshed:/ˈslɒʃt/ | adj. v. | 烂醉的；泼洒；冲洗 |  Highly intoxicated;  to splash liquid around carelessly; to rinse something thoroughly. | 例句：He was completely sloshed after drinking too much beer. 他喝太多啤酒后烂醉如泥了。 |近义词：drunk, intoxicated, soaked, splashed | 反义词：sober, dry


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He sloshes his drink around in the glass. = 他把饮料在杯子里晃来晃去。
They slosh water onto the floor. = 他们把水泼到地板上。


一般过去时:
She sloshed the paint onto the canvas. = 她把颜料泼到画布上。
The waves sloshed against the rocks. = 海浪拍打着岩石。


现在完成时:
He has sloshed his way through the party. = 他在派对上喝得烂醉如泥。
The rain has sloshed the mud all over the road. = 雨水把泥巴溅得到处都是。


过去完成时:
They had sloshed the wine all over the table before I arrived. = 我到之前，他们已经把酒洒了一桌子。
She had sloshed the dirty dishes before she left the kitchen. = 她离开厨房前已经把脏盘子冲洗干净了。


将来时:
He will slosh the mixture until it's smooth. = 他会把混合物搅拌均匀。
The storm will slosh the water onto the deck. = 暴风雨会把水冲到甲板上。


虚拟语气:
If I had known he was so sloshed, I wouldn't have let him drive. = 如果我知道他醉成那样，我就不会让他开车了。
I wish I hadn't sloshed the paint everywhere. = 我真希望我没有把颜料洒得到处都是。


被动语态:
The floor was sloshed with water. = 地板被水弄湿了。
The car was sloshed clean after the accident. = 事故后，汽车被冲洗干净了。


疑问句:

否定句:
He wasn't sloshed; he was just tired. = 他没有醉，只是累了。
They didn't slosh the water intentionally. = 他们不是故意把水泼出去的。
