fungoids:/ˈfʌŋɡɔɪdz/| n. |菌类；真菌状物 |Fungoids are organisms that resemble fungi in appearance or characteristics, but may not be true fungi. They often exhibit characteristics similar to fungi, such as spore production or a filamentous structure.  |例句：The researchers identified several fungoid growths on the decaying log. 研究人员在腐烂的木头上发现了多种真菌状的生长物。  |The discovery of new fungoid species in the Amazon rainforest is exciting for mycologists. 在亚马逊雨林中发现新的真菌状物种令真菌学家兴奋不已。|近义词：fungus-like, mold-like, mushroom-like |反义词：non-fungal, bacterial, viral|名词

词源:
The word "fungoid" is derived from the Latin word "fungus," meaning "mushroom" or "fungus,"  with the suffix "-oid" added, meaning "resembling" or "having the form of."  Therefore, "fungoid" literally means "resembling a fungus."
