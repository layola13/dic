inoculate:/ɪˈnɒkjʊleɪt/ | /ˈɪnɑːkjʊleɪt/ | vt. | 接种；预防接种；灌输；接种疫苗 | To introduce a vaccine or other substance into the body to produce immunity against a disease; to introduce (a microorganism) into a culture medium.  | 例句：The doctor inoculated the child against measles. 医生给孩子接种了麻疹疫苗。

The scientists inoculated the plants with a new type of bacteria. 科学家用一种新型细菌给植物接种。


近义词：vaccinate, immunize | 反义词：infect


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The doctor inoculates patients against the flu every year. = 医生每年都给病人接种流感疫苗。
She inoculates her garden with beneficial microbes. = 她用有益微生物给她的花园接种。


一般过去时:

PHRASE->
inoculate against = 预防…接种疫苗
inoculate with = 用…接种
inoculate oneself = 自己接种
inoculate a culture = 接种培养物
inoculate a plant = 给植物接种


词源:
From Latin *inoculare, from in- "in" + oculum "eye", referring to the insertion of a bud into the eye of a plant (budding).  The modern sense developed from the practice of grafting and inoculation in plants, later extended to medicine.
