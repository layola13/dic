panning-out: /ˈpænɪŋ aʊt/ | v. | 成功；最终结果；发展；显现 | To develop or work out in a particular way, especially successfully; to become clear or apparent over time.  |例句：His business plan is panning out better than expected. 他的商业计划比预期更成功。

例句：The situation is panning out differently than we anticipated. 事态的发展与我们的预期不同。


近义词：working out, developing, unfolding, materializing, turning out

反义词：failing, collapsing, falling apart


USAGE->

The plan is panning out well. = 计划进展顺利。
The investigation is panning out slowly. = 调查进展缓慢。

The experiment panned out as expected. = 实验结果如预期一样。
His investment panned out poorly. = 他的投资失败了。

词源:
The phrase "pan out" originates from gold panning, a method of separating gold from sediment using a pan.  If the panning process resulted in finding gold, it was said to "pan out" successfully.  The metaphorical extension to refer to the successful outcome of plans or events developed over time.
