exec:/ˈɛɡzɛk/| n. vt.|执行；主管；执行官|To carry out, accomplish, or fulfill (a task, plan, etc.).  Also, a person who holds an executive position.|例句：The program will exec the compiled code. = 该程序将执行编译后的代码。
The CEO is the chief exec of the company. = 首席执行官是公司的首席执行官。|近义词：execute, implement, administer, manager, director|反义词：neglect, ignore, fail


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He executes his duties diligently. = 他勤勤恳恳地履行职责。
The software executes the commands efficiently. = 该软件高效地执行命令。

一般过去时:
She executed the plan flawlessly. = 她完美地执行了计划。
The court executed the sentence. = 法院执行了判决。

现在完成时:
They have executed all the tasks assigned to them. = 他们已经完成了分配给他们的所有任务。
The company has executed several successful marketing campaigns. = 该公司已经执行了几次成功的营销活动。

过去完成时:
He had executed the project before the deadline. = 他在截止日期前完成了项目。
The team had executed the strategy prior to the launch. = 该团队在发布前已经执行了该策略。

将来时:
We will execute the new strategy next quarter. = 我们将在下个季度执行新的策略。
They will execute the plan as soon as possible. = 他们将尽快执行该计划。

虚拟语气:
If I were the manager, I would execute a different plan. = 如果我是经理，我会执行不同的计划。
It is essential that the order be executed promptly. = 必须立即执行命令。

被动语态:
The orders were executed without delay. = 命令毫不迟延地得到了执行。
The project is being executed efficiently. = 项目正在高效地执行中。

疑问句:
Will the plan be executed on time? = 计划会按时执行吗？
Have all the tasks been executed? = 所有任务都执行了吗？

否定句:
They did not execute the plan properly. = 他们没有正确执行计划。
The order was not executed. = 命令没有得到执行。
