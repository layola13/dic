imagism:/ˈɪmədʒɪzəm/| n. |意象派（诗歌）|A movement in early 20th-century English and American poetry that emphasized precise visual imagery and the use of common speech.  Imagists rejected Victorian-era poetic conventions and aimed for clarity, concision, and direct expression. |Ezra Pound and Amy Lowell were key figures in the Imagist movement. Their poems often featured vivid descriptions of objects and scenes, capturing a moment or feeling through carefully selected words. |近义词：symbolism, expressionism |反义词：abstract expressionism
