hyperinflation: /ˌhaɪpərɪnˈfleɪʃən/ | n. |  恶性通货膨胀 | Extremely rapid and excessive inflation, usually exceeding 50% per month.  It is characterized by a dramatic and continuous increase in the general price level of goods and services in an economy, resulting in a sharp decline in the purchasing power of money.  This often leads to economic instability and social unrest. |

例句：

The Weimar Republic experienced hyperinflation in the 1920s, rendering the German mark virtually worthless.  | 魏玛共和国在20世纪20年代经历了恶性通货膨胀，使得德国马克几乎一文不值。

Hyperinflation can quickly erode savings and destabilize an entire economy. | 恶性通货膨胀会迅速侵蚀储蓄并使整个经济体系不稳定。

词源:

The word "hyperinflation" is a modern coinage, combining the prefix "hyper-" (meaning "excessive" or "above") with "inflation."  "Inflation" itself comes from the Latin word "inflare," meaning "to blow into," reflecting the expansion of the money supply.
