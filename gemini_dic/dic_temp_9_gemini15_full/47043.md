datable:/ˈdeɪtəbəl/| adj.|可约会的；可测定的；可追溯日期的|capable of being dated; able to have a date assigned;  able to be determined or specified in terms of a date or time.  This can refer to both literal dates (as in historical artifacts) and metaphorical dates (as in relationships).|例句：The painting is datable to the 17th century. 这幅画可以追溯到17世纪。

The evidence is datable to the period of the war. 证据可以追溯到战争时期。|近义词：dateable, assignable, traceable|反义词：undatable, unplaceable, indeterminate

USAGE->
一般现在时:
This artifact is datable to the Bronze Age. = 这件文物可以追溯到青铜器时代。
The letter is datable to last year. = 这封信可以追溯到去年。


一般过去时:
Historians dated the manuscript to the 15th century.  = 历史学家将手稿的年代确定为15世纪。
Experts dated the coin to the Roman Empire. = 专家们将这枚硬币的年代确定为罗马帝国时期。


现在完成时:
Researchers have dated the fossil to be millions of years old. = 研究人员已经将化石的年代测定为数百万年前。
Scientists have dated the rock formation to the Jurassic period. = 科学家们已经将岩层的年代确定为侏罗纪时期。


过去完成时:
Before the discovery, the artifact had been dated to a much later period. = 在发现之前，这件文物的年代被确定为一个更晚的时期。
By then, archaeologists had already dated the ruins to the ancient civilization. = 到那时，考古学家们已经将这些遗址的年代确定为古代文明时期。


将来时:
We will date the sample using carbon-14 testing. = 我们将使用碳14测试来测定样品的年代。
Scientists expect to date the meteorite soon. = 科学家们预计很快就能测定这颗陨石的年代。


虚拟语气:
If we had more advanced technology, we could date the artifact more precisely. = 如果我们有更先进的技术，我们可以更精确地测定这件文物的年代。
If the inscription were clearer, we might be able to date the document more accurately. = 如果铭文更清晰，我们或许能够更准确地确定文件的年代。


被动语态:
The painting was dated to the early Renaissance by experts. = 这幅画被专家们确定为早期文艺复兴时期的作品。
The pottery shards were dated using thermoluminescence dating. = 陶瓷碎片是用热释光测年法测定的年代。


疑问句:
Can this document be dated to a specific year? = 这份文件能确定具体的年份吗？
When was this building dated to? = 这座建筑物是哪个年代建造的？


否定句:
The inscription was too faded to be reliably dated. = 铭文过于模糊，无法可靠地确定年代。
The object could not be dated with the available technology. = 用现有的技术无法确定该物体的年代。
