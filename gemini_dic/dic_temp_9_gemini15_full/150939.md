paralysation: /ˌpærəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. |瘫痪；麻痹；使无力 | The state of being paralyzed; the act of paralyzing.  Paralysation can refer to the loss of function in a part of the body, or to a state of inactivity or inability to act. |例句：The accident caused paralysation of his left leg. 事故导致了他的左腿瘫痪。  The strike caused a complete paralysation of the city's transport system.罢工导致了城市交通系统的完全瘫痪。|近义词：paralysis, incapacitation, disablement |反义词：activation, enablement
