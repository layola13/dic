jettisoned:/ˈdʒɛtɪsənd/ | v. |抛弃，丢弃，放弃 | To throw or drop something from a moving vehicle, especially an aircraft or ship.  It also implies getting rid of something unwanted or burdensome. |The pilot jettisoned the fuel tanks to lighten the plane.  飞行员抛弃了油箱以减轻飞机重量。

The crew jettisoned the cargo to save the ship. 船员抛弃了货物以拯救船只。


近义词：discard, abandon, relinquish, cast off, shed

反义词：retain, keep, preserve, conserve


USAGE->

一般现在时:
They jettison unnecessary baggage. = 他们丢弃不必要的行李。
The airline jettisons fuel before landing. = 航空公司在降落前抛弃燃油。

一般过去时:
The captain jettisoned the cargo to save the ship. = 船长抛弃了货物以拯救船只。
The pilot jettisoned the fuel tanks in an emergency. = 飞行员在紧急情况下抛弃了油箱。


现在完成时:

We have jettisoned our old ideas to embrace new technologies. = 我们已经抛弃了旧观念来拥抱新技术。


过去完成时:
The sailors had jettisoned the heavy cannons before the storm hit. = 水手们在暴风雨来临之前已经抛弃了沉重的火炮。
They had jettisoned their initial plan due to unforeseen circumstances. = 由于不可预见的情况，他们已经放弃了最初的计划。

虚拟语气:

否定句:

词源:
From Middle French *jeter, from Old French jeter ("to throw"), from Vulgar Latin *iectāre, frequentative of Latin iacere ("to throw").  The nautical sense emerged in the 17th century.
