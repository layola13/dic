fibrillated: /ˈfɪbrɪleɪtɪd/ | adj. | 纤维性颤动的；纤维化的 |  Characterized by or exhibiting fibrillation; relating to or affected by fibrillation, a rapid, irregular contraction of the heart muscle.  The term often describes an irregular heartbeat. |

例句：
The patient presented with a fibrillated heart rhythm.  = 病人出现纤维性颤动的心律。

His ECG showed a fibrillated heart. = 他的心电图显示心房颤动。


近义词：fluttering (referring to the heart), irregular (referring to the heartbeat)

反义词：regular (referring to the heartbeat), normal (referring to heart rhythm)

词源：
From "fibrillation," which comes from the Latin "fibrilla," meaning a small fiber.  The suffix "-ated" indicates the past participle, suggesting a state or condition.
