feared:/fɪərd/ | adj. | 可怕的；令人恐惧的；担心的 |  Feeling or causing fear; dreaded.  The feared consequences of his actions were finally upon him. 他行为的可怕后果终于降临到他身上了。 |近义词：dreaded, terrifying, frightening, awful | 反义词：safe, secure, comforting


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The villagers fear the approaching storm. = 村民们害怕即将到来的暴风雨。
He fears failure more than anything. = 他比任何事情都害怕失败。

一般过去时:
She feared the dark as a child. = 她小时候害怕黑暗。
They feared for their lives during the earthquake. = 地震期间他们担心自己的生命安全。

现在完成时:
I have feared this moment for years. = 我多年来一直害怕这一刻。
She has feared telling him the truth. = 她一直害怕告诉他真相。

过去完成时:
He had feared the worst, but it turned out alright. = 他曾担心最坏的情况，但结果还不错。
They had feared the consequences of their actions, but nothing happened. = 他们曾害怕自己行为的后果，但什么也没发生。

将来时:
We will fear nothing once we are together. =  一旦我们在一起，我们将无所畏惧。
I will fear the consequences of my choices later on. = 我以后会害怕自己选择的代价。


虚拟语气:
If I were you, I'd fear the consequences. = 如果我是你，我会害怕后果。
I wish I didn't fear public speaking. = 我希望我不害怕公开演讲。

被动语态:
The monster was feared by everyone in the village. = 村里的每个人都害怕那个怪物。
The consequences were feared by many. = 许多人都害怕后果。

疑问句:
Are you feared by your enemies? = 你的敌人害怕你吗？
What is feared most by the people? = 人们最害怕的是什么？

否定句:
He didn't fear the challenge. = 他不怕挑战。
She didn't fear death. = 她不怕死。


PHRASE->
most feared = 最令人恐惧的
widely feared = 广泛恐惧的
deeply feared = 深度恐惧的
be feared to + 动词 = 害怕做某事
well-feared = 声名狼藉的 (表示名声不好，让人害怕)
