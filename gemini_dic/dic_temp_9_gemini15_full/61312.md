embarrassed:/ɪmˈbærəst/ | adj. | 尴尬的，窘迫的 | Feeling ashamed, shy, or awkward, especially because of a mistake or something unexpected.  |例句：I felt embarrassed when I tripped and fell in front of everyone. 我当着所有人的面绊倒摔倒了，感到很尴尬。

例句：She was embarrassed by her son's bad behavior.  她为儿子的不良行为感到尴尬。


近义词：ashamed, shy, awkward, mortified, humiliated | 反义词：proud, confident, comfortable


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I am embarrassed by my mistake. = 我为我的错误感到尴尬。
He is embarrassed to ask for help. = 他不好意思求助。


一般过去时:
I was embarrassed when I saw her. = 当我看到她时，我感到尴尬。
They were embarrassed by the public attention. = 他们因为公众的关注而感到尴尬。


现在完成时:
I have been embarrassed several times today. = 我今天已经好几次感到尴尬了。
She has never been so embarrassed in her life. = 她一生中从未如此尴尬过。


过去完成时:
I had already been embarrassed before he started talking. = 他开始说话之前，我已经感到尴尬了。
They had been embarrassed many times before that incident. =在那件事发生之前，他们已经被尴尬过很多次了。


将来时:
I will be embarrassed if I fail the test. = 如果我考试不及格，我会感到尴尬。
They will be embarrassed to admit their mistake. = 他们将不好意思承认自己的错误。


虚拟语气:
I would be embarrassed if I were in his shoes. = 如果我处于他的位置，我会感到尴尬。
If I had known, I wouldn't have been embarrassed. = 如果我知道的话，我就不会感到尴尬了。


被动语态:
He was embarrassed by his own words. = 他被自己的话弄得尴尬了。
She was embarrassed by the unexpected question. = 她被那个意外的问题弄得尴尬了。


疑问句:
Are you embarrassed about your performance? = 你对自己的表现感到尴尬吗？
Was he embarrassed by the incident? = 他因为那件事而感到尴尬吗？


否定句:
I wasn't embarrassed at all. = 我一点也不尴尬。
They weren't embarrassed by the criticism. = 他们并没有因为批评而感到尴尬。
