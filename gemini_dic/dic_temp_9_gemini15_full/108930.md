leed:/liːd/| v.|领导，引导；导致；主演|to guide, conduct, or direct the course of (something); to be the leading actor in (a play or film); to act as a leader or guide|例句：He leeds the team to victory. 他带领球队取得胜利。  He leeds the class in singing. 他带领全班唱歌。|近义词：guide, conduct, direct, head|反义词：follow, obey


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He leads the team. = 他领导这个团队。
She leads the singing group. = 她领导这个合唱团。

一般过去时:
He led the team to victory. = 他带领球队取得胜利。
She led the class in a song. = 她带领全班唱了一首歌。

现在完成时:
He has led the company for ten years. = 他领导这家公司十年了。
She has led a fulfilling life. = 她过着充实的生活。

过去完成时:
He had led the company before he retired. = 他退休前领导过这家公司。
She had led a difficult life before finding success. = 在获得成功之前，她过着艰难的生活。

将来时:
He will lead the project. = 他将领导这个项目。
She will lead the discussion. = 她将主持讨论。

虚拟语气:
If he led the team, they would win. = 如果他领导这个团队，他们就会赢。
If she had led the project, it would have been successful. = 如果她领导了这个项目，它就会成功。

被动语态:
The team is led by him. = 这个团队由他领导。
The discussion was led by her. = 讨论由她主持。

疑问句:
Who leads the team? = 谁领导这个团队？
Who led the meeting? = 谁主持了会议？

否定句:
He doesn't lead the team anymore. = 他不再领导这个团队了。
She didn't lead the project successfully. = 她没有成功地领导这个项目。


PHRASE->
lead the way =带路
lead to =导致
lead a life =过着…的生活
take the lead =带头；领先
lead by example =以身作则


词源:
The word "lead" comes from the Old English word "lǣdan," meaning "to lead, guide, conduct."  This in turn has roots in Proto-Germanic and ultimately, Proto-Indo-European languages, reflecting a widespread and ancient concept of guidance and leadership.
