preconformity: /ˌpriːkənˈfɔːrməti/ | n. |预先一致；预先顺从；预先符合 |The state or quality of conforming beforehand; prior conformity.  It often refers to a situation where individuals or groups adopt beliefs or behaviors before they are explicitly required or expected to do so.  This could be due to anticipatory socialization or a desire to avoid conflict. | Example: The preconformity shown by the students to the new dress code was surprising; they changed their attire even before the official announcement. | 近义词:  precompliance, anticipation, proactiveness | 反义词:  defiance, nonconformity, resistance | 词性: Noun
