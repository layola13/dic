leasor:/ˈliːsɔːr/| n.|出租人，租赁人|The person who grants a lease, giving another party the right to use property for a specific period in exchange for rent.  A lessor owns the property and retains ownership while the lessee uses it.|例句：The lessor increased the rent after the first year.  出租人在第一年后提高了租金。|例句：The lessor is responsible for maintaining the structural integrity of the building. 出租人负责维护建筑物的结构完整性。|近义词：landlord, owner, renter (in some contexts)|反义词：lessee|名词
