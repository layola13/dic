climaxes:/ˈklaɪmæksɪz/| n. |高潮；顶点；结局 |The highest or most intense point in the development or resolution of something; the culmination.|The novel climaxes with a dramatic courtroom scene. 这部小说以戏剧性的法庭场景告终。 The series of events climaxed in a thrilling final game.  一系列事件最终以一场激动人心的决赛告终。|peaks, culminations, apogees|anti-climaxes, lows, nadirs|名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The story climaxes with a shocking twist. = 故事以令人震惊的转折告终。
The tension in the room climaxes as the results are announced. = 当结果宣布时，房间里的紧张气氛达到了高潮。


一般过去时:
The play climaxed with a powerful speech. = 这部戏以一段强有力的演讲达到高潮。
The argument climaxed in a shouting match. = 争论以一场大声喊叫的比赛告终。


现在完成时:
The investigation has climaxed with the arrest of the suspect. = 调查以逮捕嫌疑人告终。
The conflict has climaxed in a devastating war. = 冲突最终演变成一场毁灭性的战争。


过去完成时:
By the time I arrived, the party had climaxed and people were starting to leave. = 当我到达时，聚会已经达到高潮，人们开始离开。
The negotiations had climaxed in a signed agreement. = 谈判最终以签署协议告终。


将来时:
The concert will climax with a fireworks display. = 音乐会将以烟花表演达到高潮。
The festival will climax with a grand parade. = 节日将以盛大的游行达到高潮。


虚拟语气:
If the movie had climaxed differently, it would have been a better film. = 如果电影的结局不同，它将是一部更好的电影。
I wish the book had climaxed with a more satisfying resolution. = 我希望这本书的结局更令人满意。
