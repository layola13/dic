precomprehending:/ˌpriːkɒmˈprihɛndɪŋ/ | v. | 预先理解；预读；预习 |  The act of understanding something before fully encountering it, often through prior knowledge or context clues. It involves activating prior knowledge and making predictions about upcoming information.  | Example: Precomprehending the chapter's title and introduction helped me understand the main points more quickly.  预习章节的标题和引言帮助我更快地理解了要点。 | 近义词：previewing, anticipating, preparing | 反义词：misunderstanding, misinterpreting | 动词
