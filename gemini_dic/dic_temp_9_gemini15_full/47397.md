deadhead:/ˈdedˌhed/| n., v.|枯萎的花；（尤指）枯萎的花朵；无用之人；（剧院）未售出的票；（火车）空车；除去枯萎的花；去除（某物）的顶端；|英文解析：A deadhead is a flower that has died and withered, or a person considered to be useless or unproductive.  It can also refer to an unsold ticket, an empty train car, or the act of removing dead flowers or the top of something.|例句：The gardener deadheaded the roses. 园丁剪去了玫瑰花枯萎的花朵。They are deadheads, contributing nothing to the team.  他们都是无用之人，对团队毫无贡献。|近义词： (n.) withered flower, spent flower, useless person, (v.) prune, trim|反义词： (n.) blooming flower, productive person, (v.) cultivate
