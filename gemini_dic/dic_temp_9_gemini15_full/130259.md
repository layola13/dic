non-stellar: /ˌnɒnˈstɛlə(r)/ | adj. |非恒星的；非星状的 | Not relating to or characteristic of stars.  It describes something that is not a star or does not possess the characteristics of a star, such as its brightness, heat, or celestial nature. | 例句：The object detected was non-stellar in nature, ruling out a star as the source.  该探测到的物体本质上是非恒星的，排除了恒星作为其来源的可能性。


The term refers to objects in space that are not stars, such as planets, nebulae, galaxies, or other celestial bodies.  It can also be used in a more figurative sense to describe something that lacks the prominence or brilliance of a star.  | 例句：His performance, while adequate, was considered non-stellar by the critics. 他的表演虽然足够，却被评论家认为是不够出色的。 | 近义词：extrinsic, extrasolar (in astronomical context) | 反义词：stellar
