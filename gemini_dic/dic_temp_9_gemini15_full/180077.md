reflexive:/rɪˈflɛksɪv/ | adj. | 反身的；反射的 | Relating to or denoting a pronoun or verb that refers back to the subject of the clause, e.g., *himself*, *herself*, *itself*, *themselves*.  A reflexive action is one performed on oneself. |  She looked at herself in the mirror. 她对着镜子看着自己。 He hurt himself playing football. 他踢足球时伤到了自己。 | reciprocal, self-directed | non-reflexive, involuntary


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He praises himself every day. 他每天都赞扬自己。
She rewards herself with a treat. 她用一种款待来奖赏自己。


一般过去时:
They blamed themselves for the mistake. 他们为这个错误责备自己。
He taught himself to play the guitar. 他自学吉他。


现在完成时:
She has convinced herself that it's true. 她已经说服自己这是真的。
He has hurt himself badly in the accident. 他在事故中严重受伤了。


过去完成时:
I had already prepared myself for the worst. 我已经做好了最坏的打算。
She had dressed herself quickly before the guests arrived. 客人到达之前，她迅速地打扮好了自己。


将来时:
They will express themselves more freely in the future. 他们将来会更自由地表达自己。
He will reward himself with a vacation. 他将用假期来奖励自己。


虚拟语气:
If I were you, I would look after myself better. 如果我是你，我会更好地照顾自己。
I wish I had expressed myself more clearly. 我希望我当时能更清楚地表达自己。


被动语态:
The problem can't be solved by itself.  这个问题不能靠它自己解决。
The door can be opened from both sides. 门可以从两边打开。(This is not inherently reflexive, but shows a contrast.)


疑问句:
Does he often talk to himself? 他经常自言自语吗？
Did she hurt herself during the game? 比赛中她伤到自己了吗？


否定句:
He didn't believe in himself. 他不相信自己。
She couldn't control herself. 她无法控制自己。
