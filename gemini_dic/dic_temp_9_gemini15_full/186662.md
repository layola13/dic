riggish:/ˈrɪɡɪʃ/| adj.| 傲慢的；自大的；装腔作势的 |Characterized by arrogance, self-importance, or affected elegance.  It suggests a pretentious and somewhat unpleasant display of superiority.|例句：He had a rather riggish manner, always correcting others' grammar. 他举止傲慢，总是纠正别人的语法。

例句：Her riggish display of wealth was off-putting to many guests. 她炫耀财富的做派让许多客人反感。


近义词：haughty, supercilious, arrogant, pretentious, pompous|反义词：humble, modest, unassuming, down-to-earth


词源:  The word "riggish" is of uncertain etymology.  While its precise origins are debated, it likely evolved from a now-obsolete word related to "rig" (meaning to equip or dress ostentatiously) or possibly from a dialectal variant. The sense of affected elegance and ostentation is central to its meaning.
