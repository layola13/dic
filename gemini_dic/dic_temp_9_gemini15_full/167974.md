prioritizing: /praɪˈɔːrɪtaɪzɪŋ/ | v. |  优先处理；优先考虑 |  To prioritize something means to deal with it before other things, because it is more important.  It involves making a judgment about the relative importance of tasks or activities and arranging them in order of importance. |

例句：

She needs to prioritize her tasks to meet the deadline. = 她需要优先处理她的任务才能按时完成。

We are prioritizing the development of new products. = 我们正在优先开发新产品。


近义词：prioritize, rank,  give precedence to,  focus on

反义词：neglect, ignore, disregard


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I prioritize my work over my social life. = 我优先考虑我的工作而不是我的社交生活。
He prioritizes his family's needs above all else. = 他把家人的需求放在首位。


一般过去时:
She prioritized finishing the report over attending the meeting. = 她优先完成了报告而不是参加会议。
They prioritized the safety of the passengers. = 他们优先考虑乘客的安全。


现在完成时:
I have prioritized my health recently. = 我最近优先考虑了我的健康。
She has prioritized her career over having a family. = 她把事业放在组建家庭之前。


过去完成时:

They had prioritized the urgent tasks before dealing with the less important ones. = 他们在处理不太重要的事情之前优先处理了紧急任务。


将来时:

虚拟语气:

被动语态:
