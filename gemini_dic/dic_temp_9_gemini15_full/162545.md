prealliance: /ˌpriːəˈlaɪəns/ | n. | 预先联盟；结盟前 | A prealliance is a period or state of negotiation and planning before a formal alliance is established.  It often involves informal agreements, discussions, and preparations for a future, more concrete partnership. | Example: The prealliance talks between the two nations focused on trade agreements and military cooperation.  两国结盟前的谈判集中在贸易协定和军事合作上。 | 近义词: preliminary negotiations, prior arrangements, early stages of alliance | 反义词: post-alliance, after the alliance
