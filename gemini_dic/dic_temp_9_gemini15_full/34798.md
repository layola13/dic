climb:/klaɪm/| v.|攀登，爬；上升；攀升|To ascend or go up something, using hands and feet or other means. It can also refer to a gradual increase in something.|例句：The climbers reached the summit after a grueling climb. 攀登者经过艰苦的攀登后到达了山顶。

例句：The price of oil has been climbing steadily. 油价一直在稳步上涨。|近义词：ascend, scale, mount|反义词：descend, drop, fall|


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I climb mountains every weekend. = 我每个周末都爬山。
She climbs the stairs slowly. = 她慢慢地爬楼梯。


一般过去时:
He climbed the tree to get the kite. = 他爬上树去拿风筝。
We climbed a mountain yesterday. = 我们昨天爬了一座山。


现在完成时:
I have climbed Mount Everest. = 我已经攀登过珠穆朗玛峰。
They have climbed that difficult peak many times. = 他们已经多次攀登那座险峻的山峰了。


过去完成时:
He had climbed the wall before the police arrived. = 在警察到达之前他已经爬过了墙。
She had climbed several mountains before attempting that one. = 在尝试攀登那座山之前，她已经攀登过几座山了。


将来时:
We will climb that mountain next week. = 我们下周将攀登那座山。
They will climb the stairs to the second floor. = 他们将爬楼梯到二楼。


虚拟语气:
If I were stronger, I would climb that mountain. = 如果我更强壮一些，我就会去攀登那座山。
I wish I could climb higher. = 我希望我能爬得更高。


被动语态:
The mountain was climbed by many people. = 那座山被许多人攀登过。
The wall can be climbed easily. = 那堵墙很容易就能爬上去。


疑问句:
Can you climb that high wall? = 你能爬上那堵高墙吗？
Will they climb the stairs or take the elevator? = 他们会爬楼梯还是坐电梯？


否定句:
I cannot climb that high mountain. = 我爬不了那座高山。
He didn't climb the tree. = 他没有爬树。


PHRASE->
climb the ladder = 晋升
climb the walls = 焦虑不安
climb down = 下来；让步
climb aboard = 上车；加入
climb out of = 摆脱（困境）
