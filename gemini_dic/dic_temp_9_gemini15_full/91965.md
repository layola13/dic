hypermorality:/ˌhaɪpərˈmɒrælɪti/ | n. | 超道德; 过度道德; 严苛的道德标准 | Excessively strict or rigid adherence to moral principles, often to the point of being unreasonable or impractical.  It can manifest as self-righteousness, moral absolutism, or a tendency to judge others harshly. |  His hypermorality prevented him from enjoying life's simple pleasures.  他的过度道德感让他无法享受生活中的简单乐趣。  |近义词：moral absolutism, puritanism, sanctimoniousness |反义词：moral laxity, amorality, immorality | 名词
