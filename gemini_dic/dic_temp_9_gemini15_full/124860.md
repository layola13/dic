mucousness:/ˈmjuːkəsnəs/| n. |粘液；粘液性；粘稠|The noun "mucousness" refers to the quality or state of being mucous;  it describes something that is slimy, sticky, and viscous, like mucus.  It often implies an excessive or unpleasant amount of mucus.|例句：The excessive mucousness in his throat made it difficult to swallow. 他喉咙里过多的粘液使他难以吞咽。

The patient's lungs were filled with a thick mucousness. 病人的肺部充满了浓稠的粘液。|近义词：slimy, viscous, sticky, glutinous|反义词：dry, arid


USAGE->
This word is a noun and doesn't conjugate like verbs.  Therefore, tense and voice are not applicable.  The usage focuses on describing the quality of something.

词源:
The word "mucousness" is derived from the word "mucus," which itself originates from the Latin word "mucus," meaning "mucus" or "slime." The suffix "-ness" is added to create a noun denoting a state or quality.
