let-in: /ˈlɛtɪn/ | v. | 允许进入；让…进入 | To allow someone or something to enter.  | 例句：They let him in even though he didn't have a ticket. 他们即使他没有票也让他进去了。| 例句：The dog let itself in through the cat flap. 狗自己从猫门进来了。 | 近义词：admit, allow in, welcome | 反义词：exclude, bar, shut out


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They let the cat in every night. = 他们每天晚上都让猫进来。
We let our guests in through the back door. = 我们让客人从后门进来。


一般过去时:
She let the children in early. = 她让孩子们早点进来。
He let the dog in before it started raining. = 下雨前他把狗放进来了。


现在完成时:
They have let several people in already. = 他们已经让几个人进去了。
We have let the neighbors in several times this week. = 这周我们已经让邻居进来几次了。


过去完成时:
She had let the dog in before she went to bed. = 她睡觉前已经把狗放进来了。
They had let the guests in before the party started. = 派对开始前他们已经让客人进来了。


将来时:
I will let you in when I get home. = 我回家后就让你进来。
They will let the audience in fifteen minutes before the show. = 演出开始前十五分钟他们会让观众进来。


虚拟语气:
If I had a key, I would let you in. = 如果我有钥匙，我会让你进去。
I wish I could let you in, but I don't have the code. = 我真希望我能让你进去，但我没有密码。


被动语态:
He was let in without any trouble. = 他毫不费力地进去了。
The guests were let in through the main entrance. = 客人们从正门进来了。


疑问句:
Will they let us in? = 他们会让我们进去吗？
Are you going to let the dog in? = 你打算把狗放进来吗？


否定句:
They didn't let me in. = 他们没让我进去。
She wouldn't let the stranger in. = 她不愿意让陌生人进来。


PHRASE->

let someone in on the joke = 告诉某人一个笑话
let someone in on the deal = 让某人参与交易
let someone in for a surprise = 给某人一个惊喜
