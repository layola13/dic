disestimation:/ˌdɪsˌɛstɪˈmeɪʃən/| n. |轻视；贬低；不尊重|The act of holding someone or something in low regard; lack of esteem or respect.  It often implies a lack of proper valuation or a deliberate undervaluing.|例句：His disestimation of her abilities led to her being overlooked for the promotion.  他的轻视她的能力导致她被忽视了晋升的机会。


例句：The disestimation of traditional values has led to a moral decline in society. 对传统价值观的轻视导致了社会道德的沦丧。


近义词：contempt, scorn, disregard, disrespect, slight, belittlement


反义词：esteem, respect, admiration, reverence, honor


词性：名词


词源：来自dis- (否定前缀) + estimation (估计，评价)。  It indicates a negative estimation or a lack of proper estimation.


USAGE->
(由于disestimation是名词，没有动词变化，以下例句展示名词在不同句法结构中的用法)


主语：His disestimation of her work was evident. (他对她工作的轻视是显而易见的。)
宾语：I was shocked by the disestimation shown towards the victims. (我对受害者所受到的轻视感到震惊。)
同位语：The general's decision, a clear disestimation of the enemy's strength, proved disastrous. (将军的决定——对敌军实力的明显轻视——证明是灾难性的。)
介词宾语：Their project failed because of the disestimation of the risks involved. (他们的项目失败是因为低估了所涉及的风险。)
表语：His attitude towards her was one of disestimation. (他对她的态度是一种轻视。)


PHRASE->
(由于disestimation本身就是一个完整的词，没有常见的固定词组搭配。)


a feeling of disestimation: 轻视感
show disestimation towards: 对…表示轻视
express disestimation of: 表达对…的轻视

fall into disestimation: 变得不受尊重
