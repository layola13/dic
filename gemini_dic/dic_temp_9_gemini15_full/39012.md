confiscation: /ˌkɒnfɪˈskeɪʃən/ | /ˌkɑːnfɪˈskeɪʃən/ | n. | 没收；充公 | The act of taking something away from someone, especially by authority.  This often involves property or goods that are considered illegal or are owed as a penalty. |例句：The police ordered the confiscation of all illegal weapons.  警察下令没收所有非法武器。 |例句：The government's confiscation of land led to widespread protests. 政府没收土地的行为引发了大规模抗议。| 近义词：seizure, appropriation, expropriation | 反义词：restoration, restitution, return


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government confiscates illegal drugs. = 政府没收非法毒品。
The customs officials confiscate contraband goods. = 海关官员没收违禁品。

一般过去时:
The authorities confiscated his passport. = 当局没收了他的护照。
They confiscated the stolen goods. = 他们没收了赃物。

现在完成时:
The police have confiscated a large amount of cash. = 警方已经没收了大量现金。
The court has confiscated the property. = 法院已经没收了该房产。

过去完成时:
The judge had confiscated the evidence before the trial. = 审判前法官已没收了证据。
The rebels had confiscated all the food supplies. = 叛军已经没收了所有食物供应。

将来时:
The police will confiscate any illegal substances found. = 警方将没收发现的任何非法物质。
The government will confiscate the assets of the criminal organization. = 政府将没收犯罪组织的资产。

虚拟语气:
If they found drugs, they would confiscate them. = 如果他们发现毒品，他们就会没收。
If the police had found the evidence, they would have confiscated it. = 如果警方找到了证据，他们就会没收它。

被动语态:
His property was confiscated by the government. = 他的财产被政府没收了。
The illegal weapons were confiscated by the police. = 非法武器被警方没收了。

疑问句:
Were the documents confiscated? = 文件被没收了吗？
Will the car be confiscated? = 汽车会被没收吗？

否定句:
The police did not confiscate anything. = 警方什么也没有没收。
They did not confiscate the car. = 他们没有没收汽车。
