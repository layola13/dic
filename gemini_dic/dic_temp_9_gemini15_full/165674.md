premonopolized: /ˌpriːməˈnɒpəlaɪzd/ | adj. |  垄断之前的；在垄断之前存在的 |  Existing or occurring before a monopoly was established.  Describes a period or state of affairs prior to the control of a market or industry by a single entity. | 例句：The premonopolized era saw fierce competition among many small businesses.  垄断之前的时代，许多小型企业之间竞争激烈。

The premonopolized market offered consumers a wider variety of choices. 垄断前的市场为消费者提供了更多选择。| 近义词：pre-monopoly, ante-monopoly | 反义词：monopolized, post-monopoly | 词性：形容词

词源:  The word is a combination of "pre-" (meaning before) + "monopolized" (past participle of "monopolize," meaning to control the market).  The root "mono-" comes from Greek "monos" (single, alone) and "-poly" from Greek "polein" (to sell).
