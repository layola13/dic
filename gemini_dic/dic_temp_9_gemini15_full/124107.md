mortalism:/ˈmɔːrtəlɪzəm/| n. | 唯物主义；必死论 | The philosophical doctrine that all things are material and that there is no spiritual or supernatural existence.  It emphasizes the reality and importance of the physical world and denies the existence of a soul or afterlife. |Example:  His staunch belief in science led him to embrace a form of mortalism. 他对科学的坚定信仰使他接受了一种唯物主义。  Some religious groups reject mortalism, believing in an immortal soul. 一些宗教团体拒绝唯物主义，相信有不朽的灵魂。|近义词：materialism, physicalism |反义词：spiritualism, idealism, immortalism
