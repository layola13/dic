respite:/ˈrɛspaɪt/| n. |暂缓；喘息；延期 | a short period of rest or relief from something difficult or unpleasant.  |例句：After weeks of hard work, she finally got a respite.  几周的辛勤工作后，她终于得到了一段喘息时间。
|例句：The judge granted the defendant a respite from his sentence.  法官准予被告缓刑。|近义词：relief, rest, intermission, pause |反义词：continuation, persistence, continuation

词源:
From Old French *respit, from respiter "to delay, postpone, respite," from re- "again" + spirer "to breathe."  The original sense was a delay granted to a debtor, implying a temporary pause in the pressure to pay.  The meaning evolved to encompass any temporary pause or relief.
