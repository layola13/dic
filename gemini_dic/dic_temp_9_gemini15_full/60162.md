effeminating: /ɪˈfɛmɪneɪtɪŋ/ | adj. | 使女性化的；使柔弱的 | Making someone or something appear more feminine or weak;  characterized by or suggesting femininity.  |The effeminating effect of the soft music and dim lighting was quite remarkable.  柔软的音乐和昏暗的灯光产生了相当显著的女性化效果。|近义词：feminizing, unmanly, womanly |反义词：masculating, manly, virile |


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The soft lighting effeminates the room. = 柔和的灯光使房间显得女性化。
His mannerisms effeminate his appearance. = 他的举止使他的外表显得女性化。

一般过去时:
The long hair effeminated his image. = 长发使他的形象显得女性化。
The tailored suit didn't effeminate him, but it softened his look. = 这套剪裁合体的西装并没有使他显得女性化，而是使他的外表看起来更柔和。

现在完成时:
The constant exposure to beauty products has effeminated him. = 长期接触美容产品使他变得女性化。
This new trend has effeminated the fashion industry slightly. = 这股新潮流使时尚行业略微女性化。

过去完成时:
The years of ballet had effeminated his physique. = 多年的芭蕾舞训练使他的体格变得女性化。
He felt that his upbringing had effeminated his personality. = 他觉得他的成长环境使他的性格变得女性化。


将来时:
The new designs will effeminate the traditionally masculine look. = 新的设计将会使传统阳刚的形象变得女性化。
Excessive use of these products will effeminate your appearance. = 过度使用这些产品会使你的外表显得女性化。


虚拟语气:
If he wore makeup, it would effeminate his appearance. = 如果他化妆，会使他的外表显得女性化。
I wish the film wouldn't effeminate the main character so much. = 我希望这部电影不要把主角描绘得那么女性化。


被动语态:
His appearance was effeminated by his clothing choices. = 他的衣着选择使他的外表显得女性化。
The character was deliberately effeminated by the director. = 这个角色是导演故意设计成女性化的。


疑问句:
Does this hairstyle effeminate his look? = 这种发型会使他的外表显得女性化吗？
Will this new trend effeminate men's fashion? = 这股新潮流会使男士时尚变得女性化吗？


否定句:
The strong physique didn't effeminate his overall appearance. = 强壮的体格并没有使他的整体形象显得女性化。
The director made sure that the costumes did not effeminate the character. = 导演确保服装不会使角色显得女性化。


PHRASE->
to be effeminated by something: 被某事物所女性化
to appear effeminated: 看起来女性化
to feel effeminated: 感到女性化
an effeminated manner: 女性化的举止
an effeminated voice: 女性化的嗓音


词源:
From Middle English *effeminaten, from Latin *effeminatus, past participle of effeminare (“to make womanish”), from ex- (“out, from”) + femina (“woman”).
