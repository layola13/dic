guildhalls:/ˈɡɪldhɔːlz/| n. |行会大厅；公会会所|A guildhall is a building used by a guild, a medieval association of craftsmen or merchants, for meetings and social events.  It often served as a center for the guild's activities and sometimes included workshops or storage areas.|例句：The city's guildhalls were once magnificent structures, showcasing the wealth and power of their respective guilds.  这座城市的公会会所曾经是宏伟的建筑，展示了各自行会的财富和权力。

The ancient guildhalls of London are now used for civic events and historical exhibitions. 伦敦古老的行会大厅现在用于公民活动和历史展览。|近义词：assembly hall, meeting hall, town hall|反义词：private residence, individual workshop|名词
