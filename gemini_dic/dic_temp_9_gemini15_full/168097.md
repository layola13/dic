prizer:/ˈpraɪzər/| n. |奖品获得者；奖励者|A prizer is a person who wins a prize or award.  It can also refer to someone who gives out prizes. |例句：She is a prizer in the national spelling bee. 她获得了全国拼字比赛的奖项。 |近义词：winner, award recipient, grantor|反义词：loser


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He prizes his collection of stamps. 他珍视他的邮票收藏。
She prizes honesty above all else. 她最看重诚实。


一般过去时:
They prized the antique vase highly. 他们非常珍视那个古董花瓶。
He prized the opportunity to study abroad. 他非常珍惜出国留学的机会。


现在完成时:
She has prized her family's support throughout her career. 在她的职业生涯中，她一直珍视家人的支持。
They have prized the winning design for its innovation. 他们因为其创新性而珍视获奖的设计。


过去完成时:
He had prized his grandfather's watch since he was a child. 从小他就珍视他爷爷的手表。
They had prized the opportunity to work with such talented individuals. 他们一直珍视有机会与如此有才华的人一起工作。


将来时:
She will prize the experience for years to come. 她将在未来的几年里珍视这段经历。
They will prize the chance to collaborate on this project. 他们将珍惜有机会在这个项目上合作。


虚拟语气:
If I had known the value, I would have prized it more. 如果我知道它的价值，我会更珍惜它。
I wish I had prized my friendships more when I was younger. 我希望我年轻的时候更珍惜我的友谊。


被动语态:

His contributions to the field are highly prized. 他对该领域的贡献受到高度评价。


疑问句:

否定句:
