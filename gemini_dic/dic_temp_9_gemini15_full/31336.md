characterisable: /ˈkærəktəraɪzəbəl/ | adj. | 可描述的，可刻画的 | Able to be characterized or described; having characteristics that can be identified and described.  | The painting's style is easily characterisable as Impressionist. 这幅画的风格很容易被认定为印象派。 The situation is characterisable by its complexity and uncertainty. 这种情况的特点是复杂和不确定性。 | describable, definable, identifiable | indescribable, undefinable, unidentifiable | 形容词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The artist's style is characterisable. = 艺术家的风格是可以描述的。
This problem is characterisable as complex. = 这个问题可以被描述为复杂的。


一般过去时:
His behaviour was characterisable as erratic. = 他的行为可以被描述为反复无常的。
The evidence was characterisable as insufficient. = 证据不足以被描述。


现在完成时:
The painting has been characterisable as a masterpiece. = 这幅画已被认定为杰作。
The situation has been characterisable by chaos. = 这种情况的特点是混乱。


过去完成时:

将来时:

The upcoming election will be characterisable by its intense competition. = 即将到来的选举将以激烈的竞争为特征。


虚拟语气:

If the symptoms were more characterisable, diagnosis would be easier. = 如果症状更具特征，诊断就会更容易。


被动语态:

否定句:
