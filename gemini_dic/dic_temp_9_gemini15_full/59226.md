dystrophy: /ˈdɪstrəfi/ | n. |营养不良；肌肉萎缩；发育不良 |  A group of diseases characterized by progressive degeneration of muscles or other tissues.  It often involves the wasting away of muscles and weakening of muscle function. The term is also used to describe other types of tissue degeneration. | Example: Muscular dystrophy is a genetic disorder that causes progressive muscle weakness and degeneration.  肌肉萎缩症是一种遗传性疾病，会导致进行性肌肉无力和退化。 |近义词：degeneration, wasting, atrophy | 反义词：hypertrophy, growth, development |
