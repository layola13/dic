crash:/kræʃ/| n./v.|碰撞；坠毁；失败；爆裂|A sudden, violent collision; a sudden, loud noise; a sudden failure; a sudden and dramatic decline.|例句：The car crashed into a tree.  汽车撞到了一棵树上。|例句：The stock market crashed. 股市崩盘了。|近义词：collide, smash, collapse, fail|反义词：succeed, thrive


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The plane crashes frequently. = 这架飞机经常坠毁。
He crashes his car every year. = 他每年都撞坏他的车。


一般过去时:
The stock market crashed last year. = 股市去年崩盘了。
The two cars crashed into each other. = 两辆车相撞了。


现在完成时:
The economy has crashed several times this century. = 本世纪经济已经几次崩盘了。
The system has crashed twice today. = 系统今天已经崩溃了两次。


过去完成时:
The company had crashed before the merger. = 那家公司在合并之前就已经垮台了。
The plane had crashed into the mountain before anyone could help. = 飞机在任何人能够帮忙之前就已经坠毁在山上了。


将来时:
The building might crash down if it's not reinforced. = 如果不加固，这座建筑可能会倒塌。
The server will crash if too many users log in at once. = 如果太多用户同时登录，服务器将会崩溃。


虚拟语气:
If the plane crashed, everyone would be in danger. = 如果飞机坠毁，每个人都会处于危险之中。
I wish the computer hadn't crashed during the presentation. = 我真希望电脑在演示过程中没有崩溃。


被动语态:
The car was crashed by a drunk driver. = 这辆车是被一个醉酒的司机撞坏的。
The system is often crashed by viruses. = 系统经常被病毒搞崩溃。


疑问句:
Did the plane crash? = 飞机坠毁了吗？
Has the computer crashed again? = 电脑又崩溃了吗？


否定句:
The car didn't crash. = 车没有撞坏。
The website hasn't crashed yet. = 网站还没有崩溃。
