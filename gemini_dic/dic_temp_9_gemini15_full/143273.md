outdodge:/ˌaʊtˈdɑːdʒ/| v.|躲避；避开|To evade or avoid successfully.|The boxer skillfully outdodged his opponent's punches. 拳击手巧妙地躲过了对手的拳击。|近义词：evade, avoid, circumvent, dodge|反义词：encounter, confront


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He outdodges his responsibilities. = 他逃避责任。
They outdodge the traffic skillfully. = 他们巧妙地避开了交通。


一般过去时:
She outdodged the question cleverly. = 她巧妙地避开了这个问题。
He outdodged the falling tree branch. = 他躲过了掉落的树枝。


现在完成时:
We have outdodged many challenges this year. = 今年我们克服了许多挑战。
They have outdodged several attempts to catch them. = 他们躲过了几次抓捕尝试。


过去完成时:
He had outdodged the police for weeks before they caught him. = 在被捕之前，他已经躲避警察几周了。
She had outdodged all her rivals to win the competition. = 她战胜所有竞争对手赢得了比赛。


将来时:
I will outdodge any attempt to manipulate me. = 我将躲避任何操纵我的企图。
They will outdodge the storm by seeking shelter. = 他们将通过寻求庇护来躲避风暴。


虚拟语气:
If he had outdodged the blow, he would have survived. = 如果他躲过了那一击，他就会活下来。
I wish I had outdodged the traffic jam this morning. = 我希望今天早上我能避开交通堵塞。


被动语态:
The problem was outdodged by a clever solution. = 这个问题被一个巧妙的解决方案解决了。(This usage is less common)
The question was skillfully outdodged by the politician. = 这个问题被政客巧妙地避开了。(This usage is less common)


疑问句:
How did he outdodge the attack? = 他是如何躲避攻击的？
Can we outdodge the financial crisis? = 我们能躲过金融危机吗？


否定句:
He didn't outdodge the responsibility. = 他没有逃避责任。
They couldn't outdodge the inevitable. = 他们无法避免不可避免的事情。
