chatter:/ˈtʃætər/ | v. n. |喋喋不休；闲聊；吱吱叫 |To talk rapidly and continuously, especially about unimportant things; a rapid succession of sounds, especially short, repeated sounds. |例句：The children were chattering excitedly about their trip.孩子们兴奋地喋喋不休地谈论着他们的旅行。


The birds were chattering in the trees. 鸟儿们在树上吱吱喳喳地叫着。|近义词：babble, prattle, jabber |反义词：silence, quiet

词源:
The word "chatter" comes from the Middle English word "chatren," which is of imitative origin, meaning to make a rapid succession of sounds like birds.  The ultimate origin is likely onomatopoeic, reflecting the sound itself.
