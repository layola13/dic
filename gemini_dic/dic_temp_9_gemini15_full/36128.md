coestate:/ˈkoʊeɪsteɪt/| n. |共同遗产；共同财产|A coestate refers to property or assets owned jointly by two or more individuals, often as a result of inheritance or a shared business venture.  It implies a shared ownership and responsibility for the management and disposition of the assets.  The exact nature of the coestate's ownership and management is determined by the legal framework under which it exists (e.g., joint tenancy, tenancy in common).|Example: The brothers inherited a coestate consisting of a large farm and several commercial buildings.|近义词：joint estate, common property|反义词：sole ownership, individual property|名词
