inaugurated:/ɪˈnɔːɡjʊreɪtɪd/ | v. | 开始；为…举行就职典礼；为…举行开幕式 | to begin something such as a system, policy, or period of time officially; to install someone formally into a position, especially an important one. |例句：The new president was inaugurated yesterday. 昨天新总统就职了。

The new library was inaugurated with a ribbon-cutting ceremony. 新图书馆举行了剪彩仪式开幕。|近义词：commenced, initiated, installed, instituted |反义词：concluded, ended, terminated |


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They inaugurate a new policy every year. = 他们每年都启动一项新政策。
He inaugurates his new office with a speech. = 他以演讲的方式开启了他的新办公室。


一般过去时:
The museum was inaugurated last month. = 博物馆上个月开幕了。
The new CEO was inaugurated into her position yesterday. = 新任首席执行官昨天就职。


现在完成时:
They have inaugurated several new projects this year. = 他们今年已经启动了好几个新项目。
The government has inaugurated a new education reform. = 政府已经启动了一项新的教育改革。


过去完成时:
They had inaugurated the project before the funding was secured. = 他们在资金到位之前就启动了该项目。
The previous president had inaugurated the program several years ago. = 前任总统几年前启动了这个项目。


将来时:
The company will inaugurate its new headquarters next year. = 公司明年将启用其新的总部大楼。
They will inaugurate the new bridge in the spring. = 他们将在春天启用这座新桥。


虚拟语气:
If they inaugurated the policy, it would improve the situation. = 如果他们启动这项政策，情况就会好转。
I wish they had inaugurated the new program sooner. = 我希望他们早点启动这个新项目。


被动语态:
The new stadium was inaugurated last week. = 新体育场于上周落成。
The program will be inaugurated next month. = 该项目将于下个月启动。


疑问句:
When was the building inaugurated? = 这座建筑物是什么时候落成的？
Will the new law be inaugurated soon? = 新法律很快就会实施吗？


否定句:
They did not inaugurate the project on time. = 他们没有按时启动该项目。
The new system has not yet been inaugurated. = 新系统尚未启用。
