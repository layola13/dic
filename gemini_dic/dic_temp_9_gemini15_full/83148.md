gutsy:/ˈɡʌtsi/| adj.|勇敢的；胆大的；有胆量的|Having or showing courage and determination.  It implies a willingness to take risks and face challenges, often in the face of adversity.|例句：She made a gutsy decision to quit her job and start her own business. 她做了一个大胆的决定，辞去工作，自己创业。

例句：It was a gutsy move to challenge the champion. 向冠军挑战是一个大胆的举动。


近义词：brave, courageous, bold, daring, intrepid, valiant|反义词：cowardly, timid, fearful, faint-hearted


词源:  The word "gutsy" is derived from "guts," meaning courage or determination.  The term's origins are ultimately linked to the internal organs, particularly the intestines, which were historically associated with visceral feelings and strength. The evolution suggests a metaphorical connection between physical strength and inner fortitude.
