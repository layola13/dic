fuster:/ˈfʌstər/| v.|使激动；使烦躁；|to make (someone) agitated or restless|The news fustered him considerably.  这个消息使他相当激动。|近义词：agitate, upset, disturb |反义词：calm, soothe, tranquilize


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The news fusters him every day. = 这个新闻每天都让他心烦。
Loud noises fuster the baby. = 嘈杂的声音让婴儿烦躁不安。

一般过去时:
The unexpected visitor fustered her plans. = 不速之客打乱了她的计划。
The argument fustered the children. = 那场争吵让孩子们烦躁不安。

现在完成时:
The constant interruptions have fustered me. = 不断的打断让我心烦意乱。
His actions have fustered the situation. = 他的行为使情况更加混乱。

过去完成时:
The delay had fustered the passengers. = 延误让乘客们焦躁不安。
She had fustered herself into a frenzy. = 她把自己弄得焦躁不安。

将来时:
The announcement will fuster the market. = 这一宣布将扰乱市场。
This news will fuster many people. = 这个消息会让很多人不安。

虚拟语气:
If the news were not so bad, it wouldn't fuster me so much. = 如果消息没那么糟糕，它就不会让我这么不安。
I wish that the traffic wouldn't fuster me so much. = 我希望交通不会让我这么烦躁。

被动语态:
He was fustered by the criticism. = 他因批评而感到不安。
The crowd was fustered by the delay. = 人群因延误而感到烦躁。

疑问句:
Does this situation fuster you? = 这种情况让你不安吗？
What fusters you the most about this project? = 关于这个项目，什么最让你烦躁？

否定句:
The news didn't fuster her at all. = 这个消息一点也没让她不安。
The quiet environment didn't fuster the baby. = 安静的环境并没有让婴儿烦躁。
