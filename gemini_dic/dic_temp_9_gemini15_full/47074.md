datival:/ˈdeɪtɪvəl/| adj.|与与格相关的；间接宾语的| Relating to or denoting the grammatical case of a noun or pronoun indicating the recipient or beneficiary of the action of a verb.|The sentence "He gave the book to Mary" contains a datival construction, where "Mary" is the indirect object in the dative case.|近义词：indirect object|反义词：direct object


USAGE->
This word is primarily used in linguistic analysis and doesn't have common usage in everyday conversation.  Therefore, providing examples across various tenses and moods is not relevant.  The word describes a grammatical structure, not a verb that can be conjugated.

The dative case is used to mark the indirect object of a verb.

Example 1:
He gave the book to Mary.  (Mary is the indirect object in the dative case.)  他把书给了玛丽。(玛丽是与格的间接宾语。)

Example 2:
She baked a cake for her mother. (Her mother is the indirect object, implied dative case.) 她为妈妈烤了一个蛋糕。(她妈妈是间接宾语，隐含与格。)
