intermigrating:/ˌɪntərˈmaɪɡreɪtɪŋ/ | v. | 互相迁移；相互迁徙 |  The verb "intermigrating" describes the movement of two or more groups of people or animals between different locations, often implying a reciprocal or mutual exchange.  It suggests a back-and-forth movement rather than a one-way migration. | Example:  The two bird species were intermigrating across the border region during the breeding season. 这两种鸟类在繁殖季节跨越边境地区互相迁徙。 |近义词: migrating, moving between, traversing | 反义词: staying put, remaining stationary | 动词
