lifeguard: /ˈlaɪfɡɑːrd/| n. |救生员| A person trained to rescue people from drowning or other water-related emergencies.  Lifeguards typically work at swimming pools, beaches, and other bodies of water. They are responsible for maintaining safety and providing first aid when necessary.| Example: The lifeguard blew his whistle to warn swimmers of an approaching storm. 救生员吹响哨子警告游泳者即将到来的暴风雨。|近义词：rescuer, lifesaver |反义词：None specifically.
