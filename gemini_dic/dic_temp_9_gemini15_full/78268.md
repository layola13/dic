germanism:/ˈdʒɜːrmənɪzəm/ | n. | 德国习俗；日耳曼主义；德语特色 | A Germanism is a word, phrase, or grammatical construction characteristic of the German language or German culture.  It can refer to a word borrowed from German, a way of expressing an idea that is typical of German, or a cultural trait associated with Germany. | Example: The use of compound nouns is a common Germanism in the English language.  使用复合名词是英语中常见的德语特色。 | 近义词：German word, German expression, Teutonic trait | 反义词：None specifically defined.
