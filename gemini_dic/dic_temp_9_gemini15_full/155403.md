phasing:/ˈfeɪzɪŋ/| n. |阶段性，分阶段实施|The gradual introduction of new policies, often implemented in stages or phases.|The project is in its initial phasing.|近义词：staging, implementation|反义词：simultaneity, immediacy


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The phasing of the project is crucial for success. = 项目的分阶段实施对成功至关重要。
The company is phasing in a new system. = 公司正在分阶段实施新系统。


一般过去时:
The phasing of the construction was delayed by bad weather. = 建设的分阶段实施因恶劣天气而延误。
They phased out the old equipment last year. = 他们去年淘汰了旧设备。


现在完成时:
They have phased in the new software successfully. = 他们已经成功地分阶段引入了新软件。
We have completed the first phase of phasing out the old machines. = 我们已经完成了淘汰旧机器的第一阶段。


过去完成时:
They had phased out the old model before launching the new one. = 他们在推出新车型之前已经淘汰了旧车型。
The company had completed the initial phasing of the project by the end of the year. = 到年底，公司已经完成了项目的初始阶段。


将来时:
They will phase in the new regulations next month. = 他们将在下个月分阶段实施新规章。
The government will phase out gasoline cars by 2035. = 政府将在2035年前逐步淘汰汽油车。


虚拟语气:
If they had phased the project differently, it might have been more successful. = 如果他们采用不同的分阶段实施方案，项目可能会更成功。
I wish they had phased in the new system more gradually. = 我希望他们能更逐步地实施新系统。


被动语态:
The new system is being phased in gradually. = 新系统正在逐步分阶段实施。
The old machines were phased out last year. = 旧机器去年被淘汰了。


疑问句:
How will the phasing of the project affect the budget? = 项目的分阶段实施将如何影响预算？
What is the current phasing of the development? = 开发的当前阶段是什么？


否定句:
They did not phase in the new policy effectively. = 他们没有有效地分阶段实施新政策。
The company didn't phase out the old product line quickly enough. = 公司没有足够快地淘汰旧产品线。
