self-excuse:/ˌsɛlfɪkˈskjuːs/| n.|自我辩解；借口|A self-excuse is an explanation offered to justify one's own actions or behavior, often perceived as weak or unconvincing. It's a type of excuse that focuses on the person making the excuse and their own perspective, rather than addressing the actual problem or impact of their actions.|例句：His self-excuse for missing the deadline was unconvincing.  他的错过截止日期的自我辩解令人难以信服。|近义词：apology, justification, rationalization|反义词：acceptance of responsibility, accountability|
