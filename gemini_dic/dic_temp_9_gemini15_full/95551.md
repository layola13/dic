incogitative: /ˌɪnkɒdʒɪteɪtɪv/ | adj. | 沉思的；缺乏思考的；不考虑后果的 | Not given to thinking; thoughtless; unreflective.  The word implies a lack of careful consideration or forethought, often resulting in impulsive or careless actions. |

例句：

His incognitive decision to quit his job without another lined up surprised everyone. = 他不假思索地辞掉工作，没有安排其他工作，让每个人都感到惊讶。

Her incognitive spending habits led to significant debt. = 她不考虑后果的消费习惯导致了严重的债务。


近义词：thoughtless, unthinking, impulsive, reckless, heedless, careless

反义词：thoughtful, reflective, considerate, deliberate, premeditated


词性：形容词


词源：

The word "incogitative" is derived from the Latin prefix "in-" (meaning "not") and the Latin word "cogitare" (meaning "to think").  Therefore, "incogitative" literally means "not thinking."  The word reflects a historical lineage where careful consideration and forethought were highly valued.
