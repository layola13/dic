foreordain:/fɔːrˈɔːrdeɪn/| vt.|预先注定；预定；预言|To foreordain something means to decide or determine it in advance, often by a higher power or fate. It implies an inevitability or lack of free will.|例句：Some believe that our destinies are foreordained.  一些人相信我们的命运是注定的。

例句：The ancient prophecy foreordained the king's downfall.  古老的预言预言了国王的垮台。


近义词：predestine, predetermine, preordain

反义词：leave to chance, leave undetermined
