caduceus:/kəˈdjuːsiəs/| n. |卡德修斯（墨丘利神杖）, 医疗标志 |A caduceus is a symbol depicting a winged staff with two serpents intertwined around it.  It is often associated with medicine and healing, although its original association was with Hermes (Mercury in Roman mythology), the messenger of the gods.  The caduceus is sometimes mistakenly used as a symbol of medicine, as the true symbol is the Rod of Asclepius (a single serpent entwined around a staff).| The caduceus is a powerful symbol representing healing and communication.|近义词：Rod of Asclepius (though distinct in meaning)|反义词：无
