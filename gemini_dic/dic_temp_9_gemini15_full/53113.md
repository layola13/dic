diphyozooid:/ˌdɪpfaɪˈzoʊɪd/| n. |双胚动物|A diphyozooid is a type of colonial organism, specifically a zooid in a colony where two individuals are fused together during development. This fusion results in a single organism with characteristics derived from both original individuals.  The term is primarily used in the context of certain colonial tunicates (sea squirts).|Example:  The study focused on the morphology and genetic makeup of the diphyozooid colony.|近义词:zooid (in a broader sense)|反义词:None specifically, but the opposite would be a solitary, unfused organism.
