fasciated:/ˈfæʃieɪtɪd/| adj.|  联生的；束状的；欣喜若狂的 |  Descriptive of a plant stem or leaf that is abnormally flattened and broadened, often with fused tissues. It can also describe a person overwhelmed with delight or wonder. |例句：The fasciated cactus showed unusually broad, flattened stems.  这株联生的仙人掌呈现出异常宽阔扁平的茎。

The child was utterly fasciated by the magician's tricks.  孩子被魔术师的戏法迷住了。|近义词：fused, flattened, coalesced,  delighted, enthralled, captivated |反义词：normal,  unfused,  uninterested, bored

词源:

From the Latin *fascis, meaning "bundle".  This refers to the bundled or fused appearance of the plant tissues. The meaning relating to being captivated or enthralled developed later.
