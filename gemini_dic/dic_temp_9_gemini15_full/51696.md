divine:/dɪˈvaɪn/| adj.| 神圣的；极好的；非凡的 | Relating to or proceeding from God or a god; supremely good; exceptionally good or enjoyable. |例句：The divine right of kings. 国王的君主神授权。 The food was divine.  食物棒极了。|近义词：holy, sacred, heavenly, superb, excellent |反义词：profane, unholy, wicked, terrible, awful |


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The priest divines the future. = 牧师预言未来。
She divines the meaning of the poem. = 她领会了这首诗的意思。


一般过去时:
The fortune teller divined my future.  = 算命先生预言了我的未来。
He divined the answer from the clues. = 他从线索中推断出答案。


现在完成时:
I have divined the solution to the mystery. = 我已经猜到了这个谜的答案。
She has divined the hidden meaning. = 她已经猜到了隐藏的意义。


过去完成时:
They had divined the plot before the play ended. = 在戏剧结束前他们就猜到了剧情。
The detective had divined the killer's identity. = 侦探已经猜到了凶手的身份。


将来时:
The prophet will divine the outcome of the war. = 预言家将预言战争的结果。
She will divine her sister's intentions. = 她将猜出她妹妹的意图。


虚拟语气:
If I could divine the future, I would tell you. = 如果我能预知未来，我会告诉你。
I wish I could divine the meaning of his words. = 我希望我能明白他的话的意思。


被动语态:
The future cannot be divined. = 未来无法预知。
The answer was divined by careful observation. = 答案是通过仔细观察推断出来的。


疑问句:
Can you divine the meaning of this riddle? = 你能猜出这个谜语的意思吗？
How did she divine the truth? = 她如何猜到真相的？


否定句:
I cannot divine the reason for his actions. = 我无法猜出他行为的原因。
They did not divine the consequences of their decision. = 他们没有预料到他们决定的后果。
