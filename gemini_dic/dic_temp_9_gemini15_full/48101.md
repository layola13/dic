declination:/ˌdeklɪˈneɪʃən/ | n. |偏角；倾斜；衰退；拒绝 | The act of declining or bending downward;  a downward slope; the angle between the magnetic meridian and the true meridian; a refusal.  |例句：The declination of the sun affects the length of daylight.  太阳的偏角影响日照时间。

The doctor's declination of the invitation was polite but firm. 医生礼貌而坚定地拒绝了邀请。


近义词：refusal, rejection, decline, dip, downturn,  deviation | 反义词：acceptance, assent, incline,  ascension, upswing | 词性：名词


词源:

From Middle English *declinacioun, from Old French declinacion, from Latin declinātiōn-, declinātiō "a bending down, declining," from declīnāre "to bend down, decline," from de- "down" + clīnāre "to bend."
