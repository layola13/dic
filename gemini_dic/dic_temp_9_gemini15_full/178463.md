reconstruction:/ˌriːkənˈstrʌkʃən/ | n. |重建，复原，改建 |The act of reconstructing something, especially a building, system, or society.  It involves restoring something to its former state or creating something new based on existing elements. |例句：The reconstruction of the old town is underway. 老城区的重建工作正在进行中。|例句：The historical reconstruction of the event is still debated.  对该事件的历史重建仍在争论中。|近义词：rebuilding, restoration, rehabilitation |反义词：destruction, demolition


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government reconstructs the damaged infrastructure. = 政府重建受损的基础设施。
She reconstructs her life after the tragedy. = 她在那场悲剧之后重建了自己的生活。

一般过去时:
They reconstructed the building after the fire. = 大火之后他们重建了那栋楼。
Historians reconstructed the events leading to the war. = 历史学家们重建了导致战争的事件。

现在完成时:
They have reconstructed the museum to its former glory. = 他们已将博物馆重建到昔日的辉煌。
The city has reconstructed its economy after the crisis. = 该城市在危机后重建了其经济。

过去完成时:
They had reconstructed the bridge before the flood season arrived. = 在洪水季节到来之前，他们已经重建了桥梁。
The archaeologist had reconstructed the ancient civilization based on the artifacts found. = 考古学家根据发现的文物重建了古代文明。

将来时:
They will reconstruct the damaged roads next year. = 他们将在明年重建受损的道路。
The team will reconstruct the crime scene for the investigation. = 团队将为调查重建犯罪现场。

虚拟语气:

被动语态:
