premeditate:/ˌpriːˈmedɪteɪt/ | vt. |预谋；预先考虑 |To think out or plan (an action, especially a crime) beforehand.  It implies a degree of careful planning and intentionality. |例句：He premeditated the murder for weeks before carrying it out. 他在实施谋杀前预谋了几周。

例句：They premeditated their escape route carefully. 他们仔细预谋了逃跑路线。


近义词：plan, plot, scheme, contemplate, deliberate |反义词：impromptu, spontaneous, accidental


词性:动词

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He premeditates his every move. = 他事先计划好他的每一个举动。
She premeditates her responses in interviews. = 她事先想好了在面试中的回答。

一般过去时:
The robbers premeditated the heist for months. = 强盗们预谋了几个月的抢劫案。
He premeditated his revenge. = 他预谋了他的报复。

现在完成时:
They have premeditated a complex strategy. = 他们已经预谋了一个复杂的策略。
She has premeditated her resignation for some time. = 她已经预谋辞职一段时间了。

过去完成时:
The thief had premeditated the burglary before entering the house. = 小偷在进屋之前就预谋了入室盗窃。
They had premeditated their actions for years. = 他们多年来一直在预谋他们的行动。

将来时:
He will premeditate his next move carefully. = 他会仔细地预谋他的下一步行动。
They will premeditate their response to the crisis. = 他们将预谋对危机的回应。


被动语态:
The crime was premeditated. = 犯罪是预谋的。
The attack was premeditated and brutal. = 袭击是蓄谋已久的，并且极其残忍。

疑问句:
Was the attack premeditated? = 袭击是预谋的吗？
Did they premeditate their actions? = 他们预谋了他们的行动吗？

否定句:
The accident was not premeditated. = 事故并非蓄谋。
He did not premeditate his actions. = 他没有预谋他的行动。


PHRASE->
premeditated murder:  蓄意谋杀
premeditated assault:  蓄意攻击
premeditated crime: 预谋犯罪
premeditated malice:  蓄意恶意
to premeditate a response: 预谋回应


词源:
The word "premeditate" comes from the Latin prefix "pre-" (meaning "before") and the verb "meditari" (meaning "to think, to ponder, to plan").  Therefore, the word literally means "to think before."
