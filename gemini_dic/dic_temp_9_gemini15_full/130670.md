nonadvocates: /ˌnɒnˈædvəkəts/ | n. |非拥护者，反对者 |Those who do not support or advocate for a particular cause, idea, or person.  People who are neutral or opposed to a certain viewpoint. |例句：The nonadvocates of the new policy voiced their concerns at the town hall meeting.  = 新政策的反对者在镇政厅会议上表达了他们的担忧。


The nonadvocates remained silent during the debate. = 在辩论期间，反对者保持沉默。


近义词：opponents, critics, dissenters, objectors


反义词：advocates, proponents, supporters


词性：名词


词源：

* **non-**:  A prefix meaning "not" or "opposite of".
* **advocate**: From Latin *advocatus*, meaning "one who pleads the cause of another," ultimately from *advocare*, "to call to one's aid," from *ad-* "to" + *vocare* "to call".
