estuaries:/ˈɛstʃuˌɛriz/| n.|河口；河口湾 | A partially enclosed coastal body of brackish water with one or more rivers or streams flowing into it, and with a free connection to the open sea.  Estuaries are dynamic environments where freshwater from rivers mixes with saltwater from the ocean, creating a unique ecosystem. | Example: The Amazon estuary is one of the largest and most biodiverse in the world. 亚马逊河口是世界上最大、生物多样性最丰富的河口之一。  |近义词：tidal inlet, bay, lagoon, delta |反义词：ocean (in the context of a specific location) |名词
