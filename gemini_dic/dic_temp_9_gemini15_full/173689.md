quasi-malicious: /ˈkwɑːziˈmæliʃəs/ | adj. |似恶意；准恶意的 | Seemingly malicious but not necessarily intentionally harmful; exhibiting characteristics of malice but lacking the full intent.  It suggests an action or behavior that has the appearance of malice, but may be due to negligence, incompetence, or unintentional consequences. |

例句：The quasi-malicious software update caused widespread system crashes, though it wasn't designed to be harmful.  =  这个准恶意的软件更新导致了广泛的系统崩溃，尽管它并非旨在造成危害。


例句：His quasi-malicious gossip, while not outright lies, damaged his colleagues' reputations. = 他似恶意的小道消息，虽然并非完全谎言，但却损害了同事的名誉。


近义词：Malevolent-seeming, seemingly harmful, unintentionally harmful


反义词：Benevolent, well-intentioned, harmless


词源：
* **quasi-**:  From Latin *quasi*, meaning "as if," "as it were," or "almost."
* **malicious**: From Old French *malicieux*, ultimately from Latin *malus* ("bad," "evil") + *-osus* (suffix forming adjectives meaning "full of").
