词源:

The word "penny-pincher" is a compound word.  "Penny" refers to a small British coin, representing a small amount of money. "Pincher" refers to someone who pinches or holds tightly onto something, in this case, money.  The word therefore literally means someone who tightly holds onto their pennies.  It evolved as a descriptive term through common usage, highlighting the extreme frugality of such individuals.
