outsmarts:/ˌaʊtˈsmɑːrts/ | vt. |智胜，比…聪明 | To be more clever or skillful than (someone or something), especially in a way that leads to their defeat or failure. |例句：The detective outsmarted the thief and solved the case.  侦探智胜窃贼并破案了。|例句：The cunning fox outsmarted the hunter.狡猾的狐狸智胜了猎人。|近义词：outwit, outmaneuver,  surpass, best |反义词：be outdone by, be defeated by


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He outsmarts his opponents in every game. 他在每一场比赛中都智胜对手。
The clever strategist outsmarts his rivals.  这位聪明的战略家智胜了他的对手。


一般过去时:
She outsmarted her brother in the chess game. 她在一盘棋中智胜了她的兄弟。
The clever rabbit outsmarted the hungry wolf.  聪明的兔子智胜了饥饿的狼。


现在完成时:
He has outsmarted all his competitors in the business. 他在生意场上已经智胜了所有竞争对手。
The team has outsmarted their opponents in three consecutive matches.  这支队伍在三场连续的比赛中都智胜了对手。


过去完成时:
They had outsmarted the security system before the alarm went off. 警报响起之前，他们已经智胜了安全系统。
The cunning thief had outsmarted the police for years.  狡猾的小偷多年来一直智胜警方。


将来时:

The company believes it will outsmarts its competitors by launching a new product.  该公司相信通过推出新产品将智胜其竞争对手。


虚拟语气:
If only I had outsmarted him, I would have won the competition.  如果我能智胜他，我就能赢得比赛。
I wish I had outsmarted my opponent in the debate. 我真希望在辩论中能智胜我的对手。


被动语态:
He was outsmarted by a younger player. 他被一位更年轻的选手智胜了。
The plan was outsmarted by unforeseen circumstances.  计划被不可预见的情况所挫败（智胜）。


疑问句:

否定句:
He didn't outsmarted his rival in the negotiations. 他在谈判中没有智胜他的对手。
The team didn't outsmarted the opposing team.  这支队伍没有智胜对方队伍。
