precelebrate:/ˌpriːˈsɛləbreɪt/| v.|提前庆祝|To celebrate something before it actually happens.  This often implies a sense of anticipation or excitement, but can also suggest over-eagerness or impatience.|例句：We precelebrated our anniversary by having dinner at our favorite restaurant a week early. 我们提前一周在最喜欢的餐厅吃晚餐来庆祝我们的周年纪念日。


例句：The team precelebrated their likely victory, only to lose in the final match.  这支球队提前庆祝了他们很可能取得的胜利，结果在决赛中输了。


近义词：anticipate (the celebration of), foreshadow (a celebration)|反义词：postpone (a celebration)
