early:/ˈɜːrli/| adj.|早的，早期的；早熟的|Early means happening or done before the usual or expected time.  It can also refer to something from a time long ago. |例句：He woke up early this morning. 他今天早上起得很早。

例句：The early bird catches the worm.  早起的鸟儿有虫吃。


近义词：ahead of time, premature, beforehand, prior

反义词：late, tardy, delayed


词性:形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He gets up early every day. = 他每天早起。
They usually eat early. = 他们通常吃得早。


一般过去时:
She arrived early for the meeting. = 她早到了会议。
We left early because of the rain. = 因为下雨，我们早些离开了。


现在完成时:
They have arrived early. = 他们已经早到了。
He has always been an early riser. = 他一直都是早起的人。


过去完成时:
I had already eaten early that day. = 我那天已经早早吃过饭了。
They had finished their work early. = 他们早早完成了工作。


将来时:
We will leave early tomorrow morning. = 我们明天早上会早点离开。
He will likely arrive early. = 他很可能会早到。


虚拟语气:
If I had woken up early, I wouldn't have missed the train. = 如果我早点起床，我就不会错过火车了。
I wish I had started early. = 我真希望我早点开始。


被动语态:
The meeting was finished early. = 会议提前结束了。
The project was started early. = 项目提前开始了。


疑问句:
Did you arrive early? = 你早到了吗？
Will they start early? = 他们会早点开始吗？


否定句:
He didn't arrive early. = 他没有早到。
They didn't finish early. = 他们没有早早完成。


PHRASE->
early bird = 早起的人
early days = 还在初期
early on = 在早期
in the early stages = 在初期阶段
early retirement = 提早退休

词源:
The word "early" has Old English roots, stemming from "ēare," which meant "before" or "ahead of."  Its development through Middle English saw minor phonetic shifts leading to the modern form.
