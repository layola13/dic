infer:/ˈɪnfɜːr/| vt.|推断，推论，暗示|to conclude from evidence or reasoning; to suggest or hint at something indirectly |例句：I inferred from his silence that he was angry.  我从他的沉默中推断出他生气了。

例句：The article infers a connection between poverty and crime. 这篇文章暗示贫困与犯罪之间存在联系。

近义词：deduce, conclude, imply, suggest |反义词：prove, demonstrate, confirm |词性:动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I infer that he is unhappy from his facial expression. = 我从他的表情推断出他不开心。
She infers a lot from people's body language. = 她从人们的身体语言中推断出很多东西。


一般过去时:
The detective inferred the killer's identity from the clues. = 侦探从线索中推断出凶手的身份。
He inferred that she was lying from her hesitant response. = 他从她犹豫的回答中推断出她在撒谎。


现在完成时:
Scientists have inferred the existence of dark matter from its gravitational effects. = 科学家们已经从暗物质的引力效应推断出它的存在。
We have inferred that the project will be delayed based on the current progress. = 基于目前的进展，我们推断出该项目将会延期。


过去完成时:
They had inferred that the storm was approaching based on the change in the wind. = 他们根据风向的变化推断出暴风雨即将来临。
She had inferred from his letter that he was in trouble. = 她从他的信中推断出他遇到了麻烦。


将来时:
Experts will infer the cause of the accident after a thorough investigation. = 专家们将在彻底调查后推断事故的原因。
I will infer the meaning of the poem after reading it several times. = 我将仔细阅读这首诗后推断出它的含义。


虚拟语气:
If I were to infer from the evidence, I would say he is guilty. = 如果我要根据证据推断，我会说他有罪。
If we inferred correctly, the results would be quite different. = 如果我们的推断正确，结果将会大相径庭。


被动语态:
The meaning of the text was inferred to be ambiguous. = 这篇文章的含义被推断为模棱两可的。
His guilt was inferred from the lack of an alibi. = 他的罪行是从缺乏不在场证明中推断出来的。


疑问句:
What can you infer from this data? = 你能从这些数据中推断出什么？
What can be inferred from the author's tone? = 从作者的语气中可以推断出什么？


否定句:
I cannot infer any meaning from his cryptic message. = 我无法从他神秘的信息中推断出任何含义。
They failed to infer the true cause of the problem. = 他们未能推断出问题的真正原因。



PHRASE->
infer from: 从...推断
safely infer: 可以安全地推断
reasonably infer: 合理地推断
it can be inferred that: 可以推断出
it may be inferred that: 可以推断出


词源:
The word "infer" comes from the Latin word "inferre," which means "to bring in" or "to carry in."  This reflects the sense of drawing a conclusion from available evidence, bringing the conclusion "in" from the evidence.
