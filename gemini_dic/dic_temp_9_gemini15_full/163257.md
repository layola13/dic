preconcession:/ˌpriːkənˈseʃən/| n. |预先让步；先发制人式妥协|A preconcession is a concession made before a negotiation or argument begins, often to gain an advantage or to appear more reasonable.  It's a strategic move to anticipate and address potential objections or disagreements proactively.  This can involve offering something of lesser value to secure something of greater value later.|The lawyer made a preconcession on a minor point to strengthen his position on the major issues.  律师在次要问题上先做了让步，以加强其在主要问题上的立场。|concession, compromise, yielding|confrontation, insistence|名词
