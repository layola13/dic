shtetl:/ʃtɛtl/| n.|犹太人聚居的小镇|A shtetl is a small town, especially in Eastern Europe, where a large Jewish population lived.  Shtetls were often characterized by a distinct Jewish culture and way of life. They were often self-governing communities with their own synagogues, schools, and social organizations.|Many shtetls were destroyed during the Holocaust.|近义词：Jewish village, small town|反义词：metropolis, large city
