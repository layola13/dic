extensibility:/ˌɛkˌstɛnsəˈbɪləti/ | n. | 可扩展性，可延伸性 | The ability to be extended or enlarged.  It refers to the capacity of a system, design, or object to be expanded, improved, or adapted to meet changing requirements without significant modifications to its fundamental structure.  | Example: The extensibility of the software allows us to add new features easily.  软件的可扩展性使我们能够轻松添加新功能。 |近义词: expandability, scalability, adaptability | 反义词: inflexibility, rigidity | 名词

词源:

The word "extensibility" comes from the verb "extend," meaning to make longer or larger,  combined with the suffix "-ibility," which indicates capacity or capability.  The ultimate root is the Latin word "extendere," meaning "to stretch out."
