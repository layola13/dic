prickliness:/ˈprɪklɪnəs/| n.|易怒；刺痛感；多刺；令人不快|A feeling of irritation or annoyance, often shown through easily offended behavior.  It can also refer to the physical sensation of prickles or the presence of many prickles.|例句：His prickliness was evident in his curt replies. 他的易怒从他简短的回答中显而易见。


例句：The prickliness of the rose bush scratched my arm. 玫瑰丛的刺扎了我的胳膊。|近义词：irritability, touchiness, irascibility, grumpiness|反义词：calmness, placidity, amiability


词源:
The word "prickliness" derives from the word "prickle," which itself has Old English roots.  "Prickle" ultimately stems from the Proto-Germanic "*prikkila-", suggesting a connection to words denoting points or sharp objects.  The suffix "-ness" is a common English suffix forming nouns of state or quality, indicating the condition or quality of being prickly.
