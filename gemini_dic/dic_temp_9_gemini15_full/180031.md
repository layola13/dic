/ˈriːfiks/ | n. | 前缀 | A prefix is a morpheme (the smallest unit of meaning in a language) that is added to the beginning of a word to modify its meaning. Prefixes are often used to create new words or to change the grammatical function of a word.  For example, the prefix "un-" can be added to the word "happy" to create the word "unhappy," which means "not happy." | The prefix "un-" changes the meaning of "happy".  The prefix "re-" in "rewrite" indicates repetition. | 近义词：prefixoid (less common) | 反义词：suffix
