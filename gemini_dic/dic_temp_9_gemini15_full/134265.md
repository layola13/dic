noninflected:/ˌnɒnɪnˈflektɪd/ | adj. | 不变的；无词形的 | Not changed in form to show grammatical function or relation.  This term typically refers to words that do not change their form to indicate grammatical features like tense, number, gender, or case.  Many words in English are noninflected, particularly prepositions, conjunctions, and some adverbs. | Example: The word "and" is noninflected; it remains the same regardless of the grammatical context.  The preposition "in" is also noninflected. | 近义词：unchanged, uninflected | 反义词：inflected
