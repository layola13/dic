isolation: /ˌaɪsəˈleɪʃən/ | n. | 隔离；孤立；孤立状态 | The state of being separated from other people or things.  It can refer to physical separation, social exclusion, or emotional detachment. | 例句：The patient was placed in isolation to prevent the spread of the disease.  病人被隔离以防止疾病传播。

The prolonged isolation of the island community led to unique cultural practices.  岛屿社区长期与世隔绝，导致了独特的文化习俗。


近义词：seclusion, quarantine, loneliness, solitude | 反义词：integration, connection, togetherness | 词性：名词

词源:
The word "isolation" comes from the Old French word "isolacion," which itself derives from the Latin word "insula," meaning "island."  The sense of separation and detachment inherent in being on an island is what gave rise to the modern meaning of "isolation."
