improvising:/ˈɪmprəvaɪzɪŋ/ | v. |即兴创作；临时应付 |  The act of creating or performing something spontaneously, without prior preparation.  It often involves adapting to unexpected situations or circumstances. | 例句：The jazz musician was improvising a solo on the saxophone.  爵士乐手正在即兴演奏萨克斯风独奏。

例句：She was improvising a meal using whatever ingredients she had on hand. 她用现有的食材即兴做了一顿饭。


近义词：extemporizing, ad-libbing, winging it | 反义词：preplanning, rehearsing


词源:  From the Latin "improvisare," meaning "to compose or perform without preparation."  The prefix "im-" means "not," and "providere" means "to foresee," so literally, "not foreseeing."
