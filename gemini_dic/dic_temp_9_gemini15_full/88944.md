holdup:/ˈhoʊldʌp/| n., v.| 抢劫；耽搁；阻碍；支撑物 |


英文解析:As a noun, a holdup refers to a robbery, typically at gunpoint. As a verb, it means to delay or impede progress.  It can also refer to supporting something physically.


例句:

(Noun - Robbery):
The bank was the target of a daring holdup yesterday. 昨天银行遭到了一次大胆的抢劫。

The police are investigating the holdup at the jewelry store. 警方正在调查珠宝店的抢劫案。


(Verb - Delay):
The unexpected snowstorm caused a holdup in our travel plans.  突如其来的暴风雪打乱了我们的旅行计划。

Traffic congestion caused a significant holdup on the highway.  交通堵塞导致高速公路严重延误。


(Verb - Impeding):
Lack of funding is holding up the project. 资金不足阻碍了项目的进展。

Several technical issues are holding up the release of the software.  几个技术问题阻碍了软件的发布。


近义词:

(Noun - Robbery): robbery, heist, mugging
(Verb - Delay): delay, postponement, impediment, obstruction
(Verb - Support): support, prop, brace


反义词:

(Noun - Robbery):  (No direct antonym, but the opposite situation would be security/safety)
(Verb - Delay): acceleration, speeding up
(Verb - Support): collapse, failure


词性:Noun, Verb
