rationalist:/ˈræʃənəlɪst/| n. adj.|理性主义者；理性主义的|A person who believes in the power of reason and logic, especially in rejecting traditional beliefs.|例句：Many Enlightenment thinkers were rationalists who emphasized reason over faith.  许多启蒙思想家都是理性主义者，他们强调理性胜于信仰。|近义词：intellectual, logician, empiricist|反义词：irrationalist, mystic, fideist

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He rationalizes his behavior. = 他为自己的行为找借口。
She rationalizes every decision. = 她为每一个决定找理由。

一般过去时:
They rationalized the process. = 他们使这个过程合理化。
He rationalized his fears. = 他使自己的恐惧合理化。


现在完成时:
We have rationalized many aspects of our business. = 我们已经使我们业务的许多方面合理化。
She has rationalized her investment strategy. = 她已经使自己的投资策略合理化。


过去完成时:
They had rationalized the production line before the inspection. = 他们在检查之前已经使生产线合理化。
He had rationalized his actions after the fact. = 事后他为自己的行为找了借口。


将来时:
We will rationalize our resources. = 我们将使我们的资源合理化。
They will rationalize their operations. = 他们将使他们的业务合理化。


虚拟语气:
If I were a rationalist, I would approach this differently. = 如果我是个理性主义者，我会采取不同的方法。
I wish he would rationalize his spending habits. = 我希望他能使自己的消费习惯合理化。


被动语态:
The company's structure was rationalized last year. = 该公司的结构去年进行了合理化改造。
The process has been rationalized to improve efficiency. = 为了提高效率，这个过程已经过合理化改造。


疑问句:
Does he rationalize his actions? = 他是否为自己的行为找借口？
Have they rationalized their decision-making process? = 他们是否使他们的决策过程合理化？


否定句:
He doesn't rationalize his mistakes. = 他不为自己的错误找借口。
They haven't rationalized their approach yet. = 他们还没有使自己的方法合理化。
