/ˌsɛstoʊˈiːdiə/| n. |绦虫纲|Cestoidea is a class of parasitic flatworms, commonly known as tapeworms.  They are characterized by their segmented bodies (proglottids), lack of a digestive system, and specialized structures for attachment to their hosts.  They infect a wide range of vertebrate animals, including humans, causing various diseases depending on the species. | Example:  The study focused on the life cycle of a specific cestoidea species found in cattle.  这项研究的重点是牛体内一种特定绦虫纲物种的生命周期。|近义词: Tapeworm (informal)|反义词:  None (no direct antonym)|名词
