mutilated:/ˈmjuːtɪleɪtɪd/ | adj. | 严重损坏的；残缺不全的；肢解的 | Severely damaged; maimed; disfigured; having a part or parts removed or destroyed.  | The mutilated statue was almost unrecognizable.  被毁坏的雕像几乎认不出来了。 | The accident left him with mutilated hands.  事故使他的双手严重受伤。 | damaged, destroyed, injured, defaced | intact, undamaged, complete, perfect | 形容词

词源:
From Middle French *mutiler, from Latin mutilare "to maim, mangle," from mut- "mute, dumb," + -ilare.  The original meaning related to the idea of rendering something "speechless" or "incapable of expression" through damage.  The modern sense developed later.
