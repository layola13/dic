categorises: /ˈkætəɡəraɪzɪz/ | v. | 分类，归类 | To put things into categories.  | The librarian categorises the books by subject. 图书管理员按科目对书籍进行分类。 | classify, group, sort |  ungroup, randomize | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
She categorises her emails by sender. = 她按发件人分类邮件。
He categorises his expenses meticulously. = 他一丝不苟地分类他的支出。


一般过去时:
The researcher categorised the data into three groups. = 研究人员将数据分为三组。
They categorised the responses based on age. = 他们根据年龄对回复进行了分类。


现在完成时:
She has categorised all the files on her computer. = 她已经对她电脑上的所有文件进行了分类。
He has categorised his stamp collection by country of origin. = 他已按原产国对他的邮票收藏进行了分类。


过去完成时:
Before submitting the report, she had categorised all the findings. = 在提交报告之前，她已经对所有调查结果进行了分类。
They had categorised the participants before the experiment began. = 实验开始前，他们已经对参与者进行了分类。


将来时:
He will categorise the photographs by date. = 他将按日期对照片进行分类。
She will categorise the information according to its relevance. = 她将根据相关性对信息进行分类。


虚拟语气:
If I had more time, I would categorise all my books. = 如果我有更多时间，我会把我的所有书都分类。
It would be helpful if we categorised the problems by severity. = 如果我们按严重程度对问题进行分类，将会有所帮助。


被动语态:
The data was categorised using a statistical software. = 数据是使用统计软件进行分类的。
The items are categorised alphabetically in the catalogue. = 商品在目录中按字母顺序排列。


疑问句:
How do you categorise this type of information? = 你如何对这种信息进行分类？
How are the products categorised on the website? = 网站上的产品是如何分类的？


否定句:
He didn't categorise the information properly. = 他没有正确地对信息进行分类。
She didn't categorise her expenses, so she couldn't track her spending. = 她没有分类她的支出，所以她无法追踪她的消费。


PHRASE->
categorise by: 按…分类
categorise as: 将…归类为
categorise into: 将…分成
broad categorisation: 广泛的分类
fine categorisation: 精细的分类
