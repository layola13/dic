exorbitant:/ɪɡˈzɔːrbɪtənt/| adj.|过高的；过分的；昂贵的|extremely high; excessive.  The exorbitant price of the hotel room shocked us.  |近义词：excessive, extravagant, exorbitant, immoderate |反义词：reasonable, moderate, affordable


例句：

The exorbitant cost of living in the city forced many families to move to the suburbs.  = 城市高昂的生活成本迫使许多家庭搬到了郊区。

He charged an exorbitant fee for such a simple repair. = 他对如此简单的修理收取了过高的费用。


词源:

From Late Latin *exorbitans, present participle of exorbitare "to go out of its orbit," from ex- "out of" + orbita "orbit, track."  The sense development reflects the idea of something straying beyond acceptable limits.


用法说明:

exorbitant 主要用作形容词，修饰名词，通常指价格、费用、要求等过高或过分。  它强调超出合理或正常的范围。


PHRASE->

exorbitant price: 过高的价格
exorbitant fees: 过高的费用
exorbitant demands: 过分的要求
exorbitant interest rates: 高得离谱的利率
exorbitant rents: 昂贵的租金
