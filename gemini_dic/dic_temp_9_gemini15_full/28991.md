cartesianism:/ˌkɑːrˈtiʒənɪzəm/| n. |笛卡尔主义，笛卡尔哲学|The philosophical system of René Descartes, emphasizing reason and the existence of mind as distinct from matter.  Cartesianism is characterized by its dualism (mind/body), its emphasis on rationalism (reason as the primary source of knowledge), and its mechanistic view of the universe. | Example:  Cartesianism profoundly influenced the development of modern science and philosophy.  笛卡尔主义深刻地影响了现代科学和哲学的发展。|近义词：rationalism, dualism|反义词：empiricism, materialism|名词


USAGE->
This word is primarily a noun and doesn't conjugate like a verb.  Therefore, grammatical tenses (present, past, future, etc.) and voice (active/passive) are not applicable.  The word itself describes a philosophical system, not an action.
