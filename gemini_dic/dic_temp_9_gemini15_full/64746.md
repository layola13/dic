escot: /ˈɛskɒt/ | n. |护送；陪同；护卫 |  A person who accompanies another, especially for protection or courtesy.  |例句：He hired an escot to guide him through the city. 他雇了一个人护送他穿过城市。 |近义词：guide, companion, attendant |反义词：abandon, forsake |名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The bodyguard escorts the VIP. = 保镖护送贵宾。
She escorts her children to school every day. = 她每天护送孩子上学。


一般过去时:
The police escorted the suspect to the station. = 警察护送嫌疑犯到警局。
He escorted her to the door. = 他护送她到门口。


现在完成时:
I have escorted many important guests. = 我已经护送过许多重要的客人。
The company has escorted tourists to various destinations. = 这家公司已经护送游客去了不同的目的地。


过去完成时:
They had escorted the witness before the trial. = 他们在审判前已经护送了证人。
She had escorted him to the airport earlier that day. = 她当天早些时候已经把他送到了机场。


将来时:
I will escort you to your hotel. = 我将护送您到您的酒店。
We will escort the delegates to the conference. = 我们将护送代表们去参加会议。


虚拟语气:
If I were you, I would escort her home safely. = 如果我是你，我会安全地把她送回家。
I wish I had escorted her to the hospital. = 我真希望我把她送去了医院。


被动语态:
The prisoner was escorted by two guards. = 囚犯由两名警卫押送。
The children were escorted safely home. = 孩子们被安全地送回了家。


疑问句:
Who escorted the package? = 谁护送包裹？
Will someone escort me to the meeting room? = 会有人护送我去会议室吗？


否定句:
He did not escort her to the party. = 他没有护送她去参加聚会。
They didn't escort the visitors to the train station. = 他们没有护送游客去火车站。


PHRASE->
police escort = 警方护送
armed escort = 武装护送
personal escort = 私人护送
military escort = 军事护送
escort service = 护送服务 (有时指不正当的服务，需注意语境)
