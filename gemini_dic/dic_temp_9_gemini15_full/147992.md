overrides:/ˈoʊvərˌraɪdz/ | vt. | 优先于；推翻；不顾；凌驾于 | To overrule or prevail over (something). To override something means to take precedence over it, to have more power or authority than it, or to ignore it and act in a different way. |例句：The company's safety regulations override any individual's preferences. 公司的安全规章优先于任何个人的偏好。

The emergency brake overrides the accelerator. 紧急制动器优先于油门。

近义词：supersede, annul, preempt, disregard | 反义词：obey, comply, adhere to | 词性:动词

词源:
The word "overrides" comes from the Old French word "overrider," which is a combination of "over" meaning "above" or "beyond" and "rider" meaning "to ride" or "to dominate."  The sense of "to surpass" or "to prevail" developed from the literal image of one rider overpowering another.
