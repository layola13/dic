retrial:/ˌriːˈtraɪəl/| n. |重审，再审|A retrial is a new trial of a case that has already been heard in a court of law.  This typically happens when there has been a significant legal error in the original trial, or new evidence has come to light. |例句：The defendant was granted a retrial after it was discovered that key evidence had been suppressed.  被告在发现关键证据被压制后被准予重审。

例句：The judge ordered a retrial due to jury misconduct during the first trial. 由于陪审团在第一次审判中的不当行为，法官下令重审。


近义词：new trial, rehearing|反义词：final judgment, acquittal

词源:
The word "retrial" is a combination of the prefix "re-" (meaning "again") and the noun "trial" (meaning a formal examination of evidence before a judge, and typically a jury, to decide guilt or innocence).  Therefore, "retrial" literally means "a trial again".
