preescaping: /ˌpriːɪˈskeɪpɪŋ/ | v. | 预先转义 |  The process of escaping characters or sequences in a string before they are processed by another system or application, to prevent unintended interpretation or security vulnerabilities.  This typically involves replacing special characters with their escaped equivalents (e.g., replacing "<" with "&lt;"). | Example: Preescaping user input before displaying it on a webpage is crucial to prevent cross-site scripting (XSS) attacks.  预先转义用户输入然后再在网页上显示对于防止跨站脚本攻击至关重要。  | 近义词：escaping, sanitizing | 反义词：unescaping |  动词
