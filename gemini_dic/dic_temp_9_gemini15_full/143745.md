outmaneuvering: /ˌaʊt.mənˈuː.vər.ɪŋ/ | v. |  巧妙战胜；智胜；以策略取胜 |  To outmaneuver someone or something means to cleverly defeat them or gain an advantage over them using skillful tactics or strategies.  It implies a more subtle and strategic approach than simply overpowering them. | 例句：The general outmaneuvered the enemy by using a surprise flanking maneuver.  将军通过出其不意的侧翼迂回战术巧妙地战胜了敌人。


The experienced chess player skillfully outmaneuvered his opponent, forcing a checkmate.  经验丰富的棋手巧妙地战胜了他的对手，迫使其将死。| 近义词：outwit, outsmart, outplay, circumvent,  surpass | 反义词：be outmaneuvered, be defeated, be outwitted

词源:
The word "outmaneuver" is a combination of "out-" (meaning "to a greater degree than") and "maneuver" (from French "manoeuvre," ultimately from Old French "manœuvre,"  related to the Latin "manus" meaning "hand").  It literally means to perform maneuvers better than someone else.
