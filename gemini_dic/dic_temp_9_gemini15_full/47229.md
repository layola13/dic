dazy:/ˈdeɪzi/| adj.|昏昏欲睡的；迷糊的；愚笨的；|adj.  Dazed means confused and disoriented, often as a result of a shock, blow, or other overwhelming experience. It suggests a temporary state of mental confusion.|例句：The blow to the head left him dazed and confused.  头部受到重击使他头昏眼花，不知所措。
The bright lights dazed her for a moment.  明亮的灯光让她短暂地感到眼花缭乱。|近义词：stunned, bewildered, disoriented|反义词：alert, aware, clear-headed|


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is dazed after the accident. 他事故后感到迷糊。
She is always dazed and confused. 她总是迷迷糊糊的。


一般过去时:

现在完成时:

过去完成时:

将来时:

虚拟语气:
