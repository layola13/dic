词源:

The word is a compound of "red-blooded," which itself is a figurative expression.  "Red blood" evokes images of health, vigor, and strong circulation.  The "-ness" suffix transforms the adjective into a noun, signifying the state or quality of being red-blooded.  The exact origin of the figurative use of "red-blooded" is difficult to pinpoint, but it likely developed from associating red blood with life force and health.
