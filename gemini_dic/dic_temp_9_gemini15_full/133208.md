nonexaggerated: /ˌnɒnɪɡˈzædʒəreɪtɪd/ /ˌnɑːnɪɡˈzædʒəreɪtɪd/ | adj. | 不夸张的；真实的；未夸大的 | Not exaggerated; true; factual.  The description of the event was nonexaggerated, presenting the facts without embellishment. | 近义词：uninflated, understated, factual, realistic | 反义词：exaggerated, inflated, hyperbolic


例句：

His account of the incident was entirely nonexaggerated.  他的事故描述完全没有夸张。

The nonexaggerated figures show a significant increase in sales.  未经夸大的数据显示销售额大幅增长。


词源：

* **non-**:  Prefix meaning "not" or "without".
* **exaggerate**: Verb meaning "to enlarge or represent something beyond the normal or truthful limits".  This derives from Latin "ex-" (out, beyond) + "aggerare" (to heap up).


用法说明：

"Nonexaggerated" is primarily used as an adjective to describe something that is presented truthfully and without embellishment or hyperbole.  It emphasizes the accuracy and lack of exaggeration in a statement, description, or account.  It is often used in contexts where there's a need to counter a potentially biased or inflated perspective.
