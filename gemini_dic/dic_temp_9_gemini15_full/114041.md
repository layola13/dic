magnetic:/mæɡˈnɛtɪk/|/ˌmæɡˈnɛtɪk/| adj. |磁性的；有吸引力的；有魅力的 | Relating to magnetism or magnets; possessing the properties of a magnet.  The word also describes something that is captivating or attractive. |例句：The magnetic field of the Earth protects us from harmful solar radiation. 地球的磁场保护我们免受有害的太阳辐射。

例句：His magnetic personality drew people to him. 他迷人的个性吸引了人们。


近义词：attractive, alluring, captivating, charming |反义词：repulsive, unattractive, unappealing

词源:
From Middle French *magnétique, from Late Latin magneticus, from Latin magnes "magnet," of obscure origin, possibly from Greek μαγνήτης (magnētēs) "magnet stone" (from Magnesia, a region in ancient Greece where magnets were found).  The suffix "-ic" is a common adjective-forming suffix in English, derived from Greek.
