interpleader:/ˈɪntəˌpliːdər/| n. |介入诉讼；请求法院裁定| A legal procedure whereby a person holding property claimed by two or more other parties can bring those parties into court to determine who has the rightful claim.  It allows the stakeholder to avoid potential liability by having the court decide the competing claims. | Example: The bank, holding a disputed sum of money, filed an interpleader action to have the court decide which of its two customers was the rightful owner. |近义词：intervention, action, suit|反义词：N/A|名词
