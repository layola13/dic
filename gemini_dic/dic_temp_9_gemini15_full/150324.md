/ˈpæŋɡuː/ | n. |盘古 | Pangu is a name in Chinese mythology referring to a primordial giant who created the universe by separating the primordial egg into heaven and earth.  He is often depicted as a powerful and colossal figure. |The legend of Pangu is a cornerstone of Chinese cosmology and mythology. |近义词：creator (in the context of universe creation) |反义词：destroyer (in the context of universe creation)
