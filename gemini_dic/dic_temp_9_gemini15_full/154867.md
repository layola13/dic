perversive:/pərˈvɜːrsɪv/| adj.| 变态的；不正当的；有悖常理的 |  Deviating from what is considered right or proper; characterized by a persistent disregard for accepted standards of morality or behavior.  It often implies a deliberate and often malicious intent to subvert or corrupt. |

例句：His perversive behavior shocked everyone.  他的变态行为震惊了所有人。

例句：The perversive interpretation of the law led to injustice.  对法律的歪曲解释导致了不公正。


近义词：deviant, aberrant, warped, depraved, corrupt, twisted, unnatural

反义词：normal, proper, righteous, virtuous, moral, upright

词源:
From Old French *pervers, from Latin perversus, past participle of pervertere "to overturn, pervert," from per- "through, thoroughly" + vertere "to turn."  The sense development is from "to turn around, subvert" to "morally wrong, contrary to nature."
