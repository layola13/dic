penteconter:/ˌpɛntɪˈkɒntər/| n. |五桨战船|A penteconter was an ancient Greek warship propelled by 50 oarsmen, typically arranged in two banks of 25 oars each.  It was a fast and maneuverable vessel used primarily for raiding and skirmishing. |The Athenian penteconters were renowned for their speed and agility. 雅典的五桨战船以其速度和灵活性而闻名。|trireme, bireme|  (no direct antonym, as it describes a specific type of ship)|名词
