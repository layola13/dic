hourglass:/ˈaʊərˌɡlæs/| n. |沙漏；流逝的时间|A glass container with a narrow neck, used to measure time by the flow of sand from one bulb to another.  It is also a symbol of the fleeting nature of time.|例句：The hourglass in the painting symbolized the brevity of life.  这幅画中的沙漏象征着生命的短暂。

例句：She watched the sand slowly trickle through the hourglass, each grain representing a precious moment. 她看着沙子慢慢地从沙漏中流下，每一粒沙子都代表着宝贵的时刻。


近义词：sand timer, timekeeper|反义词：None specifically, but concepts like "eternity" or "permanence" could be considered antonyms conceptually.


词源：The word "hourglass" is a compound word, combining "hour" and "glass".  The "hour" refers to the unit of time measured, and "glass" refers to the container.  Its origins trace back to the medieval period when such devices were commonly used.
