melotype:/ˈmɛləˌtaɪp/| n. |旋律类型，音调类型|A melotype is a distinctive type or pattern of melody, often used in music analysis to categorize and compare musical works.  It refers to the characteristic melodic contour and intervallic structure of a musical phrase or theme.|Example: The composer's distinctive melotype is readily apparent throughout the symphony.|近义词： melodic pattern, musical motif |反义词：None specifically.  The opposite would be a lack of distinctive melodic character.
