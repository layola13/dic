open-air: /ˈoʊpən ˈɛr/ | adj. | 户外的，露天的 |  Describing something that takes place or is situated in the open air, rather than indoors or under cover.  It emphasizes the lack of a roof or enclosed structure. | 例句：We enjoyed an open-air concert in the park. 我们在公园里享受了一场露天音乐会。 | 例句：The market offers a variety of fresh produce in an open-air setting.  这个市场在一个露天环境中提供各种新鲜农产品。| 近义词：outdoor, al fresco | 反义词：indoor, enclosed | 词性: adjective

词源:

The term "open-air" is a compound adjective.  "Open" derives from Old English *open, meaning "uncovered, exposed,"  while "air" comes from Old English æer, meaning "air, atmosphere."  The combination describes something exposed to the elements, lacking a roof or protective covering.
