collective-farms:/kəˈlɛktɪvfɑːrmz/| n. |集体农庄 |Collective farms are agricultural production systems where land and resources are owned collectively by a group of farmers, often organized as a cooperative or state-owned entity.  These farms were common in the Soviet Union and other socialist countries.  They aimed to increase agricultural output through economies of scale and collective effort.
