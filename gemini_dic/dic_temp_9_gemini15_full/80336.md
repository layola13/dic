goldfinches:/ˈɡoʊldfɪntʃɪz/| n. |金翅雀（复数）|Goldfinches are small, vibrant birds known for their bright yellow and red plumage. They are popular garden birds, often seen feeding on seeds.  |例句：Several goldfinches were flitting amongst the sunflowers.  几只金翅雀在向日葵丛中飞舞。

例句：We saw a flock of goldfinches feeding on thistle seeds in the field. 我们看到一群金翅雀在田里吃蓟种子。


近义词：goldfinch (单数),  (其他近义词取决于语境，例如某种特定类型的雀鸟)
反义词：无


词源:
The word "goldfinch" is a compound word.  "Gold" refers to the bright yellow color of the bird's plumage, particularly on its wings. "Finch" is a type of small, seed-eating bird belonging to the family Fringillidae.  Therefore, "goldfinch" literally means a "gold-colored finch".  The etymology of "finch" itself is from Old English *finc.
