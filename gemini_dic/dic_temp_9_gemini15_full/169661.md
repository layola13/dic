proportionate: /prəˈpɔːʃənət/ | adj. | 成比例的；相称的；均衡的 |  Corresponding in size, degree, or intensity; commensurable.  | 例句：The punishment should be proportionate to the crime.  惩罚应该与罪行相称。

例句：The resources allocated to each department are proportionate to its size and workload. 分配给每个部门的资源与其规模和工作量成比例。


近义词：commensurate, proportional, equivalent, balanced | 反义词：disproportionate, unequal, imbalanced


词源:  Comes from the Old French word *proporcioné*, which is derived from the Latin word *proportio*, meaning "proportion."  The suffix "-ate" is added to create the adjective form.


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The punishment is proportionate to the offense. = 惩罚与罪行相称。
The representation is proportionate to the population. = 代表比例与人口成比例。


一般过去时:
The reward was proportionate to the effort. = 奖励与付出的努力成比例。
The response was proportionate to the stimulus. = 反应与刺激成比例。


现在完成时:
The company has kept its workforce proportionate to its growth. = 公司已使其员工数量与其增长成比例。
The increase in wages has been proportionate to the rise in inflation. = 工资的增长与通货膨胀的上升成比例。


过去完成时:
The damage had been proportionate to the force of the impact. = 受损程度与撞击力成比例。
The effect had been proportionate to the amount of medication administered. = 效果与所服用药物的剂量成比例。


将来时:
The outcome will be proportionate to the investment. = 结果将与投资成比例。

虚拟语气:

被动语态:

否定句:
