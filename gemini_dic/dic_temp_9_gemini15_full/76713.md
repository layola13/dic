gambit:/ˈɡæmbɪt/| n. |策略，开场妙招；冒险行动 | An opening move in chess in which a piece is sacrificed to gain an advantage.  More generally, any opening move of a game or situation that involves some risk but offers the possibility of a significant gain. |例句：Her opening gambit in the negotiations was to demand a large sum of money.  她的谈判开场策略是索要一大笔钱。

例句：He used a risky gambit to win the game. 他用冒险的策略赢得了比赛。


近义词：strategy, tactic, maneuver, ploy, ruse |反义词：cautious approach, conservative move |词性：名词


词源：源于中古法语 *gambit,  源于意大利语 gambetto，意为“绊脚”，源于 gambare “绊倒”，可能源于中古高地德语 *kamba  “弯曲的腿”。  最初指国际象棋中牺牲棋子以换取优势的开局策略。
