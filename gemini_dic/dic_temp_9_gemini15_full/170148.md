protohippus: /ˌproʊtoʊˈhɪpəs/ | n. | 原马属 | Protohippus is an extinct genus of three-toed equids that lived during the Miocene and Pliocene epochs.  It is considered an ancestor of the modern horse.  It was larger than its predecessors and possessed features that were transitional to the modern horse, including a longer face and higher-crowned teeth adapted for grazing. | The fossils of Protohippus have been found in North America and Eurasia. | 近义词： (paleontological context)  ancestral horse,  equid ancestor | 反义词:  (no direct antonym, but could be contrasted with) modern horse, Equus
