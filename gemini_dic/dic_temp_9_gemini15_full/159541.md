politicize:/ˈpɒlətɪsaɪz/ /ˈpɑːlətɪsaɪz/ | vt. | 使政治化；赋予政治色彩 | To give a political character to (something that is not inherently political); to make political.  |例句：They attempted to politicize the issue of education reform. 他们试图将教育改革问题政治化。

例句：The media's coverage politicized the otherwise simple accident. 媒体的报道使原本简单的意外事故政治化了。


近义词：politicize, partisanize


反义词：depoliticize, depolarise


词性:动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They politicize every social issue. = 他们把每一个社会问题都政治化。
The government politicizes the judiciary. = 政府使司法政治化。


一般过去时:
The media politicized the environmental debate. = 媒体使环境辩论政治化了。
He politicized the local election. = 他使地方选举政治化了。


现在完成时:
They have politicized the charitable organization. = 他们已经使该慈善组织政治化了。
The protesters have politicized the cultural event. = 抗议者已经使文化活动政治化了。


过去完成时:
The opposition party had politicized the economic crisis before the election. = 反对党在选举前就已将经济危机政治化了。
They had politicized the environmental protection law. = 他们已经使环境保护法政治化了。


将来时:
The upcoming election will politicize many social issues. = 即将到来的选举将使许多社会问题政治化。

虚拟语气:
If they politicized the issue, it would further divide the community. = 如果他们将这个问题政治化，它将进一步分裂社区。
I wish they hadn't politicized the humanitarian aid. = 我希望他们没有将人道主义援助政治化。


被动语态:
The issue was politicized by the opposition party. = 这个问题被反对党政治化了。
The debate is being politicized by both sides. = 辩论正在被双方政治化。


疑问句:
Is the media politicizing the news? = 媒体是否在使新闻政治化？
Why are they politicizing this event? = 他们为什么要将这个事件政治化？


否定句:
They did not politicize the scientific research. = 他们没有使科学研究政治化。
We will not politicize this important decision. = 我们不会将这个重要的决定政治化。

词源:
From "politics" + "-ize" (suffix forming verbs).  The word "politics" itself originates from the Greek word "politikos," meaning "of, for, or relating to citizens."
