reagitation:/ˌriːˌæɡɪˈteɪʃən/| n. |再次激动；重新激起；再议；重新讨论|The act of agitating again; stirring up again; renewed agitation or excitement.|The reagitation of the old conflict caused widespread fear.  再次激化旧冲突引起了广泛的恐惧。
The constant reagitation of past traumas kept her from healing.  不断重提过去的创伤阻碍了她康复。|近义词：revival, resurgence, rekindling |反义词：calm, pacification, resolution

USAGE->

一般现在时:
The constant media coverage leads to a reagitation of the issue.  持续的媒体报道导致了这个问题的再次激化。
Reagitation of the problem often prevents a permanent solution.  问题的反复激化往往会阻止永久性解决方案的出现。


一般过去时:
The discovery of new evidence caused a reagitation of the case.  新证据的发现导致了案件的重新调查。
The politician's speech resulted in a reagitation of public anger.  这位政治家的讲话导致了公众愤怒的再次爆发。


现在完成时:
The recent protests have resulted in a reagitation of social unrest.  最近的抗议活动导致了社会动荡的再次出现。
Years of campaigning have brought about a reagitation of the environmental issue.  多年的宣传活动带来了环境问题的再次关注。


过去完成时:
Before the new evidence emerged, there had been no reagitation of the case. 在新的证据出现之前，案件并没有重新被调查。
They had hoped that the issue would not be subjected to further reagitation.  他们曾希望这个问题不会再次被激化。


将来时:
The upcoming election will likely lead to a reagitation of political tensions.  即将到来的选举可能会导致政治紧张局势的再次加剧。
Any further investigation will inevitably cause a reagitation of the controversy.  任何进一步的调查都不可避免地会引起争议的再次爆发。


虚拟语气:

被动语态:

否定句:
