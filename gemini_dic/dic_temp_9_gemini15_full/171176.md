psychological-warfare: /ˌsaɪkəˈlɒdʒɪkəl ˈwɔːrfɛər/ | n. | 心理战 |  The use of propaganda, disinformation, and other psychological techniques to influence the attitudes and behaviors of a population, often during wartime or political conflict.  It aims to demoralize the enemy, undermine their will to fight, and create chaos or instability. | Example: The government engaged in psychological warfare by spreading rumors about the enemy's capabilities.  政府通过散布关于敌人能力的谣言来进行心理战。  | 近义词: propaganda, brainwashing, manipulation | 反义词:  honest communication, open dialogue
