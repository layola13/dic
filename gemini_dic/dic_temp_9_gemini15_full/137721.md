nonvacuousness:/ˌnɒnˈvækjuəsnəs/ | n. |非空性；非虚无性；意义深远|The nonvacuousness of a statement refers to its meaningfulness and truthfulness, implying that it is not empty or trivial.  It signifies that the statement has genuine content and is not a tautology or a contradiction.  For example, a mathematical theorem is considered to have nonvacuousness if it applies to at least one instance and doesn't always hold true regardless of the values involved. |The theorem's nonvacuousness was proven by providing a specific example where it held true.  该定理的非空性通过提供一个成立的具体例子得到了证明。|significance, substance, meaningfulness |vacuity, emptiness, triviality |名词
