hetaira:/heɪˈtɛərə/| n.|妓女，交际花|A hetaira was a high-class courtesan in ancient Greece, distinguished from ordinary prostitutes by her education, wit, and companionship, often engaging in intellectual and philosophical discussions with her clients.  They were often well-educated and held a higher social standing than common prostitutes.|Example: Aspasia, a famous hetaira, was known for her intelligence and influence on Pericles.|近义词：courtesan, high-class prostitute|反义词：nun, virgin
