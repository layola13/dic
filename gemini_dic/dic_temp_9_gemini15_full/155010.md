peter-pans: /ˈpiːtər pænz/ | n. | 彼得潘综合症；拒绝长大 |  Refers to the psychological condition of adults who exhibit immature behaviors and avoid the responsibilities of adulthood, often clinging to childish fantasies and refusing to grow up, reminiscent of J.M. Barrie's fictional character Peter Pan.  | Example:  He's exhibiting classic peter-pans; he's 30 years old and still living with his parents. 他表现出典型的彼得潘综合症；他30岁了还和父母住在一起。|近义词：immaturity, childishness | 反义词：maturity, adulthood | 名词
