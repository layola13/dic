occipitoaxial:/ˌɒksɪpɪtoʊˈeɪksɪəl/| adj.|枕寰的，枕寰关节的 | Relating to the occiput (back of the head) and the axis (second cervical vertebra).  This term often describes anatomical structures, conditions, or movements involving the joint between these two bones. |The occipitoaxial joint allows for nodding movements of the head.  枕寰关节允许头部点头运动。|近义词：craniocervical (more general)|反义词：None specifically, but terms referring to other joints could be considered opposites in a broad sense.
