methodologies:/ˌmɛθəˈdɒlədʒiz/ | n. | 方法论；方法学 | The study of methods; a system of methods used in a particular area of study or activity.  It refers to the overall approach, principles, and procedures used to conduct research or solve a problem. |  例句：The researchers employed rigorous methodologies to ensure the validity of their findings. 研究人员采用了严格的方法论以确保其研究结果的有效性。

例句：This project requires a clear set of methodologies for data collection and analysis. 这个项目需要一套清晰的数据收集和分析方法论。


近义词：approaches, techniques, procedures, systems | 反义词：haphazardness, randomness


词源:  The word "methodologies" is the plural form of "methodology."  "Methodology" comes from the Greek words "methodos" (μέθοδος), meaning "method," "way," or "system," and "-logia" (-λογία), meaning "study of" or "theory of."  Therefore, "methodology" literally means "the study of methods."
