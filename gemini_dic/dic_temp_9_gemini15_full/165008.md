prehension:/ˌkɒmprɪˈhɛnʃən/ /ˌkɑːmprɪˈhɛnʃən/| n. |理解力；领悟；理解|The ability to understand something.  It involves grasping the meaning, significance, and implications of information.  This can relate to text, spoken words, concepts, or situations.|例句：Her comprehension of complex mathematical problems is remarkable.  她的复杂数学题的理解能力令人惊叹。|例句：The teacher assessed the students' comprehension through a reading test. 老师通过阅读测试评估学生的理解能力。|近义词：understanding, grasp, apprehension|反义词：misunderstanding, confusion


USAGE->
一般现在时:
His comprehension of the subject is excellent. = 他对这个主题的理解非常好。
My comprehension improves with practice. = 我的理解力随着练习而提高。


一般过去时:
Her comprehension failed her during the exam. = 考试期间，她的理解力让她失望了。
