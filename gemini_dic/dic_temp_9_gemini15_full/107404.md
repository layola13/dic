lamination:/ˌlæmɪˈneɪʃən/| n. |层压；薄片粘合；层状结构|The process of bonding together thin layers of material, often using heat and pressure, to create a single, stronger, and more durable product.  It's commonly used in manufacturing various products like wood flooring, safety glass, and printed materials.|例句：The lamination of the wood gave the floor increased durability.  层压木板使地板更耐用。

例句：The book cover was enhanced with a glossy lamination. 书皮经过光面层压处理，效果更好。


近义词：stratification, layering |反义词：delamination (the separation of layers)


词源：From Latin *lamināre "to roll out into thin sheets," from lamina "thin plate, layer," from the PIE root *le- "to flatten".  The English word emerged in the late 19th century, reflecting the increased industrial use of the process.
