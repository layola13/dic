on-the-make:/ˌɒnðəˈmeɪk/| adj.|急于发财的；追求名利的；投机取巧的 | Eager to succeed, often in a ruthless or dishonest way; ambitiously seeking personal gain.  |例句：He's always been on the make, trying to find shortcuts to wealth and fame. 他总是急于发财，试图寻找致富成名的捷径。

例句：She accused him of being on the make, always looking for an angle to benefit himself. 她指责他追求私利，总是寻找对自己有利的方面。


近义词：ambitious, opportunistic, grasping, scheming,  ruthless

反义词：unambitious, altruistic, selfless, honest


词源:  The phrase originates from the early 20th century.  "On the make" originally referred to someone actively engaged in making something, such as a product or deal. Over time, its meaning shifted to describe someone aggressively pursuing personal gain, often implying a lack of scruples.  The "make" here refers to the act of making money or acquiring wealth and status.
