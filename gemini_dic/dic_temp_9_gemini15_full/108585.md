leaching:/ˈliːtʃɪŋ/ | n. v. | 淋溶；浸出；过滤；溶滤 | The process of dissolving and removing soluble substances from a material by the action of a solvent.  This often refers to the removal of nutrients from soil by water.  As a verb, it means to extract or remove something by this process. |例句：Excessive rainfall can cause leaching of nutrients from the soil. 大雨会造成土壤养分流失。

The leaching of heavy metals from the mine tailings contaminated the nearby river. 矿山尾矿中重金属的淋溶污染了附近的河流。


近义词：elutriation, percolation, extraction, lixiviation |反义词：accumulation, concentration |词性：n. v.

词源:  The word "leaching" comes from the Middle English word "lechen," meaning "to lick," referring to the way water seems to "lick" away soluble materials.  The connection is through the idea of slowly dissolving and removing substances.
