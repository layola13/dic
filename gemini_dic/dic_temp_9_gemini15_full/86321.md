It can refer to a direct borrowing, a calque (loan translation), or a stylistic feature reflecting Hebrew linguistic or cultural influence.  The term often implies a certain degree of unusualness or foreignness in the context of the receiving language. | The use of "Amen" in English is a common example of a Hebrewism.  His writing style is characterized by many subtle Hebrewisms. |  Near synonyms: Hebraism, Jewish idiom | Antonyms: None directly applicable | noun
