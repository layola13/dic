presension:/ˌpreˈsɛnʃən/| n.|预感，预知|A feeling that something is going to happen, especially something bad; premonition.|例句：She had a presension of disaster. 她有一种灾难将要发生的预感。|例句：A presension of impending doom hung in the air.  一股即将到来的厄运的预感笼罩着空气。|近义词：premonition, foreboding, apprehension|反义词：certainty, assurance|名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I have a presension about this deal. = 我对这笔交易有一种预感。
He often has presensions about the future. = 他常常对未来有预感。

一般过去时:
She had a presension that something was wrong. = 她预感到有什么不对劲。
They had presensions of failure before the exam. = 考试前他们预感到会失败。

现在完成时:
I have had several presensions this week. = 这周我已经有过几次预感了。
She has had a presension about her health for months. = 她几个月来一直对自己的健康有一种不好的预感。

过去完成时:
He had had a presension about the accident before it happened. = 事故发生前他就预感到会出事。
They had had presensions that the project would fail. = 他们预感到这个项目会失败。

将来时:
I will probably have a presension before the meeting. = 我可能在会议前会有预感。
She thinks she will have a presension of what will happen tomorrow. = 她认为她会预感到明天会发生什么。

虚拟语气:
If I had a presension, I would have acted differently. = 如果我有预感，我会采取不同的行动。
I wish I hadn't had that presension about the storm. = 我真希望我没有对这场风暴产生那种预感。

被动语态:
(Presension is rarely used in the passive voice because it describes a feeling, not an action performed on something.)

疑问句:
Do you have any presensions about the future? = 你对未来有任何预感吗？
Did he have a presension before the earthquake? = 地震前他有什么预感吗？

否定句:
I don't have any presensions about the outcome. = 我对结果没有任何预感。
She didn't have a presension that he was lying. = 她没有预感到他在撒谎。
