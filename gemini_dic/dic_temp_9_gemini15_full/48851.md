deforesting: /ˌdiːfɔːˈrɛstɪŋ/ | v. | 森林砍伐；采伐森林 | The act of clearing forests; the process of removing trees from a forested area.  It often involves large-scale removal of trees for purposes such as agriculture, logging, or urban development.  |例句：Deforesting the Amazon rainforest is causing irreversible damage to the ecosystem.  亚马逊雨林的砍伐正在对生态系统造成不可逆转的破坏。


The rapid deforesting of the Indonesian rainforests is a major contributor to climate change. 印度尼西亚雨林的快速砍伐是气候变化的主要因素之一。| 近义词：deforestation (n.), clearing, logging | 反义词：reforestation, afforestation | 动词

They are deforesting the land to make way for a new housing development. = 他们正在砍伐土地为新的住宅开发项目让路。

一般过去时:

现在完成时:

Humans have deforested vast areas of land across the globe. = 人类已经砍伐了全球大片土地。

过去完成时:
Before the arrival of the colonists, they had deforested a significant portion of the land. = 在殖民者到来之前，他们已经砍伐了很大一部分土地。
