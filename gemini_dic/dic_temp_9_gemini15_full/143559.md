outhire:/ˈaʊtˌhaɪər/| vt.| 外包；雇佣外部人员 | To hire from outside a company or organization; to contract out work to an external party.  |例句：We outhire the cleaning services instead of employing our own staff. 我们外包清洁服务，而不是雇佣我们自己的员工。
例句：The company decided to outhire the software development to a specialist firm. 公司决定将软件开发外包给一家专业公司。|近义词：contract out, subcontract, outsource |反义词：in-house, employ


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company outhires its IT support. = 公司将其IT支持外包。
They outhire freelance writers for their blog. = 他们为他们的博客雇佣自由撰稿人。

一般过去时:
We outhired a consultant to help with the project. = 我们雇佣了一位顾问来帮助这个项目。
The factory outhired the security services last year. = 工厂去年外包了安保服务。

现在完成时:
They have outhired several marketing agencies this year. = 他们今年已经外包了几家营销公司。
The firm has outhired most of its manufacturing processes. = 这家公司已经将其大部分制造流程外包。

过去完成时:
They had outhired a cleaning crew before the big event. = 他们在大型活动之前已经外包了一支清洁队伍。
The company had outhired the design work by the deadline. = 公司在截止日期前已经外包了设计工作。

将来时:

虚拟语气:
If we needed more staff, we would outhire temporary workers. = 如果我们需要更多员工，我们会外包临时工。
It would have been better if we had outhired a professional. = 如果我们外包给一个专业人士就好了。
