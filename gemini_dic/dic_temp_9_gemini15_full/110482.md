lily-livered:/ˈlɪliˌlɪvərd/| adj. |胆小的，懦弱的| cowardly; lacking courage; faint-hearted.  The term refers to someone who is pale and weak, suggesting a lack of courage.  It is a compound word formed from "lily," referring to the pale color of a lily, and "livered," which historically referred to the liver as the seat of courage (a now-disproven belief). |例句：He was too lily-livered to stand up to the bully. 他太胆小了，不敢跟那个恶霸对抗。

He showed a lily-livered response to the challenge. 他对挑战表现出胆怯的反应。


近义词：timid, cowardly, faint-hearted, pusillanimous, craven|反义词：brave, courageous, bold, valiant, intrepid

词性:形容词
