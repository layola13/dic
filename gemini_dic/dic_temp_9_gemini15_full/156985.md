pillars:/ˈpɪlərz/| n.|柱子，支柱，栋梁|A pillar is a strong upright support, typically one of a number of such supports in a building or other structure.  It can also refer to something that is essential or fundamental to a system or structure.|例句：The ancient temple was supported by massive stone pillars. 这座古老的寺庙由巨大的石柱支撑。|例句：Education is one of the pillars of a strong society. 教育是强大社会支柱之一。|近义词：column, post, support, mainstay|反义词：weakness, foundation (in the sense of something lacking support)

词源:
The word "pillar" comes from the Old French word "pilere," which ultimately derives from the Latin word "pila," meaning "pier" or "post."  The evolution reflects the consistent use of upright supports in architecture throughout history.
