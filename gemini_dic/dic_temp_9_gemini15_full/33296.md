chubbier:/ˈtʃʌbiər/| adj.|较胖的，较丰满的|More plump or fleshy; slightly chubby.  It describes someone or something that is slightly thicker or fuller than usual, often in a pleasing or endearing way.|例句：The baby's chubbier cheeks made him look adorable.  婴儿胖乎乎的脸蛋让他看起来很可爱。

The cat, after a winter of plentiful food, has become considerably chubbier.  那只猫在吃了整个冬天的丰盛食物后，变得相当胖乎乎的。|近义词：plumper, fuller, rounder, stockier|反义词：thinner, leaner, slimmer


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He is chubbier than he was last year. = 他比去年胖了。
She seems chubbier in this dress. = 她穿这件衣服看起来更丰满了。

一般过去时:

He looked chubbier in that photo. = 他在那张照片里看起来更胖了。

现在完成时:

She hasn't gotten any chubbier, despite eating a lot of cake. = 尽管吃了很多蛋糕，她并没有变胖。


过去完成时:
He had been chubbier before he started his diet. = 在他开始节食之前，他更胖。
The dog had become chubbier since the last time I saw it. = 自从我上次见到它以来，那条狗变胖了。


将来时:
He will be chubbier if he continues to eat so much. = 如果他继续吃这么多，他会更胖。
The kitten will probably be chubbier by next month. = 到下个月小猫可能会更胖。


虚拟语气:

I wish I were chubbier; I'm too thin. = 我希望我胖一些；我太瘦了。(愿望)


被动语态:  (This adjective doesn't typically take a passive voice.)

否定句:

PHRASE->

词源:

From *chubby* + *-er*, the comparative suffix.  "Chubby" itself is of uncertain origin, possibly from an alteration of *chob*, an obsolete word for "a blow," suggesting roundness or fullness from being struck.
