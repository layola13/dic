hallucinates:/həˈluːsɪneɪts/| v.| 产生幻觉；幻想 | To perceive things that are not actually present.  This often involves the senses, such as seeing, hearing, or feeling things that are not there.  Hallucinations can be caused by various factors, including mental illness, drug use, and sleep deprivation. | 例句：He hallucinates vivid colors and patterns when he takes the drug. 他服用这种药物时会产生鲜艳的色彩和图案的幻觉。

He hallucinates about his deceased wife often. 他经常幻想他已故的妻子。


近义词：fantasizes, dreams, imagines | 反义词：perceives accurately, sees clearly


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He hallucinates frequently. = 他经常产生幻觉。
She hallucinates vivid images. = 她产生鲜艳的图像的幻觉。

一般过去时:
He hallucinated after taking the medication. = 他服药后产生了幻觉。
She hallucinated monsters under her bed. = 她幻想床底下有怪物。

现在完成时:
He has hallucinated since his accident. = 自从他出事以来，他就一直有幻觉。
She has never hallucinated before. = 她以前从未有过幻觉。

过去完成时:
He had hallucinated for several days before seeking help. = 在寻求帮助之前，他已经产生幻觉好几天了。
She had hallucinated vivid colors before the seizure. = 在癫痫发作之前，她产生了鲜艳色彩的幻觉。


将来时:
He will likely hallucinate if he continues using the drug. = 如果他继续使用这种药物，他很可能会产生幻觉。
She will probably hallucinate again if she's sleep-deprived. = 如果她睡眠不足，她可能会再次产生幻觉。


虚拟语气:
If he were to take that drug, he might hallucinate. = 如果他服用那种药物，他可能会产生幻觉。
I wish I hadn't hallucinated that. = 我真希望我没有产生那种幻觉。

被动语态:
Vivid colors were hallucinated by the patient. = 病人产生了鲜艳色彩的幻觉。(被动语态在此处略显不自然，一般不用被动)
No hallucinations were reported. = 没有报告产生幻觉的情况。(被动语态更常用)


疑问句:
Does he hallucinate often? = 他经常产生幻觉吗？
Has she ever hallucinated before? = 她以前有过幻觉吗？

否定句:
He doesn't hallucinate regularly. = 他不是经常产生幻觉。
She didn't hallucinate during the surgery. = 手术期间她没有产生幻觉。
