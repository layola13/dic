glycerate:/ˈɡlɪsəræt/| n.|甘油酸盐；甘油酯|A salt or ester of glyceric acid. Glycerates are compounds formed when glyceric acid reacts with a base or alcohol, resulting in the replacement of a hydrogen atom with a metal cation or an alkyl group.  They are used in various applications, including in chemistry and biochemistry.|Example:  Sodium glycerate is a common glycerate used in certain chemical processes. 钠甘油酸盐是某些化学过程中常用的甘油酸盐。|近义词:glyceride (partially)|反义词: N/A
