mockery:/ˈmɑːkəri/| n.|嘲笑；嘲弄；愚弄|The act of mocking or ridiculing someone or something; something that is worthy of ridicule or contempt.|例句：His attempts at humor were met with mockery. 他的幽默尝试遭到了嘲笑。|例句：The whole situation was a mockery of justice.  整个情况是对正义的嘲弄。|近义词：derision, ridicule, scorn, jeer|反义词：praise, admiration, respect


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He mocks his friends mercilessly. = 他无情地嘲笑他的朋友们。
The children make fun of the way he walks. = 孩子们取笑他走路的样子。


一般过去时:
They mocked his efforts to learn the language. = 他们嘲笑他学习这门语言的努力。
She ridiculed his ideas in front of everyone. = 她当着所有人的面嘲笑他的想法。


现在完成时:
They have mocked him repeatedly for his mistakes. = 他们一再嘲笑他的错误。
She has been the subject of much mockery online. = 她一直在网上受到很多嘲笑。


过去完成时:
The audience had already begun to mock the performer before he even started. = 在表演者甚至开始表演之前，观众就已经开始嘲笑他了。
They had ridiculed his proposal long before the meeting. = 在会议之前很久，他们就嘲笑了他的提议。


将来时:
They will mock him again if he tries that again. = 如果他再尝试这样做，他们还会嘲笑他。
The critics will surely mock her new film. = 批评家肯定会嘲笑她的新电影。


虚拟语气:
If he were to try again, they would mock him relentlessly. = 如果他再尝试，他们会无情地嘲笑他。
I wish they hadn't mocked my efforts so openly. = 我希望他们不要那么公开地嘲笑我的努力。


被动语态:
His efforts were mocked by his peers. = 他的努力受到了同龄人的嘲笑。
His ideas were held up to ridicule by the press. = 他的想法被媒体拿来嘲笑。


疑问句:
Why are they mocking him? = 他们为什么嘲笑他？
Is this situation a mockery of justice? = 这种情况是对正义的嘲弄吗？


否定句:
They didn't mock his attempts, they encouraged him. = 他们没有嘲笑他的尝试，而是鼓励了他。
She did not ridicule her students; instead, she helped them. = 她没有嘲笑她的学生们；相反，她帮助了他们。


PHRASE->
make a mockery of = 嘲弄；使…成为笑柄
hold something up to mockery = 嘲笑某事
become an object of mockery = 成为嘲笑的对象
subject to mockery = 遭受嘲笑
the mockery of justice = 对正义的嘲弄

词源:
The word "mockery" comes from the Old French word "moquerie," meaning "derision, mockery."  This, in turn, derives from the Old French verb "moquer," meaning "to mock, to jeer." The ultimate origin is likely a Germanic root.
