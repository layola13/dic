rearbitration:/ˌriːˈɑːrbɪˈtreɪʃən/| n. |复议；再仲裁|The process of submitting a dispute to a new arbitration panel after an initial arbitration has been completed.  This is typically done when there are concerns about the fairness or accuracy of the original arbitration process or its outcome.  Rearbitration is less common than appeals in judicial systems but can be provided for in certain contracts or arbitration agreements. |Example: After the initial arbitration ruling was deemed unsatisfactory, both parties agreed to pursue rearbitration.  复议结果令人不满后，双方同意进行复议。|近义词：rehearing, review, second arbitration|反义词：final judgment, definitive decision|名词
