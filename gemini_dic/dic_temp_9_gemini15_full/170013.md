protectionist: /prəˈtɛkʃənɪst/ | adj. | 保护主义的 | Relating to or supporting protectionism, the policy of protecting domestic industries from foreign competition, typically by imposing tariffs.  | Example:  The government implemented protectionist trade policies to safeguard its struggling textile industry.  政府实施了保护主义贸易政策以保护其苦苦挣扎的纺织业。 | 近义词: protectionary, restrictive | 反义词: free-trade, liberal

词源:
protectionist is derived from "protection" (the act of protecting) + "-ist" (a suffix indicating a person or thing associated with a particular activity or belief).  The root "protect" comes from the Old French "protegier" and ultimately from the Latin "protegere" (to cover, to protect).
