maimed:/meɪmd/ | v. |使残废；使受伤；损害 | To injure severely, especially by cutting or breaking; to inflict serious damage on. |例句：The accident maimed him for life.  事故使他终身残废。|例句：The storm maimed the landscape. 暴风雨破坏了这片风景。|近义词：cripple, disable, injure, wound |反义词：heal, cure


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He maims animals for fun. = 他以伤害动物为乐。
The war maims many innocent people. = 这场战争使许多无辜的人致残。


一般过去时:
The explosion maimed several bystanders. = 爆炸使几名旁观者受伤。
The soldier was maimed in battle. = 这名士兵在战斗中受了重伤。


现在完成时:
The accident has maimed him permanently. = 这起事故使他永久性残疾。
Years of hard work have maimed his hands. = 多年的辛勤工作使他的双手受损。


过去完成时:
He had been maimed in a previous accident. = 他在之前的意外事故中受过伤。
Before the surgery, she had already been maimed by the disease. = 在手术之前，她已经被疾病折磨得伤痕累累。


将来时:
If he falls, he will be maimed. = 如果他摔倒，他会受伤。
The careless driving may maim someone. = 粗心驾驶可能会伤害到别人。


虚拟语气:
If he had been more careful, he wouldn't have been maimed. = 如果他更小心一点，就不会受伤了。
I wish I hadn't maimed that bird. = 我真希望我没有伤害那只鸟。


被动语态:
Many soldiers were maimed in the fighting. = 许多士兵在战斗中受伤。
His reputation was maimed by the scandal. = 他的名声因丑闻而受损。


疑问句:
Were any civilians maimed in the attack? = 袭击中是否有平民受伤？

否定句:
The surgery didn't maim him. = 手术没有使他残废。
