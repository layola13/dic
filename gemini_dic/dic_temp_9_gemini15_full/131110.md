nonbindingly:/ˌnɒnˈbaɪndɪŋli/ | adv. | 不具约束力地，非约束性地 |  The agreement was reached nonbindingly, meaning it's not legally enforceable. | 近义词：tentatively, informally | 反义词：bindingly, conclusively


例句：

The preliminary discussions were conducted nonbindingly.  =  初步讨论是非约束性的。

They agreed nonbindingly to explore the possibility of a merger. = 他们非约束性地同意探讨合并的可能性。


词源:

* **non-**:  prefix meaning "not" or "opposite of"
* **binding**:  present participle of the verb "to bind," meaning to obligate or constrain.  It derives from Old English *bindan*, meaning "to bind".
* **-ly**: adverbial suffix


英文解析:

The adverb "nonbindingly" describes an action or agreement that is not legally or formally binding.  It signifies that the involved parties are not obligated to adhere to the terms of the agreement.  It suggests a preliminary or exploratory stage, where the parties are free to change their minds or negotiate further.
