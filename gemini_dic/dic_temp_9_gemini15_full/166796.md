prespecification:/ˌpriːˌspesɪfɪˈkeɪʃən/| n. |预先规定，预先说明|The act of specifying something in advance; a detailed description or specification made before something else is done.  It often involves setting parameters or conditions before a process begins, ensuring a certain outcome or preventing undesirable ones.|Example: The prespecification of the experimental design was crucial to the validity of the results. 预先规定实验设计对结果的有效性至关重要。|近义词：predetermination, prior specification, advance planning|反义词：improvisation, spontaneity|名词
