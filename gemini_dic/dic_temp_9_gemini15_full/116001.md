marshals: /ˈmɑːrʃəl/ | n. v. | 司法警员；元帅；整理；引导 |  A marshal is a person who is in charge of organizing or controlling something, often an event or a group of people.  As a verb, it means to arrange or control something in an organized way. |

例句：
The police marshalled the crowds after the concert.  警察在音乐会后疏导人群。

The chief marshal led the parade.  总指挥带队游行。


近义词：officer, usher, arrange, organize
反义词：disorganize, disarrange


词性：名词和动词


词源：源自古法语 *maréchal，意思是“马夫”，后来演变为军事领导者的含义，最终演变为现代英语中的“marshal”。
