command:/kəˈmænd/| /kəˈmɑːnd/| n. vt.|命令，指令；掌握，控制；需求，需要；海军舰队| The act of ordering someone to do something; authority;  mastery or control;  demand; a body of naval forces.|例句：The captain gave the command to fire.  船长下令开火。|例句：She has a good command of English. 她精通英语。|近义词：order, instruction, control, mastery|反义词：obedience, request, plea


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He commands the troops. = 他指挥军队。
The general commands respect. =这位将军令人敬畏。


一般过去时:
The officer commanded silence. = 军官命令保持安静。
She commanded the ship skillfully. = 她熟练地指挥着船只。


现在完成时:
The general has commanded many battles. =这位将军指挥过许多战役。
She has commanded the company for ten years. =她掌管这家公司十年了。


过去完成时:
He had commanded the regiment before the war. =他在战争前指挥过这个团。
They had commanded the resources effectively. =他们有效地指挥了资源。


将来时:
The captain will command the ship on its maiden voyage. =船长将在首航中指挥这艘船。
She will command the orchestra next season. =她将在下个季度指挥乐团。


虚拟语气:
If he commanded, they would obey. =如果他下令，他们就会服从。
I wish he commanded more effectively. =我希望他能更有效地指挥。


被动语态:
The troops were commanded by a seasoned general. =军队由一位经验丰富的将军指挥。
The ship was commanded from the bridge. =船是从桥上指挥的。


疑问句:
Who commanded the troops? = 谁指挥军队？
What did she command the soldiers to do? = 她命令士兵做什么？


否定句:
He did not command the respect of his men. = 他没有得到士兵们的尊敬。
She did not command the army during the war. = 战争期间她没有指挥军队。


PHRASE->
in command = 指挥；控制
take command = 掌握指挥权
command performance = 特别演出；应邀演出
command attention = 引人注目
