preexplodes:/ˌpriːɪkˈspləʊdz/| v.| 预先爆炸；提前爆炸 |  A verb formed by combining "pre-" (before) and "explodes" (to burst violently). It describes an explosion that occurs earlier than expected or planned.  | 例句：The bomb preexploded, causing less damage than anticipated. = 炸弹提前爆炸了，造成的破坏比预期的要小。
The experiment preexploded, ruining the data. = 实验提前爆炸了，毁掉了数据。 | 近义词：detonates prematurely, explodes early | 反义词：explodes late, detonates as planned
