philologists: /fɪˈlɒlədʒɪsts/ | n. |语言学家们| Scholars who study the history and development of languages.  Philologists analyze the structure, sounds, and meaning of words, tracing their origins and evolution over time. They often work with ancient texts and languages, seeking to understand how languages have changed and how they are related to each other.  |  The philologists presented their findings on the ancient dialect at the conference. 语言学家们在会议上展示了他们对古代方言的研究成果。| linguists, etymologists |  (No direct antonym, as it's a field of study) | 名词
