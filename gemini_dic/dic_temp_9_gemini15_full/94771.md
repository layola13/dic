imprudent:/ɪmˈpruːdənt/| adj.|轻率的，不谨慎的，鲁莽的|Not showing care for the consequences of an action; rash.  It implies a lack of wisdom or good judgment, often leading to negative outcomes. |例句：It was imprudent of him to invest all his money in one company.  他把所有钱都投资到一家公司，这是轻率的。

例句：Her imprudent spending habits led to significant debt. 她不谨慎的消费习惯导致了巨额债务。


近义词：reckless, rash, foolish, unwise, careless, thoughtless

反义词：prudent, cautious, careful, wise, judicious


词源：The word "imprudent" comes from the Old French word "imprudent," which itself derives from the Latin word "imprudens," meaning "unwise" or "lacking foresight."  The prefix "im-" means "not," and "prudent" comes from the Latin "prudens," meaning "foreseeing," "careful," or "wise."
