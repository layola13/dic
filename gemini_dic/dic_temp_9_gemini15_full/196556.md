sesquiplicate:/ˌsɛskwɪˈplɪkeɪt/| adj.| 一倍半的；三倍的 |  Meaning:  Relating to or denoting a ratio of 1.5 to 1 or 3 to 2;  one and a half times as much. It's derived from the Latin "sesqui-" (one and a half) and "plicare" (to fold).  This refers to a geometrical increase.  | Example:  The sesquiplicate ratio of 4 is 6 (4 x 1.5 = 6).  A recipe calls for a sesquiplicate amount of sugar to flour. | Near Synonyms:  one and a half times, 150%,  three-halves | Antonyms:  half, double, halved
