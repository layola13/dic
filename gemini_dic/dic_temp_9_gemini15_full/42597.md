counterproductively:/ˌkaʊntərprəˈdʌktɪvli/| adv.|适得其反地，事与愿违地 | In a way that produces a result that is the opposite of what was intended.  | 例句：He tried to help, but his actions were counterproductively resulting in more chaos. 他试图帮忙，但他的行为适得其反，导致更大的混乱。  | 例句：The company's new policy, intended to boost morale, has counterproductively led to widespread dissatisfaction. 公司旨在提高士气的新的政策，适得其反地导致了普遍的不满。|近义词：ineffectively, self-defeatingly, fruitlessly |反义词：productively, effectively |副词


USAGE->
There is no verb conjugation for adverbs. Adverbs modify verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs.  Counterproductively describes *how* an action or process occurs.

Example sentences showing the use of "counterproductively" in different contexts:

Simple Sentence:
They argued counterproductively, achieving nothing. = 他们争论不休，结果什么也没取得。

Complex Sentence:
By trying to control every detail, she counterproductively hindered the project's progress. = 她试图控制每一个细节，结果适得其反地阻碍了项目的进展。
