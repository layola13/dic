inuk:/ˈɪnuːk/| n. |因纽特人|A person belonging to any of the indigenous peoples inhabiting the Arctic regions of North America, Greenland, and Siberia.|Example:  Many Inuit communities rely on traditional hunting practices for sustenance.  许多因纽特人社区依靠传统的狩猎活动维持生计。|近义词：Inupiaq, Yupik (these are specific Inuit subgroups)|反义词：None
