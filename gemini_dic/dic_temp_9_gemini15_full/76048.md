furloughing:/ˈfɜːrloʊɪŋ/| v.|休假；暂时解雇|The act of granting employees a leave of absence, often without pay, typically due to economic downturn or operational needs.  It can also refer to the temporary suspension of an employee's job.|例句：The company is furloughing employees to cut costs. 公司正在裁员以削减成本。|例句：Due to the pandemic, many workers were furloughed. 由于疫情，许多工人被暂时解雇。|近义词：layoff, suspension, leave of absence|反义词：rehiring, reinstatement

词源:
The word "furlough" ultimately derives from the Dutch word "verlof," meaning "leave" or "permission."  It entered English via the French word "fourlo," and initially referred specifically to a soldier's leave of absence.  Over time, its usage broadened to encompass civilian employees as well.
