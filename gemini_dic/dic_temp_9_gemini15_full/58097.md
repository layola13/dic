drop-out:/ˈdrɒpˌaʊt/ /ˈdrɑːpˌaʊt/| n. v.|辍学者；退学者；退出；辍学；放弃|

英文解析:A person who leaves a school or other institution before completing their studies; the act of leaving a school or other institution before completing one's studies.  It can also refer to the act of abandoning or withdrawing from something.

例句:
He was a drop-out from college. 他是大学辍学生。

She decided to drop out of the race. 她决定退出比赛。


近义词: quitter, deserter, defector, withdrawal

反义词: graduate, completion, persistence


词性: noun, verb


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He drops out of school every year. 他每年都辍学。
They drop out of the competition. 他们退出比赛。


一般过去时:
She dropped out of college last semester. 她上学期大学辍学了。
He dropped out of the marathon. 他退出了马拉松比赛。


现在完成时:
Many students have dropped out of school due to financial difficulties. 许多学生由于经济困难而辍学。
She has dropped out of several projects this year. 她今年已经退出了好几个项目。


过去完成时:
He had dropped out of high school before he turned eighteen. 他十八岁之前就高中辍学了。
They had dropped out of the running before the final round. 他们在决赛前就退出了比赛。


将来时:
I think he will drop out of the program. 我认为他会退出这个项目。
She will drop out of the competition if she gets injured. 如果她受伤了，她会退出比赛。


虚拟语气:
If I were him, I wouldn't drop out of university. 如果我是他，我不会大学辍学。
I wish I hadn't dropped out of school when I was younger. 我真希望我年轻的时候没有辍学。


被动语态:
The student was dropped out of the school for bad behavior.  这个学生因为行为不端被学校开除了。(注意：被动语态中，drop out 的含义略有不同)

疑问句:

否定句:
