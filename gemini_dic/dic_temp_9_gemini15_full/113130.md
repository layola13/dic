lungsful: /ˈlʌŋzˌfʊl/ | n. | 一肺容量的空气；一大口（气）| A lungsful is the amount of air that can be held in the lungs at one time.  It's often used figuratively to describe a large breath or gasp.  The word is less common than "breath" or "gulp," but it emphasizes the capacity of the lungs. | He took a deep lungsful of fresh mountain air. 他深深地吸了一口清新的山间空气。 She gasped, taking a lungsful of air. 她倒吸了一口气。 | breath, gulp |  (No direct antonym, but "small breath" could be considered an opposite in meaning)| 名词
