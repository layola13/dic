overorchestrating:/ˌoʊvərˈɔːrkɪstreɪtɪŋ/| v.|过度编排；过度安排；过分操纵|The act of arranging or controlling something to an excessive degree, often resulting in a lack of spontaneity or naturalness.  It implies too much control and planning, potentially stifling creativity or natural development. |例句：He overorchestrated the presentation, losing the audience's interest in the details. 他对演示进行了过度编排，导致观众对细节失去了兴趣。

例句：The director overorchestrated the film, resulting in a stiff and unnatural feel.  导演对这部电影进行了过度编排，导致影片感觉僵硬不自然。


近义词：overmanage, overplan, micromanage, overcontrol


反义词：underplan, laissez-faire, improvise
