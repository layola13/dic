co-head:/ˈkoʊˌhed/| n.|联合主管，共同负责人|A co-head is a person who shares the leadership or responsibility of a department, project, or organization with another person.|例句：They appointed two co-heads to oversee the merger.  他们任命了两位联合主管来监督合并。|例句：The film credits listed two co-heads of the photography department. 电影字幕列出了摄影部门的两位联合主管。|近义词：joint head, co-leader |反义词：sole head, single leader

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The two co-heads manage the department effectively. = 两位联合主管有效地管理着部门。
He and she co-head the marketing team. = 他和她共同领导营销团队。

一般过去时:
They co-headed the project for three years. = 他们共同领导了这个项目三年。
The two co-heads successfully completed the merger. = 两位联合主管成功地完成了合并。

现在完成时:
She has co-headed the research team since 2018. = 自2018年以来，她一直是研究团队的联合主管。
They have co-headed the company for over a decade. = 他们共同领导这家公司超过十年了。

过去完成时:
Before the promotion, he had co-headed a smaller department. = 在晋升之前，他曾是较小部门的联合主管。
They had co-headed the fundraising campaign for six months. = 他们共同领导了六个月的筹款活动。

将来时:
She will co-head the new initiative next year. = 她明年将共同领导这项新倡议。
They will co-head the department after the retirement of the current manager. =  现任经理退休后，他们将共同领导该部门。

虚拟语气:
If they had co-headed the project earlier, it might have been more successful. = 如果他们早些共同领导这个项目，它可能会更成功。
I wish I had co-headed that research team. = 我真希望我当时是那个研究团队的联合主管。

被动语态:

The project is being co-headed by a team of experts. = 该项目正在由一个专家团队共同领导。

疑问句:

Does he still co-head the design team? = 他还是设计团队的联合主管吗？

否定句:
He does not co-head the finance department. = 他不是财务部门的联合主管。
They did not co-head the project initially. = 他们最初并没有共同领导这个项目。
