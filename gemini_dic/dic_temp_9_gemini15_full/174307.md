quibbled:/ˈkwɪbəld/| v.|吹毛求疵，挑剔，争论细枝末节|To argue or complain about small, unimportant things.  To find fault with something in a petty way.|例句：He quibbled over the price of the car. 他对汽车的价格斤斤计较。

例句：She quibbled about the wording of the contract. 她对合同措辞吹毛求疵。


近义词：cavil, carp, nitpick, quibble


反义词：accept, agree, concede


词性:动词

词源:
The word "quibble" ultimately derives from the Middle Dutch word "kwibbelen," meaning "to wrangle, to bicker."  This, in turn, is related to other Germanic words suggesting the idea of  small, sharp movements or sounds, hinting at the back-and-forth nature of a petty argument.
