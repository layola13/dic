pre-rehearsal:/ˌpriːrɪˈhɜːrsəl/| n. |彩排前的准备；预演 | A period of preparation or practice before a rehearsal, often involving individual preparation or smaller group work. It's a less formal, more preparatory phase than a full rehearsal.  |例句：The actors spent the morning in pre-rehearsal, working on their individual lines and blocking.  演员们上午都在进行彩排前的准备，练习他们的台词和走位。|近义词：preparation, practice, warm-up |反义词：performance, show
