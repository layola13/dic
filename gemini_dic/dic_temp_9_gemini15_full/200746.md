slightly:/ˈslaɪtli/ | adv. | 轻微地；稍微 |  A little; to a small degree.  The word implies a subtle or insignificant change or difference. | 例句：The weather is slightly cooler today. 今天天气稍微凉爽一些。

The soup is slightly salty. 这汤有点咸。


近义词：somewhat, a little, barely, faintly, marginally


反义词：significantly, greatly, considerably, substantially


词源:  The word "slightly" comes from the Old English word "slīhtlice," meaning "skillfully, cleverly, subtly."  Over time, the meaning shifted to emphasize the small degree of something rather than the skill involved.  The modern spelling evolved through Middle English.
