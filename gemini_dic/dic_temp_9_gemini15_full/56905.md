dot-comers:/ˈdɑːtˌkʌmər/| n. |网络公司员工，网络创业者|Individuals who joined the workforce during the dot-com boom of the late 1990s.  They often worked for internet startups and were known for their youthful enthusiasm and sometimes unrealistic expectations.|例句：Many dot-comers experienced both rapid success and devastating failure during the dot-com bubble.  许多网络公司员工在网络泡沫时期经历了快速成功和毁灭性失败。|近义词：internet entrepreneur, tech entrepreneur, web worker|反义词：brick-and-mortar worker (traditional business employee)

USAGE->

The dot-comer works long hours. = 网络公司员工工作时间很长。
She develops innovative software. = 她开发创新软件。


一般过去时:
