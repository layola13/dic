calumnize:/ˈkæləmnaɪz/| vt.|诽谤；中伤|To make false and defamatory statements about someone; to slander.|例句：He was calumnized by his rivals in the election.  他被竞选对手诽谤了。|例句：The newspaper article calumnized the mayor, accusing him of corruption. 这篇报导诽谤了市长，指控他腐败。|近义词：slander, defame, vilify, smear|反义词：praise, commend, extol


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They calumnize their opponents relentlessly. = 他们无情地诽谤他们的对手。
She calumnizes anyone who disagrees with her. = 她诽谤任何与她意见相左的人。

一般过去时:
The politician was calumnized in the media. = 这位政治家在媒体上被诽谤了。
He calumnized his former friend to get revenge. = 他诽谤他以前的友人以报复。

现在完成时:
They have calumnized him for years. = 他们多年来一直诽谤他。
The company has been calumnized by its competitors. = 这家公司一直被竞争对手诽谤。

过去完成时:
The rumors had calumnized her reputation before the trial. = 在审判之前，谣言已经诽谤了她的名誉。
He had calumnized her to the boss before she even arrived. = 甚至在她到达之前，他就已经向老板诽谤她了。

将来时:
They will calumnize him again if he speaks out. = 如果他大胆说出真相，他们将会再次诽谤他。
She will likely be calumnized by the press after the announcement. = 宣布之后，她很可能会被媒体诽谤。

虚拟语气:
If he calumnized her, I would sue him. = 如果他诽谤了她，我会起诉他。
I wish they hadn't calumnized my family. = 我希望他们没有诽谤我的家人。

被动语态:
He was calumnized in the press for his views. = 他因为自己的观点而在媒体上遭到诽谤。
The candidate was calumnized by his opponents. = 候选人被对手诽谤了。

疑问句:
Were they calumnized unjustly? = 他们是否被不公平地诽谤了？
Has she ever been calumnized before? = 她以前是否曾被诽谤过？

否定句:
They did not calumnize her, despite the accusations. = 尽管有指控，他们并没有诽谤她。
He would never calumnize his friends. = 他永远不会诽谤他的朋友。


PHRASE->
calumnize someone's character = 诽谤某人的品格
calumnize someone's reputation = 诽谤某人的名誉
be calumnized by the media = 被媒体诽谤
publicly calumnize someone = 公开诽谤某人
ruthlessly calumnize = 无情地诽谤


词源:
源于拉丁语 *calumnia，意为“false accusation”（虚假指控）。
