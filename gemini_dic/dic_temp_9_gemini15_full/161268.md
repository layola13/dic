postmodernists:/ˌpoʊstˈmɒdərnɪsts/ | n. | 后现代主义者 |  Advocates or practitioners of postmodernism, a complex and multifaceted intellectual movement that emerged in the late 20th century.  Postmodernists question grand narratives, objective truth, and traditional hierarchies, often embracing irony, fragmentation, and intertextuality in their work. | Example: Many postmodernists reject the idea of a single, universal truth.  许多后现代主义者拒绝单一普遍真理的观念。 | 近义词：postmodern thinkers, deconstructionists (in some contexts) | 反义词：modernists, traditionalists
