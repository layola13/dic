ruffle:/ˈrʌfəl/| n. v.|弄皱；褶边；激怒|

英文解析:
As a noun, "ruffle" refers to a gathered or pleated strip of fabric used as a trimming or decoration. It can also mean a state of disorder or agitation.  As a verb, "ruffle" means to make something wrinkled or uneven, or to disturb the smoothness or calmness of something or someone.

例句:
The lace ruffle on her dress was exquisitely delicate.  她的裙子上的蕾丝褶边非常精致。

The news ruffled his composure.  这个消息扰乱了他的平静。


近义词:
(n.) frill, flounce, crimp, wrinkle, disturbance, agitation
(v.) wrinkle, crumple, disturb, irritate, upset, discompose


反义词:
(n.) smoothness, calm, order
(v.) smooth, calm, soothe


词性:
Noun, Verb


词源:
Middle English: from Old French *rufler ‘to wrinkle’, of uncertain origin.
