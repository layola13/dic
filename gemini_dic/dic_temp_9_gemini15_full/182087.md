remilitarizing: /ˌriːˈmɪlɪtəraɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 再军事化；重新军事化 | The act of restoring military control or presence to an area or country that had been demilitarized.  It involves the reintroduction of military forces, equipment, and infrastructure. | 例句：The country is remilitarizing its borders after years of peace. =  这个国家在经历了多年的和平之后正在重新军事化其边界。

The government's decision to remilitarize the islands has sparked international controversy. = 政府决定对这些岛屿进行再军事化引发了国际争议。


近义词：rearm, fortify, militarize (again) | 反义词：demilitarize


USAGE->

They are remilitarizing the occupied territories. = 他们正在对被占领的地区进行再军事化。

词源:
re- (prefix indicating again) + militarize (to make military)  The word "militarize" itself comes from "military" + "-ize" (a suffix forming verbs).  "Military" originates from the Latin word "militaris," meaning "of or relating to soldiers or the army."
