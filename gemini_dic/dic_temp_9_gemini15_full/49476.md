delusional:/dɪˈluʒənəl/| adj.| 妄想的；痴迷的；虚幻的 | characterized by or holding strongly to false beliefs; relating to or caused by a delusion.  A delusional person believes things that are not true, often despite evidence to the contrary.  This can manifest in various ways, from believing one has special powers to believing one is being persecuted. |例句：His claims are completely delusional; there's no evidence to support them. 他的说法完全是妄想；没有证据支持他的说法。

例句：She's become increasingly delusional since the accident, believing she's a famous actress. 自从事故发生后，她变得越来越妄想，认为自己是一位著名的女演员。


近义词：fantastical, unreal, imaginary,  insane, psychotic

反义词：rational, sane, realistic, grounded

词源:
The word "delusional" is derived from the Latin word "deludere," meaning "to deceive" or "to mock."  The suffix "-ional" indicates an adjective form.  The concept of delusion itself relates to a deception of the mind, a false belief held despite evidence to the contrary.
