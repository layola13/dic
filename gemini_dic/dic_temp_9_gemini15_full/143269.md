outdistancing: /ˌaʊtˈdɪstənsɪŋ/ | v. |超越，超过，胜过 | To go faster than and leave behind (someone or something).  This implies a significant lead gained over time, not just a momentary advantage. |例句：The marathon runner outdistanced all her competitors.  这位马拉松运动员超越了所有竞争对手。

The company's innovative strategies outdistanced its rivals in the market. 这家公司创新的策略使其在市场上超越了竞争对手。|近义词：surpass, exceed, overtake, outstrip |反义词：lag behind, fall behind


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He outdistances his opponents in every race. = 他在每场比赛中都超越了他的对手。
The company outdistances its competitors in terms of innovation. = 在创新方面，该公司超越了竞争对手。


一般过去时:
She outdistanced the other cyclists in the final sprint. = 她在最后的冲刺中超越了其他自行车选手。
Our team outdistanced the opposition and won the championship. = 我们的团队超越了对手，赢得了冠军。


现在完成时:

The new technology has outdistanced the older models in terms of efficiency. = 新技术在效率方面已经超越了旧型号。


过去完成时:

将来时:

虚拟语气:
