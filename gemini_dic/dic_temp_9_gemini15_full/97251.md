inflexion:/ɪnˈflekʃən/| n. |弯曲；音调变化；词形变化|The change in pitch or tone of the voice.  A change in the form of a word (typically the ending) to express a grammatical function or attribute such as tense, mood, person, number, case, and gender. |例句：The inflexion of his voice suggested doubt. 他声音的音调变化暗示着怀疑。

例句：The verb "walk" shows inflexion for tense (walk, walked, walking). 动词“walk”在时态上表现出词形变化（walk, walked, walking）。


近义词：modulation, inflection, bending, curvature |反义词：straightness, directness

词源:  From Middle English *inflexioun, from Old French inflexion, from Latin inflexio, from inflexere "to bend inward," from in- "in" + flectere "to bend."
