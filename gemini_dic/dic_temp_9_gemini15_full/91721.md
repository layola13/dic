hypercriticism:/ˌhaɪpərˈkrɪtɪsɪzəm/| n. |吹毛求疵；过度批评；苛刻的批评 |Excessively harsh or severe criticism; the practice of making criticisms that are too severe or detailed.  It often involves focusing on minor flaws or imperfections while ignoring the overall merits. |例句：His hypercriticism of the artwork prevented others from appreciating its beauty.  他的作品受到了过度苛刻的批评，以至于其他人无法欣赏它的美。

例句：The professor's hypercriticism discouraged the students from participating in class discussions. 教授的过度批评让学生们不敢在课堂上参与讨论。|近义词：faultfinding, carping, nitpicking, overcriticism |反义词：praise, commendation, approval
