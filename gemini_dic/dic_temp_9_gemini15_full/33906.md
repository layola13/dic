circumvention:/ˌsɜːrkəmˈvɛnʃən/ | n. |规避；绕过；回避 | The act of circumventing or avoiding something, especially a rule, restriction, or difficulty.  It often implies cleverly finding a way around something rather than directly confronting it. | Example: The company found a way to circumvent the new regulations. 公司找到了一种方法来规避新的规章制度。|近义词：evasion, avoidance, bypass |反义词：confrontation, compliance

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He circumvents the problem by using a different approach. 他通过使用不同的方法来规避这个问题。
They circumvent the rules regularly. 他们经常规避规则。

一般过去时:
She circumvented the traffic jam by taking a back road. 她通过走一条小路绕过了交通堵塞。
The thief circumvented the security system. 小偷绕过了安全系统。

现在完成时:
They have circumvented the law for years. 他们多年来一直在规避法律。
We have circumvented many obstacles on our journey. 在我们的旅途中，我们已经克服了许多障碍。


过去完成时:
He had circumvented the issue before it became a major problem. 他在问题演变成大问题之前就避开了它。
They had circumvented the security measures before entering the building. 他们在进入大楼之前就绕过了安保措施。

将来时:
She will circumvent the issue by ignoring it. 她将通过忽视这个问题来回避它。
They will circumvent the blockade by using a different route. 他们将通过使用不同的路线来绕过封锁。

虚拟语气:
If I could circumvent the rules, I would. 如果我能规避规则，我会的。
I wish I could circumvent this problem easily. 我希望我能轻松地避开这个问题。

被动语态:
The problem was circumvented by a clever solution. 这个问题被一个巧妙的解决方案解决了。
The rules were circumvented by many people.  许多人规避了这些规则。

疑问句:
How did they circumvent the security system? 他们是如何绕过安全系统的？
Can we circumvent this obstacle? 我们能绕过这个障碍吗？

否定句:
He did not circumvent the issue; he addressed it directly. 他没有回避这个问题；他直接解决了它。
They could not circumvent the law; they were caught. 他们无法规避法律；他们被抓住了。
