fidge:/fɪdʒ/| v.|烦躁不安，坐立不安|To move or fidget restlessly; to be nervous or uneasy.|例句：He fidgeted in his seat, waiting for the interview to begin. 他在座位上坐立不安，等待面试开始。|例句：She fidgeted with her necklace, clearly nervous about the presentation. 她不停地摆弄项链，显然对演讲感到紧张。|近义词：fidget, squirm, wriggle, twitch|反义词：calm, still, relaxed|动词

一般现在时:
He often fidgets when he's nervous. = 他紧张的时候经常坐立不安。
The child fidgets constantly in class. = 这个孩子上课时总是坐立不安。

一般过去时:
She fidgeted throughout the entire meeting. = 她整个会议都坐立不安。
He fidgeted with the pen, unable to concentrate. = 他不停地玩弄钢笔，无法集中注意力。

现在完成时:
I have been fidgeting all morning. = 我整个上午都坐立不安。
She has fidgeted so much that she's worn a hole in her jeans. = 她坐立不安弄得牛仔裤磨了个洞。

过去完成时:
He had fidgeted for hours before the announcement. = 在宣布消息之前，他已经坐立不安了好几个小时。
She had fidgeted all day, waiting for the phone to ring. = 她等了一整天电话响，一直坐立不安。

将来时:
I think I'll be fidgeting during the long flight. = 我想在漫长的飞行中我会坐立不安。
He will probably fidget if he has to wait too long. = 如果他不得不等太久，他可能会坐立不安。

虚拟语气:
If I weren't so nervous, I wouldn't fidget so much. = 如果我不那么紧张，我就不会这么坐立不安了。
I wish I didn't fidget so much during presentations. = 我希望我在做演示的时候不要那么坐立不安。

被动语态:  (Fidget is rarely used in the passive voice)
*His nervousness was evident as he was seen fidgeting. = 他坐立不安，紧张的神情显而易见。(This uses "seen fidgeting" rather than a passive form of "fidget")
*His fidgeting was distracting to the other students. = 他坐立不安分散了其他学生的注意力。（This focuses on the *result* of fidgeting, not the action itself in passive voice）


疑问句:
Is he fidgeting again? = 他又在坐立不安了吗？
Why are you fidgeting so much? = 你为什么这么坐立不安？

否定句:
He didn't fidget during the speech. = 他在演讲期间没有坐立不安。
She tried not to fidget, but it was difficult. = 她努力不让自己坐立不安，但这很难。

词源:
The word "fidget" likely originated in the 16th century from the Middle English word "fyge," meaning "to strut about."  It's related to words suggesting a sense of restlessness or self-importance.  The modern form evolved through various spelling variations.
