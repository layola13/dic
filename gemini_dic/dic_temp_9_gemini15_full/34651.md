clepsydra: /ˈklɛpsɪdrə/ | n. | 水钟；沙漏 | An ancient device for measuring time, typically consisting of a vessel with a small opening through which water or sand flows at a regulated rate.  Clepsydras were used in ancient Greece and Egypt. | The museum displayed a fascinating collection of ancient clepsydras, showcasing different designs and sizes.  | hourglass, water clock |  (no direct antonym) | noun
