coryphodon:/ˈkɒrɪfədɒn/| n. |始祖象属|Coryphodon is an extinct genus of early Eocene mammals belonging to the order Pantodonta.  They were large, hippopotamus-like herbivores that thrived in North America, Europe, and Asia during the early Eocene epoch (approximately 56 to 50 million years ago).
