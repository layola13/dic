rejectors:/rɪˈdʒɛktərz/| n. |拒绝者，排斥者|Those who reject something; those who refuse to accept something.  People or things that reject or refuse to accept something. | The rejectors of the proposal voiced their concerns during the meeting.  提案的反对者在会议上表达了他们的担忧。|refusers, deniers, opponents|acceptors, supporters


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The committee rejects many applications each year. = 委员会每年都拒绝许多申请。
He rejects all forms of prejudice. = 他拒绝一切形式的偏见。

一般过去时:
She rejected his offer of marriage. = 她拒绝了他的求婚。
The company rejected his application for a job. = 公司拒绝了他的求职申请。

现在完成时:
They have rejected all our proposals. = 他们已经拒绝了我们所有的提议。
The government has rejected the new bill. = 政府否决了这项新法案。

过去完成时:
He had already rejected the idea before I could even suggest it. = 我还没来得及提出建议，他已经否决了这个想法。
They had rejected several candidates before finding the right person. = 在找到合适的人选之前，他们已经拒绝了好几个候选人。

将来时:
They will reject any attempt to compromise their principles. = 他们将拒绝任何试图损害他们原则的企图。
I suspect they will reject the plan outright. = 我怀疑他们会直接拒绝这个计划。

虚拟语气:
If they rejected the offer, we would have to find another solution. = 如果他们拒绝了这个提议，我们就得另想办法。
I wish they hadn't rejected my application. = 我真希望他们没有拒绝我的申请。

被动语态:

疑问句:
