rearrangements:/ˌriːəˈreɪndʒmənts/ | n. |重新安排；改组；调整 | The act or process of rearranging something, such as items, parts, or elements, into a new or different order or configuration. It often implies a change in structure or organization.  | Example: The rearrangements of the furniture made the room feel much larger. 重新安排家具使房间感觉大多了。


The company is undergoing major rearrangements to improve efficiency. 公司正在进行重大改组以提高效率。|近义词：reorganization, restructuring, reordering, adjustments |反义词：disorganization, disarrangement
