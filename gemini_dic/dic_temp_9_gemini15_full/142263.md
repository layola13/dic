orphanotrophism: /ˌɔːrfənəˈtroʊfɪzəm/ | n. |孤儿院生活；孤儿院制度 | The care and upbringing of orphans in an institution.  The term often carries connotations of the potential for negative impacts on the psychological development of children raised in such environments.  It refers to the system and conditions of orphanages, not simply the act of orphan care. | Example:  Studies on the long-term effects of orphanotrophism have revealed potential for emotional and social difficulties in adulthood.  研究孤儿院生活对成年后情绪和社会能力的长期影响的研究表明，成年后可能会出现情绪和社会方面的困难。| 近义词: institutionalization (of orphans), orphanage life, institutional care | 反义词: family upbringing, foster care, adoption |  词性: n.
