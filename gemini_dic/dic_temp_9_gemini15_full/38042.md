comporting:/kəmˈpɔːrtɪŋ/ | v. |举止，行为；相符；符合 | behaving in a particular way; agreeing or being consistent with something |例句：He was comporting himself with great dignity. 他举止非常端庄。  She comported herself with grace under pressure.她在压力下举止优雅。|近义词：behaving, conducting, conforming |反义词：misbehaving, behaving badly


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He comports himself well in public. = 他在公众场合举止得体。
They comport their business with efficiency. = 他们高效地处理业务。

一般过去时:
She comported herself with dignity at the funeral. = 她在葬礼上举止端庄。
He comported himself badly at the party. = 他在派对上的行为举止很糟糕。

现在完成时:
He has comported himself admirably throughout the crisis. = 在整个危机中，他的表现令人钦佩。
She has always comported herself with professionalism. = 她一直以专业的态度处事。

过去完成时:
He had comported himself impeccably until that incident. = 直到那件事发生之前，他的行为一直无可挑剔。
They had comported themselves respectfully throughout the ceremony. = 在整个仪式中，他们的举止一直很恭敬。

将来时:
He will comport himself appropriately at the interview. = 他将在面试中表现得体。
She will comport herself with confidence during the presentation. = 她将在演示中自信地展现自我。

虚拟语气:
If he comported himself better, people would respect him more. = 如果他行为举止更好，人们会更尊重他。
I wish she had comported herself more professionally. = 我希望她当时能更专业些。

被动语态:
His behavior was not comported with the company's code of conduct. = 他的行为与公司的行为准则不符。
The situation was comported with the utmost care. = 情况得到了最谨慎的处理。

疑问句:
How did he comport himself at the meeting? = 他在会议上表现如何？
Does she always comport herself so gracefully? = 她总是举止如此优雅吗？

否定句:
He didn't comport himself well under pressure. = 他在压力下表现不佳。
She did not comport herself appropriately at the formal dinner. = 她在正式晚宴上的举止不当。
