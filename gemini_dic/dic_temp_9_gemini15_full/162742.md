prebeneficiaries: /ˌpriːbɛnɪˈfɪʃəriːz/ | n. | 预受益人 |  Those who are expected to benefit from something in the future, especially a trust or inheritance, before the ultimate beneficiaries receive their share.  This term often applies to situations where assets are held in trust or are subject to a complex inheritance plan. |  The prebeneficiaries of the trust will receive annual distributions until the final beneficiary reaches the age of majority.  预受益人将每年获得分配，直到最终受益人达到法定年龄。 |  beneficiaries, heirs, legatees |  (No direct antonym, the opposite concept would be those who ultimately receive nothing.) | 名词
