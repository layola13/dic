falsifications:/ˌfɔːlsɪfɪˈkeɪʃənz/ | n. |伪造；伪证；窜改 | The act of falsifying something; the making of something false.  It refers to the creation of something false and deceptive, often to deceive or mislead. This can include forged documents, altered data, or fabricated evidence. |例句：The investigation uncovered numerous falsifications in the company's financial records.  调查发现了公司财务记录中大量的伪造行为。

The discovery of falsifications led to the cancellation of the election results. 伪造的发现导致选举结果被取消。


近义词：forgeries, fabrications, counterfeits, misrepresentations |反义词：truths, facts, verities
