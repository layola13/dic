pansied:/ˈpænzd/| adj.| 胆怯的；懦弱的；娘娘腔的 |Effeminate; timid; cowardly.  The word often carries a derogatory connotation, implying weakness or lack of masculinity.  It's used to describe someone who is perceived as lacking courage or exhibiting behavior considered unmanly.|例句：He was too pansied to stand up to the bully. 他太胆小了，不敢反抗那个恶霸。

例句：Don't be such a pansied; face your fears. 不要这么胆小；直面你的恐惧。


近义词：timid, cowardly, effeminate, weak, faint-hearted

反义词：brave, courageous, bold, manly, strong


词源:  The word derives from "pansy," a flower known for its delicate appearance.  The association of the flower with effeminacy led to the pejorative use of "pansied" to describe men exhibiting traits considered unmanly.  This usage is considered offensive by many.
