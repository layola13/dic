hos:/hɒz/| n.| 袜子 | A covering for the foot, typically made of knitted fabric. | 例句：My socks are wet. = 我的袜子湿了。| 例句：I need a new pair of socks. = 我需要一双新袜子。|近义词： stocking |反义词：shoes


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He wears socks every day. = 他每天都穿袜子。
She buys new socks every month. = 她每个月都买新袜子。

一般过去时:
I wore my favorite socks yesterday. = 我昨天穿了我最喜欢的袜子。
They lost their socks in the laundry. = 他们的袜子在洗衣房里弄丢了。

现在完成时:
I have worn these socks for a week. = 我已经穿了这双袜子一个星期了。
She has bought three pairs of socks this month. = 这个月她已经买了三双袜子。

过去完成时:
I had worn out my old socks before buying new ones. = 我在买新袜子之前已经把旧袜子穿破了。
They had lost their socks before they left for the trip. = 他们出发旅行之前就已经把袜子弄丢了。

将来时:
I will wear clean socks tomorrow. = 我明天会穿干净的袜子。
She will buy more socks next week. = 她下周会买更多的袜子。

虚拟语气:
If I had a pair of clean socks, I would wear them. = 如果我有一双干净的袜子，我会穿它们。
I wish I had more socks. = 我真希望我有更多袜子。

被动语态:
The socks were washed yesterday. = 袜子昨天洗了。
New socks are being sold at the store. = 商店正在卖新袜子。

疑问句:
Do you have any socks? = 你有袜子吗？
What color are your socks? = 你的袜子是什么颜色的？

否定句:
I don't have any clean socks. = 我没有干净的袜子。
She didn't wear socks to the party. = 她去参加派对没穿袜子。
