red-neck:/ˈrɛdˌnɛk/| n. adj.|乡巴佬；粗人；（美国南方）白人穷人|A redneck is a derogatory term for a white person, especially from the Southern United States, who is perceived as having low social status, poor education, and rural roots. The term often carries negative connotations of ignorance, prejudice, and backwardness.  It originally referred to the sunburn on the back of the neck caused by working outdoors. |例句：He was called a redneck for his rural background and simple lifestyle. 他因为他的农村背景和简单的生活方式而被称为乡巴佬。


例句：Some people consider the term "redneck" to be offensive and derogatory. 一些人认为“乡巴佬”这个词具有冒犯性和贬义。
