drawspring: /ˈdrɔːsprɪŋ/ | n. |拉簧，卷簧 | A spring that exerts force when drawn out.  It's typically a coiled spring, often made of wire, that stores energy when stretched and releases it when allowed to contract.  |例句：The drawspring mechanism in the clock ensures consistent ticking.  这个钟表中的拉簧机构确保了稳定的走时。

The drawspring in the toy car provides power for its movement. 玩具车里的拉簧为它的运动提供动力。|近义词：extension spring, tension spring |反义词：compression spring (反义词并非完全对立，而是功能相反) |名词
