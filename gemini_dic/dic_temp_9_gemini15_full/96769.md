inequality's:/ɪnɪˈkwɒlətiz/| n. |不平等的；不均等的 |The possessive form of "inequality," referring to something belonging to or associated with inequality.  It signifies the characteristics, effects, or aspects related to the state of being unequal.|例句：The inequality's impact on society is significant. 不平等对社会的影响是巨大的。|近义词：disparity's, unevenness's |反义词：equality's, fairness's |名词


USAGE->
This word is a possessive noun and doesn't have verb conjugations like the example word "what".  It describes something related to inequality.  Therefore, typical verb tenses are not applicable.  Instead, we can show its use in different sentence structures.


In a declarative sentence:
The inequality's root causes are complex. 不平等的根本原因很复杂。

In an interrogative sentence:
Are you aware of the inequality's pervasive nature? 你是否意识到不平等的普遍性？

In an exclamatory sentence:
The inequality's injustice is appalling! 不平等的不公正令人震惊！

In a sentence with a prepositional phrase:
Because of the inequality's persistence, many people suffer. 由于不平等的持续存在，许多人受苦。

In a complex sentence:
Although the inequality's effects are widespread, solutions are being explored. 尽管不平等的影响是广泛的，但正在探索解决方案。


PHRASE->
This word, being a possessive noun, doesn't typically form phrases in the same way as verbs or common nouns.  Its usage is generally within phrases describing aspects of inequality.  Examples are shown below, rather than typical phrasal verbs.

sources of inequality's persistence: 不平等持续存在的根源
effects of inequality's impact: 不平等影响的效应
measures to reduce inequality's severity: 减少不平等严重程度的措施
the inequality's devastating consequences: 不平等的毁灭性后果
understanding inequality's mechanisms: 理解不平等的机制


词源:

The word "inequality's" derives from the noun "inequality," which itself comes from the Old French "inequalite" (14th century).  This, in turn, is derived from the Latin "in-" (not) + "aequalitas" (equality), from "aequalis" (equal).  The suffix "-'s" indicates possession or association.
