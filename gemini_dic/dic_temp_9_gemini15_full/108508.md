layoff:/ˈleɪɔːf/ | n., v. | 裁员；解雇 | The termination of employment of a worker or workers, typically for economic reasons.  |例句：The company announced a layoff of 100 employees. 这家公司宣布裁员100人。

例句：Thousands of workers faced layoff during the recession.  经济衰退期间，成千上万的工人面临失业。


近义词：dismissal, redundancy, termination, job cut | 反义词：hiring, employment


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company lays off workers every year. = 公司每年都会裁员。
He lays off employees when business is slow. = 当生意清淡时，他就会裁员。


一般过去时:
They laid off 50 employees last month. = 他们上个月裁员50人。
The factory laid off half its workforce. = 工厂裁减了一半的员工。


现在完成时:
The company has laid off hundreds of workers this year. = 公司今年已经裁员数百人。
They have laid off staff in several departments. = 他们已经在几个部门裁员了。


过去完成时:
They had laid off most of the staff before the merger. = 在合并之前，他们已经裁掉了大部分员工。
The company had laid off workers several times in the past. = 这家公司过去曾多次裁员。


将来时:
The company will lay off more workers if sales don't improve. = 如果销售额没有改善，公司将裁减更多员工。
They will likely lay off staff next quarter. = 他们很可能在下个季度裁员。


虚拟语气:
If the market improved, they wouldn't have to lay off workers. = 如果市场好转，他们就不必裁员了。
I wish they hadn't laid off so many people. = 我希望他们不要裁掉这么多人。


被动语态:
Many workers were laid off during the economic crisis. = 许多工人都在经济危机期间被裁员了。
Fifty employees have been laid off this year. = 今年已有50名员工被裁员。


疑问句:
Will they lay off any more employees? = 他们还会裁员吗？
How many workers were laid off? = 裁员多少人？


否定句:
The company didn't lay off anyone last year. = 公司去年没有裁员。
They have not laid off any employees recently. = 他们最近没有裁员。



PHRASE->
mass layoff 大规模裁员
temporary layoff 临时性裁员
company-wide layoff 公司范围内的裁员
layoff notice 裁员通知
avoid layoff 避免裁员


词源:
The word "layoff" is a compound word.  "Lay" comes from Old English *leggian, meaning "to put down" or "to place".  The "-off" suffix indicates separation or detachment.  Therefore, "layoff" literally means "to put something (a worker) off" their job.
