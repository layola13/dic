misjoinder:/ˈmɪsdʒɔɪndər/| n. |不当共同起诉；不当共同被告|The improper joining of parties in a lawsuit, either as plaintiffs or defendants.  This can occur when parties lack a common interest or when the claims are unrelated.  A misjoinder can lead to the dismissal of the entire case or parts of it. |Example: The court found a misjoinder of parties because the plaintiffs' claims were entirely unrelated. 法院认定当事人构成不当共同起诉，因为原告的索赔完全无关。|近义词：improper joinder, faulty joinder|反义词：proper joinder|名词
