quasi-adopting: /ˈkwɑːzi əˈdɒptɪŋ/ | v. | 准收养；近似收养 |  The term "quasi-adopting" describes a situation where someone takes on a parental role without the formal legal process of adoption.  It might involve fostering, long-term caregiving, or a close, parent-like relationship without the legal implications of adoption.  The individual may provide significant care and support, but lacks the formal legal rights and responsibilities of an adoptive parent. | 例句：The family was quasi-adopting their niece after her parents passed away. = 在她的父母去世后，这个家庭准收养了他们的侄女。  The extended family quasi-adopted the child, providing love and care but without the legal paperwork. = 扩展的家庭准收养了这个孩子，给予爱和照顾，但没有相关的法律文件。 | 近义词：fostering, taking in, providing care for | 反义词：officially adopting, abandoning
