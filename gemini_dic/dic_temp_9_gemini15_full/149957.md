palls:/pɔːlz/| v. n.|变得令人厌倦；失去吸引力；变得暗淡；失去光泽；阴沉；丧钟；覆盖；使沮丧| to lose its appeal or attraction; to become dull or gloomy; to become less intense; a heavy, dark cloth, especially one used as a pall in a funeral|The excitement of the game began to pall after a while.  比赛的兴奋劲过了一会儿就开始减退了。

The grey sky palled the landscape. 灰色的天空使风景变得暗淡。


近义词：bore, tire, weary, fade, darken | 反义词：excite, enliven, brighten, exhilarate


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The endless work palls on me. = 没完没了的工作让我厌烦。
This food palls on my appetite. = 这食物让我没胃口。

一般过去时:
The constant rain palled the spirits of the hikers. = 持续的雨水让徒步旅行者的兴致低落。
The vibrant colors of the painting palled with age. = 画作鲜艳的色彩随着时间的推移而暗淡。

现在完成时:
The repetitive tasks have palled on her. = 重复性的工作让她厌倦了。
The once-thrilling adventure has palled on him. = 曾经令人兴奋的冒险现在让他感到索然无味。

过去完成时:
By the end of the long journey, the scenery had palled on the travelers. = 长途旅行结束时，风景已让旅行者感到厌倦。
The initial enthusiasm for the project had palled by the time the deadline approached. = 截止日期临近时，最初的热情已经消退。

将来时:
I fear the novelty of the job will pall after a few months. = 我担心这份工作的新鲜感几个月后就会消失。
The initial excitement of the holiday will likely pall by the end of the second week. = 假期的最初兴奋感很可能在第二周结束时就会消退。

虚拟语气:
If the weather remained like this, the beauty of the place would pall. = 如果天气一直这样，这个地方的美景就会变得索然无味。
I wish this monotonous routine would not pall on me so quickly. = 我希望这种单调的日常不会这么快就让我厌倦。

被动语态:
His interest in the hobby was palled by the lack of progress. = 他对这项爱好的兴趣因缺乏进步而减退。
The beauty of the sunset was palled by the approaching storm. = 夕阳的美丽被即将来临的风暴所掩盖。


疑问句:
Does the constant noise pall on you? = 持续的噪音让你厌烦吗？
Does the repetitive nature of your job pall on you? = 你工作的重复性让你厌倦吗？


否定句:
The beauty of the scenery did not pall on the tourists. = 风景的美丽并没有让游客感到厌倦。
The excitement of the event did not pall on the audience. = 活动的兴奋感并没有让观众感到厌倦。


PHRASE->
pall of gloom: 忧郁的气氛
pall of secrecy: 秘密的氛围
pall of smoke: 烟雾弥漫
pall over: 使...变得暗淡
fall into a pall: 陷入低迷


词源:
From Old French *pale, from Latin pallium ("cloak"), originally a rich woolen cloak.  The sense development is from the covering of a coffin to a general sense of gloom or dullness.
