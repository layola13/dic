quasi-foreign:/ˈkwɑːzaɪ ˈfɒrɪn/| adj. |准外来词，似外来词|An adjective describing words or phrases that, while appearing foreign, are actually formed from native elements of a language or are borrowed from a language with a shared linguistic history, and have become naturalized in the borrowing language.  They often retain a foreign appearance, pronunciation, or spelling. |Example:  The word "rendezvous" is considered a quasi-foreign word in English because while it's borrowed from French, it's now fully integrated into the English lexicon. |近义词：loanword, borrowed word, foreign-sounding word |反义词：native word, indigenous word
