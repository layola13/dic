overstimulation:/ˌoʊvərˌstɪmjʊˈleɪʃən/ | n. |过度刺激，兴奋过度 | The state of being excessively stimulated, often leading to negative consequences.  Overstimulation can manifest in various ways depending on the individual and the source of stimulation. It can involve sensory overload, emotional distress, or cognitive impairment. | Example: The bright lights and loud noises at the concert caused overstimulation in the child, leading to a meltdown.  孩子在音乐会上受到强烈的灯光和嘈杂声的过度刺激，导致情绪崩溃。|近义词：sensory overload, overexcitation |反义词：understimulation, underactivation
