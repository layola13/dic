pre-conviction: /ˌpriːkənˈvɪkʃən/ | n. |定罪前；犯罪前 | A time or state before a person is convicted of a crime.  This term often refers to the period before a criminal trial or the issuance of a guilty verdict. It can encompass various stages, such as investigation, arrest, and pre-trial proceedings. | Example:  He maintained his innocence throughout the pre-conviction stages of the legal process. 他在审判前的各个阶段都坚持自己无罪。  The social worker focused on the defendant's pre-conviction behavior and support system. 社工关注被告定罪前的行为和支持系统。 | 近义词：antecedent to conviction, pre-trial period | 反义词：post-conviction
