privative:/ˈpraɪvətɪv/| adj.| 剥夺性的；使丧失的；仅有的；特有的 |  Denoting a grammatical process in which a word is formed by adding a negative prefix to a stem, or by adding an element indicating absence or negation. It can also describe something that deprives or takes away something, or something that is exclusive or unique. | Example:  The word "unhappy" is formed through a privative process by adding the negative prefix "un-" to "happy".  Another example is the privative right of the king.  |近义词：deprivative, negative, exclusive | 反义词：additive, positive, inclusive
