excavating: /ˈɛkskeɪveɪtɪŋ/ | v. |挖掘；发掘；开凿 | The act of digging or uncovering something from the earth.  It involves removing earth or other materials to expose something buried beneath the surface. This can be done for various purposes, including archaeological research, construction, or mining. | 例句：Archaeologists are excavating the site for ancient artifacts. 考古学家正在挖掘遗址以寻找古代文物。

The workers are excavating a new subway line. 工人们正在挖掘一条新的地铁线路。


近义词：digging, unearthing, exhuming | 反义词：burying, concealing, covering


词源:  From the Latin word "excavare," meaning "to hollow out."  The prefix "ex-" means "out" or "from," and "cavare" means "to hollow."
