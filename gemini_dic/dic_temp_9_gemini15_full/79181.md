glamourise: /ˈɡlæməraɪz/ | vt. |美化；使…具有魅力；渲染；粉饰 | To make something seem more attractive, exciting, or glamorous than it really is.  | 例句：The film glamourised the life of a gangster. 这部电影美化了黑帮的生活。

The media often glamorises the lifestyles of celebrities. 媒体经常美化名人的生活方式。


近义词：romanticise, idealise, romanticize, embellish, glorify

反义词：denigrate, disparage, expose, criticize


词源：glamour + ise (suffix forming verbs)  Glamour itself originates from the Scottish Gaelic word "grammaire," meaning "magic spell" or "charm." Over time, its meaning evolved to encompass allure and attractiveness. The "-ise" suffix is a common verb-forming element in English.
