retepore: /ˈriːtɪpɔːr/ | n. | 网状孔隙 | A porous, reticulate fossil organism, often found in limestone, belonging to a group of extinct bryozoans.  Retepores are characterized by their network-like structure.  They are primarily studied by paleontologists. |  例句：The limestone sample contained numerous well-preserved retepore fossils.  = 石灰岩样本中包含许多保存完好的网状孔隙化石。
