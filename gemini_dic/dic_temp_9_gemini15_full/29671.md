Cathay: /ˈkæθeɪ/ | n. | 中国；华夏 | A historical name for China, especially used in the West.  It evokes a sense of mystery and ancient grandeur, often associated with the Silk Road and the far East. The term is now largely considered outdated and potentially insensitive, but it continues to appear in some place names and historical contexts. | Example: Marco Polo's travels took him through Cathay.  马可波罗的旅程带他走过了华夏。 |近义词：China, the Orient (dated and potentially offensive) | 反义词：None directly applicable.
