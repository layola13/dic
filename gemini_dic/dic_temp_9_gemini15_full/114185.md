mahwa:/ˈmɑːwɑː/| n. |印度丁香|Mahwa refers to the fruit of the Madhuca longifolia tree, also known as the Indian butter tree.  The fruit is a fleshy drupe, and various parts of the tree, including the flowers, fruit, and seeds, have been used for centuries in traditional medicine and as a source of food and drink in India and other parts of South Asia. The flowers are particularly valued for their intoxicating properties when fermented into an alcoholic beverage. |The mahwa tree provides a crucial source of income and sustenance for many rural communities.|近义词:Indian butter tree, Madhuca longifolia|反义词:无
