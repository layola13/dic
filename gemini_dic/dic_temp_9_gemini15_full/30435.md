centralizes:/ˈsɛntrəlaɪzɪz/ | v. | 集中；中央集权化 | To bring or pull (things) together towards a center or central point; to concentrate the control of (an activity or organization) under a single authority. | 例句：The company centralizes its operations in New York.  这家公司将其业务集中在纽约。

例句：The government is centralizing power. 政府正在集权。


近义词：concentrate, consolidate, unify | 反义词：decentralize, disperse


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government centralizes its power. = 政府集中权力。
The company centralizes its marketing efforts. = 公司集中其营销努力。


一般过去时:
The empire centralized its administration. = 这个帝国将其行政管理集中起来。
The company centralized its production in one factory. = 公司将其生产集中在一个工厂。


现在完成时:
The country has centralized its banking system. = 这个国家已经将其银行系统集中起来。
The organization has centralized its data management. = 该组织已经集中了其数据管理。


过去完成时:
The king had centralized his authority before the rebellion. = 在叛乱之前，国王已经集权。
They had centralized their resources before launching the project. = 他们在启动项目之前已经集中了资源。


将来时:
The corporation will centralize its research and development. = 公司将把研发工作集中起来。
The government will centralize control over the internet. = 政府将集中控制互联网。


虚拟语气:
If the company centralized its operations, it would be more efficient. = 如果公司将其业务集中起来，效率将会更高。
It would be better if the power were centralized. = 如果权力集中起来会更好。


被动语态:
Power was centralized by the dictator. = 权力被独裁者集中起来。

疑问句:

否定句:
