compulsions:/kəmˈpʌlʃənz/| n.|强迫行为，冲动| irresistible urges to perform certain actions or rituals, often repetitive and seemingly meaningless, that reduce anxiety.  These actions are typically performed to relieve anxiety or distress associated with obsessive thoughts.|Example: Her compulsions included repeatedly checking locks and washing her hands.  她的强迫症包括反复检查锁和洗手。

The constant hand-washing was one of her many compulsions.  不停地洗手是她众多强迫行为之一。|近义词：obsessions, urges, impulses|反义词：freedom, voluntary actions|名词

词源:
The word "compulsion" comes from the Latin word "compulsio," meaning "a driving together, a forcing," formed from the verb "compellere," meaning "to drive together, compel."  This reflects the irresistible nature of the urges involved.
