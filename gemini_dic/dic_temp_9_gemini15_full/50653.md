derival:/dɪˈraɪvəl/ | n. | 派生；起源；导出 | The act or process of deriving something; the source or origin of something.  It often refers to the origin of a word or concept. | Example: The derivation of the word "democracy" is traced back to ancient Greece.  派生词“民主”可以追溯到古希腊。|近义词：origin, source, derivation,  descent | 反义词：N/A


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The derivation of this word is complex. = 这个词的词源很复杂。
His success derives from hard work. = 他的成功源于努力工作。

一般过去时:
Scientists derived a new theory from the data. = 科学家们从数据中推导出一个新的理论。
The word "television" derived from the Greek words "tele" and "vision". = “电视”一词源于希腊词“tele”和“vision”。

现在完成时:
Linguists have derived many new words from ancient languages. = 语言学家们已经从古代语言中派生出许多新词。
We have derived great benefit from this project. = 我们从这个项目中获得了巨大的好处。

过去完成时:
They had derived their conclusions from insufficient evidence. = 他们的结论是从不足的证据中得出的。
Before the invention, they had derived energy from coal. = 在发明之前，他们是从煤炭中获取能量的。

将来时:
Researchers will derive valuable insights from this study. = 研究人员将从这项研究中获得宝贵的见解。
We will derive pleasure from our vacation. = 我们将在假期中获得快乐。

虚拟语气:
If we had more data, we could derive a more accurate model. = 如果我们有更多的数据，我们就能推导出一个更精确的模型。
I wish I could derive more satisfaction from my job. = 我希望我能从我的工作中获得更多满足感。

被动语态:
The new drug was derived from a plant extract. = 新药是从植物提取物中提取的。
The conclusion was derived from careful analysis. = 结论是从仔细的分析中得出的。

疑问句:
Where did you derive that information? = 你是从哪里获得那个信息的？
How did they derive the formula? = 他们是如何推导出这个公式的？

否定句:
We could not derive any meaningful results from the experiment. = 我们无法从实验中得出任何有意义的结果。
He did not derive any pleasure from his work. = 他从工作中没有得到任何乐趣。
