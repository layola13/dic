monograph:/ˈmɒnəɡræf/| /ˈmɑːnəɡræf/ | n. |专著，论文|A monograph is a scholarly work of writing on a single subject or a specific aspect of a larger subject, often written by a single author and usually intended for a specialized audience.  It is typically longer and more detailed than an article but shorter than a book.|Example:  He wrote a monograph on the life and works of Shakespeare. 他写了一部关于莎士比亚生平和作品的专著。


The professor published a monograph detailing her research on ancient civilizations. 教授发表了一篇专著，详细介绍了她对古代文明的研究。|近义词：treatise, dissertation, study|反义词：anthology (a collection of works), textbook|n.
