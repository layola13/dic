industrialist: /ɪnˈdʌstriəlɪst/ | n. |实业家，工业家 | A person who owns or controls a large industrial enterprise.  An industrialist is someone who is involved in owning, managing, or developing large-scale industries. They often have significant influence on the economy and society. | Example:  Andrew Carnegie was a prominent industrialist of the late 19th century.  安德鲁·卡内基是19世纪后期一位著名的实业家。  |近义词： magnate, tycoon, entrepreneur | 反义词：laborer, worker

词源:
The word "industrialist" is a combination of "industrial" and "-ist."  "Industrial" derives from "industry," ultimately from Latin "industria" (diligence, skill), and "-ist" is a suffix forming nouns denoting a person associated with a specified thing or activity.
