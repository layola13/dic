/ˌɪntərˈmɛl/| n.|幕间剧，插曲|A short entertainment, such as a musical piece, performed between the acts of a play or other performance.|The orchestra played an interlude between the acts.|近义词：interlude, entr'acte|反义词：main performance


USAGE->
This word is a noun, and therefore doesn't have verb conjugations like the example word "what".  It primarily describes a thing, not an action.  Therefore, tense, voice etc are not applicable.

例句1: The audience enjoyed the intermel between the acts of the opera.  观众欣赏了歌剧幕间演奏的插曲。
例句2: A comedic intermel lightened the mood of the serious play.  一个喜剧插曲使严肃的戏剧气氛轻松起来。


PHRASE->
There are no common idiomatic phrases using "intermel".  The word is used fairly literally.
