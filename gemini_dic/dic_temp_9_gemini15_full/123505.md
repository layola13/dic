monothelite:/ˈmɒnəθiːlaɪt/|/ˈmɑːnəθiːlaɪt/| n. |一元论者（基督教）|A monothelite is a follower of Monothelitism, a 7th-century Christian theological doctrine that held that Christ had only one will (the divine will), not two (divine and human).  This position was condemned as heretical by the Church. |The Monothelite controversy deeply divided the Eastern Church in the 7th century.|近义词：None (the term is specific)|反义词：Dithelite|名词
