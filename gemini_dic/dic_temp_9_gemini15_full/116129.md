marxists:/ˈmɑːksɪsts/| n. |马克思主义者|Adherents of Marxism, a socioeconomic theory developed by Karl Marx and Friedrich Engels.  Marxists believe in a classless society achieved through revolutionary means, emphasizing the importance of economic factors in shaping history and society.  They often advocate for social equality, workers' rights, and the abolition of private property.|例句：Many marxists believe that capitalism inevitably leads to its own destruction.|近义词：communists, socialists (but note important distinctions)|反义词：capitalists, libertarians
