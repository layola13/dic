neoplatonism:/ˌniːoʊˈpleɪtənɪzəm/| n. |新柏拉图主义|A school of philosophical thought that flourished from the 3rd to the 6th century CE, combining elements of Plato's philosophy with other philosophical and religious ideas, particularly those from the mystical traditions of the East.| Neoplatonism profoundly influenced Christian theology and later mystical traditions.  The emphasis on the One, emanation, and the soul's ascent to union with the divine, resonated across centuries.|近义词：platonism (but with crucial distinctions), mystical philosophy|反义词：materialism, empiricism
