closehauled:/ˌkloʊsˈhɔːld/| adj.| 迎风紧缩的；（船）紧顶风的 |Close-hauled refers to the sailing position of a sailboat when it is sailing as close to the wind as possible.  The sails are trimmed tightly to maximize the boat's progress into the wind.  This is often the most efficient point of sail, but it also requires careful handling and precise sail adjustments.|The boat was close-hauled, battling the strong headwind.|近义词：tight-hauled|反义词：broad reach, beam reach
