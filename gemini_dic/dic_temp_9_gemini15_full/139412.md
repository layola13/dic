observerships:/əbˈzɜːvərʃɪps/ | n. |观察员职位；旁听资格 |The opportunity to gain experience and knowledge by observing experts in a particular field.  It often involves a period of time spent shadowing or assisting experienced professionals. |例句：The program offers several observerships to aspiring doctors. 该项目为有抱负的医生提供一些观察员职位。

The university provides observerships in its various departments.  大学在其各个系提供旁听资格。


近义词：apprenticeships (if involving practical work), traineeships, fellowships (if more formal and prestigious), shadowing opportunities


反义词：None directly;  antonyms would relate to the opposite of observation, such as active participation or leadership roles.


词性：名词
