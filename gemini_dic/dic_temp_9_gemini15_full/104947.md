kelps:/kɛlps/| n. |大型褐藻；海带属植物|Large brown seaweeds belonging to the order Laminariales.  Kelps are a type of algae that form extensive underwater forests, providing habitat and food for many marine organisms. They are also harvested for various commercial uses.| Kelp forests are crucial ecosystems supporting a wide array of marine life. |seaweeds, algae|  (反义词无明确对应)|名词
例句：
The divers explored the vibrant kelp forests off the coast of California.  潜水员探索了加利福尼亚海岸附近充满活力的海带森林。

The company extracts alginate from kelp for use in food products.  该公司提取海带中的藻酸盐用于食品生产。


词源:
The word "kelp" comes from the Middle English word "kelp," which ultimately derives from the Middle Dutch word "kelp."  These words referred to various types of seaweed, especially those burned to produce potash.
