resiting:/rɪˈzɪstɪŋ/| v.|抵抗，反抗，抵制|resisting something unpleasant or unwanted.  The act of opposing or withstanding something.|例句：He was resisting arrest. 他拒捕。|例句：The soldiers were resisting the enemy's attack. 士兵们抵抗敌人的进攻。|近义词：opposing, defying, withstanding |反义词：yielding, surrendering, submitting |动词

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is resisting the temptation. = 他正在抵抗诱惑。
They are resisting the change. = 他们在抵制改变。

一般过去时:
She resisted the urge to scream. = 她忍住了尖叫的冲动。
The army resisted the invasion. = 军队抵抗了入侵。

现在完成时:
He has resisted all attempts to bribe him. = 他抵挡了所有贿赂他的企图。
We have resisted the pressure to compromise. = 我们抵制了妥协的压力。

过去完成时:
They had resisted the temptation for years. = 他们多年来一直抵制诱惑。
She had resisted telling him the truth. = 她一直忍着没告诉他真相。

将来时:
He will resist any attempt to control him. = 他将抵抗任何控制他的企图。
We will resist the temptation to give up. = 我们将抵制放弃的诱惑。

虚拟语气:
If he resisted, they would arrest him. = 如果他反抗，他们就会逮捕他。
I wish I had resisted the temptation to eat that cake. = 我真希望我没有抵制住吃那块蛋糕的诱惑。(此处例句略有调整，更符合虚拟语气的用法)

被动语态:
The new policy was resisted by many people. = 许多人抵制这项新政策。
His pleas were resisted by the judge. = 法官拒绝了他的请求。

疑问句:
Are you resisting the urge to sleep? = 你在抵抗想睡觉的冲动吗？
Why are they resisting the investigation? = 他们为什么抵制调查？

否定句:
He didn't resist the temptation. = 他没有抵制住诱惑。
She couldn't resist laughing. = 她忍不住笑了。
