novatianism:/ˌnoʊveɪʃənɪzəm/| n. |诺瓦底主义；坚持教会纪律的严格性|The theological position of Novatian, a 3rd-century Roman presbyter, who advocated for refusing to readmit lapsed Christians to the Church after their penance.|The early Church struggled with the question of whether to readmit Christians who had denied their faith during persecution. Novatianism represented a stricter approach, advocating for the exclusion of those who had fallen away, even after repentance.|近义词：rigorism, puritanism|反义词：leniency, laxity
