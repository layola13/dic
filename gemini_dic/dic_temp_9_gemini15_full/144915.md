over-talkative: /ˌoʊvərˈtɔːkətɪv/ | adj. | 话太多的；喋喋不休的 | Excessively talkative; tending to talk too much.  |例句：She's a bit over-talkative, but she has a kind heart. 她有点话多，但她心地善良。

例句：He became over-talkative after a few drinks. 他喝了几杯酒后变得喋喋不休。

近义词： garrulous, loquacious, voluble, verbose, long-winded

反义词： taciturn, reserved, reticent, silent


词源:

* **over-**:  Prefix indicating excess or too much.  From Old English *ofer, meaning "over, above."

* **talkative**: Adjective meaning "tending to talk a lot." This derives from "talk," which comes from Old English *talu, meaning "speech, talk."  The suffix "-ative" is a common adjective-forming suffix indicating a tendency or disposition.

过去完成时:

将来时:

虚拟语气:
