hedger:/ˈhɛdʒər/| n. |套期保值者；避险者；对冲者|A person or entity that uses hedging strategies to reduce financial risk.  Hedgers engage in transactions to offset potential losses from price fluctuations.|例句：The farmer acted as a hedger, buying futures contracts to protect against falling crop prices.  这位农民扮演着套期保值者的角色，购买期货合约以防范农作物价格下跌。|例句：Large corporations often employ hedgers to manage their exposure to currency fluctuations. 大型公司经常雇佣套期保值者来管理其面临的货币波动风险。|近义词：insurer, risk manager|反义词：speculator


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He hedges his bets in the stock market. = 他在股市里进行套期保值。
She hedges against inflation by investing in real estate. = 她通过投资房地产来对冲通货膨胀。
