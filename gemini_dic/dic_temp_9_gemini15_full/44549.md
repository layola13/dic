crotalus: /ˈkrɒtəlʌs/ | n. |响尾蛇属| A genus of venomous pit vipers found in the Americas.  Crotalus snakes are characterized by their heat-sensing pits, rattle on their tail, and often, a triangular head. They are known for their potent venom. |Example: The hiker carefully avoided the crotalus basking in the sun.  徒步旅行者小心翼翼地避开了在阳光下晒太阳的响尾蛇。|近义词： rattlesnake (更常用，更广义)|反义词：无
