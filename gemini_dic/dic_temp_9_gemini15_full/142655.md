ostracized:/ˈɒstrəsaɪzd/|v.|排斥，放逐，冷落|To exclude someone from a society or group.|The new student was ostracized by his classmates because of his unusual clothing.|近义词：shunned, excluded, isolated|反义词：included, welcomed


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They ostracize anyone who disagrees with them. = 他们排斥任何与他们意见相左的人。
She is often ostracized for her outspoken views. = 她经常因为直言不讳而被排斥。

一般过去时:
He was ostracized from the community after the scandal. = 丑闻发生后，他被社区排斥了。
The group ostracized her for breaking the rules. = 因为违反规定，小组排斥了她。

现在完成时:
She has been ostracized by her friends since the argument. = 争吵后，她一直被朋友们排斥。
They have been ostracized for their beliefs for many years. = 他们因为信仰多年来一直被排斥。

过去完成时:
He had been ostracized for so long that he felt completely alone. = 他被排斥了这么久，以至于感到完全孤独。
She realized she had been ostracized from the group because of a misunderstanding. = 她意识到自己因为误解而被排斥在这个群体之外。

将来时:
If he continues to behave like that, he will be ostracized by everyone. = 如果他继续那样做，他会被所有人排斥。
They fear they will be ostracized if they speak out against the government. = 他们担心如果他们公开反对政府，就会被排斥。


被动语态:
He was ostracized for his unconventional ideas. = 他因为他的非传统思想而被排斥。
She felt ostracized because of her different background. = 因为不同的背景，她感到被排斥。

疑问句:
Was he ostracized for his political views? = 他是因为政治观点而被排斥的吗？
Were they ostracized because of their religion? = 他们是因为宗教信仰而被排斥的吗？

否定句:
They were not ostracized, but simply ignored. = 他们没有被排斥，只是被忽略了。
He wasn't ostracized; he simply chose to leave the group. = 他没有被排斥；他只是选择离开小组。


PHRASE->
be ostracized from = 被...排斥
feel ostracized = 感到被排斥
become ostracized = 变得被排斥
risk being ostracized = 冒着被排斥的风险
subject to ostracization = 遭受排斥
