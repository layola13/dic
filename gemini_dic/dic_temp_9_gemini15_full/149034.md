owlishly:/ˈaʊlɪʃli/ | adv. |像猫头鹰似的；目光呆滞地；严肃地 | In a manner resembling an owl; solemnly; gravely; with a wise or knowing look. | 例句：He stared owlishly at the strange symbols. 他像猫头鹰一样盯着那些奇怪的符号。

He blinked owlishly, clearly confused. 他像猫头鹰一样眨了眨眼，显然很困惑。


近义词：wisely, solemnly, gravely, thoughtfully

反义词：foolishly, carelessly, rashly


词源：源自owl（猫头鹰）+后缀-ly（副词后缀），表示“像猫头鹰一样地”。  The word derives from the noun "owl," referring to the bird, combined with the adverbial suffix "-ly," indicating a manner or way.  The association stems from owls' large, round eyes and their often perceived wise or solemn demeanor.

USAGE->
This word is an adverb, and as such doesn't have tenses like verbs do.  It describes the manner in which an action is performed.


PHRASE->
There are no common phrases specifically using "owlishly".  Its usage is typically standalone to modify a verb.
