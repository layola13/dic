hyperinflations:/ˌhaɪpərɪnˈfleɪʃənz/| n. |恶性通货膨胀；极度通货膨胀|  Extremely rapid and out-of-control inflation, typically characterized by a monthly inflation rate exceeding 50%.  This leads to a drastic loss of purchasing power and economic instability.  It often results from government policies that print excessive amounts of money or from other severe economic crises.| The Weimar Republic in Germany experienced hyperinflation in the 1920s, rendering the currency almost worthless.|近义词：runaway inflation, galloping inflation|反义词：deflation, price stability|名词
