minstrels:/ˈmɪnstrəlz/ | n. |吟游诗人，乐师 | A minstrel was a medieval performer who sang, played music, and often told stories or jokes.  They were typically itinerant, traveling from place to place to entertain. The term often refers to the popular, but controversial, minstrel shows of the 19th century in the United States, which featured white performers in blackface. | Example:  The medieval minstrel captivated the lord and his guests with his songs and tales.  中世纪的吟游诗人用他的歌曲和故事迷住了领主和他的客人。
