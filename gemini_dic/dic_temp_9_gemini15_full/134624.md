nonlyricisms: /ˌnɒnˈlɪrɪsɪzəm/ | n. |非抒情性；非歌词部分|The absence of lyrical qualities; elements in a work that are not lyrical in nature.  This term is primarily used in literary and musical criticism to describe aspects of a work that lack the emotional expressiveness or poetic qualities typically associated with lyricism.  It refers to the non-poetic, prosaic, or even objectively descriptive parts of a piece.|例句：The novel's detailed descriptions of the setting, while meticulously crafted, contributed to its overall nonlyricisms.|近义词：prosaism, literalness|反义词：lyricism, poetry, expressiveness|
