gape:/ɡeɪp/ | vi. & n. | 张开嘴；裂开；裂缝 |  To open one's mouth wide, especially in surprise or wonder; a wide opening. | 例句：He gaped at the incredible sight. 他张大嘴巴看着这难以置信的景象。

例句：The chasm gaped before them.  巨大的裂缝出现在他们面前。


近义词：stare, yawn, open wide | 反义词：close, shut


词源:
源自古英语 *gapian，意思是“张开嘴”，与“yawn”（打哈欠）同源。


USAGE->

He gapes at the television all evening. 他整个晚上都对着电视发呆。
Birds gape their beaks when begging for food.  小鸟张开嘴乞讨食物。


一般过去时:
She gaped in astonishment at the news. 她听到这个消息惊讶地张大了嘴巴。
The wound gaped open. 伤口裂开了。


现在完成时:

The crack in the wall has gaped wider. 墙上的裂缝越来越大了。


过去完成时:
They had gaped at the magician's trick for a long time before they understood it. 在理解魔术师的戏法之前，他们张大了嘴巴看了很久。
The earth had gaped open, swallowing the car whole.  大地裂开，整个吞没了汽车。

虚拟语气:

疑问句:

否定句:

The door didn't gape open; it was firmly shut. 门没有裂开；它紧紧地关着。
