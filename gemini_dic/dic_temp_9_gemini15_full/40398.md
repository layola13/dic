contrahent:/ˈkɒntrəhent/ /ˈkɑːntrəhɛnt/| n. |缔约方，当事人 |A contrahent is a party to a contract or agreement.  It refers to either of the two parties involved in a legal or business transaction.  |例句：Both contrahents signed the contract.  = 双方当事人都签署了合同。

例句：The dispute involved three contrahents. = 这场纠纷涉及三方当事人。

近义词：party, participant, signatory

反义词：N/A (no direct antonym, the opposite would be the absence of a party to a contract)

词性：名词

词源:  From Latin *contrahere "to draw together," "to contract," from com- "together" + trahere "to draw."  The noun form reflects the parties "drawn together" by the contract.
