overmitigates: /ˌoʊvərˈmɪtɪɡeɪts/ | v. | 过度减轻；过度缓解 |  To mitigate something means to make it less severe, serious, or painful.  "Overmitigate" implies that the effort to lessen something has gone too far, resulting in an unintended negative consequence. It suggests an excessive or disproportionate response to a problem.  The word is not commonly used and is more likely to be found in academic or technical contexts. | 例句：The company overmitigated the risk by investing excessively in safety measures, resulting in high costs and reduced profitability. = 公司过度减轻风险，在安全措施上投入过多，导致成本高昂且盈利能力下降。 The government's overmitigation of the economic crisis led to inflation. = 政府过度缓解经济危机导致通货膨胀。 | 近义词：overcompensate, overreact, overdo | 反义词：undermitigate, neglect  | 动词
