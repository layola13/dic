slanderingly:/ˈslændərɪŋli/ | adv. |诽谤地，中伤地 |In a way that slanders or defames someone.  It describes the manner in which something is done, implying malicious intent to damage someone's reputation. |例句：He spoke slanderingly about his former business partner, spreading false rumors to ruin his credibility. 他诽谤他的前商业伙伴，散布谣言以毁坏他的信誉。


例句：She slanderingly accused him of theft, causing significant damage to his reputation. 她诽谤地指控他偷窃，严重损害了他的名誉。

近义词：defamatorily, calumniously, maliciously, scandalously

反义词：complimentarily, flatteringly, laudatorily


词源:

From "slander" + "-ingly".  "Slander" itself originates from Old French "eslaundrer," meaning "to soil, besmirch," ultimately from a Germanic root related to "slippery," suggesting the idea of something that is easily spread and damaging.  The "-ingly" suffix denotes a manner or way of doing something.
