shanties:/ˈʃæntiz/| n.| 船歌；简陋小屋 | A shanty is a song traditionally sung by sailors while working, often characterized by a repetitive chorus and simple melody.  It can also refer to a roughly built hut or small dwelling. |例句：The sailors sang shanties as they hauled up the anchor. 水手们在起锚时唱着船歌。

The old shanty was barely habitable. 那间旧棚屋几乎无法居住。|近义词： sea song, ballad, shack, hut |反义词：mansion, palace

词源:
The word "shanty" comes from the French word "chantier," meaning "shipyard" or "workshop."  Over time, its meaning evolved to encompass the songs sung in those shipyards and later the dwellings built there.
