infinites:/ɪnˈfɪnɪtiz/| n. |无限，无限多个|The concept of infinites is fundamental to calculus.  无限的概念是微积分的基础。|近义词：infinity, eternity|反义词：finiteness, limitation|名词


英文解析：
Infinites, in the mathematical context, refers to infinitely many things or quantities.  It often appears in discussions of calculus and set theory, representing a quantity without bound or limit.  It’s a concept used to describe something that is boundless, unending, or immeasurable.  Outside of mathematics, it can be used figuratively to represent something vast or seemingly endless.


例句：

The number of stars in the universe approaches infinites. 宇宙中恒星的数量接近无限。

There seems to be infinites possibilities when you begin to write a novel. 当你开始写小说时，似乎有无限的可能性。


词源:

The word "infinites" is derived from the Latin word "infinitas," meaning "boundlessness" or "limitlessness."  It combines the prefix "in-" (not) with "finis" (end), resulting in "without end."


用法说明:  由于infinites本身是名词，它主要用在句子中作主语或宾语。很少单独出现，通常与其他词语搭配使用，例如：a set of infinites (无限集)，the realm of infinites (无限的领域)，approaches infinites (接近无限)。  它在数学和哲学领域更为常见。  在非正式语境中，使用“infinity”更常见。
