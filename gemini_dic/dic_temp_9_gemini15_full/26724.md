cadged:/kædʒd/| v.|骗取；乞讨；蹭；|To obtain something by begging or coaxing; to sponge.  The word implies a degree of cunning or persistence in acquiring something without paying for it.|例句：He cadged a cigarette from a stranger. 他向陌生人讨了一支烟。

例句：She cadged a ride home from her friend. 她向朋友蹭了一程回家。


近义词：beg, sponge, scrounge, mooch, bum


反义词：give, donate, contribute, provide


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He cadges money from his friends. = 他向朋友骗钱。
She cadges rides to work every day. = 她每天都蹭车上班。


一般过去时:
He cadged a meal from his neighbour. = 他向邻居骗了一顿饭。
She cadged a lift to the station. = 她搭便车去了车站。


现在完成时:
He has cadged several favors from me. = 他已经从我这里骗取了好几个帮忙。
She has cadged her way across Europe. = 她一路蹭吃蹭喝地游遍了欧洲。


过去完成时:
He had cadged money from his family before. = 他以前就曾向家人骗过钱。
She had cadged a free ticket to the concert. = 她之前已经骗到了一张演唱会的免费票。


将来时:
He will cadge a cigarette from someone. = 他会向别人讨烟抽。
She will cadge a ride home tonight. = 她今晚会想办法蹭车回家。


虚拟语气:
If he needed money, he would cadge it from his parents. = 如果他需要钱，他会向父母骗钱。
I wish I hadn’t cadged that money from him. = 我真希望我没有向他骗钱。


被动语态:
A free meal was cadged from the restaurant. = 从这家餐馆骗到了一顿免费餐。
A ride home was cadged from a kind stranger. = 从一个好心的陌生人那里蹭到了一程回家。


疑问句:
Did he cadge money from you? = 他向你骗钱了吗？
Has she ever cadged anything from you? = 她以前有没有向你骗过什么东西？


否定句:
He didn't cadge anything from me. = 他没有向我骗取任何东西。
She wouldn't cadge a ride from a stranger. = 她不会向陌生人蹭车。
