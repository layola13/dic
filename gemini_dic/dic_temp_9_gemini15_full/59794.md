ecotypes:/ˈiːkəʊtaɪps/ | n. |生态型，生态变型 |A population of plants or animals having specific characteristics that enable it to thrive in a particular ecological niche.  Ecotypes are genetically distinct from other populations of the same species but can interbreed. | Example:  Different ecotypes of the same plant species might exhibit variations in size, flowering time, and tolerance to drought depending on their local environment.  不同生态型的同一种植物可能会在大小、开花时间和耐旱性方面表现出差异，这取决于它们当地的环境。 |近义词：variety, strain, subspecies |反义词：无
