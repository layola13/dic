permit:/pərˈmɪt/| vt. n. |许可，允许；执照，许可证 |to allow or consent to (something);  a document granting official permission to do something. |例句：The city council will permit the construction of a new shopping mall. 市议会将允许建造一个新的购物中心。

例句：They need a permit to build a house.他们需要许可证才能建造房屋。


近义词：allow, authorize, license | 反义词：forbid, prohibit, ban

词性：动词，名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He permits smoking in his office. 他允许在他的办公室吸烟。
The school permits students to wear casual clothes on Fridays. 学校允许学生在星期五穿便装。


一般过去时:
The teacher permitted the students to leave early. 老师允许学生早退。
My parents permitted me to go to the concert. 我的父母允许我去参加音乐会。


现在完成时:
The government has permitted the import of new technology. 政府已允许进口新技术。
She has permitted me to use her car. 她允许我使用她的汽车。


过去完成时:
They had permitted the use of the facilities before the event. 他们在活动前已允许使用这些设施。
He had permitted her to access the confidential files. 他已允许她访问机密文件。


将来时:
The authorities will permit the demonstration to proceed. 当局将允许示威游行继续进行。
They will permit visitors to enter the museum after 10 am. 他们将在上午十点后允许游客进入博物馆。


虚拟语气:
If they permitted it, I would attend the meeting. 如果他们允许，我会参加会议。
I wish they permitted us to stay longer. 我希望他们允许我们待久一点。


被动语态:
Smoking is permitted in designated areas only. 吸烟只允许在指定区域内进行。
The application for a permit was permitted. 许可证申请已被批准。


疑问句:
Does the building permit the use of drones? 这栋建筑物是否允许使用无人机？
Will they permit us to film here? 他们会允许我们在这里拍摄吗？


否定句:
They do not permit photography inside the museum. 他们不允许在博物馆内拍照。
The rules do not permit the use of cell phones during the exam. 规则不允许考试期间使用手机。
