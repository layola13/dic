differentiate:/ˌdɪfəˈrenʃieɪt/| vt.|区分，辨别|To recognize or show the difference between things.|The twins are so alike that it's hard to differentiate between them. 双胞胎长得太像了，很难区分他们。|distinguish, discriminate, discern|confuse, confound|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I differentiate between right and wrong. = 我区分是非。
She differentiates the various types of birds. = 她区分各种各样的鸟类。


一般过去时:
We differentiated the two approaches. = 我们区分了这两种方法。
He differentiated the sounds easily. = 他很容易区分这些声音。


现在完成时:
Scientists have differentiated several new strains of the virus. = 科学家们已经区分了几种新的病毒菌株。
She has differentiated herself from her competitors. = 她已经把自己和竞争对手区分开来。


过去完成时:
They had differentiated the tasks before starting the project. = 他们在开始项目之前已经区分了任务。
The researcher had differentiated the cells under a microscope. = 研究人员在显微镜下区分了细胞。


将来时:
We will differentiate the products based on customer feedback. = 我们将根据客户反馈区分产品。
She will differentiate her teaching style for different learning levels. = 她将针对不同的学习水平区分她的教学风格。


虚拟语气:
If I could differentiate the colors, I would choose a different paint. = 如果我能区分颜色，我会选择不同的油漆。
I wish I could differentiate the subtle differences in their voices. = 我希望我能区分他们声音中的细微差别。


被动语态:

疑问句:

Can you differentiate the various musical instruments? = 你能区分各种乐器吗？


否定句:

He didn't differentiate between his friends and enemies. = 他没有区分朋友和敌人。
