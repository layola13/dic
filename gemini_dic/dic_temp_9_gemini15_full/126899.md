narcolepsies:/ˈnɑːrkəˌlepsɪz/ | n. |嗜睡症（复数）|A chronic neurological disorder characterized by excessive daytime sleepiness and sudden, uncontrollable sleep attacks.  It is often accompanied by cataplexy (sudden loss of muscle tone), sleep paralysis, and hypnagogic hallucinations. |例句：His narcolepsies caused him to fall asleep at inappropriate times.他的嗜睡症导致他在不恰当的时候睡着。

例句：The doctor diagnosed her with narcolepsies after observing her symptoms.医生观察了她的症状后，诊断她患有嗜睡症。


近义词：hypersomnia (general excessive sleepiness)


反义词：insomnia (inability to sleep)


词性:名词
