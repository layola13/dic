roar:/rɔːr/| /rɔːr/ | v., n. | 咆哮；轰鸣；大声笑 | To make a loud, deep sound, like a lion.  Also, to laugh loudly and boisterously. As a noun, it refers to the sound itself. | 例句：The lion roared fiercely.  狮子凶猛地咆哮。

The engine roared to life. 引擎轰鸣着启动了。


近义词： bellow, howl, growl, thunder, shout | 反义词： whisper, murmur, mumble


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The lion roars loudly. = 狮子大声咆哮。
The engine roars constantly. = 引擎持续轰鸣。

一般过去时:
The lion roared at the hunter. = 狮子对着猎人咆哮。
The crowd roared with laughter. = 人群爆发出笑声。

The volcano has roared into life again. = 火山再次喷发。

过去完成时:
The crowd had roared its disapproval before the speaker finished. = 演讲者还没说完，人群就已经大声表示反对。
The engine had roared for hours before it finally died. = 引擎已经轰鸣了几个小时才最终熄火。

将来时:

被动语态:

His name was roared by the crowd. = 他的名字被人群高喊。

疑问句:

否定句:

词源:
The word "roar" comes from the Old English word *rurian, meaning "to cry aloud" or "to make a loud noise".  This word has Germanic roots, related to words in other Germanic languages describing similar sounds.
