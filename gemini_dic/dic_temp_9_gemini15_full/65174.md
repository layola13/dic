ethnolinguistics: /ˌeθnoʊlɪŋˈɡwɪstɪks/ | n. |民族语言学|The study of the relationship between language and culture, and how language reflects and shapes cultural identities and social structures.  It examines how language use varies across different ethnic groups and how linguistic features are connected to cultural practices and beliefs. | Example: Ethnolinguistics research often focuses on the ways language is used to express social status and power relationships within a community. 民族语言学研究通常关注语言如何用于表达社区内的社会地位和权力关系。|近义词:sociolinguistics, linguistic anthropology|反义词: (no direct antonym)|名词

词源:
The term "ethnolinguistics" is a combination of "ethno-" (from Greek ἔθνος, *ethnos*, meaning "nation" or "people") and "linguistics" (the study of language).  Therefore, it literally means the study of the language of peoples or nations, but with a strong emphasis on the cultural context.
