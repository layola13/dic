headcheeses:/ˈhɛdˌtʃiːzɪz/| n. |头奶酪；脑髓（俚语）|A headcheese is a type of cold cut made from the head of a pig or other animal, typically including the brain and other parts, boiled and then molded into a loaf.  It's a traditional dish in some cultures. In slang, it can refer to brains or intellect in a derogatory way.| Example:  The butcher displayed several headcheeses in his shop.  屠夫在他的店里展示了几块头奶酪。

The recipe for headcheese varies regionally, but it often involves simmering the animal head with spices and seasonings until tender. The meat is then removed, minced or shredded, and mixed with aspic or other gelatinous substance.  The mixture is then packed into a mold and chilled until firm.
