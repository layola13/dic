putz:/pʌts/| n., v.|笨蛋；傻瓜；乱搞；笨手笨脚地弄|英文解析：A putz is a foolish or incompetent person.  As a verb, it means to mess around clumsily or inefficiently.|例句：He's such a putz; he can't even change a tire. 他真是个笨蛋，连换个轮胎都不会。

He spent hours putzing around in the garage, but never fixed the car. 他在车库里瞎忙了几个小时，但车还是没修好。|近义词：fool, idiot, klutz, dope|反义词：expert, genius, skilled

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He puts around a lot. = 他经常闲逛。
She putzes around the kitchen. = 她笨手笨脚地在厨房里忙活。

一般过去时:
He putzed around all afternoon. = 他整个下午都在瞎忙。
She putzed with the computer for hours. = 她鼓捣电脑鼓捣了好几个小时。

现在完成时:
He has putzed away most of the day. = 他已经瞎忙了大部分时间。
She has putzed around the house all morning. = 她整个上午都在家里瞎忙。

过去完成时:
He had putzed around before she arrived. = 她来之前，他一直在瞎忙。
She had putzed with the project for weeks before giving up. = 她费了几个星期瞎忙这个项目，最后放弃了。

将来时:
He will putz around tomorrow. = 他明天会瞎忙。
She will putz with the garden all afternoon. = 她整个下午都会在花园里瞎忙。

虚拟语气:
If he knew how to fix it, he wouldn't putz around so much. = 如果他知道怎么修，他就不会这么瞎忙了。
I wish he wouldn't putz around with my things. = 我真希望他不要乱动我的东西。

被动语态: (This verb is rarely used in passive voice)

疑问句:
Is he putzing around again? = 他又在瞎忙了吗？
What is she putzing with? = 她正在瞎忙什么？

否定句:
