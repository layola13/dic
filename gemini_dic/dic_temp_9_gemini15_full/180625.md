regma:/ˈrɛɡmə/| n. |果实裂片；(某些植物的)分果|A regma is a type of dry fruit that breaks up at maturity into several one-seeded segments or mericarps.  Each mericarp is a separate fruitlet, and they detach from a central axis.  This is different from other dry fruits that split open along sutures or dehisce in other ways.|Example: The fruit of geraniums is a type of regma, splitting into five carpels when ripe.|近义词：schizocarp (though regma is a specific type of schizocarp)|反义词：None specifically, but could be contrasted with indehiscent fruits that don't split open.|名词
