quasi-living:/ˈkwɑːzaɪˈlɪvɪŋ/| adj.|准生物的，似生物的|Seeming to be alive but not fully alive; exhibiting some but not all characteristics of living organisms.  The term is often used in contexts such as discussions of viruses or certain artificial systems. |例句：Viruses are often described as quasi-living entities because they replicate but lack independent metabolism.  病毒通常被描述为准生物实体，因为它们可以复制，但缺乏独立的新陈代谢。


例句： Some researchers consider self-replicating computer programs to be quasi-living systems. 一些研究人员认为自我复制的计算机程序是准生物系统。


近义词：semi-living, borderline-living, artificial life (in certain contexts)|反义词：living, non-living, inanimate|词性：形容词


词源:
The word "quasi-living" is a compound word.  "Quasi" is derived from the Latin word "quasi," meaning "as if," "almost," or "seemingly."  "Living" is from the Old English word "lifiende," meaning "alive."  Therefore, "quasi-living" literally means "almost alive" or "seemingly alive."
