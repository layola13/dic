prohibition:/ˌproʊɪˈbɪʃən/| n. |禁止；禁令；法令|The act of prohibiting or forbidding something; the state of being prohibited.  It often refers to a legal ban or restriction.  |例句：The prohibition of alcohol led to a rise in illegal distilleries.  酒精禁令导致非法酿酒厂数量增加。

例句：There's a strict prohibition against smoking in this building. 这栋建筑物严格禁止吸烟。


近义词：ban, interdiction, embargo, restriction |反义词：permission, allowance, authorization |词性：名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
Prohibition is against the law. = 禁止是违法的。
The government prohibits the sale of drugs. = 政府禁止出售毒品。


一般过去时:
Prohibition of alcohol ended in 1933. = 禁酒令于1933年结束。
They prohibited entry to the building. = 他们禁止进入该建筑物。


现在完成时:
The city has prohibited the use of fireworks. = 该市已禁止使用烟花。
They have prohibited smoking in public places. = 他们已禁止在公共场所吸烟。


过去完成时:
They had prohibited the use of cars before the race. = 比赛前他们已禁止使用汽车。
The government had prohibited the import of certain goods. = 政府已禁止进口某些商品。


将来时:
They will prohibit the construction of new buildings. = 他们将禁止建造新的建筑物。
The council will prohibit parking in this area. = 理事会将禁止在此区域停车。


虚拟语气:
If they prohibited the sale of guns, crime would decrease. = 如果他们禁止销售枪支，犯罪率就会下降。
I wish they prohibited smoking in restaurants. = 我希望他们在餐馆里禁止吸烟。


被动语态:
The sale of alcohol was prohibited during Prohibition. = 禁酒令期间禁止销售酒精。
Smoking is prohibited in many public places. = 许多公共场所禁止吸烟。


疑问句:
Is the sale of these products prohibited? = 这些产品的销售是否被禁止？
What activities are prohibited in this park? = 这个公园禁止哪些活动？


否定句:
The law doesn't prohibit the consumption of alcohol. = 法律并不禁止饮酒。
They didn't prohibit the gathering. = 他们没有禁止这次聚会。



PHRASE->
under prohibition = 在禁止之下
a period of prohibition = 一段禁令时期
total prohibition = 完全禁止
temporary prohibition = 暂时禁止
legal prohibition = 法律禁止
