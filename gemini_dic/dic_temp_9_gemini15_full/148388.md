oversquare:/ˌoʊvərˈskwɛr/| adj. | 过方的，方形度过大的 |  Describes a shape, typically a rectangle or square, where one dimension (usually the length) is significantly longer than the other (usually the width), resulting in a longer and narrower appearance than a true square or a perfectly proportioned rectangle.  It often implies an imbalance or disproportion. | The artist deliberately used an oversquare canvas to emphasize the verticality of the subject.  | rectangular, oblong | square, perfectly proportioned |
