railroad:/ˈreɪlˌroʊd/| n. vt.|铁路，铁路公司；强行通过；阻挠|A railroad is a system of tracks along which trains travel.  It can also refer to the company that operates the railroad, or the act of forcing something through without proper consideration. |例句：The Trans-Siberian Railroad is one of the longest railroads in the world.  西伯利亚大铁路是世界上最长的铁路之一。

例句：They railroad the bill through Congress without proper debate. 他们未经适当辩论就强行通过了该法案。|近义词：railway,tramway, railway line|反义词：road, highway

词源:
The word "railroad" is a compound word.  "Rail" comes from Old French "raille," meaning "rod," "stake," or "bar."  "Road" has its roots in Old English "rōd," meaning "a ridden way."  The combination evolved to refer to a path of rails for trains.
