organelle:/ˈɔːrɡənɛl/| n. |细胞器|A specialized subunit within a cell that has a specific function.  Organelles are analogous to the organs of a multicellular organism. For example, mitochondria are organelles that generate energy for the cell.|Example:  The mitochondrion is a vital organelle responsible for cellular respiration.  线粒体是负责细胞呼吸的重要细胞器。|近义词：cell component, subcellular structure |反义词：cell (as a whole)
