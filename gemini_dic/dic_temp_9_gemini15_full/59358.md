earned:/ɜːrnd/ | /ɝːnd/ | v. | 获得；挣得；赢得 | To gain something, typically money, through work or effort.  |例句：She earned a lot of money last year. 她去年赚了很多钱。|例句：He earned a reputation for honesty. 他赢得了诚实的声誉。|近义词：gained, acquired, won, achieved | 反义词：lost, forfeited


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He earns a good salary. = 他赚取不错的薪水。
She earns her living as a teacher. = 她以教书为生。

一般过去时:
I earned a lot of money last month. = 我上个月赚了很多钱。
They earned a victory against their rivals. = 他们战胜了对手。


现在完成时:
He has earned a promotion. = 他获得了晋升。
She has earned the respect of her colleagues. = 她赢得了同事们的尊重。


过去完成时:
He had earned enough money to buy a car. = 他已经赚够了买车的钱。
By the time she retired, she had earned a considerable fortune. = 到她退休的时候，她已经赚了一大笔钱。


将来时:
I will earn more money next year. = 明年我将会赚更多钱。
They will have earned enough by the end of the year. = 到年底他们将赚够足够的钱。


虚拟语气:
If I worked harder, I would earn more. = 如果我工作更努力，我会赚更多。
I wish I had earned more money last year. = 我希望我去年赚更多钱。


被动语态:
A lot of money was earned by the company last year. = 公司去年赚了很多钱。
The award was earned through hard work and dedication. = 这个奖项是通过辛勤工作和奉献获得的。


疑问句:
How much do you earn a year? = 你一年赚多少钱？
Did he earn a bonus this year? = 他今年获得了奖金吗？


否定句:
He didn't earn enough money to pay the bills. = 他赚的钱不够付账单。
She hasn't earned a raise yet. = 她还没加薪。


PHRASE->
earn a living = 谋生
earn one's keep = 自食其力
earn respect = 赢得尊重
earn a reputation = 赢得声誉
earn one's stripes = 赢得地位/成就
