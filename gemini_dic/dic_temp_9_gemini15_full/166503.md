presages:/ˈprɛsɪdʒɪz/| v.|预示，预兆；预感|To presage something means to be a sign that it will happen in the future.  It often implies a feeling of foreboding or anticipation, suggesting something important or momentous is about to occur.  The word often carries a sense of inevitability.|例句：The dark clouds presaged a storm.  乌云预示着暴风雨的来临。

The unusual silence in the office presaged bad news. 办公室里不同寻常的寂静预示着坏消息。


近义词：foretell, foreshadow, portend, augur, forebode|反义词：ignore, disregard

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The dark clouds presage a storm. = 乌云预示着暴风雨的来临。
The unusual silence presages bad news. = 不同寻常的寂静预示着坏消息。


一般过去时:
The old woman's words presaged a tragic event. = 老妇人的话预示着一场悲剧。
The falling barometer presaged the hurricane. = 气压计下降预示着飓风的到来。


现在完成时:
Recent events have presaged a major economic downturn. = 最近发生的事件预示着经济将出现大幅下滑。
The signs have presaged a change in leadership. = 这些迹象预示着领导层的变动。


过去完成时:
The early warning signs had presaged the company's collapse. = 早期的预警信号已经预示了公司的倒闭。
The tremors had presaged the earthquake. = 震动预示着地震的到来。


将来时:
Experts predict that this trend will presage a new era in technology. = 专家预测这一趋势将预示着科技新时代的到来。
Analysts believe this decision will presage further reforms. = 分析人士认为这一决定将预示着进一步的改革。


虚拟语气:
If only I had heeded the signs that presaged my failure. = 如果我当初注意到了预示我失败的迹象就好了。
If the rumors were true, they would presage a major political upheaval. = 如果谣言是真的，它们将预示着重大的政治动荡。


被动语态:
The storm was presaged by dark clouds. = 暴风雨是由乌云预示的。
The economic crisis was presaged by several warning signs. = 经济危机是由几个警告信号预示的。


疑问句:
Does this event presage a wider conflict? = 这一事件是否预示着更大范围的冲突？
What does this trend presage for the future? = 这一趋势对未来预示着什么？


否定句:
The initial signs did not presage the severity of the problem. = 最初的迹象并没有预示问题的严重性。
Nothing presaged the sudden outbreak of violence. = 没有什么预示着暴力事件的突然爆发。


PHRASE->
presage disaster = 预示灾难
presage doom = 预示厄运
presage trouble = 预示麻烦
presage success = 预示成功
presage war = 预示战争

词源:
源自古法语 *presager,  来自拉丁语 praesagire "to feel beforehand," from prae- "before" + sagire "to perceive keenly, divine, feel."
