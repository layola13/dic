millilitres:/ˈmɪlɪˌliːtərz/| n. |毫升|A millilitre (mL or ml) is a unit of volume in the metric system, equal to one thousandth of a litre.  It is commonly used to measure small volumes of liquids. |The recipe calls for 250 millilitres of milk.  这个食谱需要250毫升牛奶。

The doctor prescribed 5 millilitres of medicine three times a day. 医生开了每天三次，每次5毫升药的处方。|近义词：cubic centimetres (cc)|反义词：litres, kilolitres|名词

词源:
milli- (from Latin mille, meaning "thousand") + litre (from Greek litra, meaning "pound"  – originally a unit of weight, later adapted for volume).  Therefore, millilitre literally means "one-thousandth of a litre".
