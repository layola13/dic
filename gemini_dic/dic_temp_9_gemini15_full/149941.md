pallid:/ˈpæləd/| adj.|苍白的，灰白的，无生气的|Pale, typically because of illness or fear.  It suggests a lack of color and often implies a sickly or unhealthy appearance.|例句：Her face was pallid with fear.  她的脸吓得苍白。|例句：The pallid moonlight cast long shadows.  苍白的月光投下长长的影子。|近义词：pale, wan, ashen, pasty|反义词：ruddy, florid, flushed

词源:
From Middle English *pallid, from Latin pallidus "pale," from pallere "to be pale."  The ultimate origin is likely a Proto-Indo-European root related to the idea of being weak or lacking in strength.
