knickerbocker:/ˈnɪkərˌbɒkər/| n. adj.|尼克尔博克裤；（尤指17世纪荷兰人的）宽松短裤；（美国）纽约客；|A knickerbocker is a loose-fitting short trouser, especially one worn by the Dutch in the 17th century.  The term is also used informally to refer to a New Yorker.  As an adjective, it describes things relating to New York or New Yorkers.|例句：He wore traditional knickerbockers while cycling. 他骑自行车时穿着传统的尼克尔博克裤。

The term "Knickerbocker" is often associated with New York City due to its historical connection to the early Dutch settlers of New Netherland (the area that later became New York).|近义词：breeches, plus fours (for the clothing)|反义词：trousers, long pants (for the clothing)
