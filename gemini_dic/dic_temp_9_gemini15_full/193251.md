seedier:/ˈsiːdiər/| adj.|更破旧的，更肮脏的，更卑鄙的 | More disreputable or sordid; shabbier or more dilapidated.  It suggests a decline in quality, morality, or cleanliness. |例句：The seedier parts of town are best avoided at night.  夜晚最好避开城里比较破败的地区。

The bar was seedier than I remembered. 酒吧比我记得的还要破旧。


近义词：shabbier, dirtier, more squalid, disreputable, sleazy, louche


反义词：cleaner, more respectable, pristine, upscale


词源：源于seed (种子)的比较级，引申义为“更像种子的”，暗示着事物堕落、腐败或低劣的本质，如同种子腐烂或发霉。  The original meaning referenced the look of seeds, implying something run-down or decaying.  This evolved to encompass moral decay and disrepute.
