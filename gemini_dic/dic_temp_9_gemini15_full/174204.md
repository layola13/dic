quelling: /ˈkwɛlɪŋ/ | v. | 压制；平息；使平静 | To suppress or put an end to something, especially a disturbance or disorder.  | 例句：The police quickly quelled the riot.  警察迅速平息了暴乱。


The government took swift action to quell the rebellion. 政府迅速采取行动平息了叛乱。 | 近义词：suppress, subdue, quell, pacify, stifle | 反义词：incite, provoke, agitate

词源:
The word "quell" comes from the Old English word *cwellan*, meaning "to kill, to slay, to put to death".  Over time, the meaning shifted to focus on suppressing something, rather than literally killing it.
