self-parodist:/ˈsɛlf ˈpærədɪst/| n. |自嘲者，自我模仿者 |A self-parodist is someone who satirizes or mocks their own work, persona, or characteristics.  They often use humor to undermine their own image or previous creations.  This can be done through various forms of media, including writing, performance, or visual art. | Example:  The comedian's new stand-up routine was a self-parodist masterpiece, poking fun at his earlier, more serious persona.  这位喜剧演员的新脱口秀节目是一部自嘲的杰作，戏弄了他早期较为严肃的形象。|近义词：satirist, caricaturist, comedian (in the context of self-mockery)|反义词：serious artist, self-aggrandizer
