come-in-for:/ˌkʌmˈɪnˈfɔːr/| phrasal verb|招致；遭受；承受；引起|To receive or experience something, typically something unpleasant.|The new policy has come in for a lot of criticism. 新政策招致了很多批评。|近义词：attract, receive, incur|反义词：avoid, escape, evade


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The new restaurant comes in for a lot of positive reviews. = 这家新餐厅收到了很多好评。
His impulsive decisions often come in for criticism from his colleagues. = 他冲动的决定经常遭到同事的批评。


一般过去时:
The film came in for harsh criticism from the reviewers. = 这部电影受到了影评人的严厉批评。
Her outspoken views came in for considerable debate. = 她直言不讳的观点引起了相当大的争论。


现在完成时:
The government's new tax plan has come in for much opposition. = 政府的新税收计划受到了很多反对。
His latest novel has come in for both praise and condemnation. = 他的最新小说既受到了赞扬也受到了谴责。


过去完成时:
Before the release, the game had already come in for some negative press. = 在发行之前，这款游戏就已经收到了一些负面新闻。
The project had come in for heavy criticism before it was even finished. = 这个项目在完成之前就已经受到了严厉批评。


将来时:
The proposed changes are likely to come in for strong resistance. = 提出的改变可能会遭到强烈的抵制。
His new book will undoubtedly come in for much discussion. = 他的新书无疑会引起很多讨论。


虚拟语气:
If the proposal were to be implemented, it would certainly come in for much debate. = 如果该提案付诸实施，它肯定会引起很多争论。
I wish my ideas hadn't come in for so much ridicule. = 我真希望我的想法没有受到那么多的嘲笑。


被动语态:
The plan was not expected to come in for such strong opposition. = 该计划并未预料到会遭到如此强烈的反对。
The movie is coming in for a lot of negative reviews. = 这部电影正在遭受大量的负面评价。


疑问句:
Will the new law come in for much public scrutiny? = 新法律会受到公众的严格审查吗？
Does this decision risk coming in for criticism? = 这个决定有风险招致批评吗？


否定句:
The project did not come in for as much criticism as we had feared. = 这个项目并没有像我们担心的那样受到那么多的批评。
The speech didn't come in for much applause. = 这篇演讲并没有得到多少掌声。



PHRASE->
come in for criticism = 招致批评
come in for attack = 遭到攻击
come in for praise = 受到赞扬
come in for scrutiny = 受到仔细审查
come in for a share of the blame = 分担一些责任
