conclamation:/ˌkɒŋkləˈmeɪʃən/ | n. |欢呼；喧嚣；大声叫喊 |A loud outcry or shouting, especially of a crowd expressing approval or excitement.  |例句：The conclamation of the crowd was deafening as the winning team took the field.  人群的欢呼声震耳欲聋，获胜的球队走上了赛场。

例句：The conclamation that greeted the speaker was a clear indication of their popularity.  迎接演讲者的欢呼声清楚地表明了他们的受欢迎程度。


近义词：acclaim, cheering, applause, shout, roar | 反义词：silence, quiet, hush


词性：名词


词源:  From Latin *conclamatio, from con- + clamare "to shout".  The word combines the prefix "con-" (together) and the verb "clamare" (to shout), thus literally meaning "a shouting together".
