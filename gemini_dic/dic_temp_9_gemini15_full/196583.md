set-back:/ˈsɛtˌbæk/| n. |挫折；倒退；障碍 |A setback is a difficulty or problem that delays or prevents progress.  It can refer to a temporary delay or a more significant reversal of progress. |例句：The project suffered a setback due to unexpected rain.  这个项目因为突如其来的雨而受阻。  The recent economic downturn is a major setback for the company. 最近的经济衰退对公司来说是一个重大挫折。|近义词：obstacle, impediment, reverse, drawback |反义词：advancement, progress, success |名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The rain presents a setback to our plans. = 雨给我们计划带来了挫折。
This delay is a significant setback for the project. = 这次延误对项目来说是一个重大挫折。


一般过去时:
The accident was a major setback for his career. = 那次事故对他事业造成了重大挫折。
The unexpected storm caused a significant setback to the harvest. = 突如其来的暴风雨对收成造成了重大挫折。


现在完成时:

We have had a few setbacks, but we are still on track. = 我们遇到了一些挫折，但我们仍在正轨上。


过去完成时:
They had already overcome several setbacks before achieving success. = 他们在取得成功之前已经克服了许多挫折。
By the time the project finished, they had faced numerous setbacks. = 到项目结束时，他们已经面临了无数的挫折。


将来时:

We anticipate some setbacks in the initial stages of the project. = 我们预计项目初期会遇到一些挫折。


虚拟语气:

被动语态:
