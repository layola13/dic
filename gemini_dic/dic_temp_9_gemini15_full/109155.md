legislatures:/ˈlɛdʒɪsleɪtʃərz/ | n. |立法机关；立法机构 |A legislature is a group of people who have the power to make laws.  It typically consists of elected representatives.  The composition and structure of legislatures vary widely across different countries. |例句：The national legislature passed a new tax law.  全国立法机关通过了一项新的税法。

The state legislatures are responsible for creating their own state laws. 各州立法机关负责制定本州法律。


近义词：lawmaking body, parliament, congress, assembly | 反义词：None (no direct antonym) | 词性：名词
