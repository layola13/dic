observership: /əbˈzɜːrvərʃɪp/ | n. |观察员身份；观察员职位 | The state or position of being an observer.  It often refers to a formal role, perhaps with specific responsibilities, within an organization or at an event.  The observership might involve attending meetings, monitoring activities, or reporting findings. | Example:  Her observership at the United Nations gave her valuable insights into international relations.  她的联合国观察员身份让她对国际关系有了宝贵的见解。   The observership program allows students to gain practical experience.  观察员项目让学生们能够获得实践经验。|近义词：observation post, onlooker status, monitoring role |反义词：participantship, membership
