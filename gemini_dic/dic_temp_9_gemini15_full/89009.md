hollow:/ˈhɑːloʊ/ /ˈhɒləʊ/| adj., n., v.|空心的；空虚的；凹陷的|

英文解析:
As an adjective, "hollow" describes something that is empty inside, or lacking substance or meaning.  As a noun, it refers to a hole or cavity. As a verb, it means to make something hollow.


例句:
The tree trunk was hollow. = 树干是空心的。

The victory felt hollow without the satisfaction of a hard-fought battle. = 没有经过一番苦战取得的胜利感觉空虚。

The wind howled through the hollow. = 风在山谷中呼啸。

The sculptor hollowed out the inside of the statue. = 雕塑家掏空了雕像的内部。


近义词:
empty, vacant, void,  cavernous (for large hollows),  unsubstantial


反义词:
solid, filled, substantial,  full, complete


词性:
adjective, noun, verb
