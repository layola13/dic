iodate:/ˈaɪədeɪt/| n.|碘酸盐|Iodate is a salt or ester of iodic acid.  It typically refers to the anion IO₃⁻, which is formed by the oxidation of iodide. Iodates are used in various applications, including as a source of iodine in food and as an oxidizing agent in chemical reactions.|The potassium iodate is used as a source of iodine in food.|近义词: iodate salt|反义词:N/A
