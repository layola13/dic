roasts:/roʊsts/| /rəʊsts/ | v. n. | 烤肉；烘烤；取笑；严厉批评 | to cook (food) by dry heat without direct exposure to a flame, typically in an oven or over hot coals; to criticize harshly | 例句：She roasted a chicken for dinner. 她烤了一只鸡当晚餐。  The comedian roasted the politician mercilessly.  这位喜剧演员无情地嘲讽了这位政治家。|近义词：bake, grill, criticize, satirize | 反义词：praise, commend


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He roasts vegetables every Sunday. = 他每个星期天都烤蔬菜。
The chef roasts the meat over an open fire. = 厨师在篝火上烤肉。


一般过去时:
She roasted a turkey for Thanksgiving. = 她烤了一只火鸡过感恩节。
They roasted marshmallows around the campfire. = 他们在篝火旁烤棉花糖。


现在完成时:
I have roasted a chicken three times this week. = 我这周已经烤了三次鸡肉。
She has never roasted a whole pig before. = 她以前从未烤过整猪。


过去完成时:
He had roasted the potatoes before the guests arrived. = 客人到达之前，他已经烤好了土豆。
We had already roasted the marshmallows before it started to rain. = 下雨之前我们已经烤好棉花糖了。


将来时:
I will roast a leg of lamb for the party. = 我将为派对烤一条羊腿。
They will roast the coffee beans before grinding them. = 他们会在研磨咖啡豆之前先烘烤它们。


虚拟语气:
If I had more time, I would roast a duck. = 如果我有更多时间，我会烤一只鸭子。
I wish I had roasted the vegetables longer. = 我希望我把蔬菜烤得久一点。


被动语态:
The chicken was roasted to perfection. = 鸡肉烤得完美无缺。
The marshmallows are being roasted over the fire. = 棉花糖正在火上烤。


疑问句:
