hydrophyte:/ˈhaɪdrəfaɪt/| n. |水生植物|A hydrophyte is a plant that grows in water, either wholly or partially submerged.  Hydrophytes are adapted to their aquatic environment, often exhibiting features such as floating leaves, reduced root systems, and aerenchyma (air spaces) in their tissues. | Example: Water lilies and duckweed are common examples of hydrophytes. 睡莲和浮萍是常见的例子。|近义词：aquatic plant, water plant|反义词：xerophyte
