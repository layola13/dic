medit:/ˈmɛdɪt/| v.|沉思，冥想|To think deeply or carefully about something; to engage in contemplation or reflection.|例句：I often meditate to clear my mind. 我经常冥想以清理我的思绪。|例句：She meditated on the meaning of life. 她沉思生命的意义。|近义词：contemplate, contemplate, reflect, ponder|反义词：act, rush


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I meditate every morning. = 我每天早上都冥想。
He meditates before important decisions. = 他在做重要决定之前会冥想。


一般过去时:
I meditated for an hour yesterday. = 我昨天冥想了一个小时。
She meditated on the problem all night. = 她整夜都在思考这个问题。


现在完成时:
I have meditated daily for a year. = 我已经每天冥想一年了。
She has meditated on this matter for weeks. = 她已经思考这个问题好几个星期了。


过去完成时:
I had meditated for years before I found inner peace. = 在我找到内心平静之前，我已经冥想了许多年。
She had meditated extensively before giving her speech. = 在发表演讲之前，她做了大量的思考。


将来时:
I will meditate tomorrow morning. = 我明天早上会冥想。
He will meditate before the interview. = 他会在面试前冥想。


虚拟语气:
If I had more time, I would meditate more often. = 如果我有更多的时间，我会更频繁地冥想。
I wish I could meditate without distractions. = 我希望我能不受干扰地冥想。


被动语态:
The benefits of meditation are widely discussed. = 冥想的益处被广泛讨论。
The technique was carefully meditated upon by the master. = 这位大师仔细地思考了这项技术。


疑问句:
Do you meditate regularly? = 你经常冥想吗？
How often does he meditate? = 他多久冥想一次？


否定句:
I don't meditate often enough. = 我冥想不够频繁。
He didn't meditate before the exam. = 他考试前没有冥想。


PHRASE->
meditate on = 沉思...

guided meditation = 引导式冥想
