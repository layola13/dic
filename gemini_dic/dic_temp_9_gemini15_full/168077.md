privileges:/ˈprɪvəlɪdʒɪz/| n. |特权；优待；权利|A special right, advantage, or immunity granted or available only to a particular person or group.|例句：Members enjoy exclusive privileges, such as access to the club's private lounge. 会员享有独家特权，例如可以使用俱乐部的私人休息室。|例句：Her position gave her certain privileges unavailable to others. 她的职位赋予了她一些其他人无法享有的特权。|近义词：rights, benefits, immunities, advantages |反义词：disadvantages, liabilities, burdens, responsibilities |名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He enjoys many privileges. = 他享有很多特权。
They have special privileges. = 他们拥有特殊特权。

一般过去时:
She lost her privileges. = 她失去了她的特权。
We did not have those privileges. = 我们没有那些特权。

现在完成时:
They have enjoyed many privileges in their lives. = 他们一生享有很多特权。
She has never abused her privileges. = 她从未滥用她的特权。

过去完成时:
He had already lost his privileges before the incident. = 在事件发生之前，他已经失去了特权。
They had never experienced such privileges before. = 他们以前从未体验过这样的特权。

将来时:
He will gain more privileges. = 他将获得更多特权。
They will lose their privileges soon. = 他们很快就会失去特权。

虚拟语气:
If I had those privileges, I would travel the world. = 如果我有那些特权，我会环游世界。
I wish I had the privilege of meeting him. = 我希望我有机会见到他。

被动语态:
Privileges are often abused. = 特权经常被滥用。
His privileges were revoked. = 他的特权被撤销了。

疑问句:
What privileges do you have? = 你有什么特权？
Do they have any special privileges? = 他们有任何特殊特权吗？

否定句:
He doesn't have many privileges. = 他没有很多特权。
They don't deserve those privileges. = 他们不配拥有那些特权。

词源:
源自古法语 *privelege，源自拉丁语 *privilegium，由 *privus（私人的）和 *legis（法律）构成，字面意思为“私法”。  因此，最初是指超越普通法律的特殊权利或豁免。
