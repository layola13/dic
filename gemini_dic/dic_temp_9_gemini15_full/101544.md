ironicalness:/ˌaɪrˈɒnɪkəlnəs/ | n. | 讽刺意味；反语；具有讽刺意味的情况 | The ironicalness of the situation was that he had warned everyone about the very thing that happened.  | 近义词：irony, sarcasm, paradox | 反义词：seriousness, earnestness


USAGE->
This word is a noun and doesn't conjugate like verbs.  Therefore, tense information is not applicable.  It's used to describe the quality or state of being ironical.


例句：

The sheer ironicalness of his promotion after his disastrous speech was astounding.  = 他在发表灾难性演讲后竟然得到晋升，这情况真是令人震惊。

There's a certain dark ironicalness to the way the story ends. = 故事的结尾带有一种黑暗的讽刺意味。
