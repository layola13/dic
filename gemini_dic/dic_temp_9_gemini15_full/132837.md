noneducated: /ˌnɑːnˈɛdʒʊkeɪtɪd/ | adj. | 未受教育的，缺乏教育的 | Lacking education; uneducated.  The term often implies a lack of formal schooling or a deficiency in basic literacy and numeracy skills, rather than a lack of intelligence or knowledge gained through other means.  It can carry a negative connotation, suggesting a disadvantage or limitation. |

近义词：uneducated, illiterate,unschooled, untutored

词源:

The word is a combination of "non-" (a prefix meaning "not") and "educated" (meaning having received education).  "Educated" comes from the past participle of the verb "educate," ultimately deriving from Latin "ēducāre," meaning "to bring up, to rear, to train."
