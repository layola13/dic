seraph:/ˈsɛrəf/| n. |炽天使，六翼天使|A seraph is a celestial being, often depicted in religious art and literature as having six wings.  They are typically associated with immense power, holiness, and proximity to God.  The term is primarily found in religious contexts, particularly within Abrahamic traditions.|Example: In the vision, John saw a seraph standing before the throne of God.  在异象中，约翰看见一个炽天使站在上帝的宝座前。|近义词：angel, cherub (less powerful)|反义词：demon, devil
