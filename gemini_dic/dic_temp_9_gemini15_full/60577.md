elate:/ɪˈleɪt/| vt. adj.|使兴高采烈；兴高采烈的|To make (someone) feel very happy and excited.  |例句：The good news elated her. 好消息使她兴高采烈。
例句：She felt elated after winning the race. 赢得比赛后，她感到兴高采烈。|近义词：exhilarate, thrill, uplift|反义词：depress, dispirit,deject|形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The good news elates her. = 好消息使她兴高采烈。
Winning the lottery elates many people. = 中彩票使许多人兴高采烈。


一般过去时:
The victory elated the team. = 胜利使球队兴高采烈。
The news of her promotion elated her parents. = 她升职的消息使她父母兴高采烈。


现在完成时:
The success of her project has elated her. = 她项目的成功使她兴高采烈。
This good news has elated me all day. = 这个好消息让我一整天都兴高采烈。


过去完成时:
They had been elated by the news of their son's graduation. = 他们听到儿子毕业的消息后非常高兴。
She had been elated to receive the award. = 她收到奖项后非常高兴。

虚拟语气:
If I won the lottery, I would be elated. = 如果我中彩票，我会非常高兴。

被动语态:
She was elated by the compliment. = 她因为赞美而兴高采烈。
The team was elated by their victory. = 球队因为胜利而兴高采烈。


疑问句:

否定句:

The results did not elate the team. = 结果并没有让球队高兴。

词源:
The word "elate" comes from the Latin word "elatus," the past participle of "effero," meaning "to carry up," "to raise," or "to lift up."  This reflects the sense of being lifted up in spirits.
