discredited:/dɪˈskredɪtɪd/ | adj. | 失去信誉的；被揭露为假的；被贬低的 |  Having lost credibility or reputation; shown to be false or worthless.  | The discredited theory was finally abandoned by the scientific community.  这个被揭露为错误的理论最终被科学界抛弃了。 |  disgraced, disgraced, debunked, refuted | credible, reputable, believable, valid | 形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The evidence discredits his claim. = 证据使他的说法失去了可信度。
His actions discredit the whole organization. = 他的行为使整个组织名誉受损。


一般过去时:
The scandal discredited the politician. = 这场丑闻使这位政治家名誉扫地。
The new findings discredited the old hypothesis. = 新的发现使旧的假设失去了可信度。


现在完成时:
Recent events have discredited the company's image. = 最近发生的事件损害了公司的形象。
The witness's testimony has discredited the defendant's alibi. = 目击证人的证词使被告的不在场证明失去了可信度。


过去完成时:
The evidence had discredited the suspect before the trial. = 在审判之前，证据已经使嫌疑人失去了可信度。
By the time the truth came out, the newspaper had already been discredited. = 当真相大白的时候，那家报纸已经名誉扫地了。


将来时:
Further investigation will discredit his claims. = 进一步的调查将会使他的说法失去可信度。
This new evidence will discredit the existing theory. = 这项新的证据将使现有的理论失去可信度。


虚拟语气:
If the evidence were stronger, it would discredit his argument. = 如果证据更充分，它就会使他的论点失去可信度。
I wish the media hadn't discredited the scientist's work. = 我真希望媒体没有败坏这位科学家的工作。


被动语态:
His reputation was discredited by the scandal. = 他的名誉被这场丑闻毁了。
The theory was discredited by new research. = 这个理论被新的研究推翻了。


疑问句:
Has the new evidence discredited the initial findings? = 新证据是否推翻了最初的发现？
Was the witness's testimony enough to discredit the defendant? = 目击证人的证词足以使被告失去信誉吗？


否定句:
The evidence did not discredit the witness's testimony. = 证据并没有使证人的证词失去可信度。
The study did not discredit the established theory. = 这项研究并没有推翻已有的理论。
