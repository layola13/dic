quasi-amiable: /ˈkwɑːziˈeɪmiəbl/ | adj. |似友好的；表面友好的 | Seemingly friendly but not truly warm or sincere; superficially amiable.  It suggests a pretense of friendliness, often masking underlying coldness or indifference. |例句：His quasi-amiable demeanor hid a deeply cynical nature. 他似友好的举止掩盖了他内心深处玩世不恭的本性。


例句：The quasi-amiable conversation quickly dissolved into a bitter argument.  表面友好的谈话很快演变成一场激烈的争吵。


近义词：affable, amicable (but less sincere), polite, courteous


反义词：hostile, unfriendly, antagonistic, cold, distant


词性：形容词


词源:  The word is a combination of "quasi" (meaning "seemingly" or "apparently") and "amiable" (meaning "friendly").  "Quasi" comes from Latin, while "amiable" has roots in the Latin word "amabilis," meaning "lovable."
