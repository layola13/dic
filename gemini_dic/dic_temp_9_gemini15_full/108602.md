lead-up-the-garden-path: /ˌliːd ˈʌp ðə ˈɡɑːrdən pɑːθ/ | phrasal verb | 欺骗；误导；吊人胃口 | To deceive or mislead someone, often by giving them false hopes or promises.  | Example:  They completely led me up the garden path about the job; the salary was far less than they initially promised.  他们关于这份工作的描述完全是在欺骗我；薪水比他们最初承诺的要低得多。 | 近义词：deceive, mislead, hoodwink, dupe,  trick | 反义词：be honest with, tell the truth to


USAGE->

This is a phrasal verb, and therefore doesn't have typical verb tenses in the same way a simple verb does.  Instead, the tense is determined by the verb "lead".

一般现在时:
They lead me up the garden path with their promises. = 他们用他们的承诺欺骗了我。
He leads everyone up the garden path with his charm. = 他用他的魅力欺骗了所有人。


一般过去时:
The salesman led her up the garden path about the car's features. = 销售员关于汽车的功能欺骗了她。
They led us up the garden path regarding the project's timeline. = 他们关于项目的进度欺骗了我们。


现在完成时:
They have led me up the garden path several times. = 他们已经多次欺骗我了。
She has led him up the garden path with false hopes. = 她用虚假的希望欺骗了他。


过去完成时:
The politician had led the public up the garden path for years. = 这个政治家多年来一直在欺骗公众。
I realised that they had led me up the garden path when I saw the contract. = 当我看到合同的时候，我意识到他们一直在欺骗我。


将来时:

虚拟语气:
If he knew the truth, he wouldn't have been led up the garden path. = 如果他知道真相，他就不会被欺骗了。

被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
I wasn't led up the garden path; I knew exactly what was happening. = 我没有被欺骗；我确切地知道发生了什么。
They didn't lead us up the garden path; they were very transparent. = 他们没有欺骗我们；他们非常透明。

There are no common multi-word phrases using "lead up the garden path".  The phrase itself is a complete idiomatic expression.
