rebullition:/ˌriːbəˈlɪʃən/| n. |再次沸腾；再起；骚动 |A renewed state of commotion or agitation; a fresh outbreak of turmoil or rebellion.  It implies a resurgence of something previously active, often violent or disruptive. |例句：The rebullition of the crowd after the announcement caused a stampede.  人群在公告后的再次骚动引发了踩踏事件。|例句：The rebullition of the protests surprised the government.  抗议活动的再次爆发令政府感到意外。|近义词：resurgence, revival, uprising, ferment |反义词：calm, peace, quiet, tranquility |名词
