coun: /kaʊn/ | v. | 计数，计算；认为，视为 | to count something is to determine the number of things in a set, to consider something to be of a certain importance or value  |例句：I counted ten sheep.我数了十只羊。He counts his friends as his most valuable asset. 他认为朋友是他最宝贵的财富。|近义词：calculate, enumerate, reckon, consider |反义词：neglect, disregard


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He counts the money every day. = 他每天都数钱。
They count their blessings. = 他们珍惜他们的福气。

一般过去时:
She counted the votes. = 她清点选票。
We counted the stars. = 我们数星星。

现在完成时:
I have counted to one hundred. = 我已经数到一百了。
She has counted all the apples. = 她已经数了所有的苹果。

过去完成时:
He had counted the cards before the game started. = 游戏开始前他已经数过牌了。
They had counted the losses before the meeting. = 会议前他们已经计算过损失了。

将来时:
We will count the votes tomorrow. = 我们明天将清点选票。
She will count the money later. = 她稍后会数钱。

虚拟语气:
If I counted correctly, there are twenty. = 如果我数得对，那就有二十个。
If he had counted the votes carefully, the result would have been different. = 如果他仔细清点选票，结果就会不同。

被动语态:
The money was counted by the bank teller. = 钱被银行出纳员清点了。
The votes are being counted now. = 选票正在清点中。

疑问句:
How many sheep did you count? = 你数了多少只羊？
Did you count all the items? = 你数了所有东西了吗？

否定句:
I didn't count the apples. = 我没有数苹果。
They didn't count the cost. = 他们没有计算成本。



PHRASE->
count on = 依靠，依赖
count down = 倒计时
count in = 包含在内
count out = 排除在外
count up = 总结，合计
