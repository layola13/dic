overshadowed:/ˌoʊvərˈʃædoʊd/ | v. | 使黯然失色；使相形见绌；遮蔽 |  To overshadow something means to make it less important or noticeable by comparison. It can also mean to cast a shadow over something. |

例句：

His great success overshadowed his earlier failures. = 他巨大的成功使他早期的失败黯然失色。

The tall buildings overshadowed the small houses. = 高大的建筑物遮蔽了小房子。


近义词：eclipse, obscure, dwarf, dim

反义词：highlight, illuminate, enhance

USAGE->

His achievements overshadow his faults. = 他的成就掩盖了他的缺点。
The mountain overshadows the valley. = 这座山遮蔽着山谷。


一般过去时:
