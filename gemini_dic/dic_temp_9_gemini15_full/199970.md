skanky:/ˈskæŋki/| adj.| 肮脏的；邋遢的；低俗的 |  Describes something or someone as being dirty, unkempt, or morally questionable; often implies a sexually suggestive connotation of being cheap or sleazy.  The word carries a strong negative judgment. | 例句：The apartment was skanky and needed a thorough cleaning.  = 那套公寓又脏又乱，需要彻底打扫。


The bar was full of skanky people. = 酒吧里全是些不三不四的人。


近义词：dirty, filthy, squalid, sleazy, trashy, seedy, disreputable | 反义词：clean, neat, respectable, classy, elegant


词源:The etymology of "skanky" is uncertain, but it's likely a slang term that emerged in the mid-20th century.  The word's development is probably connected to other slang words with similar negative connotations related to dirtiness or low social standing.  Its exact origins remain obscure and are not definitively documented in standard etymological resources.
