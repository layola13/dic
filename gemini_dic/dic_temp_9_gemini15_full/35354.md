co-guardian:/ˌkoʊˈɡɑːrdiən/| n. |共同监护人|A person who shares the responsibility of guardianship with another person.  A co-guardian shares legal and/or physical custody of a child or other person requiring guardianship.|The court appointed a co-guardian to assist the biological parent. 法院指定了一位共同监护人来协助生父。|The divorced parents agreed to share custody and act as co-guardians.离婚的父母同意共同监护孩子。|guardian, joint guardian|sole guardian


USAGE->

一般现在时:
They are co-guardians of their niece. = 他们是他们侄女的共同监护人。
The couple act as co-guardians for their grandchildren. = 这对夫妇担任他们孙子的共同监护人。


一般过去时:
They were co-guardians for many years. = 他们做了多年的共同监护人。
My aunt and uncle were co-guardians of me after my parents' death. = 我父母去世后，我的阿姨和叔叔是我的共同监护人。


现在完成时:

We have acted as co-guardians for our nephew for five years. = 我们已经为我们的侄子担任了五年的共同监护人。


过去完成时:

虚拟语气:
