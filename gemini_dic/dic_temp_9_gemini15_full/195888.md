sensationalization: /ˌsɛnˈseɪʃənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. | 耸人听闻；夸大其词；渲染；煽情 | The act of presenting information in a way that is designed to excite, shock, or horrify, often exaggerating or distorting facts.  It aims to generate strong emotional reactions rather than provide objective reporting. | Example: The media's sensationalization of the crime led to widespread panic and prejudice against the suspect before the trial.  媒体对这起犯罪的渲染导致审判前公众对嫌疑人产生广泛的恐慌和偏见。| 近义词：dramatization, exaggeration, hype, sensationalism | 反义词：understatement, downplaying, objectivity
