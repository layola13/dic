prerogative:/ˈprɛrəɡətɪv/| n. |特权，权利，优先权 |A prerogative is a right or privilege exclusive to a particular individual or group.  It often implies a special right or power that is not shared by others.  |例句：The president has the prerogative to veto any bill passed by Congress. 总统拥有否决国会通过的任何法案的特权。

例句：It is the prerogative of the wealthy to live in luxury. 富人有权过奢侈的生活。


近义词：privilege, right, entitlement, perquisite

反义词：duty, obligation, responsibility


词源：源自古法语 *prerogative，最终源自拉丁语praerogativa，字面意思是“先被询问的权利”，指古罗马选举中，先被询问意见的一组选民的权利。
