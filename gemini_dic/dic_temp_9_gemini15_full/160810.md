/ˈpɒsər/| n. |吹牛者，自夸者，自命不凡的人 |A poser is someone who tries to impress others by pretending to be more important or knowledgeable than they really are. They often act in a showy or affected manner.  |He's such a poser, always talking about his supposed connections. 他真是个爱摆谱的人，总是吹嘘他所谓的交际圈。|show-off, pretender |humble, modest |名词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He poses as an expert. = 他假装是专家。
She poses a threat to the company. = 她对公司构成威胁。


一般过去时:
He posed as a doctor. = 他假扮医生。
She posed a difficult question. = 她提出了一个难题。


现在完成时:
He has posed as a journalist several times. = 他已经几次假扮记者了。
This issue has posed a serious challenge to our team. = 这个问题对我们的团队提出了严峻的挑战。


过去完成时:
He had posed as a detective for years before he was caught. = 他假扮侦探多年后才被抓住。
The company had posed the problem for weeks before finding a solution. = 公司在找到解决方案之前已经提出了这个问题好几个星期了。


将来时:
He will pose as a businessman at the conference. = 他将在会议上假扮商人。
The new regulations will pose a challenge to small businesses. = 新规将对小型企业构成挑战。


虚拟语气:
If he posed as a lawyer, no one would suspect him. = 如果他假扮律师，没有人会怀疑他。
I wish he wouldn't pose such difficult questions. = 我希望他不提出这么难的问题。


被动语态:
The question was posed by a member of the audience. = 这个问题是由一位听众提出的。
The problem is being posed by the changing market conditions. = 这个问题是由不断变化的市场环境造成的。


疑问句:
What question did he pose? = 他提出了什么问题？
How will this situation be posed to the public? = 这种情况将如何向公众解释？


否定句:
He didn't pose any serious threats. = 他没有构成任何严重的威胁。
She doesn't pose for photographs. = 她不拍照。
