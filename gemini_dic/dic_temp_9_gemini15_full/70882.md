fins:/fɪnz/ | n. |鳍；鱼翅 | Thin, flat, usually paired appendages on the bodies of aquatic animals, used for propulsion, balance, and steering.  They are typically found on fish, but also on other aquatic creatures like whales and sea turtles. |例句：The dolphin used its fins to navigate the coral reef. 海豚用它的鳍在珊瑚礁中航行。The shark's fins sliced through the water. 鲨鱼的鳍划破水面。|近义词：flippers, appendages |反义词：N/A
