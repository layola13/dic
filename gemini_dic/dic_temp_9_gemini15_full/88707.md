hobblingly:/ˈhɒblɪŋli/ | adv. | 蹒跚地，跛行地 |  in a way that suggests someone is hobbling; with a limp.  Describes movement that is slow and unsteady, often due to an injury or disability affecting the legs or feet. | Example: He walked hobblingly across the room, his injured leg clearly causing him pain. 他蹒跚地穿过房间，受伤的腿显然让他很痛。 | 近义词：limpingly, awkwardly, shakily | 反义词：swiftly, smoothly, effortlessly
