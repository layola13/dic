retroaction:/ˌretrəʊˈækʃən/| n. |反作用，反馈，追溯效力 |The effect of a cause that occurs later than its effect.  It often refers to a situation where a later event influences or changes a previous event or decision.  |例句：The government's new policy had a significant retroaction on existing regulations. 政府的新政策对现行法规产生了重大影响。

The company's retroaction on the employee's complaint was swift and decisive. 公司对员工投诉的回应迅速而果断。|近义词：feedback, back-action, repercussion |反义词：proaction, preaction
