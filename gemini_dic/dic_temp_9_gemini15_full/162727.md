prebelieved:/ˌpriːbɪˈliːvd/| adj.| 预先相信的，预先认定的 |  Describes something believed before a particular event or situation.  It implies a belief formed in advance, often without sufficient evidence or based on assumptions. | Example:  The prebelieved notion that the earth was flat was eventually disproven.  预先认定地球是平的观念最终被证明是错误的。| 近义词：preconceived, pre-existing, assumed | 反义词：unbelieved, doubted
