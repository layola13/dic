irreplaceable: /ˌɪrɪˈpleɪsəbl/ | adj. | 不可替代的，必不可少的 |  Something irreplaceable is so valuable or important that it cannot be replaced.  It is unique and essential. |


例句：

His contribution to the company is irreplaceable.  他的贡献对公司来说是不可替代的。

My grandmother's antique jewelry is irreplaceable. 我祖母的古董珠宝是无价之宝，无法替代。


近义词：essential, indispensable, vital, crucial, unique

反义词：replaceable, substitutable


词源:

* **ir-**:  prefix meaning "not" or "without"
* **replace**:  verb meaning "to put something back in its place" or "to take the place of something"
* **-able**: suffix forming adjectives meaning "capable of being"


用法说明:

irreplaceable 通常作形容词，修饰名词，表示某物或某人是无法替代的。  它强调的是独特性和重要性，暗示着失去它将造成无法弥补的损失。


无语法变化表格,因为irreplaceable是形容词，没有动词变化。
