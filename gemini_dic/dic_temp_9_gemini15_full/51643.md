deviant:/ˈdiːviənt/| adj., n.| 偏差的；离经叛道的；行为异常者 | differing from a norm or standard;  a person who deviates from what is considered normal or acceptable. |例句：His deviant behavior led to his dismissal from the company. 他的行为异常导致他被公司解雇。  The artist's work was considered deviant by many critics. 许多评论家认为这位艺术家的作品是离经叛道的。|近义词：abnormal, aberrant, atypical, unorthodox |反义词：normal, conventional, orthodox, typical |


USAGE->
一般现在时:
His behavior is deviant. = 他的行为是异常的。
The data deviates from the norm. = 数据偏离了标准。


一般过去时:
His actions deviated from the plan. = 他的行动偏离了计划。
The company's profits deviated significantly last year. = 该公司去年的利润大幅偏离。


现在完成时:
He has deviated from the path of righteousness. = 他已经偏离了正道。
Their behavior has deviated from acceptable social norms. = 他们的行为已经偏离了可接受的社会规范。


过去完成时:
He had deviated from the original route before realizing his mistake. = 他在意识到错误之前已经偏离了原来的路线。
The project had deviated significantly from the initial budget. = 该项目已经严重偏离了最初的预算。


将来时:
The path will deviate sharply to the left. = 路线将急剧向左偏离。
I believe the market will deviate from its current trajectory. = 我相信市场将偏离其当前的轨迹。


虚拟语气:
If he had not deviated from the rules, he wouldn't have been punished. = 如果他没有违反规定，他就不会受到惩罚。
I wish she hadn't deviated from the original plan. = 我真希望她没有偏离最初的计划。


被动语态:
The plan was deviated from by the team. = 该计划被团队偏离了。
The rules were deviated from by several members. = 规则被几个成员违反了。


疑问句:
Does his behavior deviate from the norm? = 他的行为是否偏离了常态？
How much did the results deviate from the predictions? = 结果与预测偏离了多少？


否定句:
His actions did not deviate from the company's policy. = 他的行为没有违反公司政策。
The data did not deviate significantly from the expected values. = 数据没有显著偏离预期值。
