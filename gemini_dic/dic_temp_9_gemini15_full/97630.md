inherent:/ɪnˈhɪərənt/ | adj. | 内在的；固有的；与生俱来的 | Existing in something as a permanent, essential, or characteristic attribute.  Something inherent is a basic or fundamental part of something else. |

例句：
The inherent risks of mountain climbing are well known.  登山的固有风险是众所周知的。

There are inherent dangers in this type of work.  这类工作存在固有的危险。


近义词：intrinsic, innate, inborn, ingrained, natural, fundamental

反义词：acquired, extrinsic, external, added


词源：
Middle English: from Latin *inhaerēns, present participle of inhaerēre ‘stick in, cling to’, from in- ‘in’ + haerēre ‘stick’.
