hydrofracturings:/ˌhaɪdroʊˈfrækʃərɪŋz/ | n. | 水力压裂（复数） | The process of fracturing rock formations by injecting high-pressure fluid, typically water, sand, and chemicals, to extract oil or natural gas.  This is a plural form of hydrofracturing, referring to multiple instances of the process. | Example:  The environmental impact of extensive hydrofracturings in the region is a major concern.  该地区大规模水力压裂的环保影响是一个主要问题。  | 近义词: hydraulic fracturing, fracking | 反义词:  (No direct antonym, but concepts opposed to it include) conventional drilling,  surface mining.
