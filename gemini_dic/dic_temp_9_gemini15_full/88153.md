hillbillies:/ˈhɪlˌbɪliz/| n. |乡下人；山区穷人 |Rural people, often stereotyped as uneducated, poor, and having a distinct culture, typically from the Appalachian region of the United States.  The term is considered offensive and derogatory due to its negative stereotypes. |例句：The movie portrayed a stereotypical family of hillbillies living in poverty. 这部电影描绘了一个生活在贫困中的典型山区穷人家庭。

例句：He used the term "hillbillies" to refer to the residents of the remote mountain community, causing offense to many. 他用“乡巴佬”来称呼偏远山区社区的居民，这冒犯了许多人。


近义词：mountaineers, backwoodsmen, country folk (less offensive) |反义词：city dwellers, urbanites


词源:The term originated in the late 19th and early 20th centuries in the Appalachian region of the United States. It is a combination of "hill" and "folks" or "people."  The term initially referred to people living in the hills, but over time it became associated with negative stereotypes of poverty, lack of education, and unsophisticated manners.
