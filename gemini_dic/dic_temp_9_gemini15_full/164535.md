preestimating: /ˌpriːˈɛstɪmeɪtɪŋ/ | v. | 预估，预先估计 |  To estimate or assess something before it happens. It implies making a judgment or calculation in advance, often based on incomplete information or projections.  |例句：The team was preestimating the project costs before the detailed budget was finalized.  团队在最终确定详细预算之前就对项目成本进行了预估。


The company was preestimating the market demand for their new product. 公司正在预估其新产品的市场需求。|近义词：forecasting, projecting, anticipating, predicting |反义词：retrospecting, reviewing (after the fact)
