rations:/ˈræʃənz/| n.|定量供应；口粮|Food or supplies, especially food, given out in fixed amounts, especially to soldiers or to people in a time of shortage.|The soldiers' rations consisted of hardtack, salt pork, and beans.|近义词：allowance, allotment, portion|反义词：abundance, surplus|名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The army rations its soldiers carefully. = 军队仔细地给士兵配给食物。
She rations her chocolate to make it last longer. = 她定量吃巧克力以延长它的食用时间。


一般过去时:
They rationed food during the war. = 战争期间他们对食物实行配给。
The government rationed gasoline last year. = 政府去年对汽油实行配给。


现在完成时:
We have rationed our supplies for the long journey. = 我们为长途旅行节省了我们的补给。
They have rationed water since the drought began. = 自从干旱开始以来，他们就开始节约用水了。


过去完成时:
They had rationed fuel for months before the crisis hit. = 在危机来临之前，他们已经节约燃料好几个月了。
The city had rationed electricity during the previous winter. = 该城市在之前的冬天实行过电力配给。


将来时:
We will ration our food if the storm lasts longer. = 如果暴风雨持续时间更长，我们将定量供应食物。
The authorities will probably ration water in the coming weeks. = 当局可能会在接下来的几周内对水实行配给。


虚拟语气:
If we had rationed our resources, we wouldn't be in this mess. = 如果我们节约资源，我们就不会陷入困境。
I wish we had rationed our supplies earlier. = 我希望我们早点节省我们的补给。


被动语态:

疑问句:
