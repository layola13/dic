orthoscope:/ˈɔːrθəskoʊp/| n. |正视镜；正像镜|An orthoscope is an optical instrument that provides a direct, upright image, without inversion or magnification.  It is often used in ophthalmology for examining the eye. |Example: The ophthalmologist used an orthoscope to examine the patient's retina.  眼科医生用正视镜检查病人的视网膜。|近义词：ophthalmoscope (but with a wider range of uses)|反义词：无
