kipchaks:/ˈkɪptʃæks/| n.|钦察人，钦察语|A Turkic-speaking nomadic people who historically inhabited the vast Eurasian Steppe.  They played a significant role in the history of Central Asia and Eastern Europe. Their language, Kipchak, influenced many other Turkic languages.|The Kipchaks were known for their horsemanship and military prowess.|近义词：Cuman, Polovtsian|反义词：无
