hypocapnia:/ˌhaɪpəʊˈkæpniə/| n. |低碳酸血症 |A condition characterized by abnormally low levels of carbon dioxide in the blood.  It can result from hyperventilation (rapid or deep breathing), certain lung diseases, or other medical conditions. |Example:  Hyperventilation can lead to hypocapnia and dizziness.|近义词：hypocarbia|反义词：hypercapnia
