paraxial:/pærˈæksɪəl/| adj.|近轴的，近轴线的|Near the axis; referring to rays of light or other forms of radiation that are close to the optical axis of a lens or mirror.  Paraxial rays are assumed to be parallel to the optical axis and make small angles with it, simplifying calculations in geometrical optics.| Example: Paraxial approximation is commonly used in the design of optical systems.|近义词：axial (in some contexts)|反义词：marginal, peripheral
