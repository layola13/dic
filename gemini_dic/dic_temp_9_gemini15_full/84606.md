hamstrings:/ˈhæmstrɪŋz/| n.|腘绳肌，腿筋|The hamstrings are a group of three muscles at the back of the thigh that flex the knee and extend the hip.  They are crucial for walking, running, jumping, and other activities.|例句：He pulled a hamstring during the marathon. 他在马拉松比赛中拉伤了腿筋。|例句：Strengthening your hamstrings can help prevent injuries. 加强你的腘绳肌可以帮助预防受伤。|近义词：thigh muscles|反义词：无

词源:
The word "hamstring" comes from the Old English words "hamm" (meaning "leg") and "string" (meaning "sinew" or "tendon").  Originally, "hamstring" referred to the tendon behind the knee, but the term has expanded to encompass the entire group of muscles in that area.
