materialness:/məˌtɪəriˈælənəs/| n. |重要性；实质性；物质性 |The significance or relevance of something; the state or quality of being material.  It often refers to the importance of something in relation to a specific context, like accounting or law.  |例句：The materialness of the omission was debated in court.  法院对遗漏的重要性进行了辩论。

The board considered the materialness of the new information before making a decision. 董事会在做出决定之前考虑了新信息的重大意义。


近义词：significance, importance, relevance, substance|反义词：insignificance, triviality, irrelevance|词性：名词


词源：源自"material" (adj./n.) + "-ness" (suffix indicating a state or quality).  "Material" ultimately derives from the Latin "materia," meaning "matter," "substance," or "timber."  The suffix "-ness" is a common English suffix forming abstract nouns.
