megalomania: /ˌmɛɡəlɵˈmeɪniə/ | n. |夸大狂；狂妄自大；权力欲 | A mental disorder characterized by delusions of grandeur and an inflated sense of self-importance.  It involves an unrealistic belief in one's own power and importance, often accompanied by a desire for unlimited power and control.  |例句：His megalomania led him to believe he could single-handedly solve the global financial crisis. 他的夸大狂使他相信自己能够单枪匹马地解决全球金融危机。


例句：The dictator's megalomania was evident in his extravagant monuments and pronouncements. 独裁者的夸大狂在他奢侈的纪念碑和声明中显而易见。


近义词：grandiosity, delusions of grandeur, egomania, narcissism


反义词：humility, modesty, self-effacement


词源:  The word "megalomania" is derived from Greek roots: "mega" (large, great) + "mania" (madness, obsession).  Therefore, it literally means "great madness" or "obsession with greatness."
