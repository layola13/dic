nonrationalism: /ˌnɒnˈræʃənəlɪzəm/ | n. |非理性主义|The rejection of rationalism; a belief that reason is not the primary source of knowledge or justification for action, or that it is limited in its scope.  It emphasizes factors like emotion, intuition, or experience over pure reason. |Example:  The artist's approach to the problem was characterized by nonrationalism, preferring intuition over logical deduction.  非理性主义体现在这位艺术家解决问题的方法上，他更倾向于直觉而非逻辑推演。|近义词:irrationalism, anti-intellectualism|反义词:rationalism, reason
