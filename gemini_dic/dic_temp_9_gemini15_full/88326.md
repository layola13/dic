hippobosca:/ˌhɪpəˈbɒskə/ | n. | 虱蝇属 | Hippobosca is a genus of parasitic flies in the family Hippoboscidae.  They are known for their wingless or reduced-wing forms and their parasitic lifestyle on birds and mammals.  These flies are often found clinging to their hosts' fur or feathers, feeding on blood. |  The hippoboscid fly was found clinging to the sheep's wool.  这只虱蝇被发现紧紧抓住羊毛。  Several species of Hippobosca are known to infest livestock.  已知有几种虱蝇会感染牲畜。|  Hippoboscidae (family),  Lipoptena (genus) |  N/A | noun
