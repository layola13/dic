cataractic: /ˌkætəˈræktɪk/ | adj. | 白内障的；像白内障一样的；混浊的 | Relating to or affected by cataracts (a clouding of the lens of the eye).  The term can also be used metaphorically to describe something that is clouded or obscured. | 例句：The doctor diagnosed her with cataractic lens clouding. 医生诊断她患有白内障混浊的晶状体。

The patient underwent cataractic surgery to improve his vision. 病人接受了白内障手术以改善视力。


近义词：clouded, opaque, turbid, hazy | 反义词：clear, transparent, lucid


词源:  From Latin *cataracta, from Greek καταράκτης (katarráktēs), literally "waterfall," from κατα- (kata-) "down" + ῥάκος (rhakos) "rag, torn cloth." The Greek word was used to describe a waterfall, and later, a cataract in the eye, due to the resemblance of the clouded lens to a falling waterfall.
