reorganizes:/ˌriːˈɔːrɡənaɪzɪz/| v.|重新组织；改组|To restructure or reorganize something is to change its organization or structure, often to improve efficiency or effectiveness.  The company is reorganizing its sales department to improve performance. 公司正在重组销售部门以提高业绩。|近义词：restructure, revamp, reform, reorganise|反义词：disorganize, dismantle


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company reorganizes its departments annually. = 公司每年都会重组部门。
She reorganizes her files every month. = 她每个月都会整理她的文件。

一般过去时:
They reorganized the office layout last year. = 他们去年重组了办公室的布局。
The team reorganized its strategy after the setback. = 团队在遭遇挫折后重新调整了战略。

现在完成时:
The government has reorganized the education system. = 政府已经重组了教育体系。
Management has reorganized the workflow to boost productivity. = 管理层已经重组了工作流程以提高生产力。

过去完成时:
They had reorganized the company before the merger. = 在合并之前，他们已经重组了公司。
She had reorganized her notes before the presentation. = 她在演示文稿之前已经整理好了笔记。

将来时:
The CEO will reorganize the marketing team next quarter. = CEO将在下个季度重组营销团队。
We will reorganize the schedule to accommodate the new project. = 我们将重新安排时间表以适应新的项目。

虚拟语气:
If the company reorganized its structure, it would be more efficient. = 如果公司重组其结构，它将更高效。
It is essential that the team reorganize its approach. = 团队必须重新调整其方法。

被动语态:
The department was reorganized last month. = 该部门上个月被重组了。
The project plan is being reorganized to reflect the new requirements. = 项目计划正在被重新组织以反映新的要求。

疑问句:
Will the company reorganize its operations? = 公司会重组其业务吗？
Is the team planning to reorganize its workflow? = 团队计划重组其工作流程吗？

否定句:
The company did not reorganize its structure this year. = 公司今年没有重组其结构。
They haven't reorganized their sales strategy yet. = 他们还没有重新调整他们的销售策略。
