quasi-americanized:/ˈkwɑːzaɪ əˌmɛrɪkənˌaɪzd/| adj.| 美国化了的，部分美国化的 |  Having adopted some but not all aspects of American culture or lifestyle.  This term suggests an incomplete or superficial assimilation to American norms.  It implies a blending of original cultural elements with American influences. |

例句：

The restaurant menu, while featuring some traditional dishes, was quasi-americanized with the inclusion of burgers and fries.  这家餐馆的菜单，虽然有一些传统菜肴，但也加入了汉堡和薯条，使其部分美国化了。


The band's music was a quasi-americanized blend of their native folk tunes and rock and roll rhythms.  这个乐队的音乐是他们本土民歌和摇滚节奏的混合体，部分美国化了。


近义词：Americanized, partially Americanized, acculturated (to American culture)


反义词：Unamericanized, culturally distinct


词源:

* **quasi-**:  From Latin, meaning "as if," "seemingly," or "almost."  It indicates something that resembles or approximates but isn't completely.

* **Americanized**:  From "American" + "-ized," a suffix indicating the process of becoming or being made like something.
