OPEC:/ˈoʊpɛk/| n. |石油输出国组织|Organization of the Petroleum Exporting Countries, a permanent intergovernmental organization of 13 nations.  It was created in Baghdad, Iraq, with the signing of an agreement in September 1960 by five founding members: Iran, Iraq, Kuwait, Saudi Arabia, and Venezuela.  The organization's objective is to coordinate and unify the petroleum policies of its Member Countries and ensure the stabilization of oil markets in order to secure an efficient, economic and regular supply of petroleum to consumers, a steady income to producers, and a fair return on capital for those investing in the petroleum industry.|The OPEC countries control a significant portion of the world's oil reserves and production.  OPEC's decisions significantly impact global oil prices.|例句：OPEC recently announced a production cut, which is expected to raise oil prices.  石油输出国组织最近宣布减产，预计这将导致油价上涨。|近义词：oil cartel|反义词：无
