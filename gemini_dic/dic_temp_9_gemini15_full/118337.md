mericarp:/ˈmɛrɪkɑːrp/| n. |分果, 单果 |A mericarp is a type of fruit that develops from a single carpel of a compound ovary and separates at maturity into individual units, each containing a single seed.  It is a characteristic feature of certain plant families, such as the Apiaceae (carrot family) and Geraniaceae (geranium family).| Example: The mature fruit of a parsley plant breaks apart into several mericarps.  成熟的欧芹果实会分裂成几个分果。|近义词:achene (if single-seeded),  nutlet (if small and hard) |反义词:  berry, drupe (which do not separate into units)
