noblesse:/nəˈblɛs/| n. |贵族；贵族阶级；高贵；高尚 |The inherent qualities of nobility; the state or quality of being noble.  It often refers to the privileges and obligations associated with being of noble birth, including a sense of duty and responsibility towards others.  The term can also be used more broadly to describe high moral character and refined manners. |Example: The noblesse of the French aristocracy was challenged during the Revolution. 法国贵族的贵族地位在革命期间受到了挑战。|近义词：aristocracy, gentry, elite, high society |反义词：commonality, peasantry, vulgarity
