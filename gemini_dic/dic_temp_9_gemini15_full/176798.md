readjustment's:/ˌriːəˈdʒʌstmənts/| n. |重新调整；再调整 |The readjustment of the schedule was necessary due to unforeseen circumstances.  由于不可预见的情况，重新调整日程是必要的。|近义词：readaptation, realignment, modification |反义词：disruption, disorganization


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company makes readjustments to its marketing strategy every quarter. = 公司每个季度都会对营销策略进行调整。
His readjustments to life in a new city are proving challenging. = 他适应新城市生活所做的调整正在证明是具有挑战性的。


一般过去时:
They made several readjustments to the plan after the initial meeting. = 他们在第一次会议后对计划做了一些调整。
The government made a significant readjustment to its economic policy. = 政府对其经济政策进行了重大调整。


现在完成时:
The team has made numerous readjustments to improve performance. = 团队已经做了多次调整以提高绩效。
She has made a readjustment to her work schedule to accommodate her family. = 她为了照顾家庭已经调整了工作时间安排。


过去完成时:
They had made several readjustments before the final presentation. = 他们在最终演示之前已经做了一些调整。
The company had made a readjustment to its pricing structure before the launch. = 公司在产品发布之前已经调整了价格结构。


将来时:
They will make readjustments to the software based on user feedback. = 他们将根据用户反馈对软件进行调整。
The architect will make readjustments to the building design to meet safety standards. = 建筑师将调整建筑设计以符合安全标准。


虚拟语气:
If they made readjustments sooner, the project would have been completed on time. = 如果他们早点进行调整，项目就能按时完成了。
I wish I had made a readjustment to my investment strategy earlier. = 我希望我早点调整我的投资策略。


被动语态:

The budget was readjusted to accommodate the increased costs. = 预算被调整以适应增加的成本。


疑问句:
