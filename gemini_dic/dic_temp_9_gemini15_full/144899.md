over-soaped: /ˌoʊvərˈsoʊpt/ | adj. | 肥皂用量过多的；过度使用肥皂的 |  Having used too much soap; excessively soapy.  The term implies an unpleasant result, often leading to dryness or irritation. | 例句：My hands feel dry and irritated; I think I over-soaped them.  我的手又干又痒；我想我用了太多肥皂。

The fabric feels harsh and scratchy; it was clearly over-soaped during the wash. 这布料感觉粗糙刺手；洗涤时显然用了过多的肥皂。


近义词：excessively soapy, overly lathered,  too much soap


反义词：undersoaped (not a common word, but implies insufficient soap)


词性：形容词


词源:  The word is a compound of "over-" (meaning excessively or too much) and "soaped" (the past participle of the verb "to soap," meaning to apply soap to something).  The meaning is a straightforward extension of the constituent parts.
