exorhizous:/ˌɛksoʊˈraɪzəs/| adj.| 外生的；外生的（指根）|  Referring to a plant whose roots grow outwards from the stem or base, not from a taproot.  This is in contrast to endozhizous, where roots arise from a central taproot.  The term is primarily used in botany. | Example:  Many grasses are exorhizous, developing a fibrous root system.  许多草是外生的，发育出纤维状根系。 | 近义词：fibrous-rooted (纤维根的)| 反义词：endozhizous (内生的)
