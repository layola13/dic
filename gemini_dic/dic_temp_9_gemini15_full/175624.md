rankest: /ˈræŋkɪst/ | adj. | 最恶劣的；最腐败的；最臭的 |  The most extreme or unpleasant form of something. It implies a high degree of something negative, such as decay, unpleasant smell, or moral corruption. |

例句：

The rankest smell of decay filled the air. =  腐烂的恶臭充满了空气。

The rankest corruption was uncovered during the investigation. =  调查中发现了最严重的腐败行为。


近义词：most foul, most vile, most corrupt, most putrid

反义词：purest, cleanest, freshest, most wholesome


词源:

源自古英语 *ranc,  意思是 "strong-smelling, powerful,"  后来引申出"rank" 的含义，表示等级或地位，以及不好的气味或品质。 "-est" 是最高级后缀。


USAGE->
This word is an adjective, so it doesn't have tense changes like verbs.  The usage depends on the context in which it describes something as being at its most extreme negative state.


PHRASE->
There are no common set phrases using "rankest." Its use is primarily standalone within a sentence.
