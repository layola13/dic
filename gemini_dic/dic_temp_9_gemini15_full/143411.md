outflanks:/ˌaʊtˈflæŋks/ | vt. |包抄，绕过，侧翼攻击 | To attack or defeat (an enemy) by moving around their flank.  To surpass or outmaneuver (someone or something).| 例句：The general ordered his troops to outflank the enemy.  将军命令他的部队包抄敌人。

The clever politician outflanked his opponents with a surprise announcement.  这位精明的政治家通过一个意外的声明击败了他的对手。


近义词：circumvent, outmaneuver, bypass, flank

反义词：be outflanked, be defeated

词源:
The word "outflank" is a compound word combining "out-" (meaning "surpass" or "beyond") and "flank" (referring to the side of a military formation).  Its origins trace back to the military context, reflecting the maneuver of moving around the enemy's side to gain an advantage.
