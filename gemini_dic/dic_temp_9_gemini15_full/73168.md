forbiddance:/fərˈbɪdəns/| n. |禁止，阻止 |The forbiddance of smoking in public places is a good thing.  公共场所禁止吸烟是一件好事。|近义词：prohibition, ban, interdiction |反义词：permission, allowance |名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The law forbids the sale of alcohol to minors. = 法律禁止向未成年人出售酒精。
This sign forbids parking here. = 这个标志禁止在此停车。


一般过去时:
The king forbade the publication of the book. = 国王禁止出版这本书。
They forbade him from entering the building. = 他们禁止他进入大楼。


现在完成时:
The school has forbidden the use of cell phones during class. = 学校已经禁止上课期间使用手机。
No one has forbidden me to go. = 没有人禁止我去。


过去完成时:
He had forbidden her to see him again. = 他已禁止她再次见他。
They had forbidden access to the area. = 他们此前已禁止进入该区域。


将来时:
The government will forbid the import of certain goods. = 政府将禁止进口某些商品。
They will forbid him from participating in the competition. = 他们将禁止他参加比赛。


虚拟语气:
If they forbade smoking, it would be better for everyone's health. = 如果他们禁止吸烟，对每个人的健康都会更好。
I wish they forbade the use of plastic bags. = 我希望他们禁止使用塑料袋。


被动语态:
Smoking is forbidden in this building. = 本大楼禁止吸烟。
Access to the site was forbidden. = 禁止进入该地点。


疑问句:
Is smoking forbidden here? = 这里禁止吸烟吗？
Is the sale of these products forbidden? = 这些产品的销售被禁止了吗？


否定句:
The rule doesn't forbid the use of cameras. = 规定并未禁止使用相机。
They didn't forbid me to leave. = 他们没有禁止我离开。


PHRASE->
a forbiddance against = 对…的禁止
under forbiddance = 在禁止之下
a strict forbiddance = 严格禁止
the forbiddance of = …的禁止
without forbiddance = 无需禁止
