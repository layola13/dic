oversystematizing: /ˌoʊvərˈsɪstəməˌtaɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 过度系统化 |  The act of making something overly systematic, often to the point of rigidity or inefficiency.  It implies an excessive reliance on systems and procedures, potentially hindering flexibility, creativity, or common sense. | 例句：Oversystematizing the workflow stifled creativity and slowed down the project.  过度系统化工作流程扼杀了创造力并减缓了项目进度。

The company's oversystematizing of its customer service led to long wait times and frustrated clients. 公司过度系统化客户服务导致等待时间过长，客户感到沮丧。 | 近义词：overorganizing, overstructuring, bureaucratizing | 反义词：improvising, ad-libbing, winging it


词源:

* **over-**:  prefix indicating excess or to a greater degree. (来自古英语 *ofer,  意思是"超过，之上")
* **system-**: from "system," ultimately from Greek *systema ("a whole composed of parts")
* **-atizing**:  present participle suffix indicating the process of making something systematic. (来自希腊语 *-ize,  意思是"使…化")
