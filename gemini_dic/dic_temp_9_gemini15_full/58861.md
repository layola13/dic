durability:/djʊərəˈbɪləti/ | n. | 耐久性，持久性 | The ability of something to withstand wear, pressure, or damage.  The capacity of an item to last a long time without significant deterioration. | Example: The durability of the tires is impressive, even after thousands of miles.  轮胎的耐用性令人印象深刻，即使行驶了数千英里。


The durability of this material is questionable.  这种材料的耐久性值得怀疑。|  sturdiness, resilience, longevity | fragility, breakability, ephemerality |

词源:

Durability comes from the Old French word *durabilité*, derived from the Latin *durabilitas*, meaning "lastingness."  The Latin word is from *durabilis*, meaning "lasting," which in turn comes from *durare*, meaning "to last" or "to endure."
