medicating: /ˈmɛdɪkeɪtɪŋ/ | v. | 给…服药；治疗；用药物处理 | The act of administering medication to treat or alleviate symptoms of an illness or condition.  |例句：The doctor is medicating the patient for pain. 医生正在给病人止痛。

例句：She is medicating her anxiety with yoga and meditation. 她正在通过瑜伽和冥想来缓解焦虑。


近义词：treating, dosing, administering medication | 反义词：neglecting, ignoring


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He medicates his allergies daily. = 他每天都服用抗过敏药。
They medicate their children for ADHD. = 他们给患有注意力缺陷多动障碍的孩子服用药物。

一般过去时:
The nurse medicated the patient before surgery. = 护士在手术前给病人服药。
She medicated her headache with aspirin. = 她用阿司匹林治疗头痛。


现在完成时:
He has medicated his dog for fleas. = 他已经给他家狗用了驱除跳蚤的药。
They have medicated the wound to prevent infection. = 他们已经对伤口进行了药物处理以防止感染。


过去完成时:
The doctor had medicated the patient before the emergency. = 医生在紧急情况发生前已经给病人服药了。
She had medicated herself for the flight. = 为了飞行，她已经给自己服药了。


将来时:

The veterinarian will medicate the injured bird. = 兽医将治疗受伤的鸟。


虚拟语气:
If I were feeling this bad, I would medicate myself. = 如果我感觉这么难受，我会给自己服药。
If I had known it would help, I would have medicated earlier. = 如果我知道这会有帮助，我会早点服药。


被动语态:
The patient was medicated with morphine. = 病人服用吗啡。
The wound was medicated with antiseptic cream. = 伤口涂抹了抗菌药膏。


疑问句:

否定句:

The doctor advised against medicating the child unnecessarily. = 医生建议不要不必要地给孩子服药。
