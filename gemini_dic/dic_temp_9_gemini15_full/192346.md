scrimped:/skrɪmpt/| v.| 吝啬；节俭；节省；勉强维持|To use or spend as little as possible; to be frugal or economical, often to an excessive degree.  |例句：We scrimped and saved for years to buy our house. 我们省吃俭用多年才买下房子。

例句：They had to scrimp and save to afford their children's education. 他们不得不省吃俭用才能负担得起孩子的教育费用。


近义词：save, economize, stint, pinch, husband|反义词：squander, splurge, lavish

词源:源自古英语 *scrimpan，意为“减少”。


USAGE->

They scrimp on non-essential expenses. = 他们在非必要开支上省钱。
We always scrimp on holiday gifts. = 我们总是在节日礼物上省钱。

We scrimped to pay for the repairs. = 我们省吃俭用支付维修费用。

过去完成时:
