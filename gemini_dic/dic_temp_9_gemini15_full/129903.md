non-issue:/ˈnɒnɪʃuː/| /ˈnɑːnɪʃuː/| n.|非问题；无关紧要的事|Something that is not a significant problem or matter worthy of attention or discussion.  It is often used to dismiss a concern as unimportant or trivial.|例句：His concerns about the budget were dismissed as a non-issue.  他的预算担忧被认为是无关紧要的。
例句：The proposed changes were deemed a non-issue by the committee.  该委员会认为拟议的更改无关紧要。|近义词：trifle, triviality, insignificance|反义词：major issue, significant problem, crucial matter|名词

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The budget is a non-issue for them. = 预算对他们来说不是问题。
This matter is a non-issue in this context. = 在这种情况下，这件事无关紧要。

一般过去时:
The pollution issue was considered a non-issue by the company. = 公司认为污染问题无关紧要。
His complaints were dismissed as a non-issue. = 他的抱怨被认为无关紧要。

现在完成时:
The concerns about safety have become a non-issue. = 关于安全的担忧已经成为无关紧要的事。
The controversy has proved to be a non-issue. = 这场争议已被证明无关紧要。

过去完成时:
The question of funding had already been declared a non-issue. = 资金问题已被宣布为无关紧要。
The problem of staffing had been considered a non-issue before the expansion. = 在扩张之前，人员配备问题曾被认为无关紧要。


将来时:
The environmental impact will be a non-issue with the new technology. = 新技术将使环境影响成为无关紧要的问题。
This problem will likely be a non-issue in the future. =  这个问题将来很可能无关紧要。


虚拟语气:
If the weather was good, it would be a non-issue. = 如果天气好，那就无关紧要了。
If we had more money, this would be a non-issue. = 如果我们有更多钱，这将无关紧要。

被动语态:
The problem was considered a non-issue by the authorities. = 当局认为这个问题无关紧要。
The objections were declared a non-issue by the board. =  董事会宣布这些异议无关紧要。


疑问句:
Is this really a non-issue? = 这真的是无关紧要的吗？
Is the cost a non-issue for this project? =  对于这个项目来说，成本是无关紧要的吗？

否定句:
This is not a non-issue; it's a serious problem. = 这不是无关紧要的事；这是一个严重的问题。
The lack of funding is not a non-issue; it needs to be addressed. = 资金不足不是无关紧要的事；需要解决。
