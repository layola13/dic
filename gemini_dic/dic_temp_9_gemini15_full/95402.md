inchoative: /ɪnˈkoʊeɪtɪv/ | adj. | 开始的；初期的；起初的 |  Denoting a verb, adjective, or noun that describes the beginning of an action, process, or state.  An inchoative verb indicates the beginning of a state or action.  |例句：The inchoative verb "to begin" describes the start of an action.  =  动词“开始”是一个起始动词，它描述了一个动作的开始。

The inchoative stage of the project is crucial. = 项目的起始阶段至关重要。

近义词：initial, nascent, incipient, commencing | 反义词：culminating, final, concluding | 词性：adjective


词源:  From Latin *inchoativus, from inchoare "to begin," from in- "in" + co- "together" + -are (suffix forming verbs).


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The project is beginning to show results. = 项目开始显现成果。
He is becoming more confident. = 他变得越来越自信。


一般过去时:
The rain started to fall. = 雨开始下起来了。
She began to understand. = 她开始明白了。


现在完成时:
The plant has begun to grow. = 植物已经开始生长了。
He has started to learn French. = 他已经开始学习法语了。


过去完成时:
The birds had started singing before sunrise. =  日出前鸟儿们已经开始唱歌了。
They had begun to pack their bags before the announcement. = 他们在宣布之前就已经开始收拾行李了。


将来时:
The meeting will commence at 9 am. = 会议将于上午9点开始。
The work will soon begin. = 工作很快就会开始。


虚拟语气:
If it began to rain, we would go inside. = 如果开始下雨，我们就进去。
I wish I had started earlier. = 我希望我早点开始。


被动语态:
The work was begun last week. = 工作是上周开始的。
The project is being initiated. = 项目正在启动。


疑问句:
Has the meeting commenced yet? = 会议开始了吗？
Is the process beginning to slow down? = 过程开始减慢了吗？


否定句:
The rain hasn't started yet. = 雨还没开始下。
He didn't begin to work until late. = 他直到很晚才开始工作。
