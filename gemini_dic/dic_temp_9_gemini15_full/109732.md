lever:/ˈliːvər/ | n. |杠杆；控制杆；手段 | A simple machine consisting of a rigid bar that pivots about a fixed point (fulcrum) and is used to transmit force.  It is also used figuratively to refer to something that gives power or control. |例句：He used a lever to pry open the lid. 他用撬棍撬开了盖子。

例句：The company's new marketing campaign is a lever for increased sales. 公司新的营销活动是提高销量的杠杆。


近义词：handle, control, tool, means | 反义词：obstruction, impediment

词源:
The word "lever" comes from the Old French word "levier," meaning "to raise."  This, in turn, has roots in the Latin word "levare," also meaning "to raise" or "to lift."  The evolution reflects the primary function of a lever as a tool for lifting or moving heavy objects.
