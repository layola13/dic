clambake:/ˈklæmbeɪk/| n.| 蛤蜊宴会；海滩野餐|A clambake is a social gathering, typically outdoors, where clams and other seafood are cooked over an open fire, often with other foods such as corn on the cob and potatoes.  It's often associated with New England coastal regions. |例句：We're having a clambake on the beach this weekend.  这个周末我们在海滩上举行一次蛤蜊宴会。

例句：The family enjoyed a traditional New England clambake with lobster, clams, and corn.  这个家庭享受了一次传统的包括龙虾，蛤蜊和玉米的新英格兰蛤蜊宴会。


近义词：beach picnic, seafood feast, outdoor barbecue|反义词：indoor dinner party

词源:
The word "clambake" is a compound word formed from "clam" and "bake".  "Clam" refers to the shellfish, and "bake" refers to the cooking method, typically involving burying the clams in hot coals or rocks. The term originated in New England, reflecting the region's history of clam harvesting and outdoor cooking traditions.
