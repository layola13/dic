perorational: /ˌpɛrəʊˈreɪʃənəl/ | adj. | 结尾的；总结性的 | Relating to or characteristic of a peroration; forming a conclusion.  It describes the concluding part of a speech or writing, often characterized by a summary or emphatic reiteration of the main points.  |


例句：

The perorational paragraph powerfully summarized the arguments presented earlier.  = 结尾段落有力地总结了前面提出的论点。

His perorational remarks left a lasting impression on the audience. = 他结尾的讲话给听众留下了深刻的印象。


近义词：concluding, final, summative, summary

反义词：initial, introductory, beginning


词源：

Derived from "peroration," which comes from the Late Latin *peroratio, from Latin perorare "to speak to the end," from per- "through, thoroughly" + orare "to speak."  Thus, it literally means "to speak thoroughly to the end."
