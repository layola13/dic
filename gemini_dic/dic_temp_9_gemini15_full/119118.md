metropolises:/məˈtrɒpəlɪsiːz/ | n. |大都市，大都会 | A large, important city, often the capital of a country or region.  Metropolises are typically characterized by a high population density, significant cultural influence, and economic importance. | Example: London and New York are global metropolises. 伦敦和纽约是全球大都市。  The city has grown into a bustling metropolis. 这个城市已经发展成为一个繁华的大都市。|近义词：major cities, urban centers, megalopolises |反义词：small towns, villages, rural areas

词源:
From Greek *metropolis (μήτροπολις), from *mētēr (μήτηρ) "mother" + *polis (πόλις) "city".  The original meaning referred to the "mother city" from which colonies were founded. Over time, the meaning evolved to refer to a large and important city.
