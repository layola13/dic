colliding:/kəˈlaɪdɪŋ/| v. |碰撞；冲突；抵触 |The act of two or more objects or things coming forcefully into contact with each other.  This can refer to physical objects or abstract concepts. |例句：The two cars collided at the intersection.  两辆车在十字路口相撞了。

The opposing political views collided during the debate.  在辩论中，截然相反的政治观点发生了冲突。|近义词：crashing, bumping, clashing |反义词：separating, diverging, agreeing


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The trains collide regularly at this station. = 火车经常在这个车站相撞。
Two ideas are colliding in my mind. = 我的脑海中涌现出两种相互冲突的想法。


一般过去时:
The two ships collided last night. = 两艘船昨晚相撞了。
Their plans collided with our expectations. = 他们的计划与我们的预期相冲突。


现在完成时:
Several accidents have collided on this road. = 这条路上发生过几次事故。
My personal and professional lives have collided recently. = 最近我的个人生活和职业生活发生了冲突。


过去完成时:
The cars had collided before the police arrived. = 警车到达之前，汽车已经相撞了。
The two theories had collided in previous research. = 这两种理论在之前的研究中就曾发生过冲突。


将来时:
The meteor will collide with the Earth next year. = 陨石明年将撞击地球。
Their interests will collide if they continue to collaborate on this project. = 如果他们继续合作这个项目，他们的利益将会发生冲突。


虚拟语气:
If the two planets collided, there would be a catastrophic event. = 如果两颗行星相撞，将会发生灾难性事件。
If our schedules had collided less, we could have worked better together. = 如果我们的时间安排冲突较少，我们可以更好地一起工作。


被动语态:
The vehicles were collided on the highway. = 车辆在高速公路上相撞了。(此用法不太常见，通常用被动语态描述被撞的结果，而非撞击行为本身)
The deadline was collided with another important event. = 截止日期与另一个重要事件冲突了。(此用法不太常见，通常用被动语态描述被影响的结果，而非冲突行为本身)


疑问句:
Did the cars collide? = 汽车相撞了吗？
Will their opinions collide in the upcoming meeting? = 他们将在即将召开的会议上发生意见冲突吗？


否定句:
The planes did not collide. = 飞机没有相撞。
The two projects did not collide in their requirements. = 这两个项目在需求上没有冲突。


PHRASE->
collide with = 与...相撞；与...冲突
head-on collision = 正面冲突
a mid-air collision = 空中相撞
a collision course = 碰撞路线；冲突的道路
avoid a collision = 避免碰撞
