legitimating: /ləˈdʒɪtɪmeɪtɪŋ/ | v. | 使合法；使正当；赋予合法性 |  The act of making something legitimate or acceptable.  It involves giving something a basis in law or morality, making it officially recognized or accepted. |


例句：

The government is trying to legitimating its actions. = 政府试图使其行为合法化。

The court’s decision legitimated the company's claim. = 法院的判决使该公司的索赔合法化。


近义词：authorizing, validating, justifying, ratifying, sanctioning

反义词：invalidating, discrediting, undermining, nullifying


词性：动词 (现在分词形式)


词源:

The word "legitimating" derives from the adjective "legitimate," which comes from the Latin word "legitimus," meaning "lawful" or "according to law."  The suffix "-ing" indicates the present participle, signifying an ongoing action.
