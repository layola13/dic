communalist:/ˈkɒmjunəlɪst/ /ˈkɑːmjunəlɪst/ | n. | 强调集体主义者；宗派主义者 | A communalist is someone who prioritizes the interests of their particular group or community above those of wider society, often leading to social division and conflict.  They may advocate for policies that benefit their specific group, even at the expense of others.  This can manifest in various forms, from religious or ethnic communalism to political or ideological communalism. | The rise of communalist sentiments led to increased tensions between different groups in the region.  | 近义词：sectarian, tribalist, separatist | 反义词：universalist, individualist | 名词

词源:

The word "communalist" is derived from "communal," meaning relating to a community or shared ownership.  The "-ist" suffix denotes a person who believes in or practices something.  Therefore, a communalist is someone who strongly identifies with and advocates for their community, often to the exclusion of others.
