self-taxation:/ˌsɛlf ˈtæksˈeɪʃən/| n. |自我征税；自行纳税|The act of a group or community imposing taxes on itself, often for a specific purpose, without the intervention of a higher authority.  This usually occurs in situations where a governing body is absent or ineffective. |Example: The villagers resorted to self-taxation to fund the repair of their bridge.  村民们采取自我征税的方式来资助他们桥梁的维修。|近义词：self-assessment, voluntary taxation |反义词：forced taxation, compulsory taxation
