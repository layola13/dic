freshens: /ˈfreʃənz/ | vt. |使清新；使恢复活力；使变新鲜| To make or become fresh or fresher.  The verb "freshen" implies the act of making something more fresh, lively, or invigorated.  It can refer to physical objects, environments, or even people's moods. |例句：A light breeze freshens the air.  一阵轻风使空气清新。

The rain freshened the garden. 雨水使花园焕然一新。

近义词：invigorate, revitalize, refresh |反义词：stale, dull, wither |动词

词源:
The word "freshen" comes from the Old English word "frisc," meaning "fresh."  This word evolved through Middle English "fressen" to the modern form "freshen."  The "-en" suffix is a common verb-forming suffix indicating a causative meaning (to make something fresh).
