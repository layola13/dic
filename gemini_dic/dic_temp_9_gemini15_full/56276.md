dogged:/ˈdɒɡɪd/ /ˈdɑːɡɪd/ | adj. | 顽强的；固执的；坚决的 |  Persistent in effort; stubbornly determined.  The adjective "dogged" describes someone who is very determined and persistent, even when facing difficulties. They don't give up easily. |


例句：

His dogged determination finally paid off.  = 他顽强的决心最终得到了回报。

Despite the setbacks, she remained dogged in her pursuit of her goals. = 尽管遭遇挫折，她仍然坚决地追求自己的目标。


近义词：persistent, tenacious, determined, resolute, steadfast

反义词：wavering, irresolute, fickle, yielding, giving up


词性：形容词


词源：源自古英语 *dogga，意思是“强壮的；勇敢的”。  This reflects the original sense of the word, implying a strong and unwavering nature.  The modern meaning evolved over time, associating the strength with persistence.
