hackamore:/ˈhækəˌmɔːr/| n.|无嚼子马勒；无嚼子缰绳|A hackamore is a type of bridle for horses that does not use a bit in the horse's mouth.  Instead, it uses a noseband and sometimes a curb chain or strap to control the horse.  It is often used for training young horses or for horses that are sensitive to bits.|The rancher used a hackamore to gently guide the young colt.  牧场主用无嚼子马勒轻轻地引导小马驹。|bridle, bosal|bitted bridle|名词
