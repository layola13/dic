eldest:/ˈɛldɪst/| adj.| 最年长的，最大的 |  The eldest child is responsible for their younger siblings.  最年长的孩子负责照顾他们的弟弟妹妹。|  oldest, senior | youngest | 形容词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The eldest son inherits the family business. = 长子继承家业。
She is the eldest in her family. = 她是家里最大的。


一般过去时:
The eldest daughter was very kind. = 大女儿非常善良。
He was the eldest of five children. = 他是五个孩子中最大的。


现在完成时:
The eldest sibling has always been protective of their younger siblings. = 最大的兄弟姐妹一直很保护他们的弟弟妹妹。
She has been the eldest in the family for many years. = 她多年来一直是家里最大的。


过去完成时:
The eldest child had already left home before the youngest was born. = 老大在老小出生前就已经离开家了。
By the time the accident happened, she had been the eldest sister for 10 years. = 事故发生时，她已经做了十年的大姐了。


将来时:
The eldest will likely inherit the most. = 最大的孩子可能会继承最多。
He will be the eldest in his family for many more years. = 他还会在家里做很多年的老大。


虚拟语气:
If I were the eldest, I would have more responsibilities. = 如果我是老大，我会承担更多责任。
I wish I were the eldest child; things would have been different. = 我希望我是老大，事情就会不一样了。


被动语态:
The eldest child was always chosen first. = 老大总是被第一个选中。
The eldest child's opinion was always valued. = 老大的意见总是受到重视。


疑问句:
Who is the eldest child in the family? = 家里谁是老大？
Are you the eldest in your family? = 你是你家里的老大吗？


否定句:
She is not the eldest child in her family. = 她不是她家里的老大。
He wasn't the eldest; his brother was older. = 他不是老大；他哥哥比他大。
