matronymics: /ˌmætəˈnɒmɪks/ | n. | 母系命名法 | The practice of naming a child after their mother or a female ancestor.  It involves using a mother's name or a variation of it to name a child, usually a daughter but sometimes a son as well.  This practice can be seen across various cultures and historical periods. | Example:  Naming a daughter "Eleanor" after her maternal grandmother, whose name was "Ellen." | 近义词:  maternal naming, female-line naming | 反义词: patronymics (父系命名法)
