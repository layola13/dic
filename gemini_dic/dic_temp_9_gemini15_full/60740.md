electrocuting: /ɪˌlɛktrəˈkjuːtɪŋ/ | v. | 电击；触电 | The act of killing or injuring someone or something by passing an electric current through it.  This word describes the process of causing death or injury through electricity.  It emphasizes the act of using electricity to harm. |


例句：

The man was electrocuting the fish in the pond. = 那个人正在用电击池塘里的鱼。

Careless handling of electrical equipment can lead to electrocuting oneself. = 粗心操作电器可能会导致触电。


近义词： shocking, stunning (in the sense of causing a sudden shock), electrifying (in the context of extreme surprise or excitement, not necessarily harmful)


反义词：saving, rescuing (opposite actions), insulating (preventing electrocution)


词性：动词

词源:

The word "electrocuting" is derived from "electrocution," which is a combination of "electro-" (from "electricity") and "-cution" (from the Latin "exsecutio," meaning "execution").  Therefore, the word literally means "execution by electricity."
