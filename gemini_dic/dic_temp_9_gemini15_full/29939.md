cave-ins: /ˈkeɪvɪnz/ | n. | 塌方，坍塌 | The sudden collapse of an underground cavity or structure, often involving rock, soil, or snow.  It can be caused by various factors, including mining, earthquakes, or natural erosion. |例句：The mine was shut down after a series of cave-ins.  矿井发生了一系列塌方后被关闭了。|例句：The heavy snow caused a cave-in on the mountainside. 大雪导致山腰发生塌方。|近义词：collapse, subsidence, caving in |反义词：construction, reinforcement
