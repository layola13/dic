outstriving: /ˌaʊtˈstraɪvɪŋ/ | v. |努力超越；胜过；超过 | To surpass or exceed others in achievement or effort.  | The company is outstriving its competitors in the market. 这家公司正在市场上超越其竞争对手。 | surpass, exceed, excel, outdo | fail, underperform, lag behind | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He outstrives his colleagues in work performance. = 他在工作表现上胜过他的同事。
The athlete outstrives others in the race. = 运动员在比赛中胜过其他人。


一般过去时:
She outstrove her rivals to win the championship. = 她战胜了对手，赢得了冠军。
The team outstrove the opponent and secured victory. = 这个团队战胜了对手，获得了胜利。


现在完成时:
Our company has outstriven its competitors in innovation. = 我们公司在创新方面已经超越了竞争对手。
He has outstriven himself in this project. = 他在这个项目中超越了自己。


过去完成时:
They had outstriven all expectations by the end of the year. = 到年底，他们已经超越了所有预期。
The company had outstriven its sales targets for the quarter. = 公司本季度的销售目标已经超额完成。


将来时:
We will outstrive our rivals and become the market leader. = 我们将超越竞争对手，成为市场领导者。
She believes she will outstrive her previous personal best. = 她相信她会超越她之前的个人最好成绩。


虚拟语气:
If he had worked harder, he might have outstriven his brother. = 如果他更努力工作，他可能已经超过了他的兄弟。
I wish I could outstrive my limitations. = 我希望我能克服我的局限性。


被动语态:
Their achievements were outstriven by the next generation. = 他们的成就被下一代超越了。
The record was outstriven by a younger athlete. = 这项纪录被一位年轻的运动员打破了。


疑问句:
Can we outstrive the challenges ahead? = 我们能克服未来的挑战吗？
Will their efforts outstrive the difficulties? = 他们的努力能否克服困难？


否定句:
He did not outstrive his opponent in the debate. = 他在辩论中没有胜过他的对手。
The company failed to outstrive its competitors in the new product launch. = 这家公司在新产品发布中未能超越竞争对手。
