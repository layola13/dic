/ˈɒlədʒi/ /ɑːˈlɑːdʒi/ | n. | 学科；…学 | The suffix "-ology" is used to form nouns that denote a branch of knowledge or a field of study. It is derived from the Greek words "logos" (λόγος), meaning "word," "speech," "reason," or "account," and the combining form "-logia" (-λογία).  It signifies the study or science of a particular subject. | Examples: Biology is the study of living organisms.  Geology is the science that deals with the earth's physical structure and substance. | 近义词：study, science, field | 反义词：ignorance, unawareness

He studies biology. = 他学习生物学。
She teaches anthropology. = 她教人类学。


一般过去时:
I studied geology in college. = 我大学学习地质学。
They researched paleontology for years. = 他们研究古生物学很多年了。

否定句:

PHRASE->

There are no common phrases directly using "-ology" as a complete phrase. However, many "-ology" words are used in common phrases related to their specific field of study.  For example:

* **"The field of biology..."** (生物学领域…)
* **"Advances in technology..."** (科技进步…)
* **"Principles of psychology..."** (心理学原理…)
* **"Geological formations..."** (地质构造…)
* **"Oceanographic research..."** (海洋学研究…)
