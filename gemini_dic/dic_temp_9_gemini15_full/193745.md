self-cooking: /ˌsɛlfˈkʊkɪŋ/ | adj. | 自烹的，自动烹饪的 |  Describes food or appliances that cook themselves or with minimal human intervention.  It often refers to dishes prepared using slow cookers, pressure cookers, or other automated cooking devices. | 例句：The self-cooking rice cooker made preparing dinner a breeze. = 自烹电饭煲让准备晚餐变得轻而易举。

例句：This self-cooking casserole requires only minimal preparation before being placed in the oven. = 这道自烹砂锅菜只需要在放入烤箱前进行最少的准备工作。


近义词：automatic cooking, automated cooking | 反义词：manual cooking, hand-cooked


词源:  The word is a compound of "self-" (meaning by oneself or automatically) and "cooking" (the act or process of preparing food by heating).  Both elements have Old English roots. "Self" derives from Old English *self, and "cooking" from Old English *cocian (to cook).
