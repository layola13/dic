moderates: /ˈmɒdəreɪts/ /ˈmɑːdəreɪts/ | v. adj. n. | 使缓和；调节；主持；温和的；适度的；中庸的； | to make or become less extreme, intense, or severe; to chair or preside over a meeting or discussion; a person with moderate views | 例句：The government is trying to moderate inflation. 政府正在努力抑制通货膨胀。  The chairperson moderated the debate. 主席主持了辩论。|近义词：temper, regulate, control, moderate, mild, temperate |反义词：extreme, intense, radical, severe


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He moderates the temperature in the room. = 他调节房间的温度。
She moderates the political discussion. = 她主持政治讨论。


一般过去时:
The teacher moderated the student's behavior. = 老师缓和了学生的举动。
The judge moderated the tone of the argument. = 法官缓和了争论的语气。


现在完成时:
The medicine has moderated my pain. = 药减轻了我的疼痛。
They have moderated their demands. = 他们已经缓和了他们的要求。


过去完成时:
He had moderated his speech before delivering it. = 他在发表演讲之前已经缓和了语气。
She had moderated the meeting before the disagreement escalated. = 在分歧升级之前，她已经主持了会议。


将来时:
The doctor will moderate the patient's medication. = 医生将调整病人的药物剂量。
The president will moderate the upcoming debate. = 总统将主持即将举行的辩论。


虚拟语气:
If he moderated his language, he would be more persuasive. = 如果他语气缓和些，他会更有说服力。
I wish the government would moderate its policies. = 我希望政府能缓和其政策。


被动语态:
The discussion was moderated by the professor. = 讨论由教授主持。
The temperature was moderated by the thermostat. = 温度由恒温器调节。


疑问句:
Who moderated the last meeting? = 谁主持了上次会议？
Does he moderate his alcohol consumption? = 他是否节制饮酒？


否定句:
He didn't moderate his temper. = 他没有控制住脾气。
The government didn't moderate the price increases. = 政府没有抑制物价上涨。
