epideictic:/ˌɛpɪˈdɪktɪk/| adj. | 夸饰的；展示的；用于庆典的 | Of or relating to epideictic oratory; demonstrative; rhetorical.  Epideictic rhetoric is a type of oratory focused on praise or blame, often used in ceremonial speeches.  It aims to influence the audience's emotions and values rather than persuade them to take a specific action. | Example: The mayor delivered an epideictic speech at the town's centennial celebration, praising its history and achievements.  The graduation ceremony featured an epideictic address filled with inspirational words. | 近义词：ceremonial, celebratory, demonstrative, laudatory | 反义词：persuasive (in the sense of aiming for action rather than emotional response), argumentative
