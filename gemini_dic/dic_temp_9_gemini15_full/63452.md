entrenchments:/ɪnˈtʃɛntʃmənts/| n. | 巩固；防御工事；(观点等的)确立 |  Fortifications consisting of trenches, typically with supporting embankments.  Entrenchments refer to both physical defenses (like ditches and embankments) and the strengthening of a position, idea, or habit. |例句：The soldiers dug entrenchments to protect themselves from enemy fire.  士兵们挖掘了防御工事以保护自己免受敌人的炮火袭击。

The government's policies are deeply entrenched. 政府的政策根深蒂固。|近义词：fortifications, defenses, ramparts |反义词：vulnerability, weakness, openness
