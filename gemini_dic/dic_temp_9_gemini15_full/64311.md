equivocating:/ɪˈkwɪvəkeɪtɪŋ/| v. |含糊其辞，模棱两可地说 |Using ambiguous language to conceal the truth or avoid committing oneself.  |The politician was equivocating, avoiding a direct answer to the journalist's question.  政治家含糊其辞，回避了记者的直接提问。|  prevaricating, hedging, tergiversating | being straightforward, being direct |动词

They equivocate on important issues, avoiding clear statements. 他们在重要问题上含糊其辞，避免做出明确的声明。

词源：
From Latin *aequivocus ("equal-sounding"), from aequi- ("equal") + voc- ("voice, call").  The word's evolution reflects its core meaning: using language that allows for multiple interpretations.
