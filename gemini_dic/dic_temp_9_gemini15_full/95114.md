inanimateness:/ˌɪnˈænɪmətɪs/ | n. | 无生命；非生命性 | The sheer inanimateness of the landscape was both awe-inspiring and slightly unsettling.  | 近义词：lifelessness, inertness | 反义词：liveliness, animation |


英文解析:The state or quality of being inanimate; lack of life or spirit.  It refers to the characteristic of being not alive, lacking the qualities associated with living things such as movement, growth, or reproduction.

例句:

The inanimateness of the rocks contrasted sharply with the vibrant life of the nearby forest.  = 岩石的无生命状态与附近森林的勃勃生机形成了鲜明的对比。

The museum's collection, while impressive, lacked a certain warmth due to the overall inanimateness of the artifacts. = 博物馆的收藏虽然令人印象深刻，但由于文物整体缺乏生气而显得有些冷清。


词源:

From inanimate + -ness.  "Inanimate" itself comes from the prefix "in-" (meaning "not") + "animate" (meaning "having life").  "Animate" has its roots in the Latin word "anima," meaning "soul" or "life."
