sapindus:/sæˈpɪndəs/| n. |无患子属植物|Sapindus is a genus of about thirteen species of trees and shrubs in the family Sapindaceae, native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Old World.  They are commonly known as soapberries or soap nuts because their fruits contain saponins, which create a soapy lather when mixed with water.  This lather has been used for centuries as a natural cleaning agent.|The sapindus fruit is often used as a natural detergent. = 无患子的果实常被用作天然清洁剂。

The leaves of the sapindus tree are used in traditional medicine. = 无患子的叶子在传统医学中被使用。|soapberry, soap nut|无相关反义词|名词
