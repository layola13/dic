obsessionalism: /əbˈsɛʃənəlɪzəm/ | n. | 沉迷；强迫症倾向 | The state or condition of being obsessed; excessive preoccupation with a particular idea or object.  It often manifests as repetitive thoughts and behaviors. |例句：His obsessionalism with cleanliness led him to spend hours each day scrubbing his apartment.  他的洁癖让他每天花数小时擦洗公寓。

His obsessionalism with work prevented him from enjoying his personal life. 他的工作狂倾向让他无法享受个人生活。


近义词：compulsion, mania, fixation |反义词：indifference, apathy, detachment

词源:
The word "obsessionalism" is derived from "obsession" (itself from the Latin "obsidere," meaning "to besiege") and the suffix "-alism," indicating a state, condition, or doctrine.  Thus, obsessionalism refers to the state of being obsessed.
