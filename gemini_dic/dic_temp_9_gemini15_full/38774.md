conducible:/kənˈdjuːsɪbl/ | adj. | 有助于的；有益的 | tending to promote or assist; helpful; conducive to |  A quiet environment is conducive to studying. 安静的环境有助于学习。  Regular exercise is conducive to good health.  规律的锻炼有益于健康。 | helpful, beneficial, advantageous | harmful, detrimental, obstructive

USAGE->
一般现在时:
A quiet atmosphere is conducive to concentration. = 安静的氛围有助于集中注意力。
Regular exercise is conducive to good health. = 规律的锻炼有益于健康。

一般过去时:
The warm weather was conducive to a good harvest. = 温暖的天气有利于丰收。
Her positive attitude was conducive to team success. = 她积极的态度有助于团队成功。

现在完成时:
The changes have been conducive to improved efficiency. = 这些改变促进了效率的提高。
His efforts have been conducive to the project's success. = 他的努力促成了项目的成功。

过去完成时:
The previous policies had not been conducive to economic growth. = 先前的政策不利于经济增长。
The earlier discussions had been conducive to a compromise. = 早先的讨论促成了妥协。

将来时:
The new regulations will be conducive to safer working conditions. = 新规将有助于创造更安全的工作环境。
Improved communication will be conducive to better teamwork. = 改善沟通将有助于更好的团队合作。

虚拟语气:
If the conditions were conducive, we would have a picnic. = 如果条件允许，我们会去野餐。
If the environment were more conducive,  I would work more efficiently. = 如果环境更适宜，我会工作更高效。


被动语态:
A conducive environment is needed for learning. = 学习需要一个有利的环境。
The situation was found to be conducive to conflict. = 发现这种情况容易导致冲突。


疑问句:
Is this environment conducive to productivity? = 这个环境有利于生产力吗？
Are these measures conducive to a peaceful resolution? = 这些措施有利于和平解决吗？


否定句:
The noisy environment was not conducive to studying. = 嘈杂的环境不利于学习。
His aggressive style was not conducive to collaboration. = 他咄咄逼人的风格不利于合作。


PHRASE->
be conducive to = 有助于
conducive environment = 有利环境
conducive atmosphere = 有利氛围
conducive factors = 有利因素
highly conducive = 非常有利
