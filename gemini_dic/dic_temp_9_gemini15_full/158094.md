plasticism:/ˈplæstɪsɪzəm/| n. |可塑性；造型艺术；（尤指）对形式的强调|The quality of being easily shaped or molded;  the artistic theory or practice emphasizing form or design over content.  It often refers to a style of art that prioritizes the aesthetic qualities of form and structure over representational accuracy or narrative. |例句：The sculptor demonstrated remarkable plasticism in his work.  这位雕塑家在他的作品中展现了非凡的可塑性。

The movement's plasticism was evident in its bold use of color and form.  这个运动的可塑性在其大胆的色彩和形式运用中显而易见。

近义词：moldability, malleability, pliability, formalism (in art) |反义词：rigidity, inflexibility, realism (in art)
