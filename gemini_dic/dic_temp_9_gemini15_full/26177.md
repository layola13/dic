butchers:/ˈbʊtʃərz/| n. |屠夫；肉贩；（尤指）屠宰场|A butcher is a person who slaughters and dresses meat for sale.  They often work in butcher shops or meat markets.|The butcher cut the meat into steaks.  这位屠夫把肉切成牛排。|The local butcher shop is known for its high-quality beef. 当地这家肉铺以其优质牛肉而闻名。|meat seller, slaughterer|vegetarian,  (depending on context) animal rights advocate|名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He butchers the meat every morning. 他每天早上宰杀肉类。
The butcher sells fresh chicken. 这个屠夫卖新鲜鸡肉。


一般过去时:
They butchered the pig yesterday. 他们昨天宰杀了猪。
The butcher cut the meat skillfully yesterday. 屠夫昨天熟练地切肉。


现在完成时:
He has butchered many animals in his career. 他在他职业生涯中宰杀了许多动物。
The butcher has sold out of pork today. 今天屠夫的猪肉卖完了。


过去完成时:

The butcher had already closed shop when I arrived. 我到的时候，屠夫已经关店了。


将来时:
He will butcher the lamb tomorrow. 他明天将宰杀这只羔羊。
The butcher will open a new shop next month. 屠夫下个月将开一家新店。


虚拟语气:
If I were a butcher, I would know how to cut meat properly. 如果我是个屠夫，我会知道如何正确地切肉。
I wish the butcher hadn't run out of beef. 我真希望屠夫没有卖完牛肉。


被动语态:
The meat was butchered expertly. 肉被熟练地宰杀。
The beef was butchered and packaged for sale. 牛肉被宰杀并包装好出售。


疑问句:
Does he butcher his own meat? 他自己宰杀肉类吗？
Where does the butcher buy his supplies? 屠夫在哪里购买他的用品？


否定句:
He doesn't butcher the meat himself; he buys it pre-cut. 他不自己宰杀肉类；他买预先切好的。
That butcher doesn't sell lamb. 那家肉铺不卖羊肉。
