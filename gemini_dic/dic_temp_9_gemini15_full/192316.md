scribble:/ˈskrɪbəl/| v. n.|潦草地写，涂鸦|To write or draw something quickly and carelessly.|He scribbled a note and shoved it in his pocket. 他潦草地写了个便条塞进口袋里。|doodle, scrawl, jot|write neatly, draw carefully|v.
USAGE->
一般现在时:
He scribbles notes during the lecture. 他在课堂上潦草地记笔记。
She scribbles in her notebook all day. 她整天在笔记本上乱涂乱画。

一般过去时:
I scribbled my phone number on a napkin. 我把我的电话号码潦草地写在餐巾纸上。
He scribbled a quick reply to the email. 他匆匆忙忙地回复了邮件。

现在完成时:
She has scribbled down all the important points. 她已经把所有重点都记下来了（潦草地）。
They have scribbled all over the walls. 他们在墙上到处乱涂乱画。

过去完成时:
He had scribbled a letter before he went to bed. 他睡觉前潦草地写了一封信。
She had scribbled the address on a piece of paper. 她在一张纸上潦草地写下了地址。

将来时:
I will scribble a quick thank-you note. 我将草草写个感谢信。
She will scribble down the recipe before she forgets it. 她会在忘记之前潦草地记下食谱。

虚拟语气:
If I had more time, I would not have scribbled my answer. 如果我有更多时间，我就不会潦草地写答案了。
I wish I hadn't scribbled my name on the test paper. 我真希望我没有在试卷上乱涂我的名字。

被动语态:
The message was scribbled on a piece of paper.  信息潦草地写在纸上。
The wall was scribbled on by the children. 墙壁被孩子们乱涂乱画了。

疑问句:
Did he scribble his name on the form? 他是不是在表格上潦草地签了名？
Has she scribbled all the information? 她把所有信息都潦草地记下来了吗？

否定句:
I didn't scribble anything on the document. 我没有在文件上乱涂乱画。
He didn't scribble a proper letter; it was illegible. 他没有好好写信；字迹难以辨认。

词源:
The word "scribble" comes from the Middle English word "scriblen," which is of uncertain origin, possibly from a combination of the Old French word "escribler" (to write) and a reduplicative suffix "-ble."  The precise etymology remains debated.
