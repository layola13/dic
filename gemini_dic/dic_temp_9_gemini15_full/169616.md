propionate: /ˈprɒpɪəneɪt/ | /ˈprɑːpɪəneɪt/ | n. |丙酸盐；丙酸酯| A propionate is a salt or ester of propionic acid.  Propionates are used as preservatives in food and feed to inhibit the growth of mold and bacteria. They are also used in various industrial applications. | Example: Calcium propionate is a common food preservative.  钙丙酸盐是一种常见的食品防腐剂。|近义词：None directly equivalent, but other preservatives could be considered. |反义词：None directly opposite.
