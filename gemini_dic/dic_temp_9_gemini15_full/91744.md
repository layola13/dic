/ˌhaɪpərdɪˈkrɒtəs/ | adj. | 双峰的；(脉搏)双收缩的 | Having or characterized by two prominent peaks (as in a pulse wave).  The term is primarily used in medicine, specifically in the context of pulse analysis.  A hyperdicrotous pulse indicates a sharp increase in pressure followed by a secondary smaller peak, often suggesting underlying cardiovascular issues. | The patient's pulse was hyperdicrotous, indicating possible aortic stenosis.  |  Dicrotic,  |  Monocrotous |  adj.
