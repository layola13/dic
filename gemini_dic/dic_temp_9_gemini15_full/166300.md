preredemption:/ˌpriːriˈdɛmpʃən/| n. |预赎，预先赎回|The act of redeeming something before it is due or before a specific event occurs.  It often implies a proactive measure to prevent a negative outcome or fulfill an obligation early.  The term is relatively uncommon and often found in specialized contexts, particularly in finance or theology.|Example: The company engaged in preredemption of its bonds to reduce its debt burden. 公司提前赎回了债券以减轻债务负担。|近义词：prepayment, advance redemption|反义词：postponement, deferral

词源:
The word "preredemption" is a combination of the prefix "pre-" (meaning "before") and "redemption" (meaning the act of buying something back or freeing someone from captivity or obligation).  Therefore, it literally means "before redemption."
