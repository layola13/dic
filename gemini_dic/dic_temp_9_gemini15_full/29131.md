casebooks:/ˈkeɪsˌbʊks/ | n. |案例汇编，案例书 |A collection of cases, typically legal cases, used for teaching or reference.  It often contains detailed descriptions of the facts, legal arguments, and court decisions. |Example: Law students often rely heavily on casebooks to prepare for class. 法学院学生经常依靠案例汇编来准备上课。


The professor assigned a chapter from the casebook for homework. 教授布置了案例汇编中的一章作为家庭作业。|近义词：legal texts, case studies, precedents |反义词：None specifically, but antonyms could be related to the opposite of studying legal precedents (e.g., ignoring legal precedent) |名词
