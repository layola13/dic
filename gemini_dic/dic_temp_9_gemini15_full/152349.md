pauper:/ˈpɔːpə(r)/| n. |贫民，穷人 |A pauper is a person who is very poor and has little or no money.  They often rely on charity for basic necessities.  The term often carries a connotation of destitution and social marginalization. |例句：He died a pauper, leaving nothing to his family. 他死时一贫如洗，什么也没留给家人。


例句：Many paupers lived in overcrowded slums during the Industrial Revolution.  工业革命期间，许多贫民住在拥挤的贫民窟里。


近义词：indigent, destitute, impecunious |反义词：millionaire, tycoon, wealthy

词源:
The word "pauper" comes from the Old French word "pauvre," meaning "poor."  This in turn derives from the Latin word "pauper," which also meant "poor" or "needy."  The word's journey reflects the long-standing concern and categorization of poverty across different languages and cultures.
