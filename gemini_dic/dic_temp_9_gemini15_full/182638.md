reorganise:/ˌriːˈɔːɡənʌɪz/| /ˌriːˈɔːrɡənaɪz/ | vt.|重新组织；改组；改编|To reorganize something means to change its structure or organization, often in order to improve its efficiency or effectiveness.  This can apply to businesses, systems, or even ideas.|例句：The company decided to reorganize its sales department to boost profits.  公司决定重组销售部门以提高利润。

例句：We need to reorganize our approach to this project. 我们需要重新组织我们对这个项目的方法。


近义词： restructure, rearrange, reform, revamp |反义词：disorganize, disarrange


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They reorganise the office every year. = 他们每年都会重新组织办公室。
The company reorganises its structure regularly. = 公司定期重组其结构。


一般过去时:
The team reorganised its workflow after the merger. = 并购后，团队重组了工作流程。
She reorganised her files to find the missing document. = 她重新整理了文件以找到丢失的文件。


现在完成时:
They have reorganised the entire department. = 他们已经重组了整个部门。
The company has reorganised its operations to become more efficient. = 公司已经重组了运营以提高效率。


过去完成时:
They had reorganised the schedule before the conference. = 他们在会议之前已经重新安排了日程。
The manager had reorganised the team several times before finding the right structure. = 经理在找到合适的结构之前已经多次重组了团队。


将来时:
We will reorganise the library next month. = 我们下个月将重新组织图书馆。
The government plans to reorganise the healthcare system. = 政府计划重组医疗体系。


虚拟语气:
If we reorganised the system, it would be much more efficient. = 如果我们重组系统，效率将会高得多。
It's time we reorganised our priorities.  = 是时候我们重新安排我们的优先级了。


被动语态:
The department was reorganised last year. = 该部门去年进行了重组。
The company's structure is being reorganised at the moment. = 公司的结构目前正在重组中。


疑问句:

否定句:
