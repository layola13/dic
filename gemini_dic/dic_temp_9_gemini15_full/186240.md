rhinolite: /ˈraɪnəlaɪt/ | n. |犀牛石| A type of concretion found in the nasal passages of extinct rhinoceroses.  It is composed primarily of calcium carbonate and other minerals.  These formations are often found fossilized and can provide valuable information about the extinct animals' diet and environment. | Example:  The paleontologists discovered a rhinolite in the fossilized skull, providing insights into the ancient rhino's diet. | 近义词:  (no direct synonyms, as it's a specific type of concretion) | 反义词: (no direct antonyms)
