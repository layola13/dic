outlays:/ˈaʊtleɪz/| n. |支出，开支 |The company's outlays on research and development were substantial.  这家公司在研发上的支出相当可观。|The government's outlays for infrastructure projects are increasing. 政府在基础设施项目上的支出正在增加。|expenditures, expenses |income, revenue |名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company's outlays are high this quarter. = 这个季度的公司支出很高。
Our monthly outlays on food are surprisingly low. = 我们每月的伙食费出奇的低。

一般过去时:
Their outlays last year exceeded their income. = 他们去年的支出超过了收入。
The project's outlays were carefully budgeted. = 该项目的支出经过仔细预算。

现在完成时:
We have significantly reduced our outlays this year. = 我们今年大幅减少了支出。
They have exceeded their projected outlays for the project. = 他们已经超过了该项目的预计支出。

过去完成时:
By the end of the year, they had exhausted their outlays. = 到年底，他们已经用完了所有支出。
The company had underestimated its outlays for the new building. = 公司低估了新建大楼的支出。

将来时:
Our outlays will increase next year due to expansion plans. = 由于扩张计划，明年的支出将增加。
We will carefully monitor our outlays for the upcoming project. = 我们将仔细监控即将到来的项目的支出。

虚拟语气:
If our outlays were lower, we could invest more. = 如果我们的支出更低，我们就能投资更多。
I wish our outlays were not so high. = 我希望我们的支出不要这么高。

被动语态:
The outlays were carefully reviewed by the finance department. = 支出由财务部门仔细审查。
The outlays are being monitored closely by management. = 支出正在受到管理层的密切监控。

疑问句:
What are our total outlays for this month? = 我们这个月的总支出是多少？
What were the outlays for the marketing campaign? = 市场营销活动的支出是多少？

否定句:
Our outlays were not as high as we had anticipated. = 我们的支出并不像我们预期的那样高。
The company did not disclose its outlays for the acquisition. = 公司没有披露其收购的支出。
