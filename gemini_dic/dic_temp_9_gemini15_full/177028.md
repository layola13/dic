reanchoring:/ˌriˈæŋkərɪŋ/ | n. |重新锚定，重新定位 |The process of revising or reinforcing an existing belief or attitude.  This often involves adjusting one's perspective or understanding of a situation in light of new information or experiences.  It's a cognitive process that helps maintain stability and consistency in beliefs, even when faced with contradictory evidence.| Example: The therapist helped the patient with reanchoring his negative self-perception by focusing on his strengths and past successes.  重新定位疗法帮助病人通过关注他的优势和过去的成功来重新定位他的负面自我认知。|近义词：recalibration, readjustment, repositioning |反义词：detachment, abandonment
