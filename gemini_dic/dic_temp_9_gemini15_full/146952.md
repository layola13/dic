overinvestment:/ˌoʊvərɪnˈvɛstmənt/| n. |过度投资|The act of investing more capital in a project, business, or asset than is economically justified or prudent.  This can lead to diminishing returns and even losses.  It often happens when there's a boom in a particular sector or when managers are overly optimistic about future prospects. |Example: The dot-com bubble was characterized by widespread overinvestment in internet companies, leading to many bankruptcies.  过度投资互联网公司是导致许多公司破产的网络泡沫的特征。|近义词：excess investment, excessive investment, capital misallocation|反义词：underinvestment, divestment|名词

词源:
The word "overinvestment" is a combination of the prefix "over-" (meaning "too much" or "excessively") and the noun "investment" (the act of putting money into something with the expectation of gaining a profit).  Therefore, it literally means investing too much.
