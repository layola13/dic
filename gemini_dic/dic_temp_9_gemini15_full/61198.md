eluxation: /ˌiːluːkˈseɪʃən/ | n. | 脱臼；关节脱位 | The displacement of a bone from its joint.  This typically involves a disruption of the joint capsule and ligaments.  It often results from trauma or injury, causing pain, swelling, and loss of function. | Example: The athlete suffered a painful eluxation of his shoulder during the game. 运动员在比赛中肩膀脱臼了，非常痛苦。 |近义词：dislocation |反义词：reduction (the act of putting a dislocated bone back into place)
