diarthrosis:/ˌdaɪ.ɑːrˈθroʊ.sɪs/ | n. |关节；滑膜关节 |A freely movable joint, characterized by the presence of a synovial cavity.  Diarthroses allow for a wide range of motion, including flexion, extension, abduction, adduction, rotation, and circumduction.  They are crucial for locomotion and manipulation. | Example: The knee is a diarthrosis, allowing for bending and straightening of the leg.  膝盖是一个关节，允许腿部弯曲和伸直。|近义词：synovial joint |反义词：synarthrosis, amphiarthrosis
