coasting:/ˈkoʊstɪŋ/|/ˈkəʊstɪŋ/| v.|滑行；依靠惯性前进；轻松地度过；|To move or continue moving without using power, relying on momentum.  It can also refer to getting by with minimal effort or progress.|例句：The car coasted to a stop.  这辆车滑行到停下。

The cyclist coasted down the hill.  骑自行车的人沿着山坡滑行。


近义词：gliding, drifting, sliding, cruising|反义词：struggling, striving, working hard

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He coasts along, never really trying. 他得过且过，从不真正努力。
They coast through life without a care. 他们过着无忧无虑的生活。

一般过去时:
The boat coasted gently into the harbor. 船轻轻地滑进港湾。
She coasted through the exam without studying. 她没学习就轻松通过了考试。

现在完成时:
We've been coasting for too long; we need to work harder. 我们已经懈怠太久了；我们需要更加努力。
He has coasted on his past successes for years. 多年来，他一直依靠过去的成功。

过去完成时:
The plane had coasted to a halt on the runway. 飞机在跑道上滑行到停。
After the initial excitement, the project had coasted along. 最初的兴奋过后，项目进展缓慢。

将来时:
The train will coast into the station. 火车将滑进车站。
We will coast through the next few weeks without much work. 未来几周我们将轻松度过，无需太多工作。

虚拟语气:
If the engine failed, the car would coast to a stop. 如果发动机熄火，汽车就会滑行到停下。
I wish I could coast through life like that. 我希望我能像那样轻松地生活。

被动语态:
The boat was coasted into the dock. 船被滑进码头。
The project was coasted along without much input from management. 项目在没有管理层太多投入的情况下缓慢推进。


疑问句:
Is the car still coasting? 车还在滑行吗？
Does he always coast through his responsibilities? 他总是逃避责任吗？

否定句:
The engine wouldn't coast; it just stopped. 发动机不会滑行；它只是停了。
They couldn't coast any longer; they had to work. 他们不能再懈怠了；他们必须工作。
