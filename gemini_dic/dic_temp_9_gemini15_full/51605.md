devastative:/ˈdɛvəsteɪtɪv/| adj.|毁灭性的，破坏性的，极具破坏力的| causing very great damage or destruction.  The devastating effects of the hurricane were felt for months afterward.|近义词：destructive, ruinous, catastrophic, calamitous|反义词：constructive, beneficial, helpful


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The storm is devastating the coastal areas. = 暴风雨正在摧毁沿海地区。
This policy is devastating the economy. = 这一政策正在摧毁经济。


一般过去时:
The earthquake devastated the city. = 地震摧毁了这座城市。
The fire devastated the forest. = 大火烧毁了森林。


现在完成时:
The war has devastated the country. = 战争已经摧毁了这个国家。
The drought has devastated the crops. = 干旱已经摧毁了庄稼。


过去完成时:
The previous floods had devastated the region before the latest storm hit. = 在最近的暴风雨袭击之前，之前的洪水已经摧毁了该地区。
Years of mismanagement had devastated the company's finances before the new CEO arrived. = 在新任CEO到来之前，多年的管理不善已经摧毁了公司的财务状况。


将来时:
If we don't act now, the pollution will devastate the environment. = 如果我们现在不采取行动，污染将会摧毁环境。
The predicted tsunami will devastate coastal communities. = 预计的海啸将摧毁沿海社区。


虚拟语气:
If the government had acted sooner, the crisis wouldn't have been so devastating. = 如果政府早点采取行动，这场危机就不会如此严重。
It would be devastating if the project failed. = 如果项目失败了，那将是毁灭性的。


被动语态:
The town was devastated by the tornado. = 这个城镇被龙卷风摧毁了。
The landscape has been devastated by mining. = 景观已经被采矿破坏了。


疑问句:
Is the impact of climate change going to be devastating? = 气候变化的影响将会是毁灭性的吗？
How devastating was the damage caused by the wildfire? = 野火造成的损失有多严重？


否定句:
The news wasn't devastating, but it was certainly disappointing. = 这个消息并不毁灭性，但肯定令人失望。
The effects of the medicine were not devastating, but mild. = 药物的效果并非毁灭性，而是温和的。
