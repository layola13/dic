quasi-precedent:/ˈkwɑːziˌprɛsɪdənt/| n.|准先例；类先例|A quasi-precedent is a case or decision that, while not strictly binding as a precedent, provides persuasive authority or guidance in similar future cases.  It's often used when there's a lack of directly applicable precedents or when the existing precedents are conflicting or unclear.|例句：The judge cited a quasi-precedent from a neighboring jurisdiction to support his ruling.  法官援引邻近司法管辖区的一个准先例来支持他的裁决。|近义词：analogous case, persuasive authority, guiding case|反义词：binding precedent, mandatory authority
