scalableness: /ˈskeɪləbəlnəs/ | n. | 可扩展性 | The ability of a system, network, or process to handle a growing amount of work, or its potential to be enlarged to accommodate that growth.  This often refers to the ease and efficiency with which this growth can be managed.  It encompasses both horizontal scalability (adding more resources) and vertical scalability (increasing the capacity of existing resources). |  The cloud-based system boasts impressive scalableness, easily handling a tenfold increase in user traffic.  The scalableness of the software allows it to adapt to the growing needs of our business. |  extensibility, scalability, adaptability | inflexibility, rigidity
