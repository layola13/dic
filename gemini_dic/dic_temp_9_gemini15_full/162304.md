preaccount:/priːəˈkaʊnt/| v.|预先计算，预估|To calculate or estimate something in advance.|The accountant preaccounted the expenses for the upcoming project. 会计预先计算了即将开展项目的支出。|近义词：forecast, estimate, project|反义词：post-account (This is not a standard word, but illustrates the opposite action.)


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They preaccount for potential risks. = 他们预先计算潜在风险。
We preaccount the project budget carefully. = 我们仔细地预先计算项目预算。


一般过去时:
The team preaccounted the travel costs. = 团队预先计算了差旅费用。
She preaccounted the materials needed for the construction. = 她预先计算了建筑所需的材料。


现在完成时:
They have preaccounted all the expenses. = 他们已经预先计算了所有费用。
We have preaccounted for possible delays. = 我们已经预先考虑了可能的延误。


过去完成时:
The company had preaccounted for the inflation before making the investment. = 公司在投资前已预先考虑了通货膨胀。
They had preaccounted the profit margin before launching the product. = 他们在推出产品之前已经预先计算了利润率。


将来时:
We will preaccount the revenue for the next quarter. = 我们将预先计算下一季度的收入。
They will preaccount the impact of the new policy. = 他们将预先计算新政策的影响。


虚拟语气:
If we preaccounted the expenses accurately, we would have avoided the financial crisis. = 如果我们准确地预先计算了支出，我们就不会遇到财务危机。
I wish we had preaccounted for the unexpected costs. = 我希望我们已经预先计算了意外费用。


被动语态:
The costs were preaccounted by the finance department. = 成本由财务部门预先计算。
The risks should be preaccounted for in the planning stage. = 风险应该在计划阶段预先计算。


疑问句:
Have they preaccounted for all possible scenarios? = 他们是否已经预先计算了所有可能的情况？
Did you preaccount the time needed for the project? = 你是否预先计算了项目所需的时间？


否定句:
They did not preaccount for the increase in raw material prices. = 他们没有预先计算原材料价格的上涨。
We didn't preaccount for the unforeseen circumstances. = 我们没有预先计算到不可预见的情况。
