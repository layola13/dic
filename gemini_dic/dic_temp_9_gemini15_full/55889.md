divisors:/dɪˈvaɪzərz/ | n. |除数，因子|A divisor of a number is a number that divides it exactly without leaving a remainder.  For example, the divisors of 12 are 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, and 12.|Example:  The divisors of 24 are 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 8, 12, and 24.  24的除数是1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 8, 12和24。|近义词:factor, submultiple|反义词:multiple


Etymology:  The word "divisor" comes from the Latin word "dividere," meaning "to divide."  The suffix "-or" indicates the agent or instrument of the action.


USAGE->
This word is primarily a noun and doesn't conjugate like verbs.  Its usage depends on the context of mathematical operations.
