词源:

The word "permanence" comes from the Old French word "permanence," which itself derives from the Latin word "permanentia," a noun formed from the present active participle of the verb "permanere," meaning "to remain," "to stay," or "to last."  The Latin verb "permanere" is composed of the prefix "per-" (completely, thoroughly) and "manere" (to stay, remain).


用法说明:

Permanence is a noun referring to the state or quality of being permanent; the lasting nature of something.  It implies a state that is intended to continue indefinitely or for a very long time without change.

例句:

1.  The artist sought to capture the permanence of the mountain landscape in his painting.  艺术家试图在他的画作中捕捉山区景观的永恒性。

2.  The company's commitment to environmental sustainability ensures the long-term permanence of its resources.  公司对环境可持续性的承诺确保了其资源的长期持久性。

3.  Despite the challenges, they believed in the permanence of their love.  尽管面临挑战，他们相信他们的爱情是永恒的。

4.  The inscription on the monument spoke of the permanence of their memory.  纪念碑上的铭文讲述了他们记忆的永恒性。

5.  The permanence of digital data is a growing concern. 数字数据的持久性是一个日益关注的问题。


近义词:

* durability:  The ability to withstand wear, pressure, or damage.
* lastingness:  The quality of lasting for a long time.
* perpetuity:  The state or quality of lasting forever.
* endurance: The ability to withstand hardship or difficulty.


反义词:

* transitoriness:  The quality of being temporary or fleeting.
* ephemerality:  The quality of lasting for a very short time.
* impermanence:  The state or quality of not being permanent.
* brevity: Shortness of duration.
