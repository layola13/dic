/priːˈæŋkts/ | v. |预演；预先排练；预先行动|To rehearse or practice something beforehand; to act out something in advance.|The actors preened and preenacted their roles before the opening night.  演员们在开幕式前精心打扮并预演了他们的角色。|rehearse, practice, simulate|—|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They preenact the play every day. = 他们每天预演这出戏。
She preens and preenacts her presentation before giving it. = 她会在做演讲之前精心打扮并预演。


一般过去时:
The students preenacted the historical event for their history class. = 学生们为他们的历史课预演了这一历史事件。
He preenacted the scene several times before filming. = 他在拍摄前排练了这个场景好几次。


现在完成时:
They have preenacted the scene many times. = 他们已经排练过这个场景很多次了。
She has preenacted her speech countless times. = 她已经无数次排练过她的演讲了。


过去完成时:
They had preenacted the entire play before the actual performance. = 他们在正式演出之前已经预演了整出戏。
She had preenacted the difficult parts of the dance numerous times. = 她已经多次预演了舞蹈中难度较大的部分。


将来时:

They will preenact the entire musical before the premiere. = 他们将在首映前预演整部音乐剧。
