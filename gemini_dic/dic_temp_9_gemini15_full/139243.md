obligation:/ˌɒblɪˈɡeɪʃən/ /ˌɑːblɪˈɡeɪʃən/| n. |义务；责任；职责 |A moral or legal duty to do something.  It refers to something that one is bound to do, whether by law, promise, or a sense of duty. |例句：He felt an obligation to help his family. 他觉得有义务帮助他的家人。

例句：Paying taxes is a legal obligation. 纳税是法律义务。


近义词：duty, responsibility, commitment, requirement |反义词：freedom, liberty, right


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I have an obligation to finish this project by Friday. = 我有义务在星期五之前完成这个项目。
My obligation is to protect my clients. = 我的职责是保护我的客户。


一般过去时:
He felt a strong obligation to repay the kindness. = 他感到强烈的义务要报答这份恩情。
She fulfilled her obligation to her community. = 她履行了她对社区的义务。


现在完成时:
I have fulfilled all my obligations. = 我已经履行了所有我的义务。
We have met our obligations under the contract. = 我们已经履行了合同规定的义务。


过去完成时:
He had already fulfilled his obligation before the deadline. = 他在截止日期之前就已经履行了他的义务。
They had neglected their obligation to inform the authorities. = 他们忽视了告知当局的义务。


将来时:
I will have an obligation to explain my actions. = 我将有义务解释我的行为。
She will meet her obligation to donate to charity. = 她将履行她向慈善机构捐款的义务。


虚拟语气:
If I had known, I would have felt an obligation to intervene. = 如果我知道，我会感到有义务干预。
I wish I hadn't felt obligated to attend. = 我真希望我没有感到有义务参加。


被动语态:
The company was obligated to pay compensation. = 公司有义务支付赔偿金。
All citizens are obligated to obey the law. = 所有公民都有义务遵守法律。


疑问句:
What are your obligations in this matter? = 你在这件事中有什么义务？
Do you have any obligations that prevent you from attending? = 你有什么义务妨碍你参加吗？


否定句:
I have no obligation to assist him. = 我没有义务帮助他。
She was under no obligation to answer his questions. = 她没有义务回答他的问题。
