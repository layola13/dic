musicologist:/ˌmjuːzɪˈkɒlədʒɪst/|/ˈmjuːzɪkɑːlədʒɪst/| n. |音乐学家| A musicologist is a scholar who studies music.  Their work involves researching, analyzing, and interpreting music from various historical periods and cultures. This can encompass a wide range of topics, from the history of musical instruments to the analysis of musical scores and the social context of music-making. |Example:  The musicologist delivered a fascinating lecture on the evolution of Baroque music.  音乐学家就巴洛克音乐的演变作了一个引人入胜的演讲。


Example:  A renowned musicologist recently published a groundbreaking study on the influence of Gregorian chant on early Renaissance music.一位著名的音乐学家最近发表了一项关于格里高利圣咏对早期文艺复兴时期音乐影响的开创性研究。|近义词：music scholar, music historian|反义词：无
