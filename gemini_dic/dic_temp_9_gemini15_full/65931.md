exacerbation:/ˌɛksæsərˈbeɪʃən/| n. |恶化；加剧；加重 |The exacerbation of the conflict led to more casualties. 冲突的加剧导致更多伤亡。| worsening, intensification, aggravation |improvement, alleviation, mitigation |名词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
An exacerbation of symptoms is common in this disease. = 这种疾病的症状加重是很常见的。
The current economic situation shows no sign of exacerbation. = 当前的经济形势没有恶化的迹象。


一般过去时:
The drought exacerbated the existing food shortage. = 干旱加剧了现有的粮食短缺。
His careless words exacerbated the already tense situation. = 他粗心的话语加剧了本已紧张的局势。


现在完成时:
The recent policy changes have exacerbated the inequality gap. = 最近的政策变化加剧了不平等的差距。
Years of conflict have exacerbated the humanitarian crisis. = 多年的冲突加剧了人道主义危机。


过去完成时:
The heavy rain had exacerbated the already flooded river. = 大雨加剧了已经泛滥的河流。
The previous government's policies had exacerbated the economic problems. = 前政府的政策加剧了经济问题。


将来时:
The predicted heatwave will exacerbate the water shortage. = 预计的热浪将加剧缺水问题。
Further delays will only exacerbate the already difficult situation. = 进一步的延误只会加剧本已艰难的局面。


虚拟语气:
If we don't address the issue now, it will only exacerbate the problem. = 如果我们现在不解决这个问题，只会加剧问题。
I wish the government had not implemented policies that exacerbated the situation. = 我希望政府没有实施加剧局势的政策。


被动语态:
The situation was exacerbated by the unexpected storm. = 局势因突如其来的风暴而加剧。
The tensions were exacerbated by the media's biased reporting. = 媒体的偏颇报道加剧了紧张局势。


疑问句:
Will this new policy exacerbate the existing problems? = 这项新政策会加剧现有的问题吗？
What factors exacerbated the crisis? = 哪些因素加剧了危机？


否定句:
The treatment did not exacerbate the patient's condition. = 治疗没有加重病人的病情。
The new law did not exacerbate the social inequalities. = 新法律没有加剧社会不平等。


PHRASE->
exacerbation of symptoms: 症状加剧
exacerbation of the disease: 病情加剧
exacerbation of conflict: 冲突加剧
exacerbation of the problem: 问题的加剧
exacerbation of tensions: 紧张局势的加剧


词源:  源自拉丁语“exacerbare”，由“ex-” (表示“出，外”)和“acerbus” (表示“苦涩的，尖锐的”)构成，字面意思是“使变尖锐，使更苦涩”，引申为“加剧，恶化”。
