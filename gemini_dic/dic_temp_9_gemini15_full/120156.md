mind:/maɪnd/| n. vt.|思想，头脑；介意；关心；注意；心思|英文解析：The mind is the faculty of consciousness and thought.  It can also refer to one's opinion or intention. As a verb, it means to object to something or to pay attention to something.|例句：He has a brilliant mind. 他有颗聪明的头脑。|近义词：intellect, brain, spirit, attention|反义词：body, matter, uncaring, inattentive|名词，动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I don't mind the noise. = 我不介意噪音。
He minds his own business. = 他管好自己的事。


一般过去时:
She minded her children carefully. = 她细心地照看她的孩子。
They didn't mind the long journey. = 他们不介意漫长的旅程。


现在完成时:
Have you ever minded your own business? = 你曾经注意过自己的事情吗？
I haven't minded the cold weather much. = 我并没有太在意寒冷的天气。


过去完成时:
He had minded his parents' advice before making the decision. = 他在做决定之前已经听取了父母的建议。
She had minded the dog all day. = 她一整天都在照看那条狗。


将来时:
I will mind the baby while you're out. = 你不在的时候我会照看婴儿。
He will mind the shop tomorrow. = 他明天将照看商店。


虚拟语气:
If I minded my own business, I wouldn't get into trouble. = 如果我管好自己的事，我就不会惹上麻烦。
I wish I hadn't minded the little things so much. = 我真希望我不要那么在意那些小事。


被动语态:
The children were minded by their grandmother. = 孩子们由他们的祖母照顾。
The shop will be minded by his brother. = 商店将由他哥哥照看。


疑问句:
Do you mind if I smoke? = 你介意我抽烟吗？
Would you mind opening the window? = 你介意打开窗户吗？


否定句:
I don't mind helping you. = 我不介意帮你。
He didn't mind the extra work. = 他不介意额外的工作。


PHRASE->
bear in mind = 记住
make up one's mind = 下定决心
change one's mind = 改变主意
have something in mind = 心里想着什么
out of one's mind = 疯了
