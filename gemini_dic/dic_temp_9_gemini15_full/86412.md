heed:/hiːd/ | vt. |注意，留心，理睬|To pay attention to; to take notice of.  It implies careful consideration and often suggests acting on the advice or warning.|例句：Heed my warning and don't go near the water.  注意我的警告，不要靠近水边。

例句：You should heed the advice of your elders. 你应该听从长辈的建议。|近义词：attend to, mind, consider, obey |反义词：ignore, disregard, neglect |动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He heeds my advice. = 他听从我的建议。
She heeds the warning signs. = 她注意警告标志。

一般过去时:
They heeded the call for help. = 他们响应了求救呼声。
He heeded his mother's warnings. = 他听从了他母亲的警告。

现在完成时:
I have heeded your advice and changed my plans. = 我已经听取了你的建议并改变了我的计划。
She has heeded the doctor's instructions. = 她已经听从了医生的指示。

过去完成时:
He had heeded the storm warnings and stayed home. = 他已注意到了暴风雨警告并待在家里。
They had heeded the advice and avoided the danger. = 他们听了劝告，避免了危险。

将来时:
We will heed the advice of the experts. = 我们将听取专家的意见。
She will heed her conscience. = 她将遵从她的良心。

虚拟语气:
If only he had heeded my warning! = 要是他听了我的警告就好了！
I wish she had heeded my advice. = 我希望她听了我的劝告。

被动语态:
His advice was heeded by many. = 他的建议被许多人采纳。
The warning was not heeded. = 警告没有被理会。

疑问句:
Did you heed the instructions? = 你注意到了指示吗？
Will they heed the call to action? = 他们会响应行动号召吗？

否定句:
He did not heed my warnings. = 他没有理会我的警告。
They ignored the signs; they didn't heed them. = 他们忽视了这些迹象；他们没有理会它们。
