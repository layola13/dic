polyiodide:/ˌpɒliˈaɪədaɪd/| n. |多碘化物|A polyiodide is a polyatomic anion composed of several iodine atoms.  These anions are formed by the reaction of iodine with iodide ions. They are typically found in solutions containing iodine and iodide salts.  The specific composition can vary depending on the conditions,  resulting in anions such as I3− (triiodide), I5− (pentaiodide), and higher order polyiodides. |Example: The formation of polyiodides is often observed in solutions containing iodine and potassium iodide.|近义词: iodine complex |反义词: None readily available.
