fire:/faɪər/| n., v.|火，火焰；开除；射击；点燃|A rapid oxidation process, typically accompanied by heat and light.|近义词：blaze, flame, combust, dismiss|反义词：ice, water, extinguish


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The fire burns brightly. = 火烧得很旺。
He fires the gun. = 他开枪。


一般过去时:
The fire burned all night. = 火烧了一整夜。
They fired him from his job. = 他们把他解雇了。


现在完成时:
The firefighters have extinguished the fire. = 消防员已经扑灭了大火。
She has fired several employees this year. = 今年她已经解雇了好几个员工。


过去完成时:
The building had already caught fire before the firefighters arrived. = 消防员到达之前，大楼已经着火了。
The company had fired him before the scandal broke. = 丑闻爆发之前，公司已经解雇了他。


将来时:
I will fire the oven when it's time to bake. = 到烘焙时间的时候我会打开烤箱。
They will fire the cannon at noon. = 他们将在中午开炮。


虚拟语气:

If I were you, I wouldn't fire her. = 如果我是你，我不会解雇她。


被动语态:
The forest was fired by a careless camper. = 森林是被一个粗心的露营者点燃的。
He was fired for misconduct. = 他因为行为不端而被解雇。


疑问句:
Is the fire still burning? = 火还在烧吗？
Has he been fired yet? = 他已经被解雇了吗？


否定句:
The fire didn't spread to the other buildings. = 火势没有蔓延到其他建筑物。
They didn't fire anyone this month. = 这个月他们没有解雇任何人。

词源:
The word "fire" has roots in Proto-Germanic *fēru, meaning "fire," which further traces back to Proto-Indo-European *péh₂wēr, also meaning "fire."  This reflects a common root for words related to fire across many Indo-European languages.
