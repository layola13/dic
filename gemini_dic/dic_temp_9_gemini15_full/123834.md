morale:/məˈrɑːl/| n. |士气，斗志，精神状态 | The feeling of enthusiasm and confidence of a group of people, especially an army.  It refers to the mental and emotional condition of a group, affecting their willingness to work and achieve goals. |例句：Team morale was high after their victory. 团队在胜利后士气高涨。

例句：Low morale among the troops led to a decline in combat effectiveness. 士气低落导致部队战斗力下降。


近义词：spirit, enthusiasm, confidence, optimism | 反义词：despair, depression, pessimism


词源:  The word "morale" comes from the French word "moral," meaning "moral character" or "morals," which in turn derives from the Latin word "moralis,"  having the same meaning. Over time, the meaning shifted to encompass the collective spirit and mental state of a group.
