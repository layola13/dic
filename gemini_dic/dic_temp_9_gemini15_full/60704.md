electro-acoustical: /ˌɪˌlektrəʊəˈkuːstɪkəl/ | adj. | 电声的；电声学的 | Relating to or using a combination of electrical and acoustic methods, especially in the recording or reproduction of sound.  This term describes systems or processes that involve both electrical and acoustic components in the transmission or manipulation of sound. For example, an electro-acoustical instrument uses electronic amplification or effects to modify acoustic sound. | The concert featured an electro-acoustical performance using both traditional instruments and electronic effects.  The new electro-acoustical system dramatically improved the sound quality in the auditorium. | 近义词：electronic, audio | 反义词：acoustic (when used in the context of purely acoustic sound)
