monocondyla:/ˌmɒnəˈkɒndɪlə/| n. |单髁的，单髁动物|A monocondyla is an organism or anatomical structure characterized by having a single condyle (a rounded articular process).  This term is primarily used in zoology and anatomy, often referring to the structure of the skull or jaw. For instance, mammals typically have a monocondylic jaw, meaning their lower jaw articulates with the skull at a single point.|The human skull is monocondylic at the jaw joint. = 人类的头骨在下颌关节处是单髁的。|  The term is often used in comparative anatomy to distinguish between different types of jaw articulation.|polycondylic|polycondylic
