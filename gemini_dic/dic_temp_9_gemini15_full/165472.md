preliquidations:/ˌpriːˌlɪkwɪˈdeɪʃənz/| n. |清算前的准备工作；破产前的准备|The process of preparing for a liquidation, often involving assessing assets, liabilities, and potential legal issues before the formal liquidation process begins.  This includes steps like gathering financial records, notifying creditors, and potentially seeking professional advice.|例句：The company undertook extensive preliquidations before filing for bankruptcy. 公司在申请破产前进行了广泛的清算前准备工作。 The preliquidations involved a thorough review of all financial records. 清算前的准备工作包括对所有财务记录进行彻底审查。|近义词：pre-bankruptcy preparations, pre-insolvency procedures|反义词：post-liquidation activities|名词
