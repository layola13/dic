paranoid:/ˈpærənɔɪd/| adj.|偏执狂的；患妄想症的|Characterized by or exhibiting paranoia; suffering from paranoia.  It implies a persistent feeling of being persecuted or threatened, often without any real evidence.  The term can be used to describe both a mental state and a personality trait.|例句：He became paranoid after the break-in, constantly checking the locks and windows. 他在入室盗窃后变得疑神疑鬼，不断检查门锁和窗户。|例句：Her paranoid personality made it difficult to trust anyone. 她多疑的性格让她很难相信任何人。|近义词：suspicious, distrustful, delusional, apprehensive|反义词：trusting, confident, secure, calm


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He is paranoid about germs. = 他对细菌非常偏执。
She is paranoid about her safety. = 她对自身安全非常焦虑。

一般过去时:
He was paranoid after seeing the scary movie. = 看完恐怖电影后，他变得疑神疑鬼。
They were paranoid that someone was following them. = 他们疑神疑鬼地认为有人跟踪他们。

现在完成时:
I have been paranoid ever since the incident. = 自从那件事发生后，我就一直疑神疑鬼。
She has been paranoid about her health lately. = 最近她一直担心自己的健康。

过去完成时:
He had been paranoid for months before seeking help. = 在寻求帮助之前，他已经疑神疑鬼好几个月了。
They had been paranoid about the possibility of a leak. = 他们一直担心信息泄露的可能性。


将来时:
He will be paranoid if he doesn't get enough sleep. = 如果他睡不好，他会变得疑神疑鬼。
She will be paranoid if she finds out the truth. = 如果她发现真相，她会变得疑神疑鬼。

虚拟语气:
If I were paranoid, I would install security cameras. = 如果我很疑神疑鬼，我会安装监控摄像头。
I wish I weren't so paranoid about everything. = 我希望自己不要对所有事情都那么疑神疑鬼。


被动语态:
His behavior was interpreted as paranoid. = 他的行为被解读为偏执狂。
The situation was made worse by his paranoid reactions. = 他偏执的反应使情况变得更糟。


疑问句:
Is he paranoid about something? = 他是不是对什么事情疑神疑鬼？
Are you feeling paranoid lately? = 你最近是不是感觉很焦虑？


否定句:
He is not paranoid, just cautious. = 他不是疑神疑鬼，只是谨慎。
She is not paranoid; she's simply aware of her surroundings. = 她不是疑神疑鬼，只是对周围环境很警觉。

词源:
From Greek *para- ("beside, beyond") + nous ("mind").  The term originally referred to a mental disorder characterized by delusions of persecution.  Its modern usage is broader, encompassing both clinical and informal applications.
