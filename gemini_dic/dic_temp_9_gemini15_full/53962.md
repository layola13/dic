discrediting: /dɪsˈkrɛdɪtɪŋ/ | v. | 败坏名誉；使失信；使怀疑 | To damage the reputation or credibility of someone or something.  |例句：The politician's lies are discrediting his entire career.  政治家的谎言正在败坏他的整个职业生涯。  |例句：The new evidence discredits the defendant's alibi. 新证据使被告的不在场证明不可信。 |近义词：disgracing, shaming, undermining, impugning |反义词：crediting, validating, confirming, supporting |动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is discrediting the company's image. = 他正在败坏公司的形象。
They are discrediting the witness's testimony. = 他们正在使证人的证词失去可信度。

一般过去时:
The scandal discredited the government. = 丑闻败坏了政府的名声。
His actions discredited his family. = 他的行为败坏了他的家庭名誉。

现在完成时:
The media has discredited the celebrity's claims. = 媒体已经使这位名人的说法失去了可信度。
Recent findings have discredited the old theory. = 最近的发现已经使旧理论失去了可信度。

过去完成时:
They had discredited the scientist's research before the conference. = 他们在会议之前就已经败坏了这位科学家的研究成果的名誉。
The evidence had discredited the original hypothesis. = 证据已经使最初的假设失去了可信度。

将来时:
This new information will discredit the politician. = 这个新信息将会败坏这位政治家的名誉。
The upcoming report will discredit their findings. = 即将来临的报告将使他们的发现失去可信度。

虚拟语气:
If he continued to lie, he would discredit himself. = 如果他继续说谎，他会败坏自己的名誉。
If the evidence had been stronger, it would have discredited the defense. = 如果证据更充分，它就会使辩护失去可信度。

被动语态:
The politician was discredited by the scandal. = 这位政治家被丑闻败坏了名誉。
The theory was discredited by new research. = 这个理论被新的研究成果推翻了。

疑问句:
Is the evidence sufficient to discredit the claim? = 证据是否足以使这一说法失去可信度？
Does this information discredit his reputation? = 这个信息是否败坏了他的名誉？

否定句:
The new evidence did not discredit the defendant. = 新证据并没有使被告失去可信度。
The investigation did not discredit the company's practices. = 调查并没有败坏公司的行为。
