misconceit:/ˌmɪsˈkənsiːt/| n. |误解；错误观念；幻想 | A false idea or belief; a misconception.  |例句：His belief that he could fly was a dangerous misconciept. 他相信自己能飞，这是一个危险的误解。

例句：The article corrected several misconceptions about the war. 这篇文章纠正了关于战争的几个误解。


近义词：delusion, fallacy, illusion, misconception, misapprehension | 反义词：truth, reality, fact


词源: mis- (wrongly) + conceit (a conception or idea)  源于古法语 *conceit，意为“想法，概念”，最终源于拉丁语 *concipere，意为“构思，设想”。


USAGE->
This word is a noun, and therefore doesn't conjugate through tenses like verbs.  The usage focuses on its application in sentences.

例句1：His misconciept of the situation led to a disastrous outcome. 他对局势的误解导致了灾难性的结果。

例句2：  Many people harbor misconceptions about climate change. 许多人对气候变化持有误解。


PHRASE->
There are no common idiomatic phrases directly using "misconceit."  Phrases would involve using it within a sentence structure.  For example:  "A common misconception is..." or "Under the misconception that..."
