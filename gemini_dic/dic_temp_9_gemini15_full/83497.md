hackle:/ˈhækəl/ | n. v. | 鸡皮疙瘩；（使）竖起（羽毛）；激怒 | The raised feathers on the neck of a bird, especially a fowl. Also, to raise the hackles of someone; to irritate or anger someone. |例句：The dog's hackles rose when it saw the stranger.  = 那只狗看到陌生人时毛都竖起来了。


The farmer's hackles were raised by the disrespectful remarks. = 农民被那些不敬的话激怒了。


近义词：ruff, bristle, irritate, anger | 反义词：calm, soothe


词源:  From Middle English *hakke, *hakkele, of uncertain origin.  Possibly related to Old Norse hakki ("hook," "notch"), referring to the hooked appearance of raised feathers.
