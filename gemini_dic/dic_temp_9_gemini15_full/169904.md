prospector:/ˈprɒspektə(r)/|/ˈprɑːspektər/| n. |探矿者；勘探者 |A prospector is someone who searches for valuable minerals or other geological resources, typically in remote areas.  They often use tools like picks, shovels, and pans to extract samples and assess the potential of a site. |例句：The seasoned prospector spent years searching for gold in the rugged mountains.  经验丰富的探矿者在崎岖的山区寻找黄金多年。

The lone prospector struck it rich when he discovered a vein of silver.  孤独的探矿者发现了一条银矿脉，发了财。


近义词：explorer, miner, searcher |反义词：N/A

词源:
The word "prospector" comes from the verb "prospect," which ultimately derives from the Middle English word "prospectiven," meaning "to look forward to," or "to look out."  The sense of searching for something valuable developed over time.
