ring-fences:/ˈrɪŋfɛnsɪz/| n.|隔离墙；保护措施；限制；界限|To ring-fence something means to protect it from interference or encroachment.  It's often used in financial contexts to protect assets or budgets.|The company ring-fenced a significant portion of its profits for research and development.  这家公司将很大一部分利润划拨用于研发。|The government has ring-fenced funding for education. 政府已为教育拨款划拨专款。|protective measures, safeguards, barriers, restrictions|open access, unrestricted access|名词

USAGE->
一般现在时:
They ring-fence the budget for emergencies. = 他们为紧急情况划拨专款。
The company ring-fences its intellectual property. = 公司保护其知识产权。


一般过去时:
The government ring-fenced funds for healthcare. = 政府为医疗保健划拨了专款。
They ring-fenced the area to prevent contamination. = 他们隔离了该区域以防止污染。


现在完成时:
The company has ring-fenced its profits for expansion. = 公司已将利润用于扩张。
They have ring-fenced the land for future development. = 他们已将土地圈起来用于未来的开发。


过去完成时:
The investors had ring-fenced their assets before the market crash. = 在市场崩盘之前，投资者已保护好自己的资产。
They had ring-fenced the sensitive data before the hack. = 他们在遭到黑客攻击之前已保护好敏感数据。


将来时:
The board will ring-fence the project from budget cuts. = 董事会将保护该项目免受预算削减的影响。
They will ring-fence the park from further development. = 他们将保护公园免受进一步开发的影响。


虚拟语气:
If they ring-fenced the funds, the project would succeed. = 如果他们为项目拨款，该项目就会成功。
I wish they had ring-fenced the crucial information. = 我希望他们已经保护好关键信息。


被动语态:
The budget was ring-fenced for essential services. = 预算被划拨用于基本服务。
The area was ring-fenced to prevent access. = 该区域被隔离以防止进入。


疑问句:

否定句:

The company didn't ring-fence its intellectual property effectively. = 公司没有有效地保护其知识产权。
