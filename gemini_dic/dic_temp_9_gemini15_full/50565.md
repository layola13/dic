deputyship: /ˈdepjuːtʃɪp/ | n. | 代理职务；代理权；副职 | The position or duties of a deputy; the state of being a deputy.  |例句：He was appointed to the deputyship of the sheriff. 他被任命为警长的代理人。

例句：The deputyship of the mayor requires a strong understanding of municipal affairs. 市长代理的职位需要对市政事务有深入的了解。


近义词：deputieship,  代理职位,  substitution,  代理


反义词：principalship,  本职职位


词源:  From deputy + -ship.  "-ship" is a suffix forming nouns denoting state, condition, skill, office, etc.  "Deputy" comes from the Old French "depute", meaning "appointed," from the Latin "deputare," meaning "to appoint, to delegate."
