fabricated:/ˈfæbrɪkeɪtɪd/ | adj. | 捏造的；虚构的；人造的 | Constructed artificially; not genuine or natural.  It implies something is made up or invented, often to deceive. | 例句：The evidence presented was entirely fabricated.  提供的证据完全是捏造的。

The story he told was clearly fabricated; it didn't make sense. 他讲的故事显然是编造的；讲不通。


近义词：invented, concocted, manufactured, forged, spurious, artificial


反义词：genuine, authentic, real, natural, true


词性：形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The news report is fabricated. = 这篇新闻报道是捏造的。
This evidence is fabricated. = 这证据是伪造的。


一般过去时:
The suspect fabricated the alibi. = 嫌疑人编造了不在场证明。
They fabricated a story to explain the delay. = 他们编造了一个故事来解释延误。


现在完成时:
The company has fabricated a lot of false information. = 这家公司捏造了许多虚假信息。
She has fabricated a new identity for herself. = 她为自己捏造了一个新的身份。


过去完成时:

虚拟语气:
If he had not fabricated the evidence, he would not be in jail. = 如果他没有伪造证据，他就不会在监狱里。

否定句:
The statement was not fabricated; it was based on fact. = 这份声明不是捏造的；它是基于事实的。
