honky-tonk:/ˈhɑːŋkiːtɑːŋk/| n. |廉价的乡村酒吧；喧闹的场所|A honky-tonk is a small, usually seedy bar, typically playing country music and attracting a rough crowd.  It often implies a place with loud music, cheap drinks, and a lively, sometimes rowdy atmosphere.|例句：We spent the evening at a honky-tonk in Nashville, enjoying the live music and dancing. 我们在纳什维尔的一家乡村酒吧度过了夜晚，享受着现场音乐和舞蹈。


例句：The honky-tonk was packed with people, and the air was thick with cigarette smoke and the smell of cheap beer. 这家乡村酒吧挤满了人，空气中弥漫着香烟和廉价啤酒的气味。|近义词：dive bar, saloon, roadhouse|反义词：upscale bar, elegant club|词性：名词
