postcodes:/ˈpoʊstkoʊdz/ | n. |邮政编码|A postcode is a series of letters and numbers used to identify a specific postal area, facilitating efficient mail sorting and delivery.  Postcodes are used in many countries, and their format varies. In the UK, for example, a postcode consists of a combination of letters and numbers, such as SW1A 2AA.  The system helps to speed up delivery and improve accuracy. |The package was returned because the postcode was incorrect.  包裹因为邮政编码错误而被退回。

The new house has a different postcode. 新房子有不同的邮政编码。
