keelhaul: /ˈkiːlˌhɔːl/ | vt. | 把…从船底拖过 | To drag (someone) under the keel of a ship as a punishment. This was a brutal form of punishment inflicted on sailors in the past, involving dragging them under the ship's keel, often resulting in death or severe injury.  | The mutinous sailors were keelhauled as punishment.  水手们因叛变而被拖过船底作为惩罚。| flog, scourge |  praise, reward |  动词
