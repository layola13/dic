compartmentalizes:/kəmˈpɑːrtˈmɛntəlaɪzɪz/ | vt. | 分隔；划分；区分；将…分门别类 | To divide into compartments or categories; to separate into isolated sections or areas of activity.  This often implies a deliberate or even artificial separation, sometimes to avoid dealing with difficult emotions or situations. |

例句：

He compartmentalizes his work and personal life very effectively. = 他非常有效地将工作和生活区分开来。

She compartmentalizes her feelings, refusing to let her sadness affect her work. = 她将自己的感情分隔开来，拒绝让悲伤影响她的工作。


近义词：separate, isolate, categorize, classify, section off


反义词：integrate, unify, combine, blend


词性：动词

There are no common phrases directly using "compartmentalizes."  However, phrases describing the result of compartmentalization are common:

* **Mental compartments:** 指的是心理上的隔间，用来指人们用来划分不同想法或感受的区域。
* **Watertight compartments:** 指的是完全密封的隔间，通常用于船只或其他结构，以防止水渗入其他区域。
* **Separate compartments:** 指的是分开的隔间，用来强调不同部分之间的独立性。
* **Emotional compartmentalization:** 指的是将情绪分隔开来，通常是为了应对压力或创伤。
* **Life compartments:** 指的是生活中的不同方面，例如工作、家庭和个人爱好。


词源:

The word "compartmentalizes" is derived from "compartment," which comes from the French word "compartiment," ultimately from the Latin words "cum" (with) and "partire" (to divide).  The "-ize" suffix turns the noun into a verb, indicating the action of creating or dividing into compartments.
