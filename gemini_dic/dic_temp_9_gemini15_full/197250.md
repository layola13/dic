sharia:/ʃəˈriːə/| n.|伊斯兰教法|Islamic law, derived from the Quran and the Sunnah (teachings and practices of the Prophet Muhammad), which governs many aspects of Muslim life, including religious rituals, family law, criminal justice, and commercial transactions.  It's a complex and diverse system with varying interpretations across different schools of Islamic thought.|The application of sharia varies widely across Muslim-majority countries, ranging from its complete integration into the legal system to its limited role in personal matters.  Some countries have codified aspects of sharia into secular law, while others maintain a strict separation.|近义词：Islamic law, religious law|反义词：secular law
