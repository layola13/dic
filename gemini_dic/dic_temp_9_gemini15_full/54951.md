dispraisingly: /dɪsˈpreɪzɪŋli/ | adv. | 贬抑地；轻蔑地 |  In a manner that expresses disapproval or criticism.  It implies a negative and often condescending tone. | 例句：He spoke dispraisingly of his rival's achievements. 他轻蔑地谈论了他对手的成就。

He looked at her dispraisingly, his expression clearly showing his disapproval. 他轻蔑地望着她，他的表情清楚地表明了他的不满。 | 近义词：contemptuously, scornfully, disparagingly, derisively | 反义词：praisingly, approvingly, commendably


USAGE->
This word is an adverb, so it doesn't have tenses like verbs.  Its usage is always to modify a verb, adjective, or another adverb.

Example Modifying Verbs:

He spoke dispraisingly. 他贬抑地说。
She looked at the painting dispraisingly. 她轻蔑地看了看那幅画。

Example Modifying Adjectives:

He was dispraisingly critical of her work. 他对她的作品进行了贬低性的批评。(Here it modifies the adjective 'critical')
His tone was dispraisingly harsh. 他的语气是贬抑地严厉。(Here it modifies the adjective 'harsh')

Example Modifying Adverbs:

He spoke dispraisingly quickly. 他匆匆忙忙地贬低地说。(Here it modifies the adverb 'quickly')
She reacted dispraisingly sharply. 她反应尖锐地表示轻蔑。(Here it modifies the adverb 'sharply')



PHRASE->
There aren't common set phrases specifically using "dispraisingly".  Its usage is more about modifying existing phrases.  For example:

Dispraisingly comment: 贬低的评论
Dispraisingly criticize: 轻蔑地批评
Speak dispraisingly: 贬抑地说
Look dispraisingly: 轻蔑地看
Write dispraisingly: 贬低地写

词源:

The word is formed from the prefix "dis-" (meaning "not" or "opposite of"), the verb "praise" (meaning to express approval or admiration), and the adverbial suffix "-ingly" (meaning "in a manner").  Therefore, "dispraisingly" means "in a manner of not praising," or "in a disapproving manner."
