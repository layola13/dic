delegation: /ˌdɛlɪˈɡeɪʃən/ | n. | 代表团；委派；授权 | A delegation is a group of people sent to represent others, or the act of assigning responsibility to another person or group.  It can also refer to the act of entrusting a task or responsibility to someone else. |例句：The delegation from China arrived in New York yesterday. 中国代表团昨天抵达纽约。

The manager delegated the task to his assistant. 经理将任务委派给了他的助理。|近义词：representation, assignment, commission, entrustment |反义词：retention, self-governance


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company sends a delegation to the conference every year.  公司每年都派代表团参加会议。
He delegates many tasks to his team members. 他把许多任务委派给他的团队成员。


一般过去时:
The government sent a special delegation to negotiate a peace treaty. 政府派了一个特别代表团去谈判和平条约。
She delegated the project to a junior employee. 她把项目委派给了一名初级员工。


现在完成时:
The president has delegated authority to his cabinet. 总统已将权力委托给了他的内阁。
They have sent several delegations to different countries this year. 他们今年已经派出了几个代表团前往不同的国家。


过去完成时:
The committee had delegated the responsibility before the meeting adjourned. 委员会在会议休会前已委派了责任。
The company had already sent a delegation to address the issue. 公司已经派代表团去处理这个问题了。


将来时:
The company will send a delegation to the trade show next month. 公司下个月将派代表团参加贸易展。
He will delegate the responsibility to his most experienced team member. 他将把责任委托给经验最丰富的团队成员。


虚拟语气:
If I were the manager, I would delegate more tasks. 如果我是经理，我会委派更多的任务。
If the delegation had arrived earlier, the negotiations would have been more productive. 如果代表团早点到达，谈判就会更有成效。


被动语态:
The task was delegated to the most qualified person. 任务被委派给了最合格的人。
The authority was delegated by the board of directors. 权力是由董事会授权的。


疑问句:
Who will be leading the delegation? 谁将领导代表团？
To whom was the project delegated? 项目委托给了谁？


否定句:

The company did not send a delegation to the conference. 公司没有派代表团参加会议。
