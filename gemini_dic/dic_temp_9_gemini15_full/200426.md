slacker:/ˈslækər/| n.|懒鬼，游手好闲的人|A person who avoids work or effort.|He's become a total slacker since he lost his job. 他自从丢了工作就变成一个十足的懒鬼了。|loafer, idler, goof-off|hard worker, diligent person|名词

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He slacks off at work. = 他在工作中偷懒。
They slack off during the afternoon. = 他们下午偷懒。

一般过去时:
He slacked off on his studies last semester. = 上学期他学习很懈怠。
She slacked off after winning the competition. = 比赛获胜后，她松懈了。

现在完成时:
He has slacked off all year. = 他整年都在偷懒。
She has slacked off on her responsibilities. = 她对自己的责任懈怠了。

过去完成时:
He had slacked off for weeks before the deadline. = 截止日期前几周他就开始懈怠了。
They had slacked off so much that the project was behind schedule. = 他们懈怠得厉害，以至于项目进度落后了。

将来时:
He will slack off if he gets bored. = 如果他感到无聊，他会懈怠的。
They will slack off unless the manager steps in. = 除非经理介入，否则他们将会懈怠。

虚拟语气:
If he hadn't slacked off, he would have passed the exam. = 如果他不懈怠，他就能通过考试了。
I wish I hadn't slacked off on my fitness routine. = 我真希望我没有在健身计划上懈怠。

被动语态:
Work shouldn't be slacked off on. = 工作不应该懈怠。
The project was slacked off on because of the lack of funding. = 由于缺乏资金，项目进展缓慢。

疑问句:
Are you slacking off? = 你在偷懒吗？
Why are they slacking off on their duties? = 他们为什么不认真履行职责？

否定句:
He doesn't slack off at work. = 他在工作中不偷懒。
She didn't slack off even though she was tired. = 即使她很累，她也并没有懈怠。


PHRASE->
slacker attitude = 懒散的态度
professional slacker =  职业懒人(指表面上工作，实际偷懒)
a total slacker = 一个十足的懒鬼
slacker mentality = 懒惰的心态
become a slacker = 变成一个懒鬼
