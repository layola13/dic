sensing:/ˈsɛnsɪŋ/| v.|感觉，感知，觉察|The act of perceiving something through the senses;  detecting or becoming aware of something.|例句：The dog was sensing danger.  狗狗感觉到了危险。|例句：The machine is sensing changes in temperature. 机器正在感知温度变化。|近义词：perceiving, detecting, feeling, noticing|反义词：ignoring, overlooking


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I am sensing a change in the atmosphere. = 我感觉气氛变了。
He senses danger. = 他感觉到了危险。

一般过去时:
I sensed a shift in his mood. = 我觉察到他情绪的变化。
She sensed that something was wrong. = 她感觉有什么不对劲。

现在完成时:
We have sensed a growing unease among the population. = 我们已经觉察到民众日益增长的不安。
They have sensed the opportunity. = 他们已经觉察到了机会。

过去完成时:
I had sensed his disapproval before he spoke. = 他说话之前我就感觉到了他的反对。
She had sensed the danger lurking nearby. = 她之前就感觉到附近潜伏着危险。

将来时:
I will sense the difference immediately. = 我会立即感觉到区别。
They will sense your deception. = 他们会察觉到你的欺骗。

虚拟语气:
If I sensed danger, I would leave immediately. = 如果我感觉到了危险，我会立即离开。
I wish I had sensed the problem sooner. = 我真希望我早点察觉到这个问题。

被动语态:
Danger was sensed by the animals. = 动物们感觉到了危险。
The change in temperature was sensed by the instrument. = 仪器感知到了温度变化。

疑问句:
Do you sense anything unusual? = 你感觉有什么异常吗？
Does he sense my frustration? = 他察觉到我的沮丧吗？

否定句:
I didn't sense any hostility from him. = 我没有感觉到他有任何敌意。
They didn't sense the urgency of the situation. = 他们没有感觉到情况的紧急性。
