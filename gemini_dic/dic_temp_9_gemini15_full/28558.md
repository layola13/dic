carditis:/ˌkɑːrdiˈtaɪtɪs/|/ˈkɑːrdaɪtɪs/| n. |心肌炎|Inflammation of the heart muscle.  Carditis can be caused by various factors, including infections (viral, bacterial, or fungal), autoimmune diseases, and certain medications. Symptoms can range from mild to severe, and diagnosis often involves physical examination, electrocardiogram (ECG), and blood tests.  Treatment depends on the underlying cause and may involve medications, rest, and supportive care.|The doctor suspected carditis after reviewing the patient's ECG and blood work.|myocarditis, endocarditis|healthy heart muscle|
