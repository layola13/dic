hornblende:/ˈhɔːrnbˌlɛnd/| n. |角闪石|A group of inosilicate minerals, forming a solid solution series.  Hornblendes are common rock-forming minerals found in many igneous and metamorphic rocks. They are typically dark-colored and have a prismatic crystal habit. They are characterized by their complex chemical composition, which includes various cations like calcium, magnesium, iron, and aluminum. |The dark rock contained hornblende crystals. 这块深色岩石含有角闪石晶体。The metamorphic rock was rich in hornblende. 这块变质岩富含角闪石。|amphibole, pyroxene|feldspar|
