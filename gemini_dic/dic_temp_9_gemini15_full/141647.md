optimistic: /ˌɒptɪˈmɪstɪk/ /ˌɑːptɪˈmɪstɪk/ | adj. | 乐观的；乐观主义的 |

英文解析：Having or showing the expectation that good things will happen in the future.  Characterized by or showing optimism.


例句：

She's optimistic about her chances of winning the race. = 她对赢得比赛很有信心。

He remained optimistic despite the setbacks. = 尽管遭遇挫折，他仍然保持乐观。


近义词：hopeful, positive, buoyant, cheery, sanguine


反义词：pessimistic, negative, gloomy, despondent


词源：

The word "optimistic" comes from the late 16th century.  It is derived from the French word "optimiste," which in turn originates from the Latin word "optimus" meaning "best."  The suffix "-istic" is added to create the adjectival form.  The word reflects the philosophical idea of optimism, which emphasizes the goodness of the world and the belief that things will ultimately turn out well.
