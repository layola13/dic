in-a-hole:/ɪn ə ˈhoʊl/| adj. |  处于困境中；陷入困境 |  Describes someone or something that is in a difficult or disadvantageous situation, often implying being trapped or behind. | 例句：The company is in a hole after losing its biggest client.  公司在失去最大的客户后陷入了困境。

例句：He's really in a hole with his gambling debts. 他因为赌债而深陷困境。


近义词：in trouble, in a predicament, at a disadvantage,  stuck


反义词：in the clear,  advantageous,  successful


词性：形容词
