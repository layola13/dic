commensalism: /ˈkɒmənˌsælɪzəm/| /ˈkɑːmənˌsælɪzəm/ | n. |共栖；偏利共生 |A type of symbiotic relationship between two organisms of different species in which one organism benefits and the other is neither harmed nor benefited.  |Example:  Barnacles attaching to whales are an example of commensalism; the barnacles get a place to live and feed, while the whale is unaffected.  鲸鱼身上的藤壶是共栖的例子；藤壶获得栖息和觅食的地方，而鲸鱼不受影响。|近义词：symbiosis (but with a more general meaning)|反义词：parasitism, predation
