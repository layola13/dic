reclassifies:/ˌriːˈklæsɪfaɪz/| vt.|重新分类；重新归类|To classify something again, often using a different system or criteria.|The company reclassifies its employees annually based on performance.|近义词：re-categorize, recategorise, reassign|反义词：classify (initially)


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government reclassifies endangered species every five years. = 政府每五年重新分类一次濒危物种。
She reclassifies her books according to genre. = 她根据类型重新分类她的书籍。


一般过去时:
The librarian reclassified the books after the new cataloging system was implemented. = 图书管理员在新编目系统实施后重新分类了书籍。
He reclassified the data to better understand the trends. = 他重新分类数据以便更好地理解趋势。


现在完成时:
The research team has reclassified several previously unknown plant species. = 研究小组已经重新分类了几种以前未知的植物物种。
They have reclassified the budget items to reflect the new priorities. = 他们已经重新分类了预算项目以反映新的优先事项。


过去完成时:
The scientist had reclassified the fossils before publishing his findings. = 科学家在发表他的研究结果之前已经重新分类了化石。
Before the merger, the company had reclassified its assets. = 在合并之前，公司已经重新分类了其资产。


将来时:
The committee will reclassify the documents according to their level of sensitivity. = 委员会将根据文件的敏感程度重新对其进行分类。
The company will reclassify its employees next month. = 公司下个月将重新对员工进行分类。


虚拟语气:
If the data were different, we would reclassify the results. = 如果数据不同，我们将重新分类结果。
I wish they had reclassified the information sooner. = 我希望他们早点重新分类信息。


被动语态:
