discoverable: /dɪˈskʌvərəbl/ | adj. | 可发现的，可探测的 | Able to be discovered or found.  Something that is discoverable can be found or learned about through investigation or exploration. | Example:  The lost city is believed to be discoverable, given the recent archaeological findings.  失落的城市据信是可以被发现的，鉴于最近的考古发现。

The new planet is theoretically discoverable with our advanced telescopes.  理论上来说，用我们先进的望远镜可以发现这颗新行星。


近义词: findable, detectable, traceable, locatable

反义词: undiscoverable, hidden, concealed, invisible


词性: adjective

This information is easily discoverable online.  这些信息很容易在网上找到。

词源:  Derived from the verb "discover" (from Old French *descouvrir, from des- "un-" + couvrir "to cover"), meaning to find something unexpectedly or for the first time.  The suffix "-able" indicates capability or possibility.
