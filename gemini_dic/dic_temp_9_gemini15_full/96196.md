indeterminations:/ˌɪnˌdɪˌtɜːrmɪˈneɪʃənz/| n. |不确定性；未定；未确定的事物 |The indeterminations inherent in quantum mechanics make precise predictions impossible. 量子力学中固有的不确定性使得精确预测成为不可能。|近义词：uncertainties, ambiguities, vaguenesses |反义词：certainties, definitenesses |名词


英文解析：The plural form of "indetermination," referring to the state or quality of being indeterminate;  lacking definite form or character; uncertain; not precisely determined.  It often implies a lack of clarity, precision, or predictability.

例句：

The indeterminations of the future make long-term planning difficult. 未来的不确定性使得长期规划变得困难。

The study highlighted the indeterminations in the data, leading to inconclusive results. 该研究强调了数据中的不确定性，导致结果不确定。


词源:

* **in-:**  prefix meaning "not" or "without"
* **determin-:** from Latin "determinare," meaning "to bound, limit, define"
* **-ation:** suffix forming nouns of action or process


近义词解释:

* **uncertainties:**  a lack of sureness or confidence; doubt.
* **ambiguities:**  uncertainty or inexactness of meaning in language.
* **vaguenesses:**  lack of clarity or precision.


反义词解释:

* **certainties:**  something that is known to be true or that is certain to happen.
* **definitenesses:** the quality or state of being definite; clearly defined or expressed.
