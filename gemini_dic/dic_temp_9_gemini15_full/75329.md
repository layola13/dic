frostbites:/ˈfrɔːstbaɪts/| n.|冻伤|Frostbite is injury to body tissue caused by exposure to extreme cold.  It typically affects the extremities, such as fingers, toes, ears, and nose.|例句：The climber suffered from severe frostbite on his toes after being stranded in the blizzard. 登山者在暴风雪中受困后，脚趾严重冻伤。|例句：Prolonged exposure to freezing temperatures can lead to frostbite and hypothermia. 长时间暴露在冰点以下的温度下会导致冻伤和体温过低。|近义词：freezing, chilling, congelation|反义词：healing, recovery|名词

词源:
The word "frostbite" is a compound word combining "frost" (referring to ice crystals formed from frozen water vapor) and "bite" (implying a damaging effect).  It reflects the damaging "bite" of the cold frost on the flesh.
