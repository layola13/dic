repugner:/rɪˈpʌɡnər/| v.|令人厌恶，使反感|To cause a feeling of intense distaste or disgust.|The smell of rotting meat repugned him. 腐烂肉的气味使他作呕。|近义词：disgust, repel, offend|反义词：delight, please


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The smell repugns me. = 这气味让我反胃。
This idea repugns many people. = 这个主意让很多人反感。


一般过去时:
The sight of blood repugned her. = 看到血让她作呕。
His behavior repugned his parents. = 他的行为让他父母反感。


现在完成时:
The cruelty of the war has repugned many people. = 战争的残酷已经让很多人反感。
That act has repugned me for years. = 那件事多年来一直让我反感。


过去完成时:
The evidence had repugned the jury. = 证据让陪审团感到厌恶。
I had repugned the idea from the beginning. = 我从一开始就讨厌那个主意。


将来时:
The proposed changes will repugn many voters. = 拟议中的变更将会让许多选民反感。
I think this news will repugn the public. = 我认为这个消息会让公众反感。


虚拟语气:
If the evidence were presented, it would repugn the judge. = 如果证据被呈上，它会让法官反感。
I wish that such actions did not repugn me so much. = 我希望这样的行为不会让我如此反感。


被动语态:
The proposal was repugned by the committee. = 这个提案遭到了委员会的反感。
His actions were repugned by everyone present. = 他的行为遭到在场所有人的反感。


疑问句:
Does the idea of eating insects repugn you? = 吃昆虫的想法让你反感吗？
Does this policy repugn the majority of people? = 这项政策让大多数人反感吗？


否定句:
The food didn't repugn me, actually, I quite enjoyed it. = 这食物并没有让我反感，事实上，我相当喜欢它。
The film's violence didn't repugn everyone; some found it exciting. = 电影的暴力场面并非让每个人都反感；有些人觉得它很刺激。


PHRASE->
repugn to the senses = 令人作呕
repugn to reason = 违反常理
repugn to one's nature = 违背本性
repugn to the spirit = 违背精神
repugn to morality = 违背道德
