over-polishing: /ˌoʊvərˈpɑːlɪʃɪŋ/ | n. |过度抛光；过度润色|The act of polishing something to an excessive degree, often resulting in a loss of natural character or a less desirable outcome.  It can refer to physical objects or metaphorical concepts like writing or ideas.|例句：The over-polishing of the antique furniture damaged its patina.  过度抛光古董家具损坏了它的包浆。

The over-polishing of his prose made it sound artificial.  他文章的过度润色使其听起来很做作。|近义词：overrefinement, overdoing, excessive refinement|反义词：under-polishing, roughing, naturalness|
