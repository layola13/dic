carpooled:/kɑːrˈpuːld/| v. |拼车；共乘汽车| To travel in a car with others, especially to share the cost of transportation.  |例句：We carpooled to work this morning to save on gas.  今天早上我们拼车去上班以节省汽油。|例句：Several families carpooled to the soccer tournament.好几个家庭拼车去参加足球锦标赛。|近义词：shared a ride, rode together|反义词：drove alone, solo drive|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They carpool to school every day. = 他们每天拼车去上学。
We carpool to work to save money on gas. = 我们拼车上班以节省汽油钱。

一般过去时:
We carpooled to the concert last night. = 昨晚我们拼车去参加音乐会。
They carpooled to the airport to catch their flight. = 他们拼车去机场赶飞机。

现在完成时:
I have carpooled with my neighbors several times this month. = 这个月我已经和邻居们拼车好几次了。
She has carpooled to work consistently for the past year. = 在过去的一年中，她一直坚持拼车上班。

过去完成时:
Before the new highway opened, we had carpooled for years. = 在新高速公路通车之前，我们已经拼车好多年了。
They had carpooled to the meeting because the traffic was terrible. = 他们拼车去参加会议，因为交通状况很糟糕。

将来时:
We will carpool to the beach this weekend. = 我们这个周末将拼车去海滩。
They will carpool to the conference next month. = 他们下个月将拼车去参加会议。

虚拟语气:
If we carpooled, we would save a lot of money on gas. = 如果我们拼车，我们会节省很多汽油钱。
I wish I had carpooled instead of driving alone. = 我真希望我当时拼车而不是独自开车。

被动语态:
The children were carpooled to school by their parents. = 孩子们由父母拼车送去学校。
The passengers were carpooled to the hotel after arriving at the airport. = 乘客们到达机场后被拼车送到酒店。

疑问句:
Do you carpool to work? = 你拼车上班吗？
Will they carpool to the party? = 他们会拼车去参加聚会吗？

否定句:
I don't carpool to work; I prefer to drive myself. = 我不拼车上班；我更喜欢自己开车。
They didn't carpool to the game because they were running late. = 他们没有拼车去比赛，因为他们迟到了。

词源:
The word "carpooled" is a back-formation from the noun "carpool," which itself combines "car" and "pool" (meaning a shared resource).  The "-ed" suffix indicates the past tense or past participle form of the verb.
