jacobean:/dʒəˈkoʊbiən/| adj. |雅各布一世时期的；詹姆士一世时期的|Of or relating to the reign of King James I of England (1603-1625), characterized by a style of art, architecture, and literature that blended elements of Elizabethan and classical traditions.  The Jacobean style is known for its richness, opulence, and often heavy ornamentation.|The Jacobean era saw significant advancements in literature, including the works of Shakespeare and Ben Jonson.  The Jacobean style of architecture is characterized by its elaborate carvings and use of dark wood.|近义词：Stuart, early 17th-century|反义词：N/A|
