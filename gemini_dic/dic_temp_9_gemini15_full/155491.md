pheromone:/ˈfɛrəmoʊn/ | n. | 信息素 | A chemical substance produced and released into the environment by an animal, especially an insect, affecting the behavior or physiology of others of the same species.  | Example:  Ants use pheromones to mark trails to food sources.  蚂蚁用信息素标记食物来源的路线。

The release of pheromones by female moths attracts males.  雌蛾释放的信息素吸引雄蛾。|  近义词：ectohormone, semiochemical | 反义词：None specifically, but antonyms might relate to the opposite effect (e.g., a repellent).
