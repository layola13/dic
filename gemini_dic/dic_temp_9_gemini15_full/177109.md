reapportionment:/ˌriːəˈpɔːrʃənment/| n. |重新分配；重新划分选区|The act of dividing or distributing something again, especially seats in a legislative body based on new population figures.  It often involves adjusting the boundaries of electoral districts to ensure equal representation. |例句：The state legislature is undergoing reapportionment to reflect population shifts.  州议会正在进行重新分配以反映人口变化。|近义词：redistribution, reallocation|反义词：concentration, consolidation
