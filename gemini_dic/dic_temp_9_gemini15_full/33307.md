chuckle:/ˈtʃʌkəl/ | v. | 轻笑，窃笑 | A chuckle is a quiet, soft laugh, often expressing amusement or satisfaction.  It suggests a less boisterous and more restrained reaction than a full-blown laugh. |  She chuckled at the joke. 她听到笑话轻笑了一下。 He chuckled to himself as he read the letter. 他读信时自己轻笑起来。 | giggle, snicker, titter | roar, wail


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He chuckles at his own jokes. = 他对自己讲的笑话窃笑。
They chuckle softly during the movie. = 他们在电影放映期间轻声轻笑。


一般过去时:
She chuckled at the funny story. = 她听到那个有趣的故事轻笑起来。
We chuckled when we saw the silly dog. = 我们看到那只傻狗的时候笑了。


现在完成时:
He has chuckled at that joke many times. = 他已经多次对那个笑话轻笑过。
She has chuckled throughout the entire presentation. = 她在整个演示过程中一直轻笑。


过去完成时:
They had chuckled at the prank before the victim noticed. = 在受害者注意到恶作剧之前，他们就已经偷笑了。
I had chuckled quietly before realizing it was inappropriate. = 我在意识到这不太合适之前，已经轻声轻笑了。


将来时:
