intrenchment: /ɪnˈtrentʃmənt/ | n. | 巩固；防御工事；确立的地位 | The strengthening of a position, especially in preparation for an attack or in order to resist attack;  the act of entrenching or the state of being entrenched.  It can also refer to a firmly established position or attitude that is difficult to change. | 例句：The government's intrenchment of its policies has led to widespread protests.  政府对其政策的巩固导致了广泛的抗议。


The company's intrenchment in the market made it difficult for competitors to gain a foothold. 公司在市场上的确立地位使得竞争对手难以立足。


近义词：fortification, entrenchment, consolidation, establishment | 反义词：vulnerability, weakness, exposure
