crossbone:/ˈkrɒsbəʊn/ | n. |交叉的骨头；骷髅头标志 |The symbol of two crossed bones, often associated with danger, poison, or death.  It is frequently used in pirate imagery and warning labels.| 例句：The pirate flag displayed a skull and crossbones. 海盗旗上画着一副骷髅和交叉的骨头。


The warning sign featured a skull and crossbones, indicating a highly toxic substance. 警告标志上画着骷髅和交叉的骨头，表示剧毒物质。|近义词：skull, skeleton |反义词：life, safety

词源:
The term "crossbones" is a straightforward compound of "cross" and "bones," referring to two bones positioned in a crossed or intersecting manner. The image has a long history, dating back to antiquity, with its modern association with pirates and danger solidifying primarily from the 17th and 18th centuries.
