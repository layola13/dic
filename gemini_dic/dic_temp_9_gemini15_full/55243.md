dissimilation:/dɪˌsɪmɪˈleɪʃən/| n.|异化；不同化；脱同化|The process by which sounds become less alike, typically through the influence of neighboring sounds.  This often involves a sound changing to become less similar to an adjacent sound. For example, the pronunciation of "comfortable" often involves dissimilation, where the two /m/ sounds become /m/ and /b/ or /p/.|The pronunciation of “comfortable” often shows dissimilation, with the two /m/ sounds sometimes becoming /m/ and /b/.  The word "fifth" is another example; the /f/ sound in "five" is often dissimilated to /f/ and /t/ in "fifth".|近义词：differentiation, divergence|反义词：assimilation
