cost-accountings: /ˈkɒst əˈkaʊntɪŋz/ | n. |成本会计；成本核算 | The systematic recording and analysis of business costs to determine profitability and efficiency.  It involves tracking, allocating, and controlling costs associated with production, operations, and other business activities. |例句：Accurate cost-accountings are crucial for informed decision-making in a business.  精确的成本会计对于企业的知情决策至关重要。

The company implemented a new cost-accounting system to improve its profitability. 这家公司实施了一个新的成本会计系统以提高盈利能力。


近义词：cost analysis, cost control, cost management


反义词：N/A (No direct antonym, but the opposite concept would be neglecting cost analysis)


词性:名词


词源:

* **cost:** From Old French *cost*, from Latin *costare* "to stand, to be worth".  Meaning "price" developed in Middle English.
* **accounting:** From Old French *compter* "to count," from Latin *computare* "to compute," from *com-*, intensive prefix + *putare* "to reckon, think."
