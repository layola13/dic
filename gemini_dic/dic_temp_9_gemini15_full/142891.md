out-of-the-woods:/ˌaʊt əv ðə wʊdz/ | adj. |脱离险境；摆脱困境 |  Out of the woods means to be free from danger or difficulty.  It implies a previous state of peril or trouble from which one has successfully emerged.  The phrase evokes the imagery of escaping a dangerous forest. |例句：After weeks of uncertainty, the company is finally out of the woods.  几周的不确定性之后，公司终于摆脱了困境。


例句：We're not quite out of the woods yet, but things are looking much better. 我们还没完全脱离险境，但情况看起来好多了。


近义词：safe, secure, free from danger, in the clear |反义词：in trouble, in danger, at risk
