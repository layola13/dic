hydrozoon:/ˈhaɪdroʊzuːn/| n. |水螅动物|A hydrozoon is a member of the class Hydrozoa, a large class of mostly marine invertebrates belonging to the phylum Cnidaria.  They are characterized by a life cycle that often includes both polyp and medusa stages, and they possess stinging cells called nematocysts. Many hydrozoans are colonial, meaning they live in groups of interconnected individuals. |Example:  Many hydroids are colonial hydrozoans, forming intricate branching colonies. 许多水螅虫是群体水螅动物，形成复杂的树枝状群体。|近义词：cnidarian (更广泛的分类)|反义词：无
