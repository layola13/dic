insinuation: /ˌɪnsɪnjuˈeɪʃən/ | n. | 暗示；影射；含沙射影 |

英文解析:An insinuation is a suggestion or hint that something unpleasant is true, often without directly stating it.  It implies something negative or harmful, usually in a subtle or indirect way.

例句:

His comments contained a subtle insinuation about her honesty.  他的话里暗含着她不诚实的暗示。

The newspaper article was full of insinuations about the politician's past. 这篇报纸文章充满了关于这位政治家过去的影射。


近义词: implication, suggestion, hint, innuendo, imputation

反义词: affirmation, declaration, statement (direct statement)

词源:

The word "insinuation" comes from the Latin word "insinuare," meaning "to wind in," "to insinuate," or "to suggest subtly."  It reflects the indirect and often sneaky nature of the action.
