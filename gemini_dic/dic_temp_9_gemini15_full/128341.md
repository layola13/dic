neutralises: /ˈnjuːtrəlaɪz/ | vt. | 中和；抵消；使无效 | To counteract or nullify the effects of something.  | The antidote neutralises the poison. 解药能中和毒药。 | counteract, offset, negate | exacerbate, intensify | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The acid neutralises the base. = 酸中和碱。
The medication neutralises the effects of the allergy. = 药物中和过敏反应的影响。

一般过去时:
The army neutralised the threat. = 军队消除了威胁。
The firefighters neutralised the bomb. = 消防员使炸弹失效。

现在完成时:
The government has neutralised the opposition. = 政府已经压制了反对派。
She has neutralised the negative comments with a positive response. = 她以积极的回应抵消了负面评论。

过去完成时:
They had neutralised the enemy before reinforcements arrived. = 增援到达之前，他们已经消灭了敌人。
The scientist had neutralised the virus before it could spread. = 科学家在病毒传播之前已经将其中和了。

将来时:
The new policy will neutralise the negative impacts of the previous one. = 新政策将抵消先前政策的负面影响。
We will neutralise the threat before it escalates. = 我们会在威胁升级之前将其消除。

虚拟语气:
If we had neutralised the threat earlier, the situation would be better. = 如果我们早点消除威胁，情况就会更好。
I wish we could neutralise the effects of climate change. = 我希望我们能够抵消气候变化的影响。

被动语态:
The threat was neutralised by the police. = 威胁被警方消除了。
The harmful bacteria were neutralised by the antibiotic. = 有害细菌被抗生素杀死了。

疑问句:

Can this chemical neutralise the toxins? = 这种化学物质能中和毒素吗？

否定句:
The medicine did not neutralise the pain. = 药没有止痛。
The government failed to neutralise the opposition's influence. = 政府未能消除反对派的影响。
