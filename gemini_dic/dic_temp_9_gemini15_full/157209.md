pink-slips:/pɪŋk slɪps/| n.|解雇通知，裁员通知|A pink slip is an informal term for a notice of termination of employment.  It refers to the common practice of using pink paper for such notices.  The term is mostly used in American English.|例句：He received a pink slip last Friday. = 他上周五收到了解雇通知。

He was handed a pink slip after years of dedicated service. =  在多年的忠诚服务后，他被解雇了。|近义词：termination notice, dismissal notice, layoff notice|反义词：job offer, promotion


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company issues pink slips to employees. = 公司向员工发出解雇通知。
She gets a pink slip every year. = 她每年都收到解雇通知。(This example is less common and might indicate a pattern of short-term employment)

一般过去时:
They received pink slips during the recession. = 他们在经济衰退期间收到了解雇通知。
He got a pink slip last month. = 他上个月收到了解雇通知。

现在完成时:
Many workers have received pink slips this year. = 今年许多工人收到了解雇通知。
The company has issued several pink slips recently. = 公司最近发出了几份解雇通知。

过去完成时:
Before the merger, they had already received pink slips. = 在合并之前，他们已经收到了解雇通知。
The employees had received pink slips before the announcement. = 在宣布之前，员工们已经收到了解雇通知。

将来时:
More employees will receive pink slips next quarter. = 下个季度将会有更多员工收到解雇通知。
The company will likely issue pink slips if sales don't improve. = 如果销售额没有改善，公司可能会发出解雇通知。

虚拟语气:
If the company hadn't lost money, they wouldn't have given out pink slips. = 如果公司没有亏损，他们就不会发出解雇通知。
I wish I hadn't received that pink slip. = 我真希望我没有收到那张解雇通知。

被动语态:
Pink slips were handed out unexpectedly. = 解雇通知出乎意料地被发放了。
Several employees were given pink slips last week. = 上周有几名员工收到了解雇通知。

疑问句:

否定句:
