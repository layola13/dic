capitalisable: /ˌkæpɪtəlaɪzəbl/ | adj. | 可大写的，可资本化的 | Able to be capitalized; capable of being written with an initial capital letter.  This typically refers to words or letters that can be converted to uppercase. |


例句：

The word "programming" is capitalisable at the beginning of a sentence. = 单词“编程”在句首可以大写。

Many programming languages require that variable names be capitalisable according to specific rules. = 许多编程语言要求变量名根据特定规则进行大小写转换。


近义词：capitalizable (American spelling)

反义词：uncapitalisable (opposite, though rarely used)


词源:

The word is derived from "capitalize," which itself comes from "capital," meaning a large or uppercase letter. The suffix "-able" indicates capability or possibility.  The ultimate origin traces back to the Latin word "capitalis," meaning "of the head," referring to the prominent position of a capital letter.
