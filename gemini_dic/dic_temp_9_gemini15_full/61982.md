enacted:/ɪˈnæktɪd/| v.|制定（法律），颁布，上演|To enact something, especially a law, means to make it officially into law.  It can also mean to perform a play or other dramatic work.|The government enacted new laws to combat pollution. 政府颁布了新的法律来对抗污染。|The students enacted a scene from Shakespeare. 学生们上演了莎士比亚作品中的一个场景。|established, implemented, performed|repealed, abolished


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The government enacts new laws every year. = 政府每年都会制定新的法律。
They enact a play every Friday. = 他们每个星期五都会上演一出戏。

一般过去时:
Congress enacted the Civil Rights Act of 1964. = 国会于1964年颁布了民权法案。
The theater group enacted a powerful drama. = 剧团上演了一部感人的戏剧。

现在完成时:
They have enacted several important reforms. = 他们已经实施了几项重要的改革。
The company has enacted a new safety policy. = 公司已经实施了一项新的安全政策。

过去完成时:
They had enacted the law before the incident occurred. = 事件发生前他们已经颁布了该法。
The director had enacted the play many times before. = 导演之前已经排演过这部戏很多次了。

将来时:
The parliament will enact new environmental protection laws next year. = 议会明年将颁布新的环境保护法。
They will enact a new musical next season. = 下个季度他们将上演一部新的音乐剧。

虚拟语气:
If they enacted stricter regulations, pollution would decrease. = 如果他们制定更严格的法规，污染就会减少。
If the actors had enacted their roles better, the play would have been more successful. = 如果演员们演得更好，这部戏就会更成功。

被动语态:
The law was enacted last month. = 该法律是上个月颁布的。
The play is being enacted on stage now. = 这出戏正在舞台上演出。

疑问句:
When was that law enacted? = 那部法律是什么时候颁布的？
Will the new regulations be enacted soon? = 新的法规很快就会颁布吗？

否定句:
The bill was not enacted due to opposition. = 由于反对，该法案未获通过。
They did not enact the play as planned. = 他们没有按照计划上演那出戏。
