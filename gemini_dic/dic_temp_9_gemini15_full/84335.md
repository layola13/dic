halfhazard:/ˈhæfˈhæzərd/ | adj. | 随便的；马虎的；偶然的 | Characterized by lack of care, planning, or order; haphazard.  It implies a lack of system or organization, often resulting in inefficiency or sloppiness. |例句：The renovations were done in a halfhazard manner, resulting in uneven paintwork and mismatched tiles.  翻新工程做得马马虎虎，导致油漆不均匀，瓷砖也不配套。

例句：He approached the problem in a halfhazard way, leading to several avoidable mistakes. 他以一种马虎的方式处理这个问题，导致一些可以避免的错误。


近义词：careless, slapdash, slipshod, random, haphazard | 反义词：organized, methodical, systematic, careful, deliberate


词源:  The word is a combination of "half" and "hazard,"  "hazard" originally meant "chance" or "risk." The implication is that something done in a halfhazard manner is done with only half the care or attention it requires, leaving it to chance whether it will succeed or not.
