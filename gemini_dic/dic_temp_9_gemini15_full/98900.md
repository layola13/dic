intensifier:/ɪnˈtɛnsɪfaɪər/ | n. | 强化剂；增强器；强调词 | A word or phrase used to add emphasis or strength to another word or phrase.  It makes the meaning stronger or more forceful. | Example:  "The movie was *absolutely* terrifying."  (Absolutely intensifies "terrifying") | 近义词：amplifier, booster, emphasizer | 反义词：diminisher, weakener


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is *very* happy. = 他非常开心。
She is *extremely* tired. = 她极其疲惫。


一般过去时:
The storm was *incredibly* strong. = 暴风雨极其猛烈。
He felt *really* bad about it. = 他对此感到非常糟糕。


现在完成时:
I have *completely* finished the project. = 我已经完全完成了这个项目。
She has *totally* changed her mind. = 她完全改变了主意。


过去完成时:
They had *already* left when I arrived. = 我到达时他们已经离开了。
I had *almost* forgotten about the meeting. = 我几乎忘记了会议。


将来时:
We will *definitely* go to the party. = 我们一定会去参加派对。
It will be *utterly* amazing. = 它将会非常棒。


虚拟语气:
I would be *so* happy if you came. = 如果你来，我会非常高兴。
It would be *much* better if you helped. = 如果你帮忙会好得多。


被动语态:
The damage was *severely* underestimated. = 损失被严重低估了。
The problem is *badly* handled. = 这个问题处理得很糟糕。


疑问句:
Are you *really* sure about this? = 你真的确定吗？
Is it *actually* that bad? = 情况真的那么糟吗？


否定句:
I am *not* at all happy. = 我一点也不开心。
It was *by no means* easy. = 这绝非易事。


PHRASE->
*So* much better = 好多了
*Too* bad = 太糟糕了
*Very* well = 很好
*Rather* quickly = 相当快
*Extremely* important = 极其重要
