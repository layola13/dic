link:/lɪŋk/ | n. | 链接，联系，环节 | A link between two things is a connection or relationship between them.  | Example: There is a strong link between smoking and lung cancer.  吸烟和肺癌之间有很强的联系。 |近义词: connection, bond, tie |反义词: separation, disconnection


USAGE->
一般现在时:
This link connects the two pages. = 这个链接连接了这两个页面。
The chain has several weak links. = 这条链条有好几个薄弱环节。


一般过去时:
The link broke during the storm. = 暴风雨期间链条断了。
She found a link between the two crimes. = 她找到了这两起罪案之间的联系。


现在完成时:
They have established a strong link with the local community. = 他们已经与当地社区建立了牢固的联系。
Scientists have discovered a new link between diet and health. = 科学家们发现了一种新的饮食与健康之间的联系。


过去完成时:
The engineers had already identified the weak link in the system. = 工程师们已经确定了系统中的薄弱环节。
Before the investigation, they had not found a link between the suspects. = 在调查之前，他们还没有发现嫌疑人之间的联系。


将来时:
This software will link your computer to the internet. = 这个软件将把你的电脑连接到互联网。
They will establish a new link between the two cities. = 他们将在两座城市之间建立新的联系。


虚拟语气:
If there were a link between the two events, it would be significant. = 如果这两个事件之间存在联系，那将非常重要。
I wish there was a clearer link between cause and effect. = 我希望因果关系能更清晰一些。


被动语态:
The two websites are linked together. = 这两个网站彼此链接。
The problem was linked to a software glitch. = 这个问题与软件故障有关。


疑问句:
Is there a link between stress and illness? = 压力和疾病之间有联系吗？
What is the link between these two ideas? = 这两个想法之间有什么联系？


否定句:
There is no link between the two events. = 这两个事件之间没有联系。
She denied any link to the organization. = 她否认与该组织有任何联系。
