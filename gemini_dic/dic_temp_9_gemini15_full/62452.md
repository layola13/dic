endorsement:/ɪnˈdɔːrsmənt/| n. |认可；背书；支持；赞同|The act of giving one's public approval or support to someone or something.|The athlete received numerous endorsements from major brands.这位运动员获得了许多大品牌的代言。|support, approval, sanction | disapproval, rejection | 名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He gives his endorsement to the new policy. 他认可这项新政策。
The company seeks endorsements from celebrities.  这家公司寻求名人的代言。


一般过去时:
She gave her endorsement to the candidate. 她支持这位候选人。
The magazine gave a glowing endorsement to the new book. 这本杂志对新书给予了高度评价。


现在完成时:
The product has received many endorsements. 该产品已获得许多认可。
They have given their endorsement to the project. 他们已经认可了这个项目。


过去完成时:
The company had already received several endorsements before the launch.  产品发布前，公司已获得数个代言。
He had given his endorsement to the cause long before the campaign began. 他早在竞选开始之前就已支持这项事业。


将来时:
She will give her endorsement to the charity next week. 她下周将为慈善机构背书。
The company anticipates it will receive many endorsements for its new product. 这家公司预计其新产品将获得许多认可。


虚拟语气:
If only he would give his endorsement to the plan.  如果他肯支持这项计划就好了。
I wish she had given her endorsement sooner. 我真希望她早点表示支持。


被动语态:
The candidate's policy was given a strong endorsement by the newspaper.  候选人的政策得到了报纸的大力支持。
His work has been endorsed by many experts. 他的作品得到了许多专家的认可。


疑问句:
Does the product have any celebrity endorsements?  该产品有明星代言吗？
Has the plan received sufficient endorsements? 这项计划得到了足够的认可吗？


否定句:
He refused to give his endorsement to the proposal. 他拒绝支持这项提案。
The company did not receive the endorsements they had hoped for.  该公司没有获得他们希望得到的代言。
