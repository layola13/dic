curber:/ˈkɜːrbər/| n. |抑制者；限制者；路边石安装工|A curber is someone or something that curbs or restrains something else.  It can also refer to a worker who installs or repairs curbs. |The new regulations act as a curber on excessive spending.  新的规章制度可以抑制过度支出。|近义词：restrainer, limiter, controller|反义词：encourager, promoter|名词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The government curbs inflation. = 政府抑制通货膨胀。
He curbs his spending habits. = 他抑制了他的消费习惯。


一般过去时:
They curbed the spread of the disease. = 他们控制了疾病的蔓延。
The police curbed the protests. = 警察制止了抗议活动。


现在完成时:
We have curbed our appetite for fast food. = 我们已经抑制了对快餐的食欲。
The new law has curbed illegal activities. = 新法律已经遏制了非法活动。


过去完成时:
They had curbed their enthusiasm before the announcement. = 他们在宣布之前已经抑制了他们的热情。
The company had curbed its expenses before the financial crisis. = 在金融危机之前，公司已经削减了开支。


将来时:
We will curb pollution in the future. = 我们将来会控制污染。
The government will curb the power of monopolies. = 政府将遏制垄断企业的权力。


虚拟语气:
If we curbed our spending, we would save more money. = 如果我们控制开支，我们会存更多钱。
I wish I had curbed my temper yesterday. = 我真希望昨天我能控制住我的脾气。


被动语态:
Inflation was curbed by the government's policies. = 通货膨胀被政府的政策所抑制。
The spread of the virus was curbed by strict quarantine measures. = 病毒的传播被严格的隔离措施所控制。


疑问句:
Can we curb this negative trend? = 我们能控制住这种负面趋势吗？
How can we curb our addiction to social media? = 我们如何控制对社交媒体的成瘾？


否定句:
The government failed to curb inflation. = 政府未能控制通货膨胀。
He couldn't curb his anger. = 他无法控制自己的愤怒。
