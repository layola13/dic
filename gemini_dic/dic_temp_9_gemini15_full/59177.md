dysgenic:/dɪsˈdʒɛnɪk/| adj.| 退化的；遗传败坏的；导致遗传素质下降的 | Relating to or characterized by dysgenesis; tending to cause deterioration of genetic stock.  It describes a process or condition that leads to a decline in the quality of the gene pool within a population. This decline may manifest in various ways, such as reduced intelligence, increased susceptibility to disease, or diminished physical capabilities.  | Example:  The unchecked spread of certain genetic disorders is a dysgenic trend.  不受控制的某些遗传疾病的传播是一种遗传退化趋势。|近义词：degenerative, deteriorating|反义词：eugenic
