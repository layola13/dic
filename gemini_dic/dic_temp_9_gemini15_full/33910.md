词源:

The word "circumventors" is derived from the verb "circumvent," which comes from the Latin prefix "circum-" (around) and the verb "venire" (to come).  Therefore, the original meaning was "to come around,"  later evolving to mean "to go around" or "to bypass".  The "-ors" suffix indicates the agent or doer of the action.
