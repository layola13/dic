carolingian:/ˌkærəlɪnˈdʒiən/| adj.|卡洛林王朝的；卡洛林的 | Relating to the Carolingian dynasty (751-987 AD) of Frankish kings, particularly Charlemagne and his successors, who ruled over much of Western Europe.  The term often refers to the cultural and intellectual achievements of this period, including the Carolingian Renaissance. | Example: The Carolingian dynasty significantly influenced the development of European culture. 卡洛林王朝极大地影响了欧洲文化的发展。|近义词：Franco-Saxon(在特定语境下)|反义词：None|词性：形容词
