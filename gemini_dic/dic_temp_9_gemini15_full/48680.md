deferrer:/dɪˈfɜːrər/| n. |延期者；推迟者；缓付者| A person or thing that defers something, especially payment.|The deferrer of the loan payment was fined for late payment.  延期还款的人因逾期付款而被罚款。|近义词：postponer, procrastinator |反义词：payer, prompter|名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He defers his payments regularly. = 他定期推迟付款。
She defers important decisions. = 她推迟重要的决定。


一般过去时:
They deferred the meeting until next week. = 他们把会议推迟到下周。
He deferred to his father's wishes. = 他顺从了他父亲的意愿。


现在完成时:
She has deferred her college application. = 她已经推迟了她的大学申请。
They have deferred payment on several occasions. = 他们已经多次推迟付款。


过去完成时:
He had deferred his decision until he had more information. = 他在获得更多信息之前已经推迟了他的决定。
They had deferred the project due to unforeseen circumstances. = 由于不可预见的情况，他们已经推迟了项目。


将来时:
They will defer the vote until next month. = 他们将把投票推迟到下个月。
We will defer to the expert's opinion. = 我们将尊重专家的意见。


虚拟语气:
If he deferred the payment, he would face penalties. = 如果他推迟付款，他将面临处罚。
I wish I had deferred my trip. = 我真希望我推迟了我的旅行。


被动语态:
The decision was deferred until further notice. = 决定推迟到另行通知。
The payment was deferred for six months. = 付款被推迟了六个月。


疑问句:
Will they defer the launch date? = 他们会推迟发布日期吗？
Did he defer to his boss's authority? = 他顺从了他老板的权威吗？


否定句:
He did not defer to anyone's opinion. = 他不听从任何人的意见。
They will not defer the project any longer. = 他们不会再推迟这个项目了。
