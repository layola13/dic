enmesh:/ɪnˈmɛʃ/ | vt. |使纠缠；使陷入；使卷入 | To involve someone or something in a difficult or complicated situation from which it is hard to escape.  | 例句：He became enmeshed in a web of deceit. 他陷入了一个欺骗的圈套。| 例句：The family was enmeshed in a bitter legal battle. 这个家庭卷入了一场激烈的法律诉讼。| 近义词：entangle, involve, trap | 反义词：disentangle, free, release | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The scandal enmeshes the politician in controversy. = 这起丑闻使这位政客陷入争议之中。
They are enmeshed in a complex web of relationships. = 他们陷入复杂的人际关系网中。

一般过去时:
The climber became enmeshed in the thorny bushes. = 登山者被荆棘缠住了。
The investigation enmeshed several high-profile individuals. = 调查牵涉到几位知名人士。

现在完成时:
The project has enmeshed us in a mountain of paperwork. = 这个项目让我们陷入堆积如山的文书工作中。
She has become enmeshed in a difficult financial situation. = 她陷入困境的财务状况中。

过去完成时:
Before the trial, he had already become enmeshed in the media's scrutiny. = 在审判之前，他已经陷入媒体的审查之中。
They had become enmeshed in a complex conspiracy. = 他们已经卷入了一个复杂的阴谋。

将来时:
The upcoming election will enmesh the country in further political debate. = 即将到来的选举将使国家陷入进一步的政治辩论。
This decision will likely enmesh us in a lengthy legal battle. = 这个决定可能会让我们卷入一场漫长的法律诉讼。

虚拟语气:
If he hadn't made that deal, he wouldn't have become enmeshed in the scandal. = 如果他没有签订那笔交易，他就不会卷入这起丑闻。
I wish I hadn't become enmeshed in this conflict. = 我真希望我没有卷入这场冲突。

被动语态:
The innocent bystander was enmeshed in the argument. = 无辜的旁观者被卷入争论中。
He felt enmeshed by the circumstances. = 他感到被环境所束缚。

疑问句:
Is the company enmeshed in any legal disputes? = 这家公司是否卷入了任何法律纠纷？
Are we enmeshed in a situation we can't escape? = 我们是否陷入无法摆脱的困境？

否定句:
The police were unable to disentangle the truth from the lies that enmeshed the case. = 警方无法从缠绕案件的谎言中理清真相。
He tried to avoid becoming enmeshed in their personal drama. = 他试图避免卷入他们的个人纠纷。
