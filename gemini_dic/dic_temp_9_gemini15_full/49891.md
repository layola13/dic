demurity:/dəˈmjʊərɪti/ | n. |端庄；娴静；庄重 |The quality of being demure; modesty and reserve in behavior, manner, or appearance.  It suggests a quiet dignity and often implies a certain shyness or reticence.  |例句：Her demurity was quite captivating.  她的端庄非常迷人。

例句：The demurity of her demeanor was a refreshing change from the boisterous atmosphere. 她举止的端庄娴静与喧闹的气氛形成了令人耳目一新的对比。


近义词：modesty, reserve, propriety, shyness, coyness


反义词：boldness, immodesty, forwardness, boldness, gaudiness


词源：From Middle French *demuer, from Old French demourer "to remain, stay," from Vulgar Latin *demorare, from Latin demorāri "to delay, linger," from de- "away, off" + morāri "to delay."  The sense evolved from "staying quiet" to "quiet demeanor."
