overspeech:/ˈoʊvərˌspiːtʃ/| n. |过多言语；冗言；赘述|Excessive talkativeness; more speech than is necessary or desirable.  It often involves unnecessary details or repetition.|例句：Her presentation suffered from overspeech, losing the audience's attention.  她的演讲冗言赘述，失去了听众的注意力。

例句：The professor's overspeech made the lecture drag on. 教授的冗长讲话使讲座变得冗长乏味。


近义词：verbosity, loquacity, garrulity, prolixity, wordiness|反义词：brevity, conciseness, succinctness|词性：名词


词源:  The word "overspeech" is a relatively straightforward compound word. It combines the prefix "over-" (meaning "too much" or "excessive") with the noun "speech."  Therefore, its etymology traces directly to the Old English words that formed "over" and "speech."
