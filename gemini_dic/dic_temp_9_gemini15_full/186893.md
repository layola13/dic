riot:/ˈraɪət/| n. v.|暴乱，骚乱；喧闹；兴高采烈|A riot is a violent public disorder involving a large number of people, often characterized by looting, vandalism, and clashes with law enforcement.  It can also refer to a boisterous or noisy gathering or a state of enthusiastic excitement.|例句：The city erupted in riot after the controversial election results were announced.  城市在有争议的选举结果公布后爆发了暴乱。

例句：The children were in a riot of laughter and excitement.孩子们兴奋地又叫又跳。|近义词：rebellion, uprising, tumult, commotion|反义词：peace, order, calm, tranquility|名词，动词
