claudication: /ˌklɔːdɪˈkeɪʃən/ | n. | 跛行；间歇性跛行 | Pain or discomfort in the legs during walking or exercise, often due to reduced blood flow to the legs.  This is a symptom of peripheral artery disease (PAD).  | Example:  He experienced claudication in his left leg after walking for only a short distance. 他左腿在只走了很短一段距离后就感到跛行。


The severe claudication forced him to stop running. 严重的跛行迫使他停止跑步。|近义词：limping, lameness |反义词：None (it describes a symptom) |词性:名词


Etymology:  From Latin *claudicare "to limp," from *claudus "lame."  The suffix -ation indicates a process or condition.
