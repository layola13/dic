resensitizing: /ˌriːˈsɛnsaɪtɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 再致敏；重新敏感化 |  The process of making something sensitive again, often after a period of desensitization.  This term is used in various contexts, including medicine and psychology. For example, in medicine, it might refer to restoring the sensitivity of a nerve after it has been damaged. In psychology, it could refer to renewing a person's emotional response to a stimulus after they've become desensitized. | Example: The therapist worked on resensitizing the patient to the trauma he had experienced.  = 治疗师致力于让病人对其经历的创伤重新产生敏感。


近义词: reawakening, reactivating, restimulating | 反义词: desensitizing
