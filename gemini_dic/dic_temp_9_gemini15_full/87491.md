heterocarpism:/ˌhɛtərəˈkɑːrpɪzəm/| n. |异果性，果实异形|The production of different forms of fruit by a single plant.  This phenomenon is common in certain plant species, where environmental factors or genetic variations can influence the development of fruits with varying shapes, sizes, and structures.| Example: The heterocarpism observed in this species of dandelion is likely due to environmental stress.  这种蒲公英物种中观察到的异果性可能是由于环境压力造成的。|近义词：fruit polymorphism, fruit variation|反义词：homocarpism (producing fruits of uniform shape and size)
