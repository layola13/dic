exag: /ˈɛɡzædʒ/ | v. | 夸张，夸大 | To enlarge or represent something beyond normal bounds so as to create an impression of greater significance, size, or other quality.  | He exaggerated his accomplishments in the interview. 他在面试中夸大了自己的成就。 | inflate, magnify, overstate | downplay, understate, minimize | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He exaggerates his problems. = 他夸大了他的问题。
She exaggerates her role in the project. = 她夸大了她在项目中的作用。

一般过去时:
He exaggerated his injuries to get sympathy. = 他夸大自己的伤势以博取同情。
She exaggerated the size of the fish she caught. = 她夸大了她钓到的鱼的大小。

现在完成时:
He has exaggerated his abilities throughout his career. = 他在他的职业生涯中一直夸大自己的能力。
She has exaggerated the difficulties she faced. = 她夸大了她面临的困难。

过去完成时:
He had exaggerated the risks involved before we started. = 我们开始之前他就夸大了所涉及的风险。
She had exaggerated the amount of money she spent. = 她夸大了她花费的金额。

将来时:
He will exaggerate his influence in the meeting. = 他将在会议上夸大他的影响力。
She will exaggerate the importance of her work. = 她会夸大她工作的重要性。

虚拟语气:
If he exaggerated, he would lose credibility. = 如果他夸大其词，他会失去信誉。
I wish she hadn't exaggerated the situation so much. = 我真希望她没有那么夸张地描述情况。

被动语态:
His achievements were exaggerated by the media. = 他的成就被媒体夸大了。
The danger was exaggerated to scare people. = 危险被夸大以吓唬人们。

疑问句:
Did he exaggerate the facts? = 他是否夸大了事实？
Is she exaggerating the situation? = 她是否在夸大事实？

否定句:
He didn't exaggerate his experience. = 他没有夸大他的经验。
She didn't exaggerate the importance of the matter. = 她没有夸大这件事的重要性。


PHRASE->

slightly exaggerate = 略微夸大
