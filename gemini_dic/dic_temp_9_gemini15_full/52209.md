diastyle:/ˈdaɪæstaɪl/| n.|柱式间距|A diastyle is a type of architectural arrangement where the columns are spaced at a distance of three diameters apart. This spacing is considered a relatively wide spacing compared to other column arrangements. It provides a sense of openness and grandeur.|The Parthenon in Athens is a famous example of a building using diastyle spacing between its columns.|近义词：areostyle (slightly wider spacing), systyle (narrower spacing)|反义词：systyle (narrower spacing),pycnostyle (very narrow spacing)
