lawmakers:/ˈlɔːˌmeɪkərz/ | n. |立法者；议员 |People who make laws, typically members of a legislature.  Lawmakers are responsible for creating, debating, and voting on legislation that governs a country, state, or other jurisdiction. They represent the interests of their constituents and work to shape public policy. |Example: The lawmakers are currently debating a new bill on healthcare reform.  立法者目前正在就医疗改革的新法案进行辩论。

The lawmakers voted to increase the minimum wage.  议员们投票决定提高最低工资。

|近义词：legislators, representatives, congressmen, parliamentarians |反义词：constituents (in the sense of those who are governed) |名词
