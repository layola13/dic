pusillanimity: /ˌpjuːsɪlˈænɪməti/ | n. | 懦弱；胆小；优柔寡断 | Lack of courage and resolution; pusillanimous behavior.  It refers to a lack of courage or resolution, often manifested as timidity or indecisiveness. |  His pusillanimity prevented him from speaking up against the injustice.  他的懦弱使他不敢反对不公正的行为。


近义词: cowardice, timidity, faintheartedness, irresoluteness, indecisiveness

反义词: bravery, courage, boldness, resolution, decisiveness


词源:  From Late Latin *pusillanimitas, from Latin pusillus "very small, tiny" (from pusus, past participle of puere "to be a boy, be young" + -illus diminutive suffix) + animus "mind, spirit, courage".  Thus, literally "small-mindedness" or "small-spiritedness".
