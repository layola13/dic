defamations:/ˌdɛfəˈmeɪʃənz/ | n. |诽谤；中伤；名誉损害 | The act of harming someone's reputation by making false statements about them.  It involves communicating false information that harms a person's reputation.  Defamations can be in written (libel) or spoken (slander) form. | Example:  The newspaper article contained several defamations against the politician. 这篇报纸文章包含了几条针对这位政治家的诽谤言论。


The company's CEO is suing for defamation after a blog post falsely accused him of fraud. 公司首席执行官因一篇博客文章虚假指控他欺诈而提起诽谤诉讼。 |近义词：calumnies, aspersions, vilifications |反义词：praise, commendation, accolades |名词
