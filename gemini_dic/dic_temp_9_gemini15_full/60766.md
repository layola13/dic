electrolyte:/ɪˈlɛktrəlaɪt/| n. |电解质|A substance that conducts electricity by the movement of ions, typically a solution of salts or other electrolytes dissolved in a solvent such as water.|例：Saltwater is a good electrolyte, allowing the flow of electric current.  盐水是良好的电解质，允许电流流动。|近义词：conductor (in this context), ionizer|反义词：insulator

词源:
From electro- +‎ -lyte.  electro- 来自希腊语 elektron “琥珀”（因摩擦起电而得名），-lyte 来自希腊语 lytos “可溶解的”。  字面意思为“可溶解的，并且导电的物质”。
