hypocarp:/ˈhaɪpəˌkɑːrp/ | n. | 花托 | The hypocarp is the part of a flower that is below the receptacle, often fused with the ovary and other floral parts.  It can be fleshy or dry, and contributes to the fruit structure in many plants.  For example, in apples and pears, the fleshy part we eat is largely derived from the hypocarp. |The apple's fleshy part is mostly hypocarp. 苹果多汁的部分大部分是花托。|  synonym:  receptacle (in some contexts), thalamus | antonym:  N/A (no direct antonym) | 名词
