half-proven:/ˌhɑːfˈpruːvən/| adj.| 半证实的，部分证实的 | Partially proven or supported by evidence; not fully established or verified.  The theory is half-proven, requiring further research to confirm its validity.  |近义词：partially proven, partially verified, unsubstantiated, tentative | 反义词：fully proven, verified, confirmed, established


例句：

The scientist's hypothesis is half-proven, needing more data to solidify the findings. 科学家的假设只得到部分证实，需要更多数据来巩固研究结果。

The detective's theory is half-proven; some evidence supports it, but more is needed.  侦探的理论只得到部分证实；一些证据支持它，但还需要更多证据。


词源：

* **half:**  Old English *healf*, from Proto-Germanic *halbaz.  Meaning "one of two equal parts."

* **proven:** Past participle of the verb "prove," from Old French *prover*, from Latin *probare* ("to test, try, prove").


用法说明：

half-proven 主要用作形容词，修饰名词，表示某种说法、理论、假设等只得到部分证实，尚需进一步研究或证据来完全证实。  它强调的是不确定性，暗示结论并非完全可靠。
