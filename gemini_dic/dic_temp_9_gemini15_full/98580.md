institutionalizing: /ˌɪnstɪˈtuːʃənəlaɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 使制度化；使成为制度 | To establish something as a norm or standard practice within an institution or society.  The process involves formalizing rules, procedures, and structures to ensure consistency and longevity. |

例句：

The government is institutionalizing the new environmental protection laws. = 政府正在将新的环境保护法制度化。

They are institutionalizing a new system for managing employee complaints. = 他们正在将一个新的员工投诉管理系统制度化。


近义词：formalizing, systematizing, standardizing, codifying

反义词：deinstitutionalizing, informalizing


词源：

The word "institutionalizing" comes from the noun "institution" and the suffix "-izing," which indicates the process of making something into an institution. "Institution" itself originates from the Latin word "instituere," meaning "to establish," "to set up," or "to found."  The "-ize" suffix is also of Greek origin.
