melodramatic:/ˌmɛləˈdræmætɪk/| adj.| 夸张的；煽情的；戏剧化的 | Exaggeratedly emotional or sentimental; overly dramatic.  The melodramatic scene in the movie was overly theatrical and unconvincing.  | 近义词：sentimental, theatrical, histrionic | 反义词：realistic, understated, subdued


英文解析：
Melodramatic describes something that is excessively emotional, dramatic, or sentimental.  It often implies a lack of subtlety or restraint, suggesting an artificial or exaggerated display of feeling.  The term often carries a negative connotation, implying that the emotion is contrived or insincere.


例句：
Her melodramatic reaction to the minor inconvenience was completely out of proportion.  她对这件小麻烦的夸张反应完全不成比例。

The novel's melodramatic plot twists were predictable and cliché. 这部小说的情节曲折夸张，而且老套。
