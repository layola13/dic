jurisprudences: /ˌdʒʊərɪsˈpruːdənsɪz/ | n. |法学理论；法理学；判例法 | The study and theory of law, including the principles of law, its origin, application, and philosophical basis.  It often involves examining legal systems, interpreting legal rules, and analyzing legal concepts.  Jurisprudence is not simply the application of law, but a critical examination of its nature, purpose, and effect. | Example:  The professor's lectures covered various aspects of jurisprudence, from natural law theory to legal positivism. 教授的讲座涵盖了法理学的各个方面，从自然法理论到法律实证主义。
