eardrum: /ˈɪərdrʌm/ | n. | 鼓膜；耳膜 | The eardrum is a thin membrane that separates the outer ear from the middle ear. It vibrates when sound waves hit it, transmitting the vibrations to the tiny bones in the middle ear, which then send signals to the brain.  | The loud noise burst my eardrum.  巨大的噪音震破了我的耳膜。| tympanic membrane |  (no direct antonym, but could be described as a damaged or perforated eardrum)
