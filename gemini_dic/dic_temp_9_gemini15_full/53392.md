disallegiance:/ˌdɪsəˈliːdʒəns/| n. | 不忠；背叛；效忠的终止 | The act of renouncing one's allegiance; a state of being disloyal.  It implies a breaking of loyalty or a formal rejection of one's obligation to a person, group, or country. | Example:  His disallegiance to the crown led to his imprisonment.  他的背叛王室导致了他被监禁。
The open expression of disallegiance to the government resulted in severe consequences. 公开表达对政府的不忠导致了严重的后果。|近义词：treason, disloyalty, betrayal, defection |反义词：allegiance, loyalty, fidelity |名词
