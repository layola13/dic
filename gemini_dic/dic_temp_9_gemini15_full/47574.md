debarring: /dɪˈbɑːrɪŋ/ | v. | 禁止；阻止；排斥 | Preventing someone from doing something or being part of something.  | The judge debarred him from practicing law.  法官禁止他执业。 | 近义词：prohibiting, excluding, barring, preventing | 反义词：allowing, permitting | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is debarring them from entering the building. = 他禁止他们进入大楼。
The company is debarring suppliers with poor safety records. = 公司禁止与安全记录不良的供应商合作。


一般过去时:
They debarred him from the competition. = 他们禁止他参加比赛。
The school debarred her from attending classes. = 学校禁止她上课。


现在完成时:
The government has debarred several companies from bidding on the project. = 政府已经禁止几家公司竞标该项目。
She has been debarred from driving for six months. = 她已被禁止驾驶六个月。


过去完成时:
They had debarred him from the club before the incident. = 在事件发生之前，他们就已经将他逐出俱乐部了。
The committee had debarred him from voting before the meeting. = 委员会在会议前已禁止他投票。


将来时:
They will debar him from the next tournament. = 他们将禁止他参加下一届比赛。
The court will debar him from practicing law for five years. = 法院将禁止他执业五年。


虚拟语气:
If they debarred him, the project would fail. = 如果他们禁止他，项目就会失败。
I wish they hadn't debarred him from the competition. = 我真希望他们没有禁止他参加比赛。


被动语态:
He was debarred from the meeting. = 他被禁止参加会议。
The company was debarred from government contracts. = 该公司被禁止参与政府合同。


疑问句:
Was he debarred from the club? = 他被禁止进入俱乐部了吗？
Will she be debarred from the competition? = 她会被禁止参加比赛吗？


否定句:
They did not debar him from entering. = 他们没有禁止他进入。
The court did not debar her from practicing medicine. = 法院没有禁止她行医。


PHRASE->

debar from benefits = 禁止享受福利
debar from entry = 禁止入内
