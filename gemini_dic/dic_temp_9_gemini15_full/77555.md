gdr:/ˌdʒiːdiːˈɑːr/|/ˌdʒiːdiːˈɑːr/| n. |全球存托凭证|Global Depositary Receipt (GDR) is a negotiable certificate issued by a depositary bank, representing a certain number of shares in a foreign company.  These receipts are traded on a local stock exchange, offering investors a way to buy shares in companies that are not listed on their home exchanges.  They are often used by companies to raise capital in international markets.|Example:  Many Chinese companies have issued GDRs to raise capital on European stock exchanges.|近义词: ADR (American Depositary Receipt)|反义词:无|名词
