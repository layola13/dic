impairment:/ɪmˈpɛərmənt/| n. |损害；损伤；障碍 |The loss or weakening of a function or ability.  It refers to a reduction in the quality or effectiveness of something.  This can apply to physical, mental, or cognitive abilities, as well as to things like vision or hearing.|

例句：

His hearing impairment made it difficult to follow the conversation. = 他的听力障碍使他难以跟上谈话。

The accident resulted in a significant impairment of his motor skills. = 这场事故导致他的运动技能严重受损。


近义词：deficiency, disability, handicap, defect, damage

反义词：improvement, enhancement, strengthening


USAGE->

His speech impairment affects his communication. = 他的言语障碍影响了他的沟通。
The machine's impairment hinders production. = 机器故障阻碍了生产。


一般过去时:
The stroke caused a severe impairment of his cognitive function. = 中风导致了他的认知功能严重受损。
The accident led to an impairment of his vision. = 事故导致了他的视力受损。


现在完成时:
Years of smoking have caused a significant lung impairment. = 多年吸烟已经导致了严重的肺部损伤。
The overuse of the joint has resulted in its impairment. = 关节过度使用导致了它的损伤。


过去完成时:
Before the surgery, he had already experienced some visual impairment. = 手术前，他已经经历了一些视力障碍。
The prolonged illness had caused a noticeable impairment in his memory. = 长期患病导致了他的记忆力明显下降。


将来时:
Continued exposure to loud noises will likely cause hearing impairment. = 持续暴露在高噪音环境中可能会导致听力受损。
Excessive alcohol consumption will lead to liver impairment. = 过量饮酒会导致肝功能损伤。


虚拟语气:
If he hadn't suffered that injury, his physical impairment wouldn't exist. = 如果他没有受过那次伤，就不会有现在的身体残疾。
If she had taken better care of herself, her cognitive impairment might not be so severe. = 如果她更爱惜自己，她的认知障碍可能就不会那么严重。


被动语态:
His mobility was impaired by the accident. = 他的行动能力被事故所损害。
Her vision was impaired by the bright light. = 她的视力被强光所损害。


疑问句:
What type of impairment is he experiencing? = 他正在经历哪种类型的损伤？
Does his impairment affect his daily life? = 他的损伤是否影响了他的日常生活？


否定句:
His cognitive abilities were not impaired by the disease. = 他的认知能力没有受到疾病的影响。
There was no impairment to his hearing. = 他的听力没有受损。
