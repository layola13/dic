legitimizing: /ləˈdʒɪtɪmaɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 使合法化；使正当化 |  The act of making something legitimate or acceptable.  The process of giving something a legal or rightful status. |

例句：

The government is attempting to legitimize its actions by claiming they are necessary for national security. = 政府试图通过声称这些行动对国家安全必要来使其合法化。

The recent reforms aim to legitimize the country's political system. = 最近的改革旨在使国家的政治制度合法化。


近义词：justify, validate, authorize, ratify

反义词：invalidate, discredit, undermine, delegitimize


词源:

From the verb "legitimate," which comes from the Late Latin word "legitimare," meaning "to declare lawful." This in turn comes from the Latin word "legitimus," meaning "lawful" or "legal," which is derived from "lex," meaning "law."  The suffix "-izing" indicates the act of making something.
