pulsar:/ˈpʌlsɑːr/| n. |脉冲星|A pulsar is a highly magnetized, rotating neutron star that emits beams of electromagnetic radiation out of its magnetic poles.  These beams sweep across the sky like a lighthouse beam, causing the star to appear to pulse as seen from Earth. Pulsars are incredibly dense, with a mass comparable to the Sun but squeezed into a sphere only about 20 kilometers in diameter.|Example: The discovery of pulsars revolutionized our understanding of neutron stars. 脉冲星的发现彻底改变了我们对中子星的理解。|近义词：neutron star|反义词：无
