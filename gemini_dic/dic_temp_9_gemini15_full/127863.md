neocerebellum: /ˌniːoʊˌsɛrəˈbɛləm/ | n. | 新小脑 | The neocerebellum is the phylogenetically newest part of the cerebellum, primarily involved in motor control and coordination of voluntary movements.  It receives input from the cerebral cortex and plays a crucial role in precise, skilled movements. |The neocerebellum is essential for smooth, coordinated movements. Damage to this region can lead to ataxia and dysmetria. |近义词: cerebrocerebellum (partially overlapping function) |反义词:archicerebellum, paleocerebellum (referring to older parts of the cerebellum with different functions)
