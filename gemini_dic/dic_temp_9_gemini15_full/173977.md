quasi-studious:/ˈkwɑːziˈstjuːdiəs/|/ˈkwɑːzaɪˈstʌdiəs/| adj. |似学者般的；装模作样的；假装用功的 |Seemingly studious; pretending to be studious; affectedly scholarly.  It suggests a superficial or insincere engagement with scholarly pursuits. |He affected a quasi-studious air, constantly adjusting his glasses and muttering to himself. 他装出一副假装用功的样子，不停地调整眼镜，自言自语。|近义词：pretentious, affected, bookish (in a negative sense) |反义词：genuine, sincere, unpretentious


例句：

He adopted a quasi-studious posture, but I knew he hadn't read a single page. 他摆出一副假装用功的姿势，但我清楚他一页书也没读。

Her quasi-studious demeanor belied her actual lack of interest in the subject. 她装模作样的态度掩盖了她对这个主题缺乏兴趣的事实。


词源:

* **quasi-**:  From Latin *quasi*, meaning "as if," "almost," or "similar to."  It indicates something that resembles or partially possesses a certain quality.

* **studious**: From  "study," ultimately from Latin *studēre*, meaning "to be diligent," "to apply oneself."


用法说明:

`quasi-studious` is primarily used to describe someone who gives the outward appearance of being studious, but lacks genuine intellectual curiosity or commitment to learning.  It carries a slightly negative connotation, implying pretense or affectation.  It's often used ironically or sarcastically.
