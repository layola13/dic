embarkations:/ˌɛmbɑːrˈkeɪʃənz/ | n. | 登船；启航；(尤指)大规模的迁移 | The act of embarking, especially a large-scale movement of people.  It often refers to the beginning of a journey, especially a sea voyage, but can also be used metaphorically. |例句：The embarkations at the port were delayed due to bad weather.  港口的登船因恶劣天气而延误。

The mass embarkations of refugees to the new country caused a strain on resources.  难民们大规模迁移到新国家给当地资源带来了压力。|近义词：departures, sailings, voyages, migrations |反义词：disembarkations, arrivals
