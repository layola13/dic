rainforest:/ˈreɪnfɒrɪst/ /ˈreɪnfɔːrɛst/| n. |雨林|A rainforest is a lush forest characterized by high rainfall, typically more than 200 centimeters (80 inches) per year.  Rainforests are found in tropical and subtropical regions around the world and are known for their high biodiversity and dense vegetation.| Example: The Amazon rainforest is the largest rainforest in the world. 亚马逊雨林是世界上最大的雨林。  The rainforest provides a habitat for countless species of plants and animals. 雨林为无数动植物提供了栖息地。|jungle, tropical forest|desert, tundra|名词
