extenuatingly:/ɪkˈstɛnjuˌeɪtɪŋli/| adv.| 减轻地；宽恕地；情有可原地 |  In a way that lessens the seriousness or blameworthiness of something.  It emphasizes the mitigating circumstances. |例句：The judge considered the extenuatingly circumstances before sentencing.  法官在判刑前考虑了减轻处罚的情节。

例句：He acted extenuatingly, given the pressure he was under.  考虑到他承受的压力，他的行为是可以理解的。


近义词：mitigatingly, excusably, pardonably | 反义词：aggravatingly, inexcusably


词源:  Derived from the verb "extenuate," which comes from the Late Latin *extenuāre,  from *ex- (out, from) + *tenuāre (to make thin, weaken).  The original sense was to lessen or weaken something, and this evolved to include lessening the seriousness of a fault or offense.
