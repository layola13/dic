kneadability: /ˌniːdəˈbɪləti/ | n. | 可塑性，揉合性 | The ability of a substance, especially dough, to be kneaded.  It refers to the ease with which a material can be worked and shaped by pressing, folding, and stretching.  This property is often important in food processing (especially baking) and material science. | Example: The high kneadability of the dough allowed for easy shaping of the bread.  高筋面团的可塑性使得面包易于成型。  The clay’s excellent kneadability made it perfect for sculpting.  粘土极佳的可塑性使其非常适合雕塑。 | 近义词: malleability, plasticity, workability | 反义词: brittleness, rigidity
