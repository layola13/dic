misfired:/ˌmɪsˈfaɪərd/| v. |未能发射；失败；落空|To fail to function as intended; to fail to achieve its purpose.  The plan misfired spectacularly.|近义词：backfired, failed, malfunctioned|反义词：succeeded, worked, functioned


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The engine misfires occasionally. = 引擎偶尔会失火。
His jokes often misfire. = 他的笑话经常不好笑。


一般过去时:
The rocket misfired and exploded. = 火箭未能发射并爆炸了。
My attempt to surprise her misfired completely. = 我试图给她惊喜完全失败了。


现在完成时:
The project has misfired, resulting in significant losses. = 项目失败了，导致了巨大的损失。
My plans to travel have misfired due to unforeseen circumstances. = 由于不可预见的情况，我的旅行计划失败了。


过去完成时:
They realized their strategy had misfired after the election. = 他们在选举后才意识到他们的策略失败了。
I discovered that my attempt to be subtle had misfired badly. = 我发现我试图委婉的尝试严重失败了。


将来时:
If we don't prepare properly, the operation will misfire. = 如果我们没有做好充分的准备，行动将会失败。
I fear his grand scheme will misfire in the long run. = 我担心他的宏伟计划最终会失败。


虚拟语气:
If only the plan hadn't misfired, we would have succeeded. = 如果计划没有失败，我们就会成功了。
I wish my attempt to impress her hadn't misfired so badly. = 我真希望我试图给她留下好印象的尝试没有那么失败。


被动语态:
The missile was reported to have misfired. = 据报道，导弹未能发射。
The plan was discovered to have misfired. = 发现计划失败了。


疑问句:
Did the gun misfire? = 枪走火了吗？
Has the marketing campaign misfired? = 营销活动失败了吗？


否定句:
The mechanism didn't misfire; it was deliberately sabotaged. = 机制没有失灵；它是被故意破坏的。
The experiment didn't misfire; it produced the expected results. = 实验没有失败；它产生了预期的结果。
