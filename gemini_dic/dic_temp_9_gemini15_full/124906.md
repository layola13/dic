muddy:/ˈmʌdi/ | adj. |泥泞的；浑浊的；模糊不清的 | Covered or mixed with mud; unclear or confused.  |例句：The road was muddy after the heavy rain.  大雨过后，路变得泥泞不堪。

例句：His explanation was muddy and difficult to understand.  他的解释含糊不清，难以理解。|近义词：mirey, miry, slushy, turbid, unclear |反义词：clear, clean, transparent


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The shoes are muddy. = 鞋子是泥泞的。
The river water is muddy. = 河水浑浊。

一般过去时:
The field was muddy yesterday. = 昨天田地是泥泞的。

现在完成时:

The river has been muddy for days. = 河水已经浑浊了好几天了。

过去完成时:

词源:
The word "muddy" comes from the Middle English word "muddi," meaning "covered in mud."  This, in turn, is derived from the Old English word "mudig," which also means "muddy."  The root is likely related to Proto-Germanic *mōdag, sharing a connection with words relating to moisture and earth.
