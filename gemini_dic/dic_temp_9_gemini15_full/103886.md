jud:/dʒʌdʒ/ | v. | 判断，审判，评价 | To form an opinion about something or someone, or to make a decision about a problem or dispute.  | The judge judged the defendant guilty.  法官判定被告有罪。 | assess, evaluate, determine | accept, believe  | verb


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He judges people too quickly. = 他判断人太快了。
She judges the quality of the wine. = 她评价葡萄酒的质量。

一般过去时:
The jury judged him innocent. = 陪审团判定他无罪。
I judged the distance incorrectly. = 我判断距离不准确。

现在完成时:
They have judged the competition fairly. = 他们公平地评判了比赛。
We have judged the case to be closed. = 我们已判定此案结案。

过去完成时:
She had judged the situation wrongly. = 她之前判断形势错误。
They had judged the painting to be a fake. = 他们之前判断这幅画是赝品。

将来时:
The committee will judge the entries next week. = 委员会下周将评判参赛作品。
He will judge the contest based on creativity. = 他将根据创意来评判比赛。

虚拟语气:
If I were you, I wouldn't judge him so harshly. = 如果我是你，我不会这么严厉地批评他。
I wish I hadn't judged him so quickly. = 我希望我没有那么快就判断他。


被动语态:
The case was judged fairly. = 此案审判公平。
The paintings are being judged by experts. = 画作正在由专家评判。

疑问句:
How do you judge success? = 你如何判断成功？
What criteria did they use to judge the contestants? = 他们用什么标准来评判参赛者？

否定句:
Don't judge a book by its cover. = 不要以貌取人。
I didn't judge him to be dangerous. = 我没有认为他危险。


PHRASE->
judge by = 根据…来判断
judge for oneself = 自己判断
judge someone harshly = 严厉批评某人
pass judgment on = 对…做出评价
cast judgment = 评判
