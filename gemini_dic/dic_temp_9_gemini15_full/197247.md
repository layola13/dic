shari'ah: /ʃəˈriːə/ | n. |伊斯兰教法|Islamic law; the religious law of Islam, encompassing religious duties and obligations as well as secular aspects of life.  It is derived from the Quran and the Sunnah (the teachings and practices of the Prophet Muhammad). Shari'ah governs various aspects of Muslim life, including personal status, family law, criminal justice, and commercial transactions.  The interpretation and application of Shari'ah can vary widely among different Muslim communities and schools of thought.|  Many Muslim countries incorporate elements of Shari'ah into their legal systems, although the extent of its application differs significantly.|The application of shari'ah varies greatly across different Muslim communities.| 近义词：Islamic law, religious law | 反义词：secular law
