precognitions:/ˌpriːkɒɡˈnɪʃənz/ | n. |预知，预感 |The ability to know something before it happens; a prophetic insight or intuition.  Precognitions are often described as flashes of insight or vivid dreams that seem to predict future events. |例句：Her precognitions about the accident were unsettlingly accurate. 她对事故的预知准确得令人不安。
例句：Some people believe that precognitions are a form of extrasensory perception (ESP). 一些人相信预知是一种超感官知觉 (ESP)。|近义词：foreknowledge, prescience, premonition, prophecy |反义词：hindsight, postdiction
