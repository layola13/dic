predisagreement:/ˌpriːdɪsəˈɡriːmənt/| n. |预先的不一致；潜在的分歧；先期分歧|A predisagreement is a disagreement that is likely to happen in the future, or that is already present but not yet openly expressed.  It refers to an underlying tension or difference of opinion that could easily escalate into a full-blown conflict. |Example:  The predisagreement between the two parties prevented them from reaching a consensus.  两个党派之间的预先分歧阻止了他们达成共识。|近义词：potential conflict, latent disagreement, underlying tension|反义词：consensus, agreement, harmony|名词
