/ˌhɛlɪˈnɪstɪk/ | adj. | 希腊化的；亚历山大时代的 | Relating to the Hellenistic period (roughly 323–31 BC), the age following the death of Alexander the Great, characterized by the spread of Greek culture and language throughout the Mediterranean world.  The term often implies a fusion of Greek culture with other cultures. | The Hellenistic period saw the rise of significant advancements in science and philosophy.  | 近义词：Alexandrian, Greco-Roman (depending on context) | 反义词：None directly, but could consider terms implying pre-Hellenistic or anti-Hellenistic cultures.  | 形容词
