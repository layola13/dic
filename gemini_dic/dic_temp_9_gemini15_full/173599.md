quasi-inevitable: /ˈkwɑːziɪnˈɛvɪtəbl/ | adj. | 几乎不可避免的，大概会发生的 |  Almost inevitable; very likely to happen.  It suggests a high probability but not absolute certainty.  |例句：The economic downturn seems quasi-inevitable given the current political climate.  鉴于目前的政治气候，经济衰退似乎几乎不可避免。


The rising sea levels seem quasi-inevitable due to climate change. 由于气候变化，海平面上升似乎几乎不可避免。| 近义词：almost certain, highly probable, virtually certain, inevitable (but with less certainty) | 反义词：unlikely, improbable, avoidable, preventable |  词性：形容词

词源:
* **quasi-**:  From Latin *quasi*, meaning "as if," "as it were," "almost," or "similar to."
* **inevitable**: From Latin *inevitabilis*, from *in-* (not) + *evitabilis* (avoidable), from *evitare* (to avoid).
