/ˈkwɑːzaɪ ˈplɛdʒɪz/ | n. |准承诺；似是而非的承诺 | Quasi-pledges refer to commitments or promises that are not fully binding or legally enforceable.  They lack the formal characteristics of a true pledge, often due to ambiguity, lack of specificity, or absence of consideration.  These commitments might be based on informal agreements, understandings, or statements of intent. | The government made quasi-pledges to reduce the national debt, but these were not enshrined in law.  政府做出了减少国债的准承诺，但这并没有写入法律。|  implicit commitment, informal promise, tentative undertaking |  firm commitment, binding agreement, legally enforceable contract | 名词
