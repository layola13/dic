objectivation:/ˌɒbdʒektɪˈveɪʃən/| n. |客体化；客观化；物化|The process of treating a person or group as an object, often disregarding their feelings, individuality, or agency.  It can involve reducing someone to a mere instrument or commodity.  This is often seen in sexist or racist practices, and more broadly, in power dynamics where one party dominates another.|例句：The objectification of women in advertising is a serious concern.  女性在广告中的物化是一个严重的问题。

例句：The study examined the objectification of marginalized communities in media representations. 这项研究考察了媒体对边缘社群的客体化。


近义词：dehumanization, reification, commodification, instrumentalization|反义词：empowerment, subjectification, humanization|词性：名词


词源:  The word "objectivation" derives from the Latin word "objectum," meaning "something placed before," or "object." The suffix "-ification" indicates the process of making something into an object.  Therefore, objectivation refers to the act or process of turning something (usually a person) into an object.
