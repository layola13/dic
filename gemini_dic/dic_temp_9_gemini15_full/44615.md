crowding:/ˈkraʊdɪŋ/| n. |拥挤，人群拥挤|The noun "crowding" refers to a situation where there are too many people or things in a limited space, causing discomfort or inconvenience.  It implies a feeling of being squeezed or pressed together.|例句：The crowding on the subway was unbearable.  地铁上的拥挤不堪忍受。|例句：The event was poorly planned and resulted in severe crowding.  活动计划不周，导致严重拥挤。|近义词：overcrowding, congestion, compression|反义词：spaciousness, roominess, emptiness|名词

词源:
The word "crowding" comes from the verb "crowd," which ultimately derives from the Old Norse word "krūda," meaning "a collection, a throng, or a crowd."  The suffix "-ing" transforms the verb into a noun, signifying the act or state of crowding.
