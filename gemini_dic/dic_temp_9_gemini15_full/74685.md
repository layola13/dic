freedoms:/ˈfriːdəmz/ | n. |自由（复数）|The basic rights and freedoms guaranteed by a constitution or law, such as freedom of speech, religion, and assembly.  Freedoms are often considered essential for a just and equitable society. They are often protected by law and actively fought for by citizens.| 例句：The Bill of Rights protects fundamental freedoms.  《权利法案》保护基本自由。

The freedoms enjoyed by citizens in a democracy are not always guaranteed in other countries.  在一个民主国家中，公民享有的自由并非在其他国家都能得到保障。|近义词：liberties, rights, privileges |反义词：restrictions, constraints, oppression
