prohibitive:/prəˈhɪbɪtɪv/| adj.| 禁止的；价格过高的 | Extremely high; so high as to prevent purchase or use.  |例句：The cost of the treatment was prohibitive.  治疗费用高得令人望而却步。

例句：The rent in this city is prohibitive for many young people.  这座城市的租金对许多年轻人来说都太高了。


近义词：expensive, costly, exorbitant, unaffordable | 反义词：affordable, cheap, inexpensive


词源:  From prohibit + -ive.  Prohibit comes from Latin *prohibere, from pro- "forward" + habēre "to have, hold," thus literally "to hold back."  The suffix "-ive" forms adjectives.


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The price of the car is prohibitive. = 这辆车的价格令人望而却步。
That new technology is prohibitive for small businesses. = 那项新技术对小型企业来说是难以承受的。


一般过去时:
The ticket prices were prohibitive last year. = 去年票价高得令人难以接受。
The cost of living in London was prohibitive for us. = 我们在伦敦的生活成本太高了。


现在完成时:
The new regulations have made some activities prohibitive. = 新规章已经使得一些活动受到了禁止。
Rising interest rates have made borrowing prohibitive for many. = 持续上升的利率使得许多人难以借贷。


过去完成时:
The cost of the project had already become prohibitive by the time they cancelled it. = 项目取消时，其成本已经高得令人望而却步。
Before the new law, the fines for that offense had been prohibitive. = 在新法律出台之前，对该罪行的罚款高得令人望而却步。


将来时:
The new taxes will make many products prohibitive. = 新税收将使许多产品价格高得令人难以接受。
Higher energy prices will make long-distance travel prohibitive for many people. = 较高的能源价格将使许多人难以进行长途旅行。


虚拟语气:
If the price were not prohibitive, I would buy it. = 如果价格不是那么高，我会买下它。
If the regulations weren't so prohibitive, we could have done it differently. = 如果规定不那么严格，我们可以采取不同的方式。


被动语态:
The project was deemed prohibitive due to high costs. = 由于成本过高，该项目被认为是难以承受的。
Many activities are made prohibitive by strict regulations. = 许多活动由于严格的规章制度而被禁止。


疑问句:
Is the cost of this project prohibitive? = 这个项目的成本是否过高？
Are the entrance fees prohibitive for families with children? = 入场费对于有孩子的家庭来说是否过高？


否定句:
The price was not prohibitive, so I bought it. = 价格并不高，所以我买了它。
The new law isn't prohibitive; it's quite reasonable. = 新法律并非禁止性法规；它相当合理。
