/ˈeɪmɪd/| n. |泽龟属|The word "emyd" refers to a genus of turtles,  Emydidae,  commonly known as pond turtles, marsh turtles, or river turtles. They are characterized by their smooth, relatively low-domed shells and are generally aquatic or semi-aquatic.  The genus is diverse and includes many species found across various regions of the world.  The specific characteristics vary considerably among species.|The painted turtle (Chrysemys picta) is a common example of an emydid turtle.|近义词: turtle, terrapin|反义词: (no direct antonym)|名词
