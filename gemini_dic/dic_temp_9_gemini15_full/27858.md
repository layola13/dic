canonizing: /ˈkænənaɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 尊为圣徒；承认…为经典 | The act of declaring someone a saint in the Catholic Church, or more broadly, the act of recognizing something as authoritative or classic.  | Example: The church is canonizing a new saint next month.  下个月教会要将一位新的圣徒封圣。  |近义词: sanctifying, beatifying, enshrining | 反义词: condemning, denouncing


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They are canonizing Mother Teresa. = 他们正在将德雷莎修女尊为圣徒。
The university is canonizing his work as a masterpiece. = 大学正在承认他的作品为杰作。

一般过去时:
The Pope canonized the martyr. = 教皇将这位殉道者封圣。
Critics canonized the author's early novels. = 批评家们将这位作者的早期小说视为经典。

现在完成时:
The church has canonized many saints throughout history. = 教会历史上已经封圣了许多圣徒。
Scholars have canonized Shakespeare's plays as the pinnacle of English literature. = 学者们已经将莎士比亚的戏剧奉为英语文学的巅峰之作。

过去完成时:
Before the council, they had canonized several more figures. = 在会议之前，他们已经封圣了几个人物。
By the 19th century, they had canonized many of the Romantic poets. = 到19世纪，他们已经将许多浪漫主义诗人视为经典。

将来时:
The Vatican will canonize him next year. = 梵蒂冈明年将为他封圣。
Future generations will likely canonize this film as a classic. = 后代很可能会将这部电影视为经典。

虚拟语气:
If the evidence were stronger, they would canonize her. = 如果证据更充分，他们就会将她封圣。
If his work were more widely known, it would be canonized. = 如果他的作品更广为人知，它就会被奉为经典。

被动语态:
He was canonized by the Pope. = 他被教皇封圣。
Her novels are being canonized by a new generation of readers. = 她的小说正被新一代读者视为经典。

疑问句:
Will she be canonized? = 她会被封圣吗？
Is this novel considered canonized? = 这部小说被认为是经典吗？

否定句:
They did not canonize him. = 他们没有将他封圣。
The critics did not canonize his later works. = 批评家们并没有将他的后期作品视为经典。
