quintillion: /kwɪnˈtɪljən/ | n. | 五百万亿（美式）；千万亿（英式） | A quintillion is a number equal to 10^18 (a one followed by eighteen zeros) in the short scale used in the United States and many other countries, and 10^30 in the long scale used in some European countries.  It represents an unimaginably large quantity.  The term is rarely used in everyday conversation but frequently appears in scientific or financial contexts dealing with extremely large numbers. | The estimated number of stars in the observable universe is in the quintillions.  |近义词：None (due to its magnitude)|反义词：None (due to its magnitude) |名词
