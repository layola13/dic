counterrolment: /ˌkaʊntərˈroʊlmənt/ | n. | 对卷；反向卷轴；对录 | A counterrolment is a duplicate or counterpart of a roll or document, especially one used to verify or check the accuracy of the original.  It serves as a means of cross-checking information.  The term often appears in historical contexts, particularly in relation to legal or financial records.  | Example: The counterrolment of the treaty ensured that both parties had a verifiable copy.  对条约的反向卷轴确保双方都拥有可验证的副本。 | 近义词：duplicate, counterpart, copy | 反义词：original (in the sense of a single, unverified document) | 名词
