disclose:/dɪˈskloʊz/ | vt. |公开；揭露；透露 | To make something known that was previously secret or hidden.  |例句：The company disclosed its financial results last week. 公司上周公开了其财务业绩。|例句：He disclosed the secret to his friend. 他向朋友透露了这个秘密。|近义词：reveal, unveil, divulge, expose |反义词：conceal, hide, secrete, suppress


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company discloses its financial results every quarter. = 公司每季度都会公布其财务业绩。
She discloses her personal information cautiously. = 她谨慎地透露自己的个人信息。


一般过去时:
The witness disclosed crucial information to the police. = 证人向警方透露了关键信息。
He disclosed his identity to the authorities. = 他向当局透露了自己的身份。


现在完成时:
The government has disclosed new details about the investigation. = 政府已经公布了有关调查的新细节。
They have disclosed all the relevant documents. = 他们已经公开了所有相关的文件。


过去完成时:
He had disclosed the truth before anyone else did. = 他在其他人之前就已道出了真相。
They had disclosed their plans to their families. = 他们已经向家人透露了他们的计划。


将来时:
The company will disclose its earnings next month. = 公司将于下个月公布其收益。
She will disclose the results of her research soon. = 她很快就会公布她的研究成果。


虚拟语气:
If he disclosed the truth, he would be in trouble. = 如果他公开真相，他就会有麻烦。
I wish she had disclosed the problem earlier. = 我希望她早点说出这个问题。


被动语态:
The information was disclosed to the public. = 信息已向公众公开。
The details of the agreement have been disclosed. = 协议的细节已被披露。


疑问句:
Will the company disclose its plans for expansion? = 公司会公布其扩张计划吗？
When will the results of the study be disclosed? = 研究结果何时公布？


否定句:
The witness refused to disclose any information. = 证人拒绝透露任何信息。
They did not disclose the location of the meeting. = 他们没有透露会议的地点。



PHRASE->
disclose a secret = 泄露秘密
disclose information = 透露信息
fully disclose = 全面披露
publicly disclose = 公开披露
disclose to someone = 向某人透露
