indexically:/ɪnˈdɛksɪkli/| adv.| 指标地，索引地 |  Describes something that functions as an index or indicator, pointing to or representing something else. It often implies a non-arbitrary or inherent connection between the sign and the signified. | Example: The smoke indexically signifies fire.  烟雾象征性地代表着火灾。| 近义词：symptomatically, symbolically (in some contexts) | 反义词：arbitrarily, randomly


USAGE->
This word is an adverb and doesn't have verb conjugations like "what" does.  Therefore, tense conjugations are not applicable.  The usage focuses on how the word modifies other verbs or clauses.

Example 1: The symptoms indexically pointed to a serious illness.  症状指标性地指向一种严重的疾病。
Example 2: His agitated behavior indexically suggested his guilt. 他焦躁不安的行为暗示了他有罪。


PHRASE->
There are not common set phrases using "indexically".  Its usage is more within academic or technical writing.  Phrases would depend on the specific context.
