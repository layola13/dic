dichogamy:/daɪˈkɒɡəmi/ | n. |雌雄异熟| Dichogamy refers to the condition in plants where the pistils (female reproductive organs) and stamens (male reproductive organs) mature at different times, preventing self-pollination. This mechanism promotes cross-pollination and genetic diversity.  There are two types: protandry (male organs mature first) and protogyny (female organs mature first).| Example:  Many species of plants exhibit dichogamy to avoid self-fertilization.  许多植物物种表现出雌雄异熟以避免自花授粉。|近义词：heterodichogamy |反义词：homogamy
