identification: /ˌaɪdɛntɪfɪˈkeɪʃən/ | n. | 识别；鉴定；身份证明 | The act of identifying someone or something; the process of establishing the identity of something.  | Example: The police made a positive identification of the suspect.  警方对嫌疑人进行了确认身份。|近义词：recognition, verification, determination | 反义词：misidentification, anonymity

The process of identifying someone or something is called identification. This involves establishing the identity of a person, object, or phenomenon. It can be based on various factors, such as physical characteristics, fingerprints, DNA, or other unique identifiers.  In many contexts, proper identification is crucial for security, legal proceedings, and other important matters.

例句：

The identification of the body was made possible by dental records. 通过牙科记录，对尸体进行了身份识别。

Accurate identification of the plant species is essential for effective conservation efforts.  准确识别植物物种对于有效的保护工作至关重要。
