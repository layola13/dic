fowling:/ˈfaʊlɪŋ/| n. v.|狩猎（禽鸟）；捕猎|The act of hunting or catching wild birds.  It often involves the use of various methods such as nets, traps, or guns. |例句：The fowling season opens in autumn. 秋天开始狩猎季节。

例句：He spent the morning fowling in the woods. 他上午在树林里打猎。


近义词：hunting, shooting, bird hunting|反义词：None specifically, but  "conservation" or "wildlife protection" could be considered opposites in terms of the activity's impact.|词性:noun/verb

词源:

The word "fowling" comes from the Middle English word "fowlen," which itself derives from the Old English word "fuglian," meaning "to catch birds."  The word "fowl" itself has roots in Proto-Germanic and ultimately reflects a wider Indo-European base referring to birds.
