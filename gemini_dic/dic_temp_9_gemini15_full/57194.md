down-on:/ˌdaʊnˈɑːn/|/ˌdaʊnˈɒn/| adj. | 潦倒的；失意的；受压迫的；对…持负面看法 |  Describes someone who is experiencing hardship, misfortune, or lack of success; also can describe a negative opinion or feeling towards something or someone. |例句：He's been down on his luck lately. 他最近运气不好。 He's really down on the new management. 他真的对新管理层很不满。|近义词：depressed, unlucky, disheartened, pessimistic |反义词：lucky, fortunate, optimistic, upbeat


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is down on his luck. = 他运气不好。
She is down on the new policy. = 她对新政策持负面看法。


一般过去时:
They were down on their luck after losing their jobs. = 他们失业后很倒霉。
He was down on the idea from the start. = 他从一开始就对这个主意持否定态度。


现在完成时:
I've been down on myself lately. = 我最近一直很沮丧。
She's been down on the team's performance. = 她一直对球队的表现感到不满。


过去完成时:
He had been down on his luck for years before he finally got a break. = 在他最终获得成功之前，他已经倒霉了很多年。
They had been down on the project since the initial funding was cut. = 自从最初的资金被削减后，他们就对这个项目感到失望。


将来时:
I fear I will be down on myself if I fail the exam. = 我担心如果考试不及格我会很沮丧。
They think they will be down on the results of the survey. = 他们认为他们会对调查结果感到失望。


虚拟语气:
If I were down on my luck, I would ask for help. = 如果我运气不好，我会寻求帮助。
If he were down on the proposal, he would have voiced his concerns. = 如果他对这个提议持否定态度，他会表达他的担忧。


被动语态:
The team was down on by the critics after their poor performance. = 球队在糟糕的表现后受到了评论家的批评。 (Note:  "down on" is not typically used passively in this way.  The sentence is restructured for illustrative purposes.)


疑问句:
Is he down on his job? = 他对他的工作感到沮丧吗？
Are they down on the new marketing strategy? = 他们对新的营销策略持负面看法吗？


否定句:
He is not down on his luck at all; he is very successful. = 他一点也不倒霉；他非常成功。
She is not down on the idea; in fact, she supports it. = 她并不反对这个主意；事实上，她支持它。


PHRASE->
down on one's luck = 运气不好
down on someone = 对某人有偏见
down on oneself = 沮丧的，对自己不满意的
down on the job = 工作懈怠
down on the heels = 穷困潦倒
