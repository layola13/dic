ealderman:/ˈɛəldərmən/| n. | 市政议员，长老 | An alderman is an elected member of a municipal council, especially in England.  The term historically referred to a senior member of a community, often holding significant authority. | Example: He was elected alderman for the city's west ward. 他当选为该市西区议员。 |近义词：council member, magistrate, dignitary | 反义词：citizen (in the sense of lacking the specific aldermanic role)
