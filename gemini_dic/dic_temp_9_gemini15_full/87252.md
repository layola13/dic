heriot:/ˈhɛriət/ | n. | 遗产；死者的财产（尤指被没收的） |  A heriot is a customary payment of the best beast (or other chattel) belonging to a tenant upon his death to his lord.  It was a feudal custom, often mentioned in old wills and legal documents.  The term can also refer to any goods or property inherited. | Example:  The lord claimed the heriot after the farmer's death. = 地主在农民死后要求继承他的遗产。 The will stipulated a specific heriot to be paid upon his passing. = 遗嘱规定在他去世后要支付具体的遗产。 | 近义词：inheritance, legacy, forfeit | 反义词：N/A
