/ˈhɛərɪŋ/| n. |鲱鱼|A herring is a small, silvery fish that lives in the sea and is often eaten.  Herrings are important commercially and are widely consumed worldwide, either fresh, smoked, pickled, or canned. They are also used as bait for other fish.|The fishermen caught a large shoal of herrings. 渔民捕到了一大群鲱鱼。

词源:

The word "herring" comes from the Old English word *hæring, which ultimately derives from Proto-Germanic *haringaz.  The word's origins are unclear, but it may be related to words meaning "army" or "host," reflecting the vast schools of herring that travel together.
