landlubber:/ˈlændˌlʌbər/| n. |陆地人；旱鸭子；不谙海事的人 |A landlubber is a person who is unfamiliar with the sea and ships.  They are often clumsy or inexperienced at seafaring activities. |例句：He's a complete landlubber; he gets seasick just looking at a boat. 他是个十足的旱鸭子；只要看到船就会晕船。|例句：As a landlubber, I found navigating the yacht quite challenging.作为一个陆地人，我发现驾驶游艇相当具有挑战性。|近义词：lubber, greenhorn, novice, landsman |反义词：sailor, seaman, mariner


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is a landlubber. = 他是个旱鸭子。
She is a landlubber who gets seasick easily. = 她是个容易晕船的旱鸭子。


一般过去时:
He was a landlubber before joining the navy. = 他在加入海军之前是个旱鸭子。
They were landlubbers, unused to the rocking of the ship. = 他们都是旱鸭子，不习惯船的摇晃。


现在完成时:
He has always been a landlubber. = 他一直都是个旱鸭子。
She has become a landlubber after many years at sea. = 在海上待了很多年后，她变成了一个旱鸭子。


过去完成时:
He had been a landlubber his whole life. = 他一生都是个旱鸭子。
They had never been sailors; they were landlubbers. = 他们从来都不是水手；他们是旱鸭子。


将来时:
He will remain a landlubber. = 他将仍然是个旱鸭子。
She will likely become a landlubber after this voyage. = 这次航海之后，她很可能会变成一个旱鸭子。


虚拟语气:
If he were a sailor, he wouldn't be a landlubber. = 如果他是水手，他就不会是旱鸭子。
I wish I weren't such a landlubber. = 我希望我不是这么个旱鸭子。


被动语态:
(This word is rarely used in the passive voice.)


疑问句:
Is he a landlubber? = 他是旱鸭子吗？
Are they landlubbers? = 他们是旱鸭子吗？


否定句:

They are not landlubbers; they are experienced sailors. = 他们不是旱鸭子；他们是经验丰富的水手。
