coindication:/ˌkoʊɪndɪˈkeɪʃən/| n. |同时发生的征兆；并发症；共存迹象 |The simultaneous occurrence of two or more signs or symptoms that may indicate a particular condition or disease.  Coindication often implies a relationship between the signs or symptoms, suggesting a common underlying cause.  It differs from coincidence, which simply means two things happening at the same time without any implied connection. |例句：The patient's fever and rash were a coindication of measles.  患者的发烧和皮疹是麻疹的并发症。

The presence of both hypertension and diabetes in a patient is a strong coindication for increased cardiovascular risk.  病人同时患有高血压和糖尿病，这强烈暗示着心血管疾病风险增加。|近义词：concomitance, concurrence, association |反义词：dissociation, independence
