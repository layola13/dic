nonformidableness:/ˌnɒnˈfɔːrmɪdəblˌnəs/| n. |不令人敬畏；不令人畏惧；不足为惧|The nonformidableness of the enemy army surprised everyone.  敌军的不足为惧让每个人都感到惊讶。|近义词：unimpressiveness, insignificance, weakness|反义词：formidableness, awesomeness, terribleness|


词源:

* **non-**:  Prefix meaning "not". (否定前缀，意为“非”)
* **formidable**: Adjective meaning "inspiring fear or respect through being impressively large, powerful, intense, or capable." (形容词，意为“令人敬畏的；可怕的；强大的”)  This word itself has Latin roots:  "formidābilis," from "formīdō" (fear, dread). (其词根来自拉丁语“formīdō”，意为恐惧，畏惧)
* **-ness**: Suffix forming nouns of state or quality. (名词后缀，表示状态或性质)


因此，nonformidableness 的字面意思是“非令人敬畏的”，或者更自然地说，“不令人敬畏的；不令人畏惧的；不足为惧”。


用法说明:

Nonformidableness is a relatively uncommon word.  It's used to describe something that lacks the quality of being formidable; something that doesn't inspire fear, awe, or respect due to a lack of power, strength, or impressiveness.  It often implies a surprising or unexpected lack of threat.


例句:

The nonformidableness of the task surprised us; we had expected a much greater challenge. 任务的不足为惧让我们大吃一惊；我们原本预计会面临更大的挑战。

The general underestimated the enemy due to their apparent nonformidableness.  将军因为敌人的表面不足为惧而低估了他们。
