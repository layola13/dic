leading:/ˈliːdɪŋ/| adj. v. n.| 领先的；主要的；领导的；引导的 |  The word "leading" describes something that is at the front, most important, or guiding.  As an adjective, it signifies prominence or superiority. As a verb, it means to guide or direct. As a noun, it refers to the act of leading or the leading position. | 例句：He is a leading expert in his field. 他是该领域的 leading 专家。  She took the leading role in the play. 她扮演了该剧的 leading 角色。 |近义词：chief, principal, foremost, primary, guiding |反义词：following, lagging, subordinate, trailing


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company is leading in innovation. = 这家公司在创新方面处于领先地位。
This road leads to the city center. = 这条路通往市中心。


一般过去时:
She led the team to victory. = 她带领团队取得了胜利。
The detective led the investigation. = 侦探领导了调查。


现在完成时:
They have led the industry for decades. = 几十年来，他们一直引领着这个行业。
She has led a fulfilling life. = 她过着充实的生活。


过去完成时:
He had led a difficult life before finding success. = 在获得成功之前，他过着艰难的生活。
The general had led many battles before this one. = 在这场战斗之前，这位将军已经指挥过许多次战斗。


将来时:
She will lead the meeting tomorrow. = 她明天将主持会议。
They will lead the charge. = 他们将冲锋陷阵。


虚拟语气:
If she led the team, we would win. = 如果她领导团队，我们将获胜。
I wish I had led a more adventurous life. = 我希望我过得更有冒险精神一些。


被动语态:
The team was led by a skilled captain. = 这支队伍由一位技术娴熟的队长领导。
The investigation is being led by the police. = 调查由警方负责。


疑问句:
Who is leading the project? = 谁在领导这个项目？
Which path leads to the summit? = 哪条路通向山顶？


否定句:
He didn't lead the charge. = 他没有冲锋陷阵。
The company is not leading in sales. = 这家公司在销售额上并不领先。
