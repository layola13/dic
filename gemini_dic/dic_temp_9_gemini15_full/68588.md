fans:/fæn/| n.|粉丝，扇子| A person who is enthusiastically devoted to someone or something.  A device used to create a current of air, typically by waving or rotating blades.|例句：She has millions of fans around the world. 她拥有全球数百万粉丝。|例句：He used a hand fan to cool himself down. 他用一把扇子给自己降温。|近义词：admirers, devotees, followers, enthusiasts |反义词：detractors, critics


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He has many fans. = 他有很多粉丝。
They are big fans of the band. = 他们是这个乐队的忠实粉丝。

一般过去时:
I was a big fan of that TV show. = 我曾经是那个电视节目的忠实粉丝。
She had many fans at the concert. = 她在演唱会上有很多粉丝。


现在完成时:
I have been a fan of his music for years. = 我多年来一直是他音乐的粉丝。
She has gained many fans through social media. = 她通过社交媒体获得了许多粉丝。


过去完成时:
I had already been a fan before the album was released. = 专辑发行前我就已经是粉丝了。
They had become fans after seeing their live performance. = 他们看过他们的现场表演后就成为了粉丝。


将来时:
He will have many more fans in the future. = 他将来会有更多粉丝。
She will become a fan after watching the movie. = 看完电影后她会成为粉丝。


虚拟语气:
If I were a fan, I would go to the concert. = 如果我是粉丝，我会去参加演唱会。
I wish I had been a fan earlier. = 我希望我早点成为粉丝。


被动语态:
He is loved by his fans. = 他受到粉丝们的喜爱。
The band was supported by their fans. = 乐队得到了粉丝们的支持。


疑问句:
Are you a fan of this team? = 你是这个队的粉丝吗？
How many fans does the singer have? = 这个歌手有多少粉丝？


否定句:
I am not a fan of that movie. = 我不喜欢那部电影。
He wasn't a fan of her acting. = 他不喜欢她的演技。
