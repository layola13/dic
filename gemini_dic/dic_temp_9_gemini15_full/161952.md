prancing:/ˈprænsɪŋ/| v. adj. | 腾跃；欢快地跳跃；趾高气扬的 |  To move in a lively manner, especially by taking high steps.  It can also describe a way of behaving that is proud and arrogant. |


例句：

The horse pranced proudly around the arena.  = 这匹马在竞技场里骄傲地腾跃。

She pranced into the room, her head held high. = 她趾高气扬地走进房间，昂着头。


近义词：capering, curvetting, skipping, strutting

反义词：shuffling, plodding, ambling


词源：

The word "prancing" comes from the Middle English word "praunsen," which is a variant of the Old French word "brancer," meaning "to brandish," "to flourish," or "to wave."  The sense of lively movement evolved from the image of a horse waving its legs energetically.
