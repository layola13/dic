mutual-funds: /ˈmjuːtʃuəl fʌndz/ | n. | 共同基金 |  A mutual fund is an investment vehicle that pools money from many investors to invest in a diversified portfolio of securities, such as stocks, bonds, and other assets.  The fund is managed by professional fund managers who aim to achieve specific investment objectives.  Investors own shares in the fund, representing their proportional ownership in the underlying assets. | 例句：Many people invest their savings in mutual funds to diversify their portfolios and benefit from professional management.  许多人将储蓄投资于共同基金，以分散投资组合并受益于专业管理。

Many retirees rely on mutual funds as a source of income.  许多退休人员依靠共同基金作为收入来源。


近义词：investment fund, unit trust | 反义词：individual stock (as a contrasting investment strategy)


词性: 名词
