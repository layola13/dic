katydid:/ˈkeɪtiˌdɪd/| n. |纺织娘；蟪蛄|A katydid is a type of large green insect that makes a loud chirping sound, especially at night.  It belongs to the family Tettigoniidae. |例句：The katydids were chirping loudly in the trees.  树上的纺织娘鸣叫得很响亮。|例句：  We heard the distinctive song of the katydid echoing through the night. 我们听到纺织娘独特的鸣叫声回荡在夜空中。|近义词：bush-cricket, grasshopper (less precise)|反义词：无

词源:
The name "katydid" is an onomatopoeia, imitating the sound the insect makes.  It is thought to derive from the repetitive call “Katy did, Katy didn’t”, a phrase children sometimes used to mimic the insect.
