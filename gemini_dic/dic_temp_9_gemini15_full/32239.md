chickened:/ˈtʃɪkənd/ | v. | 临阵退缩；胆怯 | To lose one's nerve and fail to do something one had planned to do.  | He chickened out of the bungee jump at the last minute. 他在最后一刻临阵退缩，没有跳蹦极。|  |  | verb


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He chickens out of every challenge. = 他每次面临挑战都临阵退缩。
She chickens out easily when facing difficult situations. = 她面对困难情况时很容易胆怯。


一般过去时:
I chickened out of asking her for a date. = 我不敢向她要约会。
They chickened out of the planned protest. = 他们放弃了计划中的抗议活动。


现在完成时:
He has chickened out of several important decisions. = 他在几个重要决定上都临阵退缩了。
She has never chickened out of a fight before. = 她以前从未在战斗中临阵退缩过。


过去完成时:
I had chickened out of the competition before it even started. = 比赛还没开始我就已经退缩了。
They had chickened out of confronting their boss about the issue. = 他们已经放弃了与老板对抗这个问题。


将来时:
I think I will chicken out of the speech. = 我想我会在演讲时临阵退缩。
She believes she will chicken out if the pressure gets too high. = 她认为如果压力太大，她会临阵退缩。


虚拟语气:
If I hadn't chickened out, I would have won the race. = 如果我没有临阵退缩，我就会赢得比赛。
I wish I hadn't chickened out of applying for that job. = 我真希望我没有放弃申请那份工作。


被动语态:  (This verb is rarely used passively.)
The plan was abandoned; they chickened out. = 计划被放弃了；他们临阵退缩了。
He was chickened out of the fight by his friends. = 他的朋友们阻止了他参加战斗。（这是一种比较非正式且不太常见的用法）


疑问句:
Did he chicken out of the boxing match? = 他在拳击比赛中临阵退缩了吗？
Will you chicken out at the last moment? = 你会在最后一刻临阵退缩吗？


否定句:
He didn't chicken out, he just changed his mind. = 他没有临阵退缩，他只是改变了主意。
She didn't chicken out despite feeling scared. = 尽管害怕，她并没有临阵退缩。


PHRASE->
chicken out of something = 逃避某事
chicken out of a challenge = 逃避挑战
chicken out of a commitment = 逃避承诺
chicken out of a date = 逃避约会
chicken out at the last minute = 最后一刻临阵退缩
