conventing: /kənˈvɛntɪŋ/ | v. |  集合；聚集；召集 |  The act of convening a meeting or gathering people together.  It implies bringing people together for a specific purpose, often formal. | 例句：The committee is convening next week to discuss the budget.  委员会下周将召开会议讨论预算。

The mayor convened a meeting of city officials to address the crisis. 市长召集了一次城市官员会议来解决危机。 | 近义词：assembling, gathering, summoning | 反义词：dispersing, scattering


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The council convenes every Tuesday. = 理事会每周二召开会议。
She convenes meetings regularly to discuss progress. = 她定期召开会议讨论进展。


一般过去时:
The judge convened the court. = 法官召集了法庭。
They convened a press conference to announce the news. = 他们召开新闻发布会宣布这个消息。


现在完成时:
The committee has convened several times this month. = 委员会本月已经召开过几次会议。
The organization has convened a summit to address climate change. = 该组织已召开峰会以应对气候变化。


过去完成时:
The meeting had convened before he arrived. = 他到达之前会议已经开始了。
They had convened a special session to deal with the emergency. = 他们已经召开特别会议来处理紧急情况。


将来时:
The board will convene next quarter to review the performance. = 董事会将在下一季度召开会议，审查业绩。
The conference will convene in London next year. = 会议将于明年在伦敦举行。


虚拟语气:
If the president convened a meeting, we could discuss the issue. = 如果总统召开会议，我们可以讨论这个问题。
I wish the committee had convened earlier to prevent the problem. = 我希望委员会早点召开会议，以防止这个问题。


被动语态:
The meeting was convened by the chairman. = 会议由主席召集。
A special session was convened to address the concerns. = 召开特别会议来解决这些担忧。


疑问句:
When will the meeting be convened? = 会议什么时候召开？
Has the conference been convened yet? = 会议已经召开了吗？


否定句:
The meeting did not convene on time. = 会议没有按时召开。
The committee has not yet convened to discuss the matter. = 委员会尚未召开会议讨论此事。


PHRASE->
convene a meeting = 召开会议
convene a conference = 召集会议
convene a summit = 召开峰会
convene an assembly = 召集集会
convene a session = 召开会议


词源:
From Old French *convenir (to agree, assemble), from Latin convenire, from com- (together) + venire (to come).
