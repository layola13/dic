retraction:/rɪˈtrækʃən/| n.|撤回，收回；缩回，退缩|The act of withdrawing or taking back something that has been said, offered, or done.  It can refer to physical retraction (like pulling something back) or a statement retraction (taking back a claim).|例句：The newspaper issued a retraction of the false story.  这家报纸发表声明收回虚假报道。

The scientist had to issue a retraction of his controversial findings.  这位科学家不得不撤回他引起争议的研究结果。|近义词：withdrawal, revocation, repudiation|反义词：affirmation, confirmation, endorsement|词性：名词

The journal requires a retraction of the article. = 期刊要求撤回该文章。
He regularly retracts his statements under pressure. = 他经常在压力下收回自己的言论。


一般过去时:
The company retracted its offer after reviewing the contract. = 公司在审查合同后撤回了其报价。
She retracted her hand quickly from the hot stove. = 她迅速地把手从热炉子上缩回。


现在完成时:

The government has retracted its previous statement on the matter. = 政府已收回先前就此事的声明。


过去完成时:
They had retracted their support before the election. = 他们在选举前就撤回了他们的支持。
The witness had retracted his testimony due to fear. = 证人因害怕而撤回了证词。


将来时:

虚拟语气:

疑问句:
