cutout:/ˈkʌtaʊt/| n. |剪纸，剪裁，挖空部分|A cutout is something that has been cut out of a larger piece of material, often to create a shape or design.  It can also refer to a person or thing that is deliberately excluded or ignored.|例句：The children made paper cutouts of snowflakes.孩子们用纸剪出了雪花形状的剪纸。|例句：The editor made some significant cutouts from the original manuscript.编辑从原始手稿中删减了一些重要的部分。|近义词： cutout, clipping,  stencil|反义词：inclusion, whole
