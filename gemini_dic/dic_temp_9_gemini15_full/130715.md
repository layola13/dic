A nonalkaloid compound lacks the characteristic properties of alkaloids, such as basicity and nitrogen-containing heterocyclic rings.  This term is primarily used in chemistry, particularly in the fields of pharmacology and natural product chemistry. |

词源:

The word "nonalkaloid" is a combination of the prefix "non-" (meaning "not") and the noun "alkaloid."  "Alkaloid" comes from the Arabic word "al-qali" (الـقَلْي), meaning "alkali," referring to the basic nature of these compounds.  The suffix "-oid" indicates resemblance or likeness, suggesting that alkaloids are similar to alkali but have more complex chemical structures.  Therefore, "nonalkaloid" literally means "not resembling or related to alkali-like compounds."
