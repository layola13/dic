oleograph:/ˈoʊliəˌɡræf/| n. |油印画，油彩石印画 |A print produced by a process of lithography using oil-based inks.  Oleographs were popular in the 19th century for their bright colors and relatively low cost, often used to reproduce works of art. |Example: The walls of the Victorian parlor were adorned with brightly colored oleographs of landscapes and still lifes.  维多利亚风格客厅的墙壁上装饰着色彩鲜艳的风景和静物油印画。|近义词：chromolithograph |反义词：original painting, hand-drawn artwork
