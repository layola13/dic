gait:/ɡeɪt/ | n. | 步态，步法，步伐 | The manner or style of walking; a person's way of moving on foot.  | Example:  Her gait was slow and unsteady after the accident.  她事故后步履蹒跚。

Example: The horse's gait changed from a trot to a canter. 马的步态从慢跑变成了小跑。


近义词：stride, walk, pace, tread | 反义词：N/A | 词性:名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
His gait is quite distinctive. = 他的步态非常独特。
Her gait reveals her age. = 她的步态暴露了她的年龄。


一般过去时:
His gait was slow and deliberate. = 他的步态缓慢而谨慎。

现在完成时:
Her gait has improved significantly since her surgery. = 她手术后步态有了显著改善。
He has maintained a steady gait throughout the race. = 他在整个比赛中都保持着稳定的步态。


过去完成时:

将来时:

词源：
The word "gait" comes from Old Norse "gata," meaning "way" or "path."  This reflects the word's core meaning of a manner of walking or moving.
