get-in:/ɡɛt ˈɪn/ | v. |进入；插话；到达；完成；影响；收取；(使)被接受|To enter or arrive somewhere; to interrupt a conversation; to succeed in doing something; to complete a task; to have an effect on someone or something; to receive or collect something; to be accepted. |例句：We finally got in after a long journey. 我们长途跋涉后终于到达了。

例句：He tried to get in a word during the meeting, but couldn't. 他试图在会议中插话，但没能成功。

近义词：enter, arrive, interrupt, influence, receive, achieve, succeed |反义词：leave, exit, depart, fail

词源:
The phrasal verb "get in" originates from the Old English verb "gettan," meaning "to obtain" or "to seize."  Over time, its meaning broadened to encompass various senses of arrival, entry, and accomplishment, as reflected in its various uses today.  The preposition "in" signifies entering or inclusion within a specific location or state.
