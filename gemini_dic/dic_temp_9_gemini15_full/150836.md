paraglobulin: /ˌpærəˈɡlɒbjʊlɪn/ | n. |副球蛋白|A globulin found in the blood plasma; a type of antibody.  It is a heterogeneous group of proteins with varied functions, some related to immune response.|Paraglobulins are crucial components of the immune system, playing a role in antibody production and defense against pathogens.  The exact composition and function of paraglobulins can vary depending on the species and individual.|Paraglobulins contribute to blood viscosity and osmotic pressure. |近义词: gamma globulin, immunoglobulin |反义词: None specifically.
