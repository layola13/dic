secularized: /ˈsɛkjʊləraɪzd/ | adj. | 世俗化的；非宗教化的 |  To remove (something) from religious or ecclesiastical influence or control; to make worldly or temporal.  The process by which a society or institution becomes increasingly detached from religious or spiritual concerns. |


例句：

Many European countries have become increasingly secularized over the past century.  = 过去一个世纪，许多欧洲国家日益世俗化。

The education system was secularized, separating religious instruction from the curriculum. = 教育体系世俗化了，将宗教教学与课程分离。


近义词：worldly, nonreligious, profane, materialist

反义词：religious, sacred, spiritual, ecclesiastic


词性: adjective (形容词)


词源:  From "secular" + "-ized". "Secular" ultimately derives from the Latin word "saecularis," meaning "of an age," "worldly," or "temporal," from "saeculum," meaning "age," "generation," or "world."  The suffix "-ized" indicates the process of making something secular.
