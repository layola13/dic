decreeing:/dɪˈkriːɪŋ/| v.|颁布法令；注定；判决|The act of officially deciding or determining something;  to issue a decree; to ordain or determine. |例句：The king was decreeing new laws. 国王正在颁布新法律。


例句：The judge decreed a life sentence for the criminal.  法官判处罪犯无期徒刑。


近义词：ordaining, enacting, establishing, determining


反义词：repealing, abolishing, overturning


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government is decreeing new environmental regulations. = 政府正在颁布新的环保法规。
He is decreeing his own future. = 他正在决定自己的未来。

一般过去时:
The emperor decreed a new tax. = 皇帝颁布了一项新税。
She decreed that the meeting would be postponed. = 她下令推迟会议。

现在完成时:
The council has decreed a new zoning plan. = 理事会已经颁布了一项新的分区规划。
They have decreed that the project must be completed by next month. = 他们已下令该项目必须在下个月完成。

过去完成时:
The court had decreed his innocence before the new evidence emerged. = 在新的证据出现之前，法院已经宣判他无罪。
He had decreed that all dissenters would be banished. = 他已下令所有异议者将被放逐。

将来时:
The president will decree a state of emergency. = 总统将宣布紧急状态。
She will decree the terms of the settlement. = 她将决定和解的条款。

虚拟语气:
If the king decreed it, it would be done. = 如果国王下令，那就会去做。
I wish the judge had decreed a lighter sentence. = 我希望法官判处较轻的刑罚。

被动语态:
New regulations were decreed by the government. = 新的法规是由政府颁布的。
The fate of the city was decreed by the gods. = 城市的命运由神灵决定。

疑问句:
What did the council decree? = 理事会决定了什么？
What will the judge decree in this case? = 在此案中法官会判决什么？

否定句:
The court did not decree a retrial. = 法院没有判决重审。
He did not decree any changes to the existing system. = 他没有下令对现有系统进行任何更改。
