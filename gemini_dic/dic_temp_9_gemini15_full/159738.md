polycratism:/ˈpɒlɪkrætɪzəm/ | n. |寡头政治；多头政治 | The rule by many; a system of government in which power is held by a small group of people, typically self-selected, who are not accountable to the wider population.  This differs from democracy, where power rests with the people, and from aristocracy, where power is inherited.  Polycratism often involves a complex network of interconnected individuals and groups vying for influence. | Example: The increasing influence of lobbyists and special interest groups is seen by some as a manifestation of polycratism in modern democracies.  一些人认为，游说团体和特殊利益集团日益增长的影响力是现代民主制度中多头政治的体现。| 近义词：oligarchy, plutocracy | 反义词：democracy
