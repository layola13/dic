prepositions:/ˌprepəˈzɪʃənz/ | n. |介词 |Prepositions are words that show the relationship between a noun or pronoun and another word in the sentence. They indicate time, place, direction, manner, or other relationships.  They are usually placed before the noun or pronoun they modify. |Examples:  The book is *on* the table. (place)  I will arrive *at* 3 o'clock. (time) She walked *to* the store. (direction) He spoke *with* enthusiasm. (manner)  |近义词:  None (as a class of words) |反义词: None (as a class of words)
