pessimist:/ˈpɛsɪmɪst/| n. |悲观主义者|A person who tends to see the worst aspect of things or believe that the worst will happen.|He's a pessimist and always expects the worst. 他是个悲观主义者，总是期待最坏的结果。|optimist, cynic|optimist, idealist|名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is a pessimist. = 他是个悲观主义者。
She is always a pessimist about the future. = 她总是对未来悲观。


一般过去时:
He was a pessimist in his youth. = 他年轻时是个悲观主义者。
She was a pessimist about the project's success. = 她对项目的成功持悲观态度。


现在完成时:
He has been a pessimist all his life. = 他一生都是个悲观主义者。
She has remained a pessimist despite the good news. = 尽管有好消息，她仍然保持悲观。


过去完成时:
He had been a pessimist before the good news arrived. = 好消息到来之前，他一直是个悲观主义者。
She had always been a pessimist until she met him. = 直到遇到他之前，她一直是个悲观主义者。


将来时:
He will likely remain a pessimist. = 他很可能会继续悲观下去。
She will probably be a pessimist about the outcome. = 她可能会对结果持悲观态度。


虚拟语气:
If he were an optimist, he would be happier. = 如果他是个乐观主义者，他会更快乐。
If she weren't a pessimist, she would have taken the chance. = 如果她不是个悲观主义者，她就会抓住机会。


被动语态:
His pessimism was noted by everyone. = 他的悲观情绪被每个人都注意到了。
Her pessimistic views were not well-received. = 她悲观的观点不受欢迎。


疑问句:
Is he a pessimist? = 他是个悲观主义者吗？
Are you a pessimist about the economy? = 你对经济持悲观态度吗？


否定句:
He is not a pessimist. = 他不是个悲观主义者。
She is not always a pessimist. = 她并不总是悲观的。



PHRASE->
a confirmed pessimist = 坚定的悲观主义者
a hopeless pessimist = 彻底的悲观主义者
a dyed-in-the-wool pessimist = 根深蒂固的悲观主义者
a congenital pessimist = 天生的悲观主义者
pessimistic outlook = 悲观的展望

词源:
From Latin *pessimus ("worst"), superlative of malus ("bad").  The word evolved through the French word "pessimiste".
