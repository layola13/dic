predetermined:/ˌpriːdɪˈtɜːrmɪnd/| adj.| 预定的，预先决定的 |  Something that is predetermined has been decided or planned in advance.  It implies a lack of spontaneity or freedom of choice. |例句：The outcome of the election seemed predetermined.  选举结果似乎是预先决定的。

The meeting's agenda was predetermined before the participants arrived. 会议议程在与会者到达之前就已经预先确定了。


近义词：prearranged, planned, fixed, settled, preordained

反义词：unplanned, spontaneous, unexpected, chance, accidental


词性：形容词

词源:
The word "predetermined" is formed from the prefix "pre-" (meaning "before") + "determined" (past participle of "determine," meaning "to decide or settle").  Therefore, it literally means "decided beforehand".
