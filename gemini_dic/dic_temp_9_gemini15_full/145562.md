overcomplacence:/ˌoʊvər kəmˈpleɪsns/ | adj. | 自满；过分自足 |  A feeling of excessive self-satisfaction or smugness;  an unwarranted sense of security or confidence. It suggests a dangerous lack of awareness of potential problems or threats.  |例句：Their overcomplacence led to their downfall. 他们的自满导致了他们的失败。

例句：The team's overcomplacence after their initial victory proved costly in the later stages of the tournament.  球队在初胜后的自满在锦标赛后期证明代价高昂。


近义词：complacency, smugness, self-satisfaction, self-contentment,  conceit

反义词：humility, vigilance, anxiety, apprehension, awareness


词源:  The word is formed from the prefix "over-" (meaning "excessively" or "too much") and the noun "complacency," which comes from the French word "complaisance" (meaning "compliance," "readiness to please," or "affability").  The sense of self-satisfaction developed over time from the original meaning.


USAGE->
This word is an adjective and thus does not have verb conjugations like the example word "what".  It describes a state of being.


PHRASE->

rest on one's laurels:  躺在功劳簿上
take things for granted: 想当然
become complacent:变得自满
a false sense of security:虚假的安全感
cocky confidence: 傲慢的自信
