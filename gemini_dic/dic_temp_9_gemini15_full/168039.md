privateer:/ˌpraɪvəˈtɪr/| n. |私掠船；私掠船船员 | A privateer is a privately owned ship that is authorized by a government to attack and capture enemy ships during wartime.  Unlike pirates, privateers operate under a letter of marque, a legal document granting them permission to engage in such activities.  This permission typically comes with rules of engagement and stipulations regarding the treatment of captured crews and cargo. |Example: During the Napoleonic Wars, many British privateers operated in the Atlantic, preying on French merchant ships.  在拿破仑战争期间，许多英国私掠船在大西洋活动，袭击法国商船。|近义词：corsair, buccaneer |反义词：naval officer, government ship
