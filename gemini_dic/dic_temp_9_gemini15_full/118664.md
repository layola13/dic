meta-ethics:/ˌmɛtəˈɛθɪks/ | n. |元伦理学 |The branch of ethics that explores the fundamental nature of moral properties, judgments, and reasoning.  It examines the meaning of moral terms, the nature of moral judgments, and the justification of moral principles. Unlike normative ethics (which deals with what is morally right or wrong), meta-ethics explores the underlying assumptions and frameworks of moral discourse.  |Example:  Meta-ethics questions the very foundations of morality, such as whether moral truths are objective or subjective. 元伦理学质疑道德的根本基础，例如道德真理是客观还是主观的。|近义词：moral philosophy, ethical theory |反义词：normative ethics, applied ethics
