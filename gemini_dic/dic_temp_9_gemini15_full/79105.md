gladden:/ˈɡlædn/| vt.|使高兴；使欣喜|To make someone feel happy; to please.  |例句：The good news gladdened her heart. 好消息使她欣喜若狂。|例句：The unexpected gift gladdened his day. 这份意外的礼物使他的一天充满了喜悦。|近义词：delight, please, cheer, elate|反义词：sadden, depress, dishearten|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The good news gladdens her. = 好消息使她高兴。
His smile gladdens my heart. = 他的微笑使我快乐。

一般过去时:
The gift gladdened her. = 这份礼物使她高兴。
The rain gladdened the thirsty plants. = 雨水使干渴的植物高兴。

现在完成时:
The news has gladdened many people. = 这个消息已经使许多人高兴。
Their success has gladdened their parents. = 他们的成功使他们的父母高兴。

过去完成时:
The letter had gladdened her before she received the bad news. = 在她收到坏消息之前，这封信曾让她高兴。
The victory had gladdened the team before the injuries. = 在受伤之前，胜利让球队欢欣鼓舞。

将来时:
The announcement will gladden many hearts. = 这一宣布将使许多人高兴。
Winning the lottery will gladden his family. = 中彩票将使他的家人高兴。

虚拟语气:
It would gladden my heart to see you. = 见到你我会很高兴。
If only the news would gladden him! = 但愿这个消息能让他高兴！

被动语态:
She was gladdened by the unexpected visit. = 她对这次意外的来访感到高兴。
Many were gladdened by the announcement. = 许多人对这一宣布感到高兴。

疑问句:
Will this news gladden her? = 这个消息会让她高兴吗？
Does the sun gladden your heart? = 阳光让你心情愉悦吗？


否定句:
The news did not gladden her. = 这个消息并没有让她高兴。
The bad weather didn't gladden anyone. = 恶劣的天气没有让任何人高兴。


PHRASE->
gladden one's heart: 使某人心花怒放
gladden the spirit: 振奋精神
gladden the eyes: 赏心悦目
gladden the soul: 使灵魂愉悦
to gladden someone's day: 使某人一天都快乐


词源:
Old English *gladdian, from glad "happy, joyful".  The -en suffix is a causative verb-forming suffix, indicating "to cause to be glad".
