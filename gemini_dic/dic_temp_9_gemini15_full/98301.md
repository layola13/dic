insidiously:/ɪnˈsɪdiəsli/ | adj. | 阴险地；潜伏地；暗中地 | Proceeding in a gradual, subtle way, but with harmful effects.  It suggests a slow, stealthy, and often deceptive process that leads to a negative outcome. | 例句：The disease insidiously spread through the population.  疾病在人群中潜伏传播。

The cancer insidiously weakened her body over many years.  癌症多年来逐渐削弱了她的身体。


近义词：treacherously, subtly, stealthily, cunningly, craftily, deceptively

反义词：openly, obviously, directly, frankly


词源：源自拉丁语 *insidiāri (to lie in wait), from *insidiae (ambush, snare).  The word evolved through Old French *insidieusement.
