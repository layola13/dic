separatrix:/ˈsɛpəreɪtrɪks/| n.| 分界线，界限 | A separatrix is a boundary separating regions of different behavior in a system, often used in mathematics and physics to describe dividing lines between different flows or regions of influence.  For example, in fluid dynamics, it separates different flow patterns. In dynamical systems, it separates different basins of attraction. | Example: The separatrix between the two basins of attraction is clearly defined.  分界线将两个吸引盆地清晰地分开。|近义词：boundary, dividing line, demarcation line |反义词：unification, merger
