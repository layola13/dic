dibses:/dɪbzɪz/| n.| 认领；优先认购权 |  A claim or right to something, especially something that has been found or acquired. This is primarily an informal term.  | Example: "I call dibs on the last slice of pizza!" = 我要那最后一片披萨！  |近义词：claim, right, first refusal | 反义词：None specifically, but implies a lack of claim or right. | 名词

USAGE->
一般现在时:
I have dibs on that seat. = 我要那个座位。
She calls dibs on the best room. = 她认领最好的房间。


一般过去时:
He called dibs on the biggest cupcake. = 他认领了最大的纸杯蛋糕。
They had dibs on the front row seats. = 他们拥有前排座位的优先权。


现在完成时:
We've had dibs on this project for weeks. = 我们几周来一直拥有这个项目的优先权。
She has called dibs on the new car. = 她已经认领了新车。


过去完成时:
He had already called dibs on the last cookie before I got there. = 在我到达之前，他已经认领了最后一块饼干。
They had had dibs on the tickets since last year. = 从去年开始，他们就拥有这些票的优先权。


将来时:
I will call dibs on the first piece of cake. = 我要认领第一块蛋糕。
They will have dibs on the best seats in the house. = 他们将拥有最好的座位。


虚拟语气:
If I had known, I would have called dibs on that book. = 如果我知道的话，我就应该认领那本书。
I wish I had called dibs on the last parking space. = 我真希望我早点认领最后一个停车位。


被动语态:
The prize was claimed; dibs were called. = 奖品已被认领；优先权已被确定。
The best seats have been claimed (dibs were called on them). = 最好的座位已被认领。


疑问句:
Does anyone have dibs on this? = 有人认领这个吗？
Who called dibs on the remote? = 谁认领了遥控器？


否定句:
I don't have dibs on anything. = 我什么都没认领。
They didn't call dibs on the extra food. = 他们没有认领额外的食物。

PHRASE->
call dibs on = 认领...
get dibs on = 获得...的优先权
have dibs on = 拥有...的优先权
put dibs on = 认领...
first dibs = 优先认领权
