resenter:/rɪˈzɛntər/| n. |抗议者；反对者；反感的人|A person who expresses their strong disapproval of something.|例句：The protesters were resenters of the new policy.  抗议者们反对这项新政策。|近义词：opponent, protester, objector |反义词：supporter, advocate


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The resenter voices his concerns regularly. = 抗议者经常表达他的担忧。
She resents the unfair treatment she receives. = 她对受到的不公平待遇感到不满。


一般过去时:
He resented the way he was treated. = 他对自己的待遇感到不满。
They resented the imposition of new rules. = 他们对新规的实施表示不满。


现在完成时:
He has resented her behavior for years. = 多年来他一直对她行为不满。
They have resented the lack of communication. = 他们一直对缺乏沟通感到不满。


过去完成时:
She had resented his interference in her life for a long time. = 她很久以前就对他干涉她生活感到不满。
They had resented the constant criticism before they finally left. = 在他们最终离开之前，他们一直对不断的批评感到不满。


将来时:
He will resent any attempt to control him. = 他将对任何试图控制他的企图感到不满。
She will resent the delay in receiving her payment. = 她将对收到付款的延误感到不满。


虚拟语气:
If I were him, I would resent such treatment. = 如果我是他，我会对这种待遇感到不满。
I wish I didn't resent my boss so much. = 我希望我不那么讨厌我的老板。


被动语态:
The new policy was resented by many people. = 许多人对这项新政策感到不满。
His actions were resented by his colleagues. = 他的行为遭到同事的反感。


疑问句:
Does he resent his job? = 他讨厌他的工作吗？
Do you resent the way you are treated? = 你讨厌你受到的待遇吗？


否定句:
I don't resent her success. = 我并不嫉妒她的成功。
They didn't resent the extra work. = 他们并不讨厌额外的工作。


PHRASE->
resent doing something:  对做某事感到不满
resent someone's interference:  对某人的干涉感到不满
resent the fact that...: 对...这个事实感到不满
have a deep-seated resentment: 有根深蒂固的怨恨
harbor resentment: 心怀怨恨
