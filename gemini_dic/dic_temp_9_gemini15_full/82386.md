grubbier:/ˈɡrʌbɪər/| adj.|更脏的；更邋遢的|More dirty or untidy.  The grubbier the better, for some people who are looking for a certain aesthetic.  |例句：His clothes were grubbier than usual after a day spent gardening. 他在花园里待了一天后，衣服比平时更脏了。

例句：The grubbier the pub, the better the beer, some say.  有人说，酒吧越脏，啤酒越好喝。


近义词：dirtier, filthier, messier, sloppier|反义词：cleaner, tidier


词源:源自动词grub，意为“挖掘，寻找食物”，引申为“肮脏，邋遢”。  The word "grub" itself has origins in Middle English, ultimately related to words meaning "to dig" or "to root."  The -ier suffix adds a comparative meaning, signifying "more" of the quality.
