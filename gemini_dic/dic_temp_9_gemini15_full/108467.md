lay-for:/leɪ fɔːr/| phrasal verb| 埋伏；设下圈套；等待时机|  To lie in wait for someone or something, usually with the intention of attacking or capturing them.  It implies a deliberate and often hidden preparation for an ambush or surprise.|例句：The police were laying for the robbers near the bank. = 警察在银行附近埋伏抓捕抢匪。
The hunter spent hours laying for deer. = 猎人花了数小时时间埋伏等待鹿。|近义词：ambush, wait for, lie in wait for|反义词：attack openly, confront directly


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They lay for the thief every night. = 他们每晚都埋伏着抓贼。
The cat lays for mice near the hole. = 猫在洞口附近等着抓老鼠。

一般过去时:
The soldiers laid for the enemy in the jungle. = 士兵们在丛林中埋伏敌人。
We laid for the bus for a long time. = 我们等了很久的公交车。


现在完成时:
They have laid for the suspect for days. = 他们已经埋伏嫌疑犯好几天了。
The police have laid for the criminal since last week. = 警察自从上周就开始埋伏罪犯了。


过去完成时:
They had laid for the burglar for weeks before he was finally caught. = 他们在窃贼被抓之前已经埋伏了他好几个星期了。
The hunters had laid for the bear all morning. = 猎人整个上午都在埋伏熊。


将来时:

虚拟语气:
If I had known they were laying for me, I would have taken a different route. = 如果我知道他们在埋伏我，我会走另一条路。
I wish I hadn't walked into their trap; they were clearly laying for me. = 我真希望我没有走进他们的陷阱；他们显然是在埋伏我。


被动语态:
The robbers were laid for by the police. = 抢匪被警察埋伏了。
The deer were laid for by hunters. = 鹿被猎人埋伏了。


疑问句:
Were they laying for us? = 他们在埋伏我们吗？
Are they laying for the escaped convict? = 他们在埋伏逃犯吗？


否定句:
They didn't lay for the thief that night. = 他们那天晚上没有埋伏窃贼。
We didn't lay for the bus; we just caught it. = 我们没有等公交车；我们只是赶上了它。
