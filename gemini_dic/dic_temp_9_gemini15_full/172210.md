push-polling:/ˈpʊʃˌpoʊlɪŋ/| n.|暗箱操作民调，负面攻击性民调|A push poll is a type of political telemarketing disguised as a poll.  It aims not to gather information but to spread negative or misleading information about a candidate or issue to influence voters' opinions.  The questions are often biased and leading, designed to evoke a negative response towards the targeted candidate.|Example: The campaign used push polling to spread false rumors about their opponent's past.|近义词：dirty tricks, negative campaigning|反义词：opinion poll, unbiased survey|名词
