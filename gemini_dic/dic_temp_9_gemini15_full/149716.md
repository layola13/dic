palaeontologist:/ˌpeɪliɒnˈtɒlədʒɪst/| n. |古生物学家|A scientist who studies fossils to learn about the history of life on Earth.  Palaeontologists examine fossils of plants, animals, and other organisms to understand their evolution, anatomy, and environment. They use a variety of techniques, including geological dating, comparative anatomy, and molecular biology, to reconstruct the past.|例句：Mary Anning was a pioneering palaeontologist who made significant discoveries of Jurassic marine fossils. 玛丽·安宁是一位开创性的古生物学家，她在侏罗纪海洋化石方面取得了重大发现。

例句：The palaeontologist carefully examined the dinosaur bone, noting its unique features.  这位古生物学家仔细检查了恐龙骨骼，并注意到了它的独特特征。


近义词：fossil expert, paleobiologist|反义词：无
