cheapest: /ˈtʃiːpɪst/ | adj. | 最便宜的 | The cheapest option is usually the least desirable. 最便宜的选择通常是最不可取的。|近义词：most inexpensive, least expensive | 反义词：most expensive, dearest


USAGE->
一般现在时:
This is the cheapest store in town. = 这是镇上最便宜的商店。
The cheapest flights are often booked months in advance. = 最便宜的航班通常提前几个月预订。


一般过去时:
That was the cheapest car I ever bought. = 那是我买过的最便宜的车。
The cheapest hotel room was already taken. = 最便宜的酒店房间已经被预订了。


现在完成时:
This has been the cheapest year for groceries.  = 这是最便宜的一年买杂货。
We have found the cheapest option for our vacation. = 我们已经找到了最便宜的度假方案。


过去完成时:
That had been the cheapest solution we'd considered. = 那曾是我们考虑过的最便宜的方案。
Before the sale, that was the cheapest item in the store. = 在促销之前，那是商店里最便宜的商品。


将来时:
This will be the cheapest time to buy a new house. = 这将是购买新房最便宜的时候。
The cheapest tickets will sell out quickly. = 最便宜的票会很快卖完。


虚拟语气:
If it were the cheapest, I would buy it. = 如果它是最便宜的，我会买它。
I wish I had chosen the cheapest option. = 我希望我选择了最便宜的方案。


被动语态:
The cheapest product was chosen by the customer. = 最便宜的产品被顾客选中了。
The cheapest flight was booked by many people. = 最便宜的航班被很多人预订了。


疑问句:
Which is the cheapest flight? = 哪趟航班最便宜？
What is the cheapest way to get there? = 到那里最便宜的方式是什么？


否定句:
That's not the cheapest option. = 那不是最便宜的选择。
This isn't the cheapest place to shop. = 这不是最便宜的购物场所。
