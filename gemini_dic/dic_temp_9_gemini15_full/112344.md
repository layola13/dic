lorica:/ˈlɒrɪkə/| n.|铠甲，护胸甲|A lorica was a piece of body armor, typically made of leather, metal, or scales, worn by Roman soldiers and others for protection.  The specific type varied depending on the era and the soldier's rank.  |Example: The Roman legionary wore a lorica segmentata, a type of segmented armor.  罗马军团士兵穿戴的是分段式铠甲。|近义词：cuirass, breastplate|反义词：None.
