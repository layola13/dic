craniums:/ˈkreɪniəm/| n. |颅骨；头盖骨 |The cranium protects the brain. 颅骨保护大脑。|近义词：skull |反义词：无


英文解析：A cranium is the skull, especially the part that encloses the brain.  It's the bony structure forming the upper part of the head.


例句：

The anthropologist carefully examined the ancient craniums. 人类学家仔细检查了古代的颅骨。

The impact fractured his cranium.  撞击使他的颅骨骨折。


词源：

From Late Middle English *cranium, borrowed from Latin cranium (“skull”), from Ancient Greek κράνιον (kránión, “skull”), diminutive of κάρα (kára, “head”).
