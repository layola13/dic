poising:/ˈpɔɪzɪŋ/| v. n. adj.| 优雅地摆放；使处于…状态；平衡；姿态；优雅；毒害|To prepare oneself for action; to balance or hold in a steady position;  the act of balancing or holding something steady; graceful and elegant bearing or posture. |例句：She was poising herself to deliver a powerful speech. 她正准备发表一篇强有力的演讲。


The bird poised on the branch, ready to fly.  鸟儿停在树枝上，准备起飞。


近义词：balancing, preparing, readying, elegant, graceful| 反义词：clumsy, awkward, ungainly,  unsteady


词性:动词，名词，形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He poises the camera carefully before taking the shot. 他在拍照前小心地摆好相机。
She poises herself for a difficult conversation. 她为一场艰难的谈话做好了准备。


一般过去时:
He poised the pen above the paper. 他把笔悬在纸上。
She poised herself gracefully before the audience. 她优雅地站在观众面前。


现在完成时:
She has poised herself to take on new challenges. 她已经准备好迎接新的挑战。
The company has poised itself for expansion into new markets. 这家公司已经准备好进军新市场。


过去完成时:
He had poised himself to jump over the hurdle. 他已经做好了越过障碍的准备。
She had poised the vase carefully on the mantelpiece. 她小心地把花瓶放在壁炉台上。


将来时:
He will poise himself for the upcoming interview. 他将为即将到来的面试做好准备。
She will poise the sculpture on its pedestal. 她将会把雕塑放在底座上。


虚拟语气:
If she had poised herself better, she would have avoided the fall. 如果她当时姿势更好，她就不会摔倒了。
If he had poised the glass more carefully, it wouldn't have broken. 如果他更小心地摆放玻璃杯，它就不会碎了。


被动语态:
The camera was poised perfectly. 相机摆放得非常完美。
The argument was poised to escalate. 争论一触即发。


疑问句:
Is she poised for success? 她准备好成功了吗？

否定句:
