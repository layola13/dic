idealist:/aɪˈdiːəlɪst/| n. adj.|理想主义者；理想主义的|A person who believes in or pursues ideals, often unrealistically.  Someone who is guided more by ideals than by practical considerations.|例句：He's a committed idealist, always striving for a better world. 他是一位坚定不移的理想主义者，总是努力创造一个更美好的世界。

例句：Her idealistic views on politics often clash with reality. 她对政治的理想主义观点常常与现实相冲突。

近义词：visionary, dreamer, utopian|反义词：realist, pragmatist|名词，形容词

词源:
The word "idealist" comes from the word "ideal," which itself derives from the Greek word "idealis," meaning "of or relating to ideas."  The "-ist" suffix indicates a person who believes in or practices something.  Therefore, an idealist is someone who is guided by ideals.
