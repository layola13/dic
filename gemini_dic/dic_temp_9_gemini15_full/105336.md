kick-off:/ˈkɪk.ɒf/| n., v.|开球；开始；启动|

英文解析:As a noun, "kick-off" refers to the action of kicking a ball to start a game, especially in football (soccer) or American football.  It can also refer to the beginning or start of something, an event, or a process. As a verb, "kick off" means to start something, often in a forceful or energetic way.


例句(Noun):

The kick-off was delayed due to heavy rain. = 由于大雨，开球被推迟了。

The kick-off of the project is scheduled for next Monday. = 该项目的启动计划在下周一。


例句(Verb):

The speaker kicked off the conference with an inspiring speech. = 演讲者以一篇鼓舞人心的演讲开始了会议。

They kicked off their campaign with a series of rallies across the country. = 他们以在全国各地举行一系列集会的方式启动了他们的竞选活动。


近义词: (Noun) commencement, start, beginning, inauguration; (Verb) begin, start, initiate, launch


反义词: (Noun) conclusion, end, finish; (Verb) conclude, end, terminate


词性: Noun, Verb
