defibrillating: /dɪˈfɪbrɪleɪtɪŋ/ | v. | 除颤的；电击除颤的 | The act of using a defibrillator to deliver an electric shock to the heart in order to restore a normal heartbeat.  | Example: The paramedics were defibrillating the patient after he went into cardiac arrest. 急救人员在他心脏骤停后正在对他进行电击除颤。 | 近义词：electroshocking, shocking (in this context) | 反义词：None specifically, but the opposite would be allowing the abnormal heartbeat to continue.
