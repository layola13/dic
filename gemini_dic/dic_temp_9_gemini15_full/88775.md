hodge-podge:/hɒdʒpɒdʒ/ | /ˈhɑːdʒpɑːdʒ/| n. |大杂烩；混合物 |A hodgepodge is a mixture of different things that are not related to each other.  It often implies a disorganized or haphazard collection. |例句：His essay was a hodge-podge of unrelated ideas. 他的文章是一堆毫无关联的想法的大杂烩。

The presentation was a hodge-podge of slides, videos, and random facts.  这次演示文稿是幻灯片、视频和随机事实的大杂烩。|近义词：mishmash, medley, jumble, gallimaufry |反义词：order, organization, system, uniformity


USAGE->
This word is a noun, and thus does not conjugate through verb tenses.  It can be modified by adjectives, however.

Example using adjectives:

A delicious hodgepodge of flavors. = 一种美味的各种风味混合物。
An interesting hodgepodge of styles. = 一种有趣的各种风格混合物。


PHRASE->
hodge-podge of ideas =  各种各样的想法

a hodge-podge of information =  各种各样的信息

词源:

The word "hodgepodge" is believed to have originated in the 16th century.  Its etymology is uncertain, but the most widely accepted theory suggests it's a combination of  "hodge" (a variant of "hodge-podge" itself, possibly referring to a type of stew or a mixed bag of things) and "podge", meaning a thick, soft substance or a clumsy person.  The term evolved to describe a haphazard mixture, reflecting the chaotic nature of its supposed origins.  The word's evolution illustrates the dynamic and often unpredictable nature of language development.
