pragma:/ˈpræɡmə/| n.|实用程序指令；编译器指令|A pragma is a compiler directive that provides information to the compiler without affecting the meaning of the program. Pragmas are used to optimize code, control compiler behavior, or provide platform-specific information.|Example: #pragma once (in C++) prevents a header file from being included more than once.  This improves compilation speed and avoids potential errors.|近义词：directive, instruction|反义词：N/A


USAGE->
N/A  (Pragmas are compiler directives, not words with grammatical tenses or voices.)


PHRASE->
#pragma once =  防止头文件重复包含 (C++)
#pragma warning(disable : 4996) = 禁用特定警告 (C++)
#pragma comment(lib, "mylib.lib") =  链接库文件 (C++)
#pragma pack(push, 1) = 设置结构体成员对齐方式 (C++)
#pragma message("This is a message") = 在编译时输出消息 (C++)
