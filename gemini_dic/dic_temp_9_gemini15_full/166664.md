preserver:/prɪˈzɜːrvər/| n. | 保护者；保存者；果酱；蜜饯 | A preserver is someone or something that protects or keeps something safe.  It can also refer to a sweet food made from preserved fruit. | Example: The museum is a preserver of history.  博物馆是历史的守护者。  |近义词：protector, conservator, keeper |反义词：destroyer, spoiler


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The museum preserves historical artifacts. = 博物馆保存着历史文物。
He preserves his health by exercising regularly. = 他通过定期锻炼来保持健康。

一般过去时:
They preserved the old building from demolition. = 他们保护了那座旧建筑免遭拆除。
She preserved the fruit by canning it. = 她通过罐装来保存水果。

现在完成时:
We have preserved this tradition for generations. = 我们世世代代都传承着这一传统。
The company has preserved its market share despite the competition. = 尽管面临竞争，该公司仍保持了其市场份额。

过去完成时:
They had preserved the documents before the fire broke out. = 大火发生之前，他们已经保存好了文件。
She had preserved the food for a long journey. = 她为长途旅行保存了食物。

将来时:
We will preserve this land for future generations. = 我们将为子孙后代保护这片土地。
They will preserve the evidence for the trial. = 他们将为审判保留证据。

虚拟语气:
If we had preserved the environment better, we wouldn't be facing this crisis. = 如果我们更好地保护环境，就不会面临这场危机。
I wish I had preserved my old photos. = 我真希望我保存了我的旧照片。

被动语态:
The old house was preserved by the local council. = 这座老房子被当地议会保存下来。
The documents are being preserved in a secure archive. = 文件保存在安全的档案馆中。

疑问句:
How do we preserve our cultural heritage? = 我们如何保护我们的文化遗产？
What methods are used to preserve food? = 用什么方法保存食物？

否定句:
They did not preserve the evidence properly. = 他们没有妥善保存证据。
The company failed to preserve its reputation after the scandal. = 丑闻发生后，该公司未能维护其声誉。
