hyperromantics: /ˌhaɪpərroʊˈmæntɪks/ | n. | 超级浪漫主义者；后期浪漫主义者 |  Hyperromantics refers to a group of artists and writers, primarily active in the late 19th and early 20th centuries, whose work was characterized by an intensification and sometimes a distortion of Romantic ideals.  They often explored themes of intense emotion, the sublime, and the irrational, but with a greater degree of self-consciousness and a more complex psychological approach than earlier Romantic artists. |  The paintings of the hyperromantics often featured dramatic landscapes and intense emotional portrayals. | 近义词: Late Romantics, Symbolists | 反义词: Classicists, Realists | 名词
