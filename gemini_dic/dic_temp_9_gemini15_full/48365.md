decurion:/dɪˈkʊəriən/| n. |罗马军队百人队长|A decurion was a Roman army officer who commanded a contubernium (a group of ten men) or, in some contexts, a century (a group of 100 men).  The term's meaning varied slightly depending on the period and branch of the Roman military.|Example: The decurion led his men into battle with courage and skill.  罗马百人队长勇敢而熟练地带领他的士兵们冲锋陷阵。|近义词:centurion (often used interchangeably, but a centurion commanded a century, while a decurion's command size varied)|反义词:legionary (a common soldier), private|
