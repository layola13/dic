opening:/ˈoʊpənɪŋ/| /ˈəʊpənɪŋ/ | n. adj. | 开幕式；开始；空缺；开头；开场白 |The act of opening something; the beginning of something; a vacant position or opportunity; an introductory part or section.|例句：The opening ceremony of the Olympics was spectacular.  奥运会的开幕式非常壮观。|例句：The opening chapter sets the tone for the entire novel.  第一章奠定了整部小说的基调。|近义词：beginning, commencement, inauguration, vacancy |反义词：closing, conclusion, end


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The opening of the new store is exciting. = 新店的开业令人兴奋。
This position offers a great opening for a young professional. = 这个职位为年轻的专业人士提供了很好的机会。

一般过去时:
The opening of the play was dramatic. = 这出戏的开场很戏剧化。
The company announced several new job openings last week. = 公司上周宣布了几个新的职位空缺。

现在完成时:

Many openings have been filled since the recruitment fair.  招聘会以来，许多空缺职位已经填补。

过去完成时:

将来时:
The museum will have its grand opening next month. = 博物馆将于下个月隆重开幕。

虚拟语气:

被动语态:
