cold-turkey: /ˈkoʊld ˈtɜːrki/ | n. |戒毒，突然戒除（坏习惯）|  The abrupt cessation of the use of a drug or other addictive substance.  It often refers to quitting something abruptly without any gradual tapering off.  The term's origin is uncertain but may relate to the shaking and shivering associated with withdrawal symptoms, likened to the coldness of a raw turkey.  |例句：He went cold turkey on cigarettes. 他突然戒了烟。


He quit smoking cold turkey. 他一下子戒了烟。


近义词：abrupt cessation, sudden withdrawal | 反义词：gradual withdrawal, tapering off
