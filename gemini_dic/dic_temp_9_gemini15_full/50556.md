deputises:/ˈdɛpjʊtaɪzɪz/| v.|代理；代表；暂时替代|To act or speak on behalf of someone else, especially in their absence.|例句：The manager deputised for the CEO at the conference.经理在会议上代表首席执行官。|例句：While the teacher was ill, another teacher deputised for her.老师生病期间，另一位老师代课。|近义词：represent, substitute, stand in for|反义词：None specifically, but the antonym would be the opposite of acting on behalf of someone else, such as "acting independently" or "acting solely on one's own behalf".|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He deputises for the manager when she is away. = 他经理不在时代理经理职务。
She regularly deputises for her colleague on Fridays. = 她经常在星期五代替她的同事。


一般过去时:
He deputised for his boss during the meeting. = 他在会议上代替他的老板。
She deputised for the head teacher while he was on leave. = 校长休假期间，她代理校长职务。


现在完成时:
He has deputised for the CEO on several occasions. = 他曾多次代理首席执行官。
She has deputised for her supervisor many times this year. = 今年她已经多次代理她的主管。


过去完成时:
He had deputised for the chairman before the crisis hit. = 危机爆发前，他曾代理过董事长。
She had deputised for her colleague on numerous occasions in the past. = 她过去多次代理过她的同事。


将来时:
He will deputise for the director next week. = 他下周将代理主任。
She will deputise for the team leader during their absence. = 他们的领导不在时，她将代理组长。


虚拟语气:
If the president were unavailable, the vice-president would deputise. = 如果总统无法出席，副总统将代理。
I wish someone would deputise for me at the meeting. = 我希望有人能在会议上代表我。


被动语态:
He was deputised to represent the company at the negotiations. = 他被委派代表公司参加谈判。
She was deputised to lead the team in his absence. = 他不在的时候，她被委派领导团队。


疑问句:
Will someone deputise for me at the meeting? = 会有人在会议上代表我吗？
Who deputised for the manager yesterday? = 昨天谁代理了经理的职务？


否定句:
He did not deputise for the manager; he simply covered for her. = 他没有代理经理，只是暂时顶替了她。
She would not deputise for him, as she had too much work of her own. = 她不会代替他，因为她自己的工作太多了。
