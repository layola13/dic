precasting: /ˈpriːkæstɪŋ/ | n. | 预制；预制构件 | The process of manufacturing components of a structure, such as building elements or infrastructure components, off-site, prior to their incorporation into the final structure.  This method is often used to improve efficiency and reduce construction time. | Example: The bridge was built using precasting techniques, allowing for faster completion.  这座桥采用预制技术建造，从而加快了完工速度。 | 近义词：prefab, prefabrication | 反义词：on-site construction
