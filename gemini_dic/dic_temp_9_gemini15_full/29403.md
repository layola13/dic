cataclasm:/ˈkætəkæzəm/| n.|大灾难；剧变；崩溃|A cataclysm is a sudden and violent change or event that causes great destruction or suffering.  It often refers to a large-scale disaster, like a flood, earthquake, or war, but it can also refer to a sudden and drastic change in a system or society.|例句：The earthquake was a cataclysm that devastated the city.  这场地震是一场摧毁了这座城市的巨大灾难。|例句：The political cataclysm led to widespread instability.  这场政治巨变导致了广泛的不稳定。|近义词：catastrophe, disaster, calamity, upheaval|反义词：calm, peace, stability|名词
