echinodermatous:/ˌɛkɪnəʊˈdɜːrmətəs/| adj. |棘皮动物的；似棘皮动物的 | Relating to or characteristic of echinoderms.  The term refers to the characteristics of echinoderms, a phylum of marine invertebrates including starfish, sea urchins, and sea cucumbers. These characteristics often include radial symmetry, a spiny or bumpy skin, and a water vascular system. |Example:  The echinodermatous specimen displayed the characteristic five-point radial symmetry.  棘皮动物标本显示出特征性的五点放射对称性。|近义词：spiny, radially symmetrical|反义词：non-echinodermatous
