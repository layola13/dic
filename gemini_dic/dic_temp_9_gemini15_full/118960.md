meteors:/ˈmiːtiɔːrz/ | n. |流星；陨星 |  Meteors are small bodies of rock or metal that burn up as they enter the Earth's atmosphere, creating streaks of light.  They are often called "shooting stars." | Example: Last night, we saw several bright meteors streaking across the sky.  昨晚，我们看到几颗明亮的流星划过天空。  |近义词：shooting stars, meteoroids (before entering atmosphere) | 反义词： N/A
