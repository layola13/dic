cannibalism:/ˈkænɪbəlɪzəm/| n.|食人族习俗；同类相食|The practice of humans eating the flesh of other humans.  This can be ritualistic, survival-based, or a result of mental illness.|例句：The evidence suggests cannibalism was practiced by some ancient tribes.  证据表明一些古代部落曾实行食人习俗。

例句：The anthropologist studied the evidence of cannibalism in the ancient ruins. 人类学家研究了古代遗址中食人行为的证据。


近义词：anthropophagy|反义词：vegetarianism

词源:
From the Spanish word *caníbal*, which originated from the Carib people, an indigenous group from the Caribbean islands known for their practice of cannibalism.  The word ultimately derives from the Taíno language.
