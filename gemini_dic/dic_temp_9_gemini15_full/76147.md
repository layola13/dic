fuselage:/ˈfjuːzəlɑːʒ/ | n. |机身，机舱|The main body of an aircraft, excluding the wings and tail unit.  It houses the crew, passengers, and cargo.|例句：The plane's fuselage was severely damaged in the crash.  飞机的机身在坠机事故中严重受损。

例句：The engineers inspected the fuselage for any signs of structural weakness. 工程师们检查了机身是否有任何结构薄弱的迹象。


近义词：body, airframe | 反义词：N/A | 词性：名词


Etymology:  The word "fuselage" comes from the French word *fuselage*, which is a combination of *fuseau* ("spindle") and the suffix *-age* ("action or result of").  The original meaning referenced the spindle-like shape of early aircraft bodies.  It entered English in the early 20th century with the advent of aviation.
