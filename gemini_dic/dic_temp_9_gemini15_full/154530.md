perpetuates:/pərˈpetʃuˌeɪts/| vt. |使永久存在；使继续；使不朽|To cause something to continue.  It often implies a negative connotation, suggesting the continuation of something undesirable or harmful.|The government's policies perpetuate inequality. |近义词：continue, prolong, maintain|反义词：terminate, end, abolish


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He perpetuates the myth. = 他延续了这个神话。
This system perpetuates poverty. = 这个制度使贫困延续下去。

一般过去时:
The war perpetuated hatred between the two countries. = 那场战争使两国之间的仇恨延续下去。
His actions perpetuated the cycle of violence. = 他的行为使暴力循环持续下去。

现在完成时:
Their actions have perpetuated the problem for years. = 他们的行为多年来一直使这个问题持续下去。
This tradition has perpetuated itself through generations. = 这个传统世世代代延续下来。

过去完成时:
The dictator's regime had perpetuated oppression for decades. = 独裁者的政权几十年来一直维持着压迫。
They had perpetuated a false narrative for years. = 他们多年来一直散布虚假说法。

将来时:
This policy will perpetuate the existing inequalities. = 这一政策将使现有的不平等持续下去。
Ignoring the problem will only perpetuate the crisis. = 忽视问题只会使危机持续下去。


被动语态:
The myth is perpetuated by many people. = 这个神话被许多人延续着。
Inequality is perpetuated by systemic issues. = 不平等是由系统性问题造成的。

疑问句:
Does this policy perpetuate discrimination? = 这一政策是否会延续歧视？
What perpetuates these harmful stereotypes? = 什么使这些有害的刻板印象持续存在？

否定句:
We must not perpetuate harmful stereotypes. = 我们决不能延续有害的刻板印象。
The new law aims to prevent perpetuating injustice. = 新法律旨在防止延续不公正。


PHRASE->

perpetuate injustice = 使不公正永久化
perpetuate a lie = 延续谎言
