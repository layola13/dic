shifts:/ʃɪfts/ | n., v. |轮班；转移；转变；移动 |

英文解析:
As a noun, "shift" refers to a period of work in a place of employment, especially one where people work different hours in turns.  It can also mean a change in position, direction, or tendency. As a verb, "shift" means to move or change something, or to change from one thing to another.

例句:
My work shift starts at 8 am. = 我的工作轮班从早上8点开始。

The wind shifted from north to south. = 风从北风转向南风。


近义词:
(n.) rotation, change, alteration, transition, movement
(v.) move, change, transfer, alter, switch


反义词:
(n.) stability, constancy, permanence
(v.) remain, stay, keep


词性:
Noun, Verb


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The earth shifts on its axis. = 地球绕其轴线旋转。
He shifts his weight from one foot to the other. = 他把体重从一只脚移到另一只脚。


一般过去时:
The company shifted its focus to online sales. = 公司将重点转移到网上销售。
She shifted the blame onto her colleague. = 她把责任推到了同事身上。


现在完成时:
The tectonic plates have shifted significantly. = 构造板块已经发生了显著的移动。
The political landscape has shifted dramatically in recent years. = 近年来，政治格局发生了巨大变化。


过去完成时:
By the time I arrived, the mood had already shifted. = 当我到达时，气氛已经变了。
They had shifted their resources to the new project. = 他们已经将资源转移到新的项目上了。


将来时:
The company will shift its headquarters to a new city. = 公司将把总部迁到一个新的城市。
We will shift our focus to customer satisfaction. = 我们将把重点放在客户满意度上。


虚拟语气:
If the wind shifted, the boat would capsize. = 如果风向改变，船就会倾覆。
I wish the government would shift its priorities. = 我希望政府能改变其优先事项。


被动语态:
The furniture was shifted to the new house. = 家具被搬到了新房子里。
The blame was shifted onto the junior employee. = 责任被推到了初级员工身上。


疑问句:
Does the schedule shift often? = 时间表经常变动吗？
Will they shift the meeting to a later time? = 他们会把会议改到稍后的时间吗？


否定句:
The policy hasn't shifted at all. = 政策根本没有改变。
He didn't shift his position on the issue. = 他在这个问题上的立场没有改变。
