over-emoting: /ˌoʊvərˈɪˈmoʊtɪŋ/ | v. |过度表达情感；感情用事 | The act of expressing emotions too intensely or excessively.  It often involves exaggerating reactions or displaying feelings disproportionate to the situation. | Example:  He was over-emoting during the presentation, making it difficult for the audience to focus on his message. 他在演示过程中过度表达情感，使得观众难以集中注意力在他的信息上。| 近义词：overreacting, melodramatic, histrionic | 反义词：understating, stoicism, composure |


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He over-emotes in most situations. = 他在大多数情况下都过度表达情感。
She over-emotes when she talks about her childhood. = 她谈论童年时会过度表达情感。

一般过去时:
He over-emoted during the interview. = 他在面试中过度表达情感。
She over-emoted at the funeral. = 她在葬礼上过度表达情感。

现在完成时:
He has over-emoted throughout the entire meeting. = 他在整个会议上都过度表达情感。
She has over-emoted since the accident. = 自从事故发生以来，她一直过度表达情感。

过去完成时:
He had over-emoted before he realized the seriousness of the situation. = 他在意识到情况的严重性之前就已经过度表达情感了。
She had over-emoted throughout the entire movie. = 她在整部电影中都过度表达情感。

将来时:

虚拟语气:
If he hadn't over-emoted, the situation would have been less tense. = 如果他不那么情绪化，情况就不会那么紧张。
I wish she hadn't over-emoted during the speech. = 我希望她在演讲中不要过度表达情感。

被动语态:

疑问句:
Is he over-emoting, or is he genuinely upset? = 他是在过度表达情感，还是真的很难过？
Was she over-emoting during her performance? = 她表演时是在过度表达情感吗？


否定句:
He didn't over-emote; he remained calm and collected. = 他并没有过度表达情感；他保持冷静和镇定。
She didn't over-emote at all, despite the difficult circumstances. = 尽管情况艰难，她一点也没有过度表达情感。
