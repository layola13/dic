caron:/ˈkærən/| n. |抑扬音符，倒钩|A diacritical mark placed above a letter, typically in Slavic languages, to indicate a change in pronunciation.|The word "caron" itself is derived from the Czech word "háček", meaning "little hook".|The caron is used in many Slavic languages, such as Czech, Slovak, and Croatian, as well as in some other languages like Vietnamese.|  The caron modifies the pronunciation of the letter it is placed above. For example, in Czech, the letter "č" (c with a caron) is pronounced differently from the letter "c".|Example: The letter "š" in Czech uses a caron to indicate a different sound than a simple "s".|近义词：háček (Czech), breve|反义词：无
