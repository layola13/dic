drifting:/ˈdrɪftɪŋ/ | v. |漂流；漂移；漫无目的的移动 |The meaning of "drifting" implies a lack of control or direction, a movement influenced by external forces rather than deliberate intention.  It can refer to physical movement (like a boat on water) or metaphorical movement (like a conversation or someone's life).|例句：The boat was drifting aimlessly on the ocean. 小船在海洋上漫无目的地漂流。

The conversation drifted towards politics.  谈话逐渐转向政治。|近义词：wandering, floating, meandering, roving |反义词：directing, steering, controlling, guiding |动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The leaves are drifting down from the trees. 叶子从树上飘落下来。
He is drifting in and out of consciousness. 他时而清醒时而昏迷。


一般过去时:
The ship drifted onto the rocks. 船漂流到礁石上。
She drifted off to sleep during the lecture. 她在讲座期间睡着了。


现在完成时:
The ice floes have been drifting south all winter.  冰块整个冬天都在向南漂流。
We have been drifting apart since we graduated.  自从我们毕业后，我们疏远了。


过去完成时:
They had been drifting for days before they were rescued.  他们在获救前已经漂流了好几天了。
The project had been drifting along without clear direction.  这个项目一直在漫无目的地进行，缺乏明确的方向。


将来时:
The balloon will drift away with the wind. 气球将随风飘走。
I fear he will drift further into depression. 我担心他会进一步陷入抑郁。


虚拟语气:
If the boat had not drifted, they would have reached the shore safely. 如果船没有漂流，他们就能安全到达岸边。
I wish I hadn't drifted so far from my goals. 我真希望我没有偏离我的目标那么远。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
