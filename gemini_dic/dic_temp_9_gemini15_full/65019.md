estuary:/ˈɛstuˌɛri/| n. |河口；河口湾 |A partially enclosed coastal body of brackish water with one or more rivers or streams flowing into it, and with a free connection to the open sea.  Estuaries are dynamic environments where freshwater from rivers mixes with saltwater from the ocean, creating a unique ecosystem. |例句：The Thames Estuary is a vital part of London's history and economy. 泰晤士河口是伦敦历史和经济的重要组成部分。The estuary is home to a wide variety of birdlife.  河口是各种鸟类的家园。|近义词：inlet, bay, mouth (of a river) |反义词：None specifically, but concepts like "open ocean" or "upstream" offer contrast.
