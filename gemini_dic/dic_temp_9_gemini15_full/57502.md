draging:/ˈdræɡɪŋ/| v.|拖拉；拖拽；使缓慢移动|The act of pulling something along a surface.  It often implies difficulty or slowness.|例句：He was dragging his feet on the project. 他在项目上拖拖拉拉。|例句：The dog dragged its leash behind it. 狗拖着它的绳子在后面。|近义词：pulling, hauling, towing|反义词：pushing, propelling


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They are dragging the heavy box across the floor. = 他们正在把沉重的箱子拖过地板。
He drags his feet when it comes to doing his homework. =  说到做作业，他总是拖拖拉拉。


一般过去时:
She dragged the suitcase through the airport. = 她把行李箱拖过机场。
We dragged the boat to the shore. = 我们把船拖到岸边。


现在完成时:
They have dragged the furniture into the new house. = 他们已经把家具拖进了新房子。
He has been dragging his feet on the project for weeks. = 他几周来在这个项目上一直拖拖拉拉。


过去完成时:
She had dragged the injured man to safety before help arrived. = 在救援到来之前，她已经把受伤的人拖到了安全的地方。
They had dragged the net for hours before catching anything. = 他们在捕到任何东西之前已经拖网几个小时了。


将来时:
They will drag the logs to the sawmill tomorrow. = 他们明天要把原木拖到锯木厂。
He will drag himself out of bed tomorrow morning. = 明天早上他会勉强起床。


虚拟语气:
If I dragged the chair closer, I could reach the book. = 如果我把椅子拉近一些，我就能拿到书了。
I wish I hadn't dragged my feet on that decision. = 我真希望我当初不要在那个决定上拖拖拉拉。


被动语态:
The heavy box was dragged across the floor. = 沉重的箱子被拖过地板。
The boat was dragged to the shore by the strong current. = 船被强劲的水流拖到了岸边。


疑问句:
Are they dragging the heavy object? = 他们正在拖那个沉重的东西吗？
Is he dragging his feet again? = 他又在拖延了吗？


否定句:
They didn't drag the furniture until the next day. = 他们直到第二天才拖动家具。
He didn't drag himself out of bed this morning. = 他今天早上没有勉强起床。


PHRASE->
drag on = 拖延；持续
drag someone down = 使某人沮丧；拖累某人
drag oneself = 勉强；费力地
drag something out = 延长；拖延
drag race = 直线加速赛
