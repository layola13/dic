/kɑːtəˈnɑːz/ | n. | 片假名 | Katakana is a Japanese syllabary, one of the two main scripts used to write Japanese, alongside hiragana.  It is primarily used to represent foreign words and loanwords, onomatopoeia, and sometimes for emphasis or stylistic effect. | Example:  Many English words are written in katakana in Japanese.  许多英语单词在日语中是用片假名写的。|近义词: hiragana (but with different usage)|反义词:  N/A (not a concept with direct opposites)
