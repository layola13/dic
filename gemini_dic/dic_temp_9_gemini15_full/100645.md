intolerants: /ɪnˈtɑːlərənts/ | adj. | 不容忍的，偏狭的 |  Lacking tolerance; unwilling to accept different views or behavior.  |例句：His intolerant attitude towards other cultures is unacceptable.  他的对其他文化的偏见态度是不可接受的。|例句：Intolerant people often create conflict. 不容忍的人常常制造冲突。|近义词：bigoted, prejudiced, narrow-minded |反义词：tolerant, open-minded, broad-minded |形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is intolerant of criticism. = 他不能容忍批评。
She is intolerant of other people's opinions. = 她不能容忍别人的意见。

一般过去时:
They were intolerant of dissenting views. = 他们不容忍异议。
He was intolerant of noise. = 他受不了噪音。

现在完成时:
She has been intolerant of my lifestyle for years. = 多年来她一直不能容忍我的生活方式。
We have become more intolerant of injustice. = 我们变得更加不能容忍不公正。


过去完成时:
They had been intolerant of his behavior for a long time. = 他们早就不能容忍他的行为。
I had been intolerant of the heat before the rain came. = 下雨之前我一直受不了这热。

将来时:
I will be intolerant of any further delays. = 我将不容忍任何进一步的延误。
They will be intolerant of anyone who disagrees with them. = 他们将不容忍任何与他们意见相左的人。

虚拟语气:
If I were less intolerant, I would have more friends. = 如果我不那么不容忍，我就会有更多朋友。
I wish I weren't so intolerant of others' flaws. = 我希望我不那么不能容忍别人的缺点。

被动语态:
His opinions are often seen as intolerant. = 他的观点常常被认为是偏狭的。
His behavior was considered intolerant by many. = 许多人认为他的行为是不能容忍的。

疑问句:
Is he intolerant of different religions? = 他对不同的宗教是否不容忍？
Are you intolerant of spicy food? = 你不能吃辣的食物吗？

否定句:
He is not intolerant of new ideas. = 他并不排斥新思想。
She is not intolerant of criticism, she uses it to improve. = 她并不排斥批评，她用批评来改进自己。
