precursive:/prɪˈkɜːrsɪv/| adj.|预兆的；先行的；先驱的 |  Relating to or denoting something that precedes and indicates or announces something else.  It suggests something that comes before and foreshadows a later event or development. |例句：The unusually warm weather in February was precursive of an early spring.  二月异常温暖的天气预示着早春的到来。

例句：The rapid economic growth in the 1990s was precursive of a period of intense financial speculation. 90年代的经济快速增长预示着随后一段时间的激烈金融投机。

近义词：antecedent, preliminary, preceding, foreshadowing

反义词：subsequent, following, consequent
