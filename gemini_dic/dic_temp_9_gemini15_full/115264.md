manipulus: /məˈnɪpjʊləs/ | n. | 手握；一小撮；一小队 | A manipulus was a basic military unit in the Roman army, typically comprising about 60 to 120 soldiers.  The term also refers to a small handful or group of something. | Example:  The Roman legion was divided into several manipuli. 罗马军团被分成几个小队。  He held a manipulus of grain in his hand. 他手里握着一小撮谷物。 | 近义词：handful, group, squad | 反义词：army, legion, multitude
