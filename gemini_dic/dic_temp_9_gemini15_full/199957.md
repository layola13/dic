skald:/skɑːld/ | n. |吟游诗人，吟诵者 | A skald was a Norse poet, traditionally one attached to a king's court, who composed and recited poems, often praising the deeds of their patron. Skaldic poetry is characterized by its complex metrical forms and use of kennings (metaphorical compounds).|Example: The skald sang a stirring ballad of the king's victory.  吟游诗人唱了一首关于国王胜利的激动人心的歌谣。|近义词：bard, poet, singer |反义词：None specifically applicable.
