mimic's: /ˈmɪmɪks/ | n. | 模仿者(的)；模仿(的) |  The word refers to the act of imitating someone or something, often in a humorous or satirical way. It can be a noun (referring to a person who mimics) or a possessive noun (showing ownership or relationship, like "the mimic's act"). | 例句：The comedian's mimicry of the politician was hilarious.  喜剧演员对这位政治家的模仿非常滑稽。


The child's mimic's skill was amazing. 孩子模仿的技巧令人惊叹。| 近义词：imitator, impersonator, copycat | 反义词：original, innovator

词源:
