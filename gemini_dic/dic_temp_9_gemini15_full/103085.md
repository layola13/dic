jeopardise:/ˈdʒepədaɪz/ | vt. | 危及；使陷于危险 | To put something or someone at risk of being harmed, damaged, or destroyed.  |例句：His reckless driving jeopardised the lives of others. 他鲁莽的驾驶危及了其他人的生命。|例句：The company's reputation was jeopardised by the scandal. 公司的声誉因丑闻而受损。|近义词：endanger, threaten, imperil, risk |反义词：protect, safeguard, secure


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He jeopardizes his health by smoking. = 他吸烟危害了他的健康。
The new policy jeopardizes the company's future. = 新政策危及公司的未来。

一般过去时:
The storm jeopardised the harvest. = 暴风雨危及了收成。
His careless mistake jeopardised the project. = 他粗心的错误危及了项目。

现在完成时:
His actions have jeopardised the team's chances of winning. = 他的行为已经危及了团队获胜的机会。
The scandal has jeopardised the politician's career. = 丑闻已经危及了这位政治家的职业生涯。

过去完成时:
They had jeopardised their position by making that decision. = 他们因做出那个决定而危及了自己的地位。
The delay had jeopardised the entire project's timeline. = 延误已经危及了整个项目的进度。


将来时:
The upcoming storm will jeopardise the outdoor concert. = 即将来临的风暴将危及户外音乐会。
Further delays will jeopardise the project's completion. = 进一步的延误将危及项目的完成。

虚拟语气:
If he continued to drink, he would jeopardise his health. = 如果他继续喝酒，他会危及自己的健康。
If they had acted sooner, they would not have jeopardised the deal. = 如果他们早点行动，就不会危及这笔交易了。

被动语态:
The project was jeopardised by the lack of funding. = 项目因缺乏资金而受损。
His reputation was jeopardised by the false accusations. = 他的名誉因不实的指控而受损。

疑问句:
Does this decision jeopardise our chances of success? = 这个决定是否会危及我们成功的几率？
Could this policy jeopardise the environment? = 这一政策会危及环境吗？

否定句:
The company took steps to ensure that the project was not jeopardised. = 公司采取措施确保项目不受损害。
He tried to avoid actions that might jeopardise his future. = 他试图避免可能危及他未来的行为。
