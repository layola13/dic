requisitive:/rɪˈkwɪzɪtɪv/ | adj. | 必需的；必要的 |  Something that is requisitive is required or necessary.  It emphasizes the essential nature of something. | The requisitive skills for the job include proficiency in coding and project management. 这份工作必需的技能包括熟练的编码和项目管理能力。 | necessary, essential, required | unnecessary, optional, superfluous | 形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The job is requisitive of strong communication skills. = 这份工作需要很强的沟通能力。
This project is requisitive for our company's success. = 这个项目对我们公司的成功至关重要。


一般过去时:
The previous role was requisitive of a master's degree. = 之前的职位需要硕士学位。
The old system was requisitive for maintaining security. = 旧系统对于维护安全是必需的。


现在完成时:
The changes have made certain skills requisitive. = 这些变化使得某些技能变得必不可少。
The new regulations have made this procedure requisitive. = 新规定使这一程序成为必需。


过去完成时:

The project had made several additional skills requisitive before completion. = 在项目完成之前，项目已经需要几项额外的技能。


将来时:

Future developments will make this technology requisitive for progress. = 未来的发展将使这项技术成为进步的必要条件。


虚拟语气:

Were the project to succeed, further funding would be requisitive. = 如果项目要成功，就需要进一步的资金。


被动语态:
Strong communication skills are requisitive for this position. = 这份职位需要很强的沟通能力。
A high level of experience is requisitive for this role. = 这个职位需要高水平的经验。


疑问句:

否定句:

Prior experience is not requisitive, but it is preferred. = 之前的经验不是必需的，但更受欢迎。


PHRASE->

词源:
From Late Latin *requisitivus, from requirere "to demand, require" (from re- "back, again" + quaerere "to seek").  The word reflects the idea of something being demanded or needed.
