sensitises: /ˈsɛnsɪtaɪzɪz/ | vt. | 使敏感；使过敏；使感光 | To make someone or something more aware of something, or more likely to react to something.  |  例句：The campaign sensitised the public to the issue of climate change.  这场运动使公众更加关注气候变化问题。

例句：Exposure to sunlight sensitises the skin.  阳光照射会使皮肤过敏。


近义词：aware, alert, responsive | 反义词：desensitise, numb


词性：动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The constant noise sensitises my ears. = 持续的噪音使我的耳朵变得敏感。
This training program sensitises employees to diversity issues. = 这项培训计划使员工更加关注多元化问题。


一般过去时:
The accident sensitised him to the dangers of speeding. = 那次事故使他意识到超速的危险。
The experience sensitised her to the needs of the homeless. = 这段经历使她体会到无家可归者的需求。


现在完成时:
Living in a big city has sensitised me to the sounds of traffic. = 住在大城市使我习惯了交通噪音。
Years of working with children have sensitised her to their emotional needs. = 多年与孩子们一起工作使她对他们的情感需求更加敏感。


过去完成时:
He had sensitised himself to the cultural differences before his trip. = 在旅行之前，他已经让自己对文化差异更加敏感。
She had sensitised her students to the importance of critical thinking. = 她已经使她的学生认识到批判性思维的重要性。


将来时:
This new technology will sensitise doctors to early signs of the disease. = 这项新技术将使医生能够及早发现疾病的早期征兆。
The upcoming campaign will sensitise the public to the dangers of drug abuse. = 即将来临的宣传活动将使公众意识到吸毒的危害。


虚拟语气:
If we sensitised the public more, we might see more action on climate change. = 如果我们让公众更加关注这个问题，我们可能会看到更多关于气候变化的行动。
I wish they had sensitised us to the risks involved earlier. = 我希望他们早点让我们意识到其中的风险。


被动语态:
The public was sensitised to the importance of voting. = 公众被教育重视投票的重要性。
The workers were sensitised to the safety regulations. = 工人们被告知安全规章。


疑问句:
What can we do to sensitise young people to environmental issues? = 我们能做些什么来提高年轻人对环境问题的认识？
Has the media sensitised the public sufficiently to this problem? = 媒体是否已充分让公众了解这个问题？


否定句:
The campaign failed to sensitise the public to the issue. = 这场运动未能使公众关注这个问题。
He remained unsensitised to the plight of the refugees. = 他仍然对难民的困境漠不关心。
