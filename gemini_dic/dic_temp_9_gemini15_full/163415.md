preconstituting: /ˌpriːkənˈstɪtjuːtɪŋ/ | v. | 预先构成；预先制定 |  The act of establishing or forming something beforehand.  It implies creating something in advance of its actual use or need.  This often involves setting parameters, structures, or components ready for later assembly or implementation. |


例句：

The committee spent months preconstituting the agenda for the upcoming summit.  = 委员会花了几个月时间预先制定了即将召开的峰会的议程。

The chef preconstituted the sauce components to save time during the busy dinner service. = 厨师预先配置好酱汁的成分，以便在繁忙的晚餐服务期间节省时间。


近义词：prearranging, prefabricating, predetermining, presetting


反义词：improvising, extemporizing


词性：动词
