clammer:/ˈklæmər/ | v. |喧闹；吵闹；大声叫喊 |To make a loud, confused noise; to shout or cry noisily. |例句：The children clamoured for attention. 孩子们吵着要人注意。  The crowd clamoured for the president to speak. 人群大声要求总统讲话。|近义词： clamor, shout, yell, roar |反义词：whisper, murmur


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They clamour for change. = 他们大声疾呼要求改变。
The birds clamour in the trees. = 鸟儿在树上喧闹。

一般过去时:
The protesters clamoured outside the building. = 抗议者在建筑物外喧闹。
The audience clamoured for an encore. = 观众大声要求再来一首。

现在完成时:
They have clamoured for justice for years. = 他们多年来一直大声疾呼要求正义。
The fans have clamoured for his return to the team. = 粉丝们一直大声疾呼要求他回到球队。

过去完成时:
The crowd had clamoured for his resignation before the announcement. = 在宣布之前，人群就已大声疾呼要求他辞职。
They had clamoured for help, but no one came. = 他们大声呼救，但没有人来。

将来时:
The people will clamour for reforms. = 人民将大声疾呼要求改革。
They will clamour for a better future. = 他们将大声疾呼要求一个更美好的未来。

虚拟语气:
If they clamoured louder, maybe someone would listen. = 如果他们喊得更大声，也许有人会听。
I wish they wouldn't clamour so much. = 我希望他们不要吵闹那么多。

被动语态:
The reforms were clamoured for by the people. = 改革受到了人民的强烈要求。
His resignation was clamoured for by the public. = 公众强烈要求他辞职。

疑问句:
Were they clamouring for attention? = 他们在吵着要人注意吗？
Why are they clamouring so loudly? = 他们为什么吵得这么大声？

否定句:
The crowd did not clamour for a change. = 人群并没有大声疾呼要求改变。
They didn't clamour for his dismissal. = 他们没有大声疾呼要求解雇他。


PHRASE->

clamour to do sth = 吵着要做某事
a clamour of voices = 一片喧闹声
clamour and uproar = 喧嚣和骚动
