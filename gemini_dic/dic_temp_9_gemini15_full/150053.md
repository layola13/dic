palpitation:/ˌpælpiˈteɪʃən/ | n. | 心悸；心跳过速 | A palpitation is a noticeably rapid, strong, or irregular heartbeat.  It can be caused by a variety of factors, including stress, anxiety, caffeine, and underlying medical conditions. | Example:  She felt a sudden palpitation in her chest after hearing the bad news. 她听到坏消息后，感到胸口一阵心悸。  I experienced palpitations after running a marathon. 我跑完马拉松后出现了心悸。|近义词：fluttering, throbbing, rapid heartbeat | 反义词：calm heartbeat, steady pulse |  词性：名词
