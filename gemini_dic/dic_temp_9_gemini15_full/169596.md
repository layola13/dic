prophragma:/ˈprɒfˌræɡmə/| n. |预言；预兆；先例| A prophragma is a prophetic utterance or sign, often considered a precursor or omen of future events.  It can also refer to a precedent or established practice that serves as a guide for future actions.|Example: The strange weather patterns were seen as a prophragma of the coming storm.  奇怪的天气模式被视为即将到来的暴风雨的预兆。|近义词：omen, portent, prediction, precedent |反义词：accident, chance, coincidence |名词
