equivocation:/ɪˌkwɪvəˈkeɪʃən/ | n. |含糊其辞；模棱两可；支吾其词 | The use of ambiguous language to conceal the truth or to avoid committing oneself; a statement that is unclear or misleading because it can be understood in more than one way. | 例句：His equivocation on the issue cost him the election.  他的含糊其辞让他输掉了选举。

例句：The politician's answer was full of equivocation, leaving the audience confused.  这位政治家的回答充满了含糊其辞，让听众感到困惑。


近义词：ambiguity, vagueness, prevarication, obfuscation, circumlocution

反义词：clarity, frankness, directness, honesty
