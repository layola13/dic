non-didactic: /ˌnɒn.dɪˈdæktɪk/ | adj. |非说教的，非教诲的 | Not intended to teach or instruct; not preachy.  This word emphasizes the absence of a moralizing or instructional tone.  It suggests a presentation of information or ideas without overt attempts to guide the audience's beliefs or actions. | 例句：The novel offers a non-didactic exploration of human relationships. 这部小说对人际关系进行了非说教式的探讨。

The film takes a non-didactic approach, allowing the audience to draw their own conclusions.这部电影采用了一种非说教的方法，让观众自己得出结论。


近义词：unmoralizing, uninstructive, non-preachy, objective, impartial


反义词：didactic, preachy, moralistic, instructional, instructive


词源:  The word is formed by combining the prefix "non-" (meaning "not") with the adjective "didactic," which derives from the Greek word "didaktikos," meaning "fit for teaching" or "apt to teach."  "Didaktikos" itself comes from the verb "didáskein," meaning "to teach."
