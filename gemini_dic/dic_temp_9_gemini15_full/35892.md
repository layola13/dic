coconuts:/ˈkoʊkənʌts/ | n. |椰子；椰子肉 | The word "coconut" comes from the 16th-century Portuguese word "coco," meaning "head" or "skull," likely referring to the coconut's three-eyed appearance.  The term was later anglicized and combined with the suffix "-nut" to create "coconut."|例句：We drank fresh coconut water on the beach.我们在海滩上喝了新鲜的椰子水。

例句：The baker used shredded coconut in the cake. 面包师在蛋糕里使用了椰蓉。
