self-discrimination: /ˌsɛlfˌdɪskrɪmɪˈneɪʃən/ | n. |自我歧视，自我贬低 | The act of discriminating against oneself; treating oneself unfairly or negatively, often stemming from internalized negative beliefs or societal pressures.  This can manifest in various ways, such as self-sabotage, low self-esteem, and negative self-talk. | Example:  Her self-discrimination prevented her from applying for the promotion, despite her qualifications.  她对自己的歧视阻止了她申请晋升，尽管她有资格。


近义词: self-depreciation, self-hate, self-rejection | 反义词: self-love, self-acceptance, self-esteem
