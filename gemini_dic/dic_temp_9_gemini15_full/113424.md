lymphocytotoxicity:/ˌlɪmfoʊˌsaɪtɒkˈsɪtɪti/ | n. |淋巴细胞毒性 |The ability of certain cells of the immune system, such as cytotoxic T lymphocytes (CTLs) and natural killer (NK) cells, to kill other cells.  This process is crucial for eliminating infected or cancerous cells.  It involves the release of cytotoxic molecules that induce apoptosis (programmed cell death) in the target cell. | Example: The study investigated the lymphocytotoxicity of a new drug against leukemia cells. 这项研究调查了一种新药对白血病细胞的淋巴细胞毒性。|近义词:cytotoxicity, cell killing, immune destruction |反义词:immunosuppression, cell protection |名词
