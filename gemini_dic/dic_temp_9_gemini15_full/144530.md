outvanishing: /ˌaʊtˈvænɪʃɪŋ/ | adj. | 消失的；超越的；胜过 |  Describes something that surpasses or exceeds something else to the point of making it insignificant or obsolete.  It implies a complete overshadowing or rendering irrelevant.  This word is relatively rare and often found in more literary or figurative contexts. |


例句：
His groundbreaking invention rendered all previous attempts at solving the problem outvanishing.  他的突破性发明使以前所有解决这个问题的尝试都显得微不足道。


The success of her latest novel made her previous works seem outvanishing. 她最新小说的成功使她以前的作品显得黯然失色。


近义词： surpassing, overshadowing, eclipsing, dwarfing, outmoding


反义词： insignificant, inferior, subordinate, overshadowed


词性:  形容词


USAGE->
(由于outvanishing是形容词，没有动词的时态变化。以下例句展现形容词在不同句法中的用法)

肯定句:
The new technology is outvanishing the old. 新技术已经淘汰了旧技术。
His achievements are outvanishing all expectations. 他的成就超越了所有人的预期。


否定句:
The old methods are not outvanishing; they still have their uses. 旧方法并没有过时；它们仍然有用。
Her performance was not outvanishing; it was just average. 她的表现并不出色；只是中等水平。


疑问句:
Is the new design outvanishing the previous one? 新设计是否胜过之前的那个？
Are his contributions outvanishing those of his colleagues? 他的贡献是否超过了他的同事们？


比较句:
The new car is far more outvanishing than the old model. 新车比旧款车先进得多。
This technology is more outvanishing than we initially thought.这项技术比我们最初想象的还要先进。


PHRASE->
(由于outvanishing本身就是一个比较强烈的形容词短语，不常与其他词组合成固定短语。以下列举一些可以修饰outvanishing的副词或介词短语)


completely outvanishing: 完全超越
remarkably outvanishing: 非常超越
outvanishing in comparison to: 与...相比，显得过时
outvanishing in terms of: 就...而言，显得过时
outvanishing by a wide margin: 以很大的优势超越
