narrative:/ˈnærətɪv/| n.|叙事，故事，记叙文| a spoken or written account of connected events; a story.|例句：The novel's narrative was compelling and kept me hooked. 这部小说的叙述引人入胜，让我着迷。|例句：He gave a vivid narrative of his childhood adventures. 他生动地讲述了他童年冒险的经历。|近义词：story, tale, account, recounting|反义词：fact (in contrast to fiction), reality

一般现在时:
The narrative focuses on the hero's journey. = 叙事重点在于英雄的旅程。
Her narrative unfolds gradually, revealing secrets along the way. = 她的叙事逐渐展开，一路揭示秘密。

一般过去时:
The author's narrative captivated readers. = 作者的叙述迷住了读者。
The detective's narrative revealed the truth. = 侦探的叙述揭露了真相。

现在完成时:
The film's narrative has resonated with audiences worldwide. = 这部电影的叙述在全球观众中引起了共鸣。
The historian's narrative has significantly shaped our understanding of the event. = 这位历史学家的叙事极大地影响了我们对这一事件的理解。

过去完成时:
By the end of the story, the narrative had taken many unexpected turns. = 到故事结尾时，叙述已经发生了许多意想不到的转折。
The narrator had already established the main conflict in the narrative before introducing the secondary characters. = 叙述者在介绍次要人物之前，已经在叙述中确立了主要的冲突。

将来时:
The director will shape the narrative to emphasize the emotional impact. = 导演将塑造叙事以强调情感影响。

虚拟语气:
If the narrative were more engaging, the book would sell better. = 如果叙述更引人入胜，这本书就会卖得更好。
I wish the narrative had been less predictable. = 我希望叙述不那么可预测。

被动语态:
The narrative is often interpreted differently by various readers. = 叙述经常被不同的读者解读。
The narrative was carefully constructed to build suspense. = 叙事被精心构建以制造悬念。

疑问句:
What is the main narrative of the film? = 这部电影的主要叙事是什么？
Does the narrative effectively convey the author's message? = 叙事是否有效地传达了作者的信息？

否定句:
The narrative lacks coherence and clarity. = 叙述缺乏连贯性和清晰度。
The film's narrative did not resonate with me. = 这部电影的叙述没有引起我的共鸣。

词源:
源自拉丁语 "narratio,"  意思是"叙述，讲述"。  "narrare" 是拉丁语动词，意思是 "to tell," "to relate," or "to recount."
