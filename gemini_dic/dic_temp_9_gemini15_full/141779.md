oratorio:/ɔːrəˈtɔːrioʊ/| n. |清唱剧|A large-scale musical work for orchestra, chorus, and soloists, typically on a religious theme, performed without action or scenery.  Oratorios are similar to operas but lack costumes, staging, and acting.  They often tell a story through the music and lyrics.|Example: Handel's Messiah is a famous example of a Baroque oratorio.|近义词：cantata (though generally smaller in scale)|反义词：opera (because of the staging and acting)|名词
