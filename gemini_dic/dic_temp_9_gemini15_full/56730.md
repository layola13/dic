doorkeepers:/ˈdɔːrˌkiːpərz/| n. |看门人，守门员，门卫|People responsible for guarding or controlling access to a place, often a building or event.  They may check identification, manage entry and exit, and maintain security.| Example: The doorkeepers checked our tickets before we entered the concert hall. 看门员在我们将进入音乐厅之前检查了我们的票。 The museum's doorkeepers were very polite and helpful. 博物馆的门卫非常有礼貌和乐于助人。|近义词：gatekeepers, guards, ushers, custodians|反义词：inhabitants (反义词较为宽泛，表示居住者，与看门人职责相反)|名词
