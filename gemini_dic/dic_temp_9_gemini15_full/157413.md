pique:/piːk/| vt.|激怒；刺痛；激起（兴趣）|to cause someone to feel annoyed or resentful; to arouse or excite (interest or curiosity)|例句：His remarks piqued her curiosity. 他的话激起了她的好奇心。
His constant criticisms piqued her. 他不断的批评激怒了她。|近义词：irritate, annoy, provoke, excite|反义词：soothe, calm, appease|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The constant noise piques my nerves. = 持续的噪音让我烦躁。
His sarcastic remarks pique my interest. = 他讽刺的言论激起了我的兴趣。


一般过去时:
Her rudeness piqued my anger. = 她的粗鲁激怒了我。
The mystery of the disappearance piqued the detective's interest. = 失踪的谜团激起了侦探的兴趣。


现在完成时:
His behavior has piqued my curiosity for years. = 他的行为多年来一直激起我的好奇心。
The news has piqued my interest in learning more. = 这个消息激起了我进一步学习的兴趣。


过去完成时:
The earlier insult had piqued her so much that she refused to speak to him. = 此前的侮辱让她非常生气，以至于她拒绝和他说话。
I had been piqued by his dismissive attitude, so I left early. = 我被他轻蔑的态度激怒了，所以我早早离开了。

虚拟语气:
If he hadn't insulted her, she wouldn't have been piqued. = 如果他没有侮辱她，她就不会生气了。
If the story were true, it would pique my interest greatly. = 如果这个故事是真的，那将会大大激起我的兴趣。


被动语态:

My curiosity was piqued by the unusual noise. = 不寻常的噪音激起了我的好奇心。


疑问句:

否定句:
His words did not pique my interest. = 他的话没有激起我的兴趣。
The situation didn't pique her anger at all. = 这种情况一点也没有激怒她。
