hypoxias:/haɪˈpɒksiəz/ | n. | 缺氧（复数） |  A plural form of hypoxia, referring to multiple instances or types of oxygen deficiency in body tissues. It encompasses various conditions where the body's tissues aren't receiving enough oxygen.  These can range from mild to life-threatening. | Examples of hypoxias include those caused by high altitude, heart failure, or lung disease.  |  oxygen deficiency, anoxia (severe hypoxia) |  hyperoxia (excess oxygen) |  n.
