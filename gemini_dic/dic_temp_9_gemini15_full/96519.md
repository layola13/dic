indoctrinating: /ɪnˈdɑːktrɪneɪtɪŋ/ | v. |灌输；教条式地灌输 |The act of teaching someone a set of beliefs, often in a forceful or persuasive way, without allowing for critical thinking or questioning.  |例句：The cult was accused of indoctrinating its members into blind obedience.  邪教被指控向其成员灌输盲目服从。

例句：The government was accused of indoctrinating children with nationalist propaganda. 政府被指控用民族主义宣传对儿童进行灌输。


近义词：brainwashing, proselytizing, propagandizing, inculcating

反义词：educating (in a critical and open-minded way), enlightening

词性：动词(现在分词)


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They are indoctrinating the young with their beliefs. = 他们正在向年轻人灌输他们的信仰。
The school indoctrinates students with traditional values. = 学校向学生灌输传统价值观。

一般过去时:
The regime indoctrinated its citizens with communist ideology. = 该政权向其公民灌输共产主义意识形态。
The teacher indoctrinated the students with his own political views. = 老师向学生灌输了他自己的政治观点。


现在完成时:
They have indoctrinated a generation with their hateful rhetoric. = 他们已经用他们充满仇恨的言辞灌输了一代人。
The church has indoctrinated its followers for centuries. = 教会几个世纪以来一直在向其信徒灌输教义。


过去完成时:
Before the war, they had indoctrinated the population against the enemy. = 在战争爆发前，他们已经向民众灌输了反对敌人的思想。
By the time he left, he had been indoctrinated into believing the false narrative. = 到他离开的时候，他已经深信不疑地接受了虚假的叙述。


将来时:

虚拟语气:

I wish they hadn't indoctrinated him so thoroughly. = 我真希望他们没有对他进行如此彻底的灌输。


被动语态:

否定句:
