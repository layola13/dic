pallium: /ˈpæliəm/ | n. |披肩；教袍；大脑皮层|

英文解析:A pallium is a cloak or mantle, especially one worn by a Roman official or a bishop. In anatomy, it refers to the outer layer of the cerebral hemispheres of the brain.  It can also refer to a type of woolen fabric.

例句:

The archbishop wore a magnificent pallium during the ceremony.  = 大主教在仪式上穿着一件华丽的教袍。

The pallium of the Roman senator was a symbol of his authority. = 罗马参议员的披肩是其权威的象征。


近义词:cloak, mantle, chasuble (for religious context),  vestment


反义词:There isn't a direct antonym, as the meaning is dependent on context.  If referring to clothing,  "bare shoulders" could be considered an opposite in a figurative sense.


词性:Noun
