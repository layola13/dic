noted:/ˈnoʊtɪd/ | adj. |著名的，知名的；值得注意的 | Well-known, especially for a particular skill, quality, or achievement.  |例句：He is a noted scholar in the field of linguistics. 他是语言学领域一位著名的学者。 |例句：There was a noted increase in sales this quarter. 本季度销售额显著增长。|近义词：famous, renowned, celebrated, prominent, remarkable |反义词：unknown, obscure, insignificant


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is a noted author. = 他是一位著名的作家。
She is noted for her kindness. = 她以善良著称。

一般过去时:
The scientist was noted for his groundbreaking research. =这位科学家因其开创性的研究而闻名。
The problem was noted and addressed immediately. = 问题已注意到并立即得到解决。

现在完成时:
The artist has become noted for her unique style. =这位艺术家因其独特的风格而闻名。
The issue has been noted and will be reviewed. = 问题已注意到，并将进行审查。

过去完成时:
It had been noted that the experiment had flaws. = 人们已经注意到实验存在缺陷。
The error had been noted prior to the report's release. = 在报告发布之前，错误已被注意到。


将来时:
The new policy will be noted in the next update. = 新政策将在下一次更新中注明。
This achievement will be noted in the company's history. = 这项成就将载入公司史册。


虚拟语气:
If his contribution were noted, it would inspire others. =如果他的贡献得到认可，将会激励其他人。
I wish my hard work were noted by my boss. =我希望我的努力工作能得到老板的认可。


被动语态:
His achievements were noted in the newspaper. =他的成就登上了报纸。
The changes were noted and implemented successfully. = 这些变化已被注意到并成功实施。


疑问句:
Was the discrepancy noted in the report? = 报告中是否提到了差异？
Have you noted any significant changes? = 你注意到任何重大变化了吗？


否定句:
The minor errors were not noted during the initial review. = 初步审查中没有注意到这些小错误。
His contributions were not noted at the time. = 当时他的贡献没有被注意到。
