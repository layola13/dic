echolocation:/ˌɛkoʊloʊˈkeɪʃən/| n. |回声定位；回声测位|The process by which animals emit sounds and then use the echoes to locate objects or navigate.  This is particularly common in bats, dolphins, and some birds. |Example: Bats rely on echolocation to find their way around in the dark and to catch insects.  蝙蝠依靠回声定位在黑暗中找到方向并捕捉昆虫。|近义词：sonar, biosonar |反义词：sight, vision
