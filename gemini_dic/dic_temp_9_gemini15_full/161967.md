pranky:/ˈpræŋki/| adj.|恶作剧的，顽皮的|Playful and mischievous, given to pranks.|例句：He's a bit pranky, always pulling silly jokes on people. 他有点顽皮，总是捉弄人。|近义词：mischievous, playful, tricksy|反义词：serious, well-behaved


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is pranky today. = 他今天很顽皮。
The children are being very pranky. = 孩子们非常顽皮。


一般过去时:
He was pranky when he was a child. = 他小时候很顽皮。
They were pranky at the party. = 他们在派对上很顽皮。


现在完成时:
He has been pranky all week. = 他整个星期都很顽皮。
She has always been a bit pranky. = 她一直有点顽皮。


过去完成时:
He had been pranky all day, so I wasn't surprised he pulled that prank. = 他整天都很顽皮，所以我对他搞恶作剧并不感到惊讶。
They had been pranky earlier, which made the teacher angry. = 他们之前很顽皮，这惹恼了老师。

I wish he weren't so pranky. = 我希望他不那么顽皮。


被动语态: (This adjective is rarely used passively.)
