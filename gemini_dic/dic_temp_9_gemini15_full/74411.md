franchisability:/ˌfrænˈtʃaɪzəˈbɪləti/| n. |特许经营能力；可特许经营性 |The ability of a business to be successfully franchised. This involves assessing various factors to determine if the business model, operations, and brand are suitable for replication through a franchise system.  It considers aspects like the business's scalability, the ability to standardize operations, the strength of the brand, and the level of training and support that can be provided to franchisees. |Example: The company conducted a thorough assessment of its franchisability before offering franchise opportunities.  这家公司在提供特许经营机会之前对其特许经营能力进行了彻底的评估。|近义词：franchise potential, suitability for franchising |反义词：unsuitability for franchising
