carpools:/ˈkɑːrpuːlz/| n. |拼车，拼车出行 |A carpool is an arrangement where several people share a car for commuting or traveling together, usually to reduce costs and traffic congestion.  |例句：Many employees use carpools to get to work. 许多员工拼车上班。

例句：We organized a carpool for the field trip. 我们为实地考察组织了拼车。


近义词：ride-sharing, shared rides

反义词：solo driving, individual commute

词性: 名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company encourages carpools to reduce emissions. = 公司鼓励拼车以减少排放。
She regularly uses the carpool lane. = 她经常使用拼车道。


一般过去时:
They carpooled to the concert last night. = 他们昨晚拼车去音乐会。
We carpooled to avoid traffic. = 我们拼车以避免交通拥堵。


现在完成时:
We have carpooled to work every day this week. = 我们本周每天都拼车上班。
They have saved a lot of money by carpooling. = 他们通过拼车节省了很多钱。


过去完成时:
Before the new highway opened, we had carpooled for years. = 在新高速公路开通之前，我们拼车多年了。
They had never carpooled before moving to the city. = 在搬到城市之前，他们从未拼车过。


将来时:
We will carpool to the airport tomorrow. = 我们明天将拼车去机场。
They will be carpooling more frequently starting next month. = 从下个月开始，他们将更频繁地拼车。


虚拟语气:
If we had carpooled, we would have arrived earlier. = 如果我们拼车的话，我们会早点到达。
I wish we had carpooled instead of driving separately. = 我真希望我们当时是拼车而不是分别开车。


被动语态:
Carpools are often organized by workplaces or schools. = 拼车通常由工作场所或学校组织。
The carpool lane is frequently used during rush hour. = 在高峰时段，拼车道经常被使用。


疑问句:
Do you carpool to work? = 你拼车上班吗？
Will you be carpooling this weekend? = 你这个周末会拼车吗？


否定句:
We don't carpool; we drive separately. = 我们不拼车；我们分别开车。
They didn't carpool to the game last night. = 他们昨晚没有拼车去看比赛。
