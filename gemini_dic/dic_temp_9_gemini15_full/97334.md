informer:/ɪnˈfɔːrmər/| n. |告密者，线人 | a person who gives information secretly to the police or other authority.  |The informer revealed the criminal's hiding place.  告密者透露了罪犯的藏身之处。|  spy, whistleblower | accomplice, confidant | 名词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He informs the police about the crime. 他向警方举报了这起犯罪。
She informs her boss about the project's progress. 她向老板汇报了项目的进展。


一般过去时:
The informer informed on his former colleagues. 告密者告发了之前的同事。
He informed me of the meeting cancellation. 他通知我会议取消了。


现在完成时:
The police have been informed of the situation. 警方已经被告知了情况。
She has informed us of her decision. 她已经通知了我们她的决定。


过去完成时:
They had informed the authorities before the incident. 他们在事件发生前已经通知了当局。
He had already informed his family about his plans. 他已经把他的计划告诉了他的家人。


将来时:
I will inform you as soon as I have news. 我一有消息就会通知你。
She will inform the team of the changes. 她会通知团队这些变化。


虚拟语气:
If I had informed him earlier, he wouldn't have missed the deadline. 如果我早点告诉他，他不会错过截止日期。
I wish I had informed them sooner. 我真希望我早点告诉他们。


被动语态:
The police were informed of the bomb threat. 警方被告知了炸弹威胁。
The public has been informed about the new policy. 公众已经被告知了新政策。


疑问句:
Has he informed the manager yet? 他已经告诉经理了吗？
Who informed the press about the scandal? 是谁向媒体爆料了这个丑闻？


否定句:
He did not inform me about the changes. 他没有告诉我这些变化。
She didn't inform anyone of her plans. 她没有告诉任何人她的计划。


PHRASE->
inform someone of something:  通知某人某事
keep someone informed:  随时告知某人
well-informed:  消息灵通的
misinformed:  被误导的
to inform a decision:  影响决定
