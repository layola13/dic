elucidators: /ɪˈluːsɪdeɪtərz/ | n. |阐明者；解释者 |Those who elucidate; those who make clear or explain something.  People or things that serve to clarify or explain. |例句：The professor's lectures were excellent elucidators of complex theories. 教授的讲座非常出色地阐明了复杂的理论。

The new research findings are elucidators of the disease's progression. 新的研究结果阐明了这种疾病的进展。


近义词：explainers, interpreters, commentators, expositors |反义词：obfuscators, confounders


词源:  From the verb "elucidate," which comes from the Latin "elucidare," meaning "to make clear, illuminate."  The suffix "-ors" indicates a person or thing that performs the action.
