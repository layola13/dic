downtick:/ˈdaʊnˌtɪk/| n. |下跌；股票价格下跌 | A downward movement in price, especially of a stock.  | Example: The market experienced a sharp downtick after the news announcement.  市场在新闻发布后经历了大幅下跌。  |近义词：decline, drop, fall |反义词：uptick, rise, increase |


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The stock price shows a downtick. = 股票价格显示下跌。
There is a downtick in sales this month. = 本月销售额下降。


一般过去时:
The market experienced a significant downtick yesterday. = 昨天市场经历了显著的下跌。
The downtick in profits worried investors. = 利润下降让投资者担忧。


现在完成时:
The stock has shown a consistent downtick this year. = 今年股价持续下跌。
There has been a noticeable downtick in consumer confidence. = 消费者信心明显下降。


过去完成时:
The downtick had already begun before the report was released. = 在报告发布之前，下跌就已经开始了。
Investors had anticipated a downtick in the housing market. = 投资者已经预料到房地产市场将下跌。


将来时:
Analysts predict a downtick in the coming quarter. = 分析师预测下个季度将会下跌。
The company expects a downtick in orders next month. = 公司预计下个月订单将减少。


虚拟语气:
If there were a downtick in the economy, many businesses would suffer. = 如果经济下滑，许多企业将遭受损失。
I wish there weren't such a sharp downtick in the stock market. = 我希望股市不要下跌这么厉害。


被动语态:
The downtick was attributed to several factors. = 下跌归因于几个因素。
The downtick in oil prices was widely reported. = 油价下跌被广泛报道。


疑问句:
Is there a downtick in the employment rate? = 就业率下降了吗？
What caused the recent downtick in the housing market? = 最近房地产市场下跌的原因是什么？


否定句:
There was no significant downtick in the economy. = 经济没有出现显著下滑。
The company did not experience a downtick in sales. = 公司销售额没有下降。


PHRASE->
a sharp downtick = 急剧下跌
a slight downtick = 轻微下跌
a noticeable downtick = 明显下跌
a sudden downtick = 突然下跌
a consistent downtick = 持续下跌
