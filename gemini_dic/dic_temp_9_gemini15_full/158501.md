plenist:/ˈpliːnɪst/| n. |充分论者，唯物论者|A plenist is someone who believes in the completeness or fullness of something, particularly in the context of philosophical or theological discussions.  They often advocate for a complete and comprehensive view of reality, rejecting any form of reductionism or minimalism. In a theological context, a plenist might emphasize the fullness of God's being and attributes. | The philosopher argued for a plenist worldview, asserting that reality is rich and multifaceted, not reducible to simple explanations. |近义词：completist, universalist |反义词：minimalist, reductionist
