hobbling:/ˈhɒblɪŋ/ | /ˈhɑːblɪŋ/ | v. |蹒跚而行，跛行 |To walk with difficulty, especially because of a limp.  |例句：The old woman was hobbling along the street.  这位老太太正蹒跚地走在街上。|例句：After the accident, he was hobbling around on crutches. 事故后，他拄着拐杖蹒跚地走动。|近义词：limping, shuffling, staggering |反义词：running, striding


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He hobbles around the house every morning. 他每天早上都在屋里蹒跚地走动。
She hobbles when she walks. 她走路的时候一瘸一拐的。

一般过去时:
He hobbled home after the marathon. 马拉松结束后，他一瘸一拐地走回了家。
She hobbled down the stairs. 她一瘸一拐地走下楼梯。

现在完成时:
He has hobbled around all day because of his injured ankle. 因为脚踝受伤，他一整天都在一瘸一拐地走动。
She has hobbled since her fall. 自从摔倒后，她一直一瘸一拐的。

过去完成时:
He had hobbled for miles before he finally found help. 他走了好几英里才最终找到帮助。
She had hobbled through the entire concert before she finally sat down.  她在整个音乐会上一瘸一拐地走来走去，最后才坐下。

将来时:
He will hobble to the finish line. 他将会一瘸一拐地走到终点线。
She will probably hobble for weeks after the surgery. 手术后她可能会一瘸一拐地走好几个星期。

虚拟语气:
If he hadn't injured his knee, he wouldn't be hobbling now. 如果他的膝盖没有受伤，他现在就不会一瘸一拐的了。
I wish I wasn't hobbling so much. 我希望我不至于这么一瘸一拐的。

被动语态:  (Hobble is rarely used in the passive voice)
*This sentence structure is grammatically correct but not natural*  His progress was hobbled by his injury.  他的进步因伤势而受阻。
*This sentence structure is grammatically correct but not natural* The race was hobbled by the bad weather. 比赛因恶劣天气而受阻。(Here, "hobbled" means hampered, not literally walking with a limp.)

疑问句:
Is he hobbling because of his bad knee? 他是因为膝盖不好才一瘸一拐的吗？
Why is she hobbling? 她为什么一瘸一拐的？

否定句:
He doesn't hobble anymore. 他不再一瘸一拐的了。
She didn't hobble after the operation. 手术后她没有一瘸一拐的。
