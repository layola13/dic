pariahs:/ˈpɑːriɑːz/| n.|贱民；被社会抛弃的人|A pariah is a person who is avoided by others because they are considered to be disreputable or undesirable.  They are often ostracized and excluded from society.|例句：He became a pariah after being convicted of fraud. 他因犯欺诈罪而成为不受欢迎的人。|例句：The community shunned the pariah, refusing to acknowledge his presence. 社区避开这个贱民，拒绝承认他的存在。|近义词：outcast, untouchable, leper, renegade|反义词：darling, favorite, insider

词源:
The word "pariah" ultimately derives from the Tamil word "paraiyan," referring to a low caste in southern India, traditionally associated with drum-making and considered untouchable by the higher castes.  The word entered English in the 18th century.
