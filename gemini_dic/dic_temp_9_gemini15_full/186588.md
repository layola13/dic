ridiculize:/rɪˈdɪkjʊlaɪz/| vt.| 嘲笑；奚落；使…显得荒谬 | To make fun of; to mock; to treat as ridiculous.  |例句：They ridiculed his ideas. 他们嘲笑他的想法。|例句：Don't ridicule her efforts, she's trying her best. 不要嘲笑她的努力，她已经尽力了。|近义词：mock, scorn, deride, sneer at |反义词：praise, commend, applaud


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He ridicules his classmates often. = 他经常嘲笑他的同学们。
They ridicule his new hairstyle. = 他们嘲笑他的新发型。

一般过去时:
She ridiculed his proposal in front of everyone. = 她当着所有人的面嘲笑他的提议。
The critics ridiculed the play. = 批评家们嘲笑了这部戏剧。

现在完成时:
They have ridiculed him for years. = 他们嘲笑他多年了。
The media has ridiculed the politician's actions. = 媒体嘲笑了这位政治家的行为。

过去完成时:
He had ridiculed her ideas before, so she didn't trust him. = 他以前就嘲笑过她的想法，所以她不信任他。
The audience had ridiculed the comedian's jokes before he even finished his set. = 在喜剧演员表演结束之前，观众就已经嘲笑他的笑话了。

将来时:
They will ridicule him mercilessly if he tries that again. = 如果他再尝试那样做，他们会无情地嘲笑他。
The newspapers will ridicule his decision. = 报纸将嘲笑他的决定。

虚拟语气:
If I were to suggest that, they would ridicule me. = 如果我提出那个建议，他们会嘲笑我。
I wish they wouldn't ridicule my attempts. = 我希望他们不要嘲笑我的尝试。

被动语态:
His ideas were ridiculed by the committee. = 他的想法被委员会嘲笑了。
His performance was ridiculed by the critics. = 他的表演被评论家们嘲笑了。

疑问句:
Why do they ridicule him so much? = 为什么他们那么嘲笑他？
Is it right to ridicule someone for their beliefs? = 嘲笑别人的信仰是对的吗？

否定句:
They did not ridicule his efforts. = 他们没有嘲笑他的努力。
She wouldn't ridicule someone who is trying their best. = 她不会嘲笑那些正在尽力的人。


PHRASE->
ridiculize someone's ideas = 嘲笑某人的想法
ridiculize someone's efforts = 嘲笑某人的努力
ridiculize someone's appearance = 嘲笑某人的外表
ridiculize someone's actions = 嘲笑某人的行为
openly ridicule = 公开嘲笑


词源:
From the Late Latin word *ridiculus*, meaning "laughable," "ridiculous," deriving from the Latin word *ridere*, meaning "to laugh."  The suffix "-ize" is a common verb-forming suffix in English, indicating "to make" or "to treat as."
