low-ranking: /ˈloʊ ˈræŋkɪŋ/ | adj. | 低级别的，低阶的 |  Describes someone or something that holds a low position or status within a hierarchy or system. It implies a lack of power, influence, or seniority. |

例句：

He held a low-ranking position in the company, with little chance of advancement.  = 他在公司担任低级职位，晋升机会渺茫。

She was a low-ranking officer in the army, responsible for logistics. = 她是军队中的一名低级军官，负责后勤工作。


近义词：inferior, subordinate, junior, minor, lowly


反义词：high-ranking, senior, superior, major


词源:

The word is a compound of "low" and "ranking."  "Low" signifies a small amount, level, or degree. "Ranking" derives from the verb "to rank," meaning to arrange in order of merit, importance, or value.  The ultimate roots trace back to Old English and Germanic origins.
