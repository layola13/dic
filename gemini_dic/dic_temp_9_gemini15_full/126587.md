nagarjuna:/nɑːɡərˈdʒʊnə/| n. |龙树|Nagarjuna was a prominent Indian philosopher and theologian who lived in the 2nd century CE. He is considered the founder of Madhyamaka, a school of Mahayana Buddhism that emphasizes the concept of emptiness (śūnyatā). His philosophical contributions greatly influenced the development of Buddhist thought in India, Tibet, China, and Japan.  He wrote extensively on various Buddhist topics, including philosophy, logic, and meditation.
