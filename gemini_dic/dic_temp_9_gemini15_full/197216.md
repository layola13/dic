shard:/ʃɑːrd/ | n. |碎片；陶片 | A shard is a broken piece of something, especially pottery.  | Example:  The archaeologist carefully brushed away the dirt to reveal a shard of ancient pottery. 考古学家仔细地拂去泥土，露出了一块古代陶器的碎片。  |近义词：fragment, piece, splinter | 反义词：whole, intact |


USAGE->

The broken vase lies in shards. = 打碎的花瓶碎成碎片。
A shard of glass cut my finger. = 一块玻璃碎片割伤了我的手指。


一般过去时:
The explosion shattered the window into countless shards. = 爆炸使窗户碎成无数碎片。
