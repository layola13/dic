periderm:/ˈpɛrɪdɜːrm/ | n. | 周皮 | The periderm is the protective outer layer of stems and roots in plants that replaces the epidermis during secondary growth. It consists of cork, cork cambium, and phelloderm.  | Example: The periderm protects the plant from desiccation, mechanical injury, and pathogens.  周皮保护植物免受干燥、机械损伤和病原体的侵害。 |近义词：bark (in some contexts) | 反义词：None directly applicable.  | 词性：名词

词源:
The word "periderm" is derived from the Greek words "peri" (around, about) and "derma" (skin).  Therefore, it literally means "around the skin," reflecting its position as an outer protective layer.
