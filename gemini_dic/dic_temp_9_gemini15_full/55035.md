disquieter:/dɪsˈkwaɪətər/ | n. |令人不安的事物；令人烦恼的人或事 |Something or someone that causes anxiety or unease.  |The constant noise was a disquieter to the residents. 持续的噪音让居民感到不安。 |近义词：disturbance, bother, nuisance |反义词：comfort, solace, peace


USAGE->
This word is primarily a noun, so verb conjugations are not applicable.  The following demonstrates usage in different sentence structures:


一般现在时:
The news is a disquieter to many people. = 这个消息让很多人不安。
That loud music is a real disquieter. = 那吵闹的音乐真是让人心烦。


一般过去时:
The unexpected visitor was a disquieter. = 不速之客令人不安。
His constant complaints were a disquieter to everyone. = 他不断的抱怨让每个人都心烦。

过去完成时:

虚拟语气:
