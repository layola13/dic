coincide:/ˌkoʊɪnˈsaɪd/ | v. | 同时发生；一致；符合 | To occur at the same time; to be in agreement or harmony. |例句：The two events coincided. = 这两件事同时发生。
His account of the incident coincided with hers. = 他对事件的描述和她的一致。|近义词：correspond, concur, agree |反义词：differ, clash, conflict |动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The meeting coincides with my vacation. = 会议与我的假期同时进行。
Their opinions coincide on this matter. = 他们在这一问题上的意见一致。


一般过去时:
Our arrival coincided with the sunset. = 我们到达的时间与日落同时。
His story coincided with the evidence. = 他的说法与证据相符。


现在完成时:
Several factors have coincided to create this situation. = 几个因素共同造成了这种情况。
My interests have coincided with the company's goals. = 我的兴趣与公司的目标一致。


过去完成时:
The protest had coincided with a national holiday. = 抗议活动恰逢国庆节。
The two timelines had coincided at a crucial point. = 这两条时间线在一个关键点上重合了。


将来时:
The conference will coincide with the trade show. = 会议将与贸易展同时举行。
The launch date will coincide with the marketing campaign. = 发布日期将与营销活动同时进行。


虚拟语气:
If the two events coincided, it would be a remarkable coincidence. = 如果这两件事同时发生，那将是一个非同寻常的巧合。
I wish my schedule coincided with yours. = 我希望我的日程安排与你的一致。


被动语态:
The dates of the trips were coincidentally coincided. = 旅行的日期巧合地重合了。
Their plans were made to coincide with the festival. = 他们的计划是与节日同时进行的。


疑问句:
Does your birthday coincide with a holiday? = 你的生日是否与某个节日重合？
Do their views coincide on this issue? = 他们在这个问题上的观点是否一致？


否定句:
The two events did not coincide. = 这两件事没有同时发生。
Her testimony did not coincide with the facts. = 她的证词与事实不符。


PHRASE->
coincide with: 与…同时发生
coincide in: 在…方面一致
roughly coincide: 大致相同
happen to coincide: 碰巧同时发生
nearly coincide: 几乎同时发生

词源:
From Latin *coincidere, from com- + incidere (to fall upon).  The prefix "com-" means "together," and "incidere" means "to fall upon," thus literally meaning "to fall upon together."
