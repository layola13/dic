preventability: /ˌprɪˌvɛntəˈbɪləti/ | n. | 可预防性，可避免性 | The ability to be prevented or avoided.  The degree to which something can be prevented. | 例句：The study highlighted the preventability of many workplace accidents. 这项研究强调了许多工作场所事故的可预防性。

The high preventability of these diseases makes their continued existence unacceptable. 这些疾病的高度可预防性使得它们的持续存在无法接受。


近义词：avoidability,  preventibility | 反义词：inevitability, unavoidability

词源:
Preventability derives from the verb "prevent"  (源自动词prevent) , which comes from the Old French word "prevenir" (来自古法语单词prevenir), meaning "to come before," "anticipate," or "forestall."  The suffix "-ability" is added to indicate the state or quality of being preventable. (-ability后缀表示可预防的状态或性质。)
