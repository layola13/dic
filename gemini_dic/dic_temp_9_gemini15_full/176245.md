re-christianizing: /ˌriːˈkrɪʃtʃənˌaɪzɪŋ/ | v. |  重新基督教化 | The act or process of reintroducing or restoring Christian beliefs, practices, or values to a person, place, or society.  This often implies a previous decline or absence of such influence. | 例句：The re-christianizing of Europe after the fall of the Roman Empire was a gradual process.  欧洲在罗马帝国衰落后重新基督教化是一个循序渐进的过程。


The government's policies aimed at re-christianizing the country faced significant opposition. 政府旨在使国家重新基督教化的政策面临着巨大的阻力。


近义词：re-evangelization, reconversion, re-indoctrination


反义词：secularization, dechristianization


词性：动词

词源:

The word is a combination of "re-" (again, anew) + "Christianizing" (making Christian). "Christianizing" derives from "Christian" (a follower of Christ) + "-izing" (making or becoming).  The ultimate roots lie in the name "Christ," from Greek *Χριστός* (Christos), meaning "anointed one."
