precipice:/ˈprɛsəpɪs/| n.|悬崖；绝境；险境| a very steep cliff; a dangerous situation|The climbers were in danger of falling from the precipice. 攀登者有从悬崖上掉下来的危险。  He stood on the precipice of a major decision. 他站在重大决定的边缘。|cliff, crag, bluff|plain, level ground|名词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The precipice overlooks the valley. = 悬崖俯瞰山谷。
The situation is a precipice. = 情况危急。


一般过去时:
The climber fell from the precipice. = 登山者从悬崖上摔下来了。
They stood on the precipice of war. = 他们站在战争的边缘。


现在完成时:
The hikers have reached the edge of the precipice. = 登山者们已经到达了悬崖边。
We have avoided the precipice of bankruptcy. = 我们避免了破产的边缘。


过去完成时:
He had already reached the precipice before the storm hit. =暴风雨来临之前，他已经到达了悬崖边。
They had narrowly escaped the precipice of failure. = 他们险些失败。


将来时:
They will approach the precipice cautiously. = 他们将谨慎地接近悬崖。
The company will be on the precipice of collapse. = 公司将面临崩溃的边缘。


虚拟语气:
If I were to stand on that precipice, I would be terrified. = 如果我站在那个悬崖上，我会很害怕。
If they had not acted quickly, they would have fallen off the precipice. =如果他们没有迅速行动，他们就会从悬崖上掉下去。


被动语态:
The precipice was eroded by the wind and rain. = 悬崖被风和雨侵蚀了。
The country was teetering on the precipice of civil war. = 该国正摇摇欲坠，濒临内战。


疑问句:
Is that a precipice? = 那是个悬崖吗？
Are we on the precipice of a recession? = 我们是否正处于经济衰退的边缘？


否定句:
That is not a precipice, it's a hill. = 那不是悬崖，而是一个小山。
The company is not on the precipice of failure. = 公司没有濒临失败。


PHRASE->

on the precipice of: 濒临…；处于…的边缘
over the precipice:  从悬崖上；一落千丈
brink of the precipice: 悬崖边缘
to the precipice: 到悬崖边
from the precipice: 从悬崖上


词源:

来自古法语 *precepice，源于拉丁语 praecipitium，意为“陡峭的地方”，由 praeceps 构成，意为“陡峭的，险峻的”。
