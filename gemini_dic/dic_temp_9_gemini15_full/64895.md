essene:/ˈɛsiːn/| n. |埃塞尼派教徒；埃塞尼派教义|A member of an ancient Jewish sect known for their communal living, ascetic practices, and apocalyptic beliefs.  They were known for their strict adherence to Jewish law and their expectation of a coming messiah.  Their writings and beliefs have significantly influenced the development of early Christianity.|Example:  The Dead Sea Scrolls, discovered in the Qumran caves, are believed to have been written by Essenes.  埃塞尼派教徒|近义词：sectarian, ascetic |反义词：worldly, hedonist
