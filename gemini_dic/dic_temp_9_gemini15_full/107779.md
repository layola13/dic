lapis-lazulis:/ˈlæpɪs ˈlæzjuːliːs/| n.|青金石|A deep blue metamorphic rock used as a gemstone and pigment.  It's prized for its intense color and is composed primarily of lazurite, with often calcite, pyrite, and other minerals.|例句：The ancient Egyptians used lapis lazuli to create stunning jewelry and adornments. 古埃及人用青金石制作出令人惊叹的珠宝和装饰品。

The vibrant blue of the lapis lazuli painting captivated the viewer. 青金石绘画的鲜艳蓝色吸引了观众。|近义词：azure stone, blue stone|反义词：无

词源:
The word "lapis lazuli" is derived from the Latin words "lapis," meaning "stone," and "lazuli," derived from the Arabic word "lāzaward," ultimately of Persian origin, meaning "blue."  Thus, the name literally translates to "blue stone."
