partitives:/ˈpɑːrtɪtɪvz/|/ˈpɑːrtɪtɪvs/| n. |部分，部分词|Partitives are words or phrases that express a part of a whole.  They indicate a portion or quantity of something.  They are often used with uncountable nouns or nouns that refer to collections of items.|例句：A slice of pizza is a partitive phrase indicating a portion of a whole pizza.  A cup of coffee is another example. |近义词：portion, fraction, segment|反义词：whole, entirety|名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I eat a piece of cake. = 我吃一块蛋糕。
She drinks a glass of water. = 她喝一杯水。


一般过去时:
He ate a bowl of soup. = 他吃了一碗汤。
They drank a bottle of wine. = 他们喝了一瓶酒。


现在完成时:
I have eaten a lot of bread. = 我已经吃了很多面包。
She has drunk several cups of tea. = 她已经喝了几杯茶。


过去完成时:
He had eaten half the pie. = 他已经吃掉了一半的馅饼。
They had drunk most of the milk. = 他们已经喝掉了大部分的牛奶。


将来时:
I will eat a portion of the dessert. = 我将吃一部分甜点。
She will drink a can of soda. = 她将喝一听苏打水。


虚拟语气:
If I had a slice of pizza, I would be happy. = 如果我有一块披萨，我会很高兴。
I wish I had a cup of coffee. = 我希望我有一杯咖啡。


被动语态:
A piece of the cake was eaten. = 一块蛋糕被吃掉了。
A glass of water was drunk. = 一杯水被喝掉了。


疑问句:
How much bread did you eat? = 你吃了多少面包？
Would you like a piece of pie? = 你想吃一块馅饼吗？


否定句:
I didn't eat any of the cake. = 我没有吃任何蛋糕。
She didn't drink any of the wine. = 她没有喝任何酒。


PHRASE->
a piece of  = 一块...
a slice of = 一片...
a cup of = 一杯...
a bottle of = 一瓶...
a bowl of = 一碗...
