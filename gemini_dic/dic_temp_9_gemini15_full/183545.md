reputations:/ˌrepjuˈteɪʃənz/ | n. | 名誉，名声 |  The overall quality of someone or something as judged by other people.  It refers to the beliefs or opinions that are generally held about someone or something. |


例句：

His reputation as a brilliant surgeon precedes him.  他的天才外科医生的名声先于他而至。

The company's reputation was damaged by the scandal. 这家公司因丑闻而名誉受损。


近义词：fame, renown, prestige, standing


反义词：disrepute, infamy, ill-repute


词性：名词


词源：

From Middle French *reputacion, from Latin reputatio(n-), from the verb reputare "to think over, consider, reckon, repute," from re- "again" + putare "to think, consider, prune."  The sense evolution is from "consideration" to "general opinion" to "reputation."
