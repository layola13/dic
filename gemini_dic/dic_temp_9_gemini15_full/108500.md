laying-down: /ˈleɪɪŋ daʊn/ | v. |放下；制定；铺设；使躺下 |  The act of putting something down or establishing something.  It can refer to physically placing something down, or to establishing rules, plans, or policies. |

例句：
He was laying down the law to his children. 他正在向他的孩子们讲规矩。

She carefully laid down the fragile vase. 她小心翼翼地放下易碎的花瓶。


近义词：putting down, establishing, setting down, enacting, formulating

反义词：picking up, lifting, abolishing, repealing


词性：动词

USAGE->
一般现在时:
They are laying down new railway tracks. 他们在铺设新的铁轨。
He lays down his tools at the end of the day. 他每天结束工作时放下工具。


一般过去时:
The government laid down new regulations. 政府制定了新的规章制度。
She laid down her burden and rested. 她放下担子休息了。


现在完成时:
They have laid down a strict code of conduct. 他们制定了一套严格的行为准则。
She has laid down her pen and started to paint. 她放下笔开始作画了。


过去完成时:
They had laid down their arms before the enemy arrived.  敌人到来之前他们已经放下武器了。
She had laid down her plans before the unexpected event.  意外发生前她已经制定好计划了。


将来时:
They will lay down new rules for the competition.  他们将为比赛制定新的规则。
He will lay down his life for his country. 他将为国捐躯。


虚拟语气:
If they laid down their weapons, the war would end. 如果他们放下武器，战争就会结束。
I wish they had laid down stricter guidelines. 我希望他们当时制定了更严格的指导方针。


被动语态:
New rules are being laid down by the committee. 新规章正在由委员会制定。

疑问句:

否定句:
