mainland:/ˈmeɪnlænd/| n. |大陆；本土|The main part of a country, as opposed to its islands or overseas territories.  It refers to the continuous landmass of a country, excluding any islands or separate land areas.|例句：We took a ferry to the mainland from the island. 我们从岛上乘渡轮去了大陆。

例句：The mainland is experiencing a heatwave. 大陆正在经历热浪。


近义词：continent, terra firma | 反义词：island, offshore


词源:  The word "mainland" is a compound word formed from "main" and "land."  "Main" in this context means "principal" or "chief,"  indicating the primary landmass of a region. "Land" refers to the solid ground not covered by water.  The word's origins trace back to Old English "mægen" (strength, power) and "land" (land).  The combination emphasizes the primary, powerful landmass.
