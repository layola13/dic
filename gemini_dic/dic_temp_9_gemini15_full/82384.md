grubber:/ˈɡrʌbər/| n. v.| 寻觅者；挖掘者；贪婪的人；（尤指）寻觅食物的人；挖掘；搜寻； | A grubber is someone who searches for something, especially food, often in a determined or persistent way. It can also refer to a tool used for digging or cultivating. |例句：The chickens were busy grubbing for worms in the garden.  鸡正在花园里忙着找虫子吃。

The old man spent hours grubbing around in the attic for his lost photographs. 老头花了几个小时在阁楼上翻找他丢失的照片。


近义词：digger, scavenger, forager, searcher |反义词：giver, provider


词性: 名词和动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He grubs for food every day. = 他每天都在寻找食物。
She grubs around in the garden. = 她在花园里四处挖掘。


一般过去时:
They grubbed for potatoes in the field. = 他们在田里挖掘土豆。
He grubbed around in the old chest. = 他在旧箱子里翻找东西。


现在完成时:
They have grubbed up all the weeds. = 他们已经挖掉了所有的杂草。
I have grubbed enough food for the week. = 我已经为一周准备了足够的粮食。


过去完成时:

They had grubbed around for hours before finding it. = 他们翻找了几个小时才找到它。


将来时:

虚拟语气:
