maistrie:/ˈmɑːstəri/| n.| 技艺，掌握，精通 | Mastery is the skill or knowledge required to do something. It also refers to complete control or authority. |例句：He demonstrated mastery of the piano. 他展现了精湛的钢琴技艺。  |例句：She achieved mastery over her emotions. 她控制住了自己的情绪。 |近义词：expertise, proficiency, skill, command, dominion |反义词：ignorance, incompetence, ineptitude |名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
His mastery of the subject is impressive. = 他对这门学科的掌握令人印象深刻。
Her mastery of several languages is remarkable. = 她掌握多种语言令人惊叹。


一般过去时:
His mastery of the violin was evident in his performance. = 他小提琴演奏技艺的高超在他的表演中显而易见。
Her mastery of the situation prevented a crisis. = 她对局势的掌控避免了一场危机。


现在完成时:
She has achieved mastery in her chosen field. = 她已在她选择的领域达到了精通的境界。
He has demonstrated mastery of several programming languages. = 他已经展现了他精通几种编程语言。


过去完成时:
By the time of the competition, she had achieved mastery of the technique. = 到比赛的时候，她已经掌握了这项技术。
Before the presentation, he had developed mastery of the subject matter. = 在演示之前，他已经对主题有了深入的了解。


将来时:

Through dedicated study, she will attain mastery of the subject. = 通过刻苦学习，她将精通这门学科。


虚拟语气:

If she had dedicated more time, she would have attained greater mastery. = 如果她投入更多时间，她本可以达到更高的水平。


被动语态:
Mastery of the language was required for the job. = 这份工作需要掌握这门外语。

否定句:
