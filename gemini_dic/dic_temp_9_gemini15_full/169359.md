promulgations:/ˌprɑːmʌlˈɡeɪʃənz/ | n. |颁布；公布；宣传 | The official promulgations of the new laws were met with mixed reactions.  官方公布新法律的举措受到了褒贬不一的反应。 | 近义词：announcement, declaration, publication | 反义词：suppression, concealment | 名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government promulgates new policies regularly. = 政府定期颁布新政策。
These regulations are frequently promulgated. = 这些规章制度经常被公布。


一般过去时:
The company promulgated its new marketing strategy last week. = 公司上周公布了新的营销策略。
They promulgated the results of the election immediately. = 他们立即公布了选举结果。


现在完成时:
The authorities have promulgated several important decrees this year. = 当局今年已经颁布了几项重要法令。
The university has promulgated a new set of admission requirements. = 大学已经公布了一套新的入学要求。


过去完成时:
The court had promulgated its decision before the public outcry. = 法院在公众抗议之前已经公布了判决。
Before the meeting, they had promulgated the agenda. = 会议之前，他们已经公布了议程。


将来时:
The president will promulgate the new economic plan next month. = 总统下个月将公布新的经济计划。
They will promulgate the findings of their research soon. = 他们很快就会公布他们的研究结果。


虚拟语气:
If the government promulgated fairer laws, there would be less unrest. = 如果政府颁布更公平的法律，社会动荡就会减少。
I wish they would promulgate clear guidelines for this process. = 我希望他们能为这个过程制定明确的指导方针。


被动语态:
The new rules were promulgated by the governing body. = 新规则是由管理机构颁布的。
The decision was promulgated to the public via a press release. = 这一决定通过新闻发布的方式向公众公布。


疑问句:
When will the revised regulations be promulgated? = 修订后的规章制度何时公布？
Have the changes to the law been promulgated yet? = 法律的修改已经公布了吗？


否定句:
The company did not promulgate the changes to its policy on time. = 公司没有按时公布其政策的变更。
The government has not yet promulgated the details of the new program. = 政府尚未公布该新项目的细节。


PHRASE->
promulgation of laws = 法律的颁布
promulgation of regulations = 规章制度的公布
promulgation of a doctrine = 教义的传播
wide promulgation = 广泛传播
official promulgation = 正式公布

词源:
From Latin *promulgare (to make known, publish), from pro- (“forward”) + vulgare (“to make public”).
