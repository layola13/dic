colonizing:/ˈkɑːlənaɪzɪŋ/ | v. |殖民；拓殖；占据；移居 |The act of establishing a colony or colonies in a new territory.  It often involves the imposition of the colonizer's culture, political system, and economic structures on the indigenous population. |例句：European powers were colonizing vast swathes of Africa during the 19th century. = 19世纪，欧洲列强正在殖民非洲的大片土地。

例句：The company is colonizing the market with its new product. = 这家公司正在用它的新产品占据市场。


近义词：settling, occupying, appropriating, establishing


反义词：decolonizing, liberating, emancipating


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They are colonizing the island. = 他们在殖民这个岛屿。
We are colonizing the new software. = 我们正在推广新软件。


一般过去时:
The British colonized India. = 英国殖民了印度。
They colonized the western territories. = 他们殖民了西部领土。


现在完成时:
They have colonized much of the continent. = 他们已经殖民了这片大陆的大部分地区。
The company has successfully colonized the market. = 这家公司成功地占据了市场。


过去完成时:

The explorers had colonized the new land before the settlers arrived. = 探险家们在定居者到达之前已经殖民了这片新土地。


将来时:

虚拟语气:
