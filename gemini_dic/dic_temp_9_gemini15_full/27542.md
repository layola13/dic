can-do:/kænˈduː/| adj.|能干的；积极主动的；有能力的 |Having the ability and willingness to succeed; positive and proactive.|  The team has a strong can-do attitude.  这个团队有一种积极主动的态度。|近义词：capable, competent, proactive, efficient |反义词：incapable, incompetent, passive, inefficient


USAGE->
一般现在时:
She is a can-do person. = 她是一个能干的人。
They have a can-do spirit. = 他们有积极进取的精神。

一般过去时:
He was a can-do employee. = 他是一位能干的员工。
The project was completed thanks to their can-do attitude. = 多亏了他们积极主动的态度，项目才得以完成。

现在完成时:
They have shown a can-do attitude throughout the project. = 在整个项目中，他们都表现出了积极主动的态度。
We have always believed in our team's can-do spirit. = 我们一直相信我们团队的积极进取的精神。

过去完成时:
They had already demonstrated a can-do attitude before the challenge. = 在挑战之前，他们已经展现出积极主动的态度。
We had been impressed by their can-do approach to problem-solving. = 我们一直对他们积极主动的解决问题的方法印象深刻。

将来时:
They will be a can-do team in the future. = 他们将来会是一个能干的团队。
The company will adopt a can-do approach to innovation. = 公司将采取积极进取的创新方法。

虚拟语气:
If only we had a more can-do manager. = 如果我们有一个更能干的经理就好了。
It would be great if they had a more can-do attitude. = 如果他们能更积极主动一些就好了。


被动语态:

Their can-do spirit was praised by the manager. = 他们的积极进取的精神受到经理的赞扬。


疑问句:
