catatonia: /ˌkætəˈtoʊniə/ | n. | 紧张症，僵硬症 | A state of immobility and unresponsiveness, often associated with schizophrenia or other mental disorders.  It is characterized by a lack of movement and responsiveness, sometimes accompanied by mutism, rigidity, or bizarre postures. | Example:  The patient presented with catatonia, exhibiting waxy flexibility and mutism.  患者表现出紧张症，表现为蜡样屈曲和缄默症。|近义词：stupor, rigidity, akinesia | 反义词：mobility, responsiveness

词源:
