housefly: /ˈhaʊsˌflaɪ/ | n. |家蝇|A common fly found in houses and other buildings, known for its buzzing sound and tendency to spread diseases.  It feeds on decaying organic matter and excrement.|例句：The housefly landed on the food, making everyone feel disgusted.  家蝇落在了食物上，让每个人都感到恶心。


例句：We need to keep our kitchen clean to prevent houseflies from breeding. 我们需要保持厨房清洁，以防止家蝇繁殖。

词源:
The word "housefly" is a compound word.  "House" refers to its common habitat, and "fly" is the general term for a two-winged insect.  The etymology of "fly" traces back to Old English *flēogan, related to other Germanic words for flying insects.
