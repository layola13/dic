constituted:/ˈkɒnstɪtjuːtɪd/ /ˈkɑːnstɪtuːtɪd/ | v. | 组成；构成；建立；制定；任命 | To compose or make up (a whole); to establish or create (something, typically an organization or system); to appoint or elect (someone) to a position.  |  The jury was constituted of twelve members. 陪审团由十二名成员组成。

The club was constituted in 1980.  这个俱乐部成立于1980年。 | constitute, form, compose, establish, create |  destroy, abolish, dismantle | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The committee constitutes five members. = 委员会由五名成员组成。
This evidence constitutes a strong case against him. = 这证据构成了对他强有力的指控。

一般过去时:
The founding fathers constituted the new nation. = 建国元勋组建了新国家。
They constituted a new government. = 他们组建了一个新政府。

现在完成时:
These actions have constituted a breach of contract. = 这些行为构成违约。
His actions have constituted a crime. = 他的行为构成犯罪。

过去完成时:
Before the war, the army had been constituted primarily of conscripts. = 战争前，军队主要由应征入伍者组成。
The team had been constituted months before the competition. = 团队在比赛前几个月就已组建完毕。

将来时:
The new parliament will be constituted next month. = 新议会将于下个月组成。
The jury will be constituted from a pool of potential jurors. = 陪审团将从一批潜在陪审员中选出。

虚拟语气:
If the treaty were constituted differently, the outcome might have been better. = 如果条约的构成有所不同，结果可能会更好。
If the team had been constituted differently, they might have won. = 如果团队的构成有所不同，他们可能会赢。

被动语态:
The team was constituted by the coach. = 球队是由教练组建的。
The new government was constituted after the election. = 大选后组建了新政府。

疑问句:
How was the committee constituted? = 委员会是如何组成的？
What factors constituted their decision? = 什么因素构成了他们的决定？


否定句:
This does not constitute a legal agreement. = 这不构成法律协议。
Their actions did not constitute a threat. = 他们的行为并不构成威胁。
