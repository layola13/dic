quasi-practical:/ˈkwɑːzaɪ ˈpræktɪkəl/| adj. |准实用的，似是而非实用的 |Approaching but not fully achieving practicality; seemingly practical but lacking in true effectiveness or application.  It suggests something that has some practical aspects but is ultimately flawed or incomplete in its practicality.| The proposed solution, while quasi-practical, lacked the necessary resources for implementation.  这个提议的方案虽然看似实用，但却缺乏必要的实施资源。|近义词：theoretical, partly practical, seemingly practical |反义词：practical, fully functional, completely effective

例句:

The new design, while quasi-practical, proved too complex and expensive to manufacture.  新的设计虽然看似实用，但证明过于复杂和昂贵而无法制造。

His quasi-practical approach to problem-solving often led to more problems than solutions. 他似是而非的解决问题的方法往往导致更多的问题而不是解决方案。


词源：

* **quasi-**:  From Latin *quasi*, meaning "as if," "as it were," "almost," or "virtually."  It indicates a resemblance or approximation to something else.

* **practical**: From Middle English *practic*, from Old French *pratique*, from Late Latin *practicus*, from Greek *praktikos*, from *praktos* ("done," "performed").


用法说明:

`quasi-practical` is primarily used as an adjective to describe ideas, plans, methods, or solutions that appear practical on the surface but ultimately fall short of true practicality due to limitations, flaws, or incomplete implementation.  It highlights a gap between the appearance of practicality and the reality of its effectiveness.
