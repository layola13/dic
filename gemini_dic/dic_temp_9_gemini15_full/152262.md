patronizes:/ˈpætrənaɪzɪz/ | vt. | 屈尊俯就；以高人一等的态度对待；惠顾；资助 | To treat (someone) in a way that shows you believe you are superior to them. To act condescendingly toward. To support or provide financial assistance to (a person or organization). |例句：He patronizes his employees, treating them like children. 他以高人一等的态度对待他的员工，把他们当孩子一样看待。  She patronizes the local bakery every Saturday. 她每个星期六都光顾当地那家面包店。| 近义词：condescends, talks down to, supports, sponsors | 反义词：treats equally, respects


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He patronizes local businesses. = 他惠顾当地企业。
She patronizes the arts. = 她资助艺术事业。


一般过去时:
They patronized the restaurant for years. = 他们多年来一直惠顾这家餐馆。

过去完成时:

虚拟语气:

被动语态:

词源:
Patronizes comes from the Old French word "patron," meaning "master" or "protector," which in turn derives from the Latin "patronus," meaning "protector," "defender," or "sponsor."  The verb form developed from the noun, signifying the act of acting as a patron.
