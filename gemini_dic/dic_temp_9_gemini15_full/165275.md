preintelligences:/ˌpriːɪnˈtɛlɪdʒənsɪz/ | n. |前智力，前智能|  A hypothetical stage of intelligence preceding fully developed human intelligence, often involving rudimentary forms of problem-solving, tool use, or social cooperation observed in some animals.  The term suggests that human intelligence may have evolved from more basic cognitive capacities. | Example:  The study of primate behavior offers clues to the possible preintelligences that paved the way for human cognitive development.  前灵长类动物的行为研究为人类认知发展铺平了道路的可能的早期智力提供了线索。|近义词：protointelligence, early intelligence, rudimentary intelligence | 反义词：highly developed intelligence, advanced intelligence | 名词
