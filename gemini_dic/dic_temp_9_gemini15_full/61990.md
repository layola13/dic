enacture:/ɪˈnæktʃər/ | n. | 制定；颁布；法令；法案 | The enactment of new laws is a complex process. 新法律的颁布是一个复杂的过程。 | 近义词：enactment, legislation, ordinance | 反义词：repeal, abolition


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government enacts new laws regularly. = 政府定期颁布新法律。
They enact policies to improve public health. = 他们制定政策以改善公共卫生。

一般过去时:
The parliament enacted the bill last week. = 议会在上周通过了该法案。
The court enacted a new ruling on the case. = 法院就该案做出了新的裁决。

现在完成时:
They have enacted several important reforms this year. = 他们今年已经实施了几项重要的改革。
The legislature has enacted a new tax law. = 立法机关已经颁布了一项新的税法。

过去完成时:
The company had enacted a new policy before the incident. = 事件发生前，公司已经实施了一项新政策。
They had enacted strict rules about data security. = 他们之前已经制定了关于数据安全的严格规定。

将来时:
The council will enact stricter environmental regulations next year. = 理事会明年将实施更严格的环境法规。
They will enact a new law to address the issue. = 他们将颁布一项新法律来解决这个问题。

虚拟语气:
If they enacted stricter laws, crime might decrease. = 如果他们制定更严格的法律，犯罪率可能会下降。
I wish they had enacted this policy sooner. = 我希望他们早点实施这项政策。

被动语态:
New laws are enacted by the legislature. = 新法律由立法机关颁布。
The policy was enacted without public consultation. = 该政策未经公众协商就已颁布。

疑问句:
When was the law enacted? = 这项法律是什么时候颁布的？
Has the new regulation been enacted yet? = 新规已经颁布了吗？

否定句:
The government did not enact the proposed bill. = 政府没有颁布该提案。
They haven't enacted any new environmental laws recently. = 他们最近没有颁布任何新的环境法律。
