persecuting: /ˈpɜːrsɪkjuːtɪŋ/ | v. |迫害，追捕 |  The act of subjecting someone to hostility and ill-treatment, usually based on their race, religion, or other beliefs.  It implies a systematic and ongoing campaign of oppression. |例句：The regime was persecuting religious minorities.  这个政权正在迫害宗教少数群体。


例句：They were persecuted for their beliefs.  他们因为信仰而受到迫害。


近义词：oppressing, harassing, tormenting, victimizing


反义词：protecting, defending, aiding, supporting


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government persecutes political dissidents. = 政府迫害政治异议人士。
He persecutes his employees with unreasonable demands. = 他用不合理的要求迫害他的员工。


一般过去时:
The church persecuted Galileo for his scientific findings. = 教会因为伽利略的科学发现而迫害了他。
They persecuted the family for generations. = 他们世世代代迫害这个家庭。


现在完成时:
They have persecuted this community for decades. = 几十年来他们一直在迫害这个社区。
The authorities have persecuted him relentlessly. = 当局一直无情地迫害他。


过去完成时:
The people had been persecuted for centuries before the revolution. = 在革命之前，人民已经遭受了几个世纪的迫害。
They had long persecuted those who disagreed with them. = 他们长期以来一直迫害那些与他们意见相左的人。


将来时:
They will continue to persecute those who oppose them. = 他们将继续迫害那些反对他们的人。
The dictator will persecute anyone who challenges his power. = 独裁者将迫害任何挑战他权力的人。


虚拟语气:
If they were in power, they would persecute their enemies. = 如果他们掌权，他们就会迫害他们的敌人。
I wish they wouldn't persecute those innocent people. = 我希望他们不要迫害那些无辜的人。


被动语态:
Religious minorities were persecuted throughout history. = 历史上宗教少数群体一直受到迫害。
He was persecuted for speaking out against the government. = 他因为公开反对政府而受到迫害。


疑问句:
Were they persecuted for their political views? = 他们因为政治观点而受到迫害吗？
Are they still being persecuted today? = 他们今天还在受迫害吗？


否定句:
They did not persecute the refugees. = 他们没有迫害难民。
The government does not persecute its citizens based on their religion. = 政府不会因为公民的宗教信仰而迫害他们。


PHRASE->
persecute relentlessly = 无情地迫害
persecute unjustly = 不公正地迫害
persecute systematically = 系统地迫害
persecute for one's beliefs = 因为信仰而受迫害
persecute without mercy = 毫不留情地迫害


词源:
From Old French *persecutir, from Latin *persequi "to follow closely, pursue," from per- "through, thoroughly" + sequi "to follow."  The sense shifted from "to pursue" to "to persecute" over time.
