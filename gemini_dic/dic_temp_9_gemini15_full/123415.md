monoplanes:/ˈmɒnəpleɪnz/|/ˈmɑːnəpleɪnz/| n. |单翼飞机|A monoplane is an aircraft with only one main wing.  This contrasts with biplanes (two wings) and other multi-wing aircraft. Monoplanes are the most common type of airplane used today, offering advantages in terms of aerodynamics and structural simplicity. |Example: The Wright brothers' first successful airplane was a monoplane.  莱特兄弟第一次成功的飞机是一架单翼飞机。The small monoplane soared gracefully through the air. 这架小型单翼飞机优雅地翱翔在空中。|近义词：single-wing aircraft|反义词：biplane, triplane
