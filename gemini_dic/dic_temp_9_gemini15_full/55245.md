dissimilitude:/ˌdɪsɪˈmɪlɪtjuːd/| n. |差异；不同；不相像 |The dissimilitude between the two paintings is striking.  两幅画之间的差异非常明显。|近义词：difference, disparity, divergence |反义词：similarity, resemblance, likeness


英文解析:Dissimilitude refers to a lack of resemblance or similarity; a difference or unlikeness between things. It emphasizes the distinctness and contrast between items, often highlighting their incompatibility or opposition.


例句:

The dissimilitude in their opinions led to a heated debate. 他们意见上的差异导致了一场激烈的辩论。

There was a marked dissimilitude between the original manuscript and the published version. 原稿和出版版本之间存在明显的差异。


词源:

The word "dissimilitude" is derived from the Old French word "dissimilitude," which itself comes from the Latin words "dis-" (meaning "not" or "apart") and "similitudo" (meaning "likeness" or "similarity").  Therefore, the word literally means "not likeness."
