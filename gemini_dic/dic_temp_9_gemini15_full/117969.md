memoirist:/ˈmɛmərwərɪst/| n. |回忆录作者|A memoirist is a person who writes memoirs, which are narratives based on the author's personal experiences and memories.  Memoirs often focus on specific periods or events in the author's life, and they can offer insights into the author's personal growth, relationships, and perspectives on the world. Unlike autobiographies, which aim for comprehensive coverage of a life, memoirs are typically more focused and thematic.|The memoirist spent years compiling her memories into a compelling narrative.|autobiographer, diarist|—|
