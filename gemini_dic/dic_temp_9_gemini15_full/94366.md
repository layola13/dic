implacentalia:/ˌɪmˌpleɪsənˈteɪliə/| n. |有胎盘类动物|Placental mammals are mammals that nourish their young through a placenta.  They are characterized by the presence of a placenta, a structure that allows for the exchange of nutrients and waste products between the mother and the developing fetus.  This group encompasses a vast majority of mammalian species, excluding monotremes and marsupials.|  Examples of placental mammals include humans, dogs, cats, elephants, whales, and bats. The diversity within this group is immense, reflecting adaptations to a wide range of habitats and lifestyles.|  近义词：Eutheria (a more technical term)|反义词：Monotremata, Marsupialia|名词
