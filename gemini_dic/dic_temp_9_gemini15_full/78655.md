giddy:/ˈɡɪdi/ | adj. | 头晕目眩的；轻飘飘的；兴奋的；轻率的 | Feeling or causing a sensation of whirling and a loss of balance; lightheaded.  Characterized by or causing giddy excitement or exhilaration; frivolous. |例句：

The roller coaster ride made me feel giddy. = 过山车让我感觉头晕目眩。

Her success made her feel giddy with excitement. = 她的成功让她兴奋得晕头转向。


近义词：dizzy, lightheaded, faint, frivolous, flighty | 反义词：sober, serious, grounded

词源:
The word "giddy" ultimately derives from the Old English word "gidig," meaning "dizzy" or "foolish."  This word is related to other Germanic words signifying a lack of balance or mental stability.  The modern meaning has expanded to include the sense of lighthearted excitement.
