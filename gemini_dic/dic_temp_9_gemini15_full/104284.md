justiciaries:/dʒʌˈstɪʃəriːz/| n. |法官；审判员；司法人员|Judges or magistrates, especially those forming a court.  The term often refers to a group of judges or a court with specific judicial authority.  The word emphasizes the judicial function and authority.|例句：The justiciaries convened to hear the appeal. = 法官们开庭审理上诉。

例句：The High Court of Justiciary in Scotland is the supreme criminal court. = 苏格兰最高法院是最高刑事法院。


近义词：judges, magistrates, justices, tribunals

反义词：defendant, accused, criminal


词性:名词

词源:
The word "justiciaries" derives from the Old French word "justicier," meaning "to do justice," which in turn originates from the Latin word "justitia," meaning "justice."  The "-aries" suffix indicates a group or body of people.
