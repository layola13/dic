radioisotope:/ˈreɪdioʊˌaɪsətoʊp/| n. |放射性同位素|A radioactive isotope is an atom with an unstable nucleus that emits radiation as it decays.  It is used in various applications, including medical imaging and treatment, industrial gauging, and scientific research.|例句：Doctors use radioisotopes in PET scans to visualize internal organs. 医生在PET扫描中使用放射性同位素来观察内部器官。|近义词： radioactive nuclide |反义词：stable isotope
