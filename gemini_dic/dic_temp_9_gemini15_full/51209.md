destitute:/ˈdɛstɪˌtjuːt/ | adj. | 贫困的；缺乏的；绝望的 |  Without the basic necessities of life; impoverished; lacking.  The word implies a severe lack of resources, often extending beyond mere poverty to encompass a state of hopelessness and despair. | Example:  After losing his job and his home, he found himself destitute and living on the streets.  =  在他失去工作和家园后，他发现自己一贫如洗，流落街头。

The family was destitute after the fire destroyed their home and belongings. = 大火烧毁了他们的家园和财物后，这家人变得一贫如洗。


近义词: impoverished, penniless, indigent, needy, impecunious | 反义词: wealthy, affluent, rich, prosperous


词源:  From Old French *destitut, past participle of destituer "to deprive, depose," from Latin destituere "to forsake, abandon, leave destitute," from de- "away, from" + statuere "to set, place, establish."  The sense development is from "abandoned, forsaken" to "lacking essential resources."
