signif:/ˈsɪɡnɪf/| v.|有意义；象征；表示|To be or have importance; to convey a meaning.|The discovery signifies a major breakthrough in the field.  这个发现标志着该领域的一次重大突破。|The symbol signifies peace. 这个符号象征和平。|imply, indicate, represent|insignificant, meaningless|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
This symbol signifies peace. = 这个符号象征着和平。
His actions signify his disapproval. = 他的行为表示他的反对。


一般过去时:
The painting signified a new artistic movement. = 这幅画标志着一种新的艺术运动。
Her silence signified agreement. = 她的沉默表示同意。


现在完成时:
Recent events have signified a shift in public opinion. = 最近发生的事件表明公众舆论发生了转变。
Her work has signified a major contribution to the field. = 她的工作对该领域做出了重大贡献。


过去完成时:
The old building had signified wealth and power. = 这座旧建筑曾经象征着财富和权力。
His early writings had signified a growing interest in philosophy. = 他早期的著作表明他对哲学越来越感兴趣。


将来时:
This discovery will signify a new era in medicine. = 这一发现将标志着医学进入一个新时代。
Her latest novel will signify a change in her writing style. = 她的最新小说将标志着她写作风格的改变。


虚拟语气:
If this discovery signified a cure, it would be revolutionary. = 如果这一发现意味着治愈疾病，那将是一场革命。
I wish my actions signified my true feelings. = 我希望我的行为能表达我的真实感受。


被动语态:
The event was signified by a large celebration. = 这件事以盛大的庆祝活动为标志。
His contribution is signified by a plaque on the wall. = 他的贡献在墙上刻有铭牌表示。


疑问句:
Does this gesture signify anything? = 这个手势意味着什么？
What does the dream signify? = 这个梦意味着什么？


否定句:
The results didn't signify anything conclusive. = 结果并没有说明任何结论性的东西。
His words didn't signify his true intentions. = 他的话语并没有表明他的真实意图。


PHRASE->
signify something to someone = 对某人来说有什么意义
signify one's approval/disapproval = 表示赞成/反对
signify one's intention = 表示某人的意图
signify one's presence = 表示某人的出席
signify consent = 表示同意
