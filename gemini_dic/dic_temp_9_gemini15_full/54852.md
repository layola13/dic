display: /dɪˈspleɪ/ | vt. n. | 显示；展示；陈列 | To show or reveal something; to exhibit something publicly. |例句：The museum displays a wide collection of ancient artifacts.  博物馆展出了大量古代文物。

例句：He proudly displayed his award to his friends. 他骄傲地向朋友们展示了他的奖项。


近义词：show, exhibit, present, showcase | 反义词：conceal, hide


词性：动词，名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He displays his paintings in the gallery. 他在画廊展出他的画作。
The store displays new products every week. 商店每周都展示新产品。

一般过去时:
She displayed her talent at the competition. 她在比赛中展示了她的才能。
The company displayed its new logo at the conference. 公司在会议上展示了它的新标志。

现在完成时:
They have displayed their commitment to environmental protection. 他们已经展示了他们对环境保护的承诺。
The artist has displayed his work in numerous galleries.这位艺术家已经在许多画廊展出过他的作品。

过去完成时:
They had displayed the artwork before the VIP guests arrived. 贵宾到来之前，他们已经展示了艺术品。
The museum had displayed the dinosaur fossils for many years. 博物馆已经展出了多年的恐龙化石。

将来时:
We will display the results of the study next month. 我们将在下个月展示这项研究的结果。
The company will display its latest technology at the trade show. 公司将在贸易展会上展示其最新的技术。

虚拟语气:
If they displayed more enthusiasm, they would get better results. 如果他们表现出更多热情，他们会取得更好的成绩。
I wish they displayed more respect for others. 我希望他们能对他人表示更多尊重。

被动语态:
The paintings were displayed in a prominent location.  这些画被摆放在显眼的位置。
The products are displayed attractively in the shop windows. 产品在商店橱窗里被吸引人的展示出来。

疑问句:
Where will the products be displayed? 产品将在哪里展出？
How are the exhibits displayed in the museum? 博物馆里的展品是如何陈列的？

否定句:
He didn't display any emotion during the interview. 他在面试中没有表现出任何情绪。
The company chose not to display its financial results. 公司选择不公开其财务业绩。
