poststimulus: /ˌpoʊstˈstɪmjʊləs/ | adj. | 刺激后；刺激后的 | Poststimulus refers to the period of time or events that occur after a stimulus has been applied or experienced.  It is often used in research contexts, particularly in neuroscience and psychology, to describe the effects or responses following a specific sensory input or event. | Example: Poststimulus activity in the brain was monitored to assess the impact of the visual cue. 刺激后的大脑活动被监测以评估视觉提示的影响。| 近义词：post-stimulus, after-stimulus | 反义词：prestimulus
