extinctions:/ɪkˈstɪŋkʃənz/ | n. |物种灭绝；绝种 | The plural form of "extinction," referring to multiple instances of species dying out.  It encompasses the complete disappearance of a species from Earth.  The current mass extinction event is largely attributed to human activities. | 例句：The ongoing extinctions of numerous animal species are a serious environmental concern. = 众多动物物种持续不断的灭绝是一个严重的环保问题。

The extinctions that occurred at the end of the Cretaceous period wiped out the dinosaurs. = 白垩纪末期发生的物种灭绝事件消灭了恐龙。|近义词：species loss, die-offs, disappearances |反义词：survival, proliferation, flourishing |名词

词源:
The word "extinctions" derives from the Latin word "extinctus," the past participle of "extinguere," meaning "to extinguish," "put out," or "destroy."  This reflects the complete cessation of a species' existence.
