disclosed:/dɪsˈkloʊzd/ | v. | 公开；泄露；揭露 | To make something known that was previously secret or unknown. | The company disclosed its financial results. 公司公布了它的财务业绩。 | revealed, unveiled, divulged | concealed, hidden, suppressed | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He discloses his secrets to his best friend. = 他向他最好的朋友透露了他的秘密。
The company discloses its financial information annually. = 公司每年都会披露其财务信息。


一般过去时:
She disclosed the truth to the police. = 她向警方透露了真相。
The journalist disclosed a major scandal. = 记者披露了一宗重大丑闻。


现在完成时:
They have disclosed all the relevant information. = 他们已经披露了所有相关信息。
The government has disclosed its plan to tackle inflation. = 政府已经公布了其应对通货膨胀的计划。


过去完成时:
He had disclosed the information before the meeting. = 他在会议之前就已透露了该信息。
The witness had disclosed crucial evidence to the investigators. = 证人在调查人员面前已经披露了关键证据。


将来时:
The company will disclose its earnings next week. = 公司下周将公布其收益。
The scientist will disclose his findings at the conference. = 科学家将在会议上公布他的研究结果。


虚拟语气:
If he disclosed the truth, he would be in trouble. = 如果他透露真相，他将会惹上麻烦。
I wish I had disclosed the problem earlier. = 我希望我早点说出这个问题。


被动语态:
The information was disclosed to the public. = 信息已向公众公开。
The details of the agreement were disclosed in the press. = 该协议的细节已在媒体上公布。


疑问句:
Has the company disclosed its profits yet? = 公司是否已经公布了其利润？
Will the results be disclosed soon? = 结果会很快公布吗？


否定句:
He did not disclose his identity. = 他没有透露他的身份。
The government refused to disclose any further details. = 政府拒绝透露任何进一步的细节。


PHRASE->
disclose a secret = 泄露秘密
disclose information = 透露信息
fully disclose = 全面披露
disclose the truth = 揭露真相
publicly disclose = 公开披露
