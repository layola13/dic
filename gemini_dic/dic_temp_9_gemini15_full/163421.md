preconstructs:/ˌpriːˈkɒnstrʌkts/| n.|预设概念，预先构建，先有观念|Preconstructs are preconceived notions or ideas that individuals hold before encountering new information or experiences.  These can significantly influence how information is interpreted and understood. They can be based on prior learning, cultural background, or personal experiences.|Example:  His preconstructs about the project made him resistant to new ideas.  他的预设观念让他抗拒新的想法。|近义词：preconceptions, presuppositions, assumptions|反义词：open-mindedness, objectivity
