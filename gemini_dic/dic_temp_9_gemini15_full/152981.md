pedlar:/ˈpedlə(r)/| n. |小贩，货郎 |A pedlar is a person who travels from place to place selling small goods.  They often carry their wares in a pack or on a cart.  The term often evokes images of a travelling salesperson, sometimes implying a less reputable or itinerant nature compared to a stationary shopkeeper.|例句：The pedlar offered me a colourful scarf.  这个小贩给我看一条色彩鲜艳的围巾。

The old pedlar walked from village to village, selling his wares.这位年迈的小贩挨家挨户地走，兜售他的货物。|近义词：hawker, vendor, itinerant merchant |反义词：shopkeeper, retailer
