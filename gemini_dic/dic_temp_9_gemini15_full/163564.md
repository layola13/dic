precreditor: /ˌpriːˈkredɪtər/ | n. | 预期债权人，潜在债权人 | A person or entity expected to become a creditor in the future, often in the context of a potential acquisition or merger.  This term is less common and often used in specialized financial contexts.  It describes an entity that is likely to become a creditor based on anticipated future transactions or events. | Example:  The due diligence process identified several precreditors who might finance the expansion project.  =尽职调查过程确定了几位可能为扩张项目提供资金的潜在债权人。 | 近义词: prospective creditor, potential creditor | 反义词: debtor, obligor | 词性: noun
