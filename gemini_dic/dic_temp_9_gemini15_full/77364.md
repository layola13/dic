gauged:/ɡeɪdʒd/| v.|测量，估计，判断|To measure or calculate the size, amount, or degree of something.|The engineer gauged the depth of the river. 工程师测量了河的深度。|近义词：measure, estimate, assess|反义词：ignore, neglect


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He gauges the distance accurately. 他精确地测量距离。
She gauges the public mood. 她揣摩民意。

一般过去时:
They gauged the amount of rainfall. 他们测量了降雨量。
The mechanic gauged the tire pressure. 技工测量了轮胎气压。

现在完成时:
We have gauged the market's potential. 我们已经评估了市场的潜力。
She has gauged the impact of the new policy. 她已经评估了新政策的影响。

过去完成时:
They had gauged the risk before investing. 他们在投资前已经评估了风险。
He had gauged the situation incorrectly. 他之前错误地判断了形势。

将来时:
We will gauge the effectiveness of the treatment. 我们将评估治疗的有效性。
They will gauge the response to the new product. 他们将评估新产品的反响。

虚拟语气:
If I had gauged the situation correctly, I would have acted differently. 如果我正确地判断了形势，我会采取不同的行动。
I wish I had gauged the depth of the problem. 我希望我当时能评估问题的严重性。

被动语态:
The water level was gauged regularly. 水位定期测量。
The public reaction was gauged by the pollsters. 民众反应由民调专家进行评估。

疑问句:
How did you gauge the effectiveness of the training? 你是如何评估培训效果的？
How is the success of the project being gauged? 项目的成功是如何衡量的？

否定句:
They did not accurately gauge the public's reaction. 他们没有准确地评估公众的反应。
I failed to gauge the difficulty of the task. 我没能评估任务的难度。
