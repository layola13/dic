reconceptualize:/ˌriːkənˈsɛptʃʊəlaɪz/| vt.|重新概念化；重新构想|To rethink or reformulate a concept or idea.  It involves a fundamental shift in understanding, often challenging existing assumptions and perspectives. |例句：We need to reconceptualize our approach to education to meet the challenges of the 21st century. 我们需要重新构想我们的教育方法，以应对21世纪的挑战。

例句：The artist reconceptualized the traditional portrait, using unconventional materials and techniques.这位艺术家重新构想了传统的肖像画，使用了非常规的材料和技巧。


近义词：rethink, reformulate, reimagine, reinterpret, revise


反义词：maintain, perpetuate (in the context of maintaining an existing concept)


词性:动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They reconceptualize their marketing strategy every year. = 他们每年都会重新构想他们的营销策略。
The company reconceptualizes its product line to attract younger consumers. = 公司正在重新构想其产品线以吸引更年轻的消费者。


一般过去时:
She reconceptualized the novel's plot after receiving feedback from her editor. = 在收到编辑的反馈后，她重新构想了小说的情节。
The researchers reconceptualized their experiment design to improve its accuracy. = 研究人员重新设计了他们的实验方案以提高其准确性。


现在完成时:
The team has reconceptualized its project goals several times. = 该团队已经多次重新构想其项目目标。
They have reconceptualized the entire learning process to make it more engaging. = 他们已经重新构想了整个学习过程，使其更具吸引力。


过去完成时:
The artist had reconceptualized his work before the exhibition opened. = 在展览开幕之前，这位艺术家已经重新构想了他的作品。
They had reconceptualized the software architecture before launching the new version. = 在发布新版本之前，他们已经重新设计了软件架构。


将来时:

The company will reconceptualize its brand image to improve its public perception. = 公司将重新构想其品牌形象以改善公众对其的认知。


虚拟语气:
If we reconceptualized the problem, we might find a better solution. = 如果我们重新构想这个问题，我们可能会找到更好的解决方案。

被动语态:
