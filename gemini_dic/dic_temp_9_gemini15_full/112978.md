lullabies:/ˈlʌləbaɪz/| n. |催眠曲，摇篮曲 |Gentle songs sung to soothe babies to sleep.  They often have repetitive melodies and simple lyrics.| 例句：My mother used to sing me lullabies every night. = 我妈妈过去每天晚上都给我唱摇篮曲。

The sweet lullabies calmed the crying baby. = 甜美的摇篮曲使哭闹的婴儿平静下来。|近义词：cradle songs, nursery rhymes |反义词：None (反义词的概念在此处不适用)

词源:
The word "lullaby" originates from the Old English phrase "lulle aby," where "lulle" was an interjection used to soothe a baby, similar to "hush" or "shhh," and "aby" meant "baby."  Over time, the phrase evolved into the modern word "lullaby."
