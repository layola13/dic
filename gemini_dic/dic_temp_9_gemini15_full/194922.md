semi-intellectual:/ˌsɛmiɪnˈtɛlektʃuəl/| adj.| 半知识分子；有点学问的 |  Someone who is semi-intellectual has some intellectual interests and abilities but is not highly intellectual or scholarly. They might enjoy reading and discussing ideas but lack the depth of knowledge or rigorous thinking of a true intellectual.  |  He's a semi-intellectual, more interested in appearing knowledgeable than actually being so. 他是个半知识分子，更感兴趣的是显得博学而不是真正博学。  |近义词：intellectualish, dilettante | 反义词：intellectual, unintellectual

词源:
The word "semi-intellectual" is a compound word.  "Semi-" is a prefix derived from Latin meaning "half" or "partly," while "intellectual" comes from the Latin "intellectus," meaning "understanding" or "intelligence."  Therefore, "semi-intellectual" literally means "partly intellectual."
