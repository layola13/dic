oralists:/ˈɔːrəlɪsts/| n. |口语使用者；口语论者|Supporters of oral methods of teaching the deaf; those who advocate teaching deaf people through spoken language rather than sign language.  Oralists believe that deaf individuals should primarily rely on speech and lip-reading to communicate. They often oppose or minimize the use of sign language in education.|The oralists debated the merits of their approach against those who favored sign language.|近义词：speech advocates, proponents of oralism|反义词：manualists
