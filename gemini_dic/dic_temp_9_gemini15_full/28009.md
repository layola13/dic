capab:/ˈkeɪpəb/ | adj. | 有能力的，能够的 | capable means having the ability to do something. It describes someone or something that possesses the necessary skills, qualities, or resources to accomplish a task or achieve a goal. |  He is capable of great things. 他有能力做大事。 She is a capable manager. 她是一位有能力的经理。 | able, competent | incapable, unable


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is capable of finishing the project on time. = 他有能力按时完成这个项目。
She is capable of speaking three languages. = 她能够说三种语言。

一般过去时:
He was capable of running a marathon. = 他曾经有能力跑马拉松。
She was capable of handling the pressure. = 她有能力应对压力。

现在完成时:
They have been capable of achieving great success. = 他们一直有能力取得巨大成功。
He has proven himself capable of leading the team. = 他已经证明自己有能力领导团队。

过去完成时:
She had been capable of playing the piano since childhood. = 她从小就有能力弹钢琴。
He had been capable of understanding complex concepts. = 他有能力理解复杂的知识。

将来时:
They will be capable of building a better future. = 他们将能够创造更美好的未来。
She will be capable of taking on more responsibility. = 她将能够承担更多责任。

虚拟语气:
If he were capable of it, he would help. = 如果他有能力的话，他会帮忙。
If she had been capable, she would have finished the task. = 如果她有能力的话，她就完成了任务。

被动语态:
The task was deemed capable of being completed quickly. = 这项任务被认为能够快速完成。
He was considered capable of solving the problem. = 他被认为有能力解决这个问题。

疑问句:
Is he capable of doing this job? = 他有能力做这份工作吗？
Are you capable of understanding the instructions? = 你能理解这些指示吗？

否定句:
He is not capable of lifting that heavy box. = 他没能力搬动那个沉重的箱子。
She is incapable of completing the assignment on time. = 她无法按时完成作业。
