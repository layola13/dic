/ˌoʊvoʊvaɪvɪˈpærɪzəm/ | n. | 卵胎生 | Ovoviviparity is a form of reproduction where fertilized eggs are retained within the female's body until they hatch, with the developing embryos deriving nourishment from the egg yolk rather than directly from the mother.  Unlike viviparity (live birth), there is no placental connection. |  Many species of sharks exhibit ovoviviparity, retaining their eggs internally until they hatch.  Many snakes also use this method. | viviparity, live birth | oviparity (egg-laying) |  名词
