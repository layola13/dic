pestalozzianism:/ˌpɛstəˈlɒziənɪzəm/| n. |培斯泰洛齐教育思想；直观教学法|The educational philosophy of Johann Heinrich Pestalozzi, emphasizing sensory experience, intuition, and the development of the whole child.  It focuses on learning through observation and activity, rather than rote memorization. Pestalozzianism advocates for a holistic approach to education, addressing the intellectual, moral, and physical development of the student.|His teaching methods were strongly influenced by Pestalozzianism, emphasizing hands-on learning and individual attention.|近义词：child-centered education, progressive education|反义词：rote learning, traditional education
