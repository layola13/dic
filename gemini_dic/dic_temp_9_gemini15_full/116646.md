matter-of-fact:/ˈmætər əv fækt/| adj.| 事实上的；平淡的；实事求是的 | Describing something presented in a straightforward, unemotional way, without exaggeration or subjective opinion. It emphasizes the factual nature of something, often lacking emotional expression or personal involvement. |例句：He gave a matter-of-fact account of the accident. 他对事故做了实事求是的描述。

例句：She spoke in a matter-of-fact tone, without any emotion. 她语气平淡地说着，没有任何情绪。

近义词：factual, realistic, unemotional, objective, plain

反义词：emotional, subjective, dramatic, exaggerated, fanciful


词源:  The phrase "matter of fact" originates from the phrase "as a matter of fact," which dates back to the 16th century.  Initially, it meant "in reality" or "actually." Over time, it evolved into an adjective describing a manner of speaking or writing characterized by a focus on facts and a lack of emotional coloring.
