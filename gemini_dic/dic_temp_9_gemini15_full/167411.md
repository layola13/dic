prevaccinate:/ˌpriːˈvæksɪneɪt/| vt.| 预先接种疫苗 | To vaccinate (someone) before exposure to a disease.  This is often done to provide protection before travel to a high-risk area or before a potential outbreak. |例句：The travelers were prevaccinated against yellow fever before their trip to Africa. 旅行者在去非洲旅行之前接种了黄热病疫苗。|例句：The healthcare workers were prevaccinated to protect them during the influenza pandemic. 医疗工作者在流感大流行期间进行了预防性接种疫苗。|近义词：pre-immunize, prophylactically vaccinate |反义词：post-vaccinate
