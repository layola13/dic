expedient:/ɪkˈspiːdiənt/| adj., n. |权宜之计；方便的；有效的 |A method or course of action that is convenient and practical but possibly improper or immoral.  An expedient solution is one that solves a problem quickly, but may not be the best long-term solution. |例句：Taking a shortcut was an expedient, but it added to the overall travel time.  抄近路是个权宜之计，但这增加了总的旅行时间。  The government adopted an expedient measure to control inflation. 政府采取了一项权宜措施来控制通货膨胀。|近义词：practical, convenient, advantageous, resourceful |反义词：inefficient, impractical, ineffective, disadvantageous |

词源:
From Middle French *expedient, from Latin *expedire "to extricate, disentangle," from ex- "out" + ped- "foot."  The sense evolution is from "to free" to "to facilitate" to "a means of facilitating."
