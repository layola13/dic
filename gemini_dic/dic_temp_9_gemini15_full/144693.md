ovalbumen:/ˌoʊvəlˈbjuːmən/| n. |卵清蛋白|Ovalbumin is the major protein of egg white, comprising roughly 55% of its total protein content.  It's a glycoprotein and a phosphoprotein, meaning it contains carbohydrate and phosphate groups. It's often used in research and various applications due to its abundance and relatively easy isolation.|Example: Researchers used ovalbumin as a model protein in their study of protein folding.  研究人员在蛋白质折叠研究中使用卵清蛋白作为模型蛋白。|近义词：egg white protein (a broader term)|反义词：无|
