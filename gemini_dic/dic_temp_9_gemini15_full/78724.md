giggish:/ˈɡɪɡɪʃ/| adj.|傻气，轻浮的，滑稽的|Characterized by or suggestive of giggling; silly or frivolous.  The word evokes a sense of childish, light-hearted silliness, often bordering on inappropriate or slightly embarrassing.|例句：Her giggish behavior at the formal dinner was quite inappropriate.  她在那场正式晚宴上的傻气行为相当不恰当。

例句：The giggish antics of the clowns amused the children. 小丑滑稽的举动逗乐了孩子们。


近义词：frivolous, silly, giddy, childish, flippant|反义词：serious, solemn, grave, sedate


词源:  The word "giggish" is derived from the noun "giggle," meaning a short, high-pitched laugh.  The suffix "-ish" is added to create an adjectival form, indicating a quality or characteristic of giggling.  The word's development reflects a natural linguistic process of creating adjectives from existing nouns.
