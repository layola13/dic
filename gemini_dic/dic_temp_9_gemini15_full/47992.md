decimation:/ˌdɛsɪˈmeɪʃən/| n.| 大量杀戮；十分之一的抽杀；大量破坏 |The killing of one tenth of a group of people as a punishment;  widespread destruction or death.  |例句：The decimation of the population by the plague was horrifying.  瘟疫使人口大量减少，令人恐惧。

例句：The forest suffered decimation due to deforestation.  由于滥砍滥伐，森林遭受了严重的破坏。


近义词：destruction, devastation, slaughter, annihilation |反义词：preservation, conservation, protection

词源:  From Latin *decimātiō, from dēcimāre "to select every tenth man," from dēcim- "tenth," from decem "ten." The original meaning referred to the Roman military practice of punishing a mutinous legion by killing every tenth soldier.  The meaning has evolved to encompass any significant reduction in population or destruction.
