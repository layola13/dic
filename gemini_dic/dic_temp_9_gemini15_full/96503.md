indo-gangetic: /ˌɪndoʊˈɡændʒɪtɪk/ | adj. | 印度-恒河平原的 | Relating to the Indo-Gangetic Plain, a fertile plain in northern India and Pakistan formed by the Indus and Ganges rivers and their tributaries.  The term often refers to the region's geography, culture, and history, which are significantly shaped by the rivers and the alluvial soil they deposit. | The Indo-Gangetic Plain is one of the most densely populated regions in the world. |  fertile, alluvial |  arid, mountainous |  adj.
