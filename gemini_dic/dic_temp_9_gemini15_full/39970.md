consubstantialism:/ˌkɒnsʌbˈstænʃəlɪzəm/| n. |共质说；同质论|The doctrine that the Son is of one substance with the Father.  This theological concept asserts a complete unity of essence between the Father and the Son in the Trinity.  It's a key tenet of Christian theology, particularly within Nicene Christianity, distinguishing it from Arianism which posited a subordinate status for the Son.|The Council of Nicaea in 325 AD formally condemned Arianism and affirmed consubstantialism as the orthodox view.  This theological debate centered on the nature of Christ and his relationship to God the Father.|近义词：homoousion (in Greek theology)|反义词：Arianism, heterousion (in Greek theology)
