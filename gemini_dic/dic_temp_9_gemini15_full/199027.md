signals:/ˈsɪɡnəlz/ | n. v. |信号；标志；预兆 |  A signal is something that conveys information, warning, or instruction. It can be a gesture, sound, light, or other form of communication. As a verb, it means to communicate or indicate something by a signal. |例句：The traffic light signals when to stop and go. 交通信号灯指示何时停止和通行。  The doctor signaled for assistance. 医生示意需要帮助。|近义词：signs, indications, symptoms |反义词：silence, quiet


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The light signals a warning. = 灯光发出警告信号。
Birds signal their intentions through their songs. = 鸟类通过鸣叫来传达它们的意图。

一般过去时:
The train signaled its approach. = 火车发出了进站信号。
He signaled to his friend across the street. = 他向街对面的朋友打手势。

现在完成时:
She has signaled her agreement to the plan. = 她已经表示同意这项计划。
They have signaled their displeasure with the decision. = 他们已经表示对这个决定的不满。

过去完成时:
The ship had signaled for help before it sank. = 这艘船沉没之前曾发出求救信号。
He had signaled his intention to leave earlier that day. = 他那天早些时候就已经表示要离开了。

将来时:
The captain will signal the start of the race. = 船长将发出比赛开始的信号。
The system will signal any unusual activity. = 系统将发出任何异常活动的信号。

虚拟语气:
If the alarm signaled, we would have evacuated immediately. = 如果警报响起，我们会立即撤离。
I wish the system had signaled the problem sooner. = 我希望系统早点发出问题的信号。

被动语态:
The message was signaled across the ocean. = 信息被传送到大洋彼岸。
The danger was signaled by a loud siren. = 危险是由一声响亮的警笛发出的信号。

疑问句:
What does this signal mean? = 这个信号是什么意思？
Does this flag signal danger? = 这个旗子表示危险吗？

否定句:
The device didn't signal any problems. = 该设备没有发出任何问题信号。
He didn't signal his intention to leave. = 他没有表示要离开的意图。
