empiricism: /ɪmˈpɪrɪsɪzəm/ | n. |经验主义|The theory that all knowledge is derived from sense-experience.  Empiricism emphasizes the role of observation and experimentation in acquiring knowledge, as opposed to relying on reason, intuition, or revelation.  |Example:  Many scientists rely on empiricism to test their hypotheses. 许多科学家依靠经验主义来检验他们的假设。

The theory of empiricism is based on the belief that knowledge comes primarily, or solely, from sensory experience.  |Example:  His approach to the problem was purely empirical, based on observation and experimentation rather than theory. 他解决问题的方法纯粹是经验主义的，是基于观察和实验而不是理论。|近义词：experientialism, positivism|反义词：rationalism, idealism, intuitionism|名词

词源:

The word "empiricism" comes from the Greek word "empeirikos," meaning "experienced" or "skilled through practice."  It ultimately derives from "empeiros," meaning "expert" or "experienced."  The suffix "-ism" indicates a doctrine or system of belief.
