shined:/ʃaɪnd/| v.|闪耀；擦亮；杰出|To emit or reflect light; to polish to a bright surface; to be exceptionally good or successful.|例句：The sun shined brightly. 太阳明亮地照耀着。 The shoes shined beautifully after polishing. 擦亮后鞋子闪闪发光。 He shined in his role as Hamlet. 他在哈姆雷特的角色中表现出色。|近义词：glowed, gleamed, excelled, shone|反义词：dulled, dimmed, failed


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The sun shines brightly today. = 今天阳光灿烂。
He shines a light on the problem. = 他阐明了这个问题。


一般过去时:
The star shined brightly in the night sky. = 星星在夜空中闪耀。
She shined her shoes before the interview. = 面试前她擦亮了鞋子。


现在完成时:
The sun has shined all day. = 太阳一整天都在照耀。
He has shined in his new role. = 他在新角色中表现出色。


过去完成时:
The sun had shined all morning before the rain started. = 下雨前，太阳整个上午都在照耀。
She had shined her shoes many times before they looked perfect. = 在鞋子看起来完美无瑕之前，她已经擦亮过很多次了。


将来时:
The sun will shine tomorrow. = 明天太阳会照耀。
I will shine my shoes before the party. = 我会在聚会前擦亮我的鞋子。


虚拟语气:
If the sun shined, we would go to the beach. = 如果太阳照耀，我们就去海滩。
I wish my work shined more. = 我希望我的工作更出色。


被动语态:
The shoes were shined by the shoe shiner. = 鞋子是由擦鞋匠擦亮的。
His performance was shined by the excellent supporting cast. = 他的表演因优秀的配角阵容而更加出色。


疑问句:
Does the sun shine today? = 今天太阳照耀吗？
Did she shine her shoes? = 她擦亮鞋子了吗？


否定句:
The sun didn't shine yesterday. = 昨天太阳没有照耀。
He didn't shine in the exam. = 他考试没有考好。
