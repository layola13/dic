profit-margins: /ˈprɑːfɪt ˈmɑːrdʒɪnz/ | n. | 利润率 | Profit margins refer to the difference between the revenue generated from selling a product or service and the cost of producing or providing it, expressed as a percentage of revenue.  It indicates the profitability of a business or a specific product line.  A higher profit margin generally suggests better financial health. |


例句：
The company's profit margins increased by 10% last quarter. = 上季度公司的利润率增长了10%。

The low profit margins on this product forced us to reconsider our pricing strategy. = 此产品的低利润率迫使我们重新考虑我们的定价策略。


近义词：profitability, markup, margin

反义词：loss, deficit
