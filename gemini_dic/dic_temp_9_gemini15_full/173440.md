quasi-everlasting: /ˈkwɑːziˌɛvərˈlæstɪŋ/ | adj. | 准永恒的，近乎永恒的 |  Almost everlasting; seemingly lasting forever, but not truly so.  It implies a very long duration, but acknowledges the possibility of an eventual end. | Example: The quasi-everlasting reign of the emperor was finally brought to an end by a rebellion.  皇帝近乎永恒的统治最终被一场叛乱终结了。 | 近义词：long-lasting, enduring, perpetual (less emphasis on the potential end) | 反义词：fleeting, ephemeral, transient | 词性：形容词
