metapodial:/ˌmɛtəˈpoʊdiəl/| n. |掌骨；跖骨|A metapodial is a bone in the foot or hand of a vertebrate animal, specifically one of the long bones between the carpus or tarsus and the phalanges.  In humans, the metacarpals are the bones of the hand, and the metatarsals are the bones of the foot. |例句：The fractured metapodial required surgery to repair.  这块断裂的掌骨/跖骨需要手术修复。

The horse's metapodials are strong bones designed for running. 这匹马的掌骨/跖骨是强壮的骨骼，适合奔跑。|近义词：metatarsal (foot), metacarpal (hand)|反义词：phalanges, carpal, tarsal
