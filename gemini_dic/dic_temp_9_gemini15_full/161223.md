/ˌpoʊstləˌdʒɪtɪməˈʃeɪʃənz/ | n. | 事后合法化；追认；事后辩护 | The act of legitimizing something after the fact.  This often involves providing justifications or rationalizations for actions that were previously questionable or illegal.  Postlegitimations are frequently employed to mitigate negative consequences or to avoid accountability. | Example: The government's postlegitimations for the invasion were unconvincing to many.  政府对入侵的事后辩护令许多人不信服。 |近义词：ratification, justification, ex post facto approval | 反义词：condemnation, repudiation
