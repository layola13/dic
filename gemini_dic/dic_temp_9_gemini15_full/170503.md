prythee:/ˈpraɪðiː/| interj.|请，求你|An archaic or literary word expressing a polite request or entreaty.  It is a contraction of "pray thee," where "pray" is used as a polite imperative and "thee" is the objective case of the second-person singular pronoun "thou."|例句：Prythee, sir, tell me the truth. 请您告诉我真相。

Prythee, lend me your ear.  求你，听我说。|近义词：please, if you please, I beg you|反义词：None directly applicable, as it's a polite request.  The opposite would be a command or rude demand.
