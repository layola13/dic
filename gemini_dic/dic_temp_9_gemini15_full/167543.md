/priːˈvoʊtɪŋ/| n. |预投票；提前投票|The act of voting before the official election date.  This is often used in contexts where voters cast ballots early for various reasons, such as convenience or absentee voting.| Example:  Early pre-voting numbers suggest a high turnout for the upcoming election.  提前投票的数字显示即将到来的选举投票率很高。  The pre-voting process was streamlined this year to improve accessibility. 今年简化了提前投票流程以提高便利性。| early voting, advance voting |  (No direct antonym, as it's the opposite of *not* voting early)|名词
