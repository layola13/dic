/ɪˈvaɪt/ | vt. | 邀请；恳请；怂恿 | To ask (someone) to come to a social event.  |  I'd like to invite you to my birthday party. 我想邀请你参加我的生日派对。|  summon, ask, request | refuse, decline | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He invites his friends to dinner every week. 他每周都邀请朋友们来吃晚饭。
She invites suggestions for improving the project. 她邀请大家提出改进项目的建议。

一般过去时:
They invited us to their wedding last year. 他们去年邀请我们参加他们的婚礼。
My boss invited me to a meeting this morning.  今天早上我的老板邀请我参加了一个会议。

现在完成时:
I have invited all my family to the celebration. 我已经邀请了我的所有家人来参加庆祝活动。
She has invited several speakers to the conference. 她已经邀请了几位发言者参加会议。

过去完成时:
He had invited them to his house before the incident happened.  事件发生之前，他已经邀请他们去他家了。
They had invited many guests to the party before realizing they didn't have enough food. 他们在意识到食物不够之前，已经邀请了许多客人来参加派对。

将来时:
We will invite them to our housewarming party next month. 下个月我们将邀请他们参加我们的乔迁派对。
She will invite her colleagues to celebrate her promotion. 她将邀请她的同事们庆祝她的晋升。

虚拟语气:
I would invite you to my home, but I'm moving next week. 我想邀请你到我家来，但我下周要搬家了。
If I had more time, I would invite more people. 如果我有更多时间，我会邀请更多的人。

被动语态:
We were invited to a formal dinner. 我们被邀请参加了一场正式的宴会。
Many people were invited to the opening ceremony. 许多人被邀请参加开幕式。

疑问句:
Were you invited to the party? 你被邀请参加聚会了吗？
Will you be invited to the meeting? 你会被邀请参加会议吗？

否定句:
I didn't invite him to the party. 我没有邀请他参加聚会。
She won't invite them to her house. 她不会邀请他们到她家。


PHRASE->
invite someone to do something  邀请某人做某事
invite someone over 邀请某人过来
invite trouble 惹麻烦
a formal invitation 正式邀请
receive an invitation 收到邀请
