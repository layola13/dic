promises:/ˈprɒmɪsɪz/| n.|承诺，允诺，希望|A statement that something will or will not happen in the future; a declaration assuring someone that something will or will not happen.  |例句：He made promises he couldn't keep. 他做了些无法兑现的承诺。
例句：The future promises exciting developments in technology. 未来科技发展充满令人兴奋的可能性。|近义词：pledges, assurances, vows|反义词：threats, denials


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He promises to help me. = 他承诺会帮助我。
She promises to be there on time. = 她承诺会准时到场。

一般过去时:
He promised to help me. = 他承诺会帮助我。
She promised to be there on time. = 她承诺会准时到场。

现在完成时:
He has promised to help me. = 他已经承诺会帮助我。
She has promised to be there on time. = 她已经承诺会准时到场。

过去完成时:
He had promised to help me, but he forgot. = 他之前承诺过会帮助我，但他忘了。
She had promised to be there, but she was delayed. = 她之前承诺过会到场，但她被耽搁了。

将来时:
He will promise to help me. = 他将会承诺帮助我。(表示将要做出承诺的动作)
She will promise to be there. = 她将会承诺到场。(表示将要做出承诺的动作)


虚拟语气:
I wish he had promised to help me. = 我希望他之前承诺过帮助我。
If he promised to help, I would feel better. = 如果他承诺帮忙，我会感觉好些。


被动语态:
A promise was made to the workers. = 对工人做出了承诺。
Many promises have been broken. = 许多承诺都被打破了。


疑问句:
Did he promise to come? = 他承诺要来吗？
What did she promise you? = 她承诺给你什么？


否定句:
He didn't promise to help. = 他没有承诺帮忙。
She didn't promise anything. = 她什么都没承诺。


PHRASE->
break a promise = 违背诺言
keep a promise = 遵守诺言
make a promise = 许诺
fulfill a promise = 实现诺言
a promise of sth = …的希望/预兆
