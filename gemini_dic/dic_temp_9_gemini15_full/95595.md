incommiscible: /ˌɪnkəˈmɪsɪbl/ | adj. | 不能混合的；不相溶的 |  Incapable of being mixed or blended; not miscible.  This term usually refers to liquids that do not form a homogeneous mixture when combined. |


Example 1: Oil and water are incommiscible; they will always separate into distinct layers.  油和水不相溶；它们总是会分离成不同的层。

Example 2: The two chemicals proved to be incommiscible, requiring a different approach to the reaction. 这两种化学物质被证明不相溶，需要采用不同的反应方法。


Near Synonyms: immiscible, unmixable


Antonyms: miscible, soluble


Word Origin:  The word "incommiscible" is formed from the prefix "in-" (meaning "not"), the word "commiscible" (meaning "capable of being mixed"), and the suffix "-ible" (meaning "capable of").  "Commiscible," in turn, comes from the Latin "commiscere," meaning "to mix together."  Therefore, "incommiscible" literally means "not capable of being mixed together."
