/klɪm/| n. |攀登；上升；爬升|A climb is the act of going up something, such as a mountain or stairs.  It can also refer to the thing being climbed, or a gradual increase in something.|例句：The climb to the summit was exhausting.  攀登山顶非常累人。|例句：There's been a climb in house prices recently. 最近房价上涨了。|近义词：ascent, rise, scaling|反义词：descent, fall, drop|名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The climbers climb the mountain every year. = 登山者每年都攀登这座山。
He climbs the stairs two at a time. = 他一次爬两级楼梯。

一般过去时:
They climbed Mount Everest last year. = 他们去年攀登了珠穆朗玛峰。
She climbed the ladder to reach the high shelf. = 她爬上梯子够到高处的架子。

现在完成时:
We have climbed that hill many times. = 我们已经爬过那座山很多次了。
She has climbed all the major peaks in the Alps. = 她已经攀登了阿尔卑斯山脉的所有主要山峰。

过去完成时:
They had climbed halfway up the mountain before the storm hit. = 暴风雨来临之前，他们已经爬到山腰了。
He had climbed the tree before I noticed him. = 我注意到他之前，他已经爬上树了。

将来时:
We will climb the highest peak next summer. = 我们明年夏天将攀登最高峰。
They will climb the rock face tomorrow. = 他们明天将攀登岩壁。

虚拟语气:
If I were stronger, I would climb that mountain. = 如果我更强壮一些，我就会攀登那座山。
I wish I could climb that cliff. = 我希望我能攀登那座悬崖。

被动语态:
The mountain was climbed by many people last year. = 去年这座山被许多人攀登。
The stairs are being climbed by the children. = 孩子们正在爬楼梯。

疑问句:
Have you ever climbed a mountain? = 你曾经攀登过山吗？
Will they climb the wall? = 他们会爬墙吗？

否定句:
He didn't climb the tree. = 他没有爬树。
They will not climb the mountain today. = 他们今天不会爬山。


PHRASE->
a steep climb = 陡峭的攀登
a challenging climb = 富有挑战性的攀登
a long climb = 漫长的攀登
a difficult climb = 艰难的攀登
a strenuous climb = 费力的攀登
