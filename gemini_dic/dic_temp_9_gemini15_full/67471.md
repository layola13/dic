extorted:/ɪkˈstɔːrtɪd/| v.|勒索，敲诈；强求|To obtain (something, especially money) from (someone) by force, threats, or other forms of coercion.  |例句：The kidnappers extorted a large ransom from the victim's family. 绑匪勒索了受害者家属一大笔赎金。

例句：He extorted a confession from the suspect by threatening to reveal his secrets. 他威胁要揭露嫌疑人的秘密，从而强迫他认罪。


近义词：blackmail, coerce, pressure, force

反义词：give freely, donate


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He extorts money from his clients. = 他向客户勒索钱财。
They extort confessions from prisoners. = 他们强迫犯人认罪。


一般过去时:
The gangsters extorted money from local businesses. = 歹徒敲诈了当地商家。
The police caught the man who extorted the money. = 警方抓住了勒索钱财的那个人。


现在完成时:
The company has extorted millions of dollars from investors. = 这家公司已经从投资者那里敲诈了数百万美元。
He has extorted information from his colleagues for years. = 他多年来一直从同事那里勒索信息。


过去完成时:
The victim had extorted money from his friend before. = 受害人以前曾勒索过朋友的钱。
They had extorted a promise from him before letting him go. = 他们在放他走之前已经强迫他做出承诺。


将来时:
The criminals will extort more money if they are not caught. = 如果罪犯不被抓住，他们会勒索更多的钱。
He will extort information from her to complete his report.  = 他将从她那里勒索信息以完成他的报告。


虚拟语气:
If he extorted the money, he would be in jail now. = 如果他勒索了钱，他现在就会在监狱里。
If they had extorted a confession, the case would have been closed. = 如果他们强迫他认罪，案件早就结案了。


被动语态:
Millions of dollars were extorted from the bank. = 数百万美元被从银行勒索。
A confession was extorted from the suspect under duress. = 嫌疑人在胁迫下被强迫认罪。


疑问句:
Was the money extorted from the victim? = 这笔钱是勒索自受害者吗？
Did they extort information from him? = 他们从他那里勒索信息了吗？


否定句:
He did not extort money from anyone. = 他没有向任何人勒索钱财。
They did not extort a confession from the witness. = 他们没有从证人那里强迫他认罪。



PHRASE->

extort money from: 从…勒索钱财
extort a confession: 勒索供词
extort information: 勒索信息
extort a promise: 强迫许诺
extort compliance: 强迫服从


词源:

源自于拉丁语 *ex- + torquēre "to twist,wrench"  字面意思为“扭转，强迫”，引申为通过暴力或威胁强迫他人屈服，从而获得所需之物。
