nucleoplasm:/ˈnjuːkliəʊˌplæzəm/| n. |核质|The ground substance of the nucleus, a colloid containing the chromatin and nucleoli. It is enclosed by the nuclear envelope and is the site of many nuclear processes, including DNA replication and RNA transcription.|The nucleoplasm is a complex mixture of proteins, enzymes, and other molecules that are essential for the cell's function. |近义词：karyoplasm|反义词：cytoplasm
