preconvinces: /ˌpriːkənˈvɪnsɪz/ | v. | 预先说服；提前劝服 | To persuade someone beforehand; to convince someone in advance. |  The salesman preconvinced the customer of the product's value before even showing it.  = 推销员在展示产品之前就先说服了顾客相信其价值。 |  persuade, influence, sway |  discourage, dissuade | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He preconvinces his clients before every meeting. = 他在每次会议之前都会预先说服他的客户。
She preconvinces the audience with her compelling speech. = 她用她令人信服的演讲预先说服了观众。


一般过去时:
The politician preconvinced the voters with his promises. = 这位政治家通过他的承诺预先说服了选民。
They preconvinced the investors before the presentation. = 他们在演示之前预先说服了投资者。


现在完成时:
The manager has preconvinced the team about the new strategy. = 经理已经预先说服了团队接受新的战略。
She has preconvinced him to accept the job offer.  = 她已经预先说服他接受这份工作。


过去完成时:
He had preconvinced himself that he would succeed. = 他已经预先说服自己他会成功。
They had preconvinced the board before the crucial vote. = 他们曾在关键投票之前预先说服了董事会。


将来时:
The lawyer will preconvince the jury with evidence. = 律师将用证据预先说服陪审团。
She will preconvince the committee before the proposal. = 她将在提案之前预先说服委员会。


虚拟语气:
If he preconvinced the judge, he might win the case. = 如果他预先说服了法官，他可能会赢得官司。
I wish I had preconvinced him to reconsider. = 我希望我预先说服他重新考虑。


被动语态:
The audience was preconvinced by his powerful speech. = 观众被他的有力演讲预先说服了。
The investors were preconvinced of the project's feasibility. = 投资者已被预先说服相信该项目的可行性。


疑问句:

否定句:
