sanscrits:/ˈsænskɪts/| n. |梵文；梵语|The word "Sanskrit" refers to the ancient classical language of India,  a liturgical language of Hinduism, Jainism, Buddhism and other religions.  It's considered one of the oldest Indo-European languages and is the ancestor of many modern Indian languages.  It's known for its rich grammatical structure and extensive literature.|Example: Many ancient Indian texts, including the Vedas and Upanishads, are written in Sanskrit.  许多古代印度文本，包括吠陀经和奥义书，都是用梵文写的。|近义词：Prakrit (a related ancient language)|反义词：None (no direct antonym)|名词
