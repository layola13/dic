ferromagnetic:/ˌfɛroʊmæɡˈnɛtɪk/| adj.| 铁磁的；强磁性的 | Exhibiting strong attraction to a magnet; possessing ferromagnetism.  A ferromagnetic material is strongly attracted to a magnet and can be magnetized itself. This property arises from the alignment of electron spins within the material. | Example: Iron is a ferromagnetic material.  铁是一种铁磁性材料。  The ferromagnetic properties of the alloy make it suitable for use in electromagnets.  该合金的铁磁特性使其适合用于电磁铁。|近义词：ferrimagnetic, strongly magnetic |反义词：diamagnetic, paramagnetic

词源:

The word "ferromagnetic" is derived from the Latin word "ferrum" meaning "iron" and the Greek word "magnetos" meaning "magnet".  The prefix "ferro-" indicates a connection to iron, reflecting the fact that iron is a prototypical ferromagnetic material.  The suffix "-magnetic" refers to the property of magnetism.
