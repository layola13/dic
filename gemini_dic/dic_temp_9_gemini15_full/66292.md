excoriates:/ɪkˈskɔːrieɪts/| vt.|严厉批评；痛斥；损伤（皮肤）|To criticize severely; to denounce; to wear away the skin of.|The critic excoriated the author's new novel, calling it "uninspired and derivative." 批评家严厉批评了作者的新小说，称其“缺乏灵感且模仿痕迹严重”。|近义词：castigate, denounce, lambaste, censure|反义词：praise, commend, applaud

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The journalist excoriates the politician's policies. = 记者严厉批评了这位政治家的政策。
He excoriates his opponents mercilessly. = 他无情地痛斥他的对手。


一般过去时:
The teacher excoriated the student for cheating. = 老师因作弊而严厉批评了学生。
She excoriated him for his behavior. = 她因他的行为而痛斥他。


现在完成时:
The media has excoriated the company for its unethical practices. = 媒体因其不道德行为而严厉批评了该公司。
He has excoriated the government's response to the crisis. = 他严厉批评了政府对危机的应对。


过去完成时:
The public had already excoriated the company before the scandal broke. = 在丑闻爆发之前，公众就已经严厉批评了该公司。
They had excoriated him for years for his beliefs. = 他们多年来一直因他的信仰而痛斥他。


将来时:

She will excoriate the company's actions in her speech. = 她将在演讲中严厉批评该公司的行为。


虚拟语气:
If I were to excoriate him, it would be for his arrogance. = 如果我要批评他，那将是因为他的傲慢。
I wish I hadn't excoriated him so harshly. = 我真希望我没有那么严厉地批评他。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
