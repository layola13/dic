run-off:/ˈrʌnˌɔːf/| n. v.|径流；决选；泄漏；溢出|The flow of water over land surfaces.  A secondary election to decide a nominee. The escape of liquid. The excess of something.|例句：The heavy rain caused significant run-off into the river. 大雨导致大量径流流入河中。|近义词：drainage, overflow, spillover|反义词：infiltration, absorption


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The water runoff contaminates the local water supply. = 水的径流污染了当地的供水。
Runoff from the fields flows into the river. = 田里的径流流入河中。

一般过去时:
The election went to a run-off. = 选举进入了决选。
Heavy rains caused significant runoff last night. = 昨晚的大雨造成了大量的径流。


现在完成时:

过去完成时:

虚拟语气:
