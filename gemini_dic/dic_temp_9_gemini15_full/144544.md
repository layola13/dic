outvoted:/ˌaʊtˈvoʊtɪd/| v. |被否决，票数少于|To defeat or reject (a person or proposal) by a majority of votes.  |The proposal was outvoted by a margin of 20 to 10.  这个提案以20比10的票数被否决了。|defeated, rejected, overruled|approved, passed, accepted|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They outvote him in every meeting. = 他们在每次会议上都投票否决他。
The majority always outvotes the minority. = 多数总是战胜少数。

一般过去时:
The amendment was outvoted last year. = 这个修正案去年被否决了。
His proposal was outvoted in the committee. = 他的提案在委员会中被否决了。

现在完成时:
They have outvoted him on this issue several times. = 他们在这个问题上已经多次投票否决了他。
The opposition party has been outvoted in every vote so far. = 反对党到目前为止在每一次投票中都被否决了。

过去完成时:
The bill had been outvoted before it even reached the floor. = 该法案甚至在提交到会议厅之前就已经被否决了。
They realized their plan had been outvoted weeks ago. = 他们意识到他们的计划几周前就被否决了。

将来时:
I predict that the budget will be outvoted next month. = 我预测预算案下个月会被否决。
I fear that our proposal will be outvoted in the upcoming election. = 我担心我们的提案在即将到来的选举中会被否决。

虚拟语气:
If we had presented our case better, we might not have been outvoted. = 如果我们更好地陈述了我们的理由，我们可能就不会被否决了。
I wish we hadn't been outvoted; the project was so important. = 我真希望我们没有被否决；这个项目太重要了。

被动语态:
The proposal was outvoted by a large margin. = 该提案以较大优势被否决。
His ideas were consistently outvoted during the negotiations. = 在谈判过程中，他的想法总是被否决。

疑问句:

否定句:
The plan wasn't outvoted; it was simply withdrawn. = 该计划并没有被否决；它只是被撤回了。
The resolution wasn't outvoted, it passed with a comfortable majority. = 该决议没有被否决，它以足够的多数票通过了。
