oxshoe:/ˈɒkʃuː/| n.|牛蹄铁|A horseshoe for an ox.  Oxshoes were historically used to protect the hooves of oxen from wear and tear, particularly when working on hard surfaces. They were typically made of iron and were larger and sturdier than horseshoes.|例句：The blacksmith crafted sturdy oxshoes for the farmer's oxen.  铁匠为农民的牛制作了结实的牛蹄铁。
