resupply's:/ˌriːsəˈplaɪz/ | v. | 再供应；补充供应 | To supply again; to replenish.  The verb "resupply" is used to describe the act of providing something again, especially essential supplies after they have been depleted.  It often implies a process of replenishment or restoration. | 例句：The army is planning to resupply the troops in the field. = 军队正计划向战场上的部队补充物资。


The base camp needs resupplying with food and water. = 基地营地需要补充食物和水。


近义词：replenish, restock, refill | 反义词：deplete, exhaust


USAGE->

They resupply the shelves daily. = 他们每天补充货架上的商品。
The store resupplies its inventory weekly. = 这家商店每周补充库存。

The soldiers resupplied their ammunition after the battle. = 战役结束后，士兵们补充了弹药。
We resupplied the camping gear before our hike. = 我们在远足前补充了露营装备。

过去完成时:

The team had resupplied their fuel before embarking on the long journey. = 团队在开始长途旅行前已经补充了燃料。

虚拟语气:

词源:
The word "resupply" is formed from the prefix "re-" (meaning "again") and the verb "supply."  The "supply" part ultimately comes from the Old French *supplier, from Latin *supplere "to fill up, complete."
