opioid:/ˈoʊpiɔɪd/ | n. |阿片类药物 |A substance that acts on opioid receptors, found primarily in the central nervous system and gastrointestinal tract, producing effects such as analgesia, euphoria, sedation, and respiratory depression.  Opioids are used medically to relieve pain, but their addictive nature is a major concern.|例句：The doctor prescribed an opioid painkiller to manage his post-surgical pain.  医生开了阿片类止痛药来缓解他术后的疼痛。|近义词：narcotic, analgesic |反义词：N/A

词源:
The word "opioid" comes from "opium," which itself has roots in the Greek word "opion" (ὀπίον), meaning "poppy juice."  The suffix "-oid" means "resembling" or "having the form of," indicating that opioids are substances resembling or having similar effects to opium.
