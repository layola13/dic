joke:/dʒoʊk/ | n. |笑话，玩笑 | A joke is something said or done to cause laughter.  It often involves a play on words, an unexpected twist, or an amusing situation. | He told a joke about a dog and a cat. 他讲了一个关于猫和狗的笑话。|  funny story, gag, pun | serious, truth


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He tells jokes every day. = 他每天都讲笑话。
She makes jokes to lighten the mood. = 她讲笑话来活跃气氛。

一般过去时:
He told a funny joke last night. = 他昨晚讲了一个好笑的笑话。
They laughed at the joke I told. = 他们听了我的笑话后哈哈大笑。

现在完成时:
I have told that joke many times. = 我已经讲过很多次那个笑话了。
She has never heard such a funny joke before. = 她以前从未听过这么好笑的笑话。

过去完成时:
He had already told the joke before I arrived. = 我到达之前他已经讲过那个笑话了。
We had never heard that joke before. = 我们以前从未听过那个笑话。

将来时:
I will tell you a joke later. = 我一会儿给你讲个笑话。
He will be telling jokes at the party. = 他将在派对上讲笑话。

虚拟语气:
If I were a comedian, I would tell jokes for a living. = 如果我是一个喜剧演员，我会靠讲笑话为生。
I wish I could tell jokes as well as he can. = 我希望我能像他一样讲笑话。

被动语态:
The joke was told by a famous comedian. = 这个笑话是一位著名的喜剧演员讲的。
Many jokes are told online every day. = 每天都有很多笑话在网上流传。

疑问句:
Did you hear the joke? = 你听到那个笑话了吗？
What kind of jokes do you like? = 你喜欢什么类型的笑话？

否定句:
I don't understand the joke. = 我不明白这个笑话。
He didn't tell any jokes at the party. = 他在派对上没有讲任何笑话。
