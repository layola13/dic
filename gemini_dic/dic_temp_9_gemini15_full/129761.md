non-existential: /ˌnɒnˌɪɡzɪˈstɛnʃəl/ | adj. | 不存在的；非实在的 | Relating to or characterized by the absence of existence or reality; not real or actual.  The term often implies a lack of concrete existence, rather than simply a lack of current presence. |

例句：

The unicorn is a non-existential creature in the real world. = 麒麟在现实世界中是不存在的生物。

His fears were largely non-existential; based on imagined threats. = 他的恐惧很大程度上是不存在的；基于想象中的威胁。


近义词：imaginary, unreal, fictitious, hypothetical, abstract

反义词：real, existent, actual, concrete, tangible


词源:

* **non-**:  prefix meaning "not" or "lacking."
* **existential**:  relating to existence; from the Latin "existentia," meaning "existence,"  derived from "existere," meaning "to stand forth," "to emerge," or "to be."


USAGE->

This word is primarily an adjective, and therefore doesn't have verb conjugations like the example word "what."  Its usage is primarily descriptive.  There are no tenses, moods, or voices to conjugate.

PHRASE->

There are no common idiomatic phrases directly using "non-existential."  Phrases using synonyms might include:  "a flight of fancy," "a pipe dream," "an imaginary friend,"  "a hypothetical scenario."
