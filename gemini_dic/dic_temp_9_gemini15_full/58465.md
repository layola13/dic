duction: /dʌkʃən/ | n. | 产生；引导；推论；作品；产品 | The act or process of producing or bringing something into existence; the process of leading or guiding someone or something; a conclusion reached through reasoning; a piece of work, such as a book or play; something that has been produced or manufactured. |  The production of the new film was delayed due to unforeseen circumstances.  这部新电影的制作因不可预见的情况而延误。 | production, creation, generation | destruction, cessation | 名词


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The factory produces cars. = 工厂生产汽车。
This method leads to better results. = 这种方法能带来更好的结果。

一般过去时:
The play was a great production. = 这部剧是一部伟大的作品。
She guided the tourists through the museum. = 她引导游客参观博物馆。

现在完成时:
They have produced several successful movies. = 他们已经制作了几部成功的电影。
The research has led to new discoveries. = 这项研究导致了新的发现。

过去完成时:
The company had produced thousands of units before the merger. = 在合并之前，该公司已经生产了数千个单位。
He had guided his team to victory many times. = 他曾多次带领他的团队取得胜利。

将来时:
They will produce a new model next year. = 他们明年将生产一款新车型。
The guide will lead us to the summit. = 向导将带领我们到达山顶。

虚拟语气:
If they produced more, they would make more profit. = 如果他们生产更多，他们就会获得更多利润。
If she had guided me better, I would have succeeded. = 如果她能更好地指导我，我就会成功了。

被动语态:
The product is produced in China. = 该产品在中国生产。
The group is being led by a young, energetic CEO. = 该团队由一位年轻有活力的首席执行官领导。

疑问句:
What did the scientist deduce from the experiment?  = 科学家从实验中推断出了什么？
Who is leading the project? = 谁在领导这个项目？

否定句:
The company didn't produce enough goods. = 公司没有生产足够的商品。
The teacher didn't guide the students effectively. = 老师没有有效地指导学生。
