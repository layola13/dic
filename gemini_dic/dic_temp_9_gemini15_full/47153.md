dawdle:/ˈdɔːdl/| v.| 闲荡；磨蹭；虚度光阴 | to spend time idly; to move slowly and wastefully |例句：Stop dawdling and get ready for school!  别磨蹭了，赶紧准备上学！

例句：He dawdled over his breakfast, enjoying the leisurely morning. 他慢慢地吃着早餐，享受着悠闲的早晨。


近义词：delay, linger, procrastinate, potter, trifle

反义词：hurry, rush, hasten


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He dawdles on his way to work every morning. 他每天早上上班路上都磨磨蹭蹭的。
They dawdle over their coffee after dinner. 他们晚饭后慢慢地喝着咖啡。


一般过去时:
She dawdled in the park, enjoying the sunshine. 她在公园里闲逛，享受着阳光。
We dawdled along the beach, collecting seashells. 我们沿着海滩慢慢地走着，收集贝壳。


现在完成时:
He has dawdled all morning and now he's behind schedule. 他整个上午都在磨蹭，现在已经落后于进度了。
She has dawdled so long that she's missed the bus. 她磨蹭了这么久，错过了公共汽车。


过去完成时:

She had dawdled all day, and consequently, her work remained unfinished. 她整天都在磨蹭，结果她的工作还没有完成。

虚拟语气:

被动语态:

疑问句:
Are you dawdling again? 你又在磨蹭吗？
Why are you dawdling? 你为什么磨蹭？


否定句:
He didn't dawdle; he rushed to the airport. 他没有磨蹭；他冲向机场。
She doesn't dawdle when she has important tasks to do. 她有重要任务要做的时候不会磨蹭。
