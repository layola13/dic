interlocating: /ˌɪntərˈloʊkeɪtɪŋ/ | adj. | 互相咬合的；互相锁定的；相互关联的 | Interlocking describes things that fit together closely and securely, often in a way that prevents them from easily separating.  It suggests a complex relationship where components depend on each other.  | Example: The interlocking pieces of the puzzle made it difficult to assemble.  这个拼图的互相咬合的碎片使得拼装很困难。The interlocking fingers of the two climbers provided stability on the steep cliff. 两位攀登者的互相紧扣的手指在陡峭的悬崖上提供了稳定性。|近义词：intertwined, interconnected, interdependent | 反义词：separate, disconnected, independent |  adj.
