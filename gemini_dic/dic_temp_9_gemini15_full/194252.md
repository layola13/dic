self-multiplying: /ˌsɛlfˈmʌltɪplaɪɪŋ/ | adj. | 自我复制的，自我繁殖的 |  Describes something that can create copies of itself without external intervention.  This term is often used in biology to describe organisms or cells that reproduce asexually, and in mathematics to describe certain functions or processes that generate similar outputs. | Example:  The self-multiplying virus quickly overwhelmed the computer system.  自我复制的病毒迅速压垮了电脑系统。 The bacteria exhibited self-multiplying behavior in the petri dish.  这种细菌在培养皿中表现出自我复制的行为。 | 近义词: self-replicating, reproductive, proliferative | 反义词: sterile, non-proliferative
