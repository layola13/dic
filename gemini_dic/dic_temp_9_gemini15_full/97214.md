inflated:/ɪnˈfleɪtɪd/ | adj. | 膨胀的；夸大的；通货膨胀的 |  Swollen or puffed up with air or gas; exaggerated or inflated in price or value.  | 例句：The balloon was inflated with helium.  氦气使气球膨胀起来。

The company's profits were inflated by one-time gains.  公司的利润因一次性收益而被夸大。


近义词：swollen, puffed up, exaggerated, overstated | 反义词：deflated, shrunken, understated | 词性：adj.


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The tires are inflated properly. = 轮胎充气充足。
He inflates his achievements. = 他夸大自己的成就。


一般过去时:
They inflated the balloon before the party. = 他们在派对前给气球充气。
She inflated the figures to impress her boss. = 她夸大数字以给老板留下印象。


现在完成时:

He has inflated his importance to the project. = 他夸大了自己在项目中的重要性。
