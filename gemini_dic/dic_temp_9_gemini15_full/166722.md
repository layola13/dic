preside:/prɪˈzaɪd/| v.|主持，担任主席|To be in charge of a meeting, ceremony, or other formal event.|The judge presided over the trial.  法官主持了审判。|The chairman presided at the meeting. 主席主持了会议。|chair, govern, lead|relinquish, abandon, abdicate|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He presides over the meeting every week. 他每周主持会议。
The mayor presides over the city council. 市长主持市议会。


一般过去时:
She presided over the graduation ceremony. 她主持了毕业典礼。
The judge presided over the trial last week. 法官上周主持了审判。


现在完成时:
He has presided over this committee for ten years. 他已经主持这个委员会十年了。
She has presided over many important events. 她主持过许多重要的活动。


过去完成时:
The old king had presided over the kingdom for fifty years before he died. 老国王驾崩前已经统治了王国五十年。
Before the new CEO arrived, the old manager had presided over the company for decades. 新任CEO到来之前，老经理已经管理公司几十年了。


将来时:
The president will preside over the upcoming summit. 总统将主持即将举行的峰会。
He will preside over the board meeting next Monday. 他将在下周一主持董事会会议。


虚拟语气:
If he were to preside, the meeting would be more efficient. 如果他来主持，会议将会更高效。
I wish she had presided over the debate; it would have been fairer. 我希望她主持了辩论；那样会更公平。


被动语态:
The meeting was presided over by the CEO. 会议由首席执行官主持。
The ceremony was presided over by a distinguished guest. 典礼由一位尊贵的客人主持。


疑问句:
Who presided over the conference? 谁主持了这次会议？
Will the principal preside over the school's graduation? 校长会主持学校的毕业典礼吗？


否定句:
He did not preside over the meeting; he was absent. 他没有主持会议；他缺席了。
She wouldn't preside over a meeting that was so poorly organized. 她不会主持组织如此糟糕的会议。


PHRASE->
preside over: 主持
preside at:  主持（尤指正式场合）
preside in: 在…主持
preside upon:  主持（较正式）
preside during: 在…期间主持
