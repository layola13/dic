hitching:/ˈhɪtʃɪŋ/| v. n.|搭便车；系紧；拉扯；|The act of getting a ride in a vehicle, especially one that is not a public transport.|例句：He's hitching a ride to the city. 他正在搭便车去城市。|近义词：thumbing, riding, securing|反义词：walking, driving


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They are hitching a ride to the festival. = 他们正在搭便车去参加节日。
He hitches his trousers up before he starts running. = 他跑步前把裤子系紧了。


一般过去时:
We hitched a ride from a friendly trucker. = 我们搭了一位友好的卡车司机的便车。
She hitched the rope to the tree. = 她把绳子系在树上了。


现在完成时:
They have hitched rides across three states. = 他们已经搭便车穿越了三个州。
He has hitched the boat securely to the dock. = 他已经把船安全地系在码头上了。


过去完成时:
We had hitched a ride before the storm broke. = 我们在暴风雨来临之前就搭上了便车。
She had hitched her skirt up to wade through the stream. = 她把裙子撩起来涉水过小溪。


将来时:
We will be hitching a ride to the airport tomorrow. = 我们明天将搭便车去机场。
He will hitch the trailer to his car. = 他会把拖车挂到他的车上。


虚拟语气:
If I didn't have a car, I would be hitching rides all the time. = 如果我没有车，我会一直搭便车。
I wish I had hitched a ride earlier; I'm freezing. = 我真希望我早点搭便车，我现在冻死了。


被动语态:
A ride was hitched by the stranded travelers. = 被困的旅行者搭上了便车。(被动语态较少用在此词上)
The horse was hitched to the cart. = 马被套在车上。


疑问句:
Are you hitching a ride home? = 你要搭便车回家吗？
Is the boat securely hitched to the pier? = 船安全地系在码头上吗？


否定句:
They didn't hitch a ride; they walked. = 他们没有搭便车；他们步行。
He didn't hitch the trailer properly; it fell off. = 他没有正确地挂上拖车；它掉了。



PHRASE->
hitch a ride: 搭便车
hitch up: 系紧；拉起
hitchhike: 搭便车旅行
give someone a hitch: 给某人搭车
hitch one's wagon to a star: 胸怀大志
