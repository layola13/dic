/ˈsɪbələnsɪz/| n. |咝音，齿擦音 |The sounds produced by forcing air through a narrow channel in the vocal tract, creating a hissing sound.  This often involves the use of the tongue and teeth. Sibilances are a common feature in many languages and can be used to create various effects in both speech and writing.|例句：The poem was full of sibilances, creating a sense of mystery and suspense. 这首诗充满了咝音，营造出神秘和悬念的感觉。 The child's speech was characterized by excessive sibilances.  孩子的语音特点是咝音过多。|近义词：hissing sounds, fricatives|反义词：vowel sounds, plosives|名词
