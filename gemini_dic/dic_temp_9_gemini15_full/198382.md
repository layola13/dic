showily:/ˈʃoʊɪli/| adj.|炫耀地；招摇地；华而不实地| ostentatiously and conspicuously; in a way intended to attract attention.|The newly-rich family lived showily, throwing lavish parties and buying expensive cars.|近义词：ostentatiously, conspicuously, flamboyantly|反义词：modestly, humbly, inconspicuously

USAGE->
一般现在时:
He dresses showily. = 他穿着打扮很炫耀。
She behaves showily. = 她行为举止很招摇。

一般过去时:
They lived showily in the past. = 他们过去生活得很奢华张扬。
He spoke showily at the meeting. = 他在会议上夸夸其谈。

现在完成时:
She has dressed showily for every party. = 她每次参加聚会都打扮得很招摇。
They have lived showily since they won the lottery. = 自从他们中了彩票后，就过着炫耀的生活。

过去完成时:
He had already dressed showily before the guests arrived. = 来宾到达之前，他已经打扮得很炫耀了。
She had lived showily for years before her downfall. = 在她垮台之前，她已经过着奢华张扬的生活好几年了。

将来时:
They will showily celebrate their anniversary. = 他们将大张旗鼓地庆祝他们的周年纪念日。
She will showily display her wealth. = 她将炫耀地展示她的财富。

虚拟语气:
If I were rich, I would live showily. = 如果我有钱，我会过着奢华的生活。
I wish I could live showily, but I can't. = 我希望我能过着奢华的生活，但我不能。

被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
