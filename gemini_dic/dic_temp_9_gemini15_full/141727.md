oralisms:/ˈɔːrəlɪzəm/| n. |口语习语，口语说法 |Words or phrases characteristic of spoken language rather than written language; colloquialisms.  Oralisms often involve informal vocabulary, grammatical structures, and pronunciation features not typically found in formal writing.  They are a key aspect of how language is used in everyday conversations. | Example: "Gonna" instead of "going to," "wanna" instead of "want to,"  "kinda" instead of "kind of."  These are all examples of oralisms common in spoken English. |近义词：colloquialisms, informalisms, slang |反义词：formalisms, literary language
