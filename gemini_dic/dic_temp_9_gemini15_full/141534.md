opport:/ˈɒpɔːrt/|n.|时机，机会|An opportune moment or situation is one that is suitable or convenient for a particular purpose.|The opportunity presented itself unexpectedly.|近义词：chance, possibility|反义词：impossibility, difficulty


USAGE->
一般现在时:
This opportunity allows for growth. = 这个机会允许成长。
An opportunity arises every day. = 每天都会出现机会。


一般过去时:
A great opportunity presented itself last week. = 上周出现了一个绝好的机会。
We missed an important opportunity. = 我们错过了一个重要的机会。


现在完成时:
Many opportunities have arisen since the merger. = 自从合并以来，已经出现了许多机会。
I have seized every opportunity that came my way. = 我抓住了每一个机会。


过去完成时:
By the time he arrived, the opportunity had vanished. = 等他到达时，机会已经消失了。
She realized that she had missed a golden opportunity. = 她意识到她错过了一个千载难逢的机会。


将来时:
Many opportunities will arise in the future. = 未来会有很多机会。
I will grab every opportunity I can find. = 我会抓住我能够找到的每一个机会。


虚拟语气:
If I had had the opportunity, I would have taken it. = 如果我有机会，我会抓住它。
I wish I had had the opportunity to study abroad. = 我希望我有机会出国留学。


被动语态:
The opportunity was missed due to unforeseen circumstances. = 由于不可预见的情况，机会错过了。
This opportunity is being considered by the committee. = 委员会正在考虑这个机会。


疑问句:
What opportunities are available to us? = 我们有哪些机会？
Is there any opportunity for advancement? = 有晋升的机会吗？


否定句:
There was no opportunity to escape. = 没有机会逃脱。
They didn't have the opportunity to learn. = 他们没有学习的机会。
