over-expenditure:/ˌoʊvərɪkˈspɛndɪtʃər/| n. |超支；过度开支 |The excessive spending beyond the budgeted amount.  It refers to spending more money than was planned or allocated. |例句：The project suffered from over-expenditure due to unforeseen complications.  这个项目由于不可预见的复杂情况而超支。|例句：Their over-expenditure on luxury goods led to financial difficulties.  他们购买奢侈品过度消费导致了财务困难。|近义词：excess spending, extravagance, overspending|反义词：underspending, saving, economy


USAGE->

The company is experiencing over-expenditure this quarter. = 这家公司本季度出现超支。
Over-expenditure often leads to financial problems. = 超支常常会导致财务问题。

一般过去时:
The government's over-expenditure last year caused a budget deficit. = 政府去年的超支导致了预算赤字。
Their over-expenditure on the renovation project was significant. = 他们在翻新项目上的超支是相当大的。

词源:
over- (prefix):  meaning "above," "beyond," or "excessively"
expenditure (noun): from "expend" (verb) meaning "to spend" + "-iture" (noun suffix) indicating an act or process.  The root of "expend" is from Latin "expendĕre," meaning "to weigh out," "to pay out."
