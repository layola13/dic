cnidaria:/naɪˈdɛəriə/| n. |刺胞动物门|Cnidaria is a phylum containing over 11,000 species of aquatic animals found in both freshwater and marine environments.  They are characterized by radial symmetry, a gastrovascular cavity, and specialized stinging cells called cnidocytes.  Examples include jellyfish, sea anemones, corals, and hydroids.|The Great Barrier Reef is a vast ecosystem built by cnidarians. = 大堡礁是由刺胞动物建造的一个巨大的生态系统。

The diverse cnidarian species exhibit a wide range of life strategies. = 多样的刺胞动物物种展现出广泛的生命策略。|近义词： coelenterate (less precise)|反义词：无|名词
