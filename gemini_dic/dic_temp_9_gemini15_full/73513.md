foreshadowing: /fɔːrˈʃædoʊɪŋ/ | n. | 预兆，预示 | The act of providing vague advance indications; a warning or indication of (a future event).  It often involves subtle hints or clues that suggest something is going to happen later in the story. | 例句：The dark clouds foreshadowed the coming storm.  乌云预示着即将到来的暴风雨。


The author's use of foreshadowing created suspense. 作者运用预兆制造了悬念。


近义词：premonition, portent, omen, prediction, hint, clue | 反义词：surprise, unexpectedness | 词性: 名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The dark clouds foreshadow the coming storm. = 乌云预示着即将到来的暴风雨。
The author's use of foreshadowing often creates suspense. = 作者运用预兆经常制造悬念。


一般过去时:
The eerie music foreshadowed the villain's arrival. = 怪异的音乐预示着反派的到来。
The author's foreshadowing in the first chapter hinted at the tragic ending. = 作者在第一章中的预示暗示了悲剧性的结局。


现在完成时:
The recurring motif has foreshadowed the protagonist's downfall. = 反复出现的主题预示了主角的垮台。
The clues scattered throughout the novel have foreshadowed the unexpected twist. = 散落在小说各处的线索预示了意想不到的转折。


过去完成时:
The ominous silence had foreshadowed the impending disaster. = 不祥的寂静预示着即将发生的灾难。
The strange dreams he'd had foreshadowed the accident. = 他做过的奇怪梦预示了这场事故。


将来时:
The recent political tensions will foreshadow a difficult election. = 最近的政治紧张局势将预示着一场艰难的选举。
The rising sea levels will foreshadow future coastal flooding. = 海平面上升将预示着未来沿海地区的洪水。


虚拟语气:
If the author had used more foreshadowing, the ending would have been less surprising. = 如果作者使用了更多的预兆，结局就不会那么令人惊讶。
If the events had been foreshadowed more clearly, the readers would have anticipated the plot twist. = 如果事件预示得更清楚，读者就会预料到情节的转折。


被动语态:
The impending war was foreshadowed by increasing military activity. = 即将到来的战争因日益增长的军事活动而被预示。
The tragic events of the story were cleverly foreshadowed by the author. = 故事中的悲剧事件被作者巧妙地预示了。


疑问句:
Does the recurring symbol foreshadow a significant event? = 这个反复出现的象征预示着什么重要的事件吗？
What does the author's use of foreshadowing suggest about the plot? = 作者使用预示暗示了关于情节的什么？


否定句:
The story lacked foreshadowing, making the ending feel abrupt. = 故事缺乏预兆，使结局感觉突然。
The author did not foreshadow the character's betrayal, leaving the reader shocked. = 作者没有预示角色的背叛，使读者感到震惊。


PHRASE->
subtle foreshadowing: 微妙的预示
heavy-handed foreshadowing:  过于明显的预示
effective foreshadowing:  有效的预示
foreshadowing of doom:  厄运的预兆
foreshadowing the future: 预示未来

词源:
Fore- (前缀，表示“在前，预先”) + shadow (影子，暗影，预兆)  字面意思是“在前面投下的影子”，引申为预兆或预示。
