/ˈpɛlɪkɔɪd/| n. |拟贝壳类生物；贝壳状物 | A pelicoid is a fossil organism that resembles a shell or has a shell-like structure.  The term often refers to extinct organisms with a shell-like exoskeleton.  It's a somewhat outdated term, more common in older paleontological literature.| Example: The newly discovered fossil was identified as a type of pelicoid, exhibiting a distinctive spiral shell.  = 新发现的化石被鉴定为一种拟贝壳类生物，具有独特的螺旋壳。|近义词：shell-like organism, conchostracan (in specific contexts)|反义词：无|名词
