devastator: /ˈdɛvəsteɪtər/ | n. |破坏者，毁灭者| A person or thing that causes great and widespread destruction.  It can refer to a weapon, a natural disaster, or even a person whose actions cause significant harm. |例句：The hurricane was a devastator, leaving a trail of destruction in its wake.  飓风是一个破坏者，在其身后留下了一条破坏的痕迹。

The earthquake was a devastator, leaving thousands homeless. 地震是一个破坏者，导致成千上万的人无家可归。|近义词：destroyer, wrecker, ruiner |反义词：benefactor, savior, protector |名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The hurricane devastates coastal areas. 飓风摧毁了沿海地区。
The new technology is a devastator of traditional industries. 新技术正在摧毁传统产业。


一般过去时:
The storm devastated the town. 暴风雨摧毁了小镇。
The army devastated the enemy's defenses.  军队摧毁了敌人的防御工事。


现在完成时:
The war has devastated the country.  战争已经摧毁了这个国家。
The drought has devastated the crops. 干旱摧毁了庄稼。


过去完成时:
The fire had devastated the forest before the firefighters arrived.  消防员到达之前，大火已经摧毁了森林。
The disease had devastated the population before the cure was found.  在找到治疗方法之前，这种疾病已经摧毁了人口。


将来时:
The volcano will devastate the surrounding villages. 火山将摧毁周围的村庄。
The economic crisis will devastate the market.  经济危机将摧毁市场。


虚拟语气:
If the bomb exploded, it would devastate the city. 如果炸弹爆炸，它将摧毁这座城市。
I wish the flood hadn't devastated our home.  我希望洪水没有摧毁我们的家园。


被动语态:
The city was devastated by the earthquake.  这座城市被地震摧毁了。
The crops were devastated by the pests.  庄稼被害虫摧毁了。


疑问句:

否定句:

The storm did not devastate the crops.  暴风雨没有摧毁庄稼。
