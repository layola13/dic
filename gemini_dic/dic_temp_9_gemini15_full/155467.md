phenomenalism: /fɪˈnɒmɪnəlɪzəm/ | /ˌfɛnɑːˈmɪnəlɪzəm/ | n. |现象主义|The philosophical theory that only phenomena (appearances) are knowable and that the existence of things outside our experience is uncertain or unknowable.  It emphasizes the role of perception in shaping our understanding of reality. |例句：Phenomenalism suggests that we can only know the world as it appears to us, not as it inherently is. 现象主义认为我们只能知道世界在我们看来是什么样子，而不是它本身是什么样子。

例句：The debate between realism and phenomenalism continues to engage philosophers. 真实主义和现象主义之间的争论仍在吸引哲学家们。


近义词：empiricism (经验主义), positivism (实证主义)


反义词：realism (实在论), metaphysical realism (形而上学实在论)
