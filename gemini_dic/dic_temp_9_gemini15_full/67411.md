externalizing: /ɪkˈstɜːrnəlaɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 外化；表现出来 |  The act of expressing or manifesting internal feelings, thoughts, or impulses outwardly.  It involves transferring internal states to the external world. This can manifest in various ways, from verbal expressions to physical actions. |

例句：

He externalized his anger by shouting. = 他通过大喊来发泄怒气。

She externalized her anxiety through excessive cleaning. = 她通过过度清洁来表现她的焦虑。


近义词： manifesting, expressing, projecting, exhibiting

反义词： internalizing, suppressing, repressing

词源:

The word "externalizing" comes from the verb "externalize," which is formed from the prefix "ex-" (out, away from) + "ternal" (relating to the outside or exterior) + "-ize" (to make or cause to be).  Therefore, it literally means "to make external" or "to bring outward."
