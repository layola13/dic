latch-on:/læʧˈɑːn/| v.|抓住；开始理解；开始依赖；|To grasp or hold onto something firmly; to begin to understand something; to become dependent on something or someone. |例句：The baby latched onto the nipple.  婴儿吸住了奶嘴。|例句：It took me a while to latch onto the main idea of the lecture. 我花了一段时间才理解讲座的主要内容。|近义词：grasp, cling to, comprehend, depend on |反义词：release, let go of, misunderstand, independent


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The baby latches onto his mother's breast. = 婴儿吸着母亲的乳房。
He latches onto new ideas quickly. = 他很快就能抓住新的想法。

一般过去时:
She latched onto the railing to steady herself. = 她抓住栏杆来稳住自己。
The dog latched onto the bone and wouldn't let go. = 狗咬住骨头不肯放。

现在完成时:
He has latched onto a new business opportunity. = 他抓住了一个新的商业机会。
She has latched onto that idea and won't let go. = 她抓住了那个想法，并且不肯放弃。

过去完成时:
Before the presentation, he had latched onto a crucial detail. = 演示之前，他已经抓住了一个关键细节。
By the time we arrived, the children had latched onto the toys. = 我们到达的时候，孩子们已经抓住了玩具。

将来时:
I think she will latch onto the concept easily. = 我认为她会很容易理解这个概念。
The climber will latch onto the rock face to ascend. = 登山者会抓住岩壁向上攀登。

虚拟语气:
If he had latched onto the problem earlier, it could have been avoided. = 如果他早点抓住这个问题，就可以避免了。
I wish I had latched onto that opportunity when it was available. = 我真希望我当时抓住那个机会。

被动语态:
The theory was latched onto by many scientists. = 许多科学家都接受了这个理论。
The new marketing strategy will hopefully be latched onto by the consumers. = 新的营销策略有望被消费者接受。

疑问句:

Has he latched onto the solution yet? = 他找到解决办法了吗？

否定句:
The student didn't latch onto the professor's explanation. = 学生没有理解教授的解释。
He couldn't latch onto the meaning of the poem. = 他无法理解这首诗的意思。
