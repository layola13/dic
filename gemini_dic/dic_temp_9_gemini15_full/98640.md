insubstantial:/ˌɪnsəbˈstænʃəl/ | adj. |不重要的；不真实的；虚弱的；缺乏实质的 |lacking substance or solidity; not real or tangible.  |例句：His arguments were insubstantial and unconvincing.他的论点缺乏实质，令人难以信服。

例句：The evidence presented was insubstantial and therefore inadmissible in court.  提交的证据不足，因此在法庭上不可采纳。


近义词：unsubstantial, flimsy, weak, tenuous, intangible, ephemeral, vapid, airy

反义词：substantial, solid, strong, real, tangible, concrete


词源:  The word "insubstantial" comes from the prefix "in-" (meaning "not") + "substantial," which derives from the Latin "substantia" meaning "substance" or "being".  Therefore, "insubstantial" literally means "not having substance".
