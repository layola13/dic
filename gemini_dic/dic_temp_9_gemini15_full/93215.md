iguanid: /ˈɪɡwənɪd/ | n. |鬣蜥科动物|Iguanid refers to any member of the family Iguanidae, a diverse group of lizards characterized by their relatively large size, distinct scales, and often vibrant colors.  Many species possess a dewlap, a flap of skin under the chin.  They are found throughout the Americas, and some species are popular in the pet trade.  |例句：Many iguanids are arboreal, spending most of their time in trees. 许多鬣蜥科动物是树栖动物，大部分时间都在树上度过。

The green iguana is a well-known example of an iguanid. 绿鬣蜥是鬣蜥科动物的一个著名例子。|近义词：lizard (a more general term) 蜥蜴（更通用的术语）|反义词：None  无|词性:noun

词源:

The word "iguanid" is derived from the genus name *Iguana*, which itself originates from the Taíno word *iwana*.  The Taíno were indigenous people of the Caribbean islands.  The suffix "-id" is a common zoological suffix indicating family membership.
