daguerreotypist:/ˌdæɡəˈraɪtəpɪst/| n. |银版摄影师|A daguerreotypist is a person who makes daguerreotypes, early photographs produced on a silver-plated copper sheet using a process invented by Louis Daguerre.  They were skilled artisans who had to master the complex chemical and photographic techniques involved in creating these unique images. |The daguerreotypist carefully prepared the silver plate before exposing it in the camera.|近义词： photographer (in the context of early photography) |反义词：None specifically, but perhaps "digital photographer" as a contrast in technology.
