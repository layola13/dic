isorropic:/ˌaɪsoʊˈtrɒpɪk/| adj. |各向同性的|Having properties that are the same when measured in different directions.  This term is commonly used in physics, materials science, and other fields to describe materials or systems that exhibit uniform behavior regardless of the direction of measurement.| Example:  Many crystalline materials are anisotropic, exhibiting different properties along different crystallographic axes, while some amorphous materials are isotropic.|近义词：homogeneous (in some contexts)|反义词：anisotropic
