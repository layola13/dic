excelled:/ɪkˈsɛld/| v.|擅长；优秀；胜过|To surpass others in performance or achievement; to be extremely good at something.|例句：She excelled in mathematics and science. 她擅长数学和科学。|例句：His performance excelled all expectations. 他的表现超出了所有人的预期。|近义词：surpassed, outshone, surpassed, mastered|反义词：failed, underperformed


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He excels in sports. = 他擅长运动。
She excels at playing the piano. = 她擅长弹钢琴。

一般过去时:
He excelled in his studies. = 他学习成绩优异。
The team excelled in the competition. = 该队在比赛中表现出色。

现在完成时:
She has excelled in her career. = 她事业有成。
He has excelled at everything he's tried. = 他尝试过的一切都做得非常出色。

过去完成时:
They had excelled in their previous projects. = 他们在之前的项目中表现出色。
She had excelled at dancing since childhood. = 她从小就擅长跳舞。

将来时:
He will excel in his chosen field. = 他将在自己选择的领域取得成功。
We expect her to excel in the upcoming exam. = 我们期望她在即将到来的考试中取得好成绩。

虚拟语气:
If he had studied harder, he would have excelled. = 如果他学习更努力，他本可以取得优异成绩。
I wish I had excelled in my youth. = 我希望我年轻时能更优秀。

被动语态:
His work was excelled by none. = 他的作品无人能及。
The performance was excelled by few. = 这场演出很少有能超过的。

疑问句:
Did he excel in any particular subject? = 他在任何特定科目上都表现出色吗？
In what areas does she excel? = 她擅长哪些领域？

否定句:
He didn't excel in his job interview. = 他在工作面试中表现不佳。
She didn't excel at public speaking. = 她不擅长公开演讲。
