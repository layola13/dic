camelid:/ˈkæməlɪd/ | n. |骆驼科动物|A camelid is a member of the family Camelidae, which includes camels, llamas, alpacas, vicuñas, and guanacos.  They are characterized by their long necks, long legs, and padded feet adapted for walking on sand or rough terrain.  They are also known for their ability to thrive in arid environments. |例句：Camelids are well-adapted to desert life. 骆驼科动物非常适应沙漠生活。|例句：The llama is a type of camelid.  羊驼是一种骆驼科动物。|近义词：camel (if referring specifically to camels), llama (if referring specifically to llamas) |反义词：None directly applicable.
