high-flown:/ˈhaɪˌfloʊn/| adj.|夸夸其谈的；不切实际的；高傲的；浮夸的|excessively lofty or elaborate in style; pompous; pretentious.  It can describe language, ideas, or people.  |例句：His high-flown language was difficult to understand. 他的夸夸其谈的语言很难理解。|例句：Her high-flown ideals were impractical in the real world. 她在现实世界中不切实际的崇高理想。|近义词：pompous, pretentious, affected, inflated, grandiose |反义词：simple, plain, modest, down-to-earth


Etymology:

The word "high-flown" originated in the late 16th century.  It literally refers to something that is "flown high," implying a soaring or elevated quality.  The metaphorical extension to describe overly elaborate or pretentious language and ideas developed over time.  The "flown" element likely connects to the idea of something being carried aloft, suggesting a sense of being elevated or removed from reality.
