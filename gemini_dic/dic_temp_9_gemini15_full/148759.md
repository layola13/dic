overturnable: /ˌoʊvərˈtɜːrnəbl/ | adj. | 可推翻的；可颠覆的 | Capable of being overturned or overthrown.  Something that is overturnable is susceptible to being reversed, changed, or defeated.  |例句：The judge's decision is overturnable if new evidence emerges. =如果出现新的证据，法官的判决是可以推翻的。

The current government's policies are overturnable in the next election. =现政府的政策在下届选举中可能会被推翻。

近义词：reversible, refutable, vulnerable, challengeable | 反义词：irreversible, irrefutable, unchallengeable


词源:  The word "overturnable" is derived from the verb "overturn," which itself comes from the Old English "ofer" (over) and "turnan" (to turn).  The suffix "-able" indicates capability or possibility.
