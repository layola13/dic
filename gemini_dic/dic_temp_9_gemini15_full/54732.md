disparager: /dɪˈspærɪdʒər/ | n. |贬低者；轻蔑者 | A person who disparages someone or something.  Someone who belittles or undervalues. |例句：He was a known disparager of modern art, preferring the classics. 他是现代艺术的公开贬低者，更喜欢古典艺术。|近义词：belittler, detractor, critic, denigrator |反义词：praiser, admirer, supporter


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The critic disparages the author's new novel. = 批评家贬低了作者的新小说。
He disparages his opponent's achievements. = 他贬低了他对手的成就。


一般过去时:
She disparaged his efforts to help. = 她贬低了他帮忙的努力。
They disparaged the value of the antique furniture. = 他们贬低了这套古董家具的价值。


现在完成时:
The media has disparaged the politician's reputation. = 媒体已经损害了这位政治家的名誉。
He has disparaged his own capabilities in the interview. = 他在采访中贬低了自己的能力。


过去完成时:
They had disparaged her work before she even presented it. = 甚至在她展示作品之前，他们就贬低了她的作品。
The company had disparaged the competitor's product in their advertisement. = 该公司在其广告中贬低了竞争对手的产品。


将来时:
She will disparage his efforts again. = 她还会再次贬低他的努力。
They will disparage the new policy in their next meeting. = 他们将在下次会议上贬低这项新政策。


虚拟语气:
If he were to disparage my work, I would ignore him. = 如果他要贬低我的作品，我会无视他。
I wish they hadn't disparaged my ideas. = 我真希望他们没有贬低我的想法。


被动语态:
His ideas were disparaged by the committee. = 他的想法被委员会贬低了。
Her achievements were disparaged in the newspaper article. = 她在报纸文章中被贬低了她的成就。


疑问句:
Does he often disparage others' accomplishments? = 他经常贬低别人的成就吗？
Will they disparage the project at the meeting? = 他们会在会议上贬低这个项目吗？


否定句:
He did not disparage her abilities. = 他没有贬低她的能力。
She would not disparage the importance of education. = 她不会贬低教育的重要性。
