nonimperiously: /ˌnɒnɪmˈpɪəriəsli/ | adv. | 不专横地；不傲慢地；不盛气凌人地 |  Not in a domineering or arrogant manner; humbly.  |  She spoke nonimperiously, listening carefully to their concerns. 她不盛气凌人地说话，仔细倾听着他们的担忧。 | 近义词：humbly, meekly, modestly, respectfully | 反义词：imperiously, arrogantly, domineeringly, haughtily

词源:
The word is formed from the prefix "non-" (meaning "not") and the adverb "imperiously" (meaning "in a domineering or arrogant manner"). "Imperiously" itself derives from the adjective "imperious," which comes from the Latin word "imperiosus," meaning "commanding, masterful."
