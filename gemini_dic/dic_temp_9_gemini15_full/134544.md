nonliquidating: /ˌnɒnˈlɪkwɪdeɪtɪŋ/ | adj. | 非清算的 |

英文解析:  Not involving the liquidation or winding up of a company or other entity.  It describes a process, event, or action that does not result in the sale of assets to pay off debts and distribute remaining funds to shareholders or creditors.  This term is commonly used in the context of business and finance, particularly regarding reorganizations or restructurings.

例句:

The company underwent a nonliquidating reorganization to address its financial difficulties. =  该公司进行了非清算重组以解决其财务困难。

The investment strategy focuses on nonliquidating assets, prioritizing long-term growth. = 投资策略侧重于非清算资产，优先考虑长期增长。


近义词:  restructuring, reorganizing, solvent

反义词: liquidating, dissolving, bankrupt


词性: adjective (形容词)
