reindulging:/ˌriːɪnˈdʌldʒɪŋ/| v.|再次沉溺，再次放纵|The act of indulging again in something pleasurable but perhaps harmful or excessive.  It implies a previous instance of indulgence and a return to that behavior.|例句：After a period of abstinence, she found herself reindulging in her love of chocolate.  再次戒除一段时间后，她发现自己又沉溺于对巧克力的热爱。

例句：He promised to quit smoking, but he's reindulging in his old habit. 他答应戒烟，但他又重新沉迷于他的老习惯。


近义词：relapsing, resuming, returning to, giving in to

反义词：abstaining, resisting, foregoing


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is reindulging in his favorite hobby. = 他正在再次沉迷于他最喜欢的爱好。
They are reindulging in their unhealthy eating habits. = 他们正在再次沉溺于他们不健康的饮食习惯。


一般过去时:
She reindulged in a long bath after a stressful day. = 压力大的一天后，她再次享受了长时间的泡澡。
They reindulged in their old arguments. = 他们又开始了他们老掉牙的争论。


现在完成时:
I have reindulged in my passion for painting. = 我已经重新拾起了我对绘画的热情。
He has reindulged in excessive spending. = 他已经再次沉迷于过度消费。


过去完成时:
Before the diet, she had reindulged in rich desserts. = 在节食之前，她曾再次沉迷于丰富的甜点。
They had reindulged in their old vices before making a fresh start. = 在重新开始之前，他们曾再次沉迷于他们过去的恶习。


将来时:
I will reindulge in a long vacation next year. = 我明年将再次享受一个漫长的假期。
She will reindulge in her love of travel soon. = 她很快就会再次沉迷于她的旅行爱好。


虚拟语气:
If I had the time, I would reindulge in my old hobby of collecting stamps. = 如果我有时间，我会重新拾起我收集邮票的旧爱好。
I wish I could reindulge in a relaxing weekend. = 我希望我能再次享受一个轻松的周末。


被动语态: (很少用被动语态)
His desire for luxury was reindulged by his wealthy family. = 他对奢华的渴望得到了他富裕家庭的再次满足。(This is an unusual usage)
His sweet tooth was reindulged with a box of chocolates. = 他对甜食的渴望被一盒巧克力满足了。(This is an unusual usage)


疑问句:
Will you reindulge in that unhealthy habit? = 你会再次沉迷于那个不健康的习惯吗？
Are they reindulging in their old arguments again? = 他们又在重温他们老掉牙的争论了吗？


否定句:
She did not reindulge in her bad habits. = 她没有再次沉迷于她的坏习惯。
They refused to reindulge in gossip. = 他们拒绝再次沉迷于八卦。
