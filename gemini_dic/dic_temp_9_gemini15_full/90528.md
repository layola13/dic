huff:/hʌf/ | v. n.|  (尤指生气地)喘气；怒气冲冲地说；吹嘘；(用力)吸气 | To breathe heavily, especially in anger; to speak in an angry or indignant way; to boast; to inhale forcefully. |例句：He huffed and puffed as he climbed the stairs. 他爬楼梯时气喘吁吁。 |例句：She huffed at me when I told her the news. 当我告诉她这个消息时，她对我怒气冲冲地说。|近义词：puff, snort, fume |反义词：whisper, murmur


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He huffs and puffs after running. = 他跑完步后气喘吁吁。
She huffs at every little thing. = 她对每一件小事都生气。

一般过去时:
He huffed angrily at the referee's decision. = 他对裁判的决定怒气冲冲。
She huffed and puffed her way up the hill. = 她气喘吁吁地爬上了山。

现在完成时:
He has huffed and puffed all morning. = 他整个上午都在气喘吁吁。
She has huffed at me several times today. = 她今天已经几次对我生气了。

过去完成时:
He had huffed and puffed before he finally reached the top. = 他到达山顶之前已经气喘吁吁了。
She had huffed at him earlier that day. = 那天早些时候她已经对他生气了。

将来时:
He will huff and puff if he has to run that far. = 如果他不得不跑那么远，他会气喘吁吁。
She will huff and puff if you don't listen to her. = 如果你不听她的，她会生气。

虚拟语气:
If he had known the truth, he wouldn't have huffed. = 如果他知道真相，他就不会生气了。
I wish she wouldn't huff so much. = 我希望她不要那么生气。
