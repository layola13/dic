callosity:/kæˈlɒsəti/|/kəˈlɑːsəti/| n. |老茧；胼胝；粗糙；无情 |A callosity is a hardened area of skin, typically on the hands or feet, caused by repeated friction or pressure.  It's a protective mechanism the body develops to cope with constant rubbing.  The term can also be used figuratively to describe someone's emotional callousness or lack of sensitivity. |例句：The gardener developed a callosity on his palm from years of working in the soil.  园丁多年在土壤中工作，手掌上长了一个老茧。

The politician's callosity towards the suffering of the poor was shocking.  这位政治家对穷人的苦难漠不关心，令人震惊。|近义词：hardness, thickening, callus, insensitivity |反义词：tenderness, sensitivity, softness
