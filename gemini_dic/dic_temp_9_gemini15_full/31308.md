chapman:/ˈtʃæpmən/| n.|小贩，行商|A chapman was a traveling peddler or merchant who sold goods from place to place.  They often carried a pack or used a horse or cart. The term is now largely archaic.|Example: In the Middle Ages, chapmen played a vital role in distributing goods across the countryside.  中世纪时，行商在农村地区分销商品方面发挥着至关重要的作用。|近义词：peddler, hawker, itinerant merchant|反义词：shopkeeper, retailer|名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The chapman sells his wares in the market. = 行商在市场上出售他的商品。
He carries a pack of goods. = 他背着一包货物。


一般过去时:
The chapman traveled from village to village. = 行商挨家挨户地旅行。
He sold his goods at a fair. = 他在集市上出售他的商品。
