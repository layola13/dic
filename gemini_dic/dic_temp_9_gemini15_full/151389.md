parliamentaryism:/ˌpɑːrliˈmɛntəriˌɪzəm/| n. |议会制度|A system of government in which the supreme power is vested in a body of representatives, typically elected by the people, and responsible for making laws and decisions for the country.  It emphasizes the importance of parliament as the central institution of power.|The UK is a classic example of parliamentaryism, with a parliament that holds the government accountable. 英国是议会制度的典型例子，议会对政府负责。|presidentialism, direct democracy |autocracy, dictatorship|名词
