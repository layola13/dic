proliferated: /prəˈlɪfəreɪtɪd/ | v. | 激增；迅速繁殖；大量涌现 | To increase rapidly in number; to multiply or spread rapidly.  | 近义词：multiply, increase, expand, mushroom, burgeon | 反义词：decrease, diminish, dwindle, decline


USAGE->
一般现在时:
Cancer cells proliferate rapidly. = 癌细胞迅速增殖。
New businesses proliferate in the city center. = 市中心涌现出许多新企业。

一般过去时:
The rumors proliferated throughout the town. = 谣言在全镇迅速传播。
The rabbits proliferated in the field. = 兔子在田野里大量繁殖。

现在完成时:
The weeds have proliferated since the last rain. = 自从上次下雨以来，杂草疯长。
Social media has proliferated in recent years. = 近年来，社交媒体迅速普及。

过去完成时:
By the time we arrived, the rumors had proliferated. = 我们到达的时候，谣言已经传开了。
Before the intervention, the pests had proliferated uncontrollably. = 干预之前，害虫已不受控制地大量繁殖。

将来时:
Experts predict that the virus will proliferate if left unchecked. = 专家预测，如果不加以控制，病毒将会迅速蔓延。
If we don't act now, the problem will proliferate. = 如果我们现在不采取行动，问题将会恶化。

虚拟语气:
If the bacteria proliferated unchecked, it would be catastrophic. = 如果细菌不受控制地增殖，后果将不堪设想。
I wish this technology hadn't proliferated so quickly. = 我真希望这项技术没有这么快就普及。

被动语态:
The disease was reported to have proliferated in the region. = 据报道，这种疾病在该地区迅速蔓延。
The idea was quickly proliferated through the internet. = 这个想法很快就在互联网上传播开来。

疑问句:
Has the use of smartphones proliferated excessively? = 智能手机的使用是否过度普及？
Why did the weeds proliferate so quickly this year? = 为什么杂草今年长得这么快？

否定句:
The government attempted to prevent the disease from proliferating. = 政府试图阻止疾病蔓延。
The plants didn't proliferate as much as expected. = 植物的生长不如预期那样茂盛。


PHRASE->

词源:
From Latin *proliferāre, from proles "offspring" + ferre "to bear, carry".  The word literally means "to bear offspring".
