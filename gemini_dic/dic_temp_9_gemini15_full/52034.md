USAGE->
This word is an adverb, so it doesn't have verb conjugations like the example word "what".  It describes *how* something is done, not an action itself.  Therefore, tense, mood, and voice are not applicable.

Example sentences demonstrating its usage in different contexts:

In a descriptive sentence:
The researcher approached the study dialectologically, focusing on the subtle nuances of regional speech. 研究人员从方言学的角度着手研究，侧重于地区口语的细微差别。

In a comparative sentence:
Compared to a purely phonetic analysis, the dialectological approach revealed more about the social and historical influences on language. 与纯粹的语音分析相比，方言学的方法揭示了更多关于社会和历史对语言的影响。
