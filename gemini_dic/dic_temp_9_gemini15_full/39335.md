conjectures:/kənˈdʒɛktʃərz/ | n. |推测；猜想 | A conjecture is an opinion or conclusion formed on the basis of incomplete information.  It's an educated guess, often based on evidence but not definitively proven. | 例句：The scientist offered several conjectures about the origin of the universe. 科学家对宇宙的起源提出了几种推测。

例句：His conjectures about the future of artificial intelligence proved to be largely accurate. 他对人工智能未来的推测被证明在很大程度上是准确的。

近义词：speculations, hypotheses, guesses, suppositions

反义词：certainties, facts, proofs


词源:  From Middle English *conjecturen, from Old French *conjecturer, from Latin *conjectūrāre "to heap together," from *con- "together" + *jacere "to throw."  The sense evolution is from "throwing things together" to forming an opinion based on incomplete data.
