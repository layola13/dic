/ˌoʊvərˈaɪdəl/ | adj. | 过度空转的；空转过多的 | Excessively idle; running at an unnecessarily high speed.  The engine was overidling, wasting fuel and causing unnecessary wear.  | 近义词：excessively idle, unnecessarily fast | 反义词：efficient, optimally functioning

USAGE->
This word is primarily an adjective and doesn't have typical verb conjugations like "What is/was/will be overidling?".  It describes a state of being.  Instead, we use it to describe something that *is* overidling.

例句1: The car's engine was overidling after I replaced the throttle cable.  汽车更换节气门线后，发动机空转过速。

例句2: The server was overidling, consuming excessive resources. 服务器空转过速，消耗了过多的资源。


PHRASE->
There are no common idiomatic phrases directly using "overidling".  The term is used descriptively in technical contexts.
