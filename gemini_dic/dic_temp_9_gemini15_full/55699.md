diuretics: /ˌdaɪjʊˈrɛtɪks/ | n. |利尿剂 | Substances that promote the excretion of urine. Diuretics increase the flow of urine by increasing the amount of water and sodium excreted by the kidneys. They are commonly used to treat conditions such as edema (swelling), hypertension (high blood pressure), and heart failure. |  The doctor prescribed diuretics to help reduce the swelling in his legs.  医生开了利尿剂来帮助减轻他腿部的肿胀。|  antihypertensives,  water pills | vasoconstrictors |  noun
