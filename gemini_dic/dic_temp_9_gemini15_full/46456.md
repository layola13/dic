daguerreotypy:/ˌdæɡəˈraɪtəpi/| n. |达盖尔照相术|A photographic process invented by Louis Daguerre in 1839, which produced a unique image on a silver-plated copper sheet.  It was the first commercially successful photographic process.|The museum displayed a stunning collection of daguerreotypes from the 19th century.  博物馆展出了19世纪令人惊叹的达盖尔照相术作品集。|photography, photoengraving |(无明显反义词)|名词
