cryptographically:/ˌkrɪptoʊˈɡræfɪkli/ | adv. | 密码学地，加密地 |  In a manner relating to or using cryptography.  The process involves mathematically transforming data to make it unintelligible without the correct decryption key.  This ensures confidentiality, integrity, and authenticity of information. |

例句：The data was cryptographically secured to prevent unauthorized access.  数据经过加密保护以防止未经授权的访问。

例句：The message was cryptographically signed to verify its authenticity.  消息已进行加密签名以验证其真实性。


近义词：securely, encryptedly

反义词：insecurely, openly


词源:

* **crypto-:**  From Greek *kryptós* meaning "hidden." This prefix is widely used in words related to secrecy and concealment.

* **-graph-:** From Greek *graphein* meaning "to write."  This root appears in many words related to writing, recording, and representation.

* **-ically:**  An adverbial suffix indicating "in a manner of" or "in the style of."


用法说明:

`cryptographically` is an adverb that modifies a verb, describing how an action is performed. It indicates that the action is done using cryptographic techniques.  The word implies a high level of security and protection against unauthorized access or modification.  It's often used in contexts related to data security, communication, and digital signatures.  There's no specific tense conjugation as it's an adverb.  It doesn't change form based on tense.
