refining:/rɪˈfaɪnɪŋ/| v.|提炼，精炼，改进|The process of purifying a substance, typically by removing impurities or unwanted components; to improve or perfect something.|例句：The oil refinery processes crude oil into gasoline. 这家炼油厂将原油加工成汽油。|例句：She spent years refining her technique. 她花了数年时间来改进她的技巧。|近义词：purifying, improving, perfecting|反义词：roughening, coarsening


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They refine crude oil into various products. = 他们将原油提炼成各种产品。
The company refines its processes regularly. = 公司定期改进其流程。


一般过去时:
The alchemist refined gold from base metals. = 炼金术士将贱金属提炼成黄金。
He refined his argument after receiving feedback. = 他在收到反馈后完善了他的论点。


现在完成时:
Scientists have refined the technology significantly. = 科学家们已经显著地改进了这项技术。
They have refined their approach to problem-solving. = 他们已经完善了他们的问题解决方法。


过去完成时:
The engineers had refined the design before the launch. = 工程师们在发布之前已经完善了设计。
She had refined her presentation skills through practice. = 她通过练习完善了她的演示技巧。


将来时:
We will refine the software based on user feedback. = 我们将根据用户反馈改进软件。
They will refine their strategy for the next quarter. = 他们将改进下一季度的战略。


虚拟语气:
If they refined their methods, they would get better results. = 如果他们改进方法，他们会得到更好的结果。
I wish I had refined my essay before submitting it. = 我希望我在提交论文之前已经修改过它。


被动语态:
The metal is refined using a complex process. = 金属是用复杂工艺提炼的。
The software is being refined to improve its performance. = 软件正在被改进以提高其性能。


疑问句:

否定句:
