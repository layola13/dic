semifunctionalisms: /ˌsɛmɪˈfʌŋkʃənəlɪzəm/ | n. | 半功能主义 |  Semifunctionalism is a theoretical approach in various fields, particularly in linguistics and cognitive science, that combines elements of functionalism with other perspectives.  It acknowledges the role of function and purpose in shaping systems, but doesn't exclusively reduce them to purely functional explanations. It often incorporates structural or formal considerations. For instance, in linguistics, it might analyze language structure considering both its communicative function and its inherent structural properties.  |  The study explored semifunctionalism in the development of language acquisition. | 近义词:  partial functionalism, integrated approach | 反义词:  pure functionalism, structuralism
