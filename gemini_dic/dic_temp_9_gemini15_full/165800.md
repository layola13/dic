preobserving: /ˌpriːəbˈzɜːrvɪŋ/ | v. | 预先观察；事先观察 |  The act of observing something before it is officially observed or studied.  It implies a preliminary or anticipatory observation, often for the purpose of preparation or planning. | 例句：The astronomer spent weeks preobserving the comet's trajectory before the main observation period. 天文学家在主要观测期之前花了数周时间预先观测彗星的轨迹。


The researchers preobserved the experimental setup to ensure everything was functioning correctly. 研究人员预先观察了实验装置，以确保一切正常运行。


近义词：preliminary observation, advance observation, scouting, reconnaissance | 反义词：postobserving (not a common word)
