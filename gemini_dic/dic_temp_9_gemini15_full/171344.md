publicise: /ˈpʌblɪsaɪz/ | vt. | 宣传，公布 | To make something known publicly, especially in order to sell it or attract people's attention.  | The company publicised its new product through a series of television commercials. 公司通过一系列电视广告宣传其新产品。 | advertise, promote, announce | suppress, conceal | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They publicise their products on social media. = 他们在社交媒体上宣传他们的产品。
The company regularly publicises its achievements. = 公司定期宣传其成就。


一般过去时:
The band publicised their new album extensively. = 乐队广泛宣传了他们的新专辑。
The government publicised the new policy to the public. = 政府向公众宣传了这项新政策。


现在完成时:
They have publicised the event through various channels. = 他们通过各种渠道宣传了此次活动。
The organisation has successfully publicised its charitable work. = 该组织成功地宣传了它的慈善工作。


过去完成时:
The company had publicised the product launch before the actual date. = 公司在产品发布日期之前就已经宣传了产品发布。
They had publicised the news widely before the press conference. = 他们在新闻发布会之前就已经广泛宣传了这条新闻。

虚拟语气:
