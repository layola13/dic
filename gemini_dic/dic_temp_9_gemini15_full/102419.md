jackal: /ˈdʒækl/ | n. | 豺；卑鄙小人 | A jackal is a wild dog, typically found in Africa and Asia, known for its scavenging habits and distinctive howl.  They are often depicted as cunning and opportunistic creatures.  The term can also be used figuratively to refer to a person who is opportunistic and unscrupulous. | Example:  The jackals scavenged the leftover food after the lion's feast.  豺在狮子盛宴后搜寻剩余的食物。|近义词：wild dog, scavenger, opportunist | 反义词：lion (in terms of predatory status), altruist
