self-regard:/ˌsɛlf rɪˈɡɑːrd/| n. |自尊；自我评价；自我尊重 | Self-regard refers to the way someone thinks and feels about themselves, their own worth, and their capabilities. It encompasses self-esteem, self-respect, and the overall opinion one holds of oneself.  A high level of self-regard often leads to confidence and positive self-image, while low self-regard can contribute to feelings of inadequacy and low self-esteem.|

例句：

Her unwavering self-regard allowed her to overcome numerous setbacks. = 她坚定不移的自尊使她克服了许多挫折。

He lacked the self-regard necessary to pursue his ambitions. = 他缺乏追求抱负所需的自我尊重。


近义词：self-esteem, self-respect, self-worth, pride (in oneself)

反义词：self-loathing, self-deprecation, low self-esteem
