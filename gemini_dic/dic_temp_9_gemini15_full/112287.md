loquacious:/ləˈkweɪʃəs/| adj.| 健谈的；多嘴的；话多的 |  Characterized by fluent and voluble speech.  Someone who is loquacious talks a lot. |  She's a loquacious woman who can talk for hours. 她是一位健谈的女士，可以聊上好几个小时。|近义词：talkative, garrulous, voluble, verbose |反义词：taciturn, reticent, reserved, silent


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is loquacious at parties. = 他在派对上很健谈。
She is always loquacious on the phone. = 她打电话时总是话很多。


一般过去时:
He was loquacious at the meeting. = 他在会议上很健谈。
She was loquacious during the interview. = 她在面试中非常健谈。


现在完成时:
He has been loquacious all evening. = 他整个晚上都很健谈。
She has always been loquacious in her family. = 她在家人的聚会上总是话很多。


过去完成时:
He had been loquacious before the incident. = 事发前他一直很健谈。
She had been loquacious throughout the entire trip. = 在整个旅途中她一直都很健谈。


将来时:
He will be loquacious at the conference. = 他在会议上将会很健谈。
She will be loquacious during the presentation. = 她将在演示过程中非常健谈。


虚拟语气:
If he were loquacious, he would have more friends. = 如果他健谈一些，他就会有更多朋友。
If she were less loquacious, people would listen more. = 如果她话少一些，人们就会更认真地听她说话。


被动语态:
His loquaciousness was noted by everyone. = 他的健谈被每个人都注意到了。
Her loquacious nature was often a source of amusement. = 她健谈的天性常常成为取乐的来源。


疑问句:
Is he always so loquacious? = 他总是这么健谈吗？
Was she loquacious during the debate? = 她在辩论中健谈吗？


否定句:
He is not loquacious in formal settings. = 他在正式场合并不健谈。
She was not loquacious at the funeral. = 她在葬礼上并不健谈。

词源:
From Latin *loquax, from loqui "to speak".
