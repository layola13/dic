impressment: /ɪmˈprɛsmənt/ | n. | 征募；强征；强行征兵 | The act of compelling someone into service, especially into the navy, without their consent.  It was a common practice in the past, often associated with harsh conditions and limited rights for those impressed. | Example: The impressment of sailors into the British navy led to significant tensions with other nations.  英国海军强行征召水手导致与其他国家关系紧张。| 近义词：conscription, coercion, press-ganging | 反义词： voluntary enlistment, free choice |


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government impresses new recruits into the army. = 政府征召新兵入伍。
They impress upon us the importance of education. = 他们让我们明白教育的重要性。


一般过去时:
The captain impressed several men into his crew. = 船长强行征召了几个人加入他的船员。
She impressed the importance of punctuality upon her employees. = 她向她的员工强调准时的重要性。


现在完成时:
The navy has impressed many sailors over the centuries. = 海军几个世纪以来征召了许多水手。
He has impressed on me the need for hard work.  = 他让我明白努力工作的必要性。


过去完成时:

She had impressed upon her son the importance of honesty. = 她已经向她的儿子强调了诚实的重要性。


将来时:

被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:

She didn't impress the urgency of the matter on him. = 她没有向他强调这件事的紧迫性。



PHRASE->

make an impression on = 给某人留下印象

impressed with = 对…印象深刻

词源:
The word "impressment" comes from the verb "impress," which ultimately derives from the Latin word "imprimere," meaning "to press in" or "to stamp."  The nautical meaning developed from the practice of literally "pressing" men into naval service.
