presubstitution:/ˌpriːˌsʌbstɪˈtjuːʃən/| n. |预先替换，预先取代|The act of substituting something beforehand;  replacing something before it's needed or used.  It often implies a proactive measure to prepare for a future event or situation.|例句：The presubstitution of the faulty component prevented a system failure. 预先替换故障部件避免了系统故障。

例句：The presubstitution of fresh batteries ensured the device's continued operation. 预先更换新电池确保了设备持续运行。


近义词：preemptive replacement, prior substitution, advance substitution


反义词：postsubstitution, replacement after the fact


词性：名词
