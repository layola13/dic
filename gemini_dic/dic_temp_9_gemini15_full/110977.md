liquidation:/ˌlɪkwɪˈdeɪʃən/ | n. | 清算；变现；结束；消灭 | The process of winding up the affairs of a business, usually involving the sale of its assets to pay off debts.  It can also refer to the act of converting something into cash or liquid form, or the destruction or elimination of something. | 例句：The company is undergoing liquidation after declaring bankruptcy. 这家公司在宣布破产后正在进行清算。

The court ordered the liquidation of the assets. 法院命令变卖资产。


近义词：winding-up, dissolution, settlement, disposal, extermination | 反义词：establishment, creation, accumulation


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company is liquidating its assets. = 这家公司正在清算其资产。
Liquidation often involves selling off property. = 清算通常包括出售房产。


一般过去时:
They liquidated their investments last year. = 他们去年清算了他们的投资。
The business was liquidated due to heavy losses. = 由于巨额亏损，这家企业被清算。


现在完成时:
The bank has liquidated its holdings in the failing company. = 该银行已经清算其在该失败公司的股份。
They have liquidated all their debts. = 他们已经清偿了所有债务。


过去完成时:
The company had liquidated its assets before the bankruptcy filing. = 在申请破产之前，该公司已经清算其资产。
The government had liquidated the rebel group's resources. = 政府已经清除了叛乱组织的资源。


将来时:
The court will order the liquidation of the company's assets. = 法院将命令清算公司资产。
They will liquidate their business next month. = 他们下个月将清算他们的业务。


虚拟语气:
If the company had not liquidated its assets, it would have faced greater losses. = 如果该公司没有清算其资产，它将面临更大的损失。
I wish I had liquidated my stocks before the market crash. = 我真希望我在市场崩盘前清算了我的股票。


被动语态:
The assets were liquidated quickly and efficiently. = 资产被迅速有效地清算。
The company is being liquidated by the court. = 公司正在被法院清算。


疑问句:
Will the company be liquidated? = 公司会被清算吗？
How will the assets be liquidated? = 资产将如何清算？


否定句:

PHRASE->

词源:
From Middle French liquidation, from Late Latin liquidatio, from Latin liquidare "to make liquid," from liquidus "liquid."  The sense evolved from "making liquid" (as in converting assets to cash) to the broader sense of "winding up" a business.
