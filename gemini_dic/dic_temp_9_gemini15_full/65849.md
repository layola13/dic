evitation:/ˌɛvɪˈteɪʃən/ | n. |规避；逃避；避免 | The act of avoiding or preventing something.  It often refers to deliberately staying away from something unpleasant or dangerous.  | Example:  The company's evitation of taxes led to a large fine. 公司逃税导致巨额罚款。 | 近义词：avoidance, evasion, prevention | 反义词：confrontation, engagement


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He avoids confrontation. = 他避免冲突。
She evades taxes. = 她逃税。


一般过去时:
They avoided the accident. = 他们避开了事故。
He evaded the question. = 他回避了这个问题。


现在完成时:
I have avoided talking to him. = 我一直避免和他说话。
She has evaded responsibility. = 她一直逃避责任。


过去完成时:
We had avoided the problem before it escalated. = 我们在问题升级之前就避免了它。
He had evaded capture for years. = 他潜逃多年未被捕获。


将来时:
They will avoid the crowded streets. = 他们将避开拥挤的街道。
She will evade answering the difficult questions. = 她将回避回答那些棘手的问题。


虚拟语气:
If I had known, I would have avoided the meeting. = 如果我知道的话，我就不会参加那个会议了。
I wish I had evaded that awkward conversation. = 我真希望我当时能避开那次尴尬的谈话。


被动语态:
The problem was avoided. = 问题得到了避免。
The question was evaded. = 这个问题被回避了。


疑问句:
How can we avoid this mistake? = 我们如何才能避免这个错误？
Can this issue be evaded? = 这个问题可以回避吗？


否定句:
They did not avoid the conflict. = 他们没有避免冲突。
She did not evade the issue. = 她没有回避这个问题。
