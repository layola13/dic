desolater: /ˈdɛsəˌleɪtər/ | n. | 使荒凉的人或事物；使人悲伤的事物 | One that desolates; something that causes desolation.  A desolater can refer to a person, event, or force that brings about devastation, ruin, or emptiness, both literally and figuratively. It often implies a sense of profound sadness and loss. |例句：The relentless drought acted as a desolater, turning fertile fields into barren wasteland.  这持续不断的干旱如同一个使荒凉的因素，将肥沃的田地变成了贫瘠的荒地。

The war was a desolater, leaving behind a trail of broken homes and shattered lives.  战争是一个使人悲伤的事物，留下了满目疮痍的家园和破碎的生活。|近义词：destroyer, ravager, spoiler, devastator |反义词：benefactor, savior, restorer, comforter |词性：名词
