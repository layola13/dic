disrupts:/dɪsˈrʌpt/| vt.|扰乱；中断；破坏 |To interrupt the normal progress or activity of something.|例句：The strike disrupted the city's transportation system.罢工扰乱了城市的交通系统。
The loud noise disrupted the meeting. 嘈杂声打断了会议。|近义词：interrupt, disturb, interfere with |反义词：organize, facilitate


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The construction work disrupts the traffic flow. = 建设工程扰乱了交通流量。
Noise disrupts my concentration. = 噪音扰乱了我的注意力。

一般过去时:
The storm disrupted the power supply. = 暴风雨打断了电力供应。
The accident disrupted the traffic for hours. = 事故使交通中断了好几个小时。

现在完成时:
The pandemic has disrupted global supply chains. = 疫情扰乱了全球供应链。
Recent events have disrupted the market. = 最近发生的事件扰乱了市场。

过去完成时:
The heavy rain had disrupted the train schedule. = 大雨已经扰乱了火车时刻表。
The previous protest had disrupted the city's peace. = 此前的抗议活动扰乱了城市的平静。

将来时:
The upcoming election will disrupt the political landscape. = 即将到来的选举将会扰乱政治格局。
The new regulations will disrupt the industry. = 新的法规将会扰乱该行业。

虚拟语气:
If the internet were disrupted, many businesses would suffer. = 如果互联网中断，许多企业将会遭受损失。
I wish the construction wouldn't disrupt my sleep. = 我希望施工不会扰乱我的睡眠。

被动语态:
The meeting was disrupted by a sudden power outage. = 会议因突然停电而中断。
The schedule has been disrupted by unforeseen circumstances. = 计划因不可预见的情况而被打乱。

疑问句:
Will the protest disrupt the city's function? = 抗议活动会扰乱城市的运作吗？
Does the noise disrupt your sleep? = 噪音扰乱你的睡眠吗？

否定句:
The meeting wasn't disrupted. = 会议没有被打扰。
The changes did not disrupt the workflow. = 这些变化没有扰乱工作流程。
