slacking:/ˈslækɪŋ/| v.|偷懒；懈怠；松懈|To be negligent or careless in one's work or duties; to avoid work or responsibilities.|例句：He was slacking off at work and got a warning from his boss. 他工作中偷懒，受到了老板的警告。
例句：Stop slacking and finish your homework! 别偷懒，快完成你的作业！|近义词：shirking, neglecting, loafing|反义词：diligent, hardworking, industrious|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is always slacking off. = 他总是偷懒。
They are slacking in their studies. = 他们在学习上懈怠了。

一般过去时:
I slacked off on my diet last week. = 我上周饮食放纵了。
She slacked off at work and missed the deadline. = 她工作懈怠，错过了最后期限。

现在完成时:
He has been slacking off all semester. = 他整个学期都在偷懒。
We have slacked off on our exercise routine. = 我们已经放松了我们的锻炼计划。

过去完成时:
They had been slacking off for months before the project failed. = 项目失败前，他们已经懈怠了几个月。
She had slacked off on her studies, so she failed the exam. = 她学习懈怠，所以考试不及格。

将来时:
I will be slacking off this weekend. = 我这个周末将会放松一下。
They will slack off if they are not given clear instructions. = 如果不给他们明确的指示，他们将会懈怠。

虚拟语气:
If I hadn't slacked off, I would have passed the exam. = 如果我没有懈怠，我就通过考试了。
I wish I hadn't slacked off on my project. = 我真希望我没有在项目上懈怠。

被动语态:
The work was slacked off due to the lack of manpower. = 由于人手不足，工作进度放缓了。
The project was slacked off because of poor management. = 由于管理不善，项目进展缓慢。

疑问句:
Are you slacking off again? = 你又在偷懒吗？
Was he slacking off during the meeting? = 他在会议期间偷懒了吗？

否定句:
He didn't slack off on his duties. = 他没有怠慢他的职责。
They did not slack off despite the difficult circumstances. = 尽管情况艰难，他们也没有懈怠。
