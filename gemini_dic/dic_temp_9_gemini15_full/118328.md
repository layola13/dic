merge:/mɜːrdʒ/| /mɝdʒ/ | vt. n.|合并；融合|To combine or blend into a single entity.  The process of combining multiple things into one.|例句：The two companies merged to form a larger corporation. = 两家公司合并组建了一家更大的公司。
例句：The river merged with the ocean. = 河流与海洋汇合。|近义词：combine, unite, consolidate, amalgamate|反义词：separate, divide, split|动词，名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They merge their resources. = 他们合并他们的资源。
The two streams merge into a river. = 两条小溪汇成一条河。

一般过去时:
The two companies merged last year. = 这两家公司去年合并了。
The images merged seamlessly. = 图片无缝融合。

现在完成时:
They have merged their businesses successfully. = 他们已经成功地合并了他们的企业。
The two teams have merged their efforts. = 这两个团队已经合并了他们的努力。

过去完成时:
They had already merged their accounts before the audit. = 审计之前他们已经合并了他们的账户。
The project had merged several different ideas. = 该项目已经合并了几种不同的想法。

将来时:
The two departments will merge next month. = 这两个部门将于下个月合并。
The software will automatically merge the files. = 该软件将自动合并文件。

虚拟语气:
If they merged their efforts, they could achieve more. = 如果他们合并他们的努力，他们可以取得更多成就。
It would be better if the two teams merged. = 如果两个团队合并会更好。

被动语态:
The files were merged into a single document. = 文件被合并到一个文档中。
The data will be merged by the program. = 数据将由程序合并。

疑问句:
Will the companies merge? = 公司会合并吗？
Have the two teams merged yet? = 两个团队已经合并了吗？

否定句:
The two companies did not merge. = 这两家公司没有合并。
The images did not merge properly. = 图片没有正确合并。
