calenture:/kæˈlɛntʃər/| n. |热病；航海热；热病性谵妄 | A fever, delirium, or madness supposedly caused by the heat of the sun, especially affecting sailors in tropical regions.  Characterized by strong delusions, often involving a desire to jump into the sea. | Example:  The sailor, suffering from calenture, believed the sea was a green field.  水手患上了航海热，认为大海是一片绿色的田野。
The ship's doctor diagnosed the patient with a severe case of calenture. 船医诊断病人患有严重的航海热。| 近义词：sunstroke, heatstroke, delirium, fever | 反义词：None specifically, but words implying health and mental clarity could be considered antonyms. | 名词
