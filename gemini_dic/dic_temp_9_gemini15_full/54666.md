disobligement:/ˌdɪsəˈblaɪdʒmənt/| n. |不乐意，违反，怠慢 |The act of failing to fulfill an obligation or request; causing offense or displeasure.  It often implies a slight or disregard for someone's wishes or expectations. |例句：He felt a sense of disobligement when his request was ignored. 他因为他的请求被忽视而感到不被重视。  The disobligement caused by the late delivery of the goods resulted in a loss of customers. 货物延迟送达造成的怠慢导致顾客流失。|近义词：offense, displeasure, slight, disregard |反义词：compliance, fulfillment, courtesy, consideration |
