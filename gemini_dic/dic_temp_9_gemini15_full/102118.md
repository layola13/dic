isotope:/ˈaɪsətoʊp/| n. |同位素|An isotope is a variant of a particular chemical element such that it has the same number of protons but a different number of neutrons in its nucleus.  This results in the same atomic number but a different atomic mass. Isotopes may be stable or unstable (radioactive).|Example: Carbon-12 and Carbon-14 are isotopes of carbon.|近义词：nuclide|反义词：无


USAGE->
This word is a noun and doesn't conjugate like verbs.  Therefore, typical verb tenses are not applicable.  The usage focuses on its grammatical function in sentences.

Example 1:  Scientists studied the radioactive isotopes to understand the decay process.  科学家研究放射性同位素以了解衰变过程。

Example 2:  The different isotopes of uranium have varying degrees of radioactivity. 铀的不同同位素具有不同程度的放射性。


PHRASE->
radioactive isotope 放射性同位素
stable isotope 稳定性同位素
isotope analysis 同位素分析
isotope ratio 同位素比率
isotope effect 同位素效应
