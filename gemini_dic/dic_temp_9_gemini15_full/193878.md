self-dislikes:/ˌsɛlfˈdɪslaɪks/ | n. | 自我厌恶 |  A feeling of strong dislike or disapproval of oneself; low self-esteem.  It often manifests as negative self-talk, self-criticism, and a lack of self-acceptance. | Example:  Her self-dislikes stemmed from a childhood filled with criticism.  她的自我厌恶源于充满批评的童年。

She struggles with deep-seated self-dislikes and needs professional help. 她与根深蒂固的自我厌恶作斗争，需要专业帮助。


近义词: self-loathing, self-hatred, low self-esteem, negative self-image | 反义词: self-love, self-acceptance, high self-esteem


词源:  The word is a compound of "self" and "dislikes." "Self" is an Old English word meaning "one's own person" and "dislikes" is a verb "dislike" in the noun form, denoting a feeling of aversion or disapproval.  The combination signifies a dislike directed towards oneself.
