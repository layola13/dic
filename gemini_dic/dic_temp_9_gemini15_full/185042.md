retheorizing: /ˌriːˈθiːəraɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 重新理论化；重新构建理论 |  The act of reconsidering and reformulating existing theories, often in light of new evidence or perspectives. It involves critically examining established assumptions and proposing alternative explanations or frameworks.  | Example:  The researchers are retheorizing the origins of the universe based on the latest data from the James Webb Telescope.  = 研究人员正在根据詹姆斯·韦伯太空望远镜的最新数据重新构建宇宙起源的理论。


近义词：rethinking, reformulating, reinterpreting, revisiting

反义词：accepting (uncritically), solidifying
