heteroscian:/ˌhɛtərəˈskiən/| adj.| 异影的；日影不同的 |  Referring to places on Earth where the sun's shadow falls in opposite directions at the same time of day.  This is due to the curvature of the Earth.  | Example: Ancient Greeks recognized that the differing shadows of objects in different locations at the same time indicated a spherical Earth, a concept they termed heteroscian.  古代希腊人认识到，在同一时间不同地点物体的影子不同，这表明地球是球形的，他们称之为异影。|近义词： having opposite shadows|反义词：  homoscian (having the same shadows)|形容词
