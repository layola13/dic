officiated:/əˈfɪʃieɪtɪd/| v.|主持，担任司仪|To perform the duties of an official position, especially in a ceremony or religious service.  |例句：The mayor officiated at the wedding ceremony. 市长主持了婚礼仪式。|例句：The priest officiated at the funeral. 牧师主持了葬礼。|近义词：presided over, conducted, performed|反义词：participated in (as a non-official)|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He officiates at many weddings each year. = 他每年主持许多婚礼。
The judge officiates in the courtroom. = 法官在法庭上主持。

一般过去时:
The priest officiated at my grandfather's funeral. = 牧师主持了我爷爷的葬礼。
She officiated the ceremony flawlessly. = 她完美地主持了仪式。

现在完成时:
He has officiated at hundreds of weddings. = 他已经主持过数百场婚礼。
She has officiated at several important events this year. = 她今年已经主持了几场重要的活动。

过去完成时:
Before the new judge arrived, the old one had officiated for 20 years. = 新法官到来之前，老法官已经主持了20年。
They had officiated at the groundbreaking ceremony before the construction began. = 在施工开始之前，他们主持了奠基仪式。

将来时:
The bishop will officiate at the confirmation ceremony. = 主教将主持坚信礼。
She will officiate at the graduation ceremony next month. = 她下个月将主持毕业典礼。

虚拟语气:

被动语态:

否定句:
