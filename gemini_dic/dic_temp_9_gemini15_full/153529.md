pentaconter:/ˌpɛntəˈkɒntər/| n. |五桨战船|A pentaconter was an ancient Greek warship, typically propelled by 50 oarsmen arranged in two banks of 25 oars each.  It was a relatively fast and maneuverable vessel, used primarily during the Classical period.
