gobline:/ˈɡɒblɪn/| n. |妖精，小鬼| A goblin is a mythical creature, often depicted as a small, ugly, mischievous, and sometimes malevolent being.  Goblins are frequently found in folklore and fantasy literature. They are often associated with caves, forests, or other dark and secluded places. |Example: The children told stories about goblins living in the woods. 孩子们讲述了住在森林里的妖精的故事。|近义词：sprite, imp, gremlin, hobgoblin |反义词：angel, saint
