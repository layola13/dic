decreed:/dɪˈkriːd/| vt.|法令；颁布；判决|To officially decide or announce something, especially a law or punishment.|The king decreed that all citizens must pay taxes. 国王下令所有公民必须纳税。|近义词：ordain, proclaim, mandate|反义词：repeal, revoke|动词

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government decrees new regulations every year. = 政府每年颁布新的规章制度。
She decrees that all work must be completed by Friday. = 她下令所有工作必须在周五前完成。

一般过去时:
The judge decreed a life sentence for the murderer. = 法官判处凶手无期徒刑。
The emperor decreed the construction of a new temple. = 皇帝下令建造一座新庙宇。

现在完成时:
The council has decreed a ban on smoking in public places. = 理事会已下令禁止在公共场所吸烟。
They have decreed that the old building must be demolished. = 他们已下令拆除旧建筑。


过去完成时:
The king had decreed a national holiday before the announcement. = 国王在宣布之前已经下令全国放假。
It had been decreed that no one could leave the city. = 之前已经下令任何人不得离开城市。


将来时:
The court will decree a fair trial for the defendant. = 法院将判决被告进行公平审判。
The president will decree new economic policies next month. = 总统下个月将颁布新的经济政策。


虚拟语气:
If the king decreed it, then it would be done. = 如果国王下令，那么它就会被执行。
I wish the government had decreed more environmental protection measures. = 我希望政府已经颁布更多环境保护措施。


被动语态:
A new law was decreed by the parliament. = 一项新法律由议会颁布。
The punishment was decreed after a thorough investigation. = 经过彻底调查后，判决下来了。


疑问句:
What has the court decreed regarding this case? = 法院就这个案子作出了什么判决？
What will be decreed by the authorities? = 当局将颁布什么？


否定句:
The emperor did not decree any new taxes this year. = 皇帝今年没有颁布任何新税。
The government has not decreed any changes to the current policy. = 政府没有对现行政策进行任何更改。


PHRASE->
decree a law = 制定法律
decree a punishment = 判处刑罚
decree a holiday = 宣布放假
decree a policy = 制定政策
decree a ban = 下令禁止


词源:
From Old French *decreer, from Latin decretum ("a thing decreed"), the past participle of decernere ("to decide, determine"), from de- ("completely") + cernere ("to separate, decide").
