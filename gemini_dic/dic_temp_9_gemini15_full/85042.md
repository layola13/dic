harbors:/ˈhɑːrbərz/ | v. n. | 港口；庇护；怀有（想法、感情等） |  To shelter or give refuge to; to contain or conceal; a place of shelter or safety, especially a sheltered body of water where ships can moor. | 例句：The small fishing village harbors a secret about its past.  这个小渔村隐藏着关于它过去的秘密。 | 近义词：shelters, protects, conceals, harbors, harbors, shelters | 反义词：reveals, exposes, endangers


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The harbor protects the boats from the storm. = 港口保护船只免受风暴侵袭。
She harbors resentment towards her former boss. = 她对前老板心怀怨恨。


一般过去时:
The ship harbored in the bay overnight. = 船在海湾过夜停泊。
He harbored a dangerous secret for years. = 他多年来隐藏着一个危险的秘密。


现在完成时:
The city has harbored many immigrants over the years. = 多年来，这座城市收留了许多移民。
She has harbored doubts about his honesty. = 她一直怀疑他的诚实。


过去完成时:
They had harbored hopes of a better future. = 他们曾对未来抱有更好的希望。
The fugitive had harbored in the mountains for weeks. = 逃犯在山里躲藏了好几个星期。


将来时:
The new port will harbor larger vessels. = 新港将容纳更大的船只。
He will harbor no ill feelings towards them. = 他不会对他们心怀恶意。


虚拟语气:
If I had a boat, I would harbor it in a safe place. = 如果我有一艘船，我会把它停泊在安全的地方。
If they hadn't harbored such suspicions, the situation would have been different. = 如果他们没有抱有这样的怀疑，情况就会有所不同。


被动语态:

疑问句:
