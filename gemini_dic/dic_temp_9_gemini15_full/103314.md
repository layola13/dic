jiggered:/ˈdʒɪɡərd/| adj. v.|搞砸的；损坏的；疲惫的；| 英文解析:  The word "jiggered" is an informal British English term. As an adjective, it means damaged, broken, or in a state of disrepair. As a verb, it implies to damage or spoil something. It can also colloquially mean exhausted or extremely tired. The origin is uncertain, but it may be related to the verb "to jigger," meaning to shake or jolt violently. |例句：The car's completely jiggered after the accident.  事故后，汽车完全坏了。|近义词:broken, damaged, ruined, knackered, exhausted, worn out|反义词: intact, undamaged, repaired, new, rested
