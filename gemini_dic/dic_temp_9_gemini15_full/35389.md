co-operationist: /ˌkoʊˌɒpəˈreɪʃənɪst/ | n. |合作主义者，合作论者 | A person who believes in or advocates for cooperation, especially in political or economic systems.  Someone who supports the principles and practices of cooperation. | Example:  The co-operationist argued for a more collaborative approach to international relations.  合作主义者主张对国际关系采取更合作的方式。


The co-operationist movement gained momentum during the early 20th century.  20世纪初，合作主义运动势头强劲。|近义词：collaborator, cooperator |反义词：individualist, competitor |名词
