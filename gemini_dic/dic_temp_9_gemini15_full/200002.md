skedaddling:/skɛˈdædlɪŋ/ | v. | 偷偷溜走；迅速离开 | To leave quickly and secretly, often to avoid something unpleasant.  | The thief skedaddled after grabbing the jewels.  小偷抢了珠宝后就溜之大吉了。| 近义词：absconding, decamping, fleeing, making off | 反义词：arriving, appearing, staying


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He skedaddles whenever he sees the police. = 他一看到警察就溜之大吉。
They skedaddle from the party early. = 他们很早就偷偷溜出了派对。

一般过去时:
She skedaddled from the boring lecture. = 她从无聊的讲座上溜走了。
We skedaddled out of the restaurant before the bill arrived. = 我们在账单来之前就偷偷溜出了餐馆。

现在完成时:
He has skedaddled and we don't know where he is. = 他已经溜走了，我们不知道他在哪里。
They have skedaddled from the scene of the accident. = 他们已经逃离了事故现场。

过去完成时:
She had skedaddled before the police arrived. = 在警察到达之前，她已经溜走了。
They had skedaddled from the burning building. = 他们在建筑物着火之前就逃跑了。

将来时:
I think he will skedaddle if he sees us. = 我想如果他看到我们，他会溜走的。
They will skedaddle as soon as they can. = 他们会尽快溜走。

虚拟语气:
If I were you, I would skedaddle. = 如果我是你，我会溜走。
I wish I had skedaddled sooner. = 我真希望我早点溜走。

被动语态:
(This verb is rarely used in the passive voice because it implies active and intentional movement.)  这个动词很少用被动语态，因为它暗示主动和有意的动作。


疑问句:
Did he skedaddle? = 他溜了吗？
Will they skedaddle? = 他们会溜走吗？

否定句:
He didn't skedaddle; he faced the music. = 他没有溜走；他勇敢地面对了这一切。
They wouldn't skedaddle, even when threatened. = 即使受到威胁，他们也不会溜走。
