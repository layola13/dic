reregulation: /ˌriːˌreɡjʊˈleɪʃən/ | n. | 再管制；重新管制 | The act of regulating something again, often after a period of deregulation or insufficient regulation.  It implies a return to stricter controls or a modification of existing regulations. | Example: The reregulation of the financial industry followed the 2008 crisis.  重新管制金融业紧随2008年危机之后。

The government announced a reregulation of the telecommunications market to improve competition. 政府宣布重新管制电信市场以改善竞争。| 近义词：re-regulation, renewed regulation, stricter regulation | 反义词：deregulation, liberalization

词源:

The word "reregulation" is a combination of the prefix "re-" (meaning "again" or "anew") and "regulation" (the act of controlling or directing according to rule).  Therefore, it literally means "regulating again".
