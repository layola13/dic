enclaving:/ɪnˈkleɪvɪŋ/ | v. |  使…成为飞地；孤立；圈定 | The process of creating or becoming an enclave; the act of surrounding and isolating a group or area.  It often implies a negative connotation, suggesting segregation or marginalization. | Example: The new highway effectively enclaved the small village, cutting it off from the surrounding region.  新的高速公路实际上把这个小村庄变成了飞地，把它与周围地区隔离开来。 |近义词：isolating, segregating, secluding | 反义词：integrating, connecting, unifying |
