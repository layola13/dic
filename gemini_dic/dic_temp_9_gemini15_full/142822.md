out-ask:/ˌaʊtˈæsk/| vt.|超过...询问；问得比...多|To ask more questions than someone else, or to ask questions that are more challenging or difficult.  |例句：The journalist out-asked all the other reporters at the press conference.  记者在新闻发布会上问的问题比其他记者都多。|例句：He completely out-asked his opponent in the debate, leaving him speechless. 他在辩论中提出的问题远超对手，使对手哑口无言。|近义词：surpass, exceed, outstrip|反义词：under-ask, avoid|动词


USAGE->

He out-asks everyone in the class. = 他问的问题比班上任何人都多。
She out-asks her colleagues during meetings. = 她在会议上问的问题比她的同事多。
