self-sovereignty:/ˌsɛlfˈsɒvrənti/| n. | 自主权，自我主权 | The right of a state or people to govern themselves without external interference.  It emphasizes the inherent right of a group or entity to control its own affairs and destiny.  This concept is central to international law and political philosophy, often invoked in discussions of national independence, indigenous rights, and individual autonomy. | Example: The nation fiercely defended its self-sovereignty against foreign pressure.  这个国家强烈捍卫其不受外国干涉的自主权。


Example: Indigenous communities often fight for self-sovereignty to preserve their culture and traditions.  土著社区经常为争取自主权而斗争，以保护他们的文化和传统。


近义词：autonomy, independence, self-determination, self-governance | 反义词：subjugation, dependence, colonialism, control
