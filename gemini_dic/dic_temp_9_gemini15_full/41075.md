copperheads:/ˈkɑːpərˌhedz/ | n. |铜头蛇；（美国内战期间的）和平主义者 | Copperheads are venomous snakes found in North America, known for their distinctive copper-colored heads.  During the American Civil War, the term "Copperhead" was used derisively to refer to Northerners who opposed the war and sympathized with the Confederacy. |例句：The hiker carefully avoided stepping on the copperhead basking in the sun.  徒步旅行者小心地避开了在阳光下晒太阳的铜头蛇。|例句：Many historians consider the Copperheads to have been unpatriotic, though their motivations were complex. 许多历史学家认为铜头蛇是不爱国的，尽管他们的动机很复杂。|近义词： (snake) rattlesnake, viper |反义词： (for the Civil War context) Unionist, Patriot |词性：名词
