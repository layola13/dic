lingering:/ˈlɪŋɡərɪŋ/| adj.|持续的；挥之不去的；缓慢的|Continuing to exist or occur for longer than expected or desired; slow to disappear or vanish.|The lingering smell of smoke filled the air.  空气中弥漫着挥之不去的烟味。|The lingering effects of the illness were debilitating.  疾病的长期影响使人衰弱。|persistent, abiding, enduring|fleeting, transient, ephemeral|形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The aroma lingers in the air. = 香气在空气中弥漫。
He lingers over his coffee. = 他慢慢地品着咖啡。


一般过去时:
The scent lingered long after she left. = 她离开很久后，香味仍然残留。
They lingered at the party until late. = 他们在派对上待到很晚。


现在完成时:
The pain has lingered for weeks. = 疼痛已经持续了好几个星期。
The memory has lingered in my mind. = 这段记忆一直萦绕在我的脑海中。


过去完成时:
The smell had lingered since the fire. = 自从火灾发生以来，气味就一直挥之不去。
She had lingered too long, missing the train. = 她逗留太久，错过了火车。


将来时:
The effects of the drug will linger for several hours. = 药物的作用将会持续几个小时。
The feeling of sadness will linger for a long time. = 悲伤的感觉将会持续很长时间。


虚拟语气:
If he lingered longer, he would have missed his flight. = 如果他逗留更久，他就会错过航班。
I wish the sweet smell would linger a little longer. = 我希望甜美的香味能多留一会儿。


被动语态:
The question was lingered upon for a long time. =  这个问题被讨论了很久。
The problem is lingered over by the committee. = 委员会对这个问题迟迟未决。


疑问句:
Does the smell linger? = 气味还在吗？
How long will the effects linger? = 效果会持续多久？


否定句:
The pain didn't linger long. = 疼痛没有持续很久。
The feeling didn't linger. = 这种感觉没有持续。
