huckster:/ˈhʌkstər/| n. v.|小贩，兜售者；吹嘘；兜售| A huckster is a person who sells goods, especially in a forceful or aggressive way, often using exaggerated claims.  They are often associated with hawking goods on the street or in a market. The verb form means to promote or sell aggressively and often dishonestly.|例句：The huckster shouted loudly to attract customers to his stall.  这个小贩大声叫喊以吸引顾客到他的摊位。

He tried to huckster his questionable investment scheme. 他试图兜售他可疑的投资计划。|近义词：peddler, hawker, vendor, salesman, tout|反义词：buyer, customer
