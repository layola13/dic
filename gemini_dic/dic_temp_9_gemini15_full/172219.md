pushback:/ˈpʊʃˌbæk/| n. v.| 反驳；阻力；反抗；延迟 | Resistance or opposition to a plan, idea, or suggestion;  the act of pushing something back. |例句：There was considerable pushback from the community against the proposed new highway. 社区对拟建的新高速公路表示强烈反对。

例句：The airline announced a pushback of the flight due to bad weather. 由于天气恶劣，航空公司宣布航班延误。


近义词：resistance, opposition, objection, protest, delay

反义词：acceptance, approval, support, acquiescence


词性: noun, verb

词源:
The word "pushback" is a compound word formed from the verb "push" and the adverb "back."  It literally means to push something back, but has evolved to figuratively represent resistance or opposition.  Its usage as a noun and verb describing opposition emerged in the latter half of the 20th century, gaining wider prominence in recent decades.
