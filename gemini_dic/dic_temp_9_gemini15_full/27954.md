cantoning: /kænˈtɔːnɪŋ/ | n. |驻扎；分遣；(尤指军队) 驻扎在营房里 |The act of cantoning, especially of troops in barracks or quarters.  It often refers to the system of quartering soldiers in private homes. |例句：The army was cantoning in the villages near the border.  军队驻扎在边境附近的村庄里。

The troops were cantoned in the town for the winter.  部队在城里过冬。


近义词： quartering, billeting, housing | 反义词： dispersing, deploying (in the sense of spreading out troops) | 词性：名词


USAGE->

The army is cantoning in the local villages. = 军队驻扎在当地的村庄里。
They are cantoning their troops for the winter. = 他们正在为冬天安置他们的军队。

一般过去时:
The soldiers were cantoned in the nearby town. = 士兵们驻扎在附近的城镇。
The regiment cantoned there for several months. = 该团在那里驻扎了几个月。

词源:  From the French word *canton*, meaning "district," "region," or "canton" (a subdivision of a Swiss state).  The military use evolved from the practice of assigning troops to specific areas or districts for lodging and accommodation.
