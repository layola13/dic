chromatographically:/ˌkroʊməˌtæɡrəˈfɪkli/| adv. |色谱法地；用色谱法|Adverb formed from "chromatography," describing a process or method using chromatography.  It indicates that something is done or analyzed using chromatographic techniques. |The mixture was separated chromatographically. |  |  |adv.


例句：

The components of the mixture were separated chromatographically using a high-performance liquid chromatograph (HPLC).  混合物的组分使用高效液相色谱仪 (HPLC) 进行色谱分离。

The scientists analyzed the sample chromatographically to identify the different compounds present. 科学家们用色谱法分析样品以鉴定存在的不同化合物。


词源:

The word is derived from "chromatography," which itself comes from the Greek words "chroma" (χρώμα), meaning "color," and "graphein" (γράφειν), meaning "to write."  The original method involved separating colored substances, hence the name.


用法说明:

"Chromatographically" is an adverb that modifies verbs, describing how an action is performed. It specifically refers to the use of chromatography, a laboratory technique used to separate and analyze mixtures of substances.  Different types of chromatography exist (e.g., gas chromatography, liquid chromatography), but the adverb applies generally to any chromatographic method.


近义词:

There isn't a direct synonym for "chromatographically," as it precisely describes a specific method.  However, words like "analytically,"  "separatively," or phrases like "using chromatographic techniques" could be used depending on the context.


反义词:

There is no direct antonym.  The opposite would be to analyze or separate a mixture using a different method, such as spectroscopy or distillation.
