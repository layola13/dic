introductress:/ˌɪntrəˈdʌktrəs/| n. |女介绍人，女主持人|A woman who introduces someone or something, or who acts as a host or presenter.  The word is largely obsolete and rarely used in modern English. It implies a more formal or ceremonial introduction than a simple introduction.| Example: The introductress gracefully welcomed the guest speaker to the stage. 女介绍人优雅地欢迎演讲嘉宾上台。|近义词：introducer, hostess, presenter|反义词：None specifically, but words implying a lack of introduction could be considered antonyms in context.


USAGE->
一般现在时:
She introduces the guests. = 她介绍宾客。
The presenter introduces the next segment. = 主持人介绍下一环节。


一般过去时:
She introduced me to her friends. = 她把我介绍给她的朋友们。
The hostess introduced the main course. = 女主人介绍了主菜。


现在完成时:
She has introduced many speakers at this conference. = 她已经在这场会议上介绍了许多演讲者。
He has introduced several innovative ideas to the team. = 他已经向团队介绍了几项创新理念。


过去完成时:
The speaker had already been introduced before I arrived. = 我到达之前，演讲者已经被介绍过了。
They had introduced the new policy before the meeting. = 会议之前，他们已经介绍了新政策。


将来时:
She will introduce the new product next week. = 她下周将介绍新产品。
He will introduce himself to the audience. = 他将向观众做自我介绍。


虚拟语气:
If she were to introduce him, he would be very happy. = 如果她介绍他，他会很高兴。
I wish I had introduced myself earlier. = 我希望我早点做自我介绍。


被动语态:
The new policy was introduced last month. = 新政策是上个月推出的。
The guests were introduced to the CEO. = 客人们被介绍给了首席执行官。


疑问句:
Who introduced you to this job? = 谁把你介绍到这份工作的？
Will someone introduce the next speaker? = 会有人介绍下一位演讲者吗？


否定句:
She did not introduce herself. = 她没有自我介绍。
They didn't introduce the new features at the meeting. = 他们在会议上没有介绍新功能。
