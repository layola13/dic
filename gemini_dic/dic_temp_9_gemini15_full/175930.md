rationalisms:/ˈræʃənəlɪzəmz/| n.|理性主义，理性论|The philosophical view that emphasizes reason and logic as the primary sources of knowledge and justification.  Rationalism often contrasts with empiricism, which prioritizes sensory experience. |Example: The Enlightenment saw a rise in rationalisms across Europe, leading to significant advancements in science and philosophy.  启蒙时代见证了理性主义在欧洲的兴起，从而导致了科学和哲学的重大进步。|近义词：reason, logic, intellectualism |反义词：irrationalism, empiricism, mysticism
