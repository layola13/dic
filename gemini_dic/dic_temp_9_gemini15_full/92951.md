ideographics:/ˌaɪdiəˈɡræfɪks/| n. |表意文字；表意文字系统 |A system of writing in which symbols represent ideas or concepts rather than sounds.  Ideographics are often used in conjunction with other writing systems, such as alphabets or syllabaries.  The most well-known example of an ideographic writing system is Chinese characters. |Example:  Many ancient civilizations used ideographics to record their history and beliefs.  许多古代文明使用表意文字来记录他们的历史和信仰。|近义词：pictography, logography |反义词：alphabetic writing, phonetic writing
