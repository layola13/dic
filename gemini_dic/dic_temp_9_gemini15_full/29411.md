catacombs:/ˈkætəˌkuːmz/| n.|地下墓穴；墓室|Underground tunnels or caves, especially those used as burial places, often with rows of niches for bodies.  The Roman catacombs are a famous example, used for Christian burials during the Roman Empire.|例句：The catacombs beneath the city are a labyrinth of dark tunnels and burial chambers. = 城市下面的地下墓穴是一个黑暗隧道和墓室的迷宫。|例句：Tourists were fascinated by the ancient frescoes preserved within the catacombs. = 游客们对保存在地下墓穴里的古代壁画很着迷。|近义词：crypt, sepulchre, tomb, mausoleum|反义词：aboveground structure, open-air burial
