disconsolate:/ˈdɪskɒnsələt/| adj.| 绝望的；郁闷的；悲伤的 | Without consolation or comfort; dejected; unhappy.  Feeling or expressing great sadness; inconsolable. |例句：She was disconsolate after the death of her pet. 她宠物死后非常悲痛。

例句：He sat disconsolate by the window, staring out at the rain. 他沮丧地坐在窗边，望着窗外的雨。


近义词：dejected, despondent, downcast, forlorn, unhappy, miserable, inconsolable

反义词：happy, cheerful, joyful, elated, comforted


词源:
The word "disconsolate" comes from the Old French word "disconsoler," which itself is composed of the prefix "dis-" (meaning "not" or "without") and the verb "consoler" (meaning "to console" or "to comfort").  The ultimate origin traces back to the Latin word "consolari," meaning "to comfort."
