nonsibilantly:/ˌnɒnˈsɪbɪləntli/ | adv. |非咝音地|Adverb describing the production of sounds without using the fricative sounds produced by the tongue and teeth.  It refers to the articulation of sounds that do not involve friction between the tongue and teeth, as opposed to sibilants like /s/, /z/, /ʃ/, /ʒ/, /tʃ/, and /dʒ/.|The speaker pronounced the word "thin" nonsibilantly, almost without any friction.|近义词：without sibilance, smoothly (in a related context)|反义词：sibilantly
