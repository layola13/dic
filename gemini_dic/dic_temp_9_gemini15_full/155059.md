petrels:/ˈpɛtrəlz/| n. |海燕，海燕属鸟类 |Any of numerous seabirds of the family Procellariidae, having long wings and typically inhabiting the open ocean.  They are known for their graceful flight and ability to glide for long distances. |例句：We saw several petrels soaring effortlessly over the waves. 我们看到几只海燕毫不费力地在波浪上空翱翔。

例句：The storm drove the petrels inland.  暴风雨把海燕赶到了内陆。


近义词：seabird, ocean bird|反义词：None specifically.

词源:
The word "petrel" comes from the Old French word "petrel," which is ultimately derived from the name of Saint Peter.  The association stems from the belief that petrels could walk on water, similar to the biblical account of Saint Peter walking on water.
