revolt:/rɪˈvoʊlt/| n., v.| 反叛；反抗；起义；令人厌恶的事物 |  To rise in rebellion against an authority or established system; something extremely unpleasant or disgusting. | 近义词：rebellion, uprising, mutiny, insurrection | 反义词：submission, obedience, compliance


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The people revolt against the oppressive regime. = 人民反抗压迫的政权。
He revolts at the sight of blood. = 他看到血就反胃。


一般过去时:
The colonists revolted against British rule. = 殖民者反抗英国统治。
She revolted at the thought of eating insects. = 她一想到吃昆虫就反感。


现在完成时:
The citizens have revolted against the corrupt government. = 公民们已经反抗腐败的政府。
I have revolted against the idea my whole life. = 我一生都反对这个想法。


过去完成时:
The army had revolted before the king could react. = 国王还没来得及反应，军队就哗变了。
She had revolted at the suggestion before he finished speaking. = 他话还没说完，她就对这个建议表示反感了。


将来时:
The people will revolt if their demands are not met. = 如果他们的要求得不到满足，人民将会起义。
He will revolt at the idea of eating that.  = 他一想到吃那个就会反胃。


虚拟语气:
If the people were to revolt, the government would fall. = 如果人民起义，政府就会垮台。
I wish I hadn't revolted against my parents' wishes. = 我真希望我没有反抗父母的意愿。


被动语态:
The government was revolted against by the people. = 政府遭到人民的反抗。
The idea was revolted at by many people. = 许多人对这个想法感到厌恶。


疑问句:
Did the soldiers revolt? = 士兵们造反了吗？
Does the smell revolt you? = 这个气味让你作呕吗？


否定句:
The people did not revolt against the new law. = 人民没有反抗新法律。
The food didn't revolt him.  = 这食物没有让他反胃。


PHRASE->
revolt against: 反抗…
revolt of: …的叛乱
in revolt:  正在反抗中
open revolt: 公开的反抗
student revolt: 学生运动


词源:
From Old French *revolter, from Latin revoltare "to turn around, revolve," from re- "again" + volvere "to roll, turn."  The sense of "rebellion" developed from the idea of turning against authority.
