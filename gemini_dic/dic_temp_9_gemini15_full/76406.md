gage:/ɡeɪdʒ/| n. v.| 抵押；测量；估计；挑战； |A gage is something used to measure something or to challenge someone.| The farmer used a rain gage to measure the rainfall.  农夫用雨量计测量降雨量。 He accepted the gage and challenged his rival to a duel. 他接受了挑战，向他的对手发起了决斗。| measure, gauge, assess |  | n. v.


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He gages the distance before jumping. = 他跳之前估量距离。
She gages the quality of the fabric. = 她评估布料的质量。


一般过去时:
They gaged the well to find out the depth. = 他们测量井的深度。
He gaged his opponent's strength. = 他估量对手的实力。


现在完成时:
We have gaged the river's flow. = 我们已经测量了河流的流量。
She has gaged the public's reaction to her new book. = 她已经评估了公众对她新书的反应。


过去完成时:
They had gaged the water level before the storm. = 暴风雨来临之前，他们已经测量了水位。
He had gaged the situation before making a decision. = 他在做决定之前已经评估了形势。


将来时:
We will gage the impact of the new policy. = 我们将评估新政策的影响。
They will gage the progress of the project next week. = 他们下周将评估项目的进展。


虚拟语气:
If I had gaged the situation correctly, I wouldn't have made that mistake. = 如果我正确地评估了形势，我就不会犯那个错误。
I wish I had gaged the depth of the river before swimming. = 我希望我在游泳之前已经测量了河的深度。


被动语态:
The water level was gaged regularly. = 水位定期测量。
The impact of the advertisement was gaged by the company. = 广告的影响由公司评估。


疑问句:
How do you gage the success of a product? = 你如何评估产品的成功？
How was the depth of the hole gaged? = 洞的深度是如何测量的？


否定句:
He didn't gage the consequences of his actions. = 他没有衡量他行为的后果。
They didn't gage the public's opinion before launching the product. = 他们在推出产品之前没有评估公众的意见。
