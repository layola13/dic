haematomas:/ˌhiːməˈtoʊməz/| n. |血肿；瘀血 | A hematoma is a collection of blood outside blood vessels.  It occurs because of a break in the wall of a blood vessel, allowing blood to leak into the surrounding tissues. Hematomas can be caused by injuries, such as a bruise or a cut, or by medical conditions.  They can range in size from small to large, and they can be superficial or deep within the body.  | Example: The patient presented with a large hematoma on his forehead after the fall.  病人摔倒后额头上出现了一个很大的血肿。


Example:  The surgeon carefully removed the hematoma during the operation.  外科医生在手术中小心地清除了血肿。|近义词：bruise, contusion, effusion |反义词：无 |词性:名词
