drown:/draʊn/| vt.  |淹死；使淹没；(声音)被压过；浸没 |to die because you are underwater and cannot breathe, or to kill someone in this way; to cover something completely in water; to be so loud that other sounds cannot be heard; to submerge something completely in liquid. |例句：He drowned in the river. 他淹死在河里。|例句：The heavy rain drowned out the birdsong. 大雨声盖过了鸟鸣声。|近义词：submerge, inundate, suffocate |反义词：save, rescue


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The rising tide drowns the low-lying areas. = 上涨的潮水淹没了低洼地区。
The loud music drowns out the conversation. = 喧闹的音乐盖过了谈话声。

一般过去时:
The child almost drowned in the pool. = 那孩子差点在游泳池里淹死。
The heavy rain drowned out the traffic noise. = 大雨声盖过了交通噪音。

现在完成时:
Three people have drowned in the river this year. = 今年已经有三人在这条河里淹死了。
The noise has drowned out my thoughts. = 噪音已经淹没了我的思绪。

过去完成时:
The boat had already drowned before the rescue team arrived. = 在救援队到达之前，船已经沉没了。
The thunder had drowned out the birdsong. = 雷声已经盖过了鸟鸣声。

将来时:
If the dam breaks, it will drown the whole village. = 如果大坝决堤，整个村庄都会被淹没。
The upcoming storm will likely drown out any attempts at communication. = 即将来临的风暴可能会淹没任何沟通尝试。

虚拟语气:
If I had swum further out, I might have drowned. = 如果我游得更远一些，我可能就淹死了。
If the music hadn't been so loud, we could have heard the announcements. = 如果音乐没有那么响，我们就能听到广播了。

被动语态:
The village was drowned by the flood. = 村庄被洪水淹没了。
The conversation was drowned out by the cheering crowd. = 谈话声被欢呼的人群盖过了。

疑问句:
Did anyone drown in the accident? = 事故中有人淹死吗？
Will the heavy rain drown out the speaker's voice? = 大雨会盖过演讲者的声音吗？

否定句:
He didn't drown, he was rescued. = 他没有淹死，他获救了。
The small sound did not drown out the larger one. = 微弱的声音没有盖过较大的声音。

PHRASE->
drown out: 盖过…的声音
drown in: 沉浸在…中；淹没在…中
drown oneself: 自杀；淹死自己
drown with sorrow: 悲伤欲绝
nearly drown: 差一点淹死
