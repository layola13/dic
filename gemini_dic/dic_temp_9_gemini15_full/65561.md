eutrophy:/juːˈtrɒfi/| /ˌjuːˈtroʊfi/ | n. | 富营养化 | The excessive richness of nutrients in a lake or other body of water, frequently due to runoff from the land, which causes a dense growth of plant life and death of animal life from lack of oxygen.  | Example:  Eutrophication of the lake led to a massive algae bloom, depleting oxygen levels and killing fish.  富营养化导致湖泊发生大规模藻类繁殖，耗尽氧气，导致鱼类死亡。 | 近义词：hypertrophy, overfertilization | 反义词：oligotrophy
