live-down: /lɪvˈdaʊn/ | v. | 逐渐淡忘；逐渐克服（不光彩的事）| To gradually overcome or get over a bad experience or reputation.  |例句：He’ll never live down that embarrassing incident. 他永远也忘不了那件令人尴尬的事。
He's trying to live down his past mistakes. 他正在努力克服过去的错误。| 近义词：overcome, get over, expunge | 反义词：perpetuate, aggravate


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He lives down his past mistakes every day. = 他每天都在努力克服过去的错误。
She lives down the scandal gradually. = 她正在逐渐淡忘这起丑闻。


一般过去时:
He lived down the humiliation with time. = 他随着时间的推移逐渐克服了羞辱。
They lived down the criticism through hard work. = 他们通过努力工作逐渐克服了批评。


现在完成时:
She has lived down the incident by focusing on her work. = 她通过专注于工作已经逐渐忘记了那件事。
He has lived down his reputation for being lazy. = 他已经逐渐摆脱了懒惰的名声。


过去完成时:
By the time the election came around, he had lived down the controversy. = 到选举的时候，他已经逐渐淡忘了这场争议。
She had lived down her initial nervousness before the presentation. = 在演讲之前，她已经克服了最初的紧张情绪。


将来时:
He will live down this setback eventually. = 他最终会克服这个挫折。
She will live down the rumors in time. = 她最终会逐渐淡忘这些谣言。


虚拟语气:
If he apologized sincerely, he might live down the incident more easily. = 如果他真诚道歉，他可能会更容易忘记那件事。
I wish I could live down my youthful indiscretions more quickly. = 我真希望我能更快地忘记我年轻时的轻率行为。


被动语态:  (This verb is rarely used in the passive voice.)


疑问句:
Will he ever live down that terrible mistake? = 他能忘记那个可怕的错误吗？
Can she live down the public criticism? = 她能克服公众的批评吗？


否定句:
He can't live down the scandal. = 他无法忘记这起丑闻。
She will never live down her association with him. = 她永远也忘不了与他之间的联系。


PHRASE->
live down a mistake = 忘记一个错误
live down a scandal = 忘记一个丑闻
live down an embarrassment = 忘记一件尴尬的事
live down a failure = 忘记一次失败
live down a reputation = 忘记一个名声
