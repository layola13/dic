reassurance:/ˌriːəˈʃʊərəns/ | n. | 安心；保证；慰藉 | The act of reassuring; the state of being reassured.  It involves restoring confidence or reducing anxiety. |例句：Her words offered me some reassurance.  她的言语给了我一些安慰。

例句：The doctor's reassurance calmed her fears. 医生的保证平息了她的恐惧。


近义词：comfort, consolation, confidence, encouragement | 反义词：alarm, anxiety, discouragement, fear


USAGE->
一般现在时:
His reassurance calms me. = 他的保证让我平静。
This information provides reassurance to investors. = 这些信息让投资者安心。

一般过去时:
Her kind words gave me great reassurance. = 她友好的话语给了我极大的安慰。
The police officer's reassurance eased the victim's worry. = 警官的保证减轻了受害者的担忧。

现在完成时:
His actions have given me much reassurance. = 他的行为给了我很多安慰。
The successful test has provided reassurance to the engineers. = 成功的测试给了工程师们信心。

过去完成时:
I had already received reassurance from my family before the surgery. = 我在手术前已经得到了家人的安慰。
The company had already given reassurance to its shareholders before the announcement. = 公司在宣布之前已经向股东们保证。

将来时:

虚拟语气:
