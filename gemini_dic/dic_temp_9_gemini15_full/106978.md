lacklustre: /ˈlæklʌstə(r)/ | adj. | 缺乏光泽的；无光彩的；无生气的；枯燥乏味的 | Lacking in vitality, force, or conviction; uninspired or uninspiring.  | The band's lacklustre performance disappointed the audience. 乐队的毫无生气的表演让观众失望了。 | 近义词：dull, lifeless, vapid, uninspired, uninteresting | 反义词：brilliant, vibrant, exciting, inspiring, lively

词源:
The word "lacklustre" comes from the obsolete word "luster," meaning shine or brightness, and "lack," meaning to be without. Therefore, "lacklustre" literally means "lacking luster."  The word "luster" itself has roots in Old French and ultimately in Latin.
