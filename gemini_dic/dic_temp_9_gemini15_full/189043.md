sabers:/ˈseɪbərz/| n.|军刀；指挥刀|A saber is a cavalry sword with a curved blade.  It is typically used by cavalry soldiers or officers.|例句：The cavalry officer drew his saber and charged into battle.  骑兵军官拔出军刀冲入战斗。

The ancient Roman legionaries used sabers in their battles. 古罗马军团士兵在战斗中使用军刀。|近义词：sword, cutlass|反义词：None

词源:
The word "saber" comes from the French word "sabre," which itself has origins in the Turkish word "sabra," meaning "sword."  The Turkish word ultimately derives from the Persian word "šamšīr," also meaning "sword."  The word's evolution reflects the historical spread of the weapon itself across various cultures and empires.
