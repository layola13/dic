proposing:/prəˈpoʊzɪŋ/| v. |提议；建议；求婚；提出；建议(某事)|  The act of suggesting or offering a plan or idea for consideration.  It can also refer to making a formal offer of marriage. |例句：He is proposing a new marketing strategy. 他正在提议一项新的营销策略。|例句：He got down on one knee and proposed to her. 他单膝跪地向她求婚。|近义词：suggesting, recommending, offering, putting forward|反义词：opposing, rejecting, refusing


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He proposes a new solution every week. = 他每周都提出一个新的解决方案。
She proposes a toast to the happy couple. = 她提议为这对幸福的夫妇敬酒。

一般过去时:
They proposed a compromise. = 他们提议了一个妥协方案。
He proposed marriage to her last night. = 他昨晚向她求婚。

现在完成时:
She has proposed several amendments to the bill. = 她已经对该法案提出了几项修正案。
They have proposed a joint venture. = 他们已经提议了一项合资企业。

过去完成时:
He had proposed a plan before the meeting. = 他在会议之前已经提议了一个计划。
She had proposed several times before he finally accepted. = 在他最终接受之前，她已经提议过几次了。

将来时:
They will propose a new budget next month. = 他们下个月将提出新的预算。
She will propose a change to the schedule. = 她将提议更改日程安排。

虚拟语气:
If he proposed, she would accept. = 如果他求婚，她会接受。
I wish he had proposed sooner. = 我希望他早点求婚。

被动语态:
A new law was proposed by the senator. = 一项新法律由参议员提出。
Many solutions have been proposed to address the problem. = 已经提出了许多解决方案来解决这个问题。

疑问句:
What are you proposing? = 你在提议什么？
What did they propose to do? = 他们提议做什么？

否定句:
He did not propose marriage. = 他没有求婚。
They did not propose any changes to the plan. = 他们没有对计划提出任何更改。
