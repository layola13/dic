pseudocollegiate: /ˌsuːdoʊkəˈliːdʒɪeɪt/ | adj. | 假大学生的；装腔作势的；伪学者气的 |  Affecting or suggesting a college student, often in a pretentious or superficial way.  It implies a display of learning or sophistication that is not genuine. |


例句：His pseudocollegiate vocabulary was filled with unnecessarily complex words that failed to impress anyone.  = 他那装腔作势的大学生的词汇充满了不必要的复杂词语，并没有给任何人留下深刻印象。


His pseudocollegiate attempts at philosophical discussion were easily seen through. = 他装腔作势的哲学讨论很容易被识破。


近义词：pretentious, affected, pedantic, bookish, highfalutin


反义词：genuine, authentic, sincere, unpretentious, simple


词性：形容词


词源：The word is derived from "pseudo" (meaning false or pretended) + "collegiate" (relating to a college or university).  It's a relatively modern coinage, reflecting a growing awareness of affected displays of intellectualism.
