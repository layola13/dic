cedillas:/səˈdɪljəz/| n.|塞迪亚斯（在某些字母下加的撇号）|A cedilla is a diacritical mark used in several languages, most notably in French, Portuguese, and Turkish. It is a small comma-like mark placed under a letter, most commonly the letter "c," to indicate a different pronunciation. For instance, in French, the cedilla under the "c" in "façade" changes the pronunciation from a hard "k" sound to a soft "s" sound.  | The word "garçon" (boy) in French uses a cedilla under the "c" to represent the /s/ sound.|近义词：diacritic, comma (in visual appearance)|反义词：无
