discriminated:/dɪˈskrɪmɪneɪtɪd/| v. |歧视，区别对待 | To treat a person or particular group of people differently, especially in a worse way, because of their race, religion, sex, etc. |例句：He felt he had been discriminated against because of his age. 他觉得因为他年龄的原因受到了歧视。|近义词：differentiate, prejudice, prejudge |反义词：include, tolerate, accept


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They discriminate against people based on their ethnicity. = 他们根据种族歧视人们。
The company discriminates in its hiring practices. = 这家公司在招聘方面存在歧视。

一般过去时:
The court ruled that the company discriminated against female applicants. = 法院裁定该公司歧视女性申请人。
He discriminated against his neighbors because of their religion. = 他因为邻居的宗教信仰而歧视他们。


现在完成时:
They have discriminated against minorities for years. = 他们多年来一直歧视少数民族。
The government has repeatedly discriminated against certain groups. = 政府一再歧视某些群体。


过去完成时:
She realized that she had been discriminated against her whole life. = 她意识到她一生都被歧视了。
The evidence showed that the company had discriminated against its employees. = 证据表明该公司歧视其员工。


将来时:
They will continue to discriminate unless laws change. = 除非法律改变，否则他们将继续歧视。
The new policy will likely prevent future discrimination. = 新政策可能会防止未来的歧视。


虚拟语气:
If the company had not discriminated, he would have gotten the job. = 如果公司没有歧视，他就会得到这份工作。
I wish they had not discriminated against me. = 我希望他们没有歧视我。


被动语态:
Many people have been discriminated against because of their skin color. = 许多人因为肤色而受到歧视。
The policy was criticized for discriminating against low-income families. = 该政策因歧视低收入家庭而受到批评。


疑问句:
Was he discriminated against because of his race? = 他是否因为种族而受到歧视？
Do they discriminate against applicants based on their gender? = 他们是否根据性别歧视申请人？


否定句:
The company does not discriminate on the basis of religion. = 公司不基于宗教信仰进行歧视。
They did not discriminate against anyone in the hiring process. = 他们在招聘过程中没有歧视任何人。


PHRASE->
racial discrimination 种族歧视
gender discrimination 性别歧视
religious discrimination 宗教歧视
age discrimination 年龄歧视
discrimination against women 对女性的歧视

词源:
discriminate 源自于拉丁语 *discernere，意思是“to separate, distinguish, discern”。  前缀 dis-  表示“分开”，词根 criminare 表示“判断，指控”。因此，其本义是“区分，辨别”，后来引申出“歧视”的含义，因为歧视行为往往基于对某些群体的负面判断和区别对待。
