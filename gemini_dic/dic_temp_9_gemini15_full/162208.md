pre-positioned:/ˌpriːpəˈzɪʃənd/| adj.|预先放置的；预先安排的 | Already placed or arranged in advance.  The term describes something that has been put into place before a particular event or action. This often implies preparation or planning. |例句：The pre-positioned troops were ready to respond immediately. 预先部署的军队随时准备立即反应。

The emergency supplies were pre-positioned in strategic locations. 紧急物资已预先放置在战略位置。|近义词：prearranged, prepared, beforehand, pre-placed |反义词：improvised, impromptu, unplanned
