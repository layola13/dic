decemvir: /dɛˈsɛmvər/| n. |十人执政官；十人委员会|A decemvir was a member of a board of ten magistrates in ancient Rome.  The term often refers to the Decemviri Legibus Scribundis, the ten men appointed in 451 BC to codify Roman law.  They are infamous for their later abuses of power.|Example: The decemvirs ruled Rome with an iron fist, eventually leading to their downfall.|近义词:decurion (in some contexts)|反义词:None specifically, but words implying single rule or less restrictive governance could be considered antonyms in a relevant context.
