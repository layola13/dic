phenom:/ˈfɛnɑːm/| n.|现象，奇才，非凡的人|a person or thing that is unusually successful or impressive|He's a phenom on the tennis court. 他是网球场上的奇才。|近义词：prodigy, wonder, sensation|反义词：nobody, nobody, commonplace


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The young singer is a phenom. = 这位年轻歌手是一位奇才。
This new technology is a phenom in the market. = 这项新技术是市场上的一个现象。


一般过去时:
He was a phenom in his college years. = 他在大学时代就是一个奇才。
The film was a cultural phenom. = 这部电影是一种文化现象。


现在完成时:
She has become a phenom in the music industry. = 她已成为乐坛的奇才。
This social media platform has become a global phenom. = 这个社交媒体平台已成为一种全球现象。


过去完成时:
Before the tournament, he had already been recognized as a phenom. = 比赛前，他已经被人认作是奇才了。
By the end of the year, the book had become a literary phenom. = 到年底，这本书已成为一种文学现象。


将来时:
He will be a phenom in the future. = 他将来会成为一个奇才。
This trend will likely become a phenom in the coming years. = 这种趋势可能会在未来几年成为一种现象。


虚拟语气:
If he continued to train hard, he would become a phenom. = 如果他继续努力训练，他就会成为一个奇才。
If this product were a phenom, we would see more sales. = 如果这个产品是一个现象级产品，我们会看到更多销售额。


被动语态:
He was considered a phenom by many critics. = 他被许多评论家认为是一个奇才。
The event was hailed as a phenom by the media. = 媒体将这一事件誉为一个现象。


疑问句:
Is he really a phenom? = 他真的是一个奇才吗？
Is this a new social phenom? = 这是一个新的社会现象吗？


否定句:
He is not just a phenom; he is a legend. = 他不仅仅是一个奇才；他是一个传奇。
This product failed to become a market phenom. = 这个产品未能成为市场现象。
