debated:/dɪˈbeɪtɪd/ | v. | 争论；辩论；讨论 | To discuss a topic or issue, often formally, in order to reach a conclusion or decision.  |例句：The senators debated the new bill for hours. 参议员们就新法案辩论了几个小时。

例句：The students debated the merits of different learning styles. 学生们就不同学习方法的优缺点进行了辩论。


近义词：discussed, argued, disputed, contested | 反义词：agreed upon, settled, resolved | 词性：动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They debate the issue every week. = 他们每周都讨论这个问题。
We debate the pros and cons of each option. = 我们讨论每个选项的利弊。


一般过去时:
They debated the proposal last night. = 他们昨晚讨论了这个提案。
The committee debated the matter thoroughly. = 委员会彻底讨论了此事。


现在完成时:
We have debated this topic many times. = 我们已经多次讨论过这个话题。
They have debated the implications of the new policy. = 他们已经讨论了这项新政策的影响。


过去完成时:
They had debated the issue before the vote. = 他们投票前已经讨论过这个问题了。
We had debated the options for hours before reaching a decision. = 我们在做出决定之前已经讨论了几个小时的方案。


将来时:
We will debate the merits of the plan tomorrow. = 我们明天将讨论该计划的优点。
They will debate the topic at the next meeting. = 他们将在下次会议上讨论这个话题。


虚拟语气:
If we debated the problem openly, we might find a solution. = 如果我们公开讨论这个问题，我们也许能找到解决办法。
I wish we had debated the consequences more thoroughly. = 我希望我们当时更彻底地讨论过后果。


被动语态:
The issue was debated extensively. = 这个问题被广泛讨论。
The proposal was debated by the council members. = 该提案被理事会成员们讨论。


疑问句:
Was the motion debated before the vote? = 在投票前是否对动议进行了讨论？
Will the topic be debated at the conference? = 会议上会讨论这个话题吗？


否定句:
They did not debate the importance of the deadline. = 他们没有讨论截止日期的重要性。
We did not debate the finer points of the agreement. = 我们没有讨论协议的细节。


PHRASE->
to debate a motion = 辩论一项动议
to debate the merits of something = 辩论某事物的优点
to debate fiercely = 激烈辩论
to debate with someone = 与某人辩论
to debate a point = 辩论一个论点

词源:
The word "debated" comes from the Old French word "debatre," meaning "to fight," "to struggle," or "to contend."  This ultimately derives from the Latin word "battuere," meaning "to beat" or "to strike."  The sense shifted over time from physical conflict to intellectual or verbal struggle.
