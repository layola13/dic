katakana:/ˈkɑːtəˌkɑːnə/| n. |片假名|Katakana is a Japanese syllabary, one of the two main scripts used to write Japanese, alongside hiragana.  It is primarily used to write foreign loanwords, onomatopoeia, and sometimes for emphasis. Each character represents a syllable. |Example: The word "computer" is written in katakana as コンピューター (konpyūtā).|近义词：hiragana (but with different usage)|反义词：N/A
