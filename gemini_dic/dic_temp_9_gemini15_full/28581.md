careerist: /kəˈrɪərɪst/ | n. | 事业主义者；热衷于事业的人 | A person who is ambitious and primarily focused on advancing their career, often at the expense of other aspects of life.  | He was a ruthless careerist, willing to step on anyone to get ahead. 他是一个冷酷无情的职业主义者，为了进步愿意踩着任何人往上爬。 | ambitious person, go-getter | altruist, humanitarian |


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He is a dedicated careerist. = 他是一位敬业的事业主义者。
She prioritizes her career above all else. = 她把事业置于一切之上。


一般过去时:
He was a careerist in his younger years. = 他年轻时是一个事业主义者。
She always strived to be a successful careerist. = 她总是努力成为一个成功的事业主义者。


现在完成时:
He has become a highly successful careerist. = 他已经成为一个非常成功的事业主义者。
She has always been a careerist, focused on climbing the corporate ladder. = 她一直都是一个事业主义者，专注于攀登公司阶梯。


过去完成时:
He had already established himself as a careerist before he turned 30. = 他在30岁之前就已经确立了自己作为事业主义者的地位。
She had been a careerist for many years before she decided to have a family. = 在她决定组建家庭之前，她已经是一个事业主义者很多年了。


将来时:
He will continue to be a careerist for the foreseeable future. = 在可预见的未来，他将继续成为一个事业主义者。
She will likely become a very influential careerist in her field. = 她很可能会成为她所在领域一个非常有影响力的事业主义者。


虚拟语气:
If he were not a careerist, he would have more time for his family. = 如果他不是一个事业主义者，他就会有更多的时间陪伴家人。
I wish she weren't such a driven careerist; she needs to relax more. = 我希望她不要那么拼命地追求事业；她需要多放松一下。


被动语态:
He was viewed as a ruthless careerist by his colleagues. = 他被同事们看作是一个冷酷无情的职业主义者。
She was often criticized for being too much of a careerist. = 她经常因为过于追求事业而受到批评。


疑问句:
Is he really a careerist, or is he just ambitious? = 他真的是一个事业主义者吗，还是仅仅是雄心勃勃？
Are all successful people necessarily careerists? = 所有成功人士一定都是事业主义者吗？


否定句:
He is not a careerist; he values his family above all else. = 他不是一个事业主义者；他最重视家庭。
She is not solely a careerist; she also enjoys volunteer work. = 她不仅仅是一个事业主义者；她也喜欢志愿者工作。
