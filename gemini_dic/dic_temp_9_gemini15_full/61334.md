embattlement:/ɪmˈbæt(ə)lmənt/ | n. | 雉堞；城垛；防御工事 | Fortifications, typically a crenelated parapet on a wall or tower, providing defensive positions for archers or other defenders.  Embattlements are a key feature of medieval castles and fortifications. | The castle's embattlements offered excellent protection against attackers.  城堡的城垛提供了极好的防御。| fortifications, ramparts, defenses | open, undefended | 名词

词源:

From Old French *embailler, from *bailler "to gape, open wide," referring to the open spaces between the merlons (the solid parts of the embattlement).  The prefix "em-" indicates intensification or completion.  The modern spelling evolved through various intermediate forms.
