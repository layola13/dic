clamours:/ˈklæmərz/| v.|喧嚣，大声抱怨，吵闹 | To make a loud and confused noise, especially of many people shouting.  |例句：The crowd clamoured for his resignation.  人群大声要求他辞职。

例句：The protesters clamoured outside the government building. 抗议者在政府大楼外大声喧哗。


近义词：shout, yell, roar, protest, complain

反义词：whisper, murmur, silence


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The crowd clamours for change. = 人群大声要求改变。
The children clamour for attention. = 孩子们吵着要人注意。

一般过去时:
The audience clamoured for an encore. = 观众大声要求再来一首。
The workers clamoured for better wages. = 工人们大声要求提高工资。

现在完成时:
The public has clamoured for justice. = 公众一直在大声呼吁正义。
The media has clamoured for answers. = 媒体一直在大声要求答案。

过去完成时:
They had clamoured for reform for years. = 他们多年来一直在大声呼吁改革。
The villagers had clamoured for a new school. = 村民们一直在大声呼吁建一所新学校。

将来时:
The people will clamour for accountability. = 人们将大声要求承担责任。
The students will clamour for better facilities. = 学生们将大声要求更好的设施。

虚拟语气:
If they clamoured louder, maybe they would be heard. = 如果他们喊得更大声，也许他们会被听到。
I wish the crowd hadn't clamoured so much. = 我希望人群不要吵闹得这么厉害。

被动语态:
His resignation was clamoured for by many. = 许多人大声要求他辞职。
Better working conditions were clamoured for by the employees. = 员工们大声要求改善工作条件。

疑问句:
Why are they clamouring? = 他们为什么在喧嚣？
What are the people clamouring about? = 人们在吵闹些什么？

否定句:
The protesters did not clamour for violence. = 抗议者没有大声呼吁暴力。
The students did not clamour against the new rules. = 学生们没有大声反对新规定。


PHRASE->
clamour for sth:  大声要求某事物
clamour against sth: 大声反对某事物
clamour to do sth: 吵着要做某事
a clamour of voices: 一片喧闹声
public clamour: 公众的喧嚣


词源:
源自古法语 *clamer，意思是“to shout”，最终源自拉丁语 *clamare，意思是“to cry out”。
