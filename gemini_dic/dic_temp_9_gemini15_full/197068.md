shamblers:/ˈʃæmblərz/| n. |蹒跚而行的人，步履蹒跚的人，摇摇晃晃的人 |A shambler is someone who walks in a clumsy or unsteady way.  They might be old, injured, or simply moving without grace.  The word evokes a sense of awkwardness and slowness.|例句：The old man, a shambler in his worn leather boots, slowly made his way down the lane.  这位老人穿着磨损的皮靴，步履蹒跚地沿着小路走着。

The zombies were shamblers, their limbs stiff and movements jerky. 僵尸们步履蹒跚，四肢僵硬，动作迟缓。|近义词：stumbler, shuffler, waddler |反义词：runner, sprinter, dancer
