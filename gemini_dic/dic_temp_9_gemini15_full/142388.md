orthotectonic:/ˌɔːrθoʊtɛkˈtɑːnɪk/| adj.| 正向构造的；直向构造的 | Relating to or denoting tectonic processes that cause relatively vertical movements of the Earth's crust.  Orthotectonic structures are formed by vertical movements, such as uplift and subsidence, rather than lateral movements.  | Example: The region displays predominantly orthotectonic structures, indicating a history of significant vertical crustal movements.  这个地区主要显示正向构造结构，表明其具有显著的垂直地壳运动历史。|近义词：epeirogenic (referring to broad, vertical movements)|反义词：paratectonic (referring to lateral movements)|形容词
