pointillists:/ˈpɔɪntɪlɪsts/ | n. | 点彩画家；点彩派画家 |  Painters who use the pointillist technique, characterized by the application of small, distinct dots of color to form an image.  The overall effect is created by the viewer's eye blending the dots from a distance. | The Impressionist painters, including the pointillists, revolutionized art. 点彩派画家，包括印象派画家，彻底改变了艺术。|近义词：divisionists, neo-impressionists | 反义词：无
