immortalizers: /ɪˈmɔːrtəlaɪzərz/ | n. | 使永生者；使不朽者 |  Things or people that make something or someone immortal or famous.  It often refers to specific cells or genes that allow cells to continue dividing indefinitely, preventing cellular senescence. |  Example:  The ancient stories and legends are immortalizers of the heroes' deeds.  古代的故事和传说使英雄的事迹永垂不朽。


The discovery of telomerase, an enzyme that maintains telomeres, has led to research into potential immortalizers for human cells. 端粒酶（一种维持端粒的酶）的发现，导致了对人类细胞潜在永生因子的研究。|近义词：preservers, perpetuators |反义词：destroyers, annihilators
