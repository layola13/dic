catching: /ˈkætʃɪŋ/ | adj. | 吸引人的；容易传染的；引人注目的 | Appealing, interesting, or attractive; easily spread from one person to another; striking or noticeable. |


例句：
The book is so catching that I couldn't put it down.  = 这本书太吸引人了，我爱不释手。

His cold is very catching, so try to avoid contact with him. = 他的感冒很传染，所以尽量避免和他接触。


近义词：attractive, captivating, infectious, contagious, compelling, engaging

反义词：uninteresting, repulsive, repellent, boring, dull


词性：形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The plot is catching the reader's attention. = 情节正吸引着读者的注意。
This song is catching on quickly. = 这首歌迅速流行起来。


一般过去时:
The movie was catching the audience's emotions. = 这部电影抓住了观众的情绪。
Her enthusiasm was catching. = 她的热情具有感染力。


现在完成时:
The flu has been catching this season. = 这个季节流感一直很流行。
The new trend has caught on in many countries. = 新潮流在许多国家流行起来。


过去完成时:
The news had been catching fire before the official announcement. = 在官方宣布之前，这个消息就已经传开了。
The rumor had caught on before we could stop it. = 我们还没来得及阻止，谣言就已经传开了。


将来时:
The new campaign will be catching the public's eye. = 新的宣传活动将吸引公众的眼球。
This idea will catch on eventually. = 这个主意最终会流行起来的。


虚拟语气:
If the idea were more catching, it would be more successful. = 如果这个主意更吸引人，它就会更成功。
If the disease were less catching, fewer people would be infected. = 如果这种疾病不易传染，感染的人就会少一些。


被动语态:
The ball was caught by the outfielder. = 球被外野手接住了。(注意此处的catching是动词catch的现在分词作形容词)
The rumour was caught on quickly by the media. = 这个谣言很快就被媒体报道了。(注意此处的caught on是习语，意为“流行起来”)


疑问句:
Is the song catching on? = 这首歌流行了吗？
Is this disease catching? = 这种疾病会传染吗？


否定句:
The presentation wasn't catching the audience's interest. = 演示并没有引起观众的兴趣。
The joke wasn't catching at all. =  笑话一点也不好笑。


PHRASE->
catching phrase:  流行语
catching sight of: 瞥见
catching up: 赶上
catching fire:  迅速流行
catching cold: 感冒


词源:
源自动词catch，其含义包含“抓住”、“捕捉”、“感染”等。作为形容词，catching继承了这些含义，引申出“吸引人的”、“容易传染的”等意思。
