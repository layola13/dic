intrusionist: /ɪnˈtruʒənɪst/ | n. |干涉主义者；入侵者 | A person who advocates or engages in intrusion, especially in the affairs of others.  Someone who interferes in the affairs of others. |例句：The government's intrusionist policies were widely criticized.  政府的干涉主义政策受到了广泛批评。

例句：He was accused of being an intrusionist in the company's internal affairs. 他被指责干涉公司内务。


近义词：interferer, meddler, busybody, interventionist | 反义词：non-interventionist, laissez-faire


词源:  From intrusion + -ist.  "Intrusion" comes from the Latin *intrudere, meaning "to thrust in." The suffix "-ist" indicates a person who performs or believes in a particular action or idea.
