outlaw:/ˈaʊtˌlɔː/|/ˈaʊtˌlɔː/| n. vt. adj.|歹徒；逃犯；被禁止的事物；宣布…为非法；使…成为非法；不法之徒；|英文解析：A person who has committed a serious crime and is hiding from the police; something that is illegal or forbidden.  To make something illegal; to declare something illegal.|例句：The government outlawed the sale of ivory. 政府禁止象牙买卖。

The gang leader was an outlaw wanted by the police.  该帮派头目是警方通缉的逃犯。


近义词：bandit, criminal, fugitive, illegal, prohibited|反义词：lawful, legal, permitted


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They outlaw the sale of drugs. = 他们禁止贩卖毒品。
The government outlaws certain weapons. = 政府禁止某些武器。


一般过去时:
They outlawed the practice of slavery. = 他们废除了奴隶制度。
The king outlawed dueling. = 国王禁止决斗。


现在完成时:
Many countries have outlawed the use of landmines. = 许多国家已经禁止使用地雷。
The city has outlawed smoking in public places. = 该城市已经禁止在公共场所吸烟。


过去完成时:
The tribe had outlawed cannibalism for generations. = 该部落几代人都禁止食人。
They had outlawed alcohol before the war. = 战争前他们已经禁酒了。


将来时:
They will outlaw the use of plastic bags. = 他们将禁止使用塑料袋。
The council will outlaw the construction of high-rise buildings. = 理事会将禁止建造高层建筑。


虚拟语气:
If they outlawed gambling, crime rates might decrease. = 如果他们禁止赌博，犯罪率可能会下降。
I wish they outlawed texting while driving. = 我希望他们禁止开车时发短信。


被动语态:
The sale of that drug was outlawed last year. = 这种药物的销售去年被禁止了。
Smoking was outlawed in this building. = 这栋楼里禁止吸烟。


疑问句:
Will they outlaw the use of artificial sweeteners? = 他们会禁止使用人工甜味剂吗？
Has the import of this product been outlawed? = 这种产品的进口已被禁止了吗？


否定句:
They did not outlaw the use of pesticides. = 他们没有禁止使用杀虫剂。
The state has not yet outlawed fracking. = 该州尚未禁止水力压裂。
