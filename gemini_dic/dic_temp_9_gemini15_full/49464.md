deluders:/dɪˈluːdərz/| n.| 欺骗者；妄想者 | Those who deceive or mislead others; people with delusions. |例句：The con men were skilled deluders, preying on the elderly's trust. 那些骗子是熟练的欺骗者，他们利用老年人的信任。

例句：He suffered from delusions of grandeur, a common trait among some deluders. 他患有夸大妄想症，这是某些妄想者的一种常见特征。


近义词：deceivers, impostors, charlatans, fantasists

反义词：truth-tellers, honest people, realists


词源：源自动词delude (欺骗，迷惑)，后缀-er 表示人。Delude itself comes from the Old French word "deluder," meaning "to lead astray" or "to deceive."  This ultimately traces back to the Latin word "ludere," meaning "to play." The prefix "de-" adds a sense of "away from" or "undoing."
