censoring: /ˈsɛnsərɪŋ/ | v. |审查；删改；压制 |The act of removing or suppressing parts of a book, film, etc., that are considered objectionable.  It involves the examination and alteration or deletion of material deemed inappropriate or harmful.  |例句：The government is censoring news reports about the protests. 政府正在审查关于抗议的新闻报道。

The movie was heavily censored before its release. 这部电影在上映前经过了大量的删改。|近义词：editing, expurgating, suppressing |反义词：allowing, permitting, publishing

They censor the news. = 他们审查新闻。
The government censors political speech. = 政府审查政治言论。

一般过去时:
They censored the book. = 他们审查了这本书。
The authorities censored the article. = 当局审查了这篇文章。

现在完成时:

过去完成时:

The editor had censored the manuscript before publication. = 编辑在出版前审查了手稿。

将来时:

被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:

词源:
From Latin *censor, from censere "to assess, judge, tax".  The original meaning related to the Roman officials (censors) who conducted censuses and assessed citizens' worth.  The modern meaning evolved from the censor's power to suppress objectionable material.
