reinstated:/ˌriːɪnˈsteɪtɪd/| v.|恢复职位；恢复；使复职|To restore to a previous position or state; to reinstate someone is to give them back their job, position, or rights after they have been taken away.|例句：He was reinstated to his former position after the investigation cleared him.  调查结果证明他是清白的，之后他被恢复了原来的职位。
例句：The committee decided to reinstate the rule that had been temporarily suspended. 委员会决定恢复暂时被暂停的规章。|近义词：restore, reinstate, reestablish|反义词：remove, dismiss, abolish|词性:动词

USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company reinstates employees after they complete their parental leave. = 公司在员工完成产假后恢复其职位。
They reinstate the old system every year. = 他们每年都恢复旧系统。

一般过去时:
The judge reinstated the defendant's driving license. = 法官恢复了被告的驾驶执照。
The university reinstated the professor after a lengthy appeal process. = 大学经过漫长的申诉程序后恢复了教授的职位。

现在完成时:
The government has reinstated the tax on luxury goods. = 政府已经恢复了对奢侈品的税收。
They have reinstated the old rules and regulations. = 他们已经恢复了旧的规章制度。

过去完成时:
They had reinstated the old system before the new one was implemented. = 新系统实施之前，他们已经恢复了旧系统。
The company had reinstated her position before the scandal broke. = 在丑闻爆发之前，公司已经恢复了她的职位。

将来时:
The court will reinstate his citizenship once the appeal is successful. = 一旦上诉成功，法院将恢复他的公民身份。
The school will reinstate the extracurricular activity next year. = 学校明年将恢复课外活动。

虚拟语气:
If the evidence were stronger, the court would reinstate the charges. = 如果证据更充分，法院就会恢复指控。
I wish they would reinstate the old tradition. = 我希望他们能恢复旧传统。

被动语态:
His driving license was reinstated after he completed the required courses. = 他完成规定的课程后，驾驶执照被恢复了。
The rule was reinstated by the board of directors. = 该规则被董事会恢复了。

疑问句:
Will the company reinstate him after his suspension? = 公司会在停职后恢复他的职位吗？
Has the government reinstated the travel ban? = 政府已经恢复旅行禁令了吗？

否定句:
The committee did not reinstate the controversial policy. = 委员会没有恢复有争议的政策。
The school did not reinstate him after he failed to meet the requirements. = 他没有达到要求，学校没有恢复他的职位。
