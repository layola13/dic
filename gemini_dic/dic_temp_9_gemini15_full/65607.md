evanescent: /ˌɛvəˈnɛsnt/ | adj. | 短暂的；转瞬即逝的；消失的 | fleeting, vanishing, ephemeral; tending to vanish like vapor |  The evanescent beauty of a sunset is often breathtaking. 日落转瞬即逝的美景常常令人叹为观止。The evanescent nature of childhood memories makes them all the more precious. 童年记忆转瞬即逝的特性使它们更加珍贵。 |  lasting, permanent, enduring | 形容词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The beauty of the sunset is evanescent. = 日落的美丽是短暂的。
His fame is evanescent. = 他的名声是短暂的。

一般过去时:
The feeling of happiness was evanescent. = 幸福的感觉是短暂的。
The opportunity was evanescent. = 机会转瞬即逝。

现在完成时:
The smell has been evanescent since the rain stopped. = 雨停后，气味已经消散了。
The evidence has proven evanescent. = 证据已经消失了。

过去完成时:
The vision had been evanescent before he could describe it. = 他还没来得及描述，景象就已经消失了。
The hope had been evanescent after the news. = 听到消息后，希望已经破灭了。

虚拟语气:

否定句:

词源:
From Late Latin *evanescens, present participle of evanescere "to vanish, disappear," from Latin e- "out, from" + vanescere "to vanish," from vanus "empty, void."  最终源于拉丁语。
