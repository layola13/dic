infraposition:/ˌɪnfrəpəˈzɪʃən/| n. |后置，后置法|The placement of a word or phrase after the element it modifies, typically an adverbial modifier placed after the verb.  This is in contrast to the more common preposed placement.  Infraposition is often used for stylistic effect or to avoid awkward sentence structures.|Example: He walked quickly down the street. (preposed adverb)  He walked down the street quickly. (infraposed adverb)|近义词：postposition (in some contexts)|反义词：preposition (in some contexts)
