demobilize:/ˌdiːˈməʊbɪlaɪz/| vt.|遣散（军队）；使复员；使停止运转|To disband or discharge (soldiers, especially from an army). To stop the operation or activity of (something).|例句：The army was demobilized after the war.  战争结束后，军队被遣散了。

例句：The company demobilized its production line after the economic downturn. 经济衰退后，公司停止了生产线的运转。

近义词：discharge, disband, decommission

反义词：mobilize

词性：动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government demobilizes the army after the conflict. = 冲突结束后，政府遣散军队。
The factory demobilizes its workers during the off-season. = 淡季时，工厂遣散工人。

一般过去时:
They demobilized the troops after the victory. = 他们胜利后遣散了军队。
The company demobilized its marketing campaign after poor sales. = 销售不佳后，公司停止了营销活动。

现在完成时:
The army has been demobilized since the peace treaty was signed. = 自和平条约签署以来，军队已被遣散。
The project has been demobilized due to lack of funding. = 由于缺乏资金，项目已被停止。


过去完成时:

将来时:
They will demobilize the fleet next month. = 他们下个月将遣散舰队。

虚拟语气:
If they demobilized the army, the country would save a lot of money. = 如果他们遣散军队，国家将节省大量资金。
If the project had been demobilized earlier, we would have avoided further losses. = 如果项目早些停止，我们将避免进一步的损失。

被动语态:
The soldiers were demobilized after many years of service. = 服役多年后，士兵们被遣散。

疑问句:

否定句:
They did not demobilize the troops despite the peace talks. = 尽管进行了和平谈判，他们也没有遣散军队。
