preobject:/ˌpriːˈɒbdʒekt/| n. |预设对象，预设目标| A preobject is a concept in object-oriented programming that refers to an object that is created before its actual use. It is often used in situations where an object needs to be initialized or configured before it can be used in a specific context.  This allows for better management of resources and can improve the efficiency of the program.|Example: In a factory pattern, a preobject might be a partially initialized product before final assembly. |近义词:prototype, antecedent|反义词:postobject
