quasi-innocently: /ˈkwɑːzaɪ ˈɪnəsəntli/ | adv. | 近乎天真地，看似无辜地 |  The adverb "quasi-innocently" describes an action or behavior that appears innocent but may have a hidden or ulterior motive. It suggests a pretense of innocence.  | 例句：He looked quasi-innocently at the broken vase, hoping no one would suspect him. 他看似无辜地看着破碎的花瓶，希望没有人怀疑他。


She smiled quasi-innocently, concealing her true intentions. 她若无其事地笑了笑，掩盖了她的真实意图。| 近义词：seemingly innocently, apparently innocently, innocently (but with a hidden motive) | 反义词：guiltily, culpably, openly
