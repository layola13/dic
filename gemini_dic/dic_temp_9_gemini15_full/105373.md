kickstarted: /ˈkɪkˌstɑːrtɪd/ | v. |  启动，发起，开始 | To give something a sudden boost or start, often referring to a project or business.  It implies a quick and effective injection of energy or resources to overcome initial inertia. |

例句：

The government's investment kickstarted the development of the new technology. = 政府的投资启动了这项新技术的开发。

The successful crowdfunding campaign kickstarted the company's growth. =  成功的众筹活动启动了公司的发展。


近义词：launched, initiated, began, jump-started

反义词：halted, stopped, ended


词源:

The word is a compound of "kick" and "start."  "Kick" in this context implies a forceful and sudden action, while "start" refers to the beginning of something. The "-ed" ending indicates the past tense.
