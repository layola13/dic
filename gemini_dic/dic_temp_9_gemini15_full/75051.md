frighten:/ˈfraɪtn/| vt.|使害怕；惊吓|To cause (someone) to feel sudden fear.|例句：The loud noise frightened the children.  巨大的噪音吓坏了孩子们。
例句：The storm frightened the animals.  暴风雨吓坏了动物们。|近义词：scare, terrify, alarm|反义词：calm, reassure, soothe|动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The loud music frightens me. = 吵闹的音乐让我害怕。
The dog frightens the cat. = 狗吓唬猫。

一般过去时:
The movie frightened me. = 那部电影吓到我了。
The sudden noise frightened the birds. = 突然的噪音吓跑了鸟儿。

现在完成时:
Nothing has frightened me so much before. = 以前没有什么东西这么吓到过我。
That experience has frightened her for life. = 那次经历把她吓破了胆。

过去完成时:
I had never been frightened like that before. = 我以前从未如此害怕过。
She had been frightened by the dark many times. = 她曾经很多次被黑暗吓到。

将来时:
The thunder will frighten the baby. = 雷声会吓到婴儿。
I think the test will frighten him. = 我认为考试会吓到他。

虚拟语气:
If the dog barked, it would frighten the neighbors. = 如果狗叫，它会吓到邻居。
I wish the movie hadn't frightened me so much. = 我希望电影没有这么吓到我。

被动语态:
The children were frightened by the fireworks. = 孩子们被烟花吓到了。
She was frightened by the unexpected visitor. = 她被不速之客吓了一跳。

疑问句:
Does the dark frighten you? = 黑暗让你害怕吗？
What frightens you most? = 什么最让你害怕？

否定句:
The darkness didn't frighten me. = 黑暗并没有吓到我。
The story didn't frighten the children. = 这个故事并没有吓到孩子们。


PHRASE->
be frightened of = 害怕...
frighten away = 吓跑
frighten off = 吓退
frighten somebody to death = 把某人吓死
frighten somebody out of their wits = 把某人吓坏了

词源:
Old English *frēosian “to shiver, tremble,” from Proto-Germanic *friusōn (source also of Old High German vriusan, Old Norse frjōsa “to freeze”), from PIE *gʰrew- “to tremble.”  The modern sense developed from the association of trembling with fear.
