nontautological:/ˌnɒntɔːtəˈlɒdʒɪkəl/ | adj. | 非同义反复的；非同语反复的 | Not tautological; not involving or expressing a tautology.  It refers to statements or arguments that are not self-evident or trivially true.  The statement avoids redundancy and offers new information or insight. | Example:  The statement "All squares are rectangles, but not all rectangles are squares" is nontautological because it presents distinct but related concepts.  This statement provides useful information that isn't already implied within the definition of either term. | 近义词：meaningful, significant, informative, insightful | 反义词：tautological, redundant, self-evident, trivial
