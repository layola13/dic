chassidism:/ˈχæsɪdɪzəm/| n. |哈西德主义|A mystical Jewish movement that originated in Eastern Europe in the 18th century, emphasizing the importance of joy, love, and ecstatic devotion in religious practice. It is characterized by its unique forms of prayer, study, and community life, and often involves a strong emphasis on the emotional connection with God.|  The Hasidic movement spread rapidly throughout Eastern Europe, profoundly impacting Jewish life and culture. |近义词：Hasidism, Jewish mysticism|反义词：secularism
