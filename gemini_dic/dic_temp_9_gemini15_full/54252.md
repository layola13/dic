disgrace:/dɪsˈɡreɪs/| n. v.|耻辱；丢脸；使蒙羞|The act of losing respect or honor; shame or dishonor; to bring shame or dishonor upon. |例句：His behavior was a disgrace to his family. 他的行为给他的家庭带来了耻辱。

例句：The scandal brought disgrace on the whole organization.  这起丑闻使整个组织蒙羞。


近义词：shame, dishonor, scandal, humiliation, opprobrium|反义词：honor, glory, pride


USAGE->
一般现在时:
His actions disgrace his family. = 他的行为使他的家庭蒙羞。
This behavior disgraces the company's reputation. = 这种行为败坏了公司的声誉。


一般过去时:
The incident disgraced the entire team. = 这起事件使整个团队蒙羞。
His words disgraced him in front of everyone. = 他的话让他在大家面前丢尽了脸。


现在完成时:
His repeated mistakes have disgraced his family. = 他反复犯错已经使他的家庭蒙羞。
The company's actions have disgraced the industry. = 该公司的行为已经使整个行业蒙羞。


过去完成时:
He had disgraced himself before the competition. = 他在比赛前就已经丢尽了脸。
The scandal had disgraced the politician before the election. = 丑闻在选举前就已经让这位政客名誉扫地。


将来时:
His actions will disgrace his family. = 他的行为将会使他的家庭蒙羞。
This decision will disgrace the company. = 这个决定将会使公司蒙羞。


虚拟语气:
If he had behaved better, he wouldn't have disgraced himself. = 如果他表现得更好，他就不会让自己丢脸了。
I wish he hadn't disgraced himself in public. = 我真希望他不要在公众面前丢脸。


被动语态:
He was disgraced by his actions. = 他因自己的行为而蒙羞。
The company was disgraced by the scandal. = 公司因丑闻而蒙羞。


疑问句:
Will his actions disgrace him? = 他的行为会让他丢脸吗？
Does this behavior disgrace the profession? = 这种行为会败坏这个行业的声誉吗？


否定句:
His actions did not disgrace him. = 他的行为并没有让他丢脸。
The company's response did not disgrace them. = 公司的回应并没有让他们蒙羞。



PHRASE->
fall from disgrace = 恢复名誉
bring into disgrace = 使丢脸
a disgrace to =  对...来说是耻辱
public disgrace = 公开羞辱
a source of disgrace = 丢脸的原因
