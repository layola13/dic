flatfishes:/ˈflætˌfɪʃɪz/| n. |比目鱼类|Flatfishes are a group of fish characterized by their flattened bodies and both eyes on one side of their head.  They are also known as Pleuronectiformes. This adaptation allows them to lie flat on the seafloor, camouflaged from predators and prey.  Many species are important food sources. | Example:  Many species of flatfishes, such as flounder and sole, are popular seafood choices.  许多比目鱼种类，例如比目鱼和鳎鱼，都是受欢迎的海鲜选择。

|Example: The camouflage of flatfishes allows them to effectively hide from predators on the seabed. 比目鱼的伪装使它们能够有效地躲避海底的捕食者。|近义词：pleuronectiforms|反义词：None specifically, but could be contrasted with fish that are not flattened.
