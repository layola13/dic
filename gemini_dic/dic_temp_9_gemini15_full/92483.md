hypothalamus:/ˌhaɪpəˈθæləməs/ | n. |下丘脑 |The hypothalamus is a small but crucial region of the brain located below the thalamus.  It plays a vital role in regulating many essential bodily functions.  It acts as a control center, linking the nervous system to the endocrine system via the pituitary gland.  Its functions include regulating body temperature, hunger, thirst, sleep-wake cycles, and emotional responses.  Damage to the hypothalamus can lead to various hormonal imbalances and disruptions of these vital functions. | Example: The hypothalamus regulates body temperature by triggering sweating or shivering.  下丘脑通过引发出汗或颤抖来调节体温。|近义词：None (unique function)|反义词：None (no direct antonym)|词性：名词
