electors:/ɪˈlɛktərz/| n.|选举人；投票人|People who have the right to vote in an election, especially in a presidential election.|The electors cast their votes for the president.|选民；投票者|voters, constituents|non-voters


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The electors cast their votes. = 选举人投票。
Many electors support the current president. = 许多选举人支持现任总统。

一般过去时:
The electors chose the candidate yesterday. = 选举人昨天选择了候选人。
The electors voted for the new governor last week. = 选举人上周投票选出了新州长。


现在完成时:
The electors have already cast their ballots. = 选举人已经投票了。
The electors have chosen the next president. = 选举人已经选出了下一任总统。


过去完成时:
The electors had already voted before the news broke. = 在新闻发布之前，选举人已经投票了。
The electors had cast their ballots by the time the polls closed. = 在投票站关闭时，选举人已经投票了。


将来时:
The electors will vote next month. = 选举人下个月将投票。
The electors will cast their ballots on election day. = 选举人将在选举日投票。


虚拟语气:
If the electors were to change their minds, the result would be different. = 如果选举人改变主意，结果就会不同。
I wish the electors had considered the other candidate. = 我希望选举人考虑过其他候选人。


被动语态:
The votes were counted by the electors. = 选票由选举人统计。
The president was elected by the electors. = 总统是由选举人选出的。


疑问句:
How many electors are there in this state? = 这个州有多少选举人？
Who are the electors voting for? = 选举人投票给谁？


否定句:
The electors did not vote for the unpopular candidate. = 选举人没有投票给那个不受欢迎的候选人。
Not all electors supported the winning candidate. = 并非所有选举人都支持获胜的候选人。
