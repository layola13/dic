infatuated: /ɪnˈfætʃueɪtɪd/ | adj. | 痴迷的；迷恋的 |  Being foolishly or blindly in love; excessively fond of.  Characterized by intense but often short-lived passion or admiration. |

例句：He was infatuated with the new girl in class. 他迷恋上了班上的新女孩。

例句：She became infatuated with the idea of traveling the world. 她对环游世界的想法着了迷。


近义词：enamored, smitten, captivated, obsessed, besotted

反义词：indifferent, apathetic, unconcerned


词源：From Middle English *infatuen, from Latin infatuāre "to infatuate," from in- "in" + fatuus "foolish."
