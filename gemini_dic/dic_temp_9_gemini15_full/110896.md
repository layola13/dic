lipaemia:/ˌlaɪpɪˈmiːə/| n. |脂血症|The presence of abnormally high concentrations of lipids in the blood.  This condition can be caused by various factors, including genetic predisposition, dietary habits, and underlying medical conditions.  It's often associated with hyperlipidemia and can increase the risk of cardiovascular diseases.|The doctor ordered blood tests to check for lipaemia after noticing the patient's elevated cholesterol levels.|hyperlipidemia, hypercholesterolemia|normolipemia
