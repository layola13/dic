enmeshed: /ɪnˈmɛʃt/ | adj. | 纠缠的；陷入困境的；复杂的 |

英文解析:  Caught or entangled in something.  Suggests a complex and difficult situation from which it is hard to escape.

例句:

The two families were enmeshed in a bitter feud.  两个家族陷入了激烈的世仇中。

He became enmeshed in a web of lies. 他陷入了一个谎言的泥潭。


近义词: entangled, involved, implicated, intertwined, caught up

反义词: free, disentangled, uninvolved, clear


词源:  From the verb "enmesh," which combines the prefix "en-" (meaning "to cause to be in") and "mesh," meaning "a network of threads."  The original meaning referred to being literally caught in a net, but the figurative meaning developed to describe being trapped in a difficult situation.
