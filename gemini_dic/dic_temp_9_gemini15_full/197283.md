sharps:/ʃɑːrps/ | n. | 尖锐的事物；（尤指）乐器上的升号；高音；敏锐的人 |  Sharp objects can be dangerous. 尖锐的物体可能很危险。| The music contained many sharps. 这音乐包含许多升号。|近义词：points, edges, highs | 反义词：flats, lows


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The knife has sharp edges. = 这把刀刃很锋利。
His words are sharp and critical. = 他的话尖锐而批判。


一般过去时:
The music contained several sharps. = 这音乐包含好几个升号。
The wind blew with sharp gusts. = 风刮得一阵阵尖厉。


现在完成时:
She has become much sharper in her thinking. = 她的思维变得敏锐多了。
The cold weather has sharpened my appetite.  = 寒冷的天气增进了我的食欲。


过去完成时:
The sound had become sharper before the explosion. = 爆炸前声音变得尖锐了。
I had sharpened my pencils before class. = 我在上课前把铅笔削尖了。


将来时:
The wind will become sharper later in the afternoon. = 下午晚些时候风会变得更强劲。
He will sharpen his skills over the next few months. = 他将在接下来的几个月里提升他的技能。


虚拟语气:
If the knife were sharper, it would cut better. = 如果刀更锋利，它会切得更好。
I wish my vision were sharper. = 我希望我的视力更敏锐。


被动语态:
The pencils were sharpened by the teacher. = 铅笔是被老师削尖的。
His arguments were sharpened by the debate. = 他的论点在辩论中得到了加强。


疑问句:

否定句:

Her words were not sharp or critical. = 她的言辞并不尖锐或批判。
