reveals:/rɪˈviːlz/ | vt. |揭露；显示；展现 | To make something known that was previously hidden or unknown.  |例句：The investigation revealed a shocking truth.  调查揭露了一个令人震惊的真相。

The report reveals a significant increase in crime rates. 报告显示犯罪率大幅上升。


近义词：discloses, unveils, exposes, shows, demonstrates | 反义词：conceals, hides, masks, obscures | 词性：动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The research reveals important new data. 这项研究揭示了重要的新的数据。
His smile reveals his happiness. 他的笑容展现了他的快乐。


一般过去时:
The letter revealed a secret affair. 这封信揭露了一段秘密的恋情。
The investigation revealed the culprit. 调查揭露了罪犯。


现在完成时:
The evidence has revealed the truth. 证据已经揭示了真相。
Recent studies have revealed the long-term effects of smoking. 最近的研究已经揭示了吸烟的长期影响。


过去完成时:
The autopsy had revealed the cause of death. 尸检已经揭示了死因。
She had revealed her secret to her best friend. 她已经向她最好的朋友透露了她的秘密。


将来时:
The upcoming report will reveal the company's financial status.  即将发布的报告将揭示公司的财务状况。
Further investigation will reveal more details.  进一步调查将揭示更多细节。


虚拟语气:
If he revealed the truth, he would be in trouble. 如果他揭露真相，他会陷入困境。
I wish he had revealed the truth sooner. 我希望他早点揭露真相。


被动语态:
The truth was revealed to the public. 真相被公诸于众。
The results of the experiment were revealed last week. 实验结果在上周公布。


疑问句:
What does the study reveal? 这项研究揭示了什么？
What will the future reveal? 未来会揭示什么？


否定句:
The investigation did not reveal any new information. 调查没有揭示任何新信息。
He refused to reveal his sources. 他拒绝透露他的消息来源。

词源:
The word "reveal" comes from the Old French word "reveiller," meaning "to awaken" or "to rouse."  This is related to the Latin word "revelare," which also means "to unveil" or "to make known."  The sense of uncovering something hidden developed over time.
