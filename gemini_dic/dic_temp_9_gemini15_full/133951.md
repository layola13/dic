/ˌnɒnˌhiːdəˈnɪstɪkli/ | adj. | 非享乐主义地 | Not motivated by or seeking pleasure; characterized by a rejection of hedonism.  It implies a focus on other values or goals beyond personal pleasure or gratification. |  The ascetic monk lived nonhedonistically, prioritizing spiritual growth over material comforts.  |近义词：ascetically, austerely, self-denyingly | 反义词：hedonistically, pleasurably, luxuriously


例句：

He approached the task nonhedonistically, focusing solely on its completion rather than the enjoyment it might bring. 他非享乐主义地处理这项任务，专注于完成它而不是它可能带来的乐趣。


The philosopher argued that a truly fulfilling life could not be solely nonhedonistically pursued. 这位哲学家认为，真正充实的生活不能仅仅是非享乐主义地追求。


USAGE->
This word is an adjective, and therefore doesn't conjugate like verbs.  It modifies nouns.  Its usage is straightforward, describing actions or lifestyles that are not driven by the pursuit of pleasure.


PHRASE->
There are no common idiomatic phrases using "nonhedonistically".  Its use is primarily within philosophical or descriptive contexts.
