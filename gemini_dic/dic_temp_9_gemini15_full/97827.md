inkling:/ˈɪŋklɪŋ/| n.|一点迹象；暗示；预感|A slight suggestion or indication; a hint; a vague feeling or suspicion.|例句：I had an inkling that something was wrong. 我隐约觉得出了什么事。|例句：She gave me an inkling of her plans. 她向我暗示了她的计划。|近义词：hint, suggestion, suspicion, intimation|反义词：certainty, full knowledge


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He has an inkling of the problem. = 他对这个问题有点了解。
I get an inkling of what she means. = 我有点明白她的意思了。

一般过去时:
She had an inkling that he was lying. = 她隐约觉得他在撒谎。
I had no inkling of the danger. = 我当时丝毫没有察觉到危险。

现在完成时:
Have you had any inkling of their plans? = 你对他们的计划有任何了解吗？
I have had an inkling that something is amiss. = 我感觉到有点不对劲。

过去完成时:
I had had an inkling that the project would fail. = 我早就有预感这个项目会失败。
She had had no inkling of his intentions. = 她对他的意图毫无察觉。

将来时:
Will you have an inkling of the results by tomorrow? = 明天你能知道结果吗？
I expect to have an inkling of the outcome soon. = 我预计很快就能知道结果了。


虚拟语气:
If I had had an inkling, I would have acted differently. = 如果我早有预感，我会采取不同的行动。
I wish I had had an inkling of the consequences. = 我真希望我早知道后果会这样。

被动语态:
No inkling of the truth was given to the public. = 公众没有得到任何真相的暗示。
An inkling of the scandal was leaked to the press. = 丑闻的蛛丝马迹被泄露给了媒体。


疑问句:
Do you have any inkling of what happened? = 你对发生的事情有任何了解吗？
Have you any inkling as to his whereabouts? = 你对他身在何处有任何线索吗？

否定句:
I have no inkling of what she is talking about. = 我不明白她在说什么。
He had no inkling that his secret was out. = 他不知道他的秘密已经被泄露了。


PHRASE->

the first inkling = 最初的迹象
