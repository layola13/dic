plodder:/ˈplɒdə(r)/| n.| 慢吞吞的人；苦干的人；老实的劳作者 | A plodder is someone who works slowly and steadily, often without much imagination or skill. They are characterized by their perseverance and diligence, even if their progress is not always rapid or spectacular.  | He's a plodder, but he always gets the job done eventually. 他是个慢吞吞的人，但他最终总是能完成工作。  | drudge, grinder, toiler |  dasher, sprinter | 名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He plods along the road every day. = 他每天沿着路慢吞吞地走。
She plods through her work. = 她埋头苦干。


一般过去时:
He plodded through the mud. = 他在泥泞中艰难跋涉。
She plodded home after a long day. = 她长途跋涉后回到家。


现在完成时:
He has plodded through many challenges in his life. = 他一生中克服了许多挑战。
She has plodded along steadily in her career. = 她在事业上稳步前进。


过去完成时:
He had plodded for hours before he reached the summit. = 他跋涉了几个小时才到达山顶。
She had plodded through the paperwork all morning. = 她整个上午都在埋头处理文件。


将来时:
He will plod on, regardless of the obstacles. = 不管遇到什么障碍，他都会继续努力。
She will plod through the next chapter of her book. = 她将继续努力完成书的下一章。


虚拟语气:
If he plodded a little faster, he would arrive on time. = 如果他走快一点，他就能准时到达。
I wish she hadn't plodded so slowly; we would have finished earlier. = 我真希望她不要走那么慢；我们就能早点完成了。


被动语态:
The work was plodded through by the team. = 这个工作由团队慢慢完成。
(Note:  The passive voice is less common with "plod".)


疑问句:
Does he always plod along like that? = 他总是这样慢吞吞地走吗？
Is she still plodding through that report? = 她还在埋头看那份报告吗？


否定句:
He didn't plod along; he ran. = 他没有慢吞吞地走；他是跑的。
She didn't plod through the assignment; she finished it quickly. = 她没有慢慢完成作业；她很快完成了。


PHRASE->
a plodding pace  = 缓慢的速度
a plodding worker = 勤勤恳恳的工人
plodding along = 缓慢而稳定地前进
plodding through = 艰难地完成
to plod one's way = 缓慢而坚定地前进
