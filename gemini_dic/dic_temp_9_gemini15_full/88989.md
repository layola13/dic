holists:/ˈhoʊlɪsts/ | n. |整体论者，整体主义者 |  A holist is someone who believes that a system (like a society, a person's mind, or the universe) should be understood as a whole, rather than by analyzing its individual parts.  Holists emphasize the interconnectedness and interdependence of elements within a system. | Example: Many environmental holists advocate for holistic approaches to conservation, considering the entire ecosystem rather than focusing on individual species. | 近义词: system theorists, integrationists | 反义词: reductionists, atomists | 名词
