goodfellow:/ˈɡʊdˌfɛloʊ/ | n. | 好人，好家伙，朋友 | A good-natured, friendly, and jovial person.  A goodfellow is someone who is generally well-liked and enjoys companionship. | Example: He was a goodfellow, always ready with a joke and a helping hand. 他是个好人，总是乐于助人，而且很爱开玩笑。 |近义词：companion, pal, buddy, chum |反义词：misanthrope, villain, scoundrel | 名词

词源:
The word "goodfellow" is a compound word, combining "good" (meaning virtuous, kind, or pleasant) and "fellow" (meaning a companion or associate).  Its origins trace back to Middle English, reflecting a long-standing appreciation for companionship and amiable character.  The term has maintained its positive connotation over time.
