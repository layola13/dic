preholder: /ˈpriːˌhoʊldər/ | n. | 预持有人，预先持有者 | A person or entity that holds something temporarily before it is transferred to the final recipient.  This term is often used in legal or financial contexts.  For example, an escrow agent might be considered a preholder of funds before they are released to the buyer in a real estate transaction. | The bank acted as a preholder of the funds until the transaction was finalized.  银行在交易完成之前充当资金的预持有人。 |  intermediary, custodian, escrow agent |  recipient, receiver | noun
