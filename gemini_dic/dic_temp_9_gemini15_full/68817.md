fascinate:/ˈfæsɪneɪt/ | vt. |使着迷；吸引；迷住 |To attract and hold the attention of someone, often to a great degree; to be extremely interesting.|例句：The history of ancient Egypt fascinates me.  埃及古代史深深吸引着我。  The magician's tricks fascinated the children. 魔术师的戏法迷住了孩子们。|近义词：captivate, enthrall, enchant, intrigue |反义词：bore, repel


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The museum fascinates many visitors. = 博物馆吸引了许多游客。
Her stories fascinate her friends. = 她的故事吸引了她的朋友们。

一般过去时:
The concert fascinated the audience. = 音乐会迷住了观众。
The novel fascinated me from beginning to end. = 这部小说从头到尾都让我着迷。

现在完成时:
This topic has fascinated scientists for years. = 这个话题多年来一直吸引着科学家们。
The mystery has fascinated detectives for months. = 这个谜团已经吸引了侦探们好几个月了。

过去完成时:
I had been fascinated by astronomy since childhood. = 从小我就对天文学很着迷。
She had been fascinated by his charm before she met him. = 在遇见他之前，她就被他迷人的魅力所吸引了。

将来时:
The new exhibit will fascinate art lovers. = 新展览将吸引艺术爱好者。
The upcoming conference will fascinate experts in the field. = 即将召开的会议将吸引该领域的专家。

虚拟语气:
If I had more time, I would fascinate myself with that project. = 如果我有更多时间，我会沉迷于那个项目。
If only I could have the time to fascinate myself with this.  要是我有时间沉迷于此就好了。


被动语态:
Many people are fascinated by the beauty of nature. = 许多人被大自然的美丽所吸引。
The audience was fascinated by the performance. = 观众被演出迷住了。

疑问句:
Does the idea of space travel fascinate you? = 你对太空旅行的想法感兴趣吗？
What fascinates you most about this job? = 你对这份工作最感兴趣的是什么？

否定句:
The movie didn't fascinate me at all. = 这部电影一点也没有吸引我。
The lecture failed to fascinate the students. = 讲座未能吸引学生们的兴趣。


PHRASE->

utterly fascinated = 完全着迷
