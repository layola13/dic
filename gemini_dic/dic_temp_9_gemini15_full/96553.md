indorsed:/ɪnˈdɔːrst/|/ɪnˈdɔːrsd/| vt.|背书；认可；赞同；签署|To write one's signature on the back of (a check or other negotiable instrument) to transfer ownership or guarantee payment.  To publicly declare one's support for or approval of (someone or something).|例句：The bank indorsed the check. 银行在支票背面签字。

The senator indorsed the new education bill. 参议员赞同新的教育法案。|近义词：approve, support, endorse, sanction|反义词：reject, oppose, condemn


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He endorses the new product. 他认可这个新产品。
She endorses the charity. 她支持这个慈善机构。


一般过去时:
They endorsed the candidate. 他们支持了这位候选人。
The company endorsed the athlete. 公司认可了这位运动员。


现在完成时:
I have endorsed this policy for years. 我多年来一直支持这项政策。
She has endorsed several environmental initiatives. 她已经支持了几项环保倡议。


过去完成时:
He had endorsed the check before he left. 他离开之前已经签署了支票。
They had endorsed the plan before the meeting. 他们在会议之前已经认可了这个计划。


将来时:
She will endorse the new book. 她将会认可这本新书。
They will endorse the proposal. 他们将会支持这个提案。


虚拟语气:
If he endorsed the project, we would get the funding. 如果他认可这个项目，我们就能获得资金。
I wish she had endorsed my application. 我希望她已经认可了我的申请。


被动语态:
The bill was endorsed by the committee.  该法案得到了委员会的认可。
The product is endorsed by a famous celebrity.  该产品由一位著名的名人代言。


疑问句:
Does he endorse this idea? 他是否认可这个想法？
Will they endorse the changes?  他们会认可这些改变吗？


否定句:
He did not endorse the plan. 他没有认可这个计划。
She doesn't endorse violence. 她不支持暴力。
