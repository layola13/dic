patristics:/pætˈrɪstɪks/| n.|教父学|The study of the writings of the early Church Fathers (roughly from the 2nd to the 8th centuries).  It encompasses their theological, philosophical, and historical perspectives, often focusing on their interpretations of scripture and their responses to heresies.|Example:  His doctoral dissertation focused on patristics, specifically the writings of Augustine.|近义词：early Christian theology, early Church studies|反义词：modern theology
