factualness:/ˈfæktʃuəlniːs/ | n. | 事实性；真实性 | The factualness of the report is questionable.  这份报告的事实性值得怀疑。|近义词：accuracy, truthfulness, veracity |反义词：falsity, inaccuracy, falsehood|

英文解析：Factualness refers to the quality of being factual or based on facts; the state or degree of being true or accurate.  It emphasizes the adherence to verifiable information and the absence of fabrication or distortion.

例句：

The factualness of her account was confirmed by several witnesses.  她叙述的真实性得到了几位证人的证实。

The judge stressed the importance of factualness in legal proceedings. 法官强调了法律程序中事实性的重要性。


词源：

Derived from the adjective "factual," which in turn comes from the noun "fact" (a thing that is known or proved to be true). The suffix "-ness" is added to create a noun denoting the quality or state of being factual.  The ultimate origin traces back to the Latin word "factum," the past participle of "facere," meaning "to do" or "to make."
