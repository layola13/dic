normanism:/ˈnɔːrmənɪzəm/| n. |诺曼主义；诺曼习俗|The adoption and spread of Norman customs, language, and institutions.  Normanism refers to the influence of the Normans, particularly after the Norman conquest of England in 1066, on the culture, language, and institutions of the conquered territories. This includes the introduction of Norman French, changes in legal systems, architecture, and social structures.  |Example: The prevalence of Norman French words in the English language is a testament to the enduring legacy of Normanism.  诺曼语在英语中的普遍存在证明了诺曼主义的持久遗产。|近义词: Norman influence, Normanization |反义词:  Anglo-Saxonism (in the context of opposing cultural influences)
