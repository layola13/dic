dioxins:/ˈdaɪɒksɪnz/| n.|二恶英|Dioxins are a group of chemically similar toxic compounds that are persistent environmental pollutants.  They are formed during incomplete combustion of organic materials, such as in waste incineration or forest fires.|例句：Exposure to dioxins can cause a range of health problems, including cancer.|近义词：polychlorinated dibenzo-p-dioxins (PCDDs), polychlorinated dibenzofurans (PCDFs)|反义词：无
