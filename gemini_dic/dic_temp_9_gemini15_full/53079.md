dip:/dɪp/| n., v.| 浸，蘸；下降；低落；凹陷 | to put something into a liquid and take it out again quickly; to go down slightly; a downward movement or slope; a small hollow or low area | 例句：She dipped her biscuit into her tea. 她把饼干蘸到茶里。 The sun dipped below the horizon. 太阳落到地平线以下。近义词：immerse, dunk, plunge, decline, decrease | 反义词：rise, ascend, increase


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He dips his bread into the soup. = 他把面包蘸到汤里。
She dips her toes into the cool water. = 她把脚趾浸入凉水中。


一般过去时:
The bird dipped its wings. = 鸟儿拍打翅膀。
The temperature dipped below freezing last night. = 昨晚气温降到冰点以下。


现在完成时:
The sales have dipped this quarter. = 本季销售额下降了。
He has dipped his hand into the paint. = 他把手伸进颜料里了。


过去完成时:
The stock market had dipped sharply before recovering. = 股市在反弹之前曾大幅下跌。
She had dipped the brush in the paint before starting the artwork. =  她在开始绘画之前已经把画笔蘸上了颜料。


将来时:
The sun will dip below the horizon soon. = 太阳很快就会落到地平线以下。
They predict that the unemployment rate will dip next year. = 他们预测失业率明年将会下降。


虚拟语气:
If the temperature dipped below zero, the pipes would freeze. = 如果气温降到零度以下，管道就会冻结。
I wish the price of gas would dip soon. = 我希望汽油价格尽快下降。


被动语态:
The bread was dipped in honey. = 面包蘸了蜂蜜。
The cookies were dipped in chocolate. = 饼干蘸了巧克力。


疑问句:
Did the temperature dip overnight? = 温度一夜之间下降了吗？
Will the plane dip below the clouds? = 飞机会下降到云层以下吗？


否定句:
He didn't dip his chips in the sauce. = 他没有把薯片蘸到酱汁里。
The plane didn't dip dangerously low. = 飞机没有危险地低飞。
