consumption:/kənˈsʌmpʃən/ | n. | 消耗；消费；消耗量；肺结核 | The act of using goods or services; the amount used.  It can also refer to the act of eating or drinking something. |例句：The consumption of alcohol is prohibited here.  这里的饮酒是被禁止的。
例句：The country's energy consumption has increased dramatically. 该国的能源消耗量急剧增加。

近义词：expenditure, use, spending, depletion | 反义词：production, creation, generation


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The factory's consumption of energy is high. = 工厂的能源消耗量很高。
Our family's monthly consumption of milk is about 10 liters. = 我们家每月牛奶的消耗量约为10升。


一般过去时:
Energy consumption decreased significantly last year. = 去年能源消耗量显著下降。
Their consumption of resources was unsustainable. = 他们对资源的消耗量是不可持续的。


现在完成时:
The company has reduced its water consumption this year. = 今年该公司减少了用水量。
We have significantly increased our consumption of organic vegetables. = 我们已经大大增加了对有机蔬菜的消费。


过去完成时:
Before the new regulations, energy consumption had been rising steadily. = 在新规出台之前，能源消耗量一直在稳步上升。
By the end of the year, their food consumption had dropped considerably. = 到年底，他们的食物消耗量已经大幅下降。


将来时:
Experts predict that energy consumption will continue to rise. = 专家预测能源消耗量将继续上升。
Our consumption of renewable energy will increase significantly in the coming years. = 未来几年，我们将大幅增加可再生能源的消耗。


虚拟语气:
If consumption remained constant, we wouldn't have this problem. = 如果消耗量保持不变，我们就不会有这个问题。
I wish our fuel consumption were lower. = 我希望我们的燃料消耗量能低一些。


被动语态:
A large amount of resources are consumed annually. = 每年消耗大量的资源。
This issue is being addressed by reducing consumption. = 这个问题正通过减少消费来解决。


疑问句:
What is the average household's consumption of electricity? = 普通家庭的平均用电量是多少？
How much is the annual consumption of water in this city? = 这个城市每年的用水量是多少？


否定句:
The government's policies did not reduce consumption enough. = 政府的政策并没有足够地减少消费。
There was no significant change in our consumption habits. = 我们的消费习惯没有发生显著变化。


PHRASE->
high consumption  高消耗
reduce consumption  减少消耗
sustainable consumption 可持续消费
consumption habits 消费习惯
energy consumption 能源消耗

词源:
The word "consumption" comes from the Latin word "consumere," meaning "to use up" or "to destroy."  It entered English in the 14th century.
