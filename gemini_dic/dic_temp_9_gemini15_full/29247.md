cassia:/ˈkæʃə/| n.|肉桂；决明属植物|Cassia refers to a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae, and also to the aromatic bark of certain species, used as a spice.  The genus includes many species, some producing edible pods and others used medicinally. The spice cassia is often used as a cinnamon substitute, although it has a slightly different flavor profile.|The bakery uses cassia in its pastries. 这家面包店在糕点中使用肉桂。|cinnamon, cinnamon bark|  (no direct antonym)|名词
