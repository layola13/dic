platonism:/ˈpleɪtənɪzəm/ | n. |柏拉图主义|The philosophical system developed by Plato and his followers, emphasizing the existence of abstract, ideal Forms as the ultimate reality, and the imperfection of the physical world as a mere shadow of these Forms.  Platonism posits that true knowledge comes from understanding these Forms, rather than sensory experience.| The philosophy department offered a course on Platonism and its influence on Western thought.  柏拉图主义及其对西方思想的影响。|idealism, essentialism|materialism, empiricism|名词
