prespeculate:/priːˈspɛkjuleɪt/| v.|预先推测；事先猜测 | To speculate beforehand; to conjecture in advance.  It often implies a less rigorous or evidence-based guess than "speculate." | 例句：He prespeculated on the likely outcome of the election before any polls were released. 他在任何民调发布之前就预先推测了选举的可能结果。


例句：The team prespeculated about the challenges they might face during the expedition.  团队预先推测了他们在探险中可能面临的挑战。


近义词：preconceive, anticipate, foreshadow, guess, surmise


反义词：verify, confirm, prove


词性：动词
