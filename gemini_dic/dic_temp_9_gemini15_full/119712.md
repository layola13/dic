midshipman:/ˈmɪdʃɪpmən/| n. |见习军官，海军学员|A midshipman is a student officer in a navy, typically at a naval academy.  They are in training to become commissioned officers.|Example:  He graduated from the Naval Academy as a midshipman.|近义词：cadet (陆军或空军学员)|反义词：None

词源:
The word "midshipman" comes from the Middle English "myddeshipman," combining "myddes" (middle) and "shipman" (sailor, ship's officer).  Originally, a midshipman was a sailor stationed in the middle of a ship, later evolving to denote a junior officer in training.
