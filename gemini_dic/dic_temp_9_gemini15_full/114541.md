malapropistic:/ˌmæləˈprɒpɪstɪk/| adj. | 错用词语的；令人啼笑皆非的 | Characterized by the mistaken use of words, especially words that are similar in sound but different in meaning; ludicrously inappropriate.  | 例句：Her malapropistic speech amused everyone.  她的错词言论逗乐了所有人。

His malapropistic comments made the meeting slightly awkward. 他的错词评论使会议略显尴尬。


近义词：comical, ludicrous, amusing, inappropriate, absurd | 反义词：accurate, precise, appropriate, correct | 词性:形容词


词源:源于18世纪英国喜剧演员Mrs. Malaprop,  她以经常混淆相似的词语而闻名。她的名字就成了这种语言错误的代名词。  The word derives from the name of Mrs. Malaprop, a character in Richard Sheridan's play "The Rivals" (1775), who habitually misused words.
