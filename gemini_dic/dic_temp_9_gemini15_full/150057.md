palpus:/ˈpæl.pəs/| n.|触须，须肢|A palpus is a sensory appendage found near the mouth of many invertebrates, such as insects and spiders.  It's used for sensing, tasting, and manipulating food.  |例句：The spider used its palpi to sense the vibrations of its prey.  蜘蛛用它的触须感知猎物的震动。

The insect's palpi were covered in pollen.  这只昆虫的触须上覆盖着花粉。|近义词：appendage, feeler|反义词：None specifically, but could be considered the opposite of a leg used for locomotion.
