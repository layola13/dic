showbread:/ˈʃoʊbred/| n. |陈设饼|The twelve loaves of unleavened bread placed on the table in the Holy Place of the Tabernacle and later the Temple in Jerusalem, representing the twelve tribes of Israel.  These loaves were replaced every Sabbath.  They were considered holy and only priests could eat them.|Example: The priests were responsible for preparing and presenting the showbread each week.  祭司负责每周准备和陈设陈设饼。|近义词：sacred bread, consecrated bread|反义词：profane bread, common bread|名词
