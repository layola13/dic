penna:/ˈpɛnə/| n.|羽毛；笔；钱币|From Latin *penna, meaning "feather,"  "wing," or "quill."  The word's primary meaning refers to a bird's feather, but its extension to "pen" comes from the use of bird quills as writing implements. The association with money likely stems from the use of feathers as decorative elements on coins or the value associated with birds themselves in certain cultures. |The bird spread its penna in the wind.  鸟儿在风中展开羽毛。|quill, plume|featherless, bald.
