hold-on: /hoʊld ˈɑːn/ | interj. | 等等；稍等 |  An interjection used to tell someone to wait a moment.  | Example: Hold on, I'll be with you in a minute. 等等，我马上就来。 |近义词：wait, hang on, just a second | 反义词：hurry up, proceed


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He holds on to the railing. = 他抓住栏杆。
She holds onto her beliefs. = 她坚持自己的信仰。


一般过去时:
He held on to the rope tightly. = 他紧紧抓住绳子。
They held on to their hope. = 他们坚持希望。


现在完成时:
I have held on to this letter for years. = 我已经保存这封信好多年了。
She has held on to her dreams despite the setbacks. = 尽管遇到挫折，她仍然坚持自己的梦想。


过去完成时:
He had held on to the secret for a long time. = 他保守这个秘密很久了。
They had held on to their positions until the end. = 他们一直坚持到最后。


将来时:
I will hold on to this memory forever. = 我将永远记住这段记忆。
She will hold on to her principles. = 她会坚持自己的原则。


虚拟语气:
If I held on a little longer, I might have succeeded. = 如果我再坚持一会儿，也许就能成功了。
I wish I had held on to that opportunity. = 我真希望我抓住了那个机会。


被动语态:
The rope was held on to firmly. = 绳子被牢牢抓住。
The traditions were held on to by the community. = 这些传统被社区传承下来。


疑问句:
Can you hold on for a moment? = 你能等一下吗？
Should I hold on to this information? = 我应该保留这个信息吗？


否定句:
He didn't hold on to the branch. = 他没有抓住树枝。
She didn't hold on to her anger. = 她没有继续生气。


PHRASE->
hold on tight = 抓紧
hold on to = 保持；坚持
hold on a second = 等一下
hold on to your hats = （表示将要发生令人兴奋或惊险的事情）做好准备
let go = 放手 (反义词组)
