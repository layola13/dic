heterotopism:/ˌhɛtərəˈtɒpɪzəm/ | n. |异位性，异位现象 |The abnormal presence of tissue in an organ or part to which it does not normally belong.  This can refer to the presence of cells, tissues, or organs in locations outside their usual anatomical sites during development.  Heterotopism can be caused by various factors, including genetic mutations and environmental influences. | Example: Gastric heterotopia in the esophagus is a condition where stomach tissue is found in the esophagus. 胃在食管中的异位是一种胃组织出现在食管中的情况。|近义词：ectopia, displacement |反义词：normality, orthoplace
