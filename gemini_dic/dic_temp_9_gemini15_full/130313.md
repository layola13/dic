non-tautological: /ˌnɒnˌtɔːtəˈlɒdʒɪkəl/ | adj. | 非同义反复的，非同语反复的 | Not expressing a tautology; not logically redundant.  It refers to statements or arguments that are not self-evident or trivially true.  They convey new information or make a non-obvious assertion. |


例句：

The argument that all bachelors are unmarried men is tautological, but the claim that most bachelors prefer cats over dogs is non-tautological.  = 所有单身汉都是未婚男人的论点是同义反复的，但大多数单身汉更喜欢猫而不是狗的说法是非同义反复的。


The theorem, while complex, proved to be non-tautological and offered new insights into the field. = 这个定理虽然复杂，但被证明是非同义反复的，并为该领域提供了新的见解。


近义词：meaningful, significant, informative, substantial


反义词：tautological, redundant, self-evident, trivial


词性：形容词
