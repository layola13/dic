gerontocracies:/dʒɛrənˈtɑːkrəsiːz/ | n. |老年统治；老年人当政 | A system of government where the power is held by old people.  This often implies that younger generations are excluded from significant decision-making roles.  The term often carries a negative connotation, suggesting stagnation, resistance to change, and a lack of representation for younger generations. |例句：Some critics argue that the country is suffering from a gerontocracy, with older politicians clinging to power and ignoring the needs of younger citizens.  一些评论家认为，这个国家正遭受着老年统治的苦难，年长的政客们紧紧抓住权力，无视年轻公民的需求。


例句：The prevalence of gerontocracies in many societies raises concerns about intergenerational equity and the responsiveness of government to the needs of all citizens. 许多社会中老年统治的盛行引发了人们对代际公平以及政府对所有公民需求的响应能力的担忧。


近义词：rule by elders, elder rule, gerontocracy (singular)


反义词：youthocracy, meritocracy (if power is based on merit rather than age)


词性:名词 (复数)
