/ˈniːtʃə/| n. |尼采|Friedrich Wilhelm Nietzsche (1844-1900), a highly influential German philosopher, cultural critic, composer, poet, writer, and classical philologist whose work has exerted a profound influence on Western philosophy and modern intellectual history. His key ideas include the death of God, the will to power, the Übermensch (Superman), eternal recurrence, and perspectivism.  He is known for his critical analyses of religion, morality, reason, and culture. | His philosophy is complex and often controversial, but his insights into human nature and the human condition continue to resonate with readers today.  His works include *Thus Spoke Zarathustra*, *Beyond Good and Evil*, and *On the Genealogy of Morality*. |近义词：philosopher, thinker |反义词：无
