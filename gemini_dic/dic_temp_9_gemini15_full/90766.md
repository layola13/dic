humorism:/ˈhjuːməˌrɪzəm/ | n. |体液说；体液论 |The theory that a person's temperament and health are determined by the balance of humors (fluids) in the body.  This ancient medical theory posited four humors: blood, phlegm, yellow bile, and black bile.  |Example:  Ancient Greek physicians based their medical practices on the principles of humorism.  古代希腊医生将他们的医疗实践建立在体液说的原则之上。|近义词：humoral theory,  four humors theory |反义词：modern medicine, scientific medicine
