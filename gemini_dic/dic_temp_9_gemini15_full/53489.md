disarticulation: /ˌdɪsˌɑːrtɪkjuˈleɪʃən/ | n. | 脱臼；关节分离；解体；清晰发音的丧失 | The separation of a joint, especially a bone from its joint.  It can also refer to the loss of clear articulation in speech. |

例句：

The accident caused a disarticulation of his shoulder. = 事故导致了他的肩膀脱臼。

The surgeon performed a disarticulation of the hip. = 外科医生进行了髋关节分离手术。


近义词：dislocation, separation, dissociation

反义词：articulation, connection, union


词源：

dis- (否定前缀) + articulation (关节；清晰表达)  From Latin *articulatus ("jointed"), from articulus ("joint"), diminutive of artus ("joint, limb").


用法说明：

disarticulation 主要用作名词，描述关节的分离或解体，尤其是在医学和解剖学背景下。它也可以指说话时清晰发音能力的丧失，这种情况通常与神经或肌肉疾病有关。

在医学上，disarticulation 通常指外科手术中将骨骼从关节处切除，例如髋关节或肩关节的截肢手术。

在语言学中，disarticulation 指的是言语表达不清，可能由各种因素引起，例如神经系统疾病、脑损伤或言语障碍。


例句补充：

His speech showed signs of disarticulation, making it difficult to understand him. = 他的言语显示出清晰发音丧失的迹象，使人们难以理解他的话。


PHRASE->

There are no common phrases directly using "disarticulation".  The word is primarily used in a clinical or technical context.
