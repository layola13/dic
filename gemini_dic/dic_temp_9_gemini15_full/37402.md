commercialises:/ˌkəˈmɜːrʃəlaɪz/| vt.|商业化；使商业化 |To make something available or suitable for commercial use or sale; to exploit for profit.|例句：The company commercialised its new technology quickly to gain a market advantage. 公司迅速将其新技术商业化以获得市场优势。|例句：They are accused of commercialising the festival, turning it into a money-making machine.  他们被指责将节日商业化，将其变成赚钱机器。|近义词：monetize, market, exploit|反义词：decommercialize, preserve


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The company commercialises its products globally. = 这家公司在全球范围内销售其产品。
He commercialises his artistic talent by selling prints online. = 他通过在线销售版画来商业化他的艺术才能。

一般过去时:
They commercialised the software last year. = 他们去年将该软件商业化。
The artist refused to commercialise his work. =这位艺术家拒绝将他的作品商业化。

现在完成时:
The band has commercialised its music through various channels. = 该乐队已通过各种渠道将其音乐商业化。
She has successfully commercialised her invention. = 她已成功地将其发明商业化。

过去完成时:

The company had commercialised its technology before its competitors. =该公司在其竞争对手之前已将其技术商业化。

将来时:
They will commercialise the drug next year. = 他们明年将把这种药物商业化。
We will commercialise our research findings. = 我们将把我们的研究成果商业化。

虚拟语气:
If they commercialised the idea, they would become very rich. = 如果他们将这个想法商业化，他们将会变得非常富有。
I wish they hadn't commercialised that beautiful tradition. = 我真希望他们没有将那美好的传统商业化。

被动语态:

疑问句:
