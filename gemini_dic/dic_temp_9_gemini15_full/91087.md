huxter:/ˈhʌkstər/| n. |小贩，叫卖小贩，兜售者 |A huxter is a person who sells small goods, often in a hawking or itinerant manner. They typically sell their wares in a public place, often shouting to attract customers.  The term often carries a connotation of being somewhat disreputable or of low social standing. |The old huxter shouted his wares from a handcart.  这个老小贩从手推车上大声叫卖他的商品。|peddler, hawker, vendor |merchant, retailer|
