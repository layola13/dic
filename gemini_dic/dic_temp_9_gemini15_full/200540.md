slashed:/ˈslæʃt/| v. adj.|砍，砍掉；削减；猛烈抨击；斜线的|To cut or strike with a sharp blow; to reduce drastically; to criticize severely;  having a slash or slashes.|例句：He slashed the price in half. 他把价格砍了一半。  The critic slashed the new movie. 批评家猛烈抨击了这部新电影。|近义词：cut, reduce, slash,criticize, attack |反义词：increase, raise, augment


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He slashes the vegetables. = 他切蔬菜。
They slash prices to attract customers. = 他们削减价格以吸引顾客。


一般过去时:
She slashed the tires of his car. = 她划破了他的汽车轮胎。
The company slashed its workforce. = 公司裁员了。


现在完成时:
They have slashed the budget by 10%. = 他们已经削减了10%的预算。
The government has slashed funding for education. = 政府已经削减了教育经费。


过去完成时:
They had slashed the prices before the sale. = 他们在促销之前就已削减了价格。
The artist had slashed the canvas with a knife. = 艺术家用刀子划破了画布。


将来时:
They will slash taxes next year. = 他们明年将减税。
The company will slash jobs to stay afloat. = 公司将裁员以维持运转。


虚拟语气:
If they slashed prices, they would sell more. = 如果他们降价，他们就会卖出更多。
I wish they had not slashed the program. = 我真希望他们没有取消那个项目。


被动语态:
The budget was slashed by 20%. = 预算被削减了20%。
The tires were slashed overnight. = 轮胎在夜间被划破了。


疑问句:
Did he slash the painting? = 他是否划破了这幅画？
Will they slash the workforce further? = 他们还会进一步裁员吗？


否定句:
He didn't slash the prices. = 他没有降价。
They haven't slashed the budget yet. = 他们还没有削减预算。
