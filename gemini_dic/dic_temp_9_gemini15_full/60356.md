egregiously:/ɪˈɡriːdʒəsli/| adj.|极其恶劣地；令人震惊地；非常糟糕地|extremely bad; in a shockingly bad way.  It implies a degree of outrageousness or flagrant disregard for norms or standards.|The company egregiously violated safety regulations, resulting in a major accident. 公司严重违反了安全条例，导致了一起重大事故。| flagrantly, outrageously, shockingly, scandalously| admirably, commendably, excellently


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He egregiously misrepresents the facts. 他极其歪曲事实。
She egregiously disregards the rules. 她公然无视规则。


一般过去时:
They egregiously underestimated the project's cost. 他们严重低估了项目的成本。
He egregiously lied to the police. 他对警察撒了弥天大谎。


现在完成时:

They have egregiously neglected their responsibilities. 他们严重忽视了他们的责任。


过去完成时:

They had egregiously mismanaged the funds. 他们严重误用了资金。


将来时:
He will egregiously fail the exam if he doesn't study. 如果他不学习，他考试肯定会不及格。
She will egregiously disappoint her parents if she continues this behavior. 如果她继续这种行为，她会严重让她的父母失望。


虚拟语气:

被动语态:
