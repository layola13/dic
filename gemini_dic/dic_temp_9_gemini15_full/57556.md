dramatic: /drəˈmætɪk/ | adj. | 戏剧性的；引人注目的；夸张的 |

英文解析:  Characterized by or involving sudden and striking events; exciting and impressive.  Something dramatic is characterized by strong emotions, striking events, or a significant change.  It can also imply exaggeration or artificiality.

例句:
The play had a dramatic climax. = 这出戏有一个戏剧性的高潮。

The news report gave a dramatic account of the accident. = 新闻报道对事故作了戏剧性的描述。


近义词: spectacular, sensational, striking, impressive, theatrical

反义词:  mundane, ordinary, commonplace, uneventful


词源:  From Late Middle English *dramatyk*, from Old French *dramatique*, from Late Latin *dramaticus*, from Greek *dramatikos*, from *drama* "drama, action".  The word's evolution reflects its connection to theatrical performances and the heightened emotions and events typical of them.
