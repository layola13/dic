mutterers:/ˈmʌtərərz/| n. |咕哝的人，低声抱怨的人 |People who mutter, especially repeatedly and in a low voice, often expressing discontent or complaint.  They often speak unclearly and indistinctly. | Example: The mutterers in the back of the room were disturbing the lecture.  房间后面的那些咕哝的人扰乱了讲座。 |近义词： grumblers, complainers, murmurers |反义词：  speakers (clearly),  articulate people

词源:
The word "mutterers" is derived from the verb "mutter," which has Old English roots.  The Old English word "muttorian" meant "to murmur, speak indistinctly."  The suffix "-ers" indicates a person who performs the action of the verb.
