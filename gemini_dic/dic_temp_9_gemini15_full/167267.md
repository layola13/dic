pretexts:/ˈpriːtekstz/| n.|借口，托辞|A pretext is a false reason or excuse given to conceal the real reason for an action.|例句：He used the meeting as a pretext to see her. 他借开会为名去看她。|近义词：excuse, justification, rationale|反义词：truth, reality


USAGE->
一般现在时:
His pretexts are always unconvincing. = 他的借口总是令人难以信服。
What are your pretexts for being late? = 你迟到的借口是什么？


一般过去时:
Her pretexts didn't fool anyone. = 她的借口没有骗到任何人。
What pretexts did he use to avoid the work? = 他用了什么借口来逃避工作？


现在完成时:
He has used various pretexts to avoid responsibility. = 他已经用了各种借口来逃避责任。
She has repeatedly used the same pretext. = 她一再使用同样的借口。


过去完成时:
They had already prepared their pretexts before the meeting. = 他们在会议前就已经准备好他们的借口了。
He had used that pretext many times before. = 他以前多次使用过那个借口。


将来时:

虚拟语气:
