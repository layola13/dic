/ˌpriːprəˈfɛtɪk/ | adj. | 预言性的，先知的，预兆的 |  Relating to or suggestive of prophecy; foreshadowing something to come. This word is a less common variant, often used to emphasize a precursory or preparatory nature of a prophetic event or statement. It suggests something prophetic is coming, but isn't yet fully realized. | The preprophetic signs were subtle, almost imperceptible, but the seasoned prophet recognized them.  预兆很微妙，几乎察觉不到，但经验丰富的先知认出了它们。 |  prophetic, prescient | unprophetic |
