cassiopeia:/ˌkæsiˈoʊpiə/| n. |仙后座|Cassiopeia is a constellation in the northern sky, named after the vain queen Cassiopeia in Greek mythology.  It is easily recognizable by its distinctive W shape. |The constellation Cassiopeia is visible in the Northern Hemisphere during autumn and winter. 仙后座在北半球的秋季和冬季可见。|近义词：constellation (星座)|反义词：无
