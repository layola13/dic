decriminalizing: /ˌdiːˈkrɪmɪnəlaɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 使非犯罪化 |  The process of removing the criminal status of an act or behavior.  It involves changing laws to make something that was previously illegal, no longer a crime. |

例句：

Many countries are decriminalizing the possession of small amounts of marijuana. = 许多国家正在使少量持有大麻的行为非犯罪化。

The government is considering decriminalizing prostitution. = 政府正在考虑使卖淫行为非犯罪化。


近义词：legalizing,  deregulating (in some contexts)

反义词：criminalizing

词源:

de- (否定前缀) + criminalize (使…犯罪化)  The verb "criminalize" is derived from "criminal" + "-ize" (a suffix that forms verbs meaning "to make or become"). "Criminal" ultimately comes from the Latin word "criminalis," meaning "pertaining to a crime."
