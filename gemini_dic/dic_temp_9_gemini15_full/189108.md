saccharins: /ˈsækərɪnz/ | n. | 糖精，糖精钠 |  A crystalline, white, odorless, and intensely sweet substance, used as an artificial sweetener. It is several hundred times sweeter than sucrose (table sugar) but has a slightly bitter aftertaste.  | Example: Many diet sodas use saccharin as a sweetener.  许多节食苏打水使用糖精作为甜味剂。 | 近义词：artificial sweetener, sweetening agent | 反义词：sugar (in the context of natural sweetness)
