rechannelled:/ˌriːˈtʃænld/| v. |重新引导；重新定向；改变方向 | To redirect or give a new course or direction to something, typically a flow of something such as water, energy, or information.  |例句：The river was rechannelled to prevent flooding. 河道重新疏导以防止洪水。

例句：The company rechannelled its resources into developing new technologies. 公司将资源重新投入到新技术的开发中。

近义词：redirect, reroute, divert, reorient

反义词：maintain, preserve (the original course)

词性：动词

词源:  The word is formed by adding the prefix "re-" (meaning "again" or "back") to "channel," which ultimately derives from the Old French "canel" and Latin "canalis" (meaning "pipe," "channel," or "gutter").  The "-ed" suffix indicates the past participle form of the verb.
