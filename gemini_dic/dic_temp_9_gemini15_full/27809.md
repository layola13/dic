cannoning: /ˈkænənɪŋ/ | v. | 猛撞；碰撞；炮击 | To strike or collide violently; to bombard with cannon fire. |

例句：

The two cars cannoned into each other at the intersection.  = 两辆车在十字路口猛烈相撞。

The enemy cannoned the city relentlessly. = 敌人无情地炮击了这座城市。


近义词：colliding, crashing, impacting, bombarding

反义词：gently touching, softly hitting

词源:

The word "cannoning" derives from the noun "cannon," referring to a large, powerful firearm. The verb form reflects the action of firing a cannon or the violent impact resulting from such an event.  The word's history traces back to the medieval period, evolving from earlier forms related to tube-shaped instruments or weapons.
