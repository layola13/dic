pancreatization: /ˌpæŋkriətaɪˈzeɪʃən/ | n. | 胰腺化；胰腺移植 | The process of surgically removing and transplanting a pancreas or a part of it.  It is a complex procedure typically performed to treat diabetes mellitus.  | Example: Pancreatization is a viable option for some patients with severe diabetes.  胰腺移植对于一些患有严重糖尿病的患者来说是一种可行的选择。 | 近义词: pancreatic transplantation, islet transplantation | 反义词:  N/A

词源:
The word "pancreatization" is derived from "pancreas" + "-ization".  "Pancreas" is from the Greek words "pan" (all) and "kreas" (flesh),  referring to its appearance. "-ization" is a suffix forming nouns that denote the action or process of doing something.
