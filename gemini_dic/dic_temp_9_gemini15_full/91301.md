hydromedusa:/ˌhaɪdroʊˈmɛdjuːsə/| n. |水母 |A hydromedusa is the sexually reproductive stage in the life cycle of many hydrozoans, a class of aquatic animals belonging to the phylum Cnidaria.  It is a free-swimming, jellyfish-like form characterized by its bell-shaped body and tentacles. Unlike the polyp stage, which is sessile, the hydromedusa is motile and plays a crucial role in the dispersal of the species.| Example:  The hydromedusa pulsed rhythmically through the water, its tentacles trailing behind.|近义词： jellyfish (in some contexts)|反义词： polyp (in the context of the hydrozoan life cycle)
