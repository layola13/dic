lungfuls: /ˈlʌŋfʊlz/ | n. |肺容量；一大口气吸入的空气| A lungful is the amount of air that can be inhaled into the lungs in one breath.  It often implies a deep or full breath.|例句：He took a deep lungful of the crisp mountain air. 他深深地吸了一口清新的山间空气。
He gasped, taking several lungfuls of air before speaking. 他喘着粗气，吸了几口气才说话。|近义词：breath, inhalation |反义词：exhalation

词源:
The word "lungful" is a compound word formed from "lung" and "ful".  "Lung" comes from the Old English "lunge," referring to the respiratory organ.  "-ful" is a suffix meaning "full of" or "characterized by," indicating a quantity sufficient to fill the lungs.
