raftering: /ˈræftɪŋ/ | n. |漂流，泛舟 |The activity of travelling down a river or stream on a raft.  It often involves navigating rapids and other challenging water features. |例句：We went rafting down the Colorado River. 我们在科罗拉多河上进行了漂流。

例句：The thrill of rafting through whitewater rapids is unforgettable.  在激流中漂流的刺激是令人难忘的。


近义词：river rafting, white-water rafting | 反义词：无


词源:  Derived from "raft" (a flat floating structure) + "-ing" (present participle suffix indicating activity).  The word's usage reflects the increasing popularity of rafting as a recreational activity.


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They are rafting down the Amazon. = 他们在亚马逊河上漂流。
We raft every weekend in the summer. = 我们夏天每个周末都去漂流。


一般过去时:
He rafted the Grand Canyon last year. = 他去年在格兰德峡谷漂流。
We rafted down the river and saw many animals. = 我们沿着河漂流，看到了许多动物。


现在完成时:
They have rafted many rivers around the world. = 他们已经在世界各地漂流过许多河流。
She has never rafted before. = 她以前从未漂流过。


过去完成时:
They had rafted the entire length of the river before sunset. = 他们在日落之前已经漂流了整条河。
We had never rafted such a wild river before. = 我们以前从未漂流过如此湍急的河流。


将来时:
We will raft the Colorado River next summer. = 我们明年夏天将在科罗拉多河漂流。
They will be rafting in Costa Rica next month. = 他们下个月将在哥斯达黎加漂流。


虚拟语气:
If I had the time, I would go rafting. = 如果我有时间，我会去漂流。
I wish I were rafting in the mountains right now. = 我希望我现在正在山里漂流。


被动语态:
The river was rafted by many tourists last year. = 去年这条河被许多游客漂流过。
The trip was planned carefully for the rafting expedition. = 这趟旅行是为漂流探险精心策划的。


疑问句:

否定句:
