preadmitting: /ˌpriːədˈmɪtɪŋ/ | v. | 预录取，预先录取 |  The act of admitting someone or something before the official admission process.  It often implies a conditional or provisional acceptance.  | 例句：The university is preadmitting students based on their preliminary applications.  大学正在根据学生的初步申请进行预录取。


The hospital is preadmitting patients for the upcoming surgery. 医院正在为即将进行的手术预先录取病人。| 近义词：pre-registering, provisionally accepting | 反义词：rejecting, refusing

词源:
The word "preadmitting" is a combination of the prefix "pre-" (meaning "before") and the verb "admitting" (the present participle of "admit").  Therefore, it literally means "admitting beforehand."
