fight-with-windmills: /faɪt wɪð wɪndmɪlz/ | n. phrase | 堂吉诃德式地战斗；徒劳无功的努力 | To fight with windmills is an idiom derived from Miguel de Cervantes's novel Don Quixote. It refers to attacking imaginary enemies or fighting a losing battle against insurmountable odds.  It implies futility and a lack of understanding of reality.  | Example: He's fighting with windmills trying to convince them to change their minds; it's a lost cause. 他试图说服他们改变主意，简直是螳臂当车。 | 近义词: tilt at windmills, fight a losing battle, engage in a futile effort | 反义词: achieve success, win a victory
