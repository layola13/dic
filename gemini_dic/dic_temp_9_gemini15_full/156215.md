phryganeid:/ˈfrɪɡəniːd/| n. |毛翅目昆虫；石蛾|A phryganeid is an insect belonging to the order Trichoptera, commonly known as caddisflies.  They are characterized by their larval stage, which constructs protective cases from various materials like silk, sand, and small stones. Adult phryganeids are typically nocturnal and resemble moths.|Example:  The stream was teeming with phryganeid larvae building their intricate cases.  这条小溪里挤满了正在建造复杂外壳的石蛾幼虫。|近义词：caddisfly (more common)|反义词：无
