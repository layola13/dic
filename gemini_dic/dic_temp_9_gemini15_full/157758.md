place-mats:/ˈpleɪsˌmæts/| n.| 餐垫 | A placemat is a small mat or piece of material placed under a plate or dishes at a table, primarily to protect the table surface. They can also add to the table's aesthetic appeal.  | Example:  We used festive placemats for the holiday dinner. 我们在假日晚餐时使用了节日餐垫。

The children spilled juice on their placemats. 孩子们把果汁洒在他们的餐垫上了。


近义词：table mat, dinner mat, place setting (as part of)
反义词：无

词源:
The word "placemat" is a compound word, combining "place" (referring to the location where a plate is placed) and "mat" (a flat piece of material).  It emerged in the 20th century as a descriptive term for this specific type of table accessory.
