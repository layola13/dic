patricians:/pəˈtrɪʃənz/ | n. | 贵族；上流社会人士 |  Members of the highest class in ancient Rome, possessing significant wealth and political power.  They formed the ruling class and often contrasted with the plebeians. | Example: The patricians held a monopoly on political office in the early Roman Republic.  罗马共和国早期，贵族垄断了政治职位。

The patricians' lavish lifestyle was a stark contrast to that of the plebeians.  贵族的奢华生活方式与平民的生活方式形成了鲜明对比。| 近义词：aristocrats, nobles, elite | 反义词：plebeians, commoners
