clinging: /ˈklɪŋɪŋ/ | adj. | 粘着的；依附的；紧贴的；执着的 |  Describes something that adheres closely or tenaciously to something else, or a person who is overly dependent or attached.  |例句：The clinging vines covered the wall.  =  藤蔓紧紧地缠绕着墙壁。

The clinging child refused to let go of his mother's hand. =  那个依恋的儿童拒绝松开他母亲的手。


近义词：adhesive, clinging, sticking, tenacious, dependent | 反义词：detached, independent, loose


词源:  Comes from the Old English *clyngan, meaning "to stick, adhere".  The modern sense of clinging evolved from this original meaning of physical adhesion to include emotional or psychological attachment.


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The ivy is clinging to the brick wall. = 常春藤紧紧地攀附在砖墙上。
The smell of smoke is clinging to his clothes. =  烟味粘在他的衣服上。


一般过去时:
She clung to the railing as the boat rocked. = 船摇晃时，她紧紧抓住栏杆。
He clung to the hope that he would recover. = 他紧紧抓住他将会康复的希望。


现在完成时:
The mud has been clinging to my shoes all day. = 泥巴整天都粘在我的鞋子上。
She has been clinging to her old beliefs despite the evidence. = 尽管有证据，她仍然坚持着她的旧观念。


过去完成时:
He had been clinging to that belief for years before he finally let it go. = 在他最终放弃之前，他已经坚持那个信念多年了。
The paint had clung stubbornly to the old wood. = 油漆顽固地粘在旧木头上。


将来时:
The fog will be clinging to the hills tomorrow. = 明天雾将笼罩山峦。
I think he will be clinging to his position for as long as possible. = 我认为他会尽可能地坚持他的立场。


虚拟语气:
If the climber had not been clinging to the rock, he would have fallen. = 如果攀登者没有抓住岩石，他就会掉下去。
I wish I hadn't been clinging to the past for so long. = 我真希望我没有那样长时间地执着于过去。


被动语态:
The poster was clinging to the wall. = 海报粘在墙上。
The child was clinging to his mother's leg. = 孩子紧紧抓住他母亲的腿。


疑问句:
Is the wallpaper still clinging to the wall? = 墙纸还粘在墙上吗？
Why is he clinging to these outdated ideas? = 他为什么坚持这些过时的想法？


否定句:
The paint didn't cling to the surface very well. = 油漆没有很好地粘附在表面。
He didn't cling to his beliefs as stubbornly as his brother. = 他不像他哥哥那样顽固地坚持他的信仰。
