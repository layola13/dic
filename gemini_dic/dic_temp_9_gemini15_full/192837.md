searchful: /ˈsɜːrtfʊl/ | adj. | 寻求的；探究的；好寻觅的 |  Characterized by a diligent or persistent seeking; inquisitive.  Showing a strong desire to find something. | 例句：He was searchful in his efforts to find the lost child. 他努力寻找丢失的孩子，表现得非常认真。

He cast a searchful look around the room. 他四处搜寻地打量着房间。


近义词：inquisitive, inquiring, investigative, seeking, exploring | 反义词：uninterested, apathetic, indifferent, unconcerned


词源:  The word "searchful" is derived from the Old English word "sēcan," meaning "to seek" or "to search."  The suffix "-ful" adds the meaning of "full of" or "characterized by."  Therefore, "searchful" literally means "full of searching" or "characterized by searching."


USAGE->  (Since "searchful" is an adjective, it doesn't have verb conjugations like "to be," "to have," etc.)
