culminating:/ˈkʌlmɪneɪtɪŋ/ | v. |达到顶点；以…告终 |  To reach a climax or point of highest development.  | The years of research culminated in a major breakthrough.  多年的研究最终取得了重大突破。 | reaching, peaking, climaxing |  beginning, starting  | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The efforts culminate in a successful project. = 这些努力最终成就了一个成功的项目。
His hard work culminates in a promotion. = 他的努力最终换来了升职。


一般过去时:
The festival culminated in a spectacular fireworks display. = 节日以一场壮观的烟火表演告终。
Their long journey culminated in reaching the summit. = 他们的漫长旅程最终到达了山顶。


现在完成时:
Years of training have culminated in her Olympic victory. = 多年的训练最终使她获得了奥运冠军。
His dedication has culminated in a remarkable achievement. = 他的奉献最终成就了一项非凡的成就。


过去完成时:
After years of struggle, their efforts had culminated in a significant victory. = 多年奋斗之后，他们的努力最终取得了重大胜利。
The investigation had culminated in the arrest of the suspect. = 调查最终导致了嫌疑人的逮捕。


将来时:
The project will culminate in a grand opening ceremony. = 项目将以盛大的开幕式结束。
Their efforts will culminate in a successful product launch. = 他们的努力将以成功的产品发布告终。


虚拟语气:
If we work hard, our efforts will culminate in success. = 如果我们努力工作，我们的努力将最终获得成功。
I wish my years of study had culminated in a better job. = 我希望我的多年学习能换来一份更好的工作。


被动语态:
The project was culminated by a grand celebration. = 项目以盛大的庆祝活动结束。
The research was culminated with the publication of a groundbreaking paper. = 研究以一篇具有开创性的论文的发表而告终。


疑问句:
Does the story culminate in a happy ending? = 故事以圆满结局告终吗？
Will the concert culminate in a performance by the famous singer? = 音乐会将以这位著名歌手的演出结束吗？


否定句:
The negotiations did not culminate in an agreement. = 谈判没有达成协议。
His efforts did not culminate in the desired results. = 他的努力没有达到预期的效果。



PHRASE->
culminate in = 以…告终
culminate with = 以…结束
culminate the = 达到…的顶点
culminate a = 使…达到顶点
culminating event = 决定性事件
