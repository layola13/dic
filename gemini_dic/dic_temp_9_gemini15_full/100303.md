/ˌɪntərˈʃɑːk/| n. |冲击之间，间歇性冲击|The term "intershock" refers to the period or interval between successive shocks or impacts, often in a series of events or occurrences.  It can describe the time between heartbeats in certain contexts or the gap between seismic events.  The word is less common and often found in specialized fields.|例句：The intershock interval varied significantly throughout the earthquake sequence. 地震序列中冲击之间的间隔变化很大。|近义词：interval, gap, pause, hiatus|反义词：simultaneous, continuous|名词
