runabout: /ˈrʌnəbaʊt/ | n. |小型汽车；轻便快艇；闲逛；四处跑的人 | A small, light car, often used for short journeys; a small motorboat;  the act of running about; a person who runs about. |例句：He bought a sporty runabout for weekend trips. 他买了一辆运动型小型汽车用于周末旅行。

The family enjoyed a relaxing day out on their runabout.  这个家庭在他们的轻便快艇上享受了一天轻松的时光。


近义词：car, motorboat, roadster,  (for the person) gadabout, vagrant | 反义词：limousine, yacht (for the vehicle)  | 词性：名词


词源:

The word "runabout" is a compound word. It combines "run" (meaning to move quickly) and "about" (meaning around or in various directions).  The first element reflects the vehicle's intended use for short, quick trips, while the second element highlights its maneuverability and suitability for navigating various routes.  Its usage as a description of a person developed naturally from the vehicle's association with frequent short journeys.
