paddlefish: /ˈpædəlfɪʃ/ | n. |匙吻鱼|A paddlefish is a freshwater fish with a long, paddle-shaped snout.  They are found in rivers and lakes in North America and China. They are filter feeders, meaning they strain food particles from the water.  Paddlefish are considered a delicacy in some cultures and are also important for ecological balance.|Example: The Mississippi paddlefish is a critically endangered species. 密西西比匙吻鱼是一种极度濒危物种。|近义词：spoonbill catfish (部分种类)|反义词: N/A
