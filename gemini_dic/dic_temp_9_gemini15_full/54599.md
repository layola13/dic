dismarry: /dɪsˈmæri/ | v. |解除婚姻；离婚 | To dissolve a marriage; to divorce.  |The couple decided to dismarry after a long period of unhappiness. 这对夫妇在长期不快乐后决定离婚。|近义词：divorce, separate | 反义词：marry


USAGE->
一般现在时:
They dismarry after ten years of marriage. = 他们结婚十年后离婚了。
He dismarries his wife due to irreconcilable differences. = 他因为不可调和的分歧与妻子离婚。

一般过去时:
The pair dismarried last year. = 这对夫妇去年离婚了。
They dismarried quietly and without much fanfare. = 他们悄悄地、没有大张旗鼓地离婚了。

现在完成时:
They have dismarried and are now living separately. = 他们已经离婚了，现在分居。
She has dismarried twice before. = 她以前离过两次婚。


过去完成时:
They had dismarried before he met her. = 在他遇到她之前，他们已经离婚了。
She had dismarried once before marrying him.  = 在嫁给他之前，她已经离过一次婚。


将来时:
They will dismarry soon if they cannot solve their problems. = 如果他们不能解决问题，他们很快就会离婚。
He thinks he will dismarry his wife in the near future. = 他认为他很快就会和妻子离婚。


虚拟语气:
If they dismarried, they would both be happier. = 如果他们离婚，他们俩都会更快乐。
I wish they had dismarried sooner. = 我希望他们早点离婚。


被动语态:
The marriage was dismarried by mutual consent. = 这段婚姻是双方同意解除的。(注意：被动语态用法较少见)
Their marriage is being dismarried in court. = 他们的婚姻正在法庭上被宣告无效。(注意：被动语态用法较少见)


疑问句:
Will they dismarry? = 他们会离婚吗？
Have they dismarried yet? = 他们离婚了吗？


否定句:
They did not dismarry; they worked through their problems. = 他们没有离婚；他们克服了他们的问题。
She will not dismarry her husband. = 她不会和她丈夫离婚。
