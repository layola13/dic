governmentalizing: /ˌɡʌvərˈmɛntəlˌaɪzɪŋ/ | v. | 使…政府化；使…受政府管制 |  The process of subjecting something to government control or influence; making something governmental in nature.  It often involves the expansion of government power and the regulation of previously unregulated areas. |


例句：

The increasing governmentalizing of healthcare has led to concerns about reduced patient choice and increased bureaucracy.  =  医疗保健日益政府化，导致人们担忧患者选择减少和官僚主义增加。

The governmentalizing of the internet raises questions about freedom of speech and online privacy. =  互联网的政府化引发了关于言论自由和网络隐私的问题。



近义词：nationalizing,  regulating, controlling, bureaucratizing


反义词：privatizing, deregulating, liberalizing


词源：

The word "governmentalizing" is a relatively modern verb formed from the noun "government" and the suffix "-alizing," which indicates the process of making something of a particular nature.  The root "govern" comes from Old French "governer" and ultimately from Latin "gubernare," meaning "to steer, direct, govern."  The suffix "-alize" comes from Latin "-izare."
