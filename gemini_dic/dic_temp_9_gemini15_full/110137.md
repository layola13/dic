lie-down:/ˈlaɪ daʊn/| v.|躺下，卧倒|To recline or assume a horizontal position.|例句：He lay down on the grass to rest. 他躺在草地上休息。|例句：The doctor told her to lie down and rest. 医生告诉她躺下休息。|近义词：recline, rest, repose|反义词：stand up, get up


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I lie down every night at 10 pm. = 我每天晚上十点躺下睡觉。
They lie down for a nap after lunch. = 他们午饭后躺下小睡一会儿。

一般过去时:
She lay down after a long day of work. = 她经过漫长的一天工作后躺下了。
He lay down on the beach and sunbathed. = 他躺在沙滩上晒日光浴。

现在完成时:
I have lain down for a while to relieve my backache. = 我已经躺了一会儿来缓解背痛。
She has lain down since she felt unwell. = 她感觉不舒服后就躺下了。

过去完成时:
He had lain down before the phone rang. = 电话响之前他已经躺下了。
They had lain down for hours before they finally fell asleep. = 他们躺了几个小时才最终睡着。

将来时:
I will lie down as soon as I finish my work. = 我一完成工作就躺下。
We will lie down on the hammock and read. = 我们将躺在吊床上看书。

虚拟语气:
If I were tired, I would lie down. = 如果我累了，我就躺下。
I wish I could lie down and relax right now. = 我希望我现在能躺下放松一下。

被动语态:
(Lie down does not typically use passive voice because it is an intransitive verb)  The bed was prepared, ready for him to lie down. (This uses "prepared" passively, not "lie down") 床已准备好，等着他躺下。

疑问句:
Do you usually lie down after dinner? = 你通常晚饭后躺下吗？
Will you lie down for a rest? = 你会躺下休息一下吗？

否定句:
I didn't lie down yesterday. = 我昨天没有躺下。
He couldn't lie down because of the pain. = 因为他疼痛所以不能躺下。
