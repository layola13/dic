semisomnolences:/ˌsɛmiˈsɑːmnələnsɪz/ | n. | 半昏睡状态；困倦；嗜睡 | A state of being half-asleep; drowsiness; sleepiness.  The word suggests a state between full wakefulness and full sleep, often characterized by a lack of alertness and a tendency to doze.  It emphasizes the partial or incomplete nature of the sleepiness. |例句：The afternoon sun induced a state of semisomnolence in the lecture hall.  下午的阳光让演讲厅里的人昏昏欲睡。

The long journey brought on a feeling of semisomnolence. 漫长的旅程让人感到昏昏欲睡。|近义词：drowsiness, lethargy, somnolence, slumber |反义词：wakefulness, alertness, vigilance |名词
