goaded:/ˈɡoʊdəd/ | v. | 刺激；驱使；激怒 | To provoke or annoy (someone) so as to stimulate some action or reaction.  |例句：He was goaded into fighting by his opponent's insults. 他被对手的侮辱激怒而大打出手。
例句：The protesters were goaded by the police's heavy-handed tactics. 抗议者被警方强硬的策略激怒了。|近义词：provoked, spurred, incited, urged |反义词：soothed, calmed, pacified | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He goads his friends into doing foolish things. = 他怂恿他的朋友们做傻事。
She constantly goads him about his weight. = 她不断地拿他的体重取笑他。


一般过去时:
The bully goaded the smaller boy into a fight. = 那个恶霸激怒了那个个子小的男孩，让他打架。
His teasing goaded her into a furious response. = 他的戏弄激怒了她，让她勃然大怒。


现在完成时:
They have goaded him into making a hasty decision. = 他们一直怂恿他做出草率的决定。
The media has goaded the public into a frenzy. = 媒体激起了公众的狂热。


过去完成时:
He had been goaded into confessing by the relentless interrogation. = 他在持续不断的审问下被激怒而招供了。
The constant criticism had goaded her into resigning. = 持续不断的批评激怒了她，让她辞职了。

虚拟语气:

I wish I hadn't been goaded into that argument. = 我真希望我没有被激怒而卷入那场争论。


被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:

PHRASE->

goad someone to action = 激励某人采取行动
goad someone to anger = 激怒某人
goad someone to fury = 激怒某人


词源:
The word "goad" comes from the Old English word "gad," meaning a pointed stick used to urge on cattle.  This reflects the word's original meaning of physically prodding an animal. Over time, the meaning shifted to include the figurative sense of provoking or inciting a person to action.
