gargoyles:/ˈɡɑːrɡɔɪlz/ | n. |石像鬼，怪兽排水口 |  A grotesque carved figure of a human or animal, often used as a decorative architectural feature, especially on medieval buildings, typically projecting from a roof gutter or a similar position to act as a waterspout.  | Example: The old cathedral was adorned with numerous gargoyles, each one a unique and frightening creature.  古老的大教堂装饰着许多石像鬼，每一个都是独一无二的可怕生物。|近义词：grotesque, monster, chimera |反义词：angel, cherub |名词

词源:
The word "gargoyle" comes from the Old French word "gargouille," which meant "gullet" or "throat."  This refers to the spout-like feature of the architectural element. The word's ultimate origin is likely onomatopoeic, imitating the gurgling sound of water.
