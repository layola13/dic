cowpoke:/ˈkaʊpoʊk/| n. |牛仔，牧童|A cowpoke is a cowboy, especially one who is unskilled or inexperienced.  The term often carries a slightly humorous or derogatory connotation, suggesting a less-skilled or less-refined cowboy. |例句：He wasn't a seasoned rancher, just a green cowpoke trying to make his way. 他不是经验丰富的牧场主，只是一个试图闯出自己天地的小牛仔。

例句：That clumsy cowpoke nearly rode his horse into the river.  那个笨手笨脚的牛仔差点把马骑到河里去了。


近义词：cowboy, wrangler, ranch hand |反义词：city slicker, sophisticated person


词源:The term "cowpoke" is believed to have originated in the American West in the late 19th and early 20th centuries. It's a blend of "cow" and "poke," with "poke" possibly referring to someone who pokes or prods cattle.  The exact etymology is debated, but the association with cattle herding is clear.  The word initially carried a neutral meaning but over time gained a slightly negative connotation.
