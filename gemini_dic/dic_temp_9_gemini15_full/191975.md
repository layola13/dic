scorching: /ˈskɔːrtʃɪŋ/ | adj. | 酷热的；极热的；严厉批评的 | Extremely hot; intensely critical. |

英文解析:  The word "scorching" describes something that is extremely hot, often to an uncomfortable or even damaging degree.  It can also be used figuratively to describe criticism that is harsh and severe.


例句:

The scorching sun beat down on us all afternoon. = 酷热的阳光整个下午都照射着我们。

It was a scorching summer day, the hottest of the year. = 那是一个酷热的夏日，是今年最热的一天。

The critic gave the play a scorching review, pointing out all its flaws. = 批评家对这部戏给予了严厉的批评，指出了它所有的缺陷。

He received a scorching rebuke from his boss for his mistakes. = 他因为自己的错误受到了老板严厉的斥责。


近义词:  blistering, sweltering, searing, torrid (for heat);  withering, scathing, blistering (for criticism)


反义词:  freezing, frigid, cool (for heat); mild, gentle, lukewarm (for criticism)


词性:  形容词
