predistrict:/ˌpriːˈdɪstrɪkt/| n.|预先划定区域，预先选区|A predistrict is a geographic area that is designated or planned before the official establishment of districts for administrative, electoral, or other purposes.  It's a preliminary division of a larger area into smaller units.|例句：The city council is considering several predistrict maps before finalizing the new ward boundaries.  市议会正在考虑几份预先划定区域的地图，然后再最终确定新的选区边界。|近义词：precinct, division, zone|反义词：final district, established district

词源:
The word "predistrict" is a combination of the prefix "pre-" (meaning "before") and "district" (meaning a region or area).  It's a relatively modern term, reflecting the increasing need for careful planning and consideration before the formal establishment of districts for various purposes.
