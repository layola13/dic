leninism:/ˈlɛnɪnɪzəm/| n. |列宁主义|A political ideology based on the writings and theories of Vladimir Lenin, advocating a vanguard party of professional revolutionaries leading the proletariat in a socialist revolution and establishing a dictatorship of the proletariat as a transitional stage to communism.  It emphasizes a centralized, highly disciplined party structure and a focus on achieving revolutionary change through strategic action.|The Soviet Union under Stalin was a practical application of Leninism, though significantly deviated from some of Lenin's original tenets.  |近义词：Marxism, communism, socialism|反义词：capitalism, liberalism
