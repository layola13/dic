immunodeficiency:/ˌɪmjuːnoʊdɪˈfɪʃənsi/| n. |免疫缺陷|The state or condition of having an impaired immune system, making an individual more susceptible to infections and diseases.  It encompasses a range of conditions from mild to severe, affecting the body's ability to fight off pathogens.|例句：The patient's immunodeficiency made him vulnerable to various infections.  这位病人的免疫缺陷使他容易受到各种感染。

例句：Hereditary immunodeficiency disorders can be life-threatening if not properly managed.  如果不能得到适当的管理，遗传性免疫缺陷疾病可能是危及生命的。


近义词：immune deficiency, immunocompromise|反义词：immune competence, immunocompetence


词源:
* **immuno-:** from Latin *immunis*, meaning "exempt, free from service,"  eventually related to the concept of being free from disease.
* **-deficiency:** from *deficient*, meaning "lacking something necessary."  This combines a prefix *de-* (meaning "away from, down from") and *ficient* (from Latin *facere*, "to make," indicating a lack of something that should be made or present).
