homie:/ˈhoʊmi/ | n. |哥们儿，朋友，老兄 | A close friend, especially one from one's neighborhood or social group.  The term often implies a sense of loyalty and familiarity. | Example: "My homie helped me move last weekend."  我的哥们儿上周末帮我搬家了。|近义词：buddy, pal, friend, mate |反义词：enemy, foe


USAGE->
一般现在时:
My homie always supports me. = 我的哥们儿总是支持我。
He is my homie. = 他是我的哥们儿。


一般过去时:
My homie helped me with my car. = 我的哥们儿帮我修车了。
I saw my homie yesterday. = 我昨天看见我的哥们儿了。


现在完成时:
I've known my homie for years. = 我认识我的哥们儿好多年了。
My homie has always been there for me. = 我的哥们儿一直都在支持我。


过去完成时:
I had already met his homie before the party. = 在聚会之前我已经见过他的哥们儿了。
They had been homies since high school. = 他们从高中起就是哥们儿了。


将来时:
I will see my homie tomorrow. = 我明天将见到我的哥们儿。
My homie will help me with my project. = 我的哥们儿将帮我完成我的项目。


虚拟语气:
If I had a homie, I wouldn't be so lonely. = 如果我有个哥们儿，我就不会这么孤单了。
I wish I had a homie like him. = 我真希望有个像他那样的哥们儿。


被动语态:
He was considered a homie by everyone. = 他被大家认为是哥们儿。
(Note: Passive voice is less common with this word)


疑问句:
Is he your homie? = 他是你的哥们儿吗？
Who is your homie? = 谁是你的哥们儿？


否定句:
He is not my homie. = 他不是我的哥们儿。
I don't consider him my homie. = 我不把他当成我的哥们儿。
