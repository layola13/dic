recalcitrant: /rɪˈkælsɪtrənt/ | adj. | 顽抗的；不服从的；难驾驭的 |  Difficult to manage or control; stubbornly resistant to authority or guidance.  |  The recalcitrant student refused to follow the rules.  这个顽皮的学生拒绝遵守规则。


The mule proved to be a recalcitrant beast, refusing to budge. 这头骡子证明是一头倔强的牲口，拒绝移动。|近义词：stubborn, defiant, unruly, intractable, refractory |反义词：obedient, compliant, docile, manageable


Etymology:

The word "recalcitrant" comes from the Latin word "recalcitrare," meaning "to kick back" or "to resist."  The prefix "re-" intensifies the meaning, and "-ant" is an adjective suffix.  The image is of a stubborn animal kicking back against its handler, resisting control.
