lichens:/ˈlaɪkənz/| n. |地衣|A lichen is a composite organism that arises from the symbiotic relationship between a fungus and an alga or cyanobacterium.  The fungus provides structure and protection, while the alga or cyanobacterium provides food through photosynthesis. Lichens are found in a wide variety of habitats, from deserts to arctic regions. They are important components of many ecosystems and play a role in nutrient cycling and soil formation. |Example:  Many types of lichens thrive in harsh environments. 许多种地衣在恶劣的环境中茁壮成长。
