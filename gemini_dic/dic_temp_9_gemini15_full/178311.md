reconciling: /ˈrɛkənˌsaɪlɪŋ/ | v. | 调和；使一致；和解；使顺从 |  The act of making compatible or consistent; resolving differences; bringing into harmony; settling a dispute; accepting something unpleasant. |

例句：
He spent hours reconciling his bank statement. = 他花了几个小时核对他的银行对账单。

She is trying to reconcile her career ambitions with her family responsibilities. = 她正试图调和她的职业抱负和家庭责任。


近义词：reconcile, harmonize, unify, adjust, accommodate

反义词：disagree, conflict, clash, separate


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I reconcile my bank account every month. = 我每个月都核对我的银行账户。
We reconcile our differences through dialogue. = 我们通过对话来调和分歧。


一般过去时:
They reconciled after a long argument. = 他们经过长时间的争吵后和解了。
The accountant reconciled the accounts at the end of the year. = 会计在年底核对账目。


现在完成时:
I have reconciled myself to the fact that I won't win. = 我已经接受了不会赢的事实。
She has reconciled her personal life with her professional commitments. = 她已经把她的私人生活与她的职业承诺协调好了。


过去完成时:
They had reconciled before the wedding. = 他们在婚礼前已经和解了。
He had reconciled the conflicting data before presenting his report. = 他在提交报告之前已经协调了相互矛盾的数据。


将来时:
I will reconcile my differences with him soon. = 我很快就会与他和解。
We will reconcile the budget with the actual spending. = 我们将把预算与实际支出相协调。


虚拟语气:
If I were you, I would reconcile with my brother. = 如果我是你，我会和我的兄弟和解。
I wish I could reconcile my conflicting feelings. = 我希望我能调和我相互矛盾的感受。


被动语态:
The accounts were reconciled by the end of the month. = 账户在月底前已核对完毕。
The differences were reconciled through mediation. = 分歧通过调解得以解决。


疑问句:
Have you reconciled your differences yet? = 你已经和解了吗？
How do you reconcile these conflicting ideas? = 你如何调和这些相互冲突的观点？


否定句:
He couldn't reconcile the two conflicting reports. = 他无法调和这两份相互矛盾的报告。
I cannot reconcile myself to this injustice. = 我无法接受这种不公正。


PHRASE->
reconcile with = 与...和解
reconcile to = 使顺从于；使适应
reconcile differences = 调和分歧
reconcile accounts = 核对账目
reconcile oneself to = 接受（不好的事情）


词源:
From Old French *reconcilier, from Latin *reconciliāre, from re- ("again") + conciliāre ("to unite, win over").  The Latin root is related to concilium ("council, assembly").  The sense of "making peace" developed from the idea of bringing people together in a council to settle disputes.
