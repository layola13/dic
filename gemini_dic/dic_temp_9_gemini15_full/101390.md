ious:/ˈiːəs/ | adj. | …的；具有…性质的 |  Suffix forming adjectives meaning "full of," "characterized by," or "tending to." It's derived from the Latin suffix "-osus," which has the same meaning.  The suffix is added to nouns and sometimes stems to create adjectives. | He is a studious student. 他是一个好学的学生。 |  studious, ambitious, glorious, anxious,  | careless, unambitious


USAGE->

一般现在时:
He is glorious. 他很光荣。
She is anxious. 她很焦虑。


一般过去时:
He was ambitious. 他很有抱负。
She was studious. 她很用功。


现在完成时:
They have been anxious. 他们一直很焦虑。
He has been glorious. 他一直很光荣。


过去完成时:
They had been ambitious. 他们曾经很有抱负。
She had been studious. 她曾经很用功。


将来时:
He will be studious. 他将很用功。
She will be anxious. 她将会焦虑。


虚拟语气:
If he were ambitious, he would succeed. 如果他雄心勃勃，他就会成功。
If she were studious, she would pass the exam. 如果她用功学习，她就会通过考试。


被动语态:
His success was considered glorious. 他的成功被认为是光荣的。
Her worry was deemed anxious. 她的担忧被认为是焦虑的。


疑问句:
Is he ambitious? 他有雄心吗？
Is she anxious? 她焦虑吗？


否定句:
He is not studious. 他不好学。
She is not ambitious. 她没有雄心壮志。


PHRASE->
There are no common phrases directly using "-ious" as a standalone unit. The suffix is used to create adjectives which then appear in phrases. Examples using adjectives formed with "-ious":

Glorious victory: 光荣的胜利
Ambitious plan: 雄心勃勃的计划
Anxious moment: 焦虑的时刻
Studious habit: 好学的习惯
Delicious food: 美味的食物
