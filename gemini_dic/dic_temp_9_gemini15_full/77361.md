gauge:/ɡeɪdʒ/| n. v.|测量仪器；规格；判断；估计 | To measure something accurately, or to assess or judge something.  |The mechanic used a gauge to check the tire pressure. 机械师用压力表检查轮胎气压。  |meter, measure, estimate | immeasurable, unmeasurable | noun, verb


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He gauges the distance accurately. 他精确地测量距离。
They gauge the public's reaction to the new policy. 他们评估公众对新政策的反应。

一般过去时:
She gauged the depth of the water before jumping in. 她跳进水里之前测量了水的深度。
The engineer gauged the strength of the bridge. 工程师评估了桥梁的强度。


现在完成时:
We have gauged the public's opinion on this matter. 我们已经探测了公众对这件事的意见。
They have gauged the market's potential. 他们已经评估了市场的潜力。


过去完成时:
He had gauged the situation correctly. 他已经正确地判断了形势。
She had gauged the impact of the storm before making a decision.  她做出决定之前已经评估了风暴的影响。


将来时:
They will gauge the effectiveness of the new drug. 他们将评估新药的有效性。
We will gauge the public response to the announcement. 我们将衡量公众对公告的反应。


虚拟语气:
If I had a gauge, I would measure the liquid level. 如果我有一个测量仪器，我就会测量液位。
If they had gauged the public sentiment correctly, they wouldn't have made that decision. 如果他们能正确判断民意，就不会做出那个决定了。


被动语态:
The water level was gauged regularly. 水位定期测量。
The public's opinion was gauged through surveys. 公众的意见是通过调查来评估的。


疑问句:
How do you gauge the success of a project? 你如何衡量一个项目的成功？
How did they gauge the risk involved? 他们是如何评估所涉及的风险的？


否定句:
He didn't gauge the difficulty of the task. 他没有评估任务的难度。
They failed to gauge the public's anger. 他们没能评估公众的愤怒。
