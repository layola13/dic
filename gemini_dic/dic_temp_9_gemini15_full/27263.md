calotype:/ˈkælətaɪp/| n. |卡罗式照片，日光型照片 |A calotype is an early photographic process invented by William Henry Fox Talbot in the 1830s. It involved creating a negative image on paper coated with silver iodide, which was then used to create a positive print.  |The calotype process was revolutionary for its time, allowing for multiple prints from a single negative. |daguerreotype, photogram |  | n.
