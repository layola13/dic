preparoxysmal: /ˌpriːpærɒkˈsɪzməl/ | adj. | 发作前的，先兆的 | Relating to the period immediately before an attack or paroxysm, especially an epileptic seizure or other convulsive episode.  The term describes the symptoms or signs that occur just before a more severe event. | Example:  The patient experienced preparoxysmal symptoms, such as aura and dizziness, before the onset of the seizure.  病人发作前出现了先兆症状，例如先兆和头晕。 | Near synonyms: prodromal, premonitory | Antonyms: postparoxysmal, postictal |  词性：形容词
