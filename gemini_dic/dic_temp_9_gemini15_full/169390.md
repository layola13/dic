proner:/ˈproʊnər/| n.| 俯卧者，倾向于…的人 | A person who is prone to something is someone who is likely to suffer from it or do it.  |He's a prone to accidents.|近义词：inclined, susceptible, disposed |反义词：unlikely, resistant


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He is prone to making mistakes. = 他容易犯错。
She is prone to anxiety. = 她容易焦虑。


一般过去时:
He was prone to anger in his youth. = 他年轻时容易生气。
She was prone to illness during the winter. = 冬天她容易生病。


现在完成时:
He has been prone to depression since the accident. = 自从事故发生后，他一直容易抑郁。
She has been prone to injury throughout her career. = 她的职业生涯中一直容易受伤。


过去完成时:
He had been prone to headaches for years before seeking medical attention. = 在就医之前，他多年来一直容易头痛。
She had been prone to procrastination, which impacted her grades. = 她一直拖延，这影响了她的成绩。


将来时:
He will likely be prone to heart problems later in life. = 他晚年可能容易患心脏病。
She will probably be prone to sunburn if she doesn't wear sunscreen. = 如果她不涂防晒霜，她可能会容易晒伤。


虚拟语气:
If he were prone to violence, I would be concerned. = 如果他容易暴力，我会担心。
If she were prone to exaggeration, I wouldn't believe her. = 如果她容易夸大其词，我不会相信她。


被动语态:
(This word doesn't typically use passive voice.)


疑问句:
Is he prone to getting colds? = 他容易感冒吗？
Is she prone to making impulsive decisions? = 她容易做出冲动决定吗？


否定句:
He is not prone to complaining. = 他不容易抱怨。
She is not prone to fits of anger. = 她不容易发脾气。
