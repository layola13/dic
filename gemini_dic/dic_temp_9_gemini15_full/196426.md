serpents:/ˈsɜːrpənts/ | n. |蛇，阴险的人| Serpents are legless reptiles that belong to the suborder Serpentes. They are characterized by their long, slender bodies, scales, and forked tongues. Many species are venomous, and some are highly dangerous to humans.  | Example:  The garden was overgrown with weeds and teeming with serpents. 花园里杂草丛生，到处都是蛇。


The ancient Greeks associated serpents with healing and medicine, symbolized by the Rod of Asclepius.  古希腊人将蛇与治疗和医学联系起来，以阿斯克勒庇俄斯之杖为象征。|近义词：snakes, vipers|反义词：无|词性：名词
