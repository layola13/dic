daguerreotypes:/dæˈɡɛroʊtaɪps/| n. | 达盖尔式银版照片 | A daguerreotype is an early photographic process invented in 1839, producing a unique, one-of-a-kind image on a silver-plated copper sheet.  The image is created through a chemical reaction caused by light exposure.  It is characterized by its unique detail and tonal range, but is also fragile and prone to deterioration.|  The museum houses a remarkable collection of 19th-century daguerreotypes.| 近义词：early photograph, photographic image | 反义词：None
