cavil:/ˈkævəl/| v.|吹毛求疵，挑剔，找茬 |To raise trivial objections; find fault with; carp. |例句：Don't cavil at minor details; the overall plan is excellent. 不要对细枝末节吹毛求疵；总体计划是很棒的。|例句：He constantly cavils at my work, making it hard to feel appreciated. 他总是吹毛求疵我的工作，让我很难感受到被欣赏。|近义词：criticize, carp, quibble |反义词：praise, commend, approve |动词

一般现在时:
He cavils at every suggestion. = 他对每一个建议都吹毛求疵。
They constantly cavil about the smallest things. = 他们不断地为一些鸡毛蒜皮的小事挑剔。

一般过去时:
She caviled at the price of the dress. = 她对裙子的价格吹毛求疵。
He caviled at my choice of words. = 他挑剔我选择的词语。

现在完成时:

They have caviled at every detail of the proposal. = 他们对提案的每一个细节都吹毛求疵。

过去完成时:

词源:
The word "cavil" originates from the Old French word "caviller," meaning "to quibble" or "to find fault."  This, in turn, likely derives from a Late Latin word of uncertain origin, possibly related to words meaning "to chatter" or "to make a fuss".
