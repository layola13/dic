reauthorizing: /ˌriːˈɔːθəraɪzɪŋ/ | v. |重新授权；再次授权 | The act of granting authorization again; giving permission or authority anew.  It implies a previous authorization that has expired, been revoked, or needs renewal. |例句：The committee is reauthorizing the environmental protection program for another five years.  委员会正在将环境保护计划重新授权五年。

The government is reauthorizing the transportation bill, making several key changes. 政府正在重新授权交通法案，并做出了一些关键的改变。


近义词：renewing, extending, reinstating | 反义词：revoking, canceling, rescinding | 词性：动词


词源:

* **re-**: prefix meaning "again," "anew," or "back."
* **author**: from Latin "auctor," meaning "originator," "founder," or "one who creates."
* **-ize**: suffix forming verbs, indicating "to make" or "to cause to be."
* **-ing**: present participle suffix.
