proportional:/prəˈpɔːrʃənəl/| adj.| 成比例的，相称的 |  Bearing a constant ratio; having the same or a constant ratio.  It describes a relationship where two or more quantities are related by a constant ratio. For example, if one quantity doubles, the other doubles proportionally. |例句：The map is drawn to a proportional scale. 这张地图是按比例绘制的。

The punishment should be proportional to the crime. 惩罚应该与罪行相称。


近义词：commensurate, proportionate, relative, equivalent |反义词：disproportionate, unequal


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The cost is proportional to the amount of work. = 成本与工作量成比例。
The size of the effect is proportional to the dose. = 效果的大小与剂量成比例。


一般过去时:
The wages were proportional to the hours worked. = 工资与工作时间成比例。
The yield was proportional to the amount of fertilizer used. = 收成与使用的肥料数量成比例。


现在完成时:
The results have been proportional to the effort invested. = 结果与投入的努力成比例。
The growth has been proportional to the investment. = 增长与投资成比例。


过去完成时:
The damage had been proportional to the force of the impact. = 损坏程度与冲击力成比例。
The success had been proportional to the preparation. = 成功与准备工作成比例。


将来时:
The profits will be proportional to the sales. = 利润将与销售额成比例。
The expansion will be proportional to the demand. = 扩张将与需求成比例。


虚拟语气:
If the input were doubled, the output would be proportionally larger. = 如果输入加倍，输出也会相应地增加。
If the effort were greater, the results would be proportionally better. = 如果付出更多努力，结果会更好。


被动语态:
The rewards are proportionally distributed among the team members. = 奖励在团队成员之间按比例分配。
The resources are proportionally allocated to each department. = 资源按比例分配给每个部门。


疑问句:
Is the representation proportional to the population? = 代表比例与人口比例相符吗？
Are the ingredients proportional in this recipe? = 这个食谱中的配料比例是否合适？


否定句:
The representation is not proportional to the population. = 代表比例与人口比例不相符。
The punishment was not proportional to the crime. = 惩罚与罪行不相称。


PHRASE->
proportional representation  比例代表制
inversely proportional 反比例的
directly proportional 正比例的
proportional control 比例控制
out of proportion 不成比例


词源:
From Middle French *proportional, from Latin *proportionālis, from proportio ("proportion").  The Latin word itself is a combination of pro ("for," "in front of") and portio ("part," "share," "portion").  Thus, the word's root suggests a relationship between parts or shares.
