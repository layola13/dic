resulted:/rɪˈzʌltɪd/ | v. | 导致；结果是 | resulted means to be the consequence or outcome of something.  It implies a direct causal relationship between an event or action and its effect. | The accident resulted in two injuries.  事故导致两人受伤。|The heavy rain resulted in flooding.  大雨导致洪水。|caused, produced, led to | improved, prevented


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The hard work results in success. = 努力工作导致成功。
Her efforts result in a promotion. = 她的努力导致升职。

一般过去时:
The experiment resulted in a new discovery. = 实验导致一项新发现。
His carelessness resulted in an accident. = 他的粗心导致了一起事故。

现在完成时:
His actions have resulted in significant changes. = 他的行为已经导致了重大的变化。
The negotiations have resulted in a compromise. = 谈判已经达成妥协。

过去完成时:
The delay had resulted from unforeseen circumstances. = 延误是由不可预见的情况造成的。
The argument had resulted in a broken friendship. = 争吵导致友谊破裂。

将来时:
The new policy will result in increased efficiency. = 新政策将提高效率。
The changes will result in better outcomes. = 变化将带来更好的结果。

虚拟语气:
If he had studied harder, he would have resulted in better grades. = 如果他学习更努力，他的成绩就会更好。
If she had been more careful, the accident would not have resulted. = 如果她更小心，事故就不会发生了。

被动语态:
The project was resulted in a successful launch. = 项目成功启动。
The decision was resulted in many complaints. = 这一决定导致了许多投诉。(注意：此句被动语态略显不自然，主动语态更佳)

疑问句:
What resulted from the investigation? = 调查结果是什么？
What will result from these changes? = 这些变化将导致什么结果？

否定句:
The meeting did not result in any decisions. = 会议没有达成任何决定。
His efforts did not result in a solution. = 他的努力没有解决问题。


PHRASE->
result in = 导致
result from = 源于
as a result = 结果
result of = ...的结果
end result = 最终结果
