kowtows: /ˈkaʊtaʊz/ | n. | 叩头；卑躬屈膝；(尤指对权势人物的) 谄媚 |  The act of kowtowing; excessive flattery or servile behavior, especially to those in power.  | 例句：He kowtowed to the boss to get a promotion. 他对老板卑躬屈膝以求升职。

例句：The company's kowtowing to the demands of its biggest client damaged its reputation. 公司对最大客户的要求卑躬屈膝损害了其声誉。


近义词：fawn, cringe, grovel,  obsequiousness,  flattery

反义词：defiance,  resistance,  independence,  assertiveness

词性: noun (plural)


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He kowtows to his superiors. 他对上司卑躬屈膝。
They always kowtow to the latest trends. 他们总是盲目追随最新的潮流。

一般过去时:
She kowtowed to the pressure and agreed to the deal. 她屈服于压力，同意了这笔交易。
He kowtowed to the dictator's demands. 他屈服于独裁者的要求。

现在完成时:
They have kowtowed to the demands of the market for too long. 他们对市场的需求俯首帖耳太久了。
She has never kowtowed to anyone in her life. 她一生从未对任何人卑躬屈膝。

过去完成时:
He had kowtowed to his father's wishes all his life. 他一生都顺从父亲的意愿。
They had kowtowed to the authorities for years. 他们多年来一直对当局俯首帖耳。

将来时:
He will kowtow to anyone for power. 他为了权力会向任何人卑躬屈膝。
They will not kowtow to unfair regulations. 他们不会屈服于不公平的法规。

虚拟语气:
If he wanted the job, he would kowtow to the manager. 如果他想要这份工作，他会对经理卑躬屈膝。
I wish he wouldn't kowtow to every whim of his boss. 我希望他不必事事顺从老板的意愿。

被动语态:  (Kowtow is rarely used passively)  The company's image was damaged because it was perceived to be kowtowing to the government. 公司的形象受损，因为人们认为它对政府卑躬屈膝。


疑问句:
Does he kowtow to everyone he meets? 他遇到每个人都卑躬屈膝吗？
Will they kowtow to the pressure from investors? 他们会屈服于投资者的压力吗？

否定句:
He doesn't kowtow to anyone. 他不会对任何人卑躬屈膝。
She refused to kowtow to the demands of the media. 她拒绝屈服于媒体的要求。

词源:  From Chinese  磕头 (kētóu), literally "knock head".  This practice of bowing deeply, often touching the forehead to the ground, was a traditional Chinese custom of showing respect or submission. The word entered English in the 19th century.
