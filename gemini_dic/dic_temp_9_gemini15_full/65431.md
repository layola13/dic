euphuist:/ˈjuːfjʊɪst/| n.|尤物主义者；文体华丽的作家|A person who writes or speaks in a highly ornate and affected style, characterized by elaborate figures of speech and a preference for alliteration, antithesis, and other rhetorical devices.  Such writing was popular in the Elizabethan era. |Example: John Lyly was a prominent euphuist of the Elizabethan age, his works characterized by their elaborate style.  约翰·利利是伊丽莎白时代一位著名的尤物主义作家，他的作品以其华丽的文风为特征。|近义词：rhetorician, stylist|反义词：plain speaker, minimalist writer|名词
