/ˈpærəɡræf/ | n. | 段落 | A paragraph is a distinct section of a piece of writing, usually dealing with a single theme and indicated by a new line, indentation, or numbering.  | Example:  The first paragraph of the essay introduced the main topic.  第一段文章介绍了主题。 |近义词：section, passage |反义词：N/A


USAGE->
一般现在时:
This paragraph explains the rules. = 这段话解释了规则。
The paragraph contains important information. = 这段包含重要信息。

一般过去时:
The last paragraph summarized the key points. = 最后一段总结了要点。
That paragraph described a beautiful sunset. = 那段话描述了一个美丽的日落。

现在完成时:
I have written three paragraphs so far. = 我目前已经写了三段。
She has edited several paragraphs in the report. = 她已经修改了报告中的几段。

过去完成时:
He had already written the first paragraph before the meeting. = 他在会议前已经写好了第一段。
They had revised the paragraph multiple times before submitting the paper. = 他们提交论文前多次修改了这段。

将来时:
I will write another paragraph tomorrow. = 我明天会再写一段。
She will add a new paragraph to the conclusion. = 她将在结论中添加一段新内容。

虚拟语气:
If this paragraph were longer, it would be more detailed. = 如果这段话更长，它会更详细。
I wish this paragraph were easier to understand. = 我希望这段话更容易理解。

被动语态:
The paragraph was written by a renowned author. = 这段话是一位著名作家写的。
The paragraph is being revised by the editor. = 这段话正在由编辑修改。

疑问句:
What does this paragraph mean? = 这段话是什么意思？
Which paragraph are you referring to? = 你指的是哪一段？

否定句:
This paragraph does not contain any errors. = 这段话没有任何错误。
The paragraph didn't explain the concept clearly. = 这段话没有清晰地解释这个概念。
