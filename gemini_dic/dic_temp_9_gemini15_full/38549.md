conclusible: /kənˈkluːzəbl/ | adj. | 可下结论的，可推断的 | Capable of being concluded; able to be brought to a definite end or decision.  It suggests that a conclusion can be reasonably reached based on available evidence or information. | The evidence presented was conclusible, leading the jury to a guilty verdict.  证据确凿，导致陪审团作出有罪判决。 | conclusive, decidable | inconclusive, undecidable | 形容词

词源:
From "conclude" + "-able", meaning capable of being concluded.  The word "conclude" itself originates from the Latin "concludere," meaning "to shut up, enclose, finish."
