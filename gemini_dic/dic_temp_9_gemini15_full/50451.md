depreciations:/dɪˌpriːʃiˈeɪʃənz/| n.|贬值；跌价；减值|The reduction in the value of an asset over time, due to wear and tear, obsolescence, or other factors.  It's often used in accounting and finance to describe the decrease in the value of a fixed asset. |例句：The company reported significant depreciations in its equipment due to heavy use. 公司报告称其设备由于使用频繁而大幅贬值。|例句：The rapid depreciations of the national currency led to economic instability.  国家货币的快速贬值导致了经济不稳定。|近义词：devaluation, decline, decrease, diminution|反义词：appreciation, increase, growth|名词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The equipment depreciates over time. = 设备随着时间推移而贬值。
Currency depreciation affects trade balances. = 货币贬值影响贸易平衡。

一般过去时:
The value of the property depreciated significantly last year. = 去年房产价值大幅贬值。
The car depreciated rapidly after it left the dealership. = 这辆车离开经销商后迅速贬值。

现在完成时:
The machine has depreciated considerably since its purchase. = 这台机器自购买以来已大幅贬值。
Our investments have depreciated due to market fluctuations. = 由于市场波动，我们的投资已经贬值。

过去完成时:
The asset had depreciated substantially before the company went bankrupt. = 在公司破产之前，资产已经大幅贬值。
The currency had depreciated sharply before the government intervened. = 在政府干预之前，货币已经大幅贬值。

将来时:
The value of this artwork will depreciate slowly. = 这件艺术品的价值将会缓慢贬值。
The car is expected to depreciate significantly within the first few years. = 这辆车预计在最初几年内会大幅贬值。

虚拟语气:
If the currency had not depreciated, the economy would be stronger. = 如果货币没有贬值，经济将会更强劲。
I wish the value of my house hadn't depreciated so much. = 我希望我的房子价值没有贬值这么多。

被动语态:
The building was depreciated over a period of twenty years. = 这座建筑物在二十年内被计提折旧。
The assets are being depreciated using the straight-line method. = 资产正在使用直线法计提折旧。

疑问句:
How much did the equipment depreciate this year? = 今年设备贬值了多少？
Has the value of our investment depreciated significantly? = 我们的投资价值是否已大幅贬值？

否定句:
The value of the land did not depreciate; it actually appreciated. = 土地价值没有贬值；它实际上升值了。
The company's assets did not depreciate as rapidly as predicted. = 公司的资产贬值速度没有预测的那么快。
