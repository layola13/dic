governors:/ˈɡʌvənərz/ | n. | 州长；管理者；统治者 |  The word "governors" refers to individuals who govern or administer a state, region, or organization.  It can refer to elected officials heading a state (as in the US), or those in charge of a company, institution, or machine. |

例句：

The governors of all fifty states met to discuss the national budget.  所有五十个州的州长们聚会讨论国家预算。

The school governors are responsible for overseeing the school's finances. 学校的管理者负责监督学校的财务。


近义词：administrators, rulers, executives, directors

反义词：subjects, ruled

词源:
The word "governor" comes from the Old French "governer," meaning "to govern," which in turn derives from the Latin "gubernator," meaning "steersman," "pilot," or "ruler."  The suffix "-or" indicates the agent or doer of the action.
