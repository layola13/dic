cartilages:/ˈkɑːrtɪlɪdʒɪz/ | n. |软骨（复数） | Flexible connective tissue found in various parts of the body, providing support and cushioning.  Cartilages are avascular, meaning they lack blood vessels, and receive nutrients through diffusion. |The knee cartilages are often damaged by overuse.  膝盖软骨经常因过度使用而受损。

The articular cartilages in the hip joint allow for smooth movement.  髋关节的关节软骨使关节运动更顺畅。


近义词：none (as a plural noun,  individual cartilages might have synonyms depending on location and function) | 反义词：bone (in terms of function and composition)

词性:名词(复数)


Etymology:

The word "cartilage" comes from the Late Latin word *cartilago*, which itself is derived from the Greek word *kάρτιλαγος* (*kártilagos*).  The Greek word's etymology is uncertain, but it likely involved a combination of elements referring to "gristle" or "firmness."
