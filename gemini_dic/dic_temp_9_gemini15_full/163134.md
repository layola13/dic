precombination:/ˌpriːkɒmbɪˈneɪʃən/ | n. |预先组合，预先结合 |A precombination is a combination or arrangement of things that is made or planned before something else happens.  It implies a preparation or prior arrangement of elements before their final combination or use.  The term suggests a deliberate and planned process, rather than a spontaneous or accidental one. | Example: The precombination of ingredients ensured a smooth and efficient baking process. 预先组合的配料确保了烘焙过程顺利高效。|近义词：prearrangement, preparation, prior combination |反义词：improvisation, spontaneity
