intersectionality:/ˌɪntərˌsekʃənˈæləti/ | n. | 交叉性，交织性 | The interconnected nature of social categorizations such as race, class, and gender, creating overlapping and interdependent systems of discrimination or disadvantage.  It examines how different aspects of a person's social and political identities combine to create unique modes of discrimination and privilege.  | Example:  The study highlighted the intersectionality of race and gender in shaping women's experiences in the workplace. 这项研究强调了种族和性别在塑造女性职场经历中的交叉性。  | 近义词：overlapping, interconnectedness | 反义词：separation, isolation |  词性：名词
