elegancy:/ˈɛlɪɡənsɪ/| n. |优雅；高雅；精致|The elegance and grace of her movements captivated the audience.  她的举止优雅动人，迷住了观众。|近义词：refinement, grace, sophistication|反义词：clumsiness, crudeness, gaudiness


USAGE->
This word is a noun and doesn't conjugate like verbs.  Therefore, grammatical tenses don't apply.  Instead, we'll show examples of how to use it in different sentence structures.


Simple Sentence:
Her dress displayed remarkable elegance. = 她的裙子展现出非凡的优雅。

Complex Sentence:
Because of its elegant design, the building is a popular tourist attraction. = 由于其优雅的设计，这座建筑成为热门的旅游景点。


Compound Sentence:
The dancer possessed great elegance, and the audience was mesmerized. = 舞者拥有极大的优雅，观众被迷住了。

Sentence with Adjective Modifier:
The breathtaking elegance of the ballroom was unforgettable. = 舞厅令人叹为观止的优雅令人难忘。


Sentence with Prepositional Phrase:
The elegance in her demeanor impressed everyone. = 她举止中的优雅给每个人留下了深刻印象.

Sentence with a relative clause:
The necklace, which was known for its elegance, was sold at auction. = 那条以其优雅而闻名的项链在拍卖会上售出。

词源:
From Middle French *élégance, from Latin *elegantia, from elegans (elegant).  The Latin root suggests a sense of "choosing carefully" or "picking out," implying a refined selection and resulting aesthetic appeal.
