incoagulable: /ˌɪnkoʊˈæɡjələbl/ | adj. |不能凝固的，不易凝固的 |  Unable to be coagulated; not coagulable.  This term refers to substances that do not readily clot or solidify.  It often relates to blood or other fluids. |


例句：

The patient's blood was incoagulable, requiring immediate medical attention. = 病人的血液不能凝固，需要立即就医。

The incoagulable nature of the solution posed challenges for the experiment. = 该溶液不易凝固的特性给实验带来了挑战。


近义词：uncoagulable, noncoagulable


反义词：coagulable


词源:

* **in-**:  A prefix meaning "not" or "without."
* **coagulable**:  Derived from the verb "coagulate," meaning "to change from a liquid to a solid or semi-solid state."  "Coagulate" ultimately comes from the Latin word *coagulare*, meaning "to curdle" or "to thicken."
