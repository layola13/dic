in for it:/ɪn fɔːr ɪt/| phrasal verb|自找苦吃；难逃惩罚；即将倒霉|This phrase implies that someone is about to experience unpleasant consequences due to their actions or decisions. It suggests a situation where negative repercussions are inevitable.|例句：He lied to his boss, and now he's really in for it. 他向老板撒谎，现在他真的要倒霉了。
He skipped class repeatedly, and now he's in for it. 他屡次逃课，现在要倒霉了。|近义词：in trouble, facing consequences, about to get it|反义词：getting away with it, scot-free
