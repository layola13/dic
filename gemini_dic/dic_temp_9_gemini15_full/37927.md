completely:/kəmˈpliːtli/ | adv. | 完全地，彻底地 | Completely means in every way or to the greatest extent possible. It indicates a total or absolute degree.  |  例句：He completely forgot my birthday. 他完全忘记了我的生日。  | 例句：The project was completely successful. 这个项目完全成功了。| 近义词：totally, entirely, utterly, fully | 反义词：partially, incompletely, imperfectly


USAGE->
一般现在时:
I completely understand your point. = 我完全理解你的观点。
She completely ignores him. = 她完全忽视他。


一般过去时:

词源:
The word "completely" comes from the Old French word "completement," which is a combination of "completer" (to complete) and the suffix "-ment" (forming adverbs).  "Completer" itself originates from the Latin word "complere," meaning "to fill up," "to complete," or "to fulfill."
