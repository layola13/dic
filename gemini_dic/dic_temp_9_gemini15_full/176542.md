re-samples:/ˌriːˈsæmplz/| v.|重新采样|To re-sample something means to take a new sample of it, often after some processing or transformation has been applied to the original data.  This is commonly used in digital signal processing, image processing, and statistics.  The goal is often to improve the quality, reduce noise, or change the resolution of the data.|例句：The audio engineer re-sampled the recording to improve its clarity.  音频工程师重新采样了录音以提高其清晰度。
The scientist re-sampled the data to remove outliers. 科学家重新采样了数据以去除异常值。|近义词：resample, reprocess|反义词：original sample, first sample|动词
