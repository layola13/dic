self-reconstruction:/ˌsɛlfˌriːkənˈstrʌkʃən/ | n. | 自我重建，自我修复 | The process by which something rebuilds or repairs itself, often after damage or disruption.  This term is commonly used in various fields, including biology, psychology, and sociology. | Example: The ecosystem showed remarkable self-reconstruction after the wildfire.  生态系统在野火之后展现出显著的自我重建能力。  The city underwent a period of self-reconstruction after the war.  这座城市在战后经历了一段自我重建时期。|近义词：self-repair, regeneration, renewal | 反义词：destruction, demolition, decay | 名词
