fell through: /fɛl θruː/ | v. | 失败；落空；泡汤 |  To fail to happen or be completed; to collapse.  | The deal fell through at the last minute. 这笔交易在最后一刻泡汤了。  The planned party fell through because of the bad weather.  由于天气恶劣，原计划的聚会取消了。 |  collapse, fail, miscarry | succeed, succeed, materialize | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The plan usually falls through due to lack of funding. =  这个计划通常由于缺乏资金而失败。
This project rarely falls through. = 这个项目很少失败。


一般过去时:
Our vacation plans fell through because of a family emergency. = 我们的假期计划由于家庭紧急情况而泡汤了。
The negotiations fell through after weeks of discussion. =  经过几周的讨论，谈判最终破裂了。


现在完成时:
The deal has fallen through, and we're back to square one. =  交易失败了，我们又回到了原点。
Unfortunately, my attempt to buy the house has fallen through. = 不幸的是，我买房的计划失败了。


过去完成时:
The project had fallen through before they secured additional funding. =  他们在获得额外资金之前，项目就已经失败了。
It had fallen through because of unforeseen circumstances. = 由于不可预见的情况，它失败了。


将来时:
If we don't get the funding, the project will fall through. = 如果我们得不到资金，项目就会失败。
I fear the negotiations will fall through without compromise. = 我担心如果没有妥协，谈判将会失败。


虚拟语气:
If only the deal hadn't fallen through. = 要是那笔交易没有失败就好了。
If the funding had been secured, the project wouldn't have fallen through. = 如果资金到位，项目就不会失败了。


被动语态:
The agreement was reported to have fallen through. = 据报道，协议已经失败了。
The merger was feared to have fallen through. = 人们担心合并会失败。


疑问句:
Did the negotiations fall through? = 谈判失败了吗？
Has the project fallen through yet? = 项目失败了吗？


否定句:
The plan didn't fall through; it was successfully completed. = 计划没有失败；它成功完成了。
The attempt didn't fall through despite many challenges. = 尽管面临许多挑战，尝试并没有失败。


PHRASE->
fall through the cracks = 被忽视；被遗漏
fall through the floor = 完全失败
fall through the net =  漏网之鱼; 被忽视
fall through on sth = 没有履行某事
fall through the roof = 价格暴涨
