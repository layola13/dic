emboss:/ɪmˈbɒs/ | vt. | 浮雕；压印；使凸起 | To raise a design in relief on (a surface), usually by pressing.  | The artist embossed the leather with a floral pattern. 艺术家在皮革上压印了花卉图案。 |  decorate, imprint, stamp | flatten, smooth | 动词


USAGE->
一般现在时:
He embosses leather goods. = 他压印皮革制品。
She embosses invitations with gold leaf. = 她用金箔压印请柬。

一般过去时:
The craftsman embossed the silver plate. = 工匠在银盘上压印了图案。
They embossed their logo onto the pens. = 他们在笔上压印了他们的标志。

现在完成时:
The company has embossed thousands of trophies. = 公司已经压印了数千个奖杯。
She has embossed the invitations for the wedding. = 她已经压印了婚礼请柬。

过去完成时:
He had embossed the certificate before the ceremony. = 他在仪式前已经压印好了证书。
They had embossed the medals before the awards ceremony. = 他们在颁奖典礼前已经压印好了奖牌。

将来时:
We will emboss the company logo on all our products. = 我们将在所有产品上压印公司标志。
She will emboss the design onto the metal sheet. = 她将在金属板上压印图案。

虚拟语气:
If I had an embossing machine, I would emboss my own stationery. = 如果我有一台压印机，我会自己压印文具。
I wish I could emboss the leather more elegantly. = 我希望我能更优雅地压印皮革。

被动语态:

疑问句:

否定句:
