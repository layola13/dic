nonabsolutenesses:/ˌnɒnˌæbsəˈluːtnəsɪz/| n. |非绝对性；相对性 | The plural form of "nonabsoluteness," referring to the quality or state of not being absolute or unconditional; the existence of multiple perspectives or interpretations.  It emphasizes the lack of definitive truth or universally applicable principles. |Example: The study highlights the nonabsolutenesses of moral judgments, demonstrating that ethical decisions are often context-dependent.  研究强调了道德判断的非绝对性，表明伦理决策往往取决于具体情境。|近义词：relativities, ambiguities, uncertainties|反义词：absolutes, certainties|名词


USAGE->
This word is a noun and doesn't conjugate like a verb.  Therefore, tense considerations are irrelevant.  The usage focuses on the noun's function within a sentence.

Example 1: The artist's work explores the nonabsolutenesses of beauty, showing how perceptions vary across cultures.  这位艺术家的作品探索了美的非绝对性，展示了不同文化之间感知的差异。

Example 2:  Recognizing the nonabsolutenesses of scientific truths allows for ongoing research and refinement of theories.  认识到科学真理的非绝对性，才能进行持续的研究和理论完善。
