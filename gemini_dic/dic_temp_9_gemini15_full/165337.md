preinviting: /ˌpriːɪnˈvaɪtɪŋ/ | adj. | 预先邀请的；提前邀请的 |  Describing an action or event that involves inviting someone beforehand or in advance of the main event.  It suggests a preliminary or preparatory invitation. |  The preinviting emails were sent out a week before the conference.  预先邀请的邮件在一周前发出。  | 近义词：advance, preliminary, prior | 反义词：postponed, delayed


USAGE->

一般现在时:
The company is preinviting potential clients to a product demonstration. = 公司正在预先邀请潜在客户参加产品演示。
He is preinviting guests to his birthday party next month. = 他正在预先邀请客人参加下个月的生日派对。


一般过去时:
They preinvited all the stakeholders to the meeting. = 他们预先邀请了所有利益相关者参加会议。
She preinvited her friends to a surprise party. = 她预先邀请了她的朋友参加一个惊喜派对。


现在完成时:
We have preinvited several speakers for the upcoming seminar. = 我们已经为即将举行的研讨会预先邀请了几位演讲嘉宾。
They have preinvited the press to the product launch. = 他们已经预先邀请了媒体参加产品发布会。


过去完成时:
The organizers had preinvited the VIP guests before the official announcement. = 组织者在正式宣布之前就已经预先邀请了贵宾。
Before the wedding, they had preinvited close family members to a small gathering. = 婚礼之前，他们已经预先邀请了近亲参加一个小型的聚会。


将来时:

The school will preinvite parents to the upcoming school play. = 学校将预先邀请家长参加即将上演的学校戏剧。


虚拟语气:

I wish I had preinvited him to the dinner party. = 我真希望我预先邀请他参加宴会。
