reddens:/ˈrɛdnz/| v.|变红；使变红|To become or make red.  The word implies a gradual change in color, often due to blushing, anger, or heat.|例句：Her cheeks reddened with embarrassment. 她羞红了脸。

The sun reddened the western sky. 夕阳把西边的天空染红了。|近义词：blush, flush, redden|反义词：pale, blanch

The word "redden" comes from the Old English word "rēdan," meaning "to become red."  This is related to the Old English word "rēad," meaning "red."  The suffix "-en" is a common verb-forming suffix in English, indicating a process of becoming something.
