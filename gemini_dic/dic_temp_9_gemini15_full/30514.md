ceorl:/ˈtʃɜːrl/| n. |农奴，自由农民|A ceorl was a free peasant or farmer in Anglo-Saxon England, ranking below a thegn but above a slave.  They owned land and were obligated to provide services and taxes to their lord. |Example:  The ceorl tilled his small plot of land, paying taxes to the lord of the manor.  这位农奴耕种着他的一小块土地，向庄园领主缴税。|近义词： churl, peasant, freeman (in context)|反义词：lord, thegn, slave|
