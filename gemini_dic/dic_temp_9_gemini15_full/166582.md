prescriptiveness:/prɪˈskrɪptɪvnəs/| n. |规定性；规范性；命令性 |The tendency to prescribe rules or standards, often in language or grammar.  It refers to the approach that emphasizes correct usage and adherence to established rules.  This contrasts with descriptivism, which focuses on describing how language is actually used. |Example: The prescriptiveness of the style guide limited the author's creativity.  规定性风格指南限制了作者的创造力。|近义词：prescriptivism, authoritarianism, dogmatism |反义词：descriptivism, permissiveness
