newton:/ˈnjuːtən/| n.|牛顿，单位|The word "newton" refers to the SI unit of force.  It is defined as the force required to accelerate a mass of one kilogram at a rate of one meter per second squared.  The unit is named after Sir Isaac Newton, who developed the laws of motion and universal gravitation.|例句：The force was measured in newtons.  这力是用牛顿来测量的。
The apple fell to the ground with a force of one newton. 苹果以一牛顿的力落到地上。|近义词：unit of force (力单位)|反义词：无|名词
