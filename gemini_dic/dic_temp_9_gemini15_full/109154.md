legislature:/ˈlɛdʒɪsleɪtʃər/| n. |立法机关；立法机构|A legislature is a group of people who have the power to make laws for a country or state.  It typically consists of elected representatives.  For example, the United States Congress is a bicameral legislature, meaning it has two houses: the Senate and the House of Representatives.|The state legislature passed a new law regarding environmental protection.  州议会通过了一项关于环境保护的新法律。|parliament, congress, assembly|executive branch, judiciary|名词
