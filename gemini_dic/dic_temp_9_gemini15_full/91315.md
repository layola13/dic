hydronephrosis:/ˌhaɪdrəʊnɪˈfroʊsɪs/ | n. |肾积水 |  The abnormal dilation of the renal pelvis and calyces caused by obstruction of the outflow of urine.  This condition is characterized by the accumulation of urine in the kidney, leading to swelling and potential damage to the kidney tissue.
