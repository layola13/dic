liberalizes:/ˈlɪbərəlaɪzɪz/ | v. | 自由化；放宽管制 | To make (something) more liberal; to remove or relax restrictions on something.  | The government liberalized its trade policies. 政府放宽了贸易政策。|The country is liberalizing its economic policies. 这个国家正在放宽其经济政策。 |restricts, regulates |restricts, controls, tightens


USAGE->
一般现在时:
The government liberalizes its immigration laws. = 政府正在放宽移民法。
She liberalizes her parenting style. = 她正在放宽她的养育方式。


一般过去时:
The company liberalized its dress code last year. = 公司去年放宽了着装规定。
They liberalized the censorship laws. = 他们放宽了审查制度。


现在完成时:
The reforms have liberalized the market. = 改革已经使市场自由化了。
They have liberalized their investment policies. = 他们已经放宽了投资政策。


过去完成时:
The country had liberalized its media before the revolution. = 在革命之前，这个国家已经放宽了媒体管制。
They had liberalized their visa requirements. = 他们已经放宽了签证要求。


将来时:
The government will liberalize the telecommunications sector next year. = 政府明年将放宽电信行业的管制。
They will liberalize the access to information. = 他们将放宽获取信息的渠道。


虚拟语气:
If the government liberalized the economy, it would boost growth. = 如果政府放宽经济管制，将会促进经济增长。
I wish they would liberalize the immigration laws. = 我希望他们能放宽移民法。


被动语态:
The trade restrictions were liberalized. = 贸易限制被放宽了。

疑问句:
