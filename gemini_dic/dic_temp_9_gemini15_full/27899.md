cantata:/kænˈtɑːtə/ | n. |康塔塔，清唱剧 | A cantata is a musical composition for voices, typically with instrumental accompaniment, that is often based on a narrative or dramatic text.  It's generally longer and more complex than a song or aria, but shorter than an opera. | Example: The choir performed a beautiful Baroque cantata.  合唱团表演了一首美丽的巴洛克风格清唱剧。  The composer's new cantata received rave reviews.  作曲家的新清唱剧获得了极高的评价。|近义词：oratorio, choral work |反义词：solo, song (in the sense of brevity and simplicity)


USAGE->
This word is a noun and doesn't have verb conjugations.  The usage is primarily descriptive.  Sentences involving "cantata" will use verbs to describe the action related to the cantata (e.g., perform, compose, listen to).

Example sentences illustrating different aspects of usage:


Simple Present:  He composes cantatas in his spare time. 他在业余时间创作清唱剧。
The choir sings a cantata every Sunday. 合唱团每周日演唱一首清唱剧。

Simple Past: They performed a cantata last night. 他们昨晚表演了一出清唱剧。
The audience enjoyed the cantata immensely. 观众非常享受这出清唱剧。

Present Perfect:  She has written three cantatas this year. 她今年已经创作了三部清唱剧。
We have never heard such a moving cantata before. 我们以前从未听过如此感人的清唱剧。

Future Simple: The orchestra will play a cantata next week. 乐团下周将演奏一首清唱剧。
They will compose a new cantata for the festival. 他们将为节日创作一首新的清唱剧。


PHRASE->
There aren't common phrasal verbs directly associated with "cantata".  Instead, phrases describe the cantata or its performance:

a sacred cantata  一首宗教清唱剧
a secular cantata 一首世俗清唱剧
a new cantata 一首新的清唱剧
perform a cantata 表演清唱剧
listen to a cantata 听清唱剧
