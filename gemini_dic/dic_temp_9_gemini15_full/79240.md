glass's:/ˈɡlæsəz/| n. |玻璃的；玻璃杯的|The possessive form of "glass," indicating ownership or relationship. It refers to something belonging to or associated with a glass or glasses.  |例句：The glass's surface was scratched. 玻璃的表面被刮伤了。  The glass's contents spilled. 玻璃杯里的东西洒了。|近义词：glassy (形容词，指像玻璃一样的),  of the glass|反义词：None directly, but antonyms could relate to the material or container it modifies.


USAGE->
This word is a possessive noun and doesn't have verb conjugations like "to be," "to have," etc.  It's grammatical function is solely as a modifier. Therefore, tense conjugations are not applicable.


PHRASE->
The phrase "glass's" is often used in context and doesn't have common set phrases.  Instead, it's used to modify other nouns.  For example: glass's fragility, the glass's clarity,  the glass's contents.
