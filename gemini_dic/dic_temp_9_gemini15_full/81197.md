grandity:/ˈɡrændɪti/| n. |宏伟；壮丽；伟大；庄严|The grandity of the mountains was breathtaking.  山脉的宏伟令人叹为观止。|近义词：magnificence, splendor, majesty, grandeur|反义词：meanness, insignificance, pettiness


英文解析:Grandity refers to the quality or state of being grand; impressive in scale, scope, or quality. It suggests a sense of awe-inspiring magnificence or impressiveness, often associated with size, power, or beauty.


例句:

The grandity of the palace left us speechless. 宫殿的宏伟让我们说不出话来。

The composer aimed for grandity in his symphony, using a massive orchestra and complex harmonies.  作曲家在他的交响乐中力求宏伟，使用了大型管弦乐队和复杂的和声。


词源:

The word "grandity" is derived from the word "grand," which ultimately comes from the Old French word "grant," meaning "large," "great," or "important."  The suffix "-ity" is added to form a noun, indicating a state or quality.  Therefore, "grandity" literally signifies the state or quality of being grand.
