### Additional Context
The Ordovician period lasted from approximately 485 to 443 million years ago and is notable for its evolutionary developments, particularly in marine ecosystems. It marked a time when a significant increase in biodiversity took place, including the first appearance of many mollusks and the early vertebrates.
