### Detailed Analysis of the English Word "What"

**Basic Information:**
- **Word:** What
- **Phonetics:** /wɑːt/
- **Part of Speech:** Pronoun, Adjective, Adverb, Conjunction
- **Translation:** 什么

**Definition:**
"What" is used to ask for information regarding someone or something. It is versatile, serving various functions in questions, exclamations, and clauses.

**Examples:**
- **Interrogative Uses:** "What is your name?" (你叫什么名字？)

**Synonyms:**
- Which
- That

**Antonyms:**
- None.

### Usage Across Tenses:

1. **Simple Present:**
   - "What do you want for dinner?" (你晚饭想吃什么？)

2. **Present Continuous:**
   - "What are you doing now?" (你现在在做什么？)

3. **Present Perfect:**
   - "What have you done?" (你做了什么？)

4. **Present Perfect Continuous:**
   - "What have you been doing all day?" (你一整天都在做什么？)

5. **Simple Past:**
   - "What did he say?" (他说了什么？)

6. **Past Continuous:**
   - "What were you doing when I called?" (我打电话时你在做什么？)

7. **Past Perfect:**
   - "What had happened before I arrived?" (在我到达之前发生了什么事？)

8. **Past Perfect Continuous:**
   - "What had they been discussing for so long?" (他们一直在讨论什么？)

9. **Simple Future:**
   - "What will you do tomorrow?" (你明天要做什么？)

10. **Future Continuous:**
    - "What will you be doing this time tomorrow?" (明天这个时候你会在做什么？)

11. **Future Perfect:**
    - "What will you have accomplished by next year?" (到明年你会完成什么？)

12. **Future Perfect Continuous:**
    - "What will she have been studying by graduation?" (到毕业时她将已经学习什么专业多久了？)

### Subjunctive Mood:
- "If I knew what to do, I would do it." (如果我知道该做什么，我就会去做。)

### Passive Voice:
- "What was decided at the meeting?" (会议上决定了什么？)

### Interrogative Sentences:
- "What time is it?" (现在几点了？)

### Negative Sentences:
- "I don't know what to say." (我不知道该说什么。)

### Common Phrases and Idioms:
- **Examples:**
  - "What's up?" (怎么了？/ 你好吗？)
  - "What the hell!" (搞什么鬼！)

- **Idioms:**
  - "What if" (如果...会怎么样)
  - "What for" (为了什么)
  - "No matter what" (无论如何)
  - "What's more" (而且；更重要的是)
  - "What about" (...怎么样)
  - "So what" (那又怎样)

### Conclusion:
The word "what" serves as a crucial part of English grammar, spanning various contexts and grammatical structures. Its flexibility allows it to be employed in simple questions, complex sentences, idiomatic expressions, and more, making it an essential term for effective communication.
