### Detailed Analysis of the Word "What"

#### Basic Information
- **Word**: What
- **Phonetics**: /wɑːt/
- **Part of Speech**: Pronoun, Adjective, Adverb, Conjunction
- **Translation**: 什么

#### Definition
"What" is primarily used to ask for information about something or someone, making it a versatile word in English.

#### Examples
- **Basic Question**: "What is your name?"  
  - *Translation*: 你叫什么名字？

#### Synonyms and Antonyms
- **Synonyms**: which, that
- **Antonyms**: None

#### Usage in Different Tenses

- **Simple Present**
  - "What do you want for dinner?"  
    - *Translation*: 你晚饭想吃什么？

- **Present Continuous**
  - "What are you doing now?"  
    - *Translation*: 你现在在做什么？

- **Present Perfect**
  - "What have you done?"  
    - *Translation*: 你做了什么？

- **Simple Past**
  - "What did he say?"  
    - *Translation*: 他说了什么？

- **Future Simple**
  - "What will you do tomorrow?"  
    - *Translation*: 你明天要做什么？

#### Additional Phrase Usage
- **Interrogative Sentences**
  - "What time is it?"  
    - *Translation*: 现在几点了？

- **Negative Sentences**
  - "I don't know what to say."  
    - *Translation*: 我不知道该说什么。

#### Phrasing and Idioms
- **Common Phrases**:
  - "What's up?"  
    - *Translation*: 怎么了？/ 你好吗？
  - "What the hell!"  
    - *Translation*: 搞什么鬼！

- **Idiomatic Expressions**:
  - "What if" (如果...会怎么样)
  - "No matter what" (无论如何)
  - "What's more" (而且；更重要的是)

With this detailed breakdown, "what" serves as a crucial element in ensuring clarity and obtaining information in both written and spoken English.
