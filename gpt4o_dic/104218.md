### Additional Context
The Jurchens were a significant ethnic group in the history of China, known for establishing the Jin dynasty, which ruled over large parts of northern China in the 12th and 13th centuries. They spoke a Tungusic language and had a distinct culture influenced by both their nomadic lifestyle and sedentary agricultural practices. Understanding their history helps provide insights into the dynamics of power and culture in East Asia during that period.
