### Additional Context

- **Overview**: Sclerodermite, often referred to as scleroderma, is an autoimmune condition where the body’s immune system attacks its own tissues, leading to excessive collagen production. This results in skin hardening and can affect internal organs.

- **Symptoms**: Patients may experience tightness of the skin, changes in skin color, and joint pain. In severe cases, it can impact lungs, heart, and kidneys.

- **Diagnosis**: Diagnosis typically involves clinical evaluations, blood tests for specific autoantibodies, and sometimes skin biopsies.

- **Treatment**: Management includes medications to control symptoms and related conditions, as well as physical therapy to maintain mobility.
