### Context and Usage
The phrase "in so many words" is commonly used in both spoken and written English to stress that something should be taken literally as stated, often eliminating ambiguity. It serves to highlight the explicit nature of a statement or explanation. 

### Examples in Daily Communication
1. **Clarifying Intent**: "When asked about the decision, she stated in so many words that she disagreed."
2. **Emphasizing Directness**: "He refused to answer, stating in so many words that it was none of our business."
