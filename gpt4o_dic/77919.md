### Overview of Genitives
- Genitives often appear in English as an apostrophe followed by 's' (e.g., "girl's bag," "teacher's desk"). 
- They can also be expressed using prepositions, like "of" (e.g., "the cover of the book"). 

### Common Uses
- **Possession**: To indicate that something belongs to someone (e.g., "Georgia's book").
- **Relationships**: To express various types of relationships (e.g., "the love of a mother").
- **Descriptions**: To provide descriptive information about an object (e.g., "a day of celebration").

### Forms of Genitives
The genitive case can manifest as:
- **Strong Genitive**: Using 's (e.g., "Mark's car").
- **Weak Genitive**: Using "of" (e.g., "the durability of the material").

### Conclusion
Understanding genitives is crucial for grasping possession and association in English and can enhance both written and spoken communication skills.
