### Contextual Overview
The term "Doryphoros" originates from ancient Greece and refers to a statue type that embodies the ideals of male beauty and physical prowess. The most renowned version is attributed to the sculptor Polykleitos, who used it to illustrate his canon of proportions. The Doryphoros not only represents martial readiness due to its stance and spear but also serves as an icon of the philosophical and aesthetic values of classical antiquity. The statue has been influential through the ages, impacting Western art concepts regarding the human figure and proportion.
