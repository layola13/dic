Here's a detailed analysis of the English word "what":

### Word Analysis

- **Word**: what
- **Phonetics**: /wɑːt/
- **Part of Speech**: pronoun, adjective, adverb, conjunction
- **Translation**: 什么
- **Definition**: Used to ask for information about someone or something.

### Examples
- **Basic Example**: What is your name?
- **Contextual Examples**:
  - **Simple Present**: 
    - What do you want for dinner?
    - Translation: 你晚饭想吃什么？
  - **Present Continuous**: 
    - What are you doing now?
    - Translation: 你现在在做什么？
  - **Present Perfect**: 
    - What have you done?
    - Translation: 你做了什么？
  - **Simple Past**: 
    - What did he say?
    - Translation: 他说了什么？
  - **Future Simple**: 
    - What will you do tomorrow?
    - Translation: 你明天要做什么？

### Grammar Usage
- **Interrogative Sentences**: 
  - What time is it? (现在几点了？)
  
- **Negative Sentences**:
  - I don't know what to say. (我不知道该说什么。)

### Phrases and Idioms
- **Common Phrases**:
  - What’s up? — 怎么了？/ 你好吗？
  - What the hell! — 搞什么鬼！
  
- **Idiomatic Expressions**:
  - What if — 如果...会怎么样
  - What for — 为了什么
  - No matter what — 无论如何
  - What’s more — 而且；更重要的是
  - What about — ...怎么样
  - So what — 那又怎样
  
### Synonyms and Antonyms
- **Synonyms**: which, that
- **Antonyms**: None

### Conclusion
The word "what" plays a versatile role in English grammar, primarily as an interrogative tool used to elicit information and clarify inquiries in various contexts. Whether in simple statements or more complex structures, "what" serves as a fundamental building block in communication.
