以下是英文单词“what”的详细解析：

### Word Overview
- **Word**: what
- **Phonetics**: /wɑːt/
- **Part of Speech**: pronoun, adjective, adverb, conjunction
- **Translation**: 什么

### Definition
"What" is used to ask for information about someone or something, making it essential in both inquiries and conversations.

### Example
- **Sentence**: What is your name?
- **Translation**: 你叫什么名字？

### Synonyms
- which
- that

### Usage
1. **Simple Present**:
   - What do you want for dinner? 
     - 你晚饭想吃什么？
   
2. **Present Continuous**:
   - What are you doing now? 
     - 你现在在做什么？

3. **Present Perfect**:
   - What have you done? 
     - 你做了什么？

4. **Present Perfect Continuous**:
   - What have you been doing all day? 
     - 你一整天都在做什么？

5. **Simple Past**:
   - What did he say? 
     - 他说了什么？

6. **Past Continuous**:
   - What were you doing when I called? 
     - 我打电话时你在做什么？

7. **Past Perfect**:
   - What had happened before I arrived? 
     - 在我到达之前发生了什么？

8. **Past Perfect Continuous**:
   - What had you been doing before the accident? 
     - 事故发生前你一直在做什么？

9. **Simple Future**:
   - What will you do tomorrow? 
     - 你明天要做什么？

10. **Future Continuous**:
    - What will you be doing this time tomorrow? 
      - 明天这个时候你会在做什么？

11. **Future Perfect**:
    - What will you have accomplished by next year? 
      - 到明年你会完成什么？

12. **Future Perfect Continuous**:
    - What will you have been doing for ten years? 
      - 到时候你将已经做这件事十年了？

13. **Subjunctive Mood**:
    - If I knew what to do, I would do it. 
      - 如果我知道该做什么，我就会去做。

14. **Passive Voice**:
    - What was decided at the meeting? 
      - 会议上决定了什么？

15. **Interrogative Sentences**:
    - What time is it? 
      - 现在几点了？

16. **Negative Sentences**:
    - I don't know what to say. 
      - 我不知道该说什么。

### Phrases and Idioms
- **Examples**:
  - What's up? (怎么了？/ 你好吗？)
  - What the hell! (搞什么鬼！)

- **Idioms**:
  - what if (如果...会怎么样)
  - what for (为了什么)
  - no matter what (无论如何)
  - what's more (而且；更重要的是)
  - what about (...怎么样)
  - so what (那又怎样) 

### Summary
"What" serves as a fundamental question word in English, allowing speakers to elicit information on a wide range of topics. Its various uses across different tenses and constructions make it versatile in both spoken and written forms.
