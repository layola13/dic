Here’s a detailed breakdown of the English word “what”:

### Word: What
- **Phonetics**: /wɑːt/
- **Part of Speech**: Pronoun, Adjective, Adverb, Conjunction
- **Translation**: 什么
- **Definition**: Used to ask for information about someone or something.

### Examples
- **Basic Question**: "What is your name?" (你叫什么名字？)
- **Information Request**: "What do you want for dinner?" (你晚饭想吃什么？)

### Synonyms
- which
- that

### Usage in Different Tenses
1. **Simple Present**:
   - "What is your name?" (你叫什么名字？)
   - "What do you want for dinner?" (你晚饭想吃什么？)

2. **Present Continuous**:
   - "What are you doing now?" (你现在在做什么？)
   - "What is she reading at the moment?" (她这会儿在读什么？)

3. **Present Perfect**:
   - "What have you done?" (你做了什么？)
   - "What has she achieved so far?" (到目前为止她取得了什么成就？)

4. **Past Simple**:
   - "What did he say?" (他说了什么？)
   - "What happened yesterday?" (昨天发生了什么事？)

5. **Future Simple**:
   - "What will you do tomorrow?" (你明天要做什么？)
   - "What will the weather be like next week?" (下周天气会怎样？)

### Phrases and Idioms
- **Common Phrases**:
  - "What's up?" (怎么了？/ 你好吗？)
  - "What the hell!" (搞什么鬼！)

- **Idiomatic Expressions**:
  - "What if" (如果...会怎么样)
  - "What for" (为了什么)
  - "No matter what" (无论如何)
  - "What's more" (而且；更重要的是)
  - "What about" (...怎么样)
  - "So what" (那又怎样)

### Notable Grammar Points
- **Subjunctive Mood**: 
  - "If I knew what to do, I would do it." (如果我知道该做什么，我就会去做。)
  
- **Passive Voice**: 
  - "What was decided at the meeting?" (会议上决定了什么？)
  
- **Interrogative Sentences**: 
  - "What time is it?" (现在几点了？)
  
- **Negative Sentences**: 
  - "I don't know what to say." (我不知道该说什么。)

This comprehensive overview of the word "what" encapsulates its meanings, usages, and variations across different contexts in English.
