Here's a detailed analysis of the English word "what":

### Word Overview
- **Word**: what
- **Phonetics**: /wɑːt/
- **Part of Speech**: pronoun, adjective, adverb, conjunction
- **Translation**: 什么

### Definition
"What" is primarily used to ask for information regarding people, objects, events, or ideas. It can introduce questions and inquiries, indicating a desire to know something specific.

### Examples of Usage
1. **Simple Present**: 
   - *Question*: What is your name? 
   - *Translation*: 你叫什么名字？
   - *Question*: What do you want for dinner? 
   - *Translation*: 你晚饭想吃什么？

2. **Present Continuous**: 
   - *Question*: What are you doing now? 
   - *Translation*: 你现在在做什么？
   - *Question*: What is she reading at the moment? 
   - *Translation*: 她这会儿在读什么？
   
3. **Present Perfect**: 
   - *Question*: What have you done? 
   - *Translation*: 你做了什么？
   - *Question*: What has she achieved so far? 
   - *Translation*: 到目前为止她取得了什么成就？

4. **Simple Past**: 
   - *Question*: What did he say? 
   - *Translation*: 他说了什么？
   - *Question*: What happened yesterday? 
   - *Translation*: 昨天发生了什么事？

5. **Future Simple**: 
   - *Question*: What will you do tomorrow? 
   - *Translation*: 你明天要做什么？
   - *Question*: What will the weather be like next week? 
   - *Translation*: 下周天气会怎样？

### Synonyms
- which
- that

### Antonyms
- (None listed)

### Usage in Sentences
- **Interrogative Sentences**: 
  - *Question*: What time is it? 
  - *Translation*: 现在几点了？
  - *Question*: What are you talking about? 
  - *Translation*: 你在说什么？
  
- **Negative Sentences**: 
  - *Example*: I don't know what to say. 
  - *Translation*: 我不知道该说什么。
  - *Example*: She didn't tell me what she wanted. 
  - *Translation*: 她没有告诉我她想要什么。

### Common Phrases and Idioms
- **Examples**:
  - "What's up?" (怎么了？/ 你好吗？)
  - "What the hell!" (搞什么鬼！)
  
- **Idioms**:
  - "what if" (如果...会怎么样)
  - "what for" (为了什么)
  - "no matter what" (无论如何)
  - "what's more" (而且；更重要的是)
  - "what about" (...怎么样)
  - "so what" (那又怎样)

### Contextual Use
"What" can be used across various grammatical structures, making it versatile in both spoken and written English. It is often one of the first words taught to English language learners due to its fundamental role in forming questions and engaging in dialogue. The usage varies with tense and mood, reflecting different nuances of inquiry. 

Overall, "what" plays a crucial role in communication, allowing individuals to seek clarification, information, and understanding.
