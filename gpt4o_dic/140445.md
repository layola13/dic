### Contextual Information
Okras, also known as lady's fingers, are commonly used in various culinary dishes, especially in Southern United States cooking and in many African and Asian cuisines. They are prized for their unique texture and the ability to thicken soups and stews due to their mucilaginous properties when cooked.
