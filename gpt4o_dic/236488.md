### Context
"Unobjectible" is a term often used in legal or formal contexts to describe information, arguments, or evidence that cannot be disputed or contradicted. Its usage emphasizes the strength or validity of claims, underscoring their acceptance without objections. This word combines the prefix "un-" (meaning "not") with "objectible," pointing to the absence of legitimate challenges.
