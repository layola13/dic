### Overview:
- **Uncausative verbs** signify actions that occur without an overt agent causing them. They lack the capability to take a direct object, contrasting with **causative verbs** that imply an action has been caused by someone or something. 

### Key Examples:
- Common uncausative verbs include **sleep**, **lie**, **arrive**, **exist**, and **die**. Understanding their usage supports clarity in both spoken and written English, aiding in correctly identifying the subject's relation to the action.
