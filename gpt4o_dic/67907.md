### Context and Usage
"Face the music" often refers to a situation where someone must deal with the unpleasant consequences of their actions or decisions. It is commonly used in scenarios involving accountability, such as professional settings or personal relationships. The idiom emphasizes the necessity of confronting reality, despite the discomfort it may bring.

### Examples in Sentences
1. **Consequence Avoidance**: "He thought he could escape the consequences, but eventually, he had to face the music."
2. **Personal Responsibility**: "She failed her exam and now has to face the music by explaining it to her parents."

By using this idiom, speakers convey the importance of taking responsibility for one's actions in a straightforward and relatable manner.
