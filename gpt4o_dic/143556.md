### Contextual Information

The term "outhector" derives from the name Hector, a figure from Greek mythology known for his strength and prowess in battle, particularly in the "Iliad." To "outhector" implies not just to overpower in a physical sense but to dominate someone psychologically or socially, creating an atmosphere of fear or submission. The term is often used in competitive contexts, such as debates, negotiations, or sports, highlighting aggressive tactics used to undermine opponents.
