Here's a detailed analysis of the English word "what":

### Overview
- **Word**: what
- **Phonetics**: /wɑːt/
- **Part of Speech**: Pronoun, Adjective, Adverb, Conjunction
- **Translation**: 什么

### Definition
"What" is primarily used to ask for information about someone or something. It serves both interrogative and exclamatory functions in English.

### Example
- **Sentence**: What is your name?
- **Translation**: 你叫什么名字？

### Synonyms
- which
- that

### Antonyms
- None listed

### Usage in Different Tenses

#### Simple Present
- **Questions**: 
  - What do you want for dinner? (你晚饭想吃什么？)

#### Present Continuous
- **Questions**: 
  - What are you doing now? (你现在在做什么？)

#### Present Perfect
- **Questions**: 
  - What have you done? (你做了什么？)

#### Simple Past
- **Questions**: 
  - What did he say? (他说了什么？)

#### Future Simple
- **Questions**: 
  - What will you do tomorrow? (你明天要做什么？)

### Subjunctive Mood
- **Examples**: 
  - If I knew what to do, I would do it. (如果我知道该做什么，我就会去做。)

### Passive Voice
- **Examples**: 
  - What was decided at the meeting? (会议上决定了什么？)

### Interrogative Sentences
- **Questions**: 
  - What time is it? (现在几点了？)

### Negative Sentences
- **Examples**: 
  - I don't know what to say. (我不知道该说什么。)

### Common Phrases
- **Examples**: 
  - What's up? (怎么了？/ 你好吗？)
  - What the hell! (搞什么鬼！)

### Idioms
- **Examples**: 
  - what if (如果...会怎么样)
  - what for (为了什么)
  - no matter what (无论如何)
  - what's more (而且；更重要的是)
  - what about (...怎么样)
  - so what (那又怎样)

This analysis presents "what" in various contexts, demonstrating its flexibility and utility in English communication.
