Here’s a detailed analysis of the English word "what":

### Word Overview
- **Word**: what
- **Phonetics**: /wɑːt/
- **Part of Speech**: pronoun, adjective, adverb, conjunction
- **Translation**: 什么

### Definition
"What" is primarily used to ask for information about a person or thing. It can also serve various grammatical functions in different contexts.

### Examples
- **Question**: What is your name?
  - **Translation**: 你叫什么名字？
- **Contextual Use**: 
  - "What do you want for dinner?" (你晚饭想吃什么？)

### Synonyms
- which
- that

### Usage in Various Tenses

#### Simple Present
- **Examples**:
  - What are you doing?
    - Translation: 你在做什么？
    
#### Present Continuous
- **Examples**:
  - What is she reading?
    - Translation: 她在读什么？

#### Present Perfect
- **Examples**:
  - What have you done?
    - Translation: 你做了什么？

#### Simple Past
- **Examples**:
  - What did he say?
    - Translation: 他说了什么？

#### Future
- **Examples**:
  - What will you do tomorrow?
    - Translation: 你明天要做什么？

### Subjunctive Mood
- **Examples**:
  - If I knew what to do, I would do it.
    - Translation: 如果我知道该做什么，我就会去做。

### Passive Voice
- **Examples**:
  - What was decided at the meeting?
    - Translation: 会议上决定了什么？

### Interrogative Sentences
- **Examples**:
  - What time is it?
    - Translation: 现在几点了？

### Negative Sentences
- **Examples**:
  - I don’t know what to say.
    - Translation: 我不知道该说什么。

### Phrases and Idioms
- **Common Phrases**:
  - What's up? (怎么了？/ 你好吗？)
  - What the hell! (搞什么鬼！)

- **Idioms**:
  - what if (如果...会怎么样)
  - what for (为了什么)
  - no matter what (无论如何)
  - what's more (而且；更重要的是)
