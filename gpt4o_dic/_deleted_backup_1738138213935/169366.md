### Word Analysis: "What"

- **Phonetics**: /wɑːt/
- **Part of Speech**: Pronoun, Adjective, Adverb, Conjunction
- **Translation**: 什么

#### Definition:
"What" is primarily used to ask for information about people, objects, or situations. It can function in various contexts depending on its grammatical role.

#### Example Sentence:
- **Usage**: "What is your name?"
  
#### Synonyms:
- Which
- That

#### Antonyms:
- None

### Usage Across Tenses:

- **Simple Present**: 
  - *Question*: What is your name?
  - *Translation*: 你叫什么名字？
  
- **Present Continuous**: 
  - *Question*: What are you doing now?
  - *Translation*: 你现在在做什么？

- **Present Perfect**: 
  - *Question*: What have you done?
  - *Translation*: 你做了什么？

- **Past Continuous**: 
  - *Question*: What were you doing when I called?
  - *Translation*: 我打电话时你在做什么？

- **Future Simple**: 
  - *Question*: What will you do tomorrow?
  - *Translation*: 你明天要做什么？

- **Subjunctive Mood**: 
  - *Example*: If I knew what to do, I would do it.
  - *Translation*: 如果我知道该做什么，我就会去做。

- **Passive Voice**: 
  - *Example*: What was decided at the meeting?
  - *Translation*: 会议上决定了什么？

### Phrases and Idioms:

#### Examples:
- **Phrase**: What's up?
  - *Translation*: 怎么了？ / 你好吗？

- **Phrase**: What the hell!
  - *Translation*: 搞什么鬼！

#### Idioms:
- **What if**: 如果...会怎么样
- **What for**: 为了什么
- **No matter what**: 无论如何
- **What's more**: 而且；更重要的是
- **What about**: ...怎么样
- **So what**: 那又怎样

This comprehensive analysis highlights the diverse usages and contexts in which "what" can be applied, making it a fundamental part of English communication.
