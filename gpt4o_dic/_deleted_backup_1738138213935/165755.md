Here's a detailed analysis of the English word "what" presented in a structured format:

### Word Analysis: "What"

- **Phonetics**: /wɑːt/
- **Part of Speech**: Pronoun, Adjective, Adverb, Conjunction
- **Translation**: 什么 (shénme)
- **Definition**: Used to ask for information about someone or something.

### Examples:
- **Usage in Questions**: 
  - "What is your name?" (你叫什么名字？)
  - "What do you want for dinner?" (你晚饭想吃什么？)

### Synonyms:
- which
- that

### Antonyms:
- (None)

### Grammatical Usage:
#### Simple Present:
- **Questions**: 
  - "What is your name?" 
  - "What do you want for dinner?"

#### Present Continuous:
- **Questions**:
  - "What are you doing now?" 
  - "What is she reading at the moment?"

#### Present Perfect:
- **Questions**:
  - "What have you done?" 
  - "What has she achieved so far?"

#### Present Perfect Continuous:
- **Questions**:
  - "What have you been doing all day?" 
  - "What has he been working on lately?"

#### Simple Past:
- **Questions**: 
  - "What did he say?" 
  - "What happened yesterday?"

#### Past Continuous:
- **Questions**:
  - "What were you doing when I called?" 
  - "What was she thinking about?"

#### Past Perfect:
- **Questions**:
  - "What had happened before I arrived?" 
  - "What had they discussed before the meeting?"

#### Past Perfect Continuous:
- **Questions**:
  - "What had you been doing before the accident?" 
  - "What had they been discussing for so long?"

#### Simple Future:
- **Questions**: 
  - "What will you do tomorrow?" 
  - "What will the weather be like next week?"

#### Future Continuous:
- **Questions**:
  - "What will you be doing this time tomorrow?" 
  - "What will they be discussing at the meeting?"

#### Future Perfect:
- **Questions**:
  - "What will you have accomplished by next year?" 
  - "What will have changed by then?"

#### Future Perfect Continuous:
- **Questions**:
  - "What will you have been doing for ten years?" 
  - "What will she have been studying by graduation?"

#### Subjunctive Mood:
- **Examples**:
  - "If I knew what to do, I would do it." 
  - "I wish I knew what was going on."

#### Passive Voice:
- **Examples**:
  - "What was decided at the meeting?" 
  - "What is being done to solve the problem?"

#### Interrogative Sentences:
- **Questions**:
  - "What time is it?" 
  - "What are you talking about?"

#### Negative Sentences:
- **Examples**:
  - "I don't know what to say."
  - "She didn't tell me what she wanted."

### Phrases:
#### Common Expressions:
- **Examples**:
  - "What's up?" (怎么了？/ 你好吗？)
  - "What the hell!" (搞什么鬼！)

#### Idioms:
- "what if" (如果...会怎么样)
- "what for" (为了什么)
- "no matter what" (无论如何)
- "what's more" (而且；更重要的是)
- "what about" (...怎么样)
- "so what" (那又怎样)

This comprehensive breakdown captures the various uses, forms, and contexts of the word "what," helping understanding and application in different grammatical structures.
