Here's a breakdown of the word "what":

### Word Information
- **Word**: What
- **Phonetics**: /wɑːt/
- **Part of Speech**: Pronoun, Adjective, Adverb, Conjunction
- **Translation**: 什么

### Definition
"What" is primarily used to ask for information about someone or something, making it a versatile word in English.

### Examples
- **Question**: What is your name?  
  **Translation**: 你叫什么名字？
  
- **Usage**: What do you want for dinner?  
  **Translation**: 你晚饭想吃什么？

### Synonyms
- Which
- That

### Usage in Different Tenses
#### Simple Present
- **Examples**:
  - What is your name?  
    Translation: 你叫什么名字？
  - What do you want for dinner?  
    Translation: 你晚饭想吃什么？

#### Present Continuous
- **Examples**:
  - What are you doing now?  
    Translation: 你现在在做什么？
  - What is she reading at the moment?  
    Translation: 她这会儿在读什么？

#### Present Perfect
- **Examples**:
  - What have you done?  
    Translation: 你做了什么？
  - What has she achieved so far?  
    Translation: 到目前为止她取得了什么成就？

#### Simple Past
- **Examples**:
  - What did he say?  
    Translation: 他说了什么？
  - What happened yesterday?  
    Translation: 昨天发生了什么事？

#### Future Simple
- **Examples**:
  - What will you do tomorrow?  
    Translation: 你明天要做什么？
  - What will the weather be like next week?  
    Translation: 下周天气会怎样？

### Phrases
- **Common Phrases**:
  - What's up?  
    Translation: 怎么了？/ 你好吗？
  - What the hell!  
    Translation: 搞什么鬼！

### Idioms
- What if: 如果...会怎么样
- What for: 为了什么
- No matter what: 无论如何
- What's more: 而且；更重要的是
- What about: ...怎么样
- So what: 那又怎样

### Additional Context
"What" can also be used in various sentence structures, including interrogative sentences, passive voice constructions, and within subjunctive mood contexts. This flexibility contributes to its frequent use in everyday language, making it essential for inquiries and expressing curiosity.
