### Context
"**Sedarim**" is primarily used in Jewish cultural and religious contexts, especially pertaining to traditions around Passover and other holidays. Each seder involves reading from the Haggadah, eating specific foods, and following set rituals that commemorate the Exodus from Egypt. The plural form reflects the multiple gatherings or instances of these traditional meals during festive observances.
