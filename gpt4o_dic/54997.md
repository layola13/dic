### **单词解析：dispurpose**

#### **Word**  
**dispurpose**  

#### **Phonetics**  
/dɪsˈpɜːrpəs/

#### **Part of Speech**  
verb  

#### **Translation**  
"使失去目标" 或 "使无目的"

#### **Definition**  
"To deprive of purpose or aim; to make purposeless."  
（使失去目标或目的；使无目的）

#### **Example**  
"The sudden change in plans dispurposed the team, leaving them uncertain about their next steps."  
（计划的突然改变使团队失去了目标，让他们对下一步感到不确定。）

#### **Synonyms**  
- discourage  
- demotivate  
- derail  

#### **Antonyms**  
- motivate  
- inspire  
- encourage  

#### **Usage**  
**Simple Present:**  
- "The lack of clear direction dispurposes the employees."  
  （缺乏明确的方向使员工失去了目标。）

**Present Continuous:**  
- "The constant changes are dispurposing the team, making it hard for them to focus."  
  （不断的改变正使得团队失去目标，使他们难以集中精力。）

**Simple Past:**  
- "The cancellation of the project dispurposed the entire department."  
  （项目的取消使整个部门失去了目标。）

**Future Tense:**  
- "If the management continues to ignore our concerns, it will dispurpose the entire workforce."  
  （如果管理层继续忽视我们的担忧，这将使整个员工队伍失去目标。）

#### **Phrases**  
**Examples:**  
- "The sudden shift in strategy dispurposed the team, leaving them confused and unmotivated."  
  （战略的突然转变使团队失去了目标，让他们感到困惑和缺乏动力。）

**Idioms:**  
- "It's easy to dispurpose someone if you constantly change the rules."  
  （如果你不断改变规则，很容易让某人失去目标。）
