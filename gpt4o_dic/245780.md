Here’s a detailed analysis of the word "what":

### Basic Information
- **Word**: *what*
- **Phonetics**: /wɑːt/
- **Part of Speech**: Pronoun, Adjective, Adverb, Conjunction
- **Translation**: 什么
- **Definition**: Used to ask for information about someone or something.

### Examples
- **Sentence Example**: "What is your name?" (你叫什么名字？)

### Synonyms
- **Similar Words**: ["which", "that"]

### Usage 
#### Simple Present
- **Questions**:
  - "What do you want for dinner?" (你晚饭想吃什么？)

#### Present Continuous
- **Questions**:
  - "What are you doing now?" (你现在在做什么？)

#### Present Perfect
- **Questions**:
  - "What have you done?" (你做了什么？)

#### Simple Past
- **Questions**:
  - "What did he say?" (他说了什么？)

#### Future Simple
- **Questions**:
  - "What will you do tomorrow?" (你明天要做什么？)

#### Negative Sentences
- "I don't know what to say." (我不知道该说什么。)

### Phrases
- **Common Phrases**:
  - "What's up?" (怎么了？/ 你好吗？)
  - "What the hell!" (搞什么鬼！)

### Idioms
- **Common Idioms**:
  - "What if" (如果...会怎么样)
  - "No matter what" (无论如何)

### Conclusion
The word *what* serves as a versatile interrogative and is fundamental in formulating questions across various tenses. It can express curiosity, seek clarification, and initiate discussions in both formal and informal contexts.
