Here's a detailed analysis of the English word "what":

### Word Analysis: "What"

- **Phonetics**: /wɑːt/
- **Part of Speech**: Pronoun, Adjective, Adverb, Conjunction
- **Translation**: 什么

#### Definition
"What" is used to ask for information about someone or something. It serves various functions in questions, exclamations, and clauses.

#### Examples
- **Question**: "What is your name?"  
  **Translation**: 你叫什么名字？
  
- **Exclamation**: "What a beautiful day!"  
  **Translation**: 多美的一天啊！

#### Synonyms
- Which
- That

#### Antonyms
- N/A

### Usage in Different Tenses

- **Simple Present**:
  - "What do you want for dinner?"  
    你晚饭想吃什么？

- **Present Continuous**:
  - "What are you doing now?"  
    你现在在做什么？

- **Present Perfect**:
  - "What have you done?"  
    你做了什么？

- **Simple Past**:
  - "What did he say?"  
    他说了什么？

- **Future Simple**:
  - "What will you do tomorrow?"  
    你明天要做什么？

### Subjunctive Mood
- "If I knew what to do, I would do it."  
  如果我知道该做什么，我就会去做。

### Passive Voice Examples
- "What was decided at the meeting?"  
  会议上决定了什么？

### Interrogative Sentences
- "What time is it?"  
  现在几点了？

### Negative Sentences
- "I don't know what to say."  
  我不知道该说什么。

### Common Phrases
- **Examples**:
  - "What's up?"  
    怎么了？/ 你好吗？
    
  - "What the hell!"  
    搞什么鬼！

- **Idioms**:
  - "What if?"  
    如果...会怎么样。
  
  - "What for?"  
    为了什么。
  
  - "No matter what."  
    无论如何。
    
  - "What's more?"  
    而且；更重要的是。

This analysis encapsulates the various functions, meanings, and usages of the word "what," providing a comprehensive understanding of its role in the English language.
