### Contextual Explanation
"Counter-potent" is primarily used in scientific and pharmacological contexts to describe substances that diminish or negate the effectiveness of other substances or agents. In practice, it suggests a relationship where one agent undermines the action of another, often essential in fields like medicine or biochemistry to assess interactions between drugs or treatments.
