Here's a detailed analysis of the English word "what":

### Word Analysis
- **Word**: what
- **Phonetics**: /wɑːt/
- **Part of Speech**: pronoun, adjective, adverb, conjunction
- **Translation**: 什么

### Definition
"What" is used primarily to ask questions, requesting information about people, objects, or ideas.

### Examples
- **Interrogative**: "What is your name?" (你叫什么名字？)
- **Contextual Use**: "What do you want for dinner?" (你晚饭想吃什么？)

### Synonyms
- **Which**
- **That**

### Usage in Different Tenses
1. **Simple Present**
   - "What is your name?" (你叫什么名字？)
   - "What do you want for lunch?" (你午饭想吃什么？)
   
2. **Present Continuous**
   - "What are you doing now?" (你现在在做什么？)
   - "What is she reading at the moment?" (她这会儿在读什么？)

3. **Present Perfect**
   - "What have you done?" (你做了什么？)
   - "What has she achieved so far?" (到目前为止她取得了什么成就？)

4. **Simple Past**
   - "What did he say?" (他说了什么？)
   - "What happened yesterday?" (昨天发生了什么事？)

5. **Future Tenses**
   - Simple Future: "What will you do tomorrow?" (你明天要做什么？)
   - Future Continuous: "What will you be doing this time tomorrow?" (明天这个时候你会在做什么？)

### Phrases and Idioms
- **Examples**:
  - "What's up?" (怎么了？/ 你好吗？)
  - "What the hell!" (搞什么鬼！)

- **Idioms**:
  - "what if" (如果...会怎么样)
  - "what for" (为了什么)
  - "no matter what" (无论如何)
  - "what's more" (而且；更重要的是)
  - "what about" (...怎么样)
  - "so what" (那又怎样)

### Summary
The word "what" serves various grammatical functions and is essential for inquiry in English. It enables speakers to seek clarification or information across a wide range of contexts, providing a foundational tool for communication.
