Here’s a detailed analysis of the English word "what":

### Basic Information
- **Word**: what
- **Phonetics**: /wɑːt/
- **Part of Speech**: pronoun, adjective, adverb, conjunction
- **Translation**: 什么

### Definition
"What" is primarily used to ask for information about a person or thing, making it a fundamental word in English for inquiries and clarifications.

### Examples
- **Common Usage**: 
  - *What is your name?* (你叫什么名字？)
  - *What do you want for dinner?* (你晚饭想吃什么？)

### Synonyms
- which
- that

### Usage in Different Tenses

1. **Simple Present**
   - **Questions**: 
     - *What is your name?* (你叫什么名字？)
     - *What do you want for dinner?* (你晚饭想吃什么？)

2. **Present Continuous**
   - **Questions**:
     - *What are you doing now?* (你现在在做什么？)
     - *What is she reading?* (她在读什么？)

3. **Present Perfect**
   - **Questions**:
     - *What have you done?* (你做了什么？)
     - *What has she achieved?* (她取得了什么成就？)

4. **Past Tenses**
   - **Simple Past**:
     - *What did he say?* (他说了什么？)
   - **Past Continuous**:
     - *What were you doing?* (你在做什么？)

5. **Future Tenses**
   - **Simple Future**:
     - *What will you do tomorrow?* (你明天要做什么？)

### Other Sentence Structures

- **Subjunctive Mood**:
  - *If I knew what to do, I would do it.* (如果我知道该做什么，我就会去做。)

- **Passive Voice**:
  - *What was decided at the meeting?* (会议上决定了什么？)

- **Interrogative Sentences**:
  - *What time is it?* (现在几点了？)

- **Negative Sentences**:
  - *I don't know what to say.* (我不知道该说什么。)

### Common Phrases and Idioms

- **Examples**:
  - *What's up?* (怎么了？)
  - *What the hell!* (搞什么鬼！)

- **Idioms**:
  - *what if* (如果...会怎么样)
  - *what for* (为了什么)
  - *no matter what* (无论如何)
  - *what's more* (而且，更重要的是)
  - *what about* (...怎么样)
  - *so what* (那又怎样)

This comprehensive breakdown illustrates the versatility and significance of "what" in English language usage.
