### Word Analysis: "What"

- **Word:** what
- **Phonetics:** /wɑːt/
- **Part of Speech:** pronoun, adjective, adverb, conjunction
- **Translation:** 什么
- **Definition:** Used to ask for information about someone or something.

#### Examples:
- **Basic Usage:** "What is your name?" (你叫什么名字？)

#### Synonyms:
- which
- that

#### Antonyms:
- None

#### Usage:
1. **Simple Present:** 
   - Q: What do you want for dinner? (你晚饭想吃什么？)

2. **Present Continuous:** 
   - Q: What are you doing now? (你现在在做什么？)

3. **Present Perfect:** 
   - Q: What have you done? (你做了什么？)

4. **Present Perfect Continuous:** 
   - Q: What have you been doing all day? (你一整天都在做什么？)

5. **Simple Past:** 
   - Q: What did he say? (他说了什么？)

6. **Past Continuous:** 
   - Q: What were you doing when I called? (我打电话时你在做什么？)

7. **Past Perfect:** 
   - Q: What had happened before I arrived? (在我到达之前发生了什么事？)

8. **Past Perfect Continuous:** 
   - Q: What had you been doing before the accident? (事故发生前你一直在做什么？)

9. **Simple Future:** 
   - Q: What will you do tomorrow? (你明天要做什么？)

10. **Future Continuous:** 
    - Q: What will you be doing this time tomorrow? (明天这个时候你会在做什么？)

11. **Future Perfect:** 
    - Q: What will you have accomplished by next year? (到明年你会完成什么？)

12. **Future Perfect Continuous:** 
    - Q: What will you have been doing for ten years? (到时候你将已经做这件事十年了？)

13. **Subjunctive Mood:** 
    - "If I knew what to do, I would do it." (如果我知道该做什么，我就会去做。)

14. **Passive Voice:** 
    - "What was decided at the meeting?" (会议上决定了什么？)

15. **Interrogative Sentences:** 
    - Q: What time is it? (现在几点了？)

16. **Negative Sentences:** 
   - "I don't know what to say." (我不知道该说什么。)

#### Phrases:
- **Common Expressions:**
  - "What's up?" (怎么了？/ 你好吗？)
  - "What the hell!" (搞什么鬼！)

- **Idioms:**
  - "what if" (如果...会怎么样)
  - "what for" (为了什么)
  - "no matter what" (无论如何)
  - "what's more" (而且；更重要的是)
  - "what about" (...怎么样)
  - "so what" (那又怎样)

This comprehensive breakdown captures the various usages, examples, phrases, and contexts in which "what" is employed in English.
