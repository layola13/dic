### Word Analysis: "what"

- **Phonetics**: /wɑːt/
- **Part of Speech**: Pronoun, Adjective, Adverb, Conjunction
- **Translation**: 什么

#### Definition
"what" is primarily used to request information about something or someone, serving as a fundamental interrogative word in English.

#### Examples
- **Question**: What is your name?
  - **Translation**: 你叫什么名字？
- **Question**: What do you want for dinner?
  - **Translation**: 你晚饭想吃什么？

### Usage in Tenses

#### Simple Present
- **What is your name?** (你叫什么名字？)
- **What do you want for dinner?** (你晚饭想吃什么？)

#### Present Continuous
- **What are you doing now?** (你现在在做什么？)
- **What is she reading at the moment?** (她这会儿在读什么？)

#### Present Perfect
- **What have you done?** (你做了什么？)
- **What has she achieved so far?** (到目前为止她取得了什么成就？)

#### Simple Past
- **What did he say?** (他说了什么？)
- **What happened yesterday?** (昨天发生了什么事？)

#### Future Simple
- **What will you do tomorrow?** (你明天要做什么？)
- **What will the weather be like next week?** (下周天气会怎样？)

### Phrases and Idioms
- **Examples**:
  - **What's up?** (怎么了？/ 你好吗？)
  - **What the hell!** (搞什么鬼！)
  
- **Idioms**:
  - **what if** (如果...会怎么样)
  - **what for** (为了什么)
  - **no matter what** (无论如何)
  - **what's more** (而且；更重要的是)
  - **what about** (...怎么样)
  - **so what** (那又怎样)

### Synonyms
- **which**
- **that**

### Antonyms
- None

### Additional Context
In various contexts, "what" can indicate surprise, disbelief, or inquiry, making it a versatile component of everyday English conversation. The usage across different tenses illustrates its importance in forming questions and seeking clarity in communication.
