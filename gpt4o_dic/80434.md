### Additional Context
Gondor is a prominent setting in Tolkien's "The Lord of the Rings" and "The Hobbit." It symbolizes hope and resistance against evil. The kingdom is characterized by its leadership, particularly under figures like Aragorn and Denethor, and its architecture, especially the city of Minas Tirith, which is central to the narrative.
