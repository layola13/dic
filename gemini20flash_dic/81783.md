The word "gremlin" refers to a mythical creature often blamed for malfunctions, especially in machinery. It is a noun with the phonetic transcription /ˈɡremlɪn/. Synonyms include "imp," "sprite," "troublemaker," and "mischief-maker." There are no direct antonyms for the word "gremlin."

The term is often used when people face unexplained technical issues or errors. For example, one might say "gremlins at work" to describe a situation where something is malfunctioning for an unknown reason. The origin of the word comes from airmen during World War II, who would blame these imaginary creatures for issues with their aircraft. The concept of gremlins has also been used in popular culture, such as movies and books, to represent mischievous or problematic entities.
